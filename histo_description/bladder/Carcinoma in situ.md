# Carcinoma in situ

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Cytological features
  + Nuclear enlargement (often 5 - 6 times the size of a lymphocyte), with or without pleomorphism
  + May have prominent nucleoli, irregular nuclear contour or uneven chromatin distribution
  + Frequent mitotic figures seen in the mid to upper layers of the urothelium; atypical figures may be present ([Am J Surg Pathol 2001;25:356](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11224606), [Pathology 2021;53:86](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33234229), [J Natl Compr Canc Netw 2009;7:48](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19176205), [Adv Anat Pathol 2021;28:179](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34128483))
* Architectural features
  + Loss of cellular polarity (urothelial cells lose their perpendicular orientation to the basement membrane)
  + Nuclear crowding with or without nuclear overlapping
  + Irregular thickness characterized by hyperplasia, attenuation or denudation
* Patterns of urothelial CIS
  + Large cell pleomorphic: abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm and nuclear pleomorphism
  + Large cell nonpleomorphic: abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm without nuclear pleomorphism
  + Small cell: scant eosinophilic cytoplasm without nuclear pleomorphism
  + Clinging: variable denudation of the urothelium with a patchy monolayer of atypical cells
  + Pagetoid: clusters or isolated atypical cells within the urothelium
  + Undermining / overriding: presence of atypical cells that undermine / override normal urothelium adjacent to another pattern of urothelial CIS
  + Micropapillary: slender tufts of CIS morphologically similar to serous borderline tumor of the ovary; absence of fibrovascular core ([Hum Pathol 2012;43:2124](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22939957))
  + Plasmacytoid: round nuclear shape with eccentric, enlarged nuclei shape and globular eosinophilic cytoplasm ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:1638](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31368912))
  + With glandular differentiation: atypical urothelial cells characterized by columnar epithelium with apical cytoplasm ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:971](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29683821))
* Urothelial CIS may be seen in association with inflammation and vascular congestion of the underlying lamina propria ([J Natl Compr Canc Netw 2009;7:48](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19176205))
