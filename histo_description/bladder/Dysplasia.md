# Dysplasia

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Flat urothelial lesions with normal thickness but may be increased or decreased ([Hum Pathol 2010;41:155](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19762067))
* No maturation in basal and intermediate cell layers; umbrella cells are usually present
* Mild degree of loss of polarity ([Hum Pathol 2010;41:155](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19762067))
* Slight nuclear enlargement with variation in nuclear size and shape, hyperchromasia but not to the severity of urothelial carcinoma in situ
* Early low grade papillary urothelial carcinoma for flat dysplasia with foci of early papillary formation ([Adv Anat Pathol 2021;28:179](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34128483))
* Some authors report a lack of reproducible criteria to distinguish from atypia of unknown significance ([Histopathology 2019;74:68](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30565304))
