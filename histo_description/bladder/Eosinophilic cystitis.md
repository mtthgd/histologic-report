# Eosinophilic cystitis

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Hispathological findgings can be divided in "acute" and "chronic" phase:
* Acute phase: prominent eosinophilic infiltrate (Yamada and Taguchi criteria are 20 or more eosinophils per five 20x fields) with edema and occasional muscle necrosis; Charcot-Leyden crystals may be present ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2009;133:289](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19195972))
* Chronic phase: fewer eosinophils but more prominent mast cells, plasma cells and muscle fibrosis
