# Lymphoepithelioma-like

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Large pleomorphic nuclei with prominent nucleoli, ill defined borders and frequent mitosis
* Arranged in syncytial sheets, nests or individual cells with abundant mixed inflammatory cells that often obscure the neoplastic cells ([Am J Surg Pathol 1994;18:466](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8172321))
* Nonneoplastic cells are a mixture of polyclonal B and T lymphocytes, histiocytes, eosinophils, plasma cells and occasional neutrophils or eosinophils that invade the epithelial nests; T cells usually predominate ([Histopathology 2019;74:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30565299))
* May be pure or mixed, either predominant or focal; if mixed, consider recording proportion of subtype ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2009;2:194](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19079656))
  + May be admixed with conventional urothelial carcinoma, urothelial carcinoma with squamous differentiation, glandular differentiation or sarcomatoid components ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:474](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21383609))
* Surface urothelium may demonstrate concurrent carcinoma in situ (CIS), up to 50% of cases in one report or papillary urothelial carcinoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:474](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21383609))
