# Nested

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Anastomosing, irregularly sized nests lacking cytoarchitectural orientation to overlying urothelium and with more irregular projections into stroma, particularly at deep aspect ([Mod Pathol 2009;22:S96](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19494856))
* Overlying urothelium is usually unremarkable
* Cytologically bland, usually with focal atypia
* Low to intermediate nuclear grade and occasional nuclear enlargement and greater cytologic atypia in deep aspects of tumor ([Histopathology 2019;74:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30565299), [Mod Pathol 2009;22:S96](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19494856))
* May exhibit small tubular lumens which may contain necrotic cellular debris ([Histopathology 2019;74:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30565299), [Stanford Medicine: Nested Variant Urothelial (Transitional Cell) Carcinoma [Accessed 19 April 2021]](http://surgpathcriteria.stanford.edu/bladder/tcc-nested-variant-transitional-urothelial-carcinoma/))
* Stroma may be normal or desmoplastic
* A component of conventional urothelial carcinoma is present in 64% of cases ([Hum Pathol 2010;41:163](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19800100))
