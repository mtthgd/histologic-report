# Sarcomatoid variant

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Most common component is undifferentiated high grade spindle cell sarcoma ([Histopathology 2019;74:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30565299))
* Sarcomatoid areas admixed with conventional high grade urothelial carcinoma
* Spindle cell component may account for 10% to > 60% of tumor
* Epithelial component has urothelial differentiation or less commonly squamous or glandular differentiation
* Highly variable and can mimic nonepithelial neoplasms ([J Biol Chem 2019;294:1579](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30530497))
* Most common heterologous element is osteosarcoma, followed by chondrosarcoma, rhabdomyosarcoma, leiomyosarcoma, liposarcoma and angiosarcoma ([Histopathology 2019;74:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30565299))
