# Schistosomiasis bilharziasis

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Bladder mucosa with ulceration, necrosis and mixed inflammatory infiltrate of lymphocytes, neutrophils and eosinophils ([Hum Pathol 1986;17:333](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3082740))
* Calcified eggs with chronic granulomatous inflammation detected in the bladder mucosa and muscularis ([BMC Clin Pathol 2018;18:13](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30519144))
* Calcified eggs measure approximately 120 - 170 μm with terminal spine (*Schistosoma haematobium*) or lateral spine (*Schistosoma mansoni*) ([Clin Microbiol Rev 2015;28:939](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26224883))
* Flattening of bladder mucosa with keratinizing [squamous cell metaplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladdersquamousmetaplasia.html) or [intestinal metaplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderintestinalmetaplasia.html) ([Hum Pathol 1986;17:333](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3082740))
* Fibrosis with thickening of the bladder wall ([Hum Pathol 1986;17:333](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3082740))
* Infiltrating dysplastic squamous cells with keratinization present in schistosomiasis associated [squamous cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladdersquamous.html)
