# BCORCCNB3 sarcoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Round or spindle cells arranged in sheets or fascicles
* Angulated and hyperchromatic nuclei with fine chromatin and inconspicuous nucleoli
* Wide range of mitosis (5 - 60/10 high power fields, median of 30) ([Pediatr Blood Cancer 2014;61:2191](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25176412))
* Small foci of necrosis (50 - 90%)
* Significant variation in cellularity and morphological patterns, usually with an abrupt transition:
  + Small clusters of epithelioid cells ([Virchows Arch 2017;470:373](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28197724))
  + Nests and cords of tumor cells
  + Myxoid background (50%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:604](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29300189))
  + Collagenous stroma (36%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:604](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29300189))
  + Hemangiopericytic pattern
  + Whorling pattern
  + Hemorrhagic / telangiectatic areas
