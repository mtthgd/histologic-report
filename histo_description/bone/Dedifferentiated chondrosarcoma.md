# Dedifferentiated chondrosarcoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Conventional chondrosarcoma juxtaposed with an abrupt transition to high grade pleomorphic or spindle cell sarcoma
* Cartilaginous component ranges from an enchondroma-like appearance to grade 1 or grade 2 chondrosarcoma
* High grade dedifferentiated component usually has the appearance of high grade undifferentiated pleomorphic sarcoma or osteosarcoma
* Less frequently, it may have features of angiosarcoma, leiomyosarcoma, rhabdomyosarcoma
* Can even have epithelial differentiation to include squamous and adamantinoma-like morphologies ([Oncol Res Treat 2018;41:456](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29902785), [Am J Surg Pathol 1996;20:293](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8772782), [Pathol Res Pract 2017;213:698](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28551382))
* Amount of dedifferentiation is highly variable and can range from 2 - 98% ([Cancer 2006;106:2682](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16691621))
