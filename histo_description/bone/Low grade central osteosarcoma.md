# Low grade central osteosarcoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Mildly to moderately cellular fascicles of spindle (fibroblast-like) cells with mild nuclear atypia embedded in a fibrosclerotic stroma
* Neoplastic bone component which typically consists of irregular anastomosing long and thick bony trabeculae, often in parallel arrangement
* Bone is woven or lamellar
* Pagetoid bone may be present ([Mod Pathol 2004;17:288](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14976532))
* Invasive growth pattern (infiltration of the medullary spaces with encasement of preexisting trabeculae)
* Cortical destruction and soft tissue infiltration may be present
* Low mitotic activity ([Cancer 1977;40:1337](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/269000))
* Cartilage formation may be focally present ([Cancer 1990;65:1418](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2306687))
* Some tumors focally lack bone matrix
* Can progress high grade sarcoma (dedifferentiation) ([Hum Pathol 2000;31:615](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10836302))
* High grade areas often show high grade osteosarcoma histology
* Variant morphology
  + Fibrous dysplasia-like pattern of bone deposition ([Cancer 1990;65:1418](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2306687), [Cancer 1993;71:338](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8422626), [Mod Pathol 2010;23:1279](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20601938))
  + Extensive fibrous zone resembling desmoplastic fibroma ([Cancer 1990;65:1418](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2306687), [Cancer 1993;71:338](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8422626), [Mod Pathol 2010;23:1279](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20601938))
  + Prominent cartilage cap mimicking osteochondroma ([Mod Pathol 2010;23:1279](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20601938))
