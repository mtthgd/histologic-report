Microscopic (histologic) description
  • Once liberated into the joint space, loose bodies may become embedded in synovium, leading to resorption or serving as a nidus for new cartilage deposition
  • Newly formed fibrous or hyaline cartilage may undergo endochondral ossification, forming concentric layers around the loose body
  • As the loose body grows, the central portion may undergo necrosis and calcification as it becomes unable to be supported by the diffusion of synovial fluid
  • Reference: J Orthop Sci 2006;11:607
