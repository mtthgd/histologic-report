# AML with BCRABL1

Microscopic (histologic) description

* AML with *BCR::ABL1* has a broad morphologic spectrum, which includes AML with minimal differentiation (FAB M0), without maturation (FAB M1) or with monocytic differentiation (FAB M5) ([Haematologica 1996;81:423](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8952155), [Leuk Lymphoma 2013;54:138](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22691121))
* AML with *BCR::ABL1* demonstrates a marrow cellularity of 80%, which is lower than the 98% median marrow of CML MBP ([Am J Clin Pathol 2007;127:642](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17369142))
* In comparison to CML MBP, the bone marrow in AML with *BCR::ABL1* shows lower myeloid/erythroid ratio at diagnosis (median: 2.0 versus 4.8) and a lower bone marrow aspirate basophil percentage (median: 0% versus 4.5%) ([Am J Clin Pathol 2007;127:642](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17369142))
* Dwarf megakaryocytes are rarely seen ([Leuk Lymphoma 2013;54:138](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22691121))
