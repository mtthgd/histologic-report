# Chronic myeloid leukemia CML BCRABL1 positive

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Bone marrow (chronic phase):
  + Hypercellular marrow with absolute myeloid hyperplasia and myelocyte bulge
  + Myeloid to erythroid ratio ≥ 10:1
  + Layer of immature granulocytes (5 - 10 cells in thickness) around the bone trabeculae (2 - 3 cells layer in normal marrow)
  + No significant dysplasia
  + Increased basophils and eosinophils ([Intern Med 2011;50:501](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21372467))
  + Blasts Normal / slightly decreased megakaryocytes; 40 - 50% show moderate to marked megakaryocytes proliferation
  + Megakaryocytes often smaller and hyposegmented with dwarf morphology
  + Pseudo-Gaucher cells are common ([Leukemia 1998;12:233](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9519787))
* Bone marrow (accelerated phase):
  + 10 - 19% blasts ([Am Soc Clin Oncol Educ Book 2015;e381](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25993200))
  + Large clusters or sheets of small, abnormal megakaryocytes associated with marked reticulin or collagen fibrosis may be considered presumptive evidence of accelerated phase
* Bone marrow (blast phase):
  + ≥ 20% blasts in marrow or blood or the presence of an extramedullary proliferation of blasts ([Leuk Lymphoma 2014;55:1451](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24050507))
  + Approximately 70% have myeloid blasts (may include neutrophilic, monocytic, megakaryocytic, basophilic, eosinophilic or erythroid blasts) and approximately 30% have lymphoid blasts (usually B lymphoblasts but T and NK blasts have been reported) ([J Clin Invest 2010;120:2254](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20592475))
