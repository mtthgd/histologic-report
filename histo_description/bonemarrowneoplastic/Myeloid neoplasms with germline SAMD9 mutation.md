# Myeloid neoplasms with germline SAMD9 mutation

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Bone marrow is typically hypocellular for age, with or without dysplastic features ([Virchows Arch 2023;482:113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36445482))
* Baseline megakaryocytic atypia (small hypolobated forms, separated nuclear lobes) with or without erythroid atypia is common ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2023;40:429](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37507252))
  + Morphologically abnormal megakaryocytes with monosomy 7 can be seen in patients with normal, stable platelet counts and may not be indicative of progression to MDS
* Progression to MDS should be considered in the context of new or worsening cytopenias, multilineage dysplasia, increased blasts or genetic results showing monosomy 7 or acquisition of somatic mutations in other cancer related genes
