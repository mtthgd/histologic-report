# PDGFRA rearrangement

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Monocytes and basophil counts are normal
* Hypercellular marrow with increased eosinophils and its precursors (see [Microscopic [histologic] images](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/myeloproliferativePDGFRA.html#myeloproliferativePDGFRAmicro)); clusters or scattered, increased spindle shaped CD25+ or CD2- mast cells present ([Blood 2003;101:4660](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12676775))
* Reticulin fibrosis is identified; sometimes can be normal ([Blood 2004;103:473](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14504092))
* Increased blasts identified in accelerated / blastic phase of myeloproliferative neoplasms or de novo acute myeloid leukemia or T cell acute lymphoblastic leukemia (see [Microscopic [histologic] images](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/myeloproliferativePDGFRA.html#myeloproliferativePDGFRAmicro))
* Tissue eosinophilic infiltrate forming eosinophilic microabscesses or identifiable Charcot-Leyden crystals
