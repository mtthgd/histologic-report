# Adenosis

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Simple adenosis: proliferation of acini, with 2 layers (inner epithelial and outer myoepithelial), surrounded by basement membrane, without distortion of lobular architecture
* [Adenomyoepithelial adenosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastadenomyoepithelialadenosis.html): adenosis, with proliferation of glands (2 layers) with prominent myoepithelial cells
* [Apocrine adenosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastapocrineadenosis.html): adenosis, with proliferation of glands (2 layers) with predominant apocrine cytology
* [Sclerosing adenosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastsclerosingadenosis.html): adenosis, with proliferation of glands (2 layers) with glandular compression and (mild to marked) distortion due to stromal fibrosis / sclerosis; entrapped secretions frequently become calcified
* [Tubular adenosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breasttubularadenoma.html): adenosis with haphazard proliferation of branching tubules (2 layers)
* Nodular adenosis / adenosis tumor: adenosis that is mass forming
* Cytologic atypia is uncommon
* See also separate topics for specific subtypes
