Microscopic (histologic) description
  • Infiltrating tumor cells with apocrine morphology: abundant eosinophilic and granular cytoplasm, well defined cell borders, enlarged round nuclei and prominent nucleoli
  • Apocrine morphology in > 90% of tumor cells
  • Typically high grade / poorly differentiated
  • Often associated with ductal carcinoma in situ (DCIS) with apocrine features
