# BRCA associated carcinoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Majority of *BRCA1* and *BRCA2* breast cancers are invasive ductal adenocarcinoma (~74%) ([Crit Rev Oncol Hematol 2006;59:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16530420))
* Specific features of *BRCA1* associated breast cancer
  + Higher frequency of invasive breast carcinoma, NST with medullary pattern and basal-like features than sporadic breast cancer (13% versus 2%) ([Lancet 1997;349:1505](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9167459), [Mod Pathol 2010;23:S46](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20436502))
  + Mostly (66 - 100%) poorly differentiated grade 3 carcinoma with syncytial appearance, large pleomorphic cells and indistinct cell membranes ([Crit Rev Oncol Hematol 2006;59:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16530420), [Cell Oncol (Dordr) 2011;34:71](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21336636))
  + Usually well demarcated with pushing borders, solid growth and prominent lymphoplasmacytic infiltrate ([Crit Rev Oncol Hematol 2006;59:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16530420), [Histopathology 2023;82:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36468211))
  + A high proportion (~48%) show extensive areas of necrosis ([Crit Rev Oncol Hematol 2006;59:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16530420))
  + Low grade subtypes such as tubular carcinoma are rare ([J Clin Oncol 2010;28:5265](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21079137))
* Specific features of *BRCA2* associated breast cancer
  + More heterogeneous in grade and spectrum of histological type ([Mod Pathol 2010;23:S46](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20436502))
  + Generally low to intermediate grade compared to *BRCA1* associated breast cancer ([Crit Rev Oncol Hematol 2006;59:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16530420), [Histopathology 2023;82:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36468211))
  + Studies have documented a higher incidence of tubular, lobular and cribriform carcinomas ([Mod Pathol 2010;23:S46](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20436502), [Histopathology 2023;82:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36468211))
