# Core biopsy

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Number and size of cores per block / slide can vary significantly depending on the size of the biopsy needle, number of cores obtained and laboratory protocols ([Breast Cancer Res Treat 2011;132:917](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21698409), [Br J Radiol 2017;90:20160594](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27648482))
* Cores may be separated into those containing calcifications and those without calcifications if this is the main indication for biopsy ([Breast Cancer Res Treat 2011;132:917](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21698409), [Br J Radiol 2017;90:20160594](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27648482))
* Blocks may be later radiographed to assess for calcifications if not found on initial slides
* Multiple levels per block, with intervening unstained slides, are often performed to ensure adequate microscopic assessment of the tissue, diagnostic stains or biomarker studies
* Grossly, H&E stained breast core biopsy slides tend to have a pink appearance for benign and blue appearance for malignant lesions; however, there are many exceptions to this general rule of thumb:
  + Exception: pink appearing slide from a malignancy with low tumor volume or low cellularity tumor on microscopic evaluation (e.g., scant low grade [invasive lobular carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantlobularclassic.html))
  + Exception: blue appearing slide of a benign lesion with abundant proliferation on microscopic evaluation (e.g., benign [intraductal papilloma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastpapilloma.html) with florid [usual ductal hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastepithelialductalhyperplasia.html))
* Core biopsy site can range from a small area of fibrous tissue with mild myofibroblast and vascular proliferation or may have significant associated [fat necrosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastfatnecrosis.html), inflammation, hemosiderin or hematoidin blood breakdown products, blood and hematoma, depending on the timing of the biopsy and subsequent excision, degree of tissue damage from the biopsy and patient wound healing
* Core biopsy site may have a defect in the tissue from the removal of the clip
* Core biopsy site often displays foreign material or other features specific to the [biopsy clip or marking device](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastbiopsymarkingdevices.html) deployed
