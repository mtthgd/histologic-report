# Cystic hypersecretory hyperplasia

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Dilated cysts with intraluminal homogeneous thyroid-like material consisting of acellular, eosinophilic, colloid-like secretions ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2003;127:e389](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12951989), [Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:1670](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11117789))
  + Cysts are lined by a single orderly layer of flat, cuboidal or columnar epithelium
  + Epithelial lining cells are monotonous with low N:C ratios, bland nuclei, eosinophilic cytoplasm and focal secretory changes
* Frequent, artifactual shrinking / retraction of cyst contents away from the lining epithelium as well as the presence of parallel cracks in the secretion ([Cancer 1988;61:1611](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2832048))
* Occasional spillage of cyst contents into adjacent stroma through disrupted cyst lining, creating inflammatory milieu of lymphocytes, foamy histiocytes and focal hemorrhage ([Cancer 1988;61:1611](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2832048), [Cytopathology 2002;13:175](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12060081))
* CHH frequently merges with pregnancy-like (pseudolactational) hyperplasia ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:789](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15166671))
  + CHH / PLH cases tend to contain DCIS, usually micropapillary or cystic hypersecretory types ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:789](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15166671))
* CHH with atypia (atypical CHH)
  + Mild to moderate degree of cytologic or architectural atypia without fully attaining the level of DCIS ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24121179))
    - Nuclear pleomorphism, hyperchromasia, epithelial crowding with loss of polarity, increased N:C ratios, enlarged nucleoli, rare mitotic figures ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:1670](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11117789), [Cancer 1988;61:1611](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2832048), [Cytopathology 2002;13:175](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12060081))
    - Architectural features of atypical ductal hyperplasia, often micropapillary growth or bridge formation ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24121179), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2009;133:722](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19415946))
