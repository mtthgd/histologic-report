# Pseudoangiomatous stromal hyperplasia

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Complex interanastomosing spaces in dense collagenous, keloid-like stroma
* Some of these spaces have spindle shaped myofibroblasts at their margins that simulate endothelial cells
* Spaces are usually empty but may contain rare erythrocytes
* Cellular areas or plump spindle cells may obscure pseudoangiomatous structure
* Often gynecomastia-like changes ([Mod Pathol 2008;21:201](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18084246))
* Rarely multinucleated giant cells ([Breast J 2007;13:568](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17983397))
* No mitotic figures, no necrosis, no atypia
* Fascicular PASH: cellular variant in which myofibroblasts aggregate into fascicles with reduced or absent clefting, resembles myofibroblastoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7872425))
