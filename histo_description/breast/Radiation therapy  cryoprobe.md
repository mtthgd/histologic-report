# Radiation therapy  cryoprobe

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Giant tumor cells with bizarre nuclear changes ([J Clin Pathol 1990;43:152](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2318992)), naked nuclei and abnormal mitotic figures
* Extensive tumor necrosis demarcated by thick fibrous walls (when radiating tumor prior to surgery)
* Changes in nonneoplastic breast take place primarily in the terminal duct lobular unit and include atypia, lobular sclerosis and atrophy (may distort lobular architecture and obscure myoepithelial cells, [Hum Pathol 1984;15:545](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6724573))
* Intimal thickening in small muscular arteries ([Radiother Oncol 2009;92:477](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19541382)) and fat necrosis, but usually no mitotic activity or loss of polarity
* Epithelial atypia in larger ducts ranges from mild to severe but proliferation of the epithelium is generally seen only with mild cytologic atypia ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14707863))
* Atypical stromal fibroblasts
* Changes do not regress over time ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14707863))
