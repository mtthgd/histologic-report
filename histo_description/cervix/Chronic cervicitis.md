# Chronic cervicitis

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Transformation zone of the cervix predominantly involved
* Dense lymphoplasmacytic inflammation of the cervix, with or without lymphoid follicle formation ([Goldblum: Rosai and Ackerman's Surgical Pathology, 11th Edition, 2017](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0323263399/pathologyoutl-20))
* Ulceration and necrosis may suggest infective etiology ([Goldblum: Rosai and Ackerman's Surgical Pathology, 11th Edition, 2017](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0323263399/pathologyoutl-20))
* Dense plasmacytic inflammation is suggestive of *Treponema pallidum* infection ([Nucci: Gynecologic Pathology - A Volume in Foundations in Diagnostic Pathology Series, 2nd Edition, 2020](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0323359094/pathologyoutl-20))
* Multinucleated cells with ground glass chromatin seen in viral induced cases; cytomegalovirus (CMV) infection shows evidence of large, basophilic intranuclear inclusions, which affect some of the epithelial cells; herpes simplex virus (HSV) infection shows ground glass chromatin with peripheral margination and Cowdry type A nuclear inclusions ([Mutter: Robboy's Pathology of the Female Reproductive Tract, 2nd Edition, 2008](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0443074771/pathologyoutl-20))
* Mild lymphocytic inflammation of the cervix may represent normal histology ([Goldblum: Rosai and Ackerman's Surgical Pathology, 11th Edition, 2017](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0323263399/pathologyoutl-20))
* Papillary endocervicitis:
  + Chronic cervicitis with papillary architecture at surface
  + Papillae are short and edematous, often with lymphoid aggregates, covered by simple columnar epithelium with reactive nuclear changes
  + Cells have finely stippled chromatin and prominent nucleoli
  + Mitotic figures may be present but no atypia
  + No infiltrative pattern
  + Often mast cells ([Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2004;47:178](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16295462))
