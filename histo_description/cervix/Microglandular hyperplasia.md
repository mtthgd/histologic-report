# Microglandular hyperplasia

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Solitary or multiple polypoid lesions
* Often superficial location
* Complex proliferation of back to back tubular or cystically dilated glands with scant intervening stroma
* Intraluminal mucin with acute inflammation
* Mixed stromal inflammatory infiltrate
* May be associated with immature or mature squamous metaplasia
* Glands are lined by bland cuboidal, columnar or flattened cells with subnuclear and supranuclear vacuoles (may not be well developed in some cases), indistinct nucleoli, absent or rare (≤ 3 per 10 high power fields) mitoses ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2003;22:261](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12819393))
* Variable reserve cells or immature squamous cells beneath endocervical cells
* Rare unusual features (atypical microglandular hyperplasia):
  + Solid, reticular, trabecular or pseudoinfiltrative growth ([Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:50](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2535775))
  + Abundant myxoid or hyalinized stroma and mucin pools
  + Signet ring or hobnail cells ([Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:50](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2535775))
  + Cytologic atypia with nuclear enlargement and prominent nucleoli ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2021;40:224](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32897963), [Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:50](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2535775))
  + Increased mitoses ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2014;33:524](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25083971), [Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:50](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2535775))
