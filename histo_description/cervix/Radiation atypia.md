# Radiation atypia

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Mucosa tends to be thin, friable and atrophic ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 1996;15:242](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8811386), [Diagn Cytopathol 1995;13:107](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8542788))
* Fibrosis and hyalinization of stroma with occasional atypical fibroblasts ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 1996;15:242](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8811386), [Diagn Cytopathol 1995;13:107](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8542788))
* Prominent cellular changes affecting both squamous and glandular epithelial cells may include nuclear enlargement and hyperchromasia associated with abundant cytoplasm, uniform nuclear spacing with minimal crowding, low mitotic index and cytoplasmic degeneration with vacuoles ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 1996;15:242](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8811386), [Diagn Cytopathol 1995;13:107](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8542788))
* Nuclear chromatin is often indistinct or smudged ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 1996;15:242](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8811386))
* Low nuclear to cytoplasmic (N:C) ratio
* Inflammation, including multinucleated giant cells / histiocytes ([Diagn Cytopathol 1995;13:107](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8542788))
* Irregular, ectatic vessels may be present ([South Asian J Cancer 2014;3:159](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25136522))
