# CIC rearranged sarcoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Solid and often nodular growth pattern
* Less common architectural patterns include fascicular arrangement of neoplastic cells and reticular growth ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:523](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15087672))
* Small round or ovoid cells with amphophilic or lightly eosinophilic cytoplasm ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2012;51:207](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22072439), [Mod Pathol 2016;29:1523](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27562494))
* Round or oval nuclei with variable chromatin patterns and small to medium sized nucleoli
* Higher degree of heterogeneity in nuclear shape and size, compared with the rather monomorphic appearance seen in Ewing sarcoma family of tumors ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2012;51:207](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22072439))
* Some cases may display a predominant spindle cell, epithelioid or plasmacytoid / rhabdoid morphology ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:941](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28346326))
* Mitotic figures are common ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2012;51:207](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22072439))
* Most cases show geographic necrosis ([Mod Pathol 2016;29:1523](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27562494))
* Some degree of moderate nuclear pleomorphism may be observed
* Myxoid stroma in a third of cases ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:941](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28346326))
