# Diffuse midline glioma H3 K27 altered

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Tumor cells typically have an astrocytic morphology and may be small and monomorphic to (occasionally) large and pleomorphic ([Neurol Med Chir (Tokyo) 2003;43:375](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12968803))
* Show an infiltrative growth pattern with tumor cells diffusely growing among native neurons and invading into adjacent structures
* Occasionally, an oligodendroglial-like pattern with halos may be seen
* Some cases will not show mitoses, necrosis or microvascular proliferation consistent with a WHO grade 2 histologic appearance; however, in the presence of *H3* K27-alteration, WHO grade 4 is warranted given the aggressive nature of these tumors ([Acta Neuropathol 2014;128:573](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25047029))
