Microscopic (histologic) description
  • Well demarcated epithelial-like tumor with variable proportions of loose papillary structures and more densely cellular solid areas (J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2006;65:1004)
  • In papillary areas, the vascularized papillary cores are covered by layers of large eosinophilic cells, forming plump perivascular pseudorosettes resembling astroblastic rosettes rather than pseudorosettes of ependymoma
    • Tumor cells are cuboidal to columnar with well defined borders
    • They frequently detach from the vascular wall with the formation of an apparent clear perivascular space (Neuropathol Appl Neurobiol 2006;32:278, Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:505, J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2006;65:1004, Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:916)
  • In solid areas, cells with clear or vacuolated cytoplasm may be seen
    • Ependymal-like differentiation (true rosettes, tubules or canals) is often observed (J Clin Neurosci 2011;18:1007, J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2006;65:1004, Ann Clin Lab Sci 2011;41:174, Neuropathol Appl Neurobiol 2006;32:278)
  • Nuclei are round to oval with occasional nuclear membrane indentation
    • They are often basally located
    • Small nucleolus is frequently discernible and the chromatin pattern may be fine, stippled or clumped (J Clin Neurosci 2011;18:1007)
  • Occasional tumor cells with anisonucleosis, nuclear hyperchromasia and multinucleation may be detected (J Clin Neurosci 2011;18:1007, Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:916)
  • Periodic acid-Schiff (PAS) positive, diastase sensitive glycogen granules and PAS positive, diastase resistant eosinophilic cytoplasmic inclusions are sometimes present (J Clin Neurosci 2011;18:1007)
  • Signet ring cells may be identified (J Clin Neurosci 2011;18:1007)
  • Vascular changes: hyalinization and unusual vessels with multiple lumina are frequently seen
    • Slight endothelial hyperplasia may be encountered but no vascular endothelial proliferation (Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:916, J Clin Neurosci 2011;18:1007)
  • Necrosis is often present (Neuropathol Appl Neurobiol 2006;32:278, J Clin Neurosci 2011;18:1007)
  • Mitotic activity is moderate in most cases (ranges from absent to frequent) (J Clin Neurosci 2011;18:1007)
  • Biological behavior of PTPRs is variable
    • Although the majority correspond to CNS WHO grade 2, more aggressive cases may correspond to grade 3 but definite histological grading criteria remain to be defined
