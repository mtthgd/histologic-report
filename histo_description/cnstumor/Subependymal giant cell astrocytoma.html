Microscopic (histologic) description
  • Well circumscribed and sharply demarcated tumors (Neuropathology 2009;29:25)
  • Composed of cells with variable morphologies (Neuropathology 2009;29:25)
    • Spindle cells
    • Gemistocytic-like cells with abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm
    • Ganglion-like cells with eccentric, vesicular nuclei and prominent nucleoli
    • Occasional nuclear pseudoinclusions
    • Multinucleated cells
  • Variable architectural structures including fascicles of spindle cells, sheets or nested arrangement as well as prominent vascularization or an angiocentric (perivascular rosette-like) architectural pattern (Neuropathology 2009;29:25)
  • Calcifications, marked nuclear pleomorphism and multinucleated cells are common (Folia Neuropathol 2011;49:39)
  • Fibrillary stroma with lymphocytic inflammation and mast cells are often present (Pathol Oncol Res 2004;10:219)
  • Elevated mitotic activity, microvascular proliferation and necrosis are rare (Neurol Med Chir Tokyo 2002;42:224)
  • Subependymal nodules are not distinguishable from SEGAs based on the histomorphological features, thus requiring radiological correlation (Brain Dev 2001;23:508)
