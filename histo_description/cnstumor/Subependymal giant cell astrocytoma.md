# Subependymal giant cell astrocytoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Well circumscribed and sharply demarcated tumors ([Neuropathology 2009;29:25](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18564101))
* Composed of cells with variable morphologies ([Neuropathology 2009;29:25](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18564101))
  + Spindle cells
  + Gemistocytic-like cells with abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm
  + Ganglion-like cells with eccentric, vesicular nuclei and prominent nucleoli
  + Occasional nuclear pseudoinclusions
  + Multinucleated cells
* Variable architectural structures including fascicles of spindle cells, sheets or nested arrangement as well as prominent vascularization or an angiocentric (perivascular rosette-like) architectural pattern ([Neuropathology 2009;29:25](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18564101))
* Calcifications, marked nuclear pleomorphism and multinucleated cells are common ([Folia Neuropathol 2011;49:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21455842))
* Fibrillary stroma with lymphocytic inflammation and mast cells are often present ([Pathol Oncol Res 2004;10:219](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15619643))
* Elevated mitotic activity, microvascular proliferation and necrosis are rare ([Neurol Med Chir Tokyo 2002;42:224](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12064158))
* Subependymal nodules are not distinguishable from SEGAs based on the histomorphological features, thus requiring radiological correlation ([Brain Dev 2001;23:508](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11701246))
