# Cytomegalovirus CMV

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Most commonly affected cells:
  + Endothelial cells
  + Mesenchymal cells
  + Macrophages
* Larger (cytomegalic) cells:
  + Usually 25 - 35 micrometers
  + Typically 2 - 4 times larger than normal
* Owl’s eye:
  + Large ovoid or pleomorphic nucleus with basophilic intranuclear inclusions (Cowdry bodies) surrounded by a clear halo ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2016;140:854](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27472242))
* Thickened nuclear membrane
* Coarse red intracytoplasmic granules ([Int Med Case Rep J 2011;4:55](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23754907))
* Increased apoptotic bodies
* Expansion of lamina propria by mixed inflammatory plasma cell rich infiltrate
* Neutrophilic inflammation
  + Should raise suspicion for CMV infection in graft versus host disease and mycophenolate injury ([Int J Surg Pathol 2018;26:347](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29207904), [J Clin Pathol 2013;66:8](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23038687))
* Deep fissuring ulcers, cryptitis, crypt abscess, architectural distortion and pseudopolyp formation
  + May mimic inflammatory bowel disease ([Am Surg 2007;73:58](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17249458))
* Submucosal vasculitis and thrombosis of microvessels
