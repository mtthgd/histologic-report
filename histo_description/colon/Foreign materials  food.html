Microscopic (histologic) description
  • Some foods have a particular histology and their recognition and correct attribution can be very intriguing
  • Seeds
    • Recognized by the polygonal shape of cells, by the thick cell wall and the presence of starch granules
    • Sometimes they can be surrounded by a granulomatous reaction with giant cells, especially in the case of complicated diverticulitis or acute appendicitis but also in the case of ulcerated and perforated neoplasms
  • Color (some fruits and vegetables have a distinct color)
    • Carotenoids are red, orange or yellow, such as peppers, carrots, tomatoes, bananas, strawberries
    • Anthocyanins are blue or black, such as blackberries, blueberries, grapes, figs, dates, prunes
