# Lipoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Benign, well circumscribed (but not encapsulated) adipose tissue, usually in the submucosa
* Some cases may show prominent fibrosis or vasculature ([Virchows Arch 2019;474:309](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30456582))
* Overlying mucosa usually normal but may show an adenoma or a serrated polyp ([World J Clin Cases 2013;1:124](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24303482))
* May have overlying mucosal ulceration, atypical stromal cells and florid vascular proliferation due to repeated intussusception ([Pathol Int 2005;55:160](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15743326))
* Intramucosal lipomas may occur sporadically or in [Cowden syndrome](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorcowden.html) ([Mod Pathol 2018;31:643](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29192650))
