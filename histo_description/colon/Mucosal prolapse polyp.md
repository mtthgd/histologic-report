# Mucosal prolapse polyp

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Thickening and disruption of the muscularis mucosae and muscularization (splaying of muscularis propria between crypts) of the lamina propria
* Crypt elongation distortion and branching
* Hyperplastic and serrated changes, including crypt branching, hypermucinous appearance of the epithelium ([GE Port J Gastroenterol 2016;23:214](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28868462))
* Secondary changes may include surface erosion, inflammation, hemorrhage, vascular congestion and ectasia ([J Clin Pathol 2009;62:1034](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19861563))
* Diamond shaped crypts and mucosal elastin ([Histopathology 1990;17:129](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2227837))
