# Crohns disease

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Nonnecrotizing granuloma(s) may be found in up to 50% of cases ([Gastrointest Endosc 1994;40:296](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8056231), [Inflamm Bowel Dis 2015;21:2106](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26083616))
* Frequency of nonnecrotizing granuloma(s) varies greatly depending on the type of specimen (biopsy versus resection) ([Adv Anat Pathol 2022;29:2](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34310370), [J Gastroenterol Hepatol 2018;33:355](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28708248))
* Biopsy often shows chronic inflammation only
* Transmural chronic inflammation in the esophagectomy specimen, one of the histologic hallmarks of Crohn's disease, has been reported in a patient with uncontrollable stricture due to Crohn's disease ([Rev Esp Enferm Dig 2022;114:501](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35285663))
* Concurrent eosinophilic and lymphocytic esophagitis has been described in patients with Crohn's disease ([Adv Anat Pathol 2022;29:2](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34310370), [Am J Surg Pathol 2020;44:198](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31651525), [Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:e55](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33481383))
