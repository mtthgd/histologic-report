# Epibulbar choristoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Mixture of benign tissues ([Curr Eye Res 2013;38:835](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23573994))
* Limbal dermoid
  + Stratified squamous keratinizing or nonkeratinizing epithelium
  + No rete ridges
  + Interweaving collagen bundles
  + Epidermal appendages; hair follicles, sebaceous glands or sweat glands may be seen
  + Variable amount of adipose tissue
  + Grading: size and depth of involvement ([Acta Ophthalmol 2017;95:e517](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28266178))
    1. Superficial and < 5 mm
    2. Covers nearly the entire cornea and invades deeply, stopping just short of the Descemet membrane
    3. Covers the entire cornea and extends into the anterior chamber, reaching the pigmented epithelium of the iris
* Dermolipoma
  + Stratified squamous keratinizing or nonkeratinizing epithelium
  + Dense collagen stroma
  + Pilosebaceous structures are less common
  + Disproportionate amount of adipose tissue (particularly in the deeper aspect of the lesion)
* Complex choristoma
  + 2 or more ectopic tissues derived from 2 germ layers: ectoderm (cutaneous appendage, nerve, brain, lacrimal gland) and mesoderm (bone, cartilage, muscle, connective tissue)
  + Most common combination reported is dermolipoma and bone ([Orbit 2019;38:407](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30430897))
