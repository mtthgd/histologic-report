# Fibrillary glomerulonephritis

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Fibrillary glomerulonephritis can cause several histologic patterns ([Clin J Am Soc Nephrol 2011;6:775](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21441134))
  + Most common glomerular finding is mesangial expansion by eosinophilic material that is PAS positive, silver negative or weak and Congo red negative; this characteristic finding gives a moth eaten appearance similar to that described in amyloidosis ([Case Rep Med 2013;2013:935172](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23762079))
  + In more advanced cases, there is glomerular capillary wall thickening owing to fibrillary deposits in the glomerular basement membrane (GBM)
  + Glomerular hypercellularity is usually mild
  + Occasionally, the fibrils can be seen in the tubular basement membranes and vessel walls, although usually only appreciated on immunohistochemistry or immunofluorescence staining
* Other glomerular patterns include
  + Membranoproliferative pattern with focal glomerular basement membrane duplication ([Cureus 2023;15:e47862](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37899889))
  + Crescentic glomerulonephritis
    - May occur in up to 25% of cases
    - Usually a focal process but has rarely been reported as diffuse (over 50% glomerular involvement by crescents)
    - Crescentic disease portends a worse prognosis ([Am J Kidney Dis 2023;81:368](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36228825))
* Secondary segmental sclerosis can be seen
* Tubular atrophy and interstitial fibrosis are common findings but the extent can vary depending on the severity and chronicity of the disease
* Congo red staining is negative
  + Rare Congo red positive cases have been reported but these may represent concomitant amyloid deposition
  + If the Congo red stain is positive, mass spectrometry testing is recommended to exclude renal amyloidosis
* DNAJB9 immunohistochemical stain is very sensitive and specific (up to 99% reported for both) ([Kidney Int Rep 2017;3:56](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29340314))
* References: [Cureus 2022;14:e26001](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35865414), [Am J Kidney Dis 2015;66:e27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26408242)
