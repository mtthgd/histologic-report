# Obesity related glomerulopathy

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Glomerular hypertrophy in 100% of cases ([Kidney Int 2001;59:1498](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11260414))
* Cross sectional glomerular diameter of > ½ 40x high power field
* Often perihilar segmental sclerosis ([Nephron 2017;136:273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27414023))
* Reduced glomerular density ([Clin J Am Soc Nephrol 2012;7:735](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22403274))
* Focal global glomerulosclerosis
* Mild thickening of glomerular basement membrane ([Kidney Int 2001;59:1498](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11260414))
* Mild mesangial sclerosis may be present
* Variable interstitial fibrosis and tubular atrophy
* Variable arteriosclerosis and arteriolosclerosis
* Focal afferent arteriole dilation ([Am J Physiol Renal Physiol 2000;278:F817](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10807594))
* Tubular hypertrophy may also be present
