# Low grade oncocytic tumor LOT

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Nonencapsulated, well circumscribed ([Histopathology 2019;75:174](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30895640))
* Solid / compact nested growth with well demarcated areas of edematous stroma featuring loosely arranged tumor cells ([Histopathology 2019;75:174](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30895640))
* Solid / compact nested growth may have transition to tubuloreticular areas (centrally) ([Mod Pathol 2022;35:1140](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35273336))
* Edematous stromal area with boats in a bay arrangement and hemorrhage or cord-like arrangement ([Mod Pathol 2022;35:1140](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35273336), [Histol Histopathol 2022;37:405](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35156688))
* Uniform finely granular oncocytic cytoplasm ([Histopathology 2019;75:174](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30895640))
* Often focal perinuclear halo formation or clearing, which can be prominent in some cases ([Histopathology 2019;75:174](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30895640), [Mod Pathol 2022;35:1140](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35273336))
* Uniform round / oval nuclei without nuclear membrane irregularities
* Nucleoli are typically inconspicuous (usually equivalent to WHO / ISUP grade 2)
* No mitoses, no nuclear pleomorphism or raisinoid nuclei, no coagulative necrosis ([Histol Histopathol 2022;37:405](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35156688))
