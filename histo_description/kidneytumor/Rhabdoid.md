# Rhabdoid

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Solid sheet-like growth pattern with a discohesive morphology ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:1329](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11023094), [Am J Surg Pathol 2024;48:e65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38736105))
* Large epithelioid cells with large eccentric, atypical vesicular nuclei with prominent nucleoli and abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm and possible paranuclear intracytoplasmic inclusions ([Pol J Pathol 2015;66:3](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26017874))
* Often coexist with sarcomatoid differentiation and high grade bizarre cells ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:1260](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25127094), [Pol J Pathol 2015;66:3](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26017874))
* Necrosis is common; focal to extensive inflammatory cell infiltrates may be seen ([Nat Commun 2021;12:808](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33547292))
* Considered as WHO / ISUP grade 4 ([Am J Surg Pathol 2013;37:1490](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24025520))
* It is recommended to include the percentage of rhabdoid component in the pathology report
* Specific histologic RCC types may be seen adjacent to the rhabdoid areas ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2007;131:102](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17227108), [Ann Diagn Pathol 2015;19:57](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25708024), [Histopathology 2002;41:538](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12460207))
  + Most common histologic subtype of RCC with rhabdoid features is CCRCC but other RCC types including papillary RCC, chromophobe RCC, SMARCB1 deficient renal medullary carcinoma have been described ([Histopathology 2002;41:538](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12460207), [Wien Klin Wochenschr 2012;124:419](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22696227), [Am J Surg Pathol 2013;37:368](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23348212))
  + Tumors with pure rhabdoid morphology after extensive sampling are designated as RCC, NOS
