# COVID-19

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Steatosis ([Liver Int 2020;40:2110](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32654359), [Hum Pathol 2021;109:59](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33307078))
  + Common (up to 75% of patients)
  + Predominantly macrovesicular
  + Usually mild (involving < 33% of the liver parenchyma) and often azonal
  + Usually not associated with ballooning and Mallory-Denk bodies
* Acute hepatitis (lobular necroinflammation) ([Liver Int 2020;40:2110](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32654359))
  + Present in up to 50% of cases
  + Usually mild severity (80% of cases)
  + Foci contain apoptotic hepatocytes plus lymphocytes and rare histiocytes without prominent plasma cells
  + Lobular mitoses
* Portal inflammation ([Liver Int 2020;40:2110](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32654359))
  + Up to half of cases
  + Minimal to mildly increased portal mononuclear cells (lymphocytes and macrophages)
  + Rarely interface hepatitis
* Biliary findings / COVID-19 cholangiopathy
  + Mild and focal lobular cholestasis (up to 38% of cases) ([Mod Pathol 2020;33:2147](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32792598))
  + Ductopenia, severe cholangiocyte injury, ductular reaction, variable portal inflammation, variable fibrosis ([Am J Gastroenterol 2021;116:1077](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33464757))
  + Acute or chronic large duct obstruction without clear bile duct loss ([Am J Gastroenterol 2021;116:1414](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33993134))
* Vascular pathology
  + Focal
  + Phlebosclerosis (reminiscent of veno-occlusive disease), more often with involvement of portal venule (rarely of central vein)
  + Abnormalities of portal arterioles:
    - Arteriolar muscular hyperplasia
    - Hyalinosis and rare fibrinoid necrosis with endothelial apoptosis
  + Sinusoidal erythrocyte aggregation (up to 44%) and platelet microthrombi (up to 70%) ([Hepatol Res 2021;51:1000](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34260803), [Hum Pathol 2021;109:59](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33307078))
* Uncommon findings
  + Thrombotic bodies: pale ovoid sinusoidal platelet rich inclusions seen in sinusoidal spaces (CD61+)
  + Large megakaryocytes
* Perimortem pathology
  + Congestion
  + Centrilobular ischemic necrosis
* Reference: [Mod Pathol 2020;33:2147](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32792598)
