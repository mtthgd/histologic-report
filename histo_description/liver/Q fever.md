# Q fever

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Granulomatous hepatitis, usually with fibrin ring granulomas
* Typical fibrin ring granulomas (doughnut granulomas) consist of a central fat vacuole, a fibrin ring, activated macrophages and lymphocytes
* Other forms of granulomas: epithelioid with eosinophils and multiple giant cells, lipogranulomas and extensive fibrin deposition without ring configuration, which may mimic necrotizing granuloma ([Korean J Pathol 2012;46:10](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23109972))
* May see acute cholangitis without granuloma formation ([Korean J Pathol 2012;46:10](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23109972))
