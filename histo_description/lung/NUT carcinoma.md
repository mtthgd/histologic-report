# NUT carcinoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Primitive looking, monotonous, medium sized cells with frequent cytoplasmic clearing ([Cancer Genet Cytogenet 2010;203:16](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20951314))
* Scattered foci of abrupt keratinization ([J Thorac Oncol 2015;10:951](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26001144))
* Large nuclei with prominent nucleoli ([Ultrastruct Pathol 2012;36:280](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22849529))
* Diffuse architecture with occasional spacing and discohesion of tumor cells ([Cancer Cytopathol 2016;124:901](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27400194))
* Background of necrosis with prominent neutrophils ([Cancer Cytopathol 2016;124:901](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27400194))
* Occasionally, pseudoglandular formations; mucoid stroma, crushing artifact ([Int J Surg Pathol 2019;27:225](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30149737), [J Thorac Oncol 2015;10:951](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26001144))
