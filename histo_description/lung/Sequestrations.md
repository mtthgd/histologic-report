# Sequestrations

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Systemic feeding vessel is often accompanied by hilar type structures, including an airway, nerves, and lymph nodes ([Pediatr Dev Pathol 2007;10:75](https://doi.org/10.2350/06-08-0147.1))
* Systemic artery branches within the lesion are often somewhat thicker than normal pulmonary arteries and may show pulmonary hypertension-like changes in older patients ([Histopathology 2010;57:121](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20584090))
* Cases universally have at least mild parenchymal maldevelopment with enlarged and simplified alveoli
* ~50% of cases also have cystic parenchymal maldevelopment
  + Cysts are typically round and lined by ciliated columnar epithelium; epithelial complexity is rare
  + Histologically similar to a type 2 CPAM
  + Sequestrations with cystic parenchymal maldevelopment are often referred to as a hybrid lesion ([Pediatr Dev Pathol 1999;2:454](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10441623), [J Pediatr Surg 1997;32:986](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9247218))
* Often have prominent evidence of mucostasis, including pools of mucin and foamy intra-alveolar macrophages ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29266024))
  + May have foci of skeletal muscle within septa between larger cysts
* Prominent lymphangiectasia is seen in a subset of extralobar bronchopulmonary sequestrations
