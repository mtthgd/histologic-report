# Cat scratch disease

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Early: histiocytes and follicular hyperplasia, microabscesses localized adjacent to the thickened capsule ([Am J Clin Pathol 2004;121:S71](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15298152))
* Intermediate: capsulitis and subcapsular granulomas
* Late: necrosis and abscesses, often stellate, surrounded by palisading histiocytes ([Int J Dermatol 1978;17:656](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/103848))
* Paracortical vascular proliferation ([Am J Clin Pathol 2004;121:S71](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15298152))
* Sinuses are often packed with monocytoid B cells but no epithelioid cells are present ([Am J Clin Pathol 2004;121:S71](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15298152))
* Small rods may be present with silver stain around small blood vessels and lymphatics; most readily identified early in disease ([Science 1983;221:1403](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6612349), [Am J Clin Pathol 2004;121:S71](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15298152))
* Skin shows dermal necrosis surrounded by histiocytes
* Also multinucleated giant cells, lymphocytes and eosinophils
* References: [Calif Med 1955;82:25](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/13230912), [Am Fam Physician 2011;83:152](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21243990)
