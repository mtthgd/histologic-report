# Hemangioma-spleen

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Vascular spaces lined by flat endothelial cells without significant atypia ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2003;20:128](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12945936))
* Fibrous septa of variable width separating the vascular channels
* Low mitotic rate without necrosis
* Extramedullary hematopoiesis, thrombosis, infarction, cystic degeneration and fibrosis can occur ([Srp Arh Celok Lek 2013;141:380](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23858812), [Auerbach: Diagnostic Pathology - Spleen, 2nd Edition, 2022](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0323830129/pathologyoutl-20))
* Intermixed inflammation such as lymphocytes, histiocytes, plasma cells, neutrophils and even eosinophils can be present
* Primary histologic variants
  + Cavernous
    - Dilated, congested thin walled vessels
  + Capillary
    - Small anastomosing vascular channels
* Other described histologic variants
  + Sclerosing
    - Increased sclerosis in bands of smaller amounts that can be morphologically similar / identical to sclerosing angiomatoid nodular transformation
  + Spindle cell
    - Small, slit-like blood vessels with flat endothelial cells and a spindle cell stroma
    - Must be differentiated from Kaposi sarcoma ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2019;98:e14555](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30817572))
  + Myoid
    - Small vascular channels with eosinophilic, spindled stroma that stains positive from smooth muscle markers ([Iran J Med Sci 2017;42:89](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28293056), [Histopathology 1999;35:328](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10564387))
  + Splenic cord capillary
    - Nodules composed of small vascular channels surrounded by a fibrous stroma
    - Histologically will appear similar to sclerosing angiomatoid nodular transformation but will not contain trapped CD8+ sinuses ([Diagn Pathol 2019;14:135](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31842913))
