# Kimura disease

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Hyperplastic follicles with germinal centers associated with expansion of interfollicular areas by abundant eosinophils, often forming eosinophilic microabscesses ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2007;131:650](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17425383))
* Disruption of follicles by increased eosinophils causing folliculolysis
* Proteinaceous material may be present in germinal center due to increased IgE deposition
* Admixed with polymorphous populations of lymphocytes, plasma cells, mast cells and histiocytes
* Polykaryocytes of Warthin-Finkeldey type may be present ([Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:177](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2919716))
* Vascular proliferation in interfollicular areas
* Necrosis may be present
* Varying degree of sclerosis may be seen
