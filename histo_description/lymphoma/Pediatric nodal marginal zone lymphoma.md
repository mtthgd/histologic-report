# Pediatric nodal marginal zone lymphoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Partial to complete effacement with sinusoidal obliteration ([Adv Anat Pathol 2017;24:128](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28277421))
* Markedly expanded marginal zones with interfollicular infiltration of lymphoma cells
* Diffuse areas often present ([Virchows Arch 2016;468:141](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26416032))
* Often large follicles with extension of mantle zone into germinal centers, resembling progressive transformation of germinal centers; follicular colonization is sometimes seen ([Pediatr Blood Cancer 2020;67:e28416](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32452165))
* Lymphoid infiltrate is polymorphous including monocytoid cells (small to medium sized, round nuclei, moderate cytoplasm), centrocyte-like cells (irregular nuclei, scant cytoplasm) and plasma cells ([Adv Anat Pathol 2017;24:128](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28277421))
