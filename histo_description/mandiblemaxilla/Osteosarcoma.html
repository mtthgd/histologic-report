Microscopic (histologic) description
  • Tumor comprises highly atypical cells and associated immature and lace-like neoplastic osteoid deposition (conventional osteosarcoma)
  • Extent of matrix deposition range from focal immature osteoid to heavily mineralized sclerotic bone formation
  • Entrapped pre-existing trabeculae infiltrated by tumor cells and neoplastic bone (osteoid) → osteodestructive and permeation of marrow spaces
  • Pseudo capsule (rim of periosteum around the invasive edge after cortical destruction)
  • Predominant matrix determine the conventional osteosarcoma subtypes:
    • Chondroblastic osteosarcoma: conventional osteosarcoma with predominant cartilage differentiation (loose basophilic intercellular substance with cells lying in lacunar spaces), common in jaw bones (J Cancer Res Ther 2018;14:471)
    • Osteoblastic osteosarcoma: abundant tumor osteoid production → from early dense fibrillar eosinophilic substance to lace-like pattern and variable mineralization and ossification (tumor bone formation)
    • Fibroblastic osteosarcoma: confluent proliferation of atypical spindle cells (mimicking fibrosarcoma) with high grade cytologic atypia and irregular mineralized matric deposition
  • Low grade central osteosarcoma and parosteal osteosarcoma (Low grade osteosarcoma): abundant fibroblastic stromal component with embedded irregular and interlacing bone trabeculae
  • Low cellularity and minimal cytologic atypia
