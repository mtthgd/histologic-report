# Centronuclear myopathy

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Small muscle fibers with centrally located nuclei, often with a peripheral halo
* Halos lack mitochondria and are highlighted by oxidative stains ([Neuropathol Appl Neurobiol 2017;43:5](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27976420))
* Type 1 myofiber predominance, myofiber size variation and fatty infiltration
* Necklace fibers: basophilic ring along the periphery of the cell membrane visible with H&E, [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html), Gömöri trichrome and oxidative stains in patients with *MTM1* mutation ([Acta Neuropathol 2009;117:283](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19084976))
* Radial arrangement of sarcoplasmic strands, visible with NADH stain, seen with *DNM2* mutation ([Orphanet J Rare Dis 2008;3:26](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18817572))
