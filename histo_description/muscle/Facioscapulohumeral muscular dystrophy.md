# Facioscapulohumeral muscular dystrophy

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Pathology can be quite variable and ranges from mild nonspecific changes to marked dystrophic changes
* Most common feature is chronic myopathy with excessive fiber size variation; small and large fibers are mixed type I and II, mimicking neurogenic atrophy
* May have lobulated myofiber changes (see [Cases 1 - 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/muscleFSH.html#musclefshmicroimg))
* Often shows inflammation (usually perivascular) that resembles inflammatory myopathy
* May or may not have diffuse MHC1 upregulation in myofibers; necrotic fibers and regenerative fibers are usually scanty
* May have prominent fatty replacement and fibrosis (see [Case 2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/muscleFSH.html#musclefshmicroimg2))
* May have rimmed vacuoles ([Acta Neuropathol 2004;108:257](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15221332))
* References: [Muscle Nerve Suppl 1995;2:S56](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7739627), [Am J Med Genet A 2018;176:1760](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30055030)
