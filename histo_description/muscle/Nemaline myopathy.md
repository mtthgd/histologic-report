# Nemaline myopathy

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Variation in myofiber size with areas of atrophy, hypertrophy and grouping
* Fatty replacement or fibrosis may be seen but necrosis, fiber
  regeneration, or inflammation is uncommon ([J Med Genet 1997;34:705](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9321754))
* Gomori trichome stain shows red to purple colored inclusions,
  frequently rod shaped, in sarcoplasm and subsarcolemmal region of
  myofibers ([Semin Pediatr Neurol 2011;18:230](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22172418), [J Med Genet 1997;34:705](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9321754))
  + Often more prominent in type I myofibers
  + No correlation between number of rods and clinical severity
  + Not specific for nemaline myopathies
* Nemaline rods can also be visualized on plastic sections with toluidine
  blue ([Semin Pediatr Neurol 2011;18:230](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22172418))
* Fiber typing will typically show predominantly type I fibers with
  atrophy and grouping
