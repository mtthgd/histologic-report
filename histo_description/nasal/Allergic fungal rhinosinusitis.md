# Allergic fungal rhinosinusitis

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Eosinophilic mucin (allergic mucin) is diagnostic: layered appearance of mucin admixed with cellular debris, sloughed epithelium, eosinophils (some may be degranulated), Charcot-Leyden crystals and other inflammatory cells ([Head Neck Pathol 2016;10:40](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26830404))
* Scanty fungal hyphae identified in 27 - 91% of cases (often found only with GMS stain) ([Indian J Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 2022;74:4640](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36742754), [Head Neck Pathol 2016;10:40](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26830404))
* Charcot-Leyden crystals are pink / red refractive and form long needle-like structures
* Eosinophils may have degenerative changes of smudged, elongated or basophilic nuclei
* No evidence of tissue invasion by fungi
