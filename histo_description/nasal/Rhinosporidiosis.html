Microscopic (histologic) description
  • Rhinosporidiosis is characterized by intense acute and chronic inflammation and numerous large, round, thick walled sporangia that may rupture (Am J Trop Med Hyg 2020;104:708, Medicina (B Aires) 2023;83:639)
  • Hyperplasia with squamous metaplasia may be seen in overlying epithelium
  • Thick cyst walls are birefringent (Thompson: Head and Neck Pathology, 3rd Edition, 2018)
  • Largest, most mature cysts are arranged closest to the mucosal surface and are mostly absent in the overlying mucosa (Medicina (B Aires) 2023;83:639)
  • Mucosal and submucosal cysts (sporangia) range in size from 10 to 300 µm in diameter (Wenig: Atlas of Head and Neck Pathology, 3rd Edition, 2015)
  • On H&E, sporangia contain innumerable sporangiospores (i.e., endospores) of which 2 sizes may be seen: smaller spores ~1 - 2 µm in diameter and larger spores ~5 - 10 µm in diameter (Wenig: Atlas of Head and Neck Pathology, 3rd Edition, 2015)
  • Larger spores represent the more mature forms, typically congregating towards the center, while the smaller ones are more peripheral, resulting in a zonated appearance based on spore size (Wenig: Atlas of Head and Neck Pathology, 3rd Edition, 2015)
  • Additionally, smaller cystic structures, devoid of sporangiospores and ranging from 10 to 100 µm, are observed (Wenig: Atlas of Head and Neck Pathology, 3rd Edition, 2015)
    • Termed trophocysts, these structures are believed to arise from autoinfection via mature sporangiospores released from ruptured sporangia
    • Both sporangia and trophocysts feature eosinophilic walls that are several micrometers thick
  • In cases of disseminated rhinosporidiosis, single spores may be present in body fluids (e.g., urine)
  • Rhinosporidiosis can sometimes trigger granulomatous reaction
