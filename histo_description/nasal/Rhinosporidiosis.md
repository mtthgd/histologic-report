# Rhinosporidiosis

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Rhinosporidiosis is characterized by intense acute and chronic inflammation and numerous large, round, thick walled sporangia that may rupture ([Am J Trop Med Hyg 2020;104:708](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33289469), [Medicina (B Aires) 2023;83:639](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37582141))
* Hyperplasia with squamous metaplasia may be seen in overlying epithelium
* Thick cyst walls are birefringent ([Thompson: Head and Neck Pathology, 3rd Edition, 2018](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0323479162/pathologyoutl-20))
* Largest, most mature cysts are arranged closest to the mucosal surface and are mostly absent in the overlying mucosa ([Medicina (B Aires) 2023;83:639](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37582141))
* Mucosal and submucosal cysts (sporangia) range in size from 10 to 300 µm in diameter ([Wenig: Atlas of Head and Neck Pathology, 3rd Edition, 2015](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1455733822/pathologyoutl-20))
* On H&E, sporangia contain innumerable sporangiospores (i.e., endospores) of which 2 sizes may be seen: smaller spores ~1 - 2 µm in diameter and larger spores ~5 - 10 µm in diameter ([Wenig: Atlas of Head and Neck Pathology, 3rd Edition, 2015](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1455733822/pathologyoutl-20))
* Larger spores represent the more mature forms, typically congregating towards the center, while the smaller ones are more peripheral, resulting in a zonated appearance based on spore size ([Wenig: Atlas of Head and Neck Pathology, 3rd Edition, 2015](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1455733822/pathologyoutl-20))
* Additionally, smaller cystic structures, devoid of sporangiospores and ranging from 10 to 100 µm, are observed ([Wenig: Atlas of Head and Neck Pathology, 3rd Edition, 2015](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1455733822/pathologyoutl-20))
  + Termed trophocysts, these structures are believed to arise from autoinfection via mature sporangiospores released from ruptured sporangia
  + Both sporangia and trophocysts feature eosinophilic walls that are several micrometers thick
* In cases of disseminated rhinosporidiosis, single spores may be present in body fluids (e.g., urine)
* Rhinosporidiosis can sometimes trigger granulomatous reaction
