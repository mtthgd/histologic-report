# Sinonasal lymphoepithelial carcinoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Subepithelial infiltrative neoplastic proliferation composed of syncytium of large cells (Regaud pattern) arranged in lobules, nests, trabeculae or cords ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2015;32:74](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25804344))
* Neoplastic cells may diffusely infiltrate the subepithelial tissue (Schmincke pattern) without a desmoplastic stromal reaction and may also be identified in the epithelium ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2015;32:74](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25804344))
* Associated with a dense nonneoplastic lymphoplasmacytic infiltrate, which can be florid (obscuring the tumor cells) but is typically less prominent as compared to nasopharyngeal carcinoma ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2015;32:74](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25804344))
* Malignant cells typically have large round to oval nuclei, vesicular chromatin, one or more prominent nucleoli, abundant amphophilic to pale eosinophilic cytoplasm and poorly defined cell borders (syncytial pattern) ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2015;32:74](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25804344))
* Occasional tumor cell spindling
* Necrosis is rare but may be present, including central comedo type ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2015;32:74](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25804344))
* Keratinization is typically absent
* Thick fibrous septa separating tumor islands may be observed
* Extracellular amyloid deposits may be present
