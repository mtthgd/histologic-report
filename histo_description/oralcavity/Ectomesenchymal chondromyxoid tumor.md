# Ectomesenchymal chondromyxoid tumor

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Well demarcated tumor, often entrapped within skeletal muscle and frequently multilobulated ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:1297](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29912715), [Head Neck Pathol 2015;9:315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25404177))
* Stromal background may be hyalinized or demonstrate mucoid, myxoid or chondromyxoid areas
* Cords, strands or sheets of oval, round, fusiform or polygonal bland cells, sometimes arranged in a reticular / net-like or globoid pattern ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:1297](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29912715))
* Myxoglobulosis-like changes have been noted ([Virchows Arch 2003;442:302](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12647223))
* May focally demonstrate fine calcifications, cellular atypia, necrosis, multinucleated giant cells ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:1297](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29912715), [Head Neck Pathol 2015;9:315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25404177), [Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol Oral Radiol Endod 1996;82:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8899780))
* Cystic, slit-like spaces or hemorrhagic areas often noted ([Head Neck Pathol 2014;8:329](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24288100))
* Mitotic figures not seen ([Head Neck Pathol 2015;9:315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25404177))
