# Monodermal cystic teratoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Neuroectodermal cyst
  + Lined by ependymal cells
  + May show choroid plexus-like epithelium along the cyst lining
  + Astrocytes, oligodendrocytes, microglia and ganglion cells may be present adjacent to the cyst lining ([Histopathology 1994;24:477](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8088721))
* Epidermoid cyst
  + Lined by mature, often keratinizing, stratified squamous epithelium
  + Surrounded by a rim of collagenous stroma containing fibroblasts
  + Keratinaceous debris fills the cyst lumen, with no evidence of hair or a Rokitansky tubercle ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 1996;15:69](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8852449))
* Rarely, cysts are lined by respiratory type epithelium ([Cancer 1979;43:383](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/761172))
* Lactotroph adenoma
  + Closely packed nests of small cells with eosinophilic cytoplasm as seen in the anterior hypophysis ([Obstet Gynecol 1990;75:540](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2304732))
* Corticotroph adenoma
  + Monomorphic cells with round nuclei and vacuolated or eosinophilic cytoplasm, growing in nests or cords ([Am J Surg Pathol 1987;11:218](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3548446))
