# Mural nodules in mucinous cystic neoplasms

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Associated [mucinous tumor: endocervical or intestinal](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorborderlinemucinous.html) type; [cystadenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumormucinousbenign.html), [borderline tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorborderlinemucinous.html) or [carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumormucinouscarcinoma.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2002;26:1467](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12409723), [Cancer 1979;44:1332](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/498014))
* Mural nodules: sharply circumscribed, separated from overlying cyst lining by a layer of hypocellular stroma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2002;26:1467](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12409723))
* Sarcoma-like mural nodules: 3 types ([Cancer 1979;44:1332](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/498014), [Am J Surg Pathol 2002;26:1467](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12409723))
  + Pleomorphic and epulis-like type (resemble [giant cell reparative granuloma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonegiantcellreparative.html) / [giant cell epulis of gingiva](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/oralcavityperipheralgiantcellgranuloma.html)):
    - Loose network of oval to spindled mononucleate cells with marked nuclear pleomorphism
    - Uniformly distributed, multinucleated osteoclast-like giant cells, some with phagocytized red blood cells and debris
    - Blood filled spaces
    - Variable mitotic activity, can have atypical forms
  + Pleomorphic and spindle cell type:
    - Fascicles of spindle cells with elongated hyperchromatic nuclei and prominent nucleoli
    - Multinucleated giant cells similar to epulis-like type
    - Diffuse extravasation of red blood cells; blood filled spaces
    - Variable mitotic activity, can have atypical forms
    - Mitotic index ranges from 5 to 10/10 HPF (usually in the most cellular areas)
  + Giant cell histiocytic type:
    - Round mononucleate giant cells in closely packed aggregates separated by delicate fibrous septa
    - Cells with vesicular nuclei, ill defined borders, abundant ground glass eosinophilic cytoplasm and minute vacuoles
    - No multinucleated giant cells as in other types
    - Mitoses rare
  + Variable but often marked mixed inflammatory infiltrate in all types of nodules
  + Some nodules contain scattered cuboidal epithelium lined glands (likely entrapped)
  + Most cases have multiple nodules within the tumor
  + Associated mucinous tumor is carcinoma in 50% of cases, the rest being benign and borderline in nature
* Anaplastic carcinoma mural nodules ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:383](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18300813)):
  + In general, characterized by poor circumscription, tumor infiltration into adjacent stroma and vessels, and necrosis
  + Can have scattered multinucleated giant cells of epulis type
  + Divided into 3 types:
    - Rhabdoid: diffusely arranged cells with abundant bright pink cytoplasm and atypical, eccentrically placed nuclei
    - Spindled: spindle cells with atypical vesicular nuclei and moderate eosinophilic cytoplasm, often in herringbone pattern; areas of obvious epithelial differentiation
    - Pleomorphic: admixture of above types, more variegated
* Sarcomatous mural nodules: various types of sarcoma described, including [fibrosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorfibrosarcoma.html), [rhabdomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixrhabdomyosarcoma.html), [undifferentiated sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumoress.html) ([Cancer 1979;44:1327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/498013), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 1994;13:62](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8112957))
* Other mural nodule types described:
  + Other carcinomas ([clear cell](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorclearcell.html), [neuroendocrine](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorneuroendocrinecarcinoma.html), [giant cell](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorosteoclastlike.html)) ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2015;34:19](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25473748))
  + [Carcinosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumormmt.html) ([Int J Gynecol Cancer 2005;15:549](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15882184), [Histopathology 1991;18:268](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1646153))
  + [Leiomyoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorleiomyoma.html) ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 1990;9:80](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2294066), [Am J Surg Pathol 1991;15:1055](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1656803))
* Multiple or mixed types of nodules may occur in same tumor ([Am J Surg Pathol 2002;26:1467](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12409723))
