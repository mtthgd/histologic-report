# Signet ring stromal tumor

Microscopic (histologic) description

* 2 cell populations in variable proportions
  + Diffuse growth of round cells with single to occasionally multiple intracytoplasmic, optically clear vacuoles, which peripherally displace and indent nuclei into a crescent shape (signet ring cells)
  + Fusiform cells with eosinophilic cytoplasm and ovoid nucleus
* May have fibroma-like areas that merge with signet ring component
* Absent to minimal cellular atypia, mitotic activity and necrosis
  + Rarely show brisk mitoses (13/10 and 16/10 high power fields) ([Ultrastruct Pathol 1995;19:401](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7483017), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2004;23:45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14668550))
* [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html)+ intracytoplasmic hyaline globules noted in ~50% ([Ultrastruct Pathol 1995;19:401](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7483017), [Virchows Arch A Pathol Anat Histopathol 1993;422:333](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8389502), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2004;23:45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14668550), [Int J Surg Pathol 2008;16:180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18417676), [Pathol Oncol Res 2008;14:333](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18758997), [Med Mol Morphol 2008;41:165](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18807143), [J Turk Ger Gynecol Assoc 2011;12:59](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24591961), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2020;39:193](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30676431))
