# Somatic neoplasms arising from teratomas

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Microscopic features of typical [mature cystic teratoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorteratomamature.html) or rarely, [immature teratoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorteratomaimmature.html), are definitionally present in the background
  + May contain histologic elements from all 3 germ layers
* Somatic neoplasm appears as a confluent growth of a particular tumor type within the teratomatous background; appearance differs according to the specific somatic tumor type
  + Essentially any histologic tumor type may occur
* Squamous cell carcinoma is the most common somatic malignancy and is defined by infiltrative nests of keratinizing or nonkeratinizing atypical squamous cells
  + They may have cystic degeneration or adjacent areas of dysplasia or carcinoma in situ ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2021;100:e24726](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33787574))
* Other benign and malignant cutaneous neoplasms are relatively common, including sebaceoma / sebaceous carcinoma, other skin adnexal tumors and melanocytic nevi / melanoma
* Mucinous tumors arising in teratomas may be morphologically and immunohistochemically identical to gastrointestinal adenocarcinomas
  + May resemble low grade mucinous neoplasms (LAMN) of the appendix
  + May be associated with mucin extravasation in the ovarian stroma and other peritoneal sites (pseudomyxoma ovarii) ([Case Rep Oncol 2016;9:331](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27462234))
* Thyroid carcinomas may arise within components of [struma ovarii](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorstruma.html) and are sometimes called malignant struma ovarii
  + Papillary thyroid carcinoma is most common and is defined as a confluent papillary or follicular growth of atypical cells with overlapping, optically clear nuclei with longitudinal grooves
  + Follicular thyroid carcinomas may be difficult to diagnose due to the typically disorganized nature of standard struma ovarii; features diagnostic of malignancy include confluent infiltrative growth of atypical cells arranged in follicles, vascular invasion and metastasis
* Other carcinomas of essentially any type may occur (pulmonary / bronchial, neuroendocrine, adenosquamous, etc.)
* A variety of sarcomas have been described including leiomyosarcoma, rhabdomyosarcoma, osteosarcoma, chondrosarcoma, liposarcoma, angiosarcoma and others ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2018;42:90](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29227858))
* Other unusual tumor types include paraganglioma, rhabdomyoma, glomus tumor, glioblastoma and choroid plexus papilloma, among others
