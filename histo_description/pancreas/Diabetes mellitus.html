Microscopic (histologic) description
  • Type 1
    • Inflammatory infiltrate of islets of Langerhans (also called insulitis) leads to severe beta cell depletion
    • Insulitis is predominantly lymphocytic infiltrate, though macrophages and natural killer cells can also be present (J Endocrinol 2021;252:R41)
    • Increased acinar atrophy, periductal and perivascular fibrosis and angiopathy have been described (Diabetes 2024;73:1140)
  • Type 2
    • Hyalinization of islets of Langerhans due to deposition of amylin (islet amyloid polypeptide)
    • Acinar to ductal metaplasia, intralobular fibrosis and inflammation and angiopathy have been described (Diabetes 2024;73:1140)
  • Other
    • Infants of diabetic mothers: islet cell hypertrophy / hyperplasia
