# Simple mucinous cyst

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Flat monolayer of columnar mucinous epithelium or attenuated cuboidal epithelial cells, often resembling gastric type mucinous epithelium ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2017;141:1330](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28968161), [Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:121](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27740966))
* Low grade dysplasia, which may mimic low grade pancreatic intraepithelial neoplasia (PanIN)
  + Focal high grade dysplasia in 8% cases in one study ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:121](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27740966))
* No or minimal papillary architecture ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2017;141:1330](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28968161), [Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:121](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27740966))
* Paucicellular fibrotic wall with no ovarian type stroma or lymphoid band ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2017;141:1330](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28968161), [Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:121](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27740966))
* Degenerative changes in cyst wall common:
  + Hyalinization, myxoid stroma, hemorrhage, calcifications, macrophage clusters, granulation-like tissue ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2017;141:1330](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28968161), [Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:121](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27740966))
