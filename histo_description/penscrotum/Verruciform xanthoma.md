# Verruciform xanthoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Verrucous epidermal acanthosis, papillomatosis and hyperkeratosis
* Uniform depth of rete ridges
* Parakeratosis, often wedge shaped, filling clefts between epidermal projections ([Histopathology 2020;77:841](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32598505))
* Large foamy histiocytes (lipid laden macrophages) in aggregates in elongated dermal papillae, may be scattered or scarce ([J Oral Maxillofac Pathol 2013;17:392](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24574658), [Dermatol Ther (Heidelb) 2017;7:65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27848170), [Histopathology 2020;77:841](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32598505))
* Neutrophils, particularly in the stratum corneum, as well as dermis and occasionally prominent neutrophilic exocytosis ([Australas J Dermatol 2015;56:e99](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24689840), [Dermatol Ther (Heidelb) 2017;7:65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27848170), [Histopathology 2020;77:841](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32598505))
* Mixed inflammatory infiltrate in the submucosa ([J Oral Maxillofac Pathol 2013;17:392](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24574658))
* Vascularized papillary dermis ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2015; 72:e147](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25981017))
* Absence of keratinocyte atypia
