# COVID-19 placentitis

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Trophoblast necrosis and chronic histiocytic intervillositis with variable perivillous fibrin deposition and in situ detection of virus by immunohistochemistry (IHC), in situ hybridization (ISH) or electron microcopy (EM)
  (NIH / NICHHD definition: [Am J Obstet Gynecol 2021;225:593.e1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34364845);
  other large series: [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2021;145:517](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33393592), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2021;145:1341](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34338723), [Mod Pathol 2021;34:1704](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34006935))
* Placentas affected by COVID placentitis also often have numerous intervillous thrombi ([Placenta 2021;112:97](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34329973))
* No other specific pattern of injury has consistently been observed ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2021;145:648](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33596304), [Placenta 2021;110:9](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34058611))
* Variable reports of other lesions by Amsterdam Consensus criteria are discussed above in [Pathophysiology](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/placentaCOVID-19placentitis.html#placentaCOVIDpatho); likely represent selection bias and use of historical controls ([Placenta 2020;101:13](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32911234))
