# Choriocarcinoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Solid sheets of atypical syncytiotrophoblast, cytotrophoblast and intermediate trophoblast
* Absence of chorionic villi
  + Except in intraplacental choriocarcinoma, where the tumor is surrounded by villi ([Fetal Pediatr Pathol 2005;24:21](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16175749))
* Infiltrative and destructive pattern
* High mitotic activity
* Background of necrosis and hemorrhage
* Some cases can present as mixed trophoblastic tumors with an associated placental site trophoblastic tumor or epithelioid trophoblastic tumor component ([Am J Clin Pathol 2018;150:318](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29897391), [Front Oncol 2019;9:1262](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31803628))
* References: [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2019;143:65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30407075), [Ann Diagn Pathol 2007;11:228](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17498600)
