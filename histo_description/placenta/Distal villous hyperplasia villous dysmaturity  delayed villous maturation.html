Microscopic (histologic) description
  • Difficult to diagnose prior to 34 weeks gestation
  • Increased presence of immature villi (at least 10 villi), involving at least 30% of 1 full thickness parenchymal section
  • Distal villi with increased diameter for gestational age
  • Continuous cytotrophoblast layer and decreased syncytial knots
  • Variable capillary density with centrally placed capillaries and poor vasculosyncytial membrane formation
  • Increased stromal cellularity
  • Associated features may include increased nucleated fetal red blood cells, other villous vascular lesions such as chorangiosis, chorangiomatosis and avascular villi
  • Grading schema proposed in Amsterdam Placental Workshop Consensus Statement (Arch Pathol Lab Med 2016;140:698)
    • Focal: lesion in 1 full thickness parenchymal section only
    • Diffuse: lesion present in 2 or more full thickness parenchymal sections
