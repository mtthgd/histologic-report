# Eosinophilic  T cell chorionic vasculitis

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Inflammatory infiltrate within the wall of large caliber fetal vessels consisting primarily of eosinophils and lymphocytes
* Admixed histiocytes can be present
* Inflammation is commonly orientated away from the amniotic fluid
  + Can be omnidirectional
* Frequently limited to a single chorionic vessel
  + Multifocality is being increasingly observed
  + Involvement of stem villous or umbilical cord vessels may occur
* Increased incidence of villitis of unknown etiology in placentas with eosinophilic / T cell chorionic vasculitis
* May occur in the presence of amniotic fluid infection
  + When concomitant in the same placenta, one pattern or the other predominates in each involved fetal vessel ([Pediatr Dev Pathol 2022;25:485](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35435059))
* No acute inflammation or necrosis of the vessel wall
