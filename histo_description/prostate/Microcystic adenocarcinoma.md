# Microcystic adenocarcinoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Cystic glands with marked dilatation (reminiscent of cystic atrophy; glands can become quite attenuated), approximately 10x the size of typical malignant acini ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:556](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20216381))
* Sometimes crowded growth at low power magnification but often showing luminal crystalloid, blue intraluminal mucin and prominent nucleoli at higher power magnification
* Typically coexists with usual acinar pattern [prostatic adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostateadenoNOS.html)
* One of the important deceptively bland histological patterns of prostate cancer, alongside [atrophic](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostateatrophic.html), [pseudohyperplastic](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostatepseudoCA.html) and [foamy gland prostatic adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostatefoamy.html)
