# Treatment effect

Microscopic (histologic) description

* After androgen deprivation: do not grade
  + Nucleolus poor adenocarcinoma is the hallmark; also shows cytoplasmic clearing / vacuolization, reduced cytoplasmic quantity, nuclear pyknosis, reduced gland diameter, mucinous breakdown
  + Difficult to evaluate margin status after androgen deprivation
  + Current recommendations are to not assign a Gleason grade to androgen deprivation treatment specimens ([Clin Prostate Cancer 2004;2:228](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15072606))
  + There is a tendency toward greater neuroendocrine differentiation at prostatectomy after neoadjuvant androgen deprivation and a molecular basis for promotion of this has been described ([Eur Urol 2004;45:586](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15082200), [Cancer Lett 2019;440-441:35](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30312731))
  + High grade prostatic intraepithelial neoplasia (PIN) persists after endocrine treatment; tufted PIN may be replaced by flat high grade PIN ([Hum Pathol 1999;30:1503](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10667430))
  + Normal prostate shows atrophy, basal cell hyperplasia and prominence
* After radiation treatment: assign a grade only if significant radiation change is absent
  + In cancer, the main finding is swollen cytoplasm with vacuoles and eventually glandular breakdown with possible single cells ([Mol Urol 1999;3:193](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10851323), [Urology 2005;65:76](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15667867), [Cancer 1997;79:81](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8988730))
  + Less treatment effect in radical prostatectomy specimens than needle biopsies ([Am J Surg Pathol 1999;23:1173](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10524518))
  + The optimal timing of biopsies is 24 months after initiation of external radiation therapy (plus androgen deprivation) to predict 7 year cancer free survival and to rule out upgrading; tumor may take more than 30 months to resolve ([Cancer 2009;115:673](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19117039), [Urology 2012;80:649](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22698474), [Urology 1995;45:624](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7716843))
  + Any association with later angiosarcoma is weak ([BJU Int 2012;110:E819](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22583810))
  + Normal prostate shows nuclear enlargement (86%) and prominent nucleoli (50%) with increased cytoplasm ([Am J Surg Pathol 1999;23:1173](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10524518))
* After cryotherapy:
  + The main finding is gland disappearance and washed out blue gray stroma
