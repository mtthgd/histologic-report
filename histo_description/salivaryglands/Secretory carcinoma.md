# Secretory carcinoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Circumscribed or infiltrative edge
* Lobulated architecture
* Variable arrangements, including macrocystic, microcystic, solid, tubular, follicular and papillary cystic patterns ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:599](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20410810), [Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:1483](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31464708))
* Pale eosinophilic colloid-like intraluminal secretions; secretions are periodic acid-Schiff (PAS) reagent positive and diastase resistant ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:599](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20410810))
* Tumor cells have eosinophilic or vacuolated cytoplasm and monomorphic round vesicular nuclei with small but distinctive nucleoli
* Occasional lymphovascular invasion and perineural invasion are seen ([Head Neck 2019;41:1227](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30592355))
* High grade transformation has been reported with a distinct population of tumor cells exhibiting solid or trabecular growth, infiltrative margins, desmoplastic stromal reaction, comedo-like necrosis, nuclear pleomorphism, increased mitotic rate, loss of secretory activity and perineural invasion
  + A distinct low grade (conventional) carcinomatous component is also present ([Pathol Res Pract 2017;213:1112](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28781197))
