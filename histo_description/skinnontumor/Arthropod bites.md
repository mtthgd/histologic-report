# Arthropod bites

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Skin biopsies for arthropod bite reactions most often demonstrate features of an urticarial hypersensitivity dermatitis or dermal hypersensitivity reaction: a mild or moderate superficial and deep perivascular lymphocytic infiltrate with increased eosinophils within the reticular dermis and often times minimal epidermal changes ([Chang: Allergic and Immunologic Diseases, 1st Edition, 2022](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/B9780323950619000199?via%3Dihub))
* Some cases may include focal spongiosis or parakeratosis within the overlying epidermis or focal subepidermal or intraepidermal serum filled vesicles
* Histopathologic findings of an arthropod bite may also be observed in urticaria and an urticarial reaction to a medication / drug; clinical correlation is required to appropriately distinguish between these 3 entities
