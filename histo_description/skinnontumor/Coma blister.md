# Coma blister

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Subepidermal bullae, focal necrosis of epidermis, dermis, subcutaneous tissue and all epidermal appendages ([Case Rep Dermatol 2009;1:66](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20652118), [Acta Derm Venereol 2003;83:236](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12816170))
* Necrosis of the eccrine sweat gland and sweat duct is the most notable and significant alteration, though its absence does not rule out the diagnosis ([Case Rep Dermatol 2009;1:66](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20652118), [Acta Derm Venereol 2002;82:378](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12430741))
* Absence of inflammatory infiltrate in the epidermis and the presence of thrombosis in dermal vessels suggest a nondrug etiology ([Case Rep Dermatol 2009;1:66](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20652118))
