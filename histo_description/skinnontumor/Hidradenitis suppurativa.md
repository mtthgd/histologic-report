# Hidradenitis suppurativa

Microscopic (histologic) description

* In early lesions: follicular hyperkeratosis, hyperplasia of follicular epithelium and perifolliculitis ([Br J Dermatol 2011;164:367](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20831631))
* Dermal mixed inflammatory cell infiltrate, sometimes extending into the subcutis ([Br J Dermatol 1990;122:763](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2369556))
* Neutrophilic abscesses, which may connect with squamous epithelium lined cysts and sinus tracts that extend to the skin surface ([Br J Dermatol 1990;122:763](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2369556))
* Cysts and sinus tracts contain laminated keratin and occasional hair follicles ([Br J Dermatol 1990;122:763](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2369556))
* Granulation tissue with occasional foreign body giant cells present in ~25% of cases ([Histopathology 1993;23:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8406382))
* Inflammation involves apocrine glands in a minority of cases ([J Am Acad Dermatol 1996;34:994](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8647993))
* Dense fibrosis surrounding areas of follicular rupture
