# Interface dermatitis

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Interface dermatitis is characterized by epidermal basal cell damage, which manifests as basal keratinocyte vacuoles and dyskeratotic / apoptotic keratinocytes
* Vacuolar pattern has a sparse inflammatory infiltrate
* Lichenoid pattern has a moderate to dense, band-like lymphocytic inflammatory infiltrate closely approximating the basal epidermis and dyskeratotic / apoptotic keratinocytes
* Dyskeratotic / apoptotic keratinocytes appear as
  + Shrunken, eosinophilic keratinocytes with pyknotic / absent nuclei scattered about the basal layer of the epidermis
  + Colloid bodies, which are brightly eosinophilic apoptotic keratinocytes that have been extruded into the papillary dermis

**Vacuolar**

* Lymphocyte predominant dermal inflammatory infiltrate
  + Ballooning and individual necrotic keratinocytes
    - Normal epidermal stratum corneum
      * Erythema multiforme
        + Apoptotic keratinocytes within and above the basal layer, in the form of single cells or full thickness necrosis
        + Ragged subepidermal split, where dyskeratotic keratinocytes form the roof of subepidermal vesicle
        + An acrosyringial concentration of keratinocyte necrosis has been reported in drug induced EM ([J Cutan Pathol 1997;24:235](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9138115))
        + Mild to moderate lymphocytic interface obscuring inflammatory infiltrate
        + Usually, absent to occasional eosinophils ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1989;113:36](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2910224))
        + Cannot distinguish from SJS / TEN without clinical data
      * Stevens-Johnson syndrome / toxic epidermal necrolysis
        + Subepidermal bulla with overlying partial to full thickness epidermal necrosis
        + Individual cell necrosis may take the form of lymphocyte associated apoptosis (i.e., satellite cell necrosis)
        + Inflammatory infiltrate may be denser than in EM
    - Epidermal parakeratosis
      * Pityriasis lichenoides
        + Acute form has confluent mounded parakeratosis containing lymphocytes / neutrophils or serum
        + Epidermal necrosis can range from single dyskeratotic cells to full thickness (the latter representing the ulcerated papules seen clinically)
        + Intravascular fibrin deposition may be present
        + Wedge shaped or band-like lymphocytic infiltrate with lymphocyte exocytosis, red cell extravasation and spongiosis
        + Superficial and deep perivascular infiltrates in PLEVA, only superficial in PLC
        + Lymphocytes can have an atypical morphology, with reduced expression of pan T cell markers and have a positive T cell receptor rearrangement
        + There can be scattered large atypical CD30+ cells, which lends consideration to lymphomatoid papulosis ([Int J Dermatol 2003;42:26](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12581137))
        + Clinical correlation is necessary for diagnosis of the febrile ulceronecrotic variant (FUMHD)
        + Chronic form is a milder version of acute, with a lack of ulceration
    - Prominent epidermal granular layer
      * Graft versus host disease
        + Acute
          - Keratinocyte apoptosis in basilar and lower spinous layers, especially in the tips of epidermal rete, pilosebaceous units and eccrine coils ([Yeung: Pathology of Graft vs. Host Disease, 1st Edition, 2019](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/3319420984/pathologyoutl-20))
          - Apoptosis can involve all levels of the epidermis
          - Satellite cell necrosis (lymphocytes surrounding intraepidermal apoptotic keratinocytes)
          - Diffuse vacuolization of the interface
          - Melanophages and red blood cell (RBC) extravasation may be present
          - Sparse, superficial lymphocytic infiltrate
          - Occasional eosinophils may be present
          - Grading for acute GVHD, by Horn ([J Cutan Pathol 1994;21:385](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7868748))
            * Grade 0: normal skin
            * Grade 1: basal vacuolar change
            * Grade 2: dyskeratotic cells in the epidermis or follicle, dermal lymphocytic infiltrate
            * Grade 3: fusion of basilar vacuoles to form clefts and macrovesicles
            * Grade 4: separation of epidermis from dermis
        + Chronic
          - Early lichen planus-like inflammatory phase
            * Features of lichen planus: epidermal orthohyperkeratosis, hypergranulosis, acanthosis and band-like lichenoid inflammation
            * Also, vacuolar interface change of eccrine coils ([Yeung: Pathology of Graft vs. Host Disease, 1st Edition, 2019](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/3319420984/pathologyoutl-20))
          - Morphea-like: localized thickening and homogenization of collagen throughout the dermis with overlying vacuolar interface change ([Arch Dermatol 2008;144:1229](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18794477))
          - Lichen sclerosus-like: homogenization or sclerosis of papillary dermal collagen with overlying vacuolar interface change, with melanophages and an underlying sparse lichenoid infiltrate
          - Fasciitis: thickening of fascial septa with adjacent inflammation with or without sclerosis in the subcutis
          - A few eosinophils may be present
* Eosinophilic and neutrophil predominant dermal inflammatory infiltrate
  + Ballooning and individual necrotic keratinocytes
    - Fixed drug eruption
      * Classic triad of features are
        + Interface alteration with overlapping lichenoid and vacuolar pattern
        + Papillary dermal fibrosis with melanophages / pigment incontinence
        + Dermal eosinophils
      * Neutrophil component may be prominent in acute lesions and take the form of subcorneal or intraepidermal microabscesses, a dermal neutrophilic infiltrate or a perivascular inflammatory infiltrate

**Lichenoid**

* Lymphocytes predominate
  + Jagged epidermal hyperplasia, wedged hypergranulosis, compact orthokeratosis
    - Lichen planus
      * Interface obscuring band-like lymphocytic infiltrate at the dermoepidermal junction (DEJ); eosinophils and plasma cells are rare
      * Numerous colloid bodies at the DEJ
      * Jagged epidermal hyperplasia with sawtooth appearance in the lower epidermis
      * Acanthotic or atrophic epidermis
      * Wedged hypergranulosis is the histologic correlate to Wickham striae
      * Clefting artifact between the epidermis and dermis, secondary to basal damage (Caspary-Joseph spaces) ([Int J Dermatol 1977;16:842](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/340394))
      * Lichen planopilaris
        + Peri-infundibular and peri-isthmic (bulge region of the hair follicle) band-like inflammatory infiltrate, that abuts the basilar follicular epithelium ([J Cutan Pathol 2005;32:675](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16293179))
        + Perifollicular mucin ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2008;59:91](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18571598))
        + Late stage there is a loss of hair follicles and sebaceous glands that are replaced by bands of fibrous tissue without elastic fibers ([J Cutan Pathol 2000;27:147](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10728818))
      * Hypertrophic variant
        + Markedly acanthotic epidermis
        + Dermal infiltrate is concentrated near the tips of the rete ridges
        + Margins contain changes of lichen simplex chronicus (acanthotic epidermis, hypergranulosis, compact orthokeratosis, stratum lucidum)
        + Overlying squamous epithelium can progress to squamous cell carcinoma, usually well differentiated ([Int J Dermatol 2022;61:1527](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35766459))
        + Dermal eosinophils are usually present, in contrast to conventional LP
  + Eosinophils sometimes, focally thinned epidermis, focal parakeratosis
    - Lichenoid drug eruption
      * Eosinophils, plasma cells
      * Pigment incontinence
    - Photolichenoid eruption
      * Deep extension of the infiltrate
  + Individual necrotic keratinocytes, ballooning, spongiosis, pallor in the upper part of epidermis and parakeratosis
    - Pityriasis lichenoides
      * See [Epidermal parakeratosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorinterfacedermatitis.html#interfacedermatitisepidermal) above
  + Lymphocytes along adnexal structures in the reticular dermis, individual necrotic keratinocytes
    - Lichen striatus
      * Distinguishing feature is deep perivascular and perieccrine lymphocytic inflammatory infiltrate
      * Epidermis will show spongiosis, most likely the subacute type (which is characterized by parakeratosis and acanthosis)
      * Lichenoid epidermal interface alteration
      * Intraepidermal vesicles filled with Langerhans cells may be present ([J Cutan Pathol 1995;22:18](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7751473))
    - Graft versus host disease
      * Similar to vacuolar; see [Graft versus host disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorinterfacedermatitis.html#interfacedermatitisGVHD) above
  + Necrotic keratinocytes, focal parakeratosis, residuum of solar lentigo sometimes
    - Lichen planus-like keratosis
      * Adjacent solar lentigo or seborrheic keratosis, large cell acanthoma or a verruca
      * Epidermal atrophy
      * Plasma cells, eosinophils and neutrophils may be present in the brisk inflammatory infiltrate
      * Pigment incontinence may be prominent
      * Papillary dermal fibrosis
      * Atypical melanocytic proliferation may be present, especially in sun damaged skin ([Hum Pathol 2003;34:706](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12874767))
      * Can rarely have histologic features of mycosis fungoides (Pautrier microabscesses, epidermotropism, the tagging of lymphocytes along the basal layer) ([J Cutan Pathol 2002;29:291](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12100630))
* Histiocyte predominant dermal inflammatory infiltrate
  + Discrete foci in papillary dermis
    - Lichen nitidus
      * Ball and claw configuration where a well circumscribed inflammatory infiltrate (ball) expands the dermal papillae, pushing the finger-like epidermal rete to the periphery (claw)
      * Inflammatory infiltrate is very focal - limited to 1 or 2 adjacent dermal papillae
      * Infiltrate contains lymphocytes, histiocytes, melanophages, with or without granulomas / multinucleated giant cells
      * Lichenoid interface alteration
      * Epidermis with mild hyperkeratosis
      * No deep component
