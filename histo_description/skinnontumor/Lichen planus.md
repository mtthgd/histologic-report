# Lichen planus

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Classic lichenoid dermatitis
* Hyperkeratosis, acanthosis, wedge shaped hypergranulosis, sawtoothing of rete ridges, band-like lymphohistiocytic infiltrate obscuring the dermoepidermal junction ([Int J Dermatol 1982;21:203](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11675973))
* Civatte / cytoid bodies (apoptotic basal keratinocytes, PAS+)
* Basal cell squamatization (flattening of cells)
* Parakeratosis: generally absent, think twice before diagnosing lichen planus
* Artifactual cleft formation between epidermis and papillary dermis (Max Joseph space) ([ScienceDirect: A clinicopathological study of cutaneous lichen planus [Accessed 16 October 2020]](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2210836X13000493))
* Melanin pigment within macrophages of the dermis (pigment incontinence)
* Occasional subepidermal bullae
* Presence of eosinophils favors lichenoid drug reaction in conventional lichen planus (eosinophils can be seen in hypertrophic lichen planus) ([J Cutan Pathol 2014;41:347](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24325780))
* Atrophic lichen planus: no acanthosis, fewer Civatte bodies, more pigment incontinence and minimal inflammation ([Int J Dermatol 2007;46:1237](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18173515))
* Hypertrophic lichen planus: striking hyperkeratosis and acanthosis (pseudoepitheliomatous hyperplasia) ([J Cutan Pathol 2014;41:347](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24325780))
* Ulcerative (erosive) lichen planus: epidermal ulceration with lichen planus changes at the ulcer margins; frequent plasma cells ([Clin Exp Dermatol 1993;18:169](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8481999))
* Lichen planus pigmentosus: lymphocytic infiltrate tends to be deeper, with dermal melanin incontinence ([Int J Dermatol 1992;31:90](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1559749))
* Actinic lichen planus: dermal melanin incontinence and focal parakeratosis; milder lymphocytic infiltrate ([Clin Exp Dermatol 1989;14:65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2805391))
* Bullous lichen planus: vesicle or bullae in conventional lichen planus ([Clin Exp Dermatol 1989;14:150](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2598489))
* Lichen planus pemphigoides: bulla with mild perivascular lymphocytic infiltrate, may show neutrophils ([J Am Acad Dermatol 1983;8:331](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6339569))
* Oral lichen planus: occasional parakeratosis, less acanthosis than in the skin, plasma cells in infiltrate
* Lichen planopilaris: scarring alopecia with lichenoid interface dermatitis of the hair infundibulum, with fibrous tracts and Civatte bodies
  + Perifollicular lichenoid infiltrate around infundibulum ([Arch Dermatol 1966;93:684](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/5932155))
