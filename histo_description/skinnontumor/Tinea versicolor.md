# Tinea versicolor

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Direct microscopy of lesional scale prepared with KOH or chlorazol black shows clusters of budding ovoid yeasts (blastoconidia) and hyphae (pseudomycelium) referred to as spaghetti and meatballs ([Lancet 2004;364:1173](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15451228))
* H&E of skin biopsy shows ovoid budding yeasts and hyphae (spaghetti and meatballs) within the stratum corneum ([Lancet 2004;364:1173](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15451228))
* Fungi are accentuated with periodic acid-Schiff (PAS) or Gomori methenamine silver (GMS) stains ([Lancet 2004;364:1173](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15451228))
* Other histologic changes are often subtle or absent; slight hyperkeratosis or sparse superficial perivascular inflammatory infiltrate may be present ([Patterson: Weedon’s Skin Pathology, 5th Edition, 2020](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0702075825/pathologyoutl-20))
* Atrophying tinea versicolor may have dermal elastolysis visible with Verhoeff van Gieson staining ([Int J Dermatol 2003;42:928](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14636183))
