# Acral melanoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Prominent acanthosis of epidermis with elongated rete ridges can be seen
* Pagetoid spread
* Atypical nuclei
* Increased N:C ratio
* Proliferation of melanocytes downward along eccrine ducts
* Consumption of epidermis present (attenuation of basal / suprabasal layers with rete ridge loss) ([J Cutan Pathol 2012;39:577](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22574622))
* Early lesions may show proliferation of solitary melanocytes in the epidermal rete ridge (crista profunda intermedia) underlying the ridge of the skin marking ([Am J Dermatopathol 2006;28:21](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16456320))
* Nail lesions show confluent stretches of solitary melanocytes, multinucleation, lichenoid inflammatory reaction and florid pagetoid spread ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:835](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18391745))
* Heterologous osseocartilaginous differentiation seen more commonly in acral melanoma
* ALM is the most common histopathologic subtype, followed by NM and SSM ([JAMA Dermatol 2013;149:1281](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24067997), [Sci Rep 2016;6:31432](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27545198), [Melanoma Res 2017;27:315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28296711))
* ALM
  + ALM in situ demonstrates an increased number of irregularly distributed melanocytes at basal epidermis
  + Variable cytologic atypia of melanocytes with hyperchromatic nuclei
  + Prominent dendrites and confluent distribution of melanocytes
  + Later vertical growth phase develops with invasion into dermis
  + Melanocytes comprising the vertical growth phase are spindled (*NF1* mutations) to epithelioid (*BRAF* mutations) ([J Invest Dermatol 2018;138:933](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29191620))
* Subungual melanoma: first demonstrates a mild proliferation of atypical melanocytes with hyperchromatic nuclei, increased N:C ratio and elongated dendrites ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1902](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18043047), [J Dermatol 2008;35:695](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19120763), [J Cutan Pathol 2016;43:41](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26423820))
  + Important histologic features in subungual melanoma in situ include irregular and hyperchromatic nuclei and increased melanocyte count ([Histopathology 2021;78:717](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33065755))
