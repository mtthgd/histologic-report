# CRTC1TRIM11 cutaneous tumor

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Morphology is uniform and resembles clear cell sarcoma
* Circumscribed, unencapsulated dermal nodule, no / very rare pigment, with only rare cases showing epidermal involvement ([J Cutan Pathol 2022;49:1025](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35751643))
* Some cases show extension into subcutis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:861](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30676334))
* Characteristic pattern of nests and intersecting (occasionally herringbone to vaguely palisaded) fascicles of uniform epithelioid to spindled cells with intervening delicate collagen bands ([Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:1457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35993578))
* Abundant pale eosinophilic cytoplasm, monomorphic nuclei with vesicular chromatin and prominent nucleoli
* Mitotic figures present ([Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:1457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35993578))
* Necrosis reported but uncommon ([Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:1457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35993578))
* Lymphovascular and perineural invasion uncommon ([Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:1457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35993578))
* Pigment and multinucleate giant cells may very rarely be seen
* A recent case of CTCT with ulceration was reported ([J Cutan Pathol 2024 Jun 15 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38877838))
