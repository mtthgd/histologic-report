# Cutaneous fibroepithelial polyps

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Epidermis may be hyperplastic and papillomatous and may have keratotic cysts and pigment in basal epidermal keratinocytes
* Loose fibrocollagenous stroma, abundant vessels
* Usually no adnexa
* Variable adipose tissue in larger ones - lipofibroma
* Traumatic changes: lichen simplex chronicus, epidermal necrosis, ulceration, pagetoid dyskeratosis, lichen sclerosus-like change ([Am J Dermatopathol 2006;28:478](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17122491), [Am J Dermatopathol 2019;41:e64](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30946102))
* Pseudosarcomatous change: pleomorphic stellate stromal cells, multinucleated stromal cells, myxoid to collagenous stroma ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2008;12:440](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18995210))
* On IHC, the pleomorphic cells stain diffusely for vimentin; there is variable staining for CD34 and factor 13a and the cells do not stain for SMA or desmin ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2008;12:440](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18995210))
* Some of the lesions with pseudosarcomatous change overlap with [pleomorphic fibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuepleomorphicfibroma.html)
* A subset of cellular pseudosarcomatous fibroepithelial polyps occurs in the female genital tract including the vulva ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10680891))
