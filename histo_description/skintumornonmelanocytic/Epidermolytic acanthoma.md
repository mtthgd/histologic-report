# Epidermolytic acanthoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Acanthosis and epidermolytic hyperkeratosis (covering > 50% of the lesion surface) ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30663082))
* Low power architecture generally resembles seborrheic keratosis ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30663082))
* Epidermolytic hyperkeratosis consists of lace-like degeneration of the midspinous epithelial layer, perinuclear vacuolization, epithelial eosinophilia and finely speckled keratohyaline granules ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30663082))
* 3 architectural patterns of solitary epidermolytic acanthoma have been described: papillomatous, cup shaped and acanthotic ([J Eur Acad Dermatol Venereol 2011;25:175](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20553361))
* Solitary and multiple epidermolytic acanthomas display similar histopathological findings ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30663082))
