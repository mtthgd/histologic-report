# Hidradenoma papilliferum

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Well circumscribed dermal maze-like or arborizing proliferation (rarely connected to epidermis), with a combination of papillary, cystic or glandular architecture
* 2 types of epithelia:
  + Luminal layer of tall, columnar cells; cuboidal cells with eosinophilic cytoplasm, decapitation secretion and small, round nuclei (apocrine differentiation)
    - Can occasionally exhibit clear cell change (lamprocyte-like changes) ([Pathology 2019;51:362](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31010589))
  + Underlying layer of thin or cuboidal myoepithelial cells ([Am J Dermatopathol 2006;28:322](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16871035), [Ann Coloproctol 2019;35:361](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31937076))
* Entrapment of epithelial cells in connective tissue may mimic infiltrative pattern ([Arch Gynecol Obstet 2011;284:1015](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21132312))
* May have connection to the epidermis with associated dermal plasma cell infiltrate (mimics syringocystadenoma papilliferum) ([Am J Dermatopathol 2016;38:598](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26863059))
* Stromal changes: dense stromal plasma cell infiltrate, sclerosis, hyperplasia of myofibroblast-like cells, foamy macrophages
* Metaplasia occasionally present
  + Most commonly oxyphilic (eosinophilic, granular cytoplasm, conspicuous nucleoli, occasional pleomorphism), oftentimes leading to misdiagnosis of malignancy ([Am J Dermatopathol 2005;27:102](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15798433))
  + Rarely squamous or mucinous metaplasia
* Remnants of anogenital mammary-like glands (AGMLG) often present
  + Epithelium of these glands can be normal or exhibit columnar cell change or columnar cell hyperplasia ([Am J Dermatopathol 2016;38:598](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26863059))
* Mitotic rate can be variable and sometimes high but high mitotic rate does not correlate with an aggressive outcome ([Am J Dermatopathol 2006;28:322](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16871035))
* Rare case reports with sebaceous differentiation (in association with ectopic HP) and extramammary Paget disease ([J Dermatol 2006;33:256](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16674789), [Am J Dermatopathol 2016;38:598](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26863059))
