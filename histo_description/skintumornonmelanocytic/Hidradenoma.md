# Hidradenoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Well circumscribed but unencapsulated, lobulated / cystic mass with variably sized nests and nodules of epithelial cells within the upper or mid dermis, typically with no overlying connection to the epidermis ([J Clin Pathol 2007;60:145](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16882695))
* Shows both solid and cystic components ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2005;129:e113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15859654))
  + Solid portion composed of 2 types of cells: polyhedral cells with basophilic cytoplasm and glycogen containing clear cells with eccentric round nucleus
  + Cystic areas secondary to degeneration of tumor cells
* Tubular lumina are lined by cuboidal or columnar secretory cells ([J Oral Maxillofac Pathol 2013;17:136](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23798849))
* Fibrovascular, collagenous or hyalinized stroma ([J Cutan Pathol 2007;34:497](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17518780), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2005;129:e113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15859654))
* Focal apocrine decapitation secretion, squamous differentiation, squamous eddy formation, keratinization, mucinous change or sebaceous differentiation can be seen ([Am J Dermatopathol 2012;34:461](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21993337), [J Cutan Pathol 2007;34:801](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17880588), [J Cutan Pathol 2007;34:497](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17518780))
* Malignant hidradenocarcinoma presents with infiltrative growth pattern, nuclear atypia and pleomorphism, predominantly solid cell islands, angiolymphatic invasion, necrosis and ≥ 4 mitoses per 10 high power fields ([Middle East Afr J Ophthalmol 2010;17:374](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21180442), [Mod Pathol 2009;22:600](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19252473))
* Variants:
  + Clear cell hidradenoma ([Cutis 2014;94:268](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25566574))
    - Has a biphasic cellular population: (1) round, fusiform, or
      polygonal cells with vesicular nuclei and eosinophilic cytoplasm
      and (2) cells with clear cytoplasm and often eccentrically located
      nuclei
  + Solid cystic hidradenoma ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2005;129:e113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15859654))
    - Shows both solid and cystic components
    - Cysts probably represent cystic degeneration
  + Mucinous hidradenoma ([J Cutan Pathol 2007;34:497](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17518780))
    - Demonstrates diffuse and prominent mucinous cell proliferation
  + Poroid hidradenoma ([Ann Dermatol 2021;33:289](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34079192))
    - Has architectural features of the apocrine hidradenoma and
      cytological features of poroid and cuticular cells
    - Poroid cells are uniform, small cuboidal cells with an oval to round nuclei
    - Cuticular cells have an abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm with a
      larger nucleus that shows occasional multinucleation
  + Pigmented nodular hidradenoma ([Arch Dermatol 1971;104:117](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4328525))
    - Shows melanin pigmentation
