# Inflammatory linear verrucous epidermal nevus ILVEN

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Skin biopsy shows psoriasiform epidermal hyperplasia with papillomatosis and hyperorthokeratosis alternating with parakeratosis ([Pediatr Dermatol 1985;3:15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3906609))
* Hypergranulosis is seen in areas of hyperorthokeratois; granular layer may be diminished in the areas of parakeratosis ([Pediatr Dermatol 1985;3:15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3906609))
* Variable inflammatory infiltrate may be seen ([Pediatr Dermatol 1985;3:15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3906609))
* Cases with spongiotic changes have been described in ILVEN ([Dermatology 2006;212:103](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16484814))
