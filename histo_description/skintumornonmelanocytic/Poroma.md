# Poroma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Eccrine poroma:
  + Well circumscribed
  + Replaces the epidermis and extends into the dermis in broad anastomosing bands
  + Poroma cells are monomorphic, small, cuboidal with basophilic round nuclei, inconspicuous nucleoli and compact eosinophilic cytoplasm
  + Sharp demarcation present between the normal keratinocytes and poroma cells
  + Devoid of peripheral palisading
  + Ductal lumina with single row of luminal cells covered by eosinophilic lining or cystic spaces devoid of any formal lining
  + Cells are united by conspicuous intercellular bridges and supported by a delicate fibrovascular stroma ([J Dermatol 1980;7:263](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6252254))
  + Poroma cells usually contain glycogen ([Int J Dermatol 2014;53:1053](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24697501))
  + Occasionally, pigmented variants with associated dendritic melanocytes and tumor cell melanin deposition ([J Dermatol 2010;37:542](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20536669), [J Eur Acad Dermatol Venereol 2008;22:303](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18269598))
  + Dystrophic calcification and transepidermal elimination of tumor nests are exceptional findings ([J Dermatol Case Rep 2009;3:38](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21886728), [J Dermatol 1997;24:539](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9301149))
* Apocrine poroma:
  + Shows sebaceous differentiation with the occasional presence of follicular differentiation and foci of apocrine-like features ([J Cutan Pathol 2001;28:101](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11168759))
  + Anastomosing trabeculae, displaying multiple points of origin from the epidermis and located largely in the papillary and upper reticular dermis
  + Cells are small and uniform with scanty cytoplasm and round to oval nuclei united by inconspicuous intercellular bridges
  + Foci of ductal differentiation with a well developed eosinophilic cuticle
  + Follicular differentiation in the form of epithelial lobules ([Am J Dermatopathol 1999;21:31](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10027523))
  + Sebaceous cells, singly and in clusters with bubbly cytoplasm and crenated nuclei is an infrequent feature ([Am J Dermatopathol 1996;18:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8721584))
* Eccrine porocarcinoma:
  + May remain completely intraepidermal (in situ porocarcinoma) but is more often associated with an invasive dermal component
  + Poroma cells, with typical ductal lumina, associated with cytological features of malignancy, including nuclear and cytoplasmic pleomorphism, nuclear hyperchromatism and mitotic activity ([Am J Surg Pathol 2001;25:710](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11395548))
  + Prone to have local recurrence (17%) and is occasionally associated with nodal metastases (19%); however, systemic spread is rare (11%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2001;25:710](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11395548))
