# Primary cutaneous mucinous carcinoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Islands of basaloid cells surrounded by pools or lakes of mucin ([Am J Dermatopathol 2024;46:114](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38055969))
  + Islands can be solid, cystic or cribriform
  + With or without tubular / ductal structures between or within epithelial aggregates
* Fibrous septa separate lakes ([Am J Dermatopathol 2024;46:114](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38055969))
* Aggregates of neoplastic cells can extend deep in dermis with or without subcutis (far from main component)
* Polygonal / round / oval cells with small hyperchromatic nuclei and eosinophilic cytoplasm ([Dermatol Surg 2023;49:1091](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37643246))
  + Decapitation secretion may be observed
* Low degree of atypia; mitotic figures uncommon ([Dermatol Surg 2023;49:1091](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37643246))
* Minimal inflammation
* In situ component ([Am J Surg Pathol 2005;29:764](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15897743))
  + Present in most cases (60 - 70%) but may be focal
  + Myoepithelial layer is highlighted by p63, SMA, calponin
  + May resemble (atypical) ductal hyperplasia, ductal carcinoma in situ of the breast
  + Potential architectural patterns: cribriform, solid, micropapillary, papillary
* Uncommon features / presentations
  + Psammoma bodies ([Am J Dermatopathol 2008;30:510](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18806504))
  + Plasmacytoid cells ([J Dermatol 2010;37:767](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20649728))
  + Hair follicle induction in epidermis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2005;29:764](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15897743))
  + Oxyphilic or columnar metaplasia ([Am J Surg Pathol 2005;29:764](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15897743))
  + Mucin with epithelial aggregates dissecting collagen and mimicking lymphatic invasion ([Am J Surg Pathol 2005;29:764](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15897743))
  + Peritumoral fibrosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2005;29:764](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15897743))
  + Signet ring cell changes ([Am J Surg Pathol 2005;29:764](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15897743))
  + Mixed variant with combination of invasive ductal carcinoma (representing ≥ 10% of total tumor) and classic mucin lakes (representing < 90% total tumor) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2005;29:764](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15897743))
  + Perianal lesions: may have pagetoid spread with or without anastomosing cords extending from epidermis into dermis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2005;29:764](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15897743))
