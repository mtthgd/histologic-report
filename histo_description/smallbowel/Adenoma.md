# Adenoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Tubular, tubulovillous or villous, similar to [adenomas in colon](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumoradenoma.html), with approximately half tubular and half villous ([Am J Clin Pathol 2009;132:506](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19762527))
* Low grade dysplasia demonstrates hyperchromatic, pencil shaped nuclei confined to the basal layer of cells, with limited architectural changes ([Mod Pathol 2019;32:1291](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30976103))
* High grade dysplasia demonstrates large, rounded nuclei with loss of polarity, along with architectural abnormalities such as cribriforming ([Mod Pathol 2019;32:1291](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30976103))
* Dysplastic epithelium may have only subtle changes of mild cellular stratification and fine chromatin pattern
* Often contain prominent Paneth cells (with coarse, large, red-pink, refractile granules in supranuclear cytoplasm), endocrine cells (dark, red-purple, fine, small granules in basal cytoplasm) and goblet cells
* May show high grade dysplasia or give rise to [adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ampullaadenocarcinoma.html)
