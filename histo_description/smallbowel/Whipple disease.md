# Whipple disease

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Lamina propria expanded by foamy macrophage infiltrate ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:1066](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22743287))
  + Infiltrate may be diffuse or patchy
* Blunt or distended villi ([Dig Dis Sci 2019;64:213](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29572616))
* Scattered dilated lacteals ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:1066](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22743287))
* Fat vacuoles within lamina propria ([Dig Dis Sci 2019;64:213](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29572616))
* Overlying enterocyte vacuolization due to intracytoplasmic lipid accumulation
* Acute inflammation (variable)
* Mononuclear inflammation usually absent
* Epithelioid granulomas (minority of cases) ([Virchows Arch A Pathol Anat Histol 1979;382:227](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/89752))
