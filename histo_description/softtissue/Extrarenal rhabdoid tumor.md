# Extrarenal rhabdoid tumor

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Tumor cells have classic rhabdoid or skeletal-like profile, comprised of abundant cytoplasm with eosinophilic hyaline globules and vesicular nuclei with prominent nucleoli ([Anticancer Res 2005;25:4573](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16334144), [Ann Diagn Pathol 2012;16:504](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21775180))
* Some cases may show areas of spindling of tumor cells
* Nuclear pleomorphism can be prominent
* Background stroma may be variably myxoid to fibromyxoid
* Tumor necrosis and mitotic activity can be variable
