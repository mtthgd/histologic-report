# Extraskeletal myxoid chondrosarcoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Multinodular architecture ([Cancer 1998;83:1504](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9781944))
* Fibrous septa divide the tumor in pools of abundant myxoid or chondromyxoid matrix containing tumor cells ([Cancer 1998;83:1504](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9781944), [Pathol Int 2005;55:453](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15998372))
* Uniform cells with eosinophilic to vacuolated cytoplasm, often with long delicate cytoplasmic processes and round to oval nuclei with evenly distributed chromatin and inconspicuous nucleolus ([Am J Surg Pathol 1999;23:636](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10366145))
* Tumor cells are characteristically interconnected with one another to form cords, small clusters and complex trabecular or cribriform arrays
* Spindle cell differentiation is common
* Mitotic activity is low
* Stroma is hypovascular
* Foci of intralesional hemorrhages are frequently seen in various proportions ([Cancer 1998;83:1504](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9781944))
* Usually lacks discernible cartilaginous histology ([Pathol Int 2005;55:453](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15998372))
* 80% of EMCs with variant (non-*EWSR1*) *NR4A3* gene fusions (*TAF15, TCF12*) demonstrate high grade morphology with increased cellularity, proliferation and cytologic atypia, showing epithelioid / rhabdoid features in half of the cases ([Hum Pathol 2014;45:1084](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24746215))
