# Sclerosing epithelioid fibrosarcoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Proliferation of uniform, small, round to ovoid epithelioid cells with sparse, often clear cytoplasm and round to oval nuclei with inconspicuous nucleoli ([Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:979](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7661286), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020;59:217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31675134))
* Tumor cells are arranged in nests, cords or sheets ([Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:979](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7661286), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020;59:217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31675134))
* Areas with pseudoalveolar pattern may be present
* Prominent hyalinized sclerotic collagenous stroma, sometimes reminiscent of osteoid or cartilage ([Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:979](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7661286), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020;59:217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31675134))
* Occasional myxoid zones with cyst formation and foci of hyaline cartilage, calcification and metaplastic bone ([Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:979](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7661286))
* Focal ectatic staghorn vasculature
* Mitotic figures are generally scarce ([Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:979](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7661286))
* Necrosis is uncommon
* Variant morphology:
  + Complete absence of the defining collagen (nonfibrosing variant) ([Histopathology 2016;68:760](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26212576))
  + Areas of increased cellularity, reminiscent of a round to undifferentiated neoplasm ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020;59:217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31675134))
  + Fibroma-like and conventional fibrosarcoma-like areas ([Am J Surg Pathol 2001;25:699](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11395547))
  + Low grade fibromixoid sarcoma-like areas (so called hybrid LGFMS / SEF) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:1444](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22982887), [Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:801](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24441665), [Pediatr Dev Pathol 2018;21:574](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29426275))
  + Tumor cells with eccentric nuclei and eosinophilic cytoplasm (plasma cell-like) ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020;59:217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31675134))
  + Foci of increased nuclear pleomorphism and atypia
