# Superficial CD34 fibroblastic tumor

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Tumor involving dermis and subcutaneous fat ([Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2018;61:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30004071))
* Generally circumscribed growth ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887307))
* Fascicles or sheets of epithelioid to spindle cells with abundant granular or glassy eosinophilic cytoplasm ([Am J Dermatopathol 2020;42:68](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30702454))
* Pleomorphic, hyperchromatic nuclei
* Intranuclear cytoplasmic pseudoinclusions ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2020;13:38](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32055270))
* Variably prominent nucleoli ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887307), [Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2018;61:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30004071))
* Xanthomatous change in a subpopulation of tumor cells ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887307))
* Extremely low mitotic rate ([Am J Dermatopathol 2020;42:68](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30702454), [Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2020;13:38](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32055270))
* Necrosis is uncommon ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887307))
* Mixed chronic inflammatory infiltrate of lymphocytes, plasma cells, mast cells and often eosinophils ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887307), [Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:504](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30570551), [Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2018;61:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30004071))
* Arborizing capillary sized vasculature is frequently present ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887307))
