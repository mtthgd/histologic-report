# Superficial acral fibromyxoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Poorly circumscribed lesion in dermis composed of spindle to stellate shaped fibroblastic cells arranged in a loose storiform / fascicular pattern with alternating myxoid and collagenous stroma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:789](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22367301))
* Microvasculature is prominent ([Hum Pathol 2001;32:704](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11486169))
* Multinucleated stromal cells may be present and mast cells are frequent ([Hum Pathol 2001;32:704](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11486169))
* Bland nuclei lacking significant mitoses or necrosis; occasionally degenerative atypia may be encountered
* Infiltration in adipose tissue can occur
* Cellular variants are also encountered
* Cartilaginous and osseous metaplasia may be observed
