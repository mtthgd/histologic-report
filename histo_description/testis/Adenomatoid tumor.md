# Adenomatoid tumor

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Tubules, cords or small nests
* Composed of or lined with cuboidal cells
* Moderate to abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm with single to multiple vacuoles
* Signet ring cell morphology and lipoblast-like morphologies very common ([Mod Pathol 2009;22:1228](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19543245))
* Other patterns: adenoid, angiomatoid, cystic, glandular, oncocytic, solid, tubular ([Mod Pathol 2009;22:1228](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19543245))
* Thin bridging strands crossing glandular / tubular spaces ([Mod Pathol 2009;22:1228](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19543245))
* Peripheral lymphoid aggregates ([Mod Pathol 2009;22:1228](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19543245))
* Infiltrative growth common ([Mod Pathol 2009;22:1228](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19543245))
* Can become infarcted ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14707867))
