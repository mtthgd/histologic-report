# Choriocarcinoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Solid nests and sheets of syncytial cells (syncytiotrophoblasts) and mononucleated trophoblasts (cytotrophoblasts and intermediate trophoblasts)
* Syncytiotrophoblasts:
  + Large cells
  + Abundant, dense, eosinophilic cytoplasm
  + Indistinct cell border
  + Multinucleated
  + Pleomorphic, hyperchromatic nuclei which can have a degenerated, smudged appearance
  + Grow over mononucleated cells and can form a thin compressed rim or a thicker cap
* Mononucleated trophoblasts (cytotrophoblasts and intermediate trophoblasts):
  + Medium sized cells
  + Pale, eosinophilic cytoplasm
  + Distinct cell border
  + Mitotically active
* Hemorrhage, cyst formation and necrosis are common ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24145647))
* Lymphovascular invasion is often present in tumors with pure or predominant choriocarcinoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24145647))
* Germ cell neoplasia in situ may be present
* Remaining nonneoplastic testicular parenchyma may show findings seen in other germ cell tumors, including atrophy, maturation arrest, Leydig cell hyperplasia, microlithiasis, angiopathy, Sertoli cell only tubules and Sertoli cell nodules ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:1260](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17001157))
