# Cryptorchidism

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Histopathologic differences become much more pronounced after 2 years of life and are increased with delayed orchiopexy ([J Urol 2009;182:704](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19539332))
* Peritubular fibrosis
* Seminiferous tubule atrophy
* Decreased / absent spermatogenesis
* Sertoli cells may demonstrate granular cell change with eosinophilic granular cytoplasmic lysosomes
* Sertoli cell only seminiferous tubules: tubules with only bland, monotonous pale cells with granular cytoplasm attached to the basement membrane; absent germ cells and no spermatogenesis
* [Sertoli cell nodule](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testissertolihyperplasia.html): nodules composed of immature elongated Sertoli cells (Pick adenoma) may be present; can have central microliths
* Increased microliths within seminiferous tubules
* Retained Leydig cells give appearance of hyperplasia
* Evaluate for [germ cell neoplasia in situ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisitgcn.html%20) (GCNIS): large, pleomorphic, basally located cells within the seminiferous tubules may be present; use immunostains for confirmation
