# Regressed germ cell tumor

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Intratesticular fibrous scar ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:858](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16819328), [Am J Pathol 1961;38:207](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/13685483)):
  + Acellular collagenous tissue with numerous small interspersed vessels
  + Foamy or hemosiderin laden macrophages
  + Lymphoplasmacytic infiltrate
  + May have necrosis or intratubular calcification (microlithiasis)
* Residual germ cell tumor may be present if regression is incomplete
  + Pure [seminoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisseminomas.html) most common ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:858](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16819328), [J Urol 2009;182:2303](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19762049))
* Testicular parenchyma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:858](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16819328)):
  + [Germ cell neoplasia in situ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisitgcn.html) (50% of cases)
  + Remaining parenchyma may be atrophic with:
    - Shrunken seminiferous tubules with decreased spermatogenesis
    - Sertoli cell only lined tubules
    - Thickened peritubular basement membrane
