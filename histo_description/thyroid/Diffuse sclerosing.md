# Diffuse sclerosing

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Infiltrative growth with diffuse involvement of one or both lobes (papillary, solid or follicular pattern) ([Endocr Pathol 2005;16:331](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16627920), [Crit Rev Oncol Hematol 2015;94:64](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25577570))
  + Tumor foci within large distended lymphatics throughout
  + Numerous psammoma bodies frequently arranged in large clusters ([Endocr Pathol 2005;16:331](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16627920), [Crit Rev Oncol Hematol 2015;94:64](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25577570))
* Dense fibrosis
* Extensive lymphocytic infiltration and a background of chronic lymphocytic thyroiditis
* Foci of squamous metaplasia in solid patterned areas (squamous morules) ([Endocr Pathol 2005;16:331](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16627920), [Crit Rev Oncol Hematol 2015;94:64](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25577570))
  + Diagnostic nuclear features of PTC can be identified in nonsquamous areas
* Extrathyroidal extension is common ([Endocr Pathol 2005;16:331](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16627920), [Crit Rev Oncol Hematol 2015;94:64](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25577570))
* Lymph node metastases in ~ 80%, often affecting several levels of lymph nodes bilaterally ([Endocr Pathol 2005;16:331](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16627920))
