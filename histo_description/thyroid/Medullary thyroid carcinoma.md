# Medullary thyroid carcinoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Wide variety of morphology, can mimic any other thyroid malignancy
* Round, plasmacytoid, polygonal or spindle cells in nests, cords or follicles; often mixtures of these cells
* Round nuclei with finely stippled to coarsely clumped chromatin and indistinct nucleoli, occasional nuclear pseudoinclusion
* Eosinophilic to amphophilic granular cytoplasm due to secretory granules
* Generally low mitotic figures
* Stroma has amyloid deposits from calcitonin, prominent vascularity with glomeruloid configuration or long cords of vessels ([Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:642](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7538732)), coarse calcifications, occasional psammoma-like bodies
* Mucin in 42% ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1983;107:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6687422))
* Often angiolymphatic invasion
* Occasionally marked neutrophilic infiltrate, oncocytic tumor cells, papillary patterns
* May entrap follicles
* C cell hyperplasia present in familial but not sporadic cases
* Microcarcinoma:
  + Complex microarchitectural pattern with desmoplastic stroma, often focal amyloid deposition
  + Usually a solid pattern of round, polygonal, spindled or plasmacytoid cells ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2008;132:1767](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18976013))
  + Often C cell hyperplasia ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:853](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10843288))

**Variants** (important for differential diagnosis, most are of no prognostic importance):

* Amphicrine variant: tumor cells contain mucin and express calcitonin ([Ultrastruct Pathol 1985;8:197](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4060258))
* Angiosarcoma-like variant: cleft spaces with hemorrhage ([Diagn Cytopathol 2007;35:424](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17580345))
* Clear cell variant ([Hum Pathol 1985;16:844](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4018781))
* Encapsulated variant ([J Pathol 1971;103:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4327780))
* Follicular variant: has follicles containing eosinophilic secretion ([Histopathology 1983;7:83](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6840715))
* Giant cell variant: large cells with bizarre nuclei, may be more aggressive ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1978;102:445](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/210736))
* Melanotic variant: with melanin pigmentation ([Histopathology 1990;16:227](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2332208))
* Oncocytic variant: mimics [Hürthle cell adenoma / carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidhurthle.html) but has prominent fibrovascular septa ([Cancer 1989;63:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2917321))
* Papillary variant ([Pathol Res Pract 1988;184:98](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3068650))
* Paragangioma-like variant:
  + Nested tumor cells, mimics [paraganglioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/adrenalparaganglioma.html) ([Mod Pathol 1990;3:581](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2235983))
  + Nest-like pattern with pigmented dendritic cells resembling sustentacular cells ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1994;118:1041](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7944893))
* Pseudopapillary variant ([Diagn Cytopathol 2014;42:823](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23894022))
* Small cell variant: similar to [small cell carcinoma of lung](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorsmallcell.html), highly aggressive ([Semin Diagn Pathol 1985;2:137](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3843691))
* Spindle cell variant: exclusively spindle cells ([Adv Anat Pathol 2014;21:26](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24316908))
* Squamous variant: may have focal squamous differentiation ([Cancer 1989;63:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2917321))
