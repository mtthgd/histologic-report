# Thyroglossal duct cyst

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Type of epithelial lining varies by site, and combinations of the types below can be seen in a single cyst ([Head Neck Pathol 2013;7:50](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22968435)):
  + Ciliated pseudostratified columnar (respiratory) epithelium in lower neck, perhaps due to its close proximity to upper respiratory tract
  + Nonkeratinizing squamous epithelium in higher neck (near tongue and foramen cecum), also can be of metaplastic origin in inflammatory settings
  + Stratified cuboidal epithelium at level of hyoid bone
  + Very often the cyst is denuded of epithelium, at least focally, which reflects epithelial damage by inflammation
* Secondary inflammation is common, especially in sinus tract ([J Pediatr Surg 1984;19:506](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6502417)):
  + Intense lymphocytic infiltration, rarely arranged into lymphoid follicles
  + Admixture of neutrophils (if the cyst is infected)
  + Granulation tissue and fibrosis
* Thyroid follicles in the cyst / duct wall:
  + Found in 30% - 60%, with higher yield on serial sections
  + More common in infra- versus suprahyoid remnants, on the right paramedian side ([Ann Otol Rhinol Laryngol 2000;109:1135](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11130826))
  + Seen in small irregular groups
  + Thyroid epithelium may be normal or rarely hyperplastic or neoplastic
  + Thyroid tissue often hidden by inflammation ([Laryngoscope 2001;111:1002](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11404612))
  + Absence of thyroid tissue does not exclude the diagnosis of TGD cyst
* Mucous salivary-type glands can be found in the cyst wall, frequently in lingual and suprahyoid locations ([Ann Otol Rhinol Laryngol 1996;105:996](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8973289))
* Occasional inclusions:
  + Skin adnexal structures ([J Laryngol Otol 2004;118:996](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15667693))
  + Gastric mucosa ([Gnepp: Diagnostic Surgical Pathology of the Head and Neck, 2nd Edition, 2009](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1416025898/pathologyoutl-20))
  + Cartilage ([Int J Pediatr Otorhinolaryngol 1988;15:205](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3397240))
  + Bone ([J Laryngol Otol 1994;108:168](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8163925))
  + Cholesterol granuloma ([Eur Arch Otorhinolaryngol 2009;266:1775](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19205714))
* One study found that specimens **clinically** diagnosed as TGD cyst were classified based on histology as true cysts (50%), ducts without evidence of cysts (40%) and fibrous tracts (10%) ([J Laryngol Otol 2000;114:128](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10748829))
