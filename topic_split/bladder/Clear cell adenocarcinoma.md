Definition / general

* Adenocarcinoma of the genitourinary tract that has a morphology and immunophenotype that resembles Müllerian type clear cell carcinoma of gynecologic tract

Essential features

* Nuclear pleomorphism and mitotic activity, with or without necrosis
* Tubulocystic and papillary architecture, hobnailing of nuclei
* Infiltrative growth
* Positive staining for PAX8
* References: [Int Urol Nephrol 2021;53:815](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33462714), [Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32960833)

Terminology

* Müllerian type clear cell tumor / carcinoma
* Mesonephric or mesonephroid carcinoma / adenocarcinoma (not recommended)

Epidemiology

* Strong female predominance (F:M = 4.6:1) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32960833))
* Median age of 58 years ([Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32960833))

Sites

* Bladder
* Urethra
* Very rare reports of upper urothelial tract involvement
* References: [Int Urol Nephrol 2021;53:815](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33462714), [Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32960833), [Virchows Arch 2023;483:751](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37233807)

Pathophysiology

* Poorly understood origin ([Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32960833))
* One theory proposes origin from nephrogenic adenomas; however, unlike nephrogenic adenomas, clear cell adenocarcinoma (CCA) does not have a known association with urothelial tract inflammation / injury
* Another theory suggests an association with the gynecologic tract / endometriosis, given the female predominance and morphologic similarities to clear cell carcinoma of the ovary / uterus

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Patients most commonly present with gross hematuria or lower urinary tract symptoms
* Rarely may present with recurrent urinary tract infections or obstruction
* References: [Int Urol Nephrol 2021;53:815](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33462714), [Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32960833), [Virchows Arch 2023;483:751](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37233807)

Diagnosis

* Mass on imaging
* Gross hematuria
* Urine cytology may show atypical cells
* Evaluation by cystoscopy and transurethral resection / biopsy
* References: [Int Urol Nephrol 2021;53:815](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33462714), [Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32960833), [Virchows Arch 2023;483:751](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37233807)

Radiology description

* Bladder or urethral mass on imaging
* Subset of patients may show obstructive signs (hydronephrosis, hydroureter) ([Int Urol Nephrol 2021;53:815](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33462714))

Prognostic factors

* Higher age at presentation and presence of distant spread most strongly correlated with negative outcomes ([Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32960833))

Case reports

* 57 year old woman with lower urinary tract symptoms and urethral diverticulum ([Urol Case Rep 2021;38:101659](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33868945))
* 58 year old woman with dysuria and urinary frequency ([Asian J Surg 2024;47:2006](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38220522))
* 70 year old man on active surveillance and with lower urinary tract symptoms ([Urol Case Rep 2023;47:102338](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36816610))
* 79 year old woman with gross hematuria ([Cureus 2023;15:e39575](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37378101))

Treatment

* Radical cystectomy / urethrectomy, with or without chemotherapy or radiation ([Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32960833))

Gross description

* Grossly gray-white, infiltrative mass ([Am J Clin Exp Urol 2023;11:344](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37645618))
* Polypoid or papillary ([Am J Clin Exp Urol 2023;11:344](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37645618))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Heterogeneous architecture within the same tumor: solid, tubular, tubulocystic or papillary patterns
* Luminal hobnailing of nuclei
* Abundant cytoplasm, clear to eosinophilic
* High grade nuclear features with occasional mitotic figures
* Invasive pattern of growth (muscularis propria or perivesical fat invasion)
* Necrosis and lymphovascular invasion may be present
* References: [Virchows Arch 2023;483:751](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37233807), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2009;133:987](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19492895)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Not routinely performed for diagnostic purposes
* *ARID1A* mutations ([Cancer Res Treat 2024;56:280](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37697729))
* *PIK3CA* and *KRAS* mutations, suggesting a possible role of the PI3K / AKT / mTOR pathway ([Virchows Arch 2019;475:727](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31372739))
* Other potential driver mutations include *AMER1*, *CHD4*, *KMT2D*, *KRAS*, *PBRM1*, *PIK3R1* ([Cancer Res Treat 2024;56:280](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37697729))

Sample pathology report

* Bladder and prostate, radical cystoprostatectomy:
  + Clear cell adenocarcinoma (see synoptic report)
  + Tumor invades perivesical soft tissue
  + Lymphovascular invasion is present
  + Margins are negative for tumor
  + No malignancy identified in 10 lymph nodes (0/10)
  + PT3b N0

Differential diagnosis

* [Nephrogenic adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladdernephroadenoma.html):
  + Immunohistochemistry (IHC) is often not helpful ([Cureus 2023;15:e41285](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37533615))
  + Low mitotic rate / [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) proliferation rate (Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:270)
  + Lacks overtly malignant features (muscle invasion, necrosis) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32960833))
  + Lack of [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) mutated phenotype (overexpression or null) ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2009;133:987](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19492895))
* [Urothelial carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderurothelialinvasivegen.html):
  + May have overlapping histology such as tubulopapillary growth or clearing of cytoplasm
  + Overlapping IHC ([CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgata3.html), [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html)) ([Mod Pathol 2020;33:1165](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31932679))
  + [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) positive ([Diagn Pathol 2022;17:87](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36320040))
* **Metastatic Müllerian adenocarcinoma from gynecologic tract** ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2024;148:705](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37948107)):
  + Can look and stain identically to primary clear cell adenocarcinoma of the bladder
  + Diagnosis of metastasis often requires clinical history
* [Prostatic adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostateadenoNOS.html):
  + [NKX3.1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnkx3.html) positive
  + Both CCA and prostatic adenocarcinoma may express [AMACR / racemace](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsamacr.html) ([Int Urol Nephrol 2021;53:815](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33462714))
* **Metastatic** [clear cell renal cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantrccclear.html):
  + Lacks architectural heterogeneity of clear cell adenocarcinoma ([Virchows Arch 2023;483:751](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37233807))
  + [CAIX](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscarbonicanhydraseix.html), [RCC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsrcc.html), [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html), [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html) positive (**CCA** is negative) ([Virchows Arch 2023;483:751](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37233807), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2009;133:987](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19492895))

A patient undergoes a radical cystectomy for a 5 cm papillary mass. The above tumor is seen on H&E examination. This tumor should show which immunophenotype?

1. PAX8+, BAP1 loss, calretinin+, WT1+
2. PAX8+, CAIX+, CD10+, CK7-
3. PAX8+, CK7+, vimentin-, p53 overexpression
4. PAX8+, TTF1+, CK7+

Board review style answer #1

**C.** PAX8+, CK7+, vimentin-, p53 overexpression. The images show a clear cell adenocarcinoma, which should have positive staining for PAX8, CK7 and a p53 mutated phenotype. Vimentin should be negative. Answer D is incorrect because this immunophenotype is more in keeping with metastatic lung or thyroid cancer. Answer B is incorrect because this immunophenotype is more in keeping with clear cell renal cell carcinoma. Answer A is incorrect because this immunophenotype is more in keeping with mesothelioma.

Which of the following features would be most helpful in distinguishing a clear cell adenocarcinoma from a metastatic renal cell carcinoma?

1. Mutated p53 immunophenotype (overexpression or loss)
2. PAX8+, CK7+, vimentin+, CD10+
3. Presence of papillary and tubular growth
4. *VHL* mutation on molecular studies

Board review style answer #2

**D.** *VHL* mutation on molecular studies. *VHL* mutations are not found in clear cell adenocarcinoma of the urinary tract. This would be more consistent with a clear cell renal cell carcinoma. Answer B is incorrect because this immunophenotype can be found in renal cell carcinoma. Answer A is incorrect because although a p53 mutated immunophenotype is seen in clear cell adenocarcinoma of the bladder, it can also be seen in renal cell neoplasms. Answer C is incorrect because both clear cell adenocarcinoma and renal cell carcinoma can have papillary and tubular growth.
