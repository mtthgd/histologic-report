Definition / general

* Bladder mucosa is exposed to the surface exterior through a defect in the anterior abdominal wall

Terminology

* Also called classic bladder exstrophy
* Part of exstrophy-epispadias complex (bladder exstrophy, epispadias, failure of labia fusion, lack of symphysis pubis fusion)

Epidemiology

* Prevalence at birth is 1 per 50,000-100,000; male predominance up to 5:1
* May be associated with maternal tobacco use ([J Urol 2008;179:1539](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18295266))
* High concordance in identical and non-identical twins
* Documented cases of family clusters

Etiology

* Failure of cloacal membrane differentiation leads to developmental failure within lower abdominal wall or anterior wall of the bladder
  + Persistence or overgrowth of the cloacal membrane on lower anterior abdominal region prevents the normal ingrowth of mesenchyme; this leads to divergence of lower abdominal muscular structures and causes the genital ridges to fuse more caudally than the membrane ([J R Soc Med 1996;89:39P](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8709084))
* 2 outcomes are possible and are determined by stage of ingrowth of the urorectal septum at the time of rupture
  + Exstrophic urinary tract alone (classic bladder exstrophy or epispadias)
  + Cloacal exstrophy with the hindgut located between the hemibladders
* Most cases are considered sporadic, however, individuals with a family member with exstrophic bladder are at higher risk
* *ISL1* is considered the primary candidate gene associated with exstrophy, given its role in development of the urinary tract ([Sci Rep 2017;7:42170](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28176844))

Clinical features

* Usually diagnosed with prenatal scans
* Urine drips from ureteric orifices onto bladder surface ([Orphanet J Rare Dis 2009 Oct 30;4:23](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19878548))
* Bladder mucosa is red at birth, and mucosal polyps may be seen
* If closure is delayed, may have inflammatory changes of whitish coating, ulcerations and hyperplastic formations
* Thin skin stripes mark transition between normal skin and squamous metaplastic area
* Most patients have bilateral inguinal hernias
* Male newborns have open (epispadic) urethral plate that covers whole dorsum of penis from open bladder to glandular grove
* Female newborns have completely split clitoris next to the open urethral plate; also narrow vaginal opening anterior to a shortened perineum

Treatment

* Surgical correction with several procedures over time, usually beginning in first week of life
* Rarely, adults present to physicians with exstrophy ([Indian J Urol 2008;24:164](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19468391))
* Even in surgically treated patients, bladder mucosa reverts to normal in only 25% of cases
