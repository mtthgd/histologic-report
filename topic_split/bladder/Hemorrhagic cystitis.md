Definition / general

* Clinical syndrome of gross hematuria, often with irritative bladder symptoms

Etiology

* Due to drug treatment (cyclophosphamide, methotrexate, ifosphamide), radiation therapy ([eMedicine: Hemorrhagic Cystitis [Accessed 11 July 2018]](http://emedicine.medscape.com/article/2056130)), viral infection (adenovirus, herpes simplex virus, CMV, BK virus)
* Post allogeneic hematopoietic stem cell transplantation ([Chin Med J (Engl) 2008;121:1766](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19080354))
* Occurs in 10% of pediatric bone marrow transplants ([J Pediatr Urol 2009;5:254](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19303365))

Clinical features

* Irritative voiding symptoms, gross hematuria

Case reports

* 8 year old with BK virus associated hemorrhagic cystitis presenting as mural nodules ([Pediatr Radiol 2010;40:1430](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20221593))
* 47 year old man with malaise and recent UTI, with voided urine specimen ([Case of the Week #353](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case353.htm))
* 49 year old man with symptoms after receiving penicillin for endocarditis ([Intern Med 2009;48:1667](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19755771))
* 70 year old man with hemorrhagic cystitis due to melphalan ([Case Rep Med 2010;2010:569138](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20593040))

Treatment

* Clot evacuation and hydration, bladder irrigation with saline or alkalinized saline, intravesical instillation of aluminium hydroxide / magnesium hydroxide
* More aggressive therapies include transurethral coagulation of bladder, fixation with silver nitrate and formalin, embolization of internal iliac arteries or their branches to the bladder; rarely requires cystectomy
