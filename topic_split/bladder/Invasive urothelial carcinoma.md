Definition / general

* Urothelial carcinoma that has penetrated the basement membrane and invaded into the lamina propria or deeper

Essential features

* Histologic characterization and depth of invasion are the most important factors for determining prognosis ([Adv Anat Pathol 2015;22:102](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25664945))
* Urothelial carcinoma is morphologically heterogenous with many variants and subtypes ([Surg Pathol Clin 2018;11:713](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30447837))
* Invasive urothelial carcinoma involving the lamina propria (T1) is often treated with conservative intravesical therapy and mucosal resection ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2007;11:395](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18022122))
* Invasive urothelial carcinoma involving the muscularis propria (T2) is often treated with radical cystectomy ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2007;11:395](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18022122))

Terminology

* Invasive transitional cell carcinoma (historical term)
* [WHO classification 2016](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderwhoisup.html):
  + Infiltrating urothelial carcinoma
  + Urothelial carcinoma with divergent differentiation
    - With squamous differentiation
    - With glandular differentiation
    - With trophoblastic differentiation
  + Nested urothelial carcinoma
    - Including large nested variant
  + Microcystic urothelial carcinoma
  + Micropapillary urothelial carcinoma
  + Lymphoepithelioma-like urothelial carcinoma
  + Plasmacytoid / signet ring / diffuse urothelial carcinoma
  + Sarcomatoid urothelial carcinoma
  + Giant cell urothelial carcinoma
  + Poorly differentiated urothelial carcinoma
  + Lipid rich urothelial carcinoma
  + Clear cell (glycogen rich) urothelial carcinoma

Epidemiology

* Fourth most common malignancy in U.S. men
* Eight most common malignancy associated with death in U.S. men
* Median age: 69 years in men and 71 years in women ([Adv Anat Pathol 2015;22:102](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25664945))
* Incidence:
  + 4x higher in men than women
  + 2x higher in white men than in black men

Sites

* Lower tract urothelial carcinoma: bladder and urethra represent 90 - 95% ([Eur Urol 2015;68:868](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26188393))
* Upper tract urothelial carcinoma: ureter and renal pelvis represent 5 - 10% ([Eur Urol 2015;68:868](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26188393))

Pathophysiology

* Smoking or other chemical exposures start the process of carcinogenesis by altering the DNA of the bladder mucosa; these genetic alterations lead to unregulated cell growth ([Adv Anat Pathol 2019;26:251](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31188799))

Etiology

* Tobacco smoking: population attributable risk for ever smoking is 50% for both men and women in U.S. ([Eur Urol 2013;63:234](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22877502))
* Occupational exposures: aromatic amines, chlorinated hydrocarbons and polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons including benzidine ([Eur Urol 2013;63:234](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22877502))
* Genetic predisposition ([Eur Urol 2013;63:234](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22877502))
* Unlike [squamous cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladdersquamous.html), not due to indwelling catheters

Clinical features

* Hematuria, irritation / pain during urination, increased urinary frequency or urgency ([Urology 2006;67:3](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16530066))

Diagnosis

* Urine cytology on voided samples ([Eur Urol 2013;63:4](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23083902))
* Cystoscopy with biopsy or transurethral resection ([Eur Urol 2013;63:4](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23083902))

Radiology description

* CT image findings include urothelial thickening / hyperenhancement, asymmetric collecting duct system, urothelial calcifications or the presence of a nodule / mass ([Urol Clin North Am 2018;45:389](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30031461))
* MRI T2 weighted images demonstrate hypointense thickening of bladder wall ([Abdom Radiol (NY) 2019;44:3858](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31363813))

Prognostic factors

* 5 year relative survival: 69% with local disease, 37% with regional disease and 6% with distant disease ([American Cancer Society: Survival Rates for Bladder Cancer [Accessed 6 April 2021]](https://www.cancer.org/cancer/bladder-cancer/detection-diagnosis-staging/survival-rates.html))
* [Stage](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderstaging.html), with histologic characterization and involvement of the muscularis propria (detrusor muscle) is an important factor for determining prognosis ([Adv Anat Pathol 2015;22:102](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25664945))
* Morphological variants associated with poor prognosis, possibly due to late stage diagnosis and aggressive behavior include:
  + Urothelial carcinoma with divergent differentiation ([squamous](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladdersquamousdiff.html%20) or [glandular](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignanturothelialcarcinomasubtypesglandulardiff.html))
  + [Micropapillary urothelial carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignanturothelialcarcinomasubtypesmicropap.html)
  + [Plasmacytoid urothelial carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignanturothelialcarcinomasubtypesplasma.html)
  + [Nested urothelial carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladdernested.html) ([Adv Anat Pathol 2015;22:102](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25664945))

Case reports

* 53 year old woman with IgG4 related kidney disease from the renal pelvis that mimicked urothelial carcinoma ([BMC Urol 2015;15:44](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26013281))
* 56 year old man with testicular metastasis ([Case Rep Urol 2014;2014:759858](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25120937))
* 62 year old man with lymphadenopathies diagnosed with adenocarcinoma of unknown origin ([Acta Med Okayama 2019;73:279](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31235978))
* 65 year old man with recurrence of urothelial carcinoma in an orthotopic neobladder ([BMJ Case Rep 2017;2017:bcr2017221052](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29066649))
* 65 year old man with plasmacytoid urothelial carcinoma and unique clinicopathologic profile on rapid autopsy ([Diagn Pathol 2019;14:113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31638990))
* 80 year old woman with invasive urothelial carcinoma with chordoid features ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2015;8:15344](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26823892))

Treatment

* Conservative therapy is often offered for invasion into the lamina propria (T1) ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2007;11:395](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18022122))
* Conservative therapy includes Bacillus Calmette-Guérin (BCG) intravesical immunotherapy ([American Cancer Society: Intravesical Therapy for Bladder Cancer [Accessed 8 April 2021]](https://www.cancer.org/cancer/bladder-cancer/treating/intravesical-therapy.html))
* Radical cystectomy is usually required for invasion into the muscularis propria (T2) ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2007;11:395](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18022122))
* Locally advanced cancers are typically treated with chemotherapy, immunotherapy and occasionally radiation ([J Urol 2018;199:1129](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29113841))

Frozen section description

* Positive margin is defined by the presence of [in situ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladdercis.html) or invasive urothelial carcinoma on frozen section ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2015;19:107](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25800468))
* Ureteric intraoperative frozen sections have significant clinical limitations due to skip lesions, which often occur in multifocal urothelial carcinoma in situ ([J Clin Pathol 2010;63:475](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20498023))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Neoplastic cells arranged in irregular nests or single cells invading the lamina propria and muscularis propria
* Retraction artifact is often seen and can mimic vascular invasion ([Am J Surg Pathol 2001;25:356](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11224606))
* High grade nuclear features: nuclear pleomorphism, hyperchromasia, high N/C ratio with frequent mitotic figures ([Am J Surg Pathol 2001;25:356](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11224606))
  + Note: [nested urothelial carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladdernested.html) demonstrates bland, low grade cytology ([Hum Pathol 2019;94:11](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31669177))

Cytology description

* [Urine cytologic detection](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladdercytologyneoplastic.html) of urothelial carcinoma has sensitivity and specificity of 13 - 75% and 85 - 100%, respectively ([PLoS One 2015;10:e0134940](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26241896))
* Diagnostic categories are based on [The Paris System for Reporting Urinary Cytology](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladdercytologyparissystemnew.html)
* Cytologic diagnosis of high grade urothelial carcinoma requires > 10 cells with high N/C ratio, irregular chromatin pattern and hyperchromatic nuclei ([Cancer Cytopathol 2018;126:207](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29278461))

* Immunostaining can distinguish luminal and basal phenotypes ([Sci Rep 2020;10:9743](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32546765))
  + Luminal: [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html), [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgata3.html), **FOXA1**, **XBP1**, [CD24](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20to29.html#cd24)
  + Basal: [HMWCK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck34be12.html) ([CK5, CK6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5and6.html) and [CK14](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck14.html)), **CDH3**, [CD44](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd44.html)

Negative stains

* [PSA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspsa.html), [NKX3.1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnkx3.html), [AMACR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsamacr.html), [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:1472](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22982890), [J Pathol Transl Med 2016;50:345](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27498545))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Most commonly mutated genes are associated with cell cycle progression, including *TP53*, *PIK3CA*, *RB1* and *FGFR3* ([Nature 2014;507:315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24476821))
* UroVysion is an FDA approved fluorescent in situ hybridization (FISH) that detects aneuploidy in chromosomes 3, 7 and 17 and loss of 9p21 locus (72% sensitivity and 83% specificity) ([BJU Int 2013;112:E372](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23350736), [Curr Opin Urol 2019;29:203](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30855372))
* Nuclear matrix proteins (NMP22): group of proteins produced by cancerous cells
  + NMP22 BladderChek test is an FDA approved qualitative assay for surveillance and detection of bladder cancer (50% sensitivity and 87% specificity) ([Curr Opin Urol 2019;29:203](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30855372))
* Bladder tumor antigen (BTA): antigen produced by cancerous cells
  + BTA Trak (quantitative) and BTA Stat (qualitative) are FDA approved assays for surveillance (68 - 77.5% sensitivity and 50 - 75% specificity) ([Curr Opin Urol 2019;29:203](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30855372))

Differential diagnosis

* [Inverted urothelial papilloma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderinvertedpapilloma.html):
  + Smooth or dome shaped surface with monotonous cells growing in an endophytic pattern without the presence of lamina propria invasion ([Hum Pathol 2019;92:18](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31351154))
* [Prostatic adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostateadenoNOS.html):
  + Demonstrates monomorphic cytology with prominent nucleoli
  + Negative for [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgata3.html) / [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html) and positive for [PSA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspsa.html) / [NKX3.1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnkx3.html)
* [Paraganglioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderparaganglioma.html):
  + Similar nested architecture, prominent vascular network and distinguishable by immunohistochemistry
  + Positive for [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) (sustentacular pattern), [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html) and [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html) ([Histopathology 2019;74:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30565299))
  + Negative for [AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html) and [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html)
  + Potential pitfall: also positive for [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgata3.html) ([J
    Mod Pathol 2013;26:1365](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23599157))
* [Nephrogenic adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladdernephroadenoma.html):
  + Rare nuclear atypia and distinguishable by immunohistochemistry: positive for [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html), [PAX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax2.html) and [AMACR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsamacr.html) ([Histopathology 2019;74:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30565299))
* [Cystitis cystica / glandularis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladdercystitiscystica.html):
  + Lacks cytologic atypia, necrosis, stromal reaction and invasion into lamina propria and muscularis propria ([Histopathology 2019;74:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30565299), [Ann Diagn Pathol 2019;38:11](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30380401))
* [Renal cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantrcc.html) (for renal pelvis):
  + Lack of urothelial carcinoma in situ is the most helpful distinguishing factor
  + Positive [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html), negative [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) and negative [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgata3.html) immunoprofile favors renal cell carcinoma; however, stain interpretation and immunohistochemical profile overlap can create diagnostic challenges ([Adv Anat Pathol 2018;25:387](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30157041))
