Definition / general

* Chronic inflammatory disorder that affects many organs, most commonly occurs in urinary bladder
* Single or multiple white-yellow soft raised plaques on the mucosal surface, can mimic malignancy ([Nephrourol Mon 2014;6:e18522](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25695020))

Essential features

* Histiocytic infiltrate with characteristic cytoplasmic basophilic inclusions
* Round histiocytes (von Hansemann cells) and rounded, concentric, basophilic, cytoplasmic inclusions (Michaelis-Gutmann bodies)
* Mass forming, typically polypoid bladder mass with intact mucosa

Terminology

* First described by Michaelis and Gutmann in 1902 and later named by von Hansseman ([Ztschr Kin Med 1902;47:208](https://scholar.google.com/scholar_lookup?journal=Ztschr+Kin+Med&title=Ueber+Einschlusse+in+Blasentumoren&author=L.+Michealis&author=C.+Gutmann&volume=47&publication_year=1902&pages=208-215&), [Arch Ital Urol Androl 2022;94:350](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36165484))
* Malakoplakia comes from Greek words malakos (soft) and plakos (plaque)

Epidemiology

* More common in immunocompromised patients, patients with diabetes mellitus, renal transplantation recipients ([Arch Ital Urol Androl 2022;94:350](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36165484))
* Prevalence: 4 times higher in women
* Mostly affects people over 40 years old

Sites

* Mostly occurs at genitourinary tract (most commonly urinary bladder, followed by renal parenchyma, prostate, rarely ureter) ([Arch Ital Urol Androl 2022;94:350](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36165484))
* Can affect many organs including gastrointestinal tract, skin, thyroid, testis, lung, retroperitoneum, adrenal gland, cerebrum, vertebrae, pleura, tonsils, spleen ([Proc (Bayl Univ Med Cent) 2020;33:389](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32675959), [N Engl J Med 2019;380:580](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30726684), [Int J Surg Pathol 2015;23:308](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25663335), [Transplant Proc 2022;54:173](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34973840), [J Urol 1981;125:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7009885))

Pathophysiology

* Thought to be a defect in macrophage phagolysosomal response to bacterial infection, resulting in accumulation of macrophages with calcified inclusions composed of undigested bacteria and products ([Am J Kidney Dis 2016;68:e27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27664482))

Etiology

* Exact etiology unknown
* Recurrent urinary infections (*E. coli* and other gram negative bacilli) and immunosupression

Clinical features

* Polypoid mass with intact mucosa
* Patients usually present with urinary symptoms and urinary tract infection (72% due to *E. coli*)
* Hydroureteronephrosis ([Arch Ital Urol Androl 2022;94:350](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36165484))
* Rarely associated with calcified plaques (encrusted cystitis) and renal failure ([Ir J Med Sci 2006;175:74](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17312835), [Nat Clin Pract Urol 2008;5:516](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18679396))

Diagnosis

* Histological by biopsy

Case reports

* 55 year old woman with history of kidney disease presented with general malaise and worsening renal failure and was found to have a bladder mass ([Pathol Res Pract 2022;237:153852](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35878530))
* 61 year old immunocompromised man with symptoms of urinary obstruction was found to have exuberant malakoplakia of prostate presenting as prostatic abscess ([J Endourol Case Rep 2020;6:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33102734))
* 82 year old woman with intravesical malakoplakia mimicking an aggressive transitional cell carcinoma ([Bladder (San Franc) 2020;7:e44](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32934968))

Treatment

* Antibiotics or surgical excision

Sample pathology report

* Bladder lesions, biopsy:
  + Benign urothelial mucosa with acute and chronic inflammation and prominent histiocytic infiltrate, consistent with malakoplakia (see comment)
  + Comment: Special stains for von Kossa (calcium) were performed on block A1 and highlight concentrically layered basophilic inclusions (Michaelis-Gutmann bodies), supporting the diagnosis of malakoplakia.
* Bladder lesions, biopsy:
  + Malakoplakia

Differential diagnosis

* [Urothelial carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderurothelialinvasivegen.html) ([poorly differentiated](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderundifferentiatedosteoclast.html), [plasmacytoid](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignanturothelialcarcinomasubtypesplasma.html) and [lymphoepithelioma-like](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderlymphoepithel.html)):
  + Overlying epithelium may show in situ lesion
  + [Cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) stains are positive
* [Gleason pattern 5 prostatic adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostateadenoNOS.html):
  + [Cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) stains are positive
  + [NKX3.1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnkx3.html) stain is positive

Board review style question #1

What is the name of the targetoid bodies that are pathognomonic for malakoplakia?

1. Asbestos bodies
2. Aschoff bodies
3. Howell-Jolly bodies
4. Michaelis-Gutmann bodies
5. Psammoma bodies

Board review style answer #1

**D.** Michaelis-Gutmann bodies are characteristic inclusions seen in many cases of malakoplakia. They are rounded, concentric, intracytoplasmic, basophilic inclusions, caused by accumulation of undigested bacteria and products that are calcified. They can be highlighted by von Kossa stain. Answer A is incorrect because asbestos bodies are golden brown colored and fusiform to beaded rod shaped structures. Answer B is incorrect because Aschoff bodies are microscopic nodules of cells composed of pleomorphic cells, lymphocytes, plasma cells and fibrinoid necrosis. Answer C is incorrect because Howell-Jolly bodies are spherical intracytoplasmic inclusions within red blood cells. Answer E is incorrect because psammoma bodies are a round collection of calcium, a form of calcification that is mostly extracellular and much bigger than an intracellular inclusion.

Board review style question #2

[![BRQ image](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/bladdermalakoplakiagoksel06.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/bladdermalakoplakiagoksel06.jpg)

Which of the following stains used in the diagnosis of malakoplakia targets structures shown with yellow arrows in the image above?

1. CD4
2. CD68
3. CD163
4. Von Kossa

Board review style answer #2

**D.** Von Kossa. The von Kossa stain highlights the Michaelis-Gutmann bodies in malakoplakia. Answers B and C are incorrect because CD68 and CD163 stains highlight the histiocytes in malakoplakia. Answer A is incorrect because CD4 has no role in the diagnosis of malakoplakia.
