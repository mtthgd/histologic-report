Definition / general

* Most common primaries are breast and melanoma; also lung, pancreas, ovary and stomach
* Associated with widely disseminated disease
* Urothelium is usually spared
* Tumors may also arise from local extension from prostate, uterine cervix or rectum
* Difficult to distinguish bladder adenocarcinoma morphologically from extension of colonic adenocarcinoma
* Renal cell metastases: rare (< 40 reported cases); usually men age 35 - 69 years who present with gross hematuria or urinary obstruction if tumor is in bladder neck; metastases also present in other organs; poor prognosis ([Mod Pathol 1999;12:351](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10229498)); may be due to hematogenous spread, retrograde spread from renal vein or renal hilar lymphatics or direct intraluminal transit

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Morphology varies based on the primary tumor
* Metastatic renal cell carcinoma: delicate fibrovascular stroma with abundant sinusoidal vessels; nests of polygonal cells with abundant clear cytoplasm and nuclei ranging from small and hyperchromatic with inconspicuous nucleoli to large irregular nuclei with prominent nucleoli; may resemble urothelial carcinoma with clear cell features

Positive stains

* **Breast**: positive for [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) / [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html)
* **Lung**: positive for [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html)
* **Renal cell**: [CAM 5.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscam52.html), [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html), Leu-M1 ([CD15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd15.html))

Negative stains

* [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html), [34betaE12](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck34be12.html), [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html), [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html)

Differential diagnosis

* **Colorectal carcinoma (extension)**: positive for CK20, villin, beta catenin (nuclear)
* **Prostatic adenocarinoma (extension)**: positive for PSA, PSAP, AMACR, Leu7
* **Urothelial carcinoma with glandular differentiation**: positive for CK7, CK20, 34betaE12, Uroplakin, Thrombomodulin
