Definition / general

* Rare bladder tumor similar to [colonic counterpart](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumortvadenoma.html)

Essential features

* Villous adenoma of the urinary tract is a rare but important lesion
* Morphologic features of these tumors are similar to those of colonic adenomas
* Villous adenoma of the urinary tract should be removed completely and sampled thoroughly to avoid missing a more aggressive component

Terminology

* Also called villous tumor

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8261/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=69) - villous adenoma, NOS

Epidemiology

* Rare
* Older patients ([Int J Urol 2008;15:551](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18489648))
* M > F ([Hum Pathol 2002;33:236](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11957151))
* Wide age range (40 - 70 years)

Sites

* Urinary bladder, urachus, renal pelvis, ureter and urethra ([Am J Surg Pathol 1999;23:764](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10403298))
* In bladder, dome and the posterior wall, followed by urachus and trigone are common sites ([Hum Pathol 2002;33:236](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11957151))

Pathophysiology

* Mechanism underlying the origin of villous adenoma of the urinary bladder is still speculative ([Mod Pathol 2009;22:1280](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19593329))
* It has been suggested that a cloacal rest may remain in the bladder and subsequently proliferate into a glandular neoplasm ([Am J Surg 1955;90:693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/13258971))
* Alternative theory suggests that villous adenoma is the product of a chronic irritation metaplasia → dysplasia → carcinoma sequence ([J Clin Pathol 1993;46:450](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8320324))

Clinical features

* Hematuria, mucosuria and irritative symptoms ([Am J Surg Pathol 1999;23:764](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10403298), [Hum Pathol 2002;33:236](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11957151), [BMC Urol 2013;13:36](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23870731))
* May be associated with [in situ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderadenoinsitu.html) or [invasive adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderadeno.html) at diagnosis, less often with [in situ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladdercis.html) or [invasive urothelial carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderurothelialinvasivegen.html)

Diagnosis

* Cystoscopic findings with tissue resected
* Imaging or colonoscopy findings to rule out a colonic malignancy
* Thorough microscopic examination of excised tissue

Radiology description

* No specific findings on ultrasonography, CT or MRI

Prognostic factors

* Excellent prognosis if isolated, poorer if only [in situ adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderadenoinsitu.html), poorest if [in situ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladdercis.html) or [invasive urothelial carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderurothelialinvasivegen.html) ([Hum Pathol 2002;33:236](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11957151))

Case reports

* 45 year old woman with polypoid mass in posterolateral wall of urinary bladder ([J Cancer Res Ther 2015;11:665](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26458700))
* 63 year old woman with focal well differentiated adenocarcinoma of urethral orifice ([World J Clin Cases 2019;7:891](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31024961))
* 85 year old woman with nonmuscle invasive bladder cancer ([BMC Urol 2013;13:36](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23870731))
* 90 year old woman with tubulovillous architecture arising in the diverticulum ([Pol J Pathol 2011;62:179](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22102078))
* Villous adenoma arising in the native bladder mucosa and the upper urinary tract with coexisting neuroendocrine carcinoma ([Int J Surg Pathol 2019;27:450](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30701999))

Treatment

* Complete excision
* Excellent prognosis if isolated ([Am J Surg Pathol 1999;23:764](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10403298))
* Coexisting infiltrating carcinoma or urothelial carcinoma suggests a more aggressive course

Gross description

* Resembles [papillary urothelial carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderHGpap.html)

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Resembles [colonic villous adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumortvadenoma.html) with pointed processes lined by pseudostratified columnar epithelium with nuclear stratification, nuclear crowding, nuclear hyperchromasia, occasional prominent nucleoli, occasional mitoses ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2016;140:91](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26717061))
* [Chronic cystitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderchroniccystitis.html) with [intestinal type glandular metaplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderintestinalmetaplasia.html) and extensive cellular atypia ([dysplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderdysplasia.html)) in the flat mucosa may be seen
* Must sample entire lesion to rule out [adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderadeno.html) ([in situ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderadenoinsitu.html) or invasive)

Cytology description

* Urine cytology may show mucin producing cells or cell clusters with gland formation or arrangement along basement membrane
* When glandular cells with columnar mucin filled goblet cells are seen in urine cytology, the presence of a primary glandular lesion of the urinary bladder, such as villous adenoma, should be considered possible ([Diagn Cytopathol 2016;44:632](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27121034))

Positive stains

* [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html) (100%)
* [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html) (100%)
* [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html) (luminal, 89%)
* Acidic [mucins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmucins.html) ([Alcian blue](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalcianblue.html))
* [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html) (56%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:1322](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18670358))

Negative stains

* [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html), [beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html) (is not nuclear), [thrombomodulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsthrombomodulin.html)
* Variable [PSMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspsma.html)
* [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgata3.html), [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html)

Sample pathology report

* Bladder, transurethral resection:
  + Villous adenoma (negative for carcinoma in situ or invasive carcinoma)
  + Muscularis propria is present and is negative for neoplasia

Differential diagnosis

* [Adenocarcinoma in situ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderadenoinsitu.html) arising in villous adenoma:
  + Psuedostratification, loss of polarity, increased pleomorphism with vesicular and round nuclei
  + Intraluminal cribriforming
  + No invasion
* [Colonic adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumoradenocarcinoma.html), well differentiated extending into bladder:
  + Clinical history of colonic tumor
  + Definite invasion identified
  + Usually [beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html) nuclear positive
* [Papillary urothelial carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderHGpap.html) with villoglandular differentiation:
  + Typically has concurrent high grade papillary urothelial carcinoma component
  + [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgata3.html) positive
* [Nephrogenic adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladdernephroadenoma.html), papillary variant:
  + Has bland cytology
  + [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html) positive

Board review style question #1

A urinary bladder villous adenoma is most likely to show which of the following immunohistochemical profiles?

1. CK7+, CK20+, beta catenin (nuclear)+, thrombomodulin+
2. CK7-, CK20+, beta catenin (nuclear)+, thrombomodulin-
3. CK7+, CK20+, beta catenin (nuclear)-, thrombomodulin-
4. CK7+, CK20-, beta catenin (nuclear)-, thrombomodulin+

Board review style answer #1

**C.** CK7+, CK20+, beta catenin (nuclear)-, thrombomodulin-. The immunohistochemical profile of villous adenoma has been reported to be positive for CK20 and CEA, and ~50% of cases of urinary tract villous adenomas exhibit positive results for CK7. It is very important to distinguish from metastatic adenocarcinoma derived from the colon. Most of the cases of adenocarcinoma derived from the colon are reported to be positive for beta catenin (nuclear staining) and negative for thrombomodulin and CK7, while urothelial carcinoma cases are positive for CK7, thrombomodulin and negative for beta catenin (nuclear staining).

Board review style question #2

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/bladdervillousadenomamanucha05.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/bladdervillousadenomamanucha05.jpg)

A 50 year old man with a long term history of urethral stricture and chronic cystitis presents with mucosuria. Cystoscopy reveals a papillary growth which is biopsied and is diagnosed as villous adenoma of the urinary bladder. What is the best treatment option for the patient?

1. Bacillus Calmette-Guérin (BCG) therapy
2. Chemotherapy
3. Close followup
4. Complete excision

Board review style answer #2

**D.** Complete excision. Pure and isolated villous adenomas have an excellent prognosis, even without radical resection. However, urinary tract villous adenomas have also been found in association with in situ or invasive adenocarcinoma, squamous cell carcinoma and flat in situ urothelial carcinoma. In these cases, the lesions have sometimes been clinically more aggressive, with disease recurrence or even distant metastasis. The heterogeneity of the lesions associated with urinary tract villous adenomas has important implications for diagnosis and management. Any urinary tract lesion diagnosed by biopsy as villous adenoma must be completely excised and thoroughly sampled.
