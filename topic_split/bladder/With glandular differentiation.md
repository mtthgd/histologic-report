Definition / general

* Glandular differentiation is defined by the presence of true glandular spaces, usually tubular or gland-like lumina, or with morphology similar to enteric adenocarcinomas and variable mucin production
  + Rarely may contain signet ring component ([Mod Pathol 2009;22:S96](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19494856), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2007;131:1244](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17683187))
* Cytoplasmic mucin containing cells are seen in normal urothelium and are not considered to represent glandular differentiation ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2007;131:1244](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17683187))

Essential features

* Presence of true glandular spaces
  + Cytoplasmic mucin is seen in normal urothelium and not diagnostic of glandular differentiation
* No known prognostic significance

Epidemiology

* Foci of glandular differentiation are less common than squamous differentiation, and seen in up to 10% of urothelial carcinomas ([Mod Pathol 2009;22:S96](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19494856))

Sites

* Bladder, kidney

Clinical features

* Similar to other urothelial carcinomas

Prognostic factors

* Prognostic implications of glandular differentiation are unclear; some studies suggest adverse outcome ([PLoS One 2014;9:e107027](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25191845), [Mod Pathol 2009;22:S96](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19494856)), others show similar outcomes compared to pure urothelial carcinoma ([Urol Oncol 2014;32:117](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23477878))

Case reports

* Urothelial carcinoma of renal pelvis with gland-like lumina ([Biomedical Research 2013;24:175](http://www.biomedres.info/biomedical-research/case-report-immunohistochemical-analysis-of-a-case-of-urothelialcarcinoma-of-the-renal-pelvis-showing-glandlike-lumina.pdf))

Treatment

* Too few cases to establish specific treatment recommendations

Microscopic (histologic) description

* True glandular structures within a conventional urothelial carcinoma
* Glands are of either tubular or enteric type, with a single layer of neoplastic columnar cells radially arranged around a lumen, with or without mucin production
* May resemble enteric adenocarcinomas
* May have signet ring morphology, with neoplastic signet ring cells floating in pools of mucin

Positive stains

* [MUC5AC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmuc2.html#muc5ac) ([Virchows Arch 2001;439:609](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11764379)), [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html), [S100P](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100p.html)
* Variable with [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgata3.html) and [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) ([Hum Pathol 2014;45:1473](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24780825))

Negative stains

* [Villin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvillin.html) ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2002;126:1057](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12204054)), [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html) ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2007;131:1244](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17683187))
* [Uroplakin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsuroplakiniii.html) and [thrombomodulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsthrombomodulin.html) are often negative ([Hum Pathol 2014;45:1473](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24780825))

Differential diagnosis

* [Collecting duct carcinoma of the kidney](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantcollectingduct.html): does not contain conventional urothelial carcinoma, usually positive for PAX8, CD10 and Vimentin
* [Cystitis cystica and glandularis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladdercystitiscystica.html): does not have malignant component
* **Metastatic adenocarcinoma**: clinical history, site specific antibodies
* [Microcystic](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladdermicrocystic.html) **and** [nested variants of urothelial carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladdernested.html): these may have lumina with central necrotic debris but not true glandular formation
* **Urothelial carcinoma of renal pelvis with intratubular spread**: smooth contours, limited to pyramids of medulla, no invasion or desmoplasia ([Am J Clin Exp Urol 2014;2:102](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25374911))

Additional references
