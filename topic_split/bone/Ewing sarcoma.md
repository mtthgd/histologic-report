Definition / general

* Small round cell sarcoma showing gene fusions involving one member of the FET family of genes (usually *EWSR1*) and a member of the E26 transformation specific (ETS) family of transcription factors
* American pathologist James Ewing (1866 - 1943) first described the tumor as diffuse endothelioma of bone ([CA Cancer J Clin 1972;22:95](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4622125))

Essential features

* Small round cell sarcoma
* CD99 diffuse membranous expression
* Gene fusion involving FET family of genes (usually *EWSR1*) and member of ETS family of transcription factors, most commonly *EWSR1-FLI1* (~85 - 90%)

Terminology

* Ewing sarcoma of bone
* Extraskeletal Ewing sarcoma
* Adamantinoma-like Ewing sarcoma
* Primitive neuroectodermal tumor (PNET), term no longer recommended
* Askin tumor (Ewing sarcoma arising in chest wall), term no longer recommended

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9260/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=88) - Ewing sarcoma
* ICD-11: [2B52.3](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f1650581103) - Ewing sarcoma of soft tissue
* ICD-11: [2B52.Y](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f1651102476%2fmms%2fother) - Ewing sarcoma of bone and articular cartilage of other specified sites

Epidemiology

* Second most common malignant bone tumor in children / young adults after osteosarcoma ([Cancer 1996;78:532](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8697401))
* M:F = 1.5:1 ([J Pediatr Hematol Oncol 2008;30:425](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18525458))
* Peak incidence in second decade of life
* Nearly 80% patients Uncommon in patients younger than 5 years and older than 30 years
* Predominantly Caucasians, rare in African Americans, Asians and Native Americans ([Cancer Epidemiol Biomarkers Prev 2011;20:449](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21212061))
* Overall incidence is 3 cases per million per year in United States population ([J Pediatr Hematol Oncol 2008;30:425](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18525458))

Sites

* Diaphyseal or metaphyseal - diaphyseal region of long bones (lower > upper extremity)
* Pelvic bones and ribs common
* Bone > soft tissue, approximately 12% extraskeletal ([Nat Rev Dis Primers 2018;4:5](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29977059))

Pathophysiology

* Primary mutation generates fusion gene between the FET and ETS families, altering gene expression and promoting tumorigenesis
* Additional, secondary mutations include *STAG2* (15 - 22%), *CDKN2A* (12%) and *TP53* (7%) ([PLoS Genet 2014;10:e1004475](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25010205), [Cancer Discov 2014;4:1326](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25186949), [Cancer Discov 2014;4:1342](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25223734), [N Engl J Med 2015;373:2336](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26580448))
* Majority of mutations are sporadic, while germline have been reported ([N Engl J Med 2015;373:2336](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26580448))

Etiology

* Chromosomal translocation commonly involving *EWSR1* and ETS partner
* Cell of origin possibly mesenchymal stem cell ([Cancer Cell 2007;11:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17482132))

Clinical features

* Localized pain and swelling ([J Bone Joint Surg Am 2000;82:667](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10819277))
* Painful enlarging mass
* Associated pathologic fracture sometimes present
* Systemic findings (fever, weight loss, anemia, leukocytosis and increased sedimentation rate) can occur

Diagnosis

* Integration of clinical, radiographic, immunohistochemical and molecular information
* Small round cell morphology
* CD99 diffuse membranous expression
* Genetic confirmation often required (all cases harbor *FET-ETS* fusion)

Laboratory

* Anemia, leukocytosis and elevated sedimentation rate can occur

Radiology description

* Plain radiograph
  + Osteolytic permeative lesion
  + Poorly defined margins
  + Moth eaten bone destruction
  + Aggressive periosteal reaction (onion skin appearance)
  + Sunburst periosteal reaction less common than osteosarcoma
  + Saucerization, extraosseous tumor erodes cortex
  + No evidence of tumor osteoid / matrix production
  + Often underestimates extent of tumor
* CT / MRI / PET ([Semin Musculoskelet Radiol 2019;23:36](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30699452))
  + Fully define primary lesion
  + Evaluate soft tissue extension
  + Assess for metastatic disease (~25%)
  + T1 weighted MRI: low to intermediate signal intensity
  + T2 weighted MRI: heterogeneous but predominantly high signal

Prognostic factors

* Presence of metastases main prognostic factor
* Localized, resectable disease with 5 year survival rate of 70%
* Metastatic disease with 5 year survival rate of < 30%
* Surgical removal of resectable lung metastases improves survival
* Favorable: complete pathologic response to neoadjuvant chemotherapy, small tumor size, superficial location, easily resectable ([Bone Joint J 2016;98-B:1138](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27482030))
* Unfavorable: early relapse, presence of metastases, anatomic location in trunk / pelvis

* 17 year old girl with primary superficial Ewing sarcoma of the left thigh ([J Cutan Pathol 2020;47:970](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32483824))

* 26 year old woman with Ewing sarcoma of temporal bone with prominent myxoid stroma ([Pathol Res Pract 2019;215:152665](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31585812))
* 41 year old man with sinonasal adamantinoma-like Ewing sarcoma ([Pathol Res Pract 2017;213:422](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28262274))

Treatment

* Neoadjuvant chemotherapy and surgery
* Ewing specific protocol of alternating vincristine / doxorubicin / cyclophosphamide and ifosfamide / etoposide (VDC / IE)
* Chemotherapy improved prognosis to 75% for 5 year survival
* Radiotherapy for surgically inaccessible tumors, inadequate margins and palliation
* Limited efficacy of immunotherapeutic approaches ([J Immunother Cancer 2020;8:e000653](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33293354))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Classical Ewing sarcoma ([Virchows Arch 2009;455:397](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19841938))
  + Uniform small round cells
  + Tumor cells 1 - 2x size of lymphocytes
  + Round nuclei
  + Finely stippled chromatin
  + Inconspicuous nucleoli
  + Scant clear to eosinophilic cytoplasm
  + Indistinct cytoplasmic membranes
  + Sheet-like growth pattern
  + Islands separated by dense fibrous tissue
  + Subset with neuroectodermal differentiation (Homer-Wright pseudorosettes)
* Atypical Ewing sarcoma ([Virchows Arch 2011;458:281](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21181413))
  + Nuclear enlargement
  + Irregular nuclear contours
  + Vesicular or coarse chromatin
  + Prominent nucleoli
* Adamantinoma-like Ewing sarcoma ([Head Neck Pathol 2020;14:59](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31950471), [Am J Surg Pathol 1999;23:159](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9989842), [Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1267](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26034869))
  + Nests of basaloid cells
  + Peripheral palisading and cording
  + Prominent myxoid, fibromyxoid or hyalinized stroma
  + Focal keratin pearl formation
  + High grade features with minimal pleomorphism

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Most common translocation is t(11;22)(q24;q12), resulting in *EWSR1-FLI1* fusion (~85 - 90%) ([Cancer Genet 2011;204:351](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21872822))
* Second most common is t(21;22)(q22;q12), resulting in *EWSR1-ERG* fusion (~5 - 10%) ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2016;55:340](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26690869))
* Rare fusions (< 1%) involve *ETV1* (7p22), *ETV4* (17q21) and *FEV* (2q35-36) ([Adv Anat Pathol 2018;25:314](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29911999))
* 2 rare translocations of *ERG* or *FEV* with *FUS*: t(16;21)(p11;q22) *FUS-ERG* and t(2;16)(q35;p11) *FUS-FEV* ([Adv Anat Pathol 2018;25:314](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29911999))

Ewing family of tumors

Sample pathology report

* Bone, right proximal humerus mass, core biopsy:
  + Ewing sarcoma (see comment)
  + Comment: This biopsy shows an undifferentiated small round cell sarcoma involving bone with focal areas of necrosis and scattered mitotic figures. Immunohistochemical studies show that the tumor cells are positive for CD99 (diffuse, membranous) and NKX2.2, while they are negative for cytokeratin cocktail, S100 protein, SMA, desmin and CD45. RT-PCR demonstrates the presence of an *EWSR1-FLI1* fusion, confirming the above diagnosis.

Differential diagnosis

* [Mesenchymal chondrosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueeskchondrosarcomamesenchymal.html):
  + Areas with hyaline cartilage differentiation
  + Hemangiopericytoma-like vasculature
  + *HEY1-NCOA2* gene fusion
* [Metastatic tumors](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonemetstobone.html) with small round cell morphology, including [neuroblastoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/adrenalneuroblastoma.html):
  + [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html) negative or focal / patchy
  + *N-MYC* amplified (neuroblastoma)
* [Alveolar rhabdomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuealvrhabdo.html):
  + [Desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) positive
  + [Myogenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html) and [MyoD1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyod1.html) positive
  + *PAX3-FOXO1* and *PAX7-FOXO1* gene fusions
* [Primary bone lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonelymphomageneral.html):
  + [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html) positive
  + [TdT](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstdt.html) positive
* [Small cell osteosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneosteosarcomageneral.html):
  + Tumor osteoid / matrix present
  + [SATB2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssatb2.html) positive
* [Poorly differentiated synovial sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesynovialsarc.html):
  + [Cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) variably positive
  + Biphasic type has gland-like epithelial structures
  + *SS18-SSX* gene fusion
* [CIC rearranged sarcomas](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneCICDUX4.html):
  + *CIC* gene rearrangement, often *CIC-DUX4*
  + [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html) patchy positive
  + [NKX2.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsNKX2.html) negative
  + **ETV4** and [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html) positive ([Mod Pathol 2016;29:1324](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27443513))
* [BCOR rearranged sarcomas](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneBCORCCNB3.html):
  + *BCOR* genetic alterations
  + Round to spindle cell morphology
  + Variable myxoid stromal change
* [Round cell sarcoma with *EWSR1*-non-ETS fusion](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuemolecular.html#roundcellsarcoma):
  + *EWSR1-NFATC2* and *FUS-NFATC2* gene fusions
  + *EWSR1-PATZ1* gene fusion
* [Desmoplastic small round cell tumor (DSRCT)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuedsrct.html):
  + [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html) positive
  + [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html) negative (cytoplasmic > membranous)
* [Basaloid squamous cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophagusbasaloidSCC.html) **(adamantinoma-like variant)**:
  + [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html) negative
  + Lacks *EWSR1* gene rearrangement
* **High grade neuroendocrine carcinoma (adamantinoma-like variant)**:
  + [Synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html) and [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html) positive
  + [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html) negative
  + Lacks *EWSR1* gene rearrangement
