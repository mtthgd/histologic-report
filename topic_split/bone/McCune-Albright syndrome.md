Definition / general

* McCune-Albright syndrome (MAS) is a rare condition that results from postzygotic activating somatic mutations in the alpha subunit of the *GNAS* gene, leading to continued stimulation of endocrine glands
  + Typically codon 201 and rarely codon 227 ([Histopathology 2007;50:691](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17493233))
* Exact presentation and severity depends on the extent of mosaicism and the involved tissues ([Curr Osteoporos Rep 2015;13:146](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25851935))
* Most patients are diagnosed in childhood or adolescence

Essential features

* Classically defined as the triad of
  + Polyostotic fibrous dysplasia of bone
  + Precocious puberty
  + > 3 café au lait macules
* However, a more inclusive definition has been proposed, consisting of
  + Monostotic fibrous dysplasia of bone or polyostotic fibrous dysplasia of bone
  + Hyperfunctional endocrinopathy or café au lait macules ([J Bone Miner Res 2006;21:P99](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17229019))

Terminology

* McCune-Albright syndrome (MAS) has no synonyms

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [Q78.1](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/Q00-Q99/Q65-Q79/Q78-/Q78.1) - polyostotic fibrous dysplasia
* ICD-11: [FB80.0](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f1704766818) - fibrous dysplasia of bone

Epidemiology

* Very rare disorder affecting an estimated 1 in 100,000 to 1 in 1,000,000 people ([Orphanet J Rare Dis 2008;3:12](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18489744))
* F = M

Sites

* [Fibrous dysplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonefibrousdysplasia.html) is most commonly found in the proximal femur and the skull base but can involve any bone ([J Bone Miner Res 2007;22:1468](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17501668))
* In contrast, nonsyndromic fibrous dysplasia is most commonly found in the ribs
* Any endocrine organ can be involved
* [Café au lait macules](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticcafeaulait.html) are typically near, but do not cross, the midline ([Orphanet J Rare Dis 2008;3:12](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18489744))

Pathophysiology

* Constitutively active mutant *GNAS* results in increased cyclic adenosine monophosphate, which
  + Promotes proliferation and inhibits differentiation of fibroblasts, which replace normal bone and produce fibrous dysplasia lesions
  + Promotes proliferation and autonomous function of endocrine organs
* Fibrous dysplasia elaborates fibroblast growth factor 23, which
  + Inhibits renal resorption of phosphate
  + Causes hypophosphatemia
  + Causes hyperphosphaturia
* Examples:
  + Unregulated production of cortisol, leading to [Cushing syndrome](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/adrenalcushings.html)
  + Unregulated production of gonadotropin releasing hormone, leading to precocious puberty ([Curr Osteoporos Rep 2015;13:146](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25851935))

Etiology

* Results from de novo mutations in *GNAS* ([Curr Osteoporos Rep 2015;13:146](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25851935))
  + No inherited case has ever been reported
  + Germline *GNAS* mutation is presumed embryonic lethal
* Has no risk factors

Clinical features

* 2 most common presentations are fibrous dysplasia related pain and precocious puberty
* [Fibrous dysplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonefibrousdysplasia.html)
  + May be monostotic or polyostotic
  + When polyostotic, it may be unilateral, in keeping with postzygotic mutation
* Classical and most common endocrinopathy is precocious puberty; however, other endocrinopathies include
  + Hypophosphatemia
  + [Hyperparathyroidism](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/parathyroidprimarypth.html)
  + [Cushing disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/adrenalcushings.html)
  + Acromegaly
  + Macroorchidism
* [Café au lait macules](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticcafeaulait.html) typically
  + Are light brown in color but can vary depending on patient's skin color
  + Are present at, or shortly after, birth
  + Are recognized after another feature of the disease has prompted a skin survey
  + Are near the midline
  + Respect the lines of Blaschko
  + Have jagged irregular borders, unlike the smooth borders that are characteristic of macules seen in [neurofibromatosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueNF1.html)
  + Are ipsilateral with fibrous dysplasia ([Orphanet J Rare Dis 2008;3:12](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18489744))

Diagnosis

* Essentially a clinical diagnosis
* Genetic testing:
  + Not routinely performed but available
  + False negatives are common due to mosaicism and sampling
  + Positive results merely confirm a clinical diagnosis
  + No genotype / phenotype correlation, therefore no prognostic value ([Orphanet J Rare Dis 2008;3:12](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18489744))
* When there is clinical suspicion:
  + Skeletal survey to identify lesions suspicious for fibrous dysplasia
  + CT is more sensitive for craniofacial fibrous dysplasia
  + Biopsy can confirm fibrous dysplasia
  + Skin survey to identify café au lait macules
  + If not apparent at presentation, endocrinopathies may become apparent with laboratory studies and focused examinations

Laboratory

* Some patients have multiple endocrinopathies; others have none
* The following laboratory abnormalities may be present in any combination ([Orphanet J Rare Dis 2008;3:12](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18489744)):
  + Depressed serum phosphate
  + Elevated urine phosphate
  + Elevated cortisol
  + Elevated sex steroid hormones
  + Elevated thyroid hormone
  + Depressed thyroid stimulating hormone

Prognostic factors

* Severity of hypophosphatemia and number / extent of fibrous dysplasia lesions correlates with the risk of bone pain and pathologic fracture ([J Bone Miner Res 2016;31:2167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27340799))
* Sites of fibrous dysplasia are established early and burden of disease typically plateaus by age 15 ([Expert Opin Investig Drugs 2007;16:761](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17501688))

Case reports

* Preterm neonate girl with Cushing syndrome, hyperthyroidism and café au lait macules ([J Med Case Rep 2015;9:189](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26341786))
* Neonate girl with severe cholestasis requiring liver transplant ([J Clin Res Pediatr Endocrinol 2019;11:100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29991465))
* 4 year old girl also diagnosed with Mazabraud syndrome ([Acta Biomed 2017;88:198](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28845837))
* 39 year old man whose autopsy revealed extensive mosaicism for *GNAS* R201C mutation and subclinical disease manifestations in multiple organs ([J Clin Endocrinol Metab 2014;99:E2029](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25062453))

Treatment

* There is no specific therapy
* Various endocrinopathies are treated just as their sporadic counterparts
* Fibrous dysplasia is treated just as its sporadic counterpart ([StatPearls: McCune Albright Syndrome [Accessed 27 April 2022]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30725777))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* [Fibrous dysplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonefibrousdysplasia.html) lesions are histologically indistinguishable from nonsyndromic fibrous dysplasia, characterized by ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2013;137:134](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23276185))
  + Woven bone with trabeculae that are thin, irregular and curvilinear
  + Fibrous stroma
  + Absence of osteoblastic rimming

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Mutation specific restriction enzyme digest (MSRED) method may identify mutations in codon 201 or 227 of the *GNAS1* gene ([Histopathology 2007;50:691](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17493233))

Sample pathology report

* Bone, biopsy:
  + Fibrous dysplasia of bone (see comment)
  + Comment: Fibrous dysplasia is most commonly sporadic but is also a typical presenting feature of McCune-Albright syndrome and Mazabraud syndrome. Clinical correlation is advised.

Differential diagnosis

* [Nonsyndromic fibrous dysplasia of bone](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonefibrousdysplasia.html):
  + Results from postzygotic *GNAS* mutations
  + Other features of McCune-Albright syndrome are absent or as yet undeclared
    - Endocrinopathies are absent
    - Café au lait macules are absent
* [Mazabraud syndrome](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonemazabraud.html):
  + Results from postzygotic *GNAS* mutations
  + Presents with polyostotic fibrous dysplasia and [intramuscular myxomas](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuemyxoma.html)
  + Typically diagnosed in adulthood
  + Patients may satisfy criteria for both McCune-Albright syndrome and Mazabraud syndrome
* **Hyperparathyroidism jaw tumor syndrome**:
  + Severe [hyperparathyroidism](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/parathyroidprimarypth.html) from [parathyroid adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/parathyroidpthadenoma.html) or [carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/parathyroidpthca.html)
* [Ossifying fibromas](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mandiblemaxillaossifyingfibroma.html) of jaw may be confused with fibrous dysplasia:
  + Randomly distributed mature (lamellar) bone spicules rimmed by osteoblasts admixed with a fibrous stroma

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/bonefibrousdysplasiamccunealbrightdavis03.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/bonefibrousdysplasiamccunealbrightdavis03.jpg)

A 23 year old man diagnosed at age 8 with McCune-Albright syndrome is considering starting a family and seeks genetic counseling. Which of the following best describes his likelihood of having an affected child?

1. 0% chance of having an affected child
2. 25% chance of having an affected female child
3. 25% chance of having an affected male child
4. 50% chance of having an affected child
5. 100% chance of having an affected child

Board review style answer #1

**A.** 0% chance of having an affected child. McCune-Albright syndrome results from postzygotic mutations and an inherited case has never been reported. Germline mutation in *GNAS* is believed to be embryonic lethal. The likelihood that the patient will have an affected child is effectively 0% ([Curr Osteoporos Rep 2015;13:146](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25851935)).

Board review style question #2

A 6 year old girl is brought to her pediatrician by her father who is concerned about her early breast development. On exam, the patient's breasts are Tanner stage 3 and the clinician also notes a large, irregularly contoured pigmented macule on her left flank. In addition to starting letrozole, which of the following is an appropriate next step?

1. Diagnose the patient with McCune-Albright syndrome and order a skeletal survey
2. Perform a complete skin survey in clinic and order a skeletal survey
3. Perform a punch biopsy at the edge of the macule
4. Send a blood sample for sequencing of *GNAS*
5. Send a tissue sample for sequencing of *GNAS*

Board review style answer #2

**B.** Perform a complete skin survey in clinic and order a skeletal survey. A complete skin survey and skeletal survey would potentially reveal additional findings that would rule in or provisionally rule out McCune-Albright syndrome (MAS). As presented in the question, the patient has findings suggestive of but not diagnostic for MAS, making answer A wrong. A punch biopsy, as in answer C, would likely reveal melanocyte proliferation but would not aid in diagnosis or direct treatment. Sequencing studies, as in answers D and E, if negative, would not rule out MAS and if positive, would only confirm a clinical diagnosis; thus, neither is contributory ([Curr Osteoporos Rep 2015;13:146](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25851935)).
