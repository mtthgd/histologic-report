Definition / general

* Benign, bone forming tumor composed of mature cortical type or less frequently, trabecular bone, typically involving the craniofacial skeleton

Essential features

* Benign osteogenic tumor
* Usually involves the surface of craniofacial bones
* Admixture of mature lamellar and woven bone patterns, with Haversian-like canals
* Cortical type bone architecture is typical
* Lack of cellular atypia

Terminology

* Torus palatinus (if palate is involved)
* Torus mandibularis (if mandible is involved)
* Parosteal osteoma
* Button osteoma
* Ivory exostosis

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9180/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=87) - osteoma, NOS
* ICD-11:
  + [XH4818](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f1162753778) - osteoma, NOS
  + [2E83.Z](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f1748271920%2fmms%2funspecified) - benign osteogenic tumor of unspecified site

Epidemiology

* Incidence:
  + Accounts for 0.03% of biopsied primary bone tumors
  + Paranasal sinus osteoma: 3 - 6.4% of CT scans performed for sinus conditions ([Acta Otolaryngol 2015;135:602](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25719573))
  + Present in 80% with Gardner syndrome
* Age: fourth through sixth decades
* Sex: M = F

Sites

* Essentially restricted to the craniofacial skeleton (paranasal sinuses, orbit, nasal cavity, jaw bones, cranial vault)
  + Common in frontal and ethmoid sinuses of mandible
* Rare locations: long tubular bones ([Nielson: Diagnostic Pathology - Bone, 2nd Edition, 2017](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0323477771/pathologyoutl-20))
* Typically arises from the surface of the involved bone ([Goldblum: Rosai and Ackerman's Surgical Pathology, 11th Edition, 2017](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0323263399/pathologyoutl-20))

Pathophysiology

* Controversy in the literature whether osteoma represents a true neoplasm or a developmental anomaly

Etiology

* Still unclear
* Theories: reactive bone hyperplasia triggered by trauma or infection; advanced ossification; end stage of fibrous dysplasia or subperiosteal hematomas ([Imaging Sci Dent 2011;41:107](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22010067))
* Presence of multiple osteomas is suggestive of Gardner syndrome, a variant of familial adenomatous polyposis (FAP) that is characterized by colorectal adenomas, osteomas and benign skin tumors; it is caused by mutations in the *APC* gene ([Imaging Sci Dent 2016;46:267](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28035305))

Clinical features

* Commonly an incidental finding ([Nielson: Diagnostic Pathology - Bone, 2nd Edition, 2017](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0323477771/pathologyoutl-20))
* Usually slow growing and asymptomatic ([Surg Pathol Clin 2012;5:101](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26837917))
* Typically appears as a solitary mass; multiple tumors can be seen in Gardner syndrome ([Imaging Sci Dent 2016;46:267](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28035305))
* Depending on the size and location, it can cause headache, facial pain, obstruction of paranasal sinuses, vision disturbancies, interference with mastication

Diagnosis

* Radiology findings often helpful

Radiology description

* In general, the lesion involves the surface of the bone and appears well delineated, ovoid or mushroom shaped, with broad base of attachment to cortex ([Nielson: Diagnostic Pathology - Bone, 2nd Edition, 2017](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0323477771/pathologyoutl-20))
* Xray: radiodense lesion with varying amounts of central lucency
* CT: cortical density on bone window; no contrast enhancement ([Clin Radiol 2000;55:435](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10873688))
* MRI: low signal intensity on T1 and T2 weighted images; no contrast enhancement

Prognostic factors

* Excellent prognosis
* No recurrence if completely excised

Treatment

* Observation for asymptomatic cases
* Surgical excision for symptomatic cases

Gross description

* Typically a solitary lesion ([Nielson: Diagnostic Pathology - Bone, 2nd Edition, 2017](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0323477771/pathologyoutl-20), [Surg Pathol Clin 2012;5:101](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26837917))
* Tan-white
* Oval, round or polypoid
* Firm
* Well circumscribed and attached to the underlying bone
* Cut surface with dense compact bone or trabecular bone

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Admixture of mature lamellar and woven bone patterns, with Haversian-like canals, which may have various sizes and shapes ([Nielson: Diagnostic Pathology - Bone, 2nd Edition, 2017](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0323477771/pathologyoutl-20))
* More frequent: cortical type bone architecture
* Less frequent: trabecular bone architecture
* Osteoblasts rimming bone are inconspicuous and small but can be prominent in growing lesions
* Osteocytes in the matrix are small, not atypical and randomly distributed ([Surg Pathol Clin 2012;5:101](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26837917))
* Intertrabecular marrow spaces are filled with moderately cellular, loose fibrous stroma ([BMJ Case Rep 2013;2013:bcr2013009857](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23929608))
* Inflammatory infiltrates are typically absent

Differential diagnosis

* [Parosteal osteosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneparostealosteo.html):
  + Prominent spindle cell component with atypia
* [Osteoid osteoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneosteoidosteoma.html) / [osteoblastoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneosteoblastoma.html):
  + Intracortical or intramedullary location
  + Large osteoblasts rimming the surface of the bone
* [Osteochondroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneosteochondroma.html):
  + Cartilagineous cap
  + Continuity of the tumor with the medullary cavity
* [Bone island](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneisland.html):
  + Intramedullary location
  + Cortical type bone
  + More common in males
