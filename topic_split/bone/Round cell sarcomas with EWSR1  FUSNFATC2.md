Definition / general

* *NFATC2* rearranged sarcomas are infrequent round cell tumors characterized by *EWSR1::NFATC2* and *FUS::NFATC2* fusions ([Mod Pathol 2020;33:2087](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32415264))

Essential features

* Spindled to rounded cell morphology, mostly low grade features
* Fibromyxoid and hyaline stromal changes
* *EWSR1::NFATC2* and *FUS::NFATC2* fusions
* Sarcomas with *NFATC2* fusions do not respond to Ewing sarcoma chemotherapy regimens ([Hum Pathol 2019;90:45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31078563))

Terminology

* Not recommended: Ewing-like sarcoma

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9366/3](https://seer.cancer.gov/icd-o-3/sitetype.icdo3.20220429.pdf#page=188) - round cell sarcoma with *EWSR1* non-ETS fusions

Epidemiology

* M:F = 5:1
* Patients with *NFATC2* fusion sarcomas show an older median age at diagnosis, compared to those with *EWSR1::FLI1* ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020;59:525](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32362012))
* Presentation in children and adults (age range: 12 - 67 years; median: 33) ([Mod Pathol 2020;33:1930](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32327700), [Sarcoma 2019;2019:9386390](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31049020), [Ann Diagn Pathol 2018;34:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29661713), [Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:1112](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30994538))

Sites

* *EWSR1::NATFC2* sarcomas are predominantly located in bones, with a 4:1 ratio over soft tissue ([Hum Pathol 2019;90:45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31078563))
* *FUS::NFATC2* sarcomas have been reported exclusively in long bones ([Sarcoma 2019;2019:9386390](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31049020), [J Pathol 2018;245:29](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29431183), [Hum Pathol 2019;90:45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31078563))
* Soft tissue cases involve extremities, head, neck and chest wall ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020;59:525](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32362012), [Hum Pathol 2018;81:281](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29626598), [Virchows Arch 2014;465:233](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24993903))

Pathophysiology

* Translocation event results in fusion of *NFATC2* (20q13.2) and *EWSR1* (22q12.2) or *FUS* (16p11.2), respectively ([Clin Cancer Res 2009;15:2259](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19318479), [Cancer Genet Cytogenet 2007;172:12](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17175374))
  + Fusion activates the NFATC2 transcription factor following the loss of the N terminal regulatory domain, which results in the relocation of the chimeric transcription factor to the nucleus
* In contrast to *EWSR1::NFATC2*, the *FUS::NFATC2* fusion gene has not demonstrated amplification
* Clustering analyses suggest that *FUS::NFATC2* sarcomas are a transcriptionally distinct form of *EWSR1::NFATC2* sarcomas ([J Pathol 2018;245:29](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29431183))

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* *EWSR1::NFATC2* and *FUS::NFATC2* sarcomas frequently manifest as painful, locally destructive bone lesions that may invade surrounding soft tissue
* A subset of patients experience symptoms including slow growing mass for years before diagnosis ([Hum Pathol 2018;81:281](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29626598), [Hum Pathol 2019;90:45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31078563))

Diagnosis

* Round cell sarcoma
* Identification of the *EWSR1::NFATC2* and *FUS::NFATC2* fusion transcripts remains the gold standard

Radiology description

* Tendency to occur in the diaphysis of long bones ([Pathol Int 2021;71:614](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34192825))
* Osteolytic destructive lesions, replacing the medullary cavity, associated with a massive extraosseous component ([Mod Pathol 2020;33:2087](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32415264))

Prognostic factors

* Potential for local recurrence and distant metastasis
* In terms of prognosis, *EWSR1 / FUS::NFATC2* sarcoma typically requires treatment such as surgical resection, radiation therapy and chemotherapy
* Some studies have shown that the prognosis of *EWSR1 / FUS::NFATC2* sarcoma is related to factors such as age, tumor size, lymph node metastasis and surgical resection ([Diagn Pathol 2024;19:19](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38254207))
* Lung, cutaneous and bone metastases have been reported as long as 10 years after initial diagnosis and clinical indolence ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:1112](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30994538))
* Little or no histological response has been observed in patients treated with neoadjuvant chemotherapy ([Hum Pathol 2019;90:45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31078563))

Case reports

* 23 year old woman presented with a left forearm mass ([World J Clin Cases 2024;12:2887](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38899283))
* 24 year old woman presented with a mass in the posterior right calf ([Hum Pathol 2018;81:281](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29626598))
* 25 year old man presented with an osteolytic lesion in the left femoral diaphysis ([Virchows Arch 2024;484:533](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38483612))
* 64 year old woman presented with nasal cavity mass ([Histopathology 2024;84:413](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37813649))

Treatment

* Surgical resection ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020;59:525](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32362012))
* Sarcomas with *NFATC2* fusions are resistant to Ewing sarcoma specific chemotherapy ([Hum Pathol 2019;90:45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31078563), [Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:1112](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30994538))

Gross description

* Well circumscribed and lobulated, gray-white, fleshy masses with variably necrotic and hemorrhagic areas ([Mod Pathol 2020;33:1930](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32327700))
* Most cases have a large extraosseous component
* Cortical breakthrough and soft tissue invasion ([Mod Pathol 2020;33:2087](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32415264))
* Large, fleshy mass with hemorrhagic areas and multilobular contours ([Mod Pathol 2020;33:2087](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32415264))

Frozen section description

* Monotonous proliferation of small to medium sized round or oval cells with eosinophilic or clear cytoplasm arranged in nests, cords, trabeculae or pseudoglandular patterns ([Hum Pathol 2019;90:45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31078563), [Mod Pathol 2020;33:2087](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32415264))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Monotonous proliferation of small to medium sized round or oval cells arranged in nests, cords, trabeculae or pseudoglandular patterns ([Diagn Pathol 2024;19:19](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38254207), [Mod Pathol 2020;33:2087](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32415264))
* Eosinophilic or clear cytoplasm
* Small, round, monotonous to markedly pleomorphic nuclei with smooth or irregular nuclear contours, dense hyperchromatic or vesicular chromatin and small or prominent nucleoli
* Other findings include spindle cells, epithelioid round cell morphology, cartilaginous differentiation, osteoid-like matrix and eosinophilic infiltration ([Mod Pathol 2020;33:2087](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32415264), [Hum Pathol 2018;81:281](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29626598))
* Variable amounts of fibrohyaline to myxohyaline stroma ([Mod Pathol 2020;33:2087](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32415264))
* A focal hemangiopericytic vascular network may be present
* Variable mitotic activity and tumor necrosis

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Round cell sarcomas with *EWSR1::NFATC2* fusion share a distinct DNA methylation signature and carry characteristic copy number alterations, which emphasizes that these sarcomas should be considered separately from Ewing sarcoma ([J Cancer Res Clin Oncol 2019;145:1273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30895378))
* Recurrent losses of chromosome 9q and segmental gains on 20q13 and 22q12 involving the *EWSR1* and *NFATC2* loci, respectively
* *EWSR1::NFATC2* tumors are strongly enriched in genes associated with inflammatory and immune responses ([J Pathol 2018;245:29](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29431183))
* *FUS::NFATC2* tumors are enriched in proliferation and drug resistance signatures ([J Pathol 2018;245:29](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29431183))
* In accordance with the potential areas of cartilaginous differentiation, genes involved in the extracellular matrix of cartilaginous tissues (such as *ACAN*, *COL9A2*, *MATN3*, *COMP* and *CILP2*) or encoding secreted proteins (PTN, DKK3, ANGPTL2, SBSPON and WNT5B) are preferentially expressed in *FUS::NFATC2* positive tumors
* *FUS::NFATC2* sarcomas are different from *EWSR1::NFATC2* tumors and transcriptionally resemble *CIC* fused tumor entities ([J Pathol 2018;245:29](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29431183))
* Morphologic correlation is essential; identical gene fusions have also been reported in both solitary bone cysts (SBC) and vascular malformations, which show entirely different morphology and clinical behavior ([Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:1669](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34081036), [ScienceDirect: Vascular Anomaly [Accessed 27 January 2025]](https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/medicine-and-dentistry/vascular-anomaly))

Sample pathology report

* Left tibia, core biopsy:
  + Round cell sarcoma with *FUS::NFATC2* fusion (see comment)
  + Comment: H&E stained tissue sections show a cellular neoplasm composed of small to medium sized round cells with eosinophilic and clear cytoplasm and round nuclei with smooth or irregular nuclear contours, hyperchromatic or vesicular chromatin and small nucleoli. Few mitoses and focal necrosis (~20%) are seen. Immunohistochemical stain for CD99 is positive in tumor cells. The targeted solid tumor next generation sequencing (NGS) fusion panel identified a *FUS::NFATC2* fusion. Overall, these pathologic findings are consistent with round cell sarcoma with *FUS::NFATC2* fusion. Sarcomas with *NFATC2* fusions have potential for local and distant recurrence and are resistant to Ewing sarcoma specific chemotherapy.

Differential diagnosis

* [Ewing sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneewing.html):
  + Typically lacks fibromyxoid stroma, clear cell changes or cartilaginous differentiation
  + [FLI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsFLI1.html)+, [ERG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainserg.html)+ (subset) ([Mod Pathol 2012;25:1378](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22766791))
  + Defined by fusions involving FET family (*EWSR1* or *FUS*) and ETS family genes
* [*CIC::DUX4* sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneCICDUX4.html):
  + Necrosis and brisk mitotic activity is common; may have mild pleomorphism
  + No cartilaginous differentiation
  + Most *CIC* sarcomas occur in the deep soft tissue; primary osseous involvement is rare ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:941](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28346326))
  + [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html)+, [ETV4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsetv4.html)+ ([Mod Pathol 2016;29:1324](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27443513))
  + Recurrent *CIC* fusions (most commonly with *DUX4*)
* [Sarcoma with *BCOR* alterations](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneBCORCCNB3.html):
  + Primitive round to spindled cells in nests; often with myxoid stroma and delicate vasculature
  + No cartilaginous differentiation
  + [BCOR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsBCOR.html)+, [CCNB3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsCCNB3.html)+ ([Nat Genet 2012;44:461](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22387997), [Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:1713](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28877060))
  + [TLE1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsTLE1.html)+, [SATB2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsSATB2.html)+
  + *BCOR* fusion or internal tandem duplication
* ***EWSR1::PATZ1* sarcoma**:
  + Round or spindled cell with divergent phenotype, both myogenic and neurogenic
  + No cartilaginous differentiation
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)+, [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html)+, muscle markers ([desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [myogenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html), [myoD1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyod1.html))+ ([Mod Pathol 2019;32:1593](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31189996))
  + *EWSR1::PATZ1* fusion
* [Myoepithelial tumors (myoepithelioma, myoepithelial carcinoma)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuemyoepithelialca.html):
  + Osseous or cartilaginous differentiation is observed in 10 - 15% of cases ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12960802))
  + Broad spectrum [keratins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)+, [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html)+, [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html)+ ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1813](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18043035), [Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12960802))
  + Myogenic markers ([calponin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalponin.html), [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html))+ ([Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:13](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8990137), [Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12960802))
  + Loss of expression of [SMARCB1 (INI1)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html) is observed in a subset of myoepithelial carcinomas ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1813](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18043035), [Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:542](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19033866))
  + No *NFATC2* fusions
* [Small cell osteosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneosteosarcomageneral.html):
  + Presence of a lace-like osteoid produced by tumor cells with round cell features ([Skeletal Radiol 1987;16:621](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3321462))
  + Lacks specific gene fusion
* [Clear cell sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueclearcellsarcoma.html):
  + Clear cell sarcomas typically involve deep soft tissue
  + Nested epithelioid to spindled cells with eosinophilic to clear cells
  + Scattered Touton type or wreath-like multinucleated giant cells are relatively common
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) / [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html)+, [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html)+, [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsHMB45.html)+ ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:452](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18300804), [Mod Pathol 2001;14:6](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11211309))
  + *CREB* family gene fusions
* [Mesenchymal chondrosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueeskchondrosarcomamesenchymal.html):
  + Small to medium sized round cells admixed with islands of well differentiated cartilage and staghorn vasculature
  + *HEY1::NCOA2* fusion ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2012;51:127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22034177))
* [Perivascular epithelioid cell tumors (PEComa)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uteruspecoma.html):
  + Numerous growth patterns, with sheets and nests being the most common
  + Noncohesive epithelioid or spindled cells with clear to eosinophilic granular cytoplasm
  + Thin and delicate vessels but may also have thick walled (generally peripherally located) and staghorn vessels
  + Radial / perivascular distribution of tumor cells identified in < 25% ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:1370](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30001237))
  + Melanocytic ([HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsHMB45.html), [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)) and myogenic markers ([SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html))
  + [Cathepsin K](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscathepsink.html): usually strong and diffuse ([Mod Pathol 2012;25:100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21874011))
  + [TFE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstfe3.html) may show focal staining but typically is nonspecific unless strong and diffuse ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:1395](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20871214))
  + Mutations in *TSC1* and *TSC2*; *TFE3* translocation ([Am J Surg Pathol 2023;47:535](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36856023), [Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:394](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25517951))

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/boneroundcellsarcomaewsr1Alexiev05.jpeg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/boneroundcellsarcomaewsr1Alexiev05.jpeg)

A 56 year old patient has a 10 cm mass in the diaphysis of the femur with evidence of extraosseous extension. Immunohistochemical stains show positivity for CD99 and NKX2.2 and are negative for SOX10, desmin and pancytokeratins. On FISH testing, a gene rearrangement of the *NFATC2* gene is noted. Which of the following is true about this lesion?

1. *NFATC2* sarcomas are resistant to Ewing sarcoma specific chemotherapy
2. *NFATC2* sarcomas have been reported exclusively in soft tissue
3. Patients with *NFATC2* sarcomas show a younger median age at diagnosis compared to those with *EWSR1::FLI1*
4. Predominantly arise in children
5. Strong female predominance

Board review style answer #1

**A**. *NFATC2* sarcomas are resistant to Ewing sarcoma specific chemotherapy. Answer D is incorrect because while *NFATC2* fusion sarcomas occur in children and adults (age range: 12 - 67 years), the median age is 33 years old. Answer E is incorrect because *NFATC2* fusion sarcomas have a strong male predominance. Answer B is incorrect because *NFATC2* fusion sarcomas are predominantly located in bones, with a 4:1 ratio over soft tissue. Answer C is incorrect because patients with *NFATC2* fusion sarcomas show an older median age at diagnosis compared to those with *EWSR1::FLI1*.

Board review style question #2

Which of the following is true about *NFATC2* rearranged sarcomas?

1. CD99+
2. Frequently present in the upper trunk / back of the neck in adults
3. Lack the ability to metastasize
4. Loss of INI1
5. Show a predilection to metastasize to soft tissue sites

Board review style answer #2

**A**. CD99+. *NFATC2* sarcomas are positive for CD99. Answer B is incorrect because *NFATC2* sarcomas are most commonly found in the bones, particularly the long bones, such as the femur, humerus, radius and tibia. Answer D is incorrect because INI1 expression is retained. Answer C is incorrect because *NFATC2* sarcomas have a potential for local recurrence and distant metastasis. Answer E is incorrect because lung, cutaneous and bone metastases have been reported as long as 10 years after initial diagnosis and clinical indolence.
