Definition / general

* Extrapulmonary manifestation of tuberculosis (TB) infection, presenting as insidious onset of arthritis that involves weight bearing joints
* Diagnosis requires chronic granulomatous inflammation on synovial biopsy and demonstration of acid fast bacilli on synovial fluid smear, culture or *Mycobacterium tuberculosis* (MTB) DNA on polymerase chain reaction (PCR) assay

Essential features

* Develops through hematogenous spread from distant primary focus or direct spread from adjacent osteomyelitis
* Commonly presents as chronic painful swelling that involves the knee and hip joints
* Radiological findings include joint space narrowing, bone erosions, chondromatosis bodies, extra-articular cystic spaces, etc.
* Diagnosis is established on synovial fluid smear, synovial biopsy, culture and MTB PCR
* Commonly treated with antituberculous medication and surgery is performed only in complicated cases

Terminology

* Osteoarticular tuberculosis

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [A18.02](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/A00-B99/A15-A19/A18-/A18.02) - tuberculous arthritis of other joints
* ICD-11: [1B12.40](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#570881596) - tuberculous of bones or joints

Epidemiology

* Accounts for 1 - 3% of all TB cases and 10 - 11% of extrapulmonary TB cases
* In endemic regions, it occurs in all age groups from children to middle age individuals
* In nonendemic regions, it occurs in elderly individuals with compromised host immunity
* Joint / bone trauma and pre-existing joint or bone diseases (such as rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis, systemic lupus erythematosus [SLE], gout, etc.) result in reactivation of TB arthritis ([Best Pract Res Clin Rheumatol 2003;17:319](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12787528), [J Rheum Dis 2022;29:108](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37475896), [Ann Saudi Med 1998;18:368](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17344697), [Acta Med Philipp 2022;56:21](https://doi.org/10.47895/amp.v56i2.2008))

Sites

* Hip and knee joints are most common sites
* Elbow, wrist, ankle, sacroiliac and shoulder joints can also be involved ([Best Pract Res Clin Rheumatol 2003;17:319](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12787528), [J Rheum Dis 2022;29:108](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37475896), [Ann Saudi Med 1998;18:368](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17344697), [Acta Med Philipp 2022;56:21](https://doi.org/10.47895/amp.v56i2.2008))

Pathophysiology

* Seeding of synovium through hematogenous spread from primary foci such as lung, lymph nodes or viscera
* Direct extension into synovium from adjacent TB osteomyelitis focus
* Course of TB synovitis and process of joint destruction is slower than pyogenic infection
* Initial inflammation and edema cause joint swelling, muscle spasm and movement restriction
* Later joint effusion and pannus formation, which destructs cartilage, erodes subchondral cancellous bone and disrupts nutritional supply
* Cartilage erosion leads to reduced joint space, which is also termed as TB osteoarthritis
* Bone may also show demineralization and necrosis
* Periarticular cold abscesses and fistula may also form
* Advanced joint damage heals with fibrous ankylosis
* Subluxation or dislocation of joint may occur due to prolonged muscle spasm, which may subsequently lead to limb deformity and shortening ([Best Pract Res Clin Rheumatol 2003;17:319](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12787528), [Ina J Rheum 2010;2:10](https://journalrheumatology.or.id/index.php/ijr/article/view/77))

Etiology

* *Mycobacterium tuberculosis* is the causative organism in vast majority of cases
* Other species of *Mycobacterium tuberculosis* complex rarely cause arthritis ([Best Pract Res Clin Rheumatol 2003;17:319](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12787528))

Clinical features

* Most common manifestation is chronic monoarthritis of hip or knee joint
* Oligo or polyarthritis is not rare
* Accompanied with subtle constitutional symptoms such as low grade fever with evening rise, night sweats and weight loss over weeks to months
* Affected joints develop insidious low grade pain, swelling, warmth, tenderness, early flexion contractures, joint effusion and loss of joint function
* Periarticular abscess, draining sinuses and limb deformity are late presentations ([Best Pract Res Clin Rheumatol 2003;17:319](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12787528), [Ann Saudi Med 1998;18:368](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17344697), [Ina J Rheum 2010;2:10](https://journalrheumatology.or.id/index.php/ijr/article/view/77))
* Erythema nodosum: septal panniculitis without vasculitis associated with TB arthritis / periarthritis of ankle
* Erythema induratum: lobular panniculitis with vasculitis (nodular vasculitis) associated with TB arthritis / periarthritis of ankle ([Best Pract Res Clin Rheumatol 2003;17:319](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12787528))

Diagnosis

* Diagnosis requires a high index of clinical suspicion
* Tuberculin skin test and radiological findings may suggest TB infection
* Synovial fluid smear, acid fast bacillus (AFB) culture, MTB PCR and synovial biopsy examination are required to establish the diagnosis ([Best Pract Res Clin Rheumatol 2003;17:319](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12787528), [Ann Saudi Med 1998;18:368](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17344697), [Ina J Rheum 2010;2:10](https://journalrheumatology.or.id/index.php/ijr/article/view/77), [Acta Med Philipp 2022;56:21](https://doi.org/10.47895/amp.v56i2.2008))

Laboratory

* Erythrocyte sedimentation rate (ESR) and C reactive protein (CRP) levels are high
* Serum interferon gamma test is sensitive and specific for detection of latent TB infection
* Synovial fluid (SF) is nonhemorrhagic, turbid and xanthochromic
* SF analysis shows moderately elevated white blood cell (WBC) count (predominantly polymorphonuclear leucocytes) and low glucose level
* Acid fast bacilli are identified on SF smear (20% sensitive), microbiological culture (80% sensitive) and MTB PCR (62.5% sensitive) ([Best Pract Res Clin Rheumatol 2003;17:319](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12787528), [J Rheum Dis 2022;29:108](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37475896), [BMJ Case Rep 2018;2018:bcr2018224288](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29959172), [J Arthritis 2012;1:1](https://www.iomcworld.org/open-access/septic-arthritis-and-tuberculosis-arthritis-jahs.1000102.pdf%20), [Ina J Rheum 2010;2:10](https://journalrheumatology.or.id/index.php/ijr/article/view/77), [Acta Med Philipp 2022;56:21](https://doi.org/10.47895/amp.v56i2.2008))
* Chronic granulomatous inflammation with or without necrosis on synovial biopsy (80 - 96% sensitive) ([J Rheum Dis 2022;29:108](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37475896), [Eur J Case Rep Intern Med 2018;5:000870](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30756044), [Ann Saudi Med 1998;18:368](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17344697), [Ina J Rheum 2010;2:10](https://journalrheumatology.or.id/index.php/ijr/article/view/77), [Acta Med Philipp 2022;56:21](https://doi.org/10.47895/amp.v56i2.2008))

Radiology description

* Radiological findings can mimic rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis, avascular necrosis
* In early edematous phase, the joint space is normal on plain Xray
* Joint space is reduced with cartilage erosion and destruction
* Phemister triad: characteristic radiological features of TB arthritis include juxta-articular osteoporosis, peripherally located osseous erosions and gradual narrowing of joint space ([Best Pract Res Clin Rheumatol 2003;17:319](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12787528), [Acta Med Philipp 2022;56:21](https://doi.org/10.47895/amp.v56i2.2008))
* Ultrasound shows soft tissue edema and encapsulated fluid filled masses with small solid inclusions ([Eur J Case Rep Intern Med 2018;5:000870](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30756044))
* Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) scans show uniform synovial thickening, bone erosions with rim enhancement, chondromatosis bodies in joint and extra-articular cystic masses ([AJR Am J Roentgenol 2009;193:1347](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19843752), [Eur J Case Rep Intern Med 2018;5:000870](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30756044))
* Computed tomography (CT) scans may show bone sequestration, destruction, bone defects, osteolytic lesions and involvement of surrounding soft tissue ([Ina J Rheum 2010;2:10](https://journalrheumatology.or.id/index.php/ijr/article/view/77), [Acta Med Philipp 2022;56:21](https://doi.org/10.47895/amp.v56i2.2008))
* Other radiological findings include osteopenia, bony sclerosis, periarticular calcification and periostitis ([Best Pract Res Clin Rheumatol 2003;17:319](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12787528), [Eur J Case Rep Intern Med 2018;5:000870](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30756044))

Prognostic factors

* Overall average recurrence rate is 7.4%
* Factors related to recurrence are polyarthritis, drug resistant MTB strains and poor compliance to treatment ([J Bone Jt Infect 2023;8:189](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37780528))

Case reports

* 2 year old girl presenting with tuberculosis of hip joint ([Reumatol Clin 2011;7:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22078704))
* 31 year old man presenting with PCR negative tuberculosis of knee joint ([BMJ Case Rep 2018;2018:bcr2018224288](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29959172))
* 41 year old woman presenting with subacute septic tuberculosis of hip joint ([Cureus 2023;15:e43493](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37719556))
* 53 year old man presenting with tuberculosis of knee and intra-articular rice bodies ([Case Rep Orthop 2020;2020:6369781](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32089932))
* 63 year old woman presenting with tuberculosis of ankle joint ([Eur J Case Rep Intern Med 2018;5:000870](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30756044))

Treatment

* Immobilization and rest in acute phase for pain relief
* Early active mobilization with weight bearing activities for 4 - 6 weeks following treatment
* Antimicrobial therapy is comprised of a combination of four drugs including isoniazid, rifampicin, pyrazinamide and streptomycin and ethambutol
* Severe cartilage damage, deformed joints, abscess formation, multiple drug resistance or atypical mycobacteria are indications for surgical intervention ([Best Pract Res Clin Rheumatol 2003;17:319](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12787528), [Ina J Rheum 2010;2:10](https://journalrheumatology.or.id/index.php/ijr/article/view/77))
* Surgical procedures include debridement and drainage, arthrotomy, synovectomy, arthroplasty, osteotomy, joint replacement, etc. ([Ann Saudi Med 1998;18:368](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17344697), [Acta Med Philipp 2022;56:21](https://doi.org/10.47895/amp.v56i2.2008))

* Cytological examination is not routinely performed for tuberculosis
* Epithelioid histiocytes, multinucleated giant cells and necrosis are seen ([J Skin Sex Transm Dis 2020;2:59](https://doi.org/10.25259/JSSTD_38_2019))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* PCR based molecular tests detect MTB DNA and provide rapid diagnosis
* Sensitivity and specificity of 62.5% and 91.6%, respectively, in synovial fluid and biopsy samples ([Int Orthop 2009;33:801](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18038134))
* Low sensitivity of molecular tests on synovial fluid samples can be due to paucibacillary disease, degradation by immune system, degradation during transportation or prior initiation of antituberculosis treatment (ATT) ([BMJ Case Rep 2018;2018:bcr2018224288](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29959172))

Brief summary of TB arthritis

Sample pathology report

* Knee joint; synovectomy:
  + Chronic granulomatous inflammation with necrosis and acid fast bacilli seen on Ziehl-Neelsen special stain, consistent with tuberculous arthritis (see comment)
  + Comment: Hyperplastic synovial tissue is showing multiple granulomas formed by epithelioid histiocytes and multinucleated Langhans type giant cells with central necrosis. Surrounding lymphocytes and plasma cells are also seen. Ziehl-Neelsen special stain highlights acid fast bacilli. Fungal stains are negative. Features are consistent with tuberculous arthritis. Correlation with findings of synovial fluid AFB smear, AFB culture and MTB PCR is also recommended.

Differential diagnosis

* **Poncet disease**:
  + Reactive (parainfectious) polyarthritis associated with active TB infection or adjuvant mycobacterial treatment for bladder carcinoma
  + No evidence of direct mycobacterial involvement of the joints
  + More commonly presents as polyarthritis involving both large and small to medium joints
  + More common in juveniles and young adults with female predominance
  + Good response to NSAIDs and earlier remission after anti-TB treatment ([Best Pract Res Clin Rheumatol 2003;17:319](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12787528), [J Rheum Dis 2022;29:108](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37475896))
* [Sarcoidosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungnontumorsarcoidosis.html):
  + Usually present acutely as oligopolyarthritis
  + Frequently associated with hilar lymphadenopathy and erythema nodosum (Lofgren triad)
  + Synovial fluid is less inflammatory than infectious arthritis
  + Radiologically, subchondral bone sclerosis and osteophyte formation
  + Nonnecrotizing granulomas on histological examination
  + AFB smear, AFB culture and MTB PCR are negative ([Clin Med Insights Arthritis Musculoskelet Disord 2022;15:11795441211072475](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35185345), [J Bras Pneumol 2014;40:175](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24831403), [Arthritis Rheum 1993;36:716](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8489550))
* [Septic / pyogenic arthritis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/jointssuppurativearthritis.html):
  + More commonly presents as abrupt onset of monoarthritis involving large joints
  + SF WBC count is much higher (> 50,000/ml) as compared to TB arthritis (10 - 20,000/ml)
  + SF and blood cultures identify causative organisms
  + On plain Xray, osteopenia is the initial presentation, followed by cortical bone destruction and periosteal new bone formation
  + Sequestrum formation in cases that develop chronic osteomyelitis
  + On MRI scan, more frequent abnormal subchondral marrow signal intensity, less frequent bone erosions, more irregular extra-articular boundaries and thicker irregular rim of pyogenic abscess ([Radiology 2001;218:848](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11230666), [J Arthritis 2012;1:1](https://www.iomcworld.org/open-access/septic-arthritis-and-tuberculosis-arthritis-jahs.1000102.pdf%20))
* [Rheumatoid arthritis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/jointsra.html):
  + Presents as polyarthritis, more commonly involving small to medium joints than combination of large and small to medium joints
  + Histologically presents with synovial hypertrophy, fibrin deposition with stromal mononuclear cell infiltration
  + Rheumatoid factor (RF) and anti-cyclic citrullinated peptide (anti-CCP) antibody are positive ([J Rheum Dis 2022;29:108](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37475896))
  + On MRI scan, nonuniform and greater synovial thickening, small and less frequent bone erosions without rim enhancement and less frequent extra-articular cysts ([AJR Am J Roentgenol 2009;193:1347](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19843752))
* **Crystal induced arthritis**:
  + Presents as monoarthritis, predominantly involving small to medium sized joints
  + In gout, serum uric acid level is raised ([J Rheum Dis 2022;29:108](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37475896))
  + Histologically, nodular aggregates of acellular, amorphous, pale eosinophilic material surrounded by palisading arrangement of histiocytes and multinucleated giant cells
  + Presents as intermittent episodes rather than persistent chronic pain ([Ina J Rheum 2010;2:10](https://journalrheumatology.or.id/index.php/ijr/article/view/77))
* [Osteoarthritis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/jointsDJD.html):
  + Less inflammatory than TB arthritis
  + Histologically, loss of articular cartilage, thickening of the subchondral bone, osteophytes, synovial inflammation
  + Radiologically, subchondral bone sclerosis and osteophyte formation ([Ina J Rheum 2010;2:10](https://journalrheumatology.or.id/index.php/ijr/article/view/77))

Board review style question #1

[![Tuberculous arthritis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/jointstbarthritisuddin12.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/jointstbarthritisuddin12.jpg)

A 52 year old man with a known case of pulmonary tuberculosis presented to an orthopedic clinic for painful swelling of the left knee, accompanied with a low grade fever and weight loss for 18 months. Synovial thickening and bone erosion with rim enhancement was noted on magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) scan. Arthroscopic examination revealed hyperplastic and inflamed synovium. Synovial fluid smear was negative for crystals and negative for bacteria on gram stain. Necrotizing chronic granulomatous inflammation was identified on synovial biopsy. Microbiological culture revealed growth of *Mycobacterium tuberculosis* (MTB). What is the diagnosis in this case?

1. Poncet disease
2. Rheumatoid arthritis
3. Sarcoidosis
4. Septic arthritis
5. Tuberculous arthritis

Board review style answer #1

**E**. Tuberculous arthritis presents as an insidious onset of chronic monoarthritis involving weight bearing joints. Synovial biopsy typically shows necrotizing chronic granulomatous inflammation. Diagnosis is confirmed by demonstration of MTB growth on culture or MTB DNA on PCR assays.
Answer A is incorrect because Poncet disease is reactive polyarthritis associated with active TB infection but synovial fluid and tissue samples are negative for MTB microorganisms.
Answer B is incorrect because rheumatoid arthritis presents as polyarthritis involving small and medium joints.
Answer C is incorrect because synovial biopsy reveals nonnecrotizing chronic granulomatous inflammation and absence of MTB organisms on synovial fluid smears and culture.
Answer D is incorrect because the onset is abrupt for septic arthritis. Biopsy reveals dense infiltrate of neutrophils. Gram staining and culture of synovial fluid identifies the causative organisms, which are most commonly cocci bacteria.

Board review style question #2

Which is the quickest, most specific and considerably more sensitive investigation for confirming the diagnosis of TB arthritis?

1. Erythrocyte sedimentation rate (ESR)
2. MTB PCR assay
3. Synovial biopsy
4. Synovial fluid AFB culture
5. Synovial fluid AFB smear

Board review style answer #2

**B**. MTB PCR assay. This detects MTB DNA within hours. The specificity is high (> 90%) but sensitivity is low (62.5%). Answer A is incorrect because ESR is a nonspecific test. Answer C is incorrect because synovial biopsy results take more time than PCR assay. The biopsy finding of chronic granulomatous inflammation needs further confirmation by the presence of MTB on synovial fluid smear, culture or PCR assay. Answer E is incorrect because synovial fluid smear is only 20% sensitive in detecting AFB. Answer D is incorrect because culture results for MTB take several weeks.
