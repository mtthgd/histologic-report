# Xanthoma

Xanthoma

Table of Contents

[Definition / general](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/superpage/bone.html#definitiongeneral25810) | [Radiology description](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/superpage/bone.html#radiologydescription25811) | [Microscopic (histologic) description](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/superpage/bone.html#microscopichistologicdescription25812) | [Differential diagnosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/superpage/bone.html#differentialdiagnosis25813)

Definition / general

* 65% male, age 20+ years
* Usually solitary; affects pelvis, ribs, skull

Radiology description

* Well defined, expansile lytic lesion, often with sclerotic margin

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Foam cells, multinucleated giant cells, cholesterol clefts, fibrosis

Differential diagnosis

* [Benign fibrous histiocytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuebfh.html)
* [Langerhans cell histiocytosis with secondary xanthomatous change](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodesLCH.html)
* **Post traumatic dysplasia**
* [Sinus histiocytosis with massive lymphadenopathy](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodessinushistiocytosiswithmassivelymph.html)
