Definition / general

* Cellularity averages 79% at ages 0 - 9 years, 50% at ages 30 - 69 versus 29% at ages 70 - 79
* With aging, hematopoietic tissue is replaced by fat
* Deeper medullary areas are typically more cellular than subcortical areas
* B cell production declines with age ([Curr Opin Immunol 2005;17:463](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16054808)) although the relative abundance of pro B, pre B, immature, naive and mature B cells usually does not change appreciably between ages 24 and 88 years
* Occasional patients have exceptionally low numbers of lymphocyte precursors ([Blood 2003;101:576](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12393702))
* Hypocellularity in elderly marrow may be due to increased apoptosis ([Mech Ageing Dev 2000;117:57](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10958923))
* Usually no spicules in bone marrow aspiration from very young children, perhaps due to very low or absent fat content
