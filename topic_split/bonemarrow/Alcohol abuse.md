Definition / general

* Has variable hematological effects ([Alcohol Clin Exp Res 2004;28:619](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15100613))
* May cause megaloblastic anemia due either to folate / B12 deficiency or without these deficiencies
* Cyanamide, used to prevent alcohol intake, may cause aplastic anemia ([Eur J Clin Pharmacol 2005;61:467](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15991038)) or granulocytopenia ([Intern Med 1997;36:640](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9313109))
* Alcohol abuse may also cause sideroblastic anemia ([Postgrad Med 1992;92:147](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1437911)), severe osteoporosis ([Calcif Tissue Int 2005;76:79](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15549637)) or TTP ([Eur J Intern Med 2004;15:262](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15288684))

Case reports

* 32 year old man with severe pancytopenia ([University of Pittsburg Case #428](http://path.upmc.edu/cases/case428.html))
* 50 year old man with extensive alcohol and drug abuse, hypocellular marrow and increased blasts ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2005;129:e35](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15679445))
* 50 year old woman with megaloblastic anemia due to alcohol and mild folate antagonists ([Dtsch Med Wochenschr 2005;130:2139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16172954))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* May cause hypocellular marrow or reactive myeloblastosis with up to 34% blasts
* Vacuoles in proerythroblasts and other precursors
* Also ringed sideroblasts and iron granules in plasma cells

Peripheral smear description

* Leukopenia with vacuoles, hypersegmentation of neutrophils, thrombocytopenia
* Iron granules in plasma cells and other cells
