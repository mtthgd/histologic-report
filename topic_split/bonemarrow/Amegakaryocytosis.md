Amegakaryocytic thrombocytopenia

Definition / general

* Also called CAMT (congenital amegakaryocytic thrombocytopenia)
* Extremely rare, usually diagnosed in early childhood ([Pediatr Blood Cancer 2011;57:199](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21337678), [Hematol Oncol Clin North Am 2009;23:321](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19327586))
* Initially thought to have variable inheritance (autosomal recessive or X linked) but X linked forms now reinterpreted as mild or attenuated forms of Wiskott-Aldrich syndrome

Etiology

* Most cases caused by homozygous (biallelic) or compound heterozygous mutations in thrombopoietin (TPO) receptor gene *c-mpl* at 1p34 ([Blood 2001;97:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11133753)), causing impaired expression of *c-mpl* gene, causing defective response to TPO ([OMIM: Amegakaryocytic Thrombocytopenia, Congenital; CAMT](http://omim.org/entry/604498))
* Missense mutations associated with milder or delayed course vs. loss of function mutations ([Hum Mutat 2006;27:296](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16470591))

Clinical features and diagnosis

* Isolated nonimmune thrombocytopenia with decreased marrow megakaryocytes and high serum TPO levels
* Red cell macrocytosis with normal hemoglobin level
* Bone marrow initially normocellular
* Mucocutaneous or GI bleeding, variable physical abnormalities ([Br J Haematol 2005;131:636](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16351641))

Case reports

* Girl with developmental delay, facial malformations and CNS anomalies ([Pediatr Blood Cancer 2011;56:452](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21225925))
* 61 year old woman with systemic sclerosis and anti-c-mpl antibody ([Arthritis Rheum 2003;48:1647](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12794833))

Treatment and prognosis

* Stem cell transplantation
* 50% evolve to aplastic anemia (at mean age 3.5 years); may evolve to myelodysplastic syndrome or leukemia
