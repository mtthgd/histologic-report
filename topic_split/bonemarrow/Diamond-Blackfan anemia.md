Definition / general

* A member of the inherited bone marrow failure syndromes (BMFS); typically shows an onset in the first year of life ([Br J Haematol 2016;172:782](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26763766))
* Main features include normochromic macrocytic anemia, reticulocytopenia and nearly absent erythroid progenitors in the bone marrow ([OMIM: Diamond-Blackfan Anemia 1](http://omim.org/entry/105650))
* Predisposition to acute myelogenous leukemia, myelodysplastic syndrome and solid tumors

Terminology

* Also known as Blackfan-Diamond anemia, congenital hypoplastic anemia and inherited erythroblastopenia ([Eur J Hum Genet 2013 Oct;21(10)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23463023))

Essential features

* Also known as Blackfan-Diamond anemia, congenital hypoplastic anemia and inherited erythroblastopenia
* A member of the inherited bone marrow failure syndromes (BMFS); typically shows an onset in the first year of life ([Br J Haematol 2016;172:782](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26763766))
* Underlying defect hypothesized to be faulty ribosome biogenesis, resulting in proapoptotic erythropoiesis and erythroid failure ([Hematol Oncol Clin North Am 2009;23:261](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19327583))
* Growth retardation and various congenital abnormalities commonly seen
* Normochromic macrocytic anemia, reticulocytopenia, normocellular for age marrow and nearly absent erythroid progenitors in the bone marrow ([OMIM: Diamond-Blackfan Anemia 1](http://omim.org/entry/105650))
* Predisposition to acute myelogenous leukemia, myelodysplastic syndrome and solid tumors

Epidemiology

* Incidence 1:100,000 ~ 1:200,000 live births and remains consistent across ethnicities ([GeneReviews: Diamond-Blackfan Anemia](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK7047/))
* Males and females equally affected
* ~90% diagnosed within first year of life and 35% diagnosed within first month

Pathophysiology

* ~55% are sporadic cases; ~45% familial with disease inherited mostly in an autosomal dominant pattern
* Incomplete penetrance ([GeneReviews: Diamond-Blackfan Anemia](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK7047/))
* Underlying defect hypothesized to be faulty ribosome biogenesis, resulting in proapoptotic erythropoiesis and erythroid failure ([Hematol Oncol Clin North Am 2009;23:261](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19327583))
* Heterozygous mutations in ribosomal proteins (RPs) encoding genes, both small and large ribosomal subunit, have been identified in 60% ([Br J Haematol 2016;172:782](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26763766))
* Mutations identified in 15 RP genes: *RPS7, RPS10, RPS17, RPS19, RPS24, RPS26, RPS27, RPS29, RPL5, RPL11, RPL15, RPL26, RPL27, RPL31, RPL35A* ([Br J Haematol 2016;172:782](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26763766))

Clinical features

* Pallor, weakness, failure to thrive ([GeneReviews: Diamond-Blackfan Anemia](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK7047/))
* Growth retardation and various congenital abnormalities seen in 30% - 50%, in particular craniofacial, upper limb, heart and genitourinary malformations
* Higher risk for developing certain malignancies including acute myelogenous leukemia, myelodysplastic syndrome and solid tumors

Diagnosis

According to 6th Annual Diamond-Blackfan anemia (DBA) International Consensus Conference ([Br J Haematol 2008;142:859](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18671700))* **Classic Diamond-Blackfan anemia** is diagnosed if all of the following **diagnostic criteria** are met:
  + Age < 1 year
  + Macrocytic anemia with no other significant cytopenias
  + Reticulocytopenia
  + Normal marrow cellularity with a paucity of erythroid precursors
* A **probable diagnosis** is made based on the following major and minor supporting criteria, if all of the above diagnostic criteria cannot be met :
  + Major supporting criteria:
    - The presence of a gene mutation associated with DBA
    - Positive family history
  + Minor supporting criteria
    - Elevated eADA activity
    - Congenital anomalies associated with DBA
    - Elevated Hemoglobin F
    - No evidence of another inherited bone marrow failure syndrome
  + A **probable diagnosis of Diamond-Blackfan anemia** can be made in the following settings:
    - Three diagnostic criteria and a positive family history
    - Two diagnostic criteria and three minor criteria
    - A positive family history and three minor criteria
* **Nonclassical DBA** is diagnosed when there is a DBA associated gene mutation but insufficient diagnostic criteria

Laboratory

* Macrocytic anemia with no other significant cytopenias ([GeneReviews: Diamond-Blackfan Anemia](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK7047/))
* Increased red cell mean corpuscular volume (MCV)
* Reticulocytopenia
* Elevated erythrocyte adenosine deaminase activity (eADA) (observed in 80% - 85%)
* Elevated hemoglobin F (HbF) concentration

Prognostic factors

Associated with unfavorable prognosis ([Pediatr Res 1999;46:553](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10541318))* Young age at presentation
* No family history
* Premature birth

Case reports

* Hydrops fetalis with de novo mutation in *RPS19* gene ([Am J Hematol 2013;88:160](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23349008))
* Infant female with multiple congenital midline defects ([J Pediatr Hematol Oncol 2007;29:338](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17483715))
* 3 month old boy with coexisting Johanson-Blizzard syndrome ([J Coll Physicians Surg Pak 2010;20:627](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20810061))
* 7 year old boy with subsequent Hodgkin lymphoma ([J Pediatr Hematol Oncol 2006;28:234](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16679921))
* 10 year old girl with fatal agranulocytosis after deferiprone therapy ([Blood 2007;109:5157](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17344464))
* 16 year old Japanese boy with colon tumors after hematopoietic stem cell transplantation ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2015;8:5938](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26191323))
* 17 year old girl with multihormonal hypopituitarism, hypothyroidism and hypoparathyroidism ([Pediatr Endocrinol Diabetes Metab 2013;19:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25577899))
* Child with isolated cleft palate ([Cleft Palate Craniofac J 2010 Nov 19 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21117964))
* Japanese patient with a novel mutation of ribosomal protein *S10* gene ([J Pediatr Hematol Oncol 2012;34:293](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22510774))

Treatment

* **Corticosteroids and red cell transfusions**: mainstays of therapy
* **Hematopoietic cell transplantation**: for steroid refractory patients ([Hematol Oncol Clin North Am 2009;23:261](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19327583))
* **Other therapies**: androgens, IL3, metoclopramide and immunosuppressive agents ([Blood 2007;109:2266](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17312003))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Bone marrow examination usually shows normocellular for age, complete loss of erythroid elements or only scattered isolated erythroblasts
* Increased hematogones, especially in infants and young children
* Granulocytic and megakaryocytic lineages are preserved; mild eosinophilia is often present

Peripheral smear description

* Peripheral blood examination usually shows normochromic, macrocytic red blood cells, mild anisopoikilocytosis and the absence of polychromasia ([Proytcheva: Diagnostic Pediatric Hematopathology, 1st Edition, 2011](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0521881609/pathologyoutl-20))

Differential diagnosis

* **Acquired conditions with bone marrow failure**: infections ([Parvovirus B19 infection](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonemarrowparvovirusB19.html), [HIV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonemarrowhiv.html), viral hepatitis, [mononucleosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodesebv.html) and [HTLV1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBatlv.html)), drugs and toxins, immune mediated diseases ([thymoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mediastinumthymoma.html), [myasthenia gravis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mediastinummyastheniagravis.html), [systemic lupus erythematosus](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodessle.html) and multiple endocrinopathies)
* **Other genetic conditions with bone marrow failure**: [Fanconi anemia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonemarrowfanconis.html), [Shwachman-Diamond syndrome](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonemarrowshwachmandiamond.html), [Pearson syndrome](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonemarrowpearson.html), [dyskeratosis congenita](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonemarrowdyskeratosiscongenita.html), cartilage hair hypoplasia
* **Transient erythroblastopenia of childhood (TEC)**: acquired anemia caused by decreased production of red blood cell precursors in a previously healthy child; self resolving; 80% of children are age one year or older at diagnosis; normocytic anemia
