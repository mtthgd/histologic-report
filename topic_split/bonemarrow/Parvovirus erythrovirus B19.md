Definition / general

* Associated with HIV but serology may be negative because patients cannot produce IgG antibodies ([Hum Pathol 2000;31:161](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10685629))
* Affects bone marrow erythroid precursors, causing transient aplastic crisis in patients with hemolytic anemia or chronic anemia
* Affects placenta and other tissues in fetuses, causing fetal hydrops or death
* Also causes erythema infectiosum (Fifth disease), arthropathy (note: B19 DNA is common in rheumatic patients but clinical significance is unclear, [J Clin Virol 2005;33:71](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15797368))
* B19 DNA found in bone marrow of 2% of healthy individuals ([J Clin Microbiol 2002;40:933](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11880419))
* Erythrovirus V9 causes similar pathology ([J Clin Microbiol 1999;37:2483](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10405389))
* **Chronic parvovirus infection**: associated with immunodeficiency, erythroid hyperplasia, numerous inclusions, particularly in basophilic and polychromatic erythroblasts

Case reports

* Newborn with leukoerythroblastosis ([Haematologica 2005;90:ECR38](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16266929))
* 7 year old boy with coinfection with falciparum malaria ([Haematologica 2005;90:ECR41](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16464756))
* 22 year old woman with sickle cell-like crisis and bone marrow necrosis associated with parvovirus B19 infection and heterozygosity for haemoglobins S and E ([J Intern Med 1999;245:103](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10095824))
* 29 year old woman with fatal fungal superinfection, sickle cell disease and massive bone marrow necrosis ([Haematologica 2006;91:ECR18](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16785124))
* 41 year old HIV+ man with fulminant parvovirus infection following erythropoietin treatment ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2000;124:441](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10705404))
* 42 year old woman with systemic lupus erythematosus ([Intern Med 2003;42:538](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12857057))
* 50 year old man with chronic anemia ([University of Pittsburgh: Chronic Anemia](http://path.upmc.edu/cases/case372.html))
* 63 year old man with hereditary spherocytosis ([Postgrad Med J 2003;79:244](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12743353))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Marked erythroblast hypoplasia, immature giant erythroblasts
* Occasional erythroblasts may have intranuclear inclusions with surrounding rim of residual chromatin
* Rarely hemophagocytosis ([Jpn J Infect Dis 2005;58:149](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15973005))
