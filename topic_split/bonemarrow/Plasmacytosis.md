Definition / general

* Plasmacytosis in bone marrow and peripheral blood can be monoclonal (plasma cell neoplasm) or polyclonal
* See also [lymphoid aggregates](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonemarrowlymphoidaggregates.html), [polymorphous reactive lymphoid hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonemarrowpolymorphousreactive.html) and [systemic polyclonal B immunoblastic proliferation](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonemarrowsystemicpolyclonal.html)

Epidemiology

* Reactive plamacytosis in bone marrow can be seen in both neoplastic and nonneoplastic conditions:
  + Seen as reactive component in myeloproliferative neoplasms, myelodysplastic syndromes, mastocytosis ([Sem Hop 1983;59:2119](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6312584)) or other malignancies
  + Seen in polyclonal hypergammaglobulinemia ([Int J Hematol 2005;81:62](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15717691)), systemic polyclonal B immunoblastic proliferation ([Am J Clin Pathol 1992;98:222](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1380772), [J Forensic Sci 2001;46:156](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11210903)), autoimmune disorders (e.g. SLE), immunocompromise (e.g. HIV infection) and infections ([Leuk Lymphoma 2003;44:379](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12688365))
  + Particularly common (86%) in HIV+ patients ([Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2005;48:7](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16758774), [Clin Immunol 2001;100:250](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11465955))
* When strict diagnostic criteria are applied, benign polyclonal plasmacytosis in bone marrow and peripheral blood is uncommon

Clinical features

* Can be associated with fever, leukocytosis, skin rash
* May also see lymphadenopathy, dyspnea, hepatosplenomegaly, jaundice
* Case specific presentation varies by etiology

Diagnosis

* Bone marrow biopsy for histomorphologic evaluation and immunophenotypic analysis of plasma cells to demonstrate polyclonality
* When possible, bone marrow aspirate for cytomorphologic evaluation, differential count, flow cytometric analysis (to demonstrate plasma cell polyclonality and confirm lack of aberrant immunophenotype) and cytogenetic / molecular analyses to rule out neoplastic process, if clinically indicated
* CBC and peripheral blood smear to evaluate peripheral blood involvment
* If peripheral blood is involved, use serum protein electrophoresis to assess polyclonality

Case reports

* 31 year old man with systemic inflammatory syndrome and hepatic inflammatory cell infiltration caused by IL6 producing pheochromocytoma ([Endocr J 2005;52:193](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15863947))
* 41 year old man with polyclonal reactive peripheral blood plasmacytosis mimicking plasma cell leukemia ([Leuk Lymphoma 2003;44:379](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12688365))
* 53 year old woman with methimazole induced aplastic anemia caused by hypocellular bone marrow with plasmacytosis ([Thyroid 2004;14:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15072706))
* 72 year old woman with reactive plasmacytosis mimicking multiple myeloma and primary Sjögren syndrome ([J Korean Med Sci 2005;20:506](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15953879))
* 77 year old man with polyclonal plasma cell proliferation with marked hypergammaglobulinemia and multiple autoantibodies ([Ann Clin Lab Sci 2006;36:479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17127739))
* 89 year old woman with polyclonal proliferation of plasma cells associated with marked hypergammaglobulinemia ([Int J Hematol 2005;81:62](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15717691))
* Systemic polyclonal B immunoblastic proliferation with marked peripheral blood and bone marrow plasmacytosis ([Am J Clin Pathol 1992;98:222](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1380772))

Treatment

* Mainly symptomatic and supportive
* Treat the underlying condition

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Bone marrow biopsies often show hypercellularity with an increase in plasma cells / plasmacytoid cells, in perivascular or interstitial distribution
* Background trilineage hematopoiesis is present
* Myeloid and erythroid precursors show normal morphology and may be variably decreased in number, depending on the extent of replacement by plasma cells
* Megakaryocytes are often normal in number, morphology and localization

Cytology description

* Spectrum of plasma cell differentiation
* Plasmablasts appear large with scant basophilic cytoplasm, prominent nuclei, dispersed fine chromatin, conspicuous nucleoli
* Immature plasma cells show abundant basophilic cytoplasm, round nuclei with condensed chromatin
* With differentiation, plasma cells show more basophilic cytoplasm with perinuclear halos and eccentric nuclei

Peripheral smear description

* Prominent increase of plasma cells / plasmacytoid cells in a variety of sizes and maturation stages

Stages of plasma cell differentiation

Positive stains

* Express markers in normal plasma cells:
  + On tissue sections: [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html), [CD19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd19.html), [CD79a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd79a.html), [CD138](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd138.html), [CD38](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd38.html), [MUM1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmum1.html), [kappa](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainskappa.html) and [lambda](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainskappa.html) light chains (immunohistochemical and in situ hybridization)
  + In flow cytometric analysis: CD45, CD19, [CD79b](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd79b.html), [CD138](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd138.html), [CD38](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd38.html), cytoplasmic [kappa and lambda light chains](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainskappa.html), [CD27](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd27.html), [CD81](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd80.html#cd81)

Negative stains

* [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html) ([Am J Pathol 2002;160:1293](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11943714), [Histopathology 2004;44:375](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15049904)) is negative in benign plasma cells (as benign polyclonal plasmacytosis) and ~80% of plasma cell leukemia ([Leukemia 1998;12:1977](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9844928))
* Lack of CD56 expression should not be used solely to rule out plasma cell neoplasm
* Negative for [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html) and surface light chains (as in normal plasma cells)

Flow cytometry description

* Flow cytometric analysis of plasma cells in bone marrow aspirate demonstrates polytypic plasma cells with no immunophenotypic aberration:
  + Forward scatter and side scatter larger than those of lymphocytes
  + Dimmer CD45, moderate CD19, negative CD20, bright CD38, bright CD138
  + Variable intensity of HLA-DR
  + Polytypic cytoplasmic kappa and lambda light chains
  + Negative for surface kappa and lambda light chains
  + Negative for CD56
  + A small portion of larger, brighter HLA-DR+ cells can be detected, representing plasmablasts or early plasma cells which are negative for cytoplasmic light chains while the smaller, dim HLA-DR+ cells represent mature plasma cells

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Molecular analysis demonstrates polyclonality of the plasma cells
* Cytogenetic analysis (e.g. karyotyping, FISH) confirms the lack of cytogenetic aberrations

Differential diagnosis

* [Castleman disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodescastleman.html): see [Ann Pathol 1996;16:133](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8767683)
* [Myeloma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomamyeloma.html) / [plasmacytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaplasmacytoma.html): CD56+, clonal, immature and pleomorphic plasma cells
* Plasma cell neoplasms, in particular [plasma cell leukemia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomamyeloma.html) when peripheral blood is involved
* Plasmacytic [SLL / CLL](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaCLL.html)
