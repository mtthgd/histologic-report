Definition / general

* Due to *Coxiella burnetii*, a rickettsiae usually not identifiable directly ([Am J Surg Pathol 1985;9:752](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3904493))
* Acute primary Q fever is essentially benign but may evolve into subacute Q fever endocarditis or a postinfectious fatigue syndrome
* Organism persists in bone marrow for years ([QJM 2005;98:7](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15625349), [Epidemiol Infect 2000;124:543](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10982079))

Case reports

* 71 year old man with malaise, fever, sweats ([N Engl J Med 2006;354:1937](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16672706))

Diagnosis

* PCR or serology

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Suggestive finding is a fibrin ring / doughnut granuloma defined as a small, nonnecrotizing granuloma with ring-like structure composed of fibrinoid material, often with a central fat vacuole ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1986;110:1017](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3778120))
* Rarely hemophagocytosis ([Clin Infect Dis 1995;21:1029](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8645795))
