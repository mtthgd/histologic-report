Definition / general

* Up to 10% of AML in children, 5% or less of adult AML ([Orphanet (May 2004): Acute megakaryoblastic leukemia [Accessed 6 April 2018]](http://www.orpha.net/data/patho/GB/uk-AMLM7.pdf))
* Associated with marrow fibrosis due to megakaryoblast secretion of fibrogenic cytokines, which makes marrow aspiration difficult
* In adults, median age 57 years, 59% have prior hematologic disorder or myelodysplastic syndrome ([Blood 2006;107:880](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16123215))
* 19% had prior chemotherapy, classify now as AML-MRC (myelodysplasia related changes) or t-AML

Terminology

* Acute megakaryoblastic leukemia (AMKL, M7)

Epidemiology

* Down Syndrome (DS): 150x increased risk of AML compared to non-Down children age 0 - 4 years; 70% are AML M7 compared to 3 - 6% in non-Down children
* DS children 0 - 3 years old: ALL vs AML risk is 1:1.2 compared to 4:1 for non-DS children

Clinical features

* Thrombocytopenia, may have thrombocytosis, dysplastic features in neutrophils, erythroids, megakaryocytes and platelets
* Infrequent hepatosplenomegaly
* Associated with germ cell tumors in young boys

Diagnosis

* WHO 2008: 20%+ blasts
* 50%+ blasts of megakaryocytic lineage are present in bone marrow
* Must exclude AML-MRC (myelodysplasia related changes), AML with t(1;22)(p13;q13); inv(3)(q21;q26.2); t(3;3)(q21;q26.2) and Down syndrome related
* Megakaryocytic lineage is based on CD41+, CD61+ or positive platelet peroxidase reaction on EM

Prognostic factors

* Poor, median overall survival is 6 months

Case reports

* 17 month old girl with bi-allelic deletions within 13q14 and transient trisomy 21 with absence of GATA1 ([Pediatr Blood Cancer 2011;57:516](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21538823))
* Young boy with coexisting mediastinal germ cell tumor ([Clin Transl Oncol 2007;9:329](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17525044))
* 25 year old man with findings on FNA and CSF ([Cytojournal 2011;8:17](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22022337))
* 25 year old man with i(12p) related disease after primary mediastinal germ cell tumor ([J Korean Med Sci 2011;26:1099](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21860563))
* 58 year old woman with coexisting myeloid sarcoma of femur ([Srp Arh Celok Lek 2011;139:805](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22338480))
* Diagnosis 15 years after kidney transplantation ([Ann Hematol 2011;90:843](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20857111))
* "Cannibalistic" phagocytosis in acute megakaryoblastic leukemia (AML M7) with t(10;17)(p15;q22) ([Leuk Lymphoma 2010;51:1944](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20846092))
* Coexistence of meningeal infiltration and multiple lymphadenopathy as the initial presentation of de novo adult acute megakaryoblastic leukemia ([Leuk Res 2011;35:e50](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21255836))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Megakaryoblasts (often better morphology on biopsy than smear) are medium / large cells with blue vacuolated, agranular, eosinophilic cytoplasm containing fine granules, cytoplasmic projections (blebs and pseudopods) resembling platelets, irregular cytoplasmic borders and cytoplasmic zoning; may occur in clusters
* Nuclei are round or slightly indented with finely reticular, dense chromatin and 1 - 3 nucleoli
* Myelofibrosis or increased marrow reticulin is common; may also have small lymphoid-like blasts

Peripheral smear description

Positive stains

* [CD41](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd40.html#cd41) and [CD61](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd61.html) (megakaryocyte specific), [CD42b](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd40.html#cd42b) ([Mod Pathol 2005;18:603](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15578075)), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [CD36](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30to39.html#cd36), [factor VIII](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfactorviii.html) and [von Willebrand factor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvwf.html)
* Variable [CD13](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd13.html), [CD33](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd33.html), [CD71](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd71.html), [alpha naphthyl acetate esterase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnonspecificesterase.html), [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html) and [HLA-DR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshladr.html)

Electron microscopy description

* Megakaryoblasts have demarcation membranes and “bulls-eye” alpha granules with peroxidase activity in nuclear envelope and endoplasmic reticulum, but not in granules and Golgi complex

Differential diagnosis

* [ALL](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodesALL.html)
* [AML-M0](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/leukemiaM0.html)
* Blastic transformation of CML
* [AML-M1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/leukemiaM1.html)
* [AML-M5a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/leukemiaacutemonocyticleukemiam5a.html)
* Metastatic small blue cell tumors in children
