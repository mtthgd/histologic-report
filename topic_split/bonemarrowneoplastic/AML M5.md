Definition / general

* Acute monoblastic and acute monocytic leukemia (AML M5)
* 10% of AML cases
* High incidence of bleeding disorders (including DIC), organomegaly, lymphadenopathy, gingival hyperplasia, CNS and other tissue involvement (monocytes infiltrate)
* May present with acute respiratory failure ([Am J Respir Crit Care Med 2003;167:1329](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12574074))
* M5a versus M5b: analysis of features is controversial; (a) similar features ([Leuk Res 2008;32:269](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17689610)); (b) different expression of CD68 and CD11c ([Zhongguo Shi Yan Xue Ye Xue Za Zhi 2006;14:1079](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17204168)); (c) different features ([Zhongguo Shi Yan Xue Ye Xue Za Zhi 2006;14:654](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16928293))
* Similar prognosis as other subtypes ([J Clin Oncol 2004;22:1276](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14970186))

Diagnosis

* 80% or more nonerythroid bone marrow cells are monocyte lineage (monoblasts, promonocytes and monocytes)
* A minor neutrophil component

Enzyme cytochemistry

* Monoblast granules and monocytes are strongly positive for nonspecific esterase and lysozyme, but negative for myeloperoxidase
* More mature monocyte lineage cells may be weakly myeloperoxidase positive
* **Note:** if NSE negative, confirm monocyte lineage with immunostains

Positive stains

* **At least two monocytic markers:** [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html), [CD14](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10to19.html#cd14), [CD11b](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10to19.html#cd11b) (80 - 90%, [Am J Clin Pathol 1997;107:283](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9052378)), [CD11c](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10to19.html#cd11c) (50%), [CD36](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd36.html), [CD64](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd60.html#cd64) ([Am J Clin Pathol 1998;110:797](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9844593)), [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html) and [HLA-DR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshladr.html)
* **Myeloid markers:** [CD13](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd13.html) (often very bright), [CD33](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd33.html) (often very bright), [CD15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd15.html) and [CD65](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd60.html#cd65)
* [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) (30%), [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html) and [MPO](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyeloperoxidase.html) (more often M5b and less often M5a)
* **Abberant expression:** [CD7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd7.html), [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html) (25 - 40%)
* [Lysozyme](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainslysozyme.html), [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html) and [CD163](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd163.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* 11q23 translocations in 19%, trisomy 8 in 17%
