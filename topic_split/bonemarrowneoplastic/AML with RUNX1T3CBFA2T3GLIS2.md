Definition / general

* Acute myeloid leukemia (AML) with *CBFA2T3(RUNX1T3)::GLIS2* is a rare subtype of pediatric AML

Essential features

* Pediatric acute myeloid leukemia with dismal prognosis
* *CBFA2T3::GLIS2* is the most common alteration seen in non-Down syndrome acute megakaryoblastic leukemia (non-DS AMKL)
* Often expressing the unique RAM phenotype by flow cytometry with bright CD56 and lack of HLA-DR and CD38 expression

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9861/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=99) - acute myeloid leukemia, NOS
* ICD-10: [C92.0](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C81-C96/C92-/C92.0) - acute myeloid leukemia
* ICD-11: [2A60.0](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#1430965006) - acute myeloid leukemia with recurrent genetic abnormalities

Epidemiology

* Exclusively seen in pediatric population, especially Cancer Cell 2012;22:683, [Blood 2013;121:3469](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23407549), [J Hematol Oncol 2017;10:26](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28109323))
* Comprises almost 30% of pediatric non-DS AMKL ([Nat Genet 2017;49:451](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28112737), [Cancer Cell 2012;22:683](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23153540))

Sites

* Peripheral blood, bone marrow, lymph node, 1 reported case with a paraspinal mass ([Cancer Cell 2012;22:683](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23153540), [EJHaem 2023;4:765](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37601875))

Pathophysiology

* Cryptic inversion on chromosome 16 [inv(16)(p13.3q24.3)] results in the joining of *CBFA2T3*, a member of the ETO family of nuclear corepressors, to *GLIS2*, a member of the GLI family of transcription factors ([Cancer Cell 2012;22:683](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23153540))
* *CBFA2T3* and *GLIS2* genes are both localized, in inverted orientation, on each arm of chromosome 16 close to the telomeres, in 16q24.3 and 16p13.3, respectively ([Cancer Cell 2012;22:683](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23153540))
* *CBFA2T3::GLIS2* fusion transcript leads to upregulation of BMP2 (bone morphogenetic proteins) and *GATA3*, a downstream target of Hedgehog signaling as well as JAK-STAT pathway ([J Exp Med 2012;209:2017](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23045605), [Development 2004;131:1165](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14973276), [Cancer Cell 2012;22:683](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23153540))

Etiology

* Not known

Clinical features

* Median age of diagnosis is 1.26 - 1.5 years of age ([Leukemia 2016;30:2077](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27133823), [Blood 2016;127:3424](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27114462))
* Case reports have described leukocytosis and extensive lymphadenopathy ([Leuk Res Rep 2021;17:100287](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34976721), [EJHaem 2023;4:765](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37601875))
* Extramedullary involvement is more frequent in patients expressing the *CBFA2T3::GLIS2* chimeric gene (25%) compared to the frequency reported for pediatric AML in general ([Blood 2013;121:3469](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23407549), [Blood 2013;122:170](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23673857))
* It is seen in non-Down syndrome patients

Diagnosis

* Diagnostic criteria per World Health Organization (WHO) Classification of Tumors, 2022 requires ≥ 20% myeloid blasts in the peripheral blood or bone marrow and detection of *CBFA2T3::GLIS2* ([Leukemia 2022;36:1703](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35732831))
* International Consensus Classification (ICC), 2022 requires ≥ 10% myeloid blasts in the bone marrow or peripheral blood ([Blood 2022;140:1200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35767897))
* Must exclude the diagnostic criteria for AML with other defining genetic abnormalities, AML MR (AML myelodysplasia related), AML pCT (postcytotoxic therapy) or MPAL (mixed phenotype acute leukemia)
* This entity is recognized by the WHO 5th edition under AML with other defined genetic alterations but is not a specific entity within the ICC

Laboratory

* Leukocytosis with increased blasts (≥ 10% per ICC; ≥ 20% per WHO 5th edition) ([Leukemia 2022;36:1703](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35732831), [Blood 2022;140:1200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35767897))
* No differences are reported with reference to white blood cell count (WBC), bone marrow blast percentage, platelet count or hemoglobin levels as compared to other AML subtypes ([Leukemia 2016;30:2077](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27133823))
* *CBFA2T3::GLIS2* positive AML patients tend to have a higher percentage of bone marrow blasts at diagnosis compared to fusion negative patients ([Blood 2016;127:3424](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27114462), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2017;56:394](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28063190))

Prognostic factors

* *CBFA2T3::GLIS2* AML has dismal outcome ([Nat Genet 2017;49:451](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28112737))
* 5 year overall survival is ~30% ([Cancer Cell 2012;22:683](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23153540))

Case reports

* 5 month old girl with leukocytosis, eosinophilia and paraspinal mass ([EJHaem 2023;4:765](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37601875))
* 12 month old girl presented with bruising and found to have marked leukocytosis and skin nodules ([Cold Spring Harb Mol Case Stud 2022;8:a006220](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35927023))
* 1 year old girl presented with fever, cytopenias and hepatosplenomegaly ([Leuk Lymphoma 2023;64:2042](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37548333))
* 2 year old monozygotic twins presented simultaneously with RAM AML ([Pediatr Transplant 2018;22:e13291](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30220110))
* 5 year old boy with extensive mesenteric and retroperitoneal lymphadenopathy ([Leuk Res Rep 2021;17:100287](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34976721))

Treatment

* Patients may benefit from intensive chemotherapy followed by allogeneic stem cell transplant as postremission intensification strategy ([Pediatr Transplant 2018;22:e13291](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30220110))
* Overexpression of the folate receptor 1 (*FOLR1*) gene has shown great result with targeted therapy ([J Clin Invest 2022;132:e157101](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36136600))
* Navitoclax or DT2216 treatment in combination with low dose cytarabine has been found effective in murine models ([Blood Adv 2024;8:112](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37729615))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* No specific morphological features have been found associated with this fusion gene ([Blood 2013;121:3469](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23407549))
* In AMKL *CBFA2T3::GLIS2* positive patients, megakaryoblasts are reported as the predominant component of the blast population in bone marrow or peripheral blood ([Br J Haematol 2019;184:337](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30592296))
* Megakaryoblasts are large cells with abundant cytoplasm and prominent nucleoli; they often display cytoplasmic blebs or pseudopods
* In one of the pediatric studies, 50% (N = 10) of the fusion positive patients had a non-FAB M7 subtype, others were distributed as follows: M5 (15%), M0 (15%), M1 (10%), M2 (5%), M4 (5%) ([Blood 2013;121:3469](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23407549))

Circulating blasts

Positive stains

* [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [CD43](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd43.html), [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html), [CD42b](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd40.html#cd42b) (weak), [CD61](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd61.html), [CD41](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd40.html#cd41) ([EJHaem 2023;4:765](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37601875))

Negative stains

* [Lysozyme](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainslysozyme.html), [myeloperoxidase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyeloperoxidase.html) ([Leukemia 2016;30:2077](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27133823))

Flow cytometry description

* RAM phenotype was named after the patient's initials in original study ([Leukemia 2016;30:2077](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27133823))
* Bright [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html) expression (at minimum 2 log10 units greater than normal myeloid progenitors)
* Dim to negative expression of [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html), [CD38](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd38.html) and lack of [HLA-DR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshladr.html)
* Reported cases have shown aberrant expression of T and B cell markers, such as cytoplasmic [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html) and [CD19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd19.html) ([EJHaem 2023;4:765](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37601875), [Leuk Lymphoma 2023;64:462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36346368))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Concomitant cytogenetic abnormalities are rare in patients with *CBFA2T3::GLIS2* ([Br J Haematol 2019;184:337](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30592296))
* In cases of abnormal karyotype, complex aberrations, trisomy 21 and hyperdiploidy have been noted ([Cancer Cell 2012;22:683](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23153540), [Nat Genet 2017;49:451](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28112737), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2017;56:394](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28063190))
* Somatic mutations are significantly lower than other subgroups of AMKL ([Cancer Cell 2012;22:683](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23153540), [Nat Genet 2017;49:451](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28112737))
* *GATA1, KIT* and *FLT3* mutations are less frequently noted in *CBFA2T3::GLIS2* positive than in negative cases ([Br J Haematol 2019;184:337](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30592296))
* *RAS* and JAK-STAT pathway mutations are reported more often ([Cancer Cell 2012;22:683](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23153540), [Nat Genet 2017;49:451](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28112737))

Sample pathology report

* Peripheral blood, bone marrow aspirate and left posterior iliac crest bone marrow core biopsy:
  + Acute myeloid leukemia with *CBFA2T3::GLIS2* extensively involving a hypercellular (virtually 100%) bone marrow (see comment)
  + Flow cytometric immunophenotyping of the bone marrow aspirate reveals a blast population (~90% of cellular events) with a dim to negative CD45 expression that is positive for CD34, bright positive for CD56 and negative for CD38 and HLA-DR.
  + Comment: The overall morphologic, immunophenotypic and genetic findings are consistent with a diagnosis of acute myeloid leukemia with *CBFA2T3::GLIS2*.
  + Peripheral blood smear: The peripheral blood smear shows predominantly circulating blasts (90%) that are large in size with abundant cytoplasm and frequent prominent nucleoli with occasional cytoplasmic blebs. Normocytic normochromic anemia is present with rare nucleated red blood cells. There is absolute neutropenia and lymphopenia. Thrombocytopenia with rare large platelets.
  + Bone marrow aspirate smear: The majority of the cells present are blasts with similar morphology as seen in the peripheral blood. Background hematopoiesis is markedly decreased. Rare megakaryocytes are seen.
  + Bone marrow core biopsy (decalcified) / particle clot: The bone marrow is markedly hypercellular for age (~100%) and is composed almost entirely of blasts. Very rare myeloid and erythroid precursors are present. The particle clot shows multiple bone marrow particles composed predominantly of blasts.

Differential diagnosis

* [AML with minimal differentiation (M0)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/leukemiaM0.html):
  + Median age of presentation is 50 - 60 years
  + Blasts lack [MPO](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyeloperoxidase.html) but express at least 2 myeloid associated markers ([CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html), [CD13](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd13.html), [CD33](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd33.html))
  + [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html), [CD7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1.html#cd7) and [TdT](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstdt.html) expression can be seen
* [AML with *RUNX1::RUNX1T1*](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/leukemiaAMLwitht821q22q22.html):
  + [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html) expression can be noted in blasts
  + Blasts demonstrate bright expression of [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) along with [CD15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd15.html) (maturation asynchrony) and B cell markers expression ([CD19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd19.html), [CD79a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd79a.html), [PAX5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax5.html))
  + Blasts have characteristic morphology, with large size, abundant cytoplasm and numerous azurophilic granules
* [Mixed phenotype acute leukemia, T / myeloid](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/leukemiamixedpheno.html):
  + AML with *CBFA2T3::GLIS2* have been reported to express T cell markers (cytoplasmic [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html))
  + MPAL expresses both T cell lineage specific (surface [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html) / cytoplasmic [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html)) and myeloid lineage specific markers ([MPO](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyeloperoxidase.html) / monocytic)
* [Early T precursor lymphoblastic leukemia / lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaTcelllymphoblasticleukemia.html):
  + ETP ALL has characteristic phenotype: cyto [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html)+, [CD1a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1a.html)-, [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html)-, [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html)- / dim and positive for 1 or more myeloid or stem cell markers
* [Acute megakaryoblastic leukemia (M7)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/leukemiaM7.html):
  + Blasts are positive for megakaryocytic markers, including [CD36](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30to39.html#cd36), [CD41](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd40.html#cd41) (glycoprotein IIb), [CD42b](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd40.html#cd42b) (glycoprotein Ib) and [CD61](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd61.html) (glycoprotein IIIa)
  + Among the myeloid markers, [CD33](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd33.html) is positive while [CD13](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd13.html) and [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html) are less commonly positive; [MPO](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyeloperoxidase.html) is mostly negative
  + Expression of [CD7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd7.html) and [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html) may be seen
* [Myeloid proliferations associated with Down syndrome](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/leukemiaacutemyeloiddown.html):
  + Transient abnormal myelopoiesis (TAM) and myeloid leukemia associated with Down syndrome (ML-DS) are 2 clonal conditions seen in patients with trisomy 21
  + TAM presents usually in the first 7 days of life while ML-DS onset is 1.6 years ([Br J Haematol 2018;182:200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29916557), [J Clin Oncol 2014;32:3021](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25092781), [Blood 2017;129:3304](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28389462))
  + Blast morphology ranges from undifferentiated myeloid blasts to megakaryoblasts
  + Blasts express [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html) with variable expression of [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [CD36](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30to39.html#cd36), [CD41](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd40.html#cd41) / [42b](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd40.html#cd42b) or [CD235a (glycophorin A)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd230.html#cd235)
  + Exon 2/3 somatic *GATA1* mutations are seen ([Blood 2011;118:2222](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21715302), [Nat Genet 2013;45:1293](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24056718), [Cancer Cell 2019;36:123](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31303423))
  + In ML-DS additional mutations are also identified, such as cohesin complex, *EZH2, KANSL1* or *JAK3* ([Nat Genet 2013;45:1293](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24056718), [Cancer Cell 2019;36:123](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31303423))

What characteristic phenotype panel is seen in acute myeloid leukemia with *CBFA2T3::GLIS2*?

1. CD34, CD56, CD38, HLA-DR
2. CD34, CD117, CD13, CD33
3. CD34, CD117, CD41, CD61
4. CD34, TdT, CD19, CD79a

Board review style answer #1

**A.** CD34, CD56, CD38, HLA-DR. The blasts have characteristic phenotype CD56+, CD38 and CD45 (negative to dim) and HLA-DR-. Answer B is incorrect because it is the antibody panel for acute myeloid leukemia. Answer C is incorrect because it is the panel for acute megakaryoblastic leukemia. Answer D is incorrect because it is the panel for B lymphoblastic leukemia. This phenotype is not specific to this AML subtype but is characteristically seen.
