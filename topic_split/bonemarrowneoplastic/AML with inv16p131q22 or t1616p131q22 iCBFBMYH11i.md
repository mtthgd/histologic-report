Definition / general

* Acute myeloid leukemia (AML) with inv(16)(p13.1;q22) or t(16;16)(p13.1;q22); *CBFB-MYH11* is a subtype of AML with recurrent genetic abnormalities characterized by myelomonocytic differentiation and presence of abnormal eosinophils

Essential features

* Inv(16)(p13.1;q22) or t(16;16)(p13.1;q22) results in the formation of an abnormal core binding factor beta subunit / myosin heavy chain 11 (*CBFB-MYH11*) fusion gene
* Most cases show myelomonocytic differentiation with presence of abnormal eosinophils
* Diagnosis of AML with inv(16)(p13.1;q22) or t(16;16)(p13.1;q22) can be made even when the blast percentage is less than 20%
* Prognosis is good compared with other AMLs

Terminology

* Acute myelomonocytic leukemia with abnormal eosinophils
* French American British (FAB) M4Eo
* Acute myeloid leukemia with *CBFB-MYH11*

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9871/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/%0D%0A9789241548496_eng.pdf#page=100) - acute myeloid leukemia with inv(16)(p13.1q22) or t(16;16)(p13.1;q22); *CBFB-MYH11*
* ICD-10: [C92.0](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C81-C96/C92-/C92.0) - acute myeloid leukemia

Epidemiology

* 7% of AMLs in the pediatric age group ([J Clin Oncol 2010;28:2674](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20439644))
* Blood 2006;108:3280)

Sites

* Most cases present with involvement of the blood and bone marrow
* Occasional cases present as myeloid sarcoma ([Am J Clin Pathol 2020;153:333](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31671434))

Pathophysiology

* Both inv(16)(p13.1;q22) and t(16;16)(p13.1;q22) result in a core binding factor beta subunit / myosin heavy chain 11 (*CBFB-MYH11*) fusion gene encoding an abnormal CBFB-MYH11 fusion protein ([Semin Hematol 2015;52:215](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26111469))
* CBFB-MYH11 fusion protein causes aberrant self renewal and suppresses differentiation
* Additional mutations promote leukemogenesis

Clinical features

* Fatigue resulting from anemia
* Easy bruising from thrombocytopenia
* Mass effects of myeloid sarcoma, such as intestinal obstruction
* Higher white blood cell count at diagnosis of AML with inv(16)(p.13.1q22) and t(16;16)(p.13.1;q22) compared with other AMLs ([J Clin Oncol 2005;23:5705](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16110030))

Diagnosis

* Diagnosis is based on a combination of morphology, flow cytometry, conventional cytogenetics, real time PCR and FISH analysis

Laboratory

* Anemia and thrombocytopenia

Prognostic factors

* Overall prognosis is good compared to other AMLs ([Blood 2003;102:462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12649129))
* *KIT* and *FLT3* mutations are associated with worse prognosis
* Trisomy 22 is associated with a more favorable outcome
* High levels of minimal residual disease after induction are associated with worse outcomes ([Am Soc Clin Oncol Educ Book 2018;38:555](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30231330))

Case reports

* 30 year old man presented to emergency department with 2 weeks history of fever, abdominal pain and fatigue ([Clin Med Insights Blood Disord 2017;10:1179545X17700858](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28579854))
* 30 year old woman presented with a 2 week history of fever, cough with dyspnea and chest pain, generalized weakness and erythematous papules over the dorsum of her right hand and bleeding per vaginum ([Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2016;59:104](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26960652))
* 32 year old woman was first seen for gingivitis, hypertrophic gingiva, metrorrhagia and purpura ([Hematol Oncol 2017;35:385](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28906004))
* 47 year old man presented with intermittent low grade fever and progressive fatigue ([Mol Cytogenet 2020;13:4](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32015759))

Treatment

* Induction chemotherapy with 3 days of anthracycline and 7 days of cytarabine (7+3) ([Am Soc Clin Oncol Educ Book 2018;38:555](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30231330))
* Cytarabine or HDAC inhibitors are used for consolidation therapy
* Gemtuzumab ozogamicin (toxin conjugated monoclonal antibody to CD33) may improve outcomes when combined with standard chemotherapy

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Hypercellular bone marrow core biopsy and aspirate clot sections with presence of immature cells;
  increased eosinophilic cells can be seen

Cytology description

* Increased blasts showing nuclei with fine chromatin and high nuclear to cytoplasmic ratio
* Some blasts display Auer rods
* Blasts are usually > 20% but the diagnosis is made even when blasts are Increased eosinophils at all stages of maturation, with the earlier stages displaying dual granulation ([Haematologica 2002;87:886](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12161368))
* Increased immature monocytic cells (promonocytes)

Peripheral smear description

* Presence of blasts and immature monocytic cells
* Eosinophils are not usually increased ([Blood 1986;68:1242](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3465376))

Positive stains

* Abnormal eosinophils are faintly positive for [naphthol AS-D chloroacetate esterase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschloroacetate.html)
* Myeloblasts are positive for [myeloperoxidase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyeloperoxidase.html)
* Monocytic cells are positive for [alpha naphthyl butyrate esterase (nonspecific esterase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnonspecificesterase.html))
* Immunohistochemical stains show positivity for [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) and [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)
  in the blasts

Negative stains

* Negative for nonhematopoietic markers and most lymphoid markers

Flow cytometry description

* Blasts are positive for dim [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [CD13](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd13.html), [HLA-DR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshladr.html) and [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)
* A subset of blasts may express [CD15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd15.html)
* A characteristic finding is weak expression of [CD33](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd33.html) in blasts ([Blood 2012;119:3705](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22378848))
* [CD2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1.html#cd2) expression may be seen in the blast population
* Immature monocytic cells express [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html), [CD11b](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10to19.html#cd11b), [CD11c](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10to19.html#cd11c) and [CD64](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd60.html#cd64)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Conventional cytogenetics shows presence of inv(16)(p13.1;q22) or t(16;16)(p13.1;q22)
* FISH analysis may be required to detect cryptic cases and shows the *CBFB-MYH11* fusion gene ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 1998;22:87](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9598794))
* In rare cases, real time PCR can detect the *CBFB-MYH11* transcript when cytogenetics and FISH analyses are negative
* Real time PCR is used to assess minimal / measurable residual disease (MRD)

Sample pathology report

* Bone marrow, right posterior iliac crest, core biopsy, clot section, aspirate smears and touch imprint hypercellular bone marrow (95%) with involvement by acute myeloid leukemia with inv(16)(p13.1;q22) (see comment)
  + Comment: Bone marrow core biopsy is hypercellular for age (95%) Immature cells are increased. Erythroid precursors are decreased. Mature myeloid cells are decreased. Megakaryocytes are adequate and appear normal in morphology.
  + Aspirate clot section is adequate and shows features similar to the core biopsy.
  + Bone marrow aspirate smear is adequate. Blasts are increased (30%) and show a high nuclear to cytoplasmic ratio with fine chromatin. Immature monocytic cells (promonocytes) are also increased. Scattered eosinophils and eosinophil precursors are seen throughout the aspirate smear. Some of the eosinophilic cells show dual granulation. Megakaryocytes are adequate in number and show normal morphology. Cytochemical stains show that many of the blasts are positive for myeloperoxidase. Alpha naphthyl butyrate esterase (nonspecific esterase) is positive in the immature monocytic cells.
  + Touch preparation findings are similar to the core biopsy.
  + Flow cytometry shows a large abnormal myeloid blast population with expression of CD34, CD15 (subset), CD33, CD13, HLA-DR and CD117. There is also an immature monocytic population showing characteristic expression of CD64, CD11b and CD11c, and possible absence of CD14. Gating on the lymphocytes shows no evidence of a monoclonal B cell population or T cell abnormality based on the markers assayed.
  + Cytogenetic analysis shows presence of a pericentric inversion of chromosome 16. FISH analysis using a dual color break apart probe shows rearrangement of *CBFB*, consistent with inv(16)(p13.1;q22).

Differential diagnosis

* [Chronic eosinophilic leukemia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/myeloproliferativeCEL.html):
  + Blasts are inv(16)(p13.1;q22) or t(16;16)(p13.1;q22) is absent
* [Myeloid / lymphoid neoplasms with eosinophilia and gene rearrangement](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/myeloproliferativeFGFR1.html):
  + Presence of rearrangement of *[PDGFRA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/myeloproliferativePDGFRA.html)*, *[PDGFRB](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/myeloproliferativePDGFRB.html)* or *[FGFR1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/myeloproliferativeFGFR1.html)* or with *[PCM1-JAK2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/myeloproliferativeatypicalCML.html)* fusion
  + inv(16)(p13.1;q22) or t(16;16)(p13.1;q22) is absent
* [Acute myeloid leukemia with t(8:21)(q22;q22.1); *RUNX1-RUNX1T1*](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/leukemiaAMLwitht821q22q22.html):
  + Eosinophils do not usually display morphologic or cytochemical abnormalities
  + inv(16)(p13.1;q22) or t(16;16)(p13.1;q22) is absent
