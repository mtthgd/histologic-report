Definition / general

* *MLL* gene at 11q23 (HRX) encodes a DNA binding protein that positively regulates expression of target genes, including multiple *HOX* genes, by methylation of histone H3 lys4 / chromatin remodeling ([OMIM: Lysine Specific Methyltransferase 2A; KMT2A [Accessed 9 February 2022],](http://omim.org/entry/159555) [Atlas of Genetics and Cytogenetics [Accessed 9 February 2022]](http://atlasgeneticsoncology.org/Anomalies/t0911ID1001.html))
* 9p22 is MLLT3 / AF9 gene
* Included in WHO 2008 as AML with recurrent cytogenetics
* 3 - 5% of AML ([Anticancer Res 2005;25(3B):1931](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16158928))
* 9 - 12% of childhood AML; 2% of adult AML
* Overlaps other categories, including high percentage of topo II inhibitor, therapy related AML; also ALL and biphenotypic leukemia ([Blood 2003;102:2395](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12805060))

Clinical features

* Symptoms: DIC, extramedullary myeloid sarcoma and tissue infiltration (gingiva, skin)
* Translocations occur in AML (intermediate prognosis) and ALL (poor prognosis) ([Blood Cells Mol Dis 2008;40:192](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17905612))
* Prognosis may be superior to other AML with 11q23 translocations
* MLL partial tandem duplication AML: prognosis varies from poor to similar to other AML ([Br J Haematol 2006;135:438](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16965385), [Blood 2007;109:5164](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17341662))
  + Also present in 93% of normal cord blood samples at low levels ([Leuk Res 2006;30:1091](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16540167))
  + Associated with normal karyotype or trisomy 11

Case reports

* 60 year old woman with therapy related ALL with *MLL* gene rearrangement following treatment of breast cancer ([Korean J Lab Med 2010;30:255](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20603585))
* MLL insertion with *MLL-MLLT3* gene fusion in acute leukemia ([Cancer Genet Cytogenet 2008;183:53](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18474298))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* 20%+ blasts / blast equivalents (monoblasts / monocytes) in peripheral blood or bone marrow, usually myelomonocytic or monocytic (AML M4, M5) and occasionally AML with (M2) or without (M1) maturation
* Monoblasts are large cells with abundant, moderate to intensively basophilic cytoplasm, pseudopods, azurophilic granules, vacuoles; round nuclei, lacy chromatin and one or more prominent nucleoli
* Promonocytes have basophilic cytoplasm with granules and occasional large azurophilic granules, vacuoles; irregular and delicately convoluted nuclei

Positive stains

* [Nonspecific esterase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnonspecificesterase.html)
* [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html) weak, [CD33](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd33.html) bright, [CD36](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30to39.html#cd36) / [CD64](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd60.html#cd64) coexpression, [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html), [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html) and [HLA-DR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshladr.html)

Negative stains

* [Myeloperoxidase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyeloperoxidase.html)
* Children: [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) and [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html) negative
* Adults: variable [CD14](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10to19.html#cd14), often negative / dim

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Involves MLL mixed lineage or myeloid / lymphoid leukemia gene, present in both AML and ALL; also called HTRX1, HRX and ALL1
* FISH is more sensitive than conventional cytogenetics in detecting MLL; may also detect 11q22-25 rearrangements that are MLL negative ([Am J Clin Pathol 2004;122:298](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15323147))

  **Variant MLL translocations in AML:**
* > 80 translocations described, most commonly MLLT2(AF4) causing ALL; and MLLT3(AF9) causing AML
* Mixed phenotype acute leukemia has t(v;11q23)
* AML-MRC if t(2;11)(p21;q23) or t(11;16)(q23;p13.3)
* Diagnose as t-AML if history of cytotoxic therapy
* Other common translocations: 6q27 (MLLT4), 10p12 (MLLT10), 19p13.1 (ELL) and 19p13.3 (MLLT1)
