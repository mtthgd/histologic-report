Definition / general

* 10 - 20% of AML cases, 44% in one Brazil hospital ([Sao Paulo Med J 2006;124:45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16612463))
* Usually adults (median age 46 years), present with anemia, thrombocytopenia and neutropenia; may have leukocytosis with markedly increased blasts
* 4% of childhood AML

Clinical features

* **Criteria for diagnosis:** 90%+ of nonerythroid cells in marrow are myeloblasts; **Enzyme cytochemistry:** 3%+ of blasts are positive for myeloperoxidase or Sudan Black B (confirm by immunohistochemistry if only 3 - 10% positive for MPO by enzyme cytochemistry); chloroacetate esterase positive
* Poor prognosis

Case reports

* 4 year old child with t(6;9) and basophilia ([Ann Biol Clin (Paris) 2003;61:352](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12805015))
* 44 year old man with large and small blasts ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2004;128:448](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15043460))
* Presenting with arterial thromboembolism ([Leuk Res 2007;31:869](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17011031))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Typically markedly hypercellular marrow, but normocellular and hypocellular cases occur
* Very immature cells, usually round with few azurophilic cytoplasmic granules or Auer rods
* Nuclei are round or indented; little maturation beyond myeloblast stage
* Cells may may resemble lymphoblasts and not appear myeloid

Positive stains

* Have 1+ myeloid associated antigens ([CD13](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd13.html), [CD33](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd33.html), [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html))
* [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html) and [HLA-DR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshladr.html) (70%) ([Mod Pathol 2000;13:452](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10786814))
* [CD7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1.html#cd7) (30%); [CD2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1.html#cd2), [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html), [CD19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd19.html) and [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html) in 10 - 20% of cases; rarely [CD11b](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10to19.html#cd11b)
* Variable [CD15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd15.html), [CD71](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd71.html) and [HLA-DR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshladr.html)

Negative stains

* [cCD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html), [CD14](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd14.html) (usually), [CD15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd15.html), [cCD22](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20to29.html#cd22), [CD36](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30to39.html#cd36) (usually), [CD41](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd40.html#cd41), [CD61](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd61.html), [CD64](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd60.html#cd64) (usually) ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2007;131:748](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17488160))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Associated with t(8;21)
* FLT3 ITD in 22% ([Ai Zheng 2007;26:58](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17222369))
* FLT3 mutations associated with HLA-DR negative patients ([Leuk Res 2007;31:921](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17056111))
