Definition / general
  • Acute undifferentiated leukemia (AUL) is an acute leukemia that does not express any lineage specific (i.e., myeloid or lymphoid) markers but expresses hematopoietic stem cell markers (Leukemia 2022;36:1703)
  • AUL falls in the category of acute leukemias of ambiguous lineage defined immunophenotypically along with mixed phenotype acute leukemias (MPALs) (Am J Clin Pathol 2022;158:27)
Essential features
  • Acute undifferentiated leukemia (AUL) is an acute leukemia that does not express any lineage specific (i.e., myeloid or lymphoid) hematopoietic markers (Leukemia 2022;36:1703)
  • Essential diagnostic criteria include the presence of ≥ 20% blasts in bone marrow or blood, without immunophenotypic expression of lineage specific markers (Leukemia 2022;36:1703)
  • AUL is exceedingly rare and accounts for 1 - 2% of acute leukemias (Leuk Res 2020;89:106301)
Terminology
  • Acute leukemia, not elsewhere classified
ICD coding
  • ICD-10: C95.0 - acute leukemia of unspecified cell type
Epidemiology
  • It occurs more frequently in adults
  • Its incidence has decreased over time, owing to improved diagnostic techniques (Leukemia 2022;36:1703)
Sites
  • Peripheral blood and bone marrow
Clinical features
  • Presentation similar to other leukemias, including cytopenias
Diagnosis
  • Essential diagnostic criteria include the presence of ≥ 20% blasts in bone marrow or blood, without immunophenotypic expression of lineage specific markers (Leukemia 2022;36:1703)
  • According to the WHO classification, lineage specific markers are limited to (Leukemia 2022;36:1703)
    • Myeloid lineage specific
      • Myeloperoxidase (MPO; a blast population with intensity that exceeds 50% of mature neutrophil level by flow) or
      • Monocytic phenotype with ≥ 2 of the following markers: nonspecific esterase, CD11c, CD14, CD64 or lysozyme
    • B cell lineage specific
      • Strong CD19 expression and ≥ 1 of the following markers: CD10, CD22, CD79a or PAX5
      • Weak CD19 expression and ≥ 2 of the following markers: CD10, CD22, CD79a or PAX5
    • T cell lineage specific
      • Cytoplasmic or surface CD3 (exceeds 50% intensity of mature T cells by flow cytometry)
  • Blasts should not express > 1 lineage associated marker of any given lineage; myeloid associated markers include CD13, CD33 and CD117 (Leukemia 2022;36:1703, Mod Pathol 2019;32:1373)
    • In contrast, acute myeloid leukemia (AML) with minimal differentiation, which represents ≤ 5% of all AML cases, is negative for MPO and expresses at least 2 myeloid associated markers (Mod Pathol 2019;32:1373)
  • Additionally, cases of AUL should not fulfill diagnostic criteria for mixed phenotype acute leukemia with defined genetic abnormalities or rare mixed phenotype acute leukemia subtypes (Leukemia 2022;36:1703)
Laboratory
  • Cytopenias
Prognostic factors
  • AUL generally has a worse prognosis with a median overall survival (OS) of only 9 months, which is significantly lower than that of acute lymphoblastic leukemia and acute myeloid leukemia (Leuk Res 2020;89:106301)
  • Outcomes are similar to those of patients with AML with minimal differentiation but still better than patients with AML myelodysplasia related (Mod Pathol 2019;32:1373)
Case reports
  • 23 year old man with fever was found to have AUL with undifferentiated myeloid sarcoma (Medicine 2024;103:e36948)
  • 31 year old man with leukocytosis was diagnosed with AUL with a balanced t(5;10)(q35;p12) HNRNPH1::MLLT10 (Cancer Genomics Proteomics 2023;20:354)
  • 31 year old man with difficulty breathing was found to have a mediastinal mass and enlarged neck lymph nodes, diagnosed with AUL and treated with an acute lymphoblastic leukemia (ALL) based regimen (J Clin Exp Hematop 2022;62:222)
Treatment
  • Induction therapy may be administered per the ALL treatment protocol or AML treatment regimens (Mod Pathol 2019;32:1373)
  • Successful induction with venetoclax and azacitidine has been described (Hematology 2024;29:2293494, Cancer 2021;127:2608)
  • Allogeneic hematopoietic stem cell transplantation remains the only treatment option to attain long term survival following induction remission, despite varying results (Hematology 2024;29:2293494, Leuk Lymphoma 2018;59:3006)
Microscopic (histologic) description
  • Bone marrow is often hypercellular with a diffuse infiltration by immature mononuclear cells

Blasts

Positive stains
  • Very rarely, blasts may be positive for nonspecific esterase (Leukemia 2022;36:1703)
Negative stains
  • Blasts are negative for MPO and Sudan Black B (Leukemia 2022;36:1703)
Flow cytometry description
  • CD34, HLA-DR and TdT expression has been described in majority of cases (Leukemia 2022;36:1703, Mod Pathol 2019;32:1373)
  • In > 50% of reported cases, blasts express 1 myeloid associated marker (CD13, CD33, CD117); the remaining express no myeloid associated antigens (Leukemia 2022;36:1703)
  • Limited B cell associated antigen expression (partial cCD22 or cCD79a) has been described (Mod Pathol 2019;32:1373)
  • The most frequent T cell associated antigen expressed is CD7 (Mod Pathol 2019;32:1373)

Molecular / cytogenetics description
  • ~40% of cases have an abnormal karyotype, which is often noncomplex (Leukemia 2022;36:1703)
  • Trisomy 13 has been frequently reported (Mod Pathol 2019;32:1373)
  • Gene fusions such as SET::NUP214 may be detected (Leukemia 2022;36:1703)
  • Common pathogenic mutations detected are PHF6 (most frequent), RUNX1, SRSF2, ASXL1 and BCOR (Leukemia 2022;36:1703, Mod Pathol 2019;32:1373)
Sample pathology report
  • Peripheral blood, bone marrow aspirate, touch imprint, core biopsy and clot section:
    • Acute undifferentiated leukemia (see comment)
    • Comment: By flow cytometry, lineage specific hematopoietic antigen expression is not identified, although rare lineage associated antigen expression (subset) is seen. Cytogenetic analysis revealed trisomy 13 and molecular workup revealed an oncogenic PHF6 variant.
    • Peripheral blood: The blood smear shows normocytic anemia with anisopoikilocytosis, including ovalocytes and few teardrop forms. Scattered blasts are visualized that appear immature (round nuclei, dispersed chromatin, small nucleoli and scant cytoplasm) with no morphologic features of differentiation. Platelets are decreased with unremarkable morphology.
    • Bone marrow aspirate and touch imprint: The bone marrow aspirate and touch imprint show abundant blasts with similar morphologic features as described above. Few scattered megakaryocytes and erythroid precursors are visualized.
    • Bone marrow core biopsy: The bone marrow core biopsy is hypercellular for age (80 - 90%). There is extensive replacement of the marrow by sheets of immature mononuclear cells. Background hematopoiesis is markedly decreased with few scattered megakaryocytes.
    • Clot section: The bone marrow clot section shows clotted erythrocytes with scattered immature mononuclear cells and few marrow elements.
Differential diagnosis
  • AML with minimal differentiation:
    • AUL is differentiated from AML with minimal differentiation based on the number of myeloid associated markers that are expressed
    • AML with minimal differentiation demonstrates > 2 myeloid associated markers (Leukemia 2022;36:1703)
    • Nearly 40% of cases possess an abnormal karyotype such as trisomy 13 and other structural abnormalities (Leukemia 2022;36:1703)
    • Mutations in RUNX1, ASXL1, DNMT3A, IDH1 / IDH2, FLT3 ITD, STAG2 and spliceosome genes are frequently identified (Leukemia 2022;36:1703)
  • Rare acute leukemias of ambiguous lineage (ALAL) (Leukemia 2022;36:1703):
    • ALAL with BCL11B rearrangement, which can also impart a T / myeloid immunophenotype
Board review style question #1


A 70 year old woman presents with worsening fatigue. Laboratory results are notable for anemia, thrombocytopenia and leukocytosis. A peripheral smear is obtained and submitted for pathology and flow cytometry evaluation. Peripheral smear shows scattered blasts (see above). Flow cytometry (not shown) reveals an expanded CD45 dim, low side scatter gate containing 60% of events. The aberrant blast population expresses CD34, HLA-DR, CD117 (subset), TdT, CD123, CD38 and CD7, while negative for lineage specific and other lineage associated markers. Cytogenetic and molecular workup reveal trisomy 13 and a PHF6 variant. Which of the following is the best diagnosis?

  1. Acute myeloid leukemia, myelodysplasia related
  2. Acute undifferentiated leukemia
  3. Mixed phenotype acute leukemia, B / myeloid
  4. T lymphoblastic leukemia / lymphoma
Board review style answer #1
B. Acute undifferentiated leukemia. There are ≥ 20% blasts in the bone marrow without immunophenotypic expression of lineage specific markers. Answer A is incorrect because there is no expression of myeloid lineage specific antigens such as MPO. Answer C is incorrect because there is no expression of B cell (i.e., CD19, CD20, CD22, cCD79a or PAX5) or myeloid / monocytic lineage markers (i.e., myeloperoxidase, CD13, CD14, CD15, CD64, CD65 or CD117). Answer D is incorrect because there is no immunophenotypic evidence of an aberrant T lymphoblast population.
