Definition / general

* 1 or more somatic mutation(s) of genes associated with myeloid neoplasms in the presence of 1 or more persistent cytopenias that are unexplained by any other condition

Essential features

* 1 or more somatic mutation(s) of clonal hematopoiesis (CH) driver genes in the presence of 1 or more unexplained cytopenias
* No significant dysplasia in the bone marrow
* It increases the risk of progression to myelodysplastic neoplasms (MDS)

Terminology

* Clonal idiopathic cytopenia of unknown significance
* Clonal cytopenia

ICD coding

* ICD-O: **9980/1** - clonal cytopenia of undetermined significance

Epidemiology

* Anemia and thrombocytopenia are more prevalent in older age ([Blood Adv 2021;5:3266](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34459888))
* ~30% of people with 1 or more cytopenias will have a detectable mutation in a myeloid driver gene or a chromosomal abnormality ([Blood 2021;138:965](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34255818), [Blood 2015;126:2355](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26429975))
* Cumulative incidence of progression to myeloid neoplasm increases with the number of mutations found ([Blood 2021;138:965](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34255818), [Blood 2015;126:2355](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26429975))

Etiology

* Increased risk for clonal hematopoiesis with aging, smoking, male sex and decreased risk with Hispanic ancestry ([Nature 2020;586:763](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33057201))
* Can occur sporadically or after exposure to cytotoxic treatment ([Blood 2021;138:965](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34255818))

Diagrams / tables

None

Clinical features

* 1 or more persistent unexplained cytopenias, usually for 4 months or longer
* Can be symptomatic: bleeding in thrombocytopenia, fatigue / weakness in anemia, frequent infection in leukopenia
* Once other causes are ruled out, MDS or another myeloid disorder is suspected
* Bone marrow does not meet morphologic criteria for MDS
* Studies will identify a somatic mutation in a CH driver gene or a clonal cytogenetic abnormality ([Blood 2015;126:2355](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26429975))

Diagnosis

* Detection of 1 or more somatic mutations involving CH driver genes
* 1 or more unexplained persistent cytopenias
* Absence of diagnostic features for defined myeloid neoplasms on bone marrow examination

Laboratory

* Persistent cytopenia present
* Clonal chromosomal abnormalities can be detected by karyotyping, FISH or next generation sequencing techniques

Prognostic factors

* Risk of progression to myeloid neoplasm increases with the size of the clonal population and the number of somatic alterations
* Somatic mutations in *TP53*, *PPMD1*, *JAK2*, *RUNX1*, *SF3B1*, *SRSF2*, *U2AF1*, *IDH2*, *IDH1* carry higher risk of progression ([Blood 2021;138:965](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34255818), [Blood 2017;129:3371](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28424163))
* Overall survival is decreased in patients who were exposed to cytotoxic therapy prior to developing CCUS ([Blood Adv 2024;8:3130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38564774))

Case reports

* 41 year old woman with a 19 year history of pancytopenia of unknown cause ([Intern Med 2021;60:3785](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34053991))
* 52 year old woman with history of porphyria cutanea tarda developed hematuria and pancytopenia ([JAAD Case Rep 2022;32:44](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36687304))
* 53 year old woman with 1 month of fatigue and 2 days of gingival hemorrhage ([Ther Adv Hematol 2024;15:20406207241260353](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38911444))
* Retired man in his 60s with history of neutropenia is investigated for infections, vasculitis, metabolic abnormalities and malignancies ([BMJ Case Rep 2022;15:e247154](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35351745))

Treatment

* There is no consensus for treatment
  + No standard protocols or evidence based guidelines for management
* Frequent observation and monitoring of cytopenias and transfusion if necessary

Sample pathology report

* Bone marrow, right posterior iliac crest, aspirate smears, core biopsy, particle prep and peripheral blood smear:
  + Variably cellular marrow for age with orderly trilineage hematopoiesis, relative erythroid hyperplasia and no increase in blasts (see comment)
  + Peripheral blood with normocytic anemia and leukocytosis
  + Comment: There is no morphologic and immunophenotypic evidence of myeloid neoplasm or metastatic carcinoma. Cellularity is variable, overall normocellular. No overt morphologic dysplasia is seen. The concurrent flow cytometry shows no abnormal myeloblast population identified. Clinical correlation for underlying medical conditions is recommended and correlation with pending cytogenetic and molecular studies will be required to rule out the possibility of cytogenetically defined myeloid neoplasm versus clonal cytopenia of undetermined significance (CCUS).
  + Addendum comment: The identification of *TET2* with a variant allele frequency (VAF) of 3% suggests an underlying clonal cytopenia of undetermined significance (CCUS). Clinical correlation is recommended.

Differential diagnosis

* [Clonal hematopoiesis of indeterminate potential (CHIP)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonemarrowneoplasticCHIP.html):
  + Presents with detection of 1 or more somatic mutations with VAF of ≥ 2% in the CH driver genes in a patient with normal peripheral blood indices (no cytopenias)
* **Idiopathic cytopenia of undetermined significance (ICUS)**:
  + Presents as persistent unexplained cytopenia(s) without clonal somatic mutations in the CH driver genes
* [Myelodysplastic neoplasms (MDS)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonemarrowneoplasticMDSgeneral.html):
  + Significant dysplasia (≥ 10% of cells) in 1 or more hematopoietic lineages; it can be accompanied by increased blasts but always < 20%

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/bonemarrowneoplasticCCUSGray03.jpeg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/bonemarrowneoplasticCCUSGray03.jpeg)

A 68 year old man with a history of mild anemia and neutropenia presents for evaluation. Bone marrow biopsy shows dysplasia in ~5% of erythroid precursors but no increase in blasts. Cytogenetic analysis reveals the presence of a *TET2* mutation with a variant allele frequency (VAF) of 20%. No other clinical or laboratory findings suggest an alternative explanation for the cytopenias. Which of the following diagnoses is most consistent with this clinical presentation?

1. Acute myeloid leukemia (AML)
2. Aplastic anemia
3. Clonal cytopenia of undetermined significance (CCUS)
4. Idiopathic cytopenia of undetermined significance (ICUS)
5. Myelodysplastic neoplasm (MDS)

Board review style answer #1

**C**. Clonal cytopenia of undetermined significance (CCUS). The presence of a *TET2* mutation with VAF of 20% and cytopenia without explanation fulfills the diagnostic criteria for CCUS. Answer E is incorrect because MDS requires evidence of significant dysplasia (≥ 10% in at least 1 lineage) or ≥ 5% blasts in the bone marrow. Answer D is incorrect because ICUS refers to unexplained cytopenias without clonal somatic mutation. Answer A is incorrect because AML requires ≥ 20% blasts in the bone marrow or peripheral blood or the presence of specific (diagnostic) genetic abnormalities, which are not present in this patient. Answer B is incorrect because aplastic anemia is characterized by hypocellular / acellular bone marrow with pancytopenia and no significant clonal mutations or dysplasia.

Board review style question #2

Patients diagnosed with clonal cytopenia of undetermined significance (CCUS) are at increased risk for progression to which of the following hematologic malignancies?

1. Chronic lymphocytic leukemia (CLL)
2. Essential thrombocythemia (ET)
3. Hodgkin lymphoma
4. Multiple myeloma
5. Myelodysplastic neoplasm (MDS)

Board review style answer #2

**E**. Myelodysplastic neoplasm (MDS). Clonal cytopenia of undetermined significance carries a higher risk of progression to MDS or AML. The presence of somatic mutations such as *TET2*, *ASXL1* or *DNMT3A* indicate clonal hematopoiesis, which predisposes to the development of overt hematology malignancies. Answer A is incorrect because CLL is a lymphoid malignancy and CCUS is associated with myeloid lineage abnormalities, not lymphoid malignancies. Answer D is incorrect because multiple myeloma is a plasma cell dyscrasia and unrelated to the clonal myeloid mutations seen in CCUS. Answer B is incorrect because ET involves clonal proliferation leading to thrombocytosis, whereas CCUS is defined by cytopenias. Answer C is incorrect because Hodgkin lymphoma is a B cell lymphoid malignancy with no connection to the clonal hematopoiesis of CCUS.
