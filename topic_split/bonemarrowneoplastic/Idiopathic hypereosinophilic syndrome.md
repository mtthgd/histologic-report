Definition / general

* Idiopathic hypereosinophilic syndrome is a disorder defined by peripheral blood eosinophilia (absolute eosinophil count ≥ 1.5 x 109/L) for at least 6 months with organ damage / dysfunction attributable to tissue hypereosinophilic infiltrate per biopsy and no discernible underlying etiology
* Diagnosis of exclusion

Essential features

* Hypereosinophilia (HE) is defined by an increase in eosinophils in the peripheral blood (≥ 1.5 x 109/L); however, if eosinophilia is sustained for ≥ 6 months and there is associated tissue damage, this should be classified as hypereosinophilic syndrome (HES)
* Clinically, if treatment is necessary to minimize tissue / organ damage, the criteria of 6 months might not be enforced and an eosinophil count ≥ 1.5 x 109/L on 2 occasions for ≥ 1 month apart may be sufficient ([Pathobiology 2019;86:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29961056))
* Idiopathic hypereosinophilia diagnostic criteria
  + Eosinophil count ≥ 1.5 x 109/L for ≥ 6 months
  + Tissue or organ damage associated with eosinophilic infiltration
    - Cardiac and cutaneous manifestations are common but liver, CNS, muscle, pulmonary and nasal sinus involvement may occur as well
  + No discernible underlying cause
    - Exclude reactive causes of eosinophilia (allergy, infection including parasites, drug related, connective tissue disorder, etc.)
    - Exclude acute myeloid leukemia, myeloproliferative neoplasms, myelodysplastic syndromes, myelodysplastic / myeloproliferative neoplasms, systemic mastocytosis and myeloid / lymphoid neoplasms with eosinophilia and *PDGFRA, PDGFRB, FGFR1* or *PCM1-JAK2* rearrangement
    - Exclude cytokine producing, immunophenotypically aberrant T cell population
  + No cytogenetic or molecular abnormalities, however, must exclude clonal hematopoiesis (CHIP) mutations

Terminology

* Idiopathic HES

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [D72.110](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/D50-D89/D70-D77/D72-/D72.110) - idiopathic hypereosinophilic syndrome (IHES)

Epidemiology

* Typically occurs in adults age 20 - 50 years ([Blood 1994;83:2759](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8180373), [Pediatr Hematol Oncol 2009;26:129](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19382034), [Br J Haematol 2010;151:440](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20955401))
* Estimated prevalence is 0.36 - 6.3 per 100,000 ([J Allergy Clin Immunol 2010;126:179](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20639012))
* Previously thought to have male predominance until cases with *FIP1L1-PDGFRA* fusion were excluded

Sites

* Skin and cardiac involvement common
* Minority of patients present with splenomegaly and lymphadenopathy
* Liver, CNS, muscle, pulmonary and nasal sinus involvement occur more commonly than in eosinophilic leukemia ([Haematologica 2014;99:e148](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24951465))

Etiology

* Cause is unknown

Clinical features

* Onset of symptoms is often insidious with eosinophilia being detected incidentally; however, some patients initially present with severe and life threatening problems due to the rapid progression of cardiovascular and neurologic complications
* Retrospective study of 188 patients with hypereosinophilia reported the frequency of symptoms at presentation ([J Allergy Clin Immunol 2009;124:1319](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19910029)):
  + Dermatologic symptoms were most common, followed by pulmonary, gastrointestinal, cardiac and lastly neurologic
  + 6% of patients presented initially with incidentally detected asymptomatic hypereosinophilia
* Dermatologic: angioedema, dermographism, eczema, erythroderma, pruritis, urticaria
* Pulmonary: cough, dyspnea, wheezing
* Gastrointestinal: weight loss, abdominal pain, vomiting, diarrhea, hepatitis, cholangitis, Budd-Chiari syndrome
* Cardiac: cardiac damage, valvular fibrosis, thromboembolism
* Neurologic: behavioral changes, confusion, ataxia, memory loss, peripheral neuropathy

Diagnosis

* Unexplained eosinophilia of ≥ 1.5 x 109/L for at least 6 months that leads to tissue damage
  + Exclude reactive causes of eosinophilia (parasites, drug related, allergy, etc.)
  + Exclude acute myeloid leukemia, myeloproliferative neoplasms, myelodysplastic syndromes, myelodysplastic / myeloproliferative neoplasms and systemic mastocytosis
  + Exclude cytokine producing, immunophenotypically aberrant T cell population
  + Tissue damage present due to hypereosinophilia
* Bone marrow with increased eosinophilic precursors; proposed criteria of ≥ 20% marrow cellularity with or without peripheral blood eosinophilia ([Pathobiology 2019;86:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29961056))
* Affected organs may have increased eosinophils with eosinophil degranulation ([Pathobiology 2019;86:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29961056))

Laboratory

* Serum IgE level is elevated in about half of patients
* Serum tryptase is occasionally elevated ([Blood 2003;101:4660](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12676775))
* Serum B12 and platelet counts are typically normal ([Haematologica 2014;99:e148](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24951465))

Prognostic factors

* Slowly progressive clinical course with death occurring due to cardiac damage in some cases

Case reports

* 26 year old man with ascites ([JRSM Open 2020;11:2054270419894826](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32002188))
* 36 year old man with erythroderma ([An Bras Dermatol 2018;93:451](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29924226))
* 43 year old man with pulmonary hypertension ([Pulm Circ 2019;9:2045894018793999](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30036150))
* 83 year old woman with dyspnea ([Am J Case Rep 2019;20:381](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30902963))

Treatment

* Patients are typically observed during the initial stages of the disease
* As HES progresses, initial treatment is often systemic glucocorticoid therapy with second line treatments including imatinib, interferon alfa and hydroxyurea
* Recent study showed that mepolizumab could be effective at treating flares in HES ([J Allergy Clin Immunol 2020;146:1397](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32956756))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Bone marrow
  + Normocellular or hypercellular marrow with an increase in eosinophils and eosinophilic precursors with orderly maturation
  + Bone marrow eosinophilia proposed criteria: ≥ 20% marrow cellularity with or without peripheral blood eosinophilia ([Pathobiology 2019;86:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29961056))
  + Normal differential counts
  + No increase in blasts (No neoplastic process
* Tissue / organ
  + Eosinophils are typically scattered or absent in normal tissue; affected organs may have increased eosinophils with eosinophil degranulation ([Pathobiology 2019;86:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29961056))
  + Possible eosinophilic microabscesses
  + Organs affected include gastrointestinal tract, lung, lymph nodes, spleen and thymus

Flow cytometry description

* Flow cytometry on blood, bone marrow or other tissue specimen may be important for excluding a potential associated lymphoid, myeloid or mast cell neoplasm
* No clonal myeloid or lymphoid cells
* If an aberrant T cell population is identified, lymphocytic variant of hypereosinophilic syndrome should be considered ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2016;140:1060](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27684977), [J Allergy Clin Immunol 2012;130:607](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22460074), [Immunol Allergy Clin North Am 2007;27:389](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17868856), [Medicine (Baltimore) 2014;93:255](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25398061))
* Generally, not useful when examining eosinophils

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* No cytogenetic or molecular genetic abnormalities identified by definition ([Jaffe: Hematopathology, 2nd Edition, 2016](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0323296130/pathologyoutl-20))
* Must exclude neoplasms with rearrangements of *BCR-ABL1, PDGFRA, PDGRB* or *FGFR1* or *PCM1-JAK2, ETV6-JAK2, BCR-JAK2* or *FLT3* fusions as well as activating *PDGFRA* gene mutation

Differential diagnosis

* [Acute myeloid leukemia with inv(16)(p13.1;q22) or t(16;16)(p13.1;q22) / *CBFB / MYH11*](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/leukemiainv16.html):
  + Immature eosinophils containing rough basoeosinophilic granules
  + Oligoblastic myelogenous leukemia or increased blasts, not necessarily ≥ 20%
  + FISH or cytogenetic evidence of inv(16) or t(16;16) / *CBFB-MYH11*
* [Atypical chronic myeloid leukemia (aCML), *BCR-ABL1* negative](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/myeloproliferativeatypicalCML.html):
  + Besides features of myeloproliferative neoplasms and eosinophilia
    - Leukocytosis, often > 25 x 109/µL
    - Dysplasia is present in > 10% of neutrophilic precursors
    - No absolute monocytosis
    - < 20% blasts
    - NGS study with frequent mutations involving *SETBP1*
* [Chronic myelomonocytic leukemia (CMML) with eosinophilia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/myeloproliferativecmml.html):
  + Normal or leukocytosis
  + Sustained monocytosis ≥ 1 x 109/L, > 10% of WBCs for > 6 months
  + < 20% blasts
  + Bone marrow morphologic findings: hyper or normocellular, myeloproliferative or myelodysplastic predominance with or without marrow monocytosis
  + Negative for t(5;12) or *PDGFRB* gene rearrangement
  + Negative for *BCR-ABL1* gene rearrangement
  + NGS study with frequent mutations involving *TET2, SRSF2, ASXL1* and *SETBP1*
* [Chronic eosinophilic leukemia, NOS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/myeloproliferativeCEL.html):
  + Persistent eosinophilia (≥ 1.5 x 109/L) in which reactive causes have been excluded
  + Does not meet diagnostic criteria for chronic myeloid leukemia with t(9;22)(q34.1;q11.2) / *BCR-ABL1* gene fusion, polycythemia vera, essential thrombocythemia, primary myelofibrosis, chronic neutrophilic leukemia, chronic myelomonocytic leukemia, atypical chronic myeloid leukemia (*BCR-ABL1* gene fusion negative) or systemic mastocytosis
  + Blasts
  + *PDGFRA, PDGFRB* or *FGFR1* rearrangements
  + t(8;9)(p22;p24.1) / *PCM1-JAK2*, t(9;12)(p24.1;p13.2) / *ETV6-JAK2* or t(9;22)(p24.1;q11.2) / *BCR-JAK2* fusions
  + inv(16)(p13.1;q22) or t(16;16)(p13.1;q22) / *CBFB / MYH11*, t(15;17)(q22;q11-12) / *PML-RARA* or t(8;21)(q22;q22.1) / *RUNX1 / RUNX1T1*
  + t(9;22)(q24;q31) / *BCR-ABL1* fusions
* Presence of clonal cytogenetic or molecular genetic abnormality(ies) or a blast count ≥ 2% in the peripheral blood or ≥ 5% in the bone marrow

- [Systemic mastocytosis with eosinophilia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonemarrowsystemicmastocytosis.html):
  * Clonal proliferation of atypical mast cells
  * Major diagnostic criteria: aggregates of ≥ 15 mast cells in the bone marrow or extracutaneous organ + 1 minor criterion or
  * Minor diagnostic criteria (≥ 3 of the following 4 present)
    + Morphology in BM or extracutaneous organ
      - Infiltrate: > 25% mast cells are spindled or have abnormal morphology or
      - BM aspirate: > 25% of mast cells are immature or atypical
    + Molecular: *KIT* D816V point mutation, negative for *FIP1L1-PDGFRA* gene rearrangement
    + Flow cytometry / immunohistochemistry: mast cells express aberrant [CD25](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20to29.html#cd25286854) +/- [CD2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1.html#cd2288039) in addition to normal mast cell markers
    + Laboratory findings: serum tryptase > 20 ng/ml persistently
  * Bone marrow: eosinophils are present or increased, which could be a secondary reaction or derived from a neoplastic clone
- **Idiopathic hypereosinophilia**:
  * When criteria 1 - 4 are met but there is no end organ damage
  * Eosinophil count ≥ 1.5 x 109/L for ≥ 6 months
  * Exclude reactive causes of eosinophilia
  * Exclude acute myeloid leukemia, myeloproliferative neoplasms, myelodysplastic syndromes, myelodysplastic / myeloproliferative neoplasms and systemic mastocytosis
  * Exclude cytokine producing, immunophenotypically aberrant T cell population
  * Subset of patients harbor certain gene mutations, such as *ASXL1* (43%), *TET2* (36%), *EZH2* (29%), *SETBP1* (22%), *CBL* (14%) and *NOTCH1* (14%) ([Mod Pathol 2016;29:854](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27174585))
  * Presence of clonal genetic aberration favors chronic eosinophilic leukemia, not otherwise specified
- **Lymphocytic variant of hypereosinophilic syndrome (L-HES)**:
  * Abnormal cytokine release by immunophenotypically abnormal or clonal T cells
  * Most commonly aberrant [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html)- / [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html)+ T cell population which can be detected by flow cytometry; absolute [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html)- / [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html)+ T cell count 0.01 - 28.3 k/L with median 0.35 k/L
    + *TCRγδ* rearrangements were frequently identified (76%) ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2014;93:255](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25398061))
    + High frequency of cutaneous manifestations, soft tissue involvement and lymphadenopathy ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2014;93:255](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25398061))
  * Other phenotypic changes, e.g. [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html)+ / [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html)+ / [CD7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1.html#cd7288045)- and [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html)+ / [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html)- / [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html)- [TCRαβ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstcralphabeta.html)+ have been reported ([Immunol Allergy Clin North Am 2007;27:389](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17868856), [N Engl J Med 1999;341:1112](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10511609))
  * Elevated serum IgE and polyclonal hypergammaglobulinemia produced by aberrant T cells via T helper 2 cytokines (i.e. IL5, IL4, IL13, GM-CSF) ([Br J Haematol 2000;109:540](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10886202))
  * 33% of cases have elevated absolute lymphocyte counts ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2014;93:255](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25398061))
  * T cell clone infected by EBV can also produce cytokines that stimulate eosinophil production ([Blood 2013;121:2364](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23520331))
  * Unknown molecular mechanism; however, *STAT3* gain of function mutation, upregulation of STAT3 pathways and *STAT5B* gain of function mutations have been described independently ([Blood 2016;127:948](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26702067), [Blood 2017;129:650](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27956386))
- **Medication related eosinophilia**:
  * Administration of cytokines IL2, IL3, IL5 or GMCSF
- **Myeloid neoplasms with rearrangement of *[PDGFRA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/myeloproliferativePDGFRA.html), [PDGFRB](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/myeloproliferativePDGFRB.html), [FGFR1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/myeloproliferativeFGFR1.html)*** or *[PCM1-JAK2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonemarrowneoplasticPCM1JAK2.html), **ETV6-JAK2*** or ***BCR-JAK2* fusion** ([Blood 2017;129:704](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28028030))
- **Paraneoplastic eosinophilia**:
  * [T cell lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBTcellgeneral.html), [Hodgkin lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBclassic.html), [systemic mastocytosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonemarrowsystemicmastocytosis.html), [acute lymphoblastic leukemia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodesALL.html), [myeloproliferative neoplasms](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonemarrowneoplasticMPNWHO.html) or certain solid tumors (e.g. lung cancer, [metastatic renal cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantrcc.html), etc.) associated with abnormal release of IL2, IL3, IL5 or GMCSF ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2013;137:259](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23368869), [Rinsho Ketsueki 1991;32:874](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1942528), [Acta Clin Belg 2011;66:293](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21938985), [Am J Hematol 2007;82:234](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17160990), [N Z Med J 1990;103:537](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2243642))
- **Reactive eosinophilia**:
  * **Parasitic or fungal infections, allergies, pulmonary disease ([Löffler syndrome](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungnontumorloeffler.html)), cyclical eosinophilia,** skin diseases ([angiolymphoid hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodesangiolymphoidhyperplasiawitheosinophili.html)), collagen vascular diseases such as [Churg-Strauss syndrome](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungnontumorallergicgran.html) and [Kimura disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodeskimurasdisease.html)
- **Myeloproliferative variants of HES (M-HES)**:
  * **HES with features of myeloproliferative disorder**:
    + Anemia, thrombocytopenia, circulating leukocyte precursors
    + Hepatosplenomegaly
    + Increased serum vitamin B12
    + Chromosomal abnormalities
      - Myeloid / lymphoid with following abnormalities
        * *FIP1L1-PDGFRA* fusion
        * *PDGFRB* rearrangement
        * *FGFR1* rearrangement
        * *PCM1-JAK2* fusion
        * *PDGFRA* fusion with other partner genes have been described ([Leukemia 2006;20:827](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16498388), [Br J Haematol 2007;138:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17555450), [Blood 2011;117:2935](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21224473))
      - *KIT* mutated systemic mastocytosis
    + Often refractory to glucocorticoid therapy but may respond to tyrosine kinase inhibitors depending on mutation profile
- **Familial HES**:
  * Autosomal dominant inheritance
  * Family history of eosinophilia with or without endomyocardial fibrosis
  * Aberrant gene was mapped to chromosome 5q21-33 in one affected family ([Am J Hum Genet 1998;63:1086](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9758611))
  * May present as single organ damage, e.g. [eosinophilic esophagitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophaguseosinophilic.html) and [eosinophilic fasciitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumoreosinophilicfasciitis.html) ([Ann Rheum Dis 1994;53:281](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8203961), [Clin Gastroenterol Hepatol 2008;6:621](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18434257), [Arthritis Rheum 1989;32:96](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2912468))
- **Organ restricted hypereosinophilic conditions**:
  * Disorder with single organ involvement together with eosinophilia ≥ 1.5 x 109/L or lower in the setting of clear single organ involvement
  * When eosinophilia is ≥ 1.5 x 109/L, can be difficult to distinguish from IHES
  * Can overlap with single organ restricted eosinophilic disorders, such as [eosinophilic gastrointestinal disorders](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallboweleosinophilic.html), [chronic eosinophilic pneumonia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungnontumorCEP.html) and [Wells syndrome](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorwellssyndrome.html)
- **Specific / defined syndromes associated with hypereosinophilia**:
  * Associated with immune dysregulation
    + [Autoimmune lymphoproliferative syndrome (ALPS)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodesALPS.html) ([Am J Hematol 2007;82:615](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17266055), [Blood Cells Mol Dis 1999;25:227](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10575548))
    + **CARD9 deficiency collagen vascular diseases** ([N Engl J Med 2013;369:1704](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24131138))
    + [HIV infection](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonemarrowhiv.html) ([Arch Dermatol 1994;130:119](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8285733), [Allergy 1997;52:110](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9062639))
    + **Hyperimmunoglobulin E syndromes**
    + [IgG4 related disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneyigg4related.html) ([Eur J Haematol 2017;98:378](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28005278))
    + [Inflammatory bowel disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colonindeterminate.html) (i.e [Crohn's](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelcrohns.html), [ulcerative colitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelUC.html)) ([Cardiology 2013;125:258](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23839293), [Postgrad Med J 1986;62:1101](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3658847))

  * Etiology of eosinophilia (≥ 1.5 x 109/L) is unknown but correlates with the severity of the disease
