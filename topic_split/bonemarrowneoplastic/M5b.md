Definition / general

* Acute monocytic leukemia (M5b)
* 3 - 6% of AML
* Affects all ages
* Mature monocytes or promonocytes predominate in peripheral blood ( Treatment may cause tumor lysis syndrome, DIC and falsely elevated platelet counts ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1999;123:1111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10539919))

Case reports

* 47 year old man with spontaneous remission after infection ([Int J Lab Hematol 2007;29:386](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17824921))
* 50 year old man with cutaneous disease ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2005;129:425](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15737049))
* 63 year old man with preceding aleukemic leukemia cutis ([Case Rep Oncol 2011;4:547](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22187541))
* 74 year old man with mycosis fungoides now with increased circulating immature mononuclear cells ([Univ Pittsburgh case #460](http://path.upmc.edu/cases/case460.html))
* 77 year old man with coexisting myeloma ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2003;127:1506](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14567751))
* With mononucleosis syndrome due to varicella zoster virus ([Eur J Haematol 2002;68:236](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12071940))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Leukemic cells are often promonocytes with less basophilic cytoplasm and more azurophilic granules than monoblasts
* Have folded or cerebriform nuclei with fine chromatin
* Erythrophagocytosis is common

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* 30% have cytogenetics abnormalities, including 11q23 in 12% (these cases should be classified as a recurrent genetic abnormality)
* FLT3 mutations in 30%
* t(8;16)(p11;p13) fuses MOZ gene at 8p11 with CBP gene at 16p13 and is associated with erythrophagocytosis and coagulopathy

Differential diagnosis

* [AML M4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/leukemiaM4.html)
* Malignant histiocytic disorders
* [Microgranular acute promyelocytic leukemia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/leukemiaAPL.html)
* [Myelodysplastic syndrome](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonemarrowneoplasticMDSWHO.html): to distinguish, count promonoblasts in M5 with monoblasts
