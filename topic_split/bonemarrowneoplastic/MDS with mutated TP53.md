Definition / general

* Myelodysplastic syndrome (MDS) with mutated *TP53* is a myelodysplastic syndrome characterized by ≥ 2 *TP53* mutations or a single mutation with evidence of additional *TP53* loss or loss of heterozygosity (LOH)
* Other MDS subtypes should not be diagnosed in patients with mutated *TP53* (ICC)

Essential features

* Myelodysplastic syndrome / neoplasm (MDS) with multihit or biallelic *TP53* mutation is associated with aggressive behavior with a rapid transformation to acute myeloid leukemia (AML)
* Poor prognosis, median overall survival of 0.7 years
* Genomic testing is essential to make the diagnosis
* No morphologic features specific to MDS with mutated *TP53*
* Reference: [Cancer Discov 2022;12:2954](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36458434)

Terminology

* MDS with mutated *TP53* (MDS-*TP53*) (ICC)
* MDS with biallelic *TP53* inactivation (MDS-biTP53) (WHO HAEM5)

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [D46.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D37-D48/D46-/D46.9) - myelodysplastic syndrome, unspecified

Epidemiology

* Median age: 69.7 years
* Increased frequency of *TP53* mutation in patients following cytotoxic chemotherapy or radiation therapy
* ~10% of all cytopenias
* Reference: [Blood Adv 2022;6:2847](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35073573)

Sites

* Bone marrow and peripheral blood

Pathophysiology

* Mutations affecting *TP53* function, a tumor suppressor gene that plays a critical role in cellular apoptosis ([Blood Rev 2023;60:101055](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36841672))

Etiology

* DNA damaging agents
  + Chemotherapy and radiation
* Hereditary
  + Li-Fraumeni syndrome (germline *TP53* mutations) ([Cold Spring Harb Perspect Med 2017;7:a026187](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28270529))

Clinical features

* Symptoms associated with cytopenias
  + Anemia
    - Fatigue, shortness of breath, pallor
  + Thrombocytopenia
    - Purpura, bleeding, easy bruising
  + Neutropenia
    - Infections
* Reference: [Blood 2023;142:2247](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37774372)

Diagnosis

* WHO HAEM5
  + Myelodysplastic neoplasms with defining genetic abnormalities
    - MDS with biallelic *TP53* inactivation
      * < 20% blasts in peripheral blood and bone marrow, usually complex cytogenetics, ≥ 2 *TP53* mutations or 1 mutation with *TP53* copy number loss or copy neutral loss of heterozygosity
* ICC
  + MDS cases with defining cytogenetic or molecular criteria along with blast percentage and cytopenias, no requirement for dysplasia
    - MDS with mutated *TP53*
      * 0 - 9% peripheral blood / bone marrow blasts, multihit *TP53* mutations or *TP53* mutation with variant allele frequency (VAF) > 10% and complex karyotype and often with loss of 17p
    - MDS / AML with mutated *TP53*
      * 10 - 19% bone marrow or peripheral blood blasts with any somatic *TP53* mutation with VAF ≥ 10%
* Reference: [Virchows Arch 2023;482:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36287260), [Ann Lab Med 2023;43:503](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37080752)

Laboratory

* ≥ 1 cytopenia
* Reticulocytopenia is commonly found in MDS

Prognostic factors

* *TP53* mutation is the strongest prognostic factor in MDS ([Cancer Discov 2022;12:2954](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36458434))
  + Associated with treatment resistant disease, rapid disease progression and low overall survival
  + 3 year transformation to AML rate: 40%

Case reports

* 51 year old man with dysphagia ([Oncol Lett 2020;20:266](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32989400))
* 62 year old woman with pancytopenia and dyspnea ([Mol Cytogenet 2022;15:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36474285))
* 70 year old woman presenting with palpitations ([Intern Med 2005;44:490](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15942101))

Treatment

* Stem cell transplant associated with increased overall survival (OS) of 20.6 months ([N Engl J Med 2017;376:536](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28177873))
  + Median relapse free survival of 14.5 months

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Multilineage dysplasia in most cases
* Increased erythroid precursors common
* Occasional bone marrow fibrosis
* Reference: [Blood 2023;142:2247](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37774372)

Peripheral smear description

* Red blood cells
  + Anisopoikilocytosis with macrocytosis of red blood cells
* White blood cells
  + Hyposegmentation, lack of lobation, nuclear clumping, cytoplasmic hypogranularity or agranularity
* Reference: [Blood 2023;142:2247](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37774372)

Positive stains

* Strong, 3+ staining with [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) IHC of > 2% is associated with *TP53* mutation ([Leuk Res 2016;41:21](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26651421))
  + Confirmation by molecular and cytogenetic testing
  + [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) good screening tool but not specific
  + Overexpression is never observed in wild type *TP53*
  + MDS with truncation types of mutations may have virtual absence of [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html)

Flow cytometry description

* Flow cytometry alone is not diagnostic
  + Decreased side scatter of granulocytes
  + Erythroid: increased coefficient of variation and decreased intensity of [CD71](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd71.html) or [CD36](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30to39.html#cd36)
  + Aberrant expression of [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html) or [CD7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd7.html) on progenitors, granulocytes or monocytes
  + Asynchrony of [CD15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd15.html) and [CD16](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10to19.html#cd16) on granulocytes
  + Altered expression of [CD13](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd13.html) in relation to [CD11b](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10to19.html#cd11b) or [CD16](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10to19.html#cd16)
* Reference: [Ann Hematol 2023;102:3015](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37535147)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Cytogenetics
  + Usually accompanied by complex karyotype
  + del(5q), 17p loss of heterozygosity, -7/del(7q), -18
  + Other mutations as well
* Molecular genetics
  + Often detected with next generation sequencing; VAF of ≥ 40% (WHO HAEM5) or 50% (ICC) often suggests loss of heterozygosity
  + *TP53* mutations, commonly in DNA binding domain, inactivating
  + Copy number status can be detected by FISH probe
* Reference: [Blood 2022;139:2347](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35108372)

Sample pathology report

* Bone marrow, left posterior iliac crest; core biopsy, clot section, aspirate smears and touch imprint:
  + Myelodysplastic neoplasm with low blasts
    - Hypercellular marrow with trilineage hematopoiesis showing multilineage dysplasia
    - Aspirate smear: blasts 3%, lymphocytes 11%
    - Cytogenetics: -7/del(7q)
    - Next generation sequencing: multiallelic *TP53* mutation, deletion 5q and 20q

Differential diagnosis

* [Myelodysplastic neoplasm / syndrome, other subtypes](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonemarrowneoplasticMDSgeneral.html):
  + Does not have *TP53* mutation or multihit loss of *TP53*
* **MDS / AML with mutated *TP53* (ICC)**:
  + Blasts 10 - 19% in bone marrow or peripheral blood

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/bonemarrowneoplasticMDSmutatedTP53Rehan01.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/bonemarrowneoplasticMDSmutatedTP53Rehan01.jpg)

A 71 year old man presenting with anemia and thrombocytopenia underwent a bone marrow biopsy (H&E image shown above) of his left posterior iliac crest. Which of the following mutations is associated with the worst prognosis?

1. 5q deletion
2. *JAK2* V617F
3. Multihit *TP53* inactivation
4. *SF3B1*

Board review style answer #1

**C.** Multihit *TP53* inactivation. Of the listed mutations, only *TP53* has a very poor prognosis. Myelodysplastic syndrome (MDS) with *TP53* mutation is treatment resistant and has a median survival of 0.7 months. Of the listed choices, it has the highest rate of transformation to acute myeloid leukemia (AML). Answer A is incorrect because MDS with 5q deletion without *TP53* abnormality has a longer overall survival and a lower risk of transformation to AML. These patients can have near normal levels of bone marrow cellularity. Answer B is incorrect because *JAK2* V617F mutation less commonly transforms to AML and has a therapeutic target in *JAK2*. Answer D is incorrect because *SF3B1* mutation is an independent predictor of favorable prognosis, has a 9 year average survival with isolated mutation and has a very low risk of leukemic evolution.

Board review style question #2

A 63 year old man presents with a history of myelodysplastic syndrome (MDS) with mutated *TP53*. Which of the following is a risk factor for his disease?

1. 30 pack year smoking history
2. Brother with 3 prior basal cell carcinomas and a prior meningioma
3. Deceased donor kidney transplant
4. Human immunodeficiency (HIV) infection

Board review style answer #2

**B.** Brother with 3 prior basal cell carcinomas and a prior meningioma. The patient's brother is described as having multiple soft tissue neoplasms, consistent with germline *p53* mutation. The patient's brother has Li-Fraumeni syndrome, an autosomal dominant mutation of the *p53* tumor suppressor gene. A mutation of this gene predisposes patients to a wide range of cancers, including hematological malignancies. Answer A is incorrect because tobacco use does not increase the risk of MDS with *TP53* mutation. Answer C is incorrect because immunosuppression does not increase the risk of MDS with *TP53* mutation. Answer D is incorrect because HIV or antiretroviral therapy does not increase the risk of MDS with *TP53* mutation.
