Definition / general

* An aggressive, high grade solid tumor composed of dysplastic mast cells

Essential features

* Solid tumor composed of atypical mast cells which express tryptase and CD117
* High grade morphology with an infiltrative or destructive growth pattern

Terminology

* Mast cell sarcoma

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9740/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/%0D%0A9789241548496_eng.pdf#page=97) - mast cell sarcoma
* ICD-11: [2A21.2](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#233404891) - mast cell sarcoma

Epidemiology

* Epidemiologic data is limited due to the rarity of the disease
* Slight male predominance with a M:F ratio of 9:8 ([Mod Pathol 2022;35:865](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35105959))
* In adults, the average age of diagnosis is 55 years with a range between 19 - 77 years ([Oncotarget 2016;7:66299](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27602777))

Pathophysiology

* Pathophysiology of mast cell sarcoma has not been well elucidated
* Nearly all cases of adult systemic mastocytosis have the *KIT* D816V mutation but this mutation is uncommon in mast cell sarcoma, which suggests that it is a molecularly distinct entity

Etiology

* Not known

Clinical features

* Can affect any organ and thus clinical symptoms may be different depending which site is involved ([Immunol Allergy Clin North Am 2014;34:423](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24745684))
* Symptoms related to mast cell activation may be present including urticaria, flushing, vomiting, diarrhea and diaphoresis ([Mod Pathol 2022;35:865](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35105959))
* Can occasionally present with concurrent mast cell leukemia ([Br J Haematol 2020;189:e160](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32242922))

Diagnosis

* Localized, high grade and rapidly expanding lesion composed of atypical mast cells which express CD117 and tryptase
* Does not fulfill the criteria for systemic mastocytosis
* Mast cells may express one or more aberrant markers including CD2, CD25 and CD30 ([Hemasphere 2021;5:e646](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34901755))

Laboratory

* Serum tryptase levels are commonly elevated, median 132 - 236 ng/L ([Oncotarget 2016;7:66299](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27602777), [Mod Pathol 2022;35:865](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35105959))
* Anemia may be present in roughly 50% of patients ([Mod Pathol 2022;35:865](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35105959))

Radiology description

* Localized lesion on CT and MRI which may be locally destructive ([J Clin Pathol 2007;60:424](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17405977), [Leuk Lymphoma 2017;58:1515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27808598))
* FDG avid lesions on FDG PET / CT ([Leuk Lymphoma 2017;58:1515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27808598), [Clin Nucl Med 2024;49:464](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38456450))

Prognostic factors

* There is often a minimal response to chemotherapy or radiotherapy, leading to a poor overall prognosis
* There is a median survival of 24 months ([Mod Pathol 2013;26:533](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23196796))
* *KIT* D816V mutations are resistant to treatment with some tyrosine kinase inhibitors and may have a worse prognosis compared to cases with other *KIT* mutations or those without detectable *KIT* mutations ([Int J Hematol 2011;94:491](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22020400), [Mod Pathol 2013;26:533](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23196796))

Case reports

* 8 month old boy with a large, polypoid soft tissue mass causing obstruction of the right external auditory canal ([J Pediatr Hematol Oncol 2013;35:315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23211696))
* 18 year old man with severe headaches and 5 month history of rash and flushing who was found to have a 1.5 cm soft tissue mass at the skull vertex ([Br J Haematol 2020;189:e160](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32242922))
* 23 year old woman with retroperitoneal mass initially diagnosed as a spermatocytic seminoma but later found to be mast cell sarcoma ([Clin Pathol 2022;15:2632010X221123539](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36119438))
* 34 year old woman with tonsillar mast cell sarcoma who underwent remission following allogeneic hematopoietic stem cell transplantation ([Front Oncol 2020;10:219](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32181155))
* 39 year old woman with uterine mast cell sarcoma who was successfully treated with imatinib ([Int J Hematol 2011;94:491](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22020400))
* 53 year old woman with mast cell sarcoma of the thoracic spine vertebrae ([Clin Neuropathol 2018;37:28](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28992848))
* 59 year old woman with upper abdominal discomfort, weight loss and vomiting found to have mast cell sarcoma involving the jejunum ([Gastrointest Tumors 2023;10:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36742415))
* 60 year old man with an expansile osseous lesion in the intertrochanteric region extending to the hip ([Am J Hematol 2022;97:1381](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35266568))
* 61 year old man with mast cell sarcoma involving the mandible ([Am J Hematol 2022;97:1381](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35266568))

Treatment

* For localized lesions, surgical excision may be performed ([Mod Pathol 2013;26:533](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23196796))
* Response to localized radiotherapy and conventional chemotherapy is typically poor ([Mod Pathol 2022;35:865](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35105959))
* *KIT* tyrosine kinase inhibitors such as imatinib may elicit complete or partial response for patients that lack the *KIT* D816V mutation (22020400, [Mod Pathol 2013;26:533](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23196796), [Oncotarget 2016;7:66299](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27602777))
* Hematopoietic stem cell transplantation may lead to remission in some patients although data is currently limited ([Front Oncol 2020;10:219](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32181155))
* Treatment often involves a combination of multiple different treatment options but overall survival is still poor

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Pleomorphic medium to large sized cells with lightly eosinophilic cytoplasm, oval nuclei, moderately coarse to fine chromatin and occasional small nucleoli ([Mod Pathol 1999;12:739](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10430280), [Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1013](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12826896))
* Cells are often immature appearing and demonstrate an infiltrative growth pattern ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1013](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12826896))
* A prominent background infiltrate of eosinophils is often present ([Mod Pathol 2013;26:533](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23196796))
* Multilobated nuclei or multinucleated giant cells may be appreciated ([J Clin Pathol 1986;39:596](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3088063))

Cytology description

* Mast cells can appear immature with a blast like morphology and marked cellular atypia
* Nuclei may be multilobate and have a prominent nucleolus
* May have basophilic granules or appear hypogranular ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1013](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12826896))
* Cytologic features are not specific for mast cell sarcoma and may show overlap with other mast cell disorders ([Oncotarget 2016;7:66299](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27602777))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Molecular and cytogenetic aberrations of mast cell sarcoma have not been well described
* Compared to systemic mastocytosis, *KIT* D816V mutations are uncommon and identified in less than 10% of cases
* Other *KIT* mutations may be present in roughly 21% of cases ([J Clin Pathol 1986;39:596](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3088063))

Sample pathology report

* Soft tissue, retroperitoneum, excision:
  + Mast cell sarcoma (see comment)
  + Comment: The lesion consists of sheets of pleomorphic cells which can be appreciated invading through the surrounding soft tissue. The pleomorphic cells are medium to large in size with abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm, round to oval nuclei and occasional nucleoli. Mitotic figures are appreciated at a rate of 2 per 10 high power fields. Immunohistochemical staining is performed for CD1a, CD2, CD3, CD4, CD20, CD25, CD30, CD34, CD43, CD45, CD68, CD117, CD163, MPO, tryptase and S100. The neoplastic cells are positive for CD25, CD43, CD45, CD68, CD117 and tryptase. There is weak partial positivity for CD30. The neoplastic cells are negative for CD1a, CD2, CD3, CD4, CD20, CD34, CD163, MPO and S100. Overall, these findings are consistent with a diagnosis of mast cell sarcoma. Correlation with pending molecular studies and clinical findings is recommended.

Differential diagnosis

* [Anaplastic large cell lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonbanaplastic.html) ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2011;28:190](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21850985)):
  + Hallmark cells (large cells with horseshoe shaped nuclei) are often present
  + Diffuse and strong [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html)+
  + [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)-, [tryptase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmastcelltryptase.html)-
* [Blastic plasmacytoid dendritic cell neoplasm](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonemarrowneoplasticBPDCN.html) ([Cytopathology 2022;33:522](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35119790), [Blood Res 2023;58:90](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37105563)):
  + Monomorphic cells with blastic features
  + [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html)+, [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html)+, [CD123](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd123.html)+
  + [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html) variable, [tryptase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmastcelltryptase.html)-
* **Extracutaneous mastocytoma** ([Clin Endosc 2022;55:810](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34233110), [Arch Intern Med 1966;118:358](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4162687)):
  + Mature mast cells which have minimal cytologic atypic
  + Lacks an invasive or destructive growth pattern
* [Histiocytic sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodeshistiocyticsarcoma.html) ([Histopathology 2014;65:261](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24720374)):
  + [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html)+, [CD163](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd163.html)+
  + [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)-, [tryptase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmastcelltryptase.html)-
  + [BRAF V600E](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbraf.html) present in 60% of cases
* [Langerhans cell histiocytosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mandiblemaxillaLCH.html) ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2015;139:1211](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26414464)):
  + Medium to large cells with abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm and prominent nuclear folds and grooves
  + [CD1a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1a.html)+, [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html)+, [CD207](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd200.html#cd207270862)+, [langerin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd200.html#cd207270862)+
  + [BRAF V600E](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbraf.html) in 25 - 60% of cases
* [Myeloid sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/leukemiagranulocytic.html) ([Diagn Pathol 2007;2:42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17974004)):
  + Monomorphic cells with blastic features
  + [CD33](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd33.html)+, [CD43](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd43.html)+, [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html)+, [lysozyme](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainslysozyme.html)+
  + [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html) variable, [MPO](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyeloperoxidase.html) variable, [tryptase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmastcelltryptase.html)-
* [Systemic mastocytosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonemarrowsystemicmastocytosis.html) ([Cancers 2023;15:5626](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38067330)):
  + Multifocal aggregates of mast cells, frequently involving the bone marrow
  + *KIT*  D816V mutations are present in > 90% of cases
  + Mast cells may show spindled or immature morphology but are not typically as pleomorphic as those seen in mast cell sarcoma

Board review style question #1

[![Mast cell sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/CHAPTOPICAUTH00.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/CHAPTOPICAUTH00.jpg)

A 50 year old male presented for progressive right leg pain. A MRI scan revealed an expansive osseous lesion in the right femur. The above image demonstrates a section of the lesion on H&E. The cells of interest stain positive for CD2, CD4, CD43, CD68, CD117 and tryptase. They are negative for CD1a, CD3, CD20, CD25, CD30, CD34, CD56, CD163, lysozyme and MPO. What is the most likely diagnosis?

1. Anaplastic large cell lymphoma
2. Blastic plasmacytoid dendritic cell neoplasm
3. Extracutaneous mastocytoma
4. Histiocytic sarcoma
5. Mast cell sarcoma
6. Myeloid sarcoma

Board review style answer #1

**E**. Mast cell sarcoma. The prompt describes an expansive osseous lesion involving the right femur.
Answer A is incorrect because the lesion is negative for CD30, ruling out anaplastic large cell lymphoma.
Answer B is incorrect. It is CD4+, which can be seen in blastic plasmacytoid dendritic cell neoplasms; however, blastic plasmacytoid dendritic cell neoplasms typically appear monomorphic histologically and would not express tryptase.
Answer D is incorrect because histiocytic sarcoma should be CD163+.
Answer F is incorrect because myeloid sarcoma can have a similar immunophenotype to mast cell sarcoma but is typically lysozyme- and is CD117-.
Answer C is incorrect because extracutaneous mastocytoma will have the same immunophenotype as mast cell sarcoma but it will not display the level of pleomorphism and cellular atypia which is seen in the above image.

Board review style question #2

Which of the following helps to differentiate mast cell sarcoma from an extracutaneous mastocytoma?

1. Detection of the *KIT* D816V mutation
2. Elevated serum tryptase
3. Expression of CD2 or CD25
4. Pleomorphic cells with a locally invasive growth pattern
5. Prior history of radiation

Board review style answer #2

**D**. Mast cell sarcoma and extracutaneous mastocytoma can have an identical immunophenotype. The most helpful way to differentiate them is the histologic features. Mast cell sarcoma is typically pleomorphic with an invasive growth pattern. Extracutaneous mastocytoma has minimal cytologic atypia and will not display invasive or destructive growth. The *KIT* D816V mutation is not specific for either entity. Either lesion can have aberrant expression of CD2 or CD25.
