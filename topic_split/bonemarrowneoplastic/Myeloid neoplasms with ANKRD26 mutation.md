Definition / general

* *ANKRD26* (ankyrin repeat domain containing 26) is a protein coding gene located on chromosome 10p12.1 that is responsible for normal differentiation of megakaryocytes ([Platelets 2022;33:1107](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35587581))
* Recent findings have shown that *ANKRD26* mutation is not only associated with *ANKRD26* related thrombocytopenia (*ANKRD26* RT) but also associated with increased predisposition to myeloid neoplasms (acute myeloid leukemia [AML], myelodysplastic syndrome [MDS] and chronic myeloid leukemia [CML]) ([Curr Hematol Malig Rep 2022;17:105](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35751752))

Essential features

* Myeloid neoplasms with *ANKRD26* mutation were first described in 2011 ([Blood 2011;117:6673](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21467542))
* Cases of myeloid neoplasms associated with *ANKRD26* mutation show dysmegakaryopoiesis characterized by increased number of small hypolobated megakaryocytes ([Pathobiology 2019;86:53](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30048985))
* 12 variants of *ANKRD26* mutation have been described and the majority of the mutations have been missense mutations ([Pathobiology 2019;86:53](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30048985))
* Morphologically, platelets are normal in size and mean platelet volume is within normal limit ([Platelets 2022;33:1107](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35587581))
* Prognosis of myeloid neoplasm with *ANKRD26* mutation is currently unknown ([Pathobiology 2019;86:53](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30048985))

ICD coding

* Currently unknown

Sites

* Currently, no specific parts of the body or organs have been reported in the literature

Pathophysiology

* *ANKRD26* encodes proteins containing N terminal ankyrin repeats that have been shown to regulate proteins involving mitogen activated protein kinase (MAPK) signaling pathways during megakaryocytic differentiation and platelet formation ([J Hematol Oncol 2017;10:18](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28100250))
* Mutations in the 5' untranslated region (UTR) of *ANKRD26* have the autosomal dominant form of thrombocytopenia (*ANKRD26* RT)
* *ANKRD26* RT is a form of inherited thrombocytopenia with a predisposition to myeloid neoplasms such as acute myeloid leukemia, myelodysplastic syndrome and chronic myeloid leukemia ([Curr Hematol Malig Rep 2022;17:105](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35751752))

Etiology

* No associated etiology has been reported in association with myeloid neoplasms with germline *ANKRD26* mutation

Clinical features

* Isolated thrombocytopenia has been listed as one of the clinical manifestations in patients with germline *ANKRD26* mutation and can manifest as petechiae, ecchymosis, gum bleeding, epistaxis and menorrhagia ([Blood 2011;117:6673](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21467542))
* *ANKRD26* related thrombocytopenia (or thrombocytopenia 2) is an inherited thrombocytopenia that is clinically associated with mild bleeding diathesis ([Platelets 2022;33:1107](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35587581))
* Subset of patients with *ANKRD26* mutation do not show any salient clinical symptoms until after being diagnosed with acute myeloid leukemia or myelodysplastic syndrome ([GeneReviews: *ANKRD26* Related Thrombocytopenia [Accessed 20 August 2024]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29927566))

Diagnosis

* Lifelong mild to moderate thrombocytopenia (< 150 x 109/L, confirmed with repeat examination)
* Normal platelet size (mean platelet volume [fL] per reference interval of automated instrument)
* Absent or minimal bleeding tendency
* Family history of thrombocytopenia with an autosomal dominant pattern of inheritance
* Personal or family history of myeloid neoplasms at a young age
* Previous or suspected diagnosis of immune thrombocytopenia (ITP) without improvement on immunosuppressive treatment
* Absence of features suggesting syndromic association
* Diagnosis of myeloid neoplasms with *ANKRD26* mutation is usually found through bone marrow biopsy and molecular genetic testing, such as next generation sequencing
* Reference: [GeneReviews: *ANKRD26* Related Thrombocytopenia [Accessed 20 August 2024]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29927566)

Laboratory

* Molecular genetic testing can be used to establish the diagnosis: single gene testing or multigene panel ([GeneReviews: *ANKRD26* Related Thrombocytopenia [Accessed 20 August 2024]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29927566))

Case reports

* 20 year old woman with Diamond-Blackfan anemia who developed acute myeloid leukemia with heterozygous c.5106 G>P p. (Q1702H)(CAG>CAC) in exon 34 of the *ANKRD26* gene ([Cureus 2023;15:e36152](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37065357))
* 51 year old man and 75 year old woman with inherited *ANKRD26* RT and *BCR::ABL* positive chronic myeloid leukemia ([JCO Precis Oncol 2021;5:PO.20.00318](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34250402))
* 61 and 65 year old men found to have *ANKRD26* related inherited thrombocytopenia (THC2) after initial misdiagnosis as MDS ([J Thromb Haemost 2017;15:2388](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28976612))
* 64 year old man with a lifelong history of thrombocytopenia and multiple myeloma with *ANKRD26* related thrombocytopenia ([Case Rep Hematol 2019;2019:9357572](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31281687))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Varying degree of thrombocytopenia is a common finding associated with this entity
* In addition to thrombocytopenia, additional cytopenia or cytoses in erythroid and myeloid series have been reported
* Shows dysmegakaryopoietic changes, including micromegakaryocytes and small, hypolobated forms
* Although dysmegakaryopoiesis is a common bone marrow feature associated with this entity, multilineage dysplasia has been reported
* Reference: [Blood 2013;122:1987](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24030261)

Peripheral smear description

* In patients with *ANKRD26* mutations, peripheral blood smear findings may reveal several nonspecific features including
  + Macrocytosis, where red blood cells appear larger than normal, indicating potential issues with DNA synthesis or repair
  + Anisocytosis, the presence of red blood cells of unequal sizes and poikilocytosis
    - Presence of irregularly shaped red blood cells can also be observed, suggesting abnormal erythropoiesis
  + Additionally, thrombocytopenia, a lower than normal platelet count, may be evident on the smear
  + Neutrophil hypersegmentation, with neutrophils showing more nuclear lobes than usual, might also be present, reflecting impaired DNA synthesis
* Findings collectively point to the possibility of an inherited bone marrow failure syndrome associated with *ANKRD26* mutations; however, definitive diagnosis requires a thorough evaluation, including genetic testing and clinical correlation
* References: [Blood 2011;117:6673](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21467542), [GeneReviews: *ANKRD26* Related Thrombocytopenia [Accessed 15 October 2024]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29927566)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* *ANKRD26* gene is located in chromosome 10p12.1 and encodes the protein known as ankyrin repeat domain containing protein 26 ([GeneReviews: *ANKRD26* Related Thrombocytopenia [Accessed 20 August 2024]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29927566))
* Majority of the reported pathogenic variants of *ANKRD26* gene are single nucleotide substitution and small deletions within the 5' untranslated region
* N terminal truncated *ANKRD26* isoforms have been shown to activate MAPK-ERK signaling pathway ([J Hematol Oncol 2017;10:18](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28100250))

Germline predispositions in myelodysplastic syndrome (MDS) and acute myeloid leukemia (AML)

Sample pathology report

* Bone marrow, aspirate smears, touch imprints, core biopsy and clot section:
  + Normocellular marrow for age (30% cellularity) with maturing trilineage hematopoiesis and no increase in blasts (see comment)
  + Reticulin stain shows no increase in reticulin fibrosis (MF 0/3)
  + Iron stores are present and there is no significant increase in ring sideroblasts
  + Comment: The bone marrow aspirate smear and core biopsy demonstrate a normocellular marrow with trilineage hematopoiesis, adequate megakaryocytes and no significant dysplasia, blasts or fibrosis. Molecular analysis confirms a germline *ANKRD26* mutation, consistent with familial platelet disorder with predisposition to acute myeloid leukemia (FPD / AML), a hereditary syndrome associated with thrombocytopenia and an increased risk of MDS and AML. Given the germline nature of this mutation, genetic counseling and close hematologic monitoring are recommended.

Differential diagnosis

* [Immune thrombocytopenia (ITP)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/spleenITP.html):
  + Cases can be difficult when the patients have no obvious family history of thrombocytopenia
* [Myelodysplastic syndrome (MDS)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonemarrowneoplasticMDSgeneral.html):
  + Thrombocytopenia
  + Dysmegakaryopoiesis
* [*RUNX1* related thrombocytopenia (*RUNX1* RT)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonemarrowneoplasticrunx1mutation.html):
  + 30 - 40% lifetime risk of developing MDS / AML ([Front Cell Dev Biol 2017;5:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29326930))
* [*ETV6* related thrombocytopenia (*ETV6* RT)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonemarrowneoplasticETV6mutation.html):
  + 20% lifetime risk of developing B cell acute lymphoblastic leukemia (BALL) ([Blood 2019;134:663](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31248877))
  + Overall 30% lifetime risk of developing hematologic malignancy ([Blood 2019;134:663](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31248877))
* ***CYCS* related thrombocytopenia (*CYCY* RT)**

Board review style question #1

A 45 year old man presents with easy bruising, fatigue and recurrent infections. Laboratory investigations reveal mild anemia, thrombocytopenia and leukocytosis with dysplastic changes in the granulocytic lineage. Bone marrow biopsy demonstrates hypercellularity with increased megakaryocytes and dysplastic changes in multiple cell lineages. Molecular analysis reveals a germline mutation in the *ANKRD26* gene. Which of the following conditions is most likely associated with this mutation?

1. Acute lymphoblastic leukemia (ALL)
2. Chronic myeloid leukemia (CML)
3. Essential thrombocythemia (ET)
4. Myelodysplastic neoplasm (MDS) with increased blasts
5. Myeloid neoplasms with germline *ANKRD26* mutation

Board review style answer #1

**E**. Myeloid neoplasms with germline *ANKRD26* mutation. This condition, also known as familial platelet disorder with propensity to myeloid malignancy, involves thrombocytopenia, dysplastic hematopoiesis and a high risk of developing myeloid malignancies like acute myeloid leukemia (AML) or MDS. Genetic testing and family screening are critical for diagnosis and management.

Answer A is incorrect because ALL is a lymphoid malignancy arising from precursor B or T lymphoblasts, not a myeloid neoplasm. The patient has dysplastic changes in granulocytes and megakaryocytes, features not associated with ALL. Germline *ANKRD26* mutations are linked to myeloid malignancies, not lymphoid disorders like ALL. Answer B is incorrect because CML is a myeloproliferative neoplasm driven by the *BCR::ABL1* fusion gene (Philadelphia chromosome), leading to leukocytosis with marked granulocytic proliferation and basophilia. The patient's findings (thrombocytopenia, dysplastic changes and germline *ANKRD26* mutation) are inconsistent with CML. Germline *ANKRD26* mutations are unrelated to CML. Answer C is incorrect because ET is characterized by sustained thrombocytosis and increased megakaryocytes without dysplasia. This patient has thrombocytopenia and dysplastic hematopoiesis, which are not features of ET. ET is typically associated with somatic mutations in *JAK2*, *CALR* or *MPL*, not germline *ANKRD26* mutations. Answer D is incorrect because while the patient has dysplastic changes, germline *ANKRD26* mutations indicate a hereditary predisposition rather than acquired MDS. MDS with excess blasts specifically involves 5 - 19% blasts in the bone marrow or peripheral blood, which is not described in this case. The thrombocytopenia and germline mutation are better explained by familial platelet disorder with associated myeloid malignancy (FPD MM).
