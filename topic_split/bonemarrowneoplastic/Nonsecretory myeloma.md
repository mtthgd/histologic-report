Definition / general

* Type of multiple myeloma in which there is no detectable evidence of monoclonal protein secretion by neoplastic plasma cells

Essential features

* Nonsecretory myeloma is characterized by bone marrow plasma cell proliferation that is not associated with monoclonal protein secretion ([Mediterr J Hematol Infect Dis 2017;9:e2017053](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28894562))
* Normal serum and urine protein electrophoresis and normal serum free light chain level are essential
* Prognosis and response to treatment in nonsecretory myeloma are better than those of secretory myeloma ([Eur J Haematol 2015;95:57](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25382589))
* Follow up of nonsecretory myeloma patients is challenging since there is no monoclonal protein production and CRAB features are usually mild or absent ([Mediterr J Hematol Infect Dis 2017;9:e2017053](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28894562))

Terminology

* True nonsecretory multiple myeloma: neoplastic cells produce immunoglobulins but are unable to secrete them
* False nonsecretory multiple myeloma: neoplastic cells produce immunoglobulins but they are not detectable in blood or urine
* Oligosecretory multiple myeloma: neoplastic cells produce small amounts of immunoglobulins
* Nonproducer multiple myeloma: neoplastic cells are unable to synthesize any immunoglobulins

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9732/3](https://seer.cancer.gov/seertools/hemelymph/51f6cf5ae3e27c3994bd54aa/) - nonsecretory myeloma
* ICD-11: [2A83.1](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#526287100) - plasma cell myeloma

Epidemiology

* Mean age: 60 years ([BMC Cancer 2023;23:930](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37784037))
* Affects men more than women
* More common in African American patients

Sites

* Bone marrow

Pathophysiology

* Clonal proliferation of neoplastic plasma cells involving > 10% of bone marrow displacing precursor elements and leading to lytic bone lesions and cytopenias
* IL6 overexpression with subsequent stimulation of osteoclastic activities manifested by lytic bone lesions ([Immunol Res 2014;59:188](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24845460))
* Nonsecretory myeloma can be either a nonproducer type where the cytoplasm of plasma cells is devoid of immunoglobulins or a true nonsecretor type where the cytoplasm contains immunoglobulins but they are not extruded extracellularly ([Cureus 2024;16:e54479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38510859))

Etiology

* Genetic susceptibility
* Chromosomal hyperdiploidy and translocations involving *IGH* locus 14q32.33 leading to overexpression of *CCND1*, *CCND2*, *CCND3*, *NSD2*, *MAF*, *MAFA* and *MAFB* are considered primary events

Clinical features

* Asymptomatic in up to 40% of patients ([Cureus 2024;16:e54479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38510859))
* Bone pain due to lytic bone lesions
* Anemia, hypercalcemia and renal impairment are milder and less frequent than those of secretory myeloma patients ([Cureus 2024;16:e54479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38510859))
* No monoclonal proteins detected by serum and urine protein electrophoresis

Diagnosis

* Demonstration of bone marrow involvement by clonal plasma cell proliferation using flow cytometry or immunohistochemistry
* Presence of CRAB features (hypercalcemia, renal impairment, anemia, bone lesions) in some cases
* Normal serum and urine protein electrophoresis
* Normal immunofixation
* Normal serum free light chain level (considered oligosecretory if increased) ([Mediterr J Hematol Infect Dis 2017;9:e2017053](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28894562))

Laboratory

* Calcium > 11 mg/dL
* Creatinine > 2 mg/dL
* Hemoglobin Normal serum protein electrophoresis (SPEP) and urine protein electrophoresis (UPEP)
* Normal immunofixation ([Mediterr J Hematol Infect Dis 2017;9:e2017053](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28894562))
* Normal serum free light chain level ([Mediterr J Hematol Infect Dis 2017;9:e2017053](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28894562))

Radiology description

* Lytic bone lesions with or without pathological fracture demonstrated by computed tomography (CT), magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) or Xray ([Cureus 2022;14:e29571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36312670))

Prognostic factors

* Higher overall survival and complete response to treatment compared to secretory myeloma ([Eur J Haematol 2015;95:57](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25382589), [BMC Cancer 2023;23:930](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37784037))

Case reports

* 60 year old man with gradually worsening left sided chest pain ([J Hematol 2024;13:94](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38993738))
* 67 year old woman with malaise and fever ([Clin Case Rep 2022;10:e05628](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35707676))
* 68 year old woman with shoulder pain ([Cureus 2022;14:e29571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36312670))
* 71 year old man with low back pain ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2024;103:e36951](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38306571))

Treatment

* Quadruplet therapy: daratumumab (anti-CD38 monoclonal antibody), bortezomib (proteasome inhibitor), lenalidomide (immunomodulatory drug) and dexamethasone
* CAR T cell therapy
* Bone marrow transplant ([Mediterr J Hematol Infect Dis 2017;9:e2017053](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28894562))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Mature plasma cells with abundant basophilic cytoplasm, perinuclear hofs (prominent Golgi apparatus), eccentric nuclei and clock face chromatin without nucleoli
* Immature plasma cells with dispersed nuclear chromatin, high N:C ratio and a prominent nucleolus ([J Chin Med Assoc 2011;74:464](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22036139))
* In trephine biopsy, plasma cells may form interstitial clusters, focal nodules or diffuse sheets

Cytology description

* Mature plasma cells with abundant basophilic cytoplasm, perinuclear hofs, eccentric nuclei and clock face chromatin without nucleoli
* Immature plasma cells with dispersed nuclear chromatin, high N:C ratio and a prominent nucleoli ([J Chin Med Assoc 2011;74:464](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22036139))
* Grape-like cytoplasmic inclusions due to immunoglobulin crystallization (Mott cells)
* Hyaline intracytoplasmic inclusions (Russell bodies)
* Vacuolated eosinophilic cytoplasm with granular texture (flame cells) due to IgA accumulation
* Pale cytoplasm due to collections of immunoglobulin protein that overlie or invaginate into the nucleus of plasma cells (Dutcher body), especially in oligosecretory myeloma ([Blood Res 2016;51:63](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27104195))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

Sample pathology report

* Bone marrow, left Iliac bone, biopsy and aspirate:
  + Plasma cell myeloma (see comment)
  + Hypercellular marrow with 90% plasma cells
  + Trilineage hematopoiesis is markedly reduced.
  + There is moderate patchy increase in reticulin fibers.
  + Comment: The patient's clinical presentation with bone pain and the imaging finding of lytic bone lesions are noted. The bone marrow is hypercellular with sheets of atypical plasma cells involving 90% of the intertrabecular space. Concurrent flow cytometry demonstrated a CD38+, CD138+, CD19- plasma cell population. The negative SPEP and UPEP results, along with normal serum free light chain level, support the nonsecretory nature of the process.

Differential diagnosis

* **Secretory type multiple myeloma**:
  + Monoclonal protein detection by SPEP, UPEP or monotypic serum free light chain elevation (oligosecretory type)
* **Mature B cell lymphoma with plasmacytic differentiation**:
  + Clonal population that is [CD19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd19.html)+, [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html)-
  + *MYD88* L265P mutation in lymphoplasmacytic lymphoma ([Mod Pathol 2015;28:564](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25216226))
* [Plasmablastic lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaplasmablastic.html):
  + Usually presents with oral mass
  + Lack of myeloma defining features
  + [EBER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) is more frequently positive in plasmablastic lymphoma but not entirely specific ([Front Immunol 2024;15:1354604](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38415257))
* [Monoclonal gammopathy of undetermined significance](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaMGUS.html):
  + Involvement of Lack of myeloma defining features
* [Solitary plasmacytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaplasmacytoma.html):
  + Single bone lesion with lack of CRAB features
* **Metastatic carcinoma with plasmacytoid features:**
  + Clinical history of invasive lobular carcinoma of breast or high grade urothelial carcinoma, although some cases can first present with metastasis
  + Positive epithelial markers ([AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [CAM5.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscam52.html), [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html))
  + Positive [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgata3.html)
  + [CD138](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd138.html) should not be used as it can be positive in a high percentage of carcinomas ([Dis Markers 2019;2019:4928315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31976021))

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/lymphomanonsecretorymyelomaAli01.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/lymphomanonsecretorymyelomaAli01.jpg)

The image above depicts a bone marrow biopsy from a 60 year old patient with a compression fracture. Which of the following supports the diagnosis of nonsecretory myeloma over secretory type myeloma?

1. High serum free light chain level
2. Involvement of bone marrow by CD38+ atypical plasma cells
3. Monoclonal protein detected by immunofixation
4. Multiple lytic bone lesions
5. Normal serum and urine protein electrophoresis (SPEP / UPEP) with normal serum free light chain level

Board review style answer #1

**E**. Normal serum and urine protein electrophoresis (SPEP / UPEP) with normal serum free light chain level in the presence of neoplastic plasma cells support the diagnosis of nonsecretory myeloma.
Answer C is incorrect because monoclonal protein detected by immunofixation is a feature of secretory type myeloma.
Answer D is incorrect because multiple lytic bone lesions can be observed in both nonsecretory myeloma and secretory type myeloma.
Answer B is incorrect because the involvement of bone marrow by CD38+ atypical plasma cells is an essential diagnostic feature of both nonsecretory myeloma and secretory type myeloma.
Answer A is incorrect because high serum free light chain level is seen in oligosecretory myeloma.
