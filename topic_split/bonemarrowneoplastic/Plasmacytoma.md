Definition / general

* Solitary plasma cell neoplasm without the presence of multiple myeloma (MM) or features of end organ damage (CRAB: hypercalcemia, renal insufficiency, anemia and bone lesion)

Essential features

* 2 types: solitary plasmacytoma of bone (SPB) and extramedullary plasmacytoma (EMP)
* EMP commonly involves upper respiratory tract (nasal cavity, paranasal sinuses and nasopharynx)
* Positive stains include CD38, CD138 and MUM1
* Expression of cyclin D1 and CD56 in EMP is noted in older individuals
* Patients with SPB with minimal bone marrow involvement (Plasmablastic morphology has been noted to have aggressive behavior ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2015;19:117](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25842207))

ICD coding

* ICD-11: [2A83.2 & XH4BL1](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#1811140613%20%26%202114952181) - solitary plasmacytoma & plasmacytoma, NOS

Epidemiology

* Occurs in older individuals (median age of 55 - 65 years) ([ScientificWorldJournal 2012;2012:895765](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22654647), [BMC Cancer 2017;17:13](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28056880))
* M > F ([Cancer Med 2021;10:386](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33270992), [ScientificWorldJournal 2012;2012:895765](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22654647), [BMC Cancer 2017;17:13](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28056880))
* SPB accounts for ~4 - 5% and EMP comprises 1 - 3% of plasma cell neoplasms ([J Hematol Oncol 2018;11:10](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29338789), [Eur J Haematol 2017;99:216](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28544116))
* Special form of EMP with IgA expression presents in lymph nodes of the head and neck region and occurs in younger patients with various forms of immune dysregulation

Sites

* SPB involves spine, pelvis, ribs, skull and long bones ([Am J Hematol 2012;87:647](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22549792), [Ann Hematol 2012;91:1785](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22752147), [Cancer Med 2021;10:462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33145987))
* EMP most commonly affects upper respiratory tract and less commonly lungs, gastrointestinal tract, lymph nodes, skin and genitourinary tract ([Cancer 1999;85:2305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10357398), [J Pathol 2005;205:92](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15586381), [Clin Med (Lond) 2020;20:e191](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32934063), [J Cancer Res Clin Oncol 2021;147:1773](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33201300), [Am J Clin Pathol 2001;115:119](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11190797), [BMC Cancer 2017;17:13](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28056880))

Pathophysiology

* Cytogenetically, EMP and multiple myeloma are closely related; however, the distribution of *IGH* translocation partners is different, with the notable absence of t(11;14) in EMP ([Haematologica 2008;93:623](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18326524), [Am J Dermatopathol 2013;35:357](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23000906))

Etiology

* Hypothesis behind its occurrence is chronic antigenic exposure
* It is also known to occur in patients with immunodeficiency or immune dysregulation ([Am J Clin Pathol 2017;147:129](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28395107))

Clinical features

* Depending on the location, the presenting features vary; features include pain, swelling, headache, dysphagia and fracture, vary ([Ann Hematol 2012;91:1785](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22752147))
* Some cases may have an associated paraneoplastic syndrome ([Lancet 2020;396:e21](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32861309), [Am J Hematol 2021;96:872](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34000085))
  + TEMPI syndrome (telangiectasia, elevated erythropoietin and erythrocytosis, monoclonal gammopathy, perinephric fluid collection and intrapulmonary shunting)
  + POEMS (polyneuropathy, organomegaly, endocrinopathy, myeloma protein and skin changes)
  + AESOP syndrome (adenopathy and extensive skin patch overlying plasmacytoma)

Diagnosis

* Clonal plasma cells without associated monotypic B cells in a biopsy demonstrating involvement of bone or extramedullary site
* No other lesions detected on imaging studies
* < 10% monotypic plasma cells on bone marrow biopsy
* No evidence of end organ damage (CRAB)
* References: [Mediterr J Hematol Infect Dis 2017;9:e2017052](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28894561), [Lancet Oncol 2014;15:e538](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25439696)

Laboratory

* Detection of serum or urine paraprotein on electrophoresis / immunofixation
* Increased free kappa or lambda light chains
* Most common paraproteins include IgG, IgA and light chains (Bence-Jones protein) ([Ann Hematol 2012;91:1785](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22752147), [Int J Radiat Oncol Biol Phys 2020;106:589](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31707123), [Clin Med (Lond) 2020;20:e191](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32934063))
* Elevated β2 microglobulin
* Evaluation to rule out multiple myeloma: serum calcium, creatinine and bone marrow biopsy

Radiology description

* Imaging shows solitary lytic bone lesion (in SPB) or an extramedullary mass (in EMP)

Prognostic factors

* Cytogenetic abnormalities similar to multiple myeloma are seen in plasmacytoma but they do not have much prognostic significance
* Progression risk to multiple myeloma is higher for SPB (60 - 85% after 10 years) than for EMP (12 - 35% after 10 years) but the presence of a minimal infiltrate of clonal plasma cells (Blood 2014;124:1300, [Blood 2014;124:1296](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24939658), [Lancet Oncol 2014;15:e538](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25439696))
* 3 year progression rate to multiple myeloma is ~60% for SPB with the presence of clonal BM plasma cells versus 6 - 12% without, compared to ~20% for EMP with and 6% without clonal BM plasma cells
* Risk factors associated with progression to multiple myeloma ([Hematology Am Soc Hematol Educ Program 2005;2005:373](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16304406%2C), [BMC Cancer 2006;6:118](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16677383), [ScientificWorldJournal 2012;2012:895765](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22654647), [Clin Med (Lond) 2020;20:e191](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32934063), [Am J Clin Oncol 2020;43:709](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32739971))
  + Size of lesion > 5 cm
  + Advanced age
  + Persistent paraprotein > 1 year after therapy
* References: [Cancer 1999;85:2305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10357398), [Eur J Haematol 2017;99:216](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28544116), [Br J Haematol 2010;151:525](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20880115), [J Hematol Oncol 2018;11:10](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29338789), [J Pathol 2005;205:92](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15586381)

Case reports

* 33 year old woman presented with vertigo and peripheral facial nerve palsy ([World Neurosurg 2020;134:10](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31629133))
* 47 year old man, previously diagnosed with chronic osteomyelitis, presented with repeated discharge and ulceration in right tibia ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2023;102:e33307](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36961183))
* 54 year old woman with EMP of the right mainstem bronchus ([Ann Thorac Surg 2019;108:e119](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30682355))
* 71 year old man with progressive fatigue in the setting of diffuse hypermetabolic lymphadenopathy throughout his chest, abdomen and pelvis ([J Med Case Rep 2019;13:153](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31113466))
* 74 year old woman with EMP involving larynx ([Radiologia (Engl Ed) 2022;64:69](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35180989))

Treatment

* Treatment of plasmacytoma depends on the type (SPB or EMP) and the location
* SPB: radiation therapy (most common) or rarely surgery (kyphoplasty, vertebroplasty) ([World Neurosurg 2020;134:e790](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31715413))
* EMP: radiation, surgery, chemotherapy or immunotherapy
* Chemotherapy is considered for patients with persistent disease or tumors > 5 cm in size ([Int J Radiat Oncol Biol Phys 2020;106:589](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31707123), [Ann Oncol 2021;32:309](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33549387))

Gross description

* Soft gelatinous mass on cut section

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Sheets of plasma cells with varying morphology ranging from normal appearing to large atypical plasma cells with prominent nucleoli to plasmablastic or anaplastic morphology ([Am J Clin Pathol 2001;115:119](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11190797), [J Clin Oncol 2012;30:e91](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22231043), [Skeletal Radiol 2018;47:995](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29388036))
* Russell or Dutcher bodies may be seen
* Associated amyloid deposition ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2015;19:117](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25842207))

Negative stains

* [CD19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd19.html), [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html)
* [Cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html) ([J Pathol 2005;205:92](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15586381))

* Rare cases of [EBV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html)+ EMP in immunocompetent patients (EPIC) have been reported, which have brisk [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html)+ and **TIA1**+ cytotoxic T cell infiltrate ([Histopathology 2015;67:225](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25556356))

Flow cytometry description

* Positive for [CD138](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd138.html), [CD38](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd38.html), restricted cytoplasmic [light chain](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainskappa.html) and negative for [CD19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd19.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Positive for clonal immunoglobulin heavy chain gene rearrangement
* Negative for *TP53* mutation ([J Pathol 2005;205:92](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15586381))
* Negative for *MYC* gene rearrangement
* Cytogenetic abnormalities ([Blood 2016;127:2955](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27002115), [Am J Hematol 2016;91:719](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27291302), [Nat Rev Clin Oncol 2018;15:409](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29686421), [Br J Haematol 2000;111:1116](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11227093), [Leukemia 2001;15:981](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11417487), [Haematologica 2017;102:e364](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28550191), [Leukemia 2019;33:159](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29967379), [Blood 2006;108:1724](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16705089), [Leukemia 2006;20:2034](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17024118), [Blood Cancer J 2019;9:94](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31767829))
  + Hyperdiploidy: trisomies of odd numbered chromosomes (3, 5, 7, 9, 11, 15, 19 and 21)
  + *CCND* translocations
    - t(12;14) / *CCND2::IGH*
    - t(6;14) / *CCND3::IGH*
    - t(11;14) / *CCND1::IGH* (least commonly seen)
  + *MAF* translocations
    - t(14;16) / *IGH::MAF*
    - t(8;14) / *MAFA::IGH*
    - t(14;20) / *IGH::MAFB*
  + *NSD2* translocation
    - t(4;14) / *IGH::NSD2*
  + Alterations of chromosome 1 (+1q, -1p) and -17p
  + Deletion of chromosome 13q (or monosomy 13)

Board review style question #2

Which of the following genetic abnormalities is commonly associated with extramedullary plasmacytoma?

1. *MYC* rearrangement
2. t(11;14)
3. t(12;14)
4. *TP53* mutation

Board review style answer #2

**C.** t(12;14). *CCND* translocations, which are seen in extramedullary plasmacytoma, include t(12;14) / *CCND2::IGH* and t(6;14) / *CCND3::IGH*. Answer B is incorrect because t(11;14) / *CCND1::IGH* is least commonly detected in extramedullary plasmacytoma. Answers A and D are incorrect because *TP53* mutation is noted in multiple myeloma and both *MYC* rearrangement and *TP53* mutation are identified in plasmablastic lymphoma.
