Definition / general

* 20 - 30% of childhood preB ALL; most common translocation ([Chin Med J (Engl) 2003;116:1298](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14527352)) but not infants; also 3% of adults
* 5 different patterns of gene expression involving 14 genes, can detect with gene chip ([BMC Genomics 2007;8:385](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17956600))
* Excellent prognosis due to good response to chemotherapy; 90% remissions; relapses occur later than other ALL
* Persistence of TEL-AML1 transcripts is not necessarily related to relapse ([Pediatr Int 2003;45:275](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12828580))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* No distinct morphology

Positive stains

* [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) (bright), [CD19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd19.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [HLA-DR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshladr.html)
* Also [CD13](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd13.html) and [CD33](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd33.html) ([Diagn Mol Pathol 2000;9:184](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11129441))

Negative stains

* [CD9](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd9.html), [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Fusion of TEL/ETV6 and AML1/RUNX1/CBFA2 genes
* Detect with FISH or PCR; not found by conventional cytogenetics (i.e. are cryptic) because rearranged segments are too small
* May have no other molecular abnormalities, almost never > 50 chromosomes
