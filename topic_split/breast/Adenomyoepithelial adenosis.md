Definition / general

* Uncommon variant of adenosis with proliferation of epithelial and myoepithelial cells

Essential features

* Lobulocentric proliferation of both epithelial and myoepithelial components
* Prominent myoepithelial cells, 1 - 3 layers

Terminology

* Tubular variant of adenomyoepithelioma ([Schnitt: Biopsy Interpretation of the Breast, 3rd Edition, 2017](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1496365755/pathologyoutl-20))

ICD coding

* ICD-10:
  + [N60.2](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/N00-N99/N60-N65/N60-/N60.2) - fibroadenosis of breast
* ICD-11:
  + [GB20.1](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f1186737199) - fibroadenosis of breast
  + [GB20.Y](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f1782114659%2fmms%2fother) - other specified benign breast disease

Epidemiology

* No known associations
* Wide age range but most frequent in third to fourth decades ([WHO Classification of Tumours Editorial Board: Breast Tumours, 5th Edition, 2019](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/9283245008/pathologyoutl-20))

Sites

* Lobulocentric but otherwise no specific location within the breast

Pathophysiology

* Unknown

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* May present with a palpable mass
* May be a precursor to benign [adenomyoepithelioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastadenomyo.html), malignant adenomyoepithelioma or [invasive duct carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantductalNOS.html) ([Am J Clin Pathol 1988;89:308](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2831705), [Virchows Arch A Pathol Anat Histopathol 1984;405:55](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6438900),
  [Springerplus 2013;2:50](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23450635))

Diagnosis

* Histologic examination of tissue with or without immunohistochemistry

Radiology description

* No specific features
* May show asymmetry, architectural distortion or mass with irregular margins, with or without microcalcifications

Prognostic factors

* Unknown but progression to malignancy may be associated with older age (> 50 years) and mass on imaging ([Springerplus 2013;2:50](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23450635))

Case reports

* 17 year old girl with a palpable lump ([Turk Patoloji Derg 2017;33:240](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25371021))
* 35 year old woman with adenomyoepithelial adenosis associated with breast cancer ([Springerplus 2013;2:50](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23450635))
* 46 year old woman with a palpable mass ([Breast Care (Basel) 2008;3:427](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21048915))
* Malignant progression of adenomyoepithelial adenosis of breast ([Pathol Int 1994;44:475](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8055115))

Treatment

* Excision recommended due to possibility of recurrence and association with malignancy
* Best predictors of recurrence are initial incomplete excision and close margins

Gross description

* May form a firm mass with circumscribed or ill defined borders or may be grossly indistinguishable from surrounding fibrotic breast tissue

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Multiple foci of haphazardly arranged ducts with eosinophilic granular secretions
* Lined by cuboidal to columnar cells surrounded by hyperplastic myoepithelial cells
* Resembles microglandular adenosis but with larger and more irregularly shaped glands, taller lining epithelial cells with eosinophilic granular cytoplasm and bland basal nuclei
* May show apocrine or squamous metaplasia ([Breast Care (Basel) 2008;3:427](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21048915))
* May have mild nuclear atypia
* Rare to no mitotic activity

Positive stains

* Myoepithelial cells are positive for [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html),
  [CK5/6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5and6.html),
  [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html),
  [alpha smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html)
* A [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) immunohistochemical stain highlights abundant myoepithelial cells

Sample pathology report

* Right breast, core biopsy:
  + Benign breast tissue with adenomyoepithelial adenosis

Differential diagnosis

* Cannot be distinguished from a microscopic [adenomyoepithelioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastadenomyo.html)

The breast lesion pictured (H&E and p63 IHC, 20x) is most difficult to distinguish from which of the following lesions on core biopsy?

1. Adenomyoepithelioma
2. Invasive ductal carcinoma
3. Microglandular adenosis
4. Phyllodes tumor

Board review style answer #1

**A**. Adenomyoepithelioma.
The pictured lesion is adenomyoepithelial adenosis, an uncommon variant of adenosis with a prominent myoepithelial component. This benign lesion may be difficult to distinguish from adenomyoepithelioma, which forms a circumscribed tumor but has similar microscopic features. Adenomyoepithelial adenosis, like other adenosis variants, may have similar features to microglandular adenosis or low grade invasive ductal carcinoma; however, these lesions lack myoepithelium, which is readily evident in adenomyoepithelial adenosis. Phyllodes tumor is a fibroepithelial neoplasm with dissimilar features, namely stromal proliferation with a resulting leaf-like growth pattern.
