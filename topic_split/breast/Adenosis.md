Definition / general

* Any hyperplastic process (see subtypes below) displaying increase in glands, typically within terminal duct lobular units
* Common, often accompanied by [fibrocystic changes](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastfcc.html)

Essential features

* Proliferation of glands, typically lobulocentric (expanding terminal duct lobular unit)

Terminology

* Simple adenosis
* Special subtypes:
  + [Adenomyoepithelial adenosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastadenomyoepithelialadenosis.html)
  + [Apocrine adenosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastapocrineadenosis.html)
  + [Sclerosing adenosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastsclerosingadenosis.html)
  + [Tubular adenosis (tubular adenoma)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breasttubularadenoma.html)
  + Nodular adenosis / adenosis tumor
  + Blunt duct adenosis (now classified as [columnar cell change](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastcolumnarcelllesion.html))

ICD coding

* ICD-10:
  + [N60.3](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/N00-N99/N60-N65/N60-/N60.3) - Fibrosclerosis of breast
  + [N60.8](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/N00-N99/N60-N65/N60-/N60.8) - Other benign mammary dysplasias
* ICD-11:
  + [GB20.Y](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f1782114659%2fmms%2fother) - Other specified benign breast disease
  + [GB20.Z](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f1782114659%2fmms%2funspecified) - Benign breast disease, unspecified

Epidemiology

* Wide age range, highest in third and fourth decades, paralleling [fibrocystic changes](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastfcc.html)
* Premenopausal: lesions with predominant epithelial component (proliferation of acini and ducts); nodular adenosis
* Postmenopausal: lesions with more sclerosis (sclerosing adenosis)

Sites

* Terminal duct lobular unit; otherwise, no specific location within the breast

Clinical features

* Considered a part of the spectrum of fibrocystic changes
* Can present as a palpable mass if nodular adenosis / adenosis tumor

Diagnosis

* Histologic examination of tissue with or without immunohistochemistry

Radiology description

* Not visualized on imaging if focal (incidental finding when there is another indication for tissue evaluation)
* Increased mammographic breast density ([Breast Cancer Res 2017;19:134](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29258587))
* Architectural distortion, calcifications and asymmetry may prompt biopsy ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2019;98:e17061](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31574804))
* Mass can be seen with nodular adenosis / adenosis tumor; may mimic invasive carcinoma ([Breast Cancer Res Treat 2020;181:127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32227257))

Prognostic factors

* With some subtypes (e.g. sclerosing adenosis), the risk of subsequent breast cancer is 1.5 - 2 times higher, as seen with proliferative [fibrocystic changes](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastfcc.html)
* 2 times higher risk of breast cancer with increased [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) proliferation index in sclerosing adenosis or associated normal background breast tissue ([Breast Cancer Res Treat 2015;151:89](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25863475))

Case reports

* 35 year old woman with heterogeneous echogenicity who underwent excisional biopsy revealing adenomyoepithelial adenosis ([Int J Clin Exp Med 2014;7:1166](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24955201))
* 51 year old woman with nodular adenosis detected an enhancing mass ([Radiol Case Rep 2020;15:1211](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32566066))
* 63 year old woman with adenosis tumor evaluated on cytology with surgical pathology correlation ([Diagn Cytopathol 2008;36:496](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18528894))

Treatment

* Does not require treatment unless there is cytologic atypia (rare)

Gross description

* Variable - may be indistinguishable from surrounding benign breast tissue, ill defined fibrosis or fibrotic mass with relatively well defined borders
* See separate adenosis topics for specific subtypes

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Simple adenosis: proliferation of acini, with 2 layers (inner epithelial and outer myoepithelial), surrounded by basement membrane, without distortion of lobular architecture
* [Adenomyoepithelial adenosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastadenomyoepithelialadenosis.html): adenosis, with proliferation of glands (2 layers) with prominent myoepithelial cells
* [Apocrine adenosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastapocrineadenosis.html): adenosis, with proliferation of glands (2 layers) with predominant apocrine cytology
* [Sclerosing adenosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastsclerosingadenosis.html): adenosis, with proliferation of glands (2 layers) with glandular compression and (mild to marked) distortion due to stromal fibrosis / sclerosis; entrapped secretions frequently become calcified
* [Tubular adenosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breasttubularadenoma.html): adenosis with haphazard proliferation of branching tubules (2 layers)
* Nodular adenosis / adenosis tumor: adenosis that is mass forming
* Cytologic atypia is uncommon
* See also separate topics for specific subtypes

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/breastadenosisgeneralJorns05.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/breastadenosisgeneralJorns05.jpg)

A 48 year old woman had calcifications detected on a screening mammogram, which showed the pictured lesion. What should be done next?

1. Biopsy adjacent breast tissue
2. Excision of the lesion
3. Mammography per routine screening
4. Neoadjuvant chemotherapy
5. Perform breast MRI

Board review style answer #1

**C.** Mammography per routine screening. The pictured lesion is sclerosing adenosis, which is a benign lobular proliferation with distortion of glands by fibrosis. Microcalcifications (as seen above as dark purple intraluminal material) are frequently seen in sclerosing adenosis and are a result of calcification of entrapped secretions, making this biopsy benign and concordant with imaging findings. Therefore, no treatment or additional imaging is needed outside of routine screening.

Board review style question #2

[![Simple adenosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/breastadenosisgeneralJorns01.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/breastadenosisgeneralJorns01.jpg)

Which of the following is usually true regarding simple adenosis of the breast?

1. Clinically palpable
2. Displays cytologic atypia
3. Involves terminal duct lobular units
4. Lacks myoepithelium
5. Requires immunohistochemistry for diagnosis

Board review style answer #2

**C.** Involves terminal duct lobular units. Simple adenosis of the breast is a proliferation of glands with normal 2 cell layer (inner epithelial and outer myoepithelial) configuration involving terminal duct lobular units. It rarely harbors atypia and does not show distortion by fibrosis as seen in other forms of adenosis (e.g. sclerosing, tubular), making diagnosis relatively easy via H&E staining alone. It is often incidental or may less frequently show distortion or calcifications on imaging. This contrasts with nodular adenosis / adenosis tumor, which is mass forming and may be clinically palpable.
