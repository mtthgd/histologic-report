Definition / general

* Low grade infiltrative spindle cell neoplasm composed of fibroblasts and myofibroblasts

Essential features

* Rare low grade neoplasm with infiltrative pattern
* Bland spindle cells arranged in long intersecting fascicles within a collagenous stroma
* Characterized by alterations in WNT / beta catenin pathway
* Does not metastasize but can locally recur after surgical excision

Terminology

* Desmoid tumor, aggressive fibromatosis, desmoid type fibromatosis, extra-abdominal desmoid

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8821/1](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=80) - desmoid type fibromatosis
* ICD-11: [2F75 & XH13Z3](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#1714106490%20%26%201314962913) - neoplasms of uncertain behavior of breast & aggressive fibromatosis

Epidemiology

* < 0.2% of tumors of the breast
* F > M, third through fifth decades
* Reference: [Breast Care (Basel) 2021;16:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33708054)

Sites

* May be primary to the breast or be secondary extension into the breast from the chest wall

Pathophysiology

* Up to 95% harbor *CTNNB1* activating mutation (sporadic); *APC* gene inactivating mutation responsible for remainder of cases (hereditary) ([Cancers (Basel) 2020;12:1851](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32660036))
* Through the WNT / beta catenin pathway, these mutations motivate cells to proliferate while destabilizing beta catenin that then accumulates within the nucleus ([J Clin Oncol 2018;36:202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29220303))

Etiology

* Can consequently occur after surgery, trauma or rarely, after breast implants ([Breast Care (Basel) 2021;16:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33708054), [Cancers (Basel) 2020;12:1851](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32660036))
* Estrogen thought to influence growth ([Breast Care (Basel) 2021;16:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33708054), [Indian J Surg Oncol 2020;11:71](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32205974))
* Can be associated with Gardner syndrome or familial adenomatous polyposis

Clinical features

* Slow growing, often self detected firm mass

Diagnosis

* May arise as palpable mass that appears as abnormality on mammography
* Rarely presents with breast pain, nipple discharge and skin retraction ([Breast Care (Basel) 2021;16:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33708054))

Radiology description

* Imaging may show well circumscribed mass or poorly defined / spiculated mass that can be mistaken for malignant process ([Breast Care (Basel) 2021;16:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33708054))

Prognostic factors

* Does not metastasize ([Pathologica 2019;111:344](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31965112))
* Locally aggressive, local recurrence rates reported in 29% to as high as 40 - 50% of cases after excision ([Ann Surg Oncol 2008;15:274](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17896146), [J Clin Oncol 2018;36:202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29220303))
* Margin status not reliable risk factor for recurrence ([J Clin Oncol 2018;36:202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29220303), [Cancers (Basel) 2020;12:1851](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32660036))

Case reports

* 25 year old woman with indentation and pain in left inframammary fold ([Radiol Case Rep 2018;13:1174](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30233754))
* 31 year old woman with dimpling in the left breast ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2017;41:392](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29545999))
* 36 year old woman with rapidly growing lump in right breast ([Oncol Lett 2017;14:1433](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28789360))
* 42 year old woman with breast implants and palpable lump in left breast ([Radiol Case Rep 2017;12:431](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28828097))
* 60 year old man with 2 year history of right breast mass ([Chirurgia (Bucur) 2019;114:664](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31670643))

Treatment

* Surgical local excision
* Other options include active surveillance for asymptomatic patients and systemic therapy, such as adjuvant radiation for patients with multiple recurrences ([Breast Care (Basel) 2021;16:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33708054), [Cancers (Basel) 2020;12:1851](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32660036))

Frozen section description

* Bland spindle cells seen on intraoperative frozen section would prompt benign diagnosis

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Long intersecting fascicles composed of bland spindle cells with indistinct borders, hyperchromatic nuclei with occasional nucleoli and eosinophilic cytoplasm
* Cells infiltrate normal ducts and lobules, adipose tissue and skeletal muscle, thus mimicking invasive process
* Mitosis is rare
* Background stroma has thickened collagen resembling keloid
* Older lesions may calcify
* Lymphoid aggregates may be seen at the periphery of the lesion
* Reference: [Pathologica 2019;111:344](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31965112)

Cytology description

* Fine needle aspiration is difficult and often yields fibrous nondiagnostic material
* When successful, fine needle aspiration shows bland spindle cells with hyperchromatic nuclei and wispy cytoplasm within abundant stroma ([Cancer 2007;111:166](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17477374))

Review of fibromatosis

Sample pathology report

* Left breast, needle localized lumpectomy:
  + Fibromatosis, measuring 2.8 cm in greatest dimension
  + Margins negative with tumor > 0.5 cm from all margins

Differential diagnosis

* **Scar**:
  + Focally infiltrative lesion composed of bland spindle cells with associated histiocytes, foreign body giant cell reaction and lymphocytes
  + History of prior procedure
* [Nodular fasciitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuenf.html):
  + Focally infiltrative lesion composed of spindle cells forming short fascicles or storiform pattern with increased mitotic activity
  + Does not display nuclear [beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html) staining
* [Dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticdfsp.html):
  + Focally infiltrative lesion with spindle cells in storiform growth pattern and low mitotic activity
* [Low grade fibromatosis-like metaplastic carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantmetaplasticfibro.html):
  + Infiltrative lesion composed of spindle to epithelioid cells arranged in small clusters with low mitotic activity
  + [Cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) and [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) positive
* [Low grade myofibroblastic sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantRMS.html#lowgrademyofibrosarc):
  + Focally infiltrative lesion composed of spindle cells arranged in fascicles
  + Mild to moderate nuclear pleomorphism and brisk mitosis
* [Myofibroblastoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmyofibroblastoma.html):
  + Spindle cells arranged in short, disorganized fascicles with intersecting thick collagen bands resembling keloid; do not typically entrap normal ducts or lobules
* [Benign phyllodes tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastphyllodesgeneral.html):
  + Well circumscribed mass with hyperplastic epithelium within a hypercellular stroma composed of spindle cells with mild atypia and low mitotic activity

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/breastfibromatosisLaBoy06.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/breastfibromatosisLaBoy06.jpg)

What is the pattern of beta catenin immunohistochemical staining for fibromatosis pictured above?

1. Cytoplasmic
2. Cytoplasmic and membranous
3. Membranous
4. Nuclear

Board review style answer #1

**D.** Nuclear. The pathophysiology of fibromatosis involves an activating *CTNNB1* mutation in sporadic cases and an *APC* gene inactivating mutation in hereditary cases. Both types of mutations activate the WNT / beta catenin pathway, motivating cells to proliferate while degrading beta catenin, which then accumulates within the nucleus. This in turn yields a nuclear pattern of staining by immunohistochemistry.

Board review style question #2

Which of the following is correct regarding the histologic features and clinical behavior of breast fibromatosis?

1. Chemotherapy is the preferred treatment due to high mitotic activity
2. Metastasis is a common feature of this tumor
3. Necrosis and pleomorphism make this a high grade tumor
4. Tumor has a high frequency of local recurrence

Board review style answer #2

**D.** Tumor has a high frequency of local recurrence. Breast fibromatosis is a low grade tumor consisting of bland spindle cells with rare or no mitotic activity and no metastatic potential. However, due to its infiltrative nature, this tumor has a tendency to locally recur after surgical excision.
