Definition / general

* Breast carcinoma in which at least 90% of the neoplastic cells have abundant clear cytoplasm due to glycogen
* First described in 1981 ([Cancer 1981;48:2003](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6271388))
* Rare, 1% - 3% of breast carcinomas
* May be a variant of apocrine carcinoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Solid or solid / papillary patterns of large clear cells with distinct cell borders containing glycogen in 90% or more cells
* Often associated with intraductal component of varied type
* Often has apocrine features
* Cells have clear to granular cytoplasm
* May have scant intracellular mucin ([Histopathology 1987;11:857](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3305289))
* No cytoplasmic vacuoles

Cytology description

* Hypercellular with tumor cells in loosely cohesive syncytial groups and some single cells
* Most tumor cells have abundant, finely granular eosinophilic cytoplasm or foamy to clear cytoplasm with well defined cytoplasmic membranes and moderate / marked nuclear pleomorphism with central round/oval nuclei containing prominent nucleoli ([Acta Cytol 2008;52:65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18323277))
* PAS staining may be helpful ([J Med Invest 2002;49:193](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12323010))

Positive stains

* [PAS diastase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html) sensitive (glycogen)

Negative stains

* [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html), [lipid stains](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoilredo.html) (done on fresh / frozen tissue)

Electron microscopy description

* Nonmembrane bound glycogen and empty glycogen lakes
* Tight junctions between tumor cells, immature desmosomes, occasional short microvilli ([Am J Surg Pathol 1986;10:553](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3017138))

Differential diagnosis

* [Apocrine carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantapocrine.html)
* [Clear cell sugar tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorclearcell.html):
  + Positive for melanocytic markers ([Am J Surg Pathol 2002;26:670](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11979098))
* [Lipid rich carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantlipidrich.html):
  + Positive for lipid stains, negative for glycogen
* **Myoepithelial lesions**
* [Secretory carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantjuvenile.html):
  + Younger patients, low grade histology
