Definition / general

* Benign enlargement of the male breast, typically presenting as a palpable subareolar mass
* Histologically identical gynecomastoid hyperplasia can be seen in female breast

Essential features

* Benign ductal and stromal proliferation with characteristic stromal cuffing, typically with 3 layered ductal lining and pseudoangiomatous stromal hyperplasia

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [N62](http://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/N00-N99/N60-N65/N62-/N62) - hypertrophy of breast
* ICD-11: [GB22](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f2078176266) - hypertrophy of breast

Epidemiology

* Trimodal age distribution:
  + Infancy and neonatal period
  + Puberty (peak incidence age 13 - 14)
  + Older males age 50 - 80 (likely due to increased adiposity, decreased testosterone, medications)
* Estimated lifetime prevalence of 32 - 65% of men, depending on method of assessment ([Indian J Endocrinol Metab 2014;18:150](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24741509))

Sites

* Most cases retroareolar and bilateral
  + Rare cases of unilateral gynecomastia related to trauma or of unknown pathophysiology ([Andrologia 2006;38:31](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16420240), [J Plast Reconstr Aesthet Surg 2011;64:1684](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21782537), [GMS Interdiscip Plast Reconstr Surg DGPW 2012;1:Doc01](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26504685))
* Can be localized or generalized

Etiology

* Imbalance of estrogens and androgens by various mechanisms
* Idiopathic in > 40% of cases ([Biomed Res Int 2018;2018:8364824](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30003107))
* Physiologic: in newborns and infants, due to in utero estrogen exposure and tends to resolve spontaneously; hormonal changes in puberty ([Pediatr Endocrinol Rev 2017;14:371](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28613047))
* Drugs: finasteride, anabolic steroids, digitalis, spironolactone, metronidazole ([Eur J Clin Pharmacol 2015;71:569](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25827472), [Biomed Res Int 2018;2018:8364824](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30003107))
* Systemic disorders: obesity, hyperthyroidism, hypogonadism, cirrhosis, chronic renal failure, chronic pulmonary disease
* Syndromes: androgen insensitivity, Klinefelter, Carney complex, Peutz-Jeghers, spinobulbar muscular atrophy ([Andrology 2016;4:1178](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27611179), [Case Rep Pediatr 2015;2015:439239](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26366315), [Am J Transl Res 2017;9:2639](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28560011), [J Neurol 2018;265:1026](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29464380))
* Paraneoplastic hormone production by various testicular germ cells, pulmonary and adrenocortical neoplasms
* Mechanical: repeated impact against the body as seen in male rifle shooters with unilateral gynecomastia ([GMS Interdiscip Plast Reconstr Surg DGPW 2012;1:Doc01](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26504685))

Clinical features

* Most common male breast lesion
* Mastalgia in 88% ([J Family Med Prim Care 2021;10:648](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34041055))

Diagnosis

* Histologic changes are necessary for the diagnosis; important to exclude malignancy

Laboratory

* Usually not needed, except to identify underlying causes
  + Estrogen to testosterone ratio (E2/TTE), follicle stimulating hormone (FSH), luteinizing hormone (LH), prolactin, thyroid stimulating hormone (TSH), hCG, AFP, dehydroepiandrosterone (DHEA), cortisol, karyotyping

Radiology description

* 3 patterns of mammographic findings ([Radiographics 1999;19:559](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10336188), [Andrology 2021 May 25 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34033252))
  + Nodular: 72%, corresponds to florid histologic phase; fan shape radiating from nipple into surrounding fat
  + Dendritic: 18%, corresponds to fibrous histologic phase; flame shaped opacity with radiating projections penetrate surrounding fat / upper outer quadrant
  + Diffuse: ~10%, typically in transgender females undergoing gender affirming hormonal therapy; resembles dense female breast but heterogeneous and without Cooper ligaments
* Imaging useful to exclude pseudogynecomastia and carcinoma

Prognostic factors

* May recur if underlying cause is not addressed
* No strong evidence of increased risk of breast carcinoma
  + Present in 3 - 46% of male breast carcinoma ([Cancer 2004;101:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15221988), [Cancer 1996;77:490](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8630956))

Case reports

* 4 year old boy with adrenocortical carcinoma, with gynecomastia as presenting symptom ([Clin Pediatr Endocrinol 2018;27:9](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29403152))
* 13 year old boy with gynecomastia secondary to imatinib related hypogonadism ([Indian J Hematol Blood Transfus 2017;33:448](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28824259))
* 38 year old male to female transgender individual with concern for breast malignancy following hormonal replacement therapy ([Case Rep Oncol Med 2017;2017:5172072](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28421153))

Treatment

* Some regress spontaneously within 2 years and do not require treatment
* Sudden or symptomatic gynecomastia may be treated medically
  + Aromatase inhibitor, anti-estrogen, androgen therapy (tamoxifen, danazol) ([Breast 2006;15:276](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15998589),
    [J Pediatr Endocrinol Metab 2013;26:803](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23729603))
* Identify and treat any underlying causes
* Excision in adult patients to rule out malignancy and for cosmesis; however, recurrence is common if the underlying cause is not identified and treated

Gross description

* Soft, rubbery to firm gray-white subareolar mass
* Can be ill defined subareolar induration

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Increased number of ducts with characteristic 3 layer epithelium ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:762](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22314184)):
  + Luminal epithelium expressing CK5/6, CK14
  + Intermediate cuboidal to columnar epithelium expressing ER, PR, AR
  + Outer myoepithelium expressing CK5/6 and CK14
* 3 patterns of proliferative changes, without correlation to age or duration of lesion ([Am J Clin Pathol 1972;57:431](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/5012934),  [J Plast Surg Hand Surg 2018;52:166](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28876176))
  + Florid: irregular branching ducts, mild to moderate epithelial hyperplasia, cellular / myxoid / edematous stroma cuffing around ducts; can have papillary or cribriform architecture, myoepithelial hyperplasia
  + Intermediate: mixture of florid and fibrous patterns
  + Fibrous: quiescent epithelium, hyalinized hypocellular stroma
* Other findings can include pseudoangiomatous stromal hyperplasia (PASH), apocrine or squamous metaplasia
* Atypical ductal hyperplasia (ADH) in 0.4 - 5.4% ([Ann Plast Surg 2015;74:163](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23788148), [Histopathology 2015;66:398](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25215584))
  + Not clearly precancerous ([Breast Cancer Res Treat 2019;175:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30666539))

Cytology description

* Biphasic epithelial and stromal fragments in a background of scattered single bipolar nuclei, similar to fibroadenoma
* Spindle cells, apocrine cells, foamy macrophages can be present

Sample pathology report

* Breast, retroareolar; core biopsy:
  + Gynecomastia, (florid, intermediate or fibrotic) stage.

Differential diagnosis

* [Male breast cancer](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantmalecarcinoma.html):
  + Histology identical to carcinoma of the female breast
* [Atypical ductal hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastadh.html):
  + Monomorphic intraductal cells in rigid cribriform, micropapillary or solid formations
* [Myofibroblastoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantmalemyofibroblastoma.html):
  + Mass forming spindle cell proliferation that may entrap normal glandular elements
  + Variable morphology and cellularity
* [Mammary hamartoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breasthamartoma.html):
  + Circumscribed mass
  + Additional mesenchymal elements (smooth muscle, adipose tissue, cartilage, etc.) may be present
* [Fibroadenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastfibroadenoma.html):
  + Circumscribed mass
  + Uniform stroma without significant periductal cuffing
  + Intracanalicular or pericanalicular pattern of glandular elements
