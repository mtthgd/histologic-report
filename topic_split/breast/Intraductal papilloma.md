Definition / general

* Benign intraductal proliferation of epithelial cells with fibrovascular cores and underlying myoepithelial cells

Essential features

* Common benign breast lesion
* Distinction with malignant papillary lesions may be difficult on biopsy and immunohistochemistry may be necessary
* No consensus regarding management of papilloma without atypia
* Surgical excision of intraductal papilloma with atypical epithelial proliferation is recommended

Terminology

* Duct papilloma of the breast
* Intracystic papilloma of the breast (old terminology)

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [D24](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D24-) - benign neoplasm of the breast

Epidemiology

* 5% of benign breast lesions ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:665](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16723843))
* Involves women of all ages ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2016;140:628](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27362568))
* Uncommon in men

Sites

* Central intraductal papilloma: arises from large lactiferous ducts, usually solitary ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2016;140:628](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27362568))
* Peripheral intraductal papilloma: involves terminal duct lobular unit, usually multiple (papillomatosis) ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2016;140:628](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27362568))

Pathophysiology

* Poorly understood
* Intraductal papilloma might originate from bipotent progenitor cells that differentiate as luminal and myoepithelial cells ([Am J Pathol 2018;188:1106](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29454754))

Clinical features

* Solitary central intraductal papilloma may present as a retroareolar mass with serous or serosanguineous nipple discharge
* Peripheral intraductal papilloma is often discovered incidentally

Diagnosis

* Screening mammography
* Core needle biopsy, vacuum assisted biopsy, excisional biopsy ([Surg Pathol Clin 2018;11:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29413652))

Radiology description

* Nonspecific findings
* Mammography ([Diagn Interv Radiol 2013;19:471](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23996839), [AJR Am J Roentgenol 2012;198:264](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22268167))
  + Can be large (usually central papilloma), rounded or ovoid well circumscribed mass
  + Calcifications may be seen
  + Small lesions (usually peripheral papilloma) may not be visible
* Ultrasonography ([Diagn Interv Radiol 2013;19:471](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23996839), [AJR Am J Roentgenol 2012;198:264](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22268167))
  + Classic pattern is solid mural nodule within a dilated duct
  + Vascular pedicle may be seen on color Doppler
* MRI ([Diagn Interv Radiol 2013;19:471](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23996839), [AJR Am J Roentgenol 2012;198:264](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22268167))
  + Well circumscribed round or ovoid intraductal mass with variable enhancement
  + May be associated with ductal dilation

Prognostic factors

* Long term risk of developing breast carcinoma ([Mod Pathol 2021;34:78](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33106592))
  + Papilloma without atypia: ~X2 if single; ~X3 if multiple
  + Papilloma with atypia: ~X5 if single; ~X7 if multiple
* Risk of upgrade to malignancy (DCIS or invasive breast carcinoma)
  + Upgrade rate of papilloma without atypia and with pathology / radiology concordance is low ([Mod Pathol 2021;34:78](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33106592), [Am J Surg 2020;220:677](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32007236), [Cancer 2016;122:2819](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27315013), [Clin Imaging 2020;60:67](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31864203), [Breast Cancer Res Treat 2020;183:577](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32705378), [Ann Surg Oncol 2021;28:2573](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33047246))
  + Upgrade rate of intraductal papilloma with atypia is high ([Hum Pathol 2021;110:43](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33159966), [Breast Cancer Res Treat 2020;183:577](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32705378), [Ann Surg Oncol 2021;28:2573](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33047246))

Case reports

* 52 year old man with intraductal papilloma that progressed to ductal carcinoma in situ ([Radiol Case Rep 2018;13:602](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30073044))
* 53 year old woman with a large intraductal papilloma ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2019;55:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30557652))
* 55 year old man with unilateral bloody discharge and intraductal papilloma ([J Surg Case Rep 2019;2019:rjz023](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30788101))

Treatment

* No consensus for intraductal papilloma without atypia
  + Complete surgical excision often performed ([Am J Surg 2020;220:677](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32007236))
  + Different management among institutions ([Clin Imaging 2020;60:67](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31864203))
* Complete surgical excision should be performed for intraductal papilloma with atypia

Frozen section description

* Frozen section not performed on lesions suspected to be intraductal papilloma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Circumscribed intraductal proliferation comprised of arborizing fibrovascular cores lined by outer layer of luminal cells and an inner layer of myoepithelial cells ([Histopathology 2016;68:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26768027))
* Myoepithelial cells might be inconspicuous in sclerotic areas
* Myoepithelial cells also present at the periphery of the involved duct
* Frequently associated with usual type ductal hyperplasia and apocrine metaplasia, less frequently squamous, osseous or chondroid metaplasia may be seen
* May undergo ischemic or hemorrhagic changes, either spontaneously or secondary to biopsy / trauma
* May show prominent fibrosis / sclerosis which may contained entrapped glands (sclerosing papilloma)
* May be involved by:
  + [Collagenous spherulosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastcollageneousspherulosis.html)
  + [Atypical ductal hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastadh.html)
  + [Atypical lobular hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastalh.html) / [lobular carcinoma in situ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantlcis.html)
  + [Ductal carcinoma in situ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantDCIS.html)
  + [Invasive carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantductalNOS.html)

Cytology description

* Large stellate tissue fragments of benign ductal cells in a proteinaceous background ([Diagn Cytopathol 2007;35:386](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17580343))

Positive stains

* Myoepithelial cell markers ([CK5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5and6.html), [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html), [p40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdeltaNp63.html), [calponin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalponin.html), [alpha smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [smooth muscle myosin heavy chain / SMMHC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssmmhc.html)) show myoepithelial cells in fibrovascular cores and at the periphery of the involved duct ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2016;140:628](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27362568))
* In intraductal papilloma involved by usual ductal hyperplasia
  + [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) is patchy positive ([Pathol Int 2012;62:381](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22612506))
  + [CK5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5and6.html) is patchy positive in a mosaic pattern ([Pathol Int 2012;62:381](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22612506))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* PIK3CA / AKT1 pathway mutations commonly involved ([Mod Pathol 2010;23:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19898424), [Mod Pathol 2021;34:1044](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33462367)):
  + *AKT1* mutations are commonly seen in papilloma without (54%) or with mild hyperplasia (15%)
  + *PIK3CA* mutations can be seen in 21 - 42% in papilloma with or without atypia

Sample pathology report

* Right breast, 9 o'clock, 1 cm from the nipple, ultrasound guided core biopsy:
  + Intraductal papilloma with apocrine metaplasia
  + Comment: Patchy positivity for CK5 and ER supports the above diagnosis.

Differential diagnosis

* [Atypical ductal hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastadh.html) involving an intraductal papilloma (atypical papilloma) ([Histopathology 2016;68:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26768027)):
  + Monotonous low grade cellular proliferation
    - Atypical proliferation is small (≤ 3 mm) ([Cancer 1996;78:258](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8674001), [Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:665](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16723843))
  + Decreased myoepithelial cells
  + [CK5/6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5and6.html) negative, [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) diffusely positive
* [Intraductal papillary carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantpapillary.html) (**papillary** [DCIS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantDCIS.html)) ([Histopathology 2016;68:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26768027)):
  + Monotonous intraductal cellular proliferation with low - intermediate nuclear grade but may show high nuclear grade
  + Fibrovascular cores are slender and more delicate
  + No myoepithelial cells in fibrovascular cores
  + [CK5/6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5and6.html) negative, [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) diffusely positive
* [Encapsulated papillary carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantencapsulatedpapillary.html) ([Histopathology 2016;68:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26768027)):
  + Surrounded by thick fibrous capsule
  + Low to intermediate nuclear grade
  + No myoepithelial cells at the periphery of the involved duct or within fibrovascular cores
  + [CK5/6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5and6.html) negative, [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) diffusely positive
* [Adenomyoepithelioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastadenomyo.html) ([Mod Pathol 2021;34:1044](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33462367), [Mod Pathol 2020;33:1764](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32355271), [Am J Surg Pathol 1991;15:554](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1709559)):
  + Rare, usually benign, well circumscribed nonencapsulated biphasic tumor with myoepithelial and epithelial components
  + Multiple patterns (spindle cell, tubular, lobulated, papillary)
  + Papillary pattern might be difficult to differentiate from intraductal papilloma
    - In adenomyoepithelioma, myoepithelial cells are more conspicuous
* [Invasive papillary carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantpapillary.html) ([Mod Pathol 2021;34:1044](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33462367)):
  + Uncommon subtype of breast carcinoma
  + Invasive carcinoma with > 90% papillary architecture
  + Negative for myoepithelial cells
* [Tall cell carcinoma with reverse polarity](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantspcrp.html) ([Mod Pathol 2021;34:1044](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33462367)):
  + Rare papillary lesion with uncertain malignant potential
  + Distinctive morphology:
    - Columnar epithelial cells with apically located nucleus with papillary, solid and follicular architecture
  + Nuclei with clearing, groove and pseudoinclusion (reminiscent of [tall cell variant of papillary thyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidtallcellvariant.html))
  + Recurrent *IDH2* R172 hotspot mutation
* [Solid papillary carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantsolidpapillarycarcinoma.html) ([Mod Pathol 2021;34:1044](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33462367)):
  + Uncommon subtype of breast carcinoma
  + Expansile solid nodules with interspersed fibrovascular cores composed of monotonous atypical cells (low or intermediate grade)
  + Considered as an in situ disease
    - Myoepithelial cells may be attenuated at the periphery

1. Intraductal papillary carcinoma
2. Intraductal papilloma with apocrine metaplasia
3. Intraductal papilloma with DCIS
4. Intraductal papilloma with LCIS
5. Invasive papillary carcinoma

Board review style answer #1

**B.** This intraductal papilloma shows typical features of apocrine metaplasia.

Board review style question #2

What is the staining pattern of p63 for intraductal papilloma of the breast without atypia?

1. At the periphery of involved duct only
2. In the fibrovascular cores only
3. Intraductal papillomas do not stain for p63
4. At the periphery and in the fibrovascular cores

Board review style answer #2

**D.** p63 stain for myoepithelial cells is positive throughout the lesion, including in the fibrovascular cores and at the periphery of the involved duct.

Board review style question #3

What is the typical staining pattern of atypical epithelial proliferation involving an intraductal papilloma of the breast?

1. Diffusely positive for ER and patchy positive for CK5/6, negative for p63
2. Diffusely positive for ER, negative for p63 and CK5/6
3. Diffusely positive for ER, p63 and CK5/6
4. Negative for ER, p63 and CK5/6
5. Patchy positive for ER, negative for p63 and CK5/6

Board review style answer #3

**B.** The atypical low grade epithelial proliferation is diffusely positive for ER, negative for CK5/6 and negative for p63.
