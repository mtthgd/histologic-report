Definition / general

* 90%+ cells have prominent intracytoplasmic neutral lipid

Clinical features

* 1 - 2% of breast carcinomas
* Axillary metastases may resemble histiocytes

Prognostic factors

* Poor prognosis due to frequent (70%) nodal metastases at presentation

Case reports

* 53 year old woman ([Acta Chir Belg 2008;108:115](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18411586))
* 55 year old man ([Pathology 1995;27:280](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8532397))
* 56 year old woman with focal chondroid metaplasia in tumor ([Pathol Int 1998;48:912](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9832063))
* 62 year old woman ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2003;127:e396](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12951994))
* 78 year old woman with solid alveolar pattern in tumor ([Breast Cancer 1998;5:171](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11091643))

Gross description

* Lobulated, variable circumscription, firm
* 1 - 15 cm

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Nests, cords and sheets of large polygonal cells with foamy or vacuolated cytoplasm containing lipid
* May resemble clear cells or lipoblasts
* Irregular nuclei with coarse chromatin, moderate atypia, prominent nucleoli
* Other patterns are large pleomorphic cells in alveolar pattern with hobnail appearance, oncocytic or apocrine type change

Positive stains

* Lipid stains ([Sudan black](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssudanblackb.html), [Oil red O](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoilredo.html) on fresh tissue)
* [HER2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbreasther2.html) (71%+) ([Tumori 2008;94:342](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18705401), [Ann Diagn Pathol 2011;15:225](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21396871))

Negative stains

* Glycogen ([PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html))
* [Mucin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmucins.html)
* Usually [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html), [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html)
* [CK5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5.html) / [6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck6.html), [CK14](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck14.html), [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html)

Electron microscopy description

* Numerous intracytoplasmic nonmembrane bound lipid droplets, often within autophagocytic vacuoles
* No evidence of lipid synthesis by rough ER or Golgi complexes ([Virchows Arch A Pathol Anat Histopathol 1988;413:381](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2845641))

Differential diagnosis

* [Apocrine carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantapocrine.html):
  + Uniformly granular and eosinophilic cytoplasm, [GCDFP-15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgcdfp15.html)+, no lipid
* [Glycogen rich carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantglycogenrich.html):
  + Clear cytoplasm, secretions are glycogen ([PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html)+), not lipid
* **Oncocytic carcinoma:**
  + Granular and markedly eosinophilic cytoplasm, no lipid
* [Secretory carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantjuvenile.html):
  + Low grade, [PASd](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html)+ secretions, no lipid
* [Xanthogranulomatous mastitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastgranulomatousmastitis.html) (on core biopsy):
  + Not invasive, cells are [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html)+, [alpha-1 antitrypsin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsa1a.html)+ histiocytes ([Pathol Int 2009;59:234](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19351366))
