Definition / general

* Histologic triad of keloidal type fibrosis, lymphocytic inflammation and epithelioid myofibroblasts
* Associated with type I (insulin dependent) diabetes or other autoimmune diseases

Essential features

* Young to middle aged women presenting with breast mass(es)
* Histologic triad of keloidal type fibrosis, lymphocytic infiltrates and epithelioid stromal myofibroblasts
* Associated with type I (insulin dependent) diabetes or other autoimmune diseases

Terminology

* Lymphocytic mastopathy / diabetic mastopathy
* Sclerosing lymphocytic mastitis / lobulitis
* Diabetic fibrous breast disease

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [N61](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/N00-N99/N60-N65/N61-/N61) - inflammatory disorders of breast
* ICD-11: [GB21](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f259204343) - inflammatory disorders of breast

Epidemiology

* Uncommon, < 1% benign breast disease ([Arch Surg 2000;135:1190](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11030878))
* Usually women, may occur in men, premenopausal, average age 45 years ([Ann Plast Surg 2017;78:471](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28272124), [Am J Clin Pathol 2000;113:541](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10761456), [Arch Surg 2000;135:1190](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11030878))
* Often associated with longstanding type I diabetes (insulin dependent) or other autoimmune disease (Hashimoto thyroiditis, Graves disease, pernicious anemia, rheumatoid arthritis, Sjögren syndrome, systemic lupus erythematosus) but may occur in type II diabetes or nondiabetic patients ([Diabetes Care 2002;25:121](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11772912), [Hum Pathol 1994;25:819](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8056424), [Hum Pathol 1992;23:780](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1612578), [Ann Plast Surg 2017;78:471](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28272124))

Sites

* Breast

Pathophysiology

* Unknown
* Theories include:
  + Hyperglycemia leads to stromal matrix expansion and accumulation of glycosylation end products and a B cell inflammatory response ([Hum Pathol 1992;23:780](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1612578))
  + Immunologic response to exogenous insulin ([Hum Pathol 1994;25:819](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8056424))

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Associated with insulin dependent diabetes (type I > II), autoimmune disorders and endocrine disorders
* Firm, palpable, nontender, mobile masses or diffuse nodularity at presentation common ([Am J Clin Pathol 2000;113:541](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10761456), [Ann Plast Surg 2017;78:471](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28272124))

Diagnosis

* Needle core biopsy or surgical excision with histologic examination

Laboratory

* Correlate with serologic markers for diabetes and other autoimmune diseases

Radiology description

* Mammography: frequently negative; may show asymmetric density or dense breast tissue, discrete mass not seen ([Breast J 2007;13:607](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17983405))
* Ultrasound: irregular shaped mass(es), indistinct margins, parallel orientation to chest wall, hypoechogenicity, posterior shadowing ([Clin Imaging 2019;53:204](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30423508), [Breast J 2000;6:183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11348362))

Prognostic factors

* Recurrence rates vary; 10 - 30% ([Am J Clin Pathol 2000;113:541](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10761456), [Ann Plast Surg 2017;78:471](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28272124))
* No associated increased risk of lymphoma ([Mod Pathol 2003;16:223](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12640102))

Case reports

* 29 year old woman with diabetic mastopathy ([Cureus 2020;12:e7003](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32206467))
* 32 year old woman with bilateral diabetic mastopathy ([Ann Plast Surg 2017;78:471](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28272124))
* 37 year old woman with lymphocytic mastitis mimicking breast carcinoma ([Malays J Med Sci 2013;20:83](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23966831))
* 47 year old woman with lymphocytic mastitis and invasive carcinoma ([BJR Case Rep 2016;2:20150234](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30363690))
* 50 year old woman with metabolic syndrome and lymphocytic mastitis ([Breast Care (Basel) 2012;7:493](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24715834))

Treatment

* Conservative management
* Surgical excision (partial mastectomy, mastectomy) an option if symptomatic, especially if multiple and bilateral masses present ([Breast J 2006;12:559](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17238986), [Ann Plast Surg 2017;78:471](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28272124))

Gross description

* Firm, ill defined, homogenous, tan-white or white-gray cut surface
* Size variable, up to 6.0 cm

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Triad of dense, keloidal type fibrosis, periductal, perilobular and perivascular lymphocytic infiltrates and epithelioid stromal myofibroblasts
* Epithelioid characteristics of myofibroblasts include enlarged cells with round to polygonal nuclei, prominent nucleoli and abundant cytoplasm
* Epithelioid features of myofibroblasts may be subtle or absent
* Stromal mitoses absent
* Mature lymphocytic infiltrates are nonclonal, predominantly B lymphocytes and lack germinal centers ([Mod Pathol 2003;16:223](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12640102))

Cytology description

* Clusters of ductal cells, lymphocytes and epithelioid fibroblasts in fragments of dense connective tissue ([Acta Cytol 1997;41:1349](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9990273))
* Cytology usually insufficient for diagnosis ([Arch Surg 2000;135:1190](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11030878))

Positive stains

* Stromal cells stain for myofibroblastic markers ([CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html), [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) and [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)) ([Histopathology 2008;52:648](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18312350))
* Lymphocytes stain with B cell markers ([CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html))

Negative stains

* [Cytokeratins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) (useful in differential of invasive carcinoma)
* Nuclear [beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html) (useful in differential of fibromatosis)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Lymphocytes are not clonal ([Mod Pathol 2003;16:223](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12640102))

Sample pathology report

* Left breast, needle core biopsy:
  + Breast tissue with dense stromal keloidal type fibrosis, epithelioid myofibroblasts and perilobular lymphocytic inflammation (see comment)
  + Comment: The histologic findings are compatible with lymphocytic / diabetic mastopathy in the appropriate clinical context.

Differential diagnosis

* [Invasive carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantductalNOS.html):
  + Epithelioid myofibroblasts may be mistaken for carcinoma tumor cells
  + Supportive of invasive carcinoma: [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) positive, gland formation if invasive ductal carcinoma, mitoses, background of in situ carcinoma, lacks keloid-like fibrosis
* [Low grade fibromatosis-like metaplastic carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantmetaplasticfibro.html):
  + [Cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) and [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) positive, infiltrative, background of in situ carcinoma, rare mitoses
* [Fibromatosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastfibromatosis.html):
  + [Beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html) nuclear expression
  + Lacks keloid-like fibrosis
* [Amyloid](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastamyloidtumor.html):
  + Stromal amyloid is amorphous and acellular, lacks epithelioid myofibroblasts
  + [Congo red](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscongored.html) positive
* [MALT Lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantlymphoma.html):
  + Clonal B cells
  + Lacks keloid-like fibrosis and epithelioid myofibroblasts
