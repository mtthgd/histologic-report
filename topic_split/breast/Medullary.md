Definition / general

* Medullary pattern is a histological pattern that can be applied to an invasive breast carcinoma of no special type that contains pushing borders, syncytial growth, high grade nuclei and prominent lymphoid infiltrate
* Represents one end of the spectrum of tumor infiltrating lymphocyte (TIL) rich invasive breast carcinomas of no special type rather than a distinct morphological subtype

Essential features

* Medullary pattern of invasive breast carcinoma no special type has 4 characteristic histological features (ideally, all 4 should be present for the diagnosis):
  + Pushing border
  + Syncytial growth pattern
  + High grade nuclei
  + Prominent lymphoid infiltrate

Terminology

* Preferred term according to WHO: invasive breast carcinoma of no special type with medullary pattern
* Historically described as medullary carcinoma, atypical medullary carcinoma, medullary features

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8500/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=74) - infiltrating duct carcinoma, NOS
* ICD-11: [2C61.0 & XH7KH3](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f175963120?data=%7B%22dataType%22%3A%22pc%22%2C%22postcoordinationCodeSet%22%3A%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F175963120%22%2C%22axisToValueIds%22%3A%7B%22specificAnatomy%22%3A%5B%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F1605887153%22%7D%5D%7D%7D%7D) - invasive carcinoma of breast, NOS & infiltrating duct carcinoma, NOS

Epidemiology

* Often present earlier in life, median age of 53 (45 - 62) years ([JAMA Netw Open 2021;4:e214123](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33844001))
* Rare: less than 5% of all invasive breast cancers

Sites

* Breast

Pathophysiology

* Basal-like molecular subtype
* Same as molecular subtype matched [invasive breast carcinoma of no special type](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantductalNOS.html) ([Breast Cancer Res Treat 2013;142:257](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24162157))

Etiology

* Same as [invasive breast carcinoma of no special type](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantductalNOS.html)

Clinical features

* Younger age
* Soft, palpable, circumscribed mass
* May have lymphadenopathy (due to hyperplasia rather than metastasis, which is uncommon)

Diagnosis

* Diagnostic steps are identical to invasive breast carcinoma (screening mammogram, diagnostic mammogram, ultrasound, core biopsy, etc.)

Radiology description

* Round, oval or lobulated mass on mammography
* Hypoechoic mass on ultrasound with thick echogenic halo

Prognostic factors

* Prognostic factors identical to invasive breast carcinoma of no special type: patient age, tumor histological grade, tumor stage, lymphovascular invasion, ER, PR and HER2 status, molecular subtype
* Additional prognostic factors:
  + [Triple negative breast cancers](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignanttripleneg.html) have more unfavorable prognosis then nontriple negative breast cancer ([JAMA Netw Open 2021;4:e214123](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33844001))
  + Similar prognosis to stage matched grade 3 ductal carcinoma with prominent inflammation
  + Better prognosis than grade 3 ductal carcinoma without prominent inflammation ([Eur J Cancer 2009;45:1780](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19286369))
  + Tumor infiltrating lymphocytes have been shown to be of prognostic value ([Ann Oncol 2015;26:259](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25214542))

Case reports

* 38 year old woman who presented with a lump in both breasts for 3 months ([J Cancer Res Ther 2012;8:129](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22531532))
* 72 year old woman with invasive breast carcinoma with medullary pattern that had spontaneous pathological complete regression ([Pol J Pathol 2019;70:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31556565))

Treatment

* Identical to treatment options for invasive breast carcinoma of no special type with considerations for targeted therapy based on ER, PR, HER2 status
* Tumor infiltrating lymphocytes do not alter therapy currently

Gross description

* Well circumscribed, 2 - 3 cm in size, soft and fleshy (may resemble [fibroadenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastfibroadenoma.html))
* Homogenous with white to gray appearance

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Well circumscribed pushing border
* Cells in syncytial growth pattern with no glandular structures
* High histologic grade, high grade nuclei with prominent nucleoli
* Prominent tumor infiltrating lymphocyte infiltrate

Invasive breast carcinoma of no special type with medullary pattern

Cytology description

* Hypercellular specimen
* Numerous isolated cells and loose clusters
* Markedly enlarged, vesicular nuclei
* Prominent, often irregular macronucleolus
* Many lymphocytes and some plasma cells

Positive stains

* Variably expresses basal markers such as [CK5/6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5and6.html), [CK14](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck14.html), [EGFR (HER1)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsegfr.html) and [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html)
* Higher rate of [PDL1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsPDL1.html) expression (83.1%) as compared to other [triple negative breast cancers](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignanttripleneg.html) ([Eur J Breast Health 2019;15:235](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31620682))

Negative stains

* Most often negative for hormone receptors ([ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) and [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html)) and [HER2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbreasther2.html) ([triple negative breast cancer](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignanttripleneg.html))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Genomic instability is common
* Majority of these tumors fall within the basal-like molecular profile
* Approximately 15% of tumors in *BRCA1* mutation carriers are classified as invasive breast carcinoma with medullary pattern ([J Natl Cancer Inst 1998;90:1138](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9701363))

Sample pathology report

* Breast, right, segmental mastectomy:
  + Invasive breast carcinoma of no special type with medullary pattern (see synoptic report)
  + Carcinoma contains 90% stromal tumor infiltrating lymphocytes

Differential diagnosis

* [Metastatic high grade carcinoma to the breast from another site](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantmet.html):
  + Lacks a coexistent in situ component
  + May be within a lymph node or have abundant associated angiolymphatic invasion
  + Immunohistochemistry is often needed for distinction
* [Lymph node](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantaxillary.html):
  + Contains capsule or subcapsular sinus; both may have germinal centers
* [Lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantlymphoma.html):
  + Tumor cells will stain with lymphoid markers, [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) negative
