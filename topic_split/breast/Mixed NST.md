Definition / general

* Primary breast carcinoma with a mixture of no special type ductal (NST) morphology and special type histology, most commonly of lobular morphology

Essential features

* Tumors presenting with areas of 10 - 90% of a special histology admixed with at least 10% NST
* Mixed NST most often presents with lobular as the special histology ([Breast Cancer Res Treat 2013;138:719](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23535842))
* Mixed NST is associated with lower grade, larger tumor size and more lymph node positivity when compared to breast carcinoma NST ([Breast 2024;75:103732](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38653060))

Terminology

* Mixed invasive ductal and lobular carcinoma (IDC-L)
* Invasive breast carcinoma - no special type / invasive lobular carcinoma (IBC NST / ILC)
* Mixed ductal and lobular carcinoma (mDLC)
* Invasive carcinoma with ductal and lobular features

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [C50.919](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C50-C50/C50-/C50.919) - malignant neoplasm of unspecified site of unspecified female breast

Epidemiology

* Breast cancer is the most commonly diagnosed cancer in females and is the leading cause of female cancer related death worldwide ([Br J Cancer 2023;128:1030](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36604587), [CA Cancer J Clin 2018;68:394](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30207593))
* Most breast cancers are sporadic, however, up to 10% of breast cancers are hereditary ([Am J Hum Genet 1991;48:232](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1990835), [Semin Oncol 2016;43:528](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27899183), [Eur J Hum Genet 2009;17:722](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19092773))
* Mixed NST comprises ~5% of primary breast carcinomas ([Oncologist 2019;24:e441](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30518616), [Breast 2024;75:103732](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38653060))
* Mixed NST is identified in younger women when compared to ILC ([Breast 2024;75:103732](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38653060))
* Endogenous and exogenous estrogen exposure increases risk ([Breast Cancer Res 2015;17:37](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25848941))

Sites

* Breast
* Can occur in axillary accessory breast tissue

Pathophysiology

* Exact pathophysiology is unknown and this entity may represent distinct lesions (i.e., collision) or a single lesion with divergent differentiation

Etiology

* Risk factors
  + Hormone related
    - Prolonged estrogen exposure (early menarche, late menopause, obesity, estrogen producing ovarian tumors, nulliparity) ([Br J Cancer 2009;100:538](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19190634), [BJOG 2006;113:1160](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16945118))
  + Genetic
    - *BRCA1* / *BRCA2* mutations associated with early onset breast carcinoma account for up to 60% of familial breast cancer cases
    - Other mutations associated with breast carcinoma: *TP53, STK11, CD1, PTEN, MDM2, RB, CHEK2* ([Acta Oncol 2019;58:135](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30606073))
  + Other
    - Proliferative breast disease; in situ lesions are a precursor to invasive carcinoma
    - History of carcinoma of the contralateral breast or endometrial carcinoma
    - Radiation exposure
    - High density breast tissue ([Breast Cancer Res 2011;13:R132](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22188651))

Clinical features

* Commonly presents as a palpable spiculated mass, with or without calcifications
* Skin retraction or change in color / texture, nipple discharge or inversion and change in size / shape of breast may be seen
* Similar to ILC, mixed NST more often presents as a larger mass than IDC ([Br J Cancer 2023;128:1030](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36604587))
* Mixed NST tends to clinically behave similarly to ILC ([Br J Cancer 2023;128:1030](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36604587))
* Mixed NST most often presents with lobular special type histology ([Breast Cancer Res Treat 2013;138:719](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23535842))
* May be bilateral or multifocal

Diagnosis

* Diagnosis is made on histologic evaluation of lesional tissue

Laboratory

Radiology description

* Mammographically may present as architectural distortion or as a spiculated mass with or without calcifications, often underrepresenting any lobular component as it is typically not mass forming
* Ultrasound may identify a hypoechoic mass with spiculated to well defined margins
* MRI may better visualize the lobular component and may identify multifocal disease

Prognostic factors

* Mixed NST is associated with lower grade, larger tumor size and more lymph node positivity when compared to breast carcinoma NST ([Breast 2024;75:103732](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38653060))
* Mixed NST is associated with increased likelihood of hormone sensitivity and a lack of HER2 amplification ([Breast 2024;75:103732](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38653060))
* Histologic grade of mixed NST is an important prognostic factor, particularly between grade II and grade III ([Acta Oncol 2007;46:803](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17653904))
* Mixed NST has better breast cancer specific survival when compared to IDC and ILC ([Oncologist 2019;24:e441](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30518616))
* Mixed NST overall has superior survival outcomes when stratified by menopausal status, especially in postmenopausal women ([Oncologist 2019;24:e441](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30518616))
* Mixed NST has an increased continuous long term risk of relapse compared to IDC ([Oncologist 2019;24:e441](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30518616))
* Mixed NST is less likely to present with intra-abdominal relapses when compared to ILC ([Oncologist 2019;24:e441](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30518616))

Case reports

* 64 year old woman with calcifications on screening mammogram with palpable breast anomaly ([Radiol Case Rep 2021;16:2450](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34093928))

Treatment

* Complete surgical excision, to include breast conserving surgery and mastectomy, for invasive carcinomas
* Local radiotherapy, systemic chemotherapy and other targeted therapies (e.g., anti-HER2) may be offered, however, consideration and selection of treatment is based on several factors such as location, histologic grade, genetic alterations and hormone receptor status

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Tumor is composed of 10 - 90% of a special histology (most commonly ILC) admixed with at least 10% NST
* Ductal (NST) component
  + Histologic and cytologic features are often quite variable
  + Variable architecture of tubules, nests, clusters or sheets may be seen
  + Absence of myoepithelial layer
  + Calcification and desmoplasia may be seen
* Lobular component (alternate special histology may be present)
  + Tumor cells are small, discohesive and typically monomorphic with mild atypia
  + Tumor cells can dissect intervening tissue in a single file fashion, arranged in cords or as single cells
  + Other nonclassical patterns may be seen
* Overall histologic grade of tumor based on Nottingham / modified Bloom & Richardson as follows ([Histopathology 1991;19:403](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1757079))
  + Tubule formation (1 - 3 pts)
    - > 75% (1 pt)
    - 10 - 75% (2 pts)
    - < 10% (3 pts)
  + Nuclear pleomorphism (1 - 3 pts)
    - Small, regular, uniform nuclei, similar in appearance to normal ductal nuclei, 2 - 3x RBC (1 pt)
    - Moderate increase in size and variability (2 pts)
    - Large nuclei with marked variation, vesicular chromatin, prominent nucleoli (3 pts)
  + Mitotic count (1 - 3 pts), cutoff points determined by field area assessed using the diameter of the high power field (HPF) (see [Table](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmixedNST.html#mixednsttable)) ([Virchows Arch 2022;480:33](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34196797))
* Total score (add points for tubule formation, nuclear pleomorphism and mitotic count)
  1. 3 - 5 pts: grade 1
  2. 6 - 7 pts: grade 2
  3. 8 - 9 pts: grade 3
* Reporting the specific special type histology and its percentage of the overall tumor is suggested
* Reporting biomarker status of both components is advised
* Reference: [Schnitt: Biopsy Interpretation of the Breast, 3rd Edition, 2017](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1496365755/pathologyoutl-20)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Mixed NST is a molecularly heterogenous group of tumors ([Proc Natl Acad Sci U S A 2024;121:e2322068121](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/39042692))
* IDC is commonly with 1q, 11q13 gains and 16q, 13q losses
* ILC is commonly with 1q gain, 16p gain and 16q loss
  + *CDH1* gene encoding E-cadherin is located at 16q22.1 ([Br J Cancer 1997;76:1131](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9365159))
  + *CDH1* gene mutations are common, however, promoter hypermethylation is seen ([Proc Natl Acad Sci U S A 2024;121:e2322068121](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/39042692))
* 5 - 10% of breast cancers are hereditary with mutations in genes such as *BRCA1, BRCA2, TP53, STK11, CD1, PTEN, MDM2, RB, CHEK2* ([Acta Oncol 2019;58:135](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30606073))

Sample pathology report

* Left breast, mastectomy:
  + Invasive carcinoma with mixed ductal and lobular features, grade 2, measuring 1.1 cm (see comment and synoptic report)
  + Ductal carcinoma in situ, nuclear grade 2, solid and cribriform patterns, with focal necrosis and rare calcifications
  + Resection margins are negative for carcinoma
  + All margins are > 0.2 cm for invasive carcinoma and ductal carcinoma in situ
  + No lymphovascular invasion is identified
  + Extensive lobular carcinoma in situ, classic type
  + Biopsy site changes, biopsy clips and SAVI reflector
  + Pathologic stage (AJCC 8th edition): pT1c N1mi(sn)
  + Comment: The invasive carcinoma demonstrates admixed 15% ductal and 85% lobular components. While the majority of the tumor demonstrates a lobular phenotype with loss of E-cadherin expression, a smaller ductal component is identified adjacent to ductal carcinoma in situ in block 1G, demonstrating retained E-cadherin expression. This may represent a collision tumor of 2 separate invasive carcinomas versus a single malignant process with divergent differentiation.

Differential diagnosis

* [Tubulolobular carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignanttubulolobular.html):
  + Small, round to angulated tubules with low grade nuclei
  + Typically [E-cadherin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsecadherin.html) positive
* [Ductal carcinoma in situ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantDCIS.html) involving sclerosing lesions (e.g., [sclerosing adenosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastsclerosingadenosis.html)):
  + Myoepithelial cell layer will be retained
* [Lobular carcinoma in situ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantlcis.html) involving [sclerosing adenosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastsclerosingadenosis.html):
  + Retained lobular architecture
  + Myoepithelial cell layer will be retained
* **Inflammatory infiltrates** and [lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantlymphoma.html):
  + Lacks background lobular carcinoma in situ
  + Lymphoid cells may be atypical
  + [Cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) negative and lymphocyte markers positive
* [Melanoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticmelanoma.html):
  + Can mimic high grade breast cancer
  + Melanin pigment, lack of [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) expression, positive for melanoma melanocytic markers ([melanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html), [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html))

Board review style question #1

[![Gland forming population and discohesive population](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/breastmixedNSTbachert01.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/breastmixedNSTbachert01.jpg)

Which of the following best fits the 2019 WHO definition of mixed NST of the breast?

1. At least 5% NST admixed with 10 - 95% special histology
2. At least 5% special histology admixed with 10 - 95% NST
3. At least 10% NST admixed with 10 - 90% of carcinoma in situ
4. At least 10% NST admixed with 10 - 90% special histology
5. At least 50% special histology admixed with 10 - 49% NST

Board review style answer #1

**D.** At least 10% NST admixed with 10 - 90% special histology. Per the 2019 WHO classification, mixed NST is defined as tumors presenting with areas of 10 - 90% of a special histology admixed with at least 10% NST. Answer E is incorrect because this is criteria that is no longer used for mixed NST. Answers A, B and C are incorrect because these combinations do not satisfy the current definition of mixed NST.

Board review style question #2

Which of the following immunoprofiles would generally be expected of mixed NST of the breast and would aid in its diagnosis?

1. 2 distinct components, with both negative for CK20
2. 2 distinct components, with both positive for E-cadherin
3. 2 distinct components, with both positive for ER, PR and HER2
4. 2 distinct components, with one positive and the other negative for cytokeratin AE1 / AE3
5. 2 distinct components, with one positive and the other negative for E-cadherin

Board review style answer #2

**E.** 2 distinct components, with one positive and the other negative for E-cadherin. E-cadherin can be used to distinguish the ductal NST component from a lobular special type histology. Answers A - D are incorrect because these combinations of immunohistochemical stains are not useful in distinguishing NST from special type histology.
