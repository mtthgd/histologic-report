Definition / general

* Very rare as primary breast tumor (< 50 cases reported)
* Carcinoma composed of tall columnar cells with basal, bland nuclei and abundant intracytoplasmic and extracytoplasmic mucin (as opposed to mucinous / colloid carcinoma with only extracellular mucin, and signet ring cell carcinoma with only intracellular mucin)
* Either grossly cystic (mucinous cystadenocarcinoma) or solid (columnar cell mucinous carcinoma)
* Resembles similar tumors of ovary, pancreas and appendix ([Am J Surg Pathol 1998;22:698](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9630176))

Epidemiology

* Mean age 58 - 68 years, range 49 - 96 years

Case reports

* 41 year old woman with a basal-like immunophenotype ([Pathol Int 2012;62:429](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22612513))
* 52 year old woman ([Case Rep Oncol 2009;2:210](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20737039))
* 65 year old woman whose tumor coexisted with infiltrating ductal carcinoma ([Pathol Int 2004;54:781](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15482568))
* 65 year old woman with primary mammary mucinous cystadenocarcinoma ([Diagn Cytopathol 2012;40:624](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21472867))
* 96 year old woman with mucinous cystadenocarcinoma of breast ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2003;127:1031](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12873181))
* Mucinous cystadenocarcinoma of the breast with Her2 amplification ([Hum Pathol 2010;41:910](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20338619))

Gross description

* Multicystic, up to 19 cm
* Resembles ovarian and pancreatic cystadenocarcinoma, cystic hypersecretory carcinoma of breast

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Cystic spaces lined by bland, tall columnar cells with abundant intracytoplasmic mucin
* Tumor cells exhibit stratification, tufting and papillary formation
* Variable cytologic atypia
* Gradual loss of intracytoplasmic mucin and transformation to eosinophilic squamoid cells that are focally invasive
* Columnar cell variant has round and convoluted glands in loose aggregates, lined by tall columnar mucinous epithelium with basal bland nuclei
* Demonstrates both intracellular and extracellular mucin

Positive stains

* [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) (high % of cells) and [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) (rarely positive, [Case Rep Oncol 2009;2:210](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20737039))

Negative stains

* [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html), [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html), ER and [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html)

Differential diagnosis

* [Colloid / mucinous carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantmucinous.html): clusters of tumor cells floating in lakes of extracellular mucin
* [Cystic hypersecretory carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantcystichypersecretoryinvasive.html): prominent epithelial hyperplasia and eosinophilic secretions
* **Metastatic tumor**
