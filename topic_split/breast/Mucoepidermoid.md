Terminology

* See also [adenosquamous carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantadenosquamous.html); also called adenoacanthoma

Clinical features

* Very rare
* Grading system used for salivary gland tumors is recommended ([Virchows Arch 2004;444:13](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14634807))

Case reports

* 46 year old woman with high grade tumor and axillary metastases ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1981;105:612](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6895301))
* 54 year old woman with mucoepidermoid carcinoma of breast ([Pathol Int 2006;56:549](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16930336))
* Two low grade tumors ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1979;103:196](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/218522))
* High grade and low grade tumors ([Neoplasma 2007;54:168](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17319792))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Resembles salivary gland counterpart ([Virchows Arch 2004;444:13](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14634807))
* Mixture of neoplastic mucus secreting, squamous and intermediate cells

Cytology description

***High grade tumors***:

* Clusters of epithelial ductal cells with mixed glandular, squamous and intermediate cells
* May be scant intra- and extracellular mucin ([Acta Cytol 2006;50:344](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16780034))

Positive stains

* [Keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) and [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsCEA.html)
* Mucin is [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html)+
* Have basal-like phenotype: strong and diffuse expression of [CK5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5.html) / [6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck6.html), [p-cadherin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsecadherin.html), [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html), [EGFR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsegfr.html) ([Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2013;21:283](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22935826))

Negative stains

* [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html), [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html) and [HER2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsher2.html) ([Mod Pathol 2010;23:951](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20453835))

Electron microscopy description

* Intermediate cells are modified myoepithelial cells ([Hum Pathol 1985;16:941](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4029947))

Differential diagnosis

* **Metastatic salivary gland tumor** ([Acta Cytol 2002;46:377](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11917589))
