Definition / general

* Benign spindle cell tumor of mammary stroma composed of fibroblasts and myofibroblasts with recurrent monoallelic loss of 13q14 region including *RB1*
* First recognized in 1981 and described by Wagotz in 1987 ([Am J Surg Pathol 1987;11:493](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3037930))
* When present in mesenchymal soft tissue, termed mammary type myofibroblastoma
* Rarely involves the lower genital tract, predominantly vagina (less commonly vulva and cervix)

Essential features

* Well circumscribed spindle cell tumor comprised of short fascicles of bland spindled cells and dense hyalinized collagen bundles
* Variants include collagenized / fibrous, cellular, infiltrative, myxoid, deciduoid, epithelioid, lipomatous and atypical
* Part of 13q / Rb family of tumors, as majority show deletion or rearrangement of 13q (13q14), which may result in loss of Rb expression by immunohistochemistry
* Benign and adequately treated by excision alone

Terminology

* Mammary type myofibroblastoma
* Benign stromal spindle cell tumor with predominant myofibroblastic differentiation

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8825/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf#page=81) - myofibroblastoma
* ICD-10: [D24.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D24-/D24.9) - benign neoplasm of unspecified breast
* ICD-11: [2F30 & XH3NQ0](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#2120366482%20%26%20456084400) - benign neoplasm of breast & myofibroblastoma

Epidemiology

* Breast: 25 - 87 years, F = M, predominantly seen in postmenopausal women and older men ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2008;132:1813](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18976021))
* Soft tissue: median age 55 years, 80% men
* No race predilection ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2008;132:1813](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18976021))
* Limited cases reported in the setting of gynecomastia ([Int J Surg Pathol 2001;9:331](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12574852), [Oncol Rep 1998;5:731](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9538186))

Sites

* Breast, can also occur in extramammary sites including vagina, vulva, inguinal / groin, abdominal wall, perineum and paratesticular region ([Oncol Rep 1998;5:731](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9538186), [Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:361](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26523539), [Am J Surg Pathol 2001;25:1022](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11474286), [Ann Diagn Pathol 2010;14:358](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20850700))
* Soft tissue: usually subcutis but may be deeper

Pathophysiology

* Arises from CD34+ mammary stromal fibroblasts and myofibroblasts capable of multidirectional mesenchymal differentiation ([Ultrastruct Pathol 1999;23:249](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10503744))
* Some debate exists over cells of origin and differentiation, myofibroblastic versus smooth muscle ([Histopathology 2003;42:233](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12605642), [Ultrastruct Pathol 1999;23:249](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10503744))
* Part of the so called 13q / Rb family of tumors, which have deletion or rearrangement of 13q14 resulting in loss of Rb expression by immunohistochemistry ([J Pathol 2000;191:282](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10878550), [Hum Pathol 2012;43:1887](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22575260), [Mod Pathol 2011;24:82](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20852591))

Etiology

* Pathogenetic role of hormones has been suggested due to ER, PR and AR expression ([Histopathology 2000;36:515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10849093), [Hum Pathol 1998;29:347](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9563783))

Clinical features

* Painless, nontender, slow growing, mobile mass
* Clinically mistaken for [fibroadenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastfibroadenoma.html)

Diagnosis

* Imaging: ultrasound, mammogram, MRI
* Invasive procedure: biopsy, fine needle aspiration (FNA)

Radiology description

* Well circumscribed, homogenous, hyperdense or isodense, solid mass ([Clin Imaging 2018;51:300](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29945056))
* Typically devoid of calcifications
* Hypoechoic on ultrasound, may have posterior enhancement
* May be multilobulated
* Bilateral and multicentric tumors rare ([Mod Pathol 1996;9:786](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8832563), [Int J Surg Pathol 2018;26:242](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29017376))

Prognostic factors

* Clinically benign behavior, local excision is curative
* No tendency for recurrence or distant metastasis ([Virchows Arch 2002;440:249](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11889594))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Well circumscribed, lacks true capsule, rarely infiltrative
* Bland, uniform, short to elongated spindle cells arranged as short haphazard intersecting fascicles admixed with bands of hyalinized, brightly eosinophilic collagen and variable amounts of fat
* No more than mild nuclear atypia
* Mitoses usually absent, atypical mitoses and necrosis absent
* Mast cells common, perivascular lymphocytic infiltrates on occasion
* Focal myxoid stromal changes common
* May show smooth muscle leiomyomatous differentiation (elongated spindle cells with cigar shaped nuclei and pink cytoplasm)
* Rarely cartilaginous or osseous components
* Variants:
  + Collagenized / fibrous: collagenous stroma predominates, may have hypocellular myofibroblastic spindle cell component
  + Cellular: dense proliferation of myofibroblasts, ratio of spindle cells to collagen increased compared with classical variant, may have infiltrative borders, storiform or herringbone arrangement
  + Infiltrative: irregular margins, grows into surrounding mammary parenchyma entrapping glandular tissue
  + Myxoid: stellate and spindle cells in abundant myxoid stroma ([Histol Histopathol 2016;31:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26328916))
  + Deciduoid: large round, polygonal cells with abundant eosinophilic glassy cytoplasm, single or multiple prominent nucleoli which may be eccentrically placed, binucleation, sharp cell borders, eosinophilic intracytoplasmic inclusions ([Histopathology 2008;52:652](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18370961))
  + Lipomatous: abundant adipocytic component
  + Epithelioid: oval to polygonal cells arranged in clusters, cords, alveolar groups, linear strands, mono, bi or multinucleated, may have eccentrically placed nuclei with small nucleoli, well defined cell borders, single file arrangement may mimic invasive lobular carcinoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:1085](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19390423))
  + Atypical: single or scattered atypical mono or multinucleated cells with mild to severe nuclear pleomorphism, regarded as degenerative ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2008;132:1813](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18976021))

Positive stains

* [Desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) (91%), [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html), [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html), [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html), [AR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsAR.html), [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html), [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html), [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html) ([Histopathology 2000;36:515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10849093), [Virchows Arch 2007;450:727](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17497167))
* [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) (89%) diffuse and strong characteristic but may uncommonly show absent / focal expression ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:361](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26523539), [Breast J 2018;24:55](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28557150))
* [H-caldesmon](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscaldesmon.html) if smooth muscle differentiation / leiomyomatous variant ([Hum Pathol 2016;58:54](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27498061), [Histopathology 2003;42:233](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12605642))

Negative stains

* [Rb](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsrb.html) (negative in 89 - 92%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:361](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26523539), [Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:1119](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22790852))
* [Actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsactin.html) (negative in 63%)
* [Cytokeratins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html)

Electron microscopy description

* Features of smooth muscle and myofibroblasts: rough endoplasmic reticulum, bundles of myofilaments with focal densities, intermediate filaments and attachment plaques ([Pathol Res Pract 1999;195:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10048088), [Ultrastruct Pathol 1999;23:249](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10503744))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Recurrent monoallelic loss of 13q14 region, including *RB1* and *FOXO1* loci ([J Pathol 2000;191:282](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10878550), [Hum Pathol 2012;43:1887](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22575260))
* Related to multiple CD34 positive neoplasms with similar morphology and deletion of 13q14: spindle cell lipoma, extramammary myofibroblastoma, cellular angiofibroma and superficial acral fibromyxoma ([J Pathol 2000;191:282](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10878550), [Hum Pathol 2017;60:192](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27825811))

Sample pathology report

* Left breast, mass, needle core biopsy:
  + Bland spindle cell lesion consistent with myofibroblastoma (see comment)
  + Comment: Sections show bland spindle cells arranged in short fascicles admixed with hyalinized collagen bundles and adipose tissue. The lesional cells express CD34, desmin, smooth muscle actin, BCL2 and PR, are negative for cytokeratins and show a loss of Rb1 by immunohistochemistry. Overall, the morphologic and immunohistochemical findings are compatible with mammary type myofibroblastoma.

Differential diagnosis

* [Invasive lobular carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantlobularclassic.html):
  + Cytologic atypia, mitoses
  + Presence of [in situ carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantDCIS.html) supports malignant process
  + [Cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) positive, [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) negative, [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) negative
  + Both may be [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) and [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html) positive
* [Metaplastic spindle cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantmetaplastic.html):
  + More cytologic atypia, mitoses, infiltrative borders
  + May have areas with glandular morphology or squamous metaplasia
  + Presence of [in situ carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantDCIS.html) supports malignant process
  + [Cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) and [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) positive, [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) negative
* [Fibromatosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastfibromatosis.html):
  + Long, sweeping fascicles
  + Stellate or infiltrative appearing
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) negative, nuclear expression of [beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html)
  + *CTNNB1* mutations
* [Nodular fasciitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuenf.html):
  + Loose storiform proliferation of bland spindle cells; mitoses may be present and conspicuous
  + Extravasated erythrocytes often present
  + Scattered inflammatory cells (lymphocytes, histiocytes) present
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) and [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) negative
  + *MYH9-USP6* fusion or other *USP6* rearrangement by FISH, PCR or RNA sequencing
  + May spontaneously regress
* [Pseudoangiomatous stromal hyperplasia (PASH)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastpash.html):
  + Empty, anastomosing spaces within collagen
  + Usually not mass forming
  + [Rb1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsrb.html) positive by immunohistochemistry and lacks del 13q14
  + Similar immunoprofile ([CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) positive, [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) positive [variable], [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html), [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html) and [AR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsAR.html) positive)
* [Solitary fibrous tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesft.html):
  + [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html) positive ([Pathol Res Pract 2018;214:1544](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30072169))
  + No loss of *RB1* (13q14) by FISH ([Am J Clin Pathol 2012;137:963](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22586056))
* [Spindle cell lipoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueadiposepleomorphicgeneral.html):
  + Fat component usually predominates
  + Focal or absent myogenic marker expression
* In the lower female genital tract:
  + [Cellular angiofibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/vulvacellularangiofibroma.html):
    - Morphologic overlap with similar genetics
    - Thicker, hyalinized vessels, focal to absent myogenic marker expression
    - Lacks thick hyalinized collagen bundles
  + [Angiomyofibroblastoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/vulvaangiomyofibroblastoma.html):
    - Alternating zones of hypo and hypercellularity
    - More prominent vascular component (delicate capillary sized vessels)
    - Usually [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) negative
  + [Aggressive angiomyxoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/vulvaaggressiveangiomyxoma.html):
    - Infiltrative, not circumscribed, involves deep soft tissues
    - Hypocellular with abundant myxoid matrix
    - Medium to large sized hyalinized vessels
