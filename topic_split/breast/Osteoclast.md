Definition / general

* Part of WHO classification
* Osteoclastic giant cells are present in stroma
* Presence of giant cells does not alter prognosis

Case reports

* 44 year old and 83 year old women ([Diagn Pathol 2010;23:55](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20731838))
* 46 year old woman ([Radiographics 2002;22:691](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12006697))
* 51 year old woman with pleomorphic carcinoma and prominent osteoclastic giant cells ([Pathol Int 2009;59:91](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19154262))
* Cases with neuroendocrine carcinoma ([Pathologica 2008;100:176](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18841823))

Gross description

* Often brown due to vascular stroma with hemosiderin

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Giant cells are associated with vascular stroma with extravasated red blood cells and hemosiderin, also chronic inflammatory cells and fibroblasts
* Giant cells have variable size and variable numbers of nuclei
* Similar histologic features in nodal metastases and recurrences ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1986;110:636](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3013119))
* Carcinoma component may be any type

Cytology description

* Abundant giant cells and mononucleated stromal cells associated with carcinoma cells ([Diagn Cytopathol 2005;33:246](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16138376))

Positive stains

* Osteoclastic giant cells: [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html); also [acid phosphatase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsacidphosphatase.html), **lysosome**, [nonspecific esterase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnonspecificesterase.html); also **MMP9**, [TRAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstrap.html), [cathepsin K](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscathepsink.html)

Negative stains

* Osteoclastic giant cells: [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html), [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html), [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html), [alkaline phosphatase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalkphos.html)

Electron microscopy description
