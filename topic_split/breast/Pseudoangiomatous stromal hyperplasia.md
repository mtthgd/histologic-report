Definition / general

* Benign stromal myofibroblastic proliferation forming clefts, simulating a vascular lesion
* First described in 1986 ([Hum Pathol 1986;17:185](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3949338))

Essential features

* Benign stromal myofibroblastic proliferation
* Usually an incidental finding but may produce palpable or mammographic mass
* Complex interanastomosing spaces in dense collagenous, keloid-like stroma resembling blood vessels
* Some of these spaces have spindle shaped myofibroblasts at their margins, simulating endothelial cells
* Spindle cells usually positive for ER, PR and CD34 but negative for other vascular markers (e.g., CD31) ([Breast J 2012;18:242](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22583194))
* Pseudoangiomatous stromal hyperplasia (PASH) is an incidental microscopic finding in up to 23% of breast surgical resections ([Int J Surg 2011;9:20](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20887819))

Terminology

* Also called pseudoangiomatous hyperplasia of mammary stroma

ICD coding

* ICD-11: [GB20.Y](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#1782114659%2Fother) - other specified benign breast disease

Epidemiology

* Mostly occurs in pre and perimenopausal women but can occur in males too ([Radiol Case Rep 2022;17:2919](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35755115))
* Also occurs in children ([Pediatr Dev Pathol 2009;12:450](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19606909))

Sites

* Breast stroma

Etiology

* Myofibroblastic origin, postulated role of hormonal factors ([Breast J 2012;18:242](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22583194))

Clinical features

* Incidental finding, can occur in both females and males
* Can be associated with other benign and malignant lesions

Diagnosis

* Histologic examination of resected or biopsied tissue

Radiology description

* Incidental but may present as a mass, well defined or irregular ([SA J Radiol 2018;22:1366](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31754517))
* Nonspecific findings on magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), ranging from an enhancing mass to clumped, nonmass-like enhancement ([Clin Radiol 2010;65:145](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20103437))

Prognostic factors

* Nonneoplastic but mass forming lesion may rarely recur, especially in younger patients

Case reports

* 13 year old girl with a giant mass in the left breast for 3 months ([J Pediatr Adolesc Gynecol 2021;34:209](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33338626))
* 14 year old premenarchial girl with PASH presenting as bilateral gigantomastia ([J Breast Cancer 2023;26:391](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37272246))
* 46 year old woman with bilateral marked breast enlargement ([BMJ Case Rep 2015;2015:bcr2014204343](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26475873))

Treatment

* Local excision needed only in symptomatic mass forming lesions
* If diagnosed on core needle biopsy, no surgical excision is required, provided the diagnosis is concordant with radiologic findings

Gross description

* Usually unilateral, well circumscribed, firm rubbery mass ([Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7872425))
* Cut surface is firm, gray-white

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Complex interanastomosing spaces in dense collagenous, keloid-like stroma
* Some of these spaces have spindle shaped myofibroblasts at their margins that simulate endothelial cells
* Spaces are usually empty but may contain rare erythrocytes
* Cellular areas or plump spindle cells may obscure pseudoangiomatous structure
* Often gynecomastia-like changes ([Mod Pathol 2008;21:201](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18084246))
* Rarely multinucleated giant cells ([Breast J 2007;13:568](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17983397))
* No mitotic figures, no necrosis, no atypia
* Fascicular PASH: cellular variant in which myofibroblasts aggregate into fascicles with reduced or absent clefting, resembles myofibroblastoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7872425))

Positive stains

* Spindle cells
  + [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html) (intense), [ER beta](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html), [androgen receptor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsandrogenreceptor.html), [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)
    ([Am J Surg Pathol 1991;15:145](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1989462),
    [Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7872425))
  + Variable [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) and [actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsactin.html)

Negative stains

* Spindle cells
  + [Factor VIII](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfactorviii.html), **Ulex**, [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html), [keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html)

Sample pathology report

* Left breast, at 5 o'clock and 4 cm from the nipple, ultrasound core needle biopsy:
  + Breast tissue with pseudoangiomatous stromal hyperplasia (PASH)

Differential diagnosis

* [Low grade angiosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantangiosarcoma.html):
  + Hemorrhagic, soft, interanastomosing vascular channels containing red blood cells with invasion into breast parenchyma
  + Papillary endothelial growth and hyperchromatic endothelial cells
  + [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html)+, [factor VIII](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfactorviii.html)+ ([Breast J 2012;18:242](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22583194))
* [Myofibroblastoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmyofibroblastoma.html):
  + Absence of typical breast epithelial elements within myofibroblastoma
  + Neoplastic clonal tumors with characteristic genetic change (del 13q14) (this can be demonstrated by loss of [Rb](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsrb.html) protein immunohistochemistry in myofibroblastoma)
* **Other spindle cell tumors** (e.g., [fibromatosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastfibromatosis.html)):
  + Tumor cells arranged in long fascicles without significant clefting, nuclear [beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html) staining and [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)-

Board review style question #1

[![Missing Image](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/breastpashAgarwal04.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/breastpashAgarwal04.jpg)

The finding shown in the image above was obtained on the core needle biopsy of the breast in a 35 year old woman. What is the most likely mammographic finding?

1. Architectural distortion
2. Calcifications
3. Irregular mass
4. Mass with smooth borders
5. No abnormality; incidental finding

Board review style answer #1

**E**. No abnormality; incidental finding. Pseudoangiomatous hyperplasia is usually an incidental finding but may less commonly produce palpable or mammographic mass. Answers A, B, C and D are incorrect because the most common presentation is an incidental finding only.

Board review style question #2

Which of the following sets of immunohistochemical stains would be most suitable for the diagnosis of pseudoangiomatous hyperplasia (PASH)?

1. CD34-, CD31-, ER-, nuclear beta catenin+
2. CD34-, CD31-, nuclear beta catenin+, AE1 / AE3+
3. CD34+, CD31-, ER+, factor VIII-
4. CD34+, CD31+, ER-, factor VIII+

Board review style answer #2

**C**. CD34+, CD31-, ER+, factor VIII-. This immunoprofile is indicative of PASH.
Answer D is incorrect because the immunoprofile is most consistent with angiosarcoma or other vascular lesions, with positive vascular markers. Answer A is incorrect because it is the immunoprofile for fibromatosis with positive nuclear beta catenin. It is important to note that fibromatosis is negative for CD34 as it does not arise from myofibroblasts. Answer B is incorrect because it is the classic staining pattern for a spindle cell carcinoma with positive AE1 / AE3.
