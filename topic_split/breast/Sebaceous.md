Definition / general

* Very rare primary breast carcinoma resembling skin adnexal tumor with sebaceous differentiation, but no evidence of cutaneous derivation (see [J Med Case Rep 2008;2:276](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18706083) for cutaneous tumor of breast)

Clinical features

* Associated with Muir-Torre syndrome ([Cancer 2005;103:1018](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15662714))

Case reports

* 45 year old woman with extensive metastatic disease ([Pathol Int 2000;50:63](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10692180))
* 46 year old woman with Muir-Torre syndrome ([AJR Am J Roentgenol 2000;174:541](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10658739))
* 50 year old woman ([Pathol Int 2009;59:188](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19261098))
* 63 year old woman ([Virchows Arch 2006;449:484](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16944238))
* 83 year old woman with tumor of nipple ([J Cutan Pathol 2008;35:608](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18466364))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Well defined solid sheets or lobules of atypical epithelial cells, including large, pale or clear cells with coarsely vacuolated cytoplasm, containing Oil red O staining lipid and often scalloped nuclei
* Often focal squamous morules

Positive stains

* [Cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), including [35betaH11](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsCK35bh11.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) ([Pathol Res Pract 1993;189:888](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7508102))
* [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html), [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html), [Oil Red O](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoilredo.html)
* Mismatch repair genes: [MLH1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmlh1.html), [PMS2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspms2.html), [MSH2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmsh2.html), [MSH6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmsh6.html) ([Ceska Gynekol 2010;75:50](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20437837))
* Some cells may express neuroendocrine markers

* [GCDFP-15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgcdfp15.html), [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) and [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html)
* [Alpha smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html), [androgen receptor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsandrogenreceptor.html) (usually), [mucins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmucins.html), [HER2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbreasther2.html) and [CK15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck1.html#ck15)

Electron microscopy description

* Empty appearing, nonmembrane bound vacuoles

Differential diagnosis

* [Apocrine carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantapocrine.html):
  + > 90% of tumor cells have cytologic or immunohistochemical features of apocrine cells
* [Lipid rich carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantlipidrich.html):
  + Usually not coarsely vacuolated, often squamous morules, usually [HER2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbreasther2.html)+, [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html)-, [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html)-
