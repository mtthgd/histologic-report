Definition / general

* Ductal carcinoma with cells resembling gastric carcinoma due to acidic mucin that fills cytoplasm and displaces nucleus
* Excludes cases with lobular features ([signet ring cell variant of lobular carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantlobularclassic.html))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Mucin fills cytoplasm and displaces nucleus
* Usually coexists with invasive ductal NOS
* High nuclear grade; DCIS may be present, but no lobular features (by definition)

Cytology description

* Look for signet ring cells if plasmacytoid cells are identified
* Favor ductal carcinoma if hypercellular, single signet ring cells, high nuclear grade and tubule formation
* Favor lobular carcinoma if hypocellular, single signet ring cells and mild / moderate nuclear grade ([Cytopathology 2009;20:321](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19284410))

Differential diagnosis

* **Metastatic GI signet ring cell carcinoma**:
  + History, no DCIS, [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html)+ and [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html)- ([Am J Clin Pathol 2004;121:884](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15198362), [ISRN Obstet Gynecol 2011;2011:426150](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21637360))
* [Signet ring cell variant of lobular carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantlobularclassic.html):
  + Usually classic lobular component present, no DCIS, [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html)+ and [E-cadherin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsecadherin.html) negative
