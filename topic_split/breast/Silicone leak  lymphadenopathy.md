Definition / general

* Foreign body inflammatory reaction induced by silicone
  + Diffuse in the breast → silicone mastitis
  + Nodular in the breast → silicone granulomas
  + Leakage to regional lymph nodes → silicone lymphadenitis

Essential features

* Complication of leakage or rupture of silicone containing breast prosthesis or as a reaction to silicone injection
* Foreign body inflammatory reaction induced by silicone
* Most common sites: breast, regional lymph nodes

Terminology

* Silicone granuloma
* Silicone mastitis
* Silicone lymphadenopathy

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [T85.49XA](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/S00-T88/T80-T88/T85-/T85.49XA) - other mechanical complication of breast prosthesis and implant, initial encounter
* ICD-11: [PK9B.2](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f1475579100) - general or plastic surgery devices associated with injury or harm, prosthetic or other implants, materials or accessory devices

Epidemiology

* Incidence rates vary between manufactures and radiological detection methods ([Gland Surg 2017;6:163](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28497020)):
  + 10 year incidence for Allergan round: 9.3% (primary augmentation) and 35.4% (primary / revision reconstruction) ([Plast Reconstr Surg 2014;133:1354](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24867717))
  + 10 year incidence for Allergan shaped: 17.7% (primary augmentation) and 12.4% (primary reconstruction) ([Aesthet Surg J 2015;35:145](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25717116))
  + 6 year incidence for Mentor round: 1.1% (primary augmentation) and 3.8% (primary reconstruction) ([Aesthetic Plast Surg 2009;33:440](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19437068))
  + 6 year incidence for Mentor shaped: 2.1% (primary augmentation) and 1.5% (primary reconstruction) ([Plast Reconstr Surg 2012;129:1381](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22327894))
  + 8 year incidence for Sientra: 6.4% (primary augmentation) and 2.8% (primary reconstruction) ([Aesthet Surg J 2016;36:404](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26961987))

Sites

* Breast and regional lymph nodes

Pathophysiology

* See [etiology](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastsiliconebreastimplants.html#breastsiliconebreastimplantsetiology)

Etiology

* Leakage of silicone gel contained in prostheses

Clinical features

* In breast:
  + Breast implant rupture is often clinically silent ([Ann Plast Surg 2005;54:583](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15900139))
  + Palpable masses
  + Change in breast size, shape or firmness
  + Breast pain
  + Induration of skin, draining sinuses, deformity
* In lymph nodes:
  + Incidental finding
  + Painful or enlarged lymph nodes:
    - Axillary ([Breast J 2020;26:1821](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32469131))
    - Internal mammary ([J Med Imaging Radiat Oncol 2021;65:216](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33179438))
    - Cervical ([Ann R Coll Surg Engl 2016;98:e118](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27167311))

Diagnosis

* Can be suspected clinically and radiologically
* Histopathology is the gold standard for a definitive diagnosis

Radiology description

* Mammography: radiopaque silicone outside the implant shell in the breast or lymph nodes with or without irregular and coarse calcifications
* Ultrasound: extracapsular silicone in the breast or lymph nodes (snowstorm sign) ([Indian J Radiol Imaging 2016;26:216](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27413269))
* Magnetic resonance imaging: high signal intensity deposits in the breast or lymph nodes

* 34 year old woman with silicone breast implant associated fibromatosis ([J Surg Case Rep 2018;2018:rjy249](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30279974))
* 35 year old woman with extensive silicone lymphadenopathy mimicking lymphoma ([Ann Surg Treat Res 2017;93:331](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29250513))

* 45 year old woman with silicone nipple discharge ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2020;74:73](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32823059))

Treatment

* Replacement of the prosthesis with removal of the surrounding contracture ([Clin Plast Surg 2021;48:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33220896))
* Excision of enlarged masses or lymph nodes caused by migration of silicone ([Am J Med 2018;131:1383](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29680487))
* Total mastectomy to control significant inflammatory or cosmetic complications ([Indian J Plast Surg 2015;48:317](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26933290))

Gross description

* Firm to hard nodular surface, gritty if there is calcification ([Insights Imaging 2018;9:59](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29404980))
* Cystic spaces containing thick pale yellow or white material

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Features are not specific:
  + Chronic inflammatory cells, foreign body giant cell reaction and fibrosis
  + Fat necrosis → histiocytes containing clear, refractile, nonpolarizable material within cytoplasmic vacuoles
  + Extracellular silicone can also be seen as nonbirefringent crystals or particles within empty spaces
* Later features include:
  + Fibrous capsule or bursa: well defined band of collagenized fibrous tissue and mixed chronic inflammatory cells
  + Calcifications in the capsule or around: globular aggregates, may have bone formation

Cytology description

* Papillary synovial spherules consisting of an extracellular matrix and bland looking cells ([Diagn Cytopathol 2018;46:769](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29663703))
* Vacuolated histiocytes containing refractile particles ([Acta Cytol 1990;34:10](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2296832))

Positive stains

* Macrophages and foreign body giant cells: [CD11b](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10to19.html#cd11b), [CD44](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd44.html), [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html), [CD163](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd160.html#cd163) ([J Histochem Cytochem 2006;54:763](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16517975), [Cell Rep 2015;13:1937](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26628365))

Negative stains

* [Cytokeratins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) and [factor VIII](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfactorviii.html)

Electron microscopy description

* Calcifications are composed of hydroxyapatite crystals

Sample pathology report

* Left breast, core needle biopsy:
  + Benign breast tissue with fibrous capsule with associated foreign body giant cell reaction to silicone, chronic inflammation and fat necrosis

Differential diagnosis

* [Sinus histiocytosis with massive lymphadenopathy](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodessinushistiocytosiswithmassivelymph.html):
  + Fever, leukocytosis, anemia
  + Usually no history of breast implant
  + Large histiocytes with emperipolesis, plasma cells
  + No foreign body giant cell reaction
  + No foreign material
* [Fat necrosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastfatnecrosis.html):
  + Usually secondary to injury or previous procedures
  + No history of breast implant
  + May have foreign material from prior procedure(s) but is morphologically different from silicone
* [Abscess / acute mastitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastacutemastitis.html):
  + Associated with lactation in most cases
  + Neutrophils are predominant
  + Bacteria are usually present ([gram](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgramstain.html) positive)
  + No foreign material
* [Cystic neutrophic granulomatous mastitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastcysticgranulomatousmastitis.html):
  + Fever, leukocytosis, anemia
  + Granulomatous inflammation with cystic spaces rimmed by neutrophils
  + Coryneform bacteria is present in cystic spaces ([gram](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgramstain.html) positive, culture or molecular testing)
  + No foreign material
