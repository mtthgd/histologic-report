Definition / general

* A malignant diagnosis is an unequivocal statement that the sample is malignant; if possible, the type of malignancy should be specified
* Positive predictive value (PPV) of a malignant diagnosis has a range of 92 - 100% in the literature; however, using the Yokohama criteria, it is 99 - 100%
* Perform ER / PR / HER2 on cell block for treatment planning (if validated)
* Biopsy of axillary lymph nodes can be done
* If a malignant diagnosis is not consistent with the clinical and imaging findings, a core needle biopsy or surgical biopsy should be done

Essential features

* For a malignant diagnosis, the fine needle aspiration biopsy (FNAB) should show multiple malignancy features, since individual features can be seen in other lesions; features of malignancy are:
  + Marked cellularity
  + Prominent single cells
  + Crowded, small, discohesive epithelial tissue fragments with nuclear overlapping
  + Enlarged, pleomorphic nuclei with irregular nuclear membranes, coarse chromatin and large nucleoli
  + Lack of bare bipolar nuclei
  + Lack of myoepithelial cells
* Softer criteria for malignancy include:
  + Tissue fragments with tubular architecture
    - Can also be seen in fibroadenoma
  + Intracytoplasmic vacuoles in cells with atypical nuclei
  + Elastoid fragments
  + Multinucleated tumor giant cells

Laboratory

* [Carcinoma with medullary features](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantmedullary.html)
  + Often triple negative and basal-like
  + Associated with *BRCA1* germline mutations
* [Secretory carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantjuvenile.html)
  + *ETV6::NTRK3* gene fusion
  + Most are triple negative
  + [CK5/6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5and6.html) and [CK14](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck14.html) positive
* [Adenoid cystic carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantadenoidcystic.html)
  + t(6;9) *MYB::NFIB*
  + Epithelial cells are [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html), [CK5/6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5and6.html) and [PASD](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html) positive
  + Myoepithelial cells are [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) and [calponin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalponin.html) positive
  + Triple negative
* [Apocrine carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantapocrine.html)
  + [GCDFP-15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgcdfp15.html), [AR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsAR.html), [HER2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbreasther2.html) positive
  + [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html), [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html) negative
* [Carcinoma with neuroendocrine features](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastNEC.html)
  + Positive for the typical neuroendocrine markers
* [Non-Hodgkin lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantlymphoma.html)
  + Flow cytometry, cytogenetics, gene rearrangement studies, gene panels
* [Angiosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantangiosarcoma.html)
  + [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [ERG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsERG.html) and [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html) positive
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) negative
  + Can be positive for [cytokeratins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)
  + Rule out metastases, depending on clinical history
    - Suggested IHC panel: [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html), [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html), [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgata3.html), [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [HER2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbreasther2.html) for breast; [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html) for lung; [MART1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html) and [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) for melanoma; [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html) and [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html) for colon; [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html) and [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html) for ovarian

Radiology description

* [Invasive carcinoma of no special type (ductal carcinoma)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantductalNOS.html)
  + Typical appearance on mammogram:
    - Spiculated mass
    - Irregular or circumscribed (pushing) margin
    - Microcalcifications
  + Typical appearance on ultrasound:
    - Solid, irregular mass that is typically taller than it is wide
    - Spiculated, ill defined, microlobulated or angulated margins
    - Internal hypoechogenicity
    - Posterior shadow
* [Invasive lobular carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantlobularclassic.html)
  + Mammography: may show a typically malignant, irregularly shaped mass with spiculated margins but frequently only shows a nonspecific architectural distortion
  + Ultrasound: may show an irregular discrete mass but often only shows a nonspecific architectural disturbance with localized shadowing
* [Tubular carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignanttubular.html)
  + Mammogram: small, highly spiculated mass with fine, scattered microcalcifications from associated ductal carcinoma in situ (DCIS)
  + Ultrasound: spiculated mass with hypoechogenic center
* [Carcinoma with medullary features](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantmedullary.html)
  + Relatively circumscribed, rounded mass with pushing margins
  + Mass is rapidly growing
* [Mucinous carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantmucinous.html)
  + Mammogram:
    - Rounded mass
    - May have multiple small, rounded protrusions or adjacent lesions
    - Microcalcifications, coarse calcifications
  + Ultrasound:
    - Homogenous with posterior enhancement
    - Appearance is similar to a cyst but with indistinct margins
* [Invasive micropapillary carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantmicropapillary.html)
  + Typical malignant features (see [invasive carcinoma of no special type](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastyokohamasystemmalignant.html#breastyokohamasystemmalignantradio))
* [Metaplastic carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantmetaplastic.html)
  + Usually typical of invasive cancer (see [invasive carcinoma of no special type](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastyokohamasystemmalignant.html#breastyokohamasystemmalignantradio))
  + May appear like a complex cyst if there is necrosis
* [High grade phyllodes](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastphyllodesgeneral.html)
  + History of rapid growth
* [Secretory carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantjuvenile.html)
  + Solid, well circumscribed mass with smooth or mildly irregular margins
* [Glycogen rich clear cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantglycogenrich.html)
  + Mass with irregular borders and fine to coarse calcifications
* [Adenoid cystic carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantadenoidcystic.html)
  + Variable - rounded mass to stellate lesion

Cytology description

* [Invasive carcinoma of no special type (ductal carcinoma)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantductalNOS.html)
  + Low power:
    - High cellularity
      * However, desmoplastic stroma may lead to a hypocellular sample
    - Epithelial tissue fragment size varies with grade:
      * Low grade:
        + Larger, more cohesive fragments
      * High grade:
        + Smaller tissue fragments, more single cells
        + Tissue fragments fray at the edges
        + Nuclei are crowded, overlapping and disorganized
    - Micro biopsies of stromal fragments with infiltrating atypical cells
    - In liquid based cytology, the 3D tissue fragments appear more flattened than in smears
  + Background:
    - No myoepithelial cells or bare bipolar nuclei
    - Necrosis with calcifications suggests a high grade in situ component
  + High power:
    - Nuclear debris in tissue fragments
    - Eccentric intracytoplasmic vacuoles
    - Nuclear features vary with grade:
      * Low grade:
        + Dispersed cells are monotonous with moderately enlarged nuclei
        + Nuclei are round to pleomorphic
        + Mild to moderate hyperchromasia
        + Small nucleoli
      * High grade:
        + Dispersed cells have denser cytoplasm than low grade
        + High N:C ratio
        + Pleomorphic, enlarged nuclei
        + Dense, coarse chromatin
        + Prominent large, irregular nucleoli with perinucleolar clearing
* [Invasive lobular carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantlobularclassic.html)
  + Low power:
    - Variable cellularity but can be low
    - Mostly dispersed single cells, short linear (single file) strands
    - Loosely cohesive fragments show windows between cells
  + Background:
    - No bare bipolar nuclei
    - Stripped nuclei, chromatin smearing
  + High power:
    - Uniform, intermediate sized cells
    - Nuclei are round to angulated, with subtle nuclear envelope indentations, bland chromatin, inconspicuous nucleoli
    - Cells have eccentric, relatively dense cytoplasm
    - Some cells have intracytoplasmic vacuoles with mucin droplets (targetoid or magenta bodies)
* [Tubular carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignanttubular.html)
  + Low power:
    - Variable cellularity but typically moderate to high
    - Small, rigid, angulated, comma shaped, narrow and cohesive tubular epithelial tissue fragments
    - > 90% of tumor is made up of open tubules lined by a single layer of low columnar cells
    - Cribriform or micropapillary 3D fragments suggest an in situ component
  + Background:
    - Very few to no bare bipolar nuclei
  + High power:
    - Subtle nuclear indentations and grooves
    - Mild hyperchromasia
    - Apoptotic debris may mimic myoepithelial cells
* [Carcinoma with medullary features](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantmedullary.html)
  + Low power:
    - High cellularity
    - Small, syncytial sheets of epithelial cells infiltrated by lymphocytes
    - Variable number of dispersed single cells
  + Background:
    - Stripped, bizarre nuclei
    - Lymphocytes, plasma cells, cellular debris, lymphoglandular bodies
  + High power:
    - Large epithelial cells with a high N:C ratio
    - Nuclei have coarse chromatin and macronucleoli
    - Many mitotic figures
* [Mucinous carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantmucinous.html)
  + Low power:
    - Variable cellularity but may be high
    - Variably sized tissue fragments that may be balled up, rounded, cribriform, papillary or tubular
    - Tissue fragments do not have myoepithelial nuclei
    - Naked branching capillaries
  + Background:
    - Fibrillary mucin that is blue to purple on Giemsa and pale green to orange on Pap
    - On liquid based preparations, the mucin is reduced or lost
    - No bare bipolar nuclei
  + High power:
    - Mild to moderate nuclear atypia but occasionally high grade
    - Occasional intracytoplasmic vacuoles, signet ring cells
    - Occasional calcifications
* [Invasive papillary carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantpapillary.html)
  + Low power:
    - Slender and complex papillae with thin fibrovascular cores
    - Dissociated tall columnar cells
    - No myoepithelial cells
  + Background:
    - No bare bipolar nuclei
  + High power:
    - Variable nuclear atypia
* [Invasive micropapillary carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantmicropapillary.html)
  + Low power:
    - Moderate to high cellularity
    - Predominantly small epithelial tissue fragments and dispersed cells
    - Jigsaw pattern of tissue fragments that are close together and may show molding
    - Mucinous blush on the outside of the tissue fragments
  + Background:
    - Necrosis
    - Calcifications
    - May show mucin
  + High power:
    - Large, atypical, hyperchromatic nuclei with prominent nucleoli
    - Moderate to high N:C ratio
    - Dense apocrine-like cytoplasm or eccentric columnar cytoplasm
* [Metaplastic carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantmetaplastic.html)
  + Low power:
    - Moderately to high cellularity
    - Variable patterns depending on the cell differentiation
      * Poorly differentiated carcinoma show small, discohesive tissue fragments and dispersed single cells
      * Squamous cell carcinoma will show sheets and tissue fragments with keratinous debris
      * Low grade adenosquamous carcinoma shows small glands and tubules with focal keratinization and a spindle cell component
      * Sarcomatous differentiation will show plump spindle cells in small tissue fragments and dispersed single cells
      * Can see fibromatosis-like spindle cells or chondroid matrix
  + Background:
    - Necrosis and debris
    - Neutrophils, histiocytes, hemosiderin laden macrophages
  + High power:
    - Depends on cell differentiation
* [High grade phyllodes](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastphyllodesgeneral.html)
  + Low power:
    - Crowded tissue fragments with frequent single cells
    - Scant epithelial fragments
  + Background:
    - Necrosis, foamy histiocytes
  + High power:
    - Large, plump spindle cells
    - High N:C ratio
    - Large prominent nucleoli
    - Frequent mitoses, some atypical
    - Magenta stroma may be seen between the pale blue cytoplasm of the malignant cells
* [Secretory carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantjuvenile.html)
  + Low power:
    - Moderate to high cellularity
    - Cohesive or loosely cohesive sheets and small tissue fragments
  + Background:
    - Abundant intensely staining bubbly secretory material
    - Stripped mildly atypical nuclei
  + High power:
    - Round to polygonal cells with abundant cytoplasm
    - Round, mildly atypical nuclei with fine to moderately coarse chromatin
    - Prominent intracytoplasmic vacuoles
    - Occasional signet ring cells or plasmacytoid cells
* [Glycogen rich clear cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantglycogenrich.html)
  + Low power:
    - Moderate to high cellularity
    - Small, loosely cohesive syncytial sheets and single cells
  + Background:
    - Finely granular tigroid background
  + High power:
    - Moderate to marked nuclear pleomorphism
    - Finely granular, cleared, eccentric cytoplasm
    - Well defined cell membranes
    - Koilocyte-like cells
    - Focal apocrine change
* [Adenoid cystic carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantadenoidcystic.html)
  + Low power:
    - Moderate cellularity
    - Variably sized epithelial sheets and 3D tissue fragments
  + Background:
    - Many dispersed epithelial cells
    - Stripped nuclei
  + High power:
    - Basaloid epithelial cells with poorly defined, scant cytoplasm
    - Mildly atypical nuclei with single small nucleoli
    - Rounded hyaline globules that are magenta with Giemsa and pale gray-green with Pap stain
* [Apocrine carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantapocrine.html)
  + Low power:
    - Moderate to high cellularity
    - Crowded, small, discohesive tissue fragments and dispersed single cells
  + Background:
    - Necrosis
    - Calcifications (intraductal component)
  + High power:
    - Large pleomorphic cells with granular, well defined cytoplasm
    - Nuclei are hyperchromatic, with coarse chromatin and large nucleoli
* [Carcinoma with neuroendocrine features](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastNEC.html)
  + Low power:
    - High cellularity
    - Dispersed single cells, larger fragments with branching, thin fibrovascular cores
    - Poorly differentiated neuroendocrine carcinoma will resemble small cell carcinoma
    - Mucinous and solid papillary carcinomas can show neuroendocrine differentiation
  + Background:
    - No bare bipolar nuclei
    - No lymphoglandular bodies
  + High power:
    - Monotonous cells with bland, small, rounded nuclei, salt and pepper chromatin
    - Plasmacytoid cells with eccentric, pale, granular cytoplasm
* [Non-Hodgkin lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantlymphoma.html)
  + [Diffuse large B cell lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomadiffuse.html) is most common
    - About 67% of primary breast lymphomas
    - Dispersed single cells
    - Atypical large lymphoid cells resembling centroblasts or immunoblasts
      * Eccentric basophilic cytoplasm
    - Lymphoglandular bodies
  + [Marginal zone lymphoma involving mucosa associated lymphoid proliferations](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaMALT.html)
    - Heterogeneous lymphoid population
      * Plasmacytoid cells, lymphocytes, centrocytes, plasma cells, scattered larger lymphoid cells
    - Difficult to differentiate from reactive process
    - Core needle biopsy usually needed for ancillary studies like clonality
  + [Anaplastic large cell lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantimplantalcl.html)
    - Textured implants
    - New peri-implant fluid collection
    - Large lymphoid cells with large, atypical, hyperchromatic, with 1 or 2 nucleoli and small rim of cytoplasm
    - Hallmark horseshoe shaped cells
    - [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html) positive
* [Angiosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantangiosarcoma.html)
  + Low power:
    - Usually low cellularity but can be high
    - Small, loosely cohesive spindle cell fragments
    - Larger tissue fragments containing capillary-like structures
  + Background:
    - Hemorrhagic
  + High power:
    - Cell shape can be spindle and sarcomatous, or rounded and epithelioid
    - Variable but often marked nuclear atypia
    - Nuclei show nuclear indentations and folds and 1 or 2 nucleoli
    - Intracytoplasmic hemosiderin, vacuoles

Differential diagnosis

* Invasive carcinoma of no special type (ductal carcinoma):
  + [Ductal carcinoma in situ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantDCIS.html)
  + [Lobular carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantlobularclassic.html):
    - Eccentric, relatively dense cytoplasm containing single intracytoplasmic vacuoles
  + [Pleomorphic lobular carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantlobularpleomorphic.html)
  + [Apocrine carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantapocrine.html):
    - Cells have densely granular cytoplasm and well defined cell margins
  + [Granular cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastgct.html):
    - Finely granular background
    - Cells have even, coarsely granular cytoplasm (paler than apocrine cells)
    - Lower N:C ratio
    - Stromal fibrovascular tissue fragments
    - [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) positive, [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) negative
  + [Lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantlymphoma.html):
    - Dispersed pattern of lymphoid cells
    - Lymphoglandular bodies
  + [Metastatic tumors](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantmet.html)
  + [Radiation changes](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignanttreatmenteffectradiation.html):
    - Nuclear and cytoplasmic vacuolization
    - Blurring of chromatin
    - Preserved N:C ratio
    - Myoepithelial cells
    - Background of fat necrosis
* Tubular carcinoma:
  + [Fibroadenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastfibroadenoma.html):
    - Tubules in fibroadenoma lack a rigid architecture
    - Myoepithelial cells and bare bipolar nuclei
  + [Cribriform carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantcribriform.html):
    - Can be admixed with tubular carcinoma
  + [Sclerosing adenosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastsclerosingadenosis.html):
    - Small, cohesive ductular tissue fragments
    - Usually has myoepithelial cells
    - Some bare bipolar nuclei
* Carcinoma with medullary features:
  + [High grade lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantlymphoma.html):
    - Almost completely dispersed cells
    - Cells are smaller than cells of carcinoma with medullary features
    - Lymphoglandular bodies
  + **Carcinoma metastatic to an intramammary or axillary tail lymph node**:
    - Must correlate with imaging
  + [Lymphoepithelioma-like carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantLEL.html):
    - Rare
* Mucinous carcinoma:
  + [Mucocele-like lesion](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmucocele.html):
    - Tissue fragments have myoepithelial cells
    - Epithelial cell nuclei are bland
  + [Invasive carcinoma of no special type](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantductalNOS.html):
    - May have focal mucinous differentiation
    - Generally high grade nuclei
  + [Invasive micropapillary carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantmicropapillary.html):
    - May have an outer mucinous blush
    - Papillary tissue fragments that lack a fibrovascular core
  + [Fibroadenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastfibroadenoma.html):
    - May have a mucin-like finely granular background
    - Bare bipolar nuclei and fragments with myoepithelial cells
  + **Metastatic mucinous adenocarcinomas**
  + **Ultrasound gel**
* Invasive papillary carcinoma:
  + [Intraductal papilloma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastpapilloma.html):
    - Epithelium is hyperplastic and has bland nuclear features
    - Fewer dissociated columnar cells
* Invasive micropapillary carcinoma:
  + [Invasive carcinoma of no special type](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantductalNOS.html):
    - Lacks micropapillary architecture
  + [Intraductal papilloma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastpapilloma.html):
    - Epithelium is hyperplastic and has bland nuclear features
  + [Papillary DCIS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantDCIS.html):
    - More delicate branching fibrovascular architecture
    - Low grade nuclear atypia
* Metaplastic carcinoma:
  + [High grade phyllodes](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastphyllodesgeneral.html)
  + **Spindle cell lesions**
* High grade phyllodes:
  + [Metaplastic carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantmetaplastic.html)
  + **Spindle cell tumors**
* Secretory carcinoma:
  + [Lactational change](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastnormal.html#pregnancylactation):
    - Distinction may be difficult and clinical correlation is needed
  + [Cystic hypersecretory hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastcystichypersecretoryhyperplasia.html):
    - Background secretions are colloid-like and dense rather than bubbly
  + [Mucinous carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantmucinous.html):
    - Fibrillary mucin rather than bubbly extracellular material
* Glycogen rich clear cell carcinoma:
  + [Lipid rich carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantlipidrich.html):
    - Cytoplasmic vacuoles contain lipid ([oil red O](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoilredo.html) positive, [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html) negative)
  + [Apocrine carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantapocrine.html)
  + **Histiocytoid carcinoma**
  + **Metastatic clear cell carcinoma**
* Adenoid cystic carcinoma:
  + [Collagenous spherulosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastcollageneousspherulosis.html):
    - Bland epithelium with myoepithelial cells
    - Bare bipolar nuclei
  + [Pleomorphic adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmixedtumor.html):
    - Rare in the breast
    - Prominent myxofibrillary stroma
    - Bland epithelial component
* Apocrine carcinoma:
  + [Apocrine metaplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastapocrinemetaplasia.html):
    - Retains an ordered arrangement of nuclei and low N:C ratio
    - Nuclei have minimal pleomorphism and smaller nucleoli
* Carcinoma with neuroendocrine features:
  + **Metastatic neuroendocrine tumors / carcinomas**:
    - Immunostains and correlation with patient history
* Angiosarcoma:
  + **Granulation tissue**:
    - May have similar low power features but without nuclear atypia
  + **Spindle cell lesions**

Board review style question #1

[![BRQ image](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/breastyokohamasystemmalignantZalaznick08.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/breastyokohamasystemmalignantZalaznick08.jpg)

Which of the following is a malignant feature demonstrated in this breast fine needle aspiration (FNA) smear?

1. Bare bipolar nuclei
2. Centrally located nucleus
3. Discohesion and single cells
4. Monomorphic nuclei
5. Salt and pepper chromatin

Board review style answer #1

**C.** This image shows marked discohesion and single cells, which is a feature of malignancy in breast carcinoma. A few cells with intracytoplasmic lumens are also present. Answers A and B are not features of malignancy. D and E are not demonstrated in this image.

Board review style question #2

A breast fine needle aspiration biopsy (FNAB) smear shows many uniform single cells, moderately enlarged nuclei, eccentric cytoplasm and scattered intracytoplasmic vacuoles with targetoid mucin droplets. Which subtype of carcinoma is most likely?

1. Lobular carcinoma
2. Metaplastic carcinoma
3. Mucinous carcinoma
4. Neuroendocrine tumor
5. Secretory carcinoma

Board review style answer #2

**A.** This description best fits lobular carcinoma
