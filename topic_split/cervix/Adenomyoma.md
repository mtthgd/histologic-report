Definition / general

* Biphasic tumor composed of Müllerian type glands and smooth muscle typically located in the endocervix; rarely, mesonephric type glands can be found ([Mod Pathol 1996;9:220](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8685218))
* Rare, though likely underdiagnosed

Essential features

* Usually an incidental finding in women of reproductive age (mean age: 40 years)
* Presents as a polyp or mass protruding through the external cervical os, which may result in abnormal bleeding or vaginal discharge
* Well circumscribed neoplasm composed of irregularly shaped, benign, endocervical type glands often in a lobular arrangement, admixed with a smooth muscle component
* Epithelium lining the glands lacks cytologic atypia; mitoses are rare
* Benign, with an excellent prognosis if completely excised

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8932/0](https://iris.who.int/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=83) - adenomyoma
* ICD-11: [GA15.Y & XH4ZH4](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#551370781%2Fother%20%26%2020518834) - other specified acquired abnormalities of cervix uteri & adenomyoma

Epidemiology

* Typically premenopausal women (21 - 55 years; mean: 40 years) ([Mod Pathol 1996;9:220](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8685218))

Sites

* Endocervix

Pathophysiology

* Unknown at this time

Etiology

* Unknown at this time

Clinical features

* Usually an incidental finding discovered during a regular gynecologic examination
* May be asymptomatic
* May cause mucoid discharge or abnormal vaginal bleeding ([J Obstet Gynaecol Res 2007;33:363](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17578368))
* May present as a polyp prolapsing through the external cervical os ([J Minim Invasive Gynecol 2014;21:954](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24582628))
* Less frequently, there is cervical enlargement by a mural mass without mucosal involvement ([J Obstet Gynaecol Res 2007;33:363](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17578368))

Diagnosis

* Histologic examination is the cornerstone of diagnosis

Radiology description

* Imaging is performed for worrisome features (such as large size)
* Ultrasound (US) sonography may show a multicystic mass
* T2 weighted magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) shows a high intensity multicystic tumor; T1 weighted MRI shows multiple cysts with either slight low intensity or isointensity ([J Minim Invasive Gynecol 2014;21:954](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24582628), [J Obstet Gynaecol Res 2007;33:363](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17578368))

Prognostic factors

* Excellent prognosis if completely removed by local excision or simple hysterectomy
* May recur locally if excision is not complete

Case reports

* 28 and 35 year old women with polypoid adenomyoma of endocervical type ([IJBAMR 2015;4:162](https://www.ijbamr.com/assets/images/issues/pdf/June%202015%20162-165.pdf.pdf))
* 33 year old woman, 8 weeks pregnant, with spotting and cervical protruding mass ([Iran J Med Sci 2019;44:427](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31582868))
* Premenopausal woman in her 50s referred to the hospital because of an increasingly watery vaginal discharge ([Radiol Case Rep 2024;19:3268](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38812595))

* 10 cervical adenomyomas (30 - 55 year old women) of endocervical type ([Histopathology 2015;66:420](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25219586))

Treatment

* Local excision or simple hysterectomy

Gross description

* Frequently polypoid and well circumscribed, unencapsulated and solitary; may be intramural
* Well demarcated
* Firm, gray-tan cut surface
* Presence of cysts (usually Mod Pathol 1996;9:220)

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Irregularly shaped and sized Müllerian type glands, surrounded by variable amounts of bland smooth muscle ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2014;33:330](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24901392))
* Lobular arrangement of large glands that may show papillary infolding; purely endocervical glands are usually smaller
* Epithelium lining the glands may show tubal, endometrioid, squamous and (rarely) mesonephric differentiation ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2000;19:195](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10907166))
* Smooth muscle component merges with the preexisting wall of smooth muscle at the border of the lesion
* Bland cytology and absent / rare mitoses
* No desmoplastic response is evident

Cytology description

* Clusters of individual endocervical or endometrial type cells and smooth muscle cells with delicate cytoplasm, oval nuclei and nucleoli ([Diagn Cytopathol 2000;22:176](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10679999))
* Smooth muscle cells may have large and irregular nuclei but no mitotic figures or necrosis
* Metaplastic and reactive endocervical cells in an inflammatory background can be present as well

Positive stains

* Intracytoplasmic mucin: [Alcian blue](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalcianblue.html) / [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html)
* Endocervical epithelium: [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html), luminal [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html) (variable), [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html), [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html), [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html)
* Smooth muscle: [α smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) and [h-caldesmon](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscaldesmon.html)
* [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) and [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html) negative in mesonephric epithelium if present; [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) positive with luminal pattern
* Reference: [Case Rep Pathol 2014;2014:275421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25105047)

Negative stains

* Glandular epithelium negative for [HIK1083](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshik1083.html) is useful to distinguish from gastric type adenocarcinoma

Sample pathology report

* Uterus, hysterectomy:
  + Cervical adenomyoma (see comment)
  + Comment: The cervix contains a polypoid, well circumscribed, nonencapsulated lesion composed of cystically dilated glands. The glands are lined by benign looking epithelium with endocervical and tubal differentiation, admixed with a smooth muscle component, without atypia.

Differential diagnosis

* [Gastric type adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixGAS.html):
  + Ill defined mass or diffuse wall thickening
  + Irregular, haphazardly arranged glands with infiltrative growth
  + At least focal cytologic atypia and stromal desmoplastic reaction
  + Glands lined by cells with abundant cytoplasm reminiscent of pyloric type epithelium
  + [HIK1083](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshik1083.html), [MUC6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmuc2.html#muc6) and cytoplasmic [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html) positive
  + [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) and [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html) negative
  + [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) mutant in 50%
  + Lymphovascular and perineural invasion
* [Müllerian adenosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixadenosarcoma.html):
  + Phyllodiform glands and intracystic papillary projections
  + Moderately high cellularity with atypical stromal cells, mitoses and periglandular condensation
  + Minimal or absent smooth muscle differentiation
* [Lobular endocervical glandular hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixlobularendocerv.html):
  + Rarely polypoid
  + Lobular arrangement of hyperplastic small / medium sized, rounded gastric type glands lined mostly by single layer of columnar, mucin rich epithelium
  + Lacks a notable smooth muscle component
* [Atypical polypoid adenomyoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixapa.html):
  + Typically small, situated in the lower uterine segment
  + Architecturally complex endometrial glands with mild to moderate atypia and frequent squamous morular metaplasia, embedded in benign myomatous / fibromyomatous stroma
  + Stromal cells are negative for [h-caldesmon](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscaldesmon.html); may show nuclear positivity for [SATB2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssatb2.html)
* [Endocervicosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderendocervicosis.html):
  + Benign endocervical type mucinous glands of the outer (usually anterior) cervical wall
  + Patients of reproductive age; asymptomatic or with pelvic pain
  + Nodular, without circumscription or smooth muscle component
  + Variably sized and shaped glands lined by benign endocervical type epithelium

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/cervixadenomyomagiacometti02.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/cervixadenomyomagiacometti02.jpg)

This lesion was found in the hysterectomy specimen of a 36 year old woman who underwent surgery for uterine fibromas. The lesion presented as a polypoid cervical mass of 2.5 cm. Immunohistochemistry demonstrated a stromal component positive for smooth muscle actin and h-caldesmon. Ki67 was extremely low both in the glandular and in the stromal component. CEA was negative. Which of the following is a possible diagnosis?

1. Adenomyoma
2. Endocervicosis
3. Minimal deviation adenocarcinoma
4. Müllerian adenosarcoma

Board review style answer #1

**A**. Adenomyoma. The images show an admixture of benign looking glands, with different sizes and shapes, admixed with smooth muscle. CEA negativity excludes adenoma malignum (minimal deviation adenocarcinoma) and h-caldesmon positivity excludes atypical polypoid adenomyoma. Answer C is incorrect because minimal deviation adenocarcinoma (adenoma malignum) is composed of irregular glands with infiltrative growth pattern and reactive stromal desmoplasia. Answer B is incorrect because endocervicosis lacks a muscular component. Answer D is incorrect because Müllerian adenosarcoma typically has phylloid-like glands associated with atypical stromal cells.

Board review style question #2

Which of the following immunohistochemistry profiles would be expected in adenomyoma?

1. CEA and CD10 positive
2. ER positive, HER2 negative and Ki67 low
3. ER, PR, αSMA, PAX8 positive and HIK1083 negative
4. ER, PR, MUC6 positive and Ki67 high

Board review style answer #2

**C**. ER, PR, αSMA, PAX8 positive and HIK1083 negative. The most important differential diagnosis for adenomyoma is adenoma malignum, which is positive for HIK1083 (note that adenoma malignum can be positive for CD10, Ki67, αSMA and MUC6 but can be differentiated from adenomyoma by HIK1083, which is negative in the glandular epithelium of adenomyoma). Answer A is incorrect because CEA might be positive (luminal) but is not useful to differentiate other entities (especially adenoma malignum) and CD10 is positive only if mesonephric differentiation is present. Answer B is incorrect because Ki67 can be variably expressed and HER2 is not appropriate in this context. Answer D is incorrect because Ki67 can be variably expressed and MUC6 is also expressed in adenoma malignum.
