Definition / general

* Uncommon condition in cervix
* Patients are usually NOT immunocompromised ([J Clin Pathol 2004;57:691](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15220359))
* Viral shedding common in HIV+ women ([J Med Virol 1999;59:469](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10534728))

Epidemiology

* Rarely seen in Pap test
* Associated with HIV1 infection ([J Med Virol 1999; 59:469](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10534728))
* Some cases have no associated risk factors ([J Clin Pathol 2004; 57:691](http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15220359), [Cytopathology 1993; 4:237](http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8400058))

Case reports

* 16 year old girl ([Diagn Cytopathol 2009;37:836](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19526575))
* 20 year old woman with endocervical biopsy ([Pathology 1991;23:318](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1664515))

Treatment

* No treatment in healthy people

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Large, basophilic intranuclear inclusions or intracytoplasmic eosinophilic inclusions in occasional endocervical glandular epithelial cells
* Inclusions also in endothelial and stromal cells but not squamous cells
* Associated with fibrin thrombi, dense acute inflammatory infiltrate, lymphoid follicles, vacuoles in glandular cells, reactive changes in glandular epithelial cells

Cytology description

* Involvement of glandular and squamous cells
* Cellular enlargement, nuclear enlargement and large nuclear inclusion surrounded by a halo
  "owl's eye"
* Occasionally smaller nuclear or cytoplasmic inclusions
