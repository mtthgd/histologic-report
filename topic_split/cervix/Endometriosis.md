Definition / general

* May cause abnormal uterine bleeding, postcoital bleeding
* Mean age 37 years, range 20 to 51 years
* Superficial endometriosis may be due to mechanical disruption of endometrium after D & C or cone biopsy

Case reports

* 46 year old woman with myxoid endometriosis simulating pseudomyxoma peritonea ([Am J Surg Pathol 1994;18:849](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8037300))
* 47 year old woman with superficial cervical endometriosis with florid smooth muscle metaplasia ([Br Med J 1975;2:87](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1131562))

Gross description

* Red / blue nodules

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Similar to endometriosis elsewhere
* Two of three present - endometrial glands with basal nuclei, spindled stroma, hemorrhage
* Usually involves superficial third of cervical wall, not deep wall
* Glands are evenly spaced and without atypia, are surrounded by stroma at least focally
* Inflammation and hemorrhage may obscure endometrial storm
* May have prominent mitotic activity
* No thick collagen bundles

Endometriosis

Cytology description

* Endometrial type cells in solid and cohesive clusters (may be tridimensional) with crowded, overlapping glandular groups, loss of cellular polarity and a frequent ragged "feathered" edge appearance with protruding nuclei ([Diagn Cytopathol 2004;30:88](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14755757))
* Also hemosiderin laden macrophages and sometimes spindle stromal cells
* No pseudostratified cell strips ([Diagn Cytopathol 1999;21:188](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10450104))

Positive stains

* [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html)
* [Reticulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsreticulin.html) surrounds each cell ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2001;20:173](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11293164))

Differential diagnosis

* [Adenocarcinoma in situ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixAIS.html), [invasive carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixadenocarcinoma.html): no endometrial stroma, marked atypia
* [Endocervical glandular dysplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixendocervglandatypia.html)
* [Tuboendometrial metaplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixmetaplasia.html#tuboendometrial)

**Cytology differential diagnosis:**

Stromal endometriosis

Definition / general

* Endometriotic stroma only with no/rare glands
* Mean age 43 years, range 29 to 64 years

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Well circumscribed foci within cervical superficial stroma containing endometrial stromal cells, small blood vessels, extravasated RBCs
* Usually no endometrial type glands

Differential diagnosis

* [Kaposi's sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuekaposi.html): [Pathology 1997;29:426](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9423229)
* [Low grade endometrial stromal sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusESSlowgrade.html)

Additional references

* [Am J Surg Pathol 1990;14:449](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2327550)
