Definition / general

* Resembles [nasopharyngeal counterpart](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/nasalnasopharyngealgeneral.html)
* Usually younger patients than squamous cell carcinoma of cervix
* In the nasopharynx, lymphoepithelioma-like carcinoma is associated with Epstein Barr virus (EBV) infection
  + Such association in the cervix appears to be less clear: usually EBV+ in Asian patients ([Cancer 1997;80:91](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9210713)); EBV- in non Asian patients ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2002;126:1501](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12456211))
* EBV is not directly involved in squamous cell malignant transformation but may have an effect by infecting the inflammatory component
* An association with HPV infection has also been postulated
* Tends to be low stage at diagnosis; better outcome compared to conventional squamous cell carcinoma of the cervix ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2009;28:279](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19620947), [Cancer 1997;80:91](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9210713))

Case reports

* 21 year old black woman, EBV- ([Am J Clin Pathol 1993;99:195](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8382447))
* 32 year old woman with ectopic production and localization of beta hCG ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2000;19:179](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10782417))
* 44 year old white woman in Netherlands, EBV- but HPV+ ([Gynecol Oncol 2005;97:716](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15863191))
* Two EBV- but HPV+ cases ([Hum Pathol 2001;32:135](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11172309))

Gross description

* Usually exophytic

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Tumor is usually moderately to poorly differentiated and nonkeratinizing
* Cells are usually loose or arranged in small clusters, have variable amounts of eosinophilic cytoplasm and marked nuclear atypia
* Syncytium of large tumor cells with eosinophilic cytoplasm, vesicular nuclei, prominent nucleoli
* Prominent lymphoplasmacytic infiltration predominantly composed of T lymphocytes; the inflammatory component can be very dense and obscure the squamous cell component, in which case cytokeratin stains may be needed
* Pushing margins

Vagina: well circumscribed tumor

Cytology description

* Uniform large tumor cells, indistinct cell borders, finely granular scant cytoplasm, round / oval nuclei and prominent nucleoli, marginated chromatin
* No koilocytes, no gland formation, no keratinization ([Acta Cytol 1999;43:285](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10097727))

* [AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html), [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html), [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html), [MIB1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html)
* Variable [beta-hCG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshcg.html), [focal HER2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbreasther2.html)

Negative stains

* Lymphoid markers (stain infiltrating lymphocytes only), [bcl2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html), [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html), [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* May have EBV false positives due to EBV+ lymphocytes ([Neoplasma 2003;50:8](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12687272),
  [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2009;28:279](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19620947))

Differential diagnosis

* [Glassy cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixglassycell.html) with lymphocytic infiltrate
* Poorly differentiated [squamous cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixSCC.html)
