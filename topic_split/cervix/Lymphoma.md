Definition / general

* Primaries are rare in cervix (< 100 cases reported)

Clinical features

* Mean age approximately 40 years; range 20's to 80's
* Usually presents with abnormal uterine or vaginal bleeding
* Cervical smear may be first indication of tumor but smear is often negative or reported as SIL
* Most cases present with stage IE disease ([Am J Obstet Gynecol 2005;193:866](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16150291))
* Usually diffuse large B cell lymphoma or follicular lymphoma ([Mod Pathol 2000;13:19](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10658906))
* 5 year survival: 83% in low stage tumors, 29% in high stage tumors
* Should confirm with immunostains to rule out other unusual tumors and to classify

Case reports

* 30 and 45 year old women with NK lymphoma ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2000;124:1510](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11035585%20))
* 32 year old woman with sarcomatoid variant of B cell lymphoma ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2003;22:289](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12819398))
* 38 year old woman with precursor T cell acute lymphoblastic leukemia/lymphoma ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2002;6:125](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12004362))
* 42 year old woman with Burkitt lymphoma with HSIL ([Pathol Res Pract 2005;201:521](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16164048))
* 46 year old woman with MALT lymphoma presenting as endocervical polyp ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2001;125:537](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11260632%20))
* 49 and 51 year old women with HSIL by pap smear and diffuse large B cell lymphoma and follicular lymphoma ([Gynecol Oncol 2005;98:484](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15992915))
* 51 year old woman with relapse in cervix ([Leuk Lymphoma 2002;43:203](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11908731))
* 52 year old woman with squamous cell carcinoma ([Gynecol Oncol 2004;92:974](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14984969))
* Detection of HTLV1 in adult T cell leukemia/lymphoma by PCR on cytologic smears ([Methods Mol Biol 2005;304:183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16061975))
* Two patients with primary diffuse large B cell lymphoma ([Eur J Gynaecol Oncol 2005;26:36](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15754997))
* Two patients with diffuse large B cell lymphoma and follicular lymphoma at biopsy but HSIL by pap smear ([Gynecol Oncol 2005;98:484](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15992915))
* Three patients with atypical lymphoid cells at pap smear and diffuse large B cell lymphoma ([Am J Hematol 2003;73:176](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12827654))

Gross description

* Diffuse enlargement of cervix (barrel shaped), or polypoid mass with fish flesh appearance
* Soft, gray white

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Tumor cells infiltrate stroma without destroying glandular or squamous epithelium

Cytology description

* Round, loosely arranged neoplastic cells with scanty cytoplasm ([Cytopathology 1996;7:204](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8782993))

Differential diagnosis

* [Chronic cervicitis:](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixchroniccervicitis.html) ThinPrep specimens may show clumps of lymphocytes ([Cytopathology 2002;13:364](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12485172))
* [Granulocytic sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/leukemiagranulocytic.html): positive for myeloperoxidase, lysozyme, CD68, negative for lymphocytic markers
* **Infectious mononucleosis or other reactive changes:** polymorphic infiltrate with plasma cells and neutrophils ([Gynecol Oncol 2005;99:481](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16054203))
* [Lymphoid follicles of chronic cervicitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixchroniccervicitis.html)
