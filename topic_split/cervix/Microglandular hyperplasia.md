Definition / general

* Benign, nonneoplastic endocervical glandular proliferation

Essential features

* Benign lesion not requiring treatment
* Usually incidental microscopic finding in reproductive women
* Associated with hormonal exposure (pregnancy, postpartum, oral contraceptive pills, hormone replacement therapy)
* May mimic cervical and endometrial adenocarcinomas

Terminology

* Microglandular adenosis
* Microglandular change

Epidemiology

* Common in reproductive age women, rarely postmenopausal
* Typically incidental

Sites

* Cervix

Etiology

* Most likely due to hormonal exposure (pregnancy, postpartum, progestogen therapy) ([J Low Genit Tract Dis 2016;20:162](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26796659))

Clinical features

* Usually asymptomatic
* May present with contact bleeding
* May form solitary or multiple polypoid masses ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2010;134:393](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20196667))
* May involve an endocervical polyp

Diagnosis

* Microscopic examination

Prognostic factors

* Excellent prognosis

Case reports

* 19 year old woman with atypical endocervical microglandular hyperplasia and ligneous cervicitis associated with plasminogen deficiency ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2013;32:329](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23518917))
* 30 year old woman with atypical endocervical microglandular hyperplasia associated with plasminogen deficiency ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2021;40:224](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32897963))

Treatment

* Not required

Gross description

* Most often unremarkable
* May be multifocal
* Rarely, polypoid and ulcerated ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2010;134:393](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20196667))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Solitary or multiple polypoid lesions
* Often superficial location
* Complex proliferation of back to back tubular or cystically dilated glands with scant intervening stroma
* Intraluminal mucin with acute inflammation
* Mixed stromal inflammatory infiltrate
* May be associated with immature or mature squamous metaplasia
* Glands are lined by bland cuboidal, columnar or flattened cells with subnuclear and supranuclear vacuoles (may not be well developed in some cases), indistinct nucleoli, absent or rare (≤ 3 per 10 high power fields) mitoses ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2003;22:261](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12819393))
* Variable reserve cells or immature squamous cells beneath endocervical cells
* Rare unusual features (atypical microglandular hyperplasia):
  + Solid, reticular, trabecular or pseudoinfiltrative growth ([Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:50](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2535775))
  + Abundant myxoid or hyalinized stroma and mucin pools
  + Signet ring or hobnail cells ([Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:50](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2535775))
  + Cytologic atypia with nuclear enlargement and prominent nucleoli ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2021;40:224](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32897963), [Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:50](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2535775))
  + Increased mitoses ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2014;33:524](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25083971), [Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:50](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2535775))

Cytology description

* Nonspecific changes
* Spectrum of benign appearing to bi or tridimensional clusters of cuboidal or columnar glandular cells with vacuolated cytoplasm, microlumina or fenestration ([Acta Cytol 1999;43:110](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10097694))
* Admixture of cells with immature squamous metaplasia and reserve cells with scant cytoplasm and small, round nuclei ([Diagn Cytopathol 2004;30:57](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14696147), [Acta Cytol 1999;43:110](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10097694))
* Inflammatory cells
* Glandular cells may show cytologic atypia with enlarged, hyperchromatic, crowded nuclei, smooth nuclear contours, fine chromatin, sometimes multiple nucleoli, mitoses, apoptotic bodies and watery diathesis, mimicking high grade squamous intraepithelial lesion ([Diagn Cytopathol 1994;10:326](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7924805), [Acta Cytol 2000;44:661](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10934963))

Sample pathology report

* Endocervix, curettings:
  + Fragments of benign endocervical tissue
  + (Microglandular hyperplasia need not be reported; may be included in microscopic description)

Differential diagnosis

* [Endometrioid adenocarcinoma, microglandular pattern (microglandular hyperplasia-like endometrioid adenocarcinoma)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusendometrioid.html):
  + Usually postmenopausal women
  + At least some cytologic atypia and mitotic activity, stromal foamy macrophages, luminal mature squamous metaplasia
  + Positive for [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html), loss of [PAX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax2.html), high [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) index in some studies ([Am J Surg Pathol 1992;16:1092](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1471729), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2003;22:261](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12819393), [Ann Diagn Pathol 2003;7:180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12808571), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2015;34:90](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25473758))
* [Clear cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixclearcell.html):
  + Admixed growth patterns (solid, tubulocystic, papillary), cytologic atypia, mitotic figures, minimal inflammation, no subnuclear and supranuclear vacuoles, no reserve cells or squamous metaplasia
  + Positive for [HNF-1B](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsHNF1B.html) and [Napsin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnapsina.html), negative for [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) and [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html)
* [Endocervical adenocarcinoma, usual type](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixhpvadenocarcinoma.html):
  + Infiltrative growth, cytologic atypia, mitoses, apoptotic bodes, no subnuclear and supranuclear vacuoles, no reserve cells or squamous metaplasia
  + Positive for [p16](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp16.html), [HPV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixHPV.html), high [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) index
* [Endocervical adenocarcinoma in situ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixAIS.html):
  + Pseudostratified, hyperchromatic, crowded nuclei, mitoses, apoptotic bodes, no subnuclear and supranuclear vacuoles, no reserve cells or squamous metaplasia
  + Positive for [p16](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp16.html), [HPV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixHPV.html)

Which of the following entities is the closest mimic of microglandular hyperplasia?

1. Endometrial serous carcinoma
2. Nabothian cysts
3. Clear cell carcinoma
4. Minimal deviation adenocarcinoma
5. Endometrial endometrioid carcinoma, microglandular pattern (microglandular hyperplasia-like endometrioid adenocarcinoma)

Board review style answer #1

**E.** Endometrial endometrioid carcinoma, microglandular pattern (microglandular hyperplasia-like endometrioid adenocarcinoma)

Board review style question #2

Which of the following statements is true for microglandular hyperplasia?

1. Microglandular hyperplasia is a nonobligate precursor of usual type endocervical adenocarcinoma
2. Microglandular hyperplasia is often seen in patients with Cowden syndrome
3. Microglandular hyperplasia is associated with increased risk of clear cell carcinoma
4. Microglandular hyperplasia is often associated with endometrial endometrioid adenocarcinoma, microglandular pattern
5. Microglandular hyperplasia is usually an incidental finding in women of reproductive age

Board review style answer #2

**E.** Microglandular hyperplasia is usually an incidental finding in women of reproductive age
