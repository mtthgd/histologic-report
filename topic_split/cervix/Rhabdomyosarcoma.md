Definition / general

* Rare sarcoma showing evidence of skeletal muscle differentiation
* Multiple subtypes: embryonal, alveolar and pleomorphic

Essential features

* Most common variant in the gynecologic tract is embryonal, which typically presents as a polypoid vaginal or cervical mass in children and adolescents
* Rhabdomyosarcoma originating in the uterine corpus, fallopian tube or ovary is less common and occurs over a wider age range
* Presence of rhabdomyoblasts, typically expressing MyoD1 or myogenin, is essential for diagnosis

Terminology

* Sarcoma botryoides: refers to the macroscopic appearance of embryonal rhabdomyosarcoma (i.e. polypoid mass resembling a cluster of grapes)

ICD coding

* ICD-10:
  + [C52](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C51-C58/C52-/C52) - malignant neoplasm of vagina
  + [C53.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C51-C58/C53-/C53.9) - malignant neoplasm of cervix uteri, unspecified
  + [C54.2](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C51-C58/C54-/C54.2) - malignant neoplasm of myometrium
  + [C56.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C51-C58/C56-/C56.9) - malignant neoplasm of unspecified ovary
  + [C57.0](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C51-C58/C57-/C57.0) - malignant neoplasm of fallopian tube

Epidemiology

* Dependent upon subtype and location:
  + Embryonal (most common)
    - Vagina: most common vaginal cancer in children < 5 years of age
    - Uterine cervix: peak incidence 10 - 19 years but may occur in older women
    - Uterine corpus: middle aged and older women
  + Alveolar, pleomorphic (rare)
    - Wide age range, usually older women
* References: [Int J Gynecol Cancer 2008;18:190](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17511807), [Gynecol Oncol 1988;29:290](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3278956), [Hum Pathol 2018;74:122](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29320751)

Sites

* Most common: cervix and vagina
* Rarely occurs in the uterine corpus, fallopian tube and ovary

Pathophysiology

* *DICER1* alterations are identified in 65 - 95% of embryonal rhabdomyosarcomas of the uterine cervix, with ~50% of these patients harboring germline alterations; conversely, most *DICER1* alterations in uterine corpus tumors are somatic ([Mod Pathol 2020;33:1207](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31900434), [Mod Pathol 2021 May 20 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34017064), [Am J Surg Pathol 2020;44:738](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31990691))
* ~80% of alveolar rhabdomyosarcomas demonstrate recurrent fusions involving *FOXO1*, most commonly t(2;13)(*PAX3-FOXO1*) or t(1;13)(*PAX7-FOXO1*) ([Cancer Lett 2008;270:10](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18457914))

Etiology

* Subset of embryonal rhabdomyosarcomas, particularly of the uterine cervix, occur as a component of DICER1 syndrome with germline *DICER1* mutations

Clinical features

* Dependent on tumor location and subtype
* Tumors of the vagina, uterine cervix and corpus may present with vaginal bleeding
* Embryonal rhabdomyosarcomas form a polypoid, grape-like cluster in the vagina and uterus which may protrude from the vagina ([Am J Obstet Gynecol 1970;107:484](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4915719), [Gynecol Oncol 1988;29:290](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3278956))
* Pleomorphic and alveolar rhabdomyosarcomas are more likely to form a solitary mass centered in the myometrium, cervical stroma or ovary ([Hum Pathol 2018;74:122](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29320751), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 1998;17:113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9553806))

Diagnosis

* Based on morphologic features and the identification of rhabdomyoblasts (by morphology or immunohistochemistry)
* Confirmatory genetic testing can be helpful in select cases
* References: [Int J Gynecol Cancer 2008;18:190](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17511807), [Gynecol Oncol 1988;29:290](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3278956), [Hum Pathol 2018;74:122](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29320751)

Radiology description

* Embryonal rhabdomyosarcoma appears as a heterogenous mass, with cystic and solid components on ultrasound; CT demonstrates a similar appearance to that seen on ultrasound ([Radiographics 1997;17:919](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9225391))

Prognostic factors

* Overall 5 year survival for all subtypes: 68.4% ([Arch Gynecol Obstet 2017;296:327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28634755))
* Favorable prognostic factors:
  + Embryonal histology ([Arch Gynecol Obstet 2017;296:327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28634755))
  + Young age ([Arch Gynecol Obstet 2017;296:327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28634755))
* Poor prognostic factors:
  + Adult age ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:382](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17325479))
  + Alveolar rhabdomyosarcomas with proven *FOXO1* fusions
    - Alveolar rhabdomyosarcomas without *FOXO1* fusions have a prognosis similar to embryonal rhabdomyosarcoma ([Pediatr Blood Cancer 2016;63:634](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26756883))
  + Pleomorphic subtype ([Cancer Med 2018;7:4023](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29956493))
  + Anaplasia in cases of embryonal rhabdomyosarcoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 1993;17:443](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8470759))

Case reports

* 18 month old girl with an abdominal pelvic mass, vaginal bleeding and a protruding mass in the introitus ([J Surg Case Rep 2017;2017:rjx080](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28469838))
* 10 year old girl with a history of cystic nephroma and 3 week history of vaginal bleeding ([J Pediatr Adolesc Gynecol 2020;33:173](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31838154))
* 24 year old woman with a history of cervical embryonal rhabdomyosarcoma and a synchronous ovarian Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor ([Gynecol Oncol Rep 2018;25:94](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30014022))
* 54 year old postmenopausal woman with abdominal pain and vaginal bleeding ([Cureus 2020;12:e9841](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32953348))

Treatment

* Embryonal rhabdomyosarcoma may be treated with a combination of surgery, chemotherapy and radiation therapy ([Int J Gynecol Cancer 2008;18:190](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17511807))
* Limited data in treating adults, nonembryonal subtypes and noncervicovaginal sites in the female genital tract
  + Protocols are typically extrapolated from nongynecologic sites and involve a combination of resection (total hysterectomy and bilateral salpingo-oophorectomy) and chemotherapy with or without radiation ([Int J Radiat Oncol Biol Phys 2013;86:58](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23414767))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Embryonal rhabdomyosarcoma ([Gynecol Oncol 1988;29:290](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3278956), [Mod Pathol 2012;25:602](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22157934), [Mod Pathol 2021 May 20 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34017064))
  + Alternating hypercellular areas and hypocellular areas with myxoid / edematous (more common) or collagenous (less common) stroma
  + Densely cellular subepithelial zone (cambium layer) composed of primitive, small cells with hyperchromatic nuclei and mitotic activity
  + Rhabdomyoblasts typically seen in hypocellular foci
    - Eccentric, dense eosinophilic cytoplasm
    - Elongated strap cells (bipolar) or tadpole cells (unipolar) with eosinophilic cytoplasm and cross striations
  + Hyaline or fetal type cartilage in ~50% of cases
* Alveolar rhabdomyosarcoma ([Hum Pathol 2018;74:122](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29320751))
  + Nests and sheets of primitive small round cells with abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm
  + Noncohesive cells floating in empty spaces create characteristic alveolar pattern
* Pleomorphic rhabdomyosarcoma ([Hum Pathol 2018;74:122](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29320751), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2010;29:122](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20173498))
  + Sheet-like growth of pleomorphic round to spindled cells
  + Scattered large rhabdomyoblasts

Positive stains

* [Desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html): diffusely positive in all subtypes
* [Myogenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html) and [MyoD1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyod1.html)
  + High sensitivity and specificity
  + Diffuse nuclear staining in alveolar and pleomorphic subtypes
  + Typically focal in embryonal
* [Myoglobin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html): less sensitive than [myogenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html) and [MyoD1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyod1.html)
  + Also positive in mature tumors (rhabdomyoma)
* Reference: [Am J Surg Pathol 2001;25:1150](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11688574)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Embryonal rhabdomyosarcoma
  + *DICER1* alterations are identified in 65 - 95% of embryonal rhabdomyosarcomas of the uterine cervix, with ~50% of these patients harboring germline alterations; conversely, most *DICER1* alterations in uterine corpus tumors are somatic ([Mod Pathol 2020;33:1207](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31900434), [Mod Pathol 2021 May 20 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34017064), [Am J Surg Pathol 2020;44:738](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31990691))
* Alveolar rhabdomyosarcoma
  + Recurrent fusions of *FOXO1* gene in ~80%, most commonly t(2;13)(*PAX3-FOXO1*) or t(1;13)(*PAX7-FOXO1*) ([Cancer Lett 2008;270:10](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18457914))
* Pleomorphic rhabdomyosarcoma
  + Often complex karyotype without consistent genetic abnormality ([Cancer Genet Cytogenet 2003;140:73](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12550764))

Sample pathology report

* Cervix, polypectomy:
  + Embryonal rhabdomyosarcoma (see comment)
  + Comment: The tumor demonstrates polypoid fragments with myxoid / edematous stroma and scattered aggregates of primitive cells. Clusters of differentiating rhabdomyoblasts with focal cross striations are seen, which are highlighted by an immunohistochemical stain for desmin. There is also focal positivity for myogenin and myoD1, supporting the above diagnosis.

Differential diagnosis

* [Müllerian adenosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusadenosarcoma.html):
  + Most important differential consideration for botryoid embryonal rhabdomyosarcoma due to polypoid architecture and consolidation of stroma underneath epithelium
  + Typically shows more abundant epithelial elements with phyllodes-like architecture
  + Stromal overgrowth may make epithelial component difficult to identify but extensive sampling often reveals areas of classic adenosarcoma
* [Fibroepithelial polyp, especially those with atypical cells](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/vaginafibroepithelial.html):
  + Important differential for embryonal rhabdomyosarcoma of the vagina
  + Lacks expression of skeletal muscle markers ([MyoD1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyod1.html), [myogenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html))
  + Lacks cambium layer and primitive small cells
* [Malignant mixed Müllerian tumor (MMMT) with rhabdomyoblastic differentiation](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusmmmt.html):
  + Extensive sampling typically reveals the malignant epithelial component
* [Undifferentiated endometrial carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusundifferentiateddedifferentiatedcarcinoma.html) / [undifferentiated uterine sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterussarcoma.html):
  + Lacks expression of [MyoD1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyod1.html) / [myogenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html)
* [Uterine tumor resembling ovarian sex cord tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusUTROSCT.html):
  + *ESR1-NCOA2* fusions can show rhabdoid morphology ([Am J Surg Pathol 2020;44:1563](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32675660))
  + Sex cord-like morphology may provide clue to diagnosis (corded, retiform, trabeculated)
* **SMARCA4 deficient uterine sarcoma**:
  + Loss of [SMARCA4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsSMARCA4BRG1.html) (less commonly [INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html))
  + Negative for [MyoD1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyod1.html) / [myogenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html)
* [Rhabdomyoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueadultrhabdomyoma.html):
  + Lacks cambium layer and primitive small cells
