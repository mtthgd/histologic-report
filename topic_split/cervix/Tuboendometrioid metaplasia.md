Definition / general

* Common (1/3 of women)
* In upper portion of endocervical canal, often in deep glands ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 1992;11:89](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1582750))
* Often seen after cervical cone biopsy
* May represent response to injury
* Tubal epithelium is invariably seen at the cervix - isthmus junction and is not considered a metaplastic process at that site ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2013;32:122](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23202780))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Endocervix contains:
  + Ciliated cells (clear cytoplasm, abundant apical cilia and large, oval, variably hyperchromatic nuclei)
  + Secretory cells (nonciliated with dark eosinophilic or basophilic cytoplasm, apical cytoplasmic protrusions but no mucin vacuoles, basal nuclei)
  + Intercalated cells (also called peg cells, scant cytoplasm, thin and long nuclei), as found in normal fallopian tube
* Glands are regular
* Minimal mitotic activity, rare crowding or atypia
* Associated with endometrial type cells
* Usually near squamocolumnar junction, usually no inflammation
* May have cystic glands and periglandular stromal alterations suggestive of premalignant conditions or deep glands with periglandular edema suggestive of well differentiated adenocarcinoma but cells are ciliated with bland cytology, no mitotic figures, no definite desmoplastic stroma ([Am J Clin Pathol 1995;103:618](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7741110))

TEM and relevant stains

Positive stains

* Cytoplasmic expression of [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html) (negative in invasive and in situ adenocarcinoma) and [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) of < 10% has been noted in tuboenodmetrioid metaplasia ([J Clin Pathol 2003;56:164](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12610091))
* [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html) (not helpful in differential diagnosis below)
* [Vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html), [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) and [PAX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax2.html)

Negative staining - disease

* [p16](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp16.html) is considered to be negative or only focally positive or show a mosaic pattern in tubal metaplasia ([Adv Anat Pathol 2006;13:8](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16462152), [Cell Oncol 2007;29:37](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17429140)), useful to differentiate from high grade CIN and invasive cervical carcinomas that are diffusely positive

Cytology description

* See [endosalpingiosis - cytology](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixendosalpingiosis.html)

Differential diagnosis

* [Adenocarcinoma in situ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixAIS.html):
  + Tubal metaplasia may coexist with adenocarcinoma in situ and cases of AIS in a background of tubal metaplasia and a ciliated variant of adenocarcinoma in situ have been reported ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 1999;18:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9891235))
* [Endometrioid adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusendometrioid.html):
  + Invasive growth pattern, marked nuclear atypia, increased [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsKi67.html) staining
* [Endometriosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixendometriosis.html)
* [Mesonephric hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixmesonephrichyper.html)
* [Microglandular hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixMGH.html)
* Pseudoinfiltrative tuboendometrioid hyperplasia simulating [minimal deviation adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixGAS.html) has been reported in 3 cases of in utero diethyl stilbestrol exposure ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2005;24:391](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16175088))
* [Tunnel clusters](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixtunnelclusters.html)
