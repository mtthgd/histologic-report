Definition / general

* A meningioma of intermediate aggressiveness between benign and malignant forms, comprising 5 - 15% of meningiomas
* WHO grade 2
* Diagnostic criteria: fulfilling either 1 of 2 major criteria or 3 of 5 minor criteria
  + Major criteria:
    - 4 - 19 mitotic figures/10 high power fields
    - Brain invasion
  + Minor criteria:
    - Increased cellularity
    - Small cells with high N/C ratio
    - Large and prominent nucleoli
    - Patternless or sheet-like growth (loss of lobular architecture)
    - Foci of spontaneous or geographic necrosis

* Invasion of dura, bone or soft tissue does not affect grading
* Pleomorphic or atypical nuclei do not affect grade
* Ki67 is not a true diagnostic criteria; however, it is usually greater than 4% and up to 20%

Essential features

* Atypical meningiomas have an intermediate recurrence rate between benign and malignant meningiomas
* 29 - 52% recur (versus 7 - 25% of classic meningiomas and 50 - 94% of anaplastic meningiomas) ([Louis: WHO Classification of Tumours of the Central Nervous System, 4th Edition, 2016](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/9283244923/pathologyoutl-20))
* Molecular genetic and epigenetic signatures of atypical meningiomas are becoming increasingly important in predicting prognosis and new targeted therapy in progressive tumors

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [D32.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D32-/D32.9) - benign neoplasm of meninges, unspecified

Epidemiology

* Similar to [meningioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumormeningiomageneral.html), overall

Sites

* Intracranial, intraspinal or intraorbital

Pathophysiology

* Arising from the meningothelial cells or the arachnoid layer

Etiology

* Risk factors include male gender and prior surgery ([Louis: WHO Classification of Tumours of the Central Nervous System, 4th Edition, 2016](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/9283244923/pathologyoutl-20))
* Some risk factors may be similar to benign meningioma:
  + Ionizing radiation ([Acta Neuropathol 2017;134:155](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28474103))
  + Hormone replacement therapy or oral contraceptives ([J Clin Oncol 2008;26:279](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18182668), [J Neurosurg 2013;118:649](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23101448))
  + Germline mutations in *NF2* or *SMARCB1* predispose to multiple meningiomas ([Neurogenetics 2012;13:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22038540), [J Med Genet 2011;48:93](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20930055))

Clinical features

* Clinical presentation of atypical and anaplastic meningioma is similar to their benign counterpart
* Common symptoms include headaches, seizures and focal neurological deficit due to tumor compression ([Neurosurg Clin N Am 2016;27:239](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27012388))

Diagnosis

* Diagnose by imaging and pathology of biopsy / resection specimen

Radiology description

* Extra-axial mass with dural tail
* Uniformly contrast enhancing
* Extensive peritumoral edema is associated with brain invasion ([Neuro Oncol 2020 Aug 13 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32789495))
* Several benign meningioma variants, including angiomatous, microcystic, secretory and lymphoplasmacyterich meningiomas may also have prominent peritumoral edema ([J Neurooncol 2013;111:49](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23104516))

Prognostic factors

* Extent of surgery and WHO grading
* DNA methylation profiling may better predict tumor recurrence and prognosis than histologic classification ([Lancet Oncol 2017;18:682](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28314689))

Case reports

* 36 and 70 year old women with optic nerve seeding of atypical meningiomas presenting with subacute visual loss ([J Neurosurg 2013;119:494](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23432529))
* 44 year old man with atypical primary meningioma in the nasal septum with malignant transformation and distant metastasis ([BMC Cancer 2012;12:275](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22759338))
* Elderly man with metastatic atypical meningioma ([J Clin Neurosci 2000;7:69](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10847658))

Treatment

* Gross total resection
* Postsurgical radiation is often offered for atypical meningiomas, especially after a subtotal resection ([J Neurooncol 2013;115:241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23949108))
* Stereotactic radiosurgery

Gross description

* Rubbery, well circumscribed mass firmly attached to the inner surface of the dura
* Brain invasive meningiomas readily adherent to adjacent brain tissue
* Reference: [Perry: Practical Surgical Neuropathology - A Diagnostic Approach, 1st Edition, 2010](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0443069824/pathologyoutl-20)

Frozen section description

* Similar to [meningioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumormeningiomageneral.html)

Microscopic (histologic) description

* May have histology of any grade 1 variant meningioma with increased mitoses (4 - 19/10 high power fields)
  + Mitotic rate is defined as the highest count over 10 consecutive high power fields (1 high power field = 0.16 mm²)
* May have increased cellularity or areas of small cell collections
* May have sheet-like growth pattern
* May have areas of spontaneous necrosis
* May have macronucleoli
* Brain invasion is defined as irregular projections of tumor cells into adjacent CNS parenchyma without an intervening layer of leptomeninges at the tumor to brain interphase ([Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:1455](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9414189))

Cytology description

* Squash prep shows similar histology as standard [meningioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumormeningiomageneral.html) but may also show occasional mitoses or macronucleoli

Positive stains

* [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)
* [Progesterone receptor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html) (up to 85%) ([J Clin Neurosci 2014;21:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24231566))
* [Somatostatin receptor 2a (SSTR2a)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsSSTR2A.html) ([Acta Neuropathol 2015;130:441](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26195322))

Negative stains

* [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html) (useful in highlighting tumor brain interphase in cases with brain invasion)
* [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html) (useful to distinguish meningioma [negative] from [solitary fibrous tumor / hemangiopericytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorhpc.html) [positive]) ([Clin Neuropathol 2017;36:56](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28128724))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Majority of atypical meningiomas have loss of *NF2* combined with either genome instability (large scale chromosomal alterations) or loss of *SMARCB1* ([Nat Commun 2018;9:16215](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28195122))
* Recurrent losses of chromosome 1p, 6q, 14q,18q and gain of 1q are indicators of poor prognosis ([Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:431](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28130639))
* Non-*NF2* meningiomas are enriched in mutations in *TRAF2, KLF4, AKT1* and *SMO*, most of which are benign and preferentially locate in skull base ([Science 2013;339:1077](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23348505))
* DNA methylation profiling of meningioma distinguished 6 methylation classes (MCs), benign (ben) 1 - 3, intermediate (int) A and B and malignant (mal)
  + DNA methylation based meningioma classification is reported to better predict tumor recurrence and prognosis than the WHO histological classification ([Lancet Oncol 2017;18:682](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28314689))
* *NF2* mutant atypical meningiomas display increased H3K27me signal and a hypermethylated phenotype due to increased polycomb repressive complex 2 (PCR2) / EZH2 activity ([Nat Commun 2018;9:16215](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28195122))

Sample pathology report

* Brain, right frontal lobe mass, excision:
  + Atypical meningioma (see comment)
  + Comment: Section shows a meningioma with predominant meningothelial morphology and rare psammoma bodies. Multiple atypical features are present, including variably increased mitotic index up to 7 mitoses/10HPF (A7), multifocal microscopic necrosis, widespread small cell change, hypercellularity, and sheeted architecture. No macronucleoli or brain invasion is identified. Ki67 proliferation index is 12.7% per 1,000 nuclei count.

Differential diagnosis

* [Hemangiopericytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorhpc.html):
  + Positive for [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html) but negative for [SSTR2a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsSSTR2A.html)
* [Anaplastic meningioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumoranaplasticmeningioma.html):
  + With mitoses greater than 20/10 high power fields
* [Meningioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumormeningiomageneral.html):
  + With atypical features insufficient for criteria above
* [Necrosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnsradiationinjury.html):
  + Due to prior radiation therapy or embolization (which are not considered spontaneous)

The arrows in the above image show which of the following?

1. Blood vessels
2. Lymphoplasmacytic inflammation
3. Pseudopalisading necrosis
4. Small cell change

Board review style answer #1

**D**. Small cell change

The image shows an atypical meningioma with small cell change, characterized by reduced cytoplasm and increased N/C ratio in these regions. These regions may resemble lymphoplasmacytic inflammation on low power but on high power show nuclei that are similar to adjacent tumor cells.
