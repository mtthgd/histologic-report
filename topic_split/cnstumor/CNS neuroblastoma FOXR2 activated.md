Definition / general

* CNS neuroblastoma, *FOXR2* activated (CNS NB *FOXR2*) tumors are embryonal neoplasms characterized by poorly differentiated neuroblastic or neuronal differentiation with variable neuropil rich stroma and ganglion cells
* Characterized by the activation of *FOXR2* by structural rearrangements

Essential features

* Embryonal tumor with neuronal or neuroblastic differentiation and activation of *FOXR2* rearrangement and fusion or a DNA methylation profile aligned with CNS neuroblastoma, *FOXR2* activated

Terminology

* CNS neuroblastoma, *FOXR2* activated
* Formerly recognized as one of the primitive neuroectodermal tumors of CNS (CNS PNETs)

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9500/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=91) - neuroblastoma, NOS
* ICD-10: [C71](http://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C69-C72/C71-/C71) - malignant neoplasm of brain
* ICD-11: [2A00.1Y & XH85Z0](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f1994022806%2fmms%2fother?data=%7B%22dataType%22%3A%22pc%22%2C%22postcoordinationCodeSet%22%3A%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F1994022806%2Fmms%2Fother%22%2C%22axisToValueIds%22%3A%7B%22specificAnatomy%22%3A%5B%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F883278510%22%7D%5D%7D%7D%7D) - other specified embryonal tumors of brain & neuroblastoma, NOS

Epidemiology

* Supratentorial location in all tumors
* Slight female predilection ([J Neuroimaging 2023;33:359](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36806312))
* Range of ages at diagnosis, 1 - 20 years
  + Median age at diagnosis is 5 years ([Neuro Oncol 2021;23:1597](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34077956))

Sites

* Supratentorial

Pathophysiology

* Thought to arise from neuroectodermal cells, although the exact cell of origin is not known ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2021;80:52](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33270865))
* Most frequent genetic alterations are on the transcription factor gene *FOXR2* ([Cell 2016;164:1060](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26919435))
* Although *FOXR2* plays a causative role in the formation of CNS embryonal tumors, the exact underlying mechanisms have yet to be determined ([Neuro Oncol 2019;21:993](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30976792))

Etiology

* Risk factors have not been reported

Clinical features

* Common symptoms: headache, vomiting, seizures and focal neurologic deficits ([J Neuroimaging 2023;33:359](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36806312))

Diagnosis

* Embryonal tumor with neuronal or neuroblastic differentiation and activation of *FOXR2* rearrangement and fusion or a DNA methylation profile aligned with CNS neuroblastoma, *FOXR2* activated

Laboratory

* No specific laboratory findings for this entity

Radiology description

* Well circumscribed supratentorial mass with a mixed solid and cystic appearance ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2021;80:52](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33270865))
* Solid component may show heterogeneous enhancement

Prognostic factors

* Favorable survival for patients with CNS NB *FOXR2*; higher rate of recurrences in nonirradiated and locally irradiated patients ([Neuro Oncol 2021;23:1597](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34077956))

Case reports

* 3 year old girl with a cystic right frontal lobe tumor ([Brain Tumor Pathol 2020;37:100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32535663))
* Case series of CNS neuroblastoma, *FOXR2* activated ([J Neuroimaging 2023;33:359](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36806312))

Treatment

* Maximal surgical resection followed by radiation to the brain and spinal cord; chemotherapy may also be given ([NCI: Childhood Medulloblastoma and Other Central Nervous System Embryonal Tumors Treatment (PDQ®) - Patient Version [Accessed 5 September 2023]](https://www.cancer.gov/types/brain/patient/child-cns-embryonal-treatment-pdq))

Gross description

* CNS embryonal tumors are often pink, soft, well circumscribed masses; if a prominent desmoplastic component is present, it is often tan-white and firm

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Poorly differentiated neuroepithelial cells and neurocytic cells ([Cell 2016;164:1060](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26919435))
  + Uniform small cells with high N:C ratio, oval to round with hyperchromatic nuclei
  + Cells have little to no apparent cytoplasm in a background of neuropil with or without Homer Wright rosettes ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2021;80:52](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33270865))
* Background of neuropil rich stroma
* Abundant mitotic activity
* Palisading necrosis can be present
* Infiltration of adjacent parenchyma is variable

Cytology description

* Evaluation of cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) for the presence of tumor cells is required for staging ([Radiology 1969;93:1351](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4983156))

Positive stains

* [Olig2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsolig2.html)
* [Synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html) expression is accentuated in regions of neurocytic or ganglion cell differentiation ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2021;80:52](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33270865))
* [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) is high

Negative stains

* [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html) and [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html) are generally negative
* [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html),
  [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html),
  [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html)

Electron microscopy description

* Tumor cells are often surrounded by cell processes
* Intermediate filaments and clear vesicles can be found ([Brain Tumor Pathol 2020;37:100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32535663))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Interchromosomal and intrachromosomal rearrangements in forkhead box R2, leading to increased *FOXR2* gene expression
  + *FOXR2* locus on chromosome Xp11.21
  + Common fusion partner is *JMJD1C* locus on chromosome 10q21.3
* Gain of chromosome 1q is present in most cases ([Neuro Oncol 2021;23:1597](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34077956))
* High *FOXR2* expression can occur in a subset of high grade gliomas and medulloblastomas; however, DNA methylation profiling can distinguish these entities ([Cell 2016;164:1060](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26919435))

Case presentation of CNS NB *FOXR2*

Sample pathology report

* Brain, suprasellar mass, resection:
  + CNS neuroblastoma, *FOXR2* activated, CNS WHO grade 4 (see comment)
  + Comment: Sections of the suprasellar tumor demonstrate small, round, embryonic appearing cells with scant cytoplasm. Necrosis and abundant mitotic activity are appreciated. Immunohistochemical stains for synaptophysin and Ki67 were performed. Synaptophysin is patchy positive. Ki67 proliferation index is ~25%.
  + Molecular pathology findings
    - *FOXR2* fusion
    - Gain of chromosome 1q

Differential diagnosis

* [CNS embryonal tumors, NEC / NOS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumortumorembryotumNOS.html):
  + Lacks *FOXR2* fusion
  + Histologically similar
* [Embryonal tumor with multilayered rosettes (ETMR)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorETMR.html):
  + Strong and diffuse cytoplasmic **LIN28A**+
  + [Cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html), [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html)+ in embryonal cells and rosettes
* [Medulloblastoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumormedulloblastoma.html):
  + Larger, more atypical, hyperchromatic nuclei
  + Diffuse [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html)+
* [Atypical teratoid / rhabdoid tumor (AT / RT)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumoratypicalteratoidrhabdoid.html):
  + Presence of rhabdoid cells
  + Loss of [INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html)
* [CNS tumor with *BCOR* internal tandem duplication (ITD)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/CNStumorBCOR.html):
  + Requires ITD in exon 15 of *BCOR*
  + Histologically similar

Board review style question #1

A 2 year old boy presents with seizures. MRI reveals a well circumscribed supratentorial mass with a mixed solid and cystic appearance. Which of the following is the most likely diagnosis?

1. Ewing sarcoma
2. Lymphoma
3. Neuroblastoma
4. Testicular germ cell tumor

Board review style answer #1

**C.** Neuroblastoma. In this age group, neuroblastoma is the most common of these tumors. Answers A, B and D are incorrect because these diagnoses are less likely in this age group.

This supratentorial mass is composed of small, round, embryonic appearing cells and Homer Wright rosettes. Whole genome analysis demonstrated a *FOXR2* fusion. What is the most likely diagnosis?

1. CNS neuroblastoma, *FOXR2* activated
2. Embryonal tumors with multilayered rosettes
3. Ependymoma
4. Medulloblastoma

Board review style answer #2

**A.** CNS neuroblastoma, *FOXR2* activated. This histology along with the molecular findings are consistent with a CNS neuroblastoma, *FOXR2* activated. Answers B, C and D are incorrect because although they might be similar morphologically, they will not have a *FOXR2* fusion.
