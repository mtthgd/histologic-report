Definition / general

* Histological subtype of meningioma characterized by sheets of round or polygonal cells with a clear and glycogen rich cytoplasm and prominent perivascular and interstitial collagen
* It is classified as CNS WHO grade 2 regardless of the presence of worrisome histological features (mitoses, spontaneous necrosis, brain invasion)

Essential features

* CNS WHO grade 2
* Rare histological subtype of meningioma (0.2%) that is characterized by sheets of round or polygonal cells with a clear and glycogen rich cytoplasm and prominent perivascular and interstitial collagen ([J Neurooncol 2007;81:315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16955223))
* More aggressive behavior, with recurrence and occasional cerebrospinal fluid seeding
* Immunohistochemistry: EMA+, PAS+, possible PR+, vimentin+, GFAP-, cytokeratins-, inhibin-
* 20 - 60% chance of recurrence even with gross total resection

Terminology

* Clear cell meningioma

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9538/1](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=92) - clear cell meningioma
* ICD-11: [XH71V9](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#2005741559) - clear cell meningioma

Epidemiology

* Children and young adults (mean age: 24 years)
* F:M = 1.7:1
* This subtype accounts for Incidence of Reference: [World Neurosurg 2020;134:e1062](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31765868)

Sites

* Cerebellopontine angle and spine, especially the cauda equina region

Pathophysiology

* Originates from arachnoid border cells or dura based cells

Etiology

* Risk factors may be similar to [general meningioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumormeningiomageneral.html): ionizing radiation
* Hormone replacement therapy or oral contraceptives ([J Clin Oncol 2008;26:279](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18182668), [J Neurosurg 2013;118:649](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23101448))
* Germline mutations in *NF2* or *SMARCB1* and *SMARCE1* predispose to familial and multiple meningiomas ([Neurogenetics 2012;13:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22038540), [J Med Genet 2011;48:93](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20930055), [Nat Genet 2013;45:295](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23377182))
* In some cases, clear cell meningioma may be associated with genetic conditions, such as neurofibromatosis type 2 (NF2), which can predispose individuals to develop tumors of the central nervous system, including meningiomas

Clinical features

* Pain is the most common presenting symptom, followed by weakness, paresthesia, sphincter dysfunction, headache, neurologic deficit and hearing loss ([World Neurosurg 2020;134:e1062](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31765868))

Prognostic factors

* Age ≤ 18 years, subtotal resection and involvement of long segments (≥ 3 levels) are positive predictors of recurrence ([World Neurosurg 2020;134:e1062](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31765868))
* Gender and sagittal tumor location have no significant effects on recurrence rate ([World Neurosurg 2020;134:e1062](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31765868))
* Recurrence remains a possibility even after total tumor resection ([Neurosurg Rev 2024;47:35](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38183517))

Case reports

* 3 year old girl with a rare intraspinal lesion ([J Int Med Res 2020;48:300060520952266](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33135529))
* 44 year old woman with a filum terminale lesion ([Folia Neuropathol 2024;62:321](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/39165212))
* 45 year old woman with intraspinal clear cell meningioma without dural attachment ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2021;100:e25167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33726004))
* 58 year old woman with spinal clear cell meningioma without dural attachment ([Radiol Case Rep 2022;17:1760](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35355528))

Treatment

* Gross total resection and adjuvant radiotherapy treatment ([Neurosurg Rev 2024;47:35](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38183517))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Sheets of polygonal cells with small round monomorphic bland nuclei and abundant clear cytoplasm with prominent perivascular and interstitial collagen
* Cytoplasm is heavily laden with granular periodic acid Schiff positive and diastase sensitive material representing glycogen
* Low proliferation index (Ki67 expression); recurrences may have a higher proliferation index
* May not display any characteristic meningioma features (whorls, psammoma bodies, intranuclear inclusions)
* Mitotic activity is absent or very low
* In recurrences, increase of cellularity, pleomorphism, nucleolar prominence, focal micronecrosis and chondroid areas were observed
* Reference: [Curr Oncol 2017;24:e244](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28680293)

Sample pathology report

* Brain, cerebellopontine mass, excision:
  + Meningioma, clear cell subtype, CNS WHO grade 2 (see comment)
  + Comment: Meningothelial neoplasia showing sheets of round or polygonal cells with a clear and glycogen rich cytoplasm and prominent perivascular and interstitial collagen.

Differential diagnosis

* [Clear cell ependymoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorependymoma.html):
  + Typically associated with ventricular lining in the spinal cord or cerebrum; perivascular pseudorosettes
  + Positive for [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html)
  + Negative for [SSTR2A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsSSTR2A.html)
* [Germinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorgerminoma.html) / [seminoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mediastinumgermcell.html):
  + Often teenagers and young adults; most common site is pineal region
  + Positive for [PLAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsplap.html), [OCT3/4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct34.html) and [c-kit / CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)
* [Hemangioblastoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorhemangioblastoma.html):
  + Microscopically highly vascular; most common site is cerebellum
  + Positive for [inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html) and [NSE](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsNSE.html)
  + Negative for [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html), [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html) and [SSTR2A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsSSTR2A.html)
* [Metastatic renal cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumormetastaticcarcinoma.html):
  + Positive for [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) and [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html)
* [Oligodendroglioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumoroligodendroglioma.html):
  + Intraaxial lesion involving the white and gray matter; more rounded and less polygonal cells
  + Positive for [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html)
* [Diffuse leptomeningeal glioneuronal tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumordiffuseleptglioneurtumor.html):
  + Indolent; typically affects children
  + Bland, monomorphous, oligodendroglial-like cells
  + Positive for [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [Olig2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsolig2.html), [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html)
  + Negative for [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)
  + *KIAA1549::BRAF* fusion protein (60 - 70%)

Board review style question #1

Which of the following immunohistochemical staining patterns is most characteristic of clear cell meningioma?

1. EMA immunoreactivity
2. GFAP immunoreactivity
3. Inhibin immunoreactivity
4. Loss of nuclear expression of SMARCE1
5. Progesterone receptor immunoreactivity

Board review style answer #1

**D.** Loss of nuclear expression of SMARCE1. The loss of the nuclear expression of SMARCE1 is characteristic of clear cell meningioma, as an immunohistochemical surrogate of *SMARCE1* mutation. Answers A and E are incorrect because EMA and progesterone receptors are expressed in all variants of meningioma. Answers B and C are incorrect because GFAP and inhibin are consistently negative in meningiomas, being expressed in gliomas and hemangioblastomas, respectively.

Board review style question #2

Which grade does the 5th edition of WHO classification of CNS tumors assign to clear cell meningioma?

1. CNS WHO grade 1
2. CNS WHO grade 2
3. CNS WHO grade 3
4. CNS WHO grade 4

Board review style answer #2

**B.** CNS WHO grade 2. The 5th edition of the WHO classification of CNS tumors assigns a CNS WHO grade 2 to clear cell meningioma regardless of the presence of worrisome histological features (i.e., mitoses, spontaneous necrosis, brain invasion). Answer A is incorrect because grade 1 meningiomas are benign with low recurrence, while clear cell meningioma has a higher recurrence risk. Answer C is incorrect because grade 3 meningiomas are malignant with aggressive behavior, which clear cell meningioma does not exhibit. Answer D is incorrect because grade 4 is reserved for highly malignant tumors like glioblastomas, not meningiomas.

Board review style question #3

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/cnstumorclearcellmeningiomaMicroBroggi3.jpeg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/cnstumorclearcellmeningiomaMicroBroggi3.jpeg)

The histopathological image shown above represents a clear cell meningioma. Which of the following features is characteristic of this tumor subtype?

1. Cells with clear cytoplasm due to glycogen accumulation
2. Frequent psammoma bodies
3. High mitotic activity and necrosis leading to CNS WHO grade 3 classification
4. Strong desmoplastic reaction with abundant collagen deposition

Board review style answer #3

**A.** Cells with clear cytoplasm due to glycogen accumulation. This feature can be confirmed with PAS staining, which highlights glycogen deposits and can be digested with diastase, confirming their nature. Answer B is incorrect because psammoma bodies (concentric calcifications) are characteristic of meningiomas of the meningothelial or psammomatous subtype (WHO grade 1). Answer C is incorrect because although clear cell meningioma can show concerning features such as mitoses and necrosis, it is classified as CNS WHO grade 2, not grade 3. Answer D is incorrect because a desmoplastic reaction with abundant collagen is more typical of fibroblastic or transitional meningiomas (WHO grade 1).
