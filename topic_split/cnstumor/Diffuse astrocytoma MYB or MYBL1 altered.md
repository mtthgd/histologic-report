Definition / general

* Glial neoplasm with infiltrative growth pattern and composed of monomorphic cells with alterations in either *MYB* or *MYBL1* (CNS WHO grade 1)

Essential features

* Low grade diffuse glial neoplasm (CNS WHO grade 1)
* *MYB* or *MYBL1* alteration detected by molecular or cytogenetic testing
* Often associated with long term seizures refractory to medical management
* Absence of histological features of anaplasia

Terminology

* Diffuse astrocytoma, *MYB* altered
* Diffuse astrocytoma, *MYBL1* altered
* Not recommended
  + Isomorphic astrocytoma
  + Isomorphic diffuse glioma

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9421/1](https://seer.cancer.gov/tools/solidtumor/2023/Non_malignant_CNS_STM.pdf#page=26) - diffuse astrocytoma, *MYB* or *MYBL1* altered
* ICD-10: [C71.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C69-C72/C71-/C71.9) - malignant neoplasm of brain, unspecified
* ICD-11: [2A00.0Y & XH6PH6](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#422307135%2Fother%20%26%201264394556) - other specified gliomas of brain & astrocytoma, NOS

Epidemiology

* Typically arises in children (median age is 7.2 years for *MYB* altered and 5.0 years for *MYBL1* altered) ([Cancer Cell 2020;37:569](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32289278))
* Accounts for ~2% of pediatric low grade gliomas and < 0.5% of all brain tumors ([Cancer Cell 2020;37:569](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32289278))
* Not all patients undergo resection as children, so the age range at surgery can be variable (4 - 50 years) ([Acta Neuropathol 2020;139:193](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31563982))

Sites

* Majority occur in supratentorial locations (~90% overall; 58.7% cortex, 26.1% white matter / deep gray nuclei), with rare cases in the brainstem ([Acta Neuropathol 2019;138:1091](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31595312), [Cancer Cell 2020;37:569](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32289278), [Childs Nerv Syst 2023;39:2509](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37165121))
* Most common location is the temporal lobe (42%) ([Acta Neuropathol 2020;139:193](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31563982))

Pathophysiology

* Structural rearrangements in *MYB* or *MYBL1* result in their overexpression and tumorigenesis ([Acta Neuropathol 2010;120:731](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21046410), [Acta Neuropathol 2020;139:193](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31563982))

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Typically, patients have a long term history of epilepsy, often beginning in childhood, that is refractory to medical management ([Acta Neuropathol 2020;139:193](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31563982))
* Can also present with symptoms of increased intracranial pressure or cranial nerve deficits ([Acta Neuropathol 2019;138:1091](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31595312))

Diagnosis

* Brain imaging (computed tomography [CT] / magnetic resonance imaging [MRI])
* Surgical biopsy / resection

Radiology description

* On MRI, lesions are T2 / FLAIR hyperintense, T1 hypointense, typically do not show contrast enhancement and lack diffusion restriction ([Acta Neuropathol 2020;139:193](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31563982))
* Well defined borders, with cortical tumors showing some lobulation and brainstem tumors appearing slightly more infiltrative ([Acta Neuropathol 2019;138:1091](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31595312))

Prognostic factors

* Overall, patients have a good prognosis, with one study reporting a 10 year progression free survival of 89.6% and an overall survival of 95.2% ([Acta Neuropathol 2019;138:1091](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31595312))
* One study found that patients with *MYBL1* altered tumors were more likely to progress than those with *MYB* altered tumors ([Cancer Cell 2020;37:569](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32289278))
* For patients with epilepsy, 90% become seizure free after surgery and the remainder experience decreased seizure frequency ([Acta Neuropathol 2020;139:193](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31563982))

Case reports

* 14 month old boy with pontine *MYB* altered diffuse glioma ([Childs Nerv Syst 2023;39:2509](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37165121))
* 21 month old girl with cerebellar *MYBL1* altered diffuse glioma ([Acta Neuropathol 2022;144:385](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35727368))
* 5 year old boy with right temporo-occipital brain mass ([Neuroradiol J 2023;36:232](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36074655))

Treatment

* Surgical resection of the tumor may be curative for the patient's epilepsy or may greatly reduce the severity / frequency of seizures ([Acta Neuropathol 2020;139:193](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31563982))

Gross description

* Soft, friable, tan-gray to tan-white tissue

Frozen section description

* Mildly hypercellular brain tissue with monomorphic, mildly atypical glial cell population
* May be quite challenging to differentiate from reactive / normal brain parenchyma with frozen sections

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Mildly hypercellular, infiltrative lesion composed of monomorphic glial cells
* May see secondary structure formation or microcystic changes
* Rare to virtually absent mitotic activity
* No evidence of tumor necrosis or microvascular proliferation
* Reference: [Acta Neuropathol 2020;139:193](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31563982)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Structural alterations in *MYB* or *MYBL1*, creating fusion proteins (most common fusion partners are *PCDHGA1*, *MMP16* and *MAML2*) ([Acta Neuropathol 2020;139:193](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31563982))
* *MYB::QKI* fusion rarely found, as this fusion is much more common in angiocentric glioma ([Acta Neuropathol 2019;138:1091](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31595312))
* *MYB* / *MYBL1* copy number alterations (amplifications or deletions) can also be encountered ([Acta Neuropathol 2020;139:193](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31563982))
* By definition, no mutations in *IDH* or *H3* genes

Differential diagnosis

* [Astrocytoma, *IDH* mutant](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/CNStumorgliomasastrocytomasIDHmutant.html):
  + Tumor cells are [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html) and [Olig2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsolig2.html) positive
  + Loss of nuclear [ATRX](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsatrx.html) expression
  + Harbors an *IDH* mutation
* [Angiocentric glioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorangiocentricglioma.html):
  + Composed of bland spindle cells with unique condensation around blood vessels
  + [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) positive (dot-like or ring-like)
  + Almost all with *MYB::QKI* fusion (amplification / deletion or *MYB::QKI* fusion) but typically do not show alterations in *MYBL1*
* [Diffuse low grade glioma, MAPK pathway altered](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumordiffuselowgradegliomaMAPK.html):
  + Both [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html) and [Olig2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsolig2.html) positive
  + MAPK pathway gene alteration, most commonly *BRAF* V600E, *FGFR1* mutation or *FGFR1* tandem kinase duplication
  + No alteration in *MYB* or *MYBL1*
* [Ganglioglioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorganglioncelltumor.html):
  + Glial elements are [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html) and [Olig2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsolig2.html) positive
  + Neuronal elements present, which will be positive for **MAP2**
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) positive
  + Commonly with MAPK pathway alteration (i.e., *BRAF* V600E)
  + No alteration in *MYB* or *MYBL1*

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/cnstumordiffuseastrocytomamybAhrendsen03.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/cnstumordiffuseastrocytomamybAhrendsen03.jpg)

A 7 year old with intractable seizures is found to have a 2.2 cm ovoid, T2 hyperintense, T1 hypointense mass on magnetic resonance imaging (MRI). He undergoes resection of this mass, which shows a mildly hypercellular, infiltrative lesion composed of monomorphic glial cells that do not surround blood vessels. The tumor cells are positive for GFAP and negative for Olig2 and EMA. What gene is most likely altered in this tumor?

1. *IDH1*
2. *MN1*
3. *MYB*
4. *TERT* promoter
5. *TSC2*

Board review style answer #1

**C**. *MYB*. The histology is that of a low grade infiltrating glioma that lacks Olig2 expression and occurs in a child, all consistent with a diagnosis of diffuse astrocytoma, *MYB* or *MYBL1* altered. Answer B is incorrect because *MN1* is an alteration seen in astroblastoma, which typically shows rosette or papillary architecture. Answer A is incorrect because mutations in *IDH1* are seen in adult type diffuse gliomas (which do not typically occur in patients this young) and tumor cells are positive for both GFAP and Olig2. Answer E is incorrect because *TSC2* mutations are associated with subependymal giant cell astrocytoma (SEGA), which will show a circumscribed lesion composed of large and often pleomorphic or multinucleated astrocytic cells. Answer D is incorrect because alterations in *TERT* promoters are typically encountered in glioblastoma and oligodendroglioma, both of which would show Olig2 immunoreactivity, typically occur in older patients and would display more prominent hypercellularity and atypia.
