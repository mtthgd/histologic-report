Definition / general

* Indolent, low grade neuroepithelial neoplasm that typically shows widespread leptomeningeal and superficial parenchymal CNS dissemination and oligodendroglioma-like cytology. This tumor was added provisionally to the WHO classification scheme of CNS tumors in 2016, and is no longer a provisional entity in the 2021 WHO CNS5 classification

Essential features

* Indolent, slow growing tumor with widespread and diffuse leptomeningeal involvement
* Typically affects children; rare in adults
* Presents with signs and symptoms of obstructive hydrocephalus
* Olig2 and S100 positive, monomorphic oligodendroglial-like cells in desmoplastic or myxoid matrix
* *KIAA1549-BRAF* fusion, 1p deletion common; 1p19q codeletion and *BRAF* V600E less common
* References: [Acta Neuropathol 2018;136:239](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29766299), [Acta Neuropathol 2012;124:627](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22941225)

Terminology

* Abbreviations: diffuse leptomeningeal glioneuronal tumor (DLGNT)
* Other historical terms ([Acta Neuropathol 2016;131:803](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27157931)):
  + Primary leptomeningeal oligodendrogliomatosis
  + Disseminated oligodendroglioma-like leptomeningeal neoplasm (DOLN)
  + Disseminated oligodendroglial-like neoplasm
  + Diffuse leptomeningeal neuroepithelial tumor

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [C70.1](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C69-C72/C70-/C70.1) - malignant neoplasm of spinal meninges

Epidemiology

* Range: 5 months - 46 years
* Median age of diagnosis: 5 years
* M:F = 1.7:1
* Reference: [Acta Neuropathol 2012;124:627](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22941225)

Sites

* Widespread, diffuse involvement of spinal and intracranial leptomeninges ([Acta Neuropathol 2012;124:627](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22941225))
* Within cranium, most common around posterior fossa, brainstem and base of brain
* May fill ventricles and spread along Virchow-Robin spaces ([Acta Neuropathol 2012;124:627](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22941225))
* May form cystic or solid tumor lesions in adjacent parenchyma; spinal > intracerebral
* Spread in subarachnoid space: plaque-like or multinodular pattern ([Acta Neuropathol 2012;124:627](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22941225))

Pathophysiology

* Unknown how tumor arises

Etiology

* Precise etiology unknown, although recurrent genetic alterations are identified (see [Molecular / cytogenetics description](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumordiffuseleptglioneurtumor.html#CNSDLGTmoleculardescripion))

Clinical features

* Often acute, presents with signs and symptoms of increased intracranial pressure ([Acta Neuropathol 2012;124:627](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22941225))
* Due to obstructive hydrocephalus
* Nausea, vomiting and headache
* Opisthotonos
* Encasement or irritation of spinal or cranial nerves ([Acta Neuropathol 2012;124:627](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22941225))
* May lead to palsies related to motor, sensory or autonomic function
* Meningeal signs
* Ataxia
* Spinal cord compression
* Rarely, seizures

Diagnosis

* Primarily made on radiologic features
* Confirmed by tissue biopsy with ancillary immunohistochemistry, cytogenetic (FISH) and molecular testing

Radiology description

* MRI shows widespread contrast enhancement and thickening along spinal cord; often extends intracranially to posterior fossa, brainstem and basal brain ([J Belg Soc Radiol 2017;101:19](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30039011))
* Small cystic or nodular T2 hyperintense lesions along subpial surface of spinal cord or brain
* Discrete intraparenchymal lesions, most common in spinal cord; sometimes without diffuse spread ([Acta Neuropathol Commun 2020;8:95](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32605662))
* Obstructive hydrocephalus with associated periventricular T2 hyperintensity

Radiology images

* See [Case reports](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumordiffuseleptglioneurtumor.html#CNSDLGTcasereports)

Prognostic factors

* Mostly favorable prognosis in terms of mortality ([Brain Tumor Pathol 2015;32:49](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24770606), [Acta Neuropathol 2012;124:627](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22941225))
* Indolent tumor but with significant morbidity
* Poor prognosis ([Brain Tumor Pathol 2015;32:49](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24770606)):
  + Mitotic activity (of any degree)
  + Glomeruloid microvascular proliferation
  + MIB1 / Ki67 labeling index ≥ 4%

Case reports

* 10 children, 2 - 14 years old; 9 patients received chemotherapy, 3 with progressive disease; 6 patient harbored genomic alterations affecting the MAPK pathway ([J Neurooncol 2016;128:293](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26994902))
* 4 year old boy with diffuse leptomeningeal thickening; review of imaging features ([J Belg Soc Radiol 2017;101:19](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30039011))
* 7 year old boy with low grade tumor versus 9 year old boy with high grade tumor ([Hum Pathol 2017;70:105](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28652147))
* 8 year old girl with imaging presentation mimicking tuberculous meningitis ([Childs Nerv Syst 2011;27:187](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20635183))
* 12 year old boy with ventriculoperitoneal shunt treated with temozolomide ([Childs Nerv Syst 2020;36:459](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31897629))
* 13 year old girl with predominant spinal infiltration ([Childs Nerv Syst 2019;35:1609](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30770994))
* 31 and 35 year old women with circumscribed intraparenchymal tumors ([Acta Neuropathol Commun 2020;8:95](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32605662))
* 36 year old woman with aggressive tumor ([World Neurosurg 2018;114:53](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29526778))
* 37 year old man with a cervical spinal cord mass ([Case of the month #542](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case542.htm))
* 7 cases with typical and less common imaging features, showing the most characteristic imaging finding is diffuse intracranial and intraspinal nodular leptomeningeal thickening and enhancement ([AJNR Am J Neuroradiol 2020;41:2155](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32912870))
* Series of 36 patients; comprehensive description of presentation, imaging, histology, treatment and prognosis ([Acta Neuropathol 2012;124:627](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22941225))

Treatment

* Surgical resection, particularly for parenchymal lesions ([Childs Nerv Syst 2019;35:1609](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30770994))
* Symptomatic treatment for hydrocephalus ([Childs Nerv Syst 2020;36:459](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31897629))
  + Ventriculoperitoneal shunting
* Chemotherapy ([Child Neurol Open 2015;2:2329048X14567531](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28503587), [Childs Nerv Syst 2018;34:329](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28965234))
  + Temozolomide, others
* Radiotherapy in disease progression ([Child Neurol Open 2015;2:2329048X14567531](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28503587), [Childs Nerv Syst 2018;34:329](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28965234))
* Targeted therapy ([Pediatr Blood Cancer 2020;67:e28478](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32841504))

Gross description

* Postmortem examination confirms widespread, diffuse leptomeningeal spread in spinal and intracranial compartments ([Brain Tumor Pathol 2018;35:209](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30051174))
* Multifocal extension along Virchow-Robin spaces ([Acta Neuropathol 2012;124:627](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22941225))
* Growth along peripheral nerve roots and infiltration of basal cranial nerves and ganglia

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Low to moderate cellularity; diffuse growth or nests in leptomeninges ([Brain Tumor Pathol 2015;32:49](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24770606))
  + Often with desmoplastic or myxoid changes
* Bland, monomorphous, oligodendroglial-like cells ([Brain Tumor Pathol 2015;32:49](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24770606))
  + With or without perinuclear halo
  + Uniform round nuclei with fine chromatin and inconspicuous nucleoli
* Some with ganglion or ganglioid cells; may be associated with neuropil
* Eosinophilic granular bodies (occasional), Rosenthal fibers (rare)
* Low mitotic activity
* Anaplasia is rare; associated with more aggressive clinical course ([Brain Tumor Pathol 2015;32:49](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24770606))
  + Nuclear enlargement
  + Atypia
  + Increased mitotic activity
  + Microvascular proliferation
  + Necrosis

Cytology description

* Cerebrospinal fluid examination
  + Increased protein levels
  + Reactive lymphocytosis (rare feature)
  + Typically negative for tumor cells ([Childs Nerv Syst 2018;34:329](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28965234))

Positive stains

* [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [Olig2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsolig2.html), **MAP2**, [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html) (70%), [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html) (focal, in 50%)
* [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) ~1.5% (median)

Negative stains

* [NeuN](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsneun.html), [neurofilaments](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsneurofilament.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) and [IDH1 R132H](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsidh1.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* *KIAA1549-BRAF* fusion protein is most frequent (60 - 70%) ([Acta Neuropathol 2012;124:627](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22941225), [Acta Neuropathol 2015;129:609](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25720745))
* Chromosome 1p deletion (50 - 100%, varies by series) ([Acta Neuropathol 2012;124:627](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22941225), [Acta Neuropathol 2015;129:609](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25720745))
* DNA methylation profiling identifies 2 distinct classes of diffuse leptomeningeal glioneuronal tumor ([Acta Neuropathol 2018;136:239](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29766299))
* Chromosome 1p / 19q codeletion differs by methylation defined groups (47% versus 15%) ([Acta Neuropathol 2018;136:239](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29766299))
* Other reported alterations: *TRIM33-RAF1*, *NTRK1/2/3* fusions, *CDKN2A/B* homozygous deletion, *TERT* C228T, C228A, *ATRX* point mutation, *BCOR* frameshift alteration, *BRAF* V600E ([Acta Neuropathol 2018;136:239](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29766299))
* Negative for *IDH1* R132, *IDH* R172, *H3F3A* K27 alterations ([Acta Neuropathol 2018;136:239](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29766299))

Sample pathology report

* Spine, leptomeninges, biopsy:
  + Diffuse leptomeningeal glioneuronal tumor (see comment)
  + Comment: Hematoxylin and eosin examination of the biopsy material shows a moderately cellular, neoplastic proliferation within the leptomeninges with associated desmoplastic and myxoid change. The cells show relatively monomorphic, bland morphology, round nuclei with perinuclear haloes (reminiscent of an oligodendroglial-like appearance) and inconspicuous nucleoli. Mitotic activity is not identified. Necrosis is not identified. Immunohistochemical stains demonstrate reactivity for S100 and Olig2. Ki67 shows a low (< 2%) index of proliferation. FISH studies confirm the *KIAA1549-BRAF* rearrangement and deletion of chromosome 1p. Taken together, the radiologic, histomorphologic, immunohistochemical and cytogenetic features support a diagnosis of diffuse leptomeningeal glioneuronal tumor.

Differential diagnosis

* [Pilocytic astrocytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorpilocyticastrocytoma.html):
  + Typically intraparenchymal tumor without primary leptomeningeal spread
  + More predominant Rosenthal fibers
  + Glomeruloid microvascular proliferation does not alone confer worse prognosis
* [Oligodendroglioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumoroligodendroglioma.html):
  + Diffusely infiltrative glial tumor
  + Typically intraparenchymal
  + *IDH1* / *IDH2* mutations as a requisite feature for diagnosis
* **Macrophage rich reactive / inflammatory process**:
  + [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html) positive macrophages
* [Tumors metastatic to the leptomeninges](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumormetastaticcarcinoma.html):
  + Clinical history, more likely adults
