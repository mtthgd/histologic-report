Definition / general

* Cortex based glioneuronal neoplasm that is often located in the mesial temporal lobe of adolescents and young adults and associated with medically refractory epilepsy, usually with activating mutations of *FGFR1*, CNS WHO grade 1 ([Neurosurgery 1988;23:545](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3143922))

Essential features

* Presents clinically with intractable seizures, usually in children and young adults ([Neurosurgery 1988;23:545](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3143922), [Brain Pathol 2012;22:350](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22497610))
* Radiographically is sharply demarcated, nodular, cortical lesion(s) without edema or enhancement ([AJNR Am J Neuroradiol 2003;24:829](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12748079), [Neuroradiology 2009;51:433](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19242688), [J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2011;70:859](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21937911))
* Composed of astrocytes, oligodendrocytes (or oligodendrocyte-like cells) and neurons with neurons often appearing to float in a myxoid matrix between columns of oligodendroglial cells ([Neurosurgery 1988;23:545](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3143922), [Brain Pathol 2012;22:350](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22497610))
* Activating genetic alterations in *FGFR1* ([Acta Neuropathol 2016;131:847](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26920151))
* CNS WHO grade 1 ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2011;70:859](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21937911))

Terminology

* Dysembryoplastic neuroepithelial tumor
* Simple dysembryoplastic neuroepithelial tumor
* Complex dysembryoplastic neuroepithelial tumor

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9413/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf#page=90) - dysembryoplastic neuroepithelial tumor
* ICD-11: [2A00.21](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http:%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f1792897751) - mixed neuronal glial tumors
* ICD-11: [XH0H76](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http:%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f488878256) - dysembryoplastic neuroepithelial tumor

Epidemiology

* 1.2:1 = M:F ([N Engl J Med 2017;377:1648](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29069555))
* 5.9% of epilepsy surgery cases ([N Engl J Med 2017;377:1648](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29069555))

Sites

* Cerebral cortex
* Most common sites: temporal lobe, especially medial (67%), frontal lobe (16%), other cortex (16%) ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2011;70:859](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21937911))

Pathophysiology

* Activating alteration in *FGFR1* (receptor tyrosine kinase) > activation of MAPK pathway > dysregulated cell growth, proliferation and differentiation ([Acta Neuropathol 2016;131:847](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26920151))

Etiology

* Most cases are sporadic ([Acta Neuropathol 2016;131:833](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26810070))
* Germline mutations in MAPK pathway genes, including *FGFR1*, *NF1* and *PTPN11* ([Acta Neuropathol 2016;131:847](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26920151), [Epilepsy Res 2013;105:384](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23597854), [Am J Med Genet A 2017;173:1061](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28328117))

Clinical features

* Intractable seizures

Diagnosis

* Cortical glioneuronal tumor with presence of specific glioneuronal component
* *FGFR1* alteration or DNA methylation profile can help in challenging cases
* Preferable to make diagnosis in context of early onset focal epilepsy

Radiology description

Prognostic factors

* Benign lesion with low rate of recurrence after resection ([J Neuroradiol 2001;28:230](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11924137))
* Rare case reports of malignant transformation ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2003;7:240](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12913847), [J Neurooncol 2009;94:283](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19267228), [Radiol Case Rep 2022;17:939](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35140831))

Case reports

* 18 year old woman with left parietal mass ([Radiol Case Rep 2022;17:939](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35140831))
* 26 year old woman with superficial right frontal mass ([J Radiol Case Rep 2013;7:7](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24421925))
* 27 year old man with right temporal mass ([BMJ Case Rep 2013;2013:bcr2013010469](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24014330))

Treatment

* Surgical resection ([Childs Nerv Syst 2015;31:847](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25795072))
* Radiation or chemotherapy is generally not applicable

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Growth pattern
  + Intracortical and multinodular ([Neurosurgery 1988;23:545](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3143922))
* Pathognomonic appearance
  + Bundles of axons lined by small oligodendroglia-like cells form columns oriented perpendicularly to the cortical surface with intervening cytologically normal neurons floating in a myxoid matrix ([Neurosurgery 1988;23:545](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3143922))
* Absence of the following:
  + Dysplastic ganglion-like cells
  + High cellularity
  + Necrosis
  + Perivascular lymphoid infiltrates
  + Eosinophilic granular bodies
* Simple form
  + Pathognomonic component alone
* Complex form
  + Pathognomonic component along with glial nodules, resembling other glioma types ([J Neurooncol 1999;41:267](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10359147))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Activating alterations in *FGFR1* ([Acta Neuropathol 2016;131:847](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26920151))
  + Internal tandem duplication
  + Missense mutations
  + *FGFR1::TACC1* fusion (rare)
  + Complete duplication of *FGFR1* (rare)
* *PTPN11* alteration (rare) ([Am J Med Genet A 2017;173:1061](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28328117))
* *NF1* alteration (rare) ([Epilepsy Res 2013;105:384](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23597854))
* Chromosomal polysomies (gains of chromosome 5, chromosome 6, chromosome 7; loss of chromosome 22) unusual but reported ([Neuropathol Appl Neurobiol 2015;41:743](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25764012), [Acta Neuropathol 2016;131:847](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26920151))

Dysembryoplastic neuroepithelial tumor (DNET)

Sample pathology report

* Brain, temporal lobe, biopsy:
  + Dysembryoplastic neuroepithelial tumor, CNS WHO grade 1
  + Positive for *FGFR1* alteration

Differential diagnosis

* Neoplastic
  + [Oligodendroglioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumoroligodendroglioma.html):
    - *IDH1/2* mutation
    - Codeletion of whole chromosome arms 1p and 19q
  + [Ganglioglioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorganglioncelltumor.html):
    - Dysplastic ganglion-like cells
    - Perivascular lymphocytic infiltrate
    - Eosinophilic granular bodies
    - *BRAF* p.V600E alteration ([Acta Neuropathol 2011;121:397](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21274720))
  + **Myxoid glioneuronal tumor**:
    - Located primarily in the septum pellucidum
    - *PDGFRA* p.K385 alteration ([Brain Pathol 2020;30:479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31609499))
  + **Multinodular and vacuolating neuronal tumor**:
    - Predominant neuronal component ([Brain Pathol 2013;23:515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23324039))
    - *MAP2K1* or *BRAF* alteration
  + **Polymorphous low grade neuroepithelial tumor of the young**:
    - Diffuse growth pattern ([Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792))
    - Extensive [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) immunopositivity
    - *BRAF* p.V600E or *FGFR2/3* fusion
  + **Diffuse astrocytoma, *MYB* or *MYBL1* altered**:
    - Monomorphic glial neoplasm ([Acta Neuropathol 2020;139:193](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31563982))
    - *MYB* or *MYBL1* alteration
  + [Angiocentric glioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorangiocentricglioma.html):
    - Perivascular orientation of tapered cells
    - *MYB::QKI* fusion or other *MYB* alteration ([Nat Genet 2016;48:273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26829751))
  + **Diffuse glioneuronal tumor with oligodendroglioma-like features and nuclear clusters**:
    - Specific methylation profile ([Neuropathol Appl Neurobiol 2020;46:422](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31867747))
    - Monosomy chromosome 14

Board review style question #1

[![BRQ image](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/cnstumorDNETciminodampier08.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/cnstumorDNETciminodampier08.jpg)

The tumor shown above is from the mesial temporal lobe of a child. What is the most common underlying genetic alteration in this entity?

1. *BRAF* V600E mutation
2. *FGFR1* alteration
3. *IDH1* R132H
4. *NF1* loss
5. *TP53* mutation

Board review style answer #1

**B.** *FGFR1* alteration.
*TP53* mutations are uncommon in dysembryoplastic neuroepithelial tumor (E). *IDH1* R132H is the most common mutation in *IDH* mutant astrocytomas and oligodendrogliomas but should be negative in dysembryoplastic neuroepithelial tumor (C). *BRAF* p.V600E mutation has been reported in dysembryoplastic neuroepithelial tumor but methylation profiling indicates such tumors are probably a distinct entity (A). *NF1* loss may occur in dysembryoplastic neuroepithelial tumor but is rare (D).

Board review style question #2

Which of the following is true of dysembryoplastic neuroepithelial tumors?

1. Associated with NF2
2. Associated with seizures
3. Harbor several cytogenetic alterations
4. Most frequently occur in adults
5. Show diffuse CD34 immunoreactivity

Board review style answer #2

**B.** Associated with seizures.
Dysembryoplastic neuroepithelial tumor should not show significant CD34 immunopositivity (E). Dysembryoplastic neuroepithelial tumor usually occurs in children (D). Dysembryoplastic neuroepithelial tumor is generally without chromosomal losses or gains, resulting in a flat copy number profile (C). Dysembryoplastic neuroepithelial tumor has rarely been associated with NF1 but not NF2 (A).
