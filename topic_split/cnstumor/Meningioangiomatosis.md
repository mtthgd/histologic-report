Definition / general

* Rare, benign, hamartomatous lesion of children and young adults characterized by leptomeningeal and meningovascular proliferation
* Presents with seizures and headaches (or asymptomatic)
* Mean age 21 - 28 years, range 1 - 70 years
* 25% associated with neurofibromatosis II
* May be associated with other developmental anomalies ([Clin Neuropathol 2013;32:37](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22762891), [Pediatr Dev Pathol 2014;17:292](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24678957))

Radiology description

* See [Neuroradiology 2013;55:1439](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24153447)

Case reports

* Two cases, 9 months to 33 years ([Am J Surg Pathol 1999;23:872](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10435554))
* 7 year old girl with a 6 month history of partial complex seizures of increasing frequency ([University of Pittsburgh: Seven Year Old Girl with Seizures](http://path.upmc.edu/cases/case92.html))
* 11 year old boy with intractable seizures ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2003;127:e349](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12873202))
* 13 year old boy with sudden, unexpected death ([Pediatr Dev Pathol 2005;8:240](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15803213))
* 15 year old boy with meningioangiomatosis and oligodendroglioma ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1996;120:587](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8651864))
* 16 year old boy with a genetic alteration ([Am J Surg Pathol 2002;26:125](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11756780))
* 30 year old pregnant woman with fetal malformations ([Pediatr Dev Pathol 2014;17:292](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24678957))
* 30 year old man with a frontal lobe mass ([University of Pittsburgh: A Male in His 30s with a Frontal Lobe Mass](http://path.upmc.edu/cases/case354.html))
* 33 year old man ([Case Rep Oncol Med 2012;2012:296286](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23198201))
* Cortical blindness ([Neurology 2013;81:511](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23897877))

Treatment

* Surgical excision cures most seizures ([Clin Neurol Neurosurg 2013;115:1407](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23485253))

Gross description

* Thick and opaque leptomeninges with abnormal vessels but no evidence of neoplasia

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Often with plaque or rind-like meningothelial proliferation at cortical surface with tongue-like projections into underlying gray matter, predominantly in a perivascular pattern
* Also increased cortical vascularity
* Often leptomeningeal calcification
* Variable psammoma bodies and osteoid
* May have entrapped reactive glial tissue or neurons
* Also variable neurofibrillary tangles
* No / rare mitotic activity, no necrosis, no marked pleomorphism

Cytology description

* Numerous thin walled capillaries, bland spindle cells, occasional large cells with prominent nucleoli, variable meningothelial whorls and neurons ([Acta Cytol 2001;45:1069](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11726105))

Positive stains

* [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)
* [Progesterone receptor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html): variable
* [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html): will highlight vascular elements

Negative stains

* [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)

Differential diagnosis

* [Atypical meningioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumoratypicalmeningioma.html): increased mitotic activity and either increased cellularity, high N/C, prominent nucleoli, sheet-like growth or necrosis
* [Desmoplastic
  infantile astrocytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumordesmoplasticinfantileganglioglioma.html): prominent desmoplastic stroma with neuroepithelial cells
* [Invasive meningioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumormeningiomageneral.html): infiltrative growth into brain parenchyma with destruction, no prominent vascular component
* [Schwannoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorschwannoma.html): encapsulated,
  Schwann cells in palisading or myxoid patterns, strong S100+, EMA-
* **Vascular malformation:** Sturge-Weber syndrome or arteriovenous malformation, no perivascular meningothelial elements
