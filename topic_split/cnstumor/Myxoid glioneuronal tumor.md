Definition / general

* Low grade neoplasm arising at or near septum pellucidum
* Composed of both a glial component resembling oligodendrocytes in prominent myxoid stroma and a neuronal component similar to floating neurons found in dysembryoplastic neuroepithelial tumors

Essential features

* Oligodendrocyte-like tumor cells in myxoid stroma
* Located in septum pellucidum, septal nuclei, corpus callosum or periventricular white matter
* *PDGFRA* p.K385L/I dinucleotide mutation or other mutation in extracellular domain of *PDGFRA* ([Acta Neuropathol 2018;136;339](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30006677), [Brain Pathol 2020;30:479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31609499))
* Corresponds to CNS WHO grade 1

Terminology

* Abbreviation: MYXGNT or MGNT
* Formerly dysembryoplastic neuroepithelial tumor of septum pellucidum (not recommended) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2001;25:494](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11257624))

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9509/1](https://seer.cancer.gov/icd-o-3/sitetype.icdo3.20220429.pdf#page=344) - papillary glioneuronal tumor
* ICD-11: [2A00.21](https://icd.who.int/browse/2025-01/mms/en#1792897751) - mixed neuronal glial tumors

Epidemiology

* M = F
* Mainly children and young adults ([Pathologica 2022;114:447](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36534423))
* Age distribution of reported cases: 4 - 65 years ([Brain Pathol 2022;32:e13081](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35699689))
* Mean age is 11 years in a recent report (ranging from 6 to 17 years) ([Childs Nerv Syst 2024;41:2](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/39589532))

Sites

* Septal nuclei, septum pellucidum, corpus callosum, periventricular white matter ([Brain Pathol 2020;30:479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31609499))

Pathophysiology

* *PDGFRA* mutation as solitary genetic driver
* *PDGFRA* alterations localize in Ig-like C2 type domain, especially in the K385 residue, in the extracellular ligand binding domain portion of the receptor tyrosine kinase ([Neuro Oncol 2019;21:800](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30726976), [Brain Pathol 2020;30:479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31609499))
* Results in substitution from a basic amino acid (lysine) to a hydrophobic amino acid (leucine or isoleucine) ([Brain Pathol 2020;30:479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31609499))
* Possibly an oncogenic, gain of function mutation causing constitutive activation of the intracellular kinase domain ([Brain Pathol 2020;30:479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31609499))

Etiology

* Not known

Clinical features

* Hydrocephalus ([Neuro Oncol 2019;21:800](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30726976))
* Epilepsy ([Neuro Oncol 2019;21:800](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30726976))
* Behavioral disorders ([Neuro Oncol 2019;21:800](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30726976))
* Memory deficit ([Front Oncol 2023;13:1263556](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37829343))
* Intraventricular hemorrhage ([Neuro Oncol 2019;21:800](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30726976))
* Presenting symptoms: headache, seizures, personality change and nausea / emesis ([Childs Nerv Syst 2024;41:2](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/39589532))

Diagnosis

* Combination of imaging location, microscopic findings and *PDGFRA* mutation or methylation profiling

Radiology description

* Computed tomography (CT) imaging: hypodensity ([Neoplasia 2023;37:100885](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36764090))
* Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) with T1 hypointensity, T2 hyperintensity, no contrast enhancement or restricted diffusion ([Neoplasia 2023;37:100885](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36764090))
* Typically lacks calcifications and multinodularity, in contrast to cortical dysembryoplastic neuroepithelial tumor ([Neoplasia 2023;37:100885](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36764090))
* Common finding: obstructive hydrocephalus, especially when located in the septum pellucidum ([Neoplasia 2023;37:100885](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36764090))
* Not typical: prominent peritumoral edema ([Neoplasia 2023;37:100885](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36764090))
* Pitfall: may be mistaken for a colloid cyst on imaging ([Childs Nerv Syst 2011;27:485](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20959995))

Prognostic factors

* Slow growing with favorable long term outcomes ([Neuro Oncol 2019;21:800](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30726976), [Brain Pathol 2020;30:479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31609499))
* Median time to progression is 19 months in one series ([Neuro Oncol 2019;21:800](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30726976))
* Recurrence reported in 3 of 30 patients with clinical follow up available ([Neuro Oncol 2019;21:800](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30726976))
* Ventricular or leptomeningeal dissemination in a subset but still having indolent behavior ([Brain Pathol 2020;30:479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31609499))

Case reports

* 10 year old boy with severe headache and seizures; CT revealed a hypodense intraventricular mass ([Childs Nerv Syst 2024;41:2](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/39589532))
* 22 year old man with disconnected speech and upper limb movements, 49 year old man with headaches, nausea, behavioral abnormalities and seizures and 59 year old woman with chronic headaches ([BJR Case Rep 2021;7:20200139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35047190))

Treatment

* Surgical resection
* Due to anatomic location, only a minority achieve gross total resection ([Neuro Oncol 2019;21:800](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30726976))
* May develop pituitary hormone deficiency as a complication of surgery ([Neuro Oncol 2019;21:800](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30726976))
* Patients maintained stable disease with or without receiving radiation or chemotherapy ([Neuro Oncol 2019;21:800](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30726976))

* Gelatinous gray-tan appearance

Frozen section description

* Bland round nuclei within myxoid background with or without floating neurons
* Low grade glial / glioneuronal neoplasm

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Oligodendrocyte-like cells (OLCs) infiltrate adjacent brain tissue ([Neuro Oncol 2019;21:800](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30726976))
* Microcystic pattern with mucinous / myxoid background ([Childs Nerv Syst 2011;27:485](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20959995))
* Bundles of delicate anastomosing vasculature ([Childs Nerv Syst 2011;27:485](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20959995))
* Large ganglionic neurons noted at the periphery of the microcysts and focally floating in the myxoid matrix ([Childs Nerv Syst 2011;27:485](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20959995))
* Occasional rosettes ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:806](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26796505))
* Rare cases report eosinophilic granular bodies and Rosenthal fibers ([Brain Pathol 2020;30:479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31609499), [Neuro Oncol 2019;21:800](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30726976))
* Not reported: brisk mitotic activity, necrosis or vascular proliferation ([Neoplasia 2023;37:100885](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36764090))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Defining dinucleotide mutation at codon 385 of the *PDGFRA* oncogene replacing lysine with either leucine or isoleucine (p.K385L/I) in the encoded platelet derived growth factor receptor alpha protein ([Brain Pathol 2020;30:479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31609499))
* Distinct methylation cluster and epigenetic structure are closely related to but distinct from dysembryoplastic neuroepithelial tumor of the cerebral cortex ([Acta Neuropathol 2018;136;339](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30006677), [Neuro Oncol 2019;21:800](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30726976))
* Negative for genetic alterations in *FGFR1*, *BRAF*, *MYB* and *MYBL1*, *PIK3CA*, *PIK3R1*, *NF1*, *PTPN11* or *MAP2K1* and *IDH1 / 2* and 1p / 19q codeletions, which are specific for other glioneuronal tumors, pilocytic astrocytoma and oligodendroglioma ([Acta Neuropathol 2018;136;339](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30006677), [Acta Neuropathol Commun 2020;8:151](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32859279))
* Balanced diploid genome without significant copy number alterations ([Brain Pathol 2020;30:479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31609499))

Sample pathology report

* Brain, septum pellucidum / intraventricular, biopsy / resection:
  + Myxoid glioneuronal tumor, *PDGFRA* mutant, CNS WHO grade 1 (see comment)
  + Comment: This low grade tumor is characterized by oligodendrocyte-like tumor cells in a prominent myxoid stroma with admixed floating neurons / neurocytic rosettes / perivascular neuropil. Molecular analysis confirms a recurrent dinucleotide mutation at codon p.K385 in the *PDGFRA* gene (or matching methylation class myxoid glioneuronal tumor).

Differential diagnosis

* [Dysembryoplastic neuroepithelial tumor (DNT / DNET)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumordnet.html):
  + Defined mucin patterned cortical nodules on imaging
  + Located in any part of cerebral hemispheres with a predilection for temporal lobe and mesial structures
  + *FGFR1* alterations with either kinase domain tandem duplication or hotspot missense mutations
  + Lacks the accompanying *PIK3CA* or *PIK3R1* mutation that characterizes rosette forming glioneuronal tumor ([Acta Neuropathol Commun 2020;8:151](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32859279))
* [Rosette forming glioneuronal tumor (RGNT)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorrgnt.html):
  + Uniform neurocytes forming rosettes or perivascular pseudorosettes associated with [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html) expression
  + *FGFR1* kinase domain hotspot missense mutations in combination with either *PIK3CA* or *PIK3R1* mutation, often with accompanying *NF1* or *PTPN11* mutation ([Acta Neuropathol Commun 2020;8:151](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32859279), [Neuropathol Appl Neurobiol 2022;48:e12813](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35293634))
  + Distinct methylation profile cluster ([Acta Neuropathol Commun 2020;8:151](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32859279))
* [Subependymoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorsubependymoma.html):
  + Focally may have similar histology but lacks neuronal component ([Childs Nerv Syst 2011;27:485](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20959995))
* [Pilocytic (or pilomyxoid) astrocytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorpilocyticastrocytoma.html):
  + Low grade piloid astrocytic neoplasm
  + Lacks neuronal component
  + *KIAA1549::BRAF* fusion is the most frequent genetic alteration in pilocytic astrocytoma
  + Distinct DNA methylation pattern
* [Central neurocytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorcentralneurocytoma.html):
  + More uniform sheets of [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html) / [INSM1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsINSM1.html) positive neuronal cells
  + Lacks myxoid matrix
  + Lacks diffuse [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html)
* [Diffuse leptomeningeal glioneuronal tumor (DLGNT)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumordiffuseleptglioneurtumor.html):
  + Enters differential in cases of myxoid glioneuronal tumors (MGNT) with cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) dissemination
  + *KIAA1549::BRAF* fusion with monosomy 1p

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/cnstumormyxoidglioneuronaltumorYeaneyBRQ01.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/cnstumormyxoidglioneuronaltumorYeaneyBRQ01.jpg)

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/cnstumormyxoidglioneuronaltumorYeaneyBRQ02.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/cnstumormyxoidglioneuronaltumorYeaneyBRQ02.jpg)

A 25 year old man has a brain mass in the septum pellucidum, which shows T2 hyperintensity on MRI. The man undergoes a biopsy of the mass. The histology of the lesion is shown in the images above. What is the expected pattern of immunoreactivity?

1. GFAP negative, Olig2 negative, NeuN diffusely positive
2. GFAP negative, Olig2 positive, NeuN focally positive
3. GFAP positive, Olig2 positive, NeuN focally positive
4. GFAP positive, Olig2 positive, NeuN negative

Board review style answer #1

**C**. GFAP positive, Olig2 positive, NeuN focally positive. Myxoid glioneuronal tumors (MGNT) are diffusely positive for Olig2 and GFAP in bland round oligodendrocyte-like cells. NeuN highlights focal neuronal components (also known as floating neurons). Answer D is incorrect because it is the expected pattern of immunoreactivity for most gliomas including pilocytic astrocytoma or oligodendroglioma. Answer A is incorrect because it is possibly a pattern of immunoreactivity for gangliocytoma or possibly central neurocytoma. Answer B is incorrect because it is the expected pattern of immunoreactivity for dysembryoplastic neuroepithelial tumor. GFAP is typically limited to entrapped reactive astrocytes in cortical dysembryoplastic neuroepithelial tumor (DNT), a useful feature to differentiate it from MGNT, which is diffusely positive for GFAP.

Board review style question #2

An 11 year old girl undergoes a brain biopsy that reveals a tumor composed of bland round nuclei with abundant myxoid matrix. Immunohistochemical stains show that the tumor is positive for SOX10, Olig2, GFAP and has occasional scattered synaptophysin positive neurons within the myxoid matrix. Molecular studies reveal *PDGFRA* p.K385L/I mutation. What is the most common location of this neoplasm?

1. Brain stem
2. Cerebellar
3. Dural / leptomeningeal
4. Intraventricular
5. Superficial cortical

Board review style answer #2

**D**. Intraventricular. The question describes the morphologic, immunophenotypic and molecular features of myxoid glioneuronal tumor. Myxoid glioneuronal tumor is genetically characterized by dinucleotide mutation at codon 385 of the *PDGFRA* p.K385L/I dinucleotide mutation or (less commonly) other mutations in the extracellular ligand binding domain of the protein. These tumors occur primarily in or near the intraventricular region including septum pellucidum, septal nuclei, corpus callosum or periventricular white matter. Rosette forming glioneuronal tumors may have similar histology and location but would show different molecular features (*FGFR1* mutation, *PIK3CA* or *NF1* mutation). Answers A and B are incorrect because a tumor with this profile (such as pilocytic astrocytoma) would have different molecular features such as *KIAA1549::BRAF* fusion. Answer C is incorrect because although this may sound morphologically similar to diffuse leptomeningeal glioneuronal tumor (DLGNT), such a tumor would be expected to have 1p deletion and *MAPK* pathway alteration such as *KIAA1549::BRAF* fusion. Answer E is incorrect because the superficial cortex would be an unusual location for myxoid glioneuronal tumor.
