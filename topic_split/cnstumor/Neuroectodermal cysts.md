Arachnoid cyst

Definition / general

* Arachnoid cysts are nonneoplastic, intracranial cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) filled spaces lined with arachnoid membranes ([Cureus 2018;10:e2458](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29888162))
* 1% of intracranial masses ([Neurosurgery 1997;41:951](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9316060))

Essential features

* Nonneoplastic, intracranial CSF filled spaces lined by meningothelial cells and an outer collagenous membrane
* Most primary developmental arachnoid cysts occur in the middle frontal fossa due to the splitting of arachnoid membranes ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 1981;40:61](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7205328))
* Meningothelial cells are positive for epithelial membrane antigen (EMA)

Terminology

* Meningeal cyst

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [G93.0](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/G00-G99/G89-G99/G93-/G93.0) - cerebral cysts
* ICD-11: [8D67](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f943887241) - intracranial arachnoid cyst

Epidemiology

* Arachnoid cysts are classified as primary developmental cysts or secondary cysts ([Cureus 2018;10:e2458](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29888162))
* 50 - 65% of primary developmental cysts present in the middle cranial fossa / Sylvian fissure ([Pediatr Neurosurg 1996;25:165](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9293543))
* Arachnoid cysts in the middle cranial fossa are found more frequently in men than in women ([Pediatr Neurosurg 1996;25:165](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9293543))

Sites

* Arise within both cranial and spinal meninges
* Most are supratentorial and found in the middle fossa ([J Neurosurg 2013;118:222](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23140149))
* Other sites include retrocerebellar, convexity, cerebellopontine angle and spinal cord ([J Neurosurg 2013;118:222](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23140149))

Pathophysiology

* Primary developmental cysts occur due to the splitting of arachnoid membranes in utero, resulting in abnormal collections of cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 1981;40:61](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7205328))
* Secondary cysts are less common and often occur after trauma, infection or surgery ([Case Rep Orthop 2015;2015:250710](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25861499))
* Mutation of the *FOXC2* gene has been reported in familial forms

Clinical features

* Depends on size and location
* Headaches are the most common symptom ([J Neurol Neurosurg Psychiatry 2007;78:1129](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17299015))
* Other symptoms include hydrocephalus, intracranial hypertension, dizziness, nausea, vomiting, mental status changes, ataxia, seizures and hearing loss ([Childs Nerv Syst 2015;31:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25123786))
* Arachnoid cysts can be asymptomatic ([Childs Nerv Syst 2015;31:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25123786))

Diagnosis

* Computed tomography (CT) and magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) for radiologic assessment
* Surgical resection is required for a definitive diagnosis

Radiology description

* MRI is the diagnostic procedure of choice
* Arachnoid cysts show low signal intensity on diffusion weighted imaging (DWI) and fluid attenuated inversion recovery (FLAIR) ([Tani Girisim Radyol 2003;9:418](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14730949))
* No enhancement

Treatment

* Surgery if symptomatic

Gross description

* Variable in size; can be very large with mass effects
* Thin, transparent wall with a clear, colorless fluid
* Cyst is distinct from leptomeninges and dura
* Reference: [Love: Greenfield's Neuropathology, 9th Edition, 2015](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/144416693X/pathologyoutl-20)

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Cyst wall is composed of a single layer of meningothelial cells and an outer collagenous membrane
* Meningothelial cells often partially denuded and may not always be recognizable
* Rare foci of meningothelial hyperplasia with or without psammoma bodies
* Focal inflammation (rare)
* Reference: [Love: Greenfield's Neuropathology, 9th Edition, 2015](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1498721281/pathologyoutl-20)

Negative stains

Electron microscopy description

* Tendency of meningothelial tissues to cleave along the dura arachnoid interface layer ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 1979;38:434](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/448402))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Mutation of the *FOXC2* gene has been reported in familial forms ([PLoS One 2013;8:e80548](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24278289))

Sample pathology report

* Cyst wall, excision:
  + Arachnoid cyst (see comment)
  + Comment: The histologic section shows a cystic lesion composed of a single layer of meningothelial cells with an outer layer of delicate fibrous tissue. The meningothelial cells are positive for EMA by immunohistochemistry, which confirms the diagnosis.

Differential diagnosis

* [Epidermoid cyst](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorepidermoidcyst.html):
  + Squamous cyst lining
  + Lamellar keratin debris
  + Positive for [keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)
* [Endodermal cyst](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorenterogenouscyst.html):
  + Pseudostratified epithelium cyst lining with bronchogenic (ciliated) or gastrointestinal (glands) differentiation
  + Positive for [keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)
* [Ependymal cyst](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorneuroectodermalcyst.html#cnstumorneuroectodermalcyst_ependymal):
  + Ciliated ependymal cyst lining
  + Positive for [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html)

Choroid plexus cyst

Definition / general

* Small cyst of choroid plexus containing CSF

Sites

* May be present throughout ventricular system but usually in glomus of lateral ventricles

Clinical features

* More prevalent in fetuses with chromosomal abnormalities (trisomy 18, trisomy 21, Aicardi syndrome)
* Common form affect fetuses in 1% of pregnancies; usually asymptomatic, resolves spontaneously by birth but large cysts can cause hydrocephalus
* Chromosomal abnormalities, specifically trisomy 18, should be considered if cysts are large ( > 1 cm), bilateral or irregular or if maternal age ≥ 32 years ([AJR Am J Roentgenol 2009;192:32](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19098176))
* In adults, usually asymptomatic, incidental postmortem finding

Radiology description

* On CT and MRI, usually show CSF density

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Cyst wall lined by cuboidal to columnar epithelium with occasional cobblestone appearance typical of normal choroid plexus
* Some are devoid of epithelial lining

Positive stains

* Cyst lining has same immunohistochemical profile as normal choroid plexus: positive for [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html), [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), transthyretin, [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html)

Negative stains

* [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html), [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html)

Glioependymal / ependymal cyst

Definition / general

* Rare, benign intraparenchymal and often paraventricular cyst lined by simple epithelium or glial tissue, S100+ or GFAP+, resting on neuroglia ([J Neuroradiol 1995;22:48](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7707134))
* Usually intracranial, not midline but may affect spinal cord; may affect adult cerebellum or represent burned out pilocytic astrocytoma
* Not in communication with ventricle or CSF spaces
* Rarely ruptures and causes meningitis
* Cyst lined by glial tissue

Ependymal cyst

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Simple columnar or cuboidal cells, often ciliated, resting on neuroglia; no fibrous capsule
* Alternatively, wall lined by gliosis, Rosenthal fibers present, variable hemosiderin; no epithelial lining

Positive stains

* [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), variable [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html)

Negative stains

* [CK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)

Electron microscopy description

* Neuroepithelial origin

Differential diagnosis

* [Endodermal cyst](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorenterogenouscyst.html):
  + CK+

A 71 year old patient with altered mental status had a 7.6 cm cystic lesion in the left frontoparietal convexity. The cystic lesion was excised. What immunohistochemistry confirms the diagnosis?

1. Cytokeratin
2. EMA
3. GFAP
4. Synaptophysin

Board review style answer #1

**B.** EMA. The histologic section shows a cystic lesion with a single layer of meningothelial cells with flattened nuclei and delicate fibrous tissue suggestive of an arachnoid cyst. The meningothelial cells are positive for EMA.
