Definition / general

* CNS WHO 2021 definition: diffusely infiltrating glioma with *IDH1* or *IDH2* mutation and codeletion of chromosome arms 1p and 19q (CNS WHO grade 2 or 3)

Essential features

* Diffusely infiltrating glial neoplasm with *IDH1* or *IDH2* mutation and 1p / 19q whole arm codeletion (both features are required for diagnosis)
* Morphology resembles nonneoplastic oligodendrocytes with round monotonous nuclei and perinuclear halos
* Chicken wire vasculature, microcalcifications and microcysts are characteristic ([Neuro Oncol 2014;16:1244](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24723566))
* Astrocytic differentiation does not preclude diagnosis if molecular features are present
  + Small gemistocytes (mini gemistocytes) with rounded bellies of eosinophilic, eccentrically placed cytoplasm are occasionally seen, especially in grade 3 tumors ([Acta Neuropathol 1984;64:265](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6391068))
* Presence of other atypical features (including multinucleated giant cells, sarcomatous features, neurocytic differentiation or ganglion-like cells) does not preclude a diagnosis of oligodendroglioma if the requisite molecular features are present ([Acta Neuropathol 2010;120:237](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20464403), [J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2002;61:947](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12430711), [Neuropathology 2014;34:323](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24444336))

ICD coding

* ICD-O:
  + [9450/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=90) - oligodendroglioma, NOS
  + [9451/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=90) - oligodendroglioma, anaplastic

Epidemiology

* Most epidemiologic data is based on histologic, rather than molecular, classification of oligodendroglioma
* Incidence of 0.23 cases per 100,000 population in the United States ([Neuro Oncol 2019;21:v1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31675094))
  + Incidence of CNS WHO grade 3 oligodendroglioma is 0.11
* Of all brain tumors in the United States:
  + 0.9% are oligodendroglioma WHO grade 2
  + 0.4% are oligodendroglioma WHO grade 3
* Peak incidence in fourth and fifth decades of life ([Neuro Oncol 2020;22:iv1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33123732))
* Rare in infants and children ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:1058](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24805856))
* Slight male predominance ([Neuro Oncol 2019;21:v1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31675094))

Sites

* Infiltrative neoplasm involving the white and gray matter
* Can occur anywhere in the neuraxis; most common locations ([Neuro Oncol 2020;22:iv1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33123732)):
  + Frontal lobes: 59%
  + Temporal lobes: 14%
  + Parietal lobes: 10%
  + Occipital lobes: 1%
* Rarely observed in midline structures, brainstem, cerebellum or spinal cord
* Leptomeningeal spread occasionally observed, particularly at recurrence ([Neurology 2019;92:e2483](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31019097))

Pathophysiology

* Cell (or cells) of origin for oligodendroglioma remains unknown
* *IDH* mutation is likely the initiating event (driver mutation), which precedes 1p / 19q codeletion ([Adv Anat Pathol 2015;22:50](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25461780), [Biomed Res Int 2014;2014:540236](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24877111))
* *IDH* mutations give rise to metabolic alterations, with increased production of 2-hydroxyglutarate (2HG)
* Increased 2HG inhibits histone demethylation, causing a hypermethylation phenotype in neoplastic cells: glioma CpG island methylated phenotype (G CIMP) ([Nature 2012;483:479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22343889), [Acta Neuropathol 2013;125:621](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23512379))

Etiology

* Generally sporadic without significant known risk factors
* Rare instances of familial oligodendroglioma and genetic alterations with associated increased risk of developing oligodendroglioma ([Neuro Oncol 2018;20:1625](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30165405), [Cancer 2005;103:2363](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15834925), [J Natl Cancer Inst 2014;107:384](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25482530))

Clinical features

* About 67% of patients present with seizure ([PLoS One 2017;12:e0188419](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29186201), [Nat Rev Neurol 2017;13:340](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28497806))
* Other common presenting symptoms: headache, focal neurologic deficits or cognitive / mental status change, depending on anatomic location

Grading

* WHO grade 2:
  + Well differentiated tumor lacking anaplastic features (brisk mitotic activity, microvascular proliferation, necrosis)
* WHO grade 3:
  + Prominent anaplastic features (necrosis, microvascular proliferation or brisk mitotic activity) are compatible with anaplastic oligodendroglioma, *IDH* mutant and 1p / 19q codeleted, WHO grade 3
  + Strict mitotic activity criteria do not currently exist
  + Some authors suggest ≥ 6 mitotic figures per 10 high power fields in resection specimens for grade 3 designation ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2001;60:248](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11245209))
  + Fewer mitotic figures might be sufficient for grade 3 designation in small biopsy specimens if other anaplastic features (vascular proliferation or necrosis) or significant nuclear atypia are present
  + *CDKN2A* homozygous deletion may serve as a molecular marker of CNS WHO grade 3 in *IDH* mutant and 1p / 19q codeleted oligodendrogliomas ([Neuro Oncol 2019;21:1519](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31832685))

Diagnosis

* Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), followed by stereotactic brain biopsy or surgical resection
* Methods to detect *IDH* gene mutation:
  + Immunohistochemistry for IDH1 R132H (positive in > 90% of tumors) ([Acta Neuropathol 2009;118:599](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19798509))
    - *IDH2* mutations overrepresented in oligodendrogliomas compared with astrocytomas ([Biomed Res Int 2014;2014:540236](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24877111))
  + Sanger sequencing
  + Droplet digital polymerase chain reaction (ddPCR)
  + Next generation sequencing
  + MRI techniques to detect 2-hydroxyglutarate and therefore *IDH* mutation are under investigation ([J Clin Invest 2013;123:3659](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23999439))
* Methods to detect 1p / 19q codeletion:
  + Fluorescent in situ hybridization (FISH)
  + Array comparative genomic hybridization
  + Polymerase chain reaction (PCR)

Radiology description

* Computed topography (CT):
  + Mixed density (hypodense and isodense) located in cortex or subcortical white matter ([Radiology 2017;284:316](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28723281))
  + High attenuation areas, likely from calcifications
* MRI:
  + Heterogeneous on T1 and T2 weighted imaging
  + Typically no diffusion restriction
  + Poorly circumscribed borders ([AJNR Am J Neuroradiol 2017;38:678](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28126746))
  + Cystic changes are relatively common ([Radiology 2017;284:316](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28723281))
  + Contrast enhancement present in 70% of WHO grade 3 tumors ([AJNR Am J Neuroradiol 2012;33:852](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22268087), [Eur J Cancer 2019;107:15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30529899))
  + Elevated 2HG by magnetic resonance spectroscopy could serve as radiologic surrogate of *IDH* mutation status ([Nat Med 2012;18:624](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22281806))

Prognostic factors

* Slow growing and relatively long overall survival
  + Median overall survival: 11.6 years; 10 year overall survival rate: 51 - 63% ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2005;64:479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15977639), [Neuro Oncol 2020;22:iv1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33123732))
  + Longer median survival compared with grade 2 *IDH* mutant astrocytoma (median overall survival: 10.9 years) ([Acta Neuropathol 2015;129:867](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25962792))
* Favorable features ([Crit Rev Oncol Hematol 2008;66:262](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18272388)):
  + Younger age at diagnosis
  + Tumor location in frontal lobe
  + Presentation with seizures
  + Macroscopically complete surgical resection
  + CNS WHO grade 2 histology
  + Higher postoperative Karnofsky score
* Unfavorable features:
  + Contrast enhancement on MRI
  + CNS WHO grade 3 histology ([Eur J Cancer 2020;137:10](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32721633), [Oncotarget 2014;5:1515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24722048))
  + *CDKN2A / CDKN2B* homozygous deletion ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2004;63:314](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15099021), [Neuro Oncol 2019;21:1519](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31832685))
* Local recurrence and malignant transformation are common

Case reports

* 26 year old man presents with nausea, headache and rash ([JAAD Case Rep 2019;6:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31956684))
* 43 year old woman with headaches, blurry vision and a right parietal mass ([Front Neurol 2018;9:700](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30210430))
* 44 year old man with sudden right sided optic neuritis ([BMJ Case Rep 2018;2018:bcr2018225318](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29954769))
* 48 year old man presents with seizures ([Front Oncol 2021;10:601452](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33520712))
* 55 year old man with mass lesion in the superior left temporal gyrus ([Brain Pathol 2019;29:693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31441176))

Treatment

* Gross total resection, if possible
* Adjuvant chemotherapy (temozolomide) and radiotherapy
  + Given to patients with symptomatic or progressive tumors, tumors with CNS WHO grade 3 histology or those with large postoperative residual tumor
* References: [Crit Rev Oncol Hematol 2008;66:262](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18272388), [Lancet 2005;366:985](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16168780)

Gross description

* Variably well defined, gray-pink mass
* Mucoid change can give a gelatinous consistency
* Areas of cystic degeneration, calcifications, hemorrhage or necrosis can be seen

Frozen section description

* Moderately cellular, diffusely infiltrating neoplasm
* Glia with mild to moderate nuclear atypia
* Round nuclei with speckled chromatin
* Calcifications, perineuronal satellitosis or perivascular accumulation of tumor cells may be seen
* Will not see perinuclear halos on frozen section or smear preparations
* Anaplastic features (necrosis, vascular proliferation, mitoses) may be seen in WHO grade 3 tumors
* Reference: [J Cytol 2011;28:147](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22090687)

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Closely packed cells with small, round, monotonous nuclei (slightly larger than a normal oligodendrocyte)
* Perinuclear clearing (fried egg appearance)
  + Formalin fixation artifact
  + Will not be seen on frozen sections or smear preparations
* Network of thin walled, branching blood vessels (chicken wire vasculature)
* Microcalcifications (calcospherites) are characteristic
* Presence of perineural, perivascular or subpial aggregates of tumor cells (secondary structures of Scherer)
* Occasional mitoses and moderate nuclear atypia are still consistent with grade 2 designation ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2001;60:248](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11245209))
* Not uncommon to find well differentiated / fibrillary astrocytic morphology ([Acta Neuropathol 1984;64:265](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6391068))
* Features of CNS WHO grade 3 oligodendroglioma:
  + Presence of microvascular proliferation
  + Presence of necrosis
  + Presence of brisk mitotic activity
    - Strict mitotic figure cutoffs do not currently exist; some authors suggest ≥ 6 mitoses per 10 high power fields for WHO grade 3 designation in tumors without necrosis or vascular proliferation ([Neuro Oncol 2014;16:1244](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24723566), [Neuro Oncol 2016;18:888](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27175000))

Positive stains

* [IDH1 (R132H)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsidh1.html)
  + Positive in > 90% of oligodendrogliomas ([Acta Neuropathol 2009;118:599](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19798509))
  + Negative staining is not incompatible with oligodendroglioma if 1p / 19q codeletion is present
* [Olig2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsolig2.html), [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html)
* [ATRX](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsatrx.html) (retained; wildtype pattern)
* [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) (weak staining in rare cells; wildtype staining pattern)
* [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html):
  + Grade 2 tumors: usually Grade 3 tumors: generally > 10% of tumor nuclei ([Neuro Oncol 2014;16:1244](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24723566), [Neuro Oncol 2016;18:888](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27175000))
* Trio of [IDH1 (R132H)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsidh1.html), [ATRX](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsatrx.html) and [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) is useful to distinguish oligodendroglioma from *IDH* mutant astrocytoma ([Acta Neuropathol 2012;124:615](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22886134))

Negative stains

* Loss of [H3K27](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsh3k27me3.html) trimethylation ([Acta Neuropathol 2020;139:597](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31912209))
* [Keratins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) (although certain cytokeratin cocktails may demonstrate crossreactivity)

Electron microscopy description

* Not routinely used for diagnostic purposes

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Oligodendroglioma is a molecularly defined diagnosis requiring demonstration of both:
  + *IDH1* or *IDH2* mutation
  + Unbalanced translocation between chromosome 1 and 19, resulting in whole arm loss of 1p and 19q chromosomal material (1p / 19q codeletion)
    - Incomplete or partial deletions are not compatible with oligodendroglioma diagnosis
* Other common molecular alterations:
  + *TERT* promotor mutation in vast majority ([Nat Genet 2015;47:458](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25848751))
    - Early (clonal) event in tumorogenesis ([Oncotarget 2019;10:3641](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31217899))
    - Often absent in teenagers with oligodendroglioma ([Acta Neuropathol Commun 2018;6:95](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30231927))
  + *CIC* mutation in 70% ([N Engl J Med 2015;372:2499](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26061753))
  + *FUBP1* mutations in 20 - 30% ([Science 2011;333:1453](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21817013))
  + *NOTCH1* mutation in 15% ([Nat Genet 2015;47:458](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25848751))
  + Loss of H3K27 trimethylation by immunohistochemistry ([Acta Neuropathol 2020;139:597](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31912209))
* Molecular alterations associated with tumor progression:
  + Increased copy number alterations ([Neuro Oncol 2018;20:66](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29016839))
  + *CDKN2A / CDKN2B* deletions on chromosome 9p21 ([PLoS One 2016;11:e0168728](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28030632))
  + *PIK3CA* mutation ([Clin Cancer Res 2019;25:4375](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30975663))
  + *TCF12* mutation ([Nat Commun 2015;6:7207](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26068201))
  + *MYC* amplification ([Nat Commun 2016;7:11263](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27090007))
* Epigenetic changes:
  + Glioma CpG island methylator phenotype (G CIMP) ([Cancer Cell 2010;17:510](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20399149))
  + *MGMT* promotor methylation is detectable in the majority of tumors ([Int J Cancer 2005;113:379](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15455350))

Differential diagnosis

* [Astrocytoma, *IDH* mutant](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/CNStumorgliomasastrocytomasIDHmutant.html):
  + Lacks 1p / 19q codeletion and *TERT* promotor mutations
  + Harbors *p53* or *ATRX* mutations
    - [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) expression strong and diffuse in tumor cells
    - [ATRX](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsatrx.html) expression lost in tumor cells
* **Other tumors with oligo-like morphology**:
  + [Dysembryoplastic neuroepithelial tumor (DNET)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorDNET.html)
  + [Central neurocytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorcentralneurocytoma.html)
  + **Polymorphous low grade neuroepithelial tumor of the young (PLNTY)**
  + [Clear cell ependymoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorependymoma.html)
  + **Metastatic clear cell carcinomas**
  + These tumors lack *IDH* mutations and 1p / 19q codeletion and are generally circumscribed instead of infiltrative
* **Macrophage rich lesions**:
  + Stain positive with macrophage markers
