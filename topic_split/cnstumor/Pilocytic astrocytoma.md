Definition / general

* Circumscribed, well differentiated astrocytic neoplasm with piloid (hair-like) processes, most commonly occurring in children and young adults, WHO grade 1 ([Acta Neuropathol 2016;131:803](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27157931))

Essential features

* Most common glioma in children ([Neuro Oncol 2020;22:iv1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33123732))
* Radiologic appearance often cystic with an enhancing mural nodule ([Radiographics 2004;24:1693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15537977))
* Generally circumscribed, biphasic, astrocytic neoplasm with piloid processes, Rosenthal fibers and eosinophilic granular bodies (EGBs)
* Activating genetic alterations in components of MAPK pathway (most frequently *BRAF* fusions) ([Nat Genet 2013;45:927](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23817572), [Nat Genet 2013;45:602](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23583981))
* WHO grade 1 ([Acta Neuropathol 2016;131:803](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27157931))

Terminology

* Pilocytic astrocytoma
* Juvenile pilocytic astrocytoma (not recommended)
* Spongioblastoma (not recommended)
* Pilomyxoid astrocytoma (subtype)
* Optic nerve glioma

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9421/1](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=90) - pilocytic astrocytoma
* ICD-11: [2A00.0Y](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f422307135%2fmms%2fother) - other specified gliomas of brain
* ICD-11: [XH29Q5](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f899856825) - pilomyxoid astrocytoma

Epidemiology

* M = F ([Neuro Oncol 2014;16:iv1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25304271))
* 5.4% of all gliomas ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2005;64:479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15977639))
* Most common glioma in children ([Neuro Oncol 2014;16:iv1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25304271))
* Most common glioma during first 2 decades of life ([Neuro Oncol 2014;16:iv1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25304271))

Sites

* Found throughout neuraxis
* Most common sites: cerebellum (42%), supratentorial (36%), optic pathway / hypothalamus (9%), brainstem (9%), spinal cord (2%) ([J Neurosurg 2003;98:1170](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12816259))
* Infratentorial location most common in childhood ([J Neurooncol 2017;131:163](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27743145))

Pathophysiology

* Mutation in MAPK pathway component → pathway activation → transcription factor activation → increased cell growth and proliferation
* Biallelic inactivation of *NF1* in the setting of neurofibromatosis type 1 ([Genome Res 2013;23:431](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23222849))

Etiology

* Most cases are sporadic
* Germline mutations in MAPK pathway genes, including *FGFR1, NF1, PTPN11* and *RAF1*

Clinical features

* Neurological deficits depending on tumor location
* Nonlocalizing signs
  + Macrocephaly
  + Headache
  + Endocrinopathy
  + Increased intracranial pressure
* Optic pathway tumors in 15% of patients with neurofibromatosis type 1 ([Ophthalmology 1984;91:929](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6436764))

Diagnosis

* Diagnosis may be made on histologic features alone ([Acta Neuropathol 2015;129:775](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25792358))
* When microscopic findings are limited / ambiguous, molecular testing may be necessary to assess for gene fusions and other alterations

Radiology description

* Well demarcated enhancing mass ([Insights Imaging 2014;5:387](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24789122))
* Cystic mass with enhancing mural nodule (~66% of cases), solid or heterogeneous mixed solid - cystic
* Fusiform mass in optic pathway

Prognostic factors

* 10 year survival > 95% after surgical intervention alone ([J Neurosurg 2003;98:1170](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12816259))
* Optic nerve tumors in NF1 patients: favorable ([Ann Neurol 2007;61:189](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17387725))
* Deep, midline location: high rates of incomplete resection, recurrence, shorter survival ([Neuropathol Appl Neurobiol 2013;39:693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23278243))
* Supratentorial: poor ([Cancer 1993;72:1335](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8339223))
* Partial resection: poor ([Neurosurgery 2003;53:544](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12943571))
* Age > 40: poor ([J Neurooncol 2020;148:187](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32342331))
* Anaplastic features in adults: poor, no definitive WHO grade ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:147](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20061938), [Acta Neuropathol 2020;139:287](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31677015), [Acta Neuropathol 2016;131:803](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27157931))
* Pilomyxoid astrocytoma variant: unclear prognosis, no definitive WHO grade ([Acta Neuropathol 2016;131:803](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27157931))

Case reports

* 13 year old girl with left cerebellar cystic mass ([NMC Case Rep J 2019;6:95](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31417840))
* 33 year old woman with mesial temporal lobe mass ([Case Rep Pathol 2020;2020:5903863](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32082673))
* 39 year old woman with optic nerve glioma ([Brain Tumor Pathol 2021;38:59](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33098465))

Treatment

* Gross total surgical resection
* Radiation or chemotherapy may be considered for subtotally resected or recurrent cases
* Variable success reported for MAPK pathway targeted therapies ([Proc Natl Acad Sci U S A 2013;110:5957](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23533272), [Nat Med 2013;19:1401](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24202393))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Growth pattern
  + Predominantly solid / circumscribed; often limited peripheral infiltration
  + Frequent extension into subarachnoid space
* Biphasic appearance
  + Compact fibrillar portions: elongated nuclei, bipolar piloid processes, Rosenthal fibers
  + Loose microcystic portions: round to oval nuclei, cobweb-like processes, eosinophilic granular bodies
* Occasional "pennies on a plate" multinucleated cells
* Oligodendroglioma-like areas may be present
* Glomeruloid vessels
* Regressive / degenerative changes
  + Degenerative atypia
  + Vascular hyalinization
  + Infarct-like necrosis
  + Calcification
  + Perivascular lymphocytes
* Anaplasia in a minority of cases
  + Increased mitotic activity (> 4 mitoses/10 high power fields) with or without necrosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:147](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20061938))
* Pilomyxoid astrocytoma subtype
  + Variant with angiocentric arrangement of monophasic bipolar tumor cells in a myxoid background

Cytology description

* Smear preparation (alcohol fixed, H&E stained) ([Cancer Cytopathol 2015;123:331](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25766425))
  + Mildly atypical glial cells with long piloid processes
  + Ovoid nuclei with delicate chromatin
  + Rosenthal fibers
  + Eosinophilic granular bodies

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Activating alterations in components of MAPK pathway ([Nat Genet 2013;45:927](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23817572), [Nat Genet 2013;45:602](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23583981))
  + > 70% *KIAA1549-BRAF* fusion due to 7q34 tandem duplication
    - ~90% cerebellar cases
    - ~50% supratentorial cases
  + ~5% other *BRAF* fusion
  + ~5% *BRAF* V600E
  + ~8% *NF1* loss
  + < 5% *FGFR1* mutation
  + < 5% *FGFR1* fusions / internal tandem duplication
  + ~2% *NTRK* fusions
  + *PTPN1* mutation (rare)
  + *KRAS* mutation (rare)
  + *RAF1* fusions (rare)
* Chromosomal polysomies (5, 6, 7, 11, 15) detected by comparative genomic hybridization (CGH) ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2006;65:1049](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17086101), [Br J Cancer 2000;82:1218](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10735509))
* Infratentorial / supratentorial tumors show distinct gene expression and DNA methylation signatures ([Acta Neuropathol 2013;126:291](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23660940))
* H3 K27M mutation (rare) ([Brain Pathol 2019;29:126](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30192422))
* Negative for *IDH1* / *IDH2* exon 4 mutations
* Usually negative for *TP53* mutations

Sample pathology report

* Brain, cerebellum, biopsy:
  + Pilocytic astrocytoma, WHO grade 1 (see comment)
  + Comment: Positive for *KIAA1549-BRAF* fusion.

Differential diagnosis

* Neoplastic
  + Diffuse glioma (including [oligodendroglioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumoroligodendroglioma.html), [IDH mutant astrocytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/CNStumorgliomasastrocytomasIDHmutant.html), [IDH wildtype astrocytic gliomas](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/CNStumorgliomasglioblastomasIDHwildtype.html), [diffuse midline glioma, H3 K27M mutant](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumordmgh3k27m.html)):
    - Diffusely infiltrative
    - Eosinophilic granular bodies and Rosenthal fibers uncommon
    - Distinct molecular features
  + **High grade astrocytoma with piloid features**:
    - High grade histological features
    - Distinct molecular features
    - Frequent loss of [ATRX](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsatrx.html) nuclear staining ([Acta Neuropathol 2018;136:273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29564591))
  + [Diffuse leptomeningeal glioneuronal tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumordiffuseleptglioneurtumor.html):
    - Primary leptomeningeal location
    - Commonly not reactive for [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html)
    - Distinct molecular features
  + [Pleomorphic xanthoastrocytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorpleomorphicxanthoastrocytoma.html):
    - Most frequently in superficial cerebral cortex
    - Pleomorphic, occasionally xanthomatous cells
    - Reticulin rich foci
  + [Ganglioglioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorganglioncelltumor.html):
    - Neoplastic dysmorphic ganglion cells
  + [Dysembryoplastic neuroepithelial tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorDNET.html):
    - Predominantly cortical
    - Mucin rich nodules
    - Floating neurons in microcysts
* Nonneoplastic
  + **Reactive piloid gliosis**:
    - Hypocellular
    - Lack of genetic alterations
  + **Vascular malformation**

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/cnstumorpilocyticastrocytoma_Cimino20.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/cnstumorpilocyticastrocytoma_Cimino20.jpg)

The above tumor is from the cerebellum of a child. What is the most common underlying genetic alteration in this entity?

1. *TP53* mutation
2. *IDH1* R132H
3. *BRAF* V600E mutation
4. *BRAF* fusion involving *KIAA1549*
5. *NF1* loss

Board review style answer #1

**D**. *BRAF* fusion involving *KIAA1549*. *TP53* mutations (answer A) are uncommon in pilocytic astrocytoma. *IDH1* R132H (answer B) is the most common mutation in IDH mutant astrocytomas and oligodendrogliomas but should be negative in pilocytic astrocytomas. *BRAF* V600E mutation (answer C) and *NF1* loss (answer E) occur in pilocytic astrocytomas at lower frequencies.

Board review style question #2

Which of the following is true of pilocytic astrocytomas?

1. Show diffuse p53 immunoreactivity
2. Most frequently occur in adults
3. Demonstrate reticulin rich foci
4. Associated with neurofibromatosis 2
5. Potentially curable with resection

Board review style answer #2

**E**. Potentially curable with resection. Pilocytic astrocytomas should not show significant p53 immunopositivity (answer A) and they usually occur in children (answer B). Reticulin rich foci are a more typical feature of pleomorphic xanthoastrocytoma or gliosarcoma (answer C). In addition, pilocytic astrocytomas are associated with NF1 (answer D).
