Definition / general

* Indolent cerebral neoplasm characterized by a strong association with seizures in young individuals, diffuse growth patterns, frequent presence of oligodendroglioma-like components, calcifications, CD34 immunoreactivity and MAPK pathway activating genetic abnormalities
* CNS WHO grade 1
* Recently introduced in WHO 2021 classification of CNS tumors

Essential features

* Recently described, frequently epileptogenic, low grade neuroepithelial tumor ([Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792))
* Typically affects children and young adults
* Characterized by diffuse growth patterns, oligodendroglioma-like cellular components, CD34 immunopositivity and MAPK pathway activating genetic abnormalities
* Generally, exhibits a benign clinical course ([Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792))

Terminology

* Has been described under the generic designation of long term epilepsy associated tumors ([Acta Neuropathol 2014;128:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24858213))
* Not recommended by WHO
  + Diffuse glioneuronal tumor
  + Diffuse or nonspecific form of dysembryoplastic neuroepithelial tumor
  + Massively calcified low grade glioma

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9413/0](https://seer.cancer.gov/tools/solidtumor/2023/Non_malignant_CNS_STM.pdf#page=28) - polymorphous low grade neuroepithelial tumor of the young
* ICD-11: [2A00.2Y](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#915334913%2Fother) - other specified tumors of neuroepithelial tissue of brain

Epidemiology

* Rare; population based incidence data are unavailable
* Age: mostly occurs in the second and third decades of life (mean age at diagnosis: 22.16 years; range: 4 - 57 years) ([Acta Neurol Belg 2023;123:327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36894761))
* Slight male predominance ([Acta Neurol Belg 2023;123:327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36894761))

Sites

* Cerebral tumors, usually with cortical and subcortical components ([Neuroradiology 2022;64:1255](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35001164))
* Mostly right sided (64.4%) ([Acta Neurol Belg 2023;123:327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36894761))
* Majority involve temporal lobes (69%) with frequent involvement of medial / posteroinferior structures ([Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792), [Acta Neurol Belg 2023;123:327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36894761))
* Other sites include frontal, parietal and occipital lobes and rarely, third ventricular region ([Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792), [Acta Neurol Belg 2023;123:327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36894761))

Pathophysiology

* Aberrant CD34 expression possibly reflects an origin from developmentally dysregulated neural precursors ([Acta Neuropathol 1999;97:481](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10334485))
* Somatic MAPK pathway activating genetic abnormalities (particularly *BRAF* mutations or *FGFR* fusions) definitely drive the pathogenesis of PLNTY; however, the specific mechanisms by which these genetic alterations contribute to the development of PLNTY are not clear ([Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792))

Etiology

* Unknown predisposing factors
* Germline *ATM* mutation was identified in a single case, which was associated with progressive disease ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2019;78:1100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31617914))

Clinical features

* Typically presents with seizures (intractable epilepsy [47.6%] or a single seizure attack [42.9%]) ([Acta Neurol Belg 2023;123:327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36894761))
* Less common: headache, dizziness, visual disturbances and mood disorders ([Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792), [Acta Neurol Belg 2023;123:327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36894761))

Diagnosis

* Neuroimaging: MRI and CT ([Acta Neurol Belg 2023;123:327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36894761))
* Biopsy
* WHO essential and desirable diagnostic criteria
  + Essential diagnostic criteria (presence of the following 6 characteristics)
    - Diffuse growth pattern (at least regionally)
    - Oligodendroglioma-like components (may be minor)
    - Few (if any) mitotic figures
    - Regional CD34 expression by tumor cells and by ramified neural cells in associated cerebral cortex
    - *IDH* wild type status
    - Unequivocal expression of BRAF p.V600E on immunohistochemical assessment or molecular diagnostic evidence of *BRAF* V600E mutations, *FGFR2* or *FGFR3* fusions or potentially other MAPK pathway driving genetic abnormalities
  + Desirable diagnostic criteria
    - Conspicuous calcification (characteristic, although not constant)
    - Absence of 1p / 19q codeletion

Radiology description

* CT
  + Often calcified (76.2%) ([Neuroradiology 2019;61:1327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31396664), [Acta Neurol Belg 2023;123:327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36894761))
* MRI
  + Often superficially situated ([Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792), [Neuroradiology 2019;61:1327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31396664))
  + Circumscribed (76.7%) or blurred outlines ([Acta Neurol Belg 2023;123:327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36894761))
  + Usually cystic and solid ([Acta Neurol Belg 2023;123:327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36894761), [BMC Neurol 2020;20:123](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32252664))
  + Signal heterogeneity with T2 hyperintense or mixed signal, T1 hypointense or mixed signal ([Acta Neurol Belg 2023;123:327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36894761))
  + FLAIR hyperintense
  + Majority shows no contrast enhancement (67.5%); patchy or nodular contrast enhancement in 32.5% of cases ([Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792), [Acta Neurol Belg 2023;123:327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36894761))
  + No substantial edema or mass effect ([Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792))

Prognostic factors

* Generally exhibits a benign clinical course ([Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792))
* Amenable to control by gross total resection with relief from seizures or reduced seizure frequency in most cases ([Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792))
* Rarely exhibits local recurrence (11%), tumor progression or malignant transformation ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2019;78:1100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31617914), [Acta Neuropathol 2021;141:123](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33226472))
* Molecular events associated with less favorable prognosis include germline *ATM* mutation and somatic mutations involving *RB1, TP53, ATRX, PTEN* and *TEK* ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2021;80:821](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34363682), [BMC Neurol 2020;20:123](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32252664), [J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2019;78:1100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31617914), [Acta Neuropathol 2021;141:123](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33226472))
* Radiological contrast enhancement seems to adversely impact prognosis, recurrence and seizure control ([Acta Neurol Belg 2023;123:327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36894761))

Case reports

* 13 year old girl with PLNTY mimicking an intracranial granuloma ([Indian J Radiol Imaging 2023;33:567](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37811183))
* 15 year old girl with malignant transformation of PLNTY ([Acta Neuropathol 2021;141:123](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33226472))
* 37 year old man presented with progressive headache ([Egyptian Journal of Neurosurgery 2023;38:7](https://doi.org/10.1186/s41984-023-00192-2))
* 50 year old woman with longstanding epilepsy ([Surg Neurol Int 2021;12:470](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34621585))

Treatment

* Surgical resection
* Targeted therapies: the MAPK pathway activating genetic alterations are potentially targetable; this provides additional therapeutic options, particularly for tumors that cannot be completely resected or that are located in surgically challenging locations ([Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792), [J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2021;80:821](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34363682), [J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2019;78:1100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31617914))

Gross description

* Unencapsulated, indistinctly demarcated from normal brain
* Soft to friable, gray-white masses

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Exhibits both diffuse and compact growth patterns ([Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792))
* Typically shows an oligodendroglioma-like cellular component ([Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792))
  + Usually dominant, may be minor
  + Ranges from uniform cells with small and round nuclei with perinuclear haloes to cells exhibiting obvious nuclear pleomorphism with more oval or spindled nuclear contours, wrinkled or grooved nuclear membranes and intranuclear pseudoinclusions ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2019;78:1100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31617914), [Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792))
* Astrocytic or morphologically ambiguous elements may also be seen, including fibrillary, spindled and pleomorphic forms ([Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792))
* Subtle perivascular pseudorosettes may be seen
* Calcifications are present in the large majority, ranging from discrete calcospherules to confluent calcific masses with osseous metaplasia ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2019;78:1100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31617914), [Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792))
* Less common / rare: eosinophilic granular bodies, modestly dysmorphic neurons in small numbers and gemistocytic elements (7.7%) ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2019;78:1100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31617914), [Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792), [J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2021;80:821](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34363682))
* Few (if any) mitotic figures; rarely, increased mitotic activity (up to 4 mitoses/10 high power fields) reported (15%) ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2021;80:821](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34363682))
* Generally absent are foci of necrosis, microvascular proliferation, myxoid microcysts, neurocytic or ependymal rosettes, Rosenthal fibers and clearly neoplastic neuronal components (the nosological position of neoplasms otherwise qualifying as PLNTY but having nodular ganglion cell aggregates is unclear) ([Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792))

Cytology description

* PLNTY cannot be identified on cytological grounds alone
* Intraoperative smear preparations may show
  + Tumor cells with rounded nuclei and devoid of cytoplasm
  + Tumor cells with irregular nuclear contours and cytoplasmic processes
  + Tumor cells attached to blood vessels in a pseudorosette fashion ([Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Consistently associated with MAPK pathway activation by either *BRAF* V600E mutations or fusion events involving *FGFR2* or *FGFR3* in a mutually exclusive fashion; however, these abnormalities are hardly specific to PLNTY as a transforming molecular event ([Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792), [Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792), [J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2019;78:1100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31617914), [J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2021;80:821](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34363682))
* *FGFR* fusions include *FGFR2::CTNNA3, FGFR2:SHTN1 (KIAA1598), FGFR2::INA, FGFR3::TACC3* and *FGFR2::MPRIP* ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2021;80:821](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34363682), [Acta Neurol Belg 2023;123:327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36894761))
* Fusion positive tumors occur predominantly in pediatric patients and are more frequently associated with seizures / epilepsy, whereas *BRAF* V600E mutation positive tumors predominantly affect adult patients ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2021;80:821](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34363682))
* Has a distinct DNA methylation profile most closely aligned to that of ganglioglioma ([Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792))
* Exhibits copy number changes predominantly involving whole chromosomes, suggesting that PLNTY is also characterized by chromosomal instability ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2021;80:821](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34363682))
* PLNTY does not harbor *IDH* mutations and does not exhibit 1p / 19q codeletion
* Other reported molecular events include *NTRK2* alterations, *KIAA1549::BRAF* fusion and low level *FGFR3* amplification; molecular events associated with less favorable prognosis include germline *ATM* mutation and somatic mutations involving *RB1, TP53, ATRX, PTEN* and *TEK* ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2021;80:821](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34363682), [BMC Neurol 2020;20:123](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32252664), [J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2019;78:1100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31617914), [Acta Neuropathol 2021;141:123](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33226472))

Sample pathology report

* Brain mass lesion, temporal lobe, gross total resection:
  + Polymorphous low grade neuroepithelial tumor of the young (PLNTY), CNS WHO grade 1
  + Molecular genetics: evidence of *BRAF* V600E mutations, *FGFR2* or *FGFR3* fusions or potentially other MAPK pathway driving genetic abnormalities

Differential diagnosis

* [Adult type diffuse glioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorgliomageneral.html) ([oligodendroglioma, *IDH* mutant and 1p / 19q codeleted](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumoroligodendrogliomaidhmutant.html) or [low grade diffuse astrocytoma, *IDH* mutant](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/CNStumorgliomasastrocytomasIDHmutant.html)):
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)- ([Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792), [World Neurosurg 2019;127:47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30926558))
  + *IDH* mutations
  + 1p / 19q codeletion in oligodendroglioma
  + Lacks MAPK pathway activating genetic abnormalities
* [Ependymoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorependymoma.html):
  + Often [OLIG2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsolig2.html)-
  + Dot or ring-like [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)+ ([Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792))
* **Diffuse glioneuronal tumor with oligodendroglioma-like features and nuclear clusters (DGONC)**:
  + Characteristic feature is the presence of scattered multinucleated cells (pennies on a plate) and nuclear clusters composed of large pleomorphic nuclei
  + [Synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html)+, [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html)-, [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)- ([Neuropathol Appl Neurobiol 2021;47:464](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33325069))
* [Diffuse leptomeningeal glioneuronal tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumordiffuseleptglioneurtumor.html):
  + Leptomeningeal based
  + [Synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html)+, [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html)-
  + Concurrent *KIAA1549::BRAF* fusion with isolated 1p deletion or 1p / 19q codeletion
* [Ganglioglioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorganglioncelltumor.html):
  + Clearly neoplastic neuronal components
  + Features that support a diagnosis of PLNTY over ganglioglioma include abundant calcifications, diffuse strong [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) staining of tumor cells and *FGFR2* or *FGFR3* fusions ([Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792))
  + Nosological position of neoplasms otherwise qualifying as PLNTY but having nodular ganglion cell aggregates is unclear ([Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792))
* [Pilocytic astrocytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorpilocyticastrocytoma.html):
  + Biphasic pattern
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)- ([Balkan Med J 2019;36:3](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29983406))
* [Diffuse astrocytoma, *MYB* or *MYBL1* altered](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumordiffuseastrocytomamyb.html):
  + [Olig2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsolig2.html)- ([Acta Neuropathol 2020;139:193](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31563982))
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)- ([Acta Neuropathol 2020;139:193](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31563982))
* [Diffuse low grade glioma, MAPK pathway altered](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumordiffuselowgradegliomaMAPK.html):
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) is typically limited to a few cells, unlike the widespread expression seen in PLNTY
  + PLNTYs likely represent a subset of a larger family of tumors reported as diffuse astrocytomas or oligodendrogliomas of pediatric type having MAPK pathway activating genetic abnormalities ([Acta Neuropathol 2017;133:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27812792))

Board review style question #1

[![Diffuse growth pattern of oligodendroglioma-like cells](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/cnstumorplntyabdelzaher03.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/cnstumorplntyabdelzaher03.jpg)

A 14 year old boy presented with refractory epilepsy. MRI showed a right temporal cystic and solid nonenhancing mass lesion. The mass was surgically resected and histopathological examination showed a low grade tumor composed of oligodendroglioma-like tumor cells arranged in a diffuse fashion as shown in the image above. Immunohistochemistry for CD34 showed regional positivity associated with positive ramified neurons in the adjacent cortex. Which of the following molecular events is characteristic of this tumor?

1. 1p / 19q codeletion
2. *IDH* mutation
3. MAPK pathway activating abnormalities
4. *PRKCA* gene fusion

Board review style answer #1

**C.** MAPK pathway activating abnormalities. PLNTY is characterized by a strong association with seizures in young individuals, diffuse growth patterns, frequent presence of oligodendroglioma-like components and CD34 immunoreactivity. It is molecularly characterized by MAPK pathway activating genetic abnormalities.
Answers A and B are incorrect because PLNTY does not harbor *IDH* mutations or 1p / 19q codeletion.
Answer D is incorrect because *PRKCA* gene fusion (mainly *SLC44A1::PRKCA*) is the hallmark of papillary glioneuronal tumor (PGNT).

[Comment Here](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/)

Reference: [Polymorphous low grade neuroepithelial tumor of the young (PLNTY)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorplnty.html)

Board review style question #2

Polymorphous low grade neuroepithelial tumor of the young (PLNTY) is characterized by which of the following features?

1. Absence of calcification
2. CD34 negative immunostaining
3. Clinical presentation of headache
4. Oligodendroglioma-like components

Board review style answer #2

**D.** Oligodendroglioma-like components. PLNTY is characterized by frequent presence of oligodendroglioma-like components.
Answer A is incorrect because calcification is a frequent finding in PLNTY.
Answer B is incorrect because CD34 immunoreactivity is a characteristic finding in PLNTY.
Answer C is incorrect because PLNTY is characterized by a strong association with seizures in young individuals.
