Definition / general

* Benign neuroglial tumor that partly consists of large ganglion-like astrocytes and is usually located in the wall of the lateral ventricles, near the foramen of Monro ([Neuropathology 2009;29:25](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18564101))
* Majority of subependymal giant cell astrocytoma (SEGA) cases arise in tuberous sclerosis complex (TSC), which is due to *TSC1* gene (chromosome 9q34) and *TSC2* gene (chromosome 16p13) mutations ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2004;63:1236](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15624760))
* Classified as CNS WHO grade 1

Essential features

* Characterized by varying proportions of several different tumor cell histologies: spindle cells, gemisocytic-like cells, ganglion-like cells and polygonal cells
* Positive for glial markers (GFAP, S100), neuronal markers (variable) and TTF1 by immunohistochemistry
* Close association with tuberous sclerosis complex (TSC)

Terminology

* Subependymal giant cell astrocytoma (SEGA)
* Subependymal giant cell tumor: not recommended

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9384/1](https://iris.who.int/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf#page=89) - subependymal giant cell astrocytoma
* ICD-10: [D43.2](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D37-D48/D43-/D43.2) - neoplasm of uncertain behavior of brain, unspecified
* ICD-11: [2A00.0Y & XH1L48](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#422307135%2Fother%20%26%20887826408) - other specified gliomas of brain & subependymal giant cell astrocytoma

Epidemiology

* Typically occurs in the first 2 decades of life, with a median age of ~13 years (range: 1 - 31 years), showing no gender preference ([Clin Neuropathol 2016;35:295](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27390104))
* Represents 1 - 2% of pediatric brain tumors; often occurs with TSC ([J Clin Med 2023;12:956](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36769603), [World Neurosurg 2018;118:e263](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29966782))
* Prevalence of SEGA in TSC patients varies between 6% and 25% in different studies ([J Clin Med 2023;12:956](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36769603))
* SEGA is the most common CNS tumor in patients with TSC
* Prevalence of non-TSC associated SEGA is not clear due to the rarity of the cases ([J Neurosurg Pediatr 2015;16:134](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25978531))

Sites

* Subependymal region of the lateral ventricle near the foramen of Monro and with extension into the lateral ventricle ([Neuropathology 2009;29:25](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18564101))
* Rarely found around the third ventricle ([Pathol Oncol Res 2004;10:219](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15619643))
* Rare extraventricular masses have been reported either sporadically or in patients with TSC ([Radiol Case Rep 2020;16:180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33250950))
* Can be bilateral ([Eur J Neurol 2009;16:691](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19236458))
* Retina is a rare possible site

Pathophysiology

* TSC associated SEGA
  + Subependymal nodules (SENs) are considered the precursors of SEGAs ([Childs Nerv Syst 1989;5:43](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2702677))
  + Pathogenesis of SEN transformation into SEGA is not fully understood ([Childs Nerv Syst 1989;5:43](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2702677))
  + Some studies show a second hit inactivation of *TSC1* or *TSC2* genes in patients with SEGAs leading to complete loss of tuberin - hamartin complex followed by mTORC1 activation ([J Clin Med 2023;12:956](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36769603))
  + Additional evidence suggests SEN to SEGA transformation might be alternatively triggered by the upregulation in the genes related to the MAPK pathway, as shown by significant upregulation of p-ERK, p-MEK or p-RSK1 in SEGAs but not in SEN lesions ([Folia Neuropathol 2015;53:8](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25909870))
  + *BRAF* p. V600E mutation has not been reported in recent large case series but has been reported previously ([Brain 2020;143:131](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31834371), [J Surg Oncol 2015;111:359](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25346165))
* Non-TSC associated SEGA
  + Rarely occurs in non-TSC patients
  + Thought to be the result of 2 somatic mutations in 1 of the *TSC1* or *TSC2* genes, limited to the tumor ([J Clin Med 2023;12:956](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36769603))

Etiology

* Strong association with inherited TSC

Clinical features

* Asymptomatic tumors are identified during regular screening for TSC ([Pediatr Neurol 2013;49:439](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24138953))
* Symptoms of increased intracranial pressure (ICP) due to CSF flow obstruction
  + Hydrocephalus, headache, nausea, papilledema / visual impairment and behavioral changes or rarely, acute intramural or intraventricular hemorrhage ([Neurology 2004;63:1457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15505165), [J Clin Med 2023;12:956](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36769603))
* Seizure ([Neuropathology 2009;29:25](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18564101), [Pathol Oncol Res 2004;10:219](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15619643))

Diagnosis

* Diagnostic criteria for subependymal giant cell astrocytoma per WHO classification of tumors of the central nervous system (5th edition)
  + Essential
    - Characteristic histopathological features
    - Immunopositivity for glial markers (GFAP, S100)
    - Variable immunopositivity of neuronal markers (synaptophysin, NeuN, neurofilament)
  + Desirable
    - Immunopositivity for thyroid transcription factor 1 (TTF1)
    - Lost or reduced expression of tuberin and hamartin
    - Immunopositivity of phosphorylated S6
    - DNA methylation profile of SEGA
    - Clinical history of TSC
    - *TSC1* or *TSC2* mutation

Radiology description

* Intraventricular mass at the foramen of Monro
* Potentially bilateral
* Size is typically > 1 cm
* MRI findings
  + Heterogeneous hypointense to isointense on T1 weighted images and hyperintense on T2 weighted images and fluid attenuated inversion recovery (FLAIR) bright infiltrative lesion(s) (relative to gray matter) and possible marked enhancement on T1 C+ (Gd) ([J Clin Med 2023;12:956](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36769603))
* CT findings
  + Isoattenuating or slightly hyperattenuating compared to gray matter, with possible calcification, hemorrhage and hydrocephalus
  + Subependymal nodules with marked contrast enhancement are frequently seen ([J Clin Med 2023;12:956](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36769603))

Prognostic factors

* Generally, has a good prognosis with complete resection ([Neurosurgery 2007;60:83](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17228255))
* Larger or symptomatic tumors are often associated with higher morbidity and recurrence ([Pathol Oncol Res 2004;10:219](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15619643))
* Rare cases of transformation to a high grade glioma have been reported ([Neurosciences Riyadh 2020;25:61](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31982898))

Case reports

* 22 weeks gestational age girl with congenital subependymal giant cell astrocytoma detected on antenatal ultrasound ([Surg Neurol Int 2020;11:26](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32123614))
* 11 month old boy with infantile atypical subependymal giant cell astrocytoma with TSC ([Neurosciences Riyadh 2020;25:61](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31982898))
* 9 year old boy with subependymal giant cell astrocytoma in the absence of genetic or phenotypic evidence of TSC with a 10 year follow up ([Childs Nerv Syst 2021;37:1381](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32808065))
* 10 year old boy with subependymal giant cell astrocytoma without TSC ([J Med Case Rep 2016;10:35](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26861567))
* 12 year old boy with SEGA diagnosed by intraoperative squash cytology ([J Lab Physicians 2016;8:58](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27013816))

Treatment

* Surgery for symptomatic tumors ([Neurochirurgie 2022;68:627](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35907444), [Expert Opin Pharmacother 2020;21:1329](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32338549))
* When detected on surveillance studies of TSC patients, monitoring or immediate surgery is indicated by size ([Neurochirurgie 2022;68:627](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35907444))
* mTOR pathway inhibitors showed significant reduction in tumor size ([N Engl J Med 2010;363:1801](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21047224), [Expert Opin Pharmacother 2020;21:1329](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32338549))

Gross description

* Well defined and sharply demarcated
* Multinodular
* Solid with or without cystic change
* Firm
* Occasionally hemorrhagic with or without calcifications ([Love: Greenfield's Neuropathology, 9th Edition, 2015](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1498721281/pathologyoutl-20))

Frozen section description

* Sharply demarcated and noninfiltrating tumor
* Composed of cells with variable morphologies including spindle cells, gemistocytic-like cells, nuclear pseudoinclusions, multinucleated cells and ganglion-like cells in a fibrillary and vascularly rich background

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Well circumscribed and sharply demarcated tumors ([Neuropathology 2009;29:25](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18564101))
* Composed of cells with variable morphologies ([Neuropathology 2009;29:25](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18564101))
  + Spindle cells
  + Gemistocytic-like cells with abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm
  + Ganglion-like cells with eccentric, vesicular nuclei and prominent nucleoli
  + Occasional nuclear pseudoinclusions
  + Multinucleated cells
* Variable architectural structures including fascicles of spindle cells, sheets or nested arrangement as well as prominent vascularization or an angiocentric (perivascular rosette-like) architectural pattern ([Neuropathology 2009;29:25](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18564101))
* Calcifications, marked nuclear pleomorphism and multinucleated cells are common ([Folia Neuropathol 2011;49:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21455842))
* Fibrillary stroma with lymphocytic inflammation and mast cells are often present ([Pathol Oncol Res 2004;10:219](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15619643))
* Elevated mitotic activity, microvascular proliferation and necrosis are rare ([Neurol Med Chir Tokyo 2002;42:224](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12064158))
* Subependymal nodules are not distinguishable from SEGAs based on the histomorphological features, thus requiring radiological correlation ([Brain Dev 2001;23:508](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11701246))

Cytology description

* Loosely cohesive clusters of tumor cells arranged around delicate capillary vessels in a fibrillary background ([J Lab Physicians 2016;8:58](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27013816))
* Spindle cells with elongated nuclei ([J Lab Physicians 2016;8:58](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27013816))
* Large polygonal cells with eccentric nuclei and abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm ([J Lab Physicians 2016;8:58](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27013816))
* Some large cells show a distinct nucleolus ([J Lab Physicians 2016;8:58](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27013816))
* Binucleation and multinucleation ([J Lab Physicians 2016;8:58](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27013816))
* Inflammatory cells in the background ([J Lab Physicians 2016;8:58](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27013816))

Electron microscopy description

* Variable number of giant and multinucleated cells in the background of glial cell processes filled with intermediate filaments ([Neuropathology 2009;29:25](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18564101))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Mutations in *TSC1* (on chromosome 9q34) or *TSC2* gene (on chromosome 16p13.3) ([J Clin Med 2023;12:956](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36769603))
* Methylation class (MC) subependymal giant cell astrocytoma, *TSC1* / *TSC2* altered
* One paper identified 2 subgroups of SEGA based on the methylation data analysis, driven by changes in the adaptive immune response and MAPK pathway ([Cell Mol Neurobiol 2022;42:2863](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34709498))

Sample pathology report

* Brain, left lateral ventricle region, craniotomy for resection:
  + Subependymal giant cell astrocytoma (SEGA), *TSC1* mutated, CNS WHO grade 1
  + College of American Pathologists (CAP) checklist
    - History of prior therapy for this neoplasm: not known
    - History of previous tumor or familial syndrome: not specified
    - Neuroimaging findings: large intraventricular mass near left foramen of Monro
    - Procedure: resection
    - Specimen size, gross description greatest dimension: 3 cm
    - Tumor site: ventricle near left foramen of Monro
    - Tumor: left
    - Tumor focality: unifocal
    - Histologic type: subependymal giant cell astrocytoma
    - Histologic grade: CNS WHO grade 1
    - Treatment effect (histological evidence of prior therapy): not identified

Differential diagnosis

* [Ganglioglioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorganglioncelltumor.html):
  + Atypical glial cells
  + Prominent dysplastic ganglion cells
  + Eosinophilic granular bodies
  + Focally positive for [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) and [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html)
  + Majority harbor *BRAF* V600E mutation
* [Pleomorphic xanthoastrocytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorpleomorphicxanthoastrocytoma.html):
  + Large multinucleated cells, spindle cells and xanthomatous (lipidized) cells
  + Eosinophilic granular bodies
  + Rich reticulin network
  + Typically harbor *BRAF* V600E mutation and *CDKN2A* / *CDKN2B* homozygous deletion
* [Gemistocytic astrocytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/CNStumorgliomasastrocytomasIDHmutant.html):
  + Infiltrative tumor cells with eosinophilic cytoplasm
  + Cells are more uniform and smaller in size
  + Negative for [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html), [NSE](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsNSE.html), [neurofilament](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsneurofilament.html), [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html) and [NeuN](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsneun.html)
* [Ependymoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorependymoma.html):
  + Usually less pleomorphic and composed of monomorphic cells forming pseudorosettes and true rosettes
  + Positive for [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) (perinuclear dot-like pattern)
  + Negative for [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html)
  + Specific molecular alterations based on the location
* [Astroblastoma, *MN1* altered](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorastroblastoma.html):
  + Astroblastic pseudorosettes
  + Tumor cells with well defined eosinophilic cytoplasmic processes
  + Vascular and stromal sclerosis
  + Variably positive for [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html)
  + Positive for [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) (diffuse, membranous, dot-like or ring)
  + Rearrangement of *MN1* gene on chromosome 22q12.1

A 10 year old White girl presented with an 8 month history of seizures. A computed tomography (CT) scan revealed a voluminous mass in her perilateral ventricle. What genetic disorder has a strong association with the tumor shown above?

1. Cowden
2. Neurofibromatosis type 2
3. Tuberous sclerosis
4. Von Hippel-Lindau

Board review style answer #1

**C**. Tuberous sclerosis (TSC). Subependymal giant cell astrocytomas (SEGAs) typically occur in the first 2 decades of life and have a strong association with inherited TSC. SEGA prevalence in TSC patients varies between 6% and 25% in studies.
Answer A is incorrect because Cowden syndrome is associated with dysplastic cerebellar gangliocytoma (Lhermitte-Duclos disease). Answer B is incorrect because neurofibromatosis type 2 is associated with schwannoma, meningioma and ependymoma. Answer D is incorrect because von Hippel-Lindau syndrome is associated with hemangioblastoma.

A 7 year old girl presented with an 8 month history of seizures, frequent angiofibroma and a medical history of rhabdomyoma during infancy. Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) of the brain demonstrated an enhancing interventricular lesion centered at the right foramen of Monro. What immunohistochemistry profile is helpful to diagnose the tumor shown above?

1. GFAP, NeuN, TTF1
2. GFAP, Olig2, IDH1 R132H
3. MelanA, HMB45
4. Pancytokeratin, CK7, TTF1, napsin A

Board review style answer #2

**A**. GFAP, NeuN, TTF1. Subependymal giant cell astrocytomas (SEGAs) are positive for neuronal and glial markers as well as TTF1.
Answer B is incorrect because GFAP, Olig2 and IDH1 R132H are positive in astrocytomas with gemistocytic features. Answer C is incorrect because MelanA and HMB45 are positive in melanomas. Answer D is incorrect because pancytokeratin, CK7, TTF1 and napsin A are positive in lung adenocarcinomas. All of these tumors share morphologic features that are partially similar to SEGA.
