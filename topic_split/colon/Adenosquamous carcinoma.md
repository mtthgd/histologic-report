Definition / general

* Rare (0.1% of colonic carcinomas) WHO recognized epithelial malignancy of colon with glandular and squamous elements ([Am J Surg Pathol 1978;2:47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/637188))

Essential features

* Rare primary subtype of colorectal carcinoma
* Has higher metastatic rate and worse prognosis than conventional colorectal adenocarcinoma ([Dis Colon Rectum 2012;55:509](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22513428), [Dis Colon Rectum 1999;42:258](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10211505))

Sites

* Typically arises in right colon ([Dis Colon Rectum 2001;44:341](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11289278))

Pathophysiology

* HPV infection does not appear to play a role ([Surg Today 2009;39:619](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19562453), [Eur J Surg Oncol 2002;28:657](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12359204))

Clinical features

* Tumor stage is often advanced at presentation ([Dis Colon Rectum 1996;39:1265](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8918436))
* May cause paraneoplastic hypercalcemia ([Int J Clin Oncol 2005;10:144](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15864702), [Am Surg 2001;67:585](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11409809))
* Can occur in patients with ulcerative colitis ([Dis Colon Rectum 1988;31:323](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3282843))

Case reports

* 71 year old man with adenosquamous carcinoma of sigmoid colon ([Int J Clin Exp Med 2013;6:390](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23724159))
* 78 year old woman with associated paraneoplastic hypercalcemia ([Int J Clin Oncol 2005;10:144](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15864702))
* Adenosquamous carcinoma of colon ([Am Surg 2006;72:754](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16913323))
* Adenosquamous carcinoma of colorectum ([J Exp Clin Cancer Res 2001;20:293](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11484990))

Treatment

* Surgery with adjuvant treatment

Positive stains

* [CK5/6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5and6.html), [CD44](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd44.html) ([Int J Clin Exp Med 2013;6:390](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23724159), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2000;124:212](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10656728))

Sample pathology report

* Ascending colon, biopsy:
  + Adenosquamous carcinoma (see comment)
  + Comment: The malignancy demonstrates both glandular elements and squamous elements. The former is positive for CDX2 by immunohistochemistry and the latter is positive for p40 by immunohistochemistry.

Differential diagnosis

* [Squamous cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorscc.html):
  + Also rare
  + No glandular elements

Which of the following is true about adenosquamous carcinoma of the colon?

1. Always arises in the setting of HPV infection
2. Has a good prognosis
3. Often presents at an advanced stage
4. Represents approximately 10% of colorectal carcinomas

Board review style answer #1

**C**. Often presents at an advanced stage
