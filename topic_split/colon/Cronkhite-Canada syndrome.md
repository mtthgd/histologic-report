Definition / general

* Rare, nonhereditary polyposis syndrome of unknown etiology

Essential features

* Disorder of middle adulthood, with mean age of diagnosis of 59 years
* Poor prognosis due to associated nutritional complications
* Up to 50% of patients have remissions
* Rarely has a fulminant course ([Endoscopy 2010;42:E350](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21170844))
* Associated carcinomas documented in 15 - 25% of cases ([J Clin Pathol 2014;67:891](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25004941))

Sites

* Polyps affect entire gastrointestinal tract, except the esophagus
* Also associated with ectodermal changes including alopecia, nail atrophy and cutaneous hyperpigmentation

Pathophysiology

* No known genetic link; considered nonhereditary
* May represent an autoimmune disorder ([Digestion 2007;75:96](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17510553))

Clinical features

* Patients present with variable symptoms, including diarrhea, weight loss, nausea, GI bleeding, protein losing enteropathy

Case reports

* 17 year old boy with late onset Cronkhite-Canada syndrome ([Eur J Gastroenterol Hepatol 2005;17:1139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16148564))
* 54 year old woman with Cronkhite-Canada syndrome associated with myelodysplastic syndrome ([World J Gastroenterol 2009;15:5871](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19998513))
* 59 year old man with Cronkhite-Canada syndrome complicated with huge intramucosal gastric cancer ([Gastric Cancer 2009;12:113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19562466))
* 63 year old woman with colorectal carcinoma in Cronkhite-Canada syndrome ([Z Gastroenterol 2001;39:365](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11413916))
* 70 year old woman with Cronkhite-Canada syndrome with adenomatous and carcinomatous transformation of colonic polyp ([Indian J Gastroenterol 2003;22:189](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14658536))
* 72 year old man with Cronkhite-Canada syndrome presenting with adenomatous and inflammatory colon polyps ([Nat Rev Gastroenterol Hepatol 2010;7:460](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20628344))
* Acute brain syndrome as a consequence of Cronkhite-Canada syndrome ([Psychiatr Danub 2005;17:90](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16395849))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Multiple ill defined polyps with cystically dilated glands and crypts associated with an edematous lamina propria containing mononuclear cells and eosinophils, microscopically indistinguishable from juvenile polyps ([Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:940](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2552848))
* Intervening nonpolypoid mucosa characteristically also has dilated glands, edema, congestion and inflammation ([Gastroenterol Res Pract 2009;2009:619378](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19727405))
