Definition / general

* Colitis related to diverticular disease (diverticulosis) ([Pathology 2002;34:568](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12555996), [Colorectal Dis 2002;4:208](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12780619), [Am J Gastroenterol 1992;87:609](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1595649), [Dig Dis 2012;30:29](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22572682))
* Also called segmental colitis associated with diverticula
* Affects sigmoid colon only with rectal sparing ([Am J Surg Pathol 1996;20:94](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8540614))
* Usually presents with rectal bleeding
* Good prognosis ([Endoscopy 2006;38:610](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16612745)); does not appear to evolve to inflammatory bowel disease ([Int J Colorectal Dis 2005;20:28](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15316721))

Pathophysiology

* Due to mucosal prolapse, fecal stasis and relative mucosal ischaemia; also subserosal peridiverticulitis and suppuration causing mass effect ([Pathology 2002;34:568](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12555996))

Treatment

* High fiber diet, antibiotics, aminosalicylates; surgery may be necessary ([Colorectal Dis 2001;3:149](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12790980))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Transmural inflammation with expansion of lamina propria, cryptitis and crypt abscesses, lymphoid aggregates and distorted crypt architecture

Differential diagnosis

* [Inflammatory bowel disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colonindeterminate.html) and [acute self-limited colitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colonacuteselflimited.html) have different clinical history
