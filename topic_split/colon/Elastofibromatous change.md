Definition / general

* Increase in elastin fibers in colonic submucosa

Essential features

* Uncommon collection of amorphous elastotic material in colon
* Seemingly no clinical significance but can mimic amyloid

Terminology

* Also called elastosis
* Called elastofibroma if forms a polyp

Sites

* Occurs throughout gastrointestinal tract
* In colon, favors left side; can present in rectum

Clinical features

* Patients typically older than 45
* No sex predilection
* May cause nonspecific gastrointestinal symptoms or be asymptomatic

Case reports

* 58 year old woman ([Am J Surg Pathol 1992;16:793](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1497120))
* Elastofibromatous polyp of sigmoid colon ([Can J Gastroenterol 2003;17:275](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12704473))

Gross description

* May appear as a polyp or as a linear white lesion

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Eosinophilic to gray amorphous material, sometimes with fibrous tissue, involving submocusa or muscularis mucosae
* May be centered around blood vessels ([Am J Clin Pathol 2004;122:232](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15323140))

Elastin stain

Positive stains

* **Elastin** stain highlights [elastic fibers](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainselastic.html)

Negative stains

* [Congo red](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscongored.html)

Sample pathology report

* Descending colon, polypectomy:
  + Colon with elastofibromatous change (see comment)
  + Comment: The polyp consists of submucosal amorphous material that is negative on a Congo red special stain and positive on an elastin special stain.

Differential diagnosis

* [Amyloidosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colonamyloidosis.html):
  + Shows birefringence with [Congo red](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscongored.html) stain
* [Lifting agent granuloma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colonliftagentgranuloma.html):
  + Can contain inflammatory component
  + History of polypectomy at site
