Definition / general

* Endometriosis arising within colon

Essential features

* Similar to endometriosis elsewhere: can cause clinical symptoms; histology shows endometrial glands, endometrial stroma and hemosiderin
* Malignant transformation is rare

Epidemiology

* Up to 33% with endometriosis have involvement of large or small intestine

Sites

* Usually rectosigmoid or rectovaginal septum ([Med Sci Monit 2000;6:787](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11208411))
* Can involve any portion of colonic wall, from mucosa to serosa

Clinical features

* Typically incidental but can cause pain, obstruction or diarrhea
* Rarely associated with neoplasms or precancerous changes ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:513](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10757398))

Case reports

* 31 year old patient with schistosomiasis ([Fertil Steril 2006;85:1060.e1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16580400))
* 35 year old woman with obstruction of sigmoid colon ([Ugeskr Laeger 2005;167:3604](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16219194))
* 42 year old woman with mixed germ cell tumor ([Am J Clin Pathol 1982;78:555](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6291378))
* 48 year old woman with endometrial stroma sarcoma ([J Korean Med Sci 2002;17:412](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12068150))
* 65 year old woman with clear cell carcinoma ([J Clin Pathol 2001;54:76](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11271795))
* With endometrioid adenocarcinoma ([Am Surg 2005;71:694](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16217955))

Treatment

* Pain medication, hormonal therapy; may require surgery if causing obstructive symptoms ([Clin Colon Rectal Surg 2010;23:72](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21629624))

Gross description

* Serosal and subserosal nodules < 5 cm; smooth muscle hypertrophy may cause obstruction
* Gray cut surface with minute areas of hemorrhage; can cause adhesions if subserosal

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Endometrial glands and stroma plus hemosiderin in deeper layers of colon; usually surrounded by smooth muscle
* Epithelium may have inflammation and ulcers, simulating inflammatory bowel disease or solitary rectal ulcer syndrome
* Often infiltrates along nerves of bowel wall ([Hum Reprod 2004;19:996](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15016784))
* Mucosa usually normal; bowel wall may be fibrotic; cilia help distinguish endometrial glands from colonic glands

Sample pathology report

* Ascending colon, resection:
  + Colon with multiple mural and serosal foci of endometriosis
  + Negative for malignancy.
  + Margins of resection unremarkable.
  + Two benign lymph nodes.

Which of the following is true about colonic endometriosis?

1. It is most common in the ascending colon
2. It is typically incidental
3. It never contains hemosiderin histologically
4. It often contains endometrioid adenocarcinoma

Board review style answer #1

**B**. It is typically incidental
