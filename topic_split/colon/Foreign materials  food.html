Definition / general
  • Partially digested plant remnants are frequently found in biopsy samples, polypectomy specimens and in various types of surgical specimens including appendicectomies, diverticular disease associated resections and cancers; these foods can mimic parasites or granuloma forming diseases
Essential features
  • Foods can be aspirated or found in the gastrointestinal (GI) tract and can mimic bugs or drugs
  • It is essential to be aware of food histology in order to avoid misinterpreting a mimicker
  • Foreign material found in biopsies, surgical specimens and during autopsies
Terminology
  • Vegetable matter retained by the GI tract, which often remains undigested due to resistance from a tough external cuticle
Sites
  • Oral cavity
  • GI tract
  • Lungs
  • Skin
  • Can be seen during routine screening, within surgical specimens or found during autopsy
Pathophysiology
  • Granulomatous reaction pattern often forms in response to foreign material / foods found in the GI tract
    • This is a foreign body granuloma and it occurs when the host immune system is unable to digest the foreign body, resulting in the accumulation of macrophages and histiocytes
    • As a result, macrophages surround and isolate the foreign body, some of which will fuse to form multinucleated giant cells
    • T cells and fibroblasts also participate in this inflammatory response
Diagnosis
  • Foreign material is often an incidental finding that can mimic pathological disease; therefore, histology of foreign materials / foods is essential
  • Often deeper levels are needed to look for foreign material and apply special stains to look for microorganisms
  • Pathologist needs to be certain that the pathogenesis is a true foreign body / food and not a mimic
Case reports
  • 31 year old woman with bowel endometriosis and schistosomiasis (Fertil Steril 2006;85:1060.e1)
  • 56 year old woman with gastric foreign body granuloma caused by an embedded fishbone (World J Gastroenterol 2014;20:3388)
  • 70 year old man presented with perforated duodenal ulcer, mimicking a peritoneal carcinomatosis (Int J Surg Pathol 2018;26:561)
  • 78 year old woman with excision of rectal mass (Arch Pathol Lab Med 2001;125:822)
Microscopic (histologic) description
  • Some foods have a particular histology and their recognition and correct attribution can be very intriguing
  • Seeds
    • Recognized by the polygonal shape of cells, by the thick cell wall and the presence of starch granules
    • Sometimes they can be surrounded by a granulomatous reaction with giant cells, especially in the case of complicated diverticulitis or acute appendicitis but also in the case of ulcerated and perforated neoplasms
  • Color (some fruits and vegetables have a distinct color)
    • Carotenoids are red, orange or yellow, such as peppers, carrots, tomatoes, bananas, strawberries
    • Anthocyanins are blue or black, such as blackberries, blueberries, grapes, figs, dates, prunes

Sample pathology report
  • Colon, descending, biopsy:
    • Foreign body reaction with vegetable matter (see comment)
    • Comment: There is no evidence of malignancy in multiple levels examined.
Differential diagnosis
  • Amyloidosis:
    • Congo red positive
  • Infections:
    • Exclude with stains for microorganisms (e.g., Fite, GMS, PAS, Gram stain)
    • Parasites:
      • There is usually an abundance of inflammatory cells such as eosinophils, neutrophils or plasma cells
  • Pharmaceutical fillers:
    • Fillers are insoluble, nonabsorbable powders added to pharmaceuticals to help with the stabilization of the active pharmaceutical ingredient
      • Fillers are widely used in many medications, as well as vitamins and processed food products
    • The most frequently used are crospovidone and microcrystalline cellulose, which have been identified in 0.3 - 9.0% of gastrointestinal surgical specimens (Histopathology 2017;71:331, Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:564)
  • Resins:
    • Resins are nonabsorbable drugs, used for ion exchange in the gastrointestinal tract in various chronic conditions
    • Types of resins identifiable in the GI tract are
      • Sodium polystyrene sulfonate (kayexalate)
      • Sevelamer
      • Bile acid sequestrants (including cholestyramine, colesevelam and colestipol)
  • Iron pill gastritis / pill gastritis:
    • History of oral iron supplement use for iron deficiency anemia
    • Mucosal injury with crystalline brown pigmented material deposition
    • Iron is refractile but does not polarize
  • Lifting agent granuloma:
    • > 50% of lesions have pink hyaline rings / ribbons
    • History of polypectomy at site
  • Crohn's disease:
    • Granulomas and inflammation may mimic Crohn's but there should not be any refractile or foreign material
  • Other granuloma forming disease:
    • Limited biopsy may raise this differential diagnosis, refractile
Additional references
  • Virchows Arch 2020;477:593, Sci Data 2020;7:371, Virchows Arch 2022;480:499
Board review style question #1
Which of the following histological descriptions best fits vegetable matter (i.e., peanut)?

  1. 2 toned color, a rusty yellow background and bright pink linear accentuation with fish scale pattern
  2. Deeply basophilic coral-like or sponge shaped particle
  3. Polygonal in shape with a smooth and glassy texture, variable eosinophilic staining and lacks fish scale pattern
  4. Polygonal shaped cells with a thick cell wall and the presence of eosinophilic granules
  5. Purple color with a typical fish scale pattern
Board review style answer #1
D. Polygonal shaped cells with a thick cell wall and the presence of eosinophilic granules. Most vegetable matter has polygonal shaped cells with a thick cell wall. Within the parenchymal cells, there are lots of eosinophilic granules. Answer A is incorrect because sevelamer is a 2 toned color, a rusty yellow background and bright pink linear accentuation with fish scale pattern. Answer B is incorrect because crospovidone is a deeply basophilic coral-like or sponge shaped particle. Answer C is incorrect because bile acid sequestrants are polygonal in shape with a smooth and glassy texture, variable eosinophilic staining and lacks fish scale pattern. Answer E is incorrect because kayexalate is a purple color with a typical fish scale pattern.

Board review style question #2


A 38 year old man presents with a small and painful subcutaneous nodule on the left fourth digit. The lesion is biopsied and shown above. What is the best diagnosis?

  1. Epidermal inclusion cyst
  2. Foreign body reaction
  3. Osteoma cutis
  4. Pilomatrixoma
Board review style answer #2
B. Foreign body reaction. Fragment of plant material, composed of regularly sized cells on cross section, is seen in the dermis. There is an abscess with abundant bacterial colonies and inflamed granulation tissue in the adjacent dermis. This is a wood splinter and is usually associated with a foreign body giant cell reaction. Answer A is incorrect because a giant cell reaction surrounding shards of keratin should be seen with an inclusion cyst. Answer C is incorrect because bone is not seen. Answer D is incorrect because pilomatrixomas have islands of basaloid cells and ghost or shadow cells.

Board review style question #3


A 56 year old woman presents with progressively painful and enlarging bumps on her lips. A lesion is biopsied and shown above. What is the best diagnosis?

  1. Cutaneous mucinosis
  2. Granuloma annulare
  3. Foreign body reaction to hyaluronic fillers
  4. Metastatic mucinous adenocarcinoma
Board review style answer #3
C. Foreign body reaction to hyaluronic fillers hyaluronic fillers. Hyaluronic fillers are often seen in the lips as subcutaneous basophilic deposits of variable size and shape that are surrounded by histiocytes and multinucleated giant cells. The basophilic material is hyaluronic acid. Answer A is incorrect because cutaneous mucinosis or focal dermal mucinosis is a collection of mucin between dermal collagen bundles. Answer B is incorrect because granuloma annulare should reveal palisading histiocytes around a focus of necrobiosis and increased mucin deposition. Answer D is incorrect because metastatic mucinous adenocarcinoma should have atypical glands or nests of epithelial cells that appear to be floating in copious mucin.
