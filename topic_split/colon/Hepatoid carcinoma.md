Definition / general

* Rare, aggressive subtype of adenocarcinoma that morphologically resembles hepatocellular carcinoma and produces alpha fetoprotein

Essential features

* Extremely rare subtype of colorectal carcinoma
* Resembles hepatocellular carcinoma morphologically and sometimes immunohistochemically
* Serum alpha fetoprotein may be elevated

Terminology

* Originally termed alpha fetoprotein producing tumor

Epidemiology

* More common in men
* Fewer than 20 colonic cases reported

Sites

* Hepatoid carcinoma has also been reported in other sites, including [stomach](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stomachhepatoid.html), gallbladder and uterus ([World J Gastroenterol 2013;19:321](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23372352))
* Also ampulla, bladder, lung, [ovary](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorhepatoid.html)

Clinical features

* Can occur in patients with ulcerative colitis ([World J Gastroenterol 2014;20:12657](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25253971))
* Serum alpha fetoprotein may be elevated ([Hepatobiliary Pancreat Dis Int 2008;7:539](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18842504))

Case reports

* 42 year old man with rectal hepatoid carcinoma with liver metastases ([Hepatobiliary Pancreat Dis Int 2008;7:539](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18842504))
* 59 year old man with colonic α fetoprotein producing carcinoma ([Dis Colon Rectum 2003;46:826](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12794586))
* 71 year old man with hepatoid carcinoma colliding with liposarcoma ([World J Surg Oncol 2007;5:42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17448253))
* Rectal tumor arising in ulcerative colitis ([Dis Colon Rectum 2000;43:105](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10813132))
* Sigmoid alpha fetoprotein producing adenocarcinoma ([Pathology 1995;27:277](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8532396))

Treatment

* Surgery, possibly chemotherapy ([Pathol Oncol Res 2012;18:93](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21667305))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Resembles hepatocellular carcinoma, with trabecular or solid pattern of large polygonal cells with abundant eosinophilic or clear cytoplasm
* Component of traditional adenocarcinoma also often present
* Lymphovascular invasion is common ([Histopathology 1997;31:47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9253624))

Positive stains

* [Alpha fetoprotein](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsafp.html), [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html), [glypican 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglypican3.html)
* Variable [Hep Par1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsheppar1.html), [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html), [CK18](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck18.html), [CK19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck19.html) ([Histopathology 2007;51:123](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17532770))

Sample pathology report

* Sigmoid colon, resection:
  + Hepatoid carcinoma of the colon, moderately differentiated (see synoptic report)

Differential diagnosis

* [Metastatic hepatocellular carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorHCC.html):
  + Unusual in colon; negative for [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html) and (usually) [CK19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck19.html) ([J Gastroenterol Hepatol 2000;15:536](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10847441))
