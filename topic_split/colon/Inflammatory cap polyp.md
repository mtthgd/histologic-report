Definition / general

* First described in an abstract in 1985 (Br J Surg 1985; 72 (Suppl) S133)
* Rare
* Etiology unknown, but may be associated with mucosal prolapse ([Gut 1993;34:562](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8491408))
* Often presents with mucoid or bloody diarrhea and hypoproteinemia
* **Endoscopy**: multiple sessile rectosigmoid polyps with adherent white flecks with normal intervening mucosa ([Endoscopy 2001;33:262](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11293761))

Treatment

* Traditionally polypectomy or partial colectomy ([Dis Colon Rectum 2004;47:1208](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15164251))
* Also medical treatment in patients with *H. pylori* gastritis ([Helicobacter 2004;9:651](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15610079)) and using infliximab, an antibody to tumor necrosis alpha ([Gastroenterology 2004;126:1868](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15188181))

Gross description

* Multiple, distinctive, inflammatory rectosigmoid polyps

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Inflamed mucosa with tortuous, elongated crypts attenuated towards the mucosal surface with abundant inflammation in the lamina propria and characteristic "cap" of inflamed and ulcerated granulation tissue on the mucosal surface
