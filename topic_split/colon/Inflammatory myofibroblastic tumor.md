Definition / general

* Low grade mesenchymal neoplasm that can arise in the colonic wall or mesentery, usually of children
* See also topic in [Soft tissue](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueinflammyofibro.html) chapter

Essential features

* Mesenchymal neoplasm, often pediatric, with spindled cells admixed with inflammation
* t(2;5) (*TPM3-ALK*) translocation
* Rare aggressive variant called epithelioid inflammatory fibroblastic sarcoma

Terminology

* Older terms include called inflammatory pseudotumor, inflammatory fibrosarcoma and plasma cell granuloma
* Epithelioid inflammatory fibroblastic sarcoma refers to a rare, aggressive variant with unique morphologic and molecular features ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:135](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21164297))

Sites

* Can arise in the colonic wall or the mesentery
* Most common site in gastrointestinal tract is stomach

Clinical features

* Patients usually in the pediatric age range ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:509](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17414097))
* Symptoms include anemia, abdominal pain, fever, weight loss and high sedimentation rate ([J Pediatr Surg 2001;36:169](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11150459))
* May recur but metastases rare; these outcomes may be more common in epithelioid inflammatory fibroblastic sarcoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Bland spindle cells with abundant amphophilic cytoplasm and variably prominent nucleoli
* Lymphoplasmacytic infiltrate with polyclonal plasma cells
* Background may show myxoid change or laminated / whorled fibrosis
* Epithelioid inflammatory myofibroblastic sarcoma: similar, except cells are more epithelioid, with large nucleoli

Inflammatory cells and spindle cells

Positive stains

* [Desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html); variable cytoplasmic [ALK1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html) ([Mod Pathol 2002;15:931](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12218210))
* Epithelioid inflammatory myofibroblastic sarcoma: nuclear membrane ALK1

Negative stains

* [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Abnormalities of 2p23, usually t(2;5) (*TPM3-ALK*) translocation, in approximately half of cases; more common in children ([Mod Pathol 2001;14:569](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11406658))
* Epithelioid inflammatory myofibroblastic sarcoma: t(2;2) *RANBP2-ALK* translocation

Sample pathology report

* Ascending colon, resection:
  + Inflammatory myofibroblastic tumor (3.1 cm) (see comment)
  + Margins of resection unremarkable.
  + Two benign lymph nodes.
  + Comment: An immunostain for ALK1 is positive in the tumor.

Differential diagnosis

* [Inflammatory fibroid polyp](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorinflammatoryfibroid.html):
  + Patients older, tumors smaller with more eosinophils and [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) positivity ([Hum Pathol 2002;33:307](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11979371))
* [Fibromatosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuefibromatosisdeep.html):
  + Nuclear positivity for [beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html)
* [Schistosomiasis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colonschistosomiasis.html):
  + May rarely form inflammatory pseuotumorous reaction ([J Natl Med Assoc 2006;98:1365](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16916140))

Board review style question #1

[![Inflammatory myofibroblastic tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/colontumorimtFeely03B.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/colontumorimtFeely03B.jpg)

A young male patient presents with abdominal pain. Imaging reveals a large mesenteric mass, which is resected. An image of the tumor’s histology is seen above. Immunohistochemistry shows ALK1 staining of the nuclear membranes. What molecular abnormality is present in this tumor?

1. *ALK1* deletion
2. *ALK1* inversion
3. t(2;2) *RANBP2-ALK* translocation
4. t(2;5) *TPM3-ALK* translocation

Board review style answer #1

**C**. t(2;2) *RANBP2-ALK* translocation. This is an epithelioid inflammatory myofibroblastic sarcoma.
