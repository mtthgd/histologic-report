Definition / general

* Lymphoglandular complex-like colorectal carcinoma (LGCC) is a very rare variant of colorectal carcinoma with a tumor entirely encased by lymphoid stroma, resembling a lymphoglandular complex

Essential features

* Invasive tumor confined entirely by lymphoid stroma, typically appearing sessile or dome-like on endoscopy
* Histologically, the invasive component lacks lamina propria and features of prolapse, with an irregular distribution of glands
* Majority are associated with adenomas or sessile serrated lesions
* A subset exhibits deficient mismatch repair proteins
* While historically considered innocuous, a significant subset can metastasize to locoregional lymph nodes and extend beyond the colorectal wall

Terminology

* Dome carcinoma
* Gut associated lymphoid tissue (GALT) carcinoma

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [C18.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C15-C26/C18-/C18.9) - malignant neoplasm of colon, unspecified

Epidemiology

* Mean age of 64 years (range: 41 - 88), with a slight female predilection (60%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2024;48:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38054635))
* Possible association with ulcerative colitis ([World J Gastrointest Oncol 2019;11:59](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30984351))
* Despite the presence of lymphoid tissue, there is no clear association with deficient mismatch repair, Lynch syndrome or Epstein-Barr virus (EBV) infection

Sites

* Colon and rectum

Pathophysiology

* Usually associated with a surface precursor polyp, suggesting a comparable neoplastic progression to that seen in conventional colorectal carcinoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2024;48:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38054635))
  + Majority are typical adenomas with or without high grade dysplasia (83%)
  + Remainder are sessile serrated lesions with dysplasia
* Exact pathophysiology is unclear but may involve a robust immune response to the tumor or invasion of carcinoma into a native lymphoid follicle
* Another theory proposes that this variant could represent malignant M cells, specialized intestinal cells that present antigens to the immune system and interact with underlying lymphoid aggregates ([World J Gastrointest Oncol 2019;11:59](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30984351))

Etiology

* No definitive cause or etiology has been established

Diagnosis

* Entirely predicated on histology

Prognostic factors

* Generally favorable prognosis, as a majority of cases do not invade beyond the submucosa and are pT1
* However, a subset of cases do have locoregional lymph node metastasis and invade beyond the submucosa ([Am J Surg Pathol 2024;48:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38054635))
* No disease specific deaths have been reported

Case reports

* 49 year old man presented with hematochezia ([Int J Surg Pathol 2023;31:1132](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36803029))
* 54 year old man presented with rectal lesion ([Pathol Res Pract 2024;263:155621](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/39362134))
* 57 year old woman presented with cecal mass ([Diagn Pathol 2015;10:92](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26156870))
* 77 year old man presented with elevated transverse mass ([BMC Gastroenterol 2012;12:21](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22400807))

Treatment

* Polypectomy, endoscopic mucosal resection or surgical resection

Gross description

* Endoscopically, majority are described as sessile or dome-like, likely due to the prominent protuberance generated from the underlying lymphoid tissue
* However, may form a polyp or have a plaque-like appearance ([Anticancer Res 2016;36:5385](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27798903))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Irregular intestinal type glands confined to lymphoid stroma with germinal centers are frequently seen
* Glands often show angulation, fusion or nesting
* Single cell infiltration and desmoplasia are not always present
* Tumors are typically low grade and goblet cells are infrequent in invasive component
* No lamina propria in invasive component
* Lymphovascular invasion and perineural invasion rare
* Surface precursor 90% of the time, either adenoma with or without high grade dysplasia or sessile serrated lesion with dysplasia
* While the majority are confined to submucosa, a subset can extend into muscularis propria or beyond into pericolorectal tissue
* Lymph node metastasis in a subset of cases
* Reference: [Am J Surg Pathol 2024;48:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38054635)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Certain subset is associated with sporadic deficient mismatch repair ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1999;123:720](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10420231))
  + Given the low number of cases, it is challenging to determine whether the rate is comparable to conventional colorectal carcinoma or any relation to Lynch syndrome
* *EGFR* missense and *TP53* nonsense mutations in 1 reported case ([Pathol Res Pract 2024;263:155621](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/39362134))

Sample pathology report

* Descending colon, polyp, polypectomy:
  + Invasive adenocarcinoma extending into the submucosa, with lymphoglandular features, entirely excised (see comment and synoptic report)
  + Comment: The invasive tumor demonstrates a unique morphology, namely that the malignancy is entirely encased by lymphoid tissue. These have been termed lymphoglandular complex-like carcinoma. While the overall prognostic features of this morphology are not entirely clear, the largest study on this topic demonstrated that a subset of cases can be associated with locoregional lymph node metastasis. As such, it is recommended that these cases, for the time being, be treated and followed similarly to conventional adenocarcinoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2024;48:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38054635)).

Differential diagnosis

* [Adenoma / polyp](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumoradenoma.html) involving a lymphoglandular complex:
  + Glands regularly distributed with lamina propria
  + Leveling often shows continuity with surface polyp
  + Lack of gland angulation / fusion / nest formation and no single cell infiltration or desmoplasia ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:1083](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29738362))
* [Adenomatous polyp with pseudoinvasion](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorcarcinomaarising.html):
  + Misplaced epithelium still arranged in nests, usually without high grade nuclear features or invasive type arrangement
  + Background tissue is lamina propria, not lymphoid aggregates with germinal centers
  + Mucin lakes and hemosiderin often present ([Mod Pathol 2015;28:S88](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25560603))

A 55 year old man presents for a screening colonoscopy. A small dome-like lesion is removed from the ascending colon. Pathologic review demonstrates the findings in the image above. What is the diagnosis?

1. Adenoma with pseudoinvasion
2. Lymphoglandular complex-like carcinoma
3. Medullary carcinoma
4. Metastatic adenocarcinoma

Board review style answer #1

**B**. Lymphoglandular complex-like carcinoma. This very rare subtype of colorectal carcinoma is encased by prominent lymphoid stroma with germinal centers. Answer A is incorrect because pseudoinvasive adenomas would not demonstrate invasion beyond muscularis mucosae into the colon wall. Answer C is incorrect because medullary carcinoma forms solid sheets of tumor cells and the inflammatory infiltrate is composed of scattered tumor infiltrating lymphocytes and neutrophils. Answer D is incorrect because an adenomatous precursor lesion is visible and the glands have an intestinal type appearance.
