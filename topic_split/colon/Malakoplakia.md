Definition / general

* Marked histiocytic infiltrate, more common in [bladder](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladdermalakoplakia.html), that may diffusely involve colon
* Usually adults, associated with immunosuppression
* Due to defective inflammatory response to gram negative bacterial infection
* Rarely associated with colorectal carcinoma in elderly men ([Pathology 2002;34:332](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12190290))

Case reports

* 2 cases of pediatric malakoplakia of colon ([Pediatr Surg Int 2010;26:323](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20101508))
* 4 year old boy with coexisting celiac disease and chronic granulomatous disease ([Indian J Gastroenterol 2006;25:163](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16877840))
* 54 year old man with liver transplant ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2004;128:e133](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15387686))
* 58 year old woman with liver transplant ([Can J Gastroenterol 2007;21:753](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18026580))
* 64 year old man with colonic adenocarcinoma ([World J Gastroenterol 2007;13:6109](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18023111))
* 74 year old man with malakoplakia adjacent to adenocarcinoma ([J Clin Pathol 1993;46:959](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8227417))

Gross description

* Diffuse mucosal involvement and submucosal thickening; may cause mass

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Marked histiocytic infiltrate containing Michaelis-Gutmann bodies (calcospherites) that may resemble signet ring cells at frozen section
