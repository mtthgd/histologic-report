Definition / general
  • Mantle cell lymphoma (MCL) is a rare, aggressive non-Hodgkin lymphoma composed of small to medium sized, mature B cells that overexpress the cyclin D1 gene
  • Typically occurs in lymph nodes and commonly involves extranodal sites, particularly the gastrointestinal (GI) tract, where it can present as a single polyp, multiple polyps, submucosal masses, ulcerative lesions or diffuse mucosal infiltration
Essential features
  • Translocation, most commonly t(11;14)(q13;q32), results in the BCL1 locus juxtaposed with the Ig heavy chain gene (IGH) sequence, leading to upregulation of the CCND1 proto-oncogene and overexpression of cyclin D1
  • Typical IHC pattern: CD20+, CD5+, cyclin D1+, SOX11+, FMC7+, CD43+, CD23-, CD10-, BCL6-, LEF1-, CD200-
  • Inferior survival is associated with a Ki67 proliferation index ≥ 30%, blastoid variant or somatic mutations involving TP53, KMT2D or MYC
  • Improved survival is associated with somatic mutation involving IGHV
  • 18fluorodeoxyglucose (FDG) positron emission tomography / computed tomography (PET / CT) has a high sensitivity for nodal or splenic involvement but low sensitivity for GI or bone marrow involvement
Terminology
  • 3 subtypes of MCL
    • MCL (classic): involvement of nodes or extranodal sites
    • Leukemic nonnodal MCL: typical asymptomatic clinical presentation with involvement of blood, bone marrow or spleen and without significant nodal involvement; typically more indolent, lacks SOX11 expression and has a lower Ki67 proliferation index
    • In situ mantle cell neoplasm: rare / typically incidental finding of cyclin D1 positive (CCND1 rearranged) B cells colonizing nonexpanded mantle zones of lymphoid follicles, staging negative for overt MCL
  • Multiple lymphomatous polyposis (also malignant lymphomatous polyposis) is typically associated with MCL; however, less commonly may be associated with other lymphoid neoplasms
  • B prolymphocytic leukemia (entity no longer recognized in WHO HAEM5), when CCND1 rearrangement is present, is now classified as MCL (blastoid variant) (Leukemia 2022;36:1720)
  • Mantle zone lymphoma (obsolete)
ICD coding
  • ICD-O: 9673/3 - mantle cell lymphoma
  • ICD-10: C83.1 - mantle cell lymphoma
  • ICD-11: 2A85.5 & XH1VV1 - mantle cell lymphoma & mantle cell lymphoma
Epidemiology
  • MCL (Blood Cancer J 2021;11:8, Oncotarget 2017;8:112516)
    • 3 - 10% of all non-Hodgkin lymphomas
    • Age adjusted MCL incidence: 1.01 per 100,000 persons per year
    • Median age at diagnosis: 69 years
    • 68.75% male
    • 80.53% non-Hispanic white
    • 75.47% had advanced stage tumor at diagnosis
  • Primary GI MCL (Blood Cancer J 2021;11:8)
    • Median age at diagnosis: 62 years (range 43 - 79)
    • 86.4% male
  • Secondary GI MCL
    • 83.5% male (Blood Cancer J 2021;11:8)
Sites
  • Primary GI MCL (no nodal, extranodal or peripheral blood involvement at diagnosis) accounts for 2.8% of MCL cases (Blood Cancer J 2021;11:8)
  • Secondary GI MCL accounts for 10 - 30% of MCL cases (Leukemia 1998;12:1281, Am J Hematol 1998;59:295, Blood 2000;95:2253, Blood Cancer J 2021;11:8)
  • Site involvement in untreated patients examined with routine upper and lower endoscopy at diagnosis of MCL (regardless of presence of GI symptoms): 92% bone marrow, 88% lower GI tract, 45% peripheral blood, 43% upper GI tract (Cancer 2003;97:586)
Pathophysiology
  • Translocation, most commonly t(11;14)(q13;q32), results in the BCL1 locus juxtaposed with the Ig heavy chain gene (IGH) sequence, leading to upregulation of the CCND1 proto-oncogene and overexpression of cyclin D1
    • Cyclin D1 activates cyclin dependent kinase 4 and 6, which phosphorylate and inactivate tumor suppressor gene Rb, promoting G1 to S phase transition (Am J Pathol 1999;154:1449, Am J Hematol 2022;97:638)
  • Rarely, translocations may instead involve other genes, such as CCND2, CCND3 and Ig light chains, with overexpression of cyclin D2 or D3 and without cyclin D1 overexpression (Blood 2013;121:1394)
  • Regardless of the initial translocation, SOX11 overexpression is nearly always present (absent in nonnodal MCL) and is thought to have an early role in MCL pathogenesis, possibly by driving tumor angiogenesis and invasion (Blood 2014;124:2235)
  • Although CCND1 rearrangement is present in nearly all cases, progression to MCL and its aggressive behavior relies on a variety of secondary genetic alterations targeting genes in cell cycle regulation, DNA damage response and cell proliferation and apoptosis; MYC translocation and TP53 mutation are of particular importance (Histol Histopathol 2021;36:1037, Blood 1996;87:3351, Haematologica 2021;106:1381)
Etiology
  • Unknown etiology; however, associated with several risk factors
  • History of Borrelia burgdorferi infection (self reported or seropositivity) is associated with a 2 to 3 fold increased risk of MCL but not other types of non-Hodgkin lymphoma (Blood 2008;111:5524)
  • Family history of hematopoietic malignancy is associated with a 2 fold increased risk of MCL, particularly in men (Blood 2007;109:3479, J Natl Cancer Inst Monogr 2014;2014:76)
  • History of ever having lived on a farm is associated with a modestly increased risk of MCL (J Natl Cancer Inst Monogr 2014;2014:76)

Clinical features
  • May be asymptomatic or present with B symptoms (drenching night sweats, unintentional weight loss, fever), splenomegaly or disease site associated symptoms (Am J Hematol 2022;97:638)
    • Gastrointestinal involvement: abdominal pain, distension, diarrhea, hematochezia, melena
    • Nodal involvement: swollen lymph nodes
    • Other extranodal site involvement: kidneys, soft tissues, skin, central nervous system, liver, Waldeyer ring
Diagnosis
  • Staging (Lugano classification) (J Clin Oncol 2014;32:3059)
    • Limited
      • I: 1 node or group of adjacent nodes
      • IE: single extranodal lesion without nodal involvement
      • II: 2 or more nodal groups on the same side of diaphragm; bulky designation may classify the disease as limited or advanced based on histology and prognostic factors
      • IIE: nodal groups on the same side of diaphragm plus limited contiguous extranodal involvement
    • Advanced
      • III: nodes on both sides of diaphragm or nodes above the diaphragm with spleen involvement
      • IV: additional noncontiguous extralymphatic involvement
  • Imaging / procedures (Am J Hematol 2022;97:638)
    • Fluorine 18FDG PET / CT (Clin Lymphoma Myeloma Leuk 2020;20:422)
    • Tissue biopsy and bone marrow biopsy/aspirate for flow cytometry, immunohistochemistry and molecular / cytogenetics studies
    • Upper GI endoscopy and colonoscopy with segmental biopsies should be done even if asymptomatic (Eur J Nucl Med Mol Imaging 2010;37:1633)
Laboratory
  • Possible lymphocytosis
  • Various findings depending on extranodal site involvement
Radiology description
  • 18FDG PET / CT is useful for staging, restaging and treatment response evaluation, particularly in nodal disease where it is 18FDG avid; however, MCL in bone marrow and GI disease sites has low 18FDG avidity (J Clin Oncol 2014;32:3059, Clin Lymphoma Myeloma Leuk 2020;20:422, Haematologica 2020;105:e33)
    • Splenic involvement: average sensitivity 100%
    • Nodal involvement: average sensitivity 97.5% and average specificity 100%
    • GI involvement: average sensitivity 39% and average specificity 98%
    • BM involvement: average sensitivity 36% and average specificity 98%

Prognostic factors
  • MCL International Prognostic Index (MIPI) was originally used to separate patients into 3 groups based on independent prognostic factors (Blood 2008;111:558)
    • Age
    • Eastern Cooperative Oncology Group (ECOG) performance status
    • Serum lactate dehydrogenase (LDH)
    • Peripheral leukocyte count
  • Ki67 proliferation index ≥ 30% is associated with substantially worse 5 year overall survival (41%) compared to those with Ki67 proliferation index < 10% (73%) or 10 - 30% (75%); MIPIc combines the dichotomized Ki67 proliferation index and the MIPI to separate patients into 4 groups with median overall survival of 9.4, 4.9, 3.2 and 1.8 years (J Clin Oncol 2016;34:1386)
  • p53 expression in > 50% of cells (16% of cases) is associated with inferior, median failure free survival (1.5 versus 4.6 years) and overall survival (2.8 versus 10.7 years) (Leukemia 2023;37:1887)
  • MIPI genetic (MIPIg) adds TP53 disruptions and KMT2D mutations to help identify a phenotypically distinct and highly aggressive form of MCL with a dismal response to high dose regimens so they can be included in clinical trials testing novel targeted agents (Haematologica 2020;105:1604)
    • TP53 mutation is associated with inferior median overall survival compared to wild type (2.0 versus 12.7 years) (Haematologica 2020;105:1604)
    • KMT2D mutation is associated with inferior median overall survival (8.4 versus 12.7 years) (Haematologica 2020;105:1604)
  • Blastoid MCL is associated with inferior 5 year overall survival (35%) compared to nonblastoid MCL (68%); however, the prognostic effect of blastoid cytology was largely explained by Ki67 proliferation index (generally higher in blastoid MCL) (J Clin Oncol 2016;34:1386)
  • MYC expression in > 20% of cells (14% of cases) is associated with inferior median overall survival (1.8 versus 7.3 years) and when concomitant with TP53 / p53 alterations (5% of cases) is further reduced to an average overall survival of 0.9 years (Haematologica 2024;109:1171)
  • IGHV mutated gene is associated with improved 5 year overall survival compared to unmutated (84% versus 50%) (Cancer Res 2012;72:5307)
Case reports
  • 51 year old woman with perforated colon and peritonitis (J Surg Case Rep 2021;2021:rjab152)
  • 56 year old man with gastrointestinal obstruction (J Med Case Rep 2024;18:219)
  • 61 year old man with intussuscepting colocolonic mass (Int J Surg Case Rep 2020;69:28)
  • 69 year old man with multiple polyp eminences of sigmoid colon several months after surgery and chemotherapy for sigmoid adenocarcinoma (Case Rep Gastroenterol 2019;13:17)
  • 74 year old man with rapidly progressive glomerulonephritis and IgA nephropathy (Intern Med 2021;60:1243)
Treatment
  • Watch and wait for asymptomatic patients with indolent MCL (e.g., SOX11-) (Curr Oncol Rep 2018;20:79)
  • Involved site radiation therapy can be used for stage I or stage II MCL (Curr Oncol Rep 2018;20:79)
  • Induction / maintenance therapy (Curr Oncol Rep 2018;20:79)
    • Young, fit
      • Alternating R-DHAP (rituximab, dexamethasone, cytarabine, cisplatin) and R-CHOP (rituximab, cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin, vincristine, prednisone), followed by autologous stem cell transplantation (ASCT) or
      • Alternating maxi CHOP (higher dose R-CHOP) and HDAraC (high dose cytarabine) followed by ASCT
    • Elderly, fit
      • Bendamustine and rituximab or
      • R-CHOP with rituximab maintenance therapy or
      • VRCAP (bortezomib, rituximab, cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin, prednisone) or
      • Clinical trial
    • Elderly, unfit
      • Rituximab and chlorambucil or
      • Rituximab and bendamustine (dose reduced) or
      • RCVP (rituximab, cyclophosphamide, vincristine, prednisone) or
      • Best supportive care
  • Lenalidomide is an immunomodulatory compound with pleiotropic antitumor effects (N Engl J Med 2015;373:1835)
    • Stimulates T cell and NK cell expansion
    • Inhibits tumor associated angiogenesis
    • Downregulates cyclin D1, inducing apoptosis of lymphoma cells
    • Augments antibody dependent cell mediated cytotoxicity when combined rituximab
  • Covalent Bruton tyrosine kinase (BTK) inhibitors
    • Ibrutinib (Lancet 2024;403:2293)
    • Acalabrutinib or zanubrutinib have a favorable toxicity profile and similar efficacy for other B cell malignancies and are used as a second line therapy for relapsed or refractory MCL (J Clin Oncol 2021;39:3441, Blood Adv 2021;5:2577)
  • Pirtobrutinib is a highly selective noncovalent BTK inhibitor of wild type and C481S mutant BTK for patients with intolerance or disease refractory to covalent BTK inhibitors (Lancet Haematol 2024;11:e682)
  • CD19 directed CAR T cell therapy (brexucabtagene autoleucel or lisocabtagene maraleucel) (Mol Cancer 2023;22:67)

Microscopic (histologic) description
  • Diagnostic criteria (WHO HAEM5)
    • Cyclin D1 positive MCL
      • Essential
        • B cell lymphoma cells (CD20+, usually CD5+)
        • Classic variant (monomorphic and centrocyte-like) or variant morphology (less common)
        • Cyclin D1 positivity or CCND1 rearrangement
      • Desirable: SOX11 positivity
    • Cyclin D1 negative MCL
      • Essential
        • B cell lymphoma cells (CD20+, usually CD5+)
        • Classic variant (monomorphic and centrocyte-like) or variant morphology (less common)
        • Immunophenotype consistent with MCL (including SOX11 positivity)
        • Absence of cyclin D1 positivity and CCND1 rearrangement
  • Microscopic description (Blood 1996;87:4483)
    • Monotonous population of atypical small to medium sized lymphoid cells with scant cytoplasm, mildly irregular and indented nuclear contours, moderately coarse chromatin and inconspicuous nucleoli
    • Small round T cells without conspicuous nucleoli are variably admixed
    • Blastoid variant shows markedly angulated and cleaved nuclei
    • Pleomorphic variant shows a mixture of atypical small cells and larger blastic cells

Positive stains
  • CD19, CD20, CD22, CD79a (consider previous monoclonal antibody treatment during selection of pan B cell marker)
  • Surface IgM or IgD heavy chain with light chain restriction (lambda > kappa) (Blood 1994;84:1361, Leuk Lymphoma 1999;36:147)
  • CD5 (positive in 95% of cases) (Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:1052)
  • Cyclin D1 (positive in 91% of cases) (Cancer Cell 2003;3:185, Am J Clin Pathol 2005;124:782)
  • FMC7 (positive in 90% of cases) (Am J Clin Pathol 2003;120:754)
  • SOX11 (positive in 93% of cyclin D1 and 100% of cyclin D1- cases) (Haematologica 2009;94:1555)
Negative stains
  • LEF1 (positive in 4 - 9% of cases) (Ann Diagn Pathol 2017;26:57)
  • CD23, CD200 (positive in 13% and 4 - 16.5% of cases, respectively) (Hum Pathol 2019;89:71, Mod Pathol 2018;31:327)
    • Positivity is associated with a better prognosis
    • Often associated with leukemic nonnodal MCL
  • CD10, BCL6 (positive in 2 - 3.8% and 12% of cases, respectively) (Oncotarget 2017;9:11441, Int J Mol Sci 2023;24:10207, Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2010;18:103)
    • Positivity is associated with a worse prognosis
  • CD43 (positive in 39.4% of cases) (Turk J Haematol 2018;35:94)

Molecular / cytogenetics description
  • Translocations (Front Oncol 2021;11:739441)
    • Cyclin D1 positive MCL
      • CCND1::IGH, t(11,14)(q13;q32) is present in 95% of cases
        • CCND1 break apart FISH
        • IGH break apart FISH
        • CCND1::IGH fusion FISH
        • Karyotype
      • IGK::CCND1, t(2,11) or CCND1::IGL, t(11,22)
        • CCND1 break apart FISH
        • IGK / IGL break apart FISH
        • Karyotype
    • Cyclin D1 negative MCL
      • CCND2 translocation with IGK / IGL
        • CCND2 break apart FISH
        • IGK / IGL break apart FISH
        • Karyotype
      • Cryptic insertion of IGK / IGL near CCND2 / CCND3
        • Customized FISH probes
        • Whole exome / genome sequencing
  • TP53
    • TP53 sequencing (p53 expression by IHC can be used as a surrogate but should be confirmed by sequencing)
    • FISH for TP53 or deletion of 17p (Am J Hematol 2022;97:638)
  • Next generation sequencing (or targeted sequencing) can detect somatic mutations and copy number alterations in many known cancer genes, which are useful for prognostics
    • Gene mutation frequency (Blood Adv 2020;4:2927)
      • ATM: 43.5%
      • IGHV: 38.4%
      • TP53: 26.8%
      • RB1: 24.3%
      • CDKN2A: 23.9%
      • MYC: 20.8%
      • CCND1: 20.2%
      • NSD2: 15.0%
      • KMT2A: 8.9%
      • S1PR1: 8.6%
      • CARD11: 8.5%
  • MCL35 is a nanostring based, RNA expression based proliferation assay that when combined with MIPIc may be useful to identify a subgroup with inferior outcome despite treatment with intensified chemoimmunotherapy and high dose chemotherapy with autologous stem cell transplantation (Br J Haematol 2018;183:225)

Sample pathology report
  • Colon, ascending, polyp, biopsy:
    • Colonic involvement by mantle cell lymphoma (see comment)
  • Terminal ileum, biopsy:
    • Ileal involvement by mantle cell lymphoma (see comment)
    • Background mucosa with active ileitis and reactive changes
    • Comment: Both the ascending colon biopsy and the terminal ileum biopsy demonstrate a prominent lymphoid infiltrate. The lymphocytes are characterized by small to intermediate nuclear size and nuclear clefting. Immunohistochemical stains (performed on both specimens) demonstrate the lymphoid infiltrate to consist predominantly of CD20+ / PAX5+ B cells expressing CD5 and cyclin D1. The Ki67 index is ~30%. Kappa and lambda in situ hybridization demonstrates dim kappa light chain restriction of lymphocytes. CD3 highlights background T cells. The overall findings are consistent with mantle cell lymphoma. Next generation sequencing is pending and will be reported as an addendum.
Differential diagnosis
  • In situ mantle cell lymphoma (J Hematop 2012;5:10.1007/s12308-012-0148-6, Haematologica 2012;97:270):
    • Mantle zones are not expanded and only partially replaced by neoplastic cells
    • Cyclin D1+ cells are mostly restricted to the inner mantle zone, with relatively few residing in the outer mantle zone or interfollicular areas
  • Chronic lymphocytic leukemia / small lymphocytic lymphoma (CLL / SLL) (J Clin Exp Hematop 2020;60:124, Hum Pathol 2022;121:29, Am J Clin Pathol 2012;138:132):
    • Typically CD23+, CD200+, LEF1+, FMC7-, SOX11-, cyclin D1- and lacking IGH::CCND1 fusion
    • CLL / SLL proliferation centers may occasionally be cyclin D1+ (10.5% of cases) but lack CCND1 translocations / gains and are SOX11-
  • Diffuse large B cell lymphoma (DLBCL) (Am J Clin Pathol 2008;129:630, Leuk Lymphoma 2011;52:458, Histopathology 2012;61:685, Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:719):
    • Cyclin D1- and lacking IGH::CCND1 fusion
    • CD5-, SOX11-, CD10-, MUM1+ and BCL6+ can help distinguish cyclin D1+ DLBCL (1.5 - 15% of cases) from blastoid or pleomorphic MCL variants

Board review style question #1


Which of the following is true regarding the sensitivity of 18FDG PET / CT for staging of the mantle cell lymphoma shown above?

  1. High sensitivity for all sites of involvement
  2. Low sensitivity for all sites of involvement
  3. Low sensitivity for gastrointestinal tract and bone marrow involvement
  4. Low sensitivity for lymph nodes and spleen involvement
Board review style answer #1
C. Low sensitivity for gastrointestinal tract and bone marrow involvement. The average sensitivity of 18FDG PET / CT for mantle cell lymphoma involvement of the gastrointestinal tract and bone marrow is low (39% and 36%, respectively). Answer A is incorrect because, as mentioned above, the average sensitivity of 18FDG PET / CT for involvement of the gastrointestinal tract and bone marrow is low, therefore sensitivity for all sites is not high. Answers B and D are incorrect because the average sensitivity of the spleen and lymph nodes is high (100% and 97.5%, respectively); therefore these sites, as well as all sites are not low sensitivity on 18FDG PET / CT.

Board review style question #2


Somatic mutation involving which of the following genes is associated with improved prognosis for the mantle cell lymphoma shown above?

  1. IGHV
  2. KMT2D
  3. MYC
  4. TP53
Board review style answer #2
A. IGHV. Improved survival is associated with somatic mutation of IGHV. Answers B, C and D are incorrect because inferior survival is associated with somatic mutations of KMT2D, MYC or TP53.
