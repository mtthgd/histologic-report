Definition / general

* Deposition of dark melanin-like pigment in colonic macrophages

Essential features

* Pigment deposition in colon with striking gross and macroscopic features but minimal direct clinical consequences
* Linked to use of anthraquinone laxatives
* Common endoscopic finding

Sites

* Can involve all parts of colon and rectum but typically spares mucosal regions with lymphoid nodules, polyps or carcinomas
* Should biopsy nonpigmented regions in these patients

Etiology

* Linked to use of anthraquinone laxatives but may be secondary to any increase in colonic epithelium apoptosis ([Histopathology 1997;30:160](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9067741))

Clinical features

* Not associated with malignant changes, although it allows small polyps to be more easily identified ([Z Gastroenterol 1997;35:313](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9188145))

Diagnosis

* Colonoscopy

Case reports

* 40 year old woman with colonic lymphoid hyperplasia in melanosis coli ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2001;125:1110](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11473472))
* 50 year old woman taking laxatives ([N Engl J Med 2013;368:2303](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23758235))
* 83 year old man with melanosis coli involving pericolonic lymph nodes associated with the herbal laxative Swiss Kriss ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2004;128:565](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15086301))

Gross description

* Diffuse brown-black discoloration of colonic mucosa

Sample pathology report

* Random colon, biopsy:
  + Colonic mucosa with melanosis coli, otherwise within normal limits

Differential diagnosis

* **Blue / green / red bowel**:
  + Coloration different
  + No histologic changes
* **Brown bowel / ceroidosis**:
  + Pigment in smooth muscle cells
* [Hemochromatosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liverhemochromatosis.html):
  + Pigment in epithelial cells
* [Pseudomelanosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelpseudomelanosis.html):
  + Black pigment in macrophages of duodenum
