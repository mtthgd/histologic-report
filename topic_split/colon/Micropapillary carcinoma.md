Definition / general

* Uncommon subtype of colonic adenocarcinoma with distinctive behavior
* Officially recognized in WHO classification

Essential features

* Histology: small clusters of malignant cells with abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm and pleomorphic nuclei
* High rate of lymph node metastasis (even when early stage) and poor prognosis

Epidemiology

* Micropapillary features may be present in 9% to 19% of colorectal carcinomas ([Hum Pathol 2006;37:809](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16784979), [Mod Pathol 2007;20:729](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17464318))

Pathophysiology

* Lymph node metastasis are very common, even in pT1 / pT2 tumors ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:1287](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19471152))
* Prognosis worse than for conventional colorectal carcinoma ([Colorectal Dis 2012;14:e567](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22390187))

Case reports

* 72 year old woman with invasive micropapillary carcinoma of sigmoid colon ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2008;1:457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18787620))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Small clusters of malignant cells with abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm and pleomorphic nuclei
* Micropapillae inhabit lacunar-like spaces and demonstrate a reverse polarity configuration, with apical surfaces facing the periphery rather than the center
* Lymphovascular invasion is commonly present
* Morphology is similar to micropapillary carcinomas of other organs ([Adv Anat Pathol 2004;11:297](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15505530))
* Coexisting regions of conventional adenocarcinoma also usually present

Micropapillary carcinoma

Positive stains

* [MUC1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) (inside out staining pattern) ([Histopathology 2005;47:479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16241995))
* [SOX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumormicropapillary.html), **NOTCH3** (stem cell markers) ([Mod Pathol 2013;26:1123](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23060121))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Increased rate of *TP53* alterations and lower rate of microsatellite instability ([Mod Pathol 2011;24:729](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21336262))

Sample pathology report

* Ascending colon, mass, biopsy:
  + Carcinoma with prominent micropapillary features (see comment)
  + Comment: Micropapillary carcinoma of the colon has a reported propensity to metastasize to lymph nodes even when early stage.

Differential diagnosis

* **Tumor budding**:
  + Does not show reverse polarity ([Histopathology 2002;40:127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11952856))

1. It is characterized by high microsatellite instability

Board review style answer #1

**B.** It metastasizes to lymph nodes often
