Definition / general

* WHO recognized subtype of colorectal carcinoma with mucin lakes comprising at least 50% of tumor mass, though utility of 50% cutoff is unclear ([Histopathology 2019;74:406](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30160323))

Essential features

* Most common subtype of colorectal carcinoma
* Mucin production easily observed on histology
* Some cases are microsatellite instability (MSI) high
* Unclear whether worse prognosis than conventional colorectal carcinoma

Terminology

* Variably called colloid carcinoma

Epidemiology

* Relatively common (10% of all colorectal carcinomas) ([Cancer 1976;37:1891](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/177180))
* Incidence is stable ([Dis Colon Rectum 2005;48:1161](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15868237))

Sites

* Usually arises in the right colon ([J Clin Pathol 2012;65:381](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22259177))

Pathophysiology

* Associated with microsatellite instability, though most mucinous carcinomas are not MSI high ([Cancer 2005;103:2023](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15812832), [Am J Pathol 2001;158:527](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11159189))

Clinical features

* Typically presents at more advanced stage and with greater nodal involvement than standard colorectal carcinoma ([Dis Colon Rectum 1993;36:49](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8380140), [Gastroenterol Hepatol 2002;25:534](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12435303))
* May recur locally following excision ([World J Surg Oncol 2012;10:109](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22703761))

Prognostic factors

* May have same or worse prognosis compared with standard colorectal carcinoma ([Ann Oncol 2012;23:135](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21531784), [J Surg Oncol 2000;73:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10694641))

Case reports

* 11 year old girl with colloid carcinoma of rectum ([J Postgrad Med 1993;39:218](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7527858))
* 77 year old man with large mucinous adenocarcinoma ([Dig Surg 2003;20:69](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12637812))

Gross description

* Exophytic, gelatinous mass

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Adenocarcinoma with strips of tumor cells floating in large extracellular mucin lakes comprising at least half of tumor mass
* Signet ring cells (with intracellular mucin) may also be present but should comprise less than half of tumor

Mucinous adenocarcinoma

Positive stains

* [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html) (100%)
* [MUC2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmuc2.html) (100%)
* [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html) (98%)
* [Beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html) (nuclear) (64%)
* [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html) (19%, usually rectal)
* [MUC6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmuc2.html#muc6) (5%)
* Reference: [Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:1830](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21881489)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* [*KRAS*](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/molecularkras.html) mutations more common and *TP53* mutations less common than in standard colorectal carcinomas ([Mod Pathol 2006;19:59](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16118624))

Sample pathology report

* Ascending colon, resection:
  + Mucinous adenocarcinoma, well differentiated (see synoptic report)

Differential diagnosis

* [Colitis cystica profunda](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/coloncystitiscystica.html):
  + May form a mass and demonstrate copious extracellular mucin but epithelium is clearly benign
* [Signet ring cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorsignetring.html):
  + More than half of tumor composed of signet ring cells

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/colontumorcolloidGonzalez01.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/colontumorcolloidGonzalez01.jpg)

Which of the following is true about mucinous adenocarcinoma of the colorectum?

1. It is the most common subtype of colorectal carcinoma
2. It unequivocally has a better prognosis than standard colorectal carcinoma
3. It usually affects pediatric patients
4. Signet ring cells are always at least a minor component

Board review style answer #1

**A.** It is the most common subtype of colorectal carcinoma
