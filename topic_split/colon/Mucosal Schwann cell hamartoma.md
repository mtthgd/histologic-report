Definition / general

* Benign mucosal proliferation composed of Schwann cells

Essential features

* Benign, incidental spindle cell lesion
* Not associated with any clinical syndrome
* Positive for S100, negative for EMA

Terminology

* Has been called neuroma or neurofibroma in past

Sites

* Can arise anywhere in the colorectum but more common distally

Clinical features

* Average patient age is 62 years, with a female predominance ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:781](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19065103))
* Not associated with any particular syndrome
* Lesions do not recur

Diagnosis

* Discovered incidentally during colonoscopy

Case reports

* 20 year old man with multiple colonic lesions ([BMC Gastroenterol 2015;15:128](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26444007))
* 59 year old man with ulcerative colitis ([Gastroenterol Hepatol (N Y) 2013;9:183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23961270))

Gross description

* Small (6 mm or less) sessile polyp

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Poorly circumscribed mucosal proliferation of spindle cells, with no whorling, no palisading, no fasciculation
* Nuclei are generally small, bland and elongated

Positive stains

* [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) (lesional cells)
* [Neurofilament / NFP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsneurofilament.html) (rare intralesional axons)

Negative stains

* [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html), [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html)

Sample pathology report

* Transverse colon, polypectomy:
  + Mucosal Schwann cell hamartoma (see comment)
  + Comment: An immunohistochemical stain for S100 is positive.

Differential diagnosis

* [Perineurioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorperineurioma.html):
  + Positive for [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html), negative for [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)
* [Ganglioneuroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorganglioneuromatosis.html):
  + Ganglion cells present
* [Neurofibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueneurofibroma.html):
  + Unlikely to involve mucosa
* **Benign epithelioid peripheral nerve sheath tumor**:
  + Cells are epithelioid; lesion extends into superficial submucosa ([Am J Surg Pathol 2005;29:1310](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16160473))
* [Tactile corpuscle-like body](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontclb.html):
  + Usually small, incidental finding in mucosal biopsy ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1668](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26291509))
