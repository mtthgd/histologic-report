Definition / general

* Nonneoplastic polyp / lesion
* May be seen in association with diverticular disease or polypoid phase of solitary rectal ulcer syndrome (SRUS)

Essential features

* Can occur anywhere in the colon but is most common in the rectosigmoid
* May be seen in association with diverticular disease or polypoid phase of solitary rectal ulcer syndrome (SRUS)
* Characterized by fibromuscular obliteration of lamina propria and splayed hypertrophic muscularis mucosae ([Diagnostics (Basel) 2022;12:966](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35454014))
* Crypts may be distorted and elongated or show hyperplastic and serrated changes
* Secondary changes, including surface erosion, inflammation, vascular ectasia and congestion may be present

Terminology

* Prolapsing mucosal polyp
* Mucosal prolapse changes
* Mucosal prolapse syndrome (MPS)
* Solitary rectal ulcer syndrome (SRUS)

ICD coding

* ICD-10
  + [K63.5](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/K00-K95/K55-K64/K63-/K63.5) - polyp of colon
  + [K62.1](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/K00-K95/K55-K64/K62-/K62.1) - rectal polyp
  + [K62.3](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/K00-K95/K55-K64/K62-/K62.3) - rectal prolapse

Epidemiology

* M > F (3:1) ([Cureus 2023;15:e37958](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37223182))
* Fourth to sixth decades of life ([GE Port J Gastroenterol 2016;23:214](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28868462))
* Risk factors
  + Diverticular disease
  + Repeated colonic spastic contractions
  + Excessive straining

Sites

* Most common in rectum and sigmoid colon but may be found throughout the colon

Pathophysiology

* Unknown, although postulated that spastic contraction of the bowel wall leads to mucosal redundancy ([Am J Surg Pathol 1991;15:871](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1951844))

Etiology

* Often but not always associated with diverticular disease ([Am J Surg Pathol 1991;15:871](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1951844))
* Constipation / repetitive straining
* Solitary rectal ulcer syndrome (SRUS)

Clinical features

* Rectal bleeding, abdominal pain and constipation
* Colonoscopy examination
  + Solitary or multiple polyps or elevated patches with smooth and bright red colored surface
  + Sharply contrasted bright red color due to hyperemia or congestion with surface smoothness devoid of coarse lobulation and irregular nodularity, which are common features in neoplastic or dysplastic lesions ([Intern Med 2007;46:1701](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17938524))

Diagnosis

* Clinical history including rectal bleeding, abdominal pain and constipation
* Diagnosis is made on histological examination

Radiology description

* Abdominal computed tomography (CT): may show segmental wall thickening with layered pattern wall enhancement ([Intern Med 2007;46:1701](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17938524))
* Endoscopic ultrasound (EUS) shows 3 patterns ([Dig Liver Dis 2021;53:427](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33478871))
  + Polypoid / nodular lesion with thickening of mucosa
  + Ulcerative lesions with diffuse thickening of mucosa / submucosa and loss of architecture
  + Flat lesions with thickening of the muscularis propria

Prognostic factors

* Overall favorable prognosis

Case reports

* 15 year old boy with mucosal prolapse polyps mimicking Peutz-Jeghers syndrome ([JPGN Rep 2024;5:170](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38756112))
* 18 year old man and 62 year old woman with rectal polyposis in mucosal prolapse syndrome ([Diagnostics (Basel) 2022;12:966](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35454014))
* 60 year old man with rectal polypoid lesion with nodular surface ([Intest Res 2019;17:281](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30704157))
* 65 year old man with incidentally discovered mucosal prolapse polyps during colorectal cancer screening ([Cureus 2023;15:e37958](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37223182))

Treatment

* Endoscopic biopsy or removal often performed for diagnosis
* Larger symptomatic lesions may require additional endoscopic (e.g., argon plasma coagulation or endoscopic resection) or surgical therapy ([ACG Case Rep J 2021;8:e00563](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33928177))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Thickening and disruption of the muscularis mucosae and muscularization (splaying of muscularis propria between crypts) of the lamina propria
* Crypt elongation distortion and branching
* Hyperplastic and serrated changes, including crypt branching, hypermucinous appearance of the epithelium ([GE Port J Gastroenterol 2016;23:214](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28868462))
* Secondary changes may include surface erosion, inflammation, hemorrhage, vascular congestion and ectasia ([J Clin Pathol 2009;62:1034](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19861563))
* Diamond shaped crypts and mucosal elastin ([Histopathology 1990;17:129](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2227837))

Sample pathology report

* Sigmoid colon polyp, biopsy:
  + Mucosal prolapse polyp

Differential diagnosis

* [Hyperplastic polyp](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorhyperplastic.html):
  + Lack of splayed muscularis mucosae between crypts
  + Serrated architecture with a sawtooth appearance, with serrations generally limited to the upper half of the crypts
* [Neoplastic polyp](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorpolypgeneral.html):
  + Presence of cytologic dysplasia
* [Inflammatory polyp](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorinflammatory.html):
  + Lacks thickening and splaying of muscularis propria between crypts
* [Sessile serrated lesion / polyp / adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorsessileserrated.html):
  + Serrated crypts with dilatation or branching extending to crypt bases
* [Peutz-Jeghers polyp](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelpeutzjeghers.html):
  + Peutz-Jeghers polyps contain large arborizing bundles of smooth muscle separating lobules of epithelium, whereas smooth muscle fibers in mucosal prolapse are thinner, more disorganized and separate individual crypts

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/colonmucosalprolapsepolypMcCloskeyHutchings02.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/colonmucosalprolapsepolypMcCloskeyHutchings02.jpg)

Which of the following histological features is characteristic of a mucosal prolapse polyp of the colon?

1. Colonic crypts separated by splayed hypertrophic muscularis mucosae
2. Cytologic dysplasia
3. Hyperchromatic, elongated, penicillate nuclei
4. Sawtooth pattern glands limited to the upper half of the crypts
5. Serrated crypts with dilatation and branching extending to crypt bases

Board review style answer #1

**A.** Colonic crypts separated by splayed hypertrophic muscularis mucosae are a characteristic feature of mucosal prolapse polyp. Answer E is incorrect because serrated crypts with dilatation and branching extending to crypt bases is a feature of sessile serrated lesion / polyp / adenoma. Answer B is incorrect because mucosal prolapse polyp is a nonneoplastic lesion that lacks cytologic dysplasia. Answer C is incorrect because hyperchromatic, elongated, penicillate nuclei are a feature of low grade adenomatous dysplasia. Answer D is incorrect because sawtooth pattern glands limited to the upper half of the crypts is a feature associated with hyperplastic polyps.

The lesion shown in the image above is found in the rectum. What is your diagnosis?

1. Hyperplastic polyp, microvesicular type
2. Inflammatory polyp
3. Mucosal prolapse polyp
4. Peutz-Jeghers polyp

Board review style answer #2

**C.** Mucosal prolapse polyp. This image shows elongated crypts, surface congestion and erosion and splaying of the muscularis mucosae between individual crypts consistent with mucosal prolapse polyp. Answer D is incorrect because Peutz-Jeghers polyps typically contain larger smooth muscle bundles, which separate epithelium into lobules. Answer B is incorrect because inflammatory polyps lack splaying of muscularis mucosae between crypts. Answer A is incorrect because hyperplastic polyps, microvesicular type show serrated architecture with a sawtooth appearance in the upper half of the crypts.
