Definition / general

* Associated with bleeding, diarrhea, abdominal pain, although usual side effects are subclinical ([Am J Med Sci 2009;338:96](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19680014))

Clinical features

* **Endoscopy:** small erosions in different parts of colon ([Pathologe 2006;27:65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16369762), [Hum Pathol 2007;38:527](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17367604))
* Colonoscopy findings may be normal, inflammation, superficial ulcers, solitary / multiple deep ulcers ([Indian J Gastroenterol 2009;28:150](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19937416))

Case reports

* 39 year old man with NSAID induced colopathy misinterpreted as Crohn's disease
  ([Z Gastroenterol 2010;48:472](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20140840))
* 3 cases: rofecoxib and inflammatory bowel disease
  ([J Clin Gastroenterol 2005;39:142](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15681911))

Treatment

* Discontinuation of NSAIDs ([Z Gastroenterol 2000;38:957](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11194886))
* Selective COX2 inhibition may be an alternative to NSAIDs in rheumatologic patients ([Gastroenterol Clin North Am 2009;38:333](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19446262))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Usually mixed inflammatory infiltrate but may be predominantly neutrophilic or lymphocytic ([Am J Clin Pathol 1998;110:622](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9802347), [Adv Anat Pathol 1999;6:213](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10410174))
* Often erosions, crypt architectural disarray; no granulomas
* **Incidental chronic colitis:** cecal based chronic colitis associated with frequent NSAID use; basal plasmacytosis (94%) or crypt architectural distortion (94%) often disappears after NSAID withdrawal ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:463](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20154590))
