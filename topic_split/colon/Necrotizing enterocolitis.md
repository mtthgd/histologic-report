Definition / general

* Acute, necrotizing inflammation of terminal ileum or ascending colon, common at day 2-4 in premature or low birth weight neonates ([eMedicine #1](http://emedicine.medscape.com/article/977956-overview), [#2](http://emedicine.medscape.com/article/411616-overview), [J Clin Pathol 1979;32:1090](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/512026))
* May be associated with Hirschsprung disease, thrombosis of abdominal aorta, H2 blockers ([Pediatrics 2006;117:e137](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16390920)), chemotherapy for hematopoietic malignancies ([Int J Hematol 2005;82:319](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16298823))
* Mortality rate of 15-30% ([Semin Fetal Neonatal Med 2011;16:145](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21514258)); poorer survival in preterm infants of HIV+ mothers ([Pediatr Dev Pathol 2012;15:293](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22483319))

Etiology

* Oral feeding of neonates with immature gut immune system causes release of proinflammatory cytokines; bacteria in food produce more cytokines which injure mucosa; intestinal blood flow may be disturbed
* IL10 may prevent by attenuating intestinal inflammation ([Am J Surg 2012;203:428](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22450025))

Clinical features

* **Symptoms:** abdominal distention, loss of bowel sounds, blood-stained stool
* **Complications:** bowel perforation, short bowel syndrome, malabsorption (due to ileal resection), strictures, recurrence

Treatment

* Fluids and surgery if gangrene/perforation

Gross description

* Necrotic mucosa, submucosal gas-filled cysts

Microscopic (histologic) description

* **Early:** mucosal edema, hemorrhage, necrosis, pneumatosis cystoides intestinalis
* **Late:** hemorrhagic and gangrenous bowel wall with strictures
