Definition / general

* Benign polypoid lesion occurring in the colonic mucosa or submucosa
* Peripheral nerve sheath tumor, often with overlying serrated / hyperplastic epithelium

Essential features

* Uncommon mucosal epithelial-mesenchymal lesion of the colon
* Usually occurs in middle aged women
* Benign, does not recur

Terminology

* Also known as benign fibroblastic polyp of the colon ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:374](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15104300), [Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:1088](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18520438))

Sites

* Usually in distal colon; rarely in small intestine

Clinical features

* F > M, with a median age of 51 ([Am J Surg Pathol 2005;29:859](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15958849))

Treatment

* Excision (benign behavior)

Gross description

* Small sessile polyp (median size 0.4 cm)

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Poorly circumscribed spindle cell proliferation, usually confined to the mucosa, with ovoid nuclei and pale indistinct cytoplasm, in a background of fine collagenous stroma
* No atypia, no pleomorphism, no mitotic figures
* Entrapped / overlying colonic crypts appear hyperplastic or serrated in the majority of cases
* Perineurial proliferation sometimes focally present in otherwise typical sessile serrated polyp / adenoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:1373](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21836484))

Perineurioma

Positive stains

* Occasional [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [claudin1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsclaudin1.html),
  [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) (may be faint / focal), [GLUT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglut1.html), [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html)

Negative stains

* [KIT](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html), [neurofilament](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsneurofilament.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html)

Electron microscopy description

* Long bipolar cytoplasmic processes, prominent pinocytotic vesicles

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* *BRAF* mutation often occurs in overlying serrated epithelium, if present ([Am J Surg Pathol 2013;37:745](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23588369))

Sample pathology report

* Ascending colon, polypectomy:
  + Perineurioma (see comment)
  + Comment: Immunohistochemical stains show the lesion is positive for EMA (focal) and negative for S100.

Differential diagnosis

* [Mucosal Schwann cell hamartoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumormucosalschwann.html):
  + Positive for [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)
  + Negative for [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)
* [Ganglioneuroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorganglioneuroma.html):
  + Positive for [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)
  + Ganglion cells present
* [Inflammatory fibroid tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorinflammatoryfibroid.html):
  + Perivascular spindling of cells
  + Prominent eosinophils
  + Strong [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) positivity
* [Neurofibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueneurofibroma.html):
  + Unlikely to involve mucosa
* **Benign epithelioid peripheral nerve sheath tumor**:
  + Cells are epithelioid
  + Lesion extends into superficial submucosa ([Am J Surg Pathol 2005;29:1310](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16160473))
* [Tactile corpuscle-like body](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontclb.html):
  + Usually small, incidental finding in mucosal biopsy ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1668](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26291509))
