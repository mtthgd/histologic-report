Definition / general

* A polyp is an exophytic or sessile lesion distinct from the mucosal surface and may be neoplastic or nonneoplastic

Essential features

* > 95% of colorectal adenocarcinoma arises from polyps
* Usually asymptomatic

Epidemiology

* Varies by type of polyp
* For more information on epidemiology of colon polyps, please refer to the individual chapters pertaining to each type of polyp in Pathology Outlines

Pathophysiology

* Etiology of colonic polyps varies from neoplastic to nonneoplastic and can either arise sporadically or in syndromic context

Etiology

* Examples of risk factors for adenomatous polyps:
  + Smoking
    - Twofold - threefold increased risk of all polyp types in smokers ([Best Pract Res Clin Gastroenterol 2017;31:419](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28842051))
    - Serrated polyps: relative risk is 2.47 ([Gastroenterology 2017;152:92](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27639804))
    - Increased adenoma risk in distal colon and rectum ([Am J Epidemiol 2013;177:625](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23459948))
  + Women who use estrogen hormone replacement therapy: increased serrated polyps ([Am J Epidemiol 2013;177:625](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23459948))
  + Alcohol ([Gastroenterology 2017;152:92](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27639804))
    - Serrated polyps: relative risk is 1.33 ([Gastroenterology 2017;152:92](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27639804))
  + Diet high in fat or meat ([Gastroenterology 2017;152:92](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27639804))
  + BMI
    - Serrated polyps: relative risk of high BMI is 1.4 ([Gastroenterology 2017;152:92](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27639804))
    - BMI ≥ 30: increased distal colon and rectal serrated polyps ([Am J Epidemiol 2013;177:625](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23459948) [Gastroenterology 2017;152:92](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27639804))
  + Decreased risk:
    - NSAIDs, aspirin, diet high in folate, calcium or fiber ([Gastroenterology 2017;152:92](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27639804))

Clinical features

* Clinical impression correlates poorly with neoplasia ([J Gastroenterol Hepatol 2006;21:563](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16638099), [World J Gastroenterol 2018;24:905](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29491684))

Diagnosis

* Colonoscopy with polypectomy and histopathological evaluation is the gold standard
* 80% are diminutive polyps (1 - 5 mm) ([Gastroenterology 2017;152:1821](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28461192), [Curr Opin Gastroenterol 2016;32:38](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26574868))
  + "Resect and discard" strategy: real time optical diagnosis without histopathological evaluation for diminutive colonic polyps (1 - 5 mm) ([World J Gastroenterol 2016;22:6049](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27468196))
  + Diminutive polyps present 0 - 0.6% risk of cancer ([World J Gastroenterol 2016;22:6049](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27468196))
  + Recommended only for expert centers ([Curr Opin Gastroenterol 2016;32:38](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26574868), [Ann Gastroenterol 2017;30:592](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29118553))
* “Diagnose and leave behind” strategy: real time optical diagnosis without polypectomy for hyperplastic polyps located in the rectosigmoid, as they harbor a cancer risk even lower than diminutive polyps ([World J Gastroenterol 2016;22:6049](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27468196), [Curr Opin Gastroenterol 2016;32:38](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26574868))
  + Resection is not mandatory for endoscopists with high degree of expertise and use of novel real time optical tools (narrowband imaging [NBI], flexible spectral imaging color enhancement [FICE] and i-SCAN digital contrast [i-SCAN]) ([Endoscopy 2017;49:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28212588))
  + High confidence required for diagnosis of hyperplastic polyp without tissue sampling ([Curr Opin Gastroenterol 2016;32:38](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26574868))
* Cold forceps biopsy, hot forceps biopsy (HFB), cold snare polypectomy, hot snare polypectomy for pedunculated polyps
  + Discussed in the [Treatment](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/#colontumorpolypgeneraltreat) section
* Advanced endoscopic imaging:
  + Goal is to avoid over or undertreatment ([Bianco: Colon Polypectomy - Current Techniques and Novel Perspectives, 1st edition, 2018](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/3319594567/pathologyoutl-20))
  + Chromoendoscopy uses contrast dyes ([Gastrointest Endosc 2018;88:674](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30217241))
  + Narrow band imaging does not use dyes to enhance mucosal surface ([GE Port J Gastroenterol 2018;26:40](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30675503), [Gut 2014;63:785](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23853211), [Dig Liver Dis 2014;46:803](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24908572) [Am J Gastroenterol 2014;109:855](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24751581))
  + Examples of chromoendoscopy include:
    - Fujinon intelligent chromoendoscopy ([World J Clin Oncol 2017;8:168](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28439498))
    - i-scan ([PLoS One 2018;13:e0197520](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29768508), [Eur J Gastroenterol Hepatol 2017;29:1309](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28902739), [PLoS One 2015;10:e0126237](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25978459))
    - Others: high definition white light colonoscopy, cap assisted colonoscopy, RetroView, Full Spectrum Endoscopy Colonoscope, Third Eye Retroscope, Third Eye Panoramic, NaviAid G-EYE balloon colonoscope, EndoRings, Endocuff, magnification endoscopy, virtual chromoendoscopy, flexible spectral imaging color enhancement (FICE), STORZ, autofluorescence imaging, confocal laser endomicroscopy, endocytoscopy, optical coherence tomography, spectroscopy, colon capsule endoscopy ([Proc (Bayl Univ Med Cent) 2019;33:28](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32063760))

Laboratory

* Assays for detection of blood in stool
  + Fecal occult test detection rate is 33% ([Ann Gastroenterol 2019;32:278](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31040625))
  + Fecal immunochemical test detection rate is 27%; very low for sessile serrated adenomas / polyps ([Am J Gastroenterol 2019;114:1909](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31764091), [Clin Gastroenterol Hepatol 2017;15:880](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27847280))
* DNA in stool
  + Multitarget stool DNA test detection rate: 46% ([Am J Gastroenterol 2019;114:1909](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31764091))
  + Multitarget stool DNA test analyzes the following ([N Engl J Med 2014;370:1287](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24645800)):
    - *KRAS* mutations
    - Aberrant *NDRG4* and *BMP3* promoter methylation
    - Β-actin as reference gene
    - Hemoglobin (immunochemical assay)
  + Positive predictive value for colorectal neoplasia: 67% ([Am J Gastroenterol 2020;115:608](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32068535))
  + Commercial name: Cologuard
* Presence of key metabolites in urine: detection rate of adenomatous polyps: 43% ([Int J Colorectal Dis 2019;34:1953](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31673772))
* Iron deficiency anemia ([Arab J Gastroenterol 2016;17:67](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27426957))
  + Elderly: 15 - 30% caused by blood loss from colon, including from polyps ([PSNet: Anemia and Delayed Colon Cancer Diagnosis [Accessed 09 June 2020]](https://psnet.ahrq.gov/web-mm/anemia-and-delayed-colon-cancer-diagnosis))
* Genetic tests for familial syndromes; examples include:
  + Adenomatous polyposis: mutation in [*APC*](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorFAP.html) (classic) or [*MUTYH*](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumormyhassociated.html) (autosomal recessive)
  + [Lynch syndrome](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorlynch.html): mutation in mismatch repair genes
  + [Peutz-Jeghers syndrome](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorPeutz.html): mutation in *STK11*

Radiology description

* CT colonography is a validated screening test for colon polyps ([World J Radiol 2016;8:472](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27247713))
* CT colonography has average sensitivity of 88.8% for polyps ≥ 6 mm or colorectal carcinoma ([Eur Radiol 2014;24:1049](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24519111))
* Contrast screening during CT leads to better detection (odds ratio: 40.4) ([Radiology 2016;280:455](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26878227))
* Radiological imaging classification systems:
  + Kudo: analyze pits of colonic crypt; neoplastic versus nonneoplastic ([World J Gastroenterol 2014;20:12649](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25253970))
  + NBI International Colorectal Endoscopic (NICE): color, vascular pattern, structure of the surface ([Gastroenterol Res Pract 2018;2018:7531368](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29725349))
  + Dutch Workgroup serrAted polypS & Polyposis (WASP) ([Gut 2016;65:963](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25753029), [GE Port J Gastroenterol 2019;26:314](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31559322)):
    - Based on NICE classification
    - Distinguish serrated versus hyperplastic polyps

Prognostic factors

* Some prognostic factors for neoplastic polyps are listed below:
  + Sex: men twice as likely to have adenomas ([Best Pract Res Clin Gastroenterol 2017;31:419](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28842051))
    - Adenoma detection rate: 24.7% in men and 14.3% in women ([Br J Cancer 2016;115:1421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27764840))
  + Age: prevalence increases with age
    - Prevalence is 35 - 39% in adults aged 50 - 59 versus 55 - 70% in adults aged 80+ ([Best Pract Res Clin Gastroenterol 2017;31:419](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28842051))
    - Patients 50 years old and older are more likely to have sessile polyps and polyps in proximal colon ([Arq Bras Cir Dig Apr-Jun;27:109](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25004288))
  + Geographic location: higher rates in Australia, New Zealand, Europe and North America; lower rates in Africa and South Central Asia ([Best Pract Res Clin Gastroenterol 2017;31:419](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28842051))
  + Race: African Americans 76% relative risk, Hispanic 37% versus white for adenomas ([Best Pract Res Clin Gastroenterol 2017;31:419](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28842051))
* Serrated polyps:
  + Serrated polyps: prevalence estimated to be 13 - 52% ([Gut 2015;64:929](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25399542), [Am J Gastroenterol 2015;110:501](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25756237))
  + Higher risk with *Helicobacter pylori* infection (odds ratio: 1.71), age 50 - 75 years (odds ratio: 2.83), female (odds ratio: 1.28), white (odds ratio: 1.52), obese, smoking (odds ratio: 2.09) ([Indian J Gastroenterol 2018;37:235](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29876742))
  + Race: African Americans 35% lower relative risk of serrated polyps, Hispanic 67% lower risk versus white ([Best Pract Res Clin Gastroenterol 2017;31:419](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28842051))
  + Sessile serrated polyp more likely to harbor high grade dysplasia than other adenomas when there is associated invasive carcinoma (40% versus 25.8%) ([Intest Res 2016;14:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27433150))
    - Sessile serrated polyps with cytological dysplasia have worse prognosis, more likely to develop into colorectal carcinoma ([Gut 2016;65:437](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25731869))
* Polyp recurrence:
  + Sessile serrated polyp more likely to recur (27.3% reoccurred versus 3%) ([Surg Endosc 2020;34:2918](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31482353))
  + Sex and race not predictive ([Arch Intern Med 2010;170:1127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20625018))
  + More likely in polyp > 3 mm (odds ratio 3.4) ([Endoscopy 2019;51:253](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30674046))
  + More likely with immediate bleeding ([Endoscopy 2019;51:253](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30674046))
  + More likely in same location ([Rev Esp Enferm Dig 2016;108:563](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27604266))
    - 4x higher proximal (odds ratio: 3.5) and distal (odds ratio: 3.8) segments, 3x higher in rectum (odds ratio: 2.6) ([Rev Esp Enferm Dig 2016;108:563](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27604266))
  + More likely with left sided polyps ([Turk J Med Sci 2017;47:1370](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29151306))
  + More likely with villous polyps ([World J Gastroenterol 2019;25:1502](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30948913))
  + More likely with piecemeal resection ([World J Gastroenterol 2019;25:1502](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30948913))
  + Less likely with exercise ([Cancer Prev Res (Phila) 2017;10:478](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28584169))
* Prognostic factors of syndromes:
  + Familial adenomatous polyposis
    - Older age = worse prognosis (more likely to have colorectal carcinoma at the time of familial adenomatous polyposis diagnosis; odds ratio 4.75 for age 40 - 49 and age > 50 years versus age World J Gastroenterol 2016;22:4380)
    - Panel of peptides can be prognostic ([Clin Colorectal Cancer 2016;15:e75](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26792031))
  + Lynch syndrome
    - MSI high tumors have better prognosis ([Clin Adv Hematol Oncol 2018;16:735](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30543589))
* Risk of developing colorectal adenocarcinoma:
  + M = F ([Br J Cancer 2016;115:1421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27764840))
  + Adenoma leads to increased risk of colorectal adenocarcinoma ([JAMA 2018;319:2021](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29800214))
  + Increased chance with proximal serrated polyps, large serrated polyps ([Am J Gastroenterol 2015;110:501](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25756237), [Gut 2015;64:929](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25399542))
  + Removed adenomatous polyps: 0.2 - 9% malignant ([Gut 1998;43:669](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9824349))

Treatment

* Endoscopic resection
  + For lesions of mucosa, sometimes superficial submucosa
  + Reduces colorectal cancer mortality ([N Engl J Med 2014;371:799](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25162886))
  + Indication for each technique depends on size, morphology, likelihood of submucosal invasion of lesion ([Ann Gastroenterol 2017;30:592](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29118553))
* Endoscopic techniques
  + Standard endoscopic techniques:
    - Cold snare polypectomy:
      * Recommended technique for polyps 5 mm or less and for sessile polyps 6 - 9 mm ([Endoscopy 2017;49:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28212588), [Gastroenterol Clin North Am 2013;42:443](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23931853))
      * Usually can achieve complete resection, adequate tissue for histology, low complication rates ([Endoscopy 2017;49:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28212588))
      * Safer than hot techniques; less bleeding ([Clin Gastroenterol Hepatol 2020;18:42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31782991))
    - Hot snare polypectomy:
      * Recommended for sessile polyps 10 - 19 mm, pedunculated polyps ([Endoscopy 2017;49:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28212588))
      * Increased risk of immediate and delayed injury ([Endoscopy 2017;49:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28212588))
    - Cold forceps biopsy:
      * Most often used for diminutive lesions (1 - 5 mm)
      * High retrieval rate and low complication rate ([Dig Endosc 2014;26:98](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24750157))
      * Less likely to get complete resection compared to cold snare; takes longer to perform ([Am J Gastroenterol 2013;108:1593](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24042189), [Gastrointest Endosc 2016;83:508](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26545637))
      * Useful when cold snare is not possible ([Ann Gastroenterol 2017;30:592](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29118553))
    - Hot forceps biopsy:
      * Thermal ablation of polyps with a coagulation current through an electrosurgical unit
      * Destruction of polyp base makes histological diagnosis difficult ([Clin Endosc 2016;49:350](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27399313), [J Clin Gastroenterol 2013;47:657](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23948754))
      * Increased risk of delayed bleeding or hypercoagulation syndrome ([Clin Endosc 2016;49:350](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27399313), [J Clin Gastroenterol 2013;47:657](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23948754))
      * Considered to be less effective and less safe than cold snare ([World J Gastroenterol 2017;23:328](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28127206))
  + Advanced endoscopic techniques:
    - Endoscopic mucosal resection:
      * Technique used for removal of large flat or sessile polyps
      * “Inject and cut” is a snare resection after saline injection into the submucosal wall
      * Alternatives: cap assisted endoscopic mucosal resection, underwater endoscopic mucosal resection for fibrotic tissue, cold snare endoscopic mucosal resection for polyps > 1 cm based on single center retrospective studies ([Gastrointest Endosc 2012;75:1086](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22365184), [Gastrointest Endosc 2015;81:1238](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25746979), [Endosc Int Open 2017;5:E184](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28331902), [Endosc Int Open 2015;3:E508](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26528509))
      * For flat large polyps, size is main predictor for recurrence ([Digestion 2016;93:311](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27271329))
    - Endoscopic submucosal dissection:
      * Mainly considered for complex lesions
      * After submucosal injection, needle type ESD knife is used for circumferential incision and then submucosal layer is dissected; hybrid technique uses knife with snare ([Gastrointest Endosc 2017;86:74](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28254526))
      * Allows en bloc resection versus endoscopic mucosal resection ([Clin Endosc 2016;49:454](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27561263))
      * Higher risk of perforation and need of surgery versus endoscopic mucosal resection ([Clin Endosc 2016;49:454](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27561263))
      * Lower en bloc and R0 resection rates in non-Asian countries versus Asian countries ([Gastrointest Endosc 2017;86:74](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28254526))
      * The data in lesions > 50 mm show lower R0 resection rate and significantly increased procedure times ([Endosc Int Open 2016;4:E895](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27540580))

Gross description

* Paris classification based on explicit recognition ([Endoscopy 2005;37:570](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15933932))
  + Polypoid type:
    - Protrudes at least 2.5 mm above mucosal layer
    - Sessile: no stalk (o-Is)
    - Pedunculated: with stalk, may be due to traction on the mass (o-Ip)
    - Semipedunculated (o-Isp)
  + Nonpolypoid type:
    - Less than 2.5 mm above mucosal layer
    - Slightly elevated (o-IIa)
    - Flat (o-IIb)
    - Slightly depressed lesions (o-IIc)
  + Excavated / ulcerated: o-III ([Ann Gastroenterol 2017;30:592](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29118553))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Generally depends on type of polyp (see specific topics for details)
* Histology of submucosal lifting agents used during polypectomy
  + Some examples include ORISE and Eleview
  + Histologic appearance of lifting agents is temporally dependent, ranging from basophilic bubbly amorphous material (early) to eosinophilic hyalinized globules or ribbons (late)
  + Can mimic amyloid but differentiated based on presence of foreign body giant cell reaction to lifting agent that is not polarizable and Congo red negativity ([Am J Surg Pathol 2020;44:793](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31934919), [Am J Clin Pathol 2020;153:630](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31875886))
