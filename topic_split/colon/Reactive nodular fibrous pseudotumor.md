Definition / general

* Reactive fibroinflammatory process involving the gastrointestinal tract and mesentery ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:532](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12657940))

Essential features

* Benign fibroinflammatory mass more common in men
* Stains for AE1 / AE3, CD117 and muscle specific actin
* Does not appear to recur

Sites

* Usually arises in mesentery; may involve colon or small bowel

Etiology

* May be related to a proliferation of multipotential subserosal cells ([Int J Surg Pathol 2004;12:365](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15494862))
* Possibly linked to endometriosis and ergotamine use ([Virchows Arch 2005;447:879](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16021510))

Clinical features

* Usually occurs in patients aged 40 - 60; more common in men
* Associated with prior abdominal surgery
* Can cause acute abdominal pain but sometimes discovered incidentally

Case reports

* 71 year old man with history of abdominal surgery and a 19.5 cm abdominal mass ([Iran J Pathol 2015;10:149](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26351476))

Treatment

* Complete resection appears curative

Gross description

* Solitary or multiple tumors, usually involving outer wall of small intestine or colon
* Firm, tan white, well circumscribed
* Usually around 6 cm but may measure up to 20 cm

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Mildly to moderately cellular lesion composed of stellate or spindled fibroblasts arranged haphazardly or in intersecting fascicles
* Stroma rich in collagen (wire-like, keloidal or hyalinized)
* Sparse intralesional mononuclear cells and peripheral lymphoid aggregates usually present
* May have infiltrative borders

Positive stains

* [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html), [smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [muscle specific actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsactinmusclespecific.html), [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html), [AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html)

Negative stains

* [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [ALK1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* No substitutions, deletions or insertions in exons 9 or 11 of *KIT* gene

Sample pathology report

* Mesentery, resection:
  + Reactive fibrotic nodule, most consistent with reactive nodular fibrous pseudotumor (2.2 cm)
  + Negative for malignancy.
  + Margins of resection unremarkable.

Differential diagnosis

* [Fibromatosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorfibromatosis.html):
  + Positive for [beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html), negative for [CD117](https://pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)
* [Retroperitoneal fibrosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorirf.html):
  + Disease of retroperitoneum, associated with [Riedel thyroiditis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidriedel.html) and methylsergine, negative for [CD117](https://pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)
* [Sclerosing mesenteritis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colonsclerosingmes.html):
  + Involves mesentery or mesocolon, usually no prior trauma, thick collagen bands dissecting lobules of mesenteric fat with fat necrosis, negative for [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)

Board review style question #1

Which of the following abdominal lesions stains positive for CD117 by immunohistochemistry?

1. Desmoid fibromatosis
2. Inflammatory myofibroblastic tumor
3. Reactive nodular fibrous pseudotumor
4. Retroperitoneal fibrosis
5. Sclerosing mesenteritis

Board review style answer #1

**C**. Reactive nodular fibrous pseudotumor
