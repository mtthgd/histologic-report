Definition / general

* Multisystem autoimmune disease characterized by microvascular damage, abnormal collagen deposition and fibrosis of the muscularis propria

Essential features

* Fibrosis of the muscularis propria, abnormal collagen deposition and autoimmune dysfunction ([Clin Rheumatol 2021;40:981](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32812181))
* Intimal fibrosis in medium and small vessels
* May involve any part of the gastrointestinal tract, though esophagus and anorectum are the most common
* Autoantibodies typically present (antinuclear antibodies [ANA], anticentromere, anti-Scl-70 or anti-RNA polymerase III)

Terminology

* Systemic scleroderma
* Systemic sclerosis
* CREST syndrome
* Localized scleroderma

ICD coding

* ICD-10
  + [M34.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/M00-M99/M30-M36/M34-/M34.9) - systemic sclerosis, unspecified; also known as scleroderma
  + [M34.8](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/M00-M99/M30-M36/M34-/M34.8) - other forms of systemic sclerosis
  + [M34.2](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/M00-M99/M30-M36/M34-/M34.2) - systemic sclerosis induced by drug and chemical

Epidemiology

* F > M (5:1) ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2022;101:e31780](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36397401))
* Fourth to sixth decades of life
* Risk factors
  + Longstanding Reynaud phenomenon
  + Multiorgan involvement
  + Stress
  + Infection

Sites

* May involve any part of the gastrointestinal tract, though esophagus and anorectum are most common ([J Scleroderma Relat Disord 2016;1:247](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28133631))
* Gastrointestinal (GI) tract involved in nearly 90% of the patients with systemic sclerosis (SSc) ([Rheumatology (Sunnyvale) 2018;8:235](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30057856))
  + Colonic involvement affects 20 - 50% of SSc patients

Pathophysiology

* Mediated by vascular insult, autoimmunity and tissue fibrosis
* Initial vascular injury leads to endothelial cell activation resulting in overexpression of adhesion molecules and activation of platelets and thrombotic and fibrinolytic cascades ([StatPearls: Systemic Sclerosis (Scleroderma) [Accessed 7 February 2025]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28613625))
* Vascular insult may be caused by viruses, environmental exposures, autoantibodies, proteolytic enzymes and inflammatory cytokines
* T helper 2 (Th2) predominance leads to enhanced collagen synthesis and myofibroblast proliferations, driven by profibrotic cytokines such as TGFβ, interleukins (ILs) 4, 5 and 13 ([StatPearls: Systemic Sclerosis (Scleroderma) [Accessed 7 February 2025]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28613625))

Etiology

* Genetic factors ([StatPearls: Systemic Sclerosis (Scleroderma) [Accessed 7 February 2025]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28613625))
  + HLA DRB1\*1104, DQA1\*0501 and DQB1\*0301
* Environmental factors ([StatPearls: Systemic Sclerosis (Scleroderma) [Accessed 7 February 2025]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28613625))
  + Viral associations; cytomegalovirus (CMV), Epstein-Barr virus (EBV), parvovirus B19
  + Exposure to silica dust
  + Toluene, xylene, trichloroethylene and polyvinyl chloride

Clinical features

* Dysmotility ([Am J Gastroenterol 2019;114:1847](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31805016))
* Constipation
* Bacterial overgrowth secondary to dysmotility
* Fecal incontinence
* Volvulus

Diagnosis

* Clinical findings ([Rheumatology (Sunnyvale) 2018;8:235](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30057856))
  + Constipation
  + Malabsorptive diarrhea
  + Colonic dilatation, loss of haustra and wide mouth diverticula
* Lab testing
* Computed tomography (CT) findings
* Manometry

Laboratory

* Antinuclear antibodies present in > 90% of cases ([StatPearls: Systemic Sclerosis (Scleroderma) [Accessed 7 February 2025]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28613625))
* Up to 70% of patients exhibit at least 1 specific autoantibody
  + Anticentromere
  + Anti-Scl-70
  + Anti-RNA polymerase III

Radiology description

* Loss of haustra due to weakened wall integrity ([Rheumatology (Sunnyvale) 2018;8:235](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30057856))
* Wide mouth diverticula
* Dilatation

Prognostic factors

* Normal life expectancy but may suffer significant morbidity ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2022;101:e31780](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36397401))
* Chronic and unpredictable course
* Factors associated with poor outcome ([Curr Opin Rheumatol 2018;30:588](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30148801))
  + Male gender
  + Older age
  + High erythrocyte sedimentation rate

Case reports

* 44 year old woman with obstruction due to colonic intussusception with scleroderma ([ACG Case Rep J 2014;2:30](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26157898))
* 49 year old woman with spontaneous bowel perforation in the setting of colonic involvement with scleroderma ([BMJ Case Rep 2020;13:e233220](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31907219))
* 51 year old woman with pneumatosis intestinalis, pneumoperitoneum and ascites secondary to scleroderma ([Cureus 2022;14:e27200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36035050))
* 60 year old woman with intestinal pneumatosis as a manifestation of systemic sclerosis ([Rev Esp Enferm Dig 2023;115:220](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36645063))

Treatment

* No definitive therapeutic modalities are available for colonic involvement ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2022;101:e31780](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36397401))
* Treatment directed toward symptomatic relief and prevention of possible complications
* Motility agents such (e.g., metoclopramide) may be trialed for dysmotility

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Muscularis propria shows focal to diffuse atrophy with replacement by fibrosis ([Clin Rheumatol 2021;40:981](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32812181))
  + Fibrosis is common in the inner circular layer but may be seen in both layers
* Intimal fibrosis in submucosal arteries and small vessels ([Clin Rheumatol 2021;40:981](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32812181))
* Decreased neuronal density in myenteric plexus and fewer nerve fibers in the submucosal plexus ([Clin Rheumatol 2021;40:981](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32812181))

Systemic sclerosis and scleroderma

Sample pathology report

* Sigmoid colon, sigmoidectomy:
  + Segment of colon with extensive atrophy and fibrosis of muscularis propria consistent with involvement by scleroderma (see comment)
  + Associated diverticula present
  + Comment: The patient's clinical history of systemic scleroderma is noted.

Differential diagnosis

* [Visceral myopathies](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colonvisceralmyopathy.html):
  + Degenerative changes or vacuolization of smooth muscle of muscularis propria ([J Clin Pathol 1988;41:424](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3366929), [Am J Surg Pathol 1987;11:846](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3674282))
* **Fibrosis** related to other neuromuscular disorders (e.g., **spina bifida** or [spinal cord injury](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/forensicsneuropathologyspinalcord.html)) ([Virchows Arch 2017;470:175](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28062917)):
  + May involve loss of interstitial cells of Cajal (ICC) around the myenteric plexus
* [Diverticular disease, NOS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colondiverticulosis.html):
  + Lacks significant fibrosis of the muscularis propria

Board review style question #1

[![Fibrosis of inner circular layer](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/colonsclerodermamccloskeyhutchings04.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/colonsclerodermamccloskeyhutchings04.jpg)

Which of the following histological features are characteristic of scleroderma involving the colon?

1. Amorphous eosinophilic material positive on Congo red
2. Fibrosis of the muscularis propria
3. Prominent degenerative changes or vacuolization of smooth muscle of muscularis propria
4. Thickened subepithelial collagen band and increased lamina propria inflammation composed of lymphocytes, plasma cells and eosinophils

Board review style answer #1

**B.** Fibrosis of the muscularis propria.
Scleroderma of the colon typically shows fibrosis of the muscularis propria. Answer D is incorrect because these findings are typically seen in collagenous colitis. Answer C is incorrect because these findings are more commonly seen in visceral myopathies. Answer A is incorrect because these findings are seen in amyloidosis.

Board review style question #2

In addition to fibrosis of the muscularis propria, which of the following histologic features may be seen with scleroderma?

1. Absence of ganglion cells
2. Absence of interstitial cells of Cajal
3. Intimal fibrosis of submucosal arteries
4. Prominent degenerative changes and vacuolization of smooth muscle

Board review style answer #2

**C.** Intimal fibrosis of submucosal arteries. These features are seen in the submucosal arteries in scleroderma in addition to fibrosis of the muscularis propria. Answer D is incorrect because this is a feature of visceral myopathy. Answer A is incorrect because it is a feature of Hirschsprung disease / aganglionosis. Answer B is incorrect because the interstitial cells of Cajal are typically retained in scleroderma.
