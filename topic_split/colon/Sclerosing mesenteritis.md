Definition / general

* Uncommon nonneoplastic condition that causes thickening and shortening of colonic mesentery ([JBR-BTR 2011;94:241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22191288))

Essential features

* Benign fibrotic / inflammatory condition involving the mesentery
* Some cases are IgG4 related

Terminology

* Older terms include idiopathic retractile mesenteritis, mesenteric panniculitis and mesentery lipodystrophy ([Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:392](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9130985))

Epidemiology

* Common in rural areas of Peru, otherwise fairly uncommon ([Rev Gastroenterol Peru 1998;18:114](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12271343))
* Possibly underdiagnosed in life, as indicated by 1 autopsy series ([Schweiz Med Wochenschr 1985;115:1218](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2413530))

Sites

* Affects the mesentery; small bowel mesentery is involved more often than colonic

Etiology

* Usually idiopathic but some cases likely a form of IgG4 sclerosing disease ([J Clin Pathol 2008;61:1093](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18682417))

Clinical features

* Usually men with median age of 65 years; most common symptom is abdominal pain; can lead to patient death ([Clin Gastroenterol Hepatol 2007;5:589](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17478346))

Radiology description

* Mesentery can show fat attenuation or a fibrofatty mass

Case reports

* 40 year old man with large mesenteric mass ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2001;125:443](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11231502))
* 46 year old woman with rectosigmoid disease ([Surg Today 1996;26:435](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8782303))

Treatment

* Various medications (including corticosteroids) and surgery, depending on clinical severity ([Int Arch Med 2011;4:17](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21635777))

Gross description

* Markedly thickened and rubbery mesentery causing bowel twisting

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Fibrosis with dense collagen, fat necrosis, chronic inflammation (especially around vessels) and variable focal calcification
* Minimal atypia, no or few mitoses
* IgG4 related cases lack fat necrosis and show obliterative phlebitis and increased inflammation

Positive stains

* [IgG4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsigg4.html) (in some cases)

Negative stains

* Nuclear [beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html) staining not present

Sample pathology report

* Transverse colon and mesentery, excision:
  + Prominent bland fibrotic process involving mesentery (see comment)
  + Segment of colon with reactive change.
  + Negative for malignancy.
  + Margins of resection unremarkable.
  + Comment: The overall findings are most consistent with sclerosing mesenteritis.

Differential diagnosis

* [Fibromatosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorfibromatosis.html):
  + Positive for nuclear [beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html)
* [Idiopathic retroperitoneal fibrosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorirf.html):
  + Located in retroperitoneum, not mesentery
* [Inflammatory myofibroblastic tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorimt.html):
  + Spindle cells more prominent
  + May be positive for [ALK1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html)
* [Liposarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuewdliposarcoma.html):
  + Atypical cells visible ([Chirurg 2001;72:742](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11469099))
* **Sclerosing lymphoma**:
  + Inflammatory infiltrate more prominent
