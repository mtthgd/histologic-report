Definition / general

* Secukinumab is a novel human IgG1 monoclonal antibody administered via subcutaneous injection that antagonizes interleukin 17A (IL17A) and has been associated with new and exacerbated cases of inflammatory bowel disease (IBD) and other patterns of injury (i.e., lymphocytic colitis and vasculitis)

Essential features

* Originally approved in 2015 in the U.S. for treatment of plaque psoriasis; U.S. Food & Drug Administration (FDA) approved indications include moderate to severe psoriasis, hypertrophic palmoplantar psoriasis, generalized pustular psoriasis, psoriatic arthritis and ankylosing spondylitis
  + It can be used off label for rheumatoid arthritis, systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE), familial Mediterranean fever and tumor necrosis factor receptor associated periodic syndrome
* Cases of new onset IBD and exacerbation of existing IBD have been reported in clinical trials, adverse event surveillance systems and case reports
* Endoscopic and histologic findings are usually indistinguishable from idiopathic IBD
* Other patterns of injury, including lymphocytic colitis and vasculitis, have been reported ([J Investig Med High Impact Case Rep 2022;10:23247096221110399](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35801542), [Acta Derm Venereol 2020;100:adv00077](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32115666), [Inflamm Bowel Dis 2022;28:e47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34792606))

Epidemiology

* Relatively rare in clinical trials: 8 cases of new onset IBD among 5,773 patients in clinical trials (versus 0 of 1,844 patients who received placebo) ([Aliment Pharmacol Ther 2019;50:373](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31309607))
* Between 2015 and 2022, 348 cases of suspected secukinumab induced gastrointestinal (GI) adverse events (249 IBD and 99 colitis) were reported to U.S. FDA's Adverse Event Reporting System ([Front Pharmacol 2023;14:1124628](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37033665))
* Treatment for psoriasis: secukinumab and ixekizumab are both IL17 antagonists that are associated with colitis, compared to other treatment options (e.g., ustekinumab) that target IL12 and IL23 and are approved for use in psoriasis as well as to treat Crohn's disease and ulcerative colitis

Sites

* Colon, terminal ileum

Pathophysiology

* Unknown
* Hypothesized to be due to removal of IL17 inhibition of T helper 1 cells; a subset of these uninhibited T helper 1 cells may then go on to damage the gut ([J Transl Autoimmun 2021;4:100104](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34179741))

Clinical features

* GI symptoms: diarrhea (often bloody), abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting
* Systemic symptoms: fever, malaise, elevated inflammatory markers
* History of use of secukinumab
* Most common adverse drug reactions: nasopharyngitis, upper respiratory tract infection and lower GI symptoms (diarrhea, abdominal pain) with or without systemic signs / symptoms

Diagnosis

* Endoscopic and histologic findings compatible with IBD
* History of secukinumab use
* Exclusion of other possible colitis etiologies
* Biopsy often shows findings consistent with IBD; other patterns of injury can occur

Case reports

* 30 year old woman with perforated active colitis ([Inflamm Bowel Dis 2022;28:e47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34792606))
* 31 year old woman with lymphocytic colitis ([J Investig Med High Impact Case Rep 2022;10:23247096221110399](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35801542))
* 35 year old woman with SAPHO (synovitis, acne, pustulosis, hyperostosis and osteitis) syndrome and severe colitis and 41 year old man with psoriatic arthritis and bloody stools, abdominal pain and nausea for 5 months ([Clin J Gastroenterol 2024;17:263](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38060157))
* 52 year old man with active colitis with ulceration ([Gastroenterol Hepatol 2020;43:358](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32561217))

Treatment

* Cessation of offending agent
* Usual IBD treatments if severe
* Consideration of changing to an agent that is approved for both psoriasis and IBD

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Active colitis with or without ulceration
* Prominent apoptotic bodies
* Crypt withering, fibrinoid necrosis of small vessels and ischemic pattern of injury occasionally reported ([Inflamm Bowel Dis 2022;28:e47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34792606))
* Lymphocytic colitis pattern has also been reported ([J Investig Med High Impact Case Rep 2022;10:23247096221110399](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35801542))
* Collagenous colitis pattern ([Cureus 2021;13:e16147](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34367765))
* Well developed features of chronic injury such as crypt architectural distortion and paneth cell metaplasia are often absent

Sample pathology report

* Sigmoid colon, biopsy:
  + Severe active colitis with ulceration (see comment)
  + Comment: The sampled colonic mucosa shows widespread neutrophil mediated epithelial injury (with cryptitis and crypt abscess formation) and ulceration. Intact crypts are occasionally withered and show many apoptotic bodies. While there is basal lymphoplasmacytosis, there is no Paneth cell metaplasia or architectural distortion, arguing against chronic inflammatory bowel disease. CMV immunostain is nonreactive. The patient's current use of secukinumab is noted. In the absence of other possible infectious or immune etiologies, the findings here are most compatible with secukinumab induced colitis.

Differential diagnosis

* Exacerbation of previously undiagnosed [inflammatory bowel disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colonindeterminate.html):
  + Would be difficult to distinguish (clinical history can be helpful)
* [Infectious colitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colonacuteselflimited.html):
  + [CMV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscmv.html) IHC and microbiology testing can be helpful
* [Ischemic colitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colonischemic.html):
  + Presentation is nonspecific and highly variable; requires a high index of suspicion
    - Predisposing conditions: strenuous physical activity, dehydration, illicit drug use, thrombophilia, aortic surgery, cardiac bypass, vasculitis, obstructing colonic lesion
  + Cause is vascular in nature and can present as occlusive or nonocclusive
  + Histologic changes: edema, crypt distortion, submucosal hemorrhage, inflammatory infiltrate, granulation tissue, intravascular platelet thrombi and necrosis ([World J Gastroenterol 2008;14:7302](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19109863))
* Differential diagnoses vary depending on the exact histologic pattern (for example, [lymphocytic colitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colonlymphocytic.html) or [apoptotic colopathy](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colonbowelprep.html))

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/colonsecukinumabmicroAmofaHoGonzalo002.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/colonsecukinumabmicroAmofaHoGonzalo002.jpg)

Which of the following histologic findings is more suggestive of secukinumab induced colitis than idiopathic inflammatory bowel disease (IBD)?

1. Crypt abscesses
2. Paneth cell metaplasia
3. Prominent apoptotic bodies
4. Ulceration

Board review style answer #1

**C.** Prominent apoptotic bodies. Prominent apoptotic bodies are not usually seen in idiopathic inflammatory bowel disease but have been commonly reported in secukinumab induced colitis. Answers A and D are incorrect because crypt abscesses and ulceration can be seen in both diagnoses. Answer B is incorrect because Paneth cell metaplasia is suggestive of idiopathic IBD with chronicity.

Board review style question #2

Which of the following medications used to treat psoriasis is believed to have the highest risk of the development of colitis?

1. Adalimumab
2. Apremilast
3. Secukinumab
4. Ustekinumab

Board review style answer #2

**C.** Secukinumab is an IL17 antagonist that is associated with colitis and new or exacerbated cases of inflammatory bowel disease. It is hypothesized to cause colitis due to the removal of IL17 inhibition in T helper cells that subsequently cause damage in the gut. Answer B is incorrect because apremilast is a phosphodiesterase 4 inhibitor that increases cAMP and subsequently decreases the production of TNF-alpha, IL17 and IL23; it has been utilized for the treatment of ulcerative colitis (UC). Answer A is incorrect because adalimumab is a TNF-alpha inhibitor and is used to treat Crohn’s disease and UC. Answer D is incorrect because ustekinumab is an IL12 and IL23 inhibitor and is approved for use in psoriasis as well as for the treatment of Crohn's disease and UC.
