Definition / general

* CpG islands (also called CG islands, p indicates a phosphodiester bond connecting the C and G) are 500 to 2000 base pair regions rich in cytosine-guanosine dinucleotide repeats, present at 5' region in 50% of human genes ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CpG_site#CpG_islands),  [Web-books](http://www.web-books.com/MoBio/Free/Ch7F2.htm))
* Methylation of cytosine residues in promoter regions and proximal exons of these islands represses transcription
* Hypermethylation of the CpG dinucleotide in the promoter regions of TP16 and CDH1 tumor suppressor genes is the mechanism to inactivate these genes
* Promoter methylation of MLH1 may also occur

Terminology

* Also called epigenetic changes / epigenetic silencing

Epidemiology

* 30% of carcinomas have CpG island methylator phenotype (CIMP+)
* Patients are usually female, have more advanced age, and have smoking history

Sites

* Occurs predominantly in proximal colon

Etiology

* An important mechanism of inactivating tumor suppressor genes in the chromosomal and microsatellite instability pathways ([Curr Mol Med 2006;6:401](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16900663), [Cancer Metastasis Rev 2004;23:29](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15000147))
* However, CpG methylation can also act independently of chromosomal instability and microsatellite instability

Prognostic factors

* Although controversial, recent studies suggest that CIMP status is an independent predictor of a low colon cancer specific mortality and survival advantage and a high response rate to 5-fluorouracil based treatments ([Gut 2009;58:90](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18832519), [Int J Clin Oncol 2008;13:498](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19093176)), although further studies are required
