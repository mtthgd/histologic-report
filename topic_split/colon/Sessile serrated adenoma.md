Definition / general

* Serrated neoplastic precursor lesion of colorectal cancer ([Am J Gastroenterol 2012;107:1315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22710576))
* Defined as having 2 or 3 contiguous crypts demonstrating features of sessile serrated adenoma, as discussed below (per [Bosman: WHO Classification of Tumours of the Digestive System, 4th Edition, 2010](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/%209283224329/pathologyoutl-20))
* WHO 2019 criteria for diagnosis of [serrated polyposis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorserratedpolyposissx.html) syndrome are as follows
  + ≥ 5 serrated lesions / polyps proximal to the rectum are present with all lesions being ≥ 5 mm in size and at least 2 lesions being ≥ 10 mm in size
  + > 20 serrated lesions / polyps of any size distributed throughout the large bowel with ≥ 5 being proximal to the rectum
  + References: [Gastroenterology 2020;158:1520](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31982410), [Am J Transl Res 2021;13:5786](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34306326)

Essential features

* Neoplastic, premalignant lesion of the colorectum
* If present, patient should undergo increased colonoscopy surveillance
* May develop traditional cytologic dysplasia and progress to colorectal carcinoma with microsatellite instability

Terminology

* Current official WHO designation is sessile serrated lesion; former WHO designation was sessile serrated adenoma / polyp
* Examples that develop adenomatous epithelium are termed sessile serrated adenoma with cytologic dysplasia ([Gastrointest Endosc 2014;80:307](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24890425))

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [K63.5](http://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/K00-K95/K55-K64/K63-/K63.5) - Colon polyp

Epidemiology

* Account for approximately 3 - 9% of all colorectal polyps and 10 - 25% of all serrated polyps ([World J Gastroenterol 2014;20:2634](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24627599))
* Prevalence of sessile serrated adenoma increases slightly with age ([Am J Gastroenterol 2012;107:1315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22710576))
* More common in women and smokers ([Cancer Prev Res (Phila) 2017;10:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28325827))

Sites

* More often found in the proximal colon ([World J Gastroenterol 2010;16:3402](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20632442))

Etiology

* Associated with a high fat and meat diet, smoking, alcohol and a high BMI ([Gastroenterology 2017;152:92](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27639804))

Clinical features

* Incidental findings on colonoscopy and have no clinical features unless they progress to malignancy

Diagnosis

* Histopathology is the gold standard for diagnosing sessile serrated adenomas ([World J Gastroenterol 2016;22:7754](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27678358))
* Narrow band imaging can be used during endoscopy to detect sessile serrated adenomas ([World J Gastroenterol 2015;21:2896](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25780286), [Lancet Oncol 2009;10:1171](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19910250))

Radiology description

* Screening CT colonography may identify large (~10 mm) sessile serrated adenomas ([Radiology 2016;280:455](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26878227))

Prognostic factors

* Approximately 15% of patients with sessile serrated adenomas will develop adenomatous polyps with high grade dysplasia or colorectal carcinoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:927](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20551824))
* > 10 mm in size have a higher risk of developing colorectal carcinoma ([Cancer Prev Res (Phila) 2017;10:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28325827))

Treatment

* Polypectomy or endoscopic mucosal resection ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2014;7:1275](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24817924))
* Recommendations are to completely remove all serrated lesions proximal to the sigmoid colon and all serrated lesions > 5 mm in the rectosigmoid colon ([Am J Gastroenterol 2012;107:1315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22710576))
* Lesions > 1 cm in size or with high grade dysplasia should be managed clinically like a high risk adenoma ([Gastroenterology 2012;143:844](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22763141))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Sawtooth serrations of the epithelium with abundant mucin, similar to hyperplastic polyps
* Basal crypt dilation with mucous retention
* Bases of the crypts are more serrated compared to the surface and have mature goblet cells and mucinous cells ([Am J Gastroenterol 2012;107:1315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22710576))
* Lateral spread of the crypt bases (commonly described as boot shaped or anchor shaped crypts)
* May contain fibroblastic / perineuriomatous stroma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:1373](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21836484))
* Conventional ("adenomatous") dysplasia may also be present, with retention of MLH1 staining; no need to grade per WHO
* Nonconventional forms of dysplasia have also been described, including minimal deviation type (mild architectural and histologic changes, difficult to see on H&E but shows loss of MLH1), serrated type (tightly packed glands with rounded dysplastic nuclei and eosinophilic cytoplasm; MLH1 retained) and not otherwise specified (most common pattern; atypical changes not fitting into any other pattern; often MLH1 loss) ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:1728](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28752838), [Mod Pathol 2019;32:1390](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31028362))

Cecum

Positive stains

* Agrin positivity in muscularis mucosae may distinguish from hyperplastic polyps ([Clin Cancer Res 2020 15;26:1277](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31852835))

Negative stains

* Loss of **HES1** nuclear staining can be used to differentiate sessile serrated adenomas from hyperplastic polyps ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26448192))
* Loss of [MLH1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmlh1.html) and [PMS2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspms2.html) can be seen in areas of cytologic dysplasia ([Am J Clin Pathol 2006;126:564](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16938659))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* *BRAF* (V600E) mutation and CpG island methylator phenotype (CIMP), leading to microsatellite instability through promoter hypermethylation of *MLH1* ([World J Gastroenterol 2016;22:7754](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27678358), [Am J Gastroenterol 2012;107:1315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22710576))
* Negative for *KRAS* mutation

Sample pathology report

* Ascending colon, polyp, endoscopic mucosal resection:
  + Sessile serrated lesion
  + Lateral margins of resection unremarkable.

Differential diagnosis

* [Hyperplastic polyp](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorhyperplastic.html):
  + Crypts in hyperplastic polyps are less distorted and will not be laterally dilated at the base
  + Serrations are more pronounced at the epithelial surface than the base ([World J Gastroenterol 2016;22:7754](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27678358), [Am J Gastroenterol 2012;107:1315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22710576))
* [Traditional serrated adenoma (TSA)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumortraditionalserratedadenoma.html):
  + TSAs have a tubulovillous architecture with pseudostratified epithelium
  + Cells have abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm with thin, hyperchromatic, pencillate nuclei at the base ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:1290](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25127095))

Additional references

* [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2016;140:1079](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27684980)

Board review style question #1

What mutations can be seen in sessile serrated adenoma?

1. Beta catenin and *KRAS*
2. *BRAF* and beta catenin
3. *BRAF* and CpG island methylator phenotype (CIMP)
4. *KRAS* and CpG island methylator phenotype (CIMP)

Board review style answer #1

**C.** *BRAF* and CpG island methylator phenotype (CIMP).
*KRAS* and beta catenin mutations are not associated with sessile serrated adenomas and are instead seen in traditional serrated adenomas and tubular adenomas.
