Definition / general

* Rare subtype of colorectal carcinoma (< 1%) officially recognized by the WHO ([Ann Surg 2013;258:775](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23989057))
* > 50% of the tumor must have signet ring cell morphology to be classified as signet ring cell carcinoma

Essential features

* May form a diffuse lesion rather than a discrete mass
* Usually high stage with poor prognosis ([Dis Colon Rectum 2005;48:1161](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15868237), [Dis Colon Rectum 1999;42:1176](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10496558))
* Often metastasizes to lymph nodes, peritoneal surface and ovary (less commonly to liver) ([ANZ J Surg 2001;71:703](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11906382))

Terminology

* Sometimes called linitis plastica but this typically refers to signet ring cell carcinoma of stomach

Epidemiology

* May affect younger patients more than classic colorectal carcinoma ([Am J Gastroenterol 1996;91:2195](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8855747))
* Proximal signet ring cell cancers are a distinct subpopulation with a favorable oncologic outcome, possibly due to link with microsatellite instability ([Int J Colorectal Dis 2012;27:371](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22052041))

Sites

* Can occur anywhere in colon, though rectum is a less common location

Etiology

* Can be seen in carcinomas that arise through the microsatellite instability pathway ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1407](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14576473))

Clinical features

* Presents at earlier age and higher stage than conventional colorectal carcinoma ([J Gastrointest Oncol 2014;5:18](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24490039))

Diagnosis

* Diagnosis usually by biopsy, although magnifying endoscopy with narrow band imaging can also be used ([BMC Gastroenterol 2015;15:86](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26205810))

Prognostic factors

* Poor prognostic indicators include high tumor stage, bowel obstruction, "inefficient" surgery ([Int Surg 2014;99:691](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25437572))
* Prognosis poor even if signet ring cell component is < 50% ([PLoS One 2015;10:e0121944](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25789685))

Case reports

* 30 year old woman with primary signet ring cell carcinoma mimicking segmental Crohn's colitis ([Dig Liver Dis 2005;37:537](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15975543))
* 59 year old man with signet ring cell carcinoma in adenoma of sigmoid colon ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1999;123:957](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10506454))
* 67 year old man with primary signet ring cell carcinoma of colon at an early stage ([World J Gastroenterol 2006;12:3446](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16733868))
* 72 year old woman with signet ring cell carcinoma of colon with peritonitis carcinomatosa ([J Gastroenterol 2002;37:550](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12162414))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Proliferation of signet ring cells with intracellular mucin that displaces nucleus to side
* Pools of extracellular mucin often present as well
* Diffuse tissue infiltration less common than in gastric signet ring cell carcinoma
* May rarely be in situ or confined to a polyp ([J Surg Res 2010;163:250](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20538291))
* No gland formation in signet ring cell component, although it may be present elsewhere in the same lesion

Signet ring cell carcinoma

Positive stains

* [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html), [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html), [MUC2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmuc2.html), [MUC5AC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmuc2.html#muc5ac); variable [MUC1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) and [HepPar1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsheppar1.html) ([Am J Clin Pathol 2004;121:884](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15198362), [Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2000;8:183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10981869))

Negative stains

* [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Slightly lower rate of *KRAS* mutation than conventional colorectal carcinoma ([Jpn J Clin Oncol 1998;28:202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9614444))
* May show microsatellite instability ([Mod Pathol 2008;21:1533](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18849918))

Sample pathology report

* Ascending colon, resection:
  + Signet ring cell carcinoma, poorly differentiated (see synoptic report)

Differential diagnosis

* **Benign processes with signet ring cell change** (most commonly [pseudomembranous colitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colonAAcolitis.html)) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1429](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14576475))
* [Metastatic](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumormetastases.html) **signet ring cell carcinoma** of [stomach](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stomachdiffuse.html), [breast](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantsignetringcell.html) or [bladder](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderadeno.html):
  + Different staining patterns by immunohistochemistry ([Dig Liver Dis 2006;38:609](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16551516))
