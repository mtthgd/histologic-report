Definition / general

* Rare primary malignancy of colorectum, representing 0.1% of cases ([Am J Surg Pathol 1978;2:47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/637188))

Essential features

* Diagnosis requires finding no involvement of cloacogenic or squamous lined mucosa, no squamous cell carcinoma elsewhere and thorough sampling to exclude adenosquamous carcinoma ([Surg Today 1994;24:75](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8054782))
* Associated with ulcerative colitis, chronic colocutaneous fistula, schistosomiasis and colonic duplication ([Dis Colon Rectum 1988;31:228](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3280272), [Cancer 1981;47:602](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7226009))
* Aggressive clinical course with frequent metastasis ([Saudi Med J 2006;27:874](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16758054))

Sites

* Most common in proximal colon and rectum ([Clin Colorectal Cancer 2001;1:55](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12445380))

Etiology

* May arise from squamous metaplasia ([Int J Surg Pathol 2009;17:340](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18701516))
* Seemingly not related to HPV infection, except in rare cases ([Eur J Surg Oncol 2002;28:657](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12359204), [Gastroenterology 2001;120:988](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11231953))

Clinical features

* May cause hypercalcemia ([Dig Surg 2005;22:371](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16424667))

Prognostic factors

* Node negative cases have prognosis similar to colorectal adenocarcinoma ([Dis Colon Rectum 2001;44:341](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11289278))

Case reports

* 48 year old woman with squamous cell carcinoma arising in duplication of colon ([Cancer 1981;47:602](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7226009))
* 54 year old man with squamous cell carcinoma of colon with elevated serum SCC antigen ([Clin Colorectal Cancer 2001;1:55](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12445380))
* 59 year old woman with primary acantholytic squamous cell carcinoma of cecum ([Diagn Pathol 2011;6:5](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21223553))
* 60 year old woman with squamous cell carcinoma arising from longstanding colocutaneous fistula ([World J Gastroenterol 2005;11:5251](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16127766))
* Primary squamous cell carcinoma of colon arising in villous adenoma ([Hum Pathol 1988;19:362](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3278968))

Treatment

* Surgery, chemotherapy ([Dis Colon Rectum 1999;42:102](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10211528))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Similar to squamous cell carcinoma in other organs (polygonal eosinophilic cells with intercellular bridges and keratin formation)
* Can be poorly differentiated
* May be basaloid or acantholytic

Positive stains

* [CK5/6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5.html), [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html)

Sample pathology report

* Ascending colon, resection:
  + Squamous cell carcinoma of the colon (see synoptic report and comment)
  + Comment: An immunohistochemical stain for p40 is diffusely positive.

Differential diagnosis

* [Adenosquamous carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumoradenosquamous.html):
  + Carcinoma with squamous features but also malignant glandular component
* [Angiosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueangiosarcoma.html):
  + Squamous cell carcinoma can have anastomosing spaces mimicking vessels of angiosarcoma but is negative for [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html) and [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)
* **Extension** or [metastasis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumormetastases.html) from squamous cell carcinoma elsewhere ([anus](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/anusscc.html), [lung](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorSCC.html), etc.) ([Eur J Cardiothorac Surg 2001;19:719](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11343961))
