Definition / general

* Serrated neoplastic precursor lesion for aggressive *BRAF* mutated microsatellite instability (MSI) stable subtype of colorectal carcinoma ([World J Gastrointest Oncol 2016;8:805](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28035250))
* Characterized by prominent eosinophilic cytoplasm, elongated nuclei, tubulovillous (filiform) architecture and ectopic crypts ([Am J Gastroenterol 2012;107:1315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22710576))

Essential features

* Neoplastic polyp of the colon
* Shows dysplastic appearing nuclei and prominent eosinophilic cytoplasm

Terminology

* Abbreviated as TSA
* Filiform serrated adenoma sometimes used to refer to a possible variant ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1238](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17667549), [Pathology 2012;44:18](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22157687))

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [K63.5](http://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/K00-K95/K55-K64/K63-/K63.5) - Colon polyp
* ICD-10: [D12](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D12-) - Benign neoplasm of colon, rectum, anus and anal canal (subcodes depending on site of adenoma)

Epidemiology

* Traditional serrated adenomas account for 0.56 - 1.9% of colonic polyps ([Hum Pathol 2015;46:933](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26001333))
* No gender predilection ([Mod Pathol 2015;28:414](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25216220))
* Usually seen in patients over age 50 ([J Gastroenterol Hepatol 2018;33:1039](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29087626))

Sites

* More often found in the rectosigmoid colon ([Mod Pathol 2015;28:414](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25216220))
* A similar lesion, lacking *BRAF* mutation, may rarely arise in the small intestine ([Histopathology 2015;66:333](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24894811))

Pathophysiology

* May arise from microvesicular hyperplastic polyps (MVHP) and sessile serrated adenomas (SSA) ([Hum Pathol 2015;46:933](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26001333))
* Generally considered to be intrinsically dysplastic, though some argue against this view ([Hum Pathol 2015;46:933](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26001333))

Etiology

* Associated with obesity and increased triglyceride levels ([J Gastroenterol Hepatol 2018;33:1039](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29087626))

Clinical features

* Incidental finding on colonoscopy

Diagnosis

* Histopathology is the gold standard of diagnosis ([World J Gastroenterol 2016;22:7754](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27678358))

Radiology description

* Screening CT colonography may identify large (~14 mm) traditional serrated adenomas ([Radiology 2016;280:455](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26878227))

Prognostic factors

* Risk of progression to carcinoma is unknown ([Am J Gastroenterol 2012;107:1315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22710576))

Treatment

* Endoscopic removal of the adenoma
* US Multi Society Task Force on Colorectal Cancer recommends a 3 year surveillance interval after initial diagnosis of traditional serrated adenomas ([Gastroenterology 2012;143:844](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22763141))

Gross description

* May be flat, pinecone shaped or fern-like ([Mod Pathol 2015;28:414](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25216220), [World J Gastrointest Oncol 2016;8:805](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28035250))

Sessile lesion

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Protuberant villiform growth pattern with slit-like serrations
* Pseudostratified epithelial columnar cells with eosinophilic cytoplasm and dark, pencillate, dysplastic-like nuclei
* Ectopic crypt foci / formations are a key feature (abnormally positioned crypts with bases not seated at the muscularis mucosae) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:21](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18162766))
* High grade adenomatous dysplasia may be present
* Goblet cells present
* Mucin rich variant is characterized by > 50% goblet cells and fewer ectopic crypt formations ([Histopathology 2017;71:208](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28295534))

Traditional serrated adenoma

Positive stains

* [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html)
  stains only the surface epithelial cells and does not stain the ectopic crypt formations ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:21](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18162766))
* [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) stains only the ectopic crypt formations
* [MUC2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmuc2.html)
  and [MUC5AC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmuc2.html#muc5ac)
* [Beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html) and [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) nuclear staining is present in areas of conventional dysplasia ([Mod Pathol 2015;28:414](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25216220))

Negative stains

* [MUC6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmuc2.html#muc6)
  ([Mod Pathol 2008;21:660](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18360351))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* MAPK pathway activation is a critical initiating event, either by a *BRAF* or *KRAS* mutation ([Hum Pathol 2015;46:933](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26001333))
* MAPK pathway activation is followed by CpG island methylator phenotype (CIMP)
* *BRAF* mutated traditional serrated adenomas are more frequently CpG island methylator phenotype high ([Mod Pathol 2015;28:414](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25216220), [Hum Pathol 2015;46:933](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26001333))
* Most cases have no defects in mismatch repair (microsatellite stable)
* *PTPRK-RSPO3* gene fusions may be present ([Histopathology 2017;71:601](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28543708))

Sample pathology report

* Rectum, polypectomy:
  + Traditional serrated adenoma

Differential diagnosis

* [Hyperplastic polyp](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorhyperplastic.html) and [sessile serrated adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorsessileserrated.html):
  + Cytologic features of pseudostratification and nuclear elongation are most useful in distinguishing traditional serrated adenomas from hyperplastic polyps and sessile serrated adenomas ([Virchows Arch 2012;461:495](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23052370))
  + Ectopic crypts, goblet cells and eosinophilic cytoplasm are not distinguishing features since they may be present in sessile serrated adenomas and hyperplastic polyps as well ([Am J Gastroenterol 2012;107:1315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22710576), [Diagn Pathol 2014;9:212](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25421018))
  + Traditional serrated adenomas can be distinguished from sessile serrated adenomas and hyperplastic polyps by their low [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) staining, specifically within the ectopic crypts ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:21](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18162766))
* [Tubulovillous adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumortvadenoma.html):
  + Definitive dysplastic nuclei, no eosinophilic cytoplasm, no serrated architecture or ectopic crypts
  + Tubulovillous adenomas often have a component resembling traditional serrated adenoma, with significance unclear

Board review style question #1

Which mutation is most common in traditional serrated adenomas?

1. *BRAF* mutation
2. *CDKN2A* mutation
3. *KRAS* mutation
4. *TP53* mutation

Board review style answer #1

**A**. *BRAF* and *KRAS* mutations in traditional serrated adenomas are almost mutually exclusive with approximately 67% having *BRAF* mutations and 22% with *KRAS* mutations. *BRAF* mutated traditional serrated adenomas are more likely to be CpG island methylator phenotype high. *TP53* mutation and *CDKN2A* silencing by methylation are seen in areas with conventional dysplasia ([Mod Pathol 2015;28:414](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25216220)).
