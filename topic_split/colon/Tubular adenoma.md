Definition / general

* Neoplastic colon polyp with at least low grade dysplasia
* Precursor to invasive adenocarcinoma

Essential features

* Dysplastic nuclei (elongated and hyperchromatic pseudostratification)
* < 25% villous component

Terminology

* The word adenoma should not be used on its own, to avoid confusion with [sessile serrated adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorsessileserrated.html) and [traditional serrated adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumortraditionalserratedadenoma.html)
* > 25% villous component indicates [tubulovillous adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumortvadenoma.html) or [villous adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumortvadenoma.html)

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [K63.5](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/K00-K95/K55-K64/K63-/K63.5) - colon polyp
* ICD-10: [D12.6](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D12-/D12.6) - adenomatous polyp

Epidemiology

* Incidence increases with age; commonly encountered in patients over 50
* Estimated that 25% of men and 15% of women undergoing colonoscopy are found to have at least 1 adenoma ([N Engl J Med 2006;355:2551](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17167138))
* Men more commonly affected than women ([Gastrointest Endosc 2011;74:135](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21612774))

Sites

* Found more commonly in the left colon and rectum compared with other polyps

Pathophysiology

* Has a well documented adenoma to carcinoma sequence that involves mutations in *KRAS, TP53, APC* and beta catenin ([Cell 1990;61:759](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2188735))
* Activation of KRAS leads to downstream signaling that influences survival, antiapoptosis and proliferation, though this event is typically seen in advanced rather than early adenomas ([J Gastroenterol Hepatol 2012;27:1423](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22694276), [PLoS One 2017 27;12](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28953955))
* Wnt pathway: activation of Wnt pathway leads to beta catenin accumulation in the nucleus and subsequent transcription and cell proliferation
* Mutations include loss of function of *APC* or beta catenin mutations that resist degradation by *APC* ([J Gastroenterol Hepatol 2012;27:1423](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22694276))

Etiology

* Decreased risk of conventional colorectal adenomas is associated with
  + Increased dietary fiber intake ([Lancet 2003;361:1491](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12737857))
  + Fruit consumption in women ([Cancer Res 2006;66:3942](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16585224))
  + Folate intake ([J Nutr 2005;135:2468](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16177214))

Clinical features

* < 1 cm: usually asymptomatic and detected by screening colonoscopy
* > 1 cm: may bleed, lead to iron deficiency anemia, obstruction; increased risk of progressing to carcinoma
* Mostly left sided in spontaneous polyps and in [familial adenomatous polyposis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorFAP.html); right sided in [Lynch syndrome](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorlynch.html) ([Gut 2002;50:382](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11839719))

Diagnosis

* Diagnosis is made by histologic confirmation

Case reports

* 36 year old woman with intussusception due to adenoma ([Asian J Endosc Surg 2013;6:311](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24308592))
* 52 year old man with tubular adenoma with coexisting multifocal MALT lymphoma ([Int J Colorectal Dis 2011;26:1221](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21188391))
* 53 year old man with infectious endocarditis from *E. faecalis* associated with tubular adenoma ([Case Rep Infect Dis 2017;2017:3095031](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28848681))
* 64 year old woman with signet ring cell carcinoma metastasizing to a tubular adenoma ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2003;127:1509](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14567750))
* 85 year old woman with osseous metaplasia in adenoma ([J Clin Pathol 2005;58:220](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15677548))

Treatment

* Colonoscopy is diagnostic and potentially curative (via polypectomy)
* High grade dysplasia and large polyps may warrant endoscopic mucosal resection or partial colectomy

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Polypoid colonic mucosa covered with dysplastic epithelium comprised of hyperchromatic, elongated nuclei arranged in a pseudostratified manner
* Dysplasia is typically low grade but may also be high grade, with architectural (cribriforming, luminal necrosis) and cytologic changes (vesicular chromatin, nucleoli, loss of basal polarity)
* Abrupt transition from normal to dysplastic mucosa is commonly present
* Variable amounts of mucin loss
* Metaplasia may be present: osseous, squamous or Paneth cells ([J Clin Pathol 2005;58:220](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15677548), [J Surg Oncol 1984;26:130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6738058))
* Pseudoinvasion can mimic progression to adenocarcinoma but displaced glands are benign and surrounded by lamina propria and often hemosiderin ([Mod Pathol 2015;28:S88](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25560603))
* May rarely show clear cell features ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:1344](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20697252))

Positive stains

* [BCL2](https://pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html) (almost all cases), increased [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html) (in atypical areas)

Negative stains

* [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) (usually)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* 33% are aneuploid
* Depending on the villous component, 2 types of tubular adenomas can be identified ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:212](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21263241)):
  + TA1: less than 1% villous component, lower rate of p53 overexpression, *KRAS* mutation and *MGMT* loss
  + TA2: 1 - 20% villous component, higher rate of *TP53* and *KRAS* mutation and *MGMT* loss
* *KRAS* mutations may account for up to 60% of mutations in tubular adenomas ([J Gastroenterol Hepatol 2012;27:1423](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22694276))

Sample pathology report

* Colon, splenic flexure, polypectomy:
  + Tubular adenoma

Differential diagnosis

* [Inflammatory polyp](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorinflammatory.html):
  + Nuclei can become elongated like a tubular adenoma but nuclei are typically vesicular with round borders
* [Tubulovillous adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumortvadenoma.html), [villous adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumortvadenoma.html):
  + Surface architecture has > 25% villous component

Board review style question #1

Which of the following gene mutations is implicated in a majority of tubular adenomas?

1. *ALK- BAP1
   - KRAS
   - MDM2*

Board review style answer #1

**C**. *KRAS* mutations are implicated in up to 60% of adenomas and adenocarcinomas. *BAP1* mutations are present in a subset of melanocytic tumors and mesotheliomas. *MDM2* is amplified in liposarcoma and some of its variants. *ALK* mutations can be seen in inflammatory myofibroblastic tumor, anaplastic large cell lymphoma and a subset of lung adenocarcinomas.
