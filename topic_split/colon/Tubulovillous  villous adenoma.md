Definition / general

* Advanced precursor lesion of colorectal cancer ([Nat Rev Dis Primers 2015;1:15065](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27189416))
* Tubulovillous adenoma: 20 - 80% villosity
* Villous adenoma: > 80% villosity ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:212](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21263241))

Essential features

* Recognized as the precursor lesion of chromosomal unstable colorectal cancer ([Pathologica 2021;113:218](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34294939))
* Low grade dysplasia is an intrinsic feature
* Associated with oncogene *KRAS* mutation in 50% of cases

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [D12.6](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D12-/D12.6) - benign neoplasm of colon, unspecified

Sites

* More frequent in distal colorectum than in proximal colon (68.4% versus 31.6%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:212](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21263241))

Pathophysiology

* Conventional adenoma carcinoma sequence with oncogene (*KRAS*) activation and tumor suppressor (*APC*, *SMAD4* and *TP53*) inactivation
* Associated with chromosomal unstable colorectal cancer ([Cancer Biol Med 2016;13:120](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27144067))

Clinical features

* Mean age of 62 years ([Eur J Cancer 2005;41:416](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15691642))
* M:F = 1.6:1
* Though most cases are asymptomatic, some present with a history of rectal bleeding ([StatPearls: Villous Adenoma [Accessed 31 May 2022]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29262150))

Diagnosis

* Screening or surveillance colonoscopy identifies and removes lesion, followed by tissue diagnosis

Prognostic factors

* Villous or tubulovillous histology is associated with increased risk of colorectal neoplasia: 16.8% versus 9.7% compared with tubular adenomas
* Increased risk of malignant transformation in cases with high grade dysplasia (↑ 1.77x) ([Gastroenterology 2012;143:844](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22763141))
* Risk of recurrence with malignancy after excision is associated with piecemeal endoscopic resection ([Surg Endosc 2021;35:2500](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32472496))

Case reports

* 62 year old woman presented with rectal bleeding and fleshy mass protruding from the anal canal ([World J Surg Oncol 2019;17:109](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31238922))
* 69 year old man with acute appendicitis ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2019;61:60](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31349156))
* 78 year old woman with tubulovillous adenoma of the cecum with squamous metaplasia ([Int J Surg Pathol 2009;17:340](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18701516))

Treatment

* Endoscopic resection
* When possible, en bloc resection should be the goal for the management ([Surg Endosc 2021;35:2500](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32472496))

Gross description

* Pedunculated or sessile polypoid lesions with macroscopic finger-like projections

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Low grade dysplasia is a constituent feature
* Epithelial, finger-like projections, away from the muscularis mucosae, formed by fibrovascular cores lined by dysplastic epithelium (villous architecture)
  + Percentage of villosity defines diagnostic terminology
  + 20 - 80% = tubulovillous adenoma
  + > 80% = villous adenoma
* Crowded pseudostratification of cells with elongated nuclei occupying the basal half of the cytoplasm
* Pleomorphism and atypical mitoses should be absent or minimally present
* Mitotic activity and minimal loss of cell polarity are allowed
* Architecturally, the crypts should maintain a resemblance to normal colon, without significant crowding, cribriform or complex forms
* Squamous metaplasia as solid nests of squamous cells in direct continuity with adenomatous glands may be seen in about 0.4% of colorectal adenomas ([Histopathology 2021;78:348](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32981102))
* Paneth cell metaplasia is a frequent finding (17 - 23%), especially in proximal locations; osseous metaplasia has been reported ([Histopathology 2021;78:348](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32981102))
* Neuroendocrine metaplasia refers to the presence of scattered foci of neuroendocrine cells that lack significant nuclear atypia, mitotic activity or necrosis; these cells comprise Ann Diagn Pathol 2019;42:69)
* Clusters of neuroendocrine metaplasia are usually Histopathology 2021;78:348)
* Ectopic crypt foci are a frequent finding ([J Clin Pathol 2016;69:1063](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27281826))
* High grade dysplasia can be seen (carcinoma in situ or intraepithelial / intramucosal carcinoma are not recommended terms)
  + Increased nucleus to cytoplasm ratio
  + Significant loss of cell polarity: nuclear stratification through the entire thickness of the epithelium
  + Round nuclei with open appearing chromatin and increasingly prominent nucleoli
  + Significant pleomorphism and atypical mitoses
  + Abnormal architecture includes cribriform structures with back to back glands, prominent glandular budding and intraluminal papillary tufting ([StatPearls: Villous Adenoma [Accessed 31 May 2022]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29262150))
* Poor interobserver agreement for assessment of the villous component and high grade dysplasia have been demonstrated ([Am J Surg Pathol 2013;37:427](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23348206))

Positive stains

* [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html): positive in 95% of cases ([Dig Dis Sci 2005;50:1741](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16133982))
* [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html) ([Gut 2002;51:184](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12117877))
* [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html): more frequent and intense in adenomas with high grade dysplasia (50%) than in those with low grade dysplasia (28%) ([Am J Gastroenterol 1998;93:980](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9647033))
* [Beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html): cytoplasmic and nuclear immunoreactivity in areas of intestinal metaplasia ([Histopathology 2021;78:348](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32981102))
* [CK5/6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5and6.html) and [cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html) overexpression in areas of squamous metaplasia ([Histopathology 2021;78:348](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32981102))

Negative stains

* [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html): usually negative but may be positive in approximately 5% of cases ([Dig Dis Sci 2005;50:1741](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16133982))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* *KRAS* mutations in 52% ([Pathol Oncol Res 2012;18:1077](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22729813))
* Even subtle villous change in tubular adenoma is associated with more frequent *KRAS* and *P53* mutations ([Nat Rev Dis Primers 2015;1:15065](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27189416))
* Routine screening of mismatch repair proteins (MMR) in polyps is not effective in identifying Lynch syndrome carriers ([Clin Colorectal Cancer 2017;16:173](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28242162))
* Most cases associated with Lynch syndrome show loss of MMR protein expression, especially when high grade dysplasia in present ([Mod Pathol 2012;25:722](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22322191))

Sample pathology report

* Colon, polyp at 12 cm, endoscopic polypectomy:
  + Tubulovillous adenoma with high grade dysplasia
* Colon, polyp at 14 cm, endoscopic polypectomy:
  + Villous adenoma

Differential diagnosis

* [Traditional serrated adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumortraditionalserratedadenoma.html):
  + Exophytic tubulovillous or villous polypoid architecture with at least 2 of 3 constituent features ([J Clin Pathol 2016;69:6](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26553935), [BMJ Open Gastroenterol 2019;6:e000317](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31413858)):
    - Striking granular eosinophilic cytoplasm; prominent cytoplasmic eosinophilia that occupies > 50% of tubulovillous / villous adenoma is uncommon ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:212](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21263241))
    - Presence of ectopic crypt foci: more extensive small and short crypts with no relation to the underlying muscularis mucosae ([Histopathology 2015;66:308](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25257227))
    - Luminal serration is defined as deep clefts and slit-like spaces that lead to broad luminal fronds imparting a mushroom-like appearance (not present in tubulovillous and villous adenoma) ([Histopathology 2015;66:308](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25257227))
* [Tubular adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumortubularadenoma.html):
  + Adenomatous polyp with disorganized glands (tubular appearance) and flat surface
  + May show some villous component, representing Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:212)
* [Adenoma-like adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorvillousadenocarcinoma.html):
  + Invasive carcinoma with architectural and cytologic features resembling villous adenoma; differential diagnosis with villous adenoma may be a challenge
  + Finding of epithelial islands surrounded by desmoplastic stroma favors this diagnosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1460](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15489649))
