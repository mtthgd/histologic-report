Definition / general

* Variant familial polyposis syndrome characterized by either [Familial Adenomatous Polyposis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorFAP.html) (FAP) or
  [Lynch Syndrome](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorlynch.html) (LS), along with central nervous system tumors
  ([N Engl J Med 1995;332:839](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7661930))

Essential features

* Turcot syndrome encompasses either:
  + Patients with an [APC mutation](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorAPCgene.html): FAP+ usually [medulloblastoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumormedulloblastoma.html)
  + Patients with a mismatch repair gene mutation: Lynch Syndrome + usually [glioblastoma multiforme](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorglioblastoma.html)

Terminology

* Pronounced with silent second t (Turcot was French Canadian)

Clinical features

* Same as FAP or Lynch Syndrome, but additionally with central nervous system tumor (as above)

Case reports

* 11 year old boy with colonic adenocarcinoma and glioblastoma multiforme ([Case Rep Oncol Med 2012;2012:720273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23119205))
* 49 year old man with colon carcinoma and astrocytoma ([Neurol Med Chir (Tokyo) 2004;44:124](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15095965))

Differential diagnosis

* Constitutional mismatch repair deficiency syndrome: biallelic mismatch repair gene mutation; patients develop brain tumors, hematopoietic malignancies and Lynch syndrome associated tumors ([Haematologica 2010;95:699](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20442441))
