Definition / general

* Means inflammation of cecum ([eMedicine](http://emedicine.medscape.com/article/375779-overview)); a 19th century term for severe acute/chronic inflammation of cecum/appendix, probably due to untreated acute appendicitis
* Currently, represents a life threatening acute inflammatory destruction of cecal mucosa in neutropenic patients, possibly due to impaired mucosal immunity and compromised blood flow; also associated with secondary bacteria and fungal overgrowth but minimal active inflammation (due to neutropenia)
* Also called hemorrhagic necrosis of bowel wall, neutropenic colitis
* Often leukemia or lymphoma patients after immunosuppressive therapy
* Mechanism (not known), but probably due to a combination of ischemia, infection (especially with cytomegalovirus), mucosal hemorrhage, and perhaps neoplasia

Clinical features

* Present with fever, watery or bloody diarrhea, and abdominal pain, may be localized in right lower quadrant ([Radiographics 2000;20:399](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10715339))
* May cause perforation and require surgery
* Severity in childhood cancer is related to duration of neutropenia and presence of fever or abdominal tenderness ([Cancer 2005;104:380](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15952190))

Diagnosis

* Usually diagnosed by CT scan due to thickening of cecal wall

Prognostic factors

* Poor prognosis overall with death in up to 50% ([Rev Esp Enferm Dig 2003;95:30](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12760728))

Case reports

* 73 year old man with small cell lung carcinoma and typhilitis after chemotherapy with irinotecan and cisplatin
  ([Tuberc Respir Dis (Seoul) 2012;73:288](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23236322))
* Typhlitis in non-small cell lung cancer
  ([Lung Cancer 2009;65:251](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19346026))

Gross description

* Inflamed cecum and proximal right colon with hemorrhage and mucosal edema

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Adherent fibrinopurulent exudate over necrotic and edematous mucosa
