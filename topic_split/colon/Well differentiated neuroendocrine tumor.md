Definition / general

* Well differentiated tumors demonstrating morphological and immunohistochemical neuroendocrine differentiation

Essential features

* May be indolent and incidental or present with widespread metastases and have aggressive clinical course
* The term neuroendocrine tumor (NET) comprises WHO G1, G2 and rare G3 lesions (depending on the proliferative activity)

Terminology

* Previously termed carcinoid tumor ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2016;140:437](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27128301))
* According to the 2017 WHO classification, the term MiNEN (mixed neuroendocrine neoplasm) comprises mixed neuroendocrine and nonneuroendocrine proliferations ([World J Gastrointest Oncol 2020;12:791](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32879660))
  + The nonneuroendocrine component may be adenocarcinoma, squamous cell carcinoma, among others
  + Each component must be ≥ 30% to fall into the category of MiNEN

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [D3A.8](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D3A-D3A/D3A-/D3A.8) - other benign neuroendocrine tumors

Epidemiology

* Incidence of colorectal neuroendocrine tumors has been continuously increasing over the past few decades (incidence: 1.04 per 100,000 persons) ([JAMA Oncol 2017;3:1335](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28448665))
* Colorectal neuroendocrine tumors comprise roughly 30% of neuroendocrine tumors in the digestive system ([Cancer 2003;97:934](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12569593))
* According to the WHO classification (5th edition), there is a slight male predominance and the median age of presentation is 56 years for rectal tumors and 65 years for colonic tumors

Sites

* In the large intestine (excluding the appendix), the most frequent location is the rectum (34% of all gastrointestinal neuroendocrine tumors) ([Ann Oncol 2013;24:3040](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24050954))
* In the colon, neuroendocrine tumors are more frequent in the cecum (69.6%), followed by sigmoid (13.0%), ascending colon (13.0%) and transverse colon (4.3%) ([Arq Gastroenterol 2009;46:288](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20232008))
* Colon proper is the least common site for intestinal well differentiated neuroendocrine tumors; around 7.5% of all neuroendocrine tumors are of colonic origin ([Surg Oncol Clin N Am 2020;29:267](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32151360))

Pathophysiology

* Colonic NETs arise from Kulchitsky cells or enterochromaffin cells located within the crypts of Lieburkuhn of the colon ([World J Gastrointest Oncol 2020;12:791](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32879660))
* Rare cases are familial ([Tech Coloproctol 2006;10:143](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16773283))

Etiology

* Etiology remains unknown

Clinical features

* Patients with colonic neuroendocrine tumors may present with pain, bleeding, altered bowel habits, weight loss, anorexia or even bowel obstruction ([Best Pract Res Clin Gastroenterol 2012;26:775](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23582918))
* Carcinoid syndrome occurs in patients with liver metastasis
* Prognosis largely depends on the grade and stage of the tumor; median overall survival ranges from 22 months in G1 - G2 tumors to 12 months in G3 tumors according to the WHO classification (5th edition)
* Increased risk of lymph node metastasis according to size: around 1% in tumors < 10 mm, 23% in tumors from 10 - 20 mm and 50% in tumors > 20 mm ([J Korean Soc Coloproctol 2011;27:17](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21431092))
* G3 colorectal neuroendocrine tumors show an intermediate behavior between G2 neuroendocrine tumors and neuroendocrine carcinomas ([Cancer 2014;120:2814](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24771552))

Diagnosis

* > 50% are discovered during routine colonoscopy for colorectal cancer screening ([Best Pract Res Clin Gastroenterol 2012;26:775](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23582918))
* Rectal tumors may present with symptoms such as bleeding or change in bowel habits ([Surg Oncol Clin N Am 2020;29:267](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32151360))

Laboratory

* Since few colorectal neuroendocrine tumors produce serotonin, routine analysis of plasma or urinary 5-hydroxyindoleacetic acid (5-HIAA) is not useful ([Neuroendocrinology 2012;95:88](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22261972), [PLoS One 2017;12:e0188876](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29232390))
* Serum chromogranin A (CgA) is the most common biomarker used to assess the bulk of disease and monitor treatment; it is elevated in both functioning and nonfunctioning neuroendocrine tumors ([Neuroendocrinology 2012;95:88](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22261972), [World J Gastrointest Oncol 2020;12:791](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32879660))

Prognostic factors

* Rectal neuroendocrine tumors are commonly small and generally low to intermediate grade (G1 or G2), whereas colonic tumors are often aggressive, poorly differentiated and higher grade (G3) ([Neuroendocrinology 2016;103:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26730835))
* Colorectal tumors < 10 mm have a low rate (1.95%) of lymph node metastasis ([J Korean Soc Coloproctol 2011;27:17](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21431092), [Gut 2007;56:863](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17213340))
* Lymphatic invasion and WHO grade are independent predictive factors of lymph node metastasis ([Front Oncol 2016;6:173](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27486567), [Sci Rep 2019;9:2404](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30787304), [Ann Surg Oncol 2020;27:855](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31701298))
* Number of positive locoregional lymph nodes is an independent prognostic factor of survival ([Ann Surg Oncol 2019;26:2028](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30927196))
* HES77 expression is also linked to worse prognosis ([Anticancer Res 2015;35:3767](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26124320))

Case reports

* 47 year old man with a polypoid mass in the right colic flexure ([Pan Afr Med J 2021;40:243](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35233263))
* 57 year old man with perforated colonic tumor with liver metastasis ([Am J Case Rep 2019;20:920](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31249283))
* 64 year old man presented with carcinoid syndrome ([Ann Ital Chir 2017;6:S2239253X17027694](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29176078))
* 85 year old woman presented with mixed neuroendocrine and nonneuroendocrine neoplasms ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2020;76:125](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33032042))

Treatment

* For lesions < 10 mm with no involvement of muscularis propria, transanal endoscopic microsurgery is usually sufficient ([J Laparoendosc Adv Surg Tech A 2006;16:435](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17004864), [Neuroendocrinology 2016;103:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26730835))
* Larger or high risk tumors may require surgery ([Arch Surg 2008;143:471](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18490556))
* Positive margins may not increase risk of recurrence ([Am Surg 2011;77:198](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21337880))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Well differentiated neuroendocrine pattern:
  + Monotonous regular cells with round or oval nuclei with salt and pepper chromatin and moderate eosinophilic granular cytoplasm
  + Organoid architecture: tumor cells arranged in nests, trabecular or insular pattern ([Oncologist 2016;21:1191](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27401895))
* In some cases, nuclear pleomorphism may be encountered (endocrine atypia) but is not associated with tumor aggressiveness ([Oncologist 2016;21:1191](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27401895))
* Rarely mucin secretion
* Grading scheme is based on the proliferative rate (mitoses and Ki67 index) as follows:
  + Low grade or grade 1 (G1): mitoses < 2 per 2 mm2 and Ki67 index < 3%
  + Intermediate grade or grade 2 (G2): mitoses 2 - 20 per 2 mm2 or Ki67 index 3 - 20%
  + High grade or grade 3 (G3): mitoses > 20 per 2 mm2 or Ki67 index > 20%
* Mitotic count should be evaluated in a 2 mm2 hotspot area (roughly equivalent to 10 high power fields with a 40x objective lens) ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2016;140:437](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27128301))
* Ki67 index should be estimated in ≥ 500 cells in the hotspot regions ([Oncologist 2016;21:1191](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27401895))
* If there is any discrepancy between mitotic index and Ki67 index, the higher should be considered for the classification ([World J Gastrointest Oncol 2020;12:791](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32879660))

Positive stains

* [Synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html)
* [Chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html) may occasionally be negative in rectal tumors ([Neuroendocrinology 2017;105:196](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28190015))
* [CD56 / NCAM1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html), [Leu7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd57.html) and [neuron specific enolase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsNSE.html) are not recommended because of their low specificity ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:300](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20118772))
* [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html) may be useful in determining the site of origin as it would point to the intestine or pancreas ([Oncologist 2016;21:1191](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27401895), [Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:300](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20118772))
* [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html) is positive in the majority of rectal and pancreatic well differentiated neuroendocrine tumors ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:723](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20414099))
* [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html)
* There are no specific recommendations for the study of the hormone secretion profile, however, [somatostatin receptor 2 (SSTR2)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsSSTR2A.html) immunohistochemical expression has been shown to be an independent prognostic marker ([Eur J Nucl Med Mol Imaging 2017;44:468](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27539020))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* There is very limited data about molecular alterations in colorectal neuroendocrine tumors; most molecular analyses have been performed in pancreatic and small intestine tumors ([Ann Endocrinol (Paris) 2019;80:153](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31072588), [Mod Pathol 2018;31:1770](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30140036))

Sample pathology report

* Ascending colon mass, endoscopic biopsy:
  + Well differentiated neuroendocrine tumor, WHO grade 2 (G2), 7 mm (see comment)
  + Comment: There are 2 mitotic figures in 2 mm2 and the Ki67 proliferation index is approximately 5%, consistent with G2 in this material.

Differential diagnosis

* [Neuroendocrine carcinoma (NEC)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorneuroendocrinecarcinoma.html):
  + High grade tumors with poorly differentiated morphology
  + Atypical small or large cells organized in diffuse sheets with little nesting
  + Apoptotic bodies and necrosis are usually observed
  + They are high grade by definition (mitoses > 20 per 10 high power fields or [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) > 20%); however, the [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) index is usually > 50% ([Neuroendocrinology 2016;103:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26730835))
  + Remember that there are some G3 neuroendocrine tumors; poorly differentiated morphology is the main feature to distinguish neuroendocrine carcinomas from G3 neuroendocrine tumors
  + [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html) overexpression, loss of [Rb](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsrb.html) expression and abnormal [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) expression are seen more frequently in neuroendocrine carcinomas and may be useful in the differential diagnosis with well differentiated neuroendocrine tumors ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2016;140:437](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27128301))
