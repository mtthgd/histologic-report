Definition / general

* Localized mass forming collection of lipid bearing histiocytes

Essential features

* Rare but typically incidental and benign polypoid collection of histiocytes

Terminology

* Also called xanthomatous polyp, xanthogranulomatous inflammation, xanthelasma

Epidemiology

* Very rare in colon - stomach is most common gastrointestinal site

Sites

* Colonic examples almost always rectosigmoid

Etiology

* May be due to prior injury

Clinical features

* Colon polyp
* May clinically resemble carcinoma
* May cause intestinal obstruction if more extensive and involving deeper layers

Case reports

* 38 year old woman with xanthogranulomatous inflammation presenting as a submucosal mass of the sigmoid colon ([Pathol Int 2005;55:440](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15982221))
* 68 year old man with xanthoma disseminatum with large plaques confined to the back, pulmonary involvement and multiple intestinal xanthomas ([Dermatology 2004;208:164](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15057010))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Collection of foamy cells without mucin, typically limited to lamina propria
* Associated with surface hyperplastic change ([APMIS 2004;112:3](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14961968))

Positive stains

* [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html)

Negative stains

* [PAS-diastase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html), [Alcian blue](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalcianblue.html) (pH 2.5 or 1.0), [mucicarmine](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmucins.html)

Electron microscopy description

* Electron dense globules

Sample pathology report

* Descending colon, polypectomy:
  + Xanthoma

Differential diagnosis

* **Muciphages**:
  + Present in up to 40% of rectal biopsies but usually contain mucin ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:1009](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10895824))
