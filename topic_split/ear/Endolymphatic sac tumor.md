Definition / general

* Uncommon, associated with von Hippel-Lindau syndrome (11% have these tumors) and female adnexal tumor of presumed wolffian origin ([Am J Surg Pathol 1994;18:1254](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7977949)); also somatic mutations of VHL gene in non VHL patients ([Cancer Res 2000;60:5963](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11085513))
* Most arise within intraosseous portion of the endolymphatic duct / sac, with precursor lesions present in VHL patients ([Cancer Res 2005;65:10847](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16322231))
* Median age 30s, range of 11 - 71 years
* **Symptoms:** early sensorineural hearing loss, tinnitus and episodic vertigo

Terminology

* The 2017 WHO now separates aggressive papillary tumor from endolymphatic sac tumor
* Also called papillary adenoma of endolymphatic sac / temporal bone, adenocarcinoma of temporal bone / mastoid, low grade adenocarcinoma of probable endolymphatic sac origin, Heffner tumor

Radiology description

* Tumor in posterior medial petrous ridge of temporal bone (site of endolymphatic sac)

Case reports

* 20 year old woman with endolymphatic sac tumor associated with von Hippel-Lindau disease ([Mod Path 2001;14:727](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11455007))
* 32 year old woman with papillary neoplasm of the endolymphatic sac associated with Hippel-Lindau disease ([J Clin Pathol 1994;47:959](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7962613))
* 42 year old woman with endolymphatic sac tumor associated with von Hippel-Lindau disease ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2003;127:1387](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14521446))
* 77 year old man with aspirated cyst ([Mod Pathol 2001;14:920](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11557790))
* Patient with mutation of von Hippel-Lindau tumor suppressor gene in a sporadic endolymphatic sac tumor ([Hum Pathol 2001;32:1272](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11727270))
* Patients with low grade papillary adenomatous tumors of the temporal bone ([Mod Pathol 1995;8:603](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8532691))

Treatment

* Radical surgery including mastoidectomy and temporal bone resection with possible loss of cranial nerves
* Tumor grows slowly with only one reported metastasis ([J Neurosurg Spine 2005;3:68](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16122027)) but may recur with inadequate excision
* Is infiltrative, destructive and may cause death
* Bleeds profusely at surgery

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Simple papillary structures composed of single layer of columnar to cuboidal epithelium, often with distinct cell boundaries
* May have apparent myoepithelial layer that actually is flattened stroma; epithelial cells have pale-clear cytoplasm, uniform central or luminal nuclei
* Often granulation tissue reaction with small vascular spaces and mixed inflammatory infiltrate is present next to tumor cells
* Occasional thyroid like hypercellular areas with cystic glandular spaces containing colloid like material
* May have areas of recent hemorrhage with cholesterol clefts
* Minimal pleomorphism
* No / rare mitotic figures or necrosis
* Resembles choroid plexus papilloma

Cytology description

* Rare epithelial cell clusters, some with papillary features
* Foamy macrophages
* Epithelial cells have eosinophilic and focally vacuolated cytoplasm, some with pigmented granules resembling hemosiderin, well defined cell borders, bland nuclei

Positive stains

* [Keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) (diffuse), [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html)+ diastase resistant intracytoplasmic material (glycogen), [iron](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsiron.html)
* Also **vascular endothelial growth factor** (as with other VHL+ tumors); variable [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html), [NSE](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsNSE.html), [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html), [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html) and [Leu7/CD57](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd57.html)

Negative stains

* [Mucin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmucins.html), [thyroglobulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsthyroglobulin.html), [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html), [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html), [inhibin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html)

Electron microscopy description

* Intercellular junctional complexes, microvilli, basement membrane, rough ER, glycogen, secretory granules

Differential diagnosis

* [Choroid plexus papilloma-carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorchoroidplexuspapillomas.html): originates within brain ventricles, [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)+
* [Jugulotympanic paraganglioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/earjugulotympanicparaganglioma.html): vascular, but not papillary cystic, "zellballen", [keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) negative
* [Metastatic renal cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantrcc.html): [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)-, [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html)-, [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html)-, kidney tumor present on CT
* [Metastatic thyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidpapillary.html): [thyroglobulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsthyroglobulin.html)+, characteristic nuclear features
* [Middle ear adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/earmiddleearadenoma.html): not papillary, doesn't invade or destroy bone
