Definition / general

* Rare, benign glandular neoplasm originating from middle ear mucosa
* First described in 1976 ([Laryngoscope 1976;86:1123](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/950854), [Clin Otolaryngol Allied Sci 1976;1:17](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1031337))
* Neuroendocrine and epithelial differentiation; also called carcinoid tumor or amphicrine tumor ([Ultrastruct Pathol 2001;25:73](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11297323))
* Rosai believes they form a continuum with carcinoid tumor and could be considered adenocarcinoid tumors
* No gender preference, usually 20's to 40's, but wide age range
* Affects all sites in middle ear
* Occasionally perforates tympanic membrane and extends into external auditory canal
* Not associated with chronic otitis media or cholesteatoma
* Excellent prognosis; rarely is locally aggressive, invades vital structures, has regional metastases ([Laryngoscope 2005;115:1660](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16148713)) or causes death

Case reports

* 21 year old man and 34 year old woman with carcinoid tumors of the middle ear ([Am J Clin Pathol 1987;87:592](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3578133))
* 27 year old man and 42 year old woman with middle ear adenoma ([J Clin Pathol 1991;44:652](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1890199))
* 63 year old woman with 1 cm middle ear mass ([Case #376](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case376.htm))

Treatment

* Complete surgical excision
* Mastoidectomy may be necessary for large lesions
* Recurs with inadequate excision

Gross description

* Gray-white to red-brown, firm/rubbery masses
* Relatively well circumscribed
* Not encapsulated
* No hemorrhage, mean 0.8 cm

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Variable patterns (sheets, solid, trabecular, cystic, cribriform, glandular, NOT papillary) of glands or tubules composed of uniform single layer of cuboidal or columnar cells with variable eosinophilic cytoplasm and round/oval hyperchromatic nuclei, eccentric nucleoli (if present)
* May appear plasmacytoid, may have significant pleomorphism
* May produce PAS+ mucin
* Sparse fibrous or myxoid stroma
* No/rare mitotic figures, no necrosis
* May have neuroendocrine differentiation morphologically and immunohistochemically

Positive stains

* Keratin (90%), CK7 (90%), chromogranin (88%), CAM5.2 (81%), mucin (intraluminal), lysozyme, neuron specific enolase (50%), synaptophysin (31%), serotonin (25%), S100 (15%), CK20 (6%, focal)

Negative stains

* Actin

Electron microscopy description

* Desmosomes and microvilli
* Often membrane bound dense core granules
* May have glandular differentiation ([Am J Clin Pathol 1985;84:541](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4036884))

Differential diagnosis

* [Acoustic neuroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/earvestibularschwannoma.html)
* Glandular metaplasia: focal or haphazard in background of chronic otitis media
* [Jugulotympanic paraganglioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/earjugulotympanicparaganglioma.html): zellballen surrounded by S100+ sustentacular cells
* [Meningioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/earmeningioma.html)
* [Middle ear adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/earmeningioma.html): marked pleomorphism, mitotic activity, necrosis, invasion of bone and soft tissue
