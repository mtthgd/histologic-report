Definition / general

* Primary tumor of esophagus morphologically and immunohistochemically identical to adenoid cystic carcinoma of salivary glands
* Believed to arise from esophageal glands

Epidemiology

* Very rare, generally middle age, more common in females

Prognostic factors

* Generally favorable prognosis with excellent survival
* Superior to squamous cell carcinoma and usual adenocarcinoma of the esophagus

Case reports

* 59 year old woman
  ([Int J Clin Pract 2005;59:1101](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16115189))
* 70 year old woman
  ([Surg Today 1997;27:238](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9068105))

Gross description

* Generally well circumscribed nodule in submucosa

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Identical to tumor in [salivary gland](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsadenoidcystic.html)
* Inner ductal type epithelium and outer modified myoepithlial cells form solid nests or cribriform spaces containing balls of glyocosaminoglycans and basement membrane material

Adenoid cystic carcinoma

Positive stains

* Ductal epithelium is strongly [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) and [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html)+, while modified myoepithelial cells are weakly cytokeratin positive with strong [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsactin.html) and [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html) positivity in modified myoepithelial cells

Differential diagnosis

* [Basaloid squamous cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophagusbasaloidSCC.html)
  + More pleomorphic with greater mitotic activity
  + Does not form true lumina and lacks CEA staining
  + Generally CK19+, not seen in adenoid cystic carcinoma
