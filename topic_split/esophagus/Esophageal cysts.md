Definition / general

* Rare; mostly developmental unless due to cystic degeneration of tumor
* Simple cysts are epithelial lined; duplication cysts have 2 muscle layers ([eMedicine: Esophageal Cysts [Accessed 14 February 2019]](http://emedicine.medscape.com/article/426589-overview), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 1977;101:136](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/576577))
* Either intramural or attached to outer layers of esophagus
* Often asymptomatic, even if large; may cause obstructive symptoms
* See [Retention cysts](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophaguscysts.html#retention)

Bronchogenic cysts

Definition / general

* Often young women with dysphagia or chest pain during exercise ([Clin Imaging 2006;30:309](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16919550))
* Developmental cysts arise from anomalous budding of foregut bronchial structures; contains cartilage and mucus glands, smooth muscle and ciliated columnar epithelium

Case reports

* 26 year old man with cystic lesion at lower esophagus ([Dig Surg 2006;23:209](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16874000))

Duplication cysts

Definition / general

* Also called gastroenteric cyst, foregut cyst
* Congenital anomaly, usually lower esophagus
* Most are intramural; usually isolated anomaly; however, duplications external to esophageal wall may be associated with vertebral anomalies
* 90% do not communicate with esophagus
* Usually symptomatic causing dysphagia or respiratory difficulty

Case reports

* 22 day old boy ([Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2006;49:396](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17001896))
* 2 infants with respiratory distress ([Pediatr Emerg Care 2005;21:854](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16340764))
* 14 year old boy with fistula to lung ([Eur J Cardiothorac Surg 2000;18:117](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10869950))
* 52 year old woman ([Yonsei Med J 2005;46:859](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16385665))
* 61 year old woman with squamous cell carcinoma ([Br J Radiol 2003;76:343](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12763952))

Treatment

* Surgery (if symptoms) or possibly observation ([Endoscopy 2005;37:870](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16116541))

Inclusion cysts

Definition / general

* Lined by squamocolumnar epithelium, may be ciliated

Retention cysts

Definition / general

* Also called mucocele
* Derive from obstructed submucosal gland ducts
* Small, usually in lower esophagus
* May cause intramural pseudodiverticulosis, with multiple flask-like invaginations into esophageal wall ([Am J Clin Pathol 1976;65:314](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/816192))
* Associated with chronic esophagitis and fibrosis; also surgically isolated segments of esophagus ([Dis Esophagus 2002;15:96](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12060052))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Saccular or flask shaped dilation of submucosal gland excretory ducts; rarely reaches muscularis propria
* In large lesions, muscularis does not accompany the lesion so are not true diverticula
