Definition / general

* Characterized by fibrosis, inflammation, increased collagen and vasculitis
* Can be part of CREST syndrome (**C**alcinosis, **R**aynaud phenomenon, **E**sophageal involvement, **S**clerodactyly, **T**elangiectasia)
* Involves esophagus in 75%+ patients, usually distal 2/3, with aperistalsis and reduced tone of lower esophageal sphincter
* Commonly causes reflux esophagitis ([Semin Arthritis Rheum 2006;36:173](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17045629)), strictures, *Candida* esophagitis, Barrett esophagus ([J Clin Gastroenterol 2006;40:769](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17016130) but similar prevalence of Barrett as other reflux esophagitis patients, [Arthritis Rheum 2005;52:2882](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16142744))

Case reports

* 38 year old man with achalasia ([Dtsch Med Wochenschr 2006;131:1799](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16902902))
* 51 year old woman with CMV esophagitis ([J Clin Rheumatol 2001;7:384](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17039180))

Treatment

* No treatment for underlying disease; proton pump inhibitors for reflux esophagitis, dilation for strictures

Microscopic (histologic) description

* **Resection specimens:** atrophy and replacement fibrosis of inner circular layer of muscularis propria and resulting stenosis; longitudinal layer is usually involved; also submucosal fibrosis, mild inflammation, intimal proliferation of arterioles ([Gut 2006;55:1697](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16527835))
* **Biopsies:** ulcers or erosions resembling reflux esophagitis, *Candida* or Barrett
