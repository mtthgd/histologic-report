Definition / general

* Malignant mesenchymal tumors of the esophagus with variable degrees of differentiation, biologic behavior and prognosis
* Examples
  + [Angiosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueangiosarcoma.html): high grade malignancy of endothelial cells
  + [Ewing sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneewing.html): highly malignant small round blue cell tumor characterized by recurrent chromosomal translocations [t(11;22) *EWSR1-FLI1* or t(21;22) *EWSR1-ERG*] and membranous MIC2 / CD99 overexpression
  + [Fibrosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuefibrosarcoma.html): spindle cell neoplasm of low grade to intermediate grade malignancy
  + [Gastrointestinal stromal tumor (GIST)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophagusGIST.html): mesenchymal tumor of digestive tract, likely originating from multipotential progenitors of interstitial cells of Cajal
  + **Hemangiopericytoma** ([solitary fibrous tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesft.html)): ubiquitous mesenchymal tumor showing uncertain line of differentiation (not true microvascular pericytes)
  + [Kaposi sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuekaposi.html): uncommon, low grade, vascular malignancy caused by Kaposi sarcoma herpesvirus / human herpesvirus 8 (KSHV / HHV8) infection
  + [Leiomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stomachleiomyosarcoma.html): malignant smooth muscle tumor
  + [Liposarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophagusfibrovascularpolyp.html): malignant adipocytic / lipomatous tumor
  + [Malignant peripheral nerve sheath tumor (MPNST)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuempnst.html): malignant tumor arising from peripheral nerve or a neurofibroma or in extraneural soft tissue and showing nerve sheath differentiation
  + [Osteosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneosteosarcomageneral.html): mesenchymal malignancy characterized by neoplastic cells that produce osteoid matrix
  + [Synovial sarcoma (SS)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesynovialsarc.html): malignant mesenchymal tumor showing epithelial differentiation, either overtly (biphasic SS) or by IHC alone (monophasic SS)
  + [Undifferentiated pleomorphic sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuemfhpleo.html): highly malignant pleomorphic neoplasm lacking any specific line of differentiation

Epidemiology

* Extremely rare

Sites

* Likely arise from submucosal or intramural elements

Clinical features

* Asymptomatic mass
* Obstructive features: dysphagia, odynophagia, nausea, vomiting

Diagnosis

* Biopsy or resection with immunohistochemical or molecular ancillary studies

Radiology description

* Highly variable
  + Destructive mass with variable hemorrhage or necrosis
  + Strictures or obstruction

Prognostic factors

* Poor prognostic factors
  + High histologic grade
    - Cytologic atypia, nuclear pleomorphism
    - Necrosis
    - Mitotic activity
* Poor degree of differentiation

Treatment

* Treatment options depend on type of sarcoma and clinical stage
* Treatment modalities include surgery, chemotherapy, radiotherapy

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Highly variable
  + Round blue cell tumors: Ewing sarcoma, small cell variant of osteosarcoma
  + Spindle cell tumors: angiosarcoma, fibrosarcoma, GIST, hemangiopericytoma, Kaposi sarcoma, leiomyosarcoma, MPNST, synovial sarcoma
  + Adipocytic differentiation: liposarcoma
  + Osteoid production: osteosarcoma
  + Undifferentiated: pleomorphic sarcoma

Positive stains

* **Angiosarcoma:** endothelial markers ([factor VIII related antigen](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfactorviii.html), [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html))
* **Ewing sarcoma:** [MIC2 / CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html) (100% sensitive but not specific), [FLI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfli1.html) gene product (90%), [neuron specific enolase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsNSE.html), [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html), [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html)
* **GIST:** [CD117 / c-kit](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html), [DOG1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsDOG1.html), CD34, focal [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), focal [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), focal [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)
* **Hemangiopericytoma:** CD34 (95%), CD99 (70%), [CD57](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd57.html) (50%), focal nuclear [beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html)
* **Kaposi sarcoma:** [HHV8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshhv8.html), factor VIII related antigen, CD34, CD31, FLI1, [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html), **VEGFR3**, [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html)
* **Leiomyosarcoma:** smooth muscle markers (SMA, desmin, [caldesmon](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscaldesmon.html), [calponin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalponin.html))
* **Liposarcoma:** S100 (lipogenic zones); [MDM2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmdm2.html), [CDK4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdk4.html) (well differentiated tumors)
* **MPNST:** S100 (weak / focal in spindled MPNST, diffuse / strong in epithelioid MPNST)
* **Synovial sarcoma:** focal [high molecular weight cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck34be12.html)
* **Undifferentiated pleomorphic sarcoma:** [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html)

Flow cytometry description

* Useful to exclude hematopoietic malignancies

Electron microscopy description

* **Angiosarcoma:** Weibel-Palade bodies
* **Ewing sarcoma:** extensive cytoplasmic glycogen deposits
* **Hemangiopericytoma:** undifferentiated spindle cell or fibroblastic features
* **Leiomyosarcoma:** features of smooth muscle differentiation (thin filaments, pinocytic vesicles, attachment plaques, interrupted external lamina)
* **Liposarcoma:** nonmembrane bound intracytoplasmic lipid droplets of varying sizes / densities
* **MPNST:** tumor cells with external laminae, indicating schwannian differentiation
* **Osteosarcoma:** osteoid matrix comprises nonperiodic fibrils, scattered collagen fibers and hydroxyapatite calcium crystals

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* RT PCR or FISH
  + **Ewing sarcoma:** recurrent chromosomal translocations including t(11;22)(q24;q12) *EWS-FLI1*, t(21;22)(q22;q12) *EWS-ERG*, t(7;22) *EWS-ETV1*, t(17;22) *EWS-E1AF*, t(2;22) *EWS-FEV*
  + **GIST:** mutually exclusive mutations of activating *KIT* (95%) or platelet derived growth factor alpha (*PDGFRA*) receptor tyrosine kinase (5%)
  + **Kaposi sarcoma:** *HHV8* detected by PCR
  + **Leiomyosarcoma:** no consistent genetic events reported
  + **Well differentiated liposarcoma:** giant marker or supernumerary ring chromosomes with amplification of 12q12-15 region, including *MDM2, CDK4* and other genes
  + **Myxoid / round cell liposarcoma:** t(12;16) *DDIT3-TLS* or t(12;22) *DDIT3-EWS* balanced translocations
  + **Synovial sarcoma:** characteristic t(X;18) *SSYT-SSX1 / 2* translocations

Differential diagnosis

* **Benign spindle cell proliferations**, e.g.
  + [Inflammatory fibroid polyp](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stomachinflammatoryfibroid.html): PDGFRA+, CD34+, CD117-, mixed inflammatory infiltrate (especially eosinophils)
  + [Leiomyoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophagusleiomyoma.html): rare atypia, mitotic activity, or necrosis
  + [Ossifying fibromyxoid tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueossifyingfibromyxoid.html): usually bland cytology and peripheral ossification
  + [Schwannoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueschwannoma.html): diffuse S100+, Verocay bodies
* [Lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophaguslymphoma.html): usually CD45 (LCA)+, flow cytometry may be diagnostic
* [Melanoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophagusmelanoma.html): melanoma markers+ (S100, HMB45, MART, MITF, MelanA, SOX10, tyrosinase)
* **Other round blue cell tumors**, e.g.
  + [Alveolar rhabdomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuealvrhabdo.html): desmin+, myogenin+, myoD1+
  + [Desmoplastic small round cell tumor (DSRCT)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuedsrct.html): striking desmoplasia, coexpression of cytokeratin / desmin, WT1+
  + [Glomus tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueglomus.html): round to polygonal cells with scant cytoplasm and marked cellularity uniformity; SMA+, desmin-, S100-
  + [Neuroendocrine (small cell) carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophagussmallcell.html): neuroendocrine markers+ (CD56, chromogranin, synaptophysin)
* **Poorly differentiated** [carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophaguscarcinomageneral.html): focal cytokeratin+
