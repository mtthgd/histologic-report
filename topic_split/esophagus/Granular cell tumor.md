Definition / general

* Also called Abrikosoff tumor, myoblastoma
* Most common site in GI tract for these tumors
* #2 most common stromal tumor of esophagus after leiomyoma

Clinical features

* Usually incidental, in lower esophagus, 90% solitary ([Ann Thorac Surg 1996;62:860](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8784020))
* May cause obstruction if large
* Almost all cases of Schwannian origin
* Relatively more common in women in 40s, African Americans
* May be underdiagnosed on superficial biopsies that lack lamina propria
* **Endoscopy:** sessile, yellowish white, firm, intact epithelium
* 1 - 3% are malignant (locally recur)
  + Associated with rapid growth, > 4 cm, tumor necrosis, increased cellularity, atypia, > 2 mitotic figures/HPF

Case reports

* 39 year old man with coexisting esophageal leiomyomas ([Dig Liver Dis 2004;36:292](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15115343))
* 40 year old woman with coexisting squamous cell carcinoma ([Dis Esophagus 2002;15:88](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12060050))
* 53 year old man with multiple tumors ([J Gastroenterol 2003;38:776](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14505133))
* 59 year old Japanese man with esophageal tumor 7 years after bronchial tumor ([APMIS 2006;114:659](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16948821))

Treatment

* Local excision

Gross description

* Intramural nodule(s), poorly circumscribed, up to 2 cm

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Identical to granular cell tumors elsewhere; sheets or packets of uniform epithelioid cells with abundant eosinophilic granular cytoplasm and small nuclei that interdigitate with overlying epithelium
  + Often pseudoepitheliomatous hyperplasia, which may mimic squamous cell carcinoma on small biopsies ([J Surg Oncol 1980;13:301](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6246310))
* Often involves superficial lamina propria
  + May extend into muscularis propria
