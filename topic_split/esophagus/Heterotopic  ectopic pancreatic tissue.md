Definition / general

* Also called pancreatic heterotopia
* At gastroesophageal junction in 16% of pediatric / young adult patients ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2000;124:1165](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10923077))
* May be congenital and independent of Barrett esophagus ([Am J Surg Pathol 1996;20:1507](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8944044))
* Usually no clinical significance but theoretically may exacerbate reflux esophagitis; rarely exhibits pathologic changes of pancreatic tissue, including ductal carcinoma ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1994;118:568](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8192567)), other malignancy ([Ann Thorac Surg 2000;69:259](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10654526)) or pancreatitis ([Surg Laparosc Endosc Percutan Tech 2005;15:345](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16340566), [Dig Dis Sci 1995;40:2373](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7587817))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Benign appearing pancreatic ducts and acini

Cytology description

* Clusters of benign appearing ducts and small acini mixed with inflammatory cells ([Diagn Cytopathol 2004;31:175](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15349989))

Positive stains

* **Trypsin, chymotrypsin**, **amylase** and **lipase** ([Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:1172](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7573675))
