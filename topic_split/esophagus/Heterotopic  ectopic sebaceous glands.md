Definition / general

* Heterotopic / ectopic sebaceous glands in the esophagus

Essential features

* Incidentally found, small (3 - 4 mm) yellowish plaques with granular or lobulated appearance; the number of lesions varies from single to more than 100
* Mature sebaceous gland with excretory duct within the lamina propria of the esophagus
* Asymptomatic and benign; no need for treatment

Terminology

* Ectopic sebaceous gland
* Heterotopic sebaceous gland

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [K22.8](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/K00-K95/K20-K31/K22-/K22.8) - other specified diseases of esophagus

Epidemiology

* Reported incidence is low:
  + 0.005% ([World J Clin Cases 2014;2:311](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25032211))
  + 0.05% ([Dis Esophagus 2017;30:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26822541))
* Reported age range:
  + 45 - 79 years ([World J Gastroenterol 2015;21:1091](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25632181), [Clin Endosc 2018;51:495](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29478307), [Case Rep Gastroenterol 2012;6:217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22701398), [Pathol Int 1999;49:364](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10365859))
  + Has not been reported in pediatric patients
* Reported median age:
  + 52 years (range 45 - 69 years) ([World J Gastroenterol 2015;21:1091](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25632181))
  + 57.6 years (46 - 71 years) ([World J Clin Cases 2014;2:311](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25032211))
* Reported male to female ratios:
  + 7:1 ([Int J Surg Pathol 2020 Aug 26 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32844680))
  + 5:3 ([World J Gastroenterol 2015;21:1091](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25632181))

Sites

* Middle and lower esophagus ([World J Clin Cases 2014;2:311](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25032211), [Dig Dis Sci 2019;64:2049](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30725294), [Case Rep Gastroenterol 2012;6:217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22701398), [Clin Endosc 2018;51:495](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29478307), [Pathol Int 1999;49:364](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10365859))

Etiology

* Whether heterotopic / ectopic sebaceous glands in the esophagus are the result of congenital anomaly or metaplastic change of squamous epithelium remains unknown ([Pathol Int 1999;49:364](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10365859))
  + Since the esophagus is of endodermal origin, while sebaceous glands are of ectodermal origin, metaplastic change of squamous epithelium is suspected

Clinical features

* Incidentally found, small, yellowish plaques
* Usually asymptomatic

Diagnosis

* Confirmed via biopsy ([Clin Endosc 2018;51:495](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29478307), [Case Rep Gastroenterol 2012;6:217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22701398))

Case reports

* 47 year old man with several yellow plaques and 63 year old man with multiple yellowish patches ([Clin Endosc 2018;51:495](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29478307))
* 56 year old man with more than 100 yellowish plaques measuring 1 - 20 mm in diameter ([Case Rep Gastroenterol 2012;6:217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22701398))
* 78 year old woman with numerous 3 - 4 mm plaques in the middle and distal esophagus ([Dig Dis Sci 2019;64:2049](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30725294))
* 79 year old man with numerous esophageal heterotopic sebaceous glands accompanied by superficial esophageal cancer ([Pathol Int 1999;49:364](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10365859))

Treatment

* No treatment required

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Mature sebaceous glands characterized by large polygonal cells with clear, vacuolated cytoplasm within the lamina propria
* A squamous lined excretory duct may also be present
* Associated hair follicles or other skin appendages are absent

Sample pathology report

* Esophagus, mid, biopsy:
  + Heterotopic / ectopic sebaceous gland
  + Negative for dysplasia or carcinoma

Differential diagnosis

* [Xanthoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticxanthoma.html) ([Clin Endosc 2014;47:358](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25133126)):
  + Grossly yellowish plaques, commonly solitary
  + Aggregates of foamy macrophages beneath the squamous epithelium
* [Glycogenic acanthosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophagusglycogenic.html):
  + Acanthotic squamous epithelium with clear (glycogen containing) cytoplasm
* [Squamous papilloma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophagussquamouspapilloma.html):
  + Wart-like exophytic lesion
  + Papillary proliferation of benign squamous epithelium
* [Heterotopic / ectopic gastric mucosa](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophagusectopicgastric.html):
  + Salmon colored mucosa located just below the upper esophageal sphincter composed of heterotopic / ectopic gastric fundic glands
* [Granular cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophagusgct.html):
  + Small submucosal nodule composed of polygonal cells with small nuclei and abundant eosinophilic granular cytoplasm
  + Positive for [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html) and [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html)
* [Candidiasis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophaguscandidaesophagitis.html):
  + Whitish plaques with histologic evidence of pseudohyphae and yeast forms
* [Well differentiated neuroendocrine tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophaguscarcinoid.html):
  + Extremely rare in the esophagus
  + Positive for neuroendocrine markers
