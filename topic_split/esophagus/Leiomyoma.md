Definition / general

* Most common benign tumor of esophagus ([eMedicine: Esophageal Leiomyoma [Accessed 15 February 2019]](http://emedicine.medscape.com/article/174599-overview), [Surg Clin North Am 1983;63:625](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6310805)), 8% incidence in autopsy studies ([Hum Pathol 1981;12:1006](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7319487))

Clinical features

* Median age 35 years, 2/3 men, usually single, 24% multiple (seedling tumors)
* Usually arises from inner circular muscle; most common in distal esophagus, rarely polypoid
* Benign behavior ([Ann Thorac Surg 2005;79:1122](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15797036))
* Minute (1 - 2 mm "seedling") tumors are often near the gastroesophageal junction and are asymptomatic
* Multiple tumors are associated with MEN1 syndrome ([Am J Pathol 2001;159:1121](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11549605))
* Large tumors may cause obstructive symptoms

Case reports

* 26 year old woman with multinodular growth pattern simulating carcinoma ([Dis Esophagus 2007;20:187](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17439606))
* 64 year old man with coexisting early squamous cell carcinoma ([Jpn J Clin Oncol 2004;34:751](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15640507))
* 75 year old man with coexisting leiomyosarcoma ([J Exp Clin Cancer Res 2005;24:487](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16270537))

Treatment

* Excise if significant symptoms ([Ann Thorac Cardiovasc Surg 2007;13:78](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17505413)), endoscopic enucleation for small tumors ([Singapore Med J 2006;47:901](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16990968)), esophagectomy for large tumors ([World J Gastroenterol 2005;11:4258](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16015702))

Gross description

* Circumscribed, mural, solitary mass, 2 - 5 cm (surgical specimens), bulges into lumen, may be polypoid
* Pinkish gray white with whorled cut surface; mucosal surface is only rarely ulcerated

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Similar to classic endometrial leiomyoma; circumscribed lesion of circular muscularis propria or muscularis mucosae composed of intersecting fascicles of bland spindle cells with abundant cytoplasm
* Variable fibrosis in center of large leiomyomas
* Occasional calcification; no / rare mitotic figures; no atypia, no cellular foci

Positive stains

* [Desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [alpha smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [high molecular weight caldesmon](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscaldesmon.html)

Negative stains

* [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [DOG1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsDOG1.html) / **ANO1**

Differential diagnosis

* [GIST](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophagusGIST.html): very rare; solid, myxoid and perivascular patterns; more cellular by H&E and cytology, CD117+, CD34+, variable desmin and actin immunoreactivity ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:211](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10680889))
