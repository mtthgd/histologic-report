Definition / general

* Primary malignant melanoma developing from the esophageal mucosa / melanocyte

Essential features

* Middle aged to elderly patients, with male predominance
* Protruding tumor with pigmentation / black discoloration in the middle or lower esophagus
* Epithelioid or spindle melanoma cells with junctional melanocytic activity / junctional melanocytic component
* Aggressive tumor

Terminology

* Primary malignant melanoma of the esophagus

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [C15.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C15-C26/C15-/C15.9) - malignant neoplasm of the esophagus, unspecified

Epidemiology

* ~0.3 - 0.8% of all esophageal tumors ([Esophagus 2021;18:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33047261), [Histopathology 2021;78:240](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32654197))
* Middle aged to elderly patients, with male predominance ([Mod Pathol 2019;32:957](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30760858), [Thorac Cancer 2019;10:950](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30864295), [Histopathology 2021;78:240](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32654197))

Sites

* Most common in the distal third of the esophagus ([Mod Pathol 2019;32:957](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30760858), [Dis Esophagus 2019 Oct 30 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31665346))
* Lower esophagus (58% [44/76 cases] to 60% [12/20 cases]); middle esophagus (34% [26/76 cases] to 35% [7/20 cases]) ([Thorac Cancer 2019;10:950](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30864295), [Medicine (Baltimore) 2020;99:e20957](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32664098))

Clinical features

* Middle aged to elderly patients, with male predominance ([Mod Pathol 2019;32:957](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30760858), [Thorac Cancer 2019;10:950](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30864295), [Histopathology 2021;78:240](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32654197))
* Progressive dysphagia, weight loss and chest pain ([Mod Pathol 2019;32:957](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30760858), [Dis Esophagus 2019 Oct 30 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31665346))

Diagnosis

* Biopsy with immunohistochemistry

Radiology description

* Bulky, polypoid intraluminal masses ([Radiology 1998;209:455](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9807573))

Case reports

* 68 year old man with a 20 mm amelanotic malignant melanoma of the esophagus ([Oncol Lett 2018;15:9087](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29844820))
* 74 year old man with an amelanotic malignant melanoma of the esophagus treated with immunotherapy ([J Surg Case Rep 2021;2021:rjab393](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34659737))

Treatment

* Surgical resection
* Endoscopic resection
* Immunotherapy (PD-1 inhibitor systemic treatment) ([Thorac Cancer 2019;10:950](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30864295))
* Chemotherapy
* Targeted therapy

Melanoma

Positive stains

* Melanoma markers ([S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html), [MelanA / MART1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html), [MITF](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmitf.html), [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html), [tyrosinase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstyrosinase.html))

Negative stains

* [Cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) (mostly negative; however, anomalous cytokeratin expression has been reported) ([Mod Pathol 1990;3:494](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1699224), [J Cutan Pathol 2021;48:1246](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33934372), [Am J Dermatopathol 2019;41:502](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30562218))
* Squamous cell markers ([p40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp40.html) and [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html))
* Neuroendocrine markers ([chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html) and [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html)); neuroendocrine markers can be expressed in malignant melanomas / malignant melanomas with neuroendocrine differentiation have been reported ([Histopathology 2005;47:402](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16178895))
* [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html)
* [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Contrary to cutaneous melanoma, no (0% [0/22]) or few (up to 6.6%) *BRAF* mutations identified ([Mod Pathol 2019;32:957](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30760858), [Thorac Cancer 2019;10:950](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30864295), [Virchows Arch 2009;454:513](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19319568))
* *NRAS* mutations: 33 - 35% ([Mod Pathol 2019;32:957](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30760858), [Virchows Arch 2009;454:513](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19319568))
* Relatively common *KIT* mutations (10 - 50%) by next generation sequencing (NGS) analysis ([Histopathology 2021;78:240](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32654197) [Mod Pathol 2019;32:957](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30760858))
* NGS analysis revealed *NF1* (30%), *SF3B1* (20%), *KRAS* (10%), *BRCA2* (10%), *KIT* (10%) and *TP53* (10%) mutations; no *BRAF* mutations were detected ([Histopathology 2021;78:240](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32654197))

Sample pathology report

* Esophagus, mass, biopsy:
  + Malignant melanoma (see comment)
  + Comment: The infiltrating epithelioid tumor cells are positive for S100, HMB45, MelanA and negative for AE1 / AE3, CAM5.2, synaptophysin and chromogranin. The morphology and immunoprofile are consistent with malignant melanoma.

Differential diagnosis

* [Poorly differentiated squamous cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophagusscc.html) or [adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophagusadenocarcinoma.html):
  + [Keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) positive and melanoma marker negative
* [Neuroendocrine carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophagusneuroendocrine.html) ([small cell neuroendocrine carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophagussmallcell.html) and [large cell neuroendocrine carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophagusneuroendocrine.html)):
  + [Keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) positive; neuroendocrine marker positive and melanoma marker negative
* [Basaloid squamous carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophagusbasaloidSCC.html):
  + [Keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) positive and melanoma marker negative

Which of the following is true about primary malignant melanoma of the esophagus?

1. Junctional melanocytic activity suggests a primary malignant melanoma of the esophagus
2. Melanin pigments should be always found in the tumor cells by definition
3. No melanocytes are found in the normal esophagus
4. SOX10 is a useful marker to differentiate benign nevi from malignant melanomas

Board review style answer #1

**A**. Junctional melanocytic activity (shown in the picture) suggests a primary malignant melanoma of the esophagus

Board review style question #2

Which of the following is true about primary malignant melanoma of the esophagus?

1. *BRAF* mutation is the most common mutation, as in cutaneous melanoma
2. Cervical esophagus is the most common site
3. Most cases show protruding lesions
4. Smoking and alcohol consumption are important risk factors
5. Usually seen in young female patients

Board review style answer #2
