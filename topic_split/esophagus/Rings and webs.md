Definition / general

* Initially described as a constant, symmetrical and diaphragm-like narrowing at the GE junction, associated with a small sliding hiatus hernia ([Am J Roentgenol Radium Ther Nucl Med 1953;70:911](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/13104726))
* Now defined as concentric, smooth, thin (3 - 5 mm) protrusions of normal esophageal tissue (with mucosa, submucosa and muscularis propria) into the lumen ([eMedicine: Esophageal Webs and Rings [Accessed 8 February 2019]](https://emedicine.medscape.com/article/186561-overview), [eMedicine: Schatzki Ring [Accessed 8 February 2019]](https://emedicine.medscape.com/article/182647-overview), [GI Motility Online: Pharyngeal and Esophageal Diverticula, Rings and Webs [Accessed 8 February 2019]](https://www.nature.com/gimo/contents/pt1/full/gimo41.html), [Wikipedia: Schatzki Ring [Accessed 8 February 2019]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schatzki_ring))
* Usually in distal esophagus
* May be congenital or a scar from drinking caustic liquids
* **Type A:** lower muscular ring; rare; thickened circular smooth muscle with overlying squamous mucosa; usually 1.5 cm proximal to squamocolumnar junction, usually asymptomatic ([Am J Gastroenterol 2000;95:43](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10638557))
* **Type B:** lower mucosal ring / Schatzki ring; 5% of normal esophagi; upper surface is lined by stratified squamous epithelium, undersurface is lined by columnar type epithelium; ring contains connective tissue and fibers of muscularis mucosae; located at squamocolumnar junction at proximal margin of hiatal hernia; associated with meat impaction ("steakhouse syndrome," [Surg Endosc 1989;3:195](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2623551)), "swallow syncope" ([Dysphagia 2005;20:273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16633871)) and a *reduced* incidence of Barrett esophagus ([Dig Dis Sci 2004;49:770](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15259497))
* **Type C:** rare; refers to indentation on radiographic studies caused by diaphragmatic crura; usually asymptomatic

Case reports

* 16 year old boy with Schatzki ring causing dysphagia ([J Postgrad Med 1987;33:99](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3681757))
* 54 year old man with dysphagia ([Dis Chest 1968;54:465](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/5698593))

Treatment

* Dilation, possibly antireflux medical therapy or surgery ([Ann Thorac Surg 1984;37:103](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6696542))
