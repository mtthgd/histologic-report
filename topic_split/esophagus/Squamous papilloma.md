Definition / general

* Benign esophageal epithelial polyp composed of squamous epithelium with a papillary growth pattern

Essential features

* Papillary proliferation of nondysplastic squamous epithelium with fibrovascular cores of lamina propria
* Etiologic factors include irritation (alcohol consumption, smoking and reflux esophagitis, metal stent and chronic food impaction), infection (HPV infection) and genetic syndromes (Goltz-Gorlin syndrome and angioma serpiginosum)
* Human papilloma virus infection rate: 10.5 - 57%
* Often seen in the distal esophagus in the U.S. (58.3 - 70%) and the middle esophagus in Asian countries (52.6 [Japan] - 57.5% [Taiwan])

Terminology

* Squamous cell papilloma

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8052/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=63) - squamous cell papilloma, NOS

Epidemiology

* Incidence: 0.20 - 0.42% ([World J Clin Cases 2017;5:134](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28470005), [World J Gastroenterol 2016;22:2349](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26900297), [Acta Histochem Cytochem 2006;39:23](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17460769) )
* Average age: 48.9 - 59.2 years ([World J Clin Cases 2017;5:134](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28470005), [Dis Esophagus 2017;30:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27001250), [World J Gastroenterol 2016;22:2349](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26900297), [Acta Histochem Cytochem 2006;39:23](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17460769), [Am J Surg Pathol 1993;17:803](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8393303))
* Reported male to female ratio varies from roughly 5:2 to 1:5 ([World J Gastroenterol 2016;22:2349](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26900297), [Am J Surg Pathol 1993;17:803](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8393303))
* Average size: 3.8 - 5.0 mm ([World J Clin Cases 2017;5:134](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28470005), [World J Gastroenterol 2016;22:2349](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26900297), [Acta Histochem Cytochem 2006;39:23](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17460769), [Am J Surg Pathol 1993;17:803](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8393303))
* Most cases solitary (80 - 92%) ([Dis Esophagus 2017;30:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27001250), [World J Gastroenterol 2016;22:2349](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26900297), [Acta Histochem Cytochem 2006;39:23](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17460769), [Am J Surg Pathol 1993;17:803](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8393303))

Sites

* Distal esophagus in the U.S. (58.3 - 70%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 1993;17:803](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8393303), [Dis Esophagus 2017;30:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27001250))
* Middle esophagus in Asian countries (52.6 [Japan] - 57.5% [Taiwan]) ([Acta Histochem Cytochem 2006;39:23](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17460769), [World J Gastroenterol 2016;22:2349](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26900297))

Pathophysiology

* Chronic mucosal irritation or HPV infection followed by hyperregenerative response / proliferation of squamous epithelium

Etiology

* Hyperplasia of the squamous epithelium due to chemical irritation (alcohol consumption, smoking and reflux esophagitis), mechanical irritation (metal stent and chronic food impaction), infection (HPV infection), genetic syndromes (Goltz-Gorlin syndrome and angioma serpiginosum) ([World J Gastroenterol 2017;23:2246](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28405153), [Eur J Hum Genet 2007;15:543](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17342156), [Am J Surg Pathol 1993;17:803](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8393303), [Acta Histochem Cytochem 2006;39:23](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17460769))
* Most papillomas in the lower esophagus are associated with gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) ([Am J Surg Pathol 1993;17:803](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8393303))
* Human papilloma virus infection rate varies (10.5 - 57%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 1993;17:803](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8393303), [Acta Histochem Cytochem 2006;39:23](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17460769), [Dis Esophagus 2017;30:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27001250))

Clinical features

* Usually asymptomatic
* Rarely, dysphasia and heartburn ([Clin Gastroenterol Hepatol 2016;14:A21](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26435069))
* Incidentally found small warty polypoid lesion in the esophagus

Diagnosis

* Incidentally found on endoscopy
* Confirmed on biopsy

Prognostic factors

* No known prognostic factors (progression to dysplasia or carcinoma is extremely rare)

Case reports

* 3 year old girl with history of Goltz syndrome presented with esophageal squamous papillomatosis ([J Pediatr Gastroenterol Nutr 2018;66:e132](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29373440))
* 42 year old man with multiple esophageal squamous papillomas ([ACG Case Rep J 2019;6:e00180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31750370))
* 44 year old woman with squamous cell carcinoma in situ arising from esophageal squamous papilloma ([Clin Endosc 2019;52:72](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30021250))
* 64 year old man with history of Cronkhite-Canada syndrome presented with papilloma-like lesions of the esophagus ([Dig Liver Dis 2020;52:352](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31901311))
* 65 year old woman with carcinoma cuniculatum initially misdiagnosed as benign squamous papilloma ([Endoscopy 2014;46:E531](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25409055))

Treatment

* Endoscopic resection / polypectomy
* Ablation by radiofrequency ([World J Gastrointest Endosc 2015;7:290](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25789102))
* Cryotherapy ([ACG Case Rep J 2019;6:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31620505))

Squamous papillomatosis

Gross description

* Small (usually < 5 mm), sessile, whitish, warty polypoid lesion

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Papillary proliferation of nondysplastic squamous epithelium with fibrovascular cores of lamina propria
* Koilocytes can be seen
* Most commonly, exophytic but rarely, endophytic and tentacular type proliferation ([Mayo Clin Proc 2019;94:1551](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31378230))
* No dysplasia is seen (progression to dysplasia or carcinoma is extremely rare)

Sample pathology report

* Esophagus, mid, polyp, biopsy:
  + Squamous papilloma (see comment)
  + Comment: It is negative for dysplasia or carcinoma.

Differential diagnosis

* [Verrucous carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophagusverrucousscc.html) / **carcinoma cuniculatum** ([Endoscopy 2014;46:E531](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25409055)):
  + Usually large mass
  + Hyperkeratosis, dyskeratosis and nuclear atypia in addition to papillary growth and acanthosis
* [Squamous cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophagusscc.html):
  + Cytological and architectural atypia
* [Glycogenic acanthosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophagusglycogenic.html):
  + Acanthosis with clear cytoplasm
  + No papillary proliferation
* [Epidermoid metaplasia / epidermization](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophagusepidermization.html):
  + Occasionally, shows papillary proliferation but usually with prominent granular layer and orthokeratosis
