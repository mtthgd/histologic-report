Definition / general

* Rare and aggressive esophageal carcinoma
* Histologically characterized by sheets of cells with high grade features, frequent rhabdoid appearance and monotony

Essential features

* Neoplastic cells appear high grade with frequent rhabdoid appearance
* Background Barrett esophagus with or without dysplasia may be identified
* May have limited or absent expression of pancytokeratins
* Characterized by frequent loss of SMARCA4 or SMARCA2
* Dismal prognosis with frequent recurrence or metastasis

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8020/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=62) - carcinoma, undifferentiated, NOS
* ICD-11: [2B70.Y](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f669033105%2fmms%2fother) - other specified malignant neoplasms of esophagus
* ICD-11: [XH1YY4](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f1674951164) - carcinoma, undifferentiated, NOS

Epidemiology

* M > F
* Median age in seventh decade
* Likely related to Barrett esophagus associated adenocarcinomas
* Very rare: as low as 0.15% of esophageal carcinomas ([Dis Esophagus 2009;22:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19196264))

Sites

* Distal esophagus
* Gastroesophageal junction

Pathophysiology

* Likely dedifferentiation / high grade progression of adenocarcinoma
* Subset of cases has associated Barrett esophagus and Barrett associated dysplasia
* Mutant p53 pattern by immunohistochemistry
* Frequent loss of SMARCA4 or SMARCA2 by immunohistochemistry

Etiology

* Same as Barrett esophagus associated adenocarcinoma

Clinical features

* Presentation similar to other esophageal cancers
  + Progressive dysphagia
  + Symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux disease
  + Unexplained weight loss ([Hum Pathol 2015;46:366](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25582499))
* Typically locally advanced at diagnosis

Diagnosis

* Seen as exophytic mass on esophagogastroduodenoscopy
* Confirmed by histology and immunohistochemistry

Prognostic factors

* Poor prognosis overall: currently 20% 1 year survival rate
* As many as 67% will have local recurrence or metastasis ([Hum Pathol 2015;46:366](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25582499))

Case reports

* 67 year old man with ulcerative mass of gastroesophageal junction ([Surg Case Rep 2019;5:8](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30649642))
* 71 year old woman with mass of middle esophagus ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2020;13:1902](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32782721))
* 79 year old man with EBV infection ([Int J Clin Exp Med 2013;6:219](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23573354))

Treatment

* Neoadjuvant chemotherapy followed by esophagogastrectomy
* Palliative care

Gross description

* Exophytic mass
* Raised edges
* Central ulceration
* Pushing border

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Nests and sheets of cells
* Cytology is high grade with large nuclei and high nucleus to cytoplasm ratio
* Can have rhabdoid features
* Sheets of necrosis
* Lymphoepithelioma-like carcinoma a distinct subtype
  + Sheets of epithelioid cells surrounded by dense lymphoplasmacytic inflammatory infiltrate
* Absence of identifiable signs of differentiation (adenocarcinoma, squamous cell carcinoma, neuroendocrine carcinoma)

Positive stains

* [Pancytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html): variable expression, ranging from diffuse to patchy or negative ([Hum Pathol 2015;46:366](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25582499), [Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:414](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33027072))
* [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) can display mutant pattern of expression (either diffusely strong positive or total loss) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:414](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33027072))
* [EBV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) positivity can be seen in lymphoepithelioma-like variant

Negative stains

* [SMARCA4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsSMARCA4BRG1.html) (BRG1), **SMARCA2** (BRM) or [SMARCB1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html) (INI1) aberrant loss ([Dis Esophagus 2009;22:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19196264), [Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:414](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33027072))

Sample pathology report

* Esophagus, biopsy:
  + Undifferentiated carcinoma (see comment)
  + Comment: SMARCA4 loss is seen by immunohistochemistry.

Differential diagnosis

* [Poorly differentiated neuroendocrine carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophagusadenocarcinoma.html):
  + Potentially also with nested / sheet-like architecture but would express [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html) and [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html)
* [Poorly differentiated squamous cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophagusscc.html):
  + Stains for squamous markers (e.g. [p40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdeltaNp63.html))
  + More consistent pattern of [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html) positivity
* [Malignant melanoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophagusmelanoma.html):
  + Can also show nested architecture but would express melanocytic markers such as [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html)
* [Hematolymphoid malignancy](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophaguslymphoma.html):
  + Also would be negative for [pancytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) but would express hematolymphoid markers (e.g. [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) / [LCA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html))

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/esophagusundiffcarcinoma_Graham02.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/esophagusundiffcarcinoma_Graham02.jpg)

A patient undergoes esophagogastrectomy for a tumor of the distal esophagus. A representative hematoxylin and eosin photomicrograph of the lesion is shown. The tumor lacks any immunohistochemical signs of differentiation, including negativity for pancytokeratin, MelanA, synaptophysin, chromogranin and CD34. An esophageal carcinoma of this subtype is characterized by what immunophenotype?

1. Keratin 7 expression
2. Wild type p53
3. Loss of SMARCA4 or SMARCA2
4. Expression of Rb

Board review style answer #1

**C**. Loss of SMARCA4 or SMARCA2. This is an undifferentiated carcinoma of the esophagus.
