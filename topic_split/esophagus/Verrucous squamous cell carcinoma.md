Definition / general

* Highly differentiated variant of squamous cell carcinoma that is locally invasive with a pushing, as opposed to an infiltrative border, that is identical to lesions of the same name in the upper aerodigestive tract
* Metastases are uncommon

Epidemiology

* Extremely rare
* Male predominance
* Ages 36 - 76 years

Pathophysiology / etiology

* Cases related to chronic mucosal irritation, lye ingestion, diverticular disease, reflux esophagitis ([Gastroenterology 1996;110:904](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8608902))
* Reported case with positive HPV stain ([Gastroenterol Hepatol 2013;36:311](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23195756))

Clinical features

* Slow growing, nodal metastases are uncommon and distant metastases are not reported but still has high mortality because of advanced local disease with the development of fistulas ([J Gastroenterol Hepatol 1993;8:107](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8439656), [J Clin Gastroenterol 1991;13:102](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2007728), [Minerva Chir 2005;60:61](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15902055))

Diagnosis

* Tissue biopsy, knowledge of clinical and endoscopic findings may be essential to prevent misdiagnosis

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Similar to [oral cavity](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/oralcavityverrucous.html) counterpart
* Well differentiated with papillary fronds covered by squamous epithelium
* Bland cytologic features with mild atypia
* Variable parakeratosis and hyperkeratosis
* Pushing type of invasion with blunt epithelial projections

Differential diagnosis

* **Carcinoma cuniculatum:** deeply penetrating and burrowing growth pattern ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2005;9:134](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15944954))
* Superficial biopsies may be diagnosed as [squamous papillomas](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophagussquamouspapilloma.html) or other benign lesions
  + Papillomas are generally under 3 cm in dimension, generally do not demonstrate circumferential growth, lack ulceration, do not cause stricture and do not invade submucosa or muscularis, which are common findings in verrucous carcinoma ([Can J Gastroenterol 2004;18:459](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15229749), [Am J Gastroenterol 1996;91:1031](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8633546))
