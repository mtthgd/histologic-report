Definition / general

* Cornea is the transparent, avascular tissue located at the anterior pole of the eye, which protects the eye from environmental factors and accounts for the majority of the eye's refractive power

Essential features

* Cornea is located at the anterior pole of the eye and, together with the sclera (white), forms the exterior layer of the globe of the eye
* It is a transparent, avascular tissue that protects the eye from environmental factors (e.g., chemicals, bacteria, dirt)
* Cornea comprises 5 distinct histological layers: epithelium, Bowman membrane, stroma, Descemet membrane and endothelium; however, a sixth layer has been proposed
* Limbus is at the lateral margin of the cornea and forms the junction of the cornea and sclera; this is the location for structures involved in aqueous humor outflow (i.e., trabecular meshwork and Schlemm canal)
* Cornea and overlying tear film together account for 67% of the eye's refractive power

Terminology

* Dua layer is also known as pre-Descemet layer, Dua-Fine layer, preposterior lamina layer ([Prog Retin Eye Res 2023;97:101161](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36642673))

Anatomy & physiology

* Cornea is the clear, dome shaped tissue located at the anterior pole of the eye; it lies in front of the anterior chamber, pupil and lens and therefore is the first place where light is refracted as it enters the eye
* Main functions are
  + Light refraction: as light from the environment enters the eye, the cornea bends it in order to focus it onto the retina
    - Cornea and overlying tear film are main refractive surface of eye, accounting for 65 - 75% of light refraction as it passes through the eye
  + Protection: cornea filters out some UV light; it also forms a physical barrier to water, chemicals, dirt, microbes and other foreign objects
* There are no blood vessels or lymphatics within cornea, however, arterial plexus is present at limbus
* Limbus
  + Junction of peripheral margin of cornea and anterior sclera that represents a functional and anatomical border between the corneal and conjunctival epithelium; this area is ~1.5 - 2.0 mm wide
  + Stem cells located at the limbus are responsible for regeneration of the corneal epithelium ([Cells 2021;10:2302](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34571952))
  + Descemet membrane terminates at limbus where collagen fibers form a circular ridge called Schwalbe line
  + For surgery on anterior eye, limbus is a common incision site (i.e., for cataract and glaucoma)
  + Trabecular meshwork and Schlemm canal are located at the corneal limbus; these 2 structures are involved in the flow of aqueous humor from the eye
    - Trabecular meshwork is formed of finely branching and delicately pigmented connective tissue bands with lining cells that are continuous with the corneal endothelium; it regulates the flow of aqueous humor and helps to maintain intraocular pressure ([J Ocular Biol 2014;2:9](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25356439))
    - Schlemm canal is anterior and superficial to trabecular meshwork; it is an endothelium lined venous canal that allows the outflow of aqueous humor from the eye
* Cornea comprises 5 classical layers which have unique functions ([Indian J Ophthalmol 2018;66:190](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29380756))
  + Epithelium: physical barrier to environment, smooth optical surface as internal part of tear film, immune function (Langerhans cells)
  + Bowman layer: helps to maintain corneal shape
  + Stroma: mechanical strength, transparency, refraction
  + Descemet membrane: resting layer for endothelial cells
  + Endothelium: removes water from corneal stroma
* Embryologically, corneal stroma migrates from neural crest in the seventh week of gestation; this occurs after establishment of a primitive endothelium

Clinical features

* In myopia (nearsightedness)
  + Progression can be accelerated by paradoxical steepening of the cornea in an attempt to maintain emmetropia ([Optom Vis Sci 1998;75:132](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9503439))
  + In young patients, myopia is associated with anterior segment changes such as, differences in corneal curvature, decreased corneal thickness and decreased endothelial density ([Br J Ophthalmol 2001;85:916](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11466244))
* In hyperopia (farsightedness)
  + Decrease in the axial length of the eye or flattening of the cornea or lens are the most common causes of hyperopia
* Reshaping of the cornea via laser assisted in situ keratomileusis (LASIK) can change corneal refractive properties while maintaining tissue stability, since the anterior layers of the cornea are not disturbed ([Lasers Surg Med 1990;10:463](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2233101))
* Limbal stem cell deficiency can be induced by extensive limbal damage and may lead to vascularization and corneal opacification ([Cells 2021;10:2302](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34571952))
* Loss of corneal transparency can lead to blindness; indeed, in 2019, WHO estimated that ~4.2 million people worldwide have moderate to severe vision loss due to corneal opacity ([WHO: World Report on Vision [Accessed 26 March 2025]](https://www.who.int/docs/default-source/documents/publications/world-vision-report-accessible.pdf?sfvrsn=223f9bf7_2))

Laboratory

* Pachymetry measures corneal thickness, showing normal measures of 550 - 600 μm centrally and 600 - 800 μm peripherally; these measures are usually performed via ultrasound, optics (optical coherence tomography) or with a specular microscope ([Arch Ophthalmol 1999;117:868](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10408449))

Gross description

* Cornea is the convex, transparent tissue located at the anterior pole of the eye
* Cornea is not perfectly circular and is slightly ovoid, measuring ~9 - 11 mm vertically and 11 - 12 mm horizontally ([Indian J Ophthalmol 2018;66:190](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29380756))
* Avascular; no visible blood vessels in a healthy cornea
* Limbus appears as a slightly raised border at the peripheral margin of the cornea where it meets the sclera

Microscopic (histologic) description

* 5 distinct layers (superficial to deep)
  + Corneal epithelium
    - Stratified squamous, nonkeratinized epithelium that forms the outermost layer of the cornea
    - 5 - 7 layers thick (~5 layers thick centrally and thicker peripherally) with a total thickness of ~50 - 60 μm ([Cornea 2016;35:342](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26751987))
    - Cells are flatter superficially and become more polygonal toward the basal layer, with transitional cells (middle wing cells) in between; basal cells may have mitotic figures
    - Limbal corneal epithelium contains layers of epithelial cells, Langerhans cells and melanocytes and basal cells are cuboidal
    - Central corneal epithelium contains no Langerhans cells nor melanocytes and basal cells are columnar
  + Bowman membrane
    - Composed of randomly oriented, condensed collagen and proteoglycans
    - Acellular, 8 - 14 μm thick and does not regenerate
    - Not a true membrane but rather the most anterior portion of the stroma; it helps the cornea maintain its shape
  + Corneal stroma
    - 80 - 85% of corneal thickness and contains no blood vessels or lymphatics
    - Made of keratocytes and extracellular matrix
    - Contains regularly spaced collagen fibrils separated by glycoprotein and mucoprotein, which makes cornea transparent
    - Collagen in corneal fibrils is predominantly type I, however, type III, V and VI are also seen ([Indian J Ophthalmol 2018;66:190](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29380756))
    - Fibrils are packed into lamellae, which are arranged at right angles to the fibers of adjacent lamellae; the human eye contains ~200 lamellae in the central cornea
      * Lamellae are ~0.2 mm wide and 2 μm tall
    - Separation by clefts is a normal processing artifact; the absence of clefts is caused by stroma edema, which causes corneal clouding and is due to endothelial damage
  + Anterior to Descemet membrane lies Dua layer, a sixth distinct layer proposed in 2013 ([Ophthalmology 2013;120:1778](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23714320))
    - Also known as pre-Descemet layer, Dua-Fine layer and preposterior limiting lamina layer
    - ~10 μm thick
    - Acellular and composed of type I and VI collagen and abundant elastin ([Prog Retin Eye Res 2023;97:101161](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36642673))
  + Descemet membrane
    - Composed of type IV collagen and laminin
    - True basal lamina produced by underlying corneal endothelial cells
    - 3 - 4 μm with a banded appearance at birth, 7 - 10 μm and unbanded in adults
    - Does not regenerate and due to its elasticity, curls when severed
    - Site of copper deposition in Kayser-Fleischer ring of Wilson disease
  + Endothelium
    - Single layer of very flat cells, ~5 μm thick
    - Cells are hexagonal and metabolically active
      * Appears as a honeycomb mosaic from a posterior view
      * Many lateral interdigitations with adjacent cells
      * Tight junctions along lateral borders and hemidesmosomes at the basal surface (adhering it to Descemet membrane)
    - Does not regenerate and functions as pump to keep cornea dehydrated and transparent
    - Does not line blood vessels or lymphatic spaces
    - Directly contacts aqueous humor of anterior chamber and is often rubbed off while grossing specimen

Positive stains

* [CK3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck1.html#ck3), full thickness of cornea and suprabasal at the limbus
* Basal layer
  + [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) and [CK14](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck14.html), expressed in low levels at the basal layer of a normal cornea, primarily at the limbus (highlights corneal epithelial stem cells) ([Cells 2021;10:2302](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34571952))
* Epithelium: Langerhans cells are [CD1a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1a.html)+ and basal lamina is highlighted with [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html)
* Endothelium: [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)+ due to neural crest origin

Negative stains

* [CK19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck19.html)
* [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) and **ABCG2**: increased expression outside of the basal layer indicates expansion of the stem cell population and potential pathology ([Mol Vis 2014;20:468](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24744607))
* [MUC5AC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmuc2.html#muc5ac) minimal to absent in corneal epithelium (positive in conjunctiva)

Electron microscopy description

* Schlemm canal endothelial cells contain giant cytoplasmic vacuoles
* Stromal collagen fibrils are organized uniformly in a lamellar pattern
  + Anterior stromal collagen is loosely organized
  + Average fibril diameter is 35 nm
* Bowman membrane can be seen
* Reference: [Mol Vis 2014;20:468](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24744607)

Board review style question #1

[![Anatomy and histology-cornea](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/eyecorneaanatomyStringham_09.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/eyecorneaanatomyStringham_09.jpg)

A 3 year old patient is suspected to have corneal stem cell deficiency due to congenital aniridia. What staining pattern would support this suspicion?

1. CD1a+ in the epithelium of the peripheral cornea
2. CK3+ in suprabasal epithelium at the limbus
3. MUC5AC- throughout the corneal epithelium
4. p63- in basal epithelium at the limbus
5. S100+ in endothelium of the central cornea

Board review style answer #1

**D**. p63- in basal epithelium at the limbus. p63 highlights corneal epithelial stem cells, which reside in the limbus of the normal cornea. In cases of limbal stem cell deficiency, the normal expression, especially in the basal layer, would be decreased. Answers A, B, C and E are incorrect because the staining patterns described are true of normal cornea and therefore would not support a diagnosis of stem cell deficiency.

Reference: [Anatomy & histology-cornea](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/eyecorneaanatomy.html)

Board review style question #2

Collagen packed lamellae are a distinguishing feature of which layer of the cornea?

1. Bowman membrane
2. Corneal epithelium
3. Corneal stroma
4. Descemet membrane
5. Endothelium

Board review style answer #2

**C**. Corneal stroma. Collagen fibrils within the corneal stroma are packed into lamellae, which are arranged at right angles to the fibers of adjacent lamellae. The human cornea contains ~300 lamellae, with each measuring ~0.2 mm wide and 2 μm tall. Answers A, B, D and E are incorrect because the stroma is the only location that collagen lamellae are seen within the cornea.
