Definition / general

* Pigmented thickening of conjunctival epithelium, may be normal finding in dark skinned individuals - bilateral, continues throughout life
  ([Surv Ophthalmol 2004;49:3](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14711437))
* Present in 92% of blacks, 36% of Asians, 28% of Hispanics, 5% of whites
* Uncommon finding in Laugier-Hunziker syndrome ([Arch Dermatol 2007;143:631](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17515514))
* Can be cystic ([Cornea 2012;31:1273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23044615))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Dense pigmentation at basal layer of epithelium but normal number of melanocytes
* Cystic variant has epithelial lined cysts with goblet cells in substantia propria

Differential diagnosis

* **Early [primary acquired melanosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/eyepamconj.html):**
  + Melanocytic proliferation is present
* **Iron containing foreign bodies**
* **Medications**:
  + Epinephrine, tetracycline, silver containing products
* **Melanocyte migration associated with inflammation or trauma**
* **Ochronosis**
* [Ocular melanocytosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/eyeuveamelanosisoculi.html):
  + Pigmentation of ocular structures due to abnormal migration of melanocytes during development
