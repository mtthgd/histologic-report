# Cicatrizing conjunctivitis

Definition / general

* Cicatrizing: causing a scar and possibly affecting vision
* Conjunctival fibrosis due to chronic inflammation, leading to alterations of conjunctival architecture

Etiology

* Numerous causes ([Curr Opin Ophthalmol 2001;12:250](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11507337))
* Atopic keratoconjunctivitis
* Burns
* Discoid lupus erythematosus ([Ocul Immunol Inflamm 2002;10:287](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12854037))
* Epidermolysis bullosa acquisita ([Arch Ophthalmol 2006;124:1615](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17102010))
* Lichen planus ([Am J Ophthalmol 2003;136:239](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12888044))
* Ocular cicatricial pemphigoid
* Ocular rosacea ([Cornea 2004;23:630](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15257004))
* Paraneoplastic syndrome associated with lung tumors ([Cornea 2006;25:611](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16783152))
* Porphyria cutaneous tarda ([Am J Ophthalmol 2002;134:619](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12383830))
* Post-infectious
* Sarcoidosis ([Ann Ophthalmol 1989;21:92](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2735697))
* Sjögren syndrome
* Stevens-Johnson syndrome
* Systemic sclerosis
* Trachoma
* Granulomatosis with polyangiitis (Wegener's) ([Nephrol Dial Transplant 2006;21:3342](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16822788))

Clinical features

* Dry eyes
* Loss of transparency and vision

Diagnosis

* Positive direct immunofluorescence with IgA, IgM, IgG, complement C3, complement C4, and fibrinogen in autoimmune diseases

Treatment

* Medical management varies according to specific causes

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Fibrosis and inflammation consisting of neutrophils, lymphocytes, plasma cells
* Keratinization of superficial layer, hyperplasia of basal layers
