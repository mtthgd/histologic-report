Definition / general

* Usually caruncle ([Arch Ophthalmol 1977;95:474](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/843279), [Ophthal Plast Reconstr Surg 2012;28:14](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21956543))
* Often elderly (mean age 73), more common in women
* Proposed origin from lacrimal and accessory lacrimal glands based on cytokeratin profile
  ([Acta Ophthalmol 2011;89:263](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19843013))

Case reports

* 51 year old woman with tumor of caruncle
  ([Int Ophthalmol 2004;25:321](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16532295))

  ([Klin Monatsbl Augenheilkd 2005;222:733](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16175484))
* 72 year old woman with tumor of bulbar conjunctiva
  ([Klin Monatsbl Augenheilkd 1996;209:176](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8992082))

  ([Indian J Ophthalmol 2002;50:60](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12090093))

Gross description

* Red-orange or yellow-tan mass

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Solid nests and cords of polyhedral cells with abundant, finely granular acidophilic cytoplasm and round / oval paracentral nuclei, usually with one prominent nucleolus
* May have microcystic areas with occasional goblet cells
  ([Am J Dermatopathol 2007;29:279](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17519627)),
  may have malignant histology and behavior
