Definition / general

* See also [steatocystoma simplex](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/eyesteatocytomasimplex.html)
* Most are acquired due to surgery
  ([Ophthalmology 2006;113:1049](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16631253)),
  trauma ([Br J Anaesth 2005;95:825](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16199416)),
  foreign bodies or chronic inflammation
* Also due to parasites, vernal keratoconjunctivitis ([Korean J Ophthalmol 2007;21:251](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18063892))
* Usually on nasal conjunctiva and lower fornix
* Most (65%) conjunctival nevi are also cystic
  ([Curr Opin Ophthalmol 2007;18:351](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17700226));
  some tumors such as lymphangioma can be highly cystic
* Cyst may be injected with Healon V and trypan blue to facilitate visualization and excision
  ([Cornea 2005;24:759](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16015101))
* **Dermoid cysts or 'conjunctivoid':**
  + See also [dermoid tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/eyedermoidtumor.html)
  + Young adults in contrast to skin dermoid
  + Usually located in nasal or superonasal deep orbit
  + Lined by stratified squamous epithelium, contains cutaneous adnexae ([Ophthal Plast Reconstr Surg 2006;22:137](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16550063))
  + Rarely lipogranulomas from cyst rupture
  + May have oncocytic differentiation ([Ophthal Plast Reconstr Surg 2008;24:69](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18209655))
* **Inclusion cysts:**
  + One or two cell lining of nonkeratinized epithelium containing goblet cells
  + May have apocrine snouts resembling apocrine glands
  + May be associated with Stevens-Johnson syndrome
    ([Ophthal Plast Reconstr Surg 2006;22:475](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17117107))
