Definition / general

* Congenital benign tumor composed of mature tissues of ectodermal and mesodermal origin, usually located at the junction between the cornea and conjunctiva ([Acta Ophthalmol 2017;95:e517](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28266178), [Curr Eye Res 2013;38:835](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23573994))
* Classified as either dermoid tumor, dermolipoma or complex choristoma ([Case Rep Ophthalmol 2024;15:418](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38721041))
* Osseous choristomas correspond also to a conjunctival choristoma composed of mature bone but is not classified within the epibulbar choristoma group ([Am J Ophthalmol Case Rep 2016;5:4](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29503936))

Essential features

* Developmental lesion of histologically mature tissue
* More common in children in the inferotemporal quadrant
* Vision threatening entity that may lead to irreversible amblyopia
* Osseous choristoma is usually located in the superotemporal conjunctiva

Terminology

* Epibulbar dermoid: limbal dermoid, dermoid tumor
* Dermolipoma: lipodermoid
* Complex choristoma: mixed dermoid

ICD coding

* ICD-11: [LA14.06](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#348731277) - epibulbar choristoma

Epidemiology

* M = F
* 1 - 4% of all conjunctival tumors ([Am J Ophthalmol 2017;173:106](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27725148))
* As congenital lesions: peak detection between 0 and 5 years ([JAMA Ophthalmol 2017;135:215](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28125759))
* Frequently observed in patients with Goldenhar syndrome and linear nevus sebaceous syndrome ([Acta Ophthalmol 2017;95:e517](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28266178))

Sites

* Predominantly located in limbal area on the inferotemporal margin (dermoid tumor) or superotemporal (dermolipoma and complex choristoma) ([Eur J Ophthalmol 2022;32:NP5](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33401954), [Cureus 2023;15:e39241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37337485))
* Commonly a solitary (and unilateral) lesion; occasionally multiple or bilateral

Pathophysiology

* Unknown

Etiology

* Is a developmental anomaly
* Has been associated with altered migration from external structures of the eyelid

Complex choristoma

Clinical features

* Dome shaped tumor at limbus ([BMC Ophthalmol 2021;21:12](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33407235))
* Growth is usually limited ([PLoS One 2023;18:e0286250](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37267334))
* Often accompanied by astigmatism and amblyopia; this condition can also impact cosmesis ([Eye (Lond) 2014;28:857](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24858530), [PLoS One 2023;18:e0286250](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37267334))

Diagnosis

* Definitive diagnosis requires histologic examination: mature tissue present at conjunctival or corneal ectopic sites

Prognostic factors

* Malignant transformation has not been described
* Extent to which the cornea is invaded by the dermoid is a significant factor influencing postoperative visual acuity

Case reports

* 3 year old girl with Goldenhar syndrome and unilateral limbal dermoid ([Med J Armed Forces India 2023;79:109](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36605342))
* 12 year old girl with an epibulbar osseous choristoma ([Am J Ophthalmol Case Rep 2016;5:4](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29503936))
* 16 year old boy with extensive dermolipoma with inferolateral corneal involvement ([Orbit 2020;39:313](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31106634))
* 18 year old woman with epibulbar osseous choristoma within a dermolipoma ([Orbit 2019;38:407](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30430897))
* 22 year old man with dermolipoma and split hand / foot malformation ([Indian J Ophthalmol 2022;70:2613](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35791171))

Treatment

* Depends on the location and size of the lesion
* May include simple excision, corneal tattooing and sutureless limbal conjunctival autograft ([Eye (Lond) 2014;28:857](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24858530))

Gross description

* Well circumscribed, small tumor
* Oval and dome shaped
* Whitish, pink or yellowish surface
* May contain hair shafts or adipose tissue

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Mixture of benign tissues ([Curr Eye Res 2013;38:835](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23573994))
* Limbal dermoid
  + Stratified squamous keratinizing or nonkeratinizing epithelium
  + No rete ridges
  + Interweaving collagen bundles
  + Epidermal appendages; hair follicles, sebaceous glands or sweat glands may be seen
  + Variable amount of adipose tissue
  + Grading: size and depth of involvement ([Acta Ophthalmol 2017;95:e517](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28266178))
    1. Superficial and < 5 mm
    2. Covers nearly the entire cornea and invades deeply, stopping just short of the Descemet membrane
    3. Covers the entire cornea and extends into the anterior chamber, reaching the pigmented epithelium of the iris
* Dermolipoma
  + Stratified squamous keratinizing or nonkeratinizing epithelium
  + Dense collagen stroma
  + Pilosebaceous structures are less common
  + Disproportionate amount of adipose tissue (particularly in the deeper aspect of the lesion)
* Complex choristoma
  + 2 or more ectopic tissues derived from 2 germ layers: ectoderm (cutaneous appendage, nerve, brain, lacrimal gland) and mesoderm (bone, cartilage, muscle, connective tissue)
  + Most common combination reported is dermolipoma and bone ([Orbit 2019;38:407](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30430897))

Sample pathology report

* Conjunctiva, right / left eye, biopsy:
  + Complex choristoma / dermolipoma / dermoid tumor (see comment)
  + Comment: There are no signs of malignancy in this particular specimen.

Differential diagnosis

* **Orbital fat:**
  + Composed only of mature adipose tissue without skin adnexa
  + It usually shows a mild variability of size in the adipocytes but without nuclear atypia
* [Herniation of intraconal orbital fat](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueadiposeshof.html):
  + Due to weakened Tenon capsule due to aging, trauma or surgery
  + Admixture of mature fat, fibrous septa and a varying number of inflammatory cells ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:193](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17255763))
* [Pterygium](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/eyepterygium.html):
  + Shows degenerative elastotic degeneration

Board review style question #1

The tumor above was found in the eye of a pediatric patient. What is the diagnosis?

1. Complex choristoma
2. Dermolipoma
3. Limbal dermoid
4. Orbital prolapse
5. Pterygium

Board review style answer #1

**B**. Dermolipoma. The tumor shows a stratified squamous epithelium, with skin appendages and a striking amount of adipose tissue.
Answer C is incorrect due to the large amount of adipose tissue present in this lesion.
Answer A is incorrect because there is no ectopic tissue from a second germ layer (i.e., cartilage, muscle).
Answer D is incorrect because the lesion contains stratified squamous epithelium with skin appendages.
Answer E is incorrect because there are no signs of elastotic degeneration and the presence of skin adnexa is not compatible with this diagnosis.
