Definition / general

* Occurs in up to 80% of chronic graft versus host disease patients with allogeneic hematopoietic transplants
* Most commonly affects conjunctiva, lacrimal gland, meibomian glands; also cornea, choroid
* Bulbar conjunctiva is biopsied for diagnosis
* Conjunctival and lacrimal gland fibrosis may be due to epithelial mesenchymal transition ([Am J Pathol 2009;175:2372](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19893038))
* Modified Lerner classification of bulbar GVHD seems to have prognostic value ([Graefes Arch Clin Exp Ophthalmol 2007;245:1001](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17120011))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Reduced goblet cells
  ([Eur J Ophthalmol 2006;16:17](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16496240))
* Pseudomembrane due to loss of conjunctival epithelium
* May have keratinization
* Increased ICAM-1 expression
  ([Eur J Ophthalmol 2006;16:17](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16496240))
* Reduced mucosal microvilli
  ([Bone Marrow Transplant 2012;47:416](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21572465))

Cytology description

* **Conjunctival scrape cytology:**
  + Atypical epithelial cells
  + Loss of goblet cells
  + Epithelial cells with vacuolated or coarse, granular cytoplasm
  + Squamous metaplasia
  + Prominent inflammatory background
    ([Acta Cytol 2010;54:272](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20518410))
  + Expression of Th1-associated chemokines
    ([Bone Marrow Transplant 2010;45:1340](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20140022))
