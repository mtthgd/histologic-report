# Granulomatous conjunctivitis

Granulomatous conjunctivitis

Sites

* Unilateral or bilateral (in systemic diseases)
* Palpebral conjunctiva and upper lid more often in tuberculosis

Etiology

* Usually associated with systemic disease
* Conjunctival granulomata form in response to foreign body, allergen, infectious organism or as part of systemic disease
* With necrosis:
  + Tuberculosis ([Int Ophthalmol 2014;34:655](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23928944))
  + Parinaud oculoglandular syndrome due to cat scratch fever ([Ugeskr Laeger 1995;157:4137](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7544512)) or tularemia ([Am J Ophthalmol 2001;131:283](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11228320))
  + Granulomatosis with polyangiitis (Wegener's) ([Ophthalmology 2003;110:1770](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/13129876))
* Without necrosis:
  + Crohn's disease ([Br J Ophthalmol 1984;68:901](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6509011))
  + Foreign bodies (ophthalmia nodosa due to hair of caterpillar, surgical talc) ([J AAPOS 2007;11:402](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17446101))
  + Hodgkin lymphoma ([Am J Ophthalmol 2001;131:796](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11384580))
  + Sarcoidosis
  + Syphilis
* Other:
  + Acid fast bacilli
  + Actinic granulomas ([Ophthalmology 2013;120:1786](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23601802))
  + Churg-Strauss syndrome ([Arch Ophthalmol 2012;130:1228](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22965612))
  + Drugs (brimonidine-timolol fixed combination) ([Int Ophthalmol 2013;33:557](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23224363), [Br J Ophthalmol 2007;91:691](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17446509))
  + HIV ([Ocul Immunol Inflamm 2006;14:309](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17056466))
  + Ocular rosacea in children
  + Parasites ([Ophthalmology 2001;108:1223](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11425679))
  + Ulcerative colitis ([Eye (Lond) 2013;27:567](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23328805))

Clinical features

* Eye discharge, watering, redness and itching
* Often regional lymphadenopathy

Case reports

* 2 and 5 year old girls with conjunctivitis due to synthetic fibers from teddy bears ([BMC Ophthalmol 2011;11:17](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21689446))
* 34 year old male cattle farmer with no history of trauma ([Cornea 2002;21:831](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12410047))
* 62 year old woman with bulbar conjunctival sporotrichosis presenting as tumor ([Cornea 2010;29:573](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20335807))
* Two women with *Sporothrix schenckii* ([Cornea 2005;24:491](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15829813))

Treatment

* Varies by etiology
* Tuberculosis: anti-tuberculosis therapy, topical prednisolone
* Excision in systemic conditions

Gross description

* Sarcoidosis: ([EyeWiki: Conjunctival Biopsy in the Diagnosis of Ocular Sarcoidosis [Accessed 25 April 2018]](http://eyewiki.aao.org/Conjunctival_Biopsy_in_the_Diagnosis_of_Ocular_Sarcoidosis))
  + Small, tan conjunctival nodules
* Tuberculosis: ulcerative, nodular, hypertrophic granulomatous and pedunculated

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Chronic inflammatory infiltrate with epitheloid histiocytes and giant cells
* Elastolysis and granulomatous inflammation in actinic granuloma
