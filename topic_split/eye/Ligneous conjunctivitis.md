Definition / general

* Chronic relapsing pseudomembranous conjunctivitis with woody induration of eyelid and tarsal conjunctiva and pseudomembrane on tarsal conjunctiva
* Also affects other mucosa

Terminology

* "Ligneous" means resembling wood
* Liesegang rings:
  + Rings of precipitated iron and calcium (a phenomenon of chemical systems) seen in conjunctiva and eyelid, associated with inflammation, necrosis, fibrosis or cysts
  + May resemble helminth eggs, larvae or adults ([Am J Surg Pathol 1987;11:598](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2956899), [Wikipedia: Liesegang rings [Accessed 26 April 2018]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liesegang_rings))

Epidemiology

* Usually children (median age 5 years), but can occur in adults

Sites

* Unilateral or bilateral
* Palpebral most common but also limbal and bulbar conjunctiva

Etiology

* Due to mutations in plasminogen PLG gene at 6q26 / plasminogen deficiency ([J Thromb Haemost 2007;5:2315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17900274))
* Autosomal recessive
* Triggered with external irritants, fever, antifibrinolytic therapy and ocular surgery

Clinical features

* Mild discomfort with redness, woody induration of conjunctiva
* Similar lesions in mouth and upper respiratory tract, female genital tract or other mucosal surfaces
* May threaten sight
* Pseudomembrane typically separates from underlying epithelium without bleeding
* Corneal scarring, vascularization, infection or melting in advanced cases

Laboratory

* Decreased serum level of plasminogen type I

Case reports

* 7 month old boy with "sticky eye" ([Digital Journal of Ophthalmology 2006;12(3)](http://www.djo.harvard.edu/print.php?url=/physicians/gr/943&print=1))
* 20 year old woman with prosthetic eye ([Int Ophthalmol 2011;31:25](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20524141))
* 27 year old woman with Crohn's disease ([Clin Ophthalmol 2008;2:203](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19668406))

Treatment

* Excision, but pseudomembrane recurs ([Virchows Arch 2007;451:815](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17701212))
* Topical and systemic FFP treatment and conjunctival membrane excision ([Cornea 2008;27:501](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18434858))
* Topical or systemic plasminogen
* Topical heparin and steroids

Gross description

* Woody accumulation of fibrin

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Membrane contains large hyaline masses of fibrin and immunoglobulin but resembling amyloid
* Also T cells and B cells
* Conjunctival epithelium is thin and replaced focally with necrotic tissue
* Pink eosinophilic amorphous material within subepithelial tissue mimics amyloid

Differential diagnosis

* Adenovirus infection
* [Chlamydia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/eyetrachoma.html)
* [Graft versus host disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/eyeGVHDconjunctiva.html): see [Hum Pathol 1996;27:307](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8600048)
* Klebsiella
* [Streptococcus](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/microbiologystreptococci.html)
* Toxic epidermal necrolysis
