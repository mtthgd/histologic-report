Definition / general

* Usually indolent MALT lymphomas - difficult to predict malignant potential
* Often remains an isolated lesion
* May be preceded by apparently reactive lymphoid hyperplasia
* Mantle cell lymphomas have more aggressive behavior with median survival of 3 - 5 years

Diagnosis

* H&E for high grade lesions
* For low grade lesions, need fresh tissue for flow cytometry or gene rearrangement studies
* Immunohistochemistry (light chain restriction)

Case reports

* 8 year old boy with posttransplant lymphoproliferative disorder ([Am J Ophthalmol 2007;143:1050](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17524778))
* 53 year old woman with T cell lymphoma ([Cornea 2007;26:1147](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17893556))
* 55 year old man with primary diffuse large B cell lymphoma of palpebral conjunctiva ([Can J Ophthalmol 2007;42:630](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17641717))
* 72 year old woman with T cell lymphoma ([Arch Ophthalmol 2002;120:508](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11934328))
* CD5+ low grade marginal zone B cell lymphoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 1998;22:201](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9500221))

Treatment

* MALT: chemoradiation therapy ([Virchows Arch 2006;448:506](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16323006)), possibly antibiotics ([Am J Ophthalmol 2005;140:729](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16226528))

Gross description

* Salmon colored mass or masses

Microscopic (histologic) description

* MALT: well differentiated, monoclonal small B lymphocytes, occasionally exhibits overt monocytoid cytology, prominent plasmacytic features or lymphoepithelial lesions ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:792](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17460465))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* MALT may have t(14;18)(q32;q21) involving IgH and MALT1 genes
* B cell clonality in 55% of MALT and 60% of diffuse large B cell lymphomas ([Mod Pathol 2001;14:641](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11454995))
