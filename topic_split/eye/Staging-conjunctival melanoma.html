Definition / general
  • All melanomas arising from the bulbar and palpebral conjunctiva and the caruncle are covered by this staging system
  • Primary eyelid melanomas and secondary involvement of the conjunctiva by extraocular uveal melanoma are not covered within this chapter; cutaneous melanomas, including those primary to the eyelid, are staged according to the AJCC melanoma of the skin staging system and uveal melanomas that extend to the conjunctiva are staged according to the AJCC uveal melanoma staging system
Essential features
  • AJCC 7th edition staging was sunset on December 31, 2017; as of January 1, 2018, use of the 8th edition is mandatory
Terminology
  • Conjunctiva, along with the caruncle, forms a protective barrier that covers the anterior part of the eye and the inner surface of the eyelids
  • Clinically, the conjunctiva is divided into 4 parts: limbal, bulbar, forniceal and palpebral (StatPearls: Anatomy, Head and Neck, Eye Conjunctiva [Accessed 21 February 2025])
    • Bulbar conjunctiva is the mucosal epithelium lining the eye's surface
    • Palpebral conjunctiva is the mucosal epithelium lining the inner surface of the eyelids
    • Fornix is the flexible fold of conjunctiva where the bulbar and palpebral parts connect, found both superiorly and inferiorly
  • Caruncle is a tissue-like appendage found at the inner corner (canthus) of the eye (Eye (Lond) 2009;23:1004)
ICD coding
  • ICD-10: C69.0 - malignant neoplasm of conjunctiva
Primary tumor (pT)
  • Clinical tumor (cT)
    • cTX: primary tumor cannot be assessed
    • cT0: no evidence of primary tumor
    • cT1: tumor of the bulbar conjunctiva
      • cT1a: < 1 quadrant
      • cT1b: ≥ 1 to < 2 quadrants
      • cT1c: ≥ 2 to < 3 quadrants
      • cT1d: ≥ 3 quadrants
    • cT2: tumor of the nonbulbar (forniceal, palpebral, tarsal) conjunctiva and tumor involving the caruncle
      • cT2a: noncaruncular and ≤ 1 quadrant of the nonbulbar conjunctiva involved
      • cT2b: noncaruncular and > 1 quadrant of the nonbulbar conjunctiva involved
      • cT2c: caruncular and ≤ 1 quadrant of the nonbulbar conjunctiva involved
      • cT2d: caruncular and > 1 quadrant of the nonbulbar conjunctiva involved
    • cT3: tumor of any size with local invasion
      • cT3a: globe
      • cT3b: eyelid
      • cT3c: orbit
      • cT3d: nasolacrimal duct or lacrimal sac or paranasal sinuses
    • cT4: tumor of any size with invasion of the central nervous system
  • Pathological tumor (pT)
    • pTX: primary tumor cannot be assessed
    • pT0: no evidence of primary tumor
    • pTis: melanoma confined to the conjunctival epithelium
    • pT1: tumor of the bulbar conjunctiva
      • pT1a: tumor of the bulbar conjunctiva with invasion of the substantia propria, ≤ 2.0 mm in thickness
      • pT1b: tumor of the bulbar conjunctiva with invasion of the substantia propria, > 2.0 mm in thickness
    • pT2: tumor of the nonbulbar (forniceal, palpebral, tarsal) conjunctiva and tumor involving the caruncle
      • pT2a: tumor of the nonbulbar conjunctiva with invasion of the substantia propria, ≤ 2.0 mm in thickness
      • pT2b: tumor of the nonbulbar conjunctiva with invasion of the substantia propria, > 2.0 mm in thickness
    • pT3: tumor of any size with local invasion
      • pT3a: globe
      • pT3b: eyelid
      • pT3c: orbit
      • pT3d: nasolacrimal duct or lacrimal sac or paranasal sinuses
    • pT4: tumor of any size with invasion of the central nervous system
Regional lymph nodes (pN)
  • pNX: regional lymph nodes cannot be assessed
  • pN0: no regional lymph node metastasis
  • pN1: regional lymph node metastasis

Notes:
  • Regional lymph nodes include the preauricular (parotid), postauricular, submandibular and cervical lymph nodes
Distant metastasis (pM)
  • pM0: no distant metastasis
  • pM1: distant metastasis

Notes:
  • The c designation indicates clinical signs or symptoms, while the p designation indicates that at least 1 distant metastatic site has been histologically confirmed
  • Most common metastatic sites are the lungs, liver, brain and bones
Prefixes
  • y: preoperative radiotherapy or chemotherapy
  • r: recurrent tumor stage
Registry data collection variables
  • Invasive conjunctival melanoma
    • Tumor thickness: infiltration depth (in mm) into the substantia propria from the surface of the conjunctival epithelium
    • Mitotic count: number of mitoses per square millimeter
    • Status of surgical margins
    • Presence or absence of
      • Epithelioid cytomorphology
      • Surface ulceration
      • Growth regression
      • Angio or lymphatic invasion
      • Perineural invasion
      • Adjacent conjunctival melanoma in situ
      • Coexisting conjunctival nevus
      • Microsatellites
  • Lymph node metastases
    • Presence or absence of microscopic satellites / satellite in transit metastases (micronodule / nodule of melanoma
Histologic grade (G)
  • Grading is not performed
Histopathologic type
  • Spindle cell melanoma
  • Epithelioid melanoma
  • Anaplastic melanoma

Board review style question #2
Which of the following lesions should be staged using the conjunctival melanoma staging protocol (AJCC 8th edition)?

  1. Melanoma arising from the nose (skin) and extending to the conjunctiva
  2. Melanoma arising in the anterior, external portion of the eyeball
  3. Melanoma arising in the uvea and extending to the eyeball surface
  4. Sebaceous carcinoma of the conjunctiva
  5. Squamous cell carcinoma of the conjunctiva
Board review style answer #2
B. Melanoma arising in the anterior, external portion of the eyeball. The anterior portion of the eyeball is covered by the bulbar conjunctiva; therefore, the neoplasm would be staged using the conjunctival melanoma staging protocol (AJCC 8th edition). Answer C is incorrect because a melanoma arising from the uvea would be staged according to the uveal melanoma staging protocol. Answer A is incorrect because a cutaneous melanoma would be staged according to the melanoma of the skin protocol. Answers D and E are incorrect because sebaceous carcinoma and squamous cell carcinoma of the conjunctiva would be staged using the conjunctiva carcinoma staging protocol.
