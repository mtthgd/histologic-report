Definition / general
  • All primary carcinomas of the eyelid are covered by this staging system
  • These topics are not covered: carcinomas from other head and neck sites, metastatic carcinomas, eyelid melanoma and Merkel cell carcinoma
Essential features
  • AJCC 7th edition staging was sunset on December 31, 2017; as of January 1, 2018, use of the 8th edition is mandatory
Terminology
  • Tarsus: main structural component of eyelid; made of dense connective tissue and contains Meibomian glands and eyelash follicles (StatPearls: Anatomy, Head and Neck, Eyelid [Accessed 18 December 2024)
  • Eyelid margin: the mucocutaneous junction between the eyelid skin and conjunctiva (Eagle: Eye Pathology - An Atlas and Text, 3rd Edition, 2016)

Primary tumor (pT)
  • pTX: cannot be assessed
  • pT0: no evidence of primary tumor
  • pTis: carcinoma in situ
  • pT1: tumor ≤ 10 mm in greatest dimension
    • pT1a: tumor does not invade the tarsal plate or eyelid margin
    • pT1b: tumor invades the tarsal plate or eyelid margin
    • pT1c: tumor involves full thickness of the eyelid
  • pT2: tumor > 10 mm but ≤ 20 mm in greatest dimension
    • pT2a: tumor does not invade the tarsal plate or eyelid margin
    • pT2b: tumor invades the tarsal plate or eyelid margin
    • pT2c: tumor involves full thickness of the eyelid
  • pT3: tumor > 20 mm but ≤ 30 mm in greatest dimension
    • pT3a: tumor does not invade the tarsal plate or eyelid margin
    • pT3b: tumor invades the tarsal plate or eyelid margin
    • pT3c: tumor involves full thickness of the eyelid
  • pT4: any eyelid tumor that invades adjacent ocular, orbital or facial structures
    • pT4a: tumor invades ocular or intraorbital structures
    • pT4b: tumor invades (or erodes through) the bony walls of the orbit or extends to the paranasal sinuses or invades the lacrimal sac / nasolacrimal duct or brain
Regional lymph nodes (pN)
  • pNX: regional lymph nodes cannot be assessed
  • pN0: no evidence of lymph node involvement
  • pN1: metastasis in a single ipsilateral regional lymph node, ≤ 3 cm in greatest dimension
    • pN1a: metastasis in a single ipsilateral lymph node based on clinical evaluation or imaging findings
    • pN1b: metastasis in a single ipsilateral lymph node based on lymph node biopsy
  • pN2: metastasis in a single ipsilateral lymph node, > 3 cm in greatest dimension or in bilateral or contralateral lymph nodes
    • pN2a: metastasis documented based on clinical evaluation or imaging findings
    • pN2b: metastasis documented based on microscopic findings on lymph node biopsy
Distant metastasis (pM)
  • pM0: no distant metastasis
  • pM1: distant metastasis
Prefixes
  • y: preoperative radiotherapy or chemotherapy
  • r: recurrent tumor stage

Registry data collection variables
  • Tumor size (greatest dimension in millimeters)
  • Specific anatomic location (e.g., upper eyelid, lower eyelid, both eyelids, medial canthus, lateral canthus)
  • Tumor thickness (depth of invasion)
  • Presence / absence of perineural invasion
  • Presence / absence of lymphovascular invasion
  • Mitotic figures per square millimeter
  • Microsatellite instability markers for sebaceous carcinoma
  • Sentinel node biopsy status and number of sentinel lymph nodes (if applicable)
  • History of HIV infection
  • History of solid organ transplant
  • History of Muir-Torre syndrome
  • History of xeroderma pigmentosum

Board review style question #1


A squamous cell carcinoma of the eyelid is excised with negative margins. The tumor size in greatest dimension measures 12 mm and the tumor does not invade the tarsal plate or eyelid margin. What is the appropriate primary tumor (T) stage?

  1. Tis
  2. T1a
  3. T2a
  4. T3a
  5. T4a
Board review style answer #1
C. T2a. Squamous cell carcinoma of the eyelid measuring > 10 mm but ≤ 20 mm is a T2. More specifically, this tumor would be categorized as a T2a due to the lack of invasion to the tarsal plate of the eyelid. Answer A is incorrect because stage Tis represents an in situ lesion. Answer B is incorrect because T1 tumors are ≤ 10 mm in greatest dimension. Answer D is incorrect because T3 tumors are > 20 mm but ≤ 30 mm in greatest dimension. Answer E is incorrect because T4 tumors invade adjacent ocular, orbital or facial structures.

Board review style question #2
Which entity would be assessed using the eyelid carcinoma staging criteria as defined by the AJCC (8th edition)?

  1. Diffuse large B cell lymphoma
  2. Infiltrative basal cell carcinoma
  3. Melanoma
  4. Merkel cell carcinoma
  5. Squamous cell carcinoma of the cheek with direct extension to the eyelid
Board review style answer #2
B. Infiltrative basal cell carcinoma. Carcinomas of the eyelid, including basal cell carcinoma, are staged using the eyelid carcinoma staging system (AJCC 8th edition). Answer E is incorrect because cutaneous carcinoma of the head and neck extending to the eyelid is staged with the cutaneous carcinoma of the head and neck classification system. Answer A is incorrect because diffuse large B cell lymphoma (DLBCL) is not considered carcinoma. If DLBCL arose from the eye adnexa and extended into the eyelid, it would be staged using the AJCC Ocular Adnexal Lymphoma staging protocol. Answer D is incorrect because Merkel cell carcinoma is staged using the AJCC Skin Merkel Cell Carcinoma staging protocol. Answer C is incorrect because melanoma is not classified as carcinoma and is staged based on whether it arises from the conjunctiva (using the AJCC Conjunctival Melanoma staging protocol) or from the eyelid skin itself (using the AJCC Melanoma of Skin staging protocol).
