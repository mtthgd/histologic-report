Definition / general

* Fluid filled cyst with ciliated lining adjacent to fallopian tube

Essential features

* Ciliated cyst adjacent to fallopian tube
* Typically asymptomatic
* Almost always benign, with rare reports of borderline tumor

Terminology

* Paraovarian cyst
* Hydatid cyst
* Not recommended: hydatid of Morgagni

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [N83.8](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/N00-N99/N80-N98/N83-/N83.8) - other noninflammatory disorders of ovary, fallopian tube and broad ligament

Epidemiology

* Common benign incidental finding (~7 - 10% of women) ([J Pediatr Surg 2011;46:2161](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22075350))
* All age groups, most commonly third to fifth decade

Sites

* Paratubal (between fallopian tube and ovary)

Pathophysiology

* Unknown

Etiology

* Believed to originate from mesothelium or be remnant of Müllerian duct or Wolffian duct

Clinical features

* Most cysts are small and asymptomatic (Size of paratubal cysts may correlate with obesity ([J Pediatr Adolesc Gynecol 2017;30:571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28456695))
* May be found during surgery or incidentally on radiological study performed for another reason
* Larger lesions may become symptomatic, causing pressure or pain ([J Pediatr Surg 2011;46:2161](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22075350))
* May lead to torsion of adnexa, resulting in acute pain ([J Pediatr Surg 2011;46:2161](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22075350))

Diagnosis

* Typically noted incidentally on intraoperative or gross examination

Radiology description

* While not often diagnosed on imaging, paratubal cysts are unilocular and anechoic or hypoechoic on ultrasound ([J Clin Endocrinol Metab 2000;85:1021](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10720033))

Prognostic factors

* Paratubal cysts are benign
* Rarely gives rise to [serous borderline tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorserousborderline.html) or even more rarely malignancy ([Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2017;25:e21](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28277338))

Case reports

* 15 year old girl with giant paratubal serous cystadenoma ([J Pediatr Adolesc Gynecol 2020;33:438](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32251838))
* 17 year old girl with paratubal borderline tumor ([J Pediatr Adolesc Gynecol 2016;29:74](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26026220))
* 34 year old nulligravid woman with paratubal cyst and bilateral hydrosalpinges diagnosed during evaluation of infertility ([Taiwan J Obstet Gynecol 2014;53:239](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25017276))

Treatment

* Surgical excision of the paratubal cyst represents definitive treatment for symptomatic patients ([J Clin Endocrinol Metab 2000;85:1021](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10720033))
* Benign follow up is expected; treatment is not required for asymptomatic patients

Gross description

* Simple fluid filled cyst(s) near fallopian tube

Sample pathology report

* Fallopian tubes, bilateral salpingectomy:
  + Benign fallopian tubes with paratubal cysts

Differential diagnosis

* [Endometriotic cyst](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarynontumorendometriosis.html):
  + Associated with endometrial type stroma with or without hemosiderin laden macrophages
* [Serous cystadenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorserousbenign.html):
  + Histologically identical to paratubal cyst but > 1 cm in size
* **Hydrosalpinx**:
  + Dilation of the fallopian tube lumen with attenuation of the tubal epithelium with or without diminished plicae
  + Distinction based largely on location of the cystic space within rather than near the fallopian tube
