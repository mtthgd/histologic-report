Definition / general

* Turning of tubes inside out, back into vaginal vault or even more rarely into bladder or uterine cavity

Epidemiology

* Rare (less than 100 cases reported), ~80% occur after vaginal hysterectomy (0.5% incidence), presumed due to nonclosure of vaginal vault
* 0.06% incidence after abdominal hystertecomy, less frequent with interposition or colpotomy
* Associated factors include postoperative fever, hematoma formation / failure to maintain adequate hemostasis, poor physical condition, insufficient preoperative vaginal preparation, difficultly with surgical procedure, use of intraperitoneal vaginal drains and packs

Clinical features

* Occurs two months to eight years after surgery
* May present with watery vaginal discharge, lower abdominal pain, dyspareunia

Case reports

* 27 year old woman with peritoneovaginal fistula ([Can J Urol 2010;17:5117](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20398452))
* 35 year old woman ([J Midlife Health 2012;3:40](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22923979))
* Prolapse of fallopian tube into urinary bladder, resembling carcinoma ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1983;107:613](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6688725))

Treatment

* Surgical excision

Gross description

* Polypoid mass resembling granulation tissue, visible at vaginal apex

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Normal ciliated fallopian tube epithelium with variable surrounding fibrosis, edema, chronic inflammation

Cytology description

* May be seen on Pap smears
* Columnar cells with cilia and sheets of cells with small granular uniform nuclei in an orderly arrangement; also inflammatory cells and reactive atypia ([Diagn Cytopathol 2013;41:146](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21656703))

Differential diagnosis

* [Carcinoma (well differentiated)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/fallopiantubescarcinoma.html): has glands at odd locations
* **[Cysts](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/fallopiantubesparatubalcyst.html) of mesonephric and paramesonephric duct**
* [Endometriosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarynontumorendometriosis.html)
* [Vaginal adenosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/vaginaladenosis.html)
