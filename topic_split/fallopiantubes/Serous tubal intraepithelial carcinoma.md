Definition / general

* Lesion that is limited to the fallopian tube epithelium and a precursor to extrauterine (pelvic) high grade serous carcinoma

Essential features

* Confined to epithelium
* Histologic features: significant atypia, architectural alterations, high proliferative index and mutant pattern of p53 staining
* Important precursor lesion to recognize, as it is a criterion for assigning fallopian tube as primary site of high grade tubo-ovarian serous carcinoma irrespective of presence and size of ovarian and peritoneal disease ([Histopathology 2015;67:331](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25640750))

Terminology

* Various terms used to describe a spectrum of tubal epithelial alterations:
  + Secretory cell outgrowths (SCOUTs): secretory cell expansion with variable ciliation (type 1 / tubal differentiation and type 2 / endometrioid differentiation), wild type p53 staining
  + p53 signature: histologically normal epithelium (at least 12 cells) with mutant pattern p53 staining and low proliferation index (MIB1) (typically less than 10%)
  + Serous tubal intraepithelial lesion (STIL): abnormal histology (high N/C but preserved polarity), mutant p53 and variable MIB1; STIL is regarded as a lesion of uncertain significance and diagnostic features fall short of STIC
  + Serous tubal intraepithelial carcinoma (STIC): abnormal histology (high N/C, loss of polarity, lack of ciliated cells), mutant pattern p53 staining and high proliferation index (MIB1)

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [N83.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/N00-N99/N80-N98/N83-/N83.9) - fallopian tube disorder

Epidemiology

* Patients with hereditary *BRCA* mutation have a high risk of high grade serous carcinoma
* Incidence of STIC in risk reducing salpingo-oophorectomies in *BRCA*+ women is 5 - 8% ([Gynecol Oncol 2006;100:58](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16137750))

Sites

* Fallopian tube fimbria is the most common site of origin

Pathophysiology

* Secretory epithelium of fallopian tube is postulated as the cell of origin ([Adv Anat Pathol 2010;17:293](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20733351))

Clinical features

* Usually discovered incidentally during routine surgery or risk reducing prophylactic salpingo-oophorectomies
* May be seen adjacent to invasive carcinoma
* Reference: [Gynecol Oncol 2017;146:69](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28479065)

Diagnosis

* Careful morphologic evaluation is key to the diagnosis; immunohistochemistry (p53 and MIB1) is only supportive
* Gross protocol (sectioning and extensively examining the fimbria [SEE-FIM]) influences ability to diagnose ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:230](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16434898)):
  + Amputation and longitudinal sectioning of the infundibulum and fimbrial segment (distal 2 cm) allows maximal exposure of the tubal plicae; isthmus and ampulla are cut transversely at 2 - 3 mm intervals
  + Entire fallopian tube should be submitted for evaluation in women with *BRCA* mutations or strong family history of ovarian carcinoma
  + Extensively examine the fimbriated end
  + Controversial role of multiple levels with 1 study finding no impact and another study reporting 25% missed with a single section ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:1878](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19898224), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2013;32:353](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23722507))
* STIC is staged as pathologic stage pT1

Case reports

* 48 year old woman with STIC in cervical smears ([Diagn Cytopathol 2018;46:945](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30375200))
* 51 year old postmenopausal woman with STIC and *BRCA1* mutation ([Jpn J Clin Oncol 2014;44:597](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24719479))
* 55 year old woman with synchronous papillary serous carcinoma of ovary and endometrium and STIC ([Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2017;60:125](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28195111))
* 56 year old woman with incidental STIC ([Case Rep Obstet Gynecol 2015;2015:760429](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25802782))
* 58 year old woman with retroperitoneal high grade serous carcinoma and STIC ([Diagn Pathol 2019;14:17](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30744657))

Treatment

* No current consensus among gynecologic oncologists regarding appropriate management of incidental STIC
* Patients have been managed by surgical staging / peritoneal washings / chemotherapy or follow up without additional intervention
* Yield of surgical staging is low and short term clinical outcomes are favorable
* Individualized management is warranted until additional data become available ([Int J Gynecol Cancer 2013;23:1603](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24172097))

Gross description

* Not evident on gross examination

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Epithelial stratification
* Lack of ciliated cells ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:710](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28106106))
* High N/C ratio
* Nuclear pleomorphism, hyperchromasia, prominent nucleoli
* Loss of polarity (may be focal)
* Epithelial fractures ([Adv Anat Pathol 2010;17:293](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20733351))
* Exfoliation of small epithelial clusters

Cytology description

* Brush cytology shows clusters of cells with haphazard pleomorphic nuclei, high N/C ratio and lack of cilia ([Cancer Cytopathol 2019;127:192](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30861338))

Positive stains

* [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html): mutant pattern of staining (diffusely positive (nuclear), completely negative (null type) or less commonly, cytoplasmic staining)
* [Ki67 / MIB](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) index: high (suggested cutoff of 10%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:1766](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21989347), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2012;31:243](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22498942))

Sample pathology report

* Bilateral fallopian tubes, salpingectomy:
  + Right fallopian tube with serous tubal intraepithelial carcinoma (see comment)
  + Left fallopian tube with no pathologic change
  + Comment: Immunostain for p53 is diffusely and strongly positive in the lesional cells. Ki67 index is > 10%.

Differential diagnosis

* **p53 signature**:
  + Precursor lesion which shows normal histology with [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) mutant pattern of staining
* [Transitional cell metaplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/fallopiantubestransitionalcellmetaplasiafimbriae.html):
  + Uniform cells with nuclear grooves; [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) wild type ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18830124))
* **Florid epithelial hyperplasia**  and **reactive atypia** associated with [acute salpingitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/fallopiantubessalpingitis.html):
  + Has a background of inflammation, retained polarity and presence of ciliated cells; [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) wild type
* **Intramucosal fallopian tube metastasis** from [uterine serous carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusserous.html):
  + May closely mimic STIC; usually [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html) negative ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:161](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27776011))

Which fallopian tube lesion appears normal on histology but shows a mutant pattern of p53 immunostaining?

1. p53 signature
2. Reactive atypia
3. Secretory cell outgrowth (SCOUT)
4. Serous tubal intraepithelial carcinoma (STIC)

Board review style answer #1

**A.** p53 signature
