Definition / general

* Hyperplasia of tubal epithelium, variable atypia

Terminology

* Also known as pseudocarcinomatous hyperplasia

Epidemiology

* Typically present in women with estrogen producing ovarian tumors ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 1989;8:214](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2767870))
* May also be associated with salpingitis and PID ([Am J Surg Pathol 1994;18:1125](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7943533))

Clinical features

* Often incidental finding

Gross description

* No specific gross findings

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Papillary, gland-like or cribriform patterns, lined by epithelial cells with mild to moderate nuclear pleomorphism, loss of polarity, stratification, crowding, hyperchromasia and scattered mitotic figures; no invasion
* May be associated with severe inflammation and scarring mimicking adenocarcinoma

Differential diagnosis

* [Carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/fallopiantubescarcinoma.html): typically grossly evident, older age, solid epithelial proliferation, brisk mitotic activity, severe atypia, possible invasion
