Definition / general

* 50 - 90% of primary heart tumors in children
* Usually discovered in patients less than 1 year of age, rarely in fetuses ([Ital Heart J 2002;3:48](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11899590))
* May obstruct valvular orifice or cardiac chamber; may present with sudden cardiac death ([Pediatr Dev Pathol 2007;10:129](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17378684))
* Not a true neoplasm, but a hamartoma or malformation due to mutation in TSC1 and TSC2 genes
* 50% of patients have tuberous sclerosis; sporadic cases are occasionally associated with congenital heart disease

Case reports

* 2 year old boy with tuberous sclerosis ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2002;126:1559](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12503590))
* 13 year old boy with diffuse rhabdomyomatosis ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1977;101:78](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/576393))

Treatment

* Many tumors regress spontaneously
* Excision if left ventricular outflow tract obstruction or refractory arrhythmia

Gross description

* Small, firm, gray-white, well circumscribed myocardial masses (often multiple) that protrude into ventricles
* Average size is 3 - 4 cm, up to 10 cm, particularly in sporadic cases
* ***Rhabdomyomatosis***: numerous miliary nodules less than 1 mm

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Clear cells and large, rounded, polygonal cells ("spider cells") with glycogen vacuoles separated by strands of cytoplasm extending between cell membrane and nucleus
* No mitotic activity
* ***Adult tumors:*** more cellular with smaller cells, few spider cells and more cellular proliferation ([Hum Pathol 2002;33:1092](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12454813))

Electron microscopy description

* Altered myocytes with abundant glycogen, small and sparse mitochondria
* Cellular junctions resembling intercalated disks are extensive and randomly distributed, not just at poles of cell as in normal myocytes ([Hum Pathol 1977;8:700](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/924433))

Differential diagnosis

* [Glycogen storage disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liverglycogenstoragedisease.html): no well formed nodules, cells have intercalated disks at poles by EM
* [Granular cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuegranularcell.html): epicardial, no vacuoles, no myofibers, S100+, desmin-, myoglobin-
* Histiocytoid myopathy: small tumor nodules, finely granular cells, no large vacuoles, no spider cells; a variant of rhabdomyomatosis
* [Lipoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueadiposelipoma.html): usually epicardial, no myofibers, no glycogen
