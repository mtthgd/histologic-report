Definition / general

* Also called Canale-Smith syndrome
* First named in 1995 ([Cell 1995;81:935](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7540117))
* Inherited disorder due to defects in Fas/CD95/Apo-1 mediated apoptosis ([OMIM 601859: Autoimmune Lymphoproliferative Syndrome [Accessed 28 June 2018]](http://www.omim.org/entry/601859))
* Childhood onset of lymphadenopathy, hepatosplenomegaly, hypergammaglobulinemia and autoimmunity; also cytopenias and increased risk of lymphoma ([Hematology 2006;11:15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16522544))
* 40% have histologic features of sinus histiocytosis with massive lymphadenopathy ([Am J Surg Pathol 2005;29:903](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15958855))
* Type I: functional defects of FAS gene
* Type II: functional FAS deficiency but no FAS gene mutations

Case reports

* 6 month old girl with cervical lymphadenopathy, hepatosplenomegaly and gamma/delta+ T cell blasts ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:546](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12657942))
* 4 year old girl with type II syndrome and clonal immunoblasts but no evidence of lymphoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 1999;23:829](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10403307))
* 11 year old girl with case due to FasL mutation ([Blood 2006;108:1306](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16627752))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Paracortical hyperplasia, expanded interfollicular areas; polyclonal plasmacytosis ([Am J Pathol 1998;153:1541](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9811346))
* Frequent mitotic figures
* Often florid follicular hyperplasia with focal progressive transformation of germinal centers
* Occasional follicular involution

Positive stains

* ***T cells*** - [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html), [CD57](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd57.html), usually alpha/beta T cell receptor; also perforin, **TIA1**, [CD57](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd57.html)

Negative stains

* ***T cells*** - [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html), [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html); also [CD25](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20to29.html#cd25), [CD45RO](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd40.html#cd45ro)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Mutation in death domain of FAS gene
