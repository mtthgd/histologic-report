Definition / general

* Due to congenital enzymatic defect of NADPH oxidase in granulocytes and monocytes
* White blood cells cannot generate superoxide ion which kills microorganisms in lysosomes
* Either Y linked or autosomal recessive
* Diagnose with nitro blue tetrazolium test (NBT) (almost always negative) or flow cytometric dihydrorhodamine-1,2,3 (DHR) test
* Clinically, patients have recurrent lymphadenitis, hepatosplenomegaly, skin rash, pulmonary edema

Diagnosis

* While some physicians still use the NBT test, it has been largely replaced by the flow cytometric DHR test ([J Clin Med 2024;13:4435](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/39124702))
  + In CGD patients, due to defective NADPH oxidase, neutrophils cannot generate ROS, so NBT does not turn blue, making the test negative
  + The DHR assay is considered the current gold standard test of NADPH oxidase activity, allowing quantitative measurement of neutrophil oxidative burst activity

Laboratory

* Anemia, leukocytosis, hypergammaglobulinemia

Case reports

* Child with Hansenula polymorpha infection ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1980;104:290](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6892867))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Granulomas with central necrosis in lymph nodes and other organs
* Pigment laden histiocytes
