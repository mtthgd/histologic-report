Definition / general

* Neoplastic hematolymphoid cells circulating in the peripheral blood

Essential features

* Morphologic identification of neoplastic hematolymphoid cells on peripheral blood smears

Small lymphocytes

* [Chronic lymphocytic leukemia (CLL) / small lymphocytic lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomacll.html)
  + Mature B cell neoplasm
  + Monomorphic small lymphocytes with a minor proportion of prolymphocytes
  + Round to slightly irregular nuclear contours
  + Clumped chromatin
  + Scant amount of pale blue cytoplasm
  + Smudge cells are often seen
  + [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html)+, [CD19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd19.html)+, [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html)+ (dim), [CD23](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd23.html)+, [CD79a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd79a.html)+, [LEF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsLEF1.html)+, [CD200](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd200.html)+, [FMC7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfmc7.html)-, [cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html)-, [SOX11](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox11.html)-, [kappa or lambda surface immunoglobulin light chain](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainskappa.html) (dim)
  + *IGHV* genes are mutated in the majority of cases, while del13q is the most common cytogenetic finding
  + See [figure 1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/hematologyneoplasticWBC.html#hematologyneoplasticWBCperipheralsmear)
* [Mantle cell lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomamcl.html)
  + Mature B cell neoplasm
  + Monomorphic small - intermediate sized lymphocytes
  + Irregular nuclear contours (indented, folded or cleaved)
  + Moderately clumped chromatin
  + Scant amount of pale blue cytoplasm
  + Morphologically indistinguishable from CLL cells
  + Prolymphocytic-like morphology is common during leukemic phase
  + [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html)+, [CD19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd19.html)+, [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html)+, [cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html)+, [SOX11](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox11.html)+, [CD200](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd200.html)-, [CD23](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd23.html)-, [FMC7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfmc7.html)+
  + Associated with t(11;14)(q13;q32) / *CCND1::IGH* translocation (95%)
  + See [figure 2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/hematologyneoplasticWBC.html#hematologyneoplasticWBCperipheralsmear)
* [Follicular lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomafollicularnodalusual.html)
  + Mature B cell neoplasm
  + Irregular nuclear contour with frequent indentations, folding, angulations and cleaved nuclei
  + Moderately clumped chromatin
  + Occasional small nucleoli
  + Scant amount of basophilic cytoplasm
  + [CD19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd19.html)+, [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html)+, [CD79a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd79a.html)+, [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html)+, [FMC7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfmc7.html)+, [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html)-, [CD23](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd23.html)-
  + Genetically characterized by t(14;18)(q32;q21), a translocation resulting in fusion of the *IGH* and *BCL2* genes
  + See [figure 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/hematologyneoplasticWBC.html#hematologyneoplasticWBCperipheralsmear)
* [Hairy cell leukemia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomahcl.html)
  + Mature B cell neoplasm
  + Monomorphic small to intermediate sized lymphocytes
  + Smooth chromatin with inconspicuous nucleoli
  + Moderate amount of pale blue cytoplasm classically seen with fine, hair-like projections
  + [CD19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd19.html)+, [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html)+ (bright), [TRAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstrap.html)+, [CD11c](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10to19.html#cd11c)+, [CD25](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20to29.html#cd25)+, [CD103](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd100.html#cd103)+, [CD200](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd200.html)+, annexin A1+ (most specific), [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html)-, [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html)- (80 - 90%), [CD23](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd23.html)-
  + Splenic B cell lymphoma / leukemia with prominent nucleoli (WHO 5th edition) and hairy cell leukemia variant (ICC 2022), a chronic lymphoproliferative disorder whose morphologic characters resemble hairy cell leukemia; neoplastic cells have prominent nucleoli and are negative for HCL markers (CD25, annexin A1 and TRAP)
  + *BRAF* V600E mutation is found in most (> 90%) classic hairy cell leukemia cases
  + Associated with pancytopenia and splenomegaly
  + See [figure 4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/hematologyneoplasticWBC.html#hematologyneoplasticWBCperipheralsmear)
* Reference: [Leuk Lymphoma 2015;56:2000](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25315078), [Leukemia 2022;36:1720](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35732829), [Blood 2022;140:1229](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35653592)

Intermediate sized cells

* [T cell prolymphocytic leukemia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBTcellpro.html)
  + Mature T cell neoplasm
  + Small to intermediate sized lymphocytes
  + Round to irregular nuclei
  + Distinct nucleoli
  + Basophilic cytoplasm with cytoplasmic blebs
  + [CD2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1.html#cd2)+, [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html)+, [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html)+, [CD7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1.html#cd7)+, [TdT](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstdt.html)-, [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html)+/-, [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html)+/-, [TCL1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstcl1.html)+
  + Most cases are found with inv(14)(q11q32) and *TCL1* gene rearrangement detectable by FISH, while deletion or mutations in *ATM* have also been observed
  + Aggressive disease
  + See [figure 5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/hematologyneoplasticWBC.html#hematologyneoplasticWBCperipheralsmear)
* [Sézary syndrome](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonbsezary.html)
  + Mature T cell neoplasm
  + Medium sized to larger lymphocytes
  + Irregular nuclear contour, often described as cerebriform pattern
  + Condensed chromatin
  + Inconspicuous nucleoli
  + Abundant basophilic cytoplasm
  + [CD2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1.html#cd2)+, [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html)+, [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html)+, [TCRαβ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstcralphabeta.html)+, CLA+, usually [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html)+, [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html)-, [CD7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1.html#cd7)- and [CD26](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20to29.html#cd26)-
  + T cell receptor gene rearrangements are present; recurrent balanced chromosomal translocations have not been identified
  + Aggressive form of mycosis fungoides
  + See [figure 6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/hematologyneoplasticWBC.html#hematologyneoplasticWBCperipheralsmear)
* [Adult T cell leukemia / lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBatlv.html)
  + Mature T cell neoplasm, caused by human retrovirus HTLV1
  + Several morphological variants have been identified: small, medium, large cell and anaplastic variants
  + Convoluted and polylobated nuclei, often described as flower cells
  + Condensed chromatin
  + Small or inconspicuous nucleoli
  + Moderate amount of basophilic cytoplasm
  + [CD2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1.html#cd2)+, [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html)+, [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html)+, [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html)+/- and [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html)-/+, [CD25](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20to29.html#cd25)+, [CD7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1.html#cd7)-
  + Clonal T cell receptor gene rearrangement
  + Associated with hypercalcemia
  + See [figure 7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/hematologyneoplasticWBC.html#hematologyneoplasticWBCperipheralsmear)
* Reference: [Am J Hematol 2019;94:1027](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31313347)

Plasma cells

* [Multiple myeloma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomamyeloma.html) - [plasma cell leukemia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaplasmacellleukemia.html) (primary or secondary)
  + Plasma cell neoplasm
  + Medium sized neoplastic cells
  + Eccentric round nucleus
  + Coarse chromatin, often seen in a clockface pattern
  + Abundant amount of pale to basophilic cytoplasm with perinuclear clearing
  + According to the 2021 International Myeloma Working Group recommendation, primary plasma cell leukemia is defined as "5% or more circulating plasma cells in peripheral blood smears in patients otherwise diagnosed with symptomatic multiple myeloma" ([Blood Cancer J 2021;11:192](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34857730))
  + [CD38](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd38.html)+, [CD138](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd138.html)+, [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html)+ (80%), [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html)-, [CD19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd19.html)- (95%)
  + Immunoglobulin heavy and light chain gene rearrangement
  + Associated with Rouleaux formation of RBCs and monoclonal gammopathy
  + See [figure 8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/hematologyneoplasticWBC.html#hematologyneoplasticWBCperipheralsmear)
* Reference: [Blood Rev 2011;25:107](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21295388)

Large lymphocytes

* [Diffuse large B cell lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomadiffuse.html) (example: CLL status post-Richter transformation)
  + Mature B cell neoplasm
  + Large sized neoplastic cells
  + Irregular nuclei
  + Dispersed chromatin
  + Inconspicuous to prominent nucleoli
  + Abundant amount of cytoplasm
  + [CD19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd19.html)+, [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html)+, [CD79a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd79a.html)+, [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html)+ (30 - 60%), [BCL6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl6.html)+ (60 - 90%), [MUM1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmum1.html)+ (35 - 65%)
  + See [figure 9](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/hematologyneoplasticWBC.html#hematologyneoplasticWBCperipheralsmear)
* [T cell large granular lymphocytic leukemia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBtLGL.html)
  + Mature cytotoxic T cell neoplasm
  + Large sized lymphocytes
  + Irregular nuclear contour
  + Coarse chromatin
  + Indistinct nucleoli
  + Abundant clear cytoplasm with azurophilic granules; cytologically indistinguishable from natural killer cells (CD3-)
  + [CD2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1.html#cd2)+, [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html)+, [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html)-, [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html)+, [CD57](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd57.html)+, [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html) variable, [CD16](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10to19.html#cd16)+ (variable), loss of [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html) or [CD7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1.html#cd7) is common
  + Clonal T cell receptor gene rearrangement
  + Indolent disease; associated with cytopenia, particularly neutropenia, and rheumatoid arthritis
  + See [figure 10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/hematologyneoplasticWBC.html#hematologyneoplasticWBCperipheralsmear)
* Reference: [Autops Case Rep 2016;6:41](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27284540)

Blasts

* Myeloid: acute myeloid leukemia (example: [AML with inv(16)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/leukemiainv16.html))
  + Immature myeloid neoplasm
  + Medium to large sized myeloblasts
  + Round to irregular or folded nuclei
  + Dispersed chromatin
  + Distinct nucleoli
  + Moderately abundant cytoplasm
  + Auer rods present in some cases, abundant in acute promyelocytic leukemia
  + Typically, [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)+ and [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)+, may exhibit granulocytic lineage (positive for [CD13](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd13.html), [CD33](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd33.html), [CD15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd15.html), [CD65](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd60.html#cd65) and [MPO](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyeloperoxidase.html)) or monocytic lineage (positive for [CD14](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10to19.html#cd14), [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html), [CD11c](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10to19.html#cd11c), [CD64](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd60.html#cd64), [CD36](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30to39.html#cd36) and [lysozyme](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainslysozyme.html))
  + See [figure 11](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/hematologyneoplasticWBC.html#hematologyneoplasticWBCperipheralsmear) showing an AML with inv(16)(p13.1;q22)
* Lymphoid: B lymphoblastic leukemia / lymphoma (example: [B ALL with *KMT2A* rearrangement](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/leukemiapreballMLL.html))
  + Immature lymphoid neoplasm
  + Intermediate sized lymphoblasts
  + Round to irregular nuclei
  + Dispersed chromatin
  + Distinct nucleoli
  + Scant amount of basophilic cytoplasm
  + Typically, [CD19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd19.html)+, [CD22](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20to29.html#cd22)+, [CD79a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd79a.html)+, [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html)+, [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html) (moderate intensity) and variable expression of [TdT](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstdt.html) and [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)
  + Recurrent genetic abnormalities include t(9;22)(q34.1;q11.2) *BCR::ABL1*, t(v;11q23.3) *KMT2A* rearranged, t(12;21)(p13.2;q22.1) *ETV::RUNX1*, t(5;14)(q31.1;q32.1) *IGH::IL3* or t(1;19)(q23;p13.3) *TCF3::PBX1*
  + See [figure 12](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/hematologyneoplasticWBC.html#hematologyneoplasticWBCperipheralsmear)
* Blast equivalents (promonocytes) (example: [acute myelomonocytic leukemia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/leukemiam4.html))
  + Immature monocytic precursors that are counted towards the total blast count in monocytic and myelomonocytic neoplasms
  + Irregular and convoluted nuclear contours with fine folds
  + Fine chromatin
  + Distinct nucleoli
  + Moderate amount of gray to pale blue cytoplasm with occasional small vacuoles
  + Immature monocytic precursor cells are usually [CD14](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10to19.html#cd14)+/-, [CD64](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd60.html#cd64)+, [CD15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd15.html)+, [CD11b](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10to19.html#cd11b)+, [CD11c](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10to19.html#cd11c)+, [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html)+, [CD36](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30to39.html#cd36)+, [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html)+, [CD163](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd160.html#cd163)+ and [lysozyme](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainslysozyme.html)+
  + Nonspecific cytogenetic abnormalities
  + See [figure 13](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/hematologyneoplasticWBC.html#hematologyneoplasticWBCperipheralsmear)
  + Reference: [StatPearls: Leukemia [Accessed 11 January 2023]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32809325)

Board review style question #1

Which of the following lymphoproliferative disorders is associated with human retrovirus HTLV1?

1. Adult T cell leukemia / lymphoma
2. Hairy cell leukemia
3. Sézary syndrome
