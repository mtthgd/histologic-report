Definition / general

* Progressive hereditary cystic kidney disease due to various known mutations, with most present in genes coding for polycystin 1 (*PKD1*, chromosome 16p, most common) and polycystin 2 (*PKD2*, chromosome 4q) ([Lancet 2019;393:919](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30819518))
* End stage kidney disease in ~50% of cases by the age of 70, with a minority needing transplant ([Can J Kidney Health Dis 2018;5:2054358118778568](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29977583))

Essential features

* Progressive hereditary autosomal dominant polycystic kidney disease (ADPKD) with several known associated gene mutations that may predict prognosis
* Associated with *PKD1* mutations (85% of cases) and *PKD2* mutations (~10% of cases and milder disease)
* Symptoms generally become apparent in the third to fourth decade
* Often progresses to end stage kidney disease, requiring transplant

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [Q61.2](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/Q00-Q99/Q60-Q64/Q61-/Q61.2) - polycystic kidney, autosomal dominant

Epidemiology

* Prevalence of 1 - 2:1,000 births
* Occurs in all races
* M = F but tends to be more severe in men ([Am J Kidney Dis 2000;35:1072](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10845819))

Sites

* Cystic disease occurrence
  + Kidneys
  + Liver: most common nonkidney site; higher in females
  + Less common sites: pancreas, seminal vesicles, spleen, ovaries
* Noncystic disease sites ([Adv Chronic Kidney Dis 2010;17:173](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20219620))
  + Heart: left ventricular hypertrophy, mitral valve prolapse
  + Intracranial aneurysms: most severe extrarenal manifestation

Pathophysiology

* Polycystin 1 and 2 are membrane proteins in renal tubule epithelial cells that help regulate cellular transport
* Mutation in these proteins leads to impaired metabolism of renal tubular epithelial cells, which is thought to cause cellular proliferation ([Prim Care 2020;47:673](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33121636))
* Cystogenesis occurs after a 2 hit process, which is progressive but usually clinically silent for decades ([Prim Care 2020;47:673](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33121636))
* Normal renal function is usually maintained until fifth or sixth decade

Etiology

* Autosomal dominant mutations mostly present in genes *PDK1* or *PKD2*
* Sporadic cases without known family history are rare

Clinical features

* May be asymptomatic
* Abdominal pain, hypertension, hematuria, renal failure ([Kidney Int 2015;88:17](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25786098))
* Increased risk of urinary tract infections and calculi ([GeneReviews: Polycystic Kidney Disease, Autosomal Dominant [Accessed 1 July 2024]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20301424))
* May present with symptoms due to cysts in other organs (liver, pancreas, spleen) ([Kidney Int 2015;88:17](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25786098))

Diagnosis

* Ultrasound is useful for the initial evaluation, due to low cost and availability
* Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) or computed tomography (CT) detects much smaller cysts and is better for younger people with earlier disease
* Diagnosis can be made with 100% specificity / sensitivity with a family history in combination with sufficient radiological findings ([Lancet 2019;393:919](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30819518))
* Imaging is not as reliable in the absence of family history
* Genetic testing can confirm, particularly without a family history

Prognostic factors

* Major prognostic factors in disease progression include genotype, age, sex, kidney function and total kidney volume ([Lancet 2015;385:1993](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26090645))
* Men have faster progression than women
* Mutation type determines rate of progression (*PKD2* slower progression, *PKD1* faster progression)
* Severe disease in *PKD1* and *TSC* gene deletions (contiguous gene syndrome)
* Sickle cell trait, early onset of disease and hypertension

Case reports

* 2 year old girl diagnosed with ADPKD who subsequently developed Wilms tumor ([Front Pediatr 2024;12:1322142](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38577638))
* 48 year old Chinese man who presented with anasarca, hematuria, weight gain with acute kidney injury and acute respiratory distress ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2017;96:e8625](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29145282))
* 53 year old man with ADPKD, flank pain and (difficult to detect) sarcomatoid renal cell carcinoma ([CEN Case Rep 2021;10:199](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33064294))

Treatment

* Management of blood pressure and dietary restrictions (low sodium) ([Prim Care 2020;47:673](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33121636))
* Tolvaptan (vasopressin V2 receptor antagonist) slows progression of cysts ([N Engl J Med 2017;377:1930](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29105594))
* Laparoscopic nephrectomy for pain management
* Transplant

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Findings within the kidney
  + Expansile cysts within the cortex and medulla lined by simple flattened to cuboidal epithelial cells and containing proteinaceous grungy material
  + Occasional micropapillary projections present within cysts
  + Intervening renal parenchyma, including tubules and glomeruli between cysts
  + Extensive tubulointerstitial scar with associated chronic inflammation
* Reference: [D'Agati: Atlas of Nontumor Pathology - Non-neoplastic Kidney Diseases, 1st Edition, 2005](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1881041964/pathologyoutl-20)

Genetics

* *PKD1* gene on 16p13.3 (altered in 85 - 90% of cases) produces polycystin 1; function unknown ([OMIM: Polycystin 1; PKD1 [Accessed 1 July 2024]](http://omim.org/entry/601313))
* *PKD2* gene on 4q13-23 (altered in 10% of cases) produces polycystin 2; later onset and development of chronic renal failure than *PKD1* ([OMIM: Polycystin 2; PKD2 [Accessed 1 July 2024]](http://omim.org/entry/173910))
* *PKD3* gene: minority of cases; gene unmapped ([OMIM: Polycystic Kindey Disease 3 With or Without Polycystic Liver Disease; PKD3 [Accessed 1 July 2024]](http://omim.org/entry/600666))
* Associated with *TSC2* and *PKD1* contiguous gene syndrome ([Am J Surg Pathol 2002;26:198](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11812941))
* 10% lack a family history and are considered new mutations

Sample pathology report

* Kidney, left, nephrectomy:
  + Markedly enlarged cystic kidney, consistent with autosomal dominant polycystic kidney disease
  + Negative for malignancy

Differential diagnosis

* [Autosomal recessive polycystic kidney disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumorchildpkd.html):
  + Has the *PKHD1* mutation
  + Very rare childhood condition
* [Simple renal cysts](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumorsimplecyst.html):
  + Acquired disease
  + Usually only a few cortical cysts with normal renal function and size
* [Acquired renal cystic disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumoracquiredcyst.html):
  + Normal kidney size
  + Associated with chronic kidney disease and patients are usually on dialysis
* **Medullary cystic kidney disease**:
  + Associated with *MCKD1* mutations

Board review style question #1

[![BRQ image](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/thumb/kidneytumoradultpkdwebpath01.jpg)](https://webpath.med.utah.edu/jpeg1/RENAL049.jpg)

The findings in the image of the kidney shown above typically manifest symptoms at what age?

1. Any age
2. Childhood
3. Second to third decade of life
4. Third to fourth decade of life

Board review style answer #1

**D**. Third to fourth decade of life. Answers B and C are incorrect because autosomal dominant polycystic kidney disease (ADPKD) rarely presents in childhood and uncommonly before the third decade. Answer A is incorrect because although ADPKD can present at any age, the symptoms usually manifest from the third to fourth decade.

Board review style question #2

What gene mutation is associated with milder and later onset autosomal dominant polycystic kidney disease (ADPKD)?

1. *PKD1*
2. *PKD2*
3. *PKHD1*
4. *PKHD2*

Board review style answer #2

**B**. *PKD2* mutation is associated with milder disease and presents up to 20 years later than *PKD1*. Answer A is incorrect because *PKD1* mutation leads to earlier onset and more severe disease. Answer C is incorrect because *PKHD1* mutation is seen in autosomal recessive polycystic kidney disease. Answer D is incorrect because *PKHD2* is not associated with cystic kidney disease.
