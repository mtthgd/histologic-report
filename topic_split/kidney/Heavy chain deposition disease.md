Definition / general

* Lymphoplasmacytic neoplasm that produces an abnormally truncated immunoglobulin heavy chain protein that lacks associated light chains ([eMedicine: Gamma Heavy Chain Disease [Accessed 17 January 2018]](https://emedicine.medscape.com/article/200675-overview))
* Less common than light chain deposition disease
* May have milder hematological disorders than in light chain deposition disease ([Clin Nephrol 2009;71:9](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19203545))
* Associated with various lymphoproliferative disorders, but usually has features overlapping with cases previously reported as "vaguely nodular, polymorphous" lymphoplasmacytic lymphoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:534](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22301495))

Case reports

* 51 year old man with linear deposits of gamma 4 heavy chains along glomerular, tubular and vascular basement membrane and in mesangial regions ([Mod Pathol 1994;7:874](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7838842))
* 68 year old woman with gamma 1 heavy chain disease ([Intern Med 2010;49:1411](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20647658))
* Monotypic mu heavy chain mesangial deposits causing nodular glomerulosclerosis in woman without evident hematopoietic malignancy ([Hum Pathol 2000;31:122](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10665924))
* Follow up biopsy post chemotherapy showed remarkable diminution of both nodular glomerular lesions and IgG heavy chain deposits ([Clin Nephrol 2008;69:383](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18538103))
