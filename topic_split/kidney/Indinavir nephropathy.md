Definition / general

* Protease inhibitor used to treat HIV, strongly associated with direct nephrotoxicity ([Adv Chronic Kidney Dis 2010;17:72](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20005491))
* May cause tubulointerstitial nephritis due to intratubular precipitation of indinavir crystals, with damage to tubular epithelial cells and histiocytic reaction
* Tolerance may be higher in those from sub Saharan Africa versus US / Europe ([AIDS Res Hum Retroviruses 2007;23:62](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17263634))
* May resemble tuberculosis ([Braz J Infect Dis 2008;12:99](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18553024))

Case reports

* 49 year old man with HIV and indinavir induced nephrolithiasis 3 years after discontinuing indinavir ([Int Urol Nephrol 2011;43:571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20458537))

Treatment

* Dose reduction ([Eur J Clin Pharmacol 2007;63:901](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17690876)), discontinuation, urine acidification, early insertion of bilateral double J ureteral stents ([Int Urol Nephrol 2007;39:743](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17180736))
