Definition / general

* Also spelled mala***c***oplakia
* Foamy macrophages with PAS+ granular cytoplasm and Michaelis-Gutmann bodies (may be due to deposition of amorphous salts, [Am J Surg Pathol 1984;8:151](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6367503))
* Associated with transplants
* May be due to defective macrophage function that blocks lysosomal degradation of engulfed bacteria, filling up cytoplasm with undigested debris

Case reports

* 65 year old woman with malakoplakia of kidney extending to descending colon ([Korean J Fam Med 2011;32:367](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22745875))

Gross description

* Soft, yellow mucosal plaques

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Foamy macrophages with PAS+ granular cytoplasm due to phagosomes stuffed with bacterial debris and Michaelis-Gutmann bodies (laminated mineralized concretions)
* May have rare Michaelis-Gutmann bodies, late fibrous stage ([Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:225](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2919720))
