Definition / general

* Sarcoidosis is a multisystem granulomatous disease of unknown cause histologically characterized by epithelioid noncaseating granulomas
* Classic pathological lesion of renal sarcoidosis is granulomatous interstitial nephritis but there are other forms of renal involvement

Essential features

* Kidney disease in sarcoidosis can manifest as
  + Granulomatous interstitial nephritis
  + Nongranulomatous interstitial nephritis
  + Nephrocalcinosis of the calcium phosphate and oxalate type
  + Nephrolithiasis
  + Sarcoidosis associated glomerular diseases

Terminology

* Sarcoidosis is also known as Besnier-Boeck-Schaumann disease

ICD coding

* ICD-10
  + [D86](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/D50-D89/D80-D89/D86-/D86) - sarcoidosis
  + [D86.84](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/D50-D89/D80-D89/D86-/D86.84) - sarcoid pyelonephritis
  + [D86.8](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/D50-D89/D80-D89/D86-/D86.8) - sarcoidosis of other sites
  + [D86.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/D50-D89/D80-D89/D86-/D86.9) - sarcoidosis, unspecified

Epidemiology

* Renal manifestations of sarcoidosis can present in patients with a diagnosis of sarcoidosis of other systems (notably, hilar adenopathy or pulmonary infiltrates on chest radiographs) or be an initial or sole presentation of the disease
* Best known renal manifestation of sarcoidosis, granulomatous interstitial nephritis, is observed in 7 - 23% or 30 - 79% of native kidney biopsies from patients with sarcoidosis ([Sarcoidosis Vasc Diffuse Lung Dis 2021;38:e2021017](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34744417), [Nephrol PoC 2019;5](https://doi.org/10.1177/2059300719834928))
* In contrast to the female predominance in patients with pulmonary sarcoidosis, 63.8% of patients with granulomatous interstitial nephritis are men ([Am J Kidney Dis 2006;48:856](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17060009))
* Mean age at presentation of granulomatous interstitial nephritis is 46.9 years (range, 11 - 80 years) or is reported at 59, plus or minus 18 years ([Am J Kidney Dis 2006;48:856](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17060009), [Sarcoidosis Vasc Diffuse Lung Dis 2018;35:252](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32476910))
* 74% of patients with granulomatous interstitial nephritis are White individuals ([Am J Kidney Dis 2006;48:856](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17060009))
* Nongranulomatous interstitial nephritis is observed in 14 - 44% of native kidney biopsies from patients with sarcoidosis ([Nephrol PoC 2019;5](https://doi.org/10.1177/2059300719834928))
* In certain studies, glomerular disease is found in 4 - 26% or up to 42% of native kidney biopsies in patients with sarcoidosis but is generally thought to be a rare occurrence ([Nephrol PoC 2019;5](https://doi.org/10.1177/2059300719834928), [Orphanet J Rare Dis 2013;8:65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23631446))
* Sarcoidosis associated glomerular disorders may precede the diagnosis of sarcoidosis ([Orphanet J Rare Dis 2013;8:65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23631446))
* Dysfunctional vitamin D and calcium metabolism leading to hypercalcemia, hypercalciuria, nephrolithiasis and nephrocalcinosis are common problems in patients with sarcoidosis ([Am J Kidney Dis 2006;48:856](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17060009))
* Nephrocalcinosis is observed in 10 - 11% of sarcoidosis patients presenting with renal disease ([Nephrol PoC 2019;5](https://doi.org/10.1177/2059300719834928))
* Nephrolithiasis is found in about 10% of patients with sarcoidosis ([Int Braz J Urol 2020;46:15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31851454))
* Blau syndrome (early sarcoidosis, a familial granulomatous inflammatory disease) presents in early childhood, usually in the first year of life ([Pediatr Rheumatol Online J 2014;12:33](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25136265))

Sites

* Sarcoid granulomatous lesions preferentially involve the [lower respiratory tract](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungnontumorsarcoidosis.html) but may affect any extrapulmonary site, such as the
  [heart](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/heartsarcoidosis.html),
  [thyroid and parathyroid](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidsarcoid.html),
  [skin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorsarcoidosis.html) and
  [eye](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/eyeconjgranulomatous.html)
* This topic is dedicated to the kidney involvement in sarcoidosis

Pathophysiology

* Sarcoidosis is associated with chronic hypercalcemia and increased levels of 1,25 dihydroxy vitamin D (calcitriol) ([Int Braz J Urol 2020;46:15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31851454))
* Altered level of vitamin D and hypercalcemia suppress the parathyroid hormone ([Int Braz J Urol 2020;46:15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31851454))
* Increased calcium filtration at the glomerulus and suppression of parathyroid hormone secretion by calcitriol (diminished tubular reabsorption of calcium) lead to hypercalciuria and thus to nephrocalcinosis and nephrolithiasis ([Int Braz J Urol 2020;46:15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31851454))
* Hypercalcemia causes afferent arteriole vasoconstriction, decreasing blood flow and the glomerular filtration rate ([Int Braz J Urol 2020;46:15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31851454))
* Glomerular diseases found in patients with sarcoidosis are thought to be caused by functional disorders of the immune system
  + Cytokines produced locally by granulomas may contribute to alterations in the glomerular filtration barrier ([Orphanet J Rare Dis 2013;8:65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23631446))

Etiology

* Incompletely understood (see [Lung - Sarcoidosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungnontumorsarcoidosis.html)) ([Respir Med 2020;173:106161](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32992264))

Clinical features

* Granulomatous interstitial nephritis in sarcoidosis is usually clinically silent but in Sarcoidosis Vasc Diffuse Lung Dis 2021;38:e2021017)
* According to other studies, granulomatous interstitial nephritis accounts for 7 - 27% of clinically significant renal failure in patients with sarcoidosis ([Am J Kidney Dis 2006;48:856](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17060009), [Nephrol PoC 2019;5](https://doi.org/10.1177/2059300719834928))
* Acute kidney injury, when it develops, appears to be more severe in patients with granulomatous interstitial nephritis versus patients with nongranulomatous interstitial nephritis ([Sarcoidosis Vasc Diffuse Lung Dis 2021;38:e2021017](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34744417))
* Acute kidney injury in patients with hypercalcemia is milder than in patients without hypercalcemia ([Sarcoidosis Vasc Diffuse Lung Dis 2021;38:e2021017](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34744417))
* Granulomatous renal masses (so called pseudotumoral renal sarcoidosis) are usually associated with normal renal function ([QJM 2013;106:947](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23667183))
* Blau syndrome presents as a triad of granulomatous dermatitis, arthritis and uveitis ([Pediatr Rheumatol Online J 2014;12:33](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25136265))

Diagnosis

* Diagnosis of sarcoidosis is based on 3 major criteria: a compatible clinical presentation, the finding of nonnecrotizing granulomatous inflammation in 1 or more tissue samples and the exclusion of alternative causes of granulomatous disease ([Am J Respir Crit Care Med 2020;201:e26](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32293205))
* Renal biopsy is the key instrument in diagnosis of kidney involvement in sarcoidosis

Laboratory

* For patients with sarcoidosis who have neither renal symptoms nor established renal sarcoidosis, baseline serum creatinine testing is suggested for screening
  + Diagnosis and detection of sarcoidosis
  + Official American Thoracic Society Clinical Practice Guideline
* Disordered calcium metabolism is found in a significant number of patients ([Am J Kidney Dis 2006;48:856](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17060009), [Kidney Int 2008;74:817](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18432187))
* Levels of serum angiotensin converting enzyme, interleukin 2 receptor (IL2R), C reactive protein (CRP), serum amyloid A (SAA) and chitotriosidase may be helpful in establishing the diagnosis ([Sarcoidosis Vasc Diffuse Lung Dis 2021;38:e2021017](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34744417))

Radiology description

* Contrast enhanced computer tomography scan may show signs of interstitial nephritis or less frequently multiple hypoattenuating nodules that resemble lymphoma or metastases ([Radiopaedia: Sarcoidosis (Abdominal Manifestations) [Accessed 25 July 2024]](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/sarcoidosis-abdominal-manifestations-1?lang=us))
* On MRI, granulomatous renal masses are poorly circumscribed and infiltrative and slightly hypointense to surrounding renal cortex; the zone of transition between the mass and normal renal parenchyma is ill defined and consistent with interstitial infiltration ([AJR Am J Roentgenol 2005;185:697](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16120920))
* Attenuation corrected whole body positron emission tomography (PET) scan shows intense radiotracer uptake in the kidney and lymph nodes; this can be misconstrued as indicative of malignancy ([AJR Am J Roentgenol 2005;185:697](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16120920))

Prognostic factors

* Severe interstitial infiltrates tend to predict a worse prognosis ([Sarcoidosis Vasc Diffuse Lung Dis 2021;38:e2021017](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34744417))
* Interstitial fibrosis, severe interstitial infiltration and the presence of giant cells are associated with a worse outcome ([Sarcoidosis Vasc Diffuse Lung Dis 2018;35:252](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32476910), [Sarcoidosis Vasc Diffuse Lung Dis 2021;38:e2021017](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34744417))

Case reports

* 16 year old girl with multiorgan sarcoidosis and renal involvement ([Front Pediatr 2021;9:724728](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34589456))
* 20 year old African American man presented with acute renal failure (a rare sarcoidosis presentation) ([Cureus 2023;15:e49512](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38152816))
* 30 year old man with renal sarcoidosis without coexisting pulmonary findings ([Cureus 2021;13:e15494](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34268026))
* 45 year old woman with severe acute kidney injury and uveitis; sarcoidosis with renal, hepatic and ocular involvement diagnosed ([BMJ Case Rep 2018;11:e227023](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30567265))
* 56 year old woman with sarcoidosis and acute renal failure ([Kidney Int 2008;74:817](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18432187))

Treatment

* Corticosteroids are the standard of care in renal sarcoidosis
* Majority of cases respond to steroid therapy ([Sarcoidosis Vasc Diffuse Lung Dis 2018;35:252](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32476910))
* In steroid resistant patients, other immunosuppressive reagents (azathioprine, mycophenolate mofetil or TNF inhibitors) are used ([Nephrol Dial Transplant 2014;29:1841](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24235078), [Int Braz J Urol 2020;46:15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31851454))
* Steroid responsiveness of sarcoidosis associated glomerular diseases does not correlate with that of the main disease, suggesting that both diseases require a more aggressive therapy ([Orphanet J Rare Dis 2013;8:65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23631446))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Granulomas are well formed concentrically arranged layers of immune cells with a central core of macrophages, epithelioid cells and multinucleated giant cells and an outer layer of loosely organized lymphocytes (mostly T cells) and interposed dendritic cells, sometimes surrounded by collections of B lymphocytes
* Epithelioid cells (epithelioid histiocytes) are derivatives of activated macrophages resembling epithelial cells ([Wikipedia: Epithelioid Cell [Accessed 25 July 2024]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epithelioid_cell))
* Caseous necrosis is the grossly visible, cheese-like appearance of the granulomas; this term has no microscopy counterpart and thus, the term necrotizing is preferable ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2022;146:233](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33905479))
* Sarcoidosis granulomas are most often nonnecrotic ([Am J Respir Crit Care Med 2020;201:e26](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32293205))
* Epithelioid cells are elongated, with pale eosinophilic cytoplasm and central, ovoid nuclei, which are less dense than that of a lymphocyte ([Wikipedia: Epithelioid Cell [Accessed 25 July 2024]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epithelioid_cell))
* Presence of numerous noncaseating (nonnecrotizing) granulomas favors sarcoidosis  ([Am J Respir Crit Care Med 2020;201:e26](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32293205))
* Sarcoid granulomas feature compact, tightly formed collections of large epithelioid histiocytes and multinucleated giant cells  ([Am J Respir Crit Care Med 2020;201:e26](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32293205))
* These granulomas are confined to the renal cortex and stay discrete; interstitial inflammatory infiltration is observed in all cases ([Am J Respir Crit Care Med 2020;201:e26](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32293205))
* Blau syndrome
* Nongranulomatous interstitial nephritis may show numerous eosinophils in the interstitial infiltrate ([Am J Respir Crit Care Med 2020;201:e26](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32293205))
* [Nephrocalcinosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneynephrocalcinosis.html) manifests as calcium phosphate or [oxalate](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneyoxalosis.html) tubulointerstitial deposits
  + Such deposits are also found in patients with granulomatous and nongranulomatous interstitial nephritis ([Nephrol PoC 2019;5](https://doi.org/10.1177/2059300719834928))
* Most frequent glomerular disease occurring in sarcoidosis is [membranous glomerulopathy](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneymemgn.html) ([Orphanet J Rare Dis 2013;8:65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23631446))
* Other glomerular diseases observed in patients with sarcoidosis include [IgA nephropathy](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneyiga.html), [focal segmental glomerulosclerosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneyfsgs.html), [minimal change disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneyminchange.html) and [lupus nephritis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneysle.html) ([Orphanet J Rare Dis 2013;8:65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23631446))
* Focal segmental glomerulosclerosis and hypertensive nephrosclerosis may be found in sarcoidosis patients with interstitial nephritis ([Sarcoidosis Vasc Diffuse Lung Dis 2018;35:252](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32476910))

Immunofluorescence description

* In renal sarcoidosis, immunofluorescence staining for IgG, IgA, IgM, C3, C1q, kappa and lambda light chains is usually negative in all kidney compartments
* Rare cases of nonspecific deposits of IgM or deposits of C3 are reported ([Sarcoidosis Vasc Diffuse Lung Dis 2021;38:e2021017](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34744417))
* Sarcoidosis associated glomerular diseases are diagnosed using the appropriate immunofluorescence features (see [Microscopic description](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneysarcoidosis.html#sarcoidosismicro) section)

Positive stains

* [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html) immunohistochemical staining is used to highlight histiocytes and multinucleated giant cells to facilitate findings of granulomas ([Intern Med 2019;58:679](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30449791))

Negative stains

* Bacterial and fungal stains, such as
  + [Acid fast (Ziehl-Neelsen) stain](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsacidfast.html) ([Wikipedia: Ziehl-Neelsen Stain [Accessed 14 August 2024]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ziehl%E2%80%93Neelsen_stain))
  + [GMS (Grocott-Gomori methenamine silver) stain](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgms.html) ([Wikipedia: Grocott's Methenamine Silver Stain [Accessed 14 August 2024]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grocott%27s_methenamine_silver_stain))
  + [PAS (periodic acid-Schiff) stain](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html) ([Wikipedia: Periodic Acid-Schiff Stain [Accessed 14 August 2024]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Periodic_acid%E2%80%93Schiff_stain))

Electron microscopy description

* No specific electron microscopy findings are associated with granulomatous or nongranulomatous interstitial nephritis, nephrolithiasis or nephrocalcinosis
* Electron microscopy is used to diagnose sarcoidosis associated glomerular pathology and to exclude other etiologies (see [Microscopic description](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneysarcoidosis.html#sarcoidosismicro) section)

Genetics

* Genetic studies confirm the very strong genetic heterogeneity in sarcoidosis ([J Clin Med 2020;9:2633](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32823753))
* Familial and sporadic forms of sarcoidosis exist ([J Clin Med 2020;9:2633](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32823753))
* Familial sarcoidosis may be associated with variants in *mTOR* gene and its regulator, a GTPase Rac 1 ([J Clin Med 2020;9:2633](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32823753))
* mTOR pathway regulates autophagy and may be involved in the clearance of pathogens ([J Clin Med 2020;9:2633](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32823753))
* Genomic associations of sarcoidosis renal lesions have not been explored
* Blau syndrome, a monogenic autoinflammatory disease, is due to *NOD2* (nucleotide binding oligomerization domain containing 2) gene gain of function mutations ([Pediatr Rheumatol Online J 2014;12:33](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25136265))
* NOD2 protein is primarily expressed in the peripheral blood leukocytes and plays a role in the immune response to intracellular bacterial lipopolysaccharides ([NIH: NOD2 Nucleotide Binding Oligomerization Domain Containing 2 [Accessed 31 July 2024]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/64127))

Differential diagnosis

* [Mycobacterial infections](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytb.html):
  + History of exposure
  + Diagnosis of extrarenal mycobacterial infection
  + Mycobacterial DNA fragments may be identified by polymerase chain reaction (PCR)
  + Present with necrotizing granulomas
  + Positive [acid fast stain](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsacidfast.html)
* [Fungal infections](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungnontumorotherfungi.html):
  + History of exposure
  + Positive [GMS stain](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgms.html) and fungal culture
* [Adenoviral infection](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneyadenovirus.html):
  + Seen in renal allografts
  + Tubulocentric polymorphonuclear neutrophil (PMN) rich inflammation and [acute tubular necrosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneyatn.html)
  + Ground glass tubular epithelial cells with viral inclusions
  + Serum, urine and kidney tissue PCR positive for adenovirus
  + Positive immunohistochemical stain for [adenovirus](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsadenovirus.html)
* **Intravesical Bacillus Calmette-Guérin (BCG) therapy**:
  + History of bladder carcinoma and BCG treatment
* [Granulomatous inflammation associated with extratubal Tamm-Horsfall protein](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladdertamm.html):
  + Glassy [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html)+ acellular material
  + Associated with interstitial kidney disease
  + Positive stain with [anti-Tamm-Horsfall protein antibody](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladdertamm.html)
* [Allergic acute interstitial nephritis, medication related](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneynontumorcheckpointinhibitor.html):
  + History of medications known to cause granulomatous [interstitial nephritis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytubulointerstitialnephritis.html)
  + Predilection for the corticomedullary junction involvement
  + Poorly formed granulomas ([Clin Kidney J 2015;8:516](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26413275))
* [Berylliosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungotherpneumoconiosis.html#berylliosis) (occupational exposure):
  + History of inhalation of beryllium dust
  + Granulomas are poorly formed ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2022;146:233](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33905479))
* [Tubulointerstitial nephritis with uveitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytubulointerstitialnephritis.html):
  + Affects mainly children and young women
  + Uveitis
  + Rare nonnecrotizing granulomas
* [Crohn's disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/coloncrohns.html):
  + History of [inflammatory bowel disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colonindeterminate.html)
* [Granulomatosis with polyangiitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneyGPA.html):
  + Antineutrophilic cytoplasmic antibody (ANCA) positivity
  + Presents as necrotizing and crescentic glomerulonephritis

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/kidneysarcoidosisMikhailov07.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/kidneysarcoidosisMikhailov07.jpg)

A 46 year old woman, who has a history of pulmonary sarcoidosis diagnosed at the age of 25 years and is a frequent user of aspirin, underwent a CT with contrast due to a suspicion for a relapse of the disease. A week later, she developed a *Clostridium difficile* associated colitis, for which she received vancomycin treatment. She presents with acute kidney injury with serum creatinine of 3 mg/dL (baseline 1 mg/dL) and her urine contains eosinophils. A kidney biopsy is performed. It shows extensive focally accentuated tubulointerstitial inflammation involving the cortex and the corticomedullary junction; a representative image is shown above. You will report that, based on the clinicopathological data, the most likely etiology of the acute tubulointerstitial nephritis is

1. Aspirin
2. Computed tomography (CT) contrast material
3. Sarcoidosis
4. Vancomycin

Board review style answer #1

**D**. Vancomycin. The image shows acute tubulointerstitial nephritis and in the center of the image, a loose collection of multinucleated giant cells and epithelioid cells is seen, indicating a poorly formed granuloma. Antibiotics, especially vancomycin, are a frequent cause of granulomatous interstitial nephritis characterized by poorly formed granulomas and corticomedullary junction involvement. Urine eosinophils are suggestive but not necessary for the diagnosis of drug related allergic interstitial nephritis.
Answer A is incorrect because as opposed to other nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDS) (indomethacin, ketorolac), aspirin has not been reported to cause granulomatous interstitial nephritis. Aspirin associated renal lesions are acute tubular injury, acute interstitial nephritis and minimal change disease.
Answer B is incorrect because CT contrast media has not been reported to induce granulomatous interstitial nephritis. Acute tubular injury, isometric tubular epithelial vacuolization and acute interstitial nephritis are associated with iodinated contrast.
Answer C is incorrect because well formed granulomas located in the renal cortex are characteristic for sarcoidosis .

Board review style question #2

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/kidneysarcoidosisMikhailov08.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/kidneysarcoidosisMikhailov08.jpg)

A 55 year old man with multiorgan sarcoidosis affecting the lower respiratory tract, the skin and the eyes and a history of longstanding hypertension presents with mild elevation of serum creatinine. Kidney sarcoidosis is suspected and a kidney biopsy is done. The biopsy shows moderate arteriosclerosis and mild to moderate interstitial fibrosis and tubular atrophy with associated mononuclear infiltration. One of the biopsy cores shows an interesting structure. You examine this structure and your conclusion is

1. Artifact of tissue processing and staining; of no diagnostic consequence
2. Extratubal Tamm-Horsfall protein
3. Granulomatous interstitial nephritis, consistent with sarcoidosis kidney involvement
4. Necrotizing granuloma; need to order acid fast and GMS stains

Board review style answer #2

**B**. Extratubal Tamm-Horsfall protein. The image shows 2 pools of glassy PAS+ material, characteristic for extratubal Tamm-Horsfall protein (uromodulin), surrounded by a mononuclear cell infiltrate. Tubular release of the uromodulin may occur in chronic renal diseases affecting the interstitium, such as in this case, with renal vascular disease associated tubular injury, atrophy and damage by immune cells. Interstitial uromodulin is typically surrounded by inflammatory cells and the mechanism of uromodulin release and its role in inflammation are currently not known ([Am J Kidney Dis 2012;59:452](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22277744)).
Answer A is incorrect because the observed pattern is characteristic for interstitial Tamm-Horsfall protein (uromodulin) with a surrounding reaction.
Answer C is incorrect because there is no granuloma.
Answer D is incorrect because there is no granuloma; the PAS+ material is interstitial Tamm-Horsfall protein (uromodulin).
