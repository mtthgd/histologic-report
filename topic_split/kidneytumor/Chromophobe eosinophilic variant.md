Definition / general

* Histologic variant of chromophobe renal cell carcinoma (ChRCC) with at least 80% of cells with eosinophilic cytoplasm

Essential features

* At least 80% of the neoplastic cells must be eosinophilic
* No difference in prognosis compared with classic / mixed chromophobe renal cell carcinoma
* Must distinguish from benign mimic oncocytoma

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [C64.1](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C64-C68/C64-/C64.1) - malignant neoplasm of right kidney, except renal pelvis
* ICD-10: [C64.2](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C64-C68/C64-/C64.2) - malignant neoplasm of left kidney, except renal pelvis
* ICD-10: [C64.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C64-C68/C64-/C64.9) - malignant neoplasm of unspecified kidney, except renal pelvis

Epidemiology

* Chromophobe renal cell carcinoma is ~ 5% of all renal cell carcinomas ([Int J Urol 2012;19:894](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22715810))
* In large study case series, 41 - 51% of ChRCC were eosinophilic variant ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:1822](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18813125), [Eur J Surg Oncol 2008;34:687](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17997271))
* Studies do not mention other epidemiologic differences from classic ChRCC

Sites

* Kidney in corticomedullary parenchyma

Pathophysiology

* Same as classic ChRCC: neoplasm of intercalated cells of collecting duct ([Virchows Arch B Cell Pathol Incl Mol Pathol 1989;56:237](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2565618))
* Eosinophilic cytoplasm is due to increased mitochondria

Clinical features

* Potential presenting symptoms: hematuria, pain, flank mass, anemia, pyrexia, cachexia, fatigue and weight loss ([Int J Urol 2012;19:894](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22715810))
* Large case series found eosinophilic more likely to be bilateral (11%) and multifocal (22%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:1822](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18813125))
* Can be part of Birt-Hogg-Dubé syndrome (BHD) (*FLCN* mutation) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2002;26:1542](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12459621))
  + Fibrofolliculomas, spontaneous pneumothorax, other renal neoplasms

Diagnosis

* Mass seen on imaging with confirmatory renal biopsy or mass resection
* Biopsy may not be diagnostic, especially if limited

Radiology description

* On CT scan, ChRCC more likely to have homogenous enhancement (69%) and can have calcifications (38%) ([AJR Am J Roentgenol 2002;178:1499](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12034628))
* No studies identified differentiating findings in eosinophilic from classic ChRCC

Prognostic factors

* No difference in outcome from classic ChRCC ([Eur J Surg Oncol 2008;34:687](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17997271), [Cancers (Basel) 2019;11:1492](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31623347), [Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:1822](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18813125))

Case reports

* 31 year old man, long term renal transplant recipient, with 1.8 cm mass in allograft ([Case Rep Transplant 2017;2017:4232474](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29130016))
* 55 year old man with painless hematuria, 9.0 cm upper pole renal mass extending into the pelvicalyceal system and concurrent right atrial myxoma ([J Lab Physicians. 2011;3:116](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22219567))
* 60 year old woman with intermittent painless hematuria, 9.0 cm upper pole renal mass ([J Lab Physicians. 2011;3:116](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22219567))
* 73 year old woman with 1.8 cm upper pole renal mass and 67 year old man with incidental 3.0 cm lower pole renal mass ([Proc (Bayl Univ Med Cent) 2015;28:57](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25552800))
* 80 year old man with incidental 2.5 cm renal mass with partial papillary growth ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2015 Oct;8:13590](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26722580))

Treatment

* Typically resection via partial or total nephrectomy, the same as classic ChRCC

Gross description

* Usually well circumscribed, lobulated and with beige to brown to yellow coloration
  + Eosinophilic variant more likely to have brown coloration compared with classic,
    which is typically more beige-yellow ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:1822](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18813125))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* At least 80% of the chromophobe neoplastic cells must have eosinophilic cytoplasm to classify as eosinophilic variant
* Cells are often smaller than classic chromophobe cells
* Besides eosinophilic cytoplasm and smaller size, have otherwise similar histology to classic ChRCC including
  + Well defined / thickened cell border
  + Wrinkled, raisinoid nuclei
  + Frequent binucleation
  + Perinuclear halos / clearing
  + Rare mitoses
  + Lacking prominent vasculature
* Nested, alveolar or sheet-like architecture
  + Nested architecture more common in eosinophilic than classic ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:1822](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18813125))
* Grading is not recommended
  + Fuhrman grading system not predictive of outcome and is obsolete ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:957](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17527087))
  + WHO / ISUP system has not been validated for ChRCC ([Am J Surg Pathol 2013;37:1490](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24025520))
  + Several grading schemes proposed but no overall consensus ([Eur Urol 2016;70:93](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26935559))

Cytology description

* Single or clusters of large cells with moderate pleomorphism
* Abundant granular eosinophilic cytoplasm
* Well defined / accentuated cell border
* Perinuclear clearing / vacuolization
* Binucleation may be seen

Electron microscopy description

* Numerous cytoplasmic microvesicles ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:1247](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10976699))
* Mitochondria with tubulocystic cristae ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:1247](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10976699))
* Eosinophilic variant has more abundant mitochondria ([Pathol Int 2000;50:872](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11107062))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Compared with classic ChRCC, less frequent losses of chromosomes 1, 2, 6, 10, 13 and 17, suggesting classic has greater chromosomal instability ([Cancers (Basel) 2019;11:1492](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31623347))
* Most common chromosomal losses (in order of most to least frequent): 1, 2, 17, 6, 10, 13 and 21; no chromosomal gains ([Adv Anat Pathol 2021;28:8](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33021507))
* Doubled hypodiploidy by whole genome endoduplication is a common phenomenon in eosinophilic ChRCC ([Hum Pathol 2020;104:18](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32673684))

Distinguishing eosinophilic chromophobe RCC from oncocytoma

Sample pathology report

* Left kidney, mass, partial nephrectomy:
  + Chromophobe renal cell carcinoma, eosinophilic variant (see synoptic report)

Differential diagnosis

* [Oncocytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumoroncocytoma.html):
  + Round, hyperchromatic nuclei; smooth nuclear border
  + Cells arranged in a nested or tubular pattern
  + Lacks nuclear pleomorphism, oval nuclei, multiple nucleoli
  + [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html) usually negative
  + [Hale colloidal iron](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsiron.html) focal positive staining confined to luminal borders
  + No copy number alterations or losses of chromosomes 1 and X / Y ([Hum Pathol 2020;104:18](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32673684))
* [Hybrid oncocytic / chromophobe tumors (HOCT)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumoroncocyticchromhybrid.html) ([Histol Histopathol 2013;28:1257](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23740406)):
  + Can be sporadic but also often found in association with renal oncocytomatosis or Birt-Hogg-Dubé (BHD) syndrome
  + Sporadic / oncocytomatosis: solid alveolar pattern, scattered cells with perinuclear halos but with no raisinoid nuclei
  + BHD: admixed areas of oncocytoma and ChRCC, scattered chromophobe cells, intracytoplasmic vacuoles
  + No aggressive behavior
  + Usually [parvalbumin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/parvalbumin.html), **antimitochondrial antigen** and [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html) positive
  + Sporadic cases do not exhibit mutations in genes that are recurrently mutated in oncocytoma or ChRCC; syndromic cases with folliculin gene mutations ([Mod Pathol 2019;32:1698](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31231128))
* [Eosinophilic variant of clear cell renal cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantrccclear.html):
  + Usually area with traditional clear cell morphology (unless limited biopsy)
  + [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html) and [KIT](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html) negative, [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html) and [CAIX](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscarbonicanhydraseix.html) positive
  + Biallelic inactivation of *VHL* gene

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/kidneytumormalignantrccchromoeosinvarMiller3.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/kidneytumormalignantrccchromoeosinvarMiller3.jpg)

Which of the following is the most likely immunohistochemical profile of the renal neoplasm shown?

1. CK7+, KIT+, vimentin-, CAIX-
2. CK7+, KIT+, vimentin-, CAIX+
3. CK7+, KIT+, vimentin+, CAIX-
4. CK7-, KIT+, vimentin-, CAIX-
5. CK7-, KIT-, vimentin+, CAIX+

Board review style answer #1

**A.** CK7+, KIT+, vimentin-, CAIX-

The image shown is chromophobe renal cell carcinoma (ChRCC), eosinophilic variant. Histologic clues to the diagnosis of ChRCC are the raisinoid nuclei, perinuclear clearing / halos and well defined cell borders. Furthermore, > 80% of the cells have an eosinophilic cytoplasm, thus making it the eosinophilic variant of ChRCC. The immunoprofile of ChRCC is typically CK7+, KIT+, vimentin- and CAIX-. The other entity to consider is oncocytoma, but in this case the degree of nuclear pleomorphism (with enlarged, wrinkled and oval nuclei) favors ChRCC. Answer D (CK7-, KIT+, vimentin-, CAIX-) would be the immunohistochemical profile most typical of oncocytoma, although vimentin can often be focally positive. Answer E is the immunoprofile most typical of clear cell renal cell carcinoma; while there is an eosinophilic variant of this condition, the perinuclear clearing and raisinoid nuclei are still most characteristic of ChRCC ([Pathol Res Pract 2015;211:303](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25596994)).

Board review style question #2

Which of the following is true regarding chromophobe renal cell carcinoma (ChRCC), eosinophilic variant compared with the classic type of ChRCC?

1. No differences in cytogenetics have been found between the eosinophilic variant and classic ChRCC
2. The classic variant is more likely to have a brown coloration grossly
3. The classic variant typically has smaller cells than the eosinophilic variant
4. The eosinophilic variant must have 100% eosinophilic cells
5. The prognosis of the eosinophilic variant is the same as classic ChRCC

Board review style answer #2

**E.** The prognosis of the eosinophilic variant is the same as classic ChRCC

Studies have shown no difference in prognosis between the eosinophilic variant of ChRCC and classic ChRCC.
Answer A is wrong as a recent study showed that the eosinophilic variant frequently has less chromosomal instability compared with classic ChRCC and is often characterized by doubled hypodiploidy ([Eur J Surg Oncol 2008;34:687](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17997271), [Cancers (Basel) 2019;11:1492](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31623347), [Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:1822](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18813125), [Cancers (Basel) 2019;11:1492](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31623347), [Hum Pathol 2020;104:18](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32673684), [Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:1822](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18813125)).
Answer B is wrong as the eosinophilic variant is more likely to be brown in coloration grossly.
Answer C is wrong as the eosinophilic variant typically has smaller cells compared with classic ChRCC.
Answer D is wrong because, by definition, the eosinophilic variant only needs to have > 80% eosinophilic cells.
