Definition / general

* Clear cell sarcoma of the kidney (CCSK) is composed of uniform, small round cells with a clear appearance and evenly distributed fine chromatin separated by a delicate vascular network ([eMedicine](http://emedicine.medscape.com/article/993245))
* First described in 1978 by three distinct groups: Beckwith and Palmer ([Cancer 1978;41:1937](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/206343)), Morgan and Kidd ([Cancer 1978;42:1916](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/213187)), and Marsden and coworkers ([Cancer 1978;42:1922](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/213188)); it was initially named "bone metastasizing renal tumor of childhood" since it has predilection for skeletal metastasis ([J Neonatal Surg 2014;3:35](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26023506))
* Most frequently misdiagnosed renal tumor in children, as it is unusual, has varied morphology and there are no specific diagnostic markers ([J Neonatal Surg 2014;3:35](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26023506))

Essential features

* Typically a male child, half are diagnosed at 2 - 3 years of age
* Many histological variants exist (see below), but the classic histological appearance is nests or cords of small, plump cells separated by networks of delicate vascular septa
* Clear cells occur in only 20% of cases, despite its name
* Immunohistochemistry has limited use: it is usually only positive for vimentin, although Cyclin D1 may be useful (as of 2015, see below)
* The most important drug treatment is Doxorubicin (Adriamycin®)
* Recent molecular advances in CCSK tumorigenesis have implicated the BCOR gene (see below)

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [C64.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C64-C68/C64-/C64.9#:~:text=Malignant%20neoplasm%20of%20unspecified%20kidney%2C%20except%20renal%20pelvis,-2016%202017%202018&text=Billable%2FSpecific%20Code-,C64.,a%20diagnosis%20for%20reimbursement%20purposes.) - malignant neoplasm of unspecified kidney, except renal pelvis

Epidemiology

* Rare (20 cases per year); 3% - 5% of all pediatric renal tumors
* Mean age of diagnosis is 36 months (range, 2 months - 14 years), half are diagnosed at 2 - 3 years of age ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:4](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10632483))
* Rarely diagnosed in adolescents / adults ([Am J Surg Pathol 1999;23:1455](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10584698))
* 2 / 3 male

Clinical features

* Bone metastases occur in 17% - 40% of patients with CCSK, vs. J Neonatal Surg 2014;3:35)
* 5% of patients have metastatic disease at presentation including bone, lung, brain and liver
* Overall, survival varies with stage (5 year survival is 97% for stage I vs. 50% for stage IV, [Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:4](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10632483))

Prognostic factors

* Four important prognostic factors are:
  + Doxorubicin treatment
  + Stage
  + Age at diagnosis
  + Presence of tumor necrosis ([J Postgrad Med 2001;47:206](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11832627))
* Stage IV disease and young age are significant adverse prognostic factors for event free survival ([Eur J Cancer 2013;49:3497](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23880476))

Case reports

* Congenital tumor with metastases ([Virchows Arch 2005;446:566](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15838643))

- 28 day old boy with increasing abdominal mass ([J Neonatal Surg 2014;3:35.](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26023506))
- 8 month old boy with increasing abdominal lump for 3 months ([J Cancer Res Ther 2014;10:1104](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25579563))
- 19 month old boy with renal mass ([University of Pittsburgh case #201](http://path.upmc.edu/cases/case201.html))
- 5 year old boy with jaw swelling, hematuria and abdominal swelling with bilateral renal masses and a 1 year old girl with a 4 month history of gradual abdominal swelling followed by gross hematuria ([J Egypt Natl Canc Inst 2015;27:97](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25934445))
- 9 year old boy with a cystic structure with calcification on computed tomography ([Diagn Pathol 2015;10:108](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26182914))

Treatment

* Total nephrectomy followed by postoperative chemotherapy and flank radiation therapy (RT) ([Eur J Cancer 2013;49:3497](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23880476))
* Highly responsive to combination treatment with doxorubicin, vincristine and actinomycin, with doxorubicin the most important ([eMedicine](http://emedicine.medscape.com/article/993245-treatment))
* The essential role of doxorubicin in therapy emphasizes the need for pathologists to accurately identify CCSK ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:4](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10632483))

Gross description

* Unilateral and unicentric mass with irregular but sharp tumor / kidney interface
* Large (mean 11 cm); centered in central kidney or medulla
* Cut surface is homogenous tan / gray or gelatinous and firm with occasional cysts

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Classic pattern: nests or cords of small, plump cells separated by network of delicate vascular septa (also called chicken wire capillaries)
  + This pattern is almost always focally present, but other patterns may be present
* The 8 variant patterns are myxoid (50%), sclerosing (35%), cellular (26%), epithelioid (trabecular or acinar type) (13%), palisading verocay body (11%), spindle cell (7%), storiform (4%) and anaplastic (2.6%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:4](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10632483))
* Anaplasia is defined as nuclear hyperchromasia, atypical mitoses and giant nuclei ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:4](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10632483))
* Cells may take on a spindle appearance, particularly near the vascular septae, these cells are termed "septal cells" ([Zhou: Uropathology, 1st Edition, 2012](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1437725236/pathologyoutl-20))
* Although the border appears sharp at low power, high power reveals subtle infiltration of the cells into the normal parenchyma at the periphery
* Nuclei have smooth chromatin and appear washed out, similar to papillary thyroid carcinoma
* Nucleoli are not prominent; rare mitoses
* Only 20% have clear cells, and origin of clear cells varies per source: the clear nature of the cells is due to intracytoplasmic vacuoles or an extracellular mucopolysaccharide matrix that imparts a clear cell appearance ([Bostwick: Urologic Surgical Pathology, 3rd Edition, 2014](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1455743275/pathologyoutl-20), [Zhou: Uropathology, 1st Edition, 2012](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1437725236/pathologyoutl-20))
* Vascular invasion common

Cytology description

* Cells with moderate pale blue cytoplasm ([Cytopathology 2008;19:80](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17681013)), cord cells with few stromal fragments
* Spindle cell variant has myxoid stromal fragments and septal cells
* Anaplastic variant has bizarre pleomorphic nuclei, coarse chromatin and atypical mitotic figures ([Diagn Cytopathol 2005;33:83](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16007650))

Positive stains

* [Vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html) is strongly positive (45 / 45 cases) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:4](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10632483))
* [Cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html) may be useful (14 / 14 cases of CCSK are diffusely and strongly positive) ([Histopathology 2015;67:306](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25556515))
* [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) highlights septal capillaries ([Bostwick: Urologic Surgical Pathology, 3rd Edition, 2014](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1455743275/pathologyoutl-20))
* [MIB1 (Ki67)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) had mean staining index of 21% in 22 cases (range among cases: 8% - 32% of cells staining), and no obvious correlation with histologic pattern was noted ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:4](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10632483))
* [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) shows variable staining depending on histologic subtype: 86% (25 / 29 cases) of nonanaplastic CCSKs showed minimal (less than 5% of nuclei) staining; in contrast, 66% (2 of 3 cases) of anaplastic CCSKs showed intense, diffuse (> 75% of nuclei) staining ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:4](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10632483))

* [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html), [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html), [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Despite clear cell sarcoma of the kidney (CCSK) being the second most common renal tumor in children, its mechanism has not yet been fully elucidated
* A 2012 study demonstrated 12% (6 / 50 cases) contain a t(10;17), resulting in YWHAE - NUTM2B/E(FAM22B) fusion, confirming an earlier report ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2007;131:446](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17516747))
  + The prognostic significance of this translocation is unknown ([J Pathol 2012;227:72](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22294382))
  + The t(10;17) is the same translocation seen in high grade endometrial stromal sarcoma
* There is a correlation between BCOR gene aberration and CCSK tumorigenesis
  + Internal tandem duplications in the BCOR gene (BCL6 corepressor) were identified in 100% (20 / 20) of CCSK vs 0 of 193 other pediatric renal tumors
    - None of these 20 cases had the YWHAE – NUTM2 fusion ([Nat Genet 2015;47:861](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26098867))
    - In another study, internal tandem duplications in the BCOR gene (BCL6 corepressor) were identified in 85% (23 / 27) of CCSK ([Nat Commun 2015;6:8891](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26573325))
* Downregulated vascular endothelial growth factor A (VEGFA) and its interacting genes may initiate clear cell sarcoma of the kidney (CCSK) development by interrupting two different pathways, kinase regulation and protein tyrosine kinase metabolism
  + IIn a 2016 gene expression study of 14 CCSK and 3 fetal kidney samples, a total of 2681 differentially expressed genes were identified, of which 2138 genes were downregulated, including VEGFA, and 543 were overexpressed ([Oncol Lett 2016;11:953](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26893674))

Differential diagnosis

* Conventional [clear cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantrccclear.html) of kidney: extremely rare in children, tumor cells are cytokeratin+
* Malignant [rhabdoid tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumorrhabdoid.html) of the kidney: vimentin staining in a dot like pattern; CCSK is negative ([Sebire: Diagnostic Pediatric Surgical Pathology, 1st Edition, 2009](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0443068089/pathologyoutl-20))
* [Mesoblastic nephroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormesoblastic.html): WT1+, smooth muscle actin (SMA)+
* [Wilm tumor (WT)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumorwilmkids.html):
  + Pushing border, more aggressive invasion, nephrogenic rests rule out CCSK; Wilm tumor is WT1+, epithelioid areas are keratin+
  + In a pure stromal type WT treated with preoperative chemotherapy, the stroma may show a striking clear cell sarcoma-like appearance, and extensive sampling may be required to find foci with other WT components ([Popov: Wilms Tumor, 2016](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27512769))
