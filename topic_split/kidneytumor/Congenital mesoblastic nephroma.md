Definition / general

* Mesoblastic nephroma is a mesenchymal / myofibroblastic renal tumor of low grade malignancy
* It typically occurs in infancy, often congenital but not always
* First described by Bolande et al. in 1967 ([Pediatrics 1967;40:272](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4378178))

Essential features

* Most common renal tumor in the first month of life
* 90% of cases diagnosed in the first 9 months of life
* Virtually never after 3 years of age
* Adult mesoblastic nephroma does not exist
* 3 histologic subtypes: classic (~25% of cases), cellular (~65%) and mixed (~10%)
* Cellular type: *ETV6-NTRK3* gene fusion in ~70% of cases
* Classic type: *EGFR* internal tandem duplication mutation
* Most important prognostic factor is completeness of surgical resection

Terminology

* Historic synonyms: renal leiomyomatous hamartoma, congenital mesoblastic nephroma
* Although "congenital" is a part of the WHO stated name for this tumor, it is sometimes a misnomer as the tumor is not always present from birth

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8960/1](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf#page=83) - Mesoblastic nephroma
* ICD-10: [D41.0](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D37-D48/D41-/D41.0) - Neoplasm of uncertain behavior of kidney

Epidemiology

* Represents 3 - 4% of all renal tumors of childhood ([Pediatr Blood Cancer 2011;56:744](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21370406))
* Most common renal tumor in the first month of life ([Pediatr Blood Cancer 2008;50:1130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18095319))
* 90% of cases occur in the first 9 months of life ([Pediatr Blood Cancer 2008;50:1130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18095319))
* Median age < 1 month in 63% of cases ([Pediatr Blood Cancer 2017;64:e26437](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28124468))
* Median age differs between classic (7 days), mixed (1.9 months) and cellular (4.6 months) types ([Pediatr Blood Cancer 2011;56:744](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21370406))
* Virtually never occurs after the age of 3 years ([Pediatr Blood Cancer 2017;64:e26437](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28124468))
* Adult mesoblastic nephroma does not exist
* Not associated with syndromes or congenital anomalies

Sites

* Kidney; never bilateral, never multifocal

Pathophysiology

* Unknown

Etiology

* No recognized risk factors
* No familial cases have been reported
* Cellular type associated with t(12;15)(p13;q25) in ~70% of cases ([Pediatr Blood Cancer 2018;65:e26925](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29286563))
* Classic type associated with *EGFR* internal tandem duplication mutation ([Histopathology 2020;77:611](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32590884))
* Not associated with nephrogenic rests

Clinical features

* Abdominal mass (~75% of patients) ([Pediatr Blood Cancer 2017;64:e26437](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28124468))
* ~15% detected prenatally, usually associated with polyhydramnios
* Hypertension (~20%)
* Hematuria (~10%)
* Never presents as metastatic or bilateral disease
* Virtually never associated with syndromes or anomalies typical for [Wilms tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumorwilmkids.html)

Diagnosis

* Imaging techniques cannot distinguish it from other renal tumors ([J Pediatr Surg 2008;43:1301](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18639686))
* Biopsy not recommended
* Diagnosis made on nephrectomy

Laboratory

* No specific laboratory findings
* Hypercalcemia (4% of cases)
* Hyperreninemia (1% of cases) ([Pediatr Blood Cancer 2017;64:e26437](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28124468))

Radiology description

* Unilateral, well demarcated, hypoechogenic mass

Prognostic factors

* Most important prognostic factor is completeness of surgical resection
* Histologic type is of no prognostic significance
* Overall survival reported as ~96% but is almost certainly higher since cases with unfavorable outcome are more likely to be reported ([Pediatr Blood Cancer 2008;50:1130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18095319))
* Of 12/276 patients with mesoblastic nephroma who died, 5 died of tumor related and 7 of treatment related causes ([Pediatr Blood Cancer 2017;64:e26437](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28124468))
* Very rare local relapses and metastases; 38 cases reported in the literature ([Pediatr Surg Int 2017;33:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28856451))
  + Relapses and metastases occur within 12 months after the diagnosis (range 1 - 12 months, median 6 months)
  + Local relapses in 27/38 (71%) of these cases
  + 21/27 (76%) had incomplete resection (stage 3)
  + All but 1 patient had cellular type but even then, cellular type had no significant difference in prognosis
  + Metastases developed in 18 children (7 also had local recurrence)
  + Lung metastases in 7/18 (39%), liver in 5/18 (28%), brain in 4/18 (22%) and heart, bone and peritoneum in 5.5% each
  + 38% of patients with metastases died (all who had brain, bone, heart and peritoneum metastases)

Case reports

* Prematurely born boy presented with neonatal hypertension ([Turk J Pediatr 2018;60:198](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30325129))
* Prematurely born boy with a prenatal diagnosis of polyhydramnios ([Urol Case Rep 2015;3:157](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26793537))
* 7 day old boy presented with abdominal distension and vomiting ([J Neonatal Surg 2017;6:45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28770142))
* 11 month old boy presented with a renal mass ([Urol Case Rep 2019;26:100979](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31388496))

Treatment

* Complete surgical resection (total nephrectomy) with wide resection margins ([Pediatr Blood Cancer 2017;64:e26437](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28124468), [Pediatr Surg Int 2017;33:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28856451))
* Even stages II and III treated with surgery only
* Relapses also treated with surgery
* Chemotherapy treatment of relapses only in unresectable cases

Gross description

+ Always unilateral and solitary ([Adv Anat Pathol 2003;10:243](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12973047))
+ Typically found in the medial renal sinus
+ Classic type:
  - Firm, whorled, leiomyomatous consistency
  - Poorly demarcated from the normal renal parenchyma

* Cellular type:
  + Usually soft
  + Tumor-kidney junction relatively clearly demarcated
  + Cystic areas
  + Can show hemorrhage and necrosis

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Classic type (~25% of cases) ([Adv Anat Pathol 2003;10:243](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12973047), [Histopathology 1985;9:741](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2995226)):
  + Intermingling fascicles of spindle cells with low mitotic activity
  + Collagen deposition
  + Often prominent dilated, thin walled vascular spaces
  + No capsule
  + Tumor-kidney border is irregular with finger-like protrusions of the tumor into the renal parenchyma
  + Islands of hyaline cartilage can be seen at the tumor-kidney interface
  + Entrapped islands of renal parenchyma
  + Foci of extramedullary hematopoiesis common
* Cellular type (~65% of cases):
  + High cellularity of plump cells with vesicular nuclei, moderate amount of cytoplasm
  + Sheet-like growth pattern
  + High mitotic activity (which is of no prognostic significance)
  + No capsule
  + Clear tumor-kidney border but subtle infiltration into the renal parenchyma
  + Isolated entrapped tubules, which may be mistaken for neoplastic tubules
* Mixed type (~10% of cases):
  + Features of both types in a variable proportion
* Staging criteria are as for [nephroblastoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumorwilmkids.html)
  + Vast majority are stage 2 due to tumor's infiltrative growth into the renal sinus or perirenal fat

Cytology description

* Fine needle aspiration is not indicated

Positive stains

* [Cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html): diffuse in ~70% of cases ([Histopathology 2015;67:306](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25556515))
* [Pan-TRK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsntrk.html): cytoplasmic or nuclear positivity in cellular type with *ETV6-NTRK3* gene fusion ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:927](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29683818))
* [Alpha smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html)
* [Vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html)

Negative stains

* [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html) might rarely be weak or focally positive ([J Pediatr Hematol Oncol 2008;30:768](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19011477))
* [BCOR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcor.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html), [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html), [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Cellular type:
  + Specific chromosomal translocation t(12;15)(p13;q25), which results in a fusion of *ETV6* and *NTRK3* genes in ~70% of cases ([Pediatr Blood Cancer 2017;64:e26925](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29286563))
  + The same translocation is found in [infantile fibrosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuefibrosarcomainfantile.html) ([Mod Pathol 2000;13:29](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10658907), [Mod Pathol 2001;14:1246](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11743047))
  + Few cases reported with *EGFR* internal tandem duplication (ITD) mutation ([Nat Commun 2018;9:2378](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29915264))
  + Rare cases with *BRAF* rearrangements but not *BRAF* V600E mutation ([Histopathology 2020;77:611](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32590884), [Nat Commun 2018;9:2378](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29915264))
* Classic type: *EGFR* ITD mutation is a consistent and recurrent genetic event ([Histopathology 2020;77:611](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32590884))
* Mixed type: may have either *EGFR* ITD or *ETV6-NTRK3* gene fusion

Sample pathology report

* Right kidney, total nephrectomy:
  + Mesoblastic nephroma, cellular type, stage II (due to renal sinus and perirenal fat invasion) (see comment)
  + Comment: Tumor shows dense cellularity with plump cells with vesicular nuclei and with a high mitotic activity. Tumor is infiltrating the renal sinus and perirenal fat but is not reaching the resection margins. Lymph nodes are free of tumor.

Differential diagnosis

* [Metanephric stromal tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormetastromal.html):
  + Alternating, nodular cellularity, angiodysplasia, onion skinning
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)+
  + *BRAF* V600E mutation
* [Stromal type Wilms tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumorwilmkids.html):
  + Often rhabdomyoblasts and adipose tissue
  + Often associated with nephrogenic rests
  + More sampling might reveal epithelial and or blastemal elements
  + [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html)+
* [Clear cell sarcoma of the kidney](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumorclearcellsarcoma.html):
  + Characteristic arborising vascular pattern
  + [BCOR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcor.html)+
* [Rhabdoid tumor of the kidney](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumorrhabdoid.html):
  + [INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html) loss
* [Inflammatory myofibroblastic tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueinflammyofibro.html):
  + [ALK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html)+

2. *BRAF* V600E mutation
3. *ETV6-NTRK3* gene fusion

Board review style answer #1

**C.** *ETV6-NTRK3* gene fusion. This is a mesoblastic nephroma, cellular type.
