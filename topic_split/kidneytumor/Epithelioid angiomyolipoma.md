Definition / general

* Epithelioid angiomyolipoma (EAML) is a distinct rare subtype of angiomyolipoma with unique histological features
* EAML is characterized by the presence of at least 80% epithelioid cells, which is defined by round to polygonal cells with abundant pink to amphophilic to focally clear cytoplasm

Essential features

* Rare mesenchymal tumor first reported in 1995 ([Int J Surg Pathol 1995;2:539](https://scholar.google.com/scholar_lookup?title=Renal%20epithelioid%20oxyphilic%20neoplasms%20%28REON%29%2C%20a%20pleomorphic%20variant%20of%20a%20renal%20angiomyolipoma%20%28abstract%29&journal=Int%20J%20Surg%20Pathol&volume=2&issue=suppl&pages=539-539&publication_year=1995&author=Martignoni%2CG&author=Pea%2CM&author=Bonetti%2CF))
* In contrast to other angiomyolipomas, EAML have malignant potential
* Presence of at least 80% epithelial component required for EAML diagnosis
* EAML shows positive staining with melanocytic markers, smooth muscle markers and cathepsin K, while negative for PAX8 and cytokeratins

Terminology

* Pure epithelioid PEComa of the kidney
* Traditionally, the term epithelioid angiomyolipoma is used for tumors in the kidney; at other sites, the term epithelioid PEComa is used

Epidemiology

* Rare in clinical practice
* Incidence rate among renal tumor is 1% and among renal angiomyolipoma is 4.6 - 7.7% ([Mod Pathol 2013;26:1355](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23599151))
* Mean patient age is 50 years (range: 30 - 80 years) and there is no sex predilection; EAMLs represent 4.6% of all resected angiomyolipomas ([Mod Pathol 2013;26:1355](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23599151))

Sites

* Kidney

Etiology

* Tuberous sclerosis complex (TSC)
  + More than half of TSC patients have renal angiomyolipoma
  + Mutations in the *TSC1* gene on chromosome 9q34 and the *TSC2* gene on chromosome 16p13 are identified in both TSC primary disease and sporadic EAML cases
  + These proteins interact to form heterodimers with GTPase activity
  + Dysfunctional hamartin and tuberin complexes lead to increased RHEB-GTP, activating the mTORC1 pathway, promoting cell growth and vascular smooth muscle differentiation ([Nat Rev Neprol 2018;14:704](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30232410), [Nephrol Dial Transplant 2019;34:502](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29697822), [J Pathol 2008;214:387](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18085521), [Urol Case Rep 2022;45:102204](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36117733))
* *TFE3* gene rearrangements
  + Rare EAML cases exhibit rearrangements of the *TFE3* gene ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:1395](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20871214))
  + TFE3 is a transcription factor that regulates gene expression in the transforming growth factor β signaling pathway
  + It activates mTOR signaling, promoting tumorigenesis
  + EAML with *TFE3* rearrangement is considered a unique subtype of PEComa ([Oncol Clin Pract 2020;16:22](http://dx.doi.org/10.5603/OCP.2020.0003), [Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:1395](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20871214), [J Pathol 2008;214:387](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18085521), [Clin Neprol Case Stud 2018;6:11](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29951351))
* Genetic mutations in EAML: *TSC2* is a common pathogenic factor, while *TP53, ATRX* and *RB1* mutations are frequently noted in malignant cases ([J Urol 2021;206:e708](https://doi.org/10.1097/JU.0000000000002054.09))

Clinical features

* Patients with small EAML may be asymptomatic but larger tumors tend to be symptomatic, often found during physical examination
* Depending on the size and location, it may present with fatigue, fever, low back pain, abdominal pain, hematuria, bleeding, dysuria and renal dysfunction; renal rupture may rarely occur ([Urol Case Rep 2021;38:101645](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33850730))
* It is known to cause paraneoplastic syndromes like high erythrocyte sedimentation rate (ESR), high platelet count and disruption of the immune system ([J Int Med Res 2021;49:3000605211032493](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34325570), [Kaohsiung J Med Sci 2022;38:925](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36056704))

Diagnosis

* Imaging: CT scan, MRI ([World J Surg Oncol 2015;13:280](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26391670), [Asian J Surg 2020;43:967](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31959574))
* Biopsy
* Histopathological examination and immunostaining
* Genetic testing

Radiology description

* Ultrasound: lack of specific findings ([Abdom Radiol (NY) 2018;43:880](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28856401))
* Computed tomography (CT) scan
  + Shows irregular mixed density mass shadows (usually > 45 HU) with heterogeneous enhancement, "fast in and fast out" type on contrast enhanced CT scan ([Abdom Imaging 2014;39:588](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24504542), [World J Surg Oncol 2015;13:280](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26391670))
  + Features suggestive of malignancy: large tumor size > 9 cm, necrosis, metastases, extrarenal extension, formation of tumor thrombus in a vein ([Sci Rep 2015;5:10030](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25939249))
* Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI): T2 hypointense reticular enhancement and "fast in and fast out" attributes ([Kaohsiung J Med Sci 2022;38:925](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36056704))
* Unlike conventional angiomyolipomas, EAML may have a reduced amount of fat within the tumor tissue; this can make it more challenging to differentiate from other kidney tumors using imaging techniques like CT scans and MRIs

Prognostic factors

* EAML has the potential to be malignant, recur and metastasize; however, the observed variation in its aggressive behavior, ranging from 5 - 66%, should be considered ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:715](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20410812), [Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:161](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21263237), [Clin Neprol Case Stud 2018;6:11](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29951351), [Urol Case Rep 2018;18:52](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29785369))
* The following factors have been correlated with poor prognosis: epithelial nuclear atypia ≥ 70%, mitotic count ≥ 2 per 10 high power fields, atypical mitoses, necrosis, epithelial cells > 70% or atypical epithelial cells > 60% ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:715](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20410812), [Sci Rep 2015;5:10030](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25939249))
* Risk stratification model, based on prognostic parameters (presence of tuberous sclerosis complex, tumor necrosis, extrarenal extension or renal vein invasion, carcinoma-like histology and tumor size > 7.0 cm), classifies tumors into 3 distinct categories ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:161](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21263237))
  + Low risk (0 - 1 parameter)
  + Intermediate risk (2 - 3 parameters)
  + High risk (≥ 4 parameters)
* For patients possessing at least 3 adverse parameters, a substantial 80% experienced disease progression ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:161](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21263237))
  + Recent risk model has been suggested using different parameters (pT3 - pT4 stage, presence of necrosis, severe nuclear atypia, presence of atypical mitoses, mitotic count ≥ 2, Ki67 ≥ 10% and negative SMA expression) ([BMC Urol 2022;22:148](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36096809))
* Mutant p53 expression may be an adverse prognostic indicator for renal EAML ([Diagn Pathol 2023;18:14](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36740682))

Case reports

* 12 year old boy presented with episodic mild to moderate pain in the left flank for 1 month ([J Kidney Cancer VHL 2021;8:20](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34178582))
* 34 year old woman was complaining of acute left flank pain ([Oncol Lett 2023;26:409](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37600337))
* 40 year old man with a history of tuberous sclerosis presented with 5 months of painless gross hematuria ([Urol Case Rep 2022;45:102204](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36117733))
* 40 year old woman presented with complaints of right abdominal pain with tumor thrombus in inferior vena cava (IVC) and right atrium ([Autops Case Rep 2020;10:e2020190](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33344317))
* 57 year old man with lung effusion and 2 lung nodules ([J Kidney Cancer VHL 2022;9:13](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35582346))

Treatment

* Solid renal lesions > 4 cm should be treated promptly ([Onco Targets Ther 2014;7:823](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24920923))
* Surgical treatment: mainstay is surgical resection
  + Radical nephrectomy for tumors ≥ 4 cm; partial nephrectomy for tumors < 4 cm
  + Intraoperative frozen pathological examination aids differentiation from renal cancer
  + Interventional therapies (selective arterial embolization [SAE], ablation) for hemorrhage or surgery intolerance are considered when renal AML is > 4 cm or clinical symptoms are present ([Asian J Surg 2020;43:967](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31959574), [Ann Transl Med 2019;7:766](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32042782))
* Medical therapy: for noncandidate patients for surgery
  + mTOR Inhibitors (e.g., rapamycin, everolimus) target *TSC1 / TSC2* gene mutations but resistance and pathway changes can occur
  + Combination therapies (e.g., mTOR inhibitors with exemestane) effective for specific mutations ([Clin Cancer Res 2020;26:5534](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32605908))
  + Angiogenesis inhibitors (bevacizumab, pizopanib, sorafenib) for *TFE3* associated EAML ([Front Oncol 2021;11:641376](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33842348), [Front Oncol 2020;10:582087](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33330059))
  + PD-1 antibodies enhance T cell infiltration, inhibit tumor growth and are effective in EAML with *TSC* gene mutation ([Ann Transl Med 2019;7:766](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32042782), [J Immunother Cancer 2018;6:97](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30285856), [Kaohsiung J Med Sci 2022;38:925](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36056704))

Gross description

* Large nodular or irregularly infiltrative in gross view, soft in texture, heterogeneous, partially cystic appearance with a grayish tan to grayish brown cut surface
* Hemorrhage and necrosis may be seen
* Potential extension into the renal vein or IVC may also occur
* EAML could involve lymph nodes; however, in some cases, it does not represent metastatic disease ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:715](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20410812), [Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:161](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21263237), [Transl Androl Urol 2021;10:1506](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33850785))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* According to WHO, more than 80% epithelial cells is required to diagnose EAML
* Malignant features on core needle biopsy: epithelial nuclear atypia ≥ 70%, mitotic count ≥ 2 per 10 high power fields, atypical mitoses, necrosis, epithelial cells > 70% or atypical epithelial cells > 60% ([Kaohsiung J Med Sci 2022;38:925](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36056704), [Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:715](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20410812), [Sci Rep 2015;5:10030](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25939249))
* 2 distinct histologic patterns were described as follows
  + Carcinoma-like growth pattern
    - This pattern is characterized by the presence of large, polygonal cells with densely eosinophilic cytoplasm, diffusely atypical nuclei and prominent nucleoli
    - These cells are arranged in cohesive nests, broad alveoli and compartmentalized sheets that are separated by thin vascular septa
    - In some cases, intranuclear inclusions, nuclear degeneration and multinucleation are observed
    - Mitotic activity is variable, with some cases showing > 2 mitotic figures per high power field (HPF), while the majority do not exhibit mitosis; atypical mitosis is not observed
    - Hemorrhage and necrosis may be present to varying degrees
    - Combination of prominent nucleoli, intranuclear inclusions and large, discohesive cells with eosinophilic cytoplasm gives the tumor cells a ganglion cell-like appearance
  + Epithelioid and plump spindled cells in diffuse growth
    - This pattern consists of epithelioid and plump spindled cells that are densely packed in diffuse sheets with homogenous growth without separation by vascular septa unlike the carcinoma-like pattern
    - Cells are relatively uniform with epithelioid features, clear to granular, feathery eosinophilic cytoplasm and may lack atypia throughout; nuclei exhibit vesicular chromatin, prominent nucleoli, with intranuclear inclusion being less commonly observed
    - Multinucleated giant cells may be present, either singly or in groups, areas of hemorrhage are often absent in this pattern ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:161](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21263237))
* \*Besides those 2 patterns, morphology of a papillary component has been described; the papillae are by epithelioid cells containing abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm and round nuclei with conspicuous eosinophilic macronucleoli and perinucleolar halos ([Int J Surg Pathol 2023 May 9 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37160913))

Cytology description

* Round to plump spindle cells with indistinct cell borders
* Displaced eccentric nuclei with a voluminous, frothy cytoplasm and occasional nuclear pseudoinclusions ([Diagn Cytopathol 2000;23:192](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10945908), [J Am Soc Cytopathol 2023;12:142](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36702738))

Positive stains

* [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html), **HMB50**, [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html), [microphthalmia transcription factor (MITF)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmitf.html), [NKI-C3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd63.html), [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) (variable), [tyrosinase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstyrosinase.html) ([Histopathology 2018;72:441](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28837273), [Kaohsiung J Med Sci 2022;38:925](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36056704))
* [Cathepsin K](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscathepsink.html), expressed in all PEComas ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:1370](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30001237))
* **PNL2** has a higher sensitivity for differentiating PEComa from other renal tumors (85% versus 81%) and has specificity in malignant PEComa (89% versus 81%) ([Histopathology 2018;72:441](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28837273))
* [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) ≥ 10% correlates significantly with malignancy ([BMC Urol 2022;22:148](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36096809))
* **GPNMB** ([J Pathol 2022;257:158](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35072947))

Negative stains

* [Cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html)
* [Parvalbumin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/parvalbumin.html), unlike in other PEComa ([Virchows Arch 2021;478:785](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32524185))

Electron microscopy description

* Presence of glycogen, mitochondria and prominent electron dense, membrane bound granules (60 - 250 nm)
* Absence of melanosomes or premelanosomes ([Am J Surg Pathol 1998;22:663](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9630173))

Differential diagnosis

* [Clear cell renal cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantrccclear.html):
  + Nests of cells with abundant mostly clear cytoplasm surrounded by delicate intricately branching vasculature
  + Positive for [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html), [CAIX](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscarbonicanhydraseix.html) (complete membranous) and [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html)
  + Negative for [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html), [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html) and [cathepsin K](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscathepsink.html)
* [Chromophobe renal cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantrccchromo.html):
  + Solid nests of cells with abundant pink cytoplasm with incomplete fibrovascular septa
  + Hyperchromatic nuclei with irregular nuclear border and perinuclear halo
  + Usually, no significant nuclear pleomorphism
  + Diffusely positive for [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html), [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html) and [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html)
  + Negative for [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html), [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html) and [cathepsin K](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscathepsink.html)
* [Oncocytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumoroncocytoma.html):
  + Usually, small solid nests of tumor cells but can also show micro and macrocystic architecture
  + Abundant granular eosinophilic cytoplasm with uniformly round nuclei with smooth nuclear border
  + Can show some degenerative type nuclear atypia
  + Positive for [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html), [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)
  + Negative for [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html), [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html) and [cathepsin K](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscathepsink.html)
* [MITF translocation associated renal cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantASPLTFE3.html):
  + Positive for [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html); may also be positive for melanocytic markers ([cathepsin K](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscathepsink.html), [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html) and [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html))
  + Negative for [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) or [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html)
  + *TFE3* or *TFEB* gene rearrangement by FISH studies
* [Renal cell carcinoma, unclassified type with oncocytic features](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantnos.html):
  + Positive for [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html) and usually [cytokeratins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), while negative for [cathepsin K](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscathepsink.html), [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html) and [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html)
* [Malignant melanoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticmelanoma.html):
  + Adipocytes and abnormal vessels are not seen in melanoma
  + Positive for [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html), [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html), [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html) and [PRAME](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsPRAME.html)
  + Negative for [cathepsin K](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscathepsink.html), [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) and [cytokeratins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)
* [Epithelioid angiosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueangiosarcoma.html):
  + Positive for [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [ERG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainserg.html) or [FLI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfli1.html); may stain for [cytokeratins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)
  + Negative for [cathepsin K](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscathepsink.html), [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html) and [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html)
* [Dedifferentiated liposarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuededifflipo.html):
  + Well differentiated liposarcoma component may be identified
  + Positive for [CDK4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdk4.html), [MDM2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmdm2.html) by immunohistochemical stain
  + *CDK4* and *MDM2* amplification by FISH
  + Negative for [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html), [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html) and [cathepsin K](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscathepsink.html)
* [Urothelial carcinoma with extensive sarcomatoid differentiation](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladdersarcomatoidvariant.html):
  + Positive for [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html), [OSCAR cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoscarkeratin.html), [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html) and [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html) (variable)
* [Eosinophilic solid and cystic RCC (ESC RCC)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumorescrcc.html):
  + Positive for [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html) and [cytokeratin 20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html), however, could also express [melanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html), [cathepsin K](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscathepsink.html) or [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html) ([Hum Pathol 2018:80:65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29885406))
  + Usually have substantial cystic component

Board review style question #1

Which of the following is true in epithelioid angiomyolipoma?

1. Positive for cathepsin K, HMB45, MelanA and MITF
2. Positive for PAX8
3. Positive for S100, SOX10, HMB45 and MelanA
4. Positivity for TFE3 immunohistochemical stain usually correlates with *TFE3* gene rearrangement

Board review style answer #1

**A.** Positive for cathepsin K, HMB45, MelanA and MITF. Angiomyolipomas are positive for melanoma markers including cathepsin K, HMB45, MelanA and MITF; however, they are negative for S100 and SOX10. Answer D is incorrect because *TFE3* gene rearrangement is extremely uncommon in epithelioid angiomyolipoma. Positivity of TFE3 immunohistochemical stain does not correlate with *TFE3* gene rearrangement. Answer C is incorrect because malignant melanoma is positive for S100, SOX10, HMB45 and MelanA. Answer B is incorrect because angiomyolipomas are negative for PAX8 and cytokeratins.

Board review style question #2

[![BRQ image](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/kidneytumorepitheliodamlparwani01.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/kidneytumorepitheliodamlparwani01.jpg)

A radiological image of 40 year old man complaining of painless gross hematuria and left flank pain showed left renal mass with a central stellate scar measuring 16 x 14 x 11 cm. Left nephrectomy revealed a tumor with morphology depicted in the image above. Necrosis was present in ~30% of tumor cells. Immunohistochemistry showed diffuse strong staining for HMB45, MelanA and focal moderate staining for SMA. The tumor cells showed negative staining for EMA, AE1 / AE3, CK7, S100 and desmin. Which of the following genetic syndromes can be associated with this entity?

1. Birt-Hogg-Dubé syndrome
2. Succinate dehydrogenase deficiency
3. Tuberous sclerosis complex
4. Von Hippel-Lindau syndrome

Board review style answer #2

**C.** Tuberous sclerosis complex. Tuberous sclerosis complex (TSC) is an autosomal dominant genetic disorder resulting from inactivating losses of *TSC1* (9q34) or *TSC2* (16p13.3) genes. This is characterized by mental retardation, seizures and the development of neoplasms such as angiomyolipoma, subependymal giant cell tumors, cutaneous angiofibromas, cardiac rhabdomyomas, lymphangioleiomyomatosis and pulmonary multifocal micronodular hyperplasia. Answers A, B and D are incorrect because they are not associated with renal epithelioid angiomyolipoma (EAML).

Board review style question #3

Which is a true feature of epithelioid angiomyolipoma (EAML)?

1. Diagnosis requires at least 20% epithelioid cells
2. They are a common (> 10%) variant of angiomyolipoma
3. Tumors are almost always associated with tuberous sclerosis
4. Tumors may have malignant behavior

Board review style answer #3

**D.** Tumors may have malignant behavior. EAML has the potential to be malignant and recur and metastasize. Answer B is incorrect because it is a distinct rare subtype of angiomyolipoma with unique histological features.
Answer A is incorrect because EAML is characterized by the presence of at least 80% epithelioid cells. Answer C is incorrect because more than half of tuberous sclerosis (TSC) patients have renal angiomyolipoma but this does not imply it is always associated with TSC.
