Definition / general

* Rare renin secreting tumor arising from the specialized smooth muscle cells of the glomerular afferent arteriole in the renal juxtaglomerular apparatus

Essential features

* Occurs mostly in young adults; female predominance
* Frequently associated with severe and poorly controlled hypertension, hypokalemia and hyperaldosteronism secondary to tumor renin secretion
* Almost always benign with single case of metastatic disease
* Sheets of polygonal to spindle cells with well defined cell border and prominent vasculature
* Tumor cells are positive for renin, vimentin, CD34 and CD117

Terminology

* Reninoma

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8361/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=71) - juxtaglomerular tumor

Epidemiology

* ~100 cases reported
* Young adults (20 - 35 years) but has been reported in children and older adults ([Am J Clin Pathol 2001;116:854](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11764074))
* F > M (~2:1) ([Pediatr Nephrol 1993;7:404](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8398651), [Am J Clin Pathol 2001;116:854](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11764074))

Sites

* Kidney cortex

Clinical features

* 3 clinical categories ([Urol Oncol 2010;28:34](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19914097)):
  + Typical variant:
    - Majority of cases
    - Characteristic hypertension, hyperaldosteronism and hypokalemia secondary to hyperreninemia ([Clin Sci Mol Med Suppl 1973;45:279s4](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4522175), [J Urol 1973;109:349](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4571092))
    - Hypertension does not respond to medical antihypertensive therapy
  + Atypical variant:
    - Hypertension with normal potassium levels ([Pediatr Nephrol 1993;7:404](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8398651))
  + Nonfunctioning variant:
    - Very rare reports of nonfunctioning tumors without hypertension or hypokalemia ([Pathol Int 1997;47:393](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9211527), [Case Rep Pathol 2013;2013:973865](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23607027), [Medicine (Baltimore) 2020;99:e22057](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32899070))
* Other symptoms include headache, polyuria, nocturia, dizziness, vomiting ([Pediatr Nephrol 1993;7:404](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8398651), [Am J Clin Pathol 2001;116:854](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11764074))

Diagnosis

* Diagnosis is suspected in young patients presenting with severe hypertension and hypokalemia and radiologic findings consistent with renal tumor
* Clinical workup to rule out other causes of hypertension

Laboratory

* Elevated serum renin and aldosterone levels

Radiology description

* Ultrasound: hypoechoic mass ([Urology 2003;61:1259](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12809918), [Urol Int 2010;85:121](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20453480))
* CT ([Radiographics 2010;30:1525](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21071373)):
  + Hypodense or isodense solid mass
  + Dynamic CT shows hypovascular mass in arterial phase despite profuse vascularity
  + Moderate enhancement during delayed phase
* MRI ([Radiographics 2010;30:1525](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21071373), [Am J Kidney Dis 2019;73:566](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30527885)):
  + Variable findings
  + Isointensity or hypointensity on T1 weighted images
  + Hyperintensity on T2 weighted images
* Renal vein sampling may be useful, especially in small tumors
* Renal arteriography shows no evidence of renal artery stenosis

Prognostic factors

* 90% of patients will become normotensive following tumor resection
* 10% remain mildly hypertensive despite surgical resection ([Am J Clin Pathol 2001;116:854](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11764074))
* Unlikely to recur after resection
* Almost always benign, with single reported case of metastatic disease to the lung ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1098](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15252319))
* Single case of death due to massive brain hemorrhage secondary to severe hypertension ([Hum Pathol 1974;5:236](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4811608))
* Single case of juxtaglomerular cell tumor causing fetal demise ([Int Urol Nephrol 2011;43:365](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20556510))

Case reports

* 16 year old boy with hereditary *BRIP1* mutation and spindle cell hemangioma, presenting with juxtaglomerular cell tumor ([Genes (Basel) 2021;12:220](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33546375))
* 22 year old woman with juxtaglomerular cell tumor associated with membranous glomerulonephritis ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2003;7:314](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14571436))
* 29 year old woman with nonfunctioning juxtaglomerular cell tumor mimicking renal cell carcinoma ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2020;99:e22057](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32899070))
* 52 year old man with metastatic juxtaglomerular cell tumor to the lung ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1098](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15252319))
* 74 year old man with atypical juxtaglomerular cell tumor ([Case Rep Pathol 2018;2018:6407360](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30473900))

Treatment

* Surgical resection: partial or total nephrectomy

Gross description

* Unilateral and solitary ([Am J Clin Pathol 2001;116:854](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11764074), [Diagn Pathol 2011;6:80](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21871063))
* Well circumscribed; fully or partially encapsulated
* Gray to yellow-tan, solid cut surface; may have hemorrhage and be partially cystic
* Average: 2 - 3 cm (range: 0.2 - 15 cm)

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Glomoid appearance with sheets of polygonal to spindle cells with well defined cell border ([Am J Clin Pathol 2001;116:854](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11764074), [Diagn Pathol 2011;6:80](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21871063))
* Central, round, uniform nuclei
* Variable amount of clear to eosinophilic cytoplasm
* May demonstrate papillary architecture ([Hum Pathol 1993;24:1168](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8244316))
* Prominent vasculature component consisting of numerous small thin walled vessels and small clusters of thick walled, often hyalinized vessels
* Hemangiopericytoma-like capillaries with branching vessels may be seen
* Scanty, hyalinized or myxoid stroma
* Lymphoplasmacytic infiltrate commonly present
* Entrapped renal tubules in periphery (~50% of cases), sometimes hyperplastic and with papillary pattern
* Mitotic activity, necrosis and pleomorphism are uncommon ([Am J Clin Pathol 2001;116:854](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11764074))
* Rare capsular or vascular invasion ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1098](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15252319), [Nat Clin Pract Nephrol 2008;4:458](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18654602), [Am J Surg Pathol 1994;18:837](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8037298))

Cytology description

* Sheets of polygonal cells with indistinct cell borders, scant granular cytoplasm and hyperchromatic nuclei, showing mild or no pleomorphism ([Int J Surg Pathol 2011;19:93](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19098017))
* Nuclei with evenly distributed chromatin and occasional noticeable nucleoli
* Background of foamy histiocytes and red blood cells

Positive stains

* **Renin**: but may also be positive in some cases of Wilms tumor, renal cell carcinoma or renal oncocytoma ([Am J Pathol 1987;126:73](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3028144), [Am J Surg Pathol 1994;18:837](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8037298))
* [Vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html): diffuse ([Am J Clin Pathol 2001;116:854](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11764074), [Diagn Pathol 2011;6:80](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21871063))
* [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html): diffuse ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2006;130:707](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16683889), [Am J Clin Pathol 2001;116:854](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11764074), [Diagn Pathol 2011;6:80](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21871063))
* [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html): variable ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2006;130:707](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16683889), [Diagn Pathol 2011;6:80](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21871063))
* [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [h-caldesmon](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscaldesmon.html): variable ([Diagn Pathol 2011;6:80](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21871063), [Hum Pathol 2013;44:47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22939575))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Loss of chromosomes 9 and 11, as well as aneusomy ([Cancer 2005;104:504](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15968688), [Hum Pathol 2008;39:459](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18261631), [Hum Pathol 2013;44:47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22939575))
* Whole genome expression analysis (in 2 cases): 415 upregulated genes, including renin and CD117 and 325 downregulated genes ([Hum Pathol 2013;44:47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22939575))

Sample pathology report

* Right kidney, mass, partial nephrectomy:
  + Juxtaglomerular cell tumor, measuring 2.5 cm in greatest dimension (see comment)
  + Surgical margins, negative for tumor
  + Comment: The sections show a well circumscribed tumor composed of sheets of uniform round to polygonal cells with clear to slightly eosinophilic cytoplasm and distinct cell borders. Immunohistochemistry was performed and shows diffuse positivity for renin, CD34 and CD117 in the tumor cells. The overall findings support the diagnosis of juxtaglomerular cell tumor.

Differential diagnosis

* [Glomus tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumorglomus.html):
  + Not typically associated with hyperreninism and hypertension
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) and [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html) negative or focally positive
  + No ultrastructural crystals
* [Solitary fibrous tumor / hemangiopericytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesft.html):
  + Usually spindle cells but may have focal round cell areas
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) positive but [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html) and **renin** negative
  + Also positive for [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html), [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html) and [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html)
* [Metanephric adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormetanephric.html):
  + Acinar growth pattern
  + Psammoma bodies in the stroma
  + [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html) positive
* [Papillary renal cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantrccpap.html):
  + Atypia, pleomorphism
  + [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html) positive and **renin** negative
* [Collecting duct carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantcollectingduct.html):
  + Nuclear pleomorphism
  + Frequent mitosis
  + [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html) positive and [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html) negative
* [Urothelial carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderurothelialinvasivegen.html):
  + Nuclear hyperchromasia and pleomorphism
  + [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgata3.html) positive
* [Epithelioid angiomyolipoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumorepitheliodaml.html):
  + Adipose component
  + [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html) and [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html) positive
* [Wilms tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumorwilmkids.html):
  + Typically contains blastemal cells with nuclear overlapping

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case467image3.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case467image3.jpg)

A 25 year old woman presents with uncontrollable hypertension and subsequent workup found a 2 cm left kidney mass. She underwent a partial resection and sections showed the above histologic features. What is the likely diagnosis?

1. Glomus tumor
2. Juxtaglomerular cell tumor
3. Papillary renal cell carcinoma
4. Solitary fibrous tumor

Board review style answer #1

**B.**  Juxtaglomerular cell tumor should be included in the differential diagnosis of renal tumors with concurrent hypertension, especially in young patients. Histologically, it is composed by sheets of polygonal to spindle cells with well defined cell border and prominent vasculature.

Board review style question #2

A 20 year old man presents with severe hypertension, hyperreninemia and hypokalemia. Imaging during workup located a 3 cm mass in the right kidney. He underwent a partial nephrectomy and sections of the tumor show sheets of round to polygonal cells with clear to eosinophilic cytoplasm and well defined cell borders. The tumor cells will be positive for which of the following immunohistochemical stains?

1. CD34
2. CD99
3. PAX8
4. STAT6

Board review style answer #2

**A.**  CD34. Juxtaglomerular cell tumors are positive for renin, vimentin, CD34 and CD117.
