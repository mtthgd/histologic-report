Definition / general

* Rare benign mesenchymal tumor of the kidney arising from smooth muscle fibers of the renal capsule, renal pelvis or vascular smooth muscle of renal vessels

Essential features

* Benign smooth muscle tumor with female predominance
* Arises from smooth muscle fibers of the renal capsule, renal pelvis or vascular smooth muscle of renal vessels
* Bland spindle cells in intersecting fascicles with eosinophilic cytoplasm
* Positive for smooth muscle markers, ER and PR and negative for melanocytic markers, cathepsin K, cytokeratins, CD117, S100 and CD34

ICD coding

* ICD-O:
  + [8890/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=82) - leiomyoma, NOS
  + [8897/1](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=82) - smooth muscle tumor of uncertain malignant potential
* ICD-10: [D30.0](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D30-/D30.0) - benign neoplasm of the kidney
* ICD-11: [2E86.1](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http:%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f2131890863) - leiomyoma of other or unspecified sites

Epidemiology

* 0.5% in surgically resected renal tumors ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1557](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27276240))
* 4 - 5.5% in autopsy studies ([J Urol 1971;106:503](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4330359))
* Higher prevalence in middle aged women ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1557](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27276240))

Sites

* Kidney: renal cortex or renal capsule, less commonly in muscularis propria of the renal pelvis and smooth muscle of cortical vessels
* Seen equally in the left and right kidney
* More frequently reported in the lower pole of the kidney ([Urol Case Rep 2017;13:3](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28417074))

Pathophysiology

* Arises from smooth muscle fibers of the renal capsule, renal pelvis or vascular smooth muscle of renal vessels ([J Urol 1990;143:994](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2184259))

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Most are asymptomatic and found incidentally
* Larger tumors may present as a palpable mass or with flank pain or hematuria, especially if located on the renal pelvis ([Pathology 2006;38:454](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17008289))

Diagnosis

* Histologic examination of tissue
* Imaging modalities are not conclusive

Radiology description

* Ultrasound: most are hypoechoic, some with mixed echogenicity ([Radiat Med 1993;11:81](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8372241))
* CT: well circumscribed margins, minimal parenchymal distortion, no evidence of extrarenal invasion ([Urol Int 1992;48:219](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1585520), [J Urol 1987;138:853](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3656547), [J Urol 1990;143:994](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2184259))
* Angiography: hypovascularity or slight vascularity, absence of features indicative of malignancy (e.g., vessel encasement, renal vein invasion, arteriovenous shunting) ([Radiat Med 1993;11:81](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8372241))

Prognostic factors

* Benign
* Excellent prognosis

Case reports

* 20 year old woman presenting with large symptomatic renal leiomyoma ([Radiol Case Rep 2020;15:515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32140199))
* 39 year old man presenting with right flank mass ([Urol Case Rep 2017;13:3](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28417074))
* 79 year old woman with small subcortical renal leiomyoma ([Urol Case Rep 2021;39:101792](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34401346))

Treatment

* Surveillance or surgical resection depending on the tumor size, location and symptoms

Gross description

* Solid, well circumscribed, tan to white with whorled and bulging cut surface
* Focal cystic degeneration, hemorrhage or irregular calcifications may be present
* Often solitary but occasionally multiple ([Scand J Urol Nephrol 1999;33:138](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10360460))
* Most are small but the largest reported tumor measured 57.5 cm ([Br J Surg 1956;43:497](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/13316037))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Arranged in intersecting fascicles
* Spindle cells with blunt ended, cigar shaped nuclei and eosinophilic cytoplasm
* Minimal to no nuclear pleomorphism / atypia
* Low mitotic activity
* No necrosis
* Reference: [Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1557](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27276240)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Breakpoints in the q13-15 region of chromosome 12 ([Pediatr Pathol 1993;13:435](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8396768))
* Single study showed combined losses of chromosomes 4, 6, 12 and 14 ([Histol Histopathol 2007;22:883](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17503345))

Sample pathology report

* Right kidney, mass, partial nephrectomy:
  + Renal leiomyoma, measuring 1.5 cm in greatest dimension (see comment)
  + Surgical margins, negative for tumor
  + Comment: The sections show a well circumscribed tumor composed of bland spindle cells in intersecting fascicles. Immunohistochemistry was performed to show positivity for SMA, desmin, h-caldesmon, ER and PR. Cytokeratin AE1 / AE3, PAX8, S100, CD117 and CD34 are negative. The overall findings support the above diagnosis of a renal leiomyoma.

Differential diagnosis

* [Angiomyolipoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumorangiomyolipoma.html):
  + Smooth muscle component with mature adipose tissue and thick walled vessels but could be difficult to distinguish from fat poor (leiomyomatous) variant
  + Melanocytic markers ([MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html), [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html)) and [cathepsin K](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscathepsink.html) positive
  + Both smooth muscle markers ([SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html)) positive
* [Leiomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueleiomyosarcoma.html):
  + Spindle cells with moderate to severe nuclear pleomorphism
  + Tumor necrosis and high mitotic activity (with atypical mitoses)
  + Infiltrative border
* [Sarcomatoid renal cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantrccsarcoma.html):
  + Atypical spindle and giant cells with marked nuclear pleomorphism
  + Atypical mitotic figures
  + [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html), [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) positive
* [Renomedullary interstitial cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumorrenalmedfibroma.html):
  + Small, well circumscribed nodules in the renal medulla
  + Spindle to stellate cells in basophilic stroma
  + Entrapped tubules at the periphery
  + [Desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) negative
* [Mixed epithelial and stromal tumor (MEST)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormixedepithelial.html):
  + Variable solid and cystic areas
  + Stromal and epithelial components with diverse morphology and minimal cytologic atypia
  + Stroma: [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [caldesmon](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscaldesmon.html), [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html), [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html) positive
  + Epithelium: [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html), [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) positive
* [Schwannoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueschwannoma.html):
  + Elongated and wavy cells with tapered ends arranged in hypercellular (Antoni A) and hypocellular (Antoni B) areas with nuclear palisading (Verocay bodies)
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html) positive; [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) negative
* [Solitary fibrous tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumorsolitary.html):
  + Fibroblastic proliferation: spindle cells in haphazard arrangement (patternless pattern) with staghorn-like vessels
  + [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html) positive; [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) negative
* [Synovial sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesynovialsarc.html):
  + Monophasic subtype: monotonous spindle cell proliferation with staghorn vessels
  + Mast cells commonly seen
  + [TLE1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstle1.html) positive; [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [h-caldesmon](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscaldesmon.html) negative
* [Inflammatory myofibroblastic tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueinflammyofibro.html):
  + Myofibroblastic proliferation: spindle cells admixed with inflammatory cells, including lymphocytes, plasma cells and eosinophils
  + [ALK1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html) (60%) positive
* [Gastrointestinal stromal tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stomachGIST.html):
  + Spindle cell type: monotonous bland spindle cells, eosinophilic cytoplasm, indistinct cell borders
  + Variable mitotic activity
  + [DOG1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsDOG1.html), [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) positive; [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) negative

Board review style question #1

[![BRQ image](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/kidneytumorleiomyomagamachoy03.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/kidneytumorleiomyomagamachoy03.jpg)

A 26 year old woman presents with back pain. CT scan located a 2.8 cm, well circumscribed, subcapsular mass in the right kidney. She underwent partial nephrectomy. Grossly, the mass was tan-white with a whorled and bulging cut surface. Microscopically, it consisted of a low grade, monotonous, spindle cell proliferation with blunt ended, cigar shaped nuclei and eosinophilic cytoplasm. No coagulative necrosis, abnormal vessels or fatty infiltration were noted. What is the most likely diagnosis?

1. Angiomyolipoma
2. Leiomyoma
3. Leiomyosarcoma
4. Sarcomatoid renal cell carcinoma

Board review style answer #1

**B.** Leiomyoma. Renal leiomyomas are benign tumors that arise from smooth muscle fibers of the renal capsule, renal pelvis or vascular smooth muscle of renal vessels. It is characterized by a monotonous, spindle cell proliferation with blunt ended, cigar shaped nuclei and eosinophilic cytoplasm. The low grade features are distinct from its malignant counterpart (leiomyosarcoma), as well as sarcomatoid renal cell carcinoma. The lack of mature adipose tissue and thick walled vessels makes angiomyolipoma less likely.

Board review style question #2

A 45 year old woman with a 2.2 cm mass in the right renal pelvis and ipsilateral hydronephrosis, underwent a radical nephrectomy. On H&E, the tumor was composed of bland spindle cells with cigar shaped nuclei. No coagulative necrosis or abnormal vessels were noted. Which of the following immunostaining profiles supports the diagnosis of leiomyoma?

1. HMB45+, MelanA+, cathepsin K+, desmin+, caldesmon+
2. PAX8+, CK7-, CD117+, CAIX-, vimentin-, AMACR-
3. PAX8+, cytokeratins+, vimentin+, CD10+, CK7-, CAIX+ (box-like pattern), S100-
4. SMA+, desmin+, ER+, PAX8-, CD34-, cathepsin K-

Board review style answer #2

**D.** SMA+, desmin+, ER+, PAX8-, CD34-, cathepsin K-. Renal leiomyomas are positive for smooth muscle markers (SMA, desmin, h-caldesmon, calponin), ER and PR. They are negative for cytokeratins, PAX8, CD117, CD34, melanocytic markers and cathepsin K.
