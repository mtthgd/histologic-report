Definition / general

* Biphasic neoplasm composed of epithelial and stromal components
* Member of metanephric tumor family including metanephric adenoma (purely epithelial) and metanephric stromal tumor (purely stromal)

Essential features

* Biphasic neoplasm composed of varying proportions of stromal and epithelial components
* Typically solitary and nonencapsulated
* No nuclear atypia in both components
* Mitotic figures are absent or rare in epithelial component
* Epithelial component is identical to metanephric adenoma
* Stromal / mesenchymal component is identical to metanephric stromal tumor

Terminology

* Previously known as nephrogenic adenofibroma

ICD coding

* [9013/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=84) - adenofibroma, NOS

Epidemiology

* Rare
* Age range 5 months - 36 years ([Am J Surg Pathol 1992;16:325](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1373578), [Am J Surg Pathol 2001;25:433](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11257617))

Sites

* Metanephric adenofibroma occurs in renal cortex and medulla

Pathophysiology

* Unknown, however, several cases have been reported to harbor *BRAF V600E* mutations; genomic alterations in *BRAF* oncogene appear at an early stage in tumorigenesis and are preserved through the tumor progression and therefore are considered as drivers of oncogenesis ([Hum Pathol 2015;46:1153](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26014474), [Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1301](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26274032))

Etiology

* Uncommon renal neoplasm; unknown etiology

Clinical features

* Present with polycythemia, hematuria, hypertension or abdominal pain ([Am J Surg Pathol 1992;16:325](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1373578), [Am J Surg Pathol 2001;25:433](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11257617))
* Associated with papillary renal cell carcinoma and Wilms tumor ([Am J Surg Pathol 2001;25:433](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11257617))

Diagnosis

* The diagnosis is made by microscopic examination of resected tissue

Radiology description

* The imaging appearance is nonspecific, with the tumor often resembling Wilms tumor ([Radiol Case Rep 2018;13:610](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30026886))

Prognostic factors

* Benign
* One case of metanephric adenofibroma in combination with Wilms tumor and renal papillary carcinoma has been reported in literature ([Pediatr Dev Pathol 2012;15:65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21711207))

Case reports

* 5 year old boy with incidental finding of an abdominal mass ([Radiol Case Rep 2018;13:610](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30026886))
* 5 year old girl with progressively increasing abdominal swelling for 7 months ([APSP J Case Rep 2016;7:37](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27900278))
* 10 year old girl with a history of recurrent urinary tract infections ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1301](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26274032))
* 10 year old boy with a history of abdominal pain ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2015;8:3250](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26045848))
* 10 year old boy with history of left flank pain following a fall ([Can J Urol 2013;20:6737](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23587517))
* 29 year old woman ([Int Braz J Urol 2017;43:563](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27802007))

Treatment

* Excision of the tumor is adequate therapy

* Tan / gray / white-yellow cut surface
* Partially cystic

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Biphasic tumor composed of varying proportions of stromal and epithelial components
* Epithelial component identical to metanephric adenoma, tightly packed small tubules, papillary structures or glomeruloid structures (blunt short papillae resembling glomeruli)
* Epithelial cells have small round to oval nuclei with delicate chromatin and absent or inconspicuous nucleoli and slight pale cytoplasm; mitotic figures are absent or rare
* Psammoma bodies are common
* Mesenchymal component identical to metanephric stromal tumor, bland spindle and epithelioid / plump cells with thin hyperchromatic nuclei and indistinct cell borders
* Areas of intratumoral angiodysplasia, concentric cuffing of entrapped tubules ("onion skinning") and heterologous differentiation (cartilage, adipose tissue or glia) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2001;25:433](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11257617))
* No vascular invasion

Positive stains

* **Epithelial component** (stains like [metanephric adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormetanephric.html)): [cytokeratin AE1/AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html) (nuclear, diffuse), [CD57](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd57.html), [BRAF V600E](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbraf.html)
* **Stromal component**: [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) (commonly), [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html), [BRAF V600E](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbraf.html) (variable)

* *BRAF V600E* mutation ([Hum Pathol 2015;46:1153](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26014474), [Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1301](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26274032))

Sample pathology report

* Right kidney, partial nephrectomy:
  + Metanephric adenofibroma, 3.2 cm, resection margins negative for tumor (see comment)
  + Comment: There is a nonencapsulated biphasic renal tumor composed of epithelial and stromal components. There is no nuclear atypia in both components and mitotic figures are rare or absent in the epithelial component. Immunopositive for BRAF V600E.

Differential diagnosis

* [Metanephric adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormetanephric.html):
  + Lacks stromal component
* [Metanephric stromal tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormetastromal.html):
  + Lacks epithelial component
* [Congenital mesoblastic nephroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormesoblastic.html):
  + Particularly classic type - composed of interlacing fascicles of spindle cells with abundant collagen deposition, entrapped renal tubules, irregular infiltrating edges
  + Immunopositive for [actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsactin.html) (often) and immunonegative for [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) (usually) and [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)
* [Wilms tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumorwilmkids.html):
  + Particularly epithelial predominant histology, usually encapsulated and mitotically active
* [Papillary renal cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantrccpap.html):
  + Particularly solid variant, well circumscribed with prominent pseudocapsule, nuclear atypia
  + Immunopositive for [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) and [AMACR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsamacr.html) and immunonegative for [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html) and [CD57](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd57.html)
