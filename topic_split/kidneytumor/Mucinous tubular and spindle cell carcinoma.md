Definition / general

* Distinct polymorphic, malignant renal cell neoplasm composed of bland anastomosing tubules, spindle cell areas and myxoid stroma / extracellular mucin

Essential features

* Compact tubules lined by bland cuboidal or flattened cells merging with low grade spindle cell areas
* Myxoid stroma with basophilic extracellular mucin
* Absence of distinct, well formed papillae
* References: [Eur Urol 2022;82:458](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35853783), [Mod Pathol 2021;34:1392](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33664427), [Diagn Pathol 2015;10:168](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26377921), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2020;144:115](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30865490)

Terminology

* Historic terminology not recommended by WHO 2022
  + Low grade tubular mucinous renal neoplasm
  + Low grade collecting duct carcinoma
  + Low grade myxoid renal epithelial neoplasm with distal nephron differentiation

Epidemiology

* Accounts for < 1% of all renal neoplasms
* Median age: 50 - 60 years
* F > M
* References: [Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:767](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29462091), [Clin Genitourin Cancer 2019;17:268](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31151928), [BJU Int 2015;116:85](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25395040)

Sites

* Kidney

Pathophysiology

* These tumors have a phenotypic expression pattern similar to the loop of Henle region of normal nephrons ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:1571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30285995))
* Hippo pathway dysregulation with increased nuclear YAP1 protein expression ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:767](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29462091))
* *CDKN2A* or *CDKN2B* deletion may be present in high grade tumors ([Mod Pathol 2021;34:445](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32879414))

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Most cases are asymptomatic
* Incidental finding on imaging

Diagnosis

* Essential morphologic features include anastomosing tubules lined by low grade cells
* Tubules should merge with areas of bland spindle cells
* Areas of myxoid stroma or extracellular mucin should be present
* References: [Eur Urol 2022;82:458](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35853783), [Mod Pathol 2021;34:1392](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33664427), [Diagn Pathol 2015;10:168](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26377921), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2020;144:115](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30865490)

Radiology description

* Majority are homogeneous
* Small subset may show cystic changes, which is more commonly seen in papillary renal cell carcinoma (RCC)
* Typically isodense or hypodense on unweighted CT
* Hyperintense on T2 due to extracellular mucin
* Appears unencapsulated
* References: [Br J Radiol 2021;94:20210548](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34478331),
  [Abdom Radiol 2021;46:5250](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34338814)

Prognostic factors

* Indolent behavior ([Clin Genitourin Cancer 2019;17:268](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31151928))
* Rarely metastatic (e.g., with epithelial anaplasia or sarcomatoid differentiation)

Case reports

* 39 year old man with right lower back pain and 8 cm kidney mass ([Urol Case Rep 2021:40:101889](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34745892))
* 40 year old woman with incidental kidney mass ([J Kidney Cancer VHL 2022;9:10](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36381239))
* 45 year old man with incidental kidney mass ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2018;97:e12933](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30412103))
* 69 year old man with kidney mass and osseous lesions ([IJU Case Rep 2021;4:333](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34497997))

Treatment

* Surgical excision is the mainstay of treatment
* Selected cases received adjuvant therapy with tyrosine kinase inhibitors and PD-1 inhibitors ([Clin Genitourin Cancer 2019;17:268](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31151928))

Gross description

* Well circumscribed, solid, homogenous ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2020;144:115](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30865490))
* Capsule may be present ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2020;144:115](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30865490))
* Tan, gray-pink or pale yellow cut surface ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2020;144:115](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30865490))
* Typically intraparenchymal or partially exophytic mass ([BMC Cancer 2023;23:815](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37649003))
* Most masses are small ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2020;144:115](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30865490))
* Areas of hemorrhage and necrosis are rare ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2020;144:115](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30865490))

Frozen section description

* Elongated tubules with low grade epithelial lining merging with areas of low grade spindled cells in a myxoid background
* Extracellular mucin may be present

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Bland tubules merging with bland spindle cells in a myxoid stroma
* Absence of distinct, well formed papillae
* Tightly packed tubules and spindle cell areas with smooth lumina
* Compact arrangements of whorled tubules
* Areas of myxoid stroma
* Presence of a capsule or foamy macrophages may be present
* High nucleolar grade or extensive necrosis have been described as occurring in rare cases ([Histopathology 2017;71:719](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28656700))
* References: [Eur Urol 2022;82:458](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35853783), [Mod Pathol 2021;34:1392](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33664427), [Diagn Pathol 2015;10:168](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26377921), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2020;144:115](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30865490)

Electron microscopy description

* Tightly packed, often elongated tubules composed of slender attenuated cells, similar to those found in the normal loop of Henle ([Int Braz J Urol 2002;28:477](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15748380))
* Discontinuation of the tubular basement membranes ([Int Braz J Urol 2002;28:477](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15748380))
* Low columnar, cuboidal and spindle cells with a low nuclear to cytoplasmic ratio ([Pathol Res Pract 2014;210:454](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24702883))
* Small nuclei with small nucleoli, dispersed chromatin and smooth nuclear membranes ([Pathol Res Pract 2014;210:454](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24702883))
* Scant cytoplasmic organelles and intermediate filaments ([Pathol Res Pract 2014;210:454](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24702883))
* Short apical microvilli and well developed desmosomes at the site of attachment between the apposing cell membranes ([Pathol Res Pract 2014;210:454](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24702883))
* Foamy macrophages may be present ([Pathol Res Pract 2014;210:454](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24702883))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Monosomy of chromosomes 1, 6, 9, 14, 15 and 22 ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:767](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29462091))
* Multiple loss of chromosomes 1, 4, 6, 8, 9, 13, 14, 15 and 22 ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:767](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29462091))
* FISH shows no *VHL* deletions ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:767](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29462091), [Int Braz J Urol 2002;28:477](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15748380))
* Recurrent chromosomal losses and somatic mutations of genes in the Hippo pathway ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:1571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30285995))
* Moderate to high expression of *VSTM2A* via RNA in situ hybridization ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:1571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30285995))
* Biallelic loss of Hippo pathway tumor suppressor genes (*PTPN14, NF2, SAV1*) ([Cancer Discov 2016;6:1258](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27604489))

Sample pathology report

* Kidney, right, radical nephrectomy:
  + Mucinous tubular and spindle cell carcinoma, 3 cm (see comment)
  + Organ confined
  + Margins negative for tumor
  + No tumor necrosis identified
  + No sarcomatoid or rhabdoid features identified
  + No metatasis to lymph nodes
  + Pathologic stage: pT1a pN0 pMx
  + Comment: The specimen shows a kidney composed of low grade tubules merging with areas of spindled cells in a myxoid stroma. No well formed papillary areas are present. Immunohistochemical stains show positivity for PAX8, CK7, racemase and vimentin.

Differential diagnosis

* [Papillary renal cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantrccpap.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:1353](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18670354)):
  + Papillary architecture
  + May have necrosis
  + Papillary cores show psammoma bodies or foamy macrophages
  + Lack of spindled stroma and mucin
  + There is no difference in the nuclear expression of [YAP / TAZ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsyap1.html) between mucinous tubular and spindle cell carcinoma and papillary renal cell carcinoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:767](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29462091))
  + Frequently positive for [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html)
* [Renal cell carcinoma with sarcomatoid differentiation](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantrccsarcoma.html) ([Mod Pathol 2021;34:1392](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33664427)):
  + High grade cytologic atypia in spindle cells
  + Abrupt transition from epithelial to spindle cell component
  + Presence of distinct alternative histologic subtype (clear cell, papillary, etc.)
* [*ALK* rearrangement associated renal cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumorALKtranslocationrcc.html) ([Diagn Pathol 2022;17:52](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35718773)):
  + Negative IHC for [racemase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsamacr.html)
  + Positive IHC for [ALK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html)
  + *TPM3* (exon 8)*::ALK* (exon 20) fusion gene detection by RNA sequencing

A patient undergoes a partial nephrectomy for a 4 cm renal mass. The above tumor is seen on H&E examination. This tumor should show which immunophenotype?

1. PAX8+, BAP1 loss, calretinin+, WT1+
2. PAX8+, CA9+, CD10+, CK7-
3. PAX8+, CK7+, vimentin+, racemase+
4. PAX8+, TTF1+, CK7+

Board review style answer #1

**C**. PAX8+, CK7+, vimentin+, racemase+. The images show a mucinous tubular and spindle cell carcinoma, which should show positive staining for PAX8, CK7, racemase and vimentin. Answer D is incorrect because this immunophenotype is more consistent with metastatic thyroid cancer. Answer B is incorrect because this immunophenotype is more consistent with a clear cell renal cell carcinoma. Answer A is incorrect because this immunophenotype is more consistent with mesothelioma.

Which of the following features would be most helpful in distinguishing a mucinous tubular and spindle cell carcinoma from a renal cell carcinoma with sarcomatoid features?

1. PAX8+, CK7+, vimentin+, racemase+
2. Presence of foamy macrophages
3. Retained FH and SDHB expression on IHC
4. *VHL* mutation on molecular studies

Board review style answer #2

**D**. *VHL* mutation on molecular studies. *VHL* mutations are not found in mucinous tubular and spindle cell carcinomas. This would be more consistent with a clear cell renal cell carcinoma. Answer A is incorrect because this immunophenotype can be found in papillary renal cell carcinoma. Answer C is incorrect because this immunophenotype excludes an SDHB deficient or an FH deficient RCC but many other renal cell neoplasms can have this finding. Answer B is incorrect because mesothelial tumors will express both CK5/6 and D2-40.
