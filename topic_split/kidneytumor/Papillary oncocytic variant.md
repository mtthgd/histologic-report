Definition / general

* Most experts believe it is a variant of [type 1 papillary renal cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantrccpap.html#type1)
* **NOT** considered a distinct entity by ISUP ([Am J Surg Pathol 2013;37:1469](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24025519)) or
  WHO classification

Terminology

* Synonymous term: oncocytic papillary renal cell carcinoma (OPRCC) ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2006;10:133](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16730306), [Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2013;6:1392](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23826421))
* May be the same entity as oncocytic papillary renal cell carcinoma with inverted nuclear pattern ([Pathol Int 2009;59:137](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19261090))

Sites

* Kidney

Clinical features

* Usually male, median age 71 years ([Am J Surg Pathol 2005;29:1576](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16327429))
* Low stage, indolent clinical behavior

Case reports

* 81 year old woman with oncocytic papillary renal cell carcinoma ([Int J Urol 2009;16:765](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19769658))

Treatment

* Resection

Gross description

* Median 3 cm, well circumscribed, no extrarenal extension

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Thin, nonfibrotic papillae lined by single layer of oncocytic cells
* Low grade, round, regular, nonoverlapping nuclei with central nucleolus ([Am J Surg Pathol 2005;29:1576](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16327429))
* Abundant granular eosinophilic cytoplasm
* Variable foamy macrophages within papillae
* Hemosiderin accumulation, focal necrosis
* May have luminal orientation of tumor nuclei ([Pathol Int 2009;59:137](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19261090)) or solid oncocytoma-like areas ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2006;10:133](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16730306))

Racemase, CD10, vimentin, MET

Positive stains

* [AMACR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsamacr.html),
  [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html),
  **mitochondrial antigen**,
  [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) (79%),
* Variable [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html) (17% - 100%), variable [RCC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsrcc.html) ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2013;6:1392](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23826421), [Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:887](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24919183))

Negative stains

* [c-kit / CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html), low [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) proliferative rate

Electron microscopy description

* Abundant mitochondria with lamellar cristae ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2006;10:133)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16730306)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Majority have trisomy 7 and 17, loss of Y ([Hum Pathol 2008;39:96](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17949780), [Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2013;6:1392](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23826421), [Ann Diagn Pathol 2006;10:133,](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16730306), [Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:887](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24919183))
* Gene expression studies show closer relationship to type 1 than type 2 papillary RCC ([Cancer Res 2005;65:5628](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15994935))

Differential diagnosis

* [Oncocytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumoroncocytoma.html): no papillary architecture, no necrosis; negative for vimentin, CD10, AMACR, CK7; positive for c-kit / CD117
* [Papillary adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumorrenalcortadenoma.html) with oncocytic cells: 15 mm (1.5 cm) or less (size criteria)
* [Papillary type 1 tumors](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantrccpap.html#type1): single layer of cells lining papillae but with pale basophilic cytoplasm, strong diffuse CK7 positivity
* [Papillary type 2 tumors](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantrccpap.html#type1): higher nuclear grade, crowded and pseudostratified nuclei
