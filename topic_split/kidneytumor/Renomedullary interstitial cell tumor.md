Definition / general

* Benign kidney tumor
* Asymptomatic, commonly found incidentally
* Arise from renomedullary interstitial cells
* Also known as medullary fibroma

Essential features

* Frequently found at autopsy, incidentally on imaging or resection for other reasons
* Arising from medullary interstitial cells
* Small, well circumscribed, single or multiple gray-white nodules in the renal medulla
* Spindle or stellate cells in basophilic stroma with entrapped tubules

Terminology

* Prior terms include medullary fibroma and renal hamartoma

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [D30.00](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D30-/D30.00) - Benign neoplasm of kidney

Epidemiology

* Incidence between 16% and 46% according to autopsy studies ([Hum Pathol 1972;3:559](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4264334))
* Mean age: 58 years (18 - 92 years), rarely seen in children ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27635944))
* M = F with similar incidence ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27635944))

Sites

* Predominance: right = left kidney ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27635944))
* Bilateral tumors: in 41%, bilateral tumors occurred more in the elderly ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27635944))
* Mostly occur in the renal medulla, occasionally found in renal cortex ([Urology 2014;83:1104](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24642074))
* 60% of patients have multiple interstitial cell tumors, ranging from 1 to 23, mean of 3 ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27635944))

Pathophysiology

* Appears to originate as a proliferation of renomedullary interstitial cells between medullary tubules ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27635944))
* Renomedullary interstitial cells are involved in release of renin and regulation of sodium excretion ([Eur Urol 2016;70:120](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26996660))
* As their size increases, cellularity decreases, viscous eosinophilic material is deposited and tubules disappear ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27635944))
* Viscous eosinophilic material is not associated with amyloid but with collagen type III ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27635944))
* No association with hypertension ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27635944))

Etiology

* No statistically significant associated causes ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27635944))

Clinical features

* Asymptomatic, found incidentally during surgery (0.2%), autopsy (16 - 46%) or radiologic investigation ([Urology 2014;83:1104](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24642074))
* May be symptomatic in case reports (e.g. urosepsis or hematuria) ([Urology 2014;83:1104](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24642074))

Diagnosis

* Diagnosis is made on histology post resection or on core biopsy ([Adv Anat Pathol 2000;7:47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10640201))
* Imaging modalities are not conclusive and may mimic malignant conditions ([Radiographics 2010;30:1525](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21071373))

Radiology description

* CT: small, nonenhancing noncalcified hypoattenuating solid mass within the renal medulla ([Radiographics 2010;30:1525](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21071373))
* MR imaging: medullary fibromas are hypointense on T1 and T2 weighted images ([Radiographics 2010;30:1525](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21071373))

Prognostic factors

* Favorable prognostic factors and mostly asymptomatic ([Urology 2014;83:1104](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24642074))
* After resection, no recurrence ([Urology 2014;83:1104](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24642074))

Case reports

* 20 year old woman with incidental detection at autopsy ([Int J Health Sci (Qassim) 2014;8:440](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25780364))
* 29 year old man found to have a calcium oxalate deposition in a renomedullary interstitial cell tumor at autopsy ([Pathol Oncol Res 2003;9:47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12704447))
* 32 year old man presented with hematuria, left flank pain and incidental tumor ([J Clin Imaging Sci 2013;3:43](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24228211))
* 50 year old man was found to have renomedullary interstitial cell tumor during a kidney transplant ([Indian J Urol 2012;28:202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22919140))

Treatment

* Surgical resection is recommended if imaging is inconclusive ([Urology 2014;83:1104](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24642074))

Gross description

* Well circumscribed, homogenenous white / gray-white nodules usually in the medulla
* Small (1 - 13 mm), median tumor size 4 mm and up to 5 cm has been reported in case reports ([Hum Pathol 2018;82:46](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30031100))
* Arises from medulla but occasionally from the cortex ([Urology 2014;83:1104](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24642074))
* Surrounding kidney tissue showed that 47% had normal renal parenchyma, 28% showed slight glomerulosclerosis, 19% moderate and 7% severe glomerulosclerosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27635944))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Small, spindle or stellate cells in a background of loose basophilic stroma or fibrotic stroma with entrapped tubules ([Urology 2014;83:1104](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24642074), [Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27635944))
* Some cases may contain irregular deposits of amyloid ([Eur Urol 2016;70:120](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26996660))
* Entrapped tubules may be seen diffusely at the periphery and may be cystically dilated ([Urology 2014;83:1104](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24642074), [Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27635944))
* Nuclear features: open chromatin and minimal atypia or hyperchromasia ([Urology 2014;83:1104](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24642074), [Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27635944))
* Absent / rare mitotic activity ([Urology 2014;83:1104](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24642074), [Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27635944))
* Surrounding kidney tissue showed that 47% had normal renal parenchyma, 28% showed slight glomerulosclerosis, 19% moderate and 7% severe glomerulosclerosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27635944))

Electron microscopy description

* Resembles renomedullary interstitial cell of kidney with large cytoplasmic lipid droplets ([Hum Pathol 1972;3:559](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4264334))

Sample pathology report

* Kidney, right, laparascopic complete nephrectomy:
  + Renal cell carcinoma, clear cell type (see synoptic report)
  + Renomedullary interstitial cell tumor
* Kidney, right, mass, needle core biopsy:
  + Renomedullary interstitial cell tumor

Differential diagnosis

* [Metanephric stromal tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormetastromal.html):
  + Concentric rings or collarettes of stromal cells around entrapped renal tubules and blood vessels
  + Unencapsulated infiltrative tumor of spindle and epithelioid cells
  + Rare in adults
* **Scar**:
  + History of poor renal function, renal stones or prior procedures
  + Not well circumscribed
  + Less basophilic
  + More likely to be associated with inflammation

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/kidneytumorrenalmedfibroma_Eyzaguirre01.png)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/kidneytumorrenalmedfibroma_Eyzaguirre01.png)

An 85 year old man underwent nephroureterectomy for low grade papillary urothelial carcinoma of the renal pelvis. A 3 mm white, solid, medullary based incidental lesion is noted on gross examination. Which of the following is true for this tumor?

1. They are uncommon tumors seen more frequently in children
2. They are typically medullary based tumors composed of stellated interstitial cells immersed in a basophilic matrix with entrapped renal tubules
3. Although the majority are benign, rarely metastasis have been described
4. Frequently, they present with hematuria and flank pain

Board review style answer #1

**B.** This is a renomedullary interstitial cell tumor. They are typically medullary based tumors composed of stellated interstitial cells immersed in a basophilic matrix with entrapped renal tubules.

Board review style question #2

Which is true about renomedullary interstitial cell tumors?

1. Often, these tumors are unifocal, found within the cortex and more than 10 mm in size
2. They are asymptomatic and often found incidentally during nephrectomy or at autopsies
3. They are CD34 and ER positive and desmin and smooth muscle negative
4. Congo red stain is characteristically positive in all tumors
5. Specialized myofibroblast is the probable cell of origin

Board review style answer #2

**B.** They are asymptomatic and often found incidentally during nephrectomy or at autopsies
