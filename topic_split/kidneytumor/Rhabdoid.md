Definition / general

* Adult renal cell carcinoma (RCC) with a subpopulation of neoplastic cells that have rhabdoid morphological features (resemble striated muscle / rhabdomyoblastic cells) with discohesive cytology containing irregular eccentric nuclei, large nucleoli and abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm
* They are, however, not of a myogenic origin and differ in ultrastructural features and immunophenotype ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:1329](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11023094))

Essential features

* Not recognized as a distinct histologic type of RCC in the World Health Organization (WHO) 2022 classification but rather a high grade dedifferentiation or transformation that may arise from some RCC subtypes, predominantly clear cell RCC (CCRCC) ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2007;131:102](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17227108), [Ann Diagn Pathol 2015;19:57](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25708024))
* Considered as WHO / ISUP grade 4 ([Am J Surg Pathol 2013;37:1490](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24025520))
* Associated with aggressive tumor behavior, poor prognosis and low response towards VEGF tyrosine kinase inhibitors (TKIs) however more recent studies show clinical benefits from immune checkpoint inhibitor (ICI) therapy ([Clin Genitourin Cancer 2021;19:103](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33358151), [Nat Commun 2021;12:808](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33547292), [Am J Surg Pathol 2024;48:e65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38736105))

Terminology

* Recommended: rhabdoid features or rhabdoid differentiation
* Other terminologies: focal rhabdoid / plasmacytoid appearance, rhabdoid variant, adult rhabdoid renal cell carcinoma, rhabdoid morphology, rhabdoid phenotype, rhabdoid component, rhabdoid changes, rhabdoid pattern, rhabdoid quality

Epidemiology

* Median age: 60 - 64 years old ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:1260](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25127094), [Int Urol Nephrol 2016;48:1253](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27215555), [Eur Urol 2015;68:5](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25638439))
* M:F = 1 - 2:1 ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:1260](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25127094), [Int Urol Nephrol 2016;48:1253](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27215555), [Eur Urol 2015;68:5](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25638439))
* Overall incidence of rhabdoid differentiation in RCC is 4 - 5%, with CCRCC being the subtype where it is most commonly observed ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:1329](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11023094), [Eur Urol 2015;68:5](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25638439))

Clinical features

* Symptomatic in ~90% of cases and usually present with renal mass and abdominal mass, hematuria and flank or abdominal pain ([Am J Surg Pathol 2024;48:e65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38736105), [Eur Urol 2015;68:5](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25638439))
* Frequent lymph node metastasis (up to ~20%) or distant metastasis (up to ~40%) at initial diagnosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2024;48:e65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38736105))

Diagnosis

* Based on histomorphology of a rhabdoid differentiation in a RCC tumor

Prognostic factors

* Very aggressive disease; associated with higher stage (pT3 and pT4) and metastasis (up to 70%) ([Histopathology 2002;41:538](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12460207), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2007;131:102](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17227108), [Am J Surg Pathol 2024;48:e65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38736105))
* Poor prognosis with overall survival rate of 8 - 46 months ([Am J Surg Pathol 2024;48:e65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38736105))
* Presence of any rhabdoid cells is diagnostic for RCC with rhabdoid features, indicating a poor prognosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2024;48:e65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38736105))
* Designated as WHO / ISUP grade 4 disease because rhabdoid dedifferentiation is associated with increased hazard ratio (HR 2.45) when compared to grade 3 tumors ([Eur Urol 2015;68:5](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25638439))

Case reports

* 22 year old woman presented with CCRCC exhibiting rhabdoid features ([Urol Case Rep 2020;32:101244](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32435595))
* 40 year old woman presented with CCRCC with rhabdoid features that metastasized to the colon ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2021;80:105627](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33607369))
* 54 year old man has CCRCC with rhabdoid differentiation, showing co-occurring mutations in *BAP1* and *ATM* ([Am J Clin Exp Urol 2023;11:429](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37941648))
* 81 year old man with rhabdoid RCC presented with renal vein thrombosis ([Ann Med Surg 2024;86:2194](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38576908))

Treatment

* Radical nephrectomy due to large tumor size and invasion into adjacent tissues, typically followed by adjuvant therapy due to high risk of recurrence ([Am J Surg Pathol 2024;48:e65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38736105))
* Exhibits an immune inflamed phenotype, higher PDL1 expression and is responsive to ICI based regimens therapies ([Oncologist 2020;25:252](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32162795), [Nat Commun 2021;12:808](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33547292))
  + Nivolumab as monotherapy or in combination with ipilimumab or VEGFR TKI demonstrated clinical activity in CCRCC with rhabdoid dedifferentiation ([Oncologist 2020;25:252](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32162795))
  + Role of ICI is not well established for rhabdoid RCC with non-clear cell component ([Clin Genitourin Cancer 2021;19:103](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33358151))
* Generally, rhabdoid differentiation is less studied than its sarcomatoid counterpart; studies have often lumped the sarcomatoid and rhabdoid RCC as one category ([Cancer Treat Res Commun 2022;33:100640](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36174377))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Solid sheet-like growth pattern with a discohesive morphology ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:1329](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11023094), [Am J Surg Pathol 2024;48:e65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38736105))
* Large epithelioid cells with large eccentric, atypical vesicular nuclei with prominent nucleoli and abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm and possible paranuclear intracytoplasmic inclusions ([Pol J Pathol 2015;66:3](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26017874))
* Often coexist with sarcomatoid differentiation and high grade bizarre cells ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:1260](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25127094), [Pol J Pathol 2015;66:3](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26017874))
* Necrosis is common; focal to extensive inflammatory cell infiltrates may be seen ([Nat Commun 2021;12:808](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33547292))
* Considered as WHO / ISUP grade 4 ([Am J Surg Pathol 2013;37:1490](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24025520))
* It is recommended to include the percentage of rhabdoid component in the pathology report
* Specific histologic RCC types may be seen adjacent to the rhabdoid areas ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2007;131:102](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17227108), [Ann Diagn Pathol 2015;19:57](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25708024), [Histopathology 2002;41:538](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12460207))
  + Most common histologic subtype of RCC with rhabdoid features is CCRCC but other RCC types including papillary RCC, chromophobe RCC, SMARCB1 deficient renal medullary carcinoma have been described ([Histopathology 2002;41:538](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12460207), [Wien Klin Wochenschr 2012;124:419](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22696227), [Am J Surg Pathol 2013;37:368](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23348212))
  + Tumors with pure rhabdoid morphology after extensive sampling are designated as RCC, NOS

Cytology description

* Highly cellular consisting mainly of dissociated tumor cells with some arranged in small clusters ([Cytopathology 2004;15:237](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15324454))
* Cells are enlarged with eccentrically located nuclei and abundant cytoplasm with deeply stained inclusions ([Cytopathology 2004;15:237](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15324454))
* Cells resembling those found in RCC subtypes can also be seen ([Cytopathology 2004;15:237](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15324454))

Electron microscopy description

* Aggregates of paranuclear intermediate filaments arranged in a concentric whorl-like pattern, corresponding to the intracytoplasmic inclusion bodies observed in light microscopy
* Cells lack features of rhabdomyoblastic differentiation ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:1329](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11023094))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Gains in chromosome 7 and losses in chromosomes 4, 9, 14 and 17p are frequently seen in areas with rhabdoid differentiation ([Hum Pathol 2015;46:9](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25439741))
* Loss of chromosome 11p is specific to rhabdoid differentiation, occurring in ~30% of rhabdoid components but not in clear cell areas ([Hum Pathol 2015;46:9](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25439741))
* Associated with inactivating mutations in *BAP1* and *PBRM1* and genes in SWI / SNF complex subunit such as *SMARCB1* ([Nat Genet 2012;44:751](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22683710), [Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:253](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27984237))
* Molecular features that may contribute to its aggressive behavior include mutations in the *BAP1* gene, deletions of the *CDKN2A* gene and increased expression of *MYC* transcriptional programs ([Nat Commun 2021;12:808](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33547292))

Differential diagnosis

* [Urothelial carcinoma of the renal pelvis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderurothelialinvasivegen.html) ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2015;8:9638](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26464730), [Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:e20](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25029121)):
  + Can exhibit rhabdoid differentiation if high grade
  + Associated with urothelial carcinoma in situ or a traditional papillary urothelial carcinoma
  + Negative for a low grade conventional RCC component
  + [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html)+, [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgata3.html)+, [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html)- IHC
    - It can be difficult to differentiate an aggressive RCC as *SMARCB1* deficient and collecting duct RCC and fumarate hydratase (FH) deficient RCC from urothelial carcinoma due to overlap in [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html) and [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgata3.html) staining; hence, extensive sampling is often needed to identify a lower grade morphology of a urothelial carcinoma or a conventional RCC
* **[Metastatic carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantmetastases.html) with rhabdoid features**:
  + [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html)-
  + Negative for a low grade conventional RCC component
  + Clinical and imaging correlation to assess for the presence of a primary tumor
* **Primary rhabdomyosarcoma of kidney** ([J Kidney Cancer VHL 2022;9:55](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35529801), [Urol Case Rep 2022;45:102183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36062206)):
  + Very rare in adults
  + Positive muscle specific markers like [myogenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html), [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html)
  + Negative epithelial markers including [panCK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)
  + It is important to rule out the presence of an epithelial component
  + Dedifferentiated part may lose [keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) staining and resemble a sarcoma
  + Key factor is morphology, specifically the absence of any low grade carcinoma component
* [Rhabdoid tumor of the kidney](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumorrhabdoid.html) ([Pediatr Dev Pathol 2018;21:6](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29280680)):
  + While it resembles RCC with rhabdoid features in terms of histomorphology and IHC profile, it predominantly occurs in infants and young children (~80% less than 2 years old)
  + Demonstrates *SMARCB1* or *SMARCA4* biallelic aberrations and [SMARCB1 (INI1)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html) IHC loss
  + Extrarenal rhabdoid tumors including [atypical teratoid / rhabdoid tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumoratypicalteratoidrhabdoid.html) of the brain may be present
  + No low grade RCC component in the tumor
* [Epithelioid angiomyolipoma of the kidney](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumorepitheliodaml.html) ([Mod Pathol 2013;26:1355](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23599151)):
  + Positive melanocytic markers ([HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html), [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html)), [cathepsin K](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscathepsink.html), [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), negative [keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) / epithelial markers and [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html)
* Some **soft tissue tumors** may also present with rhabdoid component:
  + [Dedifferentiated liposarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuededifflipo.html):
    - Retroperitoneal mass
    - May show [panCK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)+
    - *MDM2* and *CDK4* amplification ([Hum Pathol 2018;77:20](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29307627))
  + Primary [synovial sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesynovialsarc.html) of the kidney:
    - May mimic rhabdoid RCC especially if monophasic or poorly differentiated
    - [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html)+,
      [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html)+ and
      [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html)+
    - *SYT::SSX1* or *SYT::SSX2* fusion ([Int J Surg Pathol 2007;15:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17913954))
  + [Epithelioid angiosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueangiosarcoma.html) of the kidney:
    - Primitive vascular lumens containing RBCs may be present
    - [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html)+, [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)+, [ERG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainserg.html)+ ([Oncol Lett 2014;8:1155](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25120677), [Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:432](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21317715))
* Others:
  + [CCRCC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantrccclear.html) with predominant eosinophilic morphology:
    - Absence of high grade, bizarre, eccentric nuclei and intracytoplasmic inclusions
  + **Fixation artifact**:
    - Can make nuclei appear atypical in tissue samples

Board review style question #1

[![Rhabdoid](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/kidney/kidneytumormalignantclearcellrhabdoidAndeen03.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/kidney/kidneytumormalignantclearcellrhabdoidAndeen03.jpg)

[![Rhabdoid](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/kidney/kidneytumormalignantclearcellrhabdoidAndeen04.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/kidney/kidneytumormalignantclearcellrhabdoidAndeen04.jpg)

A 70 year old man presents with a right renal mass measuring 15 cm that extends beyond Gerota fascia. The tumor exhibits white, firm cut surfaces with areas of hemorrhage and necrosis. Please refer to the images above. Which of the following immunohistochemistry (IHC) panels supports the diagnosis of renal cell carcinoma with rhabdoid differentiation?

1. CK+, EMA+, CK20+, GATA3+, INI+, desmin-, myogenin-
2. CK-, EMA-, HMB45+, cathepsin K+, variable SMA, desmin-, myogenin-
3. CK+, EMA+, variable CAIX, variable PAX8, desmin-, myogenin-
4. CK-, EMA-, variable vimentin, desmin+, myogenin+

Board review style answer #1

**C**. CK+, EMA+, variable CAIX, variable PAX8, desmin-, myogenin-. RCCs with rhabdoid differentiation are usually immunoreactive to epithelial markers (CK, EMA), show variable for PAX8, may be positive for CAIX if associated with clear cel RCC, and negative for muscle markers (desmin, myogenin).
Answer D is incorrect because this renal cell carcinoma with rhabdoid features is positive for epithelial markers such as CK and EMA and negative for muscle specific markers like desmin and myogenin. This IHC profile describes rhabdomyosarcoma.
Answer A is incorrect because positive CK20 and GATA3 are more consistent with urothelial carcinoma.
Answer B is incorrect because immunoreactivity to HMB45 and cathepsin K is characteristic of angiomyolipoma.

[Comment Here](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/)

Reference: [Renal cell carcinoma with rhabdoid features](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantclearcellrhabdoid.html)

Board review style question #2

Which of the following statements is true regarding renal cell carcinoma with rhabdoid features?

1. This tumor is associated with favorable clinical outcomes
2. This tumor often demonstrates loss of *BAP1* and *PBRM1*
3. This tumor should be classified as WHO / ISUP grade 3
4. This tumor shows a high response rate to mTOR and VEGF inhibitors

Board review style answer #2

**B**. This tumor often demonstrates loss of *BAP1* and *PBRM1*. RCC with rhabdoid features is often associated with inactivating mutations in *BAP1*  and *PBRM1*.
Answer C is incorrect because the presence of rhabdoid features in clear cell renal cell carcinoma (CCRCC) and papillary renal cell carcinoma (PRCC) should be designated as WHO / ISUP grade 4.
Answer A is incorrect because this tumor is highly aggressive and is associated with dismal clinical outcomes.
Answer D is incorrect because mTOR and VEGF inhibitors are both less effective for this tumor; however, it is responsive to immune checkpoint inhibitor (ICI) based therapy.
