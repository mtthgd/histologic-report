Definition / general

* Succinate dehydrogenase (SDH) deficient renal cell carcinoma is defined by the WHO as a malignant epithelial tumor composed of vacuolated eosinophilic to clear cells with loss of immunohistochemical expression of SDHB, a marker of dysfunction of mitochondrial complex II (WHO 2016)

Essential features

* Characteristic flocculent cytoplasmic vacuoles with a pale eosinophilic, wispy or bubbly appearance and low grade nuclei (at least focally)
* May have high grade areas
* Oncogenesis is driven by metabolic derangements due to double hit inactivation of SDH genes, leading to dysfunction of mitochondrial complex II
* Most patients have a germline mutation in an SDH gene, usually SDHB

Terminology

* Terminology which is sometimes used but not recommended is SDHB negative renal carcinoma or SDH deficient renal oncocytoma

Epidemiology

* Rare
* Represents 0.05% to 0.2% of all renal cell carcinomas (WHO 2016)
* Median age 35 years (range 14 to 76); M:F ratio = 1:8:1
* Bilateral in 26% ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:1588](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25025441))

Sites

* Kidney

Pathophysiology

* Most cases occur in setting of germline mutation of an SDH gene; neoplasia occurs with double hit inactivation, leading to dysfunction of mitochondrial complex II, increased reactive oxygen species, DNA damage and HIF1α stabilization ([Int Journal of Cell Biology 2012:2012](http://www.hindawi.com/journals/ijcb/2012/161837/))
* SDHB is most commonly affected in SDH deficient RCC, then SDHC
* Rarely SDHA or SDHD (different for other SDH deficient tumors)

Clinical features

* Often confined to the kidney at presentation
* Presents with flank pain or as incidental finding
* Personal or family history of paragangliomas or SDH deficient gastrointestinal stromal tumor may be present (WHO 2016) ([GeneReviews 2008;NBK1548](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20301715))

Prognostic factors

* Overall metastasis rate of ~ 33% (based on 27 cases) but metastasis uncommon if only low grade features ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:1588](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25025441))
* Higher metastasis rate if high grade nuclei (70% metastasize) or coagulative necrosis present (4/4 cases metastasized, [Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:1588](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25025441))

Case reports

* Loss of SDHB by immunohistochemistry in 3 patients with known SDHB mutations ([N Engl J Med 2011;364:885](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21366490))
* With SDHA mutation ([Hum Pathol 2015;46:1951](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26476567))

Treatment

* Resection; further treatment is dependent on grade / stage, may include targeted therapy ([J Clin Oncol 2014;32;e10](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24395865))
* All patients with a diagnosis of SDH deficient RCC should be offered genetic testing (WHO 2016)

Gross description

* Well circumscribed, solid with red / brown cut surface, variable multicystic change, generally no necrosis
* Usually confined to kidney with no involvement of renal sinus, vein or fat

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Well circumscribed or pushing border, commonly entraps tubules
* Solid, nested or tubular growth pattern with scattered cysts containing eosinophilic material
* Neoplastic cells have smooth nuclear contours and fine chromatin with no nucleoli
* Characteristic finding is flocculent cytoplasmic vacuoles with a pale eosinophilic, wispy or bubbly appearance with low grade nuclei
  + This morphology is commonly diffuse and should be at least focal (WHO 2016)
* May have areas of higher grade nuclei, necrosis, sarcomatoid change
* Variant morphologies have been reported but are rare in absence of more characteristic low grade regions (WHO 2016)

SDH deficient RCC

Positive stains

* [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html), [focal pancytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html) and [CAM 5.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscam52.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) ([Mod Pathol 2015;28:80](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25034258))

Negative stains

* Loss of SDHB immunohistochemical staining (be cautious on overinterpretation of negativity in tumors with very clear cytoplasm)
  + Loss of SDHB immunohistochemical staining indicates disruption of the mitochondrial complex 2 for any reason, not just SDHB gene mutation ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:1588](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25025441))
* [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [CAIX](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscarbonicanhydraseix.html), [RCC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsrcc.html), [c-kit](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html) (mast cells only), [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html) ([Mod Pathol 2015;28:80](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25034258)), neuroendocrine markers
* Minimal [AMACR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsamacr.html) staining

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* No mutations in VHL, PIK3CA, AKT, mTOR, MET or TP53 genes (WHO 2016)
* Genes for succinate dehydrogenase subunits (SDHA, SDHB, SDHC, SDHD) encode proteins which are part of mitochondrial complex II, which links the Krebs cycle and electron transport chain ([N Engl J Med 2011;364:885](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21366490))

Differential diagnosis

* [Eosinophilic variant of chromophobe RCC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantrccchromoeosinvar.html):
  + Prominent cell borders and crinkled / raisinoid nuclei with binucleation
  + Also CK7+ with no loss of SDHB expression
* [Eosinophilic variant of clear cell RCC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantclearcelleosinophilic.html): CAIX+, vimentin+
* [Oncocytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumoroncocytoma.html): often has myxohyaline stroma; CKIT+ with no loss of SDHB expression ([Mod Pathol 2015;28:80](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25034258))
* [Papillary renal cell carcinoma (type 2)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantrccpaptyp2.html):
  + Cytoplasm is eosinophilic but not as pale wispy/bubbly
  + Nuclei often pseudostratified
* Loss of SDHB immunohistochemical expression is rare in other carcinomas ([Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2014;22:31](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23531856))
