Definition / general

* Fibroblastic mesenchymal tumor with haphazard spindle cells, staghorn vasculature and *NAB2::STAT6* gene fusion

Essential features

* Fibroblastic tumor characterized by patternless architecture and staghorn vasculature
* STAT6 expression by immunohistochemistry; *NAB2::STAT6* gene fusion
* About 16% of renal solitary fibrous tumors (SFTs) show histological features that may indicate aggressive behavior: hypercellularity, cytological atypia, increased mitosis and necrosis

Terminology

* Hemangiopericytoma (not recommended)

ICD coding

* ICD-O
  + [8815/0](https://amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/9283245024/pathologyoutl-20) - solitary fibrous tumor, benign
  + [8815/1](https://amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/9283245024/pathologyoutl-20) - solitary fibrous tumor, NOS
  + [8815/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf#page=80) - solitary fibrous tumor, malignant
* ICD-10: [D49.519](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D49-D49/D49-/D49.519) - neoplasm of unspecified behavior of unspecified kidney
* ICD-11
  + [2F7C & XH7E62](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#1369250201%20%26%201652009742) - neoplasms of uncertain behavior of connective or other soft tissue & solitary fibrous tumor, NOS
  + [2B5Y & XH1HP3](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#1965082709%2Fother%20%26%20912217173) - other specified malignant mesenchymal neoplasms & solitary fibrous tumor, malignant

Epidemiology

* M:F = 1:1.8 ([Int J Surg Pathol 2015;23:34](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23816824))
* Mean age: 49 (range: 3 - 85) ([Int J Surg Pathol 2015;23:34](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23816824))

Sites

* Any anatomic site; majority are pleural or abdominopelvic ([Mod Pathol 2012;25:1298](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22575866), [Ann Surg Oncol 2017;24:3865](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29039030))
* Rare in kidney; ~105 cases reported ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2019;62:112](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31494455))
* Metachronous SFTs of kidney and synchronous pleurorenal SFTs can happen ([Urol Case Rep 2015;4:45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26793578), [Minerva Chir 2009;64:669](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20029363))

Pathophysiology

* Intrachromosomal inversion of chromosome 12q results in *NAB2::STAT6* fusion ([Nat Genet 2013;45:131](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23313954))
* *NAB2* is a transcriptional repressor of early growth response 1 (EGR1), while *NAB2::STAT6* is a transcriptional activator of *ERG1* ([Nat Genet 2013;45:180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23313952))
* Overactivation of *EGR1* leads to increased expression of its target genes, such as *IGF2, FGF2, PDGFD, FGFR1, NTRK1*, etc. ([Nat Genet 2013;45:180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23313952))

Clinical features

* Most asymptomatic, can present with hematuria and flank pain ([Int J Surg Pathol 2015;23:34](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23816824))
* Hypoglycemia (Doege-Potter syndrome): in IGF2 ([Arch Ital Urol Androl 2020;92:392](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33348973))

Diagnosis

* Histological examination
* *NAB2::STAT6* gene fusion by immunohistochemical or molecular studies

Laboratory

* Rare cases showed hypoglycemia and hypokalemia ([Asian J Surg 2023;46:1260](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36050240))

Radiology description

* Ultrasound: heterogeneous, mainly hyperechoic ([Cureus 2016;8:e490](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27014524))
* Computed tomography (CT): heterogeneous enhanced lesion ([Mol Clin Oncol 2020;13:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32832082))
* Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI): highly variable, may show isointense in T1 and hyperintense in T2 ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2019;62:112](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31494455))

Prognostic factors

* Histological features cannot precisely predict aggressive behavior; only 36% of histologically malignant kidney SFT patients presented with recurrent / metastatic disease ([Int J Surg Pathol 2015;23:34](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23816824))
* Multivariable risk stratification model has shown to more accurately predict prognosis
* Variables in risk stratification model of SFTs from all anatomic sites are: age ≥ 55, tumor size ≥ 10 cm, mitotic count ≥ 4/10 high power fields, tumor necrosis ≥ 10% ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:1433](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28731041))

Case reports

* 39 year old woman with left kidney mass ([Cureus 2023;15:e46475](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37927755))
* 52 year old woman with right kidney tumor ([Mol Clin Oncol 2020;13:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32832082))
* 55 year old man with left kidney mass ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2019;62:112](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31494455))
* 66 year old woman with new onset hematuria ([Cureus 2016;8:e490](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27014524))
* 83 year old man with kidney mass and Doege-Potter syndrome ([Arch Ital Urol Androl 2020;92:392](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33348973))

Treatment

* Complete surgical resection
* Ablation or systemic chemotherapy for unresectable tumors

Frozen section description

* Spindle cell neoplasm with patternless architecture and staghorn vessels

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Haphazard arrangement of spindle to oval shaped cells (patternless architecture)
* Both hypo and hypercellular areas
* Staghorn branching vasculature
* Intertwined collagen bands
* Myxoid areas and pseudocystic areas can be present ([J Clin Pathol 2017;70:508](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27802414))
* Features with increased risk for malignancy: high cellularity, cytological atypia, increased mitosis, necrosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:640](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2665534), [J Clin Pathol 2017;70:508](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27802414))
* Round cell histological subtype ([Genitourinary Pathology Society: Case of the Week [Accessed 25 October 2024]](https://gupathsociety.org/COW-2021-18))
* Fat forming histological subtype ([In Vivo 2018;32:649](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29695573))

Cytology description

* SFTs with low risk of aggressive behavior: low to moderate cellularity of clusters and single cells, spindle, oval or stellate cells with monotonous nuclei, wispy cytoplasm, collagenous intercellular stroma ([Cancer Cytopathol 2018;126:36](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28914981))
* SFTs with high risk of aggressive behavior: hypercellular, crowded clusters, focal nuclear pleomorphism, mitoses, necrosis ([Cancer Cytopathol 2010;118:83](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20209623))

Positive stains

* [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html): 99 - 100% in benign SFTs of soft tissue, 93% in retroperitoneal SFTs ([Iran J Pathol 2016;11:195](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27799967), [Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2019;27:195](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28749793))
* [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html): 96.1% in benign SFTs of kidney, 76.5% in malignant SFTs ([Int J Surg Pathol 2015;23:34](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23816824))
* [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html): 68% in benign SFTs of kidney, 53.8% in malignant SFTs ([Int J Surg Pathol 2015;23:34](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23816824))
* [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html): 83.3% in benign SFTs of kidney, 75% in malignant SFTs ([Int J Surg Pathol 2015;23:34](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23816824))
* [Vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html): 98.3% in benign SFTs of kidney, 100% in malignant SFTs ([Int J Surg Pathol 2015;23:34](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23816824))

Negative stains

* [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html): positive in 43% of cases ([Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2019;27:195](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28749793))
* [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html): positive in 1.8% of cases ([Int J Surg Pathol 2015;23:34](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23816824))
* [MDM2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmdm2.html) ([Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2019;27:195](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28749793))
* [Pancytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html) ([Int J Surg Pathol 2015;23:34](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23816824))
* [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html) ([Int J Surg Pathol 2015;23:34](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23816824))

Electron microscopy description

* Cells with fibroblastic features, well developed rough endoplasmic reticulum, surrounded with collagen fibers ([Med Mol Morphol 2009;42:239](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20033371))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Multiple *NAB2::STAT6* gene fusion variants detected by whole exome sequencing ([Nat Genet 2013;45:131](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23313954), [Nat Genet 2013;45:180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23313952))
* Reverse transcriptase polymerase chain reaction (RT-PCR) based tests are more sensitive than *STAT6* FISH due to close proximity of the 2 genes ([J Clin Pathol 2017;70:508](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27802414))
* *STAT6* FISH is more sensitive than STAT6 immunostain ([J Clin Pathol 2017;70:508](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27802414))

Sample pathology report

* Left kidney, nephrectomy:
  + Solitary fibrous tumor, 3.5 cm, margins of resection free of involvement (see comment)
  + Comment: Clinically, this patient presented with hematuria and flank pain. CT showed a left renal mass. No other lesions or history of any malignancies are identified. The histological sections of the renal mass show both hypo and hypercellular areas composed of monotonous spindle cells in haphazard pattern with staghorn vascular network. No mitosis or necrosis is seen. Immunohistochemistry studies show that the spindle cells are positive for STAT6, CD34, BCL2 and vimentin and negative for PAX8, CK8/18, MDM2 and S100. The overall findings favor a solitary fibrous tumor, likely renal primary. According to the modified risk stratification criteria, this tumor is classified as low risk. Clinical correlation is recommended ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:1433](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28731041)).

Differential diagnosis

* [Angiomyolipoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumorangiomyolipoma.html) / [PEComa](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusPEComa.html):
  + Triphasic tumor with mature adipose tissue, smooth muscle component and thick walled vessels
  + Epithelioid variant may show pure population of cells with clear or eosinophilic cytoplasm
  + Positive for melanocytic markers ([HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html),
    [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html)) and smooth muscle markers ([SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html),
    [calponin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalponin.html))
  + Negative for [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html)
* [Sarcomatoid renal cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantrccsarcoma.html):
  + High grade cytological atypia
  + Positive for [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html) and [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)
  + Negative for [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html)
* [Mixed epithelial and stromal tumor (MEST)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormixedepithelial.html):
  + Most are benign, well circumscribed, with solid and cystic areas
  + Stromal and epithelial components showing diverse morphology and minimal cytological atypia
  + Stroma positive for [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html), [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html) and smooth muscle markers
  + Epithelium positive for [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) and [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html)
* [Renomedullary interstitial cell tumor (RMICT)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumorrenalmedfibroma.html):
  + Usually small nodule located in renal medulla
  + Small spindle / stellate cells in basophilic / fibrotic stroma with entrapped renal tubules
  + Negative for [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html) and [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)
* [Well differentiated liposarcoma (WDL)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuewdliposarcoma.html):
  + Contains adipose tissue and mimics fat forming SFTs but usually no staghorn vessels
  + Positive for [MDM2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmdm2.html) and [CDK4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdk4.html)
  + Dedifferentiated liposarcoma may be positive for [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html), when amplification of *CDK4* and *MDM2* genes involves contiguous *STAT6* gene ([Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2019;27:195](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28749793))
* [Synovial sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesynovialsarc.html):
  + Monophasic spindle cell subtype shows hypercellular monotonous spindle cells and staghorn vessels
  + t(X;18)(p11.2;q11.2) translocation leads to *SS18::SSX* fusions (most common *SSX1* and then *SSX2* and infrequently *SSX4*)
  + Positive for [TLE1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstle1.html), [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html) and [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html)
  + Negative for [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) and [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html)
* [Schwannoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueschwannoma.html):
  + Hypercellular (Antoni A) and hypocellular (Antoni B) areas
  + Nuclear palisading (Verocay bodies)
  + Strong diffuse positive for [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)
  + Negative for [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html) and [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)
* [Inflammatory myofibroblastic tumor (IMT)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueinflammyofibro.html):
  + Myofibroblastic proliferation with mixed inflammation
  + Most show spindle cells with haphazard arrangement but no staghorn vessels
  + Positive for [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html), [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) and ~60% are positive for [ALK1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html) (cytoplasmic staining in spindle cells, nuclear membrane or perinuclear staining in epithelioid variant)
  + Negative for [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html) and [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)

Board review style question #1

[![Solitary fibrous tumor (SFT)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/kidneytumorsolitaryTretiakova04.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/kidneytumorsolitaryTretiakova04.jpg)

Which of the following are the most commonly seen histological features of solitary fibrous tumor of kidney?

1. Admixture of adipose tissue, smooth muscle and thick wall vessels
2. Haphazard arrangement of spindle cells and staghorn vasculatures
3. Spindle cells with cytoplasmic microvacuoles and complex capillary network
4. Storiform fibrosis
5. Sustentacular cells wrap around chief cells and form zellballen pattern

Board review style answer #1

**B**. Haphazard arrangement of spindle cells and staghorn vasculatures are seen in solitary fibrous tumors.
