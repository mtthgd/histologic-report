Definition / general

* Well differentiated epithelial neoplasm that arises from renal parenchyma and shows neuroendocrine differentiation

Essential features

* Rare tumor that arises from renal parenchyma; associated with horseshoe kidney and mature teratoma of kidney
* Expresses neuroendocrine markers histologically but most are clinically nonfunctional
* Distant metastasis is common at diagnosis and Dual Tracer positron emission tomography (PET) imaging (DOTATATE scan) can help identify metastases

Terminology

* Antiquated names: carcinoid tumor, atypical carcinoid tumor
* Well differentiated neuroendocrine carcinoma is not a recommended term

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8240/3](https://tumourclassification.iarc.who.int/welcome/) - neuroendocrine tumor, well differentiated
* ICD-10: [C7A.093](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C7A-C7A/C7A-/C7A.093) - malignant carcinoid tumor of the kidney
* ICD-11: [2C90.Y](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#1048613131%2Fother) - other specified malignant neoplasms of kidney, except renal pelvis

Epidemiology

* Rare primary tumor in kidney; 58 cases reported in Surveillance, Epidemiology and End Results (SEER) database ([Front Endocrinol (Lausanne) 2021;11:624251](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33613455))
* 227 cases reported in the English literature between 1966 - 2023 ([Virchows Arch 2023;483:465](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37405461))
* Median age of 51; F:M = 125:93 ([Virchows Arch 2023;483:465](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37405461))
* 20 - 30% of cases are associated with horseshoe kidney ([Clin Genitourin Cancer 2020;18:e343](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31911122))
* Can arise within mature teratoma of the kidney ([Diagn Pathol 2007;2:15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17509135))

Sites

* Arises from renal parenchyma; can extend into renal pelvis and inferior vena cava ([Urology 2020;135:e2](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31593703))

Pathophysiology

* Indeterminate but may originate from renal stem cells or misplaced neural crest cells since no neuroendocrine cells have been identified in adult renal parenchyma ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2006;130:1693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17076534), [Front Endocrinol (Lausanne) 2021;11:624251](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33613455))

Etiology

* Uncertain

Clinical features

* Symptoms include abdominal / back / flank pain, hematuria, palpable mass, etc. ([Front Endocrinol (Lausanne) 2021;11:624251](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33613455))
* 33% of patients are asymptomatic ([J Urol 2006;176:2359](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17085102))
* Metastasis presented at diagnosis in 12/21 (57%) patients ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1539](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17895755))
* Tumors are usually nonfunctional but rarely show ectopic adrenocorticotropic hormone (ACTH) production (Cushing syndrome) ([Endocrinol Diabetes Metab Case Rep 2021;2021:20](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33847280))

Diagnosis

* Imaging study followed by histological examination of biopsy or resection specimen

Laboratory

* May show increased serum chromogranin A and urine 5-HIAA ([Front Endocrinol (Lausanne) 2021;11:624251](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33613455))

Radiology description

* Computed tomography (CT): solid hypodense (55%) or hyperdense (29%) mass with calcifications (33%), mimicking renal cell carcinoma ([Radiol Case Rep 2015;9:923](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27186242))
* Gallium 68 DOTATATE PET / CT: hypermetabolic / increased uptake ([Perm J 2020;24:19.147](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32240087), [Pathol Oncol Res 2020;26:341](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30357753))

Prognostic factors

* Unfavorable prognostic factors
  + Age: > 40 years
  + Size: > 4 cm
  + > 1 mitotic figure/10 high power fields (HPF)
  + Metastasis at diagnosis
  + Extension throughout renal capsule
* Reference: [J Urol 2006;176:2359](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17085102)

Case reports

* Late 40s woman presented with a 14.9 cm mass arising from horseshoe kidney ([Mayo Clin Proc 2021;96:1687](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34088430))
* 51 year old man presented with left renal mass and ectopic ACTH syndrome ([Endocrinol Diabetes Metab Case Rep 2021;2021:20](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33847280))
* 55 year old man with renal well differentiated neuroendocrine tumor and bilateral multiple clear cell papillary renal cell carcinomas ([Case Rep Pathol 2017;2017:9672368](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28620558))
* 64 year old woman with hematuria and right kidney mass ([Radiol Case Rep 2023;19:586](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38074443))
* 5 cases of primary renal well differentiated neuroendocrine tumor in 1 institution ([Pathol Oncol Res 2020;26:341](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30357753))

Treatment

* Nephrectomy with lymph node dissection
* Somatostatin analogs, tyrosine kinase inhibitors and peptide receptor radionuclide therapy (PRRT) for metastasis ([Front Endocrinol (Lausanne) 2021;11:624251](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33613455))

Gross description

* Well circumscribed, soft, brown-tan mass with focal hemorrhage, necrosis or cystic degeneration

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Variable architecture: tightly packed cords with minimal stroma (most common, 81%), sheet-like / solid pattern, nests / organoid pattern, trabeculae, ribbons, pseudo glands, etc. ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1539](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17895755))
* Round to oval / elongated nuclei with stippled / speckled chromatin, inconspicuous nucleoli and moderate eosinophilic / granular cytoplasm
* Occasional calcifications (5/21, 24%) of variable sizes ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1539](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17895755))
* Usually lack of necrosis and low mitotic activity ( Grading of neuroendocrine neoplasms in nonendocrine organs by measuring proliferation either by mitotic count or Ki67 index assessment
  + WHO grade 1: 2 or Ki67 index WHO grade 2: 2 - 20 mitoses/2 mm2 or Ki67 index 3 - 20%
  + WHO grade 3: > 20 mitoses/2 mm2 or Ki67 > 20%

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Genetic alterations are variable and none of them are specific
* Most frequently observed genetic changes are mutations in *CDH1* and *TET2*
* Reference: [Histopathology 2019;75:104](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30851202)

Sample pathology report

* Right kidney, core biopsy:
  + Well differentiated neuroendocrine tumor, likely renal primary (see comment)
  + Comment: The tumor cells show nested growth pattern, with round to oval nuclei, stippled chromatin and eosinophilic granular cytoplasm. No mitosis or necrosis is seen. Immunohistochemistry studies show that the tumor cells are positive for CK AE1 / AE3, synaptophysin and chromogranin and negative for PAX8, TTF1, CDX2 and GATA3. The overall findings are consistent with a well differentiated neuroendocrine tumor. Given the radiographic finding of solitary renal mass and lack of clinical history of neuroendocrine tumors elsewhere, this tumor is most likely a renal primary.

Differential diagnosis

* [Metastatic well differentiated neuroendocrine tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantmetastases.html):
  + Clinical history of well differentiated neuroendocrine tumor in other body sites
  + May show [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html) or [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html) positivity
* [Paraganglioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/adrenalparaganglioma.html):
  + Zellballen / organoid growth pattern
  + Sustentacular cells are positive for [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) and chief cells are positive for neuroendocrine markers
  + Negative for cytokeratin and positive for [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgata3.html) ([Mod Pathol 2013;26:1365](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23599157))
* [Metanephric adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormetanephric.html):
  + Composed of tightly packed tubules, ducts and abortive glomeruli; psammomatous calcifications are common
  + Positive for [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html), [BRAF](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbraf.html) and [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html)
* [Nested variants of urothelial carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladdernested.html):
  + Variable sized anastomosing nests with irregular projection into stroma
  + Positive for [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html), [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgata3.html) and [uroplakin II](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsUroplakinii.html)
* **Poorly differentiated neuroendocrine carcinomas of kidney**:
  + High grade cytological atypia, necrosis is common; brisk mitotic activity and high [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) proliferative index
  + Positive for cytokeratin and neuroendocrine markers
  + Small cell carcinoma: high N:C ratios, hyperchromatic nuclei, nuclear molding
  + Large cell carcinoma: large nuclei, abundant cytoplasm, prominent nucleoli
* [Ewing sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneewing.html):
  + Usually in children or young adults Small round blue cell tumor, [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html) positive
  + Gene rearrangements involving *FET* and *ETS* families of genes (most commonly *EWSR1::FLI1* gene rearrangement)

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/kidneytumormalignantcarcinoidTretiakova03.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/kidneytumormalignantcarcinoidTretiakova03.jpg)

A 45 year old man presented with hematuria. Computed tomography (CT) showed a well demarcated 3 cm left renal mass in the lower pole. Core biopsy was performed and the H&E slide is shown above. Immunostains show that the tumor cells are positive for CK8/18, synaptophysin and chromogranin and negative for PAX8, GATA3 and S100. What is the diagnosis of this tumor?

1. Metanephric adenoma
2. Nested variant of urothelial carcinoma
3. Papillary renal cell carcinoma
4. Paraganglioma
5. Well differentiated neuroendocrine tumor

Board review style answer #1

**E**. Well differentiated neuroendocrine tumor. Both the histological features and the immunostain profile are consistent with well differentiated neuroendocrine tumor.
Answer A is incorrect because metanephric adenomas are usually composed of tightly packed tubules, ducts and abortive glomeruli. They are PAX8 and WT1 positive and negative for neuroendocrine markers.
Answer B is incorrect because nested variant of urothelial carcinomas are commonly seen in the urinary bladder. They are usually positive for CK7, CK20 and GATA3, while negative for PAX8 and neuroendocrine markers.
Answer C is incorrect because papillary renal cell carcinomas usually show papillary architecture, with positive staining for CK7 and PAX8 and negative staining for WT1 and neuroendocrine markers.
Answer D is incorrect because paragangliomas are positive for S100 in sustentacular cells and negative for cytokeratin.
