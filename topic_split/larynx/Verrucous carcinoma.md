Definition / general

* Verrucous carcinoma (VC) is a highly differentiated, slow growing, locally invasive, nonmetastasizing squamous cell carcinoma (SCC) lacking cytologic features of malignancy
* VC is characterized by a warty keratinized surface and distinctive architecture, with a lateral spread and pushing invasion below the level of adjacent epithelium

Essential features

* Well differentiated, locally invasive SCC with pushing margin
* No overt cytologic atypia
* Thorough sampling required to exclude conventional SCC component

Terminology

* Verrucous carcinoma, NOS
* Not recommended: Ackerman tumor

Epidemiology

* 3% of laryngeal squamous cell carcinomas ([Surg Pathol Clin 2017;10:15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28153131))
* Mean age of diagnosis is ~60 ([J Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 2023;52:81](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38093339), [Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 2017;156:38](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27484231))
* More frequent in males (M:F = 14:1) ([Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 2017;156:38](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27484231))
* Risk factors
  + Alcohol consumption ([Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 2017;156:38](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27484231))
  + Smoking ([World J Clin Oncol 2024;15:464](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38689630))

Sites

* Head and neck
  + After oral cavity, VC is most commonly found in the larynx
* Typically involves the glottis (~80%) ([J Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 2023;52:81](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38093339), [Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 2017;156:38](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27484231))
* Other sites include supraglottis, subglottis or hypopharynx ([Cancer 2001;92:110](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11443616))

Pathophysiology

* Loss of heterozygosity in microsatellite markers on chromosome 9p ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1299](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15371945))

Etiology

* Related to tobacco consumption ([Cancer 2001;92:110](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11443616), [World J Clin Oncol 2024;15:464](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38689630))
* No association with transcriptionally active HPV ([Surg Pathol Clin 2017;10:15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28153131))

Clinical features

* Patients typically present with hoarseness (90%) or dyspnea (10%) ([Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 2017;156:38](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27484231))
* Approximately half present early (stage I or T1 disease) ([Cancer 2001;92:110](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11443616), [J Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 2023;52:81](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38093339), [Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 2017;156:38](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27484231))
* Radiographic features are similar to conventional SCC, stage for stage

Diagnosis

* Diagnosis of VC involves a combination of clinical history, symptoms, physical examination and imaging findings
* Macroscopic findings of a fungating, exophytic, tan to white, broad based tumor with a granular, shaggy surface
  + Cut surfaces are firm, tan to white, with clefts and well defined margins ([Head Neck 1998;20:197](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9570624))
* Histologically, well differentiated squamoproliferative tumor with deep bulbous pushing invasion below the level of adjacent epithelium; no stromal desmoplasia seen
* WHO essential and desirable diagnostic criteria
  + Essential
    - Acanthotic, hyperkeratotic and undulating bland squamous epithelium
    - Smooth interface with the stroma
    - Keratotic crypts
    - Mitoses limited to basal / parabasal layers
    - Pushing invasion below the level of the surrounding normal epithelium
  + Desirable
    - Dense lymphoplasmacytic submucosal chronic inflammation
    - Glassy, eosinophilic cytoplasm

Prognostic factors

* Excellent prognosis; it does not metastasize and has better prognosis than conventional SCC, with overall 5 year survival of 85 - 95% ([Surg Pathol Clin 2017;10:15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28153131))
* Staging follows the AJCC 8th edition TNM staging system for conventional laryngeal SCC
* Prognosis is related to ([Laryngoscope 2018;128:393](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28850720))
  + Surgery: favorable prognosis
  + Advanced age or stage (especially T stage): unfavorable prognosis

Case reports

* 55 year old man with hoarseness and hairy whitish tumor on the larynx ([Diagn Ther Endosc 1997;3:249](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18493443))
* 58 year old man presented with verrucous carcinoma with spindle cell carcinoma component of the right vocal chord ([Auris Nasus Larynx 2007;34:557](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17485187))
* 67 year old man presented with exophytic mass of the right vocal chord ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2005;129:115](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15628892))
* 68 year old man presented with large obstructing laryngeal tumor ([J Voice 2009;23:140](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18082368))

Treatment

* Surgical resection is often sufficient without other interventions; neck dissection may not be required in most cases ([Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 2017;156:38](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27484231))
* Radiotherapy allows for better laryngeal preservation but may be less effective in more advanced stages ([Int Arch Otorhinolaryngol 2021;26:e348](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35846806))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Low power view shows an undulating, broad based neoplastic proliferation with bulbous enlargement of rete ridges, extending below level of surrounding epithelium, imparting an elephant foot appearance
* Densely keratinized surface with parakeratosis and church spire keratosis between exophytic warty tumor projections ([Surg Pathol Clin 2017;10:15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28153131), [Cancer 2001;92:110](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11443616))
* Higher power view shows well differentiated squamous epithelium with markedly expanded spinous layer of cells with prominent, eosinophilic glassy cytoplasm ([Cancer 1980;46:1617](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7417957))
* Isolated to absent mitoses confined to the basal / parabasal zone; no pleomorphism or atypia
* Dense lymphoplasmacytic inflammatory infiltrate in stroma adjacent to invasive component is typical, often without associated desmoplasia
* Overtly invasive tumors with moderate to severe dysplastic features consistent with conventional SCC may arise within VC

Sample pathology report

* Laryngeal mass, excision biopsy:
  + Verrucous carcinoma (see comment)
  + Comment: Section shows squamous mucosa with a well differentiated squamoproliferative lesion featuring broad, bulbous rete ridges with pushing invasion into the deeper stroma. There is interface lymphoplasmacytic infiltrate. No overt cytologic atypia or stromal desmoplasia is present. No conventional squamous cell carcinoma component is identified in entirely submitted lesion. Resection margins are clear.

Differential diagnosis

* [Verrucous hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/larynxverrucoushyperplasia.html):
  + Squamous hyperplasia with exophytic / papillary surface and broadened rete ridges
  + Lacks the deep pushing invasion below level of adjacent normal epithelium seen in verrucous carcinoma ([J Oral Maxillofac Pathol 2011;15:187](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22529578))
* [Pseudoepitheliomatous hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/oralcavitypseudoephyperplasia.html) ([Am J Dermatopathol 2011;33:112](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21399447)):
  + Benign squamous hyperplastic lesion usually in association with an underlying infectious, inflammatory or neoplastic process (e.g., [granular cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuegranularcell.html))
  + Lacks the dense, glassy eosinophilic cytoplasm and bulbous rete ridges seen in verrucous carcinoma
  + More dense inflammation: either acute, chronic or giant cell / granulomatous, depending on the etiology
* [Squamous papilloma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/larynxpapilloma.html) ([Head Neck Pathol 2019;13:80](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30693456)):
  + Often pedunculated
  + Varying keratinization
  + Koilocytic atypia
* [SCC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/larynxscc.html) ([conventional](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/larynxcarcinomageneral.html), [papillary](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/larynxpapillaryscc.html) or **exophytic**):
  + Greater degree of atypia with hyperchromatic and pleomorphic nuclei
  + Irregular, infiltrative growth with stromal reaction
  + Disruption of the basement membrane and invasion into contiguous structures is common
  + SCC may arise within VC (hybrid VC)

The histology image shown above is from a laryngeal tumor in a 60 year old man. What is the diagnosis?

1. Proliferative verrucous leukoplakia
2. Squamous papilloma
3. Verrucous carcinoma
4. Verrucous hyperplasia

Board review style answer #1

**C.** Verrucous carcinoma.
The image shows a well differentiated squamoproliferative lesion with bulbous rete ridges showing deep pushing type invasion of stroma, in keeping with verrucous carcinoma. Answers A, B and D are incorrect because these options are also well differentiated squamoproliferative lesions but none of them show the deep pushing invasion seen here.

Board review style question #2

Which of the following statements is true for verrucous carcinoma?

1. Conventional squamous cell carcinoma (SCC) does not occur in the background of verrucous carcinoma
2. Typically, verrucous carcinoma is a highly radioresponsive tumor
3. Verrucous carcinoma can metastasize and has a worse prognosis than conventional SCC
4. Verrucous carcinoma is locally destructive through pushing invasion and expansive growth

Board review style answer #2

**D.** Verrucous carcinoma is locally destructive through pushing invasion and expansive growth.
Answer C is incorrect because verrucous carcinoma does not metastasize and has a better prognosis than conventional SCC. Answer B is incorrect because patients are best treated with primary surgery. Primary radiotherapy may also be sometimes effective but it's not typically a highly radioresponsive tumor. Answer A is incorrect because conventional SCC can occur in the background of verrucous carcinoma; such lesions will have potential for metastasis and should be treated as conventional SCC.
