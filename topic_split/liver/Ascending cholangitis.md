Definition / general

* Infection of the extrahepatic biliary system, often due to obstruction ([Infect Dis Clin North Am 2000;14:521](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10987108))

Essential features

* Highly morbid infection of large bile ducts
* Histologic correlate is neutrophils within epithelium and lumen of interlobular bile ducts

Terminology

* Also called acute cholangitis, suppurative cholangitis

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [K83.09](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/K00-K95/K80-K87/K83-/K83.09) - other cholangitis

Epidemiology

* Typically a secondary process that occurs in an already diseased bile duct (stricture, etc.) ([Postgrad Med J 2007;83:773](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18057178))
* Can occur following the Kasai procedure to correct [extrahepatic biliary atresia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liverextrahepaticbiliaryatresia.html) ([J Pediatr Surg 1989;24:729](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2769536), [J Pediatr Surg 2004;39:1800](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15616934))

Etiology

* Common infectious causes include *E. coli, Klebsiella*, enterococci ([Postgrad Med J 2007;83:773](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18057178))
* Rarely due to adenovirus ([Pediatr Dev Pathol 2003;6:156](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12574911))

Clinical features

* Symptoms include Charcot cholangitis triad: jaundice, fever and right upper quadrant abdominal pain ([World J Gastrointest Pathophysiol 2018;9:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29487761))
* May lead to a hepatic pyogenic abscess

Diagnosis

* Based on the Tokyo Guidelines, which incorporate systemic inflammation, cholestasis and imaging ([J Hepatobiliary Pancreat Sci 2018;25:17](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29032610), [ANZ J Surg 2017;87:554](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28337833))

Laboratory

* Endoscopic retrograde cholangiopancreatography (ERCP) obtained bile can be cultured to guide antibiotic therapy ([Dig Dis 2019;37:155](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30282078))
  + Blood culture may be less successful

Case reports

* 64 year old woman with *Ascaris* infection ([Eur J Intern Med 2016;34:e7](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27157828))
* 72 year old man with *Lactococcus lactis cremoris* bacteremia ([J Med Case Rep 2009;3:3](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19126198))
* 73 year old man who has recently done heavy lifting ([Case Rep Gastroenterol 2017;11:500](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29033769))
* 93 year old woman with biliary enteric fistula ([ACG Case Rep J 2018;5:e60](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30214909))

Treatment

* Biliary drainage and antibiotic therapy

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Neutrophils within lumens of interlobular bile ducts and also infiltrating duct (not ductule) epithelium ([Lefkowitch: Scheuer's Liver Biopsy Interpretation, 9th Edition, 2015](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0702055484/pathologyoutl-20))
  + Note: neutrophils sprinkled throughout ductular reaction is a typical component of ductular reaction and should NOT be considered evidence of infection or of ascending cholangitis
* Larger ducts often unaffected
* Findings of other concomitant biliary processes (e.g. large duct obstruction) may also be present

Sample pathology report

* Liver, biopsy:
  + Hepatic parenchyma with cholestasis and neutrophils involving bile duct lumens and epithelium (see comment)
  + Comment: The findings are concerning for acute (ascending) cholangitis in the proper clinical context.

Differential diagnosis

* [Acute cellular rejection of liver transplant](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liveracutetcellmediatedrejection.html):
  + Neutrophils within duct lumen may rarely be present
  + Clinical correlation necessary, as duct complications / infection can also occur in the posttransplant setting
* **Sepsis**:
  + May occur alongside ascending cholangitis
  + Nonspecific cholestasis and acute / chronic inflammation, though ductular cholestasis is highly suggestive of sepsis

Board review style question #1

Neutrophils filling the lumens of native interlobular bile ducts are most suggestive of which diagnosis?

1. Ascending cholangitis
2. Cholangiocarcinoma
3. Primary biliary cholangitis
4. Primary sclerosing cholangitis
5. Recurrent pyogenic cholangitis

Board review style answer #1

**A.** Ascending cholangitis
