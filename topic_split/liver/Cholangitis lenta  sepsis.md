Definition / general

* Cholangitis lenta was originally a term referring to a rare clinical form of chronic sepsis ([Hum Pathol 1982;13:19](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7076191))
* Now typically used by pathologists to refer to microscopic bile ductular cholestasis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:1607](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28984672)), often in the context of sepsis

Essential features

* Cholangitis lenta currently functions as a synonym for bile ductular cholestasis
* Ductular reaction with bile ductular cholestasis is concerning (but not specific) for sepsis

Terminology

* Additional terms include cholangiolar cholestasis or subacute nonsuppurative cholangitis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:1607](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28984672))
* Lenta appears to mean slow

Pathophysiology

* Cause of biliary abnormalities in septic patients is complex, likely multifactorial ([Crit Care 2012;16:235](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23134597))

Laboratory

* Positive microbiology cultures common ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:1607](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28984672))
* Bilirubin levels often high ([Lefkowich: Scheuer's Liver Biopsy Interpretation, 9th Edition, 2015](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0702055484/pathologyoutl-20))

Case reports

* Child with sepsis and hepatorenal syndrome ([Fetal Pediatr Pathol 2018;37:424](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30351239))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Dilated, peripherally located bile ductular reaction that contains inspissated bile
* Other common findings include portal tract inflammation, duct injury, hepatocyte ballooning or necrosis and canalicular cholestasis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:1607](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28984672))

Ductular cholestasis in cirrhosis

Sample pathology report

* Liver, biopsy:
  + Biliary pattern injury with ductular reaction, prominent ductular cholestasis and moderate portal inflammation (see comment)
  + Comment: The clinical concern for liver failure is noted. The histologic findings raise the possibility of sepsis, though they are not specific for that diagnosis.

Differential diagnosis

* Ductular cholestasis can be seen in:
  + **Sepsis**:
    - Histology not entirely specific, with other biliary pattern injury and inflammation often seen; clinical correlation needed
  + [Neonatal alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liverAAT.html):
    - [PASD](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html) positive protein globules in hepatocytes
  + [Extrahepatic biliary atresia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liverextrahepaticbiliaryatresia.html):
    - Biliary pattern injury in young patients; clinical correlation needed
  + [Cirrhosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livercirrhosis.html):
    - Cause of cirrhosis not necessarily biliary
  + [Post transplant liver biopsies](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertransplantgeneral.html):
    - Occurs in both adult and pediatric patients ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:1607](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28984672), [Transplantation 1998;65:692](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9521205))
    - Clinical correlation needed
