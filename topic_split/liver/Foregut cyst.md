Definition / general

* Most foregut cysts are 4 cm or smaller; the epithelium resembles bronchiolar epithelium, with smooth muscle bundles in the cyst wall
* Medial segment of the left hepatic lobe (segment IV) is the most common location

Essential features

* Rare; ~100 cases reported
* The only ciliated cyst of the liver
* More common in the medial segment of the left hepatic lobe

Terminology

* Ciliated hepatic foregut cyst ([Hum Pathol 2000;31:241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10685641))

ICD coding

* SNOMED: [M33400](https://bioportal.bioontology.org/ontologies/SNMI?p=classes&conceptid=M-33400) - cyst, NOS

Epidemiology

* Slightly more common in women; some articles state this entity is more common in men ([Diagn Pathol 2015;10:81](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26122082))
* Ages between 20 weeks of gestation and 82 years (mean: 48 - 52.7 years) ([Hum Pathol 2000;31:241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10685641), [Am J Surg Pathol 1999;23:671](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10366149), [Case Rep Med 2013;2013:372017](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24222771))
* ~100 cases reported in the English literature; true incidence is likely to be higher because most are asymptomatic ([Ann Surg 1974;179:922](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4835513))

Sites

* Usually subcapsular and localized in the left lobe of the liver (50% cases) in the medial segment (segment IV) ([Hum Pathol 2000;31:241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10685641))
* Cases in the right lobe (36% of cases) and gallbladder have been described ([Pathol Res Pract 2009;205:498](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19410383), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 1999;123:1115](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10539920), [Hum Pathol 2000;31:241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10685641))

Pathophysiology

* Congenital in nature
* Arises from evaginations from the embryonic foregut; a bronchial remnant from the proximal intestine becomes trapped in the liver during embryonic development ([Am J Surg Pathol 1999;23:671](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10366149))

Clinical features

* Nonspecific, abdominal pain ([Hum Pathol 2000;31:241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10685641))
* Exceptionally, a foregut cyst can cause portal hypertension and splenomegaly by compression of the portal vein or jaundice by compression of the hepatic duct ([Hum Pathol 2000;31:241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10685641), [Am J Surg Pathol 1999;23:671](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10366149))

Diagnosis

* Most commonly an incidental diagnosis during ultrasonography, surgical exploration or autopsy ([Pathol Oncol Res 2002;8:278](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12579217))

Laboratory

* CA 19-9, AFP and CEA serological markers are usually negative ([Pathol Res Pract 2009;205:498](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19410383))
* CEA may be positive ([Case Reports Hepatol 2023;2023:6637890](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37503330))
* CA 19-9 is not useful as it may be positive in benign cysts and negative in malignancy ([Am J Gastroenterol 1998;93:2212](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9820398))

Radiology description

* Appearance is variable
* Well defined anechoic or hypoechoic small masses ([Hum Pathol 2000;31:241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10685641), [Gastroenterol Rep (Oxf) 2017;5:75](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26126985))
* Hypodense cysts with no enhancement after contrast injection in computed tomography (CT) ([Hum Pathol 2000;31:241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10685641))
* On magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), they are T2 hyperintense and have a variable T1 appearance on weighted images, including T1 hyperintensity or hypointensity ([Gastroenterol Rep (Oxf) 2017;5:75](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26126985))
* Differential diagnosis radiologically might include other hepatic cysts, echinococcal cyst, pyogenic abscess, mesenchymal hamartoma ([Gastroenterol Rep (Oxf) 2017;5:75](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26126985))

Prognostic factors

* Almost always benign; complete surgical excision is recommended because of small risk of malignancy ([Pathol Res Pract 2009;205:498](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19410383))

Case reports

* 51 year old man with ciliated foregut cyst with squamous cell carcinoma ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1999;123:1115](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10539920))
* 60 year old woman with ciliated foregut cyst with squamous cell carcinoma ([Pathol Res Pract 2009;205:498](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19410383))
* 75 year old man underwent screening for lung cancer, with incidental discovery of a ciliated hepatic foregut cyst ([Case Reports Hepatol 2023;2023:6637890](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37503330))
* Series of ciliated hepatic foregut cyst cases, with 6 additional cases and extensive review of all reported cases in the English literature ([Diagn Pathol 2015;10:81](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26122082))

Treatment

* Treatment of choice is cyst aspiration, injection of sclerosing agents or surgical excision ([Burt: MacSween's Pathology of the Liver, 6th Edition, 2011](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7158313/))
* Surgical excision is curative and generally the preferred method ([Pathol Res Pract 2009;205:498](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19410383))

* Thin and whitish wall with a smooth to focally granular surface ([Hum Pathol 2000;31:241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10685641))

Frozen section description

* Not performed

Microscopic (histologic) description

* The only ciliated cyst of the liver
* 4 layers can be appreciated
  + Ciliated pseudostratified columnar epithelium
  + Subepithelial connective tissue
  + Layer of smooth muscle
  + Fibrous capsule
* Pseudostratified lining of ciliated columnar epithelium with goblet cells
* Subepithelial loose connective tissue, fibrous and paucicellular ([Hum Pathol 2000;31:241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10685641))
* Smooth muscle layer composed of 1 - 3 discontinuous smooth muscle layers, surrounded by an outer fibrous capsule ([Hum Pathol 2000;31:241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10685641))
* Fibrous capsule with arterioles and nerve trunks can be seen
* Cyst wall can be disrupted with inflammatory changes in the surrounding liver parenchyma ([Hum Pathol 2000;31:241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10685641))
* Squamous metaplasia without evidence of dysplasia might occur ([Diagn Pathol 2015;10:81](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26122082))
* Gastric antral metaplasia might be present ([Diagn Pathol 2015;10:81](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26122082))
* Rarely, malignant transformation can occur and is associated with a bigger size ([Pathol Res Pract 2009;205:498](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19410383), [Pathol Res Pract 2002;198:697](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12498226), [Am J Surg Pathol 1984;8:467](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6731667))

Cytology description

* Usually not performed; fine needle aspiration was diagnostic in 62% of cases ([Case Reports Hepatol 2023;2023:6637890](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37503330))
* May show ciliated cells, some inflammatory cells, goblet cells and mucus ([Diagn Cytopathol 2006;34:846](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17183760))
* In cases of malignant transformation, atypical cells may be present

Positive stains

* Epithelial layer stains positively for cytokeratins [CAM 5.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscam52.html), [AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html) and [BerEP4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsepcam.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 1999;23:671](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10366149))
* Epithelial basement membrane and outer connective tissue layer stains for [collagen IV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscollagentypeIV.html) and **laminin** ([Am J Surg Pathol 1999;23:671](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10366149))
* Muscle fibers stain for [smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 1999;23:671](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10366149))
* Goblet cells stain for [mucicarmine](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmucins.html), [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html), [PASD](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html) and [Alcian blue](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalcianblue.html) ([Hum Pathol 2000;31:241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10685641))
* Scattered endocrine cells in the epithelium are positive for endocrine markers [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html), [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html) and [calcitonin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalcitonin.html) ([Hum Pathol 2000;31:241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10685641))

Negative stains

* Weakly positive for [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) and [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 1999;23:671](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10366149))

Electron microscopy description

* Confirms the presence of cilia as well as goblet cells ([Hum Pathol 2000;31:241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10685641))

Sample pathology report

* Liver, cyst excision:
  + Ciliated hepatic foregut cyst, no evidence of malignant transformation

Differential diagnosis

* [Simple cyst](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liversolitarycyst.html):
  + Simple cuboidal or mesothelial lining
* [Hydatid cyst](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liverechinococcal.html):
  + Fibrous capsule without epithelial lining
  + Parasitic remnants (acellular lamellate layer and protoscolices)
* [Hepatobiliary cystadenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorMCN.html):
  + Usually multilocular
  + Mesenchymal (ovarian-like) stroma, cuboidal or columnar epithelium with a xanthogranulomatous response
* [Intrahepatic choledochal cyst](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livercholedochalcyst.html):
  + Columnar epithelium, with walls composed of dense fibrous tissue and scattered smooth muscle and elastic fibers
  + Variable chronic inflammatory infiltrate
* [Cystic metastasis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumormet.html):
  + Distinguishing metastasis from ciliated hepatic foregut cyst is usually straightforward
    - Metastatic tumors can have different morphologies but are usually accompanied by stromal desmoplasia
    - Cells have marked atypia, atypical mitoses and necrosis
  + Distinguishing malignant transformation of ciliated hepatic foregut cyst from metastasis may be challenging
    - According to the primary site, immunohistochemistry may help in differentiating both
    - Clinical information and radiology are of utmost importance
* [Endosalpingiosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pleuraendosalpingiosis.html):
  + Morphology helps in the distinction; in endosalpingiosis, the lining is composed of 3 distinct cells: ciliated columnar cells, nonciliated columnar secretory mucous cells and intercalated or peg cells, while in ciliated hepatic foregut cyst, the lining is composed of only 1 type of cell (ciliated pseudostratified columnar epithelium)

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/liverforegutcystoliveira03.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/liverforegutcystoliveira03.jpg)

What is the only ciliated cyst of the liver?

1. Ciliated hepatic foregut cyst
2. Hepatobiliary cystadenoma
3. Hydatid cyst
4. Mesenchymal hamartoma

Board review style answer #1

**A**. Ciliated hepatic foregut cyst. It has a pseudostratified epithelium that is ciliated. Answer C is incorrect because hydatid cysts are typically lined by a thick, acellular layer and a thin germinal layer but do not have ciliated epithelium. Answer B is incorrect because the epithelium of hepatobiliary cystadenomas is typically cuboidal or columnar and lacks cilia. Answer D is incorrect because the cysts in mesenchymal hamartomas are lined with biliary type epithelium, which lacks cilia.

Board review style question #2

What is the most common location of the ciliated hepatic foregut cyst?

1. Lateral segment of left hepatic lobe
2. Lateral segment of right hepatic lobe
3. Medial segment of left hepatic lobe
4. Medial segment of right hepatic lobe

Board review style answer #2

**C**. Medial segment of left hepatic lobe. The ciliated hepatic foregut cyst is most commonly located in the medial segment of the left hepatic lobe, accounting for 50% of cases. Answer D is incorrect because while the cyst occurs in the right lobe in ~36% of cases, the medial segment of the left lobe remains the most frequent site. Answers A and B are incorrect because ciliated hepatic foregut cyst is much less commonly located in the lateral segments of the left or right hepatic lobes compared to the medial segments.
