Definition / general

* Rare subtype of hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) designated by the WHO; characterized by a dense lymphoid infiltrate with more lymphocytes than tumor cells

Essential features

* Striking lymphocytic infiltrate is a defining feature
* Not associated with Epstein-Barr virus (EBV) or microsatellite instability

Terminology

* May be called lymphoepithelioma-like HCC when the tumor is poorly differentiated ([Gastroenterol Clin North Am 2017;46:365](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28506370))

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [C22.7](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C15-C26/C22-/C22.7) - other specified carcinomas of liver

Epidemiology

* Incidence ≤ 1% of HCC
* Based on a review of 42 cases reported in the literature, there appears to be a male predominance (M:F = 2.8:1), with mean age of 58 years (range: 39 - 81 years) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:304](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25675010), [Hepatology 1998;27:407](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9462638), [Histopathology 2000;37:523](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11122434))

Sites

* Liver

Etiology

* Approximately half of cases have underlying cirrhosis or chronic infection with hepatitis B / C ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:304](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25675010))

Clinical features

* Very rare tumor with limited clinical and outcome data

Diagnosis

* Imaging modalities for diagnosis of HCC: multiphasic computed tomography (CT) or magnetic resonance imaging (MRI)
* Tissue biopsy is indicated if imaging is not diagnostic of HCC

Laboratory

* Elevation in serum alpha fetoprotein (AFP)

Radiology description

* Dynamic perfusion pattern of arterial hyperenhancement and venous or delayed phase washout is characteristic of HCC

Prognostic factors

* May have better prognosis in comparison to [classic HCC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorHCC.html) ([Am J Pathol 2017;187:1438](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28500863), [Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:304](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25675010))
* Majority of patients present with low stage disease, a single lesion, and without vascular invasion ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:304](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25675010))
* Prognostic factors for HCC: stage (TNM), single lesion (versus multifocal), size, vascular invasion, portal vein thrombosis, severity of underlying liver disease ([Liver Int 2009;29:502](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19141028), [J Surg Oncol 2018;117:644](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29127719))

Case reports

* 37 year old woman with chronic hepatitis B and C infection ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2017;96:e9416](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29390565))
* 58 year old man with elevated serum AFP ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2017;10:7893](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31966638))
* 78 year old woman with a liver mass ([J Liver Cancer 2021;21:69](https://doi.org/10.17998/jlc.21.1.69))
* 79 year old man with a liver mass ([World J Surg Oncol 2013;11:97](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23642182))
* 81 year old woman with chronic hepatitis C infection ([World J Gastroenterol 2015;21:10468](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26420974))

Treatment

* Surgical resection
* Radiofrequency ablation
* Transarterial chemoembolization (TACE)
* Transplantation (e.g., Milan criteria, modified by some institutions) ([Nat Rev Gastroenterol Hepatol 2017;14:203](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28053342))

Gross description

* Majority present as a solitary lesion ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:304](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25675010))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Cords and trabeculae of tumor cells in a dense lymphoid stroma (lymphocytes outnumber tumor cells in most fields)
* Lymphoid infiltrate predominantly T cells with scattered B cells and germinal centers
* May show piecemeal necrosis
* Histologic grade usually well to moderately differentiated
* If poorly differentiated, the term lymphoepithelioma-like HCC may be used ([Gastroenterol Clin North Am 2017;46:365](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28506370))
* May have focal syncytial growth

Positive stains

* [Cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)
* Hepatocellular markers ([arginase1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsarginase1.html), [glypican 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglypican3.html), [HepPar1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsheppar1.html)) variably positive
* Majority of lymphocytes are [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html)+ / [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html)+ cytotoxic T cells ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:304](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25675010))

Negative stains

* [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [CK19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck19.html), [mucicarmine](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmucins.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Most cases negative for [EBV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) in situ hybridization (ISH); EBV positivity reported in one study ([Diagn Mol Pathol 2004;13:183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15322431))
* Tumor infiltrating lymphocytes not associated with microsatellite instability ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:304](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25675010))
* Lymphocyte rich hepatocellular carcinoma has been shown to have focal amplification of chromosome 11q13.3, which is considered to be an immune checkpoint signature, making them a promising target for immunotherapy ([J Pathol 2019;249:166](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31168847))

Sample pathology report

* Liver, mass, core biopsy:
  + Lymphocyte rich hepatocellular carcinoma (see comment)
  + Comment: The tumor shows histologic features of lymphocyte rich hepatocellular carcinoma with nests and cords of hepatoid cells within a dense lymphoid stroma. The tumor cells are positive for pancytokeratin, HepPar1 and glypican 3, and negative for CK7, CK19 and mucicarmine. EBV in situ hybridization is negative.

Differential diagnosis

* [Lymphoepithelioma-like cholangiocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorcholangiocarcinoma.html):
  + Syncytial growth of glands and cords of epithelial cells in a lymphoid stroma
  + May have luminal or cytoplasmic mucin
  + [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html)+, [CK19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck19.html)+, [EBV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) ISH+, negative hepatocellular markers
* [Classic HCC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorHCC.html) with increased lymphocytes:
  + Tumors with only mild to moderate increase in lymphocytes should be classified as conventional HCC
* Lymphoma (**primary hepatic lymphoma** or secondary involvement by systemic disease):
  + Dense infiltrate of atypical B or T cells
  + No intermixed neoplastic hepatocytes
  + Positive hematolymphoid markers (depends on the entity), negative [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) and hepatocellular markers
* [Inflammatory myofibroblastic tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorIMT.html):
  + Tumor of myofibroblasts with plasma cells, lymphocytes and eosinophils
  + [ALK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html)+ spindle cells (subset)
* [IgG4 related disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneyigg4related.html):
  + Abundant [IgG4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsigg4.html)+ plasma cells, systemic disease, elevated serum IgG4
* [Inflammatory variant of angiomyolipoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorAML.html):
  + Mature adipose tissue, smooth muscle cells and thick walled blood vessels with an inflammatory infiltrate
  + [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html)+, [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html)+, [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html)+
* [Inflammatory pseudotumor-like follicular / fibroblastic dendritic cell sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodesFDCsarcoma.html):
  + Neoplastic spindle cells are positive for [CD21](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd21.html) (follicular differentiation) or [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) (fibroblastic differentiation), [EBV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) ISH+
* [Inflammatory pseudotumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorIMT.html) (nonneoplastic):
  + Mixed inflammation, granulomas, necrosis / abscess, rule out [AFB](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsacidfast.html), *T. pallidum*, parasites
  + No intermixed neoplastic hepatocytes
