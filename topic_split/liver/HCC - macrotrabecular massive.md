Definition / general

* Subtype of hepatocellular carcinoma with macrotrabecular pattern (≥ 6 - 10 cells thick) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:943](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31135484), [Hepatology 2018;68:103](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29281854), [J Hepatocell Carcinoma 2022;9:661](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35923611))
* Macrotrabecular pattern in > 50% of the tumor

Essential features

* Hepatocellular carcinoma with > 50% growth having a macrotrabecular pattern (≥ 6 - 10 cells thick) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:943](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31135484), [Hepatology 2018;68:103](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29281854), [J Hepatocell Carcinoma 2022;9:661](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35923611))
* Presents with higher grade and stage compared to conventional hepatocellular carcinoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:943](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31135484))
* Associated with high serum alpha fetoprotein (AFP) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:943](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31135484), [Hepatology 2018;68:103](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29281854))
* Poor prognosis with early recurrence and poor overall survival ([Hepatology 2018;68:103](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29281854), [J Hepatol 2017;67:727](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28532995))
* Vascular invasion is common ([J Hepatol 2017;67:727](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28532995))
* Associated with *TP53* mutations and *FGF19* amplification ([J Hepatol 2017;67:727](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28532995))

Terminology

* Macrotrabecular massive hepatocellular carcinoma (MTM HCC)
* Macrotrabecular hepatocellular carcinoma (MT HCC)

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8170/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=67) - hepatocellular carcinoma
* ICD-11: [2C12 & H00048](https://www.genome.jp/kegg-bin/get_htext?br08403+H00048) - malignant neoplasms of liver or intrahepatic bile ducts & hepatocellular carcinoma

Epidemiology

* Relative frequency: 10 - 15% ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:943](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31135484), [Hepatology 2018;68:103](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29281854), [J Hepatocell Carcinoma 2022;9:661](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35923611))
* Male predominant (71 - 79%)

Sites

* Liver

Etiology

* HBV infection, HCV infection, alcohol intake, nonalcoholic steatohepatitis (NASH) or other / undetermined etiologies ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:943](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31135484), [Hepatology 2018;68:103](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29281854))

Diagnosis

* Based on light microscopic examination of tissue with compatible histomorphology findings (hepatocellular carcinoma with > 50% growth having a macrotrabecular pattern [≥ 6 - 10 cells thick])

Laboratory

* Serum AFP level (> 100 ng/mL) is elevated in 40 - 73% patients ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:943](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31135484), [Hepatology 2018;68:103](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29281854))

Prognostic factors

* Worse prognosis with larger tumor size, macrovascular invasion, microvascular invasion, poor differentiation; these are frequent findings ([Hepatology 2018;68:103](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29281854), [J Hepatol 2017;67:727](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28532995))
* High tumor stage compared to conventional hepatocellular carcinoma
* High early and overall recurrence; low recurrence free and overall survival ([Hepatology 2018;68:103](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29281854), [Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:943](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31135484), [J Hepatol 2017;67:727](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28532995))

Treatment

* Surgical resection
* Radiofrequency ablation
* Transarterial chemoembolization (TACE)
* Transplantation

Gross description

* Large tumor size (66% > 50 mm) with satellite nodules or macrovascular invasion ([Hepatology 2018;68:103](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29281854), [J Hepatol 2017;67:727](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28532995))
* Background liver with or without cirrhosis

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Hepatocellular carcinoma with > 50% growth having a macrotrabecular pattern (≥ 6 - 10 cells thick) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:943](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31135484), [Hepatology 2018;68:103](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29281854))
* Trabeculae observed in cross sections are bordered by a complete rim of CD34 positive endothelial cells in more than half of the tumor area (vessels encapsulating tumor clusters [VETC]) ([Hepatology 2020;71:183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31206715))
* Associated with micro and macrovascular invasion (> 60%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:943](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31135484), [J Hepatol 2017;67:727](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28532995))
* Associated with higher tumor cell grade and presence of anaplastic tumor cells ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:943](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31135484))

Macrotrabecular massive hepatocellular carcinoma

Positive stains

* Same as markers for conventional hepatocellular carcinoma (variably positive): hepatocytic differentiation markers ([HepPar1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsheppar1.html), [arginase1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsarginase1.html), polyclonal [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html) and [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) in canalicular pattern, [AFP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsafp.html) and [albumin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalbumin.html) mRNA ISH), [cytokeratin CAM 5.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscam52.html) and malignant markers ([glypican 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglypican3.html), [glutamine synthetase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/glutaminesynthetase.html) and [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html))
* **Endothelial specific molecule 1 (ESM1)** stains the sinusoidal stromal cells forming nearly a continuous positive surrounding of macrotrabeculae ([Clin Cancer Res 2019;25:5859](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31358545))

Negative stains

* [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [CK19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck19.html), [mucin (mucicarmine)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmucins.html), [MOC31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsepcam.html)
  and [BerEP4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsepcam.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Often harbor *TP53* mutations or *FGF19* amplifications ([J Hepatol 2017;67:727](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28532995))
* Positive for [albumin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalbumin.html) mRNA ISH

Sample pathology report

* Liver, segment 5, mass, biopsy:
  + Hepatocellular carcinoma, poorly differentiated, with macrotrabecular pattern (see comment)
  + Comment: Histologic sections show thickened cords that are composed of polygonal tumor cells with distinct cell membranes, eosinophilic cytoplasm, high N:C ratio, prominent nucleoli, conspicuous mitoses and necrosis. The thickened cords contain more than 6 - 10 cells thick tumor cells, consistent with macrotrabecular pattern. This pattern of hepatocellular carcinoma has been recently categorized as macrotrabecular massive subtype.
* Liver, right lobe, right hepatectomy:
  + Poorly differentiated hepatocellular carcinoma, macrotrabecular massive subtype (see comment and synoptic report)
  + Nonneoplastic background liver with chronic hepatitis B and portal fibrosis (grade 1 of 4, stage 1 of 4)
  + Comment: The current hepatocellular carcinoma shows mixed acinar, trabecular and macrotrabecular histologic patterns. The macrotrabecular pattern component accounts for 70% of the entire tumor. The tumor cells in the macrotrabeculae (≥ 6 - 10 cells thick) show marked nuclear pleomorphism with numerous mitoses and prominent nucleoli. Anaplastic giant tumor cells are present. Immunohistochemical stains show that the tumor cells are positive for arginase1, HepPar1 and focally positive for glypican 3. Peritumoral microvascular invasion is present. Also noted grossly is that there is a tumor thrombus in one of the portal vein branches; this is confirmed microscopically.

Differential diagnosis

* **Trabecular [hepatocellular carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorhcc.html)**:
  + Hepatocellular carcinoma with trabecular growth pattern of < 6 cells thick
