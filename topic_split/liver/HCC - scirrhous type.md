Definition / general

* Subtype of hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) designated by the WHO, with abundant fibrous stroma separating nests and trabecula of tumor cells

Essential features

* Unencapsulated, often subcapsular location, may have satellite lesions
* Abundant fibrous stroma (> 50% of tumor) but lacks central scar or radiating fibrous bands
* May mimic intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma on imaging and gross evaluation

Terminology

* Also called sclerosing variant of HCC

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [C22.7](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C15-C26/C22-/C22.7) - other specified carcinomas of liver

Epidemiology

* Incidence ranges from 0.2 - 4.6% of all HCC ([J Gastroenterol Hepatol 2006;21:1470](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16911695))

Sites

* Liver, often subcapsular

Pathophysiology

* Molecular alterations: *TSC1* / *TSC2* mutations ([J Hepatol 2017;67:727](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28532995))
* Associated with expression of transforming growth factor beta (TGFβ), epithelial - mesenchymal transition (EMT) and stem cell / progenitor related genes ([J Hepatol 2017;67:727](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28532995), [Hepatology 2012;55:1776](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22234953))

Etiology

* Similar etiologic factors to [classic HCC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorHCC.html)
* May arise in cirrhotic and noncirrhotic liver ([Dig Dis Sci 2012;57:1698](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22327241))

Clinical features

* No differences in patient demographics, presence of cirrhosis or serum alpha fetoprotein (AFP) levels, in comparison with [classic HCC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorHCC.html) ([Surg Pathol Clin 2013;6:367](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26838979))
* Association with hypercalcemia and hypophosphatemia has been described ([Liver 1981;1:33](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6294435))

Diagnosis

* Imaging modalities for diagnosis of HCC: multiphasic computed tomography (CT) or magnetic resonance imaging (MRI)
* Tissue biopsy is indicated if imaging not diagnostic of HCC

Laboratory

* Elevation in serum alpha fetoprotein (AFP)

Radiology description

* Radiographic appearance may be similar to [intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorcholangiocarcinoma.html)
* Contrast enhanced CT scan shows peripheral ring enhancement in arterial phase and delayed central enhancement in venous phase ([Eur J Radiol 2009;69:123](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17976942))

Prognostic factors

* Comparison of prognosis with [classic HCC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorHCC.html) shows conflicting results ([Surg Pathol Clin 2013;6:367](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26838979))
* Prognostic factors for HCC: stage (TNM), single lesion versus multifocal, size, vascular invasion, portal vein thrombosis, severity of underlying liver disease ([Liver Int 2009;29:502](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19141028), [J Surg Oncol 2018;117:644](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29127719))

Case reports

* 60 year old man with cirrhosis and a 1.5 cm liver nodule ([World J Gastroenterol 2009;15:2296](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19437576))
* 67 year old woman with epigastric pain ([Clin Gastroenterol Hepatol 2009;7:A28](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19008158))
* 68 year old man with hereditary hemochromatosis ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2017;34:126](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28143798))

Treatment

* Surgical resection
* Radiofrequency ablation
* Transarterial chemoembolization (TACE)
* Transplantation (e.g., Milan criteria, modified by some institutions) ([Nat Rev Gastroenterol Hepatol 2017;14:203](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28053342))

Gross description

* Unencapsulated, gray-white, firm, lobulated mass, often with serrated border
* Often subcapsular, may have satellite nodules
* More extensive fibrosis than [fibrolamellar variant](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorfibrolamellar.html); no radiating fibrous bands, no central scar
* Gross appearance may be similar to [intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorcholangiocarcinoma.html)
* Reference: [Surg Pathol Clin 2013;6:367](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26838979)

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Abundant fibrous stroma separating nests / trabeculae of tumor cells
* Criteria for amount of intratumoral fibrosis is > 50% ([Gastroenterol Clin North Am 2017;46:365](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28506370))
* Absence of fibrous capsule, no necrosis or hemorrhage, may contain intratumoral portal tracts and prominent tumor infiltrating lymphocytes ([Pathol Int 2005;55:724](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16271085), [Hepatogastroenterology 2009;56:1086](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19760947))
* Clear cell change, steatotic change and hyaline bodies may be present ([J Gastroenterol Hepatol 2006;21:1470](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16911695), [Hum Pathol 2019;86:222](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30597153))
* Less frequently poorly differentiated and more likely to have steatosis than nonscirrhous HCC ([Hum Pathol 2019;86:222](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30597153))

Cytology description

* Cells are polygonal with distinct cell membranes, high N:C ratio and round nuclei and may have prominent nucleoli (similar to [moderately differentiated HCC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorhcc.html))

Positive stains

* [Glypican 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglypican3.html) and [arginase1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsarginase1.html) in 90% of cases ([Mod Pathol 2013;26:782](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23348905))
* [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [CK19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck19.html) and [EpCAM](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsepcam.html) in majority of cases ([Histopathology 2005;47:382](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16178893))

Negative stains

* [HepPar1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsheppar1.html) and polyclonal [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html) in > 50% ([Surg Pathol Clin 2013;6:367](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26838979))
* [Mucin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmucins.html) (e.g., [mucicarmine](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmucins.html))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Associated with *TSC1* / *TSC2* mutations ([J Hepatol 2017;67:727](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28532995))

Sample pathology report

* Liver, mass, partial hepatectomy:
  + Scirrhous hepatocellular carcinoma (see comment)
  + Comment: This tumor shows histologic features of scirrhous hepatocellular carcinoma with nests and trabecula of hepatoid cells within a dense fibrous stroma. The tumor cells show positive staining for glypican 3, arginase1 and CK7, as is typical for this variant.

Differential diagnosis

* [Fibrolamellar variant](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorfibrolamellar.html):
  + Triad of histologic features: lamellar fibrous stroma, abundant eosinophilic granular cytoplasm and vesicular nuclei with prominent nucleolus
  + Usually in young adults with no [cirrhosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livercirrhosis.html) or underlying liver disease
  + [HepPar1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsheppar1.html)+, polyclonal [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html)+, [arginase1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsarginase1.html)+, [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html)+, [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html)+; may have focal mucin
* [Intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorcholangiocarcinoma.html):
  + May form glands / tubules with cytoplasmic or intraluminal mucin
  + [Arginase1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsarginase1.html)-, [HepPar1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsheppar1.html)-, [pCEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html)-, [albumin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalbumin.html) ISH+, [BAP1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbap1.html)- (subset)
* [Metastatic carcinoma of pancreas](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pancreasductal.html):
  + May form glands / tubules with cytoplasmic or intraluminal mucin
  + [Glypican 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglypican3.html)-, [arginase1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsarginase1.html)-, [albumin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalbumin.html) ISH-
* [Epithelioid hemangioendothelioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorEHE.html):
  + Myxoid stroma, blister cells with red blood cells in lumens or vacuoles
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)+, [ERG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainserg.html)+, [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html)+, [FLI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfli1.html)+, [mucicarmine](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmucins.html)-, variable [pankeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [CAMTA1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscamta1.html)+, [TFE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstfe3.html)+ (subset) ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2009;133:967](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19492891))
* [Hepatocellular carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorHCC.html) with therapy induced fibrosis:
  + May be difficult or impossible to distinguish from scirrhous HCC
  + Check treatment history; look for other evidence of injury (e.g., necrosis, embolization beads)
