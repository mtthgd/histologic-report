Definition / general

* Hepatic failure is acute and severe hepatic abnormalities with a high short term mortality ([Gut 2017;66:541](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28053053))
* Acute hepatic failure is hepatic failure in patients without pre-existing chronic liver diseases
* Acute on chronic hepatic failure is hepatic failure in patients with pre-existing chronic liver diseases

Essential features

* Hepatic failure is acute and severe hepatic abnormalities with a high short term mortality ([Gut 2017;66:541](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28053053))
* Hepatic failure is a clinical, not pathological, diagnosis with varied definitions according to different professional organizations
* Depending on whether the patients have preexisting chronic liver diseases, hepatic failure is stratified into acute hepatic failure and acute on chronic hepatic failure

Terminology

* Fulminant hepatic failure may also refer to fulminant hepatitis or massive hepatic necrosis

ICD coding

* ICD-10
  + [K72](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/K00-K95/K70-K77/K72-/K72) - hepatic failure, not elsewhere classified
  + [K70.4](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/K00-K95/K70-K77/K70-/K70.4) - alcoholic hepatic failure
  + [K71.1](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/K00-K95/K70-K77/K71-/K71.1) - toxic liver disease with hepatic necrosis
  + [K91.82](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/K00-K95/K90-K95/K91-/K91.82) - postprocedural hepatic failure
  + [B15 - B19](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/A00-B99/B15-B19) - viral hepatitis

Epidemiology

* Acute hepatic failure: 2,000 cases per year in the U.S. ([Hepatology 2012;55:965](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22213561))
* Acute on chronic hepatic failure: 24 - 40% of hospitalized patients with cirrhosis ([Gut 2017;66:541](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28053053))

Etiology

* Acute hepatic failure ([J Hepatol 2017;66:1047](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28417882))
  + [Acetaminophen](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liverdrugtoxinacetaminophen.html) (up to 43%)
  + Nonacetaminophen drug induced liver injury (up to 32%)
  + [Acute hepatotropic virus hepatitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liverhepatitisgeneral.html) (up to 68%)
  + Others (up to 27%)
  + Unknown (up to 27%)
* Acute on chronic hepatic failure ([Gut 2017;66:541](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28053053))
  + [Chronic viral hepatitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liverchronicviralhep.html) (12 - 81%)
  + [Alcoholic liver disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liveralcoholichep.html) (7 - 75%)
  + Others (3 - 22%)
  + Unknown (4 - 20%)

Clinical features

* Jaundice
* Coagulopathy
* Hepatic encephalopathy
* In patients with acute on chronic hepatic failure, clinical features of [cirrhosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livercirrhosis.html) may also be present
* Reference: [Hepatology 2012;55:965](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22213561)

Diagnosis

* Hepatic failure is a clinical, not a pathological, diagnosis with varied definitions according to different professional organizations
* Acute hepatic failure
  + North America (American Association for the Study of Liver Diseases [AASLD]): coagulopathy (international normalized ratio [INR] ≥ 1.5) and hepatic encephalopathy without pre-existing cirrhosis and with an illness of Hepatology 2012;55:965)
  + Europe (European Association for the Study of the Liver [EASL]): liver damage (elevated serum transaminases) and impaired liver function (jaundice and INR > 1.5) with an onset of encephalopathy within 24 weeks in the absence of chronic liver disease ([J Hepatol 2017;66:1047](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28417882))
* Fulminant hepatic failure
  + Severe and rapid onset (within 8 weeks) form of acute hepatic failure
* Acute on chronic hepatic failure
  + North America (AASLD): liver failure (jaundice [elevated bilirubin] and coagulopathy [elevated INR / prothrombin time (PT)]) and ≥ 1 of the 4 major extrahepatic organ failures (kidney, brain, circulation and respiration) in patients with chronic liver disease with or without cirrhosis ([Hepatology 2024;79:1463](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37939273))
  + North America (North American Consortium for the Study of End Stage Liver Disease [NACSELD]): ≥ 2 of the 4 major extrahepatic organ failures (kidney, brain, circulation and respiration) in patients with acutely decompensated cirrhosis ([Hepatology 2018;67:2367](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29315693))
  + Europe (European Association for the Study of the Liver - Chronic Liver Failure [EASL CLIF]): ≥ 1 of the 6 major organ failures (liver, coagulation, kidney, brain, circulation and respiration) in patients with acutely decompensated cirrhosis ([Gastroenterology 2013;144:1426](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23474284))
  + Asia Pacific (Asian Pacific Association for the Study of the Liver [APASL]): jaundice (serum bilirubin level of ≥ 5 mg/dL) and coagulopathy (INR ≥ 1.5 or prothrombin activity of > 40%), which are complicated within 4 weeks by clinical ascites or encephalopathy in patients with chronic liver disease with or without cirrhosis ([Hepatol Int 2019;13:353](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31172417))

Laboratory

* Deranged liver function: elevated bilirubin and transaminase
* Coagulopathy: elevated INR
* Deranged renal function (in the presence of renal failure): elevated creatinine
* Liver biopsy plays no role in establishing the diagnosis but may be helpful in looking for the underlying etiology
* References: [Hepatology 2012;55:965](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22213561), [Hepatology 2024;79:1463](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37939273)

Prognostic factors

* Acute on chronic liver failure (ACLF) grade for the short term mortality prediction of patients with acute on chronic liver failure ([Gastroenterology 2013;144:1426](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23474284))
* Chronic Liver Failure Consortium (CLIF-C) ACLF score for the short and intermediate term mortality prediction of patients with acute on chronic liver failure ([J Hepatol 2014;60:275](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24128414))
  + Age
  + White cell count
  + CLIF SOFA (Sequential Organ Failure Assessment): bilirubin, INR, creatinine, mean arterial pressure, PaO2 / SpO2, FiO2, hepatic encephalopathy grade
  + Has better predictive accuracy than the Model for End Stage Liver Disease (MELD), MELD-Na (sodium) and Child-Pugh grade

Case reports

* 15 year old boy with autoimmune hepatitis / primary sclerosing cholangitis overlapping syndrome who developed acute on chronic liver failure ([Clin Exp Hepatol 2017;3:28](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28856287))
* 51 year old woman with acute hepatic failure due to massive sinusoidal infiltration of the liver by breast cancer cells ([BMC Gastroenterol 2017;17:166](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29262779))
* 59 and 63 year old men from the same family with acute hepatic failure due to acute hepatitis A ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2017;96:e7847](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28858094))
* 60 year old man with acute hepatic failure due to pembrolizumab for metastatic liver cancer ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2017;96:e9431](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29390572))
* 62 year old man with COVID-19 triggered acute hepatic failure and rhabdomyosis ([Viruses 2023;15:1445](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37515132))
* 63 year old woman with acute on chronic hepatic failure due to sorafenib for hepatitis B related hepatocellular carcinoma ([Mol Clin Oncol 2017;7:693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28856003))
* 64 year old man with acute liver failure following immune checkpoint inhibitors for his laryngeal squamous cell carcinoma ([Clin Res Hepatol Gastroenterol 2023;47:102203](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37660741))

Treatment

* Specific treatment if the exact etiology is known
* Supportive and preventive care and management of complications (coagulopathy, renal failure, encephalopathy, sepsis, metabolic disorders)
* Liver support (extracorporeal liver assist device) and liver transplant
* Mortality without liver transplant is 25 - 90% ([Semin Liver Dis 2008;28:175](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18452117))

Gross description

* Acute hepatic failure: massive hepatic necrosis is featured by a shrunken liver with a wrinkled liver capsule and congested, variably greenish (if markedly cholestatic), necrotic cut surface
* Acute on hepatic failure: cirrhosis (multiple regenerative nodules throughout the entire liver, separated by bridging fibrosis)

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Zonal or panacinar hepatic necrosis with collapse of reticulin framework
* Variable inflammatory infiltrate, depending on etiology
* Variable ductular reaction
* Fibrosis and cirrhosis in patients with acute on chronic hepatic failure
* Reference: [Adv Anat Pathol 2016;23:144](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27058243)

Sample pathology report

* Liver, recipient explant:
  + Acute hepatocellular necrosis (see comment)
  + Comment: Extensive panacinar hepatocellular necrosis is accompanied by minimal inflammatory infiltrate and ductular necrosis. No significant fibrosis is present in the residual liver parenchyma. Provided by known drug history of acetaminophen overdose, the overall features are consistent with acetaminophen overdose associated acute hepatic failure.

Differential diagnosis

* [Severe acute hepatitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liveracutehepgeneral.html):
  + Does not fulfill the clinical diagnostic criteria of acute hepatic failure (e.g., absence of coagulopathy and hepatic encephalopathy)
  + Requires clinical correlation and investigations
* **Chronic liver disease with extrahepatic organ failure**:
  + There is no clinical feature of acute hepatic decompensation
  + Requires clinical correlation and investigations
* **Chronic liver disease with nonhepatic encephalopathy / encephalitis** (e.g., [drug / toxin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liverdrugtoxinacetaminophen.html), **uremic encephalopathy**):
  + There is no clinical feature of acute hepatic decompensation apart from altered mental status
  + Requires clinical correlation and investigations

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/liverhepaticfailureChan05.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/liverhepaticfailureChan05.jpg)

Which pathological features should be present in the liver biopsy from a patient with acute on chronic hepatic failure?

1. Ductular reaction
2. Fibrosis or cirrhosis
3. Inflammatory infiltrate
4. Necrosis of hepatocytes

Board review style answer #1

**B**. Severe fibrosis or cirrhosis. Acute on chronic hepatic failure occurs in patients with underlying chronic liver disease or cirrhosis; hence, the chronicity feature (fibrosis or cirrhosis) should be present. Answers A, C and D are incorrect because degrees of necroinflammatory activity (necrosis of hepatocytes and inflammatory infiltrate) and regenerative activity (ductular reaction) vary and depend on the underlying etiology and precipitating factors.

Reference: [Hepatic failure](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liverhepaticfailure.html)

Board review style question #2

Which of the following features fulfills part of the diagnostic criteria for acute on chronic hepatic failure?

1. Elevated activated partial thromboplastin time (aPTT)
2. Elevated alanine transaminase (ALT)
3. Hypoalbuminemia
4. Shock

Board review style answer #2

**D**. Shock. Circulatory failure is one of the extrahepatic organ failures associated with acute on chronic hepatic failure. Liver failure (jaundice [elevated bilirubin] and coagulopathy [elevated international normalized ratio (INR) / prothrombin time (PT)]) is one of defining features of acute on chronic hepatic failure. Answers A - C are incorrect because elevated aPTT, elevated ALT and hypoalbuminemia are not included in the diagnostic criteria of acute on chronic hepatic failure.
