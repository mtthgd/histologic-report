Definition / general

* Papillary neoplasm consisting of fibrovascular stalks within cystically dilated intrahepatic bile ducts

Essential features

* Papillary neoplasm within dilated intrahepatic bile duct(s)
* Lacks ovarian stroma
* High rate of associated invasive carcinoma (up to 74%) ([Hepatology 2012;56:1352](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22504729))
* WHO classification
  + Premalignant: intraductal papillary neoplasm with low grade, intermediate grade or high grade intraepithelial neoplasia
  + Malignant: intraductal papillary neoplasm with associated invasive carcinoma

Terminology

* Biliary papillomatosis
* Biliary papilloma

ICD coding

* ICD-O:
  + [8503/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf#page=75) - intraductal papillary neoplasm with low grade intraepithelial neoplasia
  + [8503/2](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf#page=75) - intraductal papillary neoplasm with high grade intraepithelial neoplasia
  + [8503/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf#page=75) - intraductal papillary neoplasm with associated invasive carcinoma
* ICD-11: [2E92.7](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f2124270688) - benign neoplasm of liver or intrahepatic bile ducts

Epidemiology

* M > F; age: 50 - 70 years ([Proc Bayl Univ Med Cent 2019;32:124](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30956606))
* Most common in Far East (Taiwan, Japan, Korea) ([World J Gastroenterol 2013;19:8595](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24379576))

Sites

* Intrahepatic bile ducts
* Similar lesions can involve extrahepatic bile ducts, pancreas or gallbladder
* Often multifocal

Pathophysiology

* Stepwise progression from low grade dysplasia to high grade dysplasia to invasive carcinoma involving inactivation of *TP53* and *p16* (early), activating mutations in *KRAS* (early) and loss of *SMAD4 / DPC4* (late) ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:73](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23828315))

Etiology

* May be related to hepatolithiasis and clonorchiasis (liver fluke infection) ([Proc Bayl Univ Med Cent 2019;32:124](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30956606))

Clinical features

* Symptoms resulting from biliary obstruction: jaundice, abdominal pain

Diagnosis

* Imaging (CT, MRI, ultrasound)
* Surgical resection: extensive histologic sampling required to exclude invasive component

Laboratory

* May have elevated bilirubin and alkaline phosphatase due to biliary obstruction

Radiology description

* Bile duct dilatation and intraductal masses can be seen on ultrasound, CT or MRI ([Proc Bayl Univ Med Cent 2019;32:124](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30956606))

Prognostic factors

* Presence of an invasive component is associated with shorter survival, related to depth and percentage of invasion ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:73](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23828315), [World J Gastroenterol 2015;21:12498](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26604656), [Hepatology 2012;56:1352](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22504729))
* Multiple / widespread lesions are difficult to treat and have a poorer prognosis

Case reports

* 38 year old woman with 1 week of acholic stools, jaundice, pruritus and dark colored urine ([World J Gastroenterol 2015;21:12498](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26604656))
* 58 year old man with mild prominence of the left hepatic biliary duct on MRI ([Proc Bayl Univ Med Cent 2019;32:124](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30956606))
* 59 year old woman with multiple episodes of abdominal pain and fever ([Endosc Ultrasound 2016;5:274](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27503163))
* 61 year old Asian man presented with severe right upper quadrant pain ([BMJ Case Rep 2017;2017:bcr2016218139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28438794))
* 72 year old previously healthy man with right upper abdominal pain ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2018;51:102](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30149325))

Treatment

* Surgical resection is first line treatment
* Palliative approaches include chemotherapy and laser ablation

Gross description

* Soft, friable, villiform mass(es)
* May be pedunculated, sessile or polypoid

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Papillary neoplasm with complex tubulovillous or micropapillary architecture
* Grows within dilated bile ducts
* Lacks ovarian stroma
* May form large, multilocular cysts with thin fibrous septations
* Epithelium may be pancreaticobiliary (most common in European and North American patients), intestinal (more common in Asian patients), gastric (more common in Asian patients) or oncocytic
* Can be classified into type 1 (pancreatic type) and type 2 (nonpancreatic type) intraductal papillary neoplasms of the bile ducts (IPNBs) ([Histol Histopathol 2017;32:1001](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28337739), [J Hepatobiliary Pancreat Sci 2018;25:181](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29272078))
  + Type 1 is similar to intraductal papillary mucinous neoplasms (IPMNs) of the pancreas
  + Type 1 is more frequently intrahepatic or hilar, while type 2 is more frequently extrahepatic
  + Type 2 is more aggressive, with more frequent associated invasive carcinoma
* Reference: [Am J Gastroenterol 2012;107:118](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21946282)

Cytology description

* Hypercellular, with broad and double layered sheets of columnar epithelial cells, fine stroma and complex, 3 dimensional branching papillary configurations

Positive stains

* Various [mucins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmucins.html), depending on differentiation ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2017;34:167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28109714))
  + [MUC5AC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmuc2.html#muc5ac) and [MUC6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmuc2.html#muc6): gastric type epithelium
  + [MUC2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmuc2.html): intestinal type epithelium
  + [MUC1 (EMA)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html): pancreatobiliary type epithelium

Negative stains

* [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) / [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html) staining is negative in stroma cells (unlike mucinous cystic neoplasm)
* [SMAD4 / DPC4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdpc4.html) staining may be lost in invasive component

Sample pathology report

* Liver, left lobe, lobectomy:
  + Intraductal papillary neoplasm with low and high grade dysplasia, involving left hepatic duct
  + No invasive carcinoma

Differential diagnosis

* [Mucinous cystic neoplasm](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorMCN.html):
  + Also is a cystic hepatic lesion that may be lined by biliary type epithelium
  + Has ovarian type stroma and does not communicate with biliary lumen
* [Cholangiocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorcholangiocarcinoma.html):
  + May show areas of cystic degeneration
  + Does not communicate with biliary lumen ([Pathol Int 2005;55:206](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15826247))
* [Metastatic carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumormet.html), especially [metastatic colorectal adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumoradenocarcinoma.html):
  + May show areas of cystic degeneration
  + Does not communicate with biliary lumen
* [Biliary intraepithelial neoplasia (BilIN)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/gallbladderBilIN.html):
  + Also is located within lumen of large intrahepatic bile ducts
  + Typically is < 3 mm in height (not mass forming)
* [Simple cyst](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liversolitarycyst.html), **simple hepatic cyst**, [simple biliary cyst](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorbiliarycyst.html), **bile duct cyst**:
  + Lacks papillary projections
* [Intraductal tubulopapillary neoplasm](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pancreasitpn.html) of the bile duct ([Mod Pathol 2015;28:1249](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26111977)):
  + Predominantly comprised of densely packed, nonmucinous tubules
  + < 30% papillary growth pattern

Board review style question #1

[![Missing Image](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/livertumorintraductalpapDong01br.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/livertumorintraductalpapDong01br.jpg)

A 72 year old man presented with jaundice. CT scan showed dilatation of the left bile duct, which contained a papillary lesion. An image from the resection specimen is shown. Which of the following is true about this entity?

1. It is almost always unifocal
2. It lacks ovarian type stroma
3. The epithelium is most commonly oncocytic
4. There is a strong male predominance
5. There is a very low risk of associated invasive carcinoma

Board review style answer #1

**B.** It lacks type ovarian stroma. This is an intraductal papillary neoplasm, which has a relatively high risk of associated invasive carcinoma (up to 74%). Sex preponderance varies by geographic region but the lesion is more common in men than women in Western countries. The epithelium is most commonly pancreatobiliary in European and North American patients. Intestinal and gastric epithelium are common in Asian patients. Oncocytic epithelium is relatively uncommon. The differential diagnosis of cystic hepatic lesions includes mucinous cystic neoplasms, which do have ovarian type stroma.

Board review style question #2

Intraductal papillary neoplasms of the bile ducts in North American and European patients most often have which type of epithelium?

1. Gastric
2. Intestinal
3. Oncocytic
4. Ovarian
5. Pancreaticobiliary

Board review style answer #2

**E.** Pancreaticobiliary. Intraductal papillary neoplasms of the bile ducts are most commonly lined by pancreatobiliary epithelium in North American and European patients. Intestinal and gastric epithelium are common in Asian patients. Oncocytic epithelium is relatively uncommon. Ovarian type stroma is associated with mucinous cystic neoplasms, a different type of cystic hepatic lesion.
