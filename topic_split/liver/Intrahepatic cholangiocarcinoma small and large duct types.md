Definition / general

* Intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma (iCCA) is a malignant intrahepatic epithelial neoplasm with biliary differentiation, arising in the liver periphery / proximal to the left and right hepatic ducts (WHO 2019)
* iCCA classification systems ([Surg Pathol Clin 2018;11:403](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29751883), [Nat Rev Gastroenterol Hepatol 2020;17:557](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32606456)):
  + Histologic features
    - Small duct type versus large duct type (2 types have morphologic and molecular but not prognostic differences) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:1334](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30001234))
  + Macroscopic growth pattern
    - Mass forming
    - Periductal infiltrating
    - Intraductal
    - Mixed growth patterns
  + Cell of origin
    - Stem cells residing in the canals of Hering
    - Stem cells within peribiliary glands

Essential features

* Nonencapsulated, white-tan and firm intrahepatic mass
* Glandular malignancy with various degrees of atypia and differentiation, infiltrating a dense fibrous stroma
* Exclusion of metastatic adenocarcinoma by review of the patient's clinical history and application of an appropriate immunohistochemistry panel
* Large duct type iCCA: often hilar masses, obstructive cholestasis; share risk factors with extrahepatic bile duct adenocarcinomas (primary sclerosing cholangitis [PSC], liver fluke infection)
* Small duct type iCCA: peripheral, larger liver masses; shares risk factors with hepatocellular carcinomas (viral hepatitis, nonbiliary cirrhosis)

Terminology

* Also known as peripheral cholangiocarcinoma and intrahepatic bile duct carcinoma

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [C22.1](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C15-C26/C22-/C22.1) - intrahepatic bile duct carcinoma

Epidemiology

* In the United States, the average annual incidence is 1.6 per 100,000/year ([Cancer Control 2017;24:1073274817729245](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28975830))
* 15% of primary liver malignancies worldwide
* Second most common malignancy arising from the liver
* 3% of all cases of gastrointestinal cancer
* ~10% of all bile duct carcinomas
* More common in East Asia, due to endemic liver fluke infection
* Most patients are aged between 55 and 75 years ([Cancer Lett 2016;379:198](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26409434))
* M > F ([Cancer Lett 2016;379:198](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26409434))

Pathophysiology

* Chronic inflammation of the intrahepatic bile ducts with sustained IL6-STAT3 signaling ([Nat Rev Gastroenterol Hepatol 2020;17:557](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32606456))
* *IDH*, *EPHA2* and *BAP1* mutations and *FGFR2* fusions in iCCA; in contrast to *PRKACA* and *PRKACB* fusions, *ELF3* and *ARID1B* mutations in extrahepatic tumors ([Nat Genet 2015;47:1003](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26258846))
* *TP53* and *SMAD4* mutations
* CpG island hypermethylation are more frequent in liver fluke associated cholangiocarcinoma

Etiology

* Common underlying risk factor: chronic inflammation of the biliary epithelium and bile stasis ([Liver Int 2019;39:19](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30851228))
* Small duct type iCCA:
  + Nonbiliary [cirrhosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livercirrhosis.html): independent risk factor ([Cancer Control 2017;24:1073274817729245](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28975830))
  + Alcoholic steatohepatitis / nonalcoholic steatohepatitis (NASH)
  + [Hepatitis B](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liverhepB.html) / [hepatitis C](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liverhepC.html) ([Cancer Control 2017;24:1073274817729245](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28975830))
* Large duct type iCCA:
  + [Primary sclerosing cholangitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liverPSC.html): 5 - 20% lifetime risk of developing cholangiocarcinoma ([Cancer Control 2017;24:1073274817729245](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28975830))
  + Hepatobiliary parasites: *Opisthorchis viverrini*, *Clonorchis sinensis*
  + Hepatolithiasis: more common in Southeast Asia and associated with an increased risk of 6 - 50 times
* [Caroli disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livercarolisdisease.html): a congenital disorder characterized by multifocal, segmental dilatation of large intrahepatic bile ducts
* [Choledochal cysts](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livercholedochalcyst.html) (types I and IV)
* Multiple bile duct hamartomas ([von Meyenburg complexes](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livermeyenburgcomplex.html)) ([Eur J Gastroenterol Hepatol 2009;21:580](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19282767))
* Thorotrast: a colloidal suspension of thorium dioxide that was used as an intravascular contrast agent until the 1950s, with an increased risk of 300 times ([Jarnagin: Blumgart's Surgery of the Liver, Biliary Tract and Pancreas, Volume 1, 6th Edition, 2016](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0323340628/pathologyoutl-20))

Clinical features

* Asymptomatic in early stages ([Nat Rev Clin Oncol 2018;15:95](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28994423))
* Nonspecific symptoms in late stages, such as abdominal pain, malaise, fever, night sweats and weight loss / cachexia
* Jaundice / biliary obstruction are uncommon in iCCA, compared with extrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma

Diagnosis

* Clinicopathologic diagnosis by correlation of radiological, laboratory and surgical pathology findings
* Broad investigation of hepatic pathology and other malignancies should be conducted
  + Colon cancer risk / screening history
  + Alcohol consumption history
  + Travel history (liver flukes)
  + Viral hepatitis risk and serologies
  + Autoimmune hepatitis serologies
  + Iron studies (hemochromatosis), copper studies (Wilson disease)
  + BMI (NASH)
  + Exposure to hepatotoxins (thorotrast, aflatoxin)
* Labs
  + CA 19-9, CEA, AFP
  + In patients with PSC with or without ulcerative colitis, development of CCA can be predicted with CA 19-9 (a proposed cutoff level of 130 U/mL carries a sensitivity and specificity of 78.6% and 98.5% respectively
* Reference: [Surg Oncol Clin N Am 2019;28:587](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31472907), [Surg Pathol Clin 2018;11:403](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29751883)

Laboratory

* Elevated serum CA 19-9
* Elevated serum CA 125
* Variably elevated serum alkaline phosphatase and bilirubin
* Reference: [J Coll Physicians Surg Pak 2019;29:962](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31564271)

Radiology description

* Usually single, large, homogeneous mass with irregular margins on CT scan
* No pathognomonic CT / MRI features: may show progressive delayed enhancement, peripheral rim enhancement, subcapsular retraction
* Reference: [Radiology 2018;288:7](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29869969)

Prognostic factors

* Serum CEA, CA 19-9, tumor diameter and number, lymph node metastasis, vascular invasion and direct invasion and local extrahepatic metastasis are important prognostic factors ([J Clin Oncol 2013;31:1188](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23358969))
* Periductal infiltration and AJCC 8th edition pT stage ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:1334](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30001234))

Case reports

* 38 year old pregnant woman with 1 week history of headache, nausea, vomiting and right upper abdominal pain ([Case Reports Hepatol 2018;2018:6939747](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29682369))
* 45 year old Thai man with 3 month history of right upper abdominal pain ([Case Rep Surg 2018;2018:3862575](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29619266))
* 58 year old woman with abdominal pain and jaundice ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2018;97:e11015](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29923988))
* 67 year old woman with chest pain and a lung nodule ([Am J Case Rep 2018;19:35](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29317591))
* 70 year old man with mass forming tumor with portal vein and bile duct thrombus ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2017;40:13](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28917216))

Treatment

* Surgical resection of the tumor is the potential curative therapy
* Frequently inoperable, often recur after surgery
* First line treatment for locally advanced or metastatic disease is gemcitabine / cisplatin
* Molecular testing recommended by National Comprehensive Cancer Network (NCCN) for unresectable and metastatic tumors (> 50% of NGS tested subjects have potentially actionable mutations)
* Ongoing clinical trials of targeted therapy: immune checkpoint inhibitors (anti-PD1, anti-PDL1, anti-CTLA4 antibodies), IDH1 inhibitors, AKT / PI3K / mTOR inhibitors, FGFR inhibitors and anti-EGFR and MEK inhibitors
* Reference: [Surg Oncol Clin N Am 2019;28:587](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31472907)

Gross description

* Usually large, nonencapsulated, well demarcated, firm (due to desmoplastic reaction), white-tan to gray and nodular intrahepatic mass
* More frequent in the right lobe of the liver
* Satellite nodules are present in 30%
* Calcification is common
* Noncirrhotic background liver in most cases
* Grossly classified into 3 growth patterns ([J Gastroenterol 2013;48:647](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23001251)):
  1. Mass forming: hepatic parenchymal solid mass
  2. Periductal infiltrating: infiltrates along the portal tracts, causing bile duct strictures
  3. Intraductal growth: papillary or polypoid growth inside a dilated bile duct

Microscopic (histologic) description

* iCCA consists of infiltrating well formed or cribriform glands in an abundant fibrous stroma ([Liver Int 2019;39:7](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30882996), [Surg Pathol Clin 2018;11:403](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29751883))
* Malignant glands are lined by cells with varying degrees of atypia and pleomorphism ([Liver Int 2019;39:7](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30882996))
* Usually well differentiated adenocarcinoma with mild atypia, intracytoplasmic lumina and intraluminal cellular debris; however, focal atypia with marked pleomorphism can also be present ([Liver Int 2019;39:7](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30882996))
* Commonly infiltrates between hepatic parenchymal cords at the periphery of the tumor
* Multicentricity and perineural invasion are common
* Histologic variants include mucinous, signet ring cell, clear cell, [lymphoepithelioma-like](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorlelhcc.html), thyroid follicular-like, adenosquamous and sarcomatoid
* Small duct type iCCA ([Liver Int 2019;39:7](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30882996))
  + Peripheral hepatic parenchyma
  + Mass forming
  + Cuboidal cells forming small tubular or anastomosing glands
  + Variable lymphovascular / perineural invasion
  + CD56, N-cadherin, CRP positive
  + Subtypes include cholangiolocarcinoma, iCCA with ductal plate malformation-like pattern
* Large duct type iCCA ([Liver Int 2019;39:7](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30882996))
  + Proximal to liver hilum
  + Periductal infiltrating or mass forming
  + Tall columnar cells that form large glands within open lumina; usually contain mucin
  + BilIN, IPNB, ITPN are precursors
  + Positive lymphovascular / perineural invasion
  + MUC5AC, MUC6, S100P and TFF1 positive

Cytology description

* Isolated clusters and sheets of cuboidal or columnar cells with various degrees of nuclear enlargement and pleomorphism
* Wide range of glandular differentiation on cell block; > 10 proliferating ductules on FNA is helpful to differentiate cholangiocarcinoma from metastatic adenocarcinomas ([Diagn Cytopathol 2000;22:359](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10820529))

Positive stains

* [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html) (~90%), [CK19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck19.html) (80 - 90%), [CK17](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck17.html), [CAM 5.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscam52.html), [MOC31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsepcam.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html), monoclonal [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html), polyclonal [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html) (diffuse cytoplasmic pattern), [claudin4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsclaudins.html), [pVHL](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvhl.html) ([Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2018;61:2](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29567877))
* [Albumin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalbumin.html) RNA ISH (~80%) but does not differentiate iCCA from hepatocellular carcinoma ([Ann Surg Oncol 2015;22:S1609](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25786746), [Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:1334](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30001234))
* [Glypican 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglypican3.html) and [HepPar1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsheppar1.html) occasionally positive in peripherally located iCCA

Negative stains

* [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html), [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html), [napsin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnapsina.html), [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) ([Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2018;61:2](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29567877), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2003;127:1591](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14632573))
* [Progesterone receptor (PR)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html) can be positive in cholangiocarcinoma (31%) ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2003;127:1591](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14632573))
* [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgata3.html) rarely positive (3%), seen in poorly differentiated iCCA ([Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2020;28:460](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31033498))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Mutations in *KRAS* (22%), *BRAF* (7%), *EGFR* (2%), *IDH1 / IDH2* (14%), *MET*, activation of mTOR, overexpression of cyclin D1, overexpression of p21, inactivating mutations of *DPC4 / SMAD4* (13 - 15%) and *TP53* (15%) ([BMC Cancer 2019;19:185](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30819129))
* Chromosomal gains of 1q, 5p, 7p, 8q, 17q and 20q and losses of 1p, 4q, 8p, 9p, 17p and 18q ([BMC Cancer 2019;19:185](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30819129))

Sample pathology report

* Right lobe of liver, partial hepatectomy:
  + Moderately differentiated adenocarcinoma, 6.5 cm, consistent with intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma, small duct type
  + Resection margins are negative for carcinoma
  + Lymphovascular invasion present
  + Metastatic adenocarcinoma involving 1 of 3 lymph nodes
  + Pathologic TNM stage (AJCC 8th edition): pT2N1
  + Background liver with mild steatosis and steatohepatitis; no advanced fibrosis

Differential diagnosis

* [Metastatic adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumormet.html):
  + Immunohistochemistry and clinical history are helpful to rule out metastasis
* [Hepatocellular carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorHCC.html):
  + Positive for hepatocyte markers ([HepPar1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsheppar1.html), [arginase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsarginase1.html), [glypican 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglypican3.html)), [pCEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html) and [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) with canalicular staining pattern
  + Usually negative for [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html)
* [Combined hepatocellular cholangiocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorcombinedHCCcholangio.html):
  + Histologic evidence of both hepatocellular and biliary differentiation by H&E morphology and supported by immunohistochemistry (WHO 2019)
* [Benign bile ductular reactions](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liverductularreaction.html):
  + Uniform growth pattern with lack of cytologic and architectural atypia and perineural invasion
* [Epithelioid hemangioendothelioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorEHE.html):
  + Epithelioid cells with intracytoplasmic vacuoles, vasoformation, myxohyaline stroma, necrosis
  + Positive for endothelial markers ([CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [ERG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsERG.html)), positive for [CAMTA1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscamta1.html)
  + Negative for [AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [villin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvillin.html) and [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html)
* [Bile duct adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorbileductadenoma.html):
  + Small and well circumscribed, no or minimal cytologic atypia, lack of lymphovascular or perineural invasion
