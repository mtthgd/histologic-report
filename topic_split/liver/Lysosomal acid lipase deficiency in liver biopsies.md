Definition / general

* Autosomal recessive lysosomal storage disease resulting from underlying *LIPA* gene mutation ([Mol Genet Metab 2012;105:450](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22227072))

Essential features

* Lysosomal storage disease encompassing a spectrum of symptoms and severity
  + Wolman disease in infancy, J Pediatr Gastroenterol Nutr 2012;54:700)
  + Cholesteryl ester storage disease (CESD) in children and adults, 1 - 12% LAL enzyme activity, leads to fibrosis and eventually micronodular cirrhosis ([J Pediatr Gastroenterol Nutr 2012;54:700](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22395188))

Terminology

* Wolman disease in infants
* Cholesteryl ester storage disease (CESD) in children and adults

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [E75.5](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/E00-E89/E70-E88/E75-/E75.5) - other lipid storage disorders

Epidemiology

* Prevalence is estimated to be between 1:40,000 to 1:300,000 ([Arterioscler Thromb Vasc Biol 2007;27:1866](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17634524), [J Hepatol 2013;58:1230](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23485521))
* Infantile onset lysosomal acid lipase deficiency (LALD) is generally fatal within the first 6 - 12 months of life

Sites

* Liver, spleen, heart, digestive tract ([Atherosclerosis 2014;235:21](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24792990))

Pathophysiology

* LAL is the sole enzyme known to degrade neutral lipids in the lysosome
* Deficiency of LAL results in diminished or absent hydrolysis of cholesteryl esters and triglycerides, trapping cholesterol esters and triglycerides (TG) within the lysosome
* Encoded by the *LIPA* gene located at 10q23.31 ([Genomics 1993;15:245](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8432549))
* Most commonly reported mutation is a splice junction mutation in exon 8

Etiology

* Over 100 *LIPA* loss of function mutations have been identified ([Atherosclerosis 2017;265:124](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28881270))

Clinical features

* Hepatomegaly, splenomegaly, vomiting, gastrointestinal bleeding, steatorrhea, premature atherosclerosis
* Infants have severe malnutrition, adrenal calcifications, hepatosplenomegaly and death within the first few months of life
* Late onset form includes dyslipidemia (elevated low density lipoprotein cholesterol [LDL-C], low high density lipoprotein cholesterol [HDL-C]), elevated liver transaminases, hepatomegaly and splenomegaly
* Reference: [Ann Diagn Pathol 2017;31:66](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28318950)

Diagnosis

* Deficient LAL enzyme activity on enzyme assay and genetic testing that identifies mutations of the *LIPA* gene ([J Hepatol 2013;58:1230](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23485521))
* *LIPA* gene analysis: over 100 *LIPA* mutations having been identified in patients with LAL deficiency ([Atherosclerosis 2017;265:124](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28881270))

Laboratory

* Elevation of total cholesterol, triglycerides and elevated LDL-C
* Increased levels of transaminases
* Cholesteryl ester storage disease is milder with residual baseline LAL activity (1 - 12% of normal)
* Wolman disease in infancy, Lancet Gastroenterol Hepatol 2017;2:670)

Radiology description

* Hepatomegaly
* Adrenal calcification in infantile form (Wolman disease)
* Reference: [Pediatr Radiol 1992;22:541](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1491919)

Prognostic factors

* Late onset form has a more favorable prognosis ([Genet Med 2016;18:452](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26312827))

Case reports

* 4.5 month old Caucasian boy presenting with fever, icterus and hepatosplenomegaly ([J Med Case Rep 2023;17:369](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37641143))
* 14 year old girl was diagnosed as LALD with the findings of hepatomegaly, splenomegaly, elevated liver enzyme levels and abnormal lipid profile ([Turk J Pediatr 2020;62:831](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33108087))
* 43 year old Brazilian man who was diagnosed with cirrhosis and then diagnosed with LALD at 53 years old ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2022;101:e30315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36107601))
* 53 year old Brazilian man who was diagnosed with liver cirrhosis of unknown etiology ([Ann Hepatol 2019;18:230](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31113597))

Treatment

* Enzyme replacement therapy (ERT) ([Ther Adv Rare Dis 2021;2:26330040211026928](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37181111))
* Sebelipase alfa, a recombinant human lysosomal acid lipase, was approved by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) in 2015 to treat LALD ([Hepatology 2013;58:950](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23348766))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Diffuse microvesicular steatosis in the hepatocytes
* Foamy macrophages, containing lipid and ceroid, are present in the sinusoids and portal tracts
* Large Kupffer cells with increased vacuoles, lipid droplets or cholesterol crystals
* Progresses to fibrosis and to micronodular cirrhosis ([Histopathology 2012;60:1107](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22621222))

Sample pathology report

* Liver, nontargeted / random, biopsy:
  + Microvesicular steatosis with scattered clusters of PAS / D positive histiocytes (see comment)
  + Comment: Sections show hepatocytes with diffuse microvesicular steatosis and accentuated cell membrane. Scattered clusters of PAS / D positive histiocytes are present. CD68 of histiocytes highlights negatively stained crystals and lipids. Electron microscopy demonstrates membrane bound lipids and cholesterol ester crystals in hepatocytes. Overall findings are suggestive of lysosomal acid lipase deficiency. Molecular testing is recommended to confirm the diagnosis.

Differential diagnosis

* [Metabolic dysfunction associated steatotic liver disease (MASLD)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livernash.html):
  + Macrovesicular steatosis, lobular inflammation and ballooning degeneration ([Hepatology 2005;41:1313](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15915461))
  + Activity graded by NAFLD (nonalcoholic fatty liver disease) activity score (NAS) and evaluated for fibrosis staging ([Hepatology 2005;41:1313](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15915461))
  + May additionally have Mallory-Denk bodies, glycogenated nuclei and mega mitochondria
* [Alcoholic liver disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liveralcoholichep.html):
  + Serum AST:ALT > 2
  + May histologically be indistinguishable from MASLD
  + More likely to have Mallory-Denk bodies and mega mitochondria than MASLD
  + May have satellitosis
* [Drug induced liver injury](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liverdrugtoxingeneral.html):
  + Various histologic patterns including necroinflammatory hepatic injury, granulomatous hepatitis, cholestatic injury, neoplastic and vascular lesions as well as steatohepatitis and steatosis pattern of injury ([J Hepatol 2019;70:1222](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30926241))
    - Microvesicular steatosis: tetracycline, valproate
    - Macrovesicular steatosis: corticosteroids, tamoxifen, estrogen

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/liverlysosomalacidlipasedeficiencyOshimaKarimi02.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/liverlysosomalacidlipasedeficiencyOshimaKarimi02.jpg)

Which of the following histologic findings is seen on liver biopsies of patients with lysosomal acid lipase deficiency?

1. Eosinophilic intracytoplasmic globules
2. Macrovesicular steatosis
3. Microvesicular steatosis
4. Stellate cell hyperplasia

Board review style answer #1

**C**. Microvesicular steatosis. In lysosomal acid lipase deficiency as depicted in the image, microvesicular steatosis is seen on biopsy. Answer B is incorrect because macrovesicular steatosis is usually seen in alcoholic fatty liver disease, metabolic dysfunction associated steatotic liver disease, etc. Answer A is incorrect because eosinophilic intracytoplasmic globules are seen in alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency. Answer D is incorrect because stellate cell hyperplasia is seen in hypervitaminosis A.

Board review style question #2

What is the most common molecular abnormality in cholesteryl ester storage disease?

1. *ATP7B* gene mutation
2. p.C282Y mutation in the *HFE* gene
3. *SERPINA1* mutation
4. Splice junction mutation in exon 8 of *LIPA* gene

Board review style answer #2

**D**. Splice junction mutation in exon 8 of *LIPA* gene. Lysosomal acid lipase deficiency (LALD) is an autosomal recessive genetic disease that arises due to mutations in the LAL locus of chromosome 10q23.2. While there are more than 100 loss of function mutations identified in the *LIPA* gene, splice junction mutation in exon 8 of *LIPA* gene is the most common mutation identified ([Atherosclerosis 2017;265:124](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28881270), [Arterioscler Thromb Vasc Biol 2007;27:1866](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17634524)). Answer C is incorrect because *SERPINA1* mutation is seen in alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency. Answer B is incorrect because this mutation is seen in hereditary hemochromatosis. Answer A is incorrect because *ATP7B* is the gene mutation involved in Wilson disease.
