Definition / general

* Primary sclerosing cholangitis (PSC) is a biliary disease causing progressive stricturing of the biliary tree, leading to cirrhosis and possibly cholangiocarcinoma

Essential features

* Etiology unclear
* Patients are at increased risk of developing ulcerative colitis and cholangiocarcinoma
* Liver biopsy rarely samples the classic "onion skin" periductal fibrosis, meaning typical imaging findings are more reliable for establishing the diagnosis

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [K83.01](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/K00-K95/K80-K87/K83-/K83.01) - primary sclerosing cholangitis

Epidemiology

* Incidence of roughly 1 per 100,000 person years ([Hepatology 2011;53:1590](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21351115))
* Rates may be lower in developing countries, though reliable data are lacking

Sites

* Large intrahepatic and extrahepatic biliary tree affected, including gallbladder ([J Hepatol 2008;48:598](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18222013))

Etiology

* Possibly autoimmune, though etiology uncertain overall
* Gut microbiome has been suggested to play a role ([Ann Transl Med 2016;4:512](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28149874), [World J Gastroenterol 2020;26:2768](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32550753))
* Siblings of primary sclerosing cholangitis patients more likely to develop primary sclerosing cholangitis, suggesting a genetic component ([Clin Gastroenterol Hepatol 2008;6:939](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18674735))

Clinical features

* M:F = 2:1
* Usually age 30 - 50; can occur in children ([Hepatology 2017;66:518](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28390159))
* Half asymptomatic at diagnosis
* Symptoms: fatigue, pruritus, jaundice, right upper quadrant pain / tenderness
* Increased risk for bacterial cholangitis and [cholangiocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorcholangiocarcinoma.html) ([Clin Liver Dis 2016;20:67](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26593291))
* Up to 70% also have [inflammatory bowel disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorinflammatory.html) (particularly [ulcerative colitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colonuc.html), although fewer than 10% with ulcerative colitis have primary sclerosing cholangitis) ([Gut Liver 2018;12:17](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28376583))
* Patients may also have an overlap syndrome with [autoimmune hepatitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liverautoimmune.html) ([J Hepatol 2011;54:374](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21067838))

Diagnosis

* Firm diagnostic criteria do not exist
* British Society of Gastroenterology recently offered extensive PSC diagnosis guidelines ([Gut 2019;68:1356](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31154395))
* Diagnosis generally established by cholestatic lab findings combined with bile duct stricturing on imaging ([Lancet 2018;391:2547](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29452711))
* Liver biopsy useful to rule out other possibilities but generally cannot establish the diagnosis

Laboratory

* Elevated alkaline phosphatase; variable bilirubin
* Many patients are pANCA positive, though this is nonspecific and serology is not helpful in general ([World J Gastroenterol 2008;14:3781](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18609700))
* Usually negative antimitochondrial antibody

Radiology description

* Multifocal bile duct strictures causing a "beads on a string" appearance on magnetic resonance cholangiography and endoscopic retrograde cholangiopancreatography ([J Hepatol 2017;66:1265](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28427764))
* Patients without such imaging findings who otherwise appear to have primary sclerosing cholangitis may have small duct primary sclerosing cholangitis ([Gastroenterology 2008;134:975](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18395078))

Prognostic factors

* Small duct primary sclerosing cholangitis has a better outcome than typical primary sclerosing cholangitis ([Gut 2002;51:731](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12377815))
* Anti-GP2 IgA autoantibodies reportedly indicate poor prognosis ([Gut 2017;66:137](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27406039))

Case reports

* 26 year old man with primary sclerosing cholangitis and ulcerative colitis with low grade dysplasia ([J Crohns Colitis 2013;7:e61](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22552273))
* 33 year old man with primary sclerosing cholangitis and Crohn's disease ([Intern Med 2012;51:2077](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22892482))
* 84 year old woman with primary sclerosing cholangitis and CREST syndrome ([J Med Case Rep 2015;9:272](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26607041))
* 2 patients with overlap between primary sclerosing cholangitis and primary biliary cholangitis ([Dig Liver Dis 2015;47:432](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25747115))

Treatment

* Ursodeoxycholic acid can improve lab values but does not halt disease progression ([Lancet Gastroenterol Hepatol 2016;1:68](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28404115))
* Obeticholic acid also emerging as a treatment ([J Hepatol 2020;73:94](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32165251))
* Only truly effective treatment is liver transplantation, though disease recurs in 33% of patients ([Hum Pathol 2003;34:1127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14652814))

Gross description

* Alternating fibrosis / stenosis and ectasia of bile ducts, with bile sludge and calculi
* Liver may be distorted or cirrhotic

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Classic finding is "onion skin" fibrosis around affected medium sized bile ducts; smaller ducts rarely affected (except in small duct primary sclerosing cholangitis), meaning biopsy sampling rarely demonstrates this finding
* Progressive atrophy of bile duct epithelium and obliteration of the lumen, leaving a fibrous scar
* Ductular reaction and cholestasis may occur
* Inflammatory infiltrate is typically mild and limited to biliary epithelium and portal tracts, though largest ducts may show increased infiltrate
* Extravasated bile may elicit xanthomatous or granulomatous response
* Portal edema and fibrosis may be seen secondary to obstruction of larger ducts
* Primary sclerosing cholangitis cirrhosis shows the typical biliary jigsaw pattern
* [Cholangiocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorcholangiocarcinoma.html) may occur in primary sclerosing cholangitis, preceded by intestinal metaplasia and biliary intraepithelial neoplasia ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19898228))
* Several staging systems exist, with the Ludwig system the most commonly used ([Hepatology 2017;65:907](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27880989)):
  1. Cholangitis / portal hepatitis
  2. Periportal fibrosis or periportal hepatitis
  3. Bridging fibrosis or necrosis
  4. Cirrhosis

Sample pathology report

* Liver, biopsy:
  + Liver parenchyma with ductular reaction, bile duct distortion with focal inflammation and minimal portal chronic inflammation (see comment)
  + Comment: The reported finding of bile duct stricturing on magnetic resonance cholangiopancreatography is noted. While the histologic findings are not specific, they are compatible with primary sclerosing cholangitis. A trichrome stain shows no significant fibrosis.

Differential diagnosis

* [Cholangiocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorcholangiocarcinoma.html):
  + May have similar radiologic findings
* [Primary biliary cholangitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liverPBC.html):
  + Florid duct lesions, more prominent inflammation
* **Secondary sclerosing cholangitis**:
  + Due to stones, prior surgery
  + "Onion skin" fibrosis and inflammation often more diffuse
* [Duct obstruction](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liveracutelargeductob.html):
  + May occur due to primary sclerosing cholangitis or other causes
  + Pure duct obstruction often has early cholestasis
* **IgG4 related sclerosing cholangitis**:
  + More prominent inflammation, with numerous [IgG4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsigg4.html) positive plasma cells by IHC
  + Disease has more aggressive clinical course (shorter time to transplant, higher likelihood of recurrence) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:88](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20035148), [World J Gastroenterol 2013;19:7661](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24282356))
