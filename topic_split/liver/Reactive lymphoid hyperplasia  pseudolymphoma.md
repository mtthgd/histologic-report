Definition / general

* Also called pseudolymphoma, may mimic lymphoma ([Mod Pathol 2010;23:244](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19915525), [Am J Surg Pathol 1999;23:302](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10078921))
* Rare, < 50 cases reported
* Age range 15 - 81 years; female predominance
* Usually incidental finding, right lobe predilection
* Associated with chronic liver disease, extrahepatic autoimmune disorders and malignancies
* May be concomitant hepatic and pancreatic involvement ([Clin Res Hepatol Gastroenterol 2012;36:e71](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22230217))
* May be immune mediated
* No associated systemic manifestations
* Resection is curative

Case reports

* 69 year old woman with stage I renal cell carcinoma and hepatic mass ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2001;125:577](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11260645))

Gross description

* Well circumscribed, tan, nonencapsulated, solitary (multiple in 20%) rubbery nodule, 0.5 - 5.5 cm

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Prominent lymphoid follicles with germinal centers, tingible body macrophages and polymorphic small lymphocytes

Positive stains

* [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html)

Negative stains

* [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html) does not stain germinal centers; no light chain restriction

Differential diagnosis

* [Autoimmune hepatitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liverautoimmune.html)
* [Castleman disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodescastleman.html)
* Chronic [hepatitis B](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liverhepB.html) or [hepatitis C](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liverhepC.html) infection
* [MALT](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaMALT.html) or other **low grade lymphoma:** lymphoepithelial lesions, cellular atypia, light chain restriction
* [Primary biliary cirrhosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liverPBC.html)
