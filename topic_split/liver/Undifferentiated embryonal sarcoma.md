Definition / general

* Also called malignant mesenchymoma, mesenchymal sarcoma
* 9 - 15% of pediatric hepatic tumors (#3 after hepatoblastoma and hepatocellular carcinoma) - usually ages 6 - 10 years
* In children, often associated with mesenchymal hamartoma ([Pediatr Dev Pathol 2011;14:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20925497)), rarely with vaginal embryonal rhabdomyosarcoma ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2011;15:250](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21414822))
* Rare in adults ([Cancer 2008;112:2274](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18361435))
* Appears to be a primitive mesenchymal neoplasm with possible foci of differentiated sarcoma, such as angiosarcoma
* Typically presents with pain, fever, abdominal mass and normal serum AFP ([J Pediatr Surg 2008;43:1912](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18926232))
* Good prognosis ([Cancer 2002;94:252](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11815984)) but large tumors may rupture and cause death ([J Pediatr Hematol Oncol 2007;29:63](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17230070))

Case reports

* 10 year old girl with abdominal pain and anorexia ([Case of the Week #134](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case134.htm))
* 16 year old girl with palpable liver mass, fever and vomiting ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2003;127:e163](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12653609))
* 74 year old woman with tumor arising from mesenchymal hamartoma ([Int J Surg Pathol 2012;20:297](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22134632))

Treatment

* Complete resection and chemotherapy ([J Gastrointest Surg 2007;11:73](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17390190)), possibly liver transplant ([J Pediatr Hematol Oncol 2013;35:451](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23138115))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Variably cellular tumor with anaplastic, spindled / oval cells with prominent hyaline globules and ill defined borders within pseudocapsule
* Nuclei have stippled chromatin, inconspicuous nucleoli
* Variably myxoid stroma with numerous thin walled veins
* Also bizarre tumor cells with prominent eosinophilic cytoplasm and PAS+ diastase resistant hyaline globules
* Extramedullary hematopoiesis common, frequent mitotic activity
* Note that trapped hepatocytes and bile duct structures are present at periphery
* Adults may have partial smooth muscle differentiation ([Hum Pathol 2003;34:246](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12673559))

Undifferentiated embryonal sarcoma

Positive stains

* [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html)+ diastase resistant hyaline globules, [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html), high [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) index ([Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2006;14:193](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16785789))
* [Glypican 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglypican3.html) (60%, [Hum Pathol 2012;43:695](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21937079)), [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html), paranuclear dot-like [CK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html), [alpha-1-antitrypsin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsa1a.html) and [alpha-1-antichymotrypsin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalpha1antichymo.html), variable **muscle markers**

Negative stains

* [Alpha fetoprotein](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsafp.html) (hyaline globules), [keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), [myogenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html)

Electron microscopy description

* Hyaline globules are lysosomal and possibly apoptotic bodies

Differential diagnosis

* [Embryonal rhabdomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueembryonalrhabdo.html): usually 2 - 6 years old, myxoid mass extending into bile duct, rhabdomyoblastic differentiation with cytoplasmic cross striations, cambium layer present, no diffuse anaplasia or hyaline globules, myogenin+ and MyoD1+ ([Pediatr Dev Pathol 2007;10:89](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17378682))
* [Gastrointestinal stromal tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stomachGIST.html): adults, CD117+, DOG1+, CD34+ (usually)
* [Hepatoblastoma, mixed form](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorhepatoblastomamixed.html)
* [Hydatid cyst](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/liverechinococcal.html) in endemic areas ([J Pediatr Surg 2008;43:E1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18558156))
* [Mesenchymal hamartoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumormesenchymalhamart.html): usually < 1 year old, cystic, bland tumor cells and no giant cells
* [Sarcomatoid hepatocellular carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorsarcomatoid.html)
* [Sclerosing variant of hepatocellular carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorscirrhousHCC.html): rare in children, has intracellular bile, Mallory-Denk bodies, HepPar1+
