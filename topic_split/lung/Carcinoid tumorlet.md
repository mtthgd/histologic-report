Definition / general

* Tumor of neuroendocrine differentiation, defined by size < 5 mm in diameter, mitotic count < 2 mitoses/2 mm² and absence of necrosis

Essential features

* Can be found incidentally in lung resections but can arise in the context of diffuse idiopathic pulmonary neuroendocrine cell hyperplasia ([DIPNECH](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumordipnech.html))
* Defined as a proliferation of neuroendocrine cells < 5 mm in diameter that extend through the bronchial basement membrane with < 2 mitoses/2 mm² and no necrosis
* Progression to [typical carcinoid](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorcarcinoid.html) tumors is possible

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8040/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=63) - tumorlet, benign
* ICD-10: [D3A.8](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D3A-D3A/D3A-/D3A.8) - other benign neuroendocrine tumors
* ICD-11: [2F00.Y](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f2043813445%2fmms%2fother) - other specified benign neoplasm of middle ear or respiratory system

Epidemiology

* Typically affects middle aged women ([Ann Oncol 2010;21:vii65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20943645), [Chest 2007;131:1635](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17400673)):
  + Frequent incidental finding in lung resection specimens or can be found in the context of [DIPNECH](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumordipnech.html)
* Real incidence and prevalence unknown:
  + Often missed on imaging and can be easily overlooked during grossing because of their small size ([J Thorac Oncol 2009;4:383](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19247084), [Med Sci Monit 2020;26:e926014](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32913178))
  + Can be found in lung specimens resected for [typical](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorcarcinoid.html) (8%) or [atypical](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumoratypicalcarcinoid.html) (4.3%) carcinoids ([J Thorac Oncol 2009;4:383](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19247084))
* Can be found in patients with multiple endocrine neoplasia type 1 ([MEN1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pancreasMEN1.html)) ([Neuroendocrinology 2016;103:240](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26113081))

Sites

* Tumorlets are located in the same region as inflammatory or fibrous lung disease ([AJR Am J Roentgenol 2004;183:293](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15269015))
* When arising in the context of [DIPNECH](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumordipnech.html), they are typically located in the terminal bronchioles

Pathophysiology

* Not well described
* Incidental finding in lung resection specimens, accompanying inflammatory or fibrous conditions ([J Thorac Dis 2012;4:655](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23205296), [Med Sci Monit 2020;26:e926014](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32913178), [Int J Surg Pathol 2018;26:660](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29673288), [Respirol Case Rep 2018;6:e00373t](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30275954))
* Presence of multiple carcinoids more likely to be part of [DIPNECH](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumordipnech.html)
* It is believed that some tumorlets may progress into carcinoid tumors, mostly [typical carcinoids](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorcarcinoid.html) ([AJR Am J Roentgenol
  2010;195:661](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20729444))

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Frequently asymptomatic, as it can be an incidental finding ([AJR Am J Roentgenol
  2010;195:661](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20729444))
* Clinical features of [DIPNECH](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumordipnech.html) can be seen in patients with tumorlets arising in this context

Diagnosis

* See [DIPNECH](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumordipnech.html) for details on diagnosis for tumorlets arising in this setting

Radiology description

* CT scan: can present as a bronchial wall thickening that can appear nodular or show multiple peribronchiolar spherical to ovoid solid or ground glass nodules ([Clin Radiol 2015;70:317](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25465294))
  + Images are similar to the ones for [DIPNECH](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumordipnech.html) when arising in this context
  + When tumorlets arise in a context of chronic pulmonary lung disease, they can be subtle and masked by the underlying process ([Med Sci Monit 2020;26:e926014](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32913178))

Prognostic factors

* Most tumorlets are benign incidental findings for which surgical resection is curative and prognosis is excellent ([Chest 2007;131:1635](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17400673))
* See [DIPNECH](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumordipnech.html) for details on prognosis of tumorlets arising in this setting

Case reports

* 18 year old man with multiple sclerosing pneumocytomas and carcinoid tumorlets ([J Endocr Soc 2019;3:937](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31020057))
* 73 year old woman with pulmonary hypoplasia and carcinoid tumorlets ([Respirol Case Rep 2018;6:e00373t](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30275954))
* 73 year old woman with a lung fungus and a carcinoid tumorlet ([Intern Med 2018;57:3485](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30101932))

Treatment

* See [DIPNECH](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumordipnech.html) for details on tumorlets arising in this setting

Gross description

* Difficult to identify but when visible, they are seen as small gray-white nodules < 5 mm in diameter, intimately associated with bronchioles

Frozen section description

* See [typical carcinoids](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorcarcinoid.html)

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Poorly defined nodule of neuroendocrine cells that cross the mucosal basal membrane in a fibrotic stroma:
  + Size < 5 mm with < 2 mitoses/2 mm² and absence of necrosis
  + Usually found in association with an airway
  + Composed of neuroendocrine cells that are oval to round or spindle shaped; cells have round to oval nuclei with salt and pepper chromatin and a moderate amount of eosinophilic cytoplasm
* Complete features of [DIPNECH](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumordipnech.html) or an underlying lung disease can be seen

Cytology description

* See [typical carcinoids](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorcarcinoid.html)

Positive stains

* Neuroendocrine markers: [chromogranin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html), [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html), [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html), [INSM1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinsm1.html) ([Chest 2007;131:1635](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17400673), [Histopathology 2018;72:1067](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29274092))
* [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html): 70% of cases ([Chest 2007;131:1635](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17400673))

Negative stains

* [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) ([Histopathology 2018;72:1067](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29274092))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* To date, molecular alterations of tumorlets are poorly described

Sample pathology report

* See [DIPNECH](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumordipnech.html) for an example of a report when tumorlets arise in this setting

Differential diagnosis

* [Minute pulmonary meningothelial-like nodules](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumormpmn.html):
  + Randomly distributed nodules in the lung that are positive for [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) and [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html), negative for other neuroendocrine markers ([chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html), [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html) and [INSM1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinsm1.html)) and [pankeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html) ([Histopathology 2018;72:1067](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29274092), [Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:487](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19047895))
* [Typical carcinoid](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorcarcinoid.html):
  + Tumor size ≥ 5 mm with < 2 mitoses/2 mm² and absence of necrosis
* [Atypical carcinoid](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumoratypicalcarcinoid.html):
  + > 2 mitoses/2 mm² or presence of necrosis

Board review style question #1

[![Missing Image](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/lungtumortumorletGagne08.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/lungtumortumorletGagne08.jpg)

Regarding the lung nodule (tumor size < 5 mm without necrosis and mitoses) presented in the image, which of the following is true?

1. EMA and CD56 stains are positive
2. Many of them have *EGFR* mutations
3. Their prognosis is poor
4. They can be seen in the context of diffuse idiopathic pulmonary neuroendocrine cell hyperplasia (DIPNECH)

Board review style answer #1

**D.** They can be seen in the context of DIPNECH. A lung tumorlet is depicted in the image. While CD56 is positive in those tumors, EMA is negative and can help to differentiate with minute pulmonary meningothelial-like nodules (A). To date, molecular alterations of tumorlets are poorly described (B). Whether they occur in association with DIPNECH or with an underlying lung process, tumorlets have a good prognosis (C).

Board review style question #2

Which of the following is true about lung tumorlets?

1. More than 2 mitoses/2 mm² can be seen
2. Rare tumorlets of more than 1 cm have been reported
3. They are confined to bronchial mucosa, without crossing the bronchial basal membrane
4. They are frequently found in association with an airway

Board review style answer #2

**D.** They are frequently found in association with an airway. Tumorlets, by definition, have a size < 5 mm and < 2 mitoses/2 mm² with absence of necrosis (A and B). Statement C refers to diffuse idiopathic pulmonary neuroendocrine cell hyperplasia.
