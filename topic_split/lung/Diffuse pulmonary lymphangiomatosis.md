Definition / general

* Diffuse proliferation of abnormal, complex lymphatic channels (lymphangiomas) that may involve lung or other organs ([Lymphat Res Biol 2011;9:191](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22196284))
* Pulmonary involvement often accompanied by chylous pleural effusions
* May be progressive and fatal from respiratory failure

Terminology

* Lymphangioma if proliferation is focal

Epidemiology

* Rare condition, typically presents in children and young adults
* No sex predilection

Sites

* Localized to lung / chest or can also involve multiple organs, including bone, spleen, soft tissue, liver

Pathophysiology / etiology

* Progressive disease with recurrent effusions and respiratory failure secondary to infections and chylous accumulation
* Congenital, possibly from abnormal lymphatic development

Clinical features

* Generally presents with dyspnea, wheezing, cough and may be confused with asthma
* Occasionally with chyloptysis or hemoptysis

Diagnosis

* Clinical presentation and imaging findings may suggest diagnosis, particularly if bone involvement is noted
* Most cases diagnosed with open lung biopsy
* Transbronchial biopsy may confirm disease if vesicles seen on bronchoscopy ([Clin Radiol 2005;60:921](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16039928))

Laboratory

* Thoracocentesis may reveal chylous effusion (elevated triglycerides)

Radiology images

* Xray: nonspecific findings, including pleural effusion, diffuse pulmonary opacities, interstitial thickening
* CT: bilateral ground glass opacities, smooth thickening of interlobular septa, bronchovascular bundles, pleura; may show infiltration of the mediastinal soft tissue by cystic fluid densities

Prognostic factors

* Children have a worse prognosis than older patients; pleural and lung involvement is adverse prognostic factor ([J Pediatr Hematol Oncol 2004;26:136](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14767208))

Case reports

* 4 year old girl with recurrent hemorrhagic pericardial effusion due to diffuse lymphangiohemangiomatosis ([J Med Case Rep 2010;4:62](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20170552))
* 35 year old man with diffuse pulmonary lymphangiomatosis ([Basic Appl Pathol 2012;5:63](http://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/j.1755-9294.2012.01130.x/full))
* Diffuse pulmonary lymphatic disease presenting as interstitial lung disease in adulthood ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:1548](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22982897))
* Kaposiform lymphangiomatosis ([Pediatr Blood Cancer 2015;62:901](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25307772))

Treatment

* Supportive, including observation and medical management
* Surgical resection for localized disease; pleurocentesis, pleurodesis, pleurodectomy, ligation of thoracic duct ([J Thorac Cardiovasc Surg 2007;133:1664](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17532983))
* Successful lung transplantation has been reported ([Am J Transplant 2008;8:1946](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18671675))

Gross description

* Subpleural and septal thickening

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Diffuse proliferation of complex, anastomotic lymphatic channels without significant dilatation, lined by benign appearing, flattened endothelial cells
* Prominence of lymphatic channels in visceral pleura, interlobular septa, and bronchovascular bundles with surrounding bundles of spindle cells, interspersed collagen, vessels
* Lymphatic channels may contain acellular, eosinophilic, proteinaceous material
* May show hemorrhagic kaposiform component, with compressed vascular channels, plump spindle cells, hemosiderin

Positive stains

* [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html), [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html), [Factor VIII](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfactorviii.html) in endothelial cells
* [Vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html), [actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsactin.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) in spindle cells

Negative stains

* [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html), [keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) ([AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html) staining of alveoli contrasts with negative lymphatics), [HHV8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshhv8.html)

Differential diagnosis

* [Kaposiform hemangioendothelioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuehemangioendotheliomakaposiform.html)
* [Kaposi sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuekaposi.html): HHV8 positive
* [Lymphangiectasis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowellymphangiectasia.html): dilation of existing lymphatics in normal distribution
* [Lymphangioleiomyomatosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorlymphangioleiomyomatosis.html): HMB45 positive, more smooth muscle cells
* [Pulmonary capillary hemangiomatosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorhemangiomatosis.html)
