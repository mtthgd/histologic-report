Definition / general

* Usually in urinary tract; very rare in lung, usually in immunocompromised patients
* Caused by macrophage dysfunction involving intracellular lysosomal mediated killing of bacteria
* In AIDS patients, usually due to [*Rhodococcus equi*](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungnontumoratypicalmyco.html#rhodococcus), an animal pathogen causing opportunistic infections ([Chest 1999;115:889](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10084511))

Case reports

* Two cases in immunocompetent patients ([Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2011;54:133](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21393896))

Treatment

* Antibiotics

Gross description

* Nodular masses or infiltrates with cavitation

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Intraalveolar histiocytes with pink or foamy cytoplasm (von Hansemann’s histiocytes) that fill and destroy alveoli, not interstitium
* Histiocytes contain PAS+ bacteria, also Michaelis-Gutmann bodies (round / oval structures, usually intracellular, 5 - 20 microns, with laminated or targetoid appearance that stain deeply with H&E, iron and calcium stains)
* Also lymphocytes, plasma cells and neutrophils

Differential diagnosis

* [**Atypical mycobacteria**](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungnontumoratypicalmyco.html): blue histiocytes, positive acid fast stain
* Also **Whipple disease**, **Gaucher disease**, [endogenous lipid pneumonia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungnontumorlipoidpneumonia.html), [amiodorone toxicity](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungnontumoramiodarone.html)
