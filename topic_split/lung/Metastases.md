Definition / general

* Lung is a common site of metastases; usually multiple, bilateral, sharply outlined, rapidly growing, more pleomorphic and necrotic than lung primaries
* May appear as multiple discrete nodules in periphery of lung or as lymphangitic carcinomatosis (peribronchial and perivascular patterns via lymphatics)
* Rarely appear as intralymphatic microscopic foci that cause pulmonary hypertension
* Metastases can also be from other lung primaries ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:1752](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19773638))

Essential features

* Lung is a common site of metastatic disease and may be the first or only site of metastatic involvement
* Lung metastases are generally multiple, well circumscribed and tend to grow rapidly
* A history of malignancy is helpful in determining the primary site but a panel of immunohistochemical stains can help support the diagnosis

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [C78.00](http://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C76-C80/C78-/C78.00) - Secondary malignant neoplasm of unspecified lung
* ICD-10: [C78.01](http://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C76-C80/C78-/C78.01) - Secondary malignant neoplasm of right lung
* ICD-10: [C78.02](http://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C76-C80/C78-/C78.02) - Secondary malignant neoplasm of left lung

Epidemiology

* In autopsy studies, 20 - 50% of patients with malignancy had lung metastasis ([Cancer 1981;47:2595](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7260854))

Sites

* Generally peripheral lung but can also be endobronchial

Pathophysiology

* Five year overall survival for all lung metastases is 36% ([J Thorac Cardiovasc Surg 1997;113:37](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9011700))

Etiology

* Mostly hematogenous or lymphogenous spread from primary site

Clinical features

* Usually asymptomatic or may present with nonspecific cough, chest pain, hemoptysis, spontaneous pneumothorax ([J Surg Oncol 2014;109:42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24301202))

Diagnosis

* Typically detected by imaging, specifically CT scans
* Confirmation by cytologic or histologic sampling

Radiology description

* CT scans are preferred modality
* Single or multiple, peripheral, well circumscribed nodule(s)
* Limited detection of subcentimeter disease

Prognostic factors

* Clinical variables associated with prolonged survival after lung metastasectomy in colorectal cancer patients
  + Prolonged disease free interval between primary tumor and metastasis, normal prethoracotomy carcinoembryonic antigen, absence of thoracic node involvement and single pulmonary lesion ([Ann Surg Oncol 2013;20:572](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23104709))
* Complete resection and longer disease free interval > 6 months associated with better survival ([Surg Oncol 2012;21:237](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22749804))

Case reports

* 48 year old man with lymphangitic spread of hepatocellular carcinoma ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2003;127:e11](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12562285))
* 50 year old man with ameloblastoma of jaw metastatic to lung ([Case of the Week #318](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case318.htm))
* 51 year old man with metastatic melanoma showing ground glass opacities ([Am J Respir Crit Care Med 2015;191:954](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25876203))
* 59 year old man with cavitary lung metastasis from urothelial carcinoma, diagnosed by FNA ([Can Respir J 2011;18:e46](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21766082))
* 65 year old woman with parotid gland acinic cell carcinoma metastatic to lung ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2007;131:970](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17550329))
* 83 year old man with epithelioid tumor of lungs ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2005;129:e7](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15628928))

Treatment

* Chemotherapy, immunotherapy, other systemic therapy
* Criteria for pulmonary metastasectomy: primary site of disease controlled, complete resection of lung metastasis feasible, patient able to tolerate procedure, no better alternative treatments ([J Surg Oncol 2014;109:42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24301202))
* Stereotactic body radiation therapy (SBRT) and radiofrequency ablation (RFA) may be considered for small, solitary, peripheral tumors

Gross description

**Patterns associated with specific primaries**
* Central cavitation: colonic adenocarcinoma, leiomyosarcoma, squamous cell carcinoma of upper aerodigestive tract
* Intrabronchial masses: breast, colon, kidney
* Lymphangitic carcinomatosis: breast, choriocarcinoma, pancreas, prostate, stomach
* Nodular metastases: breast, GI, kidney, melanoma, sarcoma
* Tumor emboli: breast, choriocarcinoma, liver, stomach

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Dependent on site of origin (colorectal cancer, bone and soft tissue sarcoma, renal cell carcinoma, melanoma, head and neck tumors, germ cell tumors and many others)
* Evidence for breast metastasis over lung primary: comedonecrosis, solid nests, trabecular architecture, cribriform growth pattern ([Am J Clin Pathol 2009;131:122](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19095575))

Positive stains

* [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html) suggests colorectal carcinoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:141](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12548159))
* Pankeratin to prove epithelial origin, [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html) / [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html) to help differentiate
* Lineage specific markers variable depending on site of origin, see [Table 2](http://www.archivesofpathology.org/action/showFullPopup?id=i1543-2165-140-6-508-t02&doi=10.5858%2Farpa.2015-0173-CP) for suggested stains ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2016;140:508](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26457625))
  + [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html): colon, other GI
  + [PSA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspsa.html), [NKX3.1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnkx3.html): prostate
  + [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgata3.html): breast, urothelial
  + [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html): breast, Müllerian
  + [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html): Müllerian, renal cell carcinoma, thyroid, thymus
  + [Hep Par1 antibody](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsheppar1.html), [Arginase1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsarginase1.html): hepatocellular carcinoma
  + [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html): germ cell tumor
  + [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html): urothelial, squamous, thymus
  + [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html): mesothelioma, Müllerian

Negative stains

* [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html) and [Napsin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnapsina.html) may be helpful to exclude primary lung adenocarcinoma

Differential diagnosis

* Primary lung carcinoma: acini, lepidic growth, nuclear pseudoinclusions, central scar ([Am J Clin Pathol 2009;131:122](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19095575))
* Inflammatory nodule or benign neoplasm

Board review style question #1

For which malignancy is pulmonary metastasectomy most commonly performed and reported?

1. Colorectal cancer
2. Germ cell tumors
3. Melanoma
4. Osteosarcoma
5. Renal cell carcinoma

Board review style answer #1

**A**. Colorectal cancer ([Cancer 2015;121:747](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25377689))
