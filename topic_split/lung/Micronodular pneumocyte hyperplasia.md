Definition / general

* First recognized in 1991 by HH Popper ([Histopathology 1991;18:347](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2071093))
* Benign, hamartomatous, multinodular proliferation of type II pneumocytes
* Often associated with tuberous sclerosis complex
* May coexist with [lymphangioleiomyomatosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorlymphangioleiomyomatosis.html) (LAM)

Essential features

* Rare pulmonary manifestation of tuberous sclerosis complex that may occur in conjunction with LAM
* Composed of multiple nodules of enlarged but benign type II pneumocytes that may mimic atypical adenomatous hyperplasia
* Positive for cytokeratins and TTF1 by immunohistochemical stains, while negative for SMA

Terminology

* Also called multifocal micronodular pneumocyte hyperplasia (MMPH)
* Older terminology: acinar atypical adenomatoid proliferation of epithelium, multiple adenomatoid lesions, micronodular hyperplasia of type II pneumocytes

Epidemiology

* Young and middle aged adults, predominantly women
* Often associated with tuberous sclerosis complex but may be sporadic

Pathophysiology

* Functional loss of TSC 1 or TSC2 and hyperphosphorylation of mTOR related protein may cause this benign neoplastic proliferation of pneumocytes ([Mod Pathol 2010;23:1251](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20526286))

Clinical features

* Non-specific; may present with pneumothorax, shortness of breath, cough

Diagnosis

* Histologic examination

Radiology description

* Xray: multiple pulmonary nodules
* CT: multiple diffuse, small, solid and ground glass pulmonary nodules that remain stable in short term follow-up ([J Comput Assist Tomogr 2012;36:518](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22992599))
* Imaging may mimic miliary tuberculosis ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2015;8:2165](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25973120))

Prognostic factors

* Typically indolent with no clinical significance
* Rare case studies report respiratory failure

Case reports

* 13 year old girl with tuberous sclerosis, renal cell carcinoma, angiomyolipomas and multifocal micronodular pneumocyte hyperplasia ([Chem Pharm Bull (Tokyo) 1990;38:498](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2337965))
* 16 year old girl with respiratory failure ([Histopathology 2002;41:263](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12207789))
* 51 year old woman with tuberous sclerosis complex but no classical clinical findings ([J Med Case Rep 2012 Oct 16;6:352](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23072249))

Gross description

* Small (1 - 10 mm), well-demarcated, randomly distributed parenchymal lung nodules

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Multiple, sharply demarcated or circumscribed nodules
* Tubulopapillary proliferation of type II pneumocytes lining fibrotic and thickened alveolar septa, moderate lymphocytic infiltration, no nuclear atypia ([Int J Surg Pathol 2010;18:522](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20667922))
* Enlarged type II pneumocytes with abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm, vesicular nuclei, distinct nucleoli, occasional eosinophilic inclusions
* Increased alveolar macrophages

Positive stains

* Pan-cytokeratin, [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html), surfactant apoproteins A and B

Negative stains

* [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html), [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html),  [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html), [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html), [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html)

Electron microscopy description

* Osmiophilic lamellar inclusions and surface microvilli of type II pneumocytes

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Associated with loss of heterozygosity on TSC1 or TSC2 ([Mod Pathol 2010;23:1251](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20526286))

Differential diagnosis

* [Atypical adenomatous hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorbronchAAH.html)
* [Lymphangioleiomyomatosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorlymphangioleiomyomatosis.html)
* **Metastases**
* **Resolving miliary inflammatory / infectious processes**
