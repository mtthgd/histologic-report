Definition / general

* Uncommon incidental pulmonary nodules composed of interstitial nodular proliferation of small oval or spindle shaped cells arranged in a zellballen nesting pattern ([J Thorac Imaging 2002;17:227](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12082375))

Essential features

* Generally detected incidentally in resected lung specimens ([Hum Pathol 2009;40:678](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19144385))
* Occasionally detected on thin section computed tomography ([Hum Pathol 2009;40:678](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19144385))
* More common in patients with malignant pulmonary tumors, especially lung adenocarcinoma, than benign disease ([Hum Pathol 2009;40:678](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19144385))
* No significant difference in clinicopathologic factors between patients with single and multiple nodules, except for the size of each nodule ([Hum Pathol 2009;40:678](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19144385))

Terminology

* Pulmonary chemodectomas were first described by Korn et al. in 1960 ([Am J Pathol 1960;37:641](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/13753180))
* In 1988, Gaffey et al. proposed to change the name of pulmonary chemodectomas to minute meningothelial-like nodules ([Am J Surg Pathol 1988;12:167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2830799))
* Terminology includes
  + Pulmonary meningothelial-like nodules (PMLNs)
  + Minute pulmonary meningothelial-like nodules (MPMNs)
  + Pulmonary chemodectoma
  + MPMN-omatosis syndrome
  + Diffuse pulmonary meningotheliomatosis (DPM)

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [R91.1](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/R00-R99/R90-R94/R91-/R91.1) - solitary pulmonary nodule

Epidemiology

* F > M (10.7% versus 4.5%) ([Hum Pathol 2009;40:678](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19144385))
* Most frequent in the sixth decade
* Absent in fetuses, infants and children, indicating that they do not represent congenital rests
* Reported incidence is 0.3 to 9.5% at autopsy or in surgical specimens ([Korean J Pathol 2012;46:87](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23109985))

Sites

* Present in all lobes; incidence is not different between lobes ([Hum Pathol 2009;40:678](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19144385))

Pathophysiology

* Usually asymptomatic but can occasionally manifest as mild restrictive lung disease ([Respiration 2013;86:145](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23796886))

Etiology

* Although recognized for decades, their nature and significance remain uncertain ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:487](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19047895))

Clinical features

* Patients are usually asymptomatic, as the behavior is benign ([Tuberc Respir Dis (Seoul) 2013;75:67](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24023559))

Diagnosis

* Often identified as incidental findings in histopathological lung sections or in high resolution CT scans conducted for unrelated causes ([Korean J Pathol 2012;46:87](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23109985))

Radiology description

* Thin section chest CT reveals randomly distributed, well defined ground glass appearance micronodules, which may simulate metastatic disease ([J Comput Assist Tomogr 2001;25:311](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11242234))

Prognostic factors

* Found more often in patients with malignant pulmonary tumors, especially [lung adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumoradenocarcinoma.html) ([Korean J Pathol 2012;46:87](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23109985))

Case reports

* 52 year old man with 1 cm nodule in the left lower lobe discovered on CT scan ([Tuberc Respir Dis (Seoul) 2013;75:67](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24023559))
* 56 year old woman with left mastectomy for breast cancer ([Nihon Kokyuki Gakkai Zasshi 2002;40:499](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12325336))
* 65 year old diabetic woman with breast cancer and smoking history ([Korean J Pathol 2012;46:87](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23109985))
* 74 year old asymptomatic woman with incidentally detected diffuse bilateral pulmonary nodules ([Respirol Case Rep 2017;5:e00238](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28469917))
* 75 year old woman with a history of invasive rectal adenocarcinoma ([Ann Saudi Med 2013;33:400](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24060722))
* Multiple incidentally detected, randomly distributed, cavitating micronodules ([J Comput Assist Tomogr 2010;34:780](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20861786))

Treatment

* Although curative surgical excision is sometimes needed, these usually run a benign course and conservative treatment is favored ([Tuberc Respir Dis (Seoul) 2013;75:67](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24023559))

Gross description

* 1 - 3 mm, tan to yellow, pleural or parenchymal nodules ([Am J Surg Pathol 1988;12:167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2830799))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Characteristic nests of bland spindle cells with pale eosinophilic cytoplasm expand alveolar septa
* Larger lesions connected by intervening collagen, often imparting a stellate configuration
* Smaller lesions have closely apposed nests with mildly thickened alveolar septa ([Am J Surg Pathol 1988;12:167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2830799))

Cytology description

* Single as well as clusters of bland spindle cells are present in aspirate smears
* However, marked cellular atypia is often seen with cytology diagnosis of atypical / suspicious for malignancy / positive for malignancy
* Immunohistochemistry of cell block will rule out the possibility of carcinoma or [squamous cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorSCC.html) since the spindle cells are negative for pankeratin ([AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html)), [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) and [p40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdeltaNp63.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Mutational analyses were performed on microdissected tissue using 20 polymorphic microsatellite markers targeting 11 genomic regions in an effort to identify genetic similarities of MPMN and [meningioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumormeningiomageneral.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:207](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15043310))
* Loss of heterozygosity was identified in 25% of single MPMN affecting 3 genomic loci
  + No solitary MPMN had > 1 loss of heterozygosity event
  + Multiple loss of heterozygosity were seen only in diffuse pulmonary meningotheliomatosis (MPMN-omatosis syndrome), where 33.3% of MPMNs showed loss of heterozygosity affecting 7 genomic loci
  + Meningioma showed the highest frequency of loss of heterozygosity with major events seen at 22q (60%), 14q (42.8%) and 1p (44.4%) that were not shared by MPMN
  + MPMNs are different from meningioma based on the major molecular genetic events seen in their formation and progression
* *NF2* deletion similar to meningioma found in a limited series ([Oncotarget 2018;9:36012](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30542514))

Sample pathology report

* Left lung, mass, CT guided needle core biopsy:
  + Minute pulmonary meningothelial-like nodule
  + Negative for malignancy
  + Adjacent lung parenchyma with mild interstitial fibrosis

Differential diagnosis

* [Metastatic carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumormetastases.html): usually positive for pankeratin ([AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html))
* [Pulmonary carcinoid tumors](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorcarcinoid.html) (or [pulmonary tumorlets](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumortumorlet.html) for lesions < 5 mm): positive for pankeratin ([AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html)), [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html) and [INSM1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinsm1.html) ([Histopathology 2018;72:1067](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29274092))
* [Paraganglioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorparaganglioma.html): positive for [neuron specific enolase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsNSE.html), [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html), [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html), [CD57](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd57.html) and [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html)
* **Pulmonary** [meningiomas](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumormeningiomageneral.html): clonal neoplastic lesions, which usually present as lung masses rather than minute nodules

Board review style question #1

An 80 year old Caucasian woman had a thyroid mass and a 1 cm pulmonary lesion. CT guided transthoracic needle biopsy was performed to rule out metastatic tumor. Microscopic examination of core needle biopsy tissue reveals the following lesion. What is your diagnosis?

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/lungtumormpmnqiao14.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/lungtumormpmnqiao14.jpg)

1. Metastatic thyroid papillary carcinoma
2. Minute pulmonary meningothelial-like nodule
3. Pulmonary carcinoid tumor
4. Pulmonary meningioma
5. Pulmonary paraganglioma

Board review style answer #1

**B**. Minute pulmonary meningothelial-like nodule. Microscopic lesion is classic of pulmonary meningothelial-like nodule, with irregular and uncircumscribed interstitial nests of spindle cells, morphologically similar to meningothelial cells.

Board review style question #2

Sometimes, immunohistochemistry is necessary to work up this spindle cell lesion. Pulmonary meningothelial-like nodule is most likely positive for which of the following immunostains?

1. CD56
2. CK5 / 6
3. ER
4. Pankeratin (AE1 / AE3)
5. Synaptophysin

Board review style answer #2

**A**. CD56. Pulmonary meningothelial-like nodule is positive for CD56, EMA, progesterone receptor / PR and vimentin.
