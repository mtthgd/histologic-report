Definition / general

* Low grade or high grade salivary gland type tumor with mucous secreting cells, squamous cells and intermediate cells
* Distinguished from other lung cancers by central or peribronchial location, mucous cells, lack of keratinization, expression of p63 and *MAML2* rearrangement ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:1479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24743219))

Essential features

* Most common salivary gland type tumor in the lung, tends to arise centrally
* Similar morphology to mucoepidermoid carcinoma arising in the head and neck with a mixture of mucous cells, squamous cells and intermediate cells; must exclude metastasis
* Strong association with t(11;19)(q21;p13) and *MAML2* rearrangement by fluorescence in situ hybridization (FISH)
* Squamous component stains with p63 and p40

Terminology

* Same diagnostic terminology as used in the salivary gland

ICD coding

* [C33](http://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C30-C39/C33-/C33) Malignant neoplasm of trachea
* [C34.00](http://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C30-C39/C34-/C34.00) Malignant neoplasm of unspecified main bronchus
* [C34.01](http://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C30-C39/C34-/C34.01) Malignant neoplasm of right main bronchus
* [C34.02](http://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C30-C39/C34-/C34.02) Malignant neoplasm of left main bronchus
* Code more peripheral lesions depending on specific lobe, laterality and extent

Epidemiology

* 0.1 - 0.2% of primary lung tumors ([J Thorac Cardiovasc Surg 1978;76:431](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/703349)) and 9 - 18% in the pediatric population ([J Surg Res 2009;156:224](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19631347), [J Pediatr Surg 2015;50:1004](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25812444))
* May occur in any age group; many cases reported in pediatric population
* Most common of the primary salivary gland type tumors of the lung but still rare

Sites

* Any lung lobe, usually in relation to large bronchi

Pathophysiology

* Usually considered to have low malignant potential with recurrences; more aggressive when high grade ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2014;7:6792](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25400760))
* Most tumors are low stage and low to intermediate grade rather than high grade ([J Thorac Oncol 2013;8:1578](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24389442))

Etiology

* May arise from submucosal bronchial glands

Clinical features

* Large central tumors cause obstructive symptoms such as dyspnea, cough, hemoptysis or pneumonia
* Peripheral lesions may be asymptomatic

Diagnosis

* Bronchoscopy may be used to directly visualize and sample an endobronchial tumor
* Diagnosis may be difficult in small biopsies; may mimic primary non small cell lung carcinoma

Radiology description

* Can be difficult to detect on imaging due to small size of tumor, endobronchial location, association with pneumonia or atelectasis ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2007;131:1400](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17824797))
* On CT, markedly enhanced homogeneous central bronchial nodule / mass suggests low grade mucoepidermoid carcinoma (MEC); high grade MEC tends to be peripheral with poorly defined margins, lobular, heterogeneous with less enhancement ([AJR Am J Roentgenol 2015;205:1160](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26587920))
* Uncommon findings included cavitation, diffuse thickening or spiculation ([Clin Imaging 2012;36:8](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22226436))

Prognostic factors

* Excellent prognosis after surgical removal ([Thorac Cardiovasc Surg 2014;62:140](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23619592), [Asian Cardiovasc Thorac Ann 2016;24:257](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26847635))
* Age, stage, margin status and pathological grade related to overall survival and disease free survival ([J Thorac Oncol 2013;8:1578](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24389442) , [Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2014;7:6792](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25400760))

Case reports

* 22 year old man with tumor in the medial segment of the right lower lobe ([Diagn Pathol 2012;7:137](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23043986))
* 26 year old man with chronic cough and an endobronchial lesion ([Respir Med Case Rep 2013;9:18](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26029623))
* 38 year old woman with mild fever and dry cough ([Oxf Med Case Reports 2015;2015:203](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25988080))
* 40 year old man with dry cough ([Asian Cardiovasc Thorac Ann 2015;23:988](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25792546))
* 46 year old woman with dyspnea on exertion ([Case Rep Oncol Med 2013;2013:625243](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24303221))
* 58 year old man with shortness of breath, dysphagia and weight loss ([Ann Clin Lab Sci 2015;45:219](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25887880))
* 58 year old woman with mixed adenocarcinoma and mucoepidermoid carcinoma ([Ann Thorac Surg 2014;98:695](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25087791))

Treatment

* Complete surgical excision ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2014;7:6792](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25400760))
* High grade tumors may require additional treatment, although chemotherapy and radiation controversial ([World J Surg Oncol 2014;12:33](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24507476))

Gross description

* Polypoid, exophytic growth or sessile tumor in major bronchi, may be > 5 cm
* Generally well circumscribed and smooth with tan to yellow cut surface
* May be solid or cystic and potentially show glistening mucoid material

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Low grade has more cysts and mucous cells; high grade has more solid or nested growth, atypia, mitotic activity, necrosis, hemorrhage
* Mucus secreting cells, squamoid cells without significant keratinization, intermediate type cells
* Mucus secreting cells usually large with light blue-gray mucinous cytoplasm; variants include columnar, goblet, cuboidal, clear or oncocytic cells ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2007;131:1400](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17824797))
* Squamous cells show intercellular bridges but no keratin whorls or pearls; intermediate cells usually polygonal with bland nucleus ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2007;131:1400](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17824797))
* May show areas of papillary growth or spindled cells
* May have dense lymphoplasmacytic infiltrate ([Am J Surg Pathol 2005;29:407](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15725811))
* Lack in situ carcinoma of overlying epithelium

Cytology description

* Mixture of squamoid cells, mucous cells and intermediate cells
* Diff-Quik with numerous tight clusters of relatively small and bland cells with well defined but scant cytoplasm, central round and uniform nuclei with small nucleoli; second population of glandular appearing cells with cytoplasmic vacuoles with mucin, focal extracellular metachromatic mucinous material ([Diagn Cytopathol 2013;41:1096](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23008143))

Positive stains

* [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html), [p40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdeltaNp63.html), [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [CK5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5.html) / [CK6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck6.html)
* [Mucicarmine](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmucins.html) in mucus cells
* [Cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html) overexpression in 20%

Negative stains

* [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html), [Napsin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnapsina.html), [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* *MAML2* rearrangement most common molecular genetic event, found more in low grade tumors ([PLoS One 2015;10:e0143169](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26575266))
* May stain with epidermal growth factor receptor (EGFR) but does not show the mutation or alterations in copy number ([Mod Pathol 2008;21:1168](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18587327))
* Reciprocal translocations, including t(1;11)(p22;q13) with overexpression of cyclin D1, t(11;19)(q14-21;p12) and t(11;19)(q21;p13) which encodes *MECT1-MAML2* (*MECT1* also known as *CRTC1*) ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2007;131:1400](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17824797))

Differential diagnosis

* [Adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumoradenocarcinoma.html): lacks squamous and intermediate cells; [TTF1+](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html)
* [Adenosquamous carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumoradenosquamous.html): usually peripheral in lung, not centered on bronchi; large tumors, no intermediate cells, contains adenocarcinoma component
* [Metastatic renal cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumormetastases.html): more monotonous with foamy or eosinophilic cytoplasm; [CK7-](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html)
* **Mucous gland adenoma**: confined to luminal aspect and not invasive; [TTF1+](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html)
* [PEComa](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorclearcell.html): lacks mucin as well as squamous and intermediate cells; [HMB45+](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html)
* [Squamous cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorSCC.html): may show more keratinization and lacks [mucin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmucins.html)
