Definition / general

* Pleomorphic carcinoma:
  + Subtype of sarcomatoid carcinoma; usually aggressive, malignant epithelial neoplasm composed of cells with significant cytologic atypia and nuclear pleomorphism
  + Contains at least 10% spindle cells and / or giant cells
  + Included under recent WHO classification of "carcinomas with pleomorphic, sarcomatoid or sarcomatous elements"
* Giant cell carcinoma:
  + Subtype of sarcomatoid carcinoma consisting of purely giant, pleomorphic tumor cells
  + Should not show differentiated non small cell components
  + Tumor stains for cytokeratins
* Spindle cell carcinoma:
  + Subtype of sarcomatoid carcinoma (WHO)
  + < 1% of all primary lung carcinomas

Essential features

* Giant cell carcinoma:
  + Classified as a subtype of sarcomatoid carcinoma that is composed almost entirely of tumor giant cells with no differentiated carcinomatous elements
  + Tumor giant cells may or may not stain for human chorionic gonadotrophin
  + Metastatic sarcoma or germ cell tumor should be excluded

Terminology

* Giant cell carcinoma:
  + First described and characterized in 1958 ([Cancer 1958;11:369](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/13511359))
  + Use specific term giant cell carcinoma rather than general term sarcomatoid carcinoma whenever possible to avoid confusion ([J Thorac Oncol 2015;10:1243](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26291008))
  + Giant cells can be a component of pleomorphic carcinoma; if tumor shows giant cells along with differentiated non small cell component, classify as pleomorphic carcinoma ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2010;134:1645](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21043818))
  + [Large cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorlargecell.html) is a different entity

ICD coding

* Giant cell carcinoma:
  + Use code specific for location of tumor
  + ICD-10: [C34.90](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C30-C39/C34-/C34.90#:~:text=Malignant%20neoplasm%20of%20unspecified%20part%20of%20unspecified%20bronchus%20or%20lung,-2016%202017%202018&text=Billable%2FSpecific%20Code-,C34.,a%20diagnosis%20for%20reimbursement%20purposes.) - malignant neoplasm of unspecified part of unspecified bronchus or lung

Epidemiology

* Pure giant cell carcinoma is rare, 0.3 - 2% of lung cancers
* Mean age: 65 years, range: 42 - 81 years
* > 90% men, 92% smokers

Sites

* Giant cell carcinoma:
  + May show predilection for upper lobes
  + Giant cell tumors frequently metastasize to small intestine

Pathophysiology

* Giant cell carcinoma: may have increased incidence of gastrointestinal tract involvement ([Cancer 1992;70:606](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1623475))

Etiology

* Giant cell carcinoma: appears to be a morphologic phenotype expressed by a heterogeneous group of tumors ([Histopathology 1998;32:225](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9568507))

Clinical features

* Pleomorphic carcinoma:
  + < 1% of all carcinomas
  + Presumed epithelial origin, although epithelial and sarcomatous components express common markers differently ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1203](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12960804))
  + Classified as carcinomas despite presence of sarcomatoid features ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2010;134:1645](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21043818))
  + Nodal metastases common
* Giant cell carcinoma:
  + Cough, chest pain, dyspnea, malaise (varies by location)

Diagnosis

* Giant cell carcinoma: cannot be made on small biopsies or cytology; definite diagnosis only on resected tumor

Prognostic factors

* Giant cell carcinoma:
  + Related to stage
  + Extent of giant cells or trophoblastic differentiation does not affect prognosis ([Histopathology 1998;32:225](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9568507))
* Pleomorphic carcinoma:
  + Stage 1 tumors have same prognosis as other stage 1 non small cell carcinomas; at higher stages, may have worse prognosis than other non small cell carcinomas of similar stage ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:311](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12604887))

Case reports

* 51 year old man with small intestinal metastases ([World J Surg Oncol 2005;3:32](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15929799))
* 55 year old woman with jejunal intussusception from metastatic giant cell carcinoma of lung ([BMJ Case Rep 2016;2016:bcr2016216030](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27485876))
* 56 year old man with hemoptysis ([J Cancer Res Ther 2011;7:363](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22044828))
* 60 year old woman with pulmonary giant cell carcinoma associated with pseudomyxoma peritonei ([J Bronchology Interv Pulmonol 2012;19:50](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23207264))
* 66 year old man with giant cell lung carcinoma and HIV ([Med Oncol 2009;26:167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18937080))

Treatment

* Giant cell carcinoma: not usually surgical since metastatic at diagnosis but resection and radiation may prolong survival

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Pleomorphic carcinoma:
  + Non small cell lung carcinoma with at least 10% neoplastic spindle or giant cells, usually with epithelial cells
  + Epithelial component 10 - 85%, usually adenocarcinoma or large cell carcinoma, also squamous cell carcinoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:1727](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18769330))
  + Usually poorly differentiated
  + Spindle cells resemble MPNST, MFH or fibrosarcoma
  + Giant cells usually bizarre with multilobulated nuclei, abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm accompanied by heavy neutrophilic infiltrate with occasional ingested white blood cells
  + Stroma often myxoid, frequent inflammatory infiltrate, collagen fibers
  + Numerous mitotic figures
  + Massive necrosis common
  + Vascular invasion in 58%
* Giant cell carcinoma:
  + Dyscohesive, polygonal, mono and multinucleated giant cells with abundant cytoplasm and prominent nucleoli
  + Neoplastic, highly pleomorphic giant cells, often in inflammatory stroma with emperipolesis ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2010;134:1645](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21043818))
  + Giant cells are multinucleated, may resemble syncytiotrophoblasts and produce human chorionic gonadotropin but are usually fewer than in primary choriocarcinoma of lung ([Histopathology 2000;36:17](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10632747))
  + 2 types of giant cells: βHCG positive syncytiotrophoblast-like giant cells with smudged nuclei and coarse chromatin with nuclear molding and βHCG negative giant cells with admixed neutrophils and cell in cell features ([Histopathology 2016;68:680](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26259572))
* Spindle cell carcinoma:
  + Carcinoma composed exclusively of spindle shaped tumor cells
  + Tumor cells often obliterate vessels

Cytology description

* Giant cell carcinoma:
  + Pleomorphic cells in flat loose clusters, with abundant, thick, well demarcated cytoplasm, multinucleation ([Acta Cytol 2011;55:173](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21325803))
  + Sharp clear nuclear membranes, acidophilic cytoplasm with finely granular or foamy appearance, irregular coarse chromatin, bizarre mitoses, neutrophil engulfment ([Diagn Cytopathol 2007;35:555](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17703447))

Positive stains

* Pleomorphic carcinoma:
  + Sarcomatoid component: [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html) (63%), [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsTTF1.html) (43%), **surfactant protein A** (6%)
  + Epithelial component: [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html) (76%), [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsTTF1.html) (59%), **surfactant protein A** (39%)
* Giant cell carcinoma:
  + [Calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html) (67%)
  + [Pancytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html) variable

Negative stains

* Pleomorphic carcinoma: [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsCK20.html)
* Giant cell carcinoma: [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html), [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html), [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html)
* Spindle cell carcinoma: **surfactant apoprotein A**

Differential diagnosis

* **[Metastatic germ cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mediastinumgermcell.html) (e.g., [choriocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/placentachoriocarcinoma.html))**
* **Other subtypes of sarcomatoid carcinoma**
* **True sarcomas, such as [undifferentiated pleomorphic sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuemfhpleo.html)**

Board review style question #1

Giant cell carcinoma of the lung is most commonly associated with which epithelial component resembling non small cell carcinoma?

1. Adenocarcinoma
2. Adenosquamous carcinoma
3. Large cell carcinoma
4. Squamous cell carcinoma
5. None of the above

Board review style answer #1

**E.** None of the above. Giant cell carcinoma is a subtype of sarcomatoid carcinoma composed almost entirely of tumor giant cells with no differentiated carcinomatous elements.
