Definition / general

**Pulmonary preneoplastic changes include** ([Respir Res 2002;3:20](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11980589)):* Bronchial squamous dysplasia and in situ carcinoma preceding invasive squamous cell carcinoma and basaloid carcinoma
* [Atypical adenomatous hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorbronchAAH.html) preceding bronchioloalveolar carcinoma
* Diffuse idiopathic pulmonary neuroendocrine cell hyperplasia, a proposed precursor for carcinoid tumor

Essential features

* Squamous dysplasia / CIS is a multifocal and clonal condition strongly associated with cigarette smoking i.e. “field cancerization”
* High grade squamous dysplasia / CIS is associated with an increased risk of invasive squamous cell carcinoma
* Squamous dysplasia / CIS tends to arise in large central airways

Terminology

* “Dysplasia” usually used for squamous bronchial lesions
* Angiogenic squamous dysplasia = capillary blood vessels closely juxtaposed to and projecting into metaplastic or dysplastic squamous bronchial epithelium ([Clin Cancer Res 2000;6:1616](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10815878))

ICD coding

* **D02.20**: Carcinoma in situ of unspecified bronchus and lung
* **D02.21**: Carcinoma in situ of right bronchus and lung
* **D02.22**: Carcinoma in situ of left bronchus and lung

Epidemiology

* Males appear to have higher prevalence of squamous dysplasia than females ([J Natl Cancer Inst 1999;91:691](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10218506))

Sites

* Squamous dysplasia / CIS with propensity for large central airways, usually around bifurcations, less commonly in trachea

Pathophysiology

* May precede mass by many years
* Pre invasive lesions and subsequent cancers are clonally related ([J Pathol 2011;224:153](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21506132))
* Evidence for stepwise progression relatively weak, but concept of field carcinogenesis is strongly supported ([Cancer Metastasis Rev 2010;29:5](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20112052))

Etiology

* Associated with smoking
* Possible progression from basal cells or metaplastic goblet cells to squamous metaplasia, dysplasia, CIS

Clinical features

* Usually not symptomatic on its own

Diagnosis

* Image enhanced endoscopy i.e. autofluorescence bronchoscopy (AFB), high magnification bronchovideoscopy (HMS), narrow band imaging (NBI), endobronchial ultrasonography (EBUS), optical coherence tomography (OCT) ([Clin Chest Med 2013;34:373](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23993810))
* Frequently encountered in resection specimens, but not often on endoscopic biopsy specimens

Prognostic factors

* Some preneoplastic lesions regress, while others progress
* No difference in progression rate and time to progression based on initial histologic grading; cannot differentiate the potentially more malignant lesions ([Clin Cancer Res 2005;11:537](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15701838))
* Persistence of dysplasia associated with development of invasive carcinoma ([Cancer Prev Res 2016;9:96](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26542061))
* Squamous dysplasia with high telomerase activity, increased Ki67 and p53 positivity tend to persist and might progress to carcinoma ([Lung Cancer 2004;46:187](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15474667))
* CIS is strong predictor of progression to invasive squamous cell carcinoma ([Cancer Metastasis Rev 2010;29:5](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20112052))

Case reports

* Three men ages 63, 65 and 71 years old with dysplastic lesions in bronchi which progressed to squamous cell carcinoma ([Br J Cancer 1997;75:678](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9043024))

Treatment

* Bronchoscopic followup of severe dysplasia and CIS, endobronchial or surgical techniques ([Chest 2007;132:221S](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17873170))

Gross description

* Either unremarkable mucosa or papillary and granular with loss of folds

Microscopic (histologic) description

* **Histological patterns of bronchial epithelial dysplasia**: basal cell dysplasia, columnar cell dysplasia, bronchial epithelial dysplasia with transitional differentiation and squamous dysplasia ([Mod Pathol 2006;19:429](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16415791))
* **Squamous dysplasia**: focal to full thickness replacement of epithelium by squamous cells with increased nuclear to cytoplasmic ratio, nuclear pleomorphism, mitotic activity but intact basement membrane
  + No invasive growth although may extend into ducts of submucosal glands
  + Graded with a 4 tier (mild / moderate / severe / CIS) or 2 tier system (low grade / high grade) ([J Clin Pathol 2001;54:257](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11304841))
  + **Mild dysplasia**: minimal abnormalities with basal expansion, increased cellularity, vertically oriented nuclei, limited to bottom third of epithelium, mitoses absent or rare, maturation present
  + **Moderate dysplasia**: more abnormalities, partial maturation, extending to lower two thirds of epithelium, mitoses limited to lower two thirds
  + **Severe dysplasia**: cellular pleomorphism, coarse chromatin, frequent nucleoli, basal zone to upper third, mitoses confined to lower two thirds, superficial cell flattening
  + **CIS**: lack of maturation, significant cytologic abnormalities, coarse chromatin, inconsistent nuclear orientation, mitoses present in full thickness
* **Diffuse idiopathic pulmonary neuroendocrine cell hyperplasia (DIPNECH):** preinvasive proliferation of pulmonary neuroendocrine cells
  + Proposed criteria for diagnosis on lung resection is multifocal neuroendocrine cell hyperplasia as defined by 5 or more pulmonary neuroendocrine cells in at least 3 separate small airways combined with 3 or more carcinoid tumorlets ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2015;32:438](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26472691), [Lung 2015;193:659](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26104490))

Positive stains

* Some cases of bronchial dysplasia show patchy or scattered staining for [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) and [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) ([Mod Pathol 2006;19:429](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16415791))
* [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) index significantly higher in severe dysplasia than in mild / moderate dysplasia ([Pol J Pathol 2015;66:38](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26017878))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Sequential changes in lung cancer pathogenesis include LOH, microsatellite alterations, telomerase dysregulation ([Clin Cancer Res 2001;7:5](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11205917), [Histopathology 2009;54:43](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19187179))
* Discordant genetics changes can exist in the same carcinogen exposed bronchial tissues ([Clin Cancer Res 2001;7:259](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11234877))

Differential diagnosis

* **Squamous metaplasia and reactive / reparative atypia**
* **Basal cell hyperplasia**
* [Squamous papilloma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorsquamouspapilloma.html)
