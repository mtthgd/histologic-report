Definition / general

* Low grade tumor
* Typically in (or close to) large airways
* Lobulated tumor with spindle and stellate cells arranged in lace-like strands and cords within prominent myxoid stroma

Essential features

* Endobronchial nodular mass with reticular / lace-like growth pattern and abundant myxoid stroma
* Expresses EMA and vimentin, negative for most other markers
* FISH positive for *EWSR1* rearrangement (85%)

Terminology

* Primary pulmonary myxoid sarcoma

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8842/3](https://seer.cancer.gov/archive/icd-o-3/errata.sitetype.20180117.pdf#page=2) - primary pulmonary myxoid sarcoma
* ICD-10: [C34.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C30-C39/C34-/C34.9) - malignant primary bronchiogenic / bronchogenic neoplasm

Epidemiology

* Mean age = 46.3 years; F:M = 1.4:1 (24 cases)
* 40% with cough, hemoptysis, chest pain; 20% present as incidental lung mass ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:1722](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21997693))

Sites

* Bilateral lungs, 76% within or close to a large bronchus ([Int J Surg Pathol 2017;25:518](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28449608))

Pathophysiology

* Probably originates from primitive mesenchymal cells with myofibroblastic or fibroblastic differentiation ([Virchows Arch 2014;465:453](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25134518))
* Controversial relationship to myxoid [angiomatoid fibrous histiocytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuefhangiomatoid.html) with similar *EWSR1* fusion genes ([Histopathology 2014;65:144](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24372335))
* Primary pulmonary myxoid sarcoma (PPMS) and myxoid variant of angiomatoid fibrous histiocytoma may represent a continuum with overlapping histologic, immunohistochemical and genetic features ([Histopathology 2014;65:144](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24372335), [Am J Surg Pathol 2020;44:1535](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32773530))

Etiology

* Smoking history in 80% ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:1722](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21997693))

Diagnosis

* Relies on histology, immunohistochemistry and FISH in biopsy or surgical resection

Radiology description

* Mass related to bronchus, predominantly endobronchial

Prognostic factors

* Patients with *EWSR1* rearrangement have favorable prognosis; wild type *EWSR1* portends poor clinical outcome ([Pol J Pathol 2017;68:261](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29363919))

Case reports

* 28 year old men and 66 year old woman with features overlapping with angiomatoid fibrous histiocytoma ([Histopathology 2014;65:144](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24372335))
* 29 year old woman with mass in a major fissure of the left lung without parenchymal invasion ([Thorac Cancer 2017;8:535](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28722216))
* 32 year old woman with tumor adjacent to the right main bronchus ([Pathology 2017;49:792](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29110880))
* 44 and 49 year old men with both components of PPMS and angiomatoid fibrous histiocytoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2020;44:1535](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32773530))
* 48 year old man with a huge mass extending into the right main bronchus ([Pol J Pathol 2017;68:261](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29363919))
* 80 year old woman with an endobronchial mass in the left main bronchus ([Int J Surg Pathol 2017;25:518](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28449608))

Treatment

* Surgical resection ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:1722](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21997693))

Gross description

* Generally < 4 cm, well circumscribed or nodular
* Glistening or gelatinous cut surface, white-gray to yellow ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:1722](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21997693))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Lobulated architecture, reticular or lace-like pattern with anastomosing cords and strands, abundant myxoid stroma, often lightly basophilic ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:1722](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21997693))
* Oval, spindle to polygonal cells with minimal to moderate atypia, infrequent mitotic figures with the majority < 5/10 high power fields
* Admixed lymphoplasmacytic infiltrate
* Some cells are chondrocyte-like or mimicking physaliferous cells in chordoma ([Diagn Pathol 2020;15:15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32039736))

Positive stains

* [Vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html), [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html) (weak focal) ([Pol J Pathol 2017;68:261](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29363919))
* Stroma is positive for [Alcian blue](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalcianblue.html) stain, which is sensitive to hyaluronidase treatment ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:1722](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21997693))

Negative stains

* [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html), [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [MSA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsactinmusclespecific.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) (rarely positive), [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html), [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html)

Electron microscopy description

* Focal dense plaques present in the plasma membrane, external lamina and intermediate junctions are rarely observed ([Virchows Arch 2014;465:453](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25134518))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* ~85% of tumors showed t(2;22)(q33;q12) with *EWSR1::CREB1* fusion gene detected by FISH or PCR ([Int J Surg Pathol 2017;25:518](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28449608))
* First report of a case with *EWSR1::ATF1* fusion gene ([Am J Surg Pathol 2020;44:1535](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32773530))
  + Second case ([Int J Surg Pathol 2022 Apr 24 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35466725))

Sample pathology report

* Lung, right lower lobe, lobectomy:
  + Primary pulmonary myxoid sarcoma (see comment)
  + Comment: H&E sections demonstrate a well circumscribed endobronchial tumor composed of lobules of spindled and stellate cells arranged in a reticular pattern within a myxoid background. A chronic inflammatory infiltrate is seen throughout the tumor. Immunohistochemically, the tumor cells are positive for EMA and negative for AE1 / AE3, CAM 5.2, pancytokeratin, p40, desmin, SMA, CD34, TTF1, KIT, vimentin, CD1a and S100. A FISH test for *EWSR1* was positive for rearrangement in the tumor cells. The morphology in combination with the FISH test result confirm the above diagnosis.

Differential diagnosis

* [Extraskeletal myxoid chondrosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueeskchondrosarcomamyxoid.html):
  + History of soft tissue primary, rarely arising intrathoracic as a primary
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)+
  + FISH positive for *EWSR1::NR4A3* fusion or others
* [Myxoid angiomatoid fibrous histiocytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuefhangiomatoid.html):
  + Peripheral lymphoid cuff
  + Whorled or storiform growth patterns common
  + 50% [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html)+
  + FISH positive for *EWSR1::ATF1* (more common), *EWSR1::CREB1* and *FUS::ATF1*
* [Inflammatory myofibroblastic tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorinflammatorypseudotumor.html):
  + Positive for [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) and [ALK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html)
* [Myoepithelioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticmyoepithelioma.html):
  + Clear and plasmacytoid cells
  + Positive for myoepithelial markers ([cytokeratins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [calponin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalponin.html) and [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html))

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/lungtumorprimpulmmyxsarcomagui06.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/lungtumorprimpulmmyxsarcomagui06.jpg)

Which of the following is a good prognostic factor for this primary pulmonary lung lesion?

1. Brisk lymphoplasmacytic infiltrate
2. FISH positive for *EWSR1* translocation
3. Lack of tumor necrosis
4. Middle age onset
5. Size > 5 cm

Board review style answer #1

**B.** FISH positive for *EWSR1* translocation. Primary pulmonary myxoid sarcoma tumors (shown in photo) with *EWSR1* rearrangement have a relatively good prognosis.

Board review style question #2

Which of the following entities is the closest mimicker of primary pulmonary myxoid sarcoma?

1. Epithelioid hemangioendothelioma
2. Extraskeletal myxoid chondrosarcoma
3. Inflammatory myofibroblastic tumor
4. Myoepithelioma
5. Myxoid angiomatoid fibrous histiocytoma

Board review style answer #2

**E.** Myxoid angiomatoid fibrous histiocytoma. Myxoid angiomatoid fibrous histiocytoma and primary pulmonary myxoid sarcoma have many overlapping features.
