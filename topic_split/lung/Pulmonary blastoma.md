Definition / general

* Rare lung malignancy, 0.25 - 1% of lung cancers
* Biphasic components with admixed epithelium and primitive stroma
* Highly aggressive type of sarcomatoid carcinomas ([BMC Res Notes 2014;7:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24885892))

Essential features

* Peripheral solitary large mass, well circumscribed, unencapsulated
* Well differentiated glandular component and blastematous stroma

Terminology

* Separated from fetal adenocarcinoma (epithelium only) and pleuropulmonary blastoma (mesenchymal only)

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8972/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=83) - pulmonary blastoma

Epidemiology

* Most common in fifth decade
* M:F = 1:1
* References: [Lung Cancer 2011;73:127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21513998), [Adv Respir Med 2021;89:511](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34725809)

Sites

* All lobes can be affected with upper lobe predominance ([Lung Cancer 2011;73:127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21513998))

Pathophysiology

* Both components derived from single pluripotent cell clone ([Lung Cancer 2011;73:127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21513998))
* *TP53* and *CTNNB1* mutations detected ([Hum Pathol 1996;27:1117](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8912818), [Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:921](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15223963))
* Rare mutations such as *EGFR, MET, BRAF* and *DICER1* mutation detected ([Onco Targets Ther 2016;9:6915](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27877056), [Eur Respir J 2016;47:1879](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27126690))

Etiology

* 80% associated with smoking ([BMC Res Notes 2014;7:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24885892))

Clinical features

* Nonspecific, 40% asymptomatic ([Cancer 1991;67:2368](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1849449))

Diagnosis

* Relies on tissue histology and IHC on biopsy or surgical resection
* Preoperative biopsy shows cytology of 2 cell patterns
* References: [Lung Cancer 2011;73:127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21513998), [Adv Respir Med 2021;89:511](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34725809)

Radiology description

* Large solitary peripheral mass with variable enhancement and necrosis ([Lung Cancer 2011;73:127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21513998))
* 25% endobronchial growth ([Respir Med 2006;100:1174](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16332433))

Prognostic factors

* Poor prognosis, 5 year survival 16% ([Lung Cancer 2011;73:127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21513998))
* Poor prognostic factors: tumor recurrence, metastasis at initial presentation and size > 5 cm ([Lung Cancer 2011;73:127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21513998))

Case reports

* 16 year old girl with a large, well defined, heterogeneously enhancing mass measuring 11 cm in right upper lobe ([Case Rep Med 2012;2012:471613](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23049565))
* 66 year old woman with smoking history presented with a mass in the posteromedial left hemithorax measuring 16 cm ([Ann Thorac Surg 2019;107:e345](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30365956))
* 67 year old male smoker with 9 cm necrotic mass from apical segments of right lower lobe ([BMC Res Notes 2014;7:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24885892))
* 68 year old man with tumor of 5.5 cm in the left upper lobe of the lung ([Case Rep Oncol Med 2015;2015:842621](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26075125))
* 71 year old female past smoker with 7 cm tumor in lower lobe ([Crit Rev Oncol Hematol 2018;125:48](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29650276))

Treatment

* Surgical resection ([Lung Cancer 2011;73:127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21513998))
* Chemoradiation and neoadjuvant therapy have limited responses in a few cases ([Lung Cancer 2011;73:127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21513998))
* Adjuvant chemotherapy cisplatin combined with etoposide may improve survival ([Cureus 2018;10:e3586](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30656089), [Cancer 1998;82:462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9452262))
* Multikinase inhibitor reported having good response in case report ([J Int Med Res 2020;48:300060520962394](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33107372))

Gross description

* Mostly peripheral, large, well circumscribed, unencapsulated mass, average 9.1 cm ([Lung Cancer 2011;73:127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21513998))
* Lobulated with cystic, necrotic and hemorrhagic degeneration

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Glandular branching tubules resembling fetal lung, lined by pseudostratified columnar cells with subnuclear and supranuclear glycogen vacuoles and little nuclear atypia ([Lung Cancer 2011;73:127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21513998))
* Loose undifferentiated mesenchymal stroma with variable cellular atypia, possible heterologous differentiation (skeletal muscle, cartilage and bone) ([Lung Cancer 2011;73:127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21513998))
* Morules and neuroendocrine cells in 67% of cases ([Cancer 1991;67:2368](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1849449))
* May have germ cell tumor or melanoma component ([J Thorac Oncol 2008;3:1185](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18827617), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 1990;114:1076](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2222150))

Electron microscopy description

* Nuclear filaments and biotin rich products contribute to nuclear clearing ([Ultrastruct Pathol 1995;19:501](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8597206))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Exon 3 of *CTNNB1* missense mutations, associated with morule formation ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:921](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15223963), [J Pathol 2003;200:214](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12754743))
* 42% with *TP53* mutation ([Hum Pathol 1996;27:1117](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8912818))

Sample pathology report

* Lung, right lower lobe, lobectomy:
  + Pulmonary blastoma, 9 cm, margins negative for tumor (see comment)
  + Comment: H&E sections demonstrate an admixture of malignant epithelial and mesenchymal components. Immunostains performed with adequate controls reveal that the glandular cells are strongly positive for pancytokeratin and TTF1. The stromal cells are vimentin positive. Beta catenin reveals nuclear staining in both epithelial and stromal components. The findings are consistent with the diagnosis of pulmonary blastoma, a subgroup of sarcomatoid carcinoma.

Differential diagnosis

* [Fetal type adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumoradenofetal.html):
  + Epithelial component only, no blastematous stroma
* [Pleuropulmonary blastoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorPPB.html):
  + Pediatric tumor, most diagnosed before 4 years of age
  + No epithelial component; admixture of blastematous and sarcomatoid components
  + Epithelium (if seen) is benign entrapped respiratory type
  + 40% with *DICER1* mutation
* [Biphasic synovial sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesynovialsarc.html):
  + Glandular component lacks [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html)
  + Focal [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), [TLE1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstle1.html) positive, *SS18::SSX* fusion present
* [Carcinosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorcarcinosarcoma.html):
  + Non-small cell carcinoma (squamous cell carcinoma or adenocarcinoma) and heterologous sarcomatous component
  + Older age
  + Absence of morules and no nuclear staining of [beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html)
