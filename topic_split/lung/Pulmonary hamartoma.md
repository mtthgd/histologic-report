Definition / general

* Benign neoplasm composed of varying amounts of mesenchymal elements (cartilage, fat, connective tissue, smooth muscle) with entrapped respiratory epithelium
* At least 2 mesenchymal elements should be present for diagnosis

Essential features

* Most common benign pulmonary neoplasm, often incidentally found
* Benign neoplasm composed of varying amounts of at least 2 mesenchymal elements combined with entrapped respiratory epithelium
* Radiographic features often very characteristic

Terminology

* Other names previously used in the literature (chondroid hamartoma, mesenchymoma, chondromatous hamartoma, hamartochondroma) are no longer recommended

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8992/0](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26291008) - pulmonary hamartoma
* ICD-11: [2F00.Y & XH3UD9](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f2043813445%2fmms%2fother?data=%7B%22dataType%22%3A%22pc%22%2C%22postcoordinationCodeSet%22%3A%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F2043813445%2Fmms%2Fother%22%2C%22axisToValueIds%22%3A%7B%22histopathology%22%3A%5B%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F491922339%22%7D%5D%7D%7D%7D) - other specified benign neoplasm of middle ear or respiratory system & pulmonary hamartoma

Epidemiology

* M > F
* Peak in the sixth decade
* Rare in children
* References: [Thorax 1987;42:790](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3321538), [StatPearls: Pulmonary Hamartoma [Accessed 5 November 2021]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30969628), [Mayo Clin Proc 1996;71:14](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8538225)

Sites

* Any lobe can be involved
* Most located in peripheral lung ([Thorax 1987;42:790](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3321538))
* Approximately 10% are endobronchial ([Thorax 1987;42:790](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3321538))
* Can present as multiple lesions, adjacent to lung cancer, as a mediastinal mass or incidentally with another neoplasm ([Am J Clin Pathol 2021;155:903](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33258901), [Clin Lab 2021;67:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33616322))

Pathophysiology

* Most hamartomas appear to harbor translocations, often t(3;12) ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2000;29:363](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11066083), [Cancer Genet Cytogenet 2003;142:153](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12699895))
* Translocation t(3;12) results in HMGA2-LPP fusion
* Fusion transcripts not universally detected in hamartomas ([Cancer Genet Cytogenet 2002;138:160](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12505264))
* Evidence suggests that HMGA2-LPP fusion protein may promote chondrogenesis and adipogenesis ([Biochem Biophys Res Commun 2006;340:476](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16375854))

Etiology

* Thought to arise from mesenchymal tissue
* Chromosomal aberrations may be a primary event in pulmonary hamartoma but unclear what other genetic events may be necessary to induce tumor development ([Cancer Genet Cytogenet 2002;138:160](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12505264))

Clinical features

* Often incidental finding on imaging
* Usually asymptomatic but endobronchial tumors may cause problems associated with obstruction ([Cureus 2021;13:e13717](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33833928), [BMJ Case Rep 2020;13:e235316](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32843453))

Diagnosis

* Usually incidental finding on imaging
* Biopsy can be performed when lesion lacks characteristic radiographic findings
* Bronchoscopic biopsy (core or fine needle aspiration [FNA]) can be used (see [Cytology description](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorhamartoma.html#lungtumorhamartomacytology) below)
* Wedge resection can be used if nonsurgical diagnostic procedures are inconclusive
* Preoperative diagnosis may not be made as hamartoma may be incidentally discovered in a lung removed for another reason
* References: [Zhongguo Fei Ai Za Zhi 2020;23:792](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32773007), [Ann Thorac Surg 2008;86:1769](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19021972), [Mayo Clin Proc 1996;71:14](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8538225)

Radiology description

* Small coin lesion, often solitary, characterized by smooth edge, focal fat or fat alternating with calcific foci ([Ann Thorac Med 2015;10:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26664560))
* Calcifications often described as popcorn calcification
* Intralesional fat and popcorn-like calcifications allow a confident diagnosis on CT ([J Thorac Imaging 2016;31:11](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26447871))

Prognostic factors

* Slowly growing with excellent prognosis; most left alone if patient is asymptomatic
* Rapid growth, recurrence and malignant transformation are very rare ([Cureus 2020;12:e12150](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33489562), [Thorax 1989;44:232](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2705156), [Case Rep Med 2013;2013:231652](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24171003))

Case reports

* 30 year old man presenting with hemoptysis and cough ([Rare Tumors 2019;11:2036361318823926](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30719262))
* 49 year old man with recurrent hemoptysis and clinical and radiographic concern for lung cancer ([Cureus 2019;11:e5489](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31656715))
* 54 year old man presenting with hemoptysis and CT scan identified lung nodule ([Respirol Case Rep 2020;8:e00553](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32257217))
* 59 year old woman with pulmonary hamartoma presenting with unusual radiographic features ([Respir Med Case Rep 2018;25:133](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30128273))
* 59 year old woman with incidental hamartoma and tibial enchondroma ([BMJ Case Rep 2019;12:e229670](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31527201))

Treatment

* Surveillance for biopsy proven asymptomatic pulmonary hamartoma
* Surgical resection is the optimal and curative treatment for endobronchial lesions and symptomatic tumors
* Enucleation and wedge resections are the most common surgical choice
* References: [Interact Cardiovasc Thorac Surg 2015;21:773](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26409563), [Chin Med Sci J 1998;13:61](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11717928)

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Composed predominantly of varying degrees of mesenchymal tissue: hyaline cartilage, fat, smooth muscle and bone
* Other connective tissue elements may be present, represented by bland spindle cells, fibrous tissue or myxoid change
* Variably conspicuous, entrapped benign epithelial cells
* Reference: [Thorax 1987;42:790](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3321538)

Pulmonary hamartoma

Cytology description

* FNA is highly reliable means of diagnosis ([Cytopathology 2008;19:185](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17388933))
* Misclassification as malignancy can occur, especially at the time of immediate adequacy assessment, prior to review of all cytologic preparations ([Cytopathology 2008;19:185](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17388933), [BMC Pulm Med 2003;3:2](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12962550))
* FNA often shows fibromyxoid tissue, fat or cartilage associated with benign reactive epithelial cells
* Lack of cytologic atypia and bland chromatin should point towards a benign diagnosis ([Acta Cytol 2009;53:201](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19365977))

Positive stains

* Immunohistochemical markers are not typically needed to confirm diagnosis
* Immunohistochemical profile should be typical for the mesenchymal tissue being stained
* Interestingly, sex steroid receptors ([ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html), [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html), [AR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsAR.html)) are often expressed ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:819](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16819323))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Often not needed for diagnostic purposes
* High frequency of translocation t(3;12)(q27-28;q14-15) resulting in gene fusion of the *HMGA2* and *LPP* genes (see [Pathophysiology](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorhamartoma.html#lungtumorhamartomapathophysiology) and [Etiology](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorhamartoma.html#lungtumorhamartomaetiology) above)

Sample pathology report

* Lung, left, needle core biopsy:
  + Pulmonary hamartoma (see comment)
  + Comment: The histologic sections reveal the presence of a benign pulmonary lesion composed of hyaline cartilage, fibroadipose tissue, bone and bland spindle cells in myxoid stroma. The mesenchymal elements appear to entrap benign respiratory epithelium. These findings support the above diagnosis.
* Lung, right, wedge resection:
  + Pulmonary hamartoma (3.2 cm) (see comment)
  + Comment: The histologic sections reveal the presence of a well circumscribed benign pulmonary lesion composed of hyaline cartilage, fibroadipose tissue, smooth muscle and bland spindle cells set in fibromyxoid stroma. The mesenchymal elements appear to entrap benign respiratory epithelium. These findings support the above diagnosis.

Differential diagnosis

* **Pulmonary chondroma**:
  + Lacks entrapped epithelium
  + Does not contain secondary mesenchymal elements
  + Often seen in patients with [Carney triad](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesarcomasyndromes.html)
* Other **soft tissue tumors** (including [leiomyoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueleiomyoma.html) and [lipoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueadiposelipoma.html)):
  + Only 1 mesenchymal component may be sampled at the biopsy
  + Radiographic (popcorn calcifications and fat) findings may help suggest the diagnosis of hamartoma
* **Primary** / [metastatic sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumormetastases.html):
  + Cytologic atypia and features of necrosis should be present for diagnosis of sarcoma
