Clinical features

* Zoonoses are diseases and infections naturally transmitted between people and vertebrate animals ([WHO: Zoonotic Disease: Emerging Public Health Threats in the Region [Accessed 18 August 2021]](https://www.emro.who.int/fr/about-who/rc61/zoonotic-diseases.html))
* 3 classes:
  1. Endemic zoonoses which are present in many places and affect many people and animals
  2. Epidemic zoonoses which are sporadic
  3. Emerging and re-emerging zoonoses which are newly appearing in a population or have existed previously but are rapidly increasing in incidence

Hantavirus

* Zoonosis transmitted by feces from infected rodents ([Emerg Infect Dis 2009;15:561](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19331732))
* Severe pulmonary disease, often in Southwest US; 25 - 35% mortality
* Symptoms eventually resolve in almost all survivors ([Lung 2010;188;387](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20524006))
* Epidemic disease occurred in Southwestern US in 1993 ([Centers for Disease Control: The Detailed Story of Hantavirus Pulmonary Syndrome (HPS) [Accessed 18 August 2021]](https://www.cdc.gov/hantavirus/outbreaks/history.html))
  + Sporadic cases have occurred since then
* Diagnosis confirmed by immunohistochemistry, serology, PCR, electron microscopy
* Reference: [Wikipedia: Orthohantavirus [Accessed 18 August 2021]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hantavirus)

Rhodococcus

* Zoonotic infection originally identified in lungs of foals; also cattle, swine, sheep, deer, goats, dogs, cats, other species
* Worldwide distribution in water and soil, especially near feces of herbivores
* Infection by inhalation or ingestion
* Resides primarily within lung macrophages of infected patients, also some alveolar cells ([Microbes Infect 2011;13:438](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21262372))
* *R. equi* is most common human pathogen of the Rhodococci
* Generally occurs in AIDS patients (may cause malakoplakia) ([Clin Infect Dis 1999;28:1334](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10451186))
  + Also other immunodeficient patients; very rarely in immunocompetent
* Incidence decreasing, likely due to HAART therapy and azithromycin prophylaxis
* Rhodo since salmon pink pigment in culture
* Treated with antibiotics but mortality is 50% in AIDS patients, 20% in other immunocompromised and 10% in immunocompetent

Case reports

* 15 year old girl with congenital AIDS and cavitary lesion ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2003;127:e315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12823069))
* 68 year old man with fatal lung infection post chemotherapy for T-PLL ([Emerg Infect Dis 2007;13:1942](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18258054))
* 68 year old man with pneumonia post heart transplant ([University of Pittsburgh: Case 146 [Accessed 18 August 2021]](https://path.upmc.edu/cases/case146.html))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Hantavirus: interstitial pneumonitis with mononuclear infiltrate, edema, focal hyaline membranes, pleura effusions; atypical lymphocytes in pulmonary vasculature may mimic lymphoma
* Rhodococcus: epithelioid macrophages containing gram positive, partially acid fast coccobacilli that resemble mycobacteria; may produce malakoplakia
