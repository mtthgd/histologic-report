Definition / general

* Rheumatoid lung disease refers to a group of pulmonary conditions seen in patients with rheumatoid arthritis, including interstitial lung disease, pleuritis and rheumatoid nodules (RN)
* These conditions result from immune system driven inflammation and fibrosis, which are secondary to the systemic autoimmune nature of rheumatoid arthritis (RA)

Essential features

* Rheumatoid lung disease includes interstitial lung disease, pleural disease and pulmonary nodules ([Clin Rheumatol 2019;38:3041](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31273637))
* Strong association with seropositive rheumatoid arthritis (rheumatoid factor [RF] and anti-cyclic citrullinated peptide [CCP] positive) ([Multidiscip Respir Med 2022;17:877](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36507116))
* Clinically significant interstitial lung disease is more common in men, smokers and older rheumatoid arthritis patients ([Biomed Res Int 2013;2013:759760](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24205507))
* Diagnosis of pulmonary rheumatoid nodules can be challenging due to overlapping clinical, radiological and histopathological features with necrotizing infectious granulomatous diseases (i.e., histoplasmosis, coccidioidomycosis, mycobacteria), to which patients with rheumatoid arthritis are often more susceptible ([Biomed Res Int 2017;2017:7915340](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29238722))
* Pulmonary rheumatoid nodules may also mimic other noninfectious granulomatous diseases (i.e., granulomatosis with polyangiitis) ([Korean J Radiol 2021;22:1416](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34132073))

Terminology

* Parenchymal lung disease in patients with rheumatoid arthritis
* Rheumatoid arthritis associated interstitial lung disease (RA ILD)
* Pulmonary rheumatoid nodule

ICD coding

* ICD-11: [CB05.1 & FA20](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#1294786485%20%2F%20576319925) - interstitial lung diseases associated with connective tissue diseases & rheumatoid arthritis

Epidemiology

* Incidence
  + The most prevalent extra-articular manifestation of rheumatoid arthritis is lung involvement, which may affect up to 60% of individuals with rheumatoid arthritis ([Respir Med 2012;106:1441](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22795504))
  + Pulmonary complications of rheumatoid arthritis account for 10 - 20% of deaths ([J Rheumatol Suppl 2004;69:3](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15053445))
  + In 10 - 20% of patients with rheumatoid arthritis, lung involvement is the first manifestation of disease ([Biomed Res Int 2017;2017:7915340](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29238722))
* Risk factors
  + Active or previous cigarette smoking ([Biomed Res Int 2013;2013:759760](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24205507), [J Rheumatol 2021;48:656](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33191286))
  + Positive rheumatoid factor and anti-cyclic citrullinated peptide antibodies ([Multidiscip Respir Med 2022;17:877](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36507116))
  + Male sex ([Biomed Res Int 2013;2013:759760](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24205507))
  + Age over 60 years old ([Biomed Res Int 2013;2013:759760](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24205507), [J Bras Pneumol 2022;48:e20220145](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36477171))
  + Obesity (body mass index [BMI] ≥ 30) ([J Rheumatol 2021;48:656](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33191286))

Sites

* Lungs (parenchyma, pleura and airways)
* Less commonly, pulmonary vasculature

Pathophysiology

* Disease is driven by chronic immune dysregulation, with autoantibody production (rheumatoid factor and anti-cyclic citrullinated peptide) leading to systemic inflammation
* This results in tissue damage, immune complex deposition and progressive fibrosis in the lungs
* Smoking and environmental exposures may exacerbate or trigger immune activity in genetically predisposed individuals
* Reference: [Int J Mol Sci 2023;24:14509](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37833957)

Etiology

* Precise mechanisms of rheumatoid lung disease are not fully understood
* Genetic factors, such as HLA-DRB1 alleles, interact with environmental exposures like smoking to contribute to lung involvement ([Arthritis Rheumatol 2019;71:1504](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30742363))
* Excessive immune activation in rheumatoid arthritis involves autoantibodies such as rheumatoid factor and anticitrullinated protein antibodies (ACPA), leading to tissue damage ([Semin Respir Crit Care Med 2014;35:222](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24668537), [Int J Mol Sci 2023;24:14509](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37833957))
* Cycle of systemic inflammation perpetuates lung tissue injury in rheumatoid arthritis, driven by autoimmune processes and persistent immune activation ([Semin Respir Crit Care Med 2014;35:222](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24668537))

Clinical features

* Symptoms include progressive dyspnea, chronic cough, chest pain and occasionally hemoptysis
* Extrapulmonary symptoms of rheumatoid arthritis (e.g., joint pain, stiffness and nodules) often coexist
* Associations include rheumatoid arthritis related complications, such as pericarditis, vasculitis and Sjögren syndrome
* Pleural involvement can manifest as pleuritic chest pain or recurrent pleural effusions
* Severe or untreated disease may lead to respiratory failure or pulmonary hypertension
* Reference: [Cureus 2022;14:e22367](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35345761)

Diagnosis

* Biopsy
  + Lung biopsy can reveal various histopathological patterns of diffuse parenchymal (including interstitial) disease, rheumatoid nodules and pleural inflammation and fibrosis
* Clinical evaluation
  + Detailed history focused on rheumatoid arthritis diagnosis, smoking status and respiratory symptoms
* Imaging
  + High resolution computed tomography (HRCT) of the chest is the gold standard for identification of interstitial lung disease patterns and nodules ([Semin Respir Crit Care Med 2014;35:222](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24668537))
* Pulmonary function tests
  + Often show restrictive patterns with reduced diffusion capacity ([Arthritis Rheumatol 2021;73:1994](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33982900))
* Bronchoscopy
  + May exclude infection or malignancy in cases with atypical findings ([J Thorac Dis 2017;9:S996](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29214060))

Laboratory

* Positive rheumatoid factor and anti-cyclic citrullinated peptide antibodies are suggestive of rheumatoid arthritis ([Semin Respir Crit Care Med 2014;35:222](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24668537))

Radiology description

* Interstitial lung disease
  + High resolution CT is the gold standard for identification of interstitial lung disease patterns in rheumatoid arthritis patients
  + CT findings suggestive of interstitial lung disease include ground glass opacities, reticular patterns, honeycombing, usual interstitial pneumonia (UIP) pattern and subpleural fibrosis ([Chest 2009;136:1397](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19892679), [Radiology 2004;232:81](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15166329), [Semin Respir Crit Care Med 2024;45:287](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38631369))
* Rheumatoid nodules
  + Can appear as circular peripheral or cavitary lesions on CT
  + Rheumatoid lung nodules are best identified when ≥ 3 CT features are present: ≥ 4 nodules, peripheral location, cavitation, satellite nodules, smooth border or subpleural rind ([Eur Radiol 2019;29:1684](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30288558))
* Bronchiectasis
  + Affects > 20% of patients with rheumatoid arthritis when assessed with CT ([Semin Arthritis Rheum 2021;51:1067](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34450505))
* Bronchiolitis obliterans
  + Mosaic attenuation and centrilobular nodules may be present on CT ([BMC Pulm Med 2018;18:105](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29929518))
* Pleural disease
  + Pleural thickening suggestive of pleuritis, pleural effusions (exudative) and pleural calcifications may be present on CT ([Cureus 2022;14:e22367](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35345761))

Prognostic factors

* Favorable
  + Early detection and management of rheumatoid arthritis and lung involvement
  + Stable or mild interstitial lung disease patterns
  + Nonsmokers or individuals who quit smoking
* Not favorable ([Semin Arthritis Rheum 2022;55:152004](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35472663))
  + Male sex
  + Usual interstitial pneumonia pattern
  + High levels of rheumatoid factor IgA

Case reports

* 35 year old woman with seropositive rheumatoid arthritis for 10 years presented with asymptomatic lung nodules on imaging ([Cureus 2024;16:e52350](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38361708))
* 49 year old man with pulmonary rheumatoid nodules mimicking tuberculosis ([Int J Rheum Dis 2024;27:e14904](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37784218))
* 80 year old man with seronegative rheumatoid arthritis presenting with pulmonary rheumatoid nodules and scleritis ([Oxf Med Case Reports 2023;2023:omac155](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36694604))

Treatment

* Rheumatoid arthritis treatment
  + Disease modifying antirheumatic drugs (DMARDs), including methotrexate, are standard treatment, although methotrexate can sometimes exacerbate lung disease
  + Biologic agents (e.g., TNF inhibitors) may be used but require caution due to infection risk
* Interstitial lung disease treatment
  + Corticosteroids and immunosuppressants (e.g., azathioprine, mycophenolate) for inflammatory lung disease
  + Antifibrotic agents (e.g., nintedanib, pirfenidone) for progressive pulmonary fibrosis
  + Oxygen therapy and pulmonary rehabilitation for advanced disease
* Supportive care
  + Smoking cessation is essential
  + Management of complications, such as infections, pleural effusions or pulmonary hypertension
  + Lung transplantation for end stage disease in select cases
* Reference: [J Clin Med 2020;9:1082](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32290218)

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Characteristic features of pulmonary rheumatoid nodules ([Biomed Res Int 2017;2017:7915340](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29238722), [Clin Rheumatol 2019;38:3041](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31273637), [Eur Radiol 2019;29:1684](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30288558))
  + Palisading histiocytes
  + Cavitation
  + Multiplicity
  + Eosinophilic and basophilic necrosis
  + Subpleural location
* Characteristic features of the rim of rheumatoid nodules ([Clin Rheumatol 2019;38:3041](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31273637))
  + Chronic inflammatory infiltrate
  + Fibrosis
* Characteristic features of lung parenchyma surrounding nodules ([Biomed Res Int 2017;2017:7915340](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29238722), [Semin Respir Crit Care Med 2014;35:201](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24668535))
  + Follicular bronchiolitis
  + Lymphoid aggregates
* Pleural disease ([Rheum Dis Clin North Am 2015;41:225](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25836639))
  + Chronic pleuritis with fibrous thickening, lymphoplasmacytic infiltrates and organizing exudates
* Usual interstitial pneumonia ([J Clin Pathol 2013;66:896](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23703852), [Chest 2009;136:1397](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19892679), [J Clin Med 2019;8:2038](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31766446), [Rheum Dis Clin North Am 2015;41:225](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25836639))
  + Patchy interstitial fibrosis with architectural distortion and honeycombing
  + Fibroblast foci and temporal heterogeneity
  + Subpleural and basal predominance
* Nonspecific interstitial pneumonia (NSIP) ([Chest 2009;136:1397](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19892679), [J Clin Med 2019;8:2038](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31766446))
  + Relative preservation of lung architecture without honeycombing
    - Fibrotic NSIP: uniform interstitial thickening with dense or loose fibrosis
    - Cellular NSIP: with chronic inflammatory cell infiltrates
    - Mixed with both fibrosis and chronic interstitial inflammation
* Organizing pneumonia (OP) ([J Clin Med 2019;8:2038](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31766446), [Reumatologia 2017;55:314](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29491540))
  + Intra-alveolar plugs of organizing connective tissue (Masson bodies)
  + Preserved underlying lung architecture
  + Mild interstitial chronic inflammation

Sample pathology report

* Lung, left upper and lower lobes, wedge biopsy:
  + Pulmonary fibrosis with usual interstitial pneumonia pattern and background lung with lymphoid hyperplasia and follicular bronchiolitis (see comment)
  + Comment: The association of pulmonary fibrosis with a usual interstitial pneumonia pattern and lymphoid hyperplasia is consistent with this patient's known connective tissue disease. The differential includes a drug reaction. Clinical correlation with this patient's medications is suggested.

Differential diagnosis

* For rheumatoid nodule
  + [Granulomatosis with polyangiitis (GPA)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungnontumorgranulomatosiswithpolyangiitis.html):
    - Microscopy: parenchymal necrosis, vasculitis and necrotizing granulomatous inflammation (with geographic borders) accompanied by inflammatory infiltrate that includes neutrophils, lymphocytes, plasma cells, macrophages, giant cells and eosinophils ([Am J Surg Pathol 1991;15:315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2006712), [Semin Respir Crit Care Med 2004;25:475](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16088493))
  + **Infectious granulomas**:
    - Microscopy: necrotizing and nonnecrotizing granulomas with infectious organisms on special stains ([Mod Pathol 2012;25:S31](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22214968), [Eur J Rheumatol 2018;5:65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30501854))
      * Examples: [histoplasmosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungnontumorhistoplasma.html), [coccidioidomycosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungnontumorcoccidio.html) and [mycobacteria](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/microbiologymyconontb.html)
      * Special stains (e.g., [GMS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgms.html), [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html), [AFB](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsacidfast.html)) highlight fungal or bacterial presence
    - Superimposed fungal colonization is a rare complication of rheumatoid nodules ([J Clin Pathol 2003;56:636](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12890823))
* For interstitial lung disease
  + [Idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis (IPF)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungnontumorUIP.html):
    - Microscopy: usual interstitial pneumonia pattern, with fibroblastic foci, dense fibrosis and honeycomb change ([Chest 2009;136:1397](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19892679))
  + [Sarcoidosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungnontumorsarcoidosis.html):
    - Microscopy: predominantly well formed, nonnecrotizing granulomas in a lymphangitic distribution and minimal other parenchymal change (e.g., organizing pneumonia); it may be systemic ([Eur Respir Rev 2017;26:170012](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28794143))
    - Sarcoidosis and rheumatoid lung disease can co-occur ([BMJ Case Rep 2015;2015:bcr2014208803](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26150618), [Ther Adv Respir Dis 2023;17:17534666231158279](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36872912))
  + [Pneumoconiosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungnontumorcoalworker.html):
    - Microscopy: nodules or macules with coal dust, silica, etc.; fibrosis with asbestos bodies (asbestosis) ([Eur Respir Rev 2015;24:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25726549))
    - Caplan syndrome: rheumatoid nodules with dust from mining ([Thorax 1953;8:29](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/13038735))

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/lungrheumatoidtazelaar03.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/lungrheumatoidtazelaar03.jpg)

A 58 year old woman presents with bilateral fibrotic lung disease, evenly distributed between the upper and lower lobes. On physical examination, she exhibits proximal joint swelling and pain. Laboratory findings show elevated rheumatoid factor (RF) and cyclic citrullinated peptide (CCP) antibodies. A lung biopsy reveals the following findings exhibited in the image shown above. Which description best describes the findings of this lung biopsy?

1. Diffuse alveolar damage
2. Lymphocytic interstitial pneumonia
3. Nonspecific interstitial pneumonia
4. Organizing pneumonia
5. Usual interstitial pneumonia

Board review style answer #1

**E**. Usual interstitial pneumonia (UIP) is the most common pattern in rheumatoid arthritis associated interstitial lung disease (this patient likely has rheumatoid arthritis), characterized by normal lung interspersed with patchy fibrosis, honeycombing and fibroblastic foci. Answer A is incorrect because diffuse alveolar damage is associated with hyaline membranes, which are not seen in this case. Answer B is incorrect because organizing pneumonia has fibroblastic plugs filling alveoli and small airways. Board review style answer #2

**C**. High resolution computed tomography (HRCT) is the gold standard for evaluating RA ILD. It provides detailed imaging of lung parenchymal changes like fibrosis and ground glass opacities. Answer A is incorrect because a chest radiograph (Xray) is less sensitive than HRCT in detecting lung changes associated with RA ILD. Answer B is incorrect because echocardiography assesses cardiac function and pulmonary hypertension but does not evaluate interstitial lung disease. Answer D is incorrect because pulmonary angiography is used to evaluate pulmonary vascular conditions. Answer E is incorrect because ventilation perfusion scan evaluates ventilation - perfusion mismatch, not interstitial lung disease.
