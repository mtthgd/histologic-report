Definition / general

* A poly (ADP-ribose) polymerase (PARP) inhibitor blocks PARP1 and PARP2, thereby preventing repair of DNA damage
* Developed by Abbvie
* Synonym: ABT-888, 912444-00-9, CHEBI:62880, 2-[(R)-2-methylpyrrolidin-2-yl]-1H-benzimidazole-4-carboxamide, veliparib

Trade name

* Veliparib®

Indications

* In 2016, U.S. Food and Drug Administration awarded orphan drug designation for treating advanced squamous cell carcinoma of lung ([FDA: Search Orphan Drug Designations and Approvals [Accessed 17 July 2020]](https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/scripts/opdlisting/oopd))

Pathophysiology

* Base excision repair and homologous recombination mediated double strand breaks system responsible for the repair of damaged DNA ([Figure 1, panel A](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/#drugsolaparibdiagram2))
* In cells with *BRCA1/2* mutated cells, base excision repair and other DNA repair mechanisms compensate for the nonfunctional homologous recombination ([Figure 1, panel B](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/#drugsolaparibdiagram2))
* In cells with nonfunctioning base excision repair because of PARP1 inhibition, at least one copy of *BRCA1/2* with functioning homologous recombination can repair the damaged DNA ([Figure 1, panel C](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/#drugsolaparibdiagram2))
* In mutated *BRCA1/2* cancer cells, PARP inhibitors prevents the base excision repair leading to cell death ([Figure 1, panel D](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/#drugsolaparibdiagram2))
* Reference:  [Mod Pathol 2020;33:944](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19553640), see [diagram](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/#drugsolaparibdiagram2) below

Clinical information

* Phase II clinical trials for treating metastatic melanoma, breast cancer, non small cell lung cancer, castration resistant prostate cancer and pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma ([ClinicalTrials: Veliparib - List Results [Accessed 17 July 2020]](https://clinicaltrials.gov/ct2/results?term=veliparib))
* Phase III ongoing clinical trials to treat non small cell lung cancer, triple negative
  BRCA breast cancer, advanced ovarian cancer and glioblastoma
  ([ClinicalTrials: Veliparib - List Results [Accessed 17 July 2020]](https://clinicaltrials.gov/ct2/results?term=veliparib))
* Clinical trials of chemotherapeutics in combination with doxorubicin, temozolomide, topotecan, carboplatin, paclitaxel, pemetrexed, cyclophosphamide, gemcitabine for treating advanced myeloproliferative disorders, acute myeloid leukemia, metastatic pancreatic cancer, locally advanced rectal cancer
  ([National Cancer Institute: Clinical Trials Using Veliparib [Accessed 17 July 2020]](https://www.cancer.gov/about-cancer/treatment/clinical-trials/intervention/veliparib))

Uses by pathologists

* None at this time

Side effects

* Gastrointestinal: nausea, vomiting
* Fatigue
* Myelosuppression (anemia, neutropenia, thrombocytopenia)
* Secondary leukemia and myelodysplastic syndrome (late serious effects)
* Reference: [Onco Targets Ther 2015;8:1931](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26251615)

Drug administration

* Drug taken by mouth

Board review style answer #1
