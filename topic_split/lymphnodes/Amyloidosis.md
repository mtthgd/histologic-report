Definition / general

* Usually secondary; rarely localized splenic nodules
* Even with diffuse involvement, spleen may still retain "pitting" function that prevents appearance of Howell-Jolly bodies in peripheral blood smears
  ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1995;119:252](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7887779))
* Rarely can result in splenic rupture ([Am J Hematol 2003;74:131](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14508801))

Case reports

* 69 year old man with coexisting malignant GIST of stomach
  ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2003;127:470](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12683877))
* Amyloid tumor in lymphoma patient
  ([Am J Surg Pathol 1987;11:723](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3307463))

Gross description

* Firm and waxy consistency

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Deposition of pink amorphous material (amyloid) limited either to germinal follicles (sago spleen) or splenic sinusoids and surrounding connective tissue (lardaceous spleen)

Positive stains

* [Congo red](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscongored.html) with characteristic birefringence (apple green)

Differential diagnosis

* **Hyaline adventitial thickening of splenic vessels** (normal, [AIDS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/spleenuncommoninfections.html) associated, [Castleman disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodescastleman.html))
