Definition / general

* Benign mesenchymal proliferation with vascular and fatty components ([Pathologica 2006;98:239](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17175794))
* Associated with Castleman lymphadenopathy, hyaline vascular variant ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1986;110:853](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3755897))

Sites

* Various nodal sites, including cervical, mediastinal and retroperitoneal lymph nodes
* Occurs in nodal parenchyma or extracapsular soft tissue
* Miscellaneous extranodal sites can be involved

Pathophysiology

* Rare disorder of unknown etiology
* Pseudotumor; not a true neoplasm

Clinical features

* Innocuous and slow growing

Case reports

* 16 year old boy with paraparesis caused by an angiolipomatous hamartoma with Proteus syndrome and scoliosis ([J Neurosurg 2005;103:282](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16238085))
* 60 year old man with a spindle cell sarcoma containing a focal angiolipomatous hamartoma component ([J Pathol 2002;197:264](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12015752))
* 70 year old man with angiolipomatous mesenchymal hamartoma (angiolipomatosis) of sigmoid mesocolon ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2011;4:210](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21326818))
* Giant lymph node hyperplasia in an angiolipomatous mediastinal mass ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1986;110:853](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3755897))
* Vascular transformation of sinuses in bilateral cervical lymph nodes ([Head Neck 1999;21:366](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10376758))

Treatment

* Surgical excision

Gross description

* Unencapsulated single or multiple yellow and fatty nodules up to 15 cm
* Tan nodules may represent accompanying hyaline vascular Castleman lymphadenopathy

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Noncircumscribed intranodal or extranodal mass
* Mature adipose tissue and haphazard thick walled muscular vessels of varying sizes
* No thrombi within disorganized vascular channels
* Lacks other mesenchymal tissue types, such as muscle
* No necrosis, dense sclerosis, lipoblasts or marked cytologic atypia

Immunohistochemistry & special stains

* [Smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) and [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) highlight muscular vessels

Differential diagnosis

* [Angiomyolipoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodesangiomyolipoma.html): contains myomatous component, common in kidney
* [Lipofibromatosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuelipofibromatosis.html): pediatric soft tissue tumor with conspicuous fibrous component
* [Liposarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuewdliposarcoma.html): malignant tumor with lipoblasts
