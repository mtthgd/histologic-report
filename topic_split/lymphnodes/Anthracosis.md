Definition / general

* Accumulation of carbon in lymph nodes, more commonly in intrapulmonary lymph nodes, due to coal dust, smoke or pollution
* May be associated with storiform pattern of histiocytes that resembles a neoplasm ([Hum Pathol 1998;29:851](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9712428))
* Associated with silica, although often no history of industrial exposure
* Associated with hyalinization in nodes of elderly Japanese ([Histol Histopathol 2003;18:1169](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12973685))

Epidemiology

* Very common

Sites

* Common in hilar and bronchial lymph nodes

Clinical features

* Enlargement of involved lymph nodes, prominent mediastinal lymphadenopathy is common

Radiology description

* Enlarged lymph nodes, especially mediastinal and bronchial lymph nodes

Prognostic factors

* Benign process with no significant clinical implications

Case reports

* 71 year old woman, with life-long exposure to soot from a wood cook stove, with anthracosis and large mediastinal mass with healed pulmonary tuberculosis ([Clin Med Res 2010;8:99](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20660934))
* Women who cooked over wood fires, with primary nodal anthracosis identified as a cause of FDG PET/CT positive mediastinal lymphadenopathy ([Respiratory Medicine Case Reports 2013;10:48](http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2213007113000385))
* A case of anthracosis presenting with mediastinal lymph nodes mimicking tuberculous lymphadenitis or malignancy ([Eur J Intern Med 2003;14:444](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14614980))

Treatment

* If significant enlargement, excision biopsy

Gross description

* Enlarged lymph nodes with firm dark brown to black cut surfaces

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Anthracotic macrophages in clusters and singly dispersed
* There may be storiform arrangement of spindle cells or granuloma like aggregates of macrophages
* Fine anthracotic pigment
* Also nodal hyaline scars and polarizable material suggestive of silica

Cytology description

* Cellular smears with a population of anthracotic macrophages that are both singly dispersed and in variously sized aggregates
* Variable foreign body type, multinucleated giant cells
* No necrosis or atypical cells

Differential diagnosis

* Clinically resembles malignancy: lymphoma, metastasis
* [Follicular dendritic cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodesFDCsarcoma.html)
* [Kaposi sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuekaposi.html)
* [MFH](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuemfhpleo.html)
* [Sarcoidosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungnontumorsarcoidosis.html)
* [Spindle cell melanoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticmelanoma.html)
