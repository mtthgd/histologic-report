Definition / general

* Rare presence of benign, well differentiated epithelial cell clusters in lymph nodes
* May coexist with breast micrometastases ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:513](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12657937))
* In axilla, may be due to pre-sentinel lymph node biopsy breast massage ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1641](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15577685), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2003;127:e25](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12562290))

Terminology

* Also called benign metastasis or heterotopia

Sites

* Axillary, cervical, mediastinal, mesenteric, para-aortic, pelvic, renal lymph nodes

Pathophysiology

* Different theories regarding the pathogenesis:
  + Benign metastasis
  + Developmental heterotopia
  + Metaplasia of multipotent cells
  + Iatrogenic displacement and transport ([J Clin Oncol 2006;24:2013](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16606970))

Clinical features

* Asymptomatic, incidental finding

Prognostic factors

* Benign with no significant clinical implications
* Rarely may undergo disease processes and become cystic, a benign tumor or malignant

Case reports

* 44 year old woman with right breast carcinoma and benign Müllerian inclusions coexisting with breast metastatic carcinoma in an axillary lymph node ([Virchows Arch 2005;446:467](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15785927))
* 48 year old woman with axillary intranodal cysts associated with breast malignancy ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2004;128:361](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14987141))
* 50 year old woman with unusual cystic epithelial choristoma in a celiac lymph node ([Hum Pathol 1987;18:866](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3610139))
* 52 year old woman with squamous inclusion cyst in a sentinel axillary lymph node associated with breast malignancy ([J Coll Physicians Surg Pak 2012;22:50](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22237193))
* 65, 66 and 75 year old women with endosalpingiosis in axillary sentinel lymph nodes obtained for staging of breast carcinoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:1211](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20631604))
* 70 year old woman with invasive ductal carcinoma of the left breast and endosalpingiosis of axillary lymph nodes ([Case Rep Pathol 2016;2016:2856358](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27088025))
* 73 year old woman with ductal carcinoma in situ of the breast and epithelial inclusions in an ipsilateral axillary lymph node ([Int J Surg Pathol 2018;26:564](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29560779))
* Epithelial inclusion in axillary lymph node associated with a breast carcinoma ([Pathol Res Pract 1999;195:263](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10337666))
* Benign inclusion of axillary lymph nodes ([Breast J 2009;15:664](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19686230))

Treatment

* No specific treatment needed, unless associated with another disease

Gross description

* Normal unremarkable lymph nodes

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Reactive follicular hyperplasia
* Well formed epithelial formations, in or near the peripheral sinuses
* Examples include salivary gland, thyroid follicles, breast tissue, respiratory type epithelium, fallopian tube lining or endometrium
* Single or tubules of epithelial cells in subcapsular sinus of draining lymph node after surgical or needle manipulation ([Am J Clin Pathol 2000;113:259](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10664628))
* Also hemosiderin laden macrophages and damaged erythrocytes
