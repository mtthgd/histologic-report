Definition / general

* May cause spontaneous splenic rupture and death, often 10 - 21 days after disease onset
* Rupture may be due to increased intrasplenic pressure due to congestion and weakening of splenic capsule from infiltration by immunoblasts

Case reports

* 16 year old girl with spontaneous splenic rupture ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2012;3:97](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22288057))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Expansion of red pulp with immunoblastic proliferation
* Immunoblasts may have strikingly atypical morphology, resemble Reed-Sternberg cells, infiltrate subintima of intratrabecular veins, occasionally exhibit hemophagocytosis

Positive stains

* Immunoblasts are positive for B and T cell markers, [EBV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) by in situ hybridization, variable [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html)

Differential diagnosis

* [Hodgkin lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBclassic.html)
* **Leukemia**
* [Non-Hodgkin lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/lymphoma.html)
