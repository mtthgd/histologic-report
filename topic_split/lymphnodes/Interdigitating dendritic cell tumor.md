Definition / general

* Rare tumor of hematopoietic origin arising from interdigitating dendritic cells (IDCs), a type of immune accessory cell found in lymphoid and nonlymphoid organs
* IDCs present antigens to T cells and regulate cellular immune responses ([Crit Rev Oncol Hematol 2013;88:253](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23755890))
* Usually arises in lymph nodes ([Am J Surg Pathol 1999;23:1141](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10478677))

Epidemiology

* IDC tumors have been reported in all age groups (median age 56 years)

Sites

* Can be nodal or extranodal; have been described throughout the body
* Only two reported cases of testicular involvement

Pathophysiology

* Dysregulation of immune system may facilitate malignant transformation of IDCs ([Crit Rev Oncol Hematol 2013;88:253](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23755890))
* Recent studies show a clonal relationship between IDC tumors and low grade B cell lymphomas in patients harboring both diseases synchronously or metachronously
* A group of IDC tumors likely develops secondarily after the malignant transformation and trans differentiation of B cells, rather than transformation of IDCs themselves
* IDC tumors have developed following the use of calcineurin inhibitors, tacrolimus and pimecrolimus; these drugs dampen the responses of T cells to which IDCs present antigens

Clinical features

* Painless testicular mass

Laboratory

* Serum tumor markers for testicular germ cell tumors (hCG and alpha fetoprotein) are normal

Radiology description

* Testicular ultrasound is imaging modality of choice for a scrotal mass ([Gordetsky: Gordo's Guide to GUPathology: A Resource for Urology and Pathology Residents, 2013](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1483918076/pathologyoutl-20))

Prognostic factors

* Worse outcomes seen in younger patients and patients with intraabdominal involvement
* Stage at presentation is most important prognostic factor ([Crit Rev Oncol Hematol 2013;88:253](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23755890))

Case reports

* 37 year old man with painless testicular mass ([Int J Surg Pathol 2011;19:104](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18805870))
* 74 year old man with a painless unilateral testicular mass ([Am J Surg Pathol 1999;23:1141](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10478677))

Treatment

* Surgery with adjuvant chemotherapy

Gross description

* Light tan, solid, may replace entire testis

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Whorls and fascicles of spindle cells mixed with small lymphocytes

Electron microscopy description

* Complex interdigitating cytoplasmic dendritic processes, abundant rough endoplasmic reticulum, abundant mitochondria

Differential diagnosis

* [Germ cell tumors](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisgctgeneral.html) with a sarcomatous component
* [Sex cord stromal tumors](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testissexcordgeneral.html)
* **Infiltration by paratesticular spindle cell neoplasms**
