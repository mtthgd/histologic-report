Definition / general

* Multisystem disorder affecting mainly middle age females, causing pulmonary and extrapulmonary disease
* Its pathologic features result from the proliferation of neoplastic cells (LAM cells), which have characteristics of both smooth muscle cells and melanocytes
* It was originally classified as benign but now is considered a "low grade, destructive metastasizing neoplasm" ([Am J Respir Crit Care Med 2012;186:1210](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23250499))

Terminology

* Also called lymphangioleiomyomatosis

Epidemiology

* Estimated median transplant free survival time for LAM patients in the U.S. is 29 years from onset of symptoms and 23 years from diagnosis
  + Estimated 10 year survival transplant free is 86% ([Lung 2013;191:35](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23007140))
* About 80% of women with TSC develop cystic disease in lungs ([Chest 2013;144:578](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23539171)), compared to 13% of male patients ([Clin Radiol 2011;66:625](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21459371))
* Relationship between pulmonary and extrapulmonary LAM needs further investigation

Etiology

* Associated with tuberous sclerosis complex (TSC), an autosomal dominant disorder caused by mutations in the *TSC1* or *TSC2* genes, characterized by intellectual disability, autism, seizures, facial angiofibroma, cortical tubers, cardiac rhabdomyoma, asrocytoma

Case reports

* 47, 59 and 71 year old women with extrapulmonary lymphangioleiomyomatosis in pelvic and paraaortic lymph nodes ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2011;30:470](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21804397))
* 55 year old woman with extrapulmonary lymphangioleiomyomatosis ([Case of the Month #522](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case522.htm))
* 59 year old woman with "malignant" uterine perivascular epithelioid cell tumor, pelvic lymph node lymphangioleiomyomatosis and gynecological pecomatosis ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2008;27:86](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18156981))
* Extrapulmonary lymphangioleiomyomatosis in pelvic lymph nodes associated with uterine cancer ([Zhonghua Bing Li Xue Za Zhi 2015;44:527](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26705051))
* Incidental pelvic and paraaortic lymph node lymphangioleiomyomatosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1015](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25786086))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Single or multiple lymph nodes, median size is 3.5 cm
* Mostly affecting nodal parenchyma but can be seen in hilum, subcapsular sinuses or extranodal extension
* LAM cells are either spindle or epitheliod
* Epithelioid cells are arranged in nests or swirls, separated by clefts which resemble lymphatic spaces
* Spindled cells show fascicular growth with some nesting and less prominent lymphatic channels
* No atypia, no necrosis, no mitosis

Cytology description

* Relatively large monomorphic spindle cells ([Acta Cytol 1997;41:877](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9167718))

Positive stains

* [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) and [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html)
* [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html): patchy or diffuse, cytoplasmic or membranous
* [B catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html): positive, strong and diffuse
* [MelanA / A103](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html): positive in 39% of cases
* [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html): highlights lymphatic endothelium

Differential diagnosis

* [Metastatic well differentiated leiomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueleiomyosarcoma.html): no prominent vascular channels, more atypia and mitotic figures, [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html)-
