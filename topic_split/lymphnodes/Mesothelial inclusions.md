Definition / general

* Inclusions of benign mesothelial cells in lymph nodes
* Often missed on routine H&E sections ([Am J Surg Pathol 1999;23:1264](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10524528))
* Hyperplastic mesothelial cells in nodal tissue may derive from reactive serosal mesothelium that is dislodged into draining lymphatics ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2000;124:609](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10747321))
* Often associated with serosal fluid collection (pericardial, pleural, abdominal) at time of nodal biopsy ([Hum Pathol 1998;29:339](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9563782)), including episodes of intraperitoneal hemorrhage and ascites ([Pathology 2001;33:239](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11358062)), perhaps because effusion allows for mesothelial cell migration into lymphatics ([Diagn Cytopathol 2003;29:163](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12951686))

Terminology

* Benign mesothelial inclusions
* Benign metastasizing mesothelial cells

Epidemiology

* Very rare occurrence

Sites

* Mediastinal and abdominal lymph nodes
* Rare - cervical lymph nodes

Etiology

* Transportation of these cells through the lymphatics to the lymph node during injury or manipulation at the primary site of the origin
* They undergo a degeneration, and thus it becomes difficult to find them

Clinical features

* Incidental finding
* Enlarged lymph nodes
* Site specific symptoms

Diagnosis

* Biopsy plus immunohistochemistry

Prognostic factors

* Benign process with no significant clinical implications

Case reports

* 12 year old boy with diffuse hyperplastic mesothelial cells in multiple lymph nodes ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2013;6:926](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23638225))
* 16 year old boy with mesothelial cell inclusions mimicking adenocarcinoma ([Indian J Med Paediatr Oncol 2010;31:62](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21209767))
* 18 year old woman with benign metastasizing mesothelial cells ([J Clin Oncol 2011;29:e546](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21482984))
* 52 year old woman with mesothelial pelvic lymph node inclusions mimicking metastatic thyroid carcinoma ([Gynecol Oncol 1998;68:210](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9514806))
* Mesothelial cell inclusions in mediastinal lymph nodes mimicking metastatic carcinoma ([Am J Clin Pathol 1990;93:741](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2161177))
* Mesothelial cell inclusions within mediastinal lymph nodes ([Histopathology 1994;25:483](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7868088))
* Mesothelial pelvic lymph node inclusion in a patient with ovarian microinvasive borderline mucinous tumor ([Int J Gynecol Cancer 2007;17:917](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17316363))
* Benign hyperplastic mesothelial cells in lymph node ([Int J Surg Pathol 2007;15:297](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17652541))

Treatment

* Depends upon the clinical presentation / underlying cause
* In incidental findings, none necessary

Gross description

* Enlarged lymph nodes with yellowish tan to gray white, smooth to mottled surface

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Small clusters and singly scattered, round to polygonal cells, seen in the subcapsular and interfollicular sinuses of the nodes
* These cells show a round, vesicular nucleus with small nucleolus
* The nuclear - cytoplasmic ratio is low
* No mitotic activity is detected
* There is no extranodal or parenchymal infiltration of the cells
* Tiny spaces are seen in between the cells of the clusters (mesothelial windows)

Positive stains

* [Cytokeratin AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [cytokeratin 7 (CK7)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html)
* [Calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html) (nuclear and cytoplasmic), [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html), [CK5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5.html) / [6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck6.html), [CAIX](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscarbonicanhydraseix.html) (membranous)
* [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) (weak and cytoplasmic)

Differential diagnosis

* [Lymphangioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuelymphangioma.html): multicystic, has smooth muscle and lymphocytes in cyst wall and lumen, [keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)+
* [Metastatic adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodesmetastases.html)
* [Metastatic mesothelioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodesmetastases.html)
* **Mucinous cystadenoma**: ovarian-like stroma, [mucin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmucins.html)+, [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html)+, negative for **hyaluronic acid**
* [Müllerian inclusions](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodesotherectopic.html#mullerianinclusions): [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html)+; negative for [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html), [CK5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5.html) / [6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck6.html); [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html) can be positive in both
* **Simple hemorrhagic cyst**: [keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)-
* [Sinus histiocytosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodessinushistiocytosiswithmassivelymph.html)
