Definition / general

* This topic only discusses features of plasmacytoma in lymph nodes different from [plasmacytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaplasmacytoma.html) and [myeloma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomamyeloma.html)
* Very rare (< 50 reported cases)
* Diagnosis of primary plasmacytoma of lymph node requires exclusion of extramedullary plasmacytoma (15% of upper respiratory tract plasmacytomas metastasize to cervical nodes) and myeloma (40% of high stage myelomas metastasize to nodes)
* 2/3 male; median age 59 years (range 39 - 76 years)
* Often involves cervical nodes
* Similar survival to other extramedullary plasmacytomas, although does not progress to myeloma ([Am J Clin Pathol 2001;115:119](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11190797), [Hum Pathol 1997;28:1083](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9308734))

Case reports

Differential diagnosis

* [Castleman disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodescastleman.html), **plasma cell variant**
* [Lymphoplasmacytic lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomalpl.html):
  + Also has neoplastic small lymphocytes
  + Often shows IgM restriction and *MYD88* gene mutation
* [Marginal zone lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomamzlgeneral.html):
  + May have plasmacytoid features but more extensive sampling may reveal B cell component
  + [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html) expression by neoplastic B cells and plasmacytoid cells
  + Clonal B cell population by flow; molecular or cytogenetic features of marginal zone lymphoma
* [Large cell lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaALKpositive.html):
  + May have immunoblastic or plasmablastic features
* [Plasma cell myeloma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomamyeloma.html) with nodal involvement:
  + Must be excluded by radiographs, bone marrow biopsy
* [Plasmablastic lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaplasmablastic.html):
  + Mostly extranodal; less frequent nodal involvement
  + Associated with HIV or other immunosuppressive states and EBV
* [Reactive plasmacytosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonemarrowplasmacytosis.html):
  + Often follicular hyperplasia
  + No light chain restriction
