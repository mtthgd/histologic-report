Definition / general

* Most SLE patients have cervical lymphadenopathy

Case reports

* 32 year old woman with coexisting Kukuchi disease ([Int J Dermatol 2006;45:454](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16650177))
* Patient with coexisting Castleman disease ([J Rheumatol 1999;26:1400](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10381064))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Architectural preservation but follicular hyperplasia with variable sized follicles, increased vascularity, interfollicular immunoblasts and plasma cells
* Often well circumscribed areas of paracortical necrosis with necrosis of small vessels ([APMIS 2001;109:141](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11398995))
* Occasionally DNA deposition / hematoxylin bodies (hematoxyphilic material) in stroma, sinuses and blood vessel walls
* May have giant follicles ([APMIS 2005;113:558](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16086828)), often disarray of follicular dendritic cell network ([Pathol Int 2000;50:304](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10849316)), no / rare granulomas, no / rare neutrophils

Necrotizing lymphadenopathy

Cytology description

* Typical and atypical immunoblasts, plasma cells, occasional Reed-Sternberg-like cells and dispersed hematoxylin bodies ([Acta Cytol 2000;44:67](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10667163))

Positive stains

* [CD11b](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10to19.html#cd11b)+ and [CD15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd15.html)+ histiocytes
* [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html)+ T cells, [EBV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) (up to 20%, [Int J Surg Pathol 2005;13:273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16086083))

Differential diagnosis

* [Castleman disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodescastleman.html) ([Pathol Res Pract 1997;193:565](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9406250))
* [Cat scratch disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodescatscratch.html): has neutrophils
* [Kikuchi disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodeskikuchi.html): less prominent follicular hyperplasia and interfollicular plasma cells, more prominent immunoblasts resembling lymphoma
* [Lymphogranuloma venereum](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodesotherinfections.html#lymphogranulomavenereum): has granulomas
* **Polykaryocytes**
