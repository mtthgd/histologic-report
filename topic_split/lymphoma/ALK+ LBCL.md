Definition / general

* WHO defines this entity as an aggressive lymphoma composed of ALK (anaplastic lymphoma kinase) positive monomorphic large immunoblast-like B cells, which usually have a plasma cell phenotype ([Blood 2016;127:2375](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26980727), [Swerdlow: WHO Classification of Tumours of Haematopoietic and Lymphoid Tissues, 4th Edition, 2017](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/928324494X/pathologyoutl-20))
* Lymphoma composed of large lymphoid cells of B lineage, positive for ALK immunohistochemical stain and negative for CD20 and CD30

Essential features

* Aggressive B cell lymphoma with monomorphic large immunoblast / plasmablast-like B cells, usually having plasma cell phenotype
* Positive for ALK immunohistochemistry with granular cytoplasmic restriction and negative for CD20 and CD30
* *ALK* gene is translocated and activated; most frequent cytogenetic abnormality is t(2;17) responsible for CLTC (clathrin)-ALK fusion protein

Terminology

* Large B cell lymphoma expressing ALK kinase and lacking t(2;5) translocation (obsolete)
* ALK positive plasmablastic B cell lymphoma (obsolete)

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9737/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf#page=96) - ALK positive large B cell lymphoma

Epidemiology

* It is a very rare lymphoma
* Occurs in immunocompetent, HIV negative young men, although reported in a wide age range and both genders
* M:F = 5:1
* Age range of 9 - 85 years; median age of 43 years ([J Clin Oncol 2009;27:4211](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19636007))

Sites

* Common sites include lymph nodes and mediastinum
* Other rare sites include nasopharynx, tongue, stomach, duodenum, bone, soft tissue, liver, spleen and skin ([Hum Pathol 2004;35:1285](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15492998), [Exp Ther Med 2014;8:409](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25009592), [Am J Dermatopathol 2019;41:602](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31335416))

Pathophysiology

* Translocation of *ALK* gene on chromosome 2 with a fusion gene partner
* ALK oncogenic protein overexpression and related activation of STAT3 pathway
* MYC expression in the absence of *MYC* translocations or amplifications, probably due to transcriptional activation downstream of STAT3 ([Mod Pathol 2013;26:1329](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23599149))
* No known association with immunosuppression

Etiology

* Not specified
* Normal counterpart: a postgerminal center B cell with plasmablastic differentiation

Clinical features

* Most patients present with generalized lymphadenopathy
* 60% of cases present in advanced stage (III / IV) and bone marrow infiltration is seen in 25% of cases ([J Clin Oncol 2009;27:4211](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19636007))

Diagnosis

* Based on a combination of light microscopy and immunohistochemical markers

Prognostic factors

* Generally, this lymphoma has a poor prognosis
* However, factors for a good prognosis include localized disease (stage I and II) and lymphoma occurring in childhood

Case reports

* 28 year old man with right submandibular lymphadenopathy ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2017;10:11219](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31966473))
* 50 year old man with mediastinal lymphadenopathy ([Pathology 2019;51:S81](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.pathol.2018.12.202))
* 54 year old man with right neck mass ([Case Rep Hematol 2018;2018:5320590](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29992063))

Treatment

* Most cases treated with chemotherapy regimens of CHOP or E-POCH / CHOEP; a few patients are also given additional radiotherapy ([PLoS One 2017;12:e0178416](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28665943))
* Since most cases are negative for CD20 and CD30, rituximab and brentuximab vedotin are not given
* There is a single case report of complete response to nivolumab ([Clin Lymphoma Myeloma Leuk 2020;20:e113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32033930))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Tumor arranged as diffuse sheets, sinusoidal infiltrate or a mixture of diffuse and sinusoidal patterns
* Tumor composed of monomorphic large immunoblast-like cells containing a large central nucleolus and abundant cytoplasm
* Some cases show plasmablastic differentiation, which can be either an exclusive population of plasmablasts or a spectrum of morphology from plasmacytes to plasmablasts
* Anaplastic / pleomorphic morphology has also been described in a few cases ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:25](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27740969))
* Atypical multinucleated tumor giant cells may be seen ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:25](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27740969))

Cytology description

* Only a few case reports are available in the literature and give a brief view of cytological findings
* One case report states, "most of the neoplastic cells had pseudopodial cytoplasmic projections and some of them had eosinophilic pseudopodial cytoplasmic projections that resembled flaming plasma cells" ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2013;6:2631](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24228132))
* A second case report states, "aspiration cytology revealed many clusters of cohesive, large and solitary cells that showed abundant cytoplasm and large round to oval nuclei with prominent nucleoli" ([Diagn Cytopathol 2014;42:69](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23457005))

Positive stains

* [ALK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html) positivity is important for diagnosis and characteristically demonstrates a restricted granular cytoplasmic staining pattern, highly indicative of the expression of **CLTC-ALK** fusion protein or *ALK-SEC31A* translocation
* Other patterns (diffuse cytoplasmic, nuclear or nucleolar) of [ALK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html) staining correspond to translocation of *ALK* with *NPM1* [t(2;5)(p23;q35)], *SQSTM1, RANBP2* or *EML4*, which are associated with a poorer prognosis ([Int J Hematol 2016;103:399](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26781614))
* [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) (73%), [CD138](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd138.html) (96%), **PRDM1 (BLIMP1)**, [MUM1 / IRF4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmum1.html) (100%), [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html) (96%), [IgA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsIgA.html) (67%), [BOB1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbob1.html) (100%), [OCT2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsOCT2.html) (81%), [PAX5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax5.html) (28%) and [CD79a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd79a.html) (30%)
* Tumor may be positive for [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html) (67%), [CD57](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd57.html), [CD43](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd43.html) and [perforin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/perforin.html)
* 83% of cases showed [kappa](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainskappa.html) or [lambda](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainskappa.html) light chain restriction by immunohistochemistry or in situ hybridization ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:25](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27740969))

Negative stains

* [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html) negativity is required for the diagnosis
* [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html)
* [EBV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) ([LMP1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) and [EBER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html)) and [HHV8 (LANA)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshhv8.html)
* [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html) weak or negative
* Pan-T cell markers, such as [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Clonal rearrangement of *IG* genes
* *ALK* is overexpressed due to fusion proteins generated by translocations
* Most common translocation is t(2;17)(p23;q23), responsible for CLTC (clathrin)-ALK fusion protein
* Few cases are associated with t(2;5) ([Blood 2003;102:2642](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12816858))
* *ALK* may have other fusion partners (given [above](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/#lymphomaALKpositivestains))

Sample pathology report

* Right cervical lymph node, excisional biopsy:
  + ALK positive large B cell lymphoma (see comment)
  + Comment: Tumor cells are positive for ALK (cytoplasmic granular staining) and MUM1 with high MIB1 proliferation index. Tumor cells are negative for CD3, CD20, CD30, CD45, EBV-LMP1 and LANA. Few cells show weak positivity for CD79a.
  + Microscopic description: Section studied shows completely effaced lymph node architecture with diffuse infiltration by a tumor arranged in sheets and composed of monomorphic large immunoblast-like cells containing a round to oval nucleus, vesicular chromatin, large central nucleolus and abundant cytoplasm. Few cells show plasmablast-like morphology. An intrasinusoidal pattern is seen focally.

Differential diagnosis

* [ALK positive anaplastic large cell lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBanaplastic.html):
  + Differentiated by presence of hallmark cells and diffuse strong [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html) positivity
  + Morphological and immunohistochemical overlap present (sinusoidal involvement, positivity for [ALK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html) and [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html))
* [Plasmablastic lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaplasmablastic.html):
  + Tumor is [EBV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) positive ([EBER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html)) and [ALK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html) and [HHV8 (LANA)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshhv8.html) negative
* [Primary effusion lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaeffusion.html):
  + Tumor is positive for [HHV8 (LANA)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshhv8.html) and [EBV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) ([EBER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html)) and negative for [ALK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html)
* [HHV8 positive diffuse large B cell lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaHHV8DLBCL.html):
  + Tumor is positive for [HHV8 (LANA)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshhv8.html) and [EBV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) ([EBER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html)) and negative for [ALK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html) and [MUM1 / IRF4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmum1.html)
* [Intravascular large B cell lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomadiffuseintra.html):
  + [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html) positive and [ALK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html) negative on IHC
* **Extraosseous** [plasma cell neoplasm](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaplasmacytoma.html) in case of extranodal ALK positive large B cell lymphoma:
  + This entity will be [ALK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html) negative
* **Metastatic carcinoma**:
  + May be considered because of the sinusoidal distribution, [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) positivity and [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) positivity in rare cases of ALK positive large B cell lymphoma

Board review style question #1

Which of the following combinations of IHC markers is diagnostic of ALK positive large B cell lymphoma that comprises monomorphic large lymphoid cells?

1. ALK positive, MUM1 positive and CD30 positive
2. ALK positive, MUM1 positive and CD30 negative
3. ALK positive, MUM1 negative and CD30 positive
4. ALK positive, MUM1 negative and CD30 negative

Board review style answer #1

**B.** ALK positive, MUM1 positive and CD30 negative
