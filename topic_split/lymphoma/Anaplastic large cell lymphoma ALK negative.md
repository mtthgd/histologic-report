Definition / general

* A CD30 positive T cell neoplasm morphologically similar to ALK positive anaplastic large cell lymphoma (ALCL) but does not express ALK protein ([Swerdlow: WHO Classification of Tumours of Haematopoietic and Lymphoid Tissues, 4th Edition, 2017](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/928324494X/pathologyoutl-20))

Essential features

* T cell lymphoma
* Characteristic hallmark cells
* Morphologically indistinguishable from ALCL, ALK positive
* CD30 diffusely and strongly positive
* ALK expression negative
* *ALK* gene rearrangement negative

Terminology

* ALK- ALCL

Epidemiology

* Adults (40 - 65 years), in contrast to ALCL, ALK+, which is common in children and young adults
* Slight male predominance; M:F = 1.5:1
* Prevalence unknown ([Orphanet: ALK Negative Anaplastic Large Cell Lymphoma [Accessed 9 April 2020]](https://www.orpha.net/consor/cgi-bin/OC_Exp.php?lng=EN&Expert=300903))

Sites

* Both lymph nodes and extranodal sites, including bone, soft tissue and skin ([primary cutaneous ALCL](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticanaplasticlargecell.html) needs to be distinguished)

Pathophysiology

* Arises from activated T cells
* T cell associated antigens often downregulated, with loss of CD3 and other markers
* Gene expression shows common features between ALK+ and ALK- ALCL ([Leukemia 2009;23:2129](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19657361), [Blood 2014;123:2915](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24632715))
* Gains of the 1q41-qter and 6p21 are common cytogenetic aberrations ([Br J Haematol 2008;140:516](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18275429))
* *DUSP22::IRF4* locus on 6p25.3 (referred to as *DUSP22* rearrangements); t(6;7)(p25.3;q32.3) ([Blood 2011;117:915](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21030553))
* Recurrent rearrangement involving the *TP63* gene ([Blood 2012;120:2280](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22855598))
* *STAT3* activating mutations ([Cancer Cell 2015;27:516](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25873174))

Etiology

* Unknown ([Orphanet: ALK Negative Anaplastic Large Cell Lymphoma [Accessed 9 April 2020]](https://www.orpha.net/consor/cgi-bin/OC_Exp.php?lng=EN&Expert=300903))

Clinical features

* Mostly presents at advanced stage (stage III - IV) and B symptoms

Diagnosis

* Biopsy of the involved lymph node with immunohistochemistry
* Morphology (see [Microscopic description](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/#lymphomanonBanaplasticnegmicro))
* Immunophenotype (see [Positive stains](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/#lymphomanonBanaplasticnegposstain))
* Detection of genetic aberrations and T cell clone (see [Molecular / cytogenetics description](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/#lymphomanonBanaplasticnegmolec))

Laboratory

* Elevated lactate dehydrogenase, pancytopenia, anemia, thrombocytopenia ([Blood 2008;111:5496](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18385450))

Radiology description

* Enlarged lymph nodes / mass lesions in extranodal sites

Prognostic factors

* Outcome poorer than [ALCL, ALK positive](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBanaplastic.html) ([Histopathology 2003;43:462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14636272), [J Pathol 2003;200:4](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12692835))
* Prognosis depends on patient age, international prognostic index score and genetic heterogeneity
* Recent studies indicate that *DUSP22* rearrangements do not confer a better prognosis, in contrast to the initial study that showed a favorable prognosis ([Haematologica 2023;108:1604](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36453104), [Br J Haematol 2019;186:e28](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30873584))
* *TP63* rearrangements poor survival rates ([Blood 2014;124:1473](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24894770))
* Loss of *PRDM1* or *TP53* poor outcome ([Blood 2013;122:2683](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24004669))
* Survival rates better than [peripheral T cell lymphoma, not otherwise specified](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonbperipheral.html) ([Blood 2008;111:5496](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18385450))

Case reports

* 13 year old girl with mediastinal ALK negative anaplastic large cell lymphoma ([Oxf Med Case Reports 2019;2019:omz077](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31772744))
* 21 year old man with a pulmonary abscess ([Clin Nucl Med 2019;44:234](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30562196))
* 53 year old man with AIDS with subcutaneous nodules on the scalp and axillae and diffuse lymphadenopathy ([BMJ Case Rep 2019;12:e230641](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31570354))
* 54 year old man misdiagnosed with Behçet disease ([World J Clin Cases 2019;7:3377](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31667195))
* 56 year old Asian man with right neck swelling for 3 months with fever and weight loss ([Int J Surg Pathol 2017;25:502](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28381153))
* 61 year old woman with endobronchial pseudocarcinomatous hyperplasia ([Histopathology 2019 Nov 9 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31705818))
* 69 year old woman with double hit of *DUSP22* and *TP63* rearrangements in anaplastic large cell lymphoma, ALK negative ([Blood 2020;135:700](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32106310))
* 69 year old woman presented with dyspnea and malaise ([Case Rep Hematol 2018;2018:3465351](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29850298))
* 73 year old man with recurrent thrombosis of his infrarenal aortic prosthesis ([Virchows Arch 2019;474:763](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30685783))
* 80 year old Japanese man with acute exacerbation of hypercalcemia ([J Clin Exp Hematop 2019;59:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30918141))
* 82 year old man with a history of cerebrovascular disease presented with weakness and iron deficiency anemia ([In Vivo 2017;31:701](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28652442))

Treatment

* Chemotherapy with CHOP (cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin, vincristine and prednisone) or CHOP plus etoposide (CHOEP) ([Curr Hematol Malig Rep 2018;13:135](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29516333))
* Dose intensive regimens like hyper-CHOP and hyper-CVAD (hyperfractionated cyclophosphamide, vincristine, doxorubicin, dexamethasone, methotrexate and cytarabine) are also in use ([Cancer 2005;103:2091](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15816054))
* Upfront consolidative autologous stem cell transplantation at first complete remission ([Blood Cancer J 2016;6:e452](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27471868), [J Clin Oncol 2012;30:3093](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22851556))
* Brentuximab vedotin for relapsed / refractory disease ([J Clin Oncol 2012;30:2190](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22614995))
* Chimeric antigen receptor (CAR) T cell therapy and checkpoint inhibitors are now under evaluation
  ([ClinicalTrials: NCT02581631 [Accessed 10 April 2020]](https://clinicaltrials.gov/ct2/results?term=NCT02581631), [ClinicalTrials: NCT02274584 [Accessed 10 April 2020]](https://clinicaltrials.gov/ct2/results?term=NCT02274584))

Gross description

* Enlarged lymph nodes
* Firm fleshy mass

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Most of the time, morphologically indistinguishable from [ALCL, ALK positive](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBanaplastic.html)
* Effaced architecture with solid, cohesive sheets of neoplastic cells
* May show preserved lymph node architecture with neoplastic cells growing intrasinusoidally or only within the T cell areas
* Large cells with round to indented nuclei, deeply staining cytoplasm, with prominent Golgi zone
* Hallmark cells (cells with eccentric, horseshoe or kidney shaped nuclei)
* Small cells, as described in the small cell or lymphohistiocytic variant of ALCL, ALK positive, are uncommon
* Cases with *DUSP22* rearrangements show smaller, monomorphic cells with prominent nucleoli and central nuclear pseudoinclusions (doughnut cells) ([Haematologica 2019;104:e158](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30361415))
* Cases positive for *DUSP22* rearrangements lack a cytotoxic phenotype
* Cases with ERBB4 and COL29A1 expression show Hodgkin-like morphology ([Blood 2016;127:221](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26463425))

Cytology description

* Large atypical neoplastic cells, multinucleation / multilobated nuclei, prominent Golgi zone, basophilic cytoplasm
* Basophilic nucleoli, generally less prominent than seen in Hodgkin Reed-Sternberg cells

Positive stains

* Strong and diffuse uniform [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html) staining in all tumor cells (membrane and Golgi zone pattern, also cytoplasmic)
* Variable expression / loss of pan-T cell antigens: [CD2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd2.html)+ and [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html)+ more often than [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html)+
* Almost always [CD43](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd43.html)+ and [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html)+; can be used to distinguish from carcinoma
* Often [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html)+, rarely [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html)+
* **TIA1**, [granzyme B](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgranzymeb.html), **perforin**, **clusterin**, [fascin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfascin.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)  (variable)
* Cases with *DUSP22* rearrangements tend to be less frequently positive for cytotoxic markers

Negative stains

* [ALK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html) negative by definition
* [CD15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd15.html)
* [EBV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) ([EBER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) and [LMP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html))
* B cell markers ([CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html), [CD79a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd79a.html), [PAX5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax5.html))
* [Cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) can be used to distinguish from carcinoma

Flow cytometry description

* Flow cytometry shows the anaplastic large cell lymphoma cells with high forward and side light scatter properties (consistent with large and complex cells) and high density CD45 expression simulating monocytes
* Tumor cells express CD30 and one or more of the T cell associated antigens ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2009;133:49](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19123736))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* T cell receptor (TCR) clonal gene rearrangement in most cases, irrespective of T cell antigen expression
* *DUSP22::IRF4* locus on 6p25.3 (referred to as *DUSP22* rearrangements); t(6;7)(p25.3;q32.3) ([Blood 2011;117:915](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21030553))
* Recurrent rearrangement involving the *TP63* gene in a minority of cases ([Blood 2012;120:2280](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22855598))
* Gains of the 1q41-qter and 6p21 are common cytogenetic aberrations ([Br J Haematol 2008;140:516](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18275429))
* ERBB4 and COL29A1 are expressed in 24% of ALK- ALCL, mutually exclusive with *TP63* rearrangement ([Blood 2016;127:221](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26463425))
* *STAT3* activating mutations in a subset of cases ([Cancer Cell 2015;27:516](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25873174))

Differential diagnosis

* [ALCL, ALK positive](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBanaplastic.html):
  + [ALK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html)+, younger age, less aggressive
* [Classical Hodgkin lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBclassic.html):
  + Characteristic Hodgkin Reed-Sternberg cells, [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html) and [CD15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd15.html) positive
  + Can be [EBV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) positive, cytotoxic markers and T cell markers negative
* [Peripheral T cell lymphoma, NOS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBperipheral.html):
  + Difficult differential, WHO recommends conservative approach (diagnose ALCL, ALK- only if very similar to ALCL, ALK+, except for [ALK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html) expression)
  + Overall worse prognosis than ALCL, ALK-
* [Primary cutaneous ALCL](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticanaplasticlargecell.html):
  + Gene expression profiling shows that ALCL, ALK negative is closely related to primary cutaneous ALCL
  + Much better prognosis, clinical correlation with staging necessary
* [Breast implant associated ALCL](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantimplantalcl.html):
  + Breast implants, typically textured
  + Triple negative genetic subtype
  + Typically [ALK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html)- also
* [Mycosis fungoides](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticmycosisfungoides.html) with [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html) expression:
  + Presents as cutaneous patches and can progress to plaques, tumors and erythroderma
  + Band-like papillary dermal lymphoid infiltrate
  + Intraepidermal lymphocytes out of proportion with spongiosis (epidermotropism) with or without Pautrier microabscesses
