Definition / general

* Subtype of classic Hodgkin lymphoma (CHL) with Hodgkin / Reed-Sternberg (HRS) cells in a nodular or less commonly diffuse background of small lymphocytes ([Blood 2022;140:1229](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35653592), [Leukemia 2022;36:1720](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35732829))

Essential features

* B cell lymphoma derived from germinal center B cells
* Scattered HRS cells in a nodular or diffuse background of small lymphocytes without eosinophils and neutrophils
* Immunophenotype of HRS cells: PAX5+ (dim), CD30+, CD15 variable, CD20 variable

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9651/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=93) - classic Hodgkin lymphoma, lymphocytic rich
* ICD-10: [C81.4](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C81-C96/C81-/C81.4) - lymphocyte rich Hodgkin lymphoma
* ICD-11: [2B30.11](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f352299041) - lymphocyte rich, classical Hodgkin lymphoma

Epidemiology

* Rare subtype of CHL that accounts for 5.8% of classic Hodgkin lymphoma ([Cancer Med 2018;7:953](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29520977))
* Most patients are adults (30 - 50 years)
* Male predominance (70%)
* EBV infection in 30 - 50% of cases; the virus is within the HRS cells and usually shows a type II latency pattern of infection

Sites

* Peripheral lymph nodes (most often cervical) are typically affected ([Leuk Lymphoma 2019;60:3426](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31287335))
* Mediastinal involvement (15%) and bulky disease are rare

Pathophysiology

* Pathophysiology is incompletely understood
* HRS cells derived from preapoptotic germinal center B cells with a disrupted B cell program ([Leukemia 2021;35:968](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33686198))
* HRS cells harbor monoclonal *IGH* rearrangements with somatic mutations of their Ig variable region genes and lack the capacity of Ig expression ([Leukemia 2021;35:968](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33686198))
* Genetic lesions that promote HRS cell proliferation, survival, immune evasion and interaction with their microenvironment, including alterations in NFκB and JAK / STAT signaling pathways ([Leukemia 2021;35:968](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33686198))
* EBV could play a crucial role by infecting HRS cells with crippled germinal center B cell receptor (BCR), rescuing them from apoptosis and thus inducing lymphomagenesis ([Blood 2005;106:4339](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16076866))
* EBV infection activates the NFκB pathway promoting survival and growth ([Histopathology 2021;79:451](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33829526))

Etiology

* Remains unknown

Clinical features

* Similar to those of nodular lymphocyte predominant Hodgkin lymphoma (WHO 5) / nodular lymphocyte predominant B cell lymphoma (ICC), except that relapses are less frequent in lymphocyte rich CHL (LRCHL) ([J Clin Oncol 1999;17:776](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10071266))
* B symptoms are rare
* Most patients are diagnosed at stage I / II ([J Clin Oncol 2005;23:5739](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16009944))

Diagnosis

* Histopathological analysis of an excisional lymph node biopsy or tissue biopsy supplemented with ancillary techniques (immunohistochemistry, EBER ISH)

Laboratory

* Nonspecific findings: anemia, leukocytosis, lymphocytopenia, elevated C reactive protein ([N Engl J Med 1998;339:1506](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9819449))

Prognostic factors

* Approximately 90 - 95% of patients with early stage CHL and 80 - 85% with advanced stage CHL are cured ([Am J Hematol 2020;95:978](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32384177))
* Prognosis is slightly better than other CHL subtypes and similar to nodular lymphocyte predominant Hodgkin lymphoma (NLPHL), unless for the relapses, that are more common in NLPHL
* Treatment is individualized according to defined risk groups based on following prognostic factors
  + Ann Arbor stage (most important predictor of outcome) ([J Clin Oncol 2014;32:3059](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25113753))
  + Bulky (mediastinal) disease
  + Extranodal involvement
  + B symptoms
  + International Prognostic Score ([N Engl J Med 1998;339:1506](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9819449))

Case reports

* 35 year old woman with a 2 month history of progressive cough and intermittent fever ([Ann Hematol 2014;93:1073](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24173088))
* 35 year old man with a 4 month history of supraclavicular and cervical lymphadenopathies ([J Clin Exp Hematop 2015;55:23](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26106003))

Treatment

* Intensive polychemotherapy (ABVD or BEACOPP) with or without radiotherapy ([Am J Hematol 2020;95:978](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32384177))
* For refractory cases: stem cell transplant, brentuximab vedotin, checkpoint inhibitors ([N Engl J Med 2018;378:331](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29224502))

Gross description

* Involved lymph node is enlarged
* Vaguely nodular
* Fleshy appearance
* Reference: [Leukemia 2022;36:1720](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35732829)

Microscopic (histologic) description

* 2 patterns of growth: nodular or less often, diffuse proliferation with numerous reactive small lymphocytes and scattered HRS cells ([Leukemia 2022;36:1720](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35732829))
* Neutrophils and eosinophils are absent from the nodules
* In lymphocyte rich classic Hodgkin lymphoma (LRCHL) nodular cases
  + Nodules are composed of small lymphocytes and may contain eccentrically located germinal centers (small or regressed)
  + Nodules contain dense meshwork of follicular dendritic cells (CD21+)
  + HRS cells are within the nodules but outside of the germinal centers
  + Some HRS cells resemble lymphocyte predominant cells (LP cells) or lacunar cells
* In LRCHL diffuse cases
  + Small lymphocytes of the background can be intermingled with histiocytes with or without epithelioid morphology

Positive stains

* Hodgkin Reed-Sternberg (HRS) cells
  + [PAX5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax5.html) (dim),
    [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html) (strong and uniform), [CD15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd15.html) variable (75% of cases),
    [MUM1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmum1.html) ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2019;39:105](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30802809))
  + [OCT2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsOCT2.html) (56%) and
    [BOB1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbob1.html) (62%) are more frequently expressed than in other CHL subtypes ([Mod Pathol 2009;22:1006](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19465900))
  + [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html)YE361 (nuclear or nuclear and cytoplasmic) ([Mod Pathol 2020;33:834](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31822802))
  + [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgata3.html) (67%) ([Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2019;27:180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28877074), [Blood 2010;116:4202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20660789), [Am J Pathol 2005;166:127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15632006),[J Clin Pathol 2007;60:1092](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17158640))
  + [PDL1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsPDL1SP142.html), **PDL2** ([Histopathology 2018;72:1156](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29380399), [Blood 2018;131:68](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29118007))
  + [Fascin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfascin.html) ([Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2010;18:16](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19550297))
  + [EBV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) positive cases: [EBV LMP1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) variable, [EBER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html)

* Immunophenotype of the small lymphocytes in the background

  - Background is composed of both B and T lymphocytes
  - B cells are most prominent in LRCHL compared to other CHL subtypes
  - B cells are [IgM](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsIgA.html)+, [IgD](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsIgA.html)+, corresponding to expanded mantle zones
  - [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html)+ T cells dominate the CHL microenvironment ([Cytometry B Clin Cytom 2008;74:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18061945), [Blood 2004;103:1755](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14604957), [Lab Invest 2008;88:482](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18362907))
  - In 50% of LRCHL cases, HRS are surrounded by rosettes of T follicular helper cells ([CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html)+, [PD-1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspd1.html)+, [CD57](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd57.html) variable) ([Mod Pathol 2009;22:1006](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19465900), [Proc Natl Acad Sci U S A
    2021;118:e2105822118](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34615710))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Aneuploidy is seen in nearly all cases ([Blood 1995;86:1464](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7632954), [Cancer Res 2006;66:10332](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17079453), [Cancers (Basel) 2018;10:91](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29587466))
* High constitutive activity of the NFκB pathway is the hallmark of HRS cells which is essential for HRS cell survival ([Semin Cancer Biol 2016;39:32](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27221964))
* Mutations in members of the JAK / STAT signaling pathway, main mediator of cytokine signaling ([Nat Genet 2014;46:329](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24531327))
* Mutations in ITPKB, PI3K / AKT and MAP / ERK pathways ([Leukemia 2020;34:151](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31431735))
* Lesions in genes involved in immune evasion of HRS: *PDL1, PDL2, B2M, CIITA, CD58* ([Blood 2010;116:3268](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20628145), [J Clin Oncol 2016;34:2690](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27069084))
* Mutations in additional genes: *ARID1A, TNFRS14, XPO1* ([Leukemia 2021;35:968](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33686198))

Sample pathology report

* Lymph node, left neck, excision:
  + Lymphocyte rich classic Hodgkin lymphoma (see comment)
  + Comment: H&E stained sections display a lymph node with architectural effacement by a nodular proliferation of abundant small lymphocytes and scattered large atypical lymphoid cells. The neoplastic cells have monolobated / bilobated / multilobated nuclei, vesicular chromatin, prominent nucleoli and amphophilic cytoplasm, consistent with HRS cells.
  + Immunophenotypically, the large atypical cells are positive for PAX5 (weak), OCT2, CD30 (strong), CD15, MUM1 and GATA3 and negative for CD20 and CD45.
  + TFH markers reveal T cell rosettes around HRS cells.

Differential diagnosis

* [Nodular lymphocyte predominant Hodgkin lymphoma (NLPHL) / nodular lymphocyte predominant B cell lymphoma (NLPBCL)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBNLPHL.html) ([Blood 2000;96:1889](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10961891)):
  + Neoplastic LP cells in NLPHL / NLPBCL are usually [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html)+, [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html)+, [OCT2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsOCT2.html)+ (strong), [BCL6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl6.html)+, [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgata3.html)-, [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html)-
  + LP cells may show *BCL6* rearrangement in contrast to HRS cells ([Blood 2003;101:706](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12393409))
* [T cell / histiocytic rich large B cell lymphoma (THRLBCL)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomadiffusetcell.html):
  + Composed of a background rich in small lymphocytes and histiocytes with scattered large neoplastic B cells [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html)+,
    [BCL6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl6.html)+,
    [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html)-,
    [CD15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd15.html)-,
    [EBER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html)-
  + Eosinophils, plasma cells and neutrophils are uncommon
  + These characteristics argue against a diagnosis of CHL ([Leukemia 2022;36:1720](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35732829))
  + B symptoms are more common
  + Aggressive clinical course
* [Small lymphocytic lymphoma / chronic lymphocytic leukemia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomacll.html):
  + Usually affects elderly patients
  + In rare cases small lymphocytic lymphoma may show scattered HRS cells, mimicking LRCHL
  + Small B cells are [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html)+,
    [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html)+,
    [CD23](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd23.html)+,
    [LEF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsLEF1.html)+ with monotypic surface light chain expression
* [Follicular lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomafollicularnodalusual.html):
  + Follicular proliferation is composed of B cells usually expressing [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html), [BCL6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl6.html) and [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html)
  + There may be scattered large B cells that are [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html)+, with HRS morphology that lack [CD15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd15.html) expression
  + *BCL2* gene rearrangement is present in the majority of cases
* [Nodal TFH T cell lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaPTCLTFH.html):
  + Scattered large B cells with an HRS-like morphology may be present
  + HRS-like cells have a preserved B cell program ([CD79a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd79a.html)+,
    [PAX5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax5.html)+,
    [OCT2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsOCT2.html)+,
    [BOB1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbob1.html)+,
    [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html) variable)
  + Some cases harbor B cells with a HRS phenotype
    ([CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html)+,
    [CD15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd15.html) variable,
    [EBV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) variable) in a background of TFH atypical cells
  + Monoclonal T cell receptor gene rearrangement and NGS showing mutation of the hotspot *RHOA* (G17V) and *IDH2* (R172) favor the diagnosis of nodal TFH ([Histopathology 2014;64:171](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24128129), [Br J Haematol 2008;141:124](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18324974))

[![LRCHL, nodular pattern](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/lymphomachllymphocyterichdeleval03.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/lymphomachllymphocyterichdeleval03.jpg)

What is the characteristic immunophenotype of the large atypical cells shown in the image above?

1. CD20+, CD30-, CD15-, BCL6+, IgD-
2. CD20+, PAX5+, CD23+, CD30+
3. CD45+, OCT2+, CD20+, CD30 -, CD15-, IgD+
4. CD45-, PAX5+, CD30+, CD15+
5. PAX5-, CD4+, CD30+, ALK-

Board review style answer #1

**D.** CD45-, PAX5+, CD30+, CD15+. This is the characteristic immunophenotype of Hodgkin / Reed-Sternberg cells. Answer A is incorrect because the large atypical cells of T cell / histiocytic rich large B cell lymphoma express this immunophenotype. Answer B is incorrect because this immunophenotype is characteristic of primary mediastinal B cell lymphoma. Answer C is incorrect because the LP cells in nodular lymphocyte Hodgkin lymphoma (WHO 5) / nodular lymphocyte predominant B cell lymphoma typically express this immunophenotype (ICC 2022). Answer E is incorrect because this immunophenotype is not specific. T cells could show this immunophenotype.

Board review style question #2

What is the typical composition of the background of lymphocyte rich classic Hodgkin lymphoma (LRCHL) with nodular pattern?

1. Small atypical B cells: CD20+, BCL2+, BCL6+, CD10+
2. Small B cells: CD20+, CD5+, CD23+, LEF1+
3. Small B cells IgD+ admixed with CD4+ PD-1+ T cells
4. Small T cells, eosinophils, plasma cells and neutrophils

Board review style answer #2

**C.** Small B cells IgD+ admixed with CD4+ PD-1+ T cells. The background is typically composed of B cells corresponding to expanded mantle zones (IgD+) admixed with CD4+, PD-1+ T cells, rosetting Hodgkin / Reed-Sternberg (HRS) cells. Answer B is incorrect because this is the characteristic composition of small lymphocytic lymphoma. Answer D is incorrect because this background is not specific but in the present of HRS cells it is diagnostic of mixed cellularity classic Hodgkin lymphoma. Answer A is incorrect because this is the characteristic composition of follicular lymphoma.
