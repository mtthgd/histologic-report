Definition / general

* Subtype of classic Hodgkin lymphoma (CHL) with Hodgkin / Reed-Sternberg (HRS) cells in a nonneoplastic mixed inflammatory background ([Blood 2022;140:1229](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35653592), [Leukemia 2022;36:1720](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35732829))

Essential features

* B cell lymphoma derived from germinal center B cells
* HRS cells in a reactive mixed inflammatory microenvironment consisting of small lymphocytes, eosinophils, plasma cells, neutrophils and histiocytes
* Immunophenotype of HRS cells: PAX5+ (dim), CD30+, CD15 variable, CD20 variable
* EBER+ in 75% of cases

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9652/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=93) - Hodgkin lymphoma, mixed cellularity, NOS
* ICD-10: [C81.2](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C81-C96/C81-/C81.2) - mixed cellularity (classical) Hodgkin lymphoma
* ICD-11: [2B30.12](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f39515681) - mixed cellularity classical Hodgkin lymphoma

Epidemiology

* Second most common subtype of classic Hodgkin lymphoma
* Accounts for 20 - 25% of classic Hodgkin lymphoma
* Bimodal incidence: pediatric population and elderly patients
* M > F
* More common in patients with immunodeficiency and in developing countries ([Expert Rev Hematol 2021;14:547](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34044724))
* EBV infection in 75% of cases; virus is located within the HRS cells and usually shows a type II latency pattern of infection ([N Engl J Med 1989;320:502](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2536894))

Sites

* Lymph nodes (70%) > spleen (30%) > bone marrow (10%) > liver (3%) > other organs (1 - 3%) ([Leuk Lymphoma 2019;60:3426](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31287335))
* Rarely limited to a single region and is more likely to involve extralymphatic tissues compared to other classic Hodgkin lymphoma subtypes
* Mediastinal involvement is rare

Pathophysiology

* Pathophysiology incompletely understood
* Hodgkin / Reed-Sternberg cells derived from preapoptotic germinal center B cells with a disrupted B cell program ([Leukemia 2021;35:968](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33686198))
* HRS harbor monoclonal *IgH* rearrangements with somatic mutations of their immunoglobulin (Ig) variable region genes, losing the capacity of Ig expression ([Leukemia 2021;35:968](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33686198))
* Genetic lesions that promote HRS cell proliferation, survival, immune evasion and interaction with their microenvironment, including alterations in NFκB and JAK-STAT signaling pathways ([Leukemia 2021;35:968](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33686198))
* EBV could play a crucial role by infecting and rescuing Hodgkin / Reed-Sternberg cells with a crippled B cell receptor in the germinal centers from apoptosis and thus inducing lymphomagenesis ([Blood 2005;106:4339](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16076866))
* EBV infection activates the NFκB pathway, promoting survival and growth ([Histopathology 2021;79:451](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33829526))

Clinical features

* B symptoms are common
* May be diagnosed in all clinical stages (more commonly with stage II and III disease)

Diagnosis

* Excisional lymph node biopsy or tissue biopsy with ancillary techniques (immunohistochemistry)

Laboratory

* Nonspecific findings: anemia, leukocytosis, lymphocytopenia, elevated C reactive protein ([N Engl J Med 1998;339:1506](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9819449))

Prognostic factors

* About 90 - 95% of patients with early stage CHL and 80 - 85% with advanced stage CHL are cured ([Am J Hematol 2020;95:978](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32384177))
* Treatment is individualized according to defined risk groups, based on the following prognostic factors:
  + Ann Arbor stage (most important predictor of outcome) ([J Clin Oncol 2014;32:3059](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25113753))
  + Bulky (mediastinal) disease
  + Extranodal involvement
  + B symptoms
  + International Prognostic Score ([N Engl J Med 1998;339:1506](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9819449))

Treatment

* Intensive polychemotherapy (ABVD or BEACOPP), with or without radiotherapy ([Am J Hematol 2020;95:978](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32384177))
* For refractory cases: stem cell transplant, brentuximab vedotin, checkpoint inhibitors ([N Engl J Med 2018;378:331](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29224502))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Diffuse or interfollicular proliferation of HRS cells (Leukemia 2022;36:1720)
* CD4+ T cells are the most abundant immune cells in the CHL microenvironment ([Cytometry B Clin Cytom 2008;74:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18061945), [Blood 2004;103:1755](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14604957), [Lab Invest 2008;88:482](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18362907))
* In EBV+ cases, there may be numerous epithelioid histiocytes and even granulomas
* Fine interstitial fibrosis may be seen but without collagen broad bands and with no capsular thickening
* Bone marrow is involved in 10% of cases and shows scattered HRS cells in a background that is rich in histiocytes with ill defined granulomas merging with inflammatory cells and normal hematopoiesis

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Aneuploidy is seen in nearly all cases ([Blood 1995;86:1464](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7632954), [Cancer Res 2006;66:10332](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17079453), [Cancers (Basel) 2018;10:91](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29587466))
* High constitutive activity of the NFκB pathway is the hallmark of HRS cells, which is essential for HRS survival ([Semin Cancer Biol 2016;39:32](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27221964))
* Mutations in members of the JAK-STAT signaling pathway, main mediator of cytokine signaling ([Nat Genet 2014;46:329](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24531327))
* Mutations in ITPKB, PI3K-AKT and MAP-ERK pathways ([Leukemia 2020;34:151](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31431735))
* Lesions in genes involved in immune evasion of HRS: *PDL1*, *PDL2*, *B2M*, *CIITA*, *CD58* ([Blood 2010;116:3268](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20628145), [J Clin Oncol 2016;34:2690](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27069084))
* Mutations in additional genes: *ARID1A*, *TNFRS14*, *XPO1* ([Leukemia 2021;35:968](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33686198))

Sample pathology report

* Lymph node, right neck, excision:
  + Mixed cellularity classic Hodgkin lymphoma (see comment)
  + Comment: H&E stained sections show a lymph node with architectural effacement by an interfollicular mixed inflammatory infiltrate comprising small lymphocytes, eosinophils, neutrophils, plasma cells, with scattered admixed large atypical lymphoid cells. The neoplastic cells have monolobated / bilobated / multilobated nuclei, vesicular chromatin, prominent nucleoli and amphophilic cytoplasm, consistent with Hodgkin and Reed-Sternberg cells.
  + Immunophenotypically, the large atypical cells are positive for PAX5 (weak), CD30 (strong), CD15 and MUM1. These cells are negative for CD20 and CD45.
  + T cell markers reveal a rich background composed of small lymphocytes.
  + In situ hybridization for EBV encoded RNA (EBER) is positive on the large atypical cells.

Differential diagnosis

* [EBV+ diffuse large B cell lymphoma, NOS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomadiffuseEBV.html):
  + Large cells are usually [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html)+, [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html)+, [CD79a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd79a.html)+, [PAX5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax5.html)+ (strong), [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html)+, [CD15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd15.html)- and may show monotypic **Ig** expression
  + [EBV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) is located within both large and small lymphoid cells
  + Frequently extranodal
  + Geographic necrosis is common
  + Adults and elderly patients
* [EBV+ mucocutaneous ulcer](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaebvmucocutulcer.html):
  + Solitary ulcers in mucocutaneous sites with or without locoregional lymph node involvement
  + In immunosuppression settings or in the elderly
  + HRS-like cells are [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html)+, [CD15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd15.html)-
* [Lymphomatoid granulomatosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomalymphomatoidgran.html):
  + Angiocentric and angiodestructive lymphoproliferation, composed of [EBV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) positive B cells, may include HRS-like cells ([CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html)+, [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html)+, [CD15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd15.html)-)
  + Pulmonary involvement is mandatory for the diagnosis ([Blood 2020;135:1344](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32107539))
* [T cell / histiocytic rich large B cell lymphoma (THRLBCL)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomadiffusetcell.html):
  + Composed of a background rich in small lymphocytes and histiocytes with scattered, large neoplastic B cells
  + [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html)+, [BCL6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl6.html)+, [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html)-, [CD15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd15.html)-, [EBER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html)-
  + Eosinophils, plasma cells and neutrophils are uncommon
  + These characteristics argue against a diagnosis of CHL ([Leukemia 2022;36:1720](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35732829))
* [Posttransplant lymphoproliferative disorder, polymorphic type](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBpolymorphicB.html):
  + A spectrum of maturation of lymphoid cells, including small lymphocytes, lymphoplasmocytoid cells, plasma cells, immunoblasts and large HRS-like cells
  + HRS-like cells are usually [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html)+, [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html)+, [PAX5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax5.html)+ (strong), [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html)+, [CD15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd15.html)-
  + Most [EBV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) positive cases reveal a type III EBV latency pattern
  + Classic Hodgkin lymphoma is uncommon in the posttransplant setting
* [Angioimmunoblastic T cell lymphoma (AITL)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBAITL.html) and other subtypes of **follicular helper T cell lymphoma**:
  + Proliferation of atypical neoplastic T follicular helper (TFH) cells
  + Proliferation of high endothelial venules
  + Expansion of the follicular dendritic cell meshwork
  + HRS-like cells are usually [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html)+, [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html)+, [CD15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd15.html)- (or sometimes [CD15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd15.html)+)
  + Rosettes of neoplastic TFH cells around HRS-like cells
  + Monoclonal T cell receptor gene rearrangement
* Reactive conditions:
  + [Infectious mononucleosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/spleeninfectiousmono.html):
    - Lymph node architecture may be partially effaced and may reveal a mottled appearance
    - Hyperplasic follicles with prominent germinal centers
    - Paracortical expansion with polymorphic lymphoid cells, including immunoblasts, polytypic plasma cells and HRS-like cells ([CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html)+, [CD15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd15.html)-), numerous mitoses and a high proliferative index ([Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html))
    - Monocytoid cells and sinus histiocytosis may be present

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/lymphomamixedcellularitydeleval03.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/lymphomamixedcellularitydeleval03.jpg)

What is the characteristic immunophenotype of the large, atypical cells shown in the image above?

1. CD2+, CD30+, CD15-, ALK+
2. CD20+, PAX5+, CD23+, CD30+
3. CD45+, OCT2+, CD20+, CD30-
4. CD45-, PAX5+, CD30+, CD15+
5. PAX5-, CD4+, CD30+, ALK-

Board review style answer #1

**D**. CD45-, PAX5+, CD30+, CD15+. This is the characteristic immunophenotype of Hodgkin / Reed-Sternberg cells. The immunophenotype in answer A is diagnostic of ALK positive anaplastic large cell lymphoma. The lymphocyte predominant (LP) cells in nodular lymphocyte Hodgkin lymphoma (WHO) / nodular lymphocyte predominant B cell lymphoma (ICC) typically express the immunophenotype in answer C. The immunophenotype in answer E is not specific. T cells could show this immunophenotype.

Board review style question #2

Which subtype of classic Hodgkin lymphoma is most frequently encountered in patients with HIV / AIDS?

1. Lymphocyte depleted
2. Lymphocyte rich
3. Mixed cellularity
4. Nodular sclerosis

Board review style answer #2

**C**. Mixed cellularity. Mixed cellularity classic Hodgkin lymphoma is the most common subtype of CHL associated with immunodeficiency states, including HIV infection / AIDS, immunodeficiency secondary to immunosenescence and immunodeficiency secondary to solid organ transplantation.
