Definition / general

* Large B cell lymphoma that is confined to and presumably arises in testis
* Recently classified under large B cell lymphomas (LBCL) of immune privileged sites in the most recent revision of the WHO, 5th edition; this is a new umbrella term that acknowledges common biological features of aggressive B cell lymphomas ([Leukemia 2022 Jun 22 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35732829))
  + This new entity now combines the previous entity of primary DLBCL of CNS with DLBCL of the vitreoretina and testis that were previously included among DLBCL, NOS
* Recently considered a distinct entity by the International Consensus Classification of Mature Lymphoid Neoplasms, closely related to primary DLBCL of the central nervous system on the basis of common clinical and molecular features ([Blood 2022 Jun 2 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35653592))

Essential features

* Comprises 80 - 90% of all primary testicular lymphomas with a mean age of presentation between 60 and 70 years of age
* Composed of large atypical lymphoid cells with a predominant centroblastic morphology that replace the testicular parenchyma
* Positive for pan B cell markers and typically positive for BCL6, MUM1 and BLC2

Terminology

* Primary DLBCL of testis
* Primary testicular lymphoma

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9680/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=94) - malignant lymphoma, large B cell, diffuse, NOS
* ICD-10: [C83.3](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C81-C96/C83-/C83.3) - diffuse large B cell lymphoma

Epidemiology

* Typically presents in adult men with a median age between 60 and 70 at diagnosis
  + Most common testicular malignancy in men > 60 years of age
* Most common testicular malignancy to present with bilateral involvement
* Increased incidence in HIV positive men ([Cancers (Basel) 2021;13:4049](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34439203))
  + HIV positive patients with primary testicular diffuse large B cell lymphoma (DLBCL PT) are younger (median age of 36)

Sites

* Most patients present with localized disease
* Approximately 20% of patients have advanced stage with spread to contralateral testis, CNS or other extranodal sites ([J Clin Oncol 2009;27:5227](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19770371))

Pathophysiology

* Evaluation of the cell of origin through IHC and gene expression profiling has demonstrated that DLBCL PT has a non-GCB phenotype in 60 - 96% of cases ([Mod Pathol 2006;19:1521](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16998463), [J Pathol 2006;210:163](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16823896))
* Characterized by deregulation of intercellular immune signaling mechanisms, including NFkB pathway activation ([Blood 2016;127:869](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26702065))
  + Oncogenic toll-like receptor (TLR) signaling (via *MYD88* activating mutations) and B cell receptor (BCR) signaling (via *CD79b* mutation) are central to the pathogenesis, leading to NFkB pathway activation
  + Prosurvival signals through mutations in *PIM1/2*, *CARD11* and *TNFAIP3* further contribute to the dysregulation in NFkB signaling
* Mutations within genes mediating immune surveillance are also central to DLBCL PT pathogenesis
  + Losses of the HLA class I and II loci are associated with reduced expression of major histocompatibility complexes (MHC)
  + Gains and amplifications of the programmed death ligand (PDL) 1 (*CD274*) and 2 (*CD273*) loci, that increase protein expression and contribute to the evasion of an antitumor immune response

Etiology

* Unknown in immunocompetent patients

Clinical features

* Firm, painless testicular mass without preference for either side ([Blood 2014;123:486](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24282217))
  + Median tumor size at presentation is 6 cm
* Systemic symptoms are infrequent, seen in approximately 25 - 40% of cases
  + Fever, anorexia, night sweats, weight loss
* Most patients present with localized disease and about 20% present with advanced stage disease, including spread to contralateral testis, CNS or other extranodal sites

Diagnosis

* Imaging modalities that may assist in diagnosis include:
  + Ultrasonography
  + Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI)
* Biopsy of the involved testis or orchiectomy
* Secondary testicular involvement by systemic lymphoma should be excluded
* Reference: [Ann Oncol 2016;27:v91](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27377716)

Laboratory

* Increased serum lactate dehydrogenase (LDH), increased serum B2 microglobulin and hypoalbuminemia have been associated with an adverse prognosis ([J Clin Oncol 2003;21:20](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12506165))

Radiology description

* Ultrasonography will show focal or diffuse areas of hypoechogenicity with hypervascularity in affected testis
* MRI allows simultaneous evaluation of both testes, paratesticular spaces and spermatic cord

Prognostic factors

* High relapse rate; recurrence most often occurs in the CNS or the contralateral testis ([Br J Haematol 2017;176:210](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27739058))
* Involvement / recurrence in the CNS is associated with a dismal prognosis; relapse in the CNS has been reported in up to 30% of the cases within 1 - 2 years from diagnosis
* Adverse prognostic factors for progression free survival include age > 70 years, advanced stage, B symptoms, involvement of extranodal sites other than testis and tumor diameter > 10 cm ([J Clin Oncol 2009;27:5227](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19770371), [J Clin Oncol 2003;21:20](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12506165), [Leuk Lymphoma 2010;51:1217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20443676))

Case reports

* 47 year old man with right testicular swelling ([Ann Clin Lab Sci 2010;40:75](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20124334))
* 48 year old man with bilateral testicular masses ([J Surg Case Rep 2021;2021:rjab431](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34729164))
* 55 year old man with bilateral testicular enlargement and pain ([Urol Case Rep 2021;38:101733](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34141587))
* 16 patients ranging from 36 - 93 years old with various clinical presentations of DLBCL PT ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2020;99:e19463](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32195944))

Treatment

* Orchiectomy followed by immunochemotherapy (R-CHOP or R-CHOP-like regimen)
* Addition of CNS prophylaxis with CNS penetrating chemotherapy or intrathecal chemotherapy, as well as irradiation or excision of the contralateral testis, are highly recommended
* References: [J Clin Oncol 2011;29:2766](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21646602), [Urol Oncol 2020;38:641.e1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32307328), [Clin Lymphoma Myeloma 2009;9:386](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19858059), [Leukemia 2016;30:361](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26308769)

Gross description

* Enlarged testicle with a fleshy, diffuse or lobulated firm mass
* Cut surface is relatively homogeneous and bulging, with a tan-yellow color
* Often demonstrates a sharp demarcation from testicular parenchyma
* Reference: [Medicine (Baltimore) 2020;99:e19463](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32195944)

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Diffuse sheets of large lymphoma cells ([Pathology 2020;52:53](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31735345))
  + Initially infiltrate between seminiferous tubules before total replacement of the parenchyma
  + Lymphoma cells frequently invade and fill the seminiferous tubules
  + Frequent mitotic figures can be seen ([Hematol Oncol 2014;32:72](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23949965))
  + Necrosis is common
* Lymphoma cells show centroblastic morphology in most cases (90%) and less commonly, immunoblastic morphology (10%)
* Associated changes in the testis include spermatogenic arrest, interstitial fibrosis and tubular hyalinization

Positive stains

* Pan B cell antigens ([CD19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd19.html), [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html), [CD79a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd79a.html), [PAX5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsPAX5.html)) ([Leukemia 2016;30:361](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26308769), [Blood Rev 2018;32:249](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29289361))
* [BCL6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsBCL6.html) (75 - 90%), [MUM1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsMUM1.html) (40 - 70%), [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) (20 - 40%)
* [TCL1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsTCL1.html) (~67%), **FOXP1** (~80%)
* [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsBCL2.html) (~75%), [MYC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscmyc.html) (13%) ([Hematol Oncol 2014;32:72](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23949965))
  + Double expressers have been reported in 13% of cases
* [OCT4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct34.html) expression has been demonstrated in up to 18% of DLBCL PT and represents a potential diagnostic pitfall with germ cell tumors ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:950](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27035612))
* [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) ranges from 40 - 90% (mean of 70%) ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2020;99:e19463](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32195944))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* *IGH* rearrangements in virtually all cases
* *BCL2* and *MYC* rearrangements occur in 10% and 15% of cases, respectively; *BCL6* rearrangement occurs in 40% ([Leuk Lymphoma 2014;55:1410](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23952247))
* Genetic analysis ([Onco Targets Ther 2019;12:10165](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32063711), [Leukemia 2014;28:719](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24253023)):
  + *MYD88*: amplifications, mutations and deletions (60 - 82%)
  + *CD79B*: mutations and deletions (19 - 34%)
  + *NFKBIZ*: copy number gain (42%)
  + *CDKN2A*: copy number alterations (71%)
  + *CD274*: rearrangements, copy number alterations, an increased protein expression (35%)
  + *CD273*: rearrangements, copy number alterations, an increased protein expression (47%)

Sample pathology report

* Right testis, orchiectomy:
  + Diffuse large B cell lymphoma, nongerminal center type (see comment)
  + Comment: In the proper clinical circumstances, findings are consistent with primary diffuse large B cell lymphoma of the testis. Clinical correlation is required to exclude systemic or extratesticular primary disease.

Differential diagnosis

* [Seminoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisseminomas.html):
  + Most common germ cell tumor in adult men
    - Mean age of 40 years
  + Nests, cords or sheets of tumor cells within thin, fibrous septa
    - Pale cytoplasm, distinct cell borders and squared off nuclei
  + Immunohistochemistry:
    - [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html)+, [OCT3/4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct34.html)+, [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)+, [PLAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsplap.html)+
    - [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html)-, [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html)-, [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html)-, [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html)-
* [Embryonal carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorundiffsarcoma.html):
  + Presents in younger men (mean age of 30)
  + Variety of histological patterns: solid, papillary, tubular
  + Large cells with pleomorphic nuclei and atypia
  + Immunohistochemistry:
    - [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html)+, [OCT3/4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct34.html)+, [SOX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsSOX2.html)+, [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html)+
    - [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)-, [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)-, [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html)-, [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html)-
* [Myeloid sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/leukemiagranulocytic.html):
  + Diffuse replacement by blasts
    - Thin nuclear membranes, fine chromatin, variable prominent nucleoli
  + [MPO](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyeloperoxidase.html)+, [lysozyme](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainslysozyme.html)+, [CD43](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd43.html)+, [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html)+, [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)+

A man presents with right testicular mass and undergoes orchiectomy. Microscopic findings reveal a diffuse process of large lymphoid cells replacing the testicular parenchyma (see image). In which age group is this entity most commonly seen?

1. < 15 years
2. 30 - 40 years

Board review style answer #1

**C.** 60 - 70 years. Diffuse large B cell lymphoma of the testis has a mean age of presentation of 60 - 70 years.
