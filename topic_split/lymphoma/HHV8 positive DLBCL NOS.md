Definition / general

* HHV8 related diffuse large B cell lymphoma (DLBCL) is a neoplasm characterized by a monoclonal proliferation of HHV8 infected lymphoid cells that resemble plasmablasts and express IgM lambda
* Neoplastic cell corresponds to a naïve IgM producing B cell without immunoglobulin somatic hypermutations

Essential features

* Usually arises in association with multicentric Castleman disease (MCD) and HIV infection
* Morphologically similar to plasmablasts but correspond to naïve IgM producing B cell without somatic hypermutations
* Sheets of HHV8 latency associated nuclear antigen (LANA1) positive plasmablasts efface lymph node architecture
* Atypical cells are strongly positive for cytoplasmic IgM with lambda light chain restriction and negative for EBV encoded small RNA (EBER)

Terminology

* HHV8+ large B cell lymphoma arising in multicentric Castleman disease (obsolete)
* HHV8 positive plasmablastic lymphoma (obsolete)
* HHV8+ diffuse large B cell lymphoma, not otherwise specified

Epidemiology

* Rare disease with overall frequency of 0.1% ([Leuk Lymphoma 2018;59:1375](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29019447))
* Median age is 47 years (range 39 - 66); M:F = 3.3:1 ([Pathology 2020;52:53](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31735345))
* Usually arises in association with HHV8 positive multicentric Castleman disease (MCD) and HIV (15 times higher risk) ([Blood 2002;99:2331](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11895764))

Sites

* Characteristically involves lymph nodes or spleen
* Can disseminate to other viscera including liver, lungs and gastrointestinal tract
* Can also manifest as a leukemia with involvement of peripheral blood ([Blood 2002;99:2331](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11895764))

Pathophysiology

* HHV8 infects singly scattered cells that are morphologically similar to plasmablasts with abundant cytoplasmic immunoglobulin but are, in fact, naïve IgM producing B cells that lack IG somatic hypermutations and are polyclonal at the molecular level
* HHV8 positive plasmablasts form aggregates (microlymphomas), which can be polyclonal or monoclonal ([Am J Clin Pathol 2017;147:171](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28395104))
* HHV8 positive aggregates rarely can expand to HHV8 positive large B cell lymphoma, which is monoclonal

Etiology

* By definition, the large plasmablastic cells in all cases are positive for HHV8
* Molecular mechanism is similar to other HHV8 positive entities ([Blood 2004;104:3349](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15271792))

Clinical features

* Usually manifests with profound immunodeficiency, enlarging lymph nodes and massive splenomegaly
* There may be manifestations of Kaposi sarcoma ([Blood 2002;99:2331](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11895764))
* Rarely, may arise in absence of MCD ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:745](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28084335))

Diagnosis

* Biopsy of involved lymph node with immunohistochemistry
* Effacement of lymph node architecture by sheets of HHV8 positive, LANA1 positive and EBER negative plasmablasts ([Am J Clin Pathol 2017;147:171](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28395104))

Laboratory

* Rarely, there is involvement of bone marrow and peripheral blood by HHV8 positive IgM lambda plasmablasts ([Eur J Haematol 2013;91:497](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23992152))

Prognostic factors

* Extremely aggressive clinical course and poor prognosis

Case reports

* 37 and 42 year old men with multifocal hepatic Kaposi sarcoma and intravascular HHV8 positive (EBV negative) systemic DLBCL, NOS ([Autops Case Rep 2020;10:e2020206](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33344322))
* 56 year old man with an unusual EBV associated HHV8 positive large B cell lymphoma ([Clin Case Rep 2019;7:587](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30899504))
* 59 year old woman with hemoglobin sickle cell disease and sequential development of HHV8 positive DLBCL and chronic myelomonocytic leukemia ([Pathol Res Pract 2019;215:152704](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31699472))

Treatment

* No definite treatment guidelines
* Can be treated with the same regimens described for patients with HIV associated DLBCL

Gross description

* Involved lymph node is enlarged
* Firm, fleshy mass

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Effacement of lymph node / splenic architecture by confluent sheets of large plasmablastic cells
* Large plasmablastic cells have amphophilic cytoplasm and vesicular, often eccentrically placed nuclei containing 1 or 2 prominent nucleoli ([Am J Clin Pathol 2017;147:171](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28395104))
* High mitotic activity

Positive stains

* Stippled nuclear staining for [HHV8 (LANA1)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshhv8.html) in large plasmablasts in all cases
* Strongly express cytoplasmic [IgM](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsIgA.html) with [lambda light chain](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainskappa.html) restriction ([Blood 2002;99:2331](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11895764))
* [MUM1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmum1.html)
* [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html) and [CD38](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd38.html) may be positive

Negative stains

* [EBV encoded small RNA (EBER)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) ([Am J Clin Pathol 2017;147:171](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28395104))
* [CD79a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd79a.html), [PAX5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax5.html), [CD138](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd138.html)
* [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html), [ALK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* PCR reveals a monoclonal B cell population
* Immunoglobulin (IG) genes are unmutated, lacking somatic hypermutation

Sample pathology report

* Lymph node, right cervical, excision:
  + Diffuse large B cell lymphoma, HHV8 positive, NOS

Differential diagnosis

* [Extracavitary primary effusion lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaeffusion.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1401](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15489644)):
  + [EBER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html)+
  + Lacks cytoplasmic immunoglobulin
  + Expresses activation and plasma cell associated antigens ([CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html), [CD38](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd38.html), [CD138](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd138.html) and [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html))
  + Arises from terminally differentiated B cells with hypermutated IG genes
* [HHV8+ multicentric Castleman disease (MCD)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodescastleman.html) ([Blood 2014;123:2924](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24622327), [Blood 2001;97:2130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11264181)):
  + Often presents with generalized lymphadenopathy and constitutional symptoms
  + Abnormal follicles with varied degree of hyalinization and involution of germinal centers
  + Widened concentric rings of mantle zones (onion skinning) and prominent penetrating venules
  + Interfollicular plasma cell hyperplasia
  + Plasmablasts predominantly in mantle zones and [lambda light chain](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainskappa.html) restricted
  + Polyclonal pattern of IG rearrangement
* [HHV8 related germinotropic lymphoproliferative disorder](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaHHV8germinotropicLPD.html) ([Blood 2002;100:3415](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12384445), [Ann Pathol 2014;34:373](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25439990)):
  + Occurs in immunocompetent patients
  + [HIV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshivp24.html)-
  + Localized or sometimes multifocal lymph node involvement
  + Retention of lymph node architecture with [HHV8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshhv8.html)+ plasmablasts involving or replacing germinal centers
  + [EBER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html)+, [CD38](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd38.html)+, [kappa or lambda light chain](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainskappa.html) restriction
  + Polyclonal or oligoclonal pattern of *IGH* rearrangement
  + Favorable response to chemotherapy or radiation
* [Plasmablastic lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaplasmablastic.html) ([Histopathology 2012;61:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22958176), [Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:1686](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20962620)):
  + Often presents in oral cavity or extranodal sites
  + Association with immunodeficiency or HIV
  + [EBER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html)+, [HHV8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshhv8.html)-
  + Usually expresses a plasma cell phenotype ([CD38](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd38.html)+, [CD138](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd138.html)+, [MUM1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmum1.html)+)
  + Cytoplasmic immunoglobulin positive (mostly [IgG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsIgA.html) and [kappa / lambda](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainskappa.html))
  + Frequently class switched and hypermutated IG genes
  + *MYC* translocation in approximately 50% of cases
