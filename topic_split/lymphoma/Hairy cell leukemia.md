Definition / general

* Hairy cell leukemia (HCL) is a rare, indolent lymphoproliferative neoplasm of mature B cells with a distinct clinical presentation that includes peripheral blood cytopenias, splenomegaly and a small number of circulating neoplastic cells with hair-like cytoplasmic projections

Essential features

* Pancytopenia and monocytopenia
* Splenomegaly
* Immunophenotype positive for CD11c, CD19, CD20 bright, CD22 bright, CD25, CD103, CD123 and CD305 (LAIR)
* *BRAF* V600E mutation is a hallmark genetic mutation in > 95% of cases

Terminology

* Hairy cell leukemia: no changes were made to this entity in the recent 5th edition of the WHO classification of hematolymphoid tumors
* Classic hairy cell leukemia (cHCL): used in some of the medical literature to best distinguish hairy cell leukemia from splenic B cell lymphoma / leukemia with prominent nucleoli (WHO 5th edition; formerly known as HCL variant [HCL-v])
* Leukemic reticuloendotheliosis: obsolete by the WHO classification of tumors of hematopoietic and lymphoid tissues

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9940/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=101) - hairy cell leukemia

Epidemiology

* Incidence: < 2% of all leukemias, < 1 per 100,000 persons/year in U.S.
* M:F = 4:1
* Median age of diagnosis is 55 years
* More common in whites and less in African, Asian and Arab populations
* Increased risk: farming and exposure to pesticides ([Scand J Work Environ Health 1996;22:285](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8881017))
* Smoking has been reported as a significant negative association and appears to be protective ([Br J Haematol 1995;91:154](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7577624))

Sites

* Common sites of involvement: bone marrow, peripheral blood and spleen
* Uncommon sites of involvement: liver, lymph node and skin

Pathophysiology

* *BRAF* V600E mutation is the causal genetic event in the vast majority of HCL
  + Mutation constitutively activates BRAF by autophosphorylation of the protein and downstream MEK-ERK signaling pathway, leading to increased expression of genes involved in survival and proliferation and promoting leukemic transformation ([Blood 2016;128:1918](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27554081))

Etiology

* Epidemiologic data shows a multifactorial etiology that is influenced by ethnicity and geographical factors
* Farming and exposure to pesticides, petroleum products, diesel and ionizing radiation are associated with an increased risk ([Best Pract Res Clin Haematol 2015;28:175](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26614895))

Clinical features

* Pancytopenia and monocytopenia are common findings
* Often reported symptoms are fatigue and infection
* Splenomegaly: massive splenomegaly that can cause left sided abdominal pain is less commonly seen today due to early detection
* Lymphadenopathy is not usually seen in HCL ([Blood 2017;129:553](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27903528))

Diagnosis

* Diagnosis is based on peripheral blood and bone marrow morphology, flow cytometry immunophenotyping, immunohistochemistry and molecular studies

Laboratory

* CBC and differential count: pancytopenia and monocytopenia ([Blood 2017;129:553](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27903528))
* Peripheral blood smear morphology: circulating neoplastic hairy cells are present but at a low frequency and they can be difficult to identify

Prognostic factors

* Chronic, relatively indolent leukemia that predominantly responds well to current chemotherapy; 5 year event free survival rate after treatment is ~90%
* Poor prognostic factors include:
  + Massive splenomegaly, leukocytosis (> 10 x 109/L), high number of neoplastic hairy cells in the blood (> 5 x 109/L) and high beta2 microglobulin ([Blood 2012;119:3330](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22210875))
  + Unmutated *IGHV* status ([Am J Hematol 2017;92:1382](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29110361))
  + *IGHV4-34* positive immunoglobulin variable heavy chain gene rearrangement ([Blood 2009;114:4687](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19745070))
  + CD38 expression ([Cancer Res 2015;75:3902](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26170397))

Case reports

* 16 year old boy presented with hairy cell leukemia ([Blood 2018;132:1216](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30213840))
* 41 year old man presented with unilateral sudden hearing loss as an initial manifestation of hairy cell leukemia ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2019;60:200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31295705))
* Man in mid-60s presented with severe Legionnaires disease pneumonia associated with undiagnosed hairy cell leukemia ([BMJ Case Rep 2019;12:e230130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31420430))
* 70 year old African American man with hairy cell leukemia presented with pancytopenia but without splenomegaly ([Case Rep Hematol 2014;2014:354810](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24782931))
* 72 year old man presented with coexisting chronic lymphocytic leukemia and hairy cell leukemia ([Blood 2019;133:1264](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30872271))
* 74 year old woman presented with osteolytic lesions in a case of hairy cell leukemia mimicking multiple myeloma ([Lancet Oncol 2019;20:e187](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30842062))
* 79 year old man with relapsed hairy cell leukemia diagnosed by endoscopic ultrasound guided FNA ([Cytojournal 2006;3:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16430774))

Treatment

* Not curative; asymptomatic patients usually do not require treatment
* Standard therapy is purine analogues (i.e., cladribine, pentostatin), which are effective in the majority of HCL
* Late relapses are not uncommon; choices for relapsed and refractory HCL include moxetumomab (anti-CD22), pasudotox (anti-CD22), vemurafenib (BRAF inhibitor) and bendamustine plus rituximab
* Splenectomy as salvage therapy ([Blood 2017;129:553](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27903528))

Gross description

* Spleen: splenomegaly, > 150 g; spleen shows a diffuse and marked enlargement without nodules

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Spleen:
  + Red pulp involvement by sheets of bland tumor cells that are larger than lymphocytes with modest, pale blue agranular cytoplasm, round or folded nuclei and no distinct nucleoli
  + There is an increase in volume, surface and length of red pulp vessels that can classically form pools or lakes of blood lined by hairy cells
* Bone marrow:
  + Bone marrow aspirate is often a dry tap due to extensive marrow fibrosis as demonstrable by reticulin stain; however, neoplastic hairy cells, as described in the peripheral blood description, can be seen ([Blood 2003;102:1051](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12689941))
  + Bone marrow biopsy: always involved
    - Neoplastic infiltrates can vary and have a patchy and subtle, interstitial or diffuse solid pattern
    - Presence of fibrosis is common; ~10% of cases can be markedly hypocellular and mimic aplastic anemia

Positive stains

* Bone marrow IHC stain is important to appreciate tumor infiltration
* Typical pattern for B cell lymphocytes includes [CD19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd19.html), [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html), [CD79a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd79a.html) and [PAX5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax5.html)
* **Annexin A1** expression is specific to distinguishing HCL from other B cell lymphomas including HCL variant; however, correlation with B cell markers is needed, particularly in hypocellular and low tumor burden cases since annexin is also expressed by T cells and myeloid cells ([Lancet 2004;363:1869](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15183626))
* [TRAP (tartrate resistant acid phophotase)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstrap.html): done by immunohistochemistry; in the aspirate smears, [TRAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstrap.html) test by enzyme stain is not commonly done anymore since it is a more difficult technical and interpretative procedure
  + [TRAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstrap.html) IHC in the core biopsy is currently more commonly used
* [DBA-44](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdba44.html): combined [TRAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstrap.html) and [DBA-44](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdba44.html) is sensitive and fairly specific ([Am J Surg Pathol 2005;29:474](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15767800))
* [Cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html): positive staining; dim staining compared to what is seen in mantle cell lymphoma ([Mod Pathol 2000;13:1308](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11144927))
* [BRAF V600E](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbraf.html): positive overexpression by immunohistochemistry studies; point mutations are also detected by molecular studies
* [Reticulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsreticulin.html) is increased due to the presence of fibrosis
* **TIA1** is seen in 56% of cases ([Mod Pathol 2004;17:840](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15073603))

Negative stains

* Usually negative for [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html), [CD23](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd23.html), [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) and [BCL6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl6.html)
* ~10% of otherwise typical HCL express [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html), which can create a potential diagnostic pitfall with follicular lymphoma in the bone marrow; however, they are negative for [BCL6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl6.html) ([Am J Clin Pathol 2003;120:228](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12931553))

Flow cytometry description

* Flow cytometry immunophenotyping is helpful for the diagnosis of HCL ([Leuk Lymphoma 2011;52:11](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21504292))
* HCL has high side scatter and it is placed in the monocytic region on a [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html) / SSC plot
* Bright expression of [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html), [CD22](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20to29.html#cd22) and monotypic surface light chain immunoglobulin
* Positive expression for [CD11c](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10to19.html#cd11c)
* Positive coexpression for specific hairy cell leukemia markers [CD123](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd123.html), [CD103](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd100.html#cd103), [CD25](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20to29.html#cd25) and **CD305 (LAIR)**
* Positive expression for [CD200](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd200.html)
* Usually negative or dim for [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html), [CD23](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd23.html), [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html), [CD79b](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd79b.html) and [CD27](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd27.html)
* ~10% of cases show [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) coexpression ([Am J Clin Pathol 2003;120:228](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12931553))

Electron microscopy description

* A cytoplasmic inclusion known as the ribosome - lamellae complex (RLC) has been described but may also occur in other hematolymphoid neoplasms ([Lab Invest 1980;42:236](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7354620), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 1991;115:1259](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1768218), [Ultrastruct Pathol 2007;31:135](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17613993))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* *BRAF* V600E mutation is a hallmark for classical HCL; this mutation is not seen in HCL variant
* Rare cases that are *BRAF* V600E mutation negative will be positive for *IGHV4-34*
* Histone methyltransferase *KMT2C (MLL3)* and *CDKN1B* are commonly mutated genes in cHCL
* *BRD4, CEBPA, CREBBP, RUNX1, EP300* and *MED12*, Notch signaling (*NOTCH1* and *NOTCH2*) and DNA repair
* Deletions of chromosomes 7q and 13q and gains of chromosome 5 ([Blood 2017;130:1644](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28801450))

Sample pathology report

* Bone marrow, (R)PSIS, aspirate, biopsy and peripheral blood:
  + Positive for classical hairy cell leukemia involving ~40% of the sample (see comment)
  + Comment: Peripheral blood with pancytopenia and rare circulating neoplastic hairy cells, BRAF V600E mutation. Flow cytometry and molecular studies support the diagnosis.

Differential diagnosis

* [Splenic B cell lymphoma / leukemia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaspleniBcellunclassifiable.html) with prominent nucleoli, formerly known as [HCL variant (HCL-v)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomahclvariant.html):
  + Usually present with absolute lymphocytosis and less severe cytopenias
  + Can have a similar flow cytometry immunophenotype with bright [CD11c](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10to19.html#cd11c) and positive coexpression for [CD103](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd100.html#cd103) but are usually negative for [CD25](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20to29.html#cd25), [CD123](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd123.html) and [CD200](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd200.html)
  + Negative for *BRAF* V600E mutations; these cases can be *IGHV4-34* positive
* [Splenic marginal zone lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomasplenicmzl.html):
  + Can have circulating neoplastic cells with cytoplasmic projections; these cells are called villous lymphocytes
  + Usually show involvement of the white pulp in the spleen and are negative for classical hairy cell markers [CD123](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd123.html), [CD103](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd100.html#cd103), [CD25](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20to29.html#cd25) and **CD305 (LAIR)** by flow cytometry
  + Negative by IHC for **annexin 1** and [cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html) and negative for *BRAF* V600E mutations
* [Splenic diffuse red pulp lymphoma (SDRPL)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomasdrpl.html):
  + Can present with splenomegaly and is positive for [CD11c](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10to19.html#cd11c)
  + Negative for classical hairy cell markers [CD123](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd123.html), [CD103](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd100.html#cd103), [CD25](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20to29.html#cd25) and **CD305 (LAIR)** by flow cytometry
  + Negative by IHC for **annexin 1** and [cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html) and negative for *BRAF* V600E mutations
