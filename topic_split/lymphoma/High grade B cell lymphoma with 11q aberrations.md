Definition / general

* Rare B cell lymphoma that resembles Burkitt lymphoma morphologically, phenotypically and by gene expression profiling
* Negative for *MYC* rearrangement

Essential features

* Rare, germinal center-derived mature B-cell lymphoma characterized by proximal gains and telomeric losses of chromosome 11q (including 11q-gain/loss, telomeric loss or telomeric LOH pattern)
* Features overlapping with Burkitt lymphoma, but lacks *MYC* rearrangement
* Most patients are children and young adults presenting with lymphadenopathy
* Diffuse infiltrate of medium sized to large cells, frequently with interspersed tingible body macrophages and apoptotic debris
* Lymphoma cells positive for CD20, CD10, and BCL6, and negative for BCL2, with Ki67 > 95%
* Lymphoma cells show LMO2 and sometimes CD56 expression ([Leukemia 2022;36:1720](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35732829))
* Favorable prognosis with intensive chemoimmunotherapy

Terminology

* Large B cell lymphoma with 11q aberration ([Blood 2022;140:1200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35767897))
* High grade B cell lymphoma with 11q aberrations ([Leukemia 2022;36:1720](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35732829))
* Not recommended: Burkitt-like lymphoma with 11q aberration
* Burkitt-like lymphoma

ICD coding

* ICD-O:  [9687/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/%0D%0A9789241548496_eng.pdf#page=95) - Burkitt lymphoma, NOS

Epidemiology

* Most common in children and young adults, median in third decade ([Mod Pathol 2018;31:732](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29327714), [Haematologica 2019;104:1822](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30733272), [Blood 2014;123:1187](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24398325))
* Male predominance
* Seen in immunocompetent and immunocompromised patients (such as posttransplantation) ([Haematologica 2015;100:e275](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25795716))

Sites

* Lymph nodes are most commonly affected
* Occasionally involves extranodal sites ([Haematologica 2019;104:1822](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30733272))

Diagnosis

* Tissue biopsy of lymph node or extranodal site

Prognostic factors

* Overall good prognosis, comparable to Burkitt lymphoma; 5 year overall survival of 80% ([Mod Pathol 2018;31:732](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29327714), [Haematologica 2019;104:1822](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30733272))

Case reports

* 17 year old girl with a neck mass ([Case Rep Hematol 2020;2020:8896322](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32963851))
* 37 year old immunocompetent woman and 56 year old man with HIV, both with cervical lymph node involvement ([Open Med (Wars) 2021;16:428](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33763601))
* 65 year old man with abdominal mass ([Clin Case Rep 2019;7:1823](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31534767))

Treatment

* Intense chemoimmunotherapy with central nervous system prophylaxis ([Haematologica 2019;104:1822](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30733272))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Diffuse involvement by sheets of lymphoma cells
* Occasional cases show a vaguely nodular pattern ([Haematologica 2019;104:1822](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30733272))
* Some cases are similar to Burkitt lymphoma, with tingible body macrophages, exhibiting starry sky pattern
* Other cases have fewer macrophages but frequently show apoptotic bodies
* Lymphoma cells are medium-sized to large and usually have more cytological pleomorphism than typical Burkitt lymphoma with more irregular nuclei
* Some cases morphologically resemble diffuse large B cell lymphoma ([Haematologica 2019;104:1822](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30733272), [Am J Clin Pathol 2017;149:17](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29272887))

Positive stains

* Pan B cell markers: [CD19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd19.html), [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html), [CD79a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd79a.html), [PAX5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax5.html)
* [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html), [BCL6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl6.html), [LMO2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainslmo2.html)
* [MYC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscmyc.html) (positive in majority of cases) ([Am J Clin Pathol 2017;149:17](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29272887), [Haematologica 2019;104:1822](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30733272))
* [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) shows high proliferative index (> 95%) ([Mod Pathol 2018;31:732](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29327714))

Negative stains

* [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html) (vast majority of cases), [MUM1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmum1.html), [TdT](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstdt.html), [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html) ([Am J Clin Pathol 2017;149:17](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29272887), [Case Rep Hematol 2020;2020:8896322](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32963851))
* [EBV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) ([Haematologica 2015;100:e275](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25795716))

Flow cytometry description

* Mature B cell lymphoma with germinal center immunophenotype, positive for CD19, CD20, CD10 and CD38 with surface light chain restriction ([Mod Pathol 2018;31:732](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29327714))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Defined by 11q aberrations, including proximal gains and telomeric losses
* Negative for *MYC* rearrangement ([Haematologica 2019;104:1822](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30733272))
* More complex karyotypes than seen in Burkitt lymphoma
* Gains of 12q12-q21.1 and losses of 6q12.1-q21 ([Haematologica 2019;104:1822](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30733272))
* Negative for 1q gain, seen in Burkitt lymphoma
* Detected mutations include *BTG2, DDX3X, ETS1, EP300* and *GNA13* ([Haematologica 2019;104:1822](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30733272))
* Negative for *ID3, TCF3* or *CCND3* mutations that are typical for Burkitt lymphoma ([Haematologica 2019;104:1822](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30733272))

Sample pathology report

* Lymph node, cervical, core needle biopsy:
  + Burkitt-like lymphoma with 11q aberration

Differential diagnosis

* [Burkitt lymphoma (BL)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaburkitt.html):
  + Morphology and immunophenotype do not reliably distinguish between the 2 entities
  + BL shows *MYC* rearrangement and usually has a less complex karyotype
  + Subset of Burkitt lymphomas are [EBV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html)+
  + Rare cases with concomitant *MYC* rearrangement and 11q aberration exist ([Am J Clin Pathol 2017;149:17](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29272887))
* [High grade B cell lymphoma, NOS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomahighgradebcell.html):
  + Morphology and immunophenotype do not reliably distinguish between the 2 entities
  + 11q aberrations usually absent but can have *MYC* rearrangement without concomitant *BCL2* or *BCL6*
  + Diagnosis reserved for cases that cannot be confidently classified as Burkitt lymphoma or diffuse large B cell lymphoma ([Insights Imaging 2019;10:56](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31115699), [Pathology 2018;50:74](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29167021))
* [High grade B cell lymphoma with *MYC* and *BCL2* or *BCL6* rearrangements](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomabunclassburkitt.html):
  + Rearranged *MYC* and *BCL2* or *BCL6* genes ([Blood 2018;131:2060](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29475959))
* [Diffuse large B cell lymphoma (DLBCL)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomadiffuse.html):
  + Lymphoma cells are usually large and more pleomorphic
  + Immunophenotypes can overlap
  + Rare cases with 11q aberrations have DLBCL morphology

Board review style question #1

[![Burkitt-like lymphoma with 11q aberration](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/lymphomaburkittlike11qShestakova02.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/lymphomaburkittlike11qShestakova02.jpg)

A 20 year old man presents with abdominal pain and recent 20 pound weight loss. He was found to have a 10 cm peripancreatic mass, which was biopsied and shown in the figure above. Cytogenetic studies are negative for *MYC* gene rearrangement. Chromosomal genetic microarray shows 11q aberration with proximal gain and telomeric loss. Which of the following is the typical immunophenotype seen in this entity?

1. CD20+, CD10+, BCL6+, MUM1-, BCL2-, Ki67 > 95%
2. CD20+, CD10+, BCL6+, MUM1+, BCL2+, Ki67 > 95%
3. CD20+, CD10-, BCL6-, MUM1+, BCL2+, Ki67 > 95%
4. CD20+, CD10-, BCL6-, MUM1+, BCL2-, Ki67 > 95%

Board review style answer #1

**A**. Burkitt-like lymphoma with 11q aberration usually shows the following immunophenotype: CD20+, CD10+, BCL6+, MUM1-, BCL2-, Ki67 > 95%
