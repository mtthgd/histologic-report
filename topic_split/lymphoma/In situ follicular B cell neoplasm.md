Definition / general

* In situ follicular neoplasia (ISFN) is a monoclonal proliferation of BCL2 positive B cells confined to follicle centers
* Defined by B cells with the chromosomal translocation t(14;18)(q32;q21) ([Blood 2002;99:3376](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11964306))

Essential features

* Histopathologic and immunohistochemical evaluation are essential
* Clinical and pathological evidence of usual follicular lymphoma is lacking in other sites
* BCL2 intensity in germinal centers is strong, stronger than adjacent mantle cuff
* CD10 is strongly positive
* Variable number of germinal centers may be involved
* Lymph node architecture is preserved

Terminology

* Formerly follicular lymphoma in situ
* In situ follicular lymphoma terminology is discouraged because risk for clinically significant lymphoma is small

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9695/1](https://belgian-society-pathology.eu/storage/files/b-cell-lymphomas.pdf) - in situ follicular neoplasia
* ICD-10: [D36.0](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D36-/D36.0) - benign neoplasm of lymph nodes

Epidemiology

* Detected in ~2 - 3% of randomly selected reactive lymph node biopsies by
  immunohistochemical screening for BCL2 ([Histopathology 2011;59:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21771030), [Haematologica 2012;97:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22058203), [Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:943](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26945339))
* Exclusively found in adults

Sites

* Lymph node otherwise showing follicular hyperplasia
* May be seen in secondary lymphoid follicles of tonsil, spleen and lymphoid tissue in extranodal sites

Pathophysiology

* Precursor lesion of conventional follicular lymphoma but progresses to follicular lymphoma in < 5% of cases
* B cells with *BCL2* rearrangement within germinal centers ([Blood 2011;118:2976](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21768298))
* Other than *BCL2* rearrangement, low level of genetic aberrations is found
* Very early step in lymphomagenesis ([Blood 2018;132:2687](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30401710))

Etiology

* High environmental exposure to pesticides and herbicides associated with increase in cells carrying t(14;18)(q32;q21) (*IGH::BCL2*) translocation in the peripheral blood

Clinical features

* Always an incidental finding
* Risk for clinically significant follicular lymphoma < 5% ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2018;142:1330](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30221980))
* Low incidence of other lymphoma subtypes in affected patients, mainly mantle cell lymphoma, chronic lymphocytic leukemia, marginal zone lymphoma and diffuse large B cell lymphoma ([Cancers (Basel) 2023;15:785](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36765742))

Diagnosis

* Diagnostic criteria
  + BCL2 and CD10 strongly positive in lesional cells
  + Neoplastic cells confined to follicle centers of secondary follicles
  + Lymph node architecture is preserved ([Pathologica 2020;112:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32031179))
  + Lymph nodes may show other pathology or involvement by unrelated lymphoma, usually of B cell lineage

Laboratory

* Low levels of B cells with the *BCL2* rearrangement in the peripheral blood may be found by polymerase chain reaction (PCR)
* In blood, referred to as follicular lymphoma-like B cells ([Haematologica 2013;98:1571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23831923))

Case reports

* 60 year old woman with concurrent overt follicular lymphoma and 69 year old man with with concurrent peripheral T cell lymphoma ([Am J Hematol 2011;86:E66](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21956856))
* 63 year old woman with 2 nonhematopoietic neoplasms ([Leuk Lymphoma 2016;57:2917](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27054481))
* 78 year old man with concomitant follicular lymphoma, grade 1 - 2 (CD10+, BCL2-) ([Blood 2011;118:3442](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21940830))
* 84 year old woman with composite follicular lymphoma and mantle cell lymphoma each with an in situ component ([Am J Clin Pathol 2011;136:481](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21846924))

Treatment

* Clinical evaluation for follicular lymphoma at other sites recommended at diagnosis
* No treatment indicated if only in situ follicular neoplasia is found

Gross description

* No gross finding is evident
* Involved lymph node may be enlarged secondary to lymphoid hyperplasia or other pathology

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Lesion generally not evident on routine H&E stained sections ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2018;142:1330](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30221980))
* Lymph node architecture is intact
* Involved follicles are generally normal in size
* Neoplastic cells (BCL2 strong+, CD10 strong+) are exclusively centrocytes, confined to the follicle centers ([Cancers (Basel) 2023;15:785](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36765742))
* Individual germinal centers show varying degrees of involvement
* By definition, no interfollicular infiltration is seen ([Blood 2011;118:2976](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21768298))
* Involved follicles show low proliferation fraction with Ki67

Positive stains

* Strong expression of [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html) and [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html)
* [BCL6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl6.html) is variably positive
* B cell phenotype intact: [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html), [CD19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd19.html), [CD79a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd79a.html), [PAX5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax5.html) ([Virchows Arch 2023;482;149](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36394631))
* Low proliferation index ([Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) ~10%) in contrast to reactive germinal center ([Blood 2011;117:3954](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21224472))

Negative stains

* [IgD](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsIgA.html), [cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html), [MUM1 / IRF4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmum1.html)
* [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html) and other T cell antigens ([CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html), [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html), [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html), [CD7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1.html#cd7)) ([J Pathol Transl Med 2022;56:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34942689))
* Immunoglobulin not detected by immunohistochemistry ([Leuk Lymphoma 2014;55:482](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23713483))

Flow cytometry description

* Uncommon detection of minor population of light chain restricted B cells that coexpress [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) ([Haematologica 2013;98:1571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23831923))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* t(14;18)(q32;q21) (*IGH::BCL2*) translocation
* Frequent mutations in *CREBBP*
* *EZH2*, *KMT2D*, *TNFRSF14*, *EP300* mutations have been identified in a small subset of cases ([Leukemia 2014;28:1103](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24153014), [Blood 2018;132:2687](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30401710))

Sample pathology report

* Inguinal lymph node, excision:
  + Follicular neoplasia in situ, no evidence of follicular lymphoma (see comment)
  + Comment: The lymph node architecture is intact but shows an increased number of secondary follicles of normal size with sharply demarcated lymphoid cuffs resembling reactive germinal centers. A subset of follicle centers, which are involved by in situ follicular neoplasia (ISFN), stain strongly positive for BCL2 and CD10 with some adjacent reactive germinal centers being completely negative. The ISFN cells have a low Ki67 proliferation fraction and do not infiltrate the interfollicular zones.

Differential diagnosis

* [Reactive follicular hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodesreactivegeneral.html):
  + Reactive follicles are negative for [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html), show polarization and have a high proliferation index
* Partial involvement by [follicular lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomafollicularnodalusual.html):
  + [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html) and [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) immunostaining more variable in intensity
  + Follicles expanded on H&E with some distortion of lymph node architecture

Board review style question #1

[![BRQ image](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/lymphomafollicularneoplsiansituJaffe_08.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/lymphomafollicularneoplsiansituJaffe_08.jpg)

Which of the following is characteristic of follicular neoplasia in situ??

1. Bone marrow involvement is common at diagnosis
2. Common in childhood
3. Lymphocytosis is a common finding
4. Tends to involve extranodal sites
5. Typically an incidental finding

Board review style answer #1

**E**. Typically an incidental finding. Follicular neoplasia in situ is typically an incidental finding with no prominent clinical features. Answer A is incorrect because although it is recommended to evaluate for follicular neoplasia at other sites at diagnosis, bone marrow involvement is not common. Answer B is incorrect because it is exclusively found in adults. Answer C is incorrect because lymphocytosis is not a hallmark finding. Answer D is incorrect because follicular neoplasia may be seen in lymphoid tissue at secondary sites; however, it is not characteristic.

Board review style question #2

[![BRQ image](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/lymphomafollicularneoplsiansituJaffe_08.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/lymphomafollicularneoplsiansituJaffe_08.jpg)

Which of the following is true about follicular neoplasia in situ?

1. Composed exclusively of centrocytes
2. High mitotic rate
3. Interfollicular infiltration of neoplastic B cells is common
4. Positive for *BCL6* rearrangement
5. Positive for *CCND1* rearrangement

Board review style answer #2

**A**. Composed exclusively of centrocytes. In situ follicular neoplasia in situ has a monomorphic appearance and is composed exclusively of centrocytes. Answer B is incorrect because involved follicles show a low proliferation rate. Answer C is incorrect because by definition, no interfollicular infiltration is seen. Answer D is incorrect because there is low incidence of other lymphoma subtypes in patients with follicular neoplasia in situ and *BCL6* rearrangement is common of high grade B cell lymphomas (most commonly diffuse large B cell lymphoma [DLBCL]). Answer E is incorrect because while *CCND1* rearrangement is positive in some mature B cell neoplasms, it is not positive in follicular neoplasia in situ.
