Definition / general

* Cyclin D1 positive B cells with *CCND1* rearrangements restricted to the mantle zones of reactive appearing lymphoid tissue ([Blood 2016;127:2375](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26980727))

Essential features

* Lymphoid architecture preserved with slight or no mantle zone expansion ([Blood 2016;127:2375](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26980727), [Semin Diagn Pathol 2018;35:76](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29129357))
* Cyclin D1 positive B cells with *CCND1* rearrangement restricted to mantle zones ([Blood 2016;127:2375](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26980727), [Mod Pathol 2012;25:1629](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22790016))
* Rare; usually an incidental finding ([Blood 2016;127:2375](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26980727))
* Typically a stable disease with indolent behavior and long term survival, even without therapy ([Blood 2016;127:2375](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26980727))
* Infrequent (Blood 2016;127:2375, [Haematologica 2012;97:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22058203))

Terminology

* In situ mantle cell neoplasia is accepted terminology
* Previous terms are not recommended: in situ mantle cell lymphoma, in situ involvement by mantle cell lymphoma-like cells or mantle cell lymphoma-like B cells of uncertain / undetermined significance ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2018;35:76](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29129357))

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [C83.1](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C81-C96/C83-/C83.1) - mantle cell lymphoma ([Western Australian Coding Rule: 1215/02 Mantle Cell Lymphoma In Situ [Accessed 1 May 2023]](https://ww2.health.wa.gov.au/~/media/Files/Corporate/general%20documents/Clinical%20coding/Index%20coding%20rules/WACR-121502.pdf))

Epidemiology

* Median age: 65 - 66 years ([Haematologica 2012;97:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22058203), [Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2014;7:1042](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24696721))
* Rare; usually an incidental finding ([Blood 2016;127:2375](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26980727))
  + 2 cases of isolated and incidental in situ mantle cell neoplasia were identified in lymph nodes resected from 341 patients (0.6%) with colorectal or breast adenocarcinoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:943](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26945339))
  + 0 cases of in situ mantle cell neoplasia were identified in 1,292 reactive lymph nodes from 131 patients without evidence of lymphoma ([Mod Pathol 2012;25:1629](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22790016))

Sites

* Frequently nodal but may be seen in extranodal locations, including appendix, gallbladder, lacrimal gland, lung, nasopharynx, small intestine, oropharynx, spleen and urinary bladder ([Haematologica 2012;97:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22058203), [Head Neck 2013;35:E333](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23280758), [J Hematop 2012;5:169](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24307917))
* Involvement of more than 1 tissue site does not exclude the diagnosis ([Blood 2016;127:2375](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26980727))
* Peripheral blood involvement is permitted
  + 5 of 17 patients showed peripheral blood or bone marrow involvement ([Haematologica 2012;97:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22058203))

Pathophysiology

* t(11;14) *IGH::CCND1* translocation results in overexpression of cyclin D1 and dysregulation of the cell cycle ([J Clin Invest 2012;122:3416](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23023712))
* Low levels of B cells with t(11;14) translocation have been identified in peripheral blood of healthy individuals ([Mod Pathol 2012;25:1629](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22790016))
* Cyclin D1 positive B cells with *CCND1* rearrangements may colonize the mantle zones of lymphoid follicles ([Blood 2016;127:2375](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26980727))
* Risk of progression is very low ([Blood 2016;127:2375](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26980727))

Etiology

* Unclear

Clinical features

* Indolent behavior ([Blood 2011;117:3954](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21224472), [Haematologica 2012;97:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22058203))
* Infrequent (Blood 2016;127:2375, [Semin Diagn Pathol 2018;35:76](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29129357))
* May be identified in association with another lymphoma, including chronic lymphocytic leukemia / small lymphocytic lymphoma, marginal zone lymphoma and follicular lymphoma ([Haematologica 2012;97:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22058203))

Diagnosis

* Usually an incidental finding discovered when cyclin D1 immunohistochemistry is performed on a lymph node

Radiology description

* No specific radiologic findings

Case reports

* Man in his 40s with involved cervical lymph node and follicular lymphoma and man in his 70s with involved cervical lymph node ([Histopathology 2008;52:256](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18184277))
* 52 year old man and 65 year old woman with in situ mantle cell lymphoma in cervical lymph nodes ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2014;7:1042](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24696721))
* 58 year old man with involved inguinal node and marginal zone lymphoma ([Am J Clin Pathol 2015;143:274](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25596254))
* 65 year old man with involved mesenteric node and follicular lymphoma ([Case Rep Hematol 2014;2014:145129](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25478252))
* 70 year old woman with involved nasopharynx ([Head Neck 2013;35:E333](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23280758))
* 75 year old woman with composite follicular lymphoma and in situ mantle cell neoplasm ([Pathol Res Pract 2020;216:153067](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32825940))

Treatment

* Treatment guided by the presence or absence of coexistent overt lymphoma ([Blood 2011;117:3954](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21224472))
* In the absence of coexistent overt lymphoma, watchful waiting is appropriate ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2018;35:76](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29129357))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Lymphoid architecture preserved with no or slight mantle zone expansion ([Blood 2016;127:2375](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26980727), [Semin Diagn Pathol 2018;35:76](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29129357))
* Cyclin D1 positive B cells identified within the mantle zone ([Blood 2016;127:2375](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26980727), [Virchows Arch 2016;468:245](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26298543), [Haematologica 2012;97:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22058203))
  + Usually restricted to the inner mantle zone
  + Rarely scattered throughout the mantle zone or present in the outer mantle zone
  + Very rarely intrafollicular or focally within germinal centers
* May involve multiple follicles in a lymph node ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:943](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26945339))
* May be identified in association with another lymphoma ([Blood 2016;127:2375](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26980727))

Positive stains

* B cell markers (e.g., [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html), [PAX5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax5.html), [CD79a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd79a.html), [BOB1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbob1.html), [OCT2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsOCT2.html)), [cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html), [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html), [IgD](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsIgA.html) ([J Hematop 2012;5:169](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24307917))
* Usually [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html) and [CD43](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd43.html)
  + More frequently [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html) negative and [CD43](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd43.html) negative than overt mantle cell lymphoma ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2018;35:76](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29129357), [J Hematop 2012;5:169](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24307917))
* Variable [SOX11](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox11.html)
  + 7/16 cases (44%) ([Haematologica 2012;97:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22058203))
  + 100% (number of cases not clearly specified) ([J Hematop 2012;5:169](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24307917))

Negative stains

* [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html), [CD23](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd23.html)

Flow cytometry description

* Clonal in situ mantle cell neoplasm cells may be detected by flow cytometry but this may require careful gating strategies due to the presence of nonclonal B cells present in involved lymph nodes
* Phenotype is indistinguishable from typical [mantle cell lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaMCL.html)
  + Positive for B cell markers ([CD19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd19.html), [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html), [CD22](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20to29.html#cd22)), [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html), [FMC7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfmc7.html) and [kappa or lambda surface immunoglobulin light chain](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainskappa.html)
  + Negative for [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html), [CD11c](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10to19.html#cd11c), [CD23](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd23.html), [CD103](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsCD103.html) and [CD200](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd200.html)
* Reference: [Am J Clin Pathol 2015;143:274](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25596254)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Characteristic translocation t(11;14) *IGH::CCND1* results in overexpression of cyclin D1 ([Virchows Arch 2016;468:245](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26298543))

Sample pathology report

* Lymph node, right axilla, excision:
  + In situ mantle cell neoplasm (see comment)
  + Comment: The H&E stained sections show morphologic features consistent with a benign lymph node with follicular hyperplasia. A cyclin D1 immunohistochemical stain demonstrates cyclin D1 positive cells confined to the inner mantle zones of lymphoid follicles without mantle zone expansion. These findings are consistent with a diagnosis of in situ mantle cell neoplasm. In situ mantle cell neoplasm typically follows an indolent course; however, rare cases may progress to overt mantle cell lymphoma.

Differential diagnosis

* [Mantle cell lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaMCL.html) with mantle zone growth pattern ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2018;35:76](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29129357)):
  + Expansion of the mantle zone, complete replacement of the mantle zone or significant extension into the interfollicular region or germinal centers by [cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html) positive B cells warrants a diagnosis of mantle cell lymphoma rather than in situ mantle cell neoplasm ([Blood 2016;127:2375](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26980727))
* [Reactive follicular hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodesreactivegeneral.html) ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2018;35:76](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29129357)):
  + Reactive follicular hyperplasia lacks [cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html) positive cells within the mantle zones ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2018;35:76](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29129357))

Board review style question #1

In situ mantle cell neoplasm is characterized by which of the following translocations?

1. t(8;14) *MYC::IGH*
2. t(9;22) *BCR::ABL1*
3. t(11;14) *IGH::CCND1*
4. t(14;18) *BCL2::IGH*

Board review style answer #1

**C**. t(11;14) *IGH::CCND1*. The characteristic translocation of in situ mantle cell neoplasia is t(11;14) *IGH::CCND1*, resulting in overexpression of cyclin D1. Answer B is incorrect because t(9;22) [*BCR::ABL1*](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/molecularBCRABL.html) is the Philadelphia chromosome associated with AML, CML and B ALL. Answer D is incorrect because t(14;18) *BCL2::IGH* is associated with [follicular lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomafollicularnodalusual.html). Answer A is incorrect because t(8;14) *MYC::IGH* is associated with [Burkitt lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaburkitt.html).

Board review style question #2

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/lymphomaisMCNbradley_01.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/lymphomaisMCNbradley_01.jpg)

In a lymph node biopsy, which of the following features supports a diagnosis of in situ mantle cell neoplasm?

1. Cyclin D1 positive B cells extending into the interfollicular region
2. Mantle zones prominently expanded by cyclin D1 positive B cells
3. Monoclonal proliferation of BCL2 positive B cells confined to germinal centers
4. Preserved lymphoid architecture

Board review style answer #2

**D**. Preserved lymphoid architecture. In situ mantle cell neoplasm is characterized by cyclin D1 positive B cells restricted to the mantle zone of reactive appearing lymphoid tissue. Lymphoid architecture is preserved. Answers A and B are incorrect because prominent expansion of the mantle zone, complete replacement of the mantle zone or significant extension into the interfollicular region or germinal center by cyclin D1 positive B cells warrants a diagnosis of mantle cell lymphoma rather than in situ mantle cell neoplasm. Answer C is incorrect because a monoclonal proliferation of BCL2 positive B cells confined to germinal centers in a hyperplastic appearing lymphoid follicle is consistent with in situ follicular B cell neoplasm.
