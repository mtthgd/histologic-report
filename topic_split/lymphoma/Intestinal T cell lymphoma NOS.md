Definition / general

* Primary intestinal T cell lymphomas (ITCL) are rare malignancies that account for < 10% of intestinal lymphomas
* Diagnosis of the not otherwise specified category (ITCL, NOS) is established when there is a failure of classification with the current schemes

Essential features

* Intestinal T cell lymphoma, not otherwise specified is a rare and aggressive variant ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2018;37:107](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30317149))
* Clinical symptoms are nonspecific and the clinical presentation can overlap with [inflammatory bowel disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colonindeterminate.html) ([Diagn Pathol 2022;17:53](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35752815))
* Imaging studies may reveal a thickened intestinal wall ([Diagn Pathol 2022;17:53](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35752815))
* Histologically characterized by extensive infiltration by atypical lymphocytic infiltrates ([Diagn Pathol 2022;17:53](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35752815), [Clin Case Rep 2022;10:e05546](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35280095))
* Exclusion of other defined categories of intestinal T cell lymphoma is required for diagnosis

Terminology

* ITCL, NOS
* ITCL, unclassified

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [C86.2](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C81-C96/C86-/C86.2) - enteropathy type (intestinal) T cell lymphoma
* ICD-11: [2A90.7](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http:%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f958629729) - enteropathy associated T cell lymphoma

Epidemiology

* Fewer than 10 cases have been reported ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2018;37:107](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30317149), [Diagn Pathol 2022;17:53](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35752815), [Clin Case Rep 2022;10:e05546](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35280095))

Sites

* The few cases reported demonstrate involvement in the small intestine

Etiology

* Cell of origin is similar to the systemic counterpart ([peripheral T cell lymphoma, NOS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBperipheral.html)) and includes 2 major groups (GATA3 versus TBX21 types) ([Blood 2019;133:1664](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30782609), [Blood 2014;123:3007](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24497534))

Differential diagnosis of intestinal T lymphomas

Clinical features

* Lacks an association with [celiac disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelceliacsprue.html)

Diagnosis

* Computed tomography (CT) scans can be performed to show intestinal wall thickening; however, a biopsy is required for diagnosis

Radiology description

* Intestinal wall thickening in CT scans

Prognostic factors

* Few cases are reported; survival ranges from weeks to < 2 years after diagnosis ([Clin Case Rep 2022;10:e05546](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35280095), [Ann Diagn Pathol 2018;37:107](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30317149))

Case reports

* 55 year old man with thickened small bowel loop ([Diagn Pathol 2022;17:53](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35752815))
* 65 and 67 year old men (in a case series of 27 patients) with involvement in the small bowel ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2018;37:107](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30317149))
* 73 year old woman with retroperitoneal inflammation and duodenal perforation ([Clin Case Rep 2022;10:e05546](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35280095))

Treatment

* Similar to the systemic [peripheral T cell lymphoma, NOS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBperipheral.html) counterpart and includes induction with CHOP chemotherapy (cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin hydrochloride [hydroxydaunorubicin], vincristine sulfate [Oncovin] and prednisone)

Gross description

* Involved areas are characterized by thickened rugal folds

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Dense and extensive infiltrates that are composed of atypical lymphocytes that are medium to large in size, with irregular nuclear contours
* Necrosis may be present
* Epitheliotropism can be present
* References: [Diagn Pathol 2022;17:53](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35752815), [Clin Case Rep 2022;10:e05546](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35280095)

Positive stains

* [CD2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1.html#cd2), [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html), [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html), [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html), [CD7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1.html#cd7), [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html), **TIA1**, [granzyme B](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgranzymeb.html), [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) (> 50%)
* [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html) (may be positive in a subset of tumor cells)
* References: [Diagn Pathol 2022;17:53](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35752815), [Clin Case Rep 2022;10:e05546](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35280095)

Negative stains

* [EBER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html)
* [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html)
* Loss of pan T cell markers ([CD2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1.html#cd2), [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html), [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html), [CD7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1.html#cd7)) is usually identified
* Loss of both [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html) and [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html) can be observed
* References: [Diagn Pathol 2022;17:53](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35752815), [Clin Case Rep 2022;10:e05546](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35280095)

Sample pathology report

* Small intestine, biopsies:
  + Atypical lymphocytic infiltrates, consistent with involvement by T cell lymphoma (see comment)
  + Comment: The overall morphological and immunohistochemical findings are most consistent with T cell lymphoma unclassified (not otherwise specified, NOS). Correlation with staging and imaging studies is required. In the right clinical context, the findings can be consistent with primary intestinal T cell lymphoma, not otherwise specified (ITCL, NOS).

Differential diagnosis

* [NK / T cell enteropathy](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBenteropathy.html) ([Blood 2011;117:1447](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20966166)):
  + Characterized by the absence of epitheliotropism
  + Tumor cells are positive for [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html)
  + [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) proliferation index is < 25%
  + Negative surface expression of the T cell receptor
  + T cell receptor rearrangement studies show polyclonal T cells
  + Indolent clinical course
* [Indolent T cell lymphoma of the GI tract](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBindolentTcell.html) ([Hematol Oncol 2017;35:3](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27353398), [Blood 2013;122:3599](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24009234)):
  + Infiltrates are composed of small lymphocytes with no atypical features
  + [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) proliferation index is < 10%
  + No areas of necrosis or gland destruction are identified
  + Indolent clinical course
* [Inflammatory bowel disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colonindeterminate.html):
  + Lymphocytic infiltrates are composed of a mixture of [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html) positive B cells and [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html) positive T cells
  + No cytological atypia is present within the lymphocytic infiltrates
  + Normal expression of pan T cell markers within the T cell subsets
  + T cell receptor rearrangement studies demonstrate polyclonal T cells
* [Enteropathy associated T cell lymphoma (EATL)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBenteropathy.html) ([Leukemia 2013;27:1688](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23399895), [Pathology 2020;52:128](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31727264)):
  + Morphology and immunophenotype do not reliably distinguish between ITCL, NOS and EATL
  + EATL is associated with [celiac disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelceliacsprue.html)
* [Monomorphic epitheliotropic intestinal T cell lymphoma (MEITL)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomameitl.html) ([Ann Hematol 2019;98:2541](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31493002)):
  + Atypical lymphocytes are monomorphic, medium in size with mild cytological atypia

Which of the following is the correct statement regarding intestinal T cell lymphoma, not otherwise specified?

1. All patients characteristically feature persistent diarrhea
2. Clinical course is indolent
3. Diagnosis requires the exclusion of celiac disease
4. Loss of the expression of pan T cell markers is rarely observed
5. Lymphocytic infiltrates are benign appearing and indistinguishable from inflammatory bowel disease

Board review style answer #1

**C.** Diagnosis requires the exclusion of celiac disease
