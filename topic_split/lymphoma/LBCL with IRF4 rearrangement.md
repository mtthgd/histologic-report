Definition / general

* Uncommon subtype of large B cell lymphoma characterized by strong expression of IRF4 / MUM1
* Commonly associated with *IRF4* gene rearrangement

Essential features

* Children and young adults
* Lymph nodes of head and neck or Waldeyer ring are affected
* Diffuse, diffuse and follicular or follicular pattern
* Medium to large cells
* Germinal center B cell phenotype with diffuse IRF4 / MUM1 expression
* Most cases show *IRF4* rearrangement

Terminology

* Lymphoma of Waldeyer ring

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9698/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=95) - follicular lymphoma, grade 3
* ICD-10: [C83.3](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C81-C96/C83-/C83.3) - diffuse large B cell lymphoma

Epidemiology

* Rare subtype accounting for 0.05% of diffuse large B cell lymphomas
* Primarily affects children and young adults
  + Age range: 4 - 79 years; median age: 12 years ([Blood 2011;118:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21487109))
* Equal gender incidence ([Blood 2011;118:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21487109))

Sites

* Lymph nodes of the head and neck ([Blood 2011;118:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21487109), [Ann Oncol 2012;23:3143](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22700993))
* Waldeyer ring ([Blood 2011;118:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21487109), [Ann Oncol 2012;23:3143](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22700993), [Am J Surg Pathol 2013;37:333](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23108024))
* Gastrointestinal tract ([Blood 2011;118:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21487109), [Ann Oncol 2012;23:3143](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22700993))

Pathophysiology

* Arises from germinal center B cells with rearrangement of the *IRF4* gene on chromosome 6p25 resulting in IRF4 / MUM1 expression ([Blood 2011;118:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21487109), [Virchows Arch 2016;468:141](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26416032))
* Cytogenetically cryptic rearrangement of *IRF4* and *IgH* loci ([Blood 2011;118:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21487109))
* Immunoglobulin light chain loci are rarely involved in the rearrangement
* *BCL6* rearrangements and *TP53* loss have also been reported ([Blood 2011;118:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21487109), [Virchows Arch 2016;468:141](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26416032))

Clinical features

* Isolated lymph node enlargement
* Tonsillar enlargement

Diagnosis

* Biopsy of the involved lymph node / tissue with immunophenotyping and confirmation using FISH analysis for *IRF4* rearrangement
* Strong, diffuse positivity for IRF4 / MUM1 in neoplastic cells
* Young age of the patient, head and lymph node / Waldeyer ring location

Prognostic factors

* Favorable response to treatment with immunochemotherapy

Case reports

* 7 year old Chinese boy with IRF4 / MUM1 positive lymphoma with strong and extensive CD5 and CD10 positivity ([Pediatr Blood Cancer 2017;64:311](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27616053))
* 12 year old boy with large B cell lymphoma with *IRF4* rearrangement in the unusual location of inguinal region ([Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2018;61:271](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29676376))

Treatment

* Responds well to chemoimmunotherapy with or without radiation

Gross description

* Involved lymph node / tonsil is enlarged
* Firm, fleshy homogenous mass

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Lymph node architecture is effaced with a diffuse, diffuse and follicular or follicular infiltrate ([Blood 2011;118:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21487109))
* Neoplastic follicles are large and arranged back to back with attenuated mantle zones ([Blood 2011;118:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21487109))
* Neoplastic cells are medium to large ([Blood 2011;118:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21487109))
* Open chromatin with small nucleoli ([Blood 2011;118:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21487109))
* Absent starry sky pattern and infrequent mitotic figures ([Blood 2011;118:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21487109))

Positive stains

* Pan B cell markers: [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html), [CD79a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd79a.html) and [PAX5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax5.html) ([Blood 2011;118:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21487109))
* [IRF4 / MUM1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmum1.html) strongly and diffusely positive ([Blood 2011;118:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21487109))
* [BCL6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl6.html) ([Blood 2011;118:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21487109))
* [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html) and [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) variably positive ([Blood 2011;118:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21487109))
* [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) proliferation rate is high
  ([Blood 2011;118:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21487109))

Negative stains

* [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html) and other T cell markers ([CD2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1.html#cd2), [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html), [CD7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1.html#cd7), [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html), [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html))
* **PRDM1 (BLIMP1)**

Flow cytometry description

* Low side light scatter and moderate forward light scatter
* Pan B cell markers and [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) positive
* Surface immunoglobulin light chain positive with monoclonal pattern

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* *IRF4* gene rearrangement ([Blood 2011;118:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21487109), [Virchows Arch 2016;468:141](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26416032))
* Most common partner gene is *IgH* ([Blood 2011;118:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21487109), [Virchows Arch 2016;468:141](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26416032))
* Rarely partner is immunoglobulin light chain genes ([Blood 2011;118:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21487109), [Virchows Arch 2016;468:141](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26416032))
* In a subset of cases, *IRF4* translocation is not detected by the available techniques (cryptic)
  + In such cases, *IgH* rearrangement may be detected ([Blood 2011;118:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21487109), [Virchows Arch 2016;468:141](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26416032))
* Breakpoints in the *BCL6* locus seen in some cases ([Blood 2011;118:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21487109), [Virchows Arch 2016;468:141](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26416032))

Sample pathology report

* Lymph node, left cervical, excision:
  + Large B cell lymphoma with *IRF4* rearrangement; see synoptic report

Differential diagnosis

* [Pediatric type follicular lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomafollicularchildhood.html): serpiginous configuration of neoplastic follicles and starry sky pattern; responds well to local management and shows good prognosis
* [Diffuse large B cell lymphoma, NOS, germinal center phenotype, MUM1+](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomadiffuse.html): older age group, diffuse growth pattern, worse prognosis
* [Follicular lymphoma, grade 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomafollicularnodalusual.html): older age group, germinal center phenotype, t(14;18), poor prognosis
