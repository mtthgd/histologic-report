Definition / general

* 2 types: blastoid and pleomorphic
* Blastoid: cells resemble lymphoblasts with high mitotic rate (> 20 - 30 mitoses /10 high power fields) ([Blood 1997;89:1421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9028966))
* Pleomorphic: large, pleomorphic cells with variable sizes, prominent nucleoli
* Definition is based on the morphology

Essential features

* Associated with t(11;14)(q13;q32) or *IGH/CCND1* and cyclin D1 overexpression
* High proliferation index and unfavorable outcome

Terminology

* Blastoid variant
  + Mantle cell lymphoma (MCL), lymphoblastoid variant
  + Mantle cell lymphoma (MCL), blastic
* Pleomorphic variant
  + Mantle cell lymphoma (MCL), large cell variant
  + Mantle cell lymphoma (MCL), anaplastic
  + Mantle cell lymphoma (MCL), nucleolated variant

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9673/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=94) – mantle cell lymphoma

Epidemiology

* 10 - 20% of all mantle cell lymphomas ([J Clin Oncol 2016;34:1386](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26926679), [Leuk Lymphoma 2015;56:2759](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25747972))
* Frequency may be higher in patients with prolonged follow up

Sites

* Lymph node, spleen, bone marrow
* Extranodal sites:
  + Gastrointestinal tract is the most common site
  + CNS: More frequently involved by blastoid / pleomorphic variant than classic variant
* Peripheral blood:
  + Leukemic involvement at diagnosis

Pathophysiology and etiology

* *CCND1* at 11q13 with *IGH* at 14q32
  + New *CCND1* will reposition near the nucleoli (nucleolin and RNA polymerase II) ([Blood 2014;123:2044](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24452204))
  + Higher level of *cyclin D1* RNA, when 3'-untranslated region is deleted (common in blastoid mantle cell lymphoma) ([Blood 2007;109:4599](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17299095))
* Higher level of chromosomal aberration in blastoid / pleomorphic mantle cell lymphoma than in classic mantle cell lymphoma ([Hum Pathol 2003;34:1022](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14608536))
* Frequent tetraploidy in blastoid / pleomorphic mantle cell lymphoma than in classic mantle cell lymphoma ([Blood 1997;89:1421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9028966))
* 8q24 abnormalities (*MYC* gene) in blastoid mantle cell lymphoma ([Mod Pathol 2002;15:1266](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12481006))
* High level of p53 expression in a subset of blastoid mantle cell lymphomas ([Blood 2017;130:1903](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28819011))
* Mutated *NOTCH1* and *NOTCH2*: affects signaling pathway ([Blood 2012;119:1963](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22210878), [Proc Natl Acad Sci U S A 2013;110:18250](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24145436))
* De novo or from classic mantle cell lymphoma progression

Clinical features

* Median age: sixth and seventh decades
* Clinical picture resembles classic mantle cell lymphoma
* Male predominance (M:F = 2 - 3:1)
* Ann Arbor clinical stage III/IV in most cases ([Leukemia 2001;15:1785](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11681422))
* Short duration of clinical response after chemotherapy ([Leukemia 2001;15:1785](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11681422))
* Poorer overall survival compared with classic mantle cell lymphoma ([Leuk Lymphoma 2016;57:1327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26377137))
* More common B symptoms
* Blastoid variant: often presents de novo
* Pleomorphic variant: often history of classic mantle cell lymphoma
* Generalized lymphadenopathy
* Extranodal involvement is common
* Leukemic forms of mantle cell lymphoma ([Am J Clin Pathol 2002;117:246](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11865846))

Diagnosis

* Based on clinical presentation (refer to [Clinical features](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/#lymphomaMCLaggressiveclinical))
* B symptoms
* Tissue biopsy (lymph node / extranodal sites)
* Cytology
* Peripheral blood exam / bone marrow biopsy

Laboratory

* Anemia and thrombocytopenia
* High LDH and β2 microglobulin
* A monoclonal serum component (low level)
* Atypical lymphocytosis
* Reference: [Cancer 1998;82:567](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9452276)

Prognostic factors

* Univariate risk factors
  + Blastoid morphology
  + Leukemic presentation
  + High Mantle cell lymphoma International Prognostic Index (MIPI) score ([J Clin Oncol 2016;34:1386](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26926679))
  + High cell proliferation (Ki67), 30% cutoff ([J Clin Oncol 2016;34:1386](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26926679))
  + Note: Ki67 index overrides the prognostic significance of the cytology subtype ([J Clin Oncol 2016;34:1386](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26926679))
* Other prognostic factors
  + CNS involvement
  + *MYC* translocation or overexpression
  + Complex karyotype
  + Ibrutinib refractory mantle cell lymphoma ([Br J Haematol 2018;183:578](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30175400))
  + *ATM*, *TP53* and *NSD2* mutations
  + TP53 protein expression or mutational analysis ([Blood 2017;130:1903](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28819011), [Blood 2015;126:604](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26022239))
  + Note: More dominant prognostic factor when TP53 detected by IHC and genomic mutations ([Int J Oncol 2010;36:699](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20126990), [Blood 2018;131:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29196411))

Case reports

* 45 year old man with blastoid mantle cell lymphoma presenting in leukemic phase ([J Clin Diagn Res 2017;11:ED16](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28571156))
* 65 year old man with blastic transformation of mantle cell lymphoma presenting with acute onset binocular diplopia and proptosis of the left eye ([Ocul Oncol Pathol 2019;5:245](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31367585))
* 66 year old man with cyclin D1 negative blastoid mantle cell lymphoma exhibiting cleaved to bilobated cytomorphology ([Blood 2017;129:2711](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28495928))
* 67 year old woman with triple hit blastoid mantle cell lymphoma presenting like acute leukemia ([Blood 2017;129:2593](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28473415))
* 74 year old man with ascites, liver lesions and growing 5 cm soft tissue abdominal mass ([Case #499](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case499.htm))
* A case of double hit mantle cell lymphoma ([Am J Clin Pathol 2020;153:49](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31433838))

Treatment

* Aggressive chemotherapy or stem cell transplantation
  + Ibrutinib with rituximab (IR) induction
  + Followed by R-HCVAD-methotrexate / ara-C
  + R-HyperCVAD used at many institutions
    - Rituximab, hyperfractionated cyclophosphamide, vincristine, doxorubicin, dexamethasone
    - Alternating with methotrexate and cytosine arabinoside (ara-C)
  + Venetoclax may be added in some trials
* Stem cell transplant (allogenic versus autologous)
  + Autologous SCT: in cases that respond well to initial chemotherapy ([Blood 2018;132:2722](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30385481))
* CNS prophylaxis
* Reference: [Am J Hematol 2019;94:710](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30963600)

Gross description

* Lymph node
  + Generalized enlargement
  + Homogenous tan cut surface
  + With or without nodules
  + Not distinctive
* GI
  + Lymphomatoid polyposis
  + Multiple lymphoid polyps, small and large bowel
  + Ulcers, tumor masses and diffuse thickening of the mucosa
* Spleen
  + Generalized micronodular pattern
  + Perivascular infiltration

Frozen section description

* Same criteria applied in microscopic evaluation
* Cytogenetics, flow cytometry, preparation of imprints should be considered
* The nuclear shape, size and chromatin structure are helpful in smears

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Blastoid variant
  + The growth pattern is usually diffuse
  + Less frequently nodular pattern
  + Starry sky appearance common in low power
  + Intermediate sized cells
  + Monotonous population
  + Immature chromatin
  + Scant cytoplasm
  + High mitotic rate > 20 - 30/10 high power fields
  + Resembles, in part, lymphoblastic lymphoma
* Pleomorphic variant
  + Variable size / heterogenous cell population including large cells
  + Prominent nucleoli +/-
  + High mitotic rate often seen
  + At least 10 mitoses per 10 high power fields and often higher rates
  + Resembles, in part, diffuse large B cell lymphoma
  + Peripheral blood lymphocytes are intermediate sized with prominent nucleoli
  + Resemble prolymphocytes to variable degree
  + Multinucleation or cerebriform nuclei often seen

Cytology description

* Blastoid variant
  + Monomorphic proliferation of intermediate sized to large lymphoid cells
  + Round to oval nuclear contours
  + Dispersed chromatin
  + Small / inconspicuous nucleoli
  + Scant, pale blue cytoplasm
* Pleomorphic variant
  + Large, pleomorphic cells
  + More nuclear irregularity
  + More variation in size of cells
  + Vesicular, open chromatin
  + Prominent nucleoli often seen ([Br J Haematol 1996;93:475](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8639452))

Peripheral smear description

* Prolymphocytoid-like morphology (leukemic form of mantle cell lymphoma)
  + Patients present with high leukocyte count (often > 100 x 109/L)
  + Extensive bone marrow involvement
  + Prominent nucleoli in circulating cells in blood
  + Intermediate to large sized cells
  + A single, prominent pink nucleolus
  + Prominent splenomegaly
  + A subset are pleomorphic mantle cell lymphomas ([Am J Clin Pathol 2001;115:571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11293906), [Leuk Lymphoma 2001;41:683](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11378587), [Am J Clin Pathol 2002;117:246](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11865846))
* Blastoid morphology (leukemic form of blastoid mantle cell lymphoma)
  + High leukocyte count
  + Extensive bone marrow involvement
  + Blastoid morphology
  + Complex karyotypes
  + 8q24 anomalies and *MYC* rearrangements ([Mod Pathol 2002;15:1266](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12481006), [Br J Haematol 2001;115:66](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11722412))

Positive stains

* Immunophenotype similar to classic mantle cell lymphoma
* [Cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html)
* [CD19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd19.html), [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html), [CD43](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd43.html), sIg, IgM with or without IgD
* [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html) (> 95%), [SOX11](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox11.html) (> 90%)
* Exceptions:
  + [SOX11](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox11.html) negative cases
    - More indolent
    - Leukemic / non-nodal involvement ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:710](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30768440))
    - Mutated *IGHV*
  + [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html) negative cases ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:1052](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31045891))
    - [Cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html) positive
  + [Cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html) negative cases
    - [SOX11](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox11.html) positive
* [MUM1 / IRF4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmum1.html) (50% in small subset)
* **BLIMP1** (50%) and **XBP1** (30%)
* Small number of mantle cell lymphomas are positive for [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) ([Oncotarget 2017;9:11441](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29545910))
* [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) count ([J Hematop 2009;2:103](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19669190))
  + Five independent high power fields count
  + Avoidance of residual germinal centers, hot spots and proliferating T cells
  + Note: Ki67 index is not sufficient to classify as blastoid or pleomorphic subtype
  + Classical mantle cell lymphoma might also show high cell proliferation ([Blood 2008;111:2385](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18077791))
* [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) in subset; intense expression correlates with *TP53* gene mutation
* Note: no protein expression; on the other hand, cannot predict the homozygous deletions of the locus ([Blood 1996;87:3351](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8605352))
* [MYC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscmyc.html) in subset
* High expression correlates with *MYC* translocation ([Blood 2017;129:333](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27864294))
* [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) or [BCL6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl6.html) expression commonly seen ([Oncotarget 2017;9:11441](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29545910), [Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1051](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15252312), [Histopathology 2008;53:20](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18518902))
* [CD23](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd23.html): small subset of cases ([Mod Pathol 1996;9:925](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8878025))

Negative stains

* [CD200](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd200.html)
  + May be positive in a subset of [SOX11](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox11.html) negative mantle cell lymphomas
* [LEF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsLEF1.html)
  + Can be positive in 4 - 9% of mantle cell lymphomas ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2017;26:57](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28038713))

Flow cytometry description

* CD5+, CD19+, CD20+, CD22+, CD79b+, FMC7+
* Monotypic Ig (high level expression of surface light chains)
* CD11c variable, CD43 variable, CD200 variable
* CD3-, CD10-, CD23-
* Rare cases have atypical immunophenotype:
  + CD5- or CD10+, CD200+ or CD23+ (dim ~10%)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Karyotypes are often complex (≥ 3 abnormalities)
* Tetraploid clones are more frequently found in pleomorphic (80%) and blastoid (36%) variants
* Some abnormalities may be specifically involved in pathogenesis
  + Chromosome 17p deletions (*TP53*)
  + Chromosome 9p deletions (*CDKN2A*)
  + Chromosome 8q24 translocations or amplification (*MYC*)
  + Chromosome 3q27translocations (*BCL6*)
* Expression abnormalities can be simplified into 2 general types by array CGH
  + Cell cycle dysregulation / impaired DNA repair (*ATM*, *cyclin D1*, *TP53* mutations)
  + Impaired apoptosis (*BIRC3*)
* IG genes are clonally rearranged
* IGV genes are unmutated or minimally mutated in most cases
* t(11;14)(q13;q32)
  + FISH is convenient; it can be performed on fixed tissue sections
  + Conventional cytogenetics if fresh material available
  + Most PCR assays detect 1 major breakpoint region in mantle cell lymphoma
* Aggressive variants of mantle cell lymphoma
  + *TP53* or *p16* mutations are common
  + t(8;14)(q24;q32)(*IGH/MYC*) in a small subset of cases
  + Mutations in many genes
    - *ATM* (~ 40%), *TP53* (~ 25%), *CDKN2A* (*TP16*) (10 - 20%)
* Other mutations correlated with blastoid or pleomorphic variant
  + *KMT2D/MLL2* (~ 10%), *WHSC1* (~ 10%), *BIRC3* (~ 5%)
  + *NOTCH1* (~ 5%), *NOTCH2* (~ 5%), *MEF2B* (~ 3%), *MYD88* (~ 1%)
  + *CARD11* (10%)

Sample pathology report

* Left retroperitoneal lymph node, needle biopsies:
  + Mantle cell lymphoma, diffuse pattern, blastoid variant (see comment)
  + Comment: Routinely stained histologic sections reveal needle shaped fragments of tissue with partial crush artifact infiltrated by a diffuse monotonous infiltrate with focal starry sky pattern composed of large cells with irregular nuclei, blastoid chromatin, small nucleoli and scant cytoplasm. There is single cell apoptosis and mitotic figures are increased (45/10 high power fields).
    We performed immunohistochemical studies using paraffin embedded tissue, which show that the neoplastic cells are positive for cyclin D1, SOX11 (diffuse nuclear) and p53 (> 90%); while negative for TDT. Ki67 demonstrates a proliferation index of 100%. PAX5/CD5 immunohistochemical cocktail shows that the neoplastic cells are positive for PAX5 without co-expression of CD5.
    Fluorescence in situ hybridization (FISH) studies for t(11;14) translocation and *MYC* rearrangements are in progress and will be reported separately.
    Flow cytometry immunophenotyping detected a lambda restricted B cell population positive for CD10, CD19, CD20, CD22, CD38, CD79b; while negative for CD5, CD11c, CD30, CD43, CD44, CD200 and kappa light chain.
  + Addendum:
    - Positive for *IGH/CCND1* gene rearrangement
    - Negative for *MYC* gene rearrangement

Differential diagnosis

* [Lymphoblastic lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodesALL.html)
  + **Blastoid variant mantle cell lymphoma** can resemble lymphoblastic lymphoma (LBL)
  + Lymphoblastic lymphomas are [TdT](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstdt.html)+, [cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html)-
* [Burkitt lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaburkitt.html)
  + Starry sky pattern can occur in blastoid mantle cell lymphoma
  + Burkitt lymphoma is [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html)+, [BCL6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl6.html)+, [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html)-, [cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html)-
* [Diffuse large B cell lymphoma (DLBCL)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomadiffuse.html)
  + In differential diagnosis of pleomorphic variant
  + Diffuse large B cell lymphoma: usually [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html)-, [cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html)-
  + [Cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html)+ diffuse large B cell lymphoma rarely occurs (~2% of all diffuse large B cell lymphoma) ([Cancer 2014;120:1818](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24648050))
  + These diffuse large B cell lymphomas do not have the t(11;14)(q13;q32) and are [SOX11](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox11.html) negative ([Histopathology 2012;61:685](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22642745))
* [B cell prolymphocytic leukemia (B-PLL)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaprolymphocyticleukemia.html)
  + The immunophenotype can overlap with other lymphoid leukemias
  + [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html) can be expressed in 20 - 30% in B cell prolymphocytic leukemia
  + [cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html)+ or t(11;14)(q13;q32) (IGH/*CCND1*) supports mantle cell lymphoma
  + *MYD88* mutations occur rarely (1 - 2% of cases) in mantle cell lymphoma
  + A subgroup of B cell prolymphocytic leukemia cases with genetic findings similar to mantle cell lymphoma
    - 50% with *TP53* mutation
    - Recurrent *MYC* abnormalities
    - In high risk category model ([Blood 2019;134:1777](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31751477))
* [Blastic plasma cell myeloma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomamyeloma.html)
  + ~ 30 - 40% of plasma cell myeloma can express [cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html)
  + Cytoplasmic Ig+, [CD138](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd138.html)+, [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html)-, [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html)-
  + A subset of plasma cell myelomas express [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html) or t(11;14) ([Blood 2003;102:1070](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12702507), [Ann Diagn Pathol 2006;10:263](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16979517))
* [Leukemic non-nodal mantle cell lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonnodalmantlecell.html) (so called indolent / smoldering mantle cell lymphoma)
  + Peripheral blood, bone marrow and sometimes splenic involvement
  + Without significant adenopathy
  + Circulating cells resemble chronic lymphocytic leukemia (CLL)
  + [SOX11](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox11.html) negative
  + Immunophenotype may overlap with chronic lymphocytic leukemia
  + Fewer genomic abnormalities in comparison to nodal mantle cell lymphoma
  + Better prognosis
