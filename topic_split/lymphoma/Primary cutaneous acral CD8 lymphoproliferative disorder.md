Definition / general

* Rare indolent lymphoproliferative disorder characterized by slow growing papules or nodules in acral sites with a dermal infiltrate of CD8+ T lymphocytes

Essential features

* Mostly characterized by a solitary, slow growing nodule ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1887](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18043044), [Mod Pathol 2020;33:83](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31664155))
* Typically occurs in adults older than 50 years, with a male predominance ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2017;141:1469](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29072952), [Br J Dermatol 2022;186:887](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34988968))
* Dense, diffuse dermal infiltrate with sparing of the epidermis
* CD8 and CD3 positive staining

Terminology

* Primary cutaneous acral CD8+ lymphoproliferative disorder ([Leukemia 2022;36:1720](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35732829))

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [C84.A0](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C81-C96/C84-/C84.A0) - cutaneous T cell lymphoma, unspecified, unspecified site

Epidemiology

* Fewer than 100 cases have been reported
* Male predilection (M:F = 1.7:1) ([J Cutan Pathol 2016;43:125](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26423705), [Br J Dermatol 2022;186:887](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34988968))
* Median age of diagnosis is 54 years ([Br J Dermatol 2022;186:887](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34988968))

Sites

* Usually localized to the dermis in the ear ([Br J Dermatol 2022;186:887](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34988968))
* Multiple sites of involvement may be present
* Other sites may include the nose, hands, elbows and feet
* No extracutaneous involvement is present

Pathophysiology

* Pathophysiology is not known

Etiology

* Cell of origin is activated mature T lymphocyte, CD3+ / CD8+ type

Clinical features

* Painful, slowly growing single papule or nodule localized in the ear
* Always an indolent course ([Blood 2016;127:2375](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26980727))
* Cure is expected after local excision or localized therapy in 70% of patients; persistent disease in 20%

Diagnosis

* Biopsy or excision

Radiology description

* Single lesion localized to dermis
* No systemic dissemination
* Rare multifocal

Prognostic factors

* Recurrences are rare and are limited to the dermis
* Surgical excision or radiotherapy usually result in cure (70%) with an overall low relapse rate (20%) ([Br J Dermatol 2022;186:887](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34988968))
* Relapsing / progressing disease more frequent in younger patients ([Br J Haematol 2019;185:598](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30351475))

Case reports

* 16 year old boy with an enlarging solitary lesion on the right lower eyelid of 1 month duration and presence of CD56 expression and high Ki67 proliferation ([J Cutan Pathol 2021;48:1489](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34218462))
* 35 year old man and 45 year old woman with nasal involvement and similar clinical and histomorphological features ([J Cutan Pathol 2010:37;977](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19891656))
* 52 year old man and 41 year old man with indolent CD8+ lymphoid proliferation of the ear ([J Cutan Pathol 2011:38;209](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21083681))
* 53 year old woman with a 12 month history of an asymptomatic solitary right leg nodule with atypical BCL6 / GATA3 positivity ([Am J Dermatopathol 2021;43:137](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32675470))
* 57 year old man with history of slowly growing ear nodule with signet ring features ([Histopathology 2009;55:468](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19817899))
* 69 year old man with a 10 year history of a solitary slow growing plaque on the left ear and a 4 year history of an identical plaque on the right ear with presence of double positive CD8 / CD4 and associated with thymic carcinoma ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30552698))
* 69 year old man with history of relapsing asymptomatic nodules confined to the left ear with extracutaneous involvement ([J Cutan Pathol 2017;44:964](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28796362))
* 3 cases with largest review of the literature, including 52 additional cases ([J Cutan Pathol 2016;43:125](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26423705))
* 3 cases with ear involvement and review of the literature ([Am J Dermatopathol 2019;41:644](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31433793))
* First report defining the entity included 4 cases with ear involvement ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007:31:1887](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18043044))

Treatment

* Surgical excision or radiotherapy

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Dense, diffuse dermal infiltrate with sparing of the epidermis and presence of a thin grenz zone
* Infiltrate is composed of medium to large size atypical lymphocytes (blast-like) with irregular nuclear membranes, small nucleoli and scant cytoplasm; signet ring cells can be present
* No fat rimming, angiodestruction or necrosis is identified
* Focal epidermotropism and exocytosis of atypical lymphocytes have been noted in rare cases
* References: [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2017;141:1469](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29072952), [Br J Dermatol 2022;186:887](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34988968)

Cytotoxic immunophenotype

Positive stains

* [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html), [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html), **TIA1** (focal), [granzyme B](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgranzymeb.html) (commonly negative; focal occasionally), T cell receptor **BF1**
* Characteristic dot-like pattern of [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html) expression (negativity doesn't exclude the diagnosis) ([Br J Dermatol 2022;186:887](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34988968))
* Aberrant loss of [CD2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1.html#cd2), [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html) and [CD7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1.html#cd7) may be present in a proportion of the atypical lymphocytes
* [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) expression is frequently low (Arch Pathol Lab Med 2017;141:1469)

Negative stains

* [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html), [EBV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html), [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html), [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html), [granzyme B](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgranzymeb.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Clonal *TCR* gene rearrangements present by PCR testing in almost 100% of cases

Sample pathology report

* Skin, left ear, punch biopsies:
  + CD8+ positive cutaneous lymphoproliferative disorder (see comment)
  + Comment: The morphological features and immunohistochemical findings are most consistent with a low grade CD8+ cutaneous lymphoproliferative disorder. If the lesion is unique and localized to the ear, the findings are most consistent with primary cutaneous acral CD8+ lymphoproliferative disorder ([Leukemia 2022;36:1720](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35732829)).

Differential diagnosis

* [Hydroa vacciniforme-like lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBhvl.html):
  + Can show a similar infiltrate with [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html)+ cytotoxic immunophenotype but is an [EBV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html)+ T cell lymphoproliferative disease that occurs in children (mean age 8 years)
  + Atypical infiltrate is mainly located around adnexa and vessels with angiodestruction
* [Primary cutaneous CD8+ aggressive epidermotropic cytotoxic T cell lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBCD8epidermotropic.html):
  + Features skin papules or nodules with an aggressive clinical course, characterized by rapid progression, ulceration, poor response to chemotherapy and a 5 year overall survival rate of 32% ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:761](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28128277))
  + Morphologically characterized by full thickness pagetoid epidermotropism
  + Occasional cases show predominant dermal infiltrates; however, this is associated with atrophic epidermis and infiltration of adjacent adnexal structures ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:761](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28128277))
  + Tumor cells display a high [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) proliferation index (more than 50%)
* [Primary cutaneous gamma delta T cell lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBcutangammadelta.html):
  + Morphologically involves the subcutaneous tissue and commonly has epidermotropism or ulceration
  + Usually the tumor cells are [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html)- / [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html)-; however, a few cases are [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html)- / [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html)+
  + Characteristically this disease features [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html) and [TCR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstcralphabeta.html) gamma expression
  + Aggressive clinical course
* [Subcutaneous panniculitis-like T cell lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBsubcutaneouspan.html):
  + Solitary or multiple deeply seated plaques or subcutaneous nodules
  + Only subcutis involved, morphologically lobular panniculitis with septal sparing; adipocyte rimming with fat necrosis
  + High [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) proliferation index within [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html)+ atypical lymphocytes rimming the fat lobules
* [Mycosis fungoides, cytotoxic](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomamycosisfungoides.html):
  + Rare variant of mycosis fungoides
  + Presents as other cases of MF, with generalized, scaly patches and plaques, ulcerated nodules
  + Predilection for buttocks and sun protected areas
  + Morphologically has epidermotropism and shows Pautrier microabscesses, as well as intraepidermal vesiculation
* [Primary cutaneous peripheral T cell lymphoma, unspecified](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaptclnos.html):
  + Solitary or multiple ulcerating nodules
  + Medium to large sized cells with moderate to marked pleomorphism
  + Positive expression of T cell markers, including loss of antigens; may coexpress **TIA1**, [granzyme B](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgranzymeb.html) or [perforin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/perforin.html)
  + Aggressive clinical behavior

Which of the following statements is correct regarding primary cutaneous acral CD8+ lymphoproliferative disorder?

1. Characterized by aggressive course with progressive disease and resistance to therapy
2. Epidermotropism is usually absent
3. Loss of expression of pan-T cell markers CD2, CD5 and CD7 is rarely observed
4. Neoplastic cells feature a Ki67 proliferation index that ranges from 50 to 90%
5. Restricted to the skin of the ear

Board review style answer #1

**B.** Epidermotropism is usually absent
