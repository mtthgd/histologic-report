Definition / general

* Transformation of a chronic lymphocytic leukemia (CLL) or small lymphocytic lymphoma (SLL) into an aggressive lymphoma, primarily diffuse large B cell lymphoma (DLBCL), with rare cases developing into Hodgkin lymphoma
* Rare cases of plasmablastic lymphoma (PBL) or B lymphoblastic leukemia / lymphoma have been reported ([Eur J Haematol 2001;67:322](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11872081))
* References: [Cancer Genet Cytogenet 1996;86:143](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8603341), [Blood 1991;78:797](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1859892), [Blood 2018;131:1258](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29437555)

Essential features

* Progression of a chronic lymphocytic leukemia / small lymphocytic lymphoma into an aggressive lymphoma, most commonly diffuse large B cell lymphoma, with rare cases transforming into Hodgkin lymphoma ([Hum Pathol 2016;55:108](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27184478))
* Common clinical presentation: sudden clinical deterioration, development of systemic symptoms, rapid lymph node enlargement and extranodal manifestations
* Association with *CDKN2A, NOTCH1, MYC, TP53* mutations ([Curr Hematol Malig Rep 2014;9:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25218362))
* Poor overall prognosis: prognosis improved with clonally unrelated diffuse large B cell lymphoma ([Blood 2011;117:3391](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21266718))

Terminology

* Richter syndrome
* Richter transformation

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [C91.10](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C81-C96/C91-/C91.10) - chronic lymphocytic leukemia of B cell type not having achieved remission

Epidemiology

* Typically older adults
* Occurs in approximately 2 - 10% of patients with chronic lymphocytic leukemia ([Ann Hematol 2018;97:1859](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29947976), [Adv Clin Exp Med 2018;27:1683](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30156387))
* Richter syndrome of Hodgkin lymphoma type is a rare disease associated with EBV, occurring in 0.4 - 0.7% of chronic lymphocytic leukemia patients ([Hum Pathol 2016;55:108](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27184478), [Med Hypotheses 2006;66:577](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16223567))
* May occur during various times after diagnosis, with some reports of chronic lymphocytic leukemia and Richter syndrome diffuse large B cell lymphoma type being diagnosed simultaneously
* Incidence increased in patients undergoing heavy pretreatment

Sites

* Primarily lymph nodes, bone marrow
* Can present in extranodal sites (40% of cases): gastrointestinal tract, testis, skin, central nervous system, eye, lung, kidney
  ([Clin Nucl Med 2019;44:e87](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30516680), [IJU Case Rep 2019;2:232](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32743423), [Dermatol Online J 2020;26:13030](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33054942), [Medicine (Baltimore) 2018;97:e12701](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30313065), [Am J Ophthalmol 1996;122:266](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8694099), [Am Rev Respir Dis 1988;138:980](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3202472))

Pathophysiology

* 80% of cases derived from underlying chronic lymphocytic leukemia clone, while 20% of cases are described as unrelated clones ([Hemasphere 2020;4:e473](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33062946))
* 2 common genetic pathways responsible for transformation ([Immunotargets Ther 2019;8:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30788335))
  + Cell cycle deregulation by *TP53* and *CDKNA2* mutations in over 50% of cases
  + *NOTCH1* mutations and trisomy 12 in almost 33% of cases
* Genetic instability and loss of cell cycle control, related to *c-MYC* abnormalities, may explain the mechanisms of transformation
* Infection with EBV associated with dysregulating the immune system in Hodgkin type Richter syndrome ([Am J Surg Pathol 1992;16:859](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1384376), [Am J Surg Pathol 1993;17:853](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8393305), [Br J Haematol 2005;129:199](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15813847), [Adv Clin Exp Med 2018;27:1683](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30156387))

Etiology

* Risk factors for high grade transformation:
  + Clinical: advanced Rai stage at diagnosis and lymph node size > 3 cm, heavily pretreated chronic lymphocytic leukemia ([Ann Hematol 2018 ;97:1859](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29947976))
  + Increased risk of Richter syndrome associated with adverse prognostic factors, including del(11q), del(17p), unmutated immunoglobulin heavy chain variable region genes (IGHV) and a high expression of ZAP70, CD38 and CD49d at diagnosis
  + Increased risk of transformation with *NOTCH1* mutation ([Leukemia 2013;27:1100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23295735))

Clinical features

* Sudden clinical deterioration
* Development of systemic symptoms (B symptoms): fatigue, night sweats, weight loss, fever ([Oncology (Williston Park) 2012;26:1146](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23413591))
* Rapid, asymmetrical lymph node growth ([Br J Haematol 2008;142:709](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18492119))
* Extranodal manifestations / sites of disease
* Specificity of the above clinical signs is limited to 50 - 60% ([Curr Treat Options Oncol 2017;18:75](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29159711))

Diagnosis

* Laboratory assessment: lactate dehydrogenase (LDH), complete blood count, calcium level ([Dermatology 2010;220:226](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20145381))
* Radiologic imaging: CT, PET / CT for evaluation of suspicious sites of disease and biopsy planning ([J Nucl Med 2006;47:1267](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16883004))
* Biopsy of the involved lymph node or extranodal site is the gold standard for diagnosis ([Int J Hematol Oncol 2019;7:IJH09](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30651968))

Laboratory

* Elevated LDH level
* Anemia, neutropenia and thrombocytopenia
* Hypercalcemia with or without lytic bone lesion
* Paraproteinemia in 44% of patients ([Cancer 2005;103:216](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15578683))

Radiology description

* Radiologic assessment demonstrates higher predictive value over clinical criteria
* Computerized tomography (CT) scan helpful criteria ([Ann Hematol 2018;97:1859](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29947976))
  + Rapid disproportionate enlargement of a single group of lymph nodes
  + Inhomogeneous attenuation of lymph nodes, reflecting central necrosis
  + Evidence of extranodal involvement
* PET / CT scan findings ([Blood 2018;131:2761](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29692342))
  + Evaluation of lymph nodes with SUVmax > 5 demonstrates high sensitivity but lower specificity for detecting Richter syndrome
  + Usefulness of PET / CT with its high negative predictive value (97 - 98%)

Prognostic factors

* Overall poor prognosis, with survival ranging from 1 - 45 months depending on risk stratification ([Blood 2018;131:2761](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29692342))
* Favorable prognostic factors:
  + Clonally unrelated chronic lymphocytic leukemia and Richter syndrome diffuse large B cell lymphoma: prognosis similar to de novo diffuse large B cell lymphoma ([Ann Hematol 2018;97:1859](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29947976))
* Unfavorable factors:
  + Platelet count < 100,000/μL ([J Clin Oncol 2006;24:2343](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16710033))
  + *TP53* ([Proc Natl Acad Sci U S A 2008;105:13520](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18765795))
  + Trisomy 12
  + LDH > 1.5 times upper limit ([J Clin Oncol 2006;24:2343](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16710033))
  + Tumor size > 5 cm ([J Clin Oncol 2006;24:2343](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16710033))
  + > 1 prior treatment ([J Clin Oncol 2006;24:2343](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16710033))
  + Failure to achieve complete remission after induction

Case reports

* 64 year old man with chronic lymphocytic leukemia and leukemic Richter syndrome occurring in peripheral blood ([J Hematol 2014;3:86](https://doi.org/10.14740/jh121w))
* 72 year old man with sudden scrotal swelling ([IJU Case Rep 2019;2:232](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32743423))
* 75 year old man presenting with enlarged cervical and subdiaphragmatic lymph nodes as well as diffuse wall thickening of the colon ([Clin Nucl Med 2019;44:e87](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30516680))
* 76 year old woman with ulcerated cutaneous Richter syndrome presenting as a 10 cm erythematous, infiltrative and irregular ulcer ([Dermatol Online J 2020;26:13030](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33054942))
* 77 year old man presenting with enlarging cervical lymph nodes and a preauricular mass ([Onco Targets Ther 2019;12:1181](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30863085))
* CLL patient with an unusual plasmablastic variant of Richter syndrome ([Eur J Haematol 2001;67:322](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11872081))

Treatment

* Richter syndrome - diffuse large B cell lymphoma type (DLBCL RS):
  + R-CHOP or R-CHOP-like regimens (i.e. R-EPOCH) are widely used as first line treatment option ([Blood 2018;131:2761](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29692342))
  + Stem cell transplantation an available option in young, fit patients
  + Emerging role of immunotherapy, including BTK inhibitors (ibrutinib, acalabrutinib), BCL2 inhibitors (venetoclax), PD-1 receptor inhibitors (pembrolizumab), among others ([Blood 2018;131:2761](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29692342), [Immunotargets Ther 2019;8:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30788335))
* Richter syndrome - Hodgkin lymphoma type (HS RS):
  + Most commonly used treatment regimen is ABVD (adriamycin, bleomycin, vinblastine and dacarbazine) ([Adv Clin Exp Med 2018;27:1683](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30156387))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Diffuse large B cell lymphoma type:
  + Diffuse effacement of lymph nodes or extranodal sites with sheets of large cells with centroblastic, immunoblastic or anaplastic morphology
  + Proposed criteria to distinguish from aggressive chronic lymphocytic leukemia ([Immunotargets Ther 2019;8:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30788335)):
    - Presence of large B cells with nuclear sizes either equal to the nucleus of macrophages or more than twice that of a normal lymphocyte
    - Diffuse growth pattern of large cells
* Hodgkin lymphoma type:
  + Presence of classic Reed-Sternberg cells in an appropriate polymorphous background of small T cells, histiocytes, eosinophils and plasma cells

Peripheral smear description

* Richter syndrome malignant cells are occasionally seen in the bone marrow and very rarely in the peripheral blood ([J Hematol. 2014;3:86](http://dx.doi.org/10.14740/jh121w))
* Morphology of transformed cells may show enlarged cells with pleomorphic nuclei, less condensed chromatin, prominent nucleoli and scant cytoplasm ([J Clin Oncol 2006;24:2343](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16710033))
* Background circulating chronic lymphocytic leukemia cells present (small lymphocytes with condensed / clumped chromatin)

Positive stains

* Diffuse large B cell lymphoma type:
  + Positive for B cell markers [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html) or [CD79a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd79a.html)
  + Variable expression of [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html) (~30%), [CD23](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd23.html) (~15%), [MYC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscmyc.html) (30 - 40%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1605](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17895764), [Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2015;8:7540](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26261666), [Am J Hematol 2016;91:1036](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27414262))
  + Majority have nongerminal center phenotype (positive for [MUM1 / IRF4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmum1.html))
  + Frequent expression of [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html) (up to 80% of cases) ([Haematologica 2020;105:765](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31197071))
  + High level of [PD-1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspd1.html) expression in RS indicates clonal relation of CLL / SLL and DLBCL RS ([Br J Haematol 2019;185:370](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30028010), [Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:843](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29762141))
* Hodgkin type:
  + Reed-Sternberg cells: [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html), [CD15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd15.html) (negative for [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html))
  + Associated with [EBV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorEBV.html) positivity

Negative stains

* Diffuse large B cell lymphoma type:
  + Rare germinal center phenotype ([CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html), [BCL6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl6.html) expression) (positive in 5 - 10% of cases)
  + [EBV negative](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorEBV.html)
* Hodgkin type:
  + Reed-Sternberg cells: negative for [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html)

Flow cytometry description

* Most cases retain a chronic lymphocytic leukemia / small lymphocytic lymphoma immunophenotype ([Am J Clin Pathol 2015;143:25](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25511139))
  + All RS cases are positive for [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html), [CD19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd19.html), [CD43](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd43.html), [HLA-DR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshladr.html), [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html), [CD38](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd38.html), [ZAP70](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsZAP70.html) and [CD71](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd71.html)
* Transformation to Richter syndrome may demonstrate some minor modulations in antigen expression ([Am J Clin Pathol 2015;143:25](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25511139))
  + Increase in size of tumor cells on FSC / SSC properties
  + Increased intensity of [CD38](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd38.html), [ZAP70](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsZAP70.html), [HLA-DR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshladr.html), [CD71](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd71.html)
  + Decreased intensity of [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html) expression; decreased or absent expression of [CD52](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd50.html#cd52), [CD62L](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsselectine.html#Lselectin)
* Majority of DLBCL transformations from CLL / SLL retain the expression of [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html) and [CD23](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd23.html) by flow cytometry ([Am J Clin Pathol 2001;115:385](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11242795))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Most molecular findings / knowledge based on research of diffuse large B cell lymphoma type Richter syndrome (findings in Hodgkin type Richter syndrome are less known)
* Deregulation of cell cycle control, proliferation and damage to DNA repair and target genes via somatic mutations of *TP53* (60 - 80%), *CDKNA2* (30%) or *MYC* itself (30%) or by affecting their regulatory functions, e.g. *NOTCH1* (30%) and *MGA* (10%)
* 2 common genetic pathways responsible for transformation ([Immunotargets Ther 2019;8:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30788335))
  + *TP53* and *CDKNA2* mutations in over 50% of cases
  + *NOTCH1* mutations and trisomy 12 in almost 33% of cases
* Increased risk of Richter syndrome associated with adverse prognostic factors, such as del(11q), del(17p), unmutated immunoglobulin heavy chain variable region genes (IGHV) and a high expression of ZAP70, CD38 and CD49d
* Cytogenetic analysis of Richter syndrome: predominantly complex karyotype
  + No characteristic chromosomal abnormality
  + Monosomies of 9, 17 and losses of 9p, 17p can be seen ([Am J Clin Pathol 2015;143:25](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25511139))

Differential diagnosis

* [Aggressive chronic lymphocytic leukemia (CLL)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaCLL.html):
  + Increase in size and proliferative activity of the CLL cells with confluent expansion of the proliferation centers in the lymph nodes (proliferation centers broader than a 20x field)
  + Proposed criteria to distinguish from aggressive CLL ([Immunotargets Ther 2019;8:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30788335)):
    - Presence of large B cells with nuclear sizes either equal to the nucleus of macrophages or more than twice that of a normal lymphocyte
    - Diffuse growth pattern of large cells
  + Distinction requires pathologist expertise
* [De novo diffuse large B cell lymphoma (DLBCL)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomadiffuse.html):
  + Morphologically indistinguishable
  + [PD-1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspd1.html) negative
  + Distinct molecular signature: typical recurrent mutations of de novo DLBCL affecting NFκB (e.g. *CARD11, TNFAIP / A20, CD79A, CD79B, BCL6, BCL2, PRDM1* and *EZH2*) or genes associated with CLL chemorefractoriness and progression (e.g. *BIRC3, MYD88, DDX3X, SF3B1* and *RPS15*) not seen in Richter syndrome DLBCL
* [Hodgkin-like lesion](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomaclassichodgkinptld.html):
  + Hodgkin/Reed-Sternberg (HRS) cells in the setting of chronic lymphocytic leukemia (CLL) (HRS-CLL) and Hodgkin lymphoma variant of Richter transformation (CHL-RT) can show similar morphology ([Hum Pathol 2016;55:108](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27184478))
  + HRS-CLL has potential to progress to CHL
  + 27% of patients with HRS-CLL have a prior known diagnosis of CLL, as opposed to 73% of CHL-RT patient who had prior diagnosis of CLL
  + Cytologic features are typical of CLL in most cases of HRS-CLL with rare mixed inflammatory cells, in contrast to CHL-RT, which shows polymorphous inflammatory background
  + CHL-RT shows a moth-eaten or segregated pattern of CHL in background of CLL
  + Immunophenotype and EBV status of the HRS cells are similar in both HRS / CLL and CHL / RT cases ([Hum Pathol 2016;55:108](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27184478))
  + Both majority of HHR-CLL and CHL-RT patients are EBV+
* **Ibrutinib related pseudo Richter transformation**:
  + Happens after a brief dose interruption of Ibrutinib or acute infection in CLL / SLL patients ([Br J Haematol 2020;191:e22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32677079))
  + Presents with lymphadenopathy, lymphocytosis or progressive cytopenia and tissue biopsy shows DLBCL transformation; return of LDH and lymphocyte count to normal after treatment resumption with rebiopsy showing CLL / SLL with no DLBCL left
  + Large cells with the same immunophenotype as CLL / SLL
