Definition / general

* Primary cutaneous cytotoxic T cell lymphoma, which typically involves subcutaneous adipose tissue ([Am J Surg Pathol 1991;15:17](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1985499), [JAMA Dermatol 2022;158:1167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36001337), [Asia Pac J Clin Oncol 2023;19:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35509196), [Leuk Lymphoma 2021;62:2130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33966586), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2021;85:1093](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33945836), [Blood 2020;135:1058](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32005988), [Blood Adv 2021;5:3919](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34535012))
  + First described by Gonzalez and colleagues in 1991
  + Defined as a distinct entity by the 2001 World Health Organization Classification of Hematopoietic and Lymphoid Tumors
    - Initially included cases of both α / β and γ / δ phenotypes

Essential features

* Primary cutaneous cytotoxic T cell lymphoma, which typically involves subcutaneous adipose tissue ([Am J Surg Pathol 1991;15:17](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1985499), [JAMA Dermatol 2022;158:1167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36001337), [Asia Pac J Clin Oncol 2023;19:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35509196), [Leuk Lymphoma 2021;62:2130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33966586), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2021;85:1093](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33945836), [Blood 2020;135:1058](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32005988), [Blood Adv 2021;5:3919](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34535012))
* Rare ([JAMA Dermatol 2022;158:1167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36001337), [Leuk Lymphoma 2021;62:2130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33966586), [Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2019;27:740](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31702703))
  + < 1% of non-Hodgkin lymphomas
* Homozygous germline *HAVCR2* (TIM-3) mutation in ~60% of patients ([Asia Pac J Clin Oncol 2023;19:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35509196), [Blood 2020;135:1058](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32005988), [Blood Adv 2021;5:3919](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34535012), [Nat Genet 2018;50:1650](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30374066))
* Solitary or multiple erythematous subcutaneous tender and deep nodules or plaques ([JAMA Dermatol 2022;158:1167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36001337), [Asia Pac J Clin Oncol 2023;19:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35509196), [Leuk Lymphoma 2021;62:2130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33966586), [Orphanet J Rare Dis 2014;9:160](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25928531), [J Clin Pathol 2015;68:954](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26602413))

Terminology

* T cell lymphoma involving subcutaneous tissue

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [C86.3](http://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C81-C96/C86-/C86.3) - subcutaneous panniculitis-like T cell lymphoma

Epidemiology

* Rare ([JAMA Dermatol 2022;158:1167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36001337), [Leuk Lymphoma 2021;62:2130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33966586), [Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2019;27:740](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31702703))
  + < 1% of non-Hodgkin lymphomas
* Median age: ~25 - 35 years ([JAMA Dermatol 2022;158:1167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36001337), [Asia Pac J Clin Oncol 2023;19:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35509196), [Leuk Lymphoma 2021;62:2130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33966586), [Blood Adv 2021;5:3919](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34535012), [Orphanet J Rare Dis 2022;17:432](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36503528))
  + < 20% of cases occur in adolescents or children
    - Similar clinical course
* F > M ([JAMA Dermatol 2022;158:1167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36001337), [Nat Genet 2018;50:1650](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30374066))
  + F:M = ~2:1

Etiology

* Homozygous germline *HAVCR2* (TIM-3) mutation ([Asia Pac J Clin Oncol 2023;19:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35509196), [Blood 2020;135:1058](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32005988), [Blood Adv 2021;5:3919](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34535012), [Nat Genet 2018;50:1650](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30374066))
  + Immune checkpoint gene frequently mutated in sporadic cases
  + Encodes T cell immunoglobulin mucin 3 (TIM-3)
    - Modulation of T cell function
      * Impairs TIM-3 protein folding, cellular expression and function
        + Induces a persistent inflammatory response and potentially causes hemophagocytic lymphohistiocytosis (HLH)
    - Regulates monocyte / macrophage activation
* Recurrent mutations in phosphoinosiditide 3 kinase (PI3K) / AKT / mammalian target of rapamycin (mTOR) and JAK / STAT pathways ([Asia Pac J Clin Oncol 2023;19:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35509196), [Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2019;27:740](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31702703), [Br J Haematol 2018;181:406](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28294301), [Blood Adv 2021;5:3919](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34535012))
* Chemokine dysregulation ([Asia Pac J Clin Oncol 2023;19:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35509196))
  + Expression of CCR5 by neoplastic T cells
    - CCR5 ligands (CCL3, CCL4, CCL5) on the adipocyte membrane
* Rare cases associated with Epstein-Barr virus (EBV) infection ([Asia Pac J Clin Oncol 2023;19:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35509196))
  + Inhibition of *SAP / SH2D1A* gene by EBV latent membrane protein 1 (LMP1)
    - Increased cytokine secretion
  + Delay in the apoptosis of T cells through TNFα / TNF receptor 1 via nuclear factor kappa B (NFκB) pathway
* Association with autoimmunity ([JAMA Dermatol 2022;158:1167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36001337), [Asia Pac J Clin Oncol 2023;19:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35509196), [Surg Pathol Clin 2021;14:177](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34023099), [Nat Genet 2018;50:1650](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30374066))
  + Personal or family history of autoimmune diseases
  + ~20% of cases
  + Lupus erythematous is the most common
    - Multiple sclerosis, rheumatoid arthritis and psoriatic arthritis have also been described
* Rare cases associated with COVID vaccine association ([JAAD Case Rep 2022;28:18](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35966352))

Clinical features

* Solitary or multiple erythematous subcutaneous tender and deep nodules or plaques ([JAMA Dermatol 2022;158:1167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36001337), [Asia Pac J Clin Oncol 2023;19:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35509196), [Leuk Lymphoma 2021;62:2130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33966586), [Orphanet J Rare Dis 2014;9:160](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25928531), [J Clin Pathol 2015;68:954](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26602413))
  + Lower / upper extremities, trunk, head and neck
    - ~75% are multifocal
  + Extent of involvement and size of lesions are variable
  + Ulceration is rare, lipoatrophy may be seen
  + Indolent clinical course
    - Wax and wane
    - Median clinical onset: ~18 months
* B symptoms in ~70% of patients ([JAMA Dermatol 2022;158:1167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36001337))
* Hemophagocytic syndrome in ~20% of patients ([JAMA Dermatol 2022;158:1167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36001337), [Asia Pac J Clin Oncol 2023;19:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35509196))
  + Usually associated with more extensive disease
* Rare cases with hepatosplenomegaly, lymph node or bone marrow involvement ([JAMA Dermatol 2022;158:1167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36001337), [J Clin Pathol 2015;68:954](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26602413))

Diagnosis

* Association of clinicopathologic features and correct interpretation of histologic findings ([JAMA Dermatol 2022;158:1167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36001337), [J Clin Oncol 2015;33:1216](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25691676))
  + Unrelated malignant and nonmalignant confounding histopathology can masquerade as subcutaneous panniculitis-like T cell lymphoma (SPTCL)

Laboratory

* Elevated rates of ferritin, lactate dehydrogenase (LDH), erythrocyte sedimentation rate / C reactive protein, beta 2 microglobulin and aspartate transaminase (AST) may be presented ([JAMA Dermatol 2022;158:1167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36001337), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2021;85:1093](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33945836))
* Cytopenias (anemia, neutropenia or lymphopenia) may be present ([JAMA Dermatol 2022;158:1167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36001337), [Orphanet J Rare Dis 2014;9:160](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25928531))

Radiology description

* Fluorine 18 fluorodeoxyglucose positron emission tomography / computed tomography (18F-FDG PET / CT) usually shows moderate to intense uptake of subcutaneous lesions ([J Clin Oncol 2015;33:1216](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25691676), [J Radiol Case Rep 2022;16:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35875366), [Front Oncol 2021;11:650822](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34277404))
  + Used for disease activity (cutaneous or extracutaneous), staging and treatment response

Prognostic factors

* Overall good prognosis ([Asia Pac J Clin Oncol 2023;19:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35509196), [Blood Adv 2021;5:3919](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34535012))
  + 5 year overall survival (OS): 85 - 91%
  + Factors associated with worse prognosis
    - Involvement of upper extremities
    - Hemophagocytic syndrome
      * 5 year overall survival: ~50%

Case reports

* 26 year old man with a diagnosis of panniculitis-like T cell lymphoma with *HAVCR2* mutation and previous history of lupus panniculitis ([Intern Med 2023;62:1537](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36171125))
* 34 year old woman with liver failure and skin lesions as main manifestation of subcutaneous panniculitis-like T cell lymphoma ([Ann Transl Med 2022;10:1408](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36660620))
* 39 year old man with a diagnosis of panniculitis-like T cell lymphoma presenting as an abscess following arthropod bite ([Dermatol Online J 2022;28:3](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36259854))
* 42 year old man diagnosed with panniculitis-like T cell lymphoma in complete remission after 2 cycles of romidepsin ([Case Rep Oncol 2022;15:1088](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36605220))
* 47 year old woman with a diagnosis of panniculitis-like T cell lymphoma with inverted FDG uptake pattern in 18F-FDG PET ([Clin Nucl Med 2023;48:186](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36607368))

Treatment

* No standard treatment
  + Immune modulatory treatments as single agents or combined ([JAMA Dermatol 2022;158:1167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36001337), [Surg Pathol Clin 2021;14:177](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34023099))
    - Cyclosporine A, systemic steroids, bexarotene, low dose methotrexate, chlorambucil, azathioprine
    - Overall response rate (ORR): ~50%
  + Chemotherapy
    - CHOP: cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin, vincristine and prednisone
    - CHOP-like regimens
  + Radiation
    - Localized disease
  + Hematopoietic stem cell transplantation ([Asia Pac J Clin Oncol 2023;19:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35509196))
    - Refractory / relapsed or disseminated cases

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Skin involvement ([JAMA Dermatol 2022;158:1167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36001337), [Surg Pathol Clin 2021;14:177](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34023099), [Histopathology 2013;62:1057](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23600665), [Blood Adv 2021;5:3919](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34535012), [J Clin Pathol 2015;68:954](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26602413))
  + Epidermis and dermis are usually spared
    - Interface dermatitis, plasma cell aggregates, follicular plugging or mucin deposits may occur
    - Minimal involvement by lymphoma cells of deep dermis can be observed
  + Lobular infiltrate
    - Variable size (small to intermediate) lymphocytes with hyperchromatic and irregular nuclei and scant pale / clear cytoplasm
      * Subset of cases with large cell or pleomorphic morphology
    - Variable density of atypical lymphocytes involving lobules and sparing septa
      * Rimming adipocytes or lipid vacuoles
    - Background
      * Scattered reactive plasma cells and neutrophils
      * Absence of plasmacytoid dendritic cells aggregates (> 10 cells)
      * Reactive histiocytes, sometimes dense and forming granulomas or lipogranulomas
  + Necrosis may vary from mild to extensive
  + Karyorrhexis, mitotic figures and hemorrhage can be present
  + Hemophagocytosis may be detected in skin or other tissues (e.g., bone marrow)
* Bone marrow is not involved by overt lymphoma cells but aggregates of small lymphocytes can be detected around adipocytes

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Monoclonal rearrangement of TCR ([Surg Pathol Clin 2021;14:177](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34023099), [Orphanet J Rare Dis 2014;9:160](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25928531), [J Clin Pathol 2015;68:954](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26602413))
* Homozygous germline mutations of *HAVCR2* ([Surg Pathol Clin 2021;14:177](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34023099), [Blood 2020;135:1058](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32005988), [Blood Adv 2021;5:3919](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34535012), [Nat Genet 2018;50:1650](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30374066))
  + *HAVCR2*Y82C is the most common mutation (hepatitis A virus cellular receptor 2)
    - Y82C, I97M and T101I are the most common recurrent germline mutations
      * Y82C variants are more common in Polynesian and East Asian populations
    - Associated with younger age, systemic illness, HLH or HLH-like syndrome, inflammatory microenvironment and shorter relapse free survival (RFS) when compared to wild type cases
    - Enriched in genes involving IL6-JAK-STAT3, TNFα and NKκB signaling
  + Wild type cases
    - Mutations in *ASXL1, UNC13D, PIAS3* and *KMT2D* are more frequent
    - Enrichment in CCR4
* Exome sequencing ([Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2019;27:740](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31702703))
  + Missense mutations in *ARID1B*
* Gene expression profiling ([Leuk Lymphoma 2021;62:2130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33966586), [Orphanet J Rare Dis 2014;9:160](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25928531))
  + Upregulation
    - Cytotoxic genes: *PRF1, NKG7, GZMB, GZMH* and *GZMA*
    - CD8 exhaustion: *IL10, LAG3* and *EOMES*
    - Th1 type cytokines
    - *KIR2DL3* and *IDO1*
  + Downregulation
    - Mast cell genes: *HDC, CPA3* and *MTA3*
    - B cell genes: *KDR*
    - Apoptosis genes: *MIB1* and *EFHC1*
    - Dendritic cell genes: *SP1, CCL3* and *CD209*

Sample pathology report

* Skin, right thigh lesion, punch:
  + Subcutaneous panniculitis-like T cell lymphoma (see comment)
  + Comment: According to clinical notes, this is a 37 year old woman with a history of a solitary, tender and painless lesion in distal thigh (3 cm in the greater axis) that the patient noted wax and wane for several months.
  + Histologic sections of the specimen designated right thigh lesion demonstrate a punch skin biopsy that includes epidermis, dermis and subcutaneous adipose tissue. Epidermis is unremarkable. Deep dermis and subcutaneous adipose tissue show dense small to medium sized lymphoid cells, neutrophils and histiocytes. The lymphocytes show irregular nuclear contours, hyperchromatic chromatin and scant pale cytoplasm. The distribution is mainly involving the lobules with fat rimming and sparing the septa. Mitotic figures are frequent and karyorrhectic debris is present.
  + Immunohistochemical studies showed the atypical lymphocytes are positive for CD2, CD3, CD8, CD43, TIA1, perforin and TCRα-β (βF1). The abnormal lymphocytes are negative for CD4, CD7, CD30, CD56 and TCRγ / δ. Ki67 index is ~70% (variable, ranging from 40 - 100%). Epstein-Barr virus encoded small RNAs (EBER) in situ hybridization is negative.
  + Polymerase chain reaction to assess for clonality of T cell receptors gamma and delta chains clonal TCRβ (*TRB*) and polyclonal TCRγ (*TRG*) and TCRδ (*TRD*).

Differential diagnosis

* [Lupus erythematosus panniculitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorlupuspanniculitis.html) ([Am J Dermatopathol 2010;32:24](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20098081), [Asia Pac J Clin Oncol 2023;19:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35509196), [Histopathology 2013;62:1057](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23600665), [Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:745](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26796503)):
  + F > M
  + Multiple or single lipoatrophy, erythema or subcutaneous nodules
  + Extremities (upper arms) > face > trunk
  + Epidermal and dermal changes
    - Lymphocytic lobular / septal panniculitis with mild or no cytological atypia
    - Intradermal mucin, hyaline lipomembranous changes, mild vasculitis, reactive germinal centers and plasma cells
    - Aggregates of [CD123](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd123.html)+ plasmacytoid dendritic cells
  + Absence of atypical T cells
    - Mixed cell infiltrate: [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html)+, [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html)+, [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html)+, [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html)+
  + Low [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) proliferation index (~< 10%)
  + Usually polyclonal *TCRγ* gene rearrangement
    - Rare cases with monoclonal peak
* **Cytophagic histiocytic panniculitis** ([Ital J Pediatr 2014;40:17](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24524345)):
  + Infiltration of subcutaneous by T cells and phagocytic histiocytes
  + Isolated in skin or associated with other malignancies
    - Skin lesions may remain
  + No evidence of monoclonal rearrangements of *TRB* or *TRG* gene
* **Cold panniculitis** ([J Clin Pathol 2015;68:954](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26602413)):
  + Physical panniculitis
  + Appropriate clinical history
  + More common in pediatric patients
    - Face (chin and cheeks)
      * Different chemical composition of the adipose tissue
  + Adults: usually after chronic use of ice pack or environmental exposure
  + Lymphoid infiltrate of lobular distribution
    - Increased density along the dermal - subcutaneous transition, dermal perivascular and periadnexial regions
    - Dermal mucin and interface dermatitis may be present
  + No evidence of monoclonal *TCB* or *TRG* gene rearrangements
* [Lyme disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorlymedisease.html) ([J Clin Pathol 2015;68:954](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26602413)):
  + Rare cases presenting as lobular panniculitis
    - Dense lymphocytic infiltrate in subcutaneous tissue with scattered plasma cells
      * Small to medium size and moderate atypia
    - No extension to overlying dermis or epidermis
  + Predominance of [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html)+ cells
    - **TIA1**+
    - [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html)-, [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html)-
  + Identification of *Borrelia burgdorferi* DNA by PCR or positive serology
* **Atypical lymphocytic lobular panniculitis** ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:206](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25118815)):
  + Indolent condition with spontaneous regression
    - Controversial entity
  + Lobular infiltration of clonal, small to intermediate sized T cells and mild nuclear atypia
    - Usually less dense infiltrate
    - Adipocyte rimming is less frequent
    - Hemorrhage and karyorrhexis are usually absent
  + Good prognosis
* [Primary cutaneous gamma delta T cell lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBcutangammadelta.html) ([Surg Pathol Clin 2021;14:177](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34023099)):
  + Plaques, nodules and tumors with rapid progression and ulceration
  + Involvement of epidermis, dermis and subcutaneous tissue
    - Medium to large size lymphocytes with irregular nuclear contours, clumped chromatin and eosinophilic cytoplasm
  + [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html)+, [CD7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1.html#cd7)+, [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html)-, [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html)-, [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html)-
  + **TIA1+**, [granzyme B](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgranzymeb.html)+, [perforin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/perforin.html)+
  + [TCRδ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstcralphabeta.html)+, [TCRγ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstcralphabeta.html)+, [TCRβ (βF1)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstcralphabeta.html)-
  + Poor prognosis
* **Primary cutaneous CD30+ T cell lymphoproliferative disorders** ([Surg Pathol Clin 2021;14:177](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34023099)):
  + Multiple papules or solitary lesion
  + Monomorphic lymphoid infiltrate predominantly in dermis
    - May occur in hypodermis
  + [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html)+, [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html) variable, [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html) variable
  + [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html) strong and diffuse
  + Indolent behavior
* [Primary cutaneous peripheral T cell lymphoma, not otherwise specified](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonbperipheral.html) ([J Eur Acad Dermatol Venereol 2021;35:658](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32997839)):
  + Solitary or disseminated papulonodular or tumor lesions with rapid growth and necrosis
  + Nodular or diffuse infiltrate in dermis and subcutaneous tissue
    - Variable morphology, usually large, pleomorphic cells
  + Epidermotropism absent or focal
  + [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html)+ / [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html)- / [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html)- or [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html)+ / [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html)+ / [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html)-
    - [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html)+ may also occur
  + Poor prognosis
    - 3 year overall survival: ~60%
    - 5 year overall survival: ~50%
* [Acute myeloid leukemia involving skin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/leukemiam4.html) ([An Bras Dermatol 2018;93:216](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29723350)):
  + Variable clinical presentation
  + Diffuse infiltration of dermis and hypodermis
    - Neoplastic cells can rim adipocytes
* [Extranodal NK / T cell lymphoma involving skin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBnasal.html) ([Surg Pathol Clin 2021;14:177](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34023099)):
  + More common in adults
  + Ulcerated and necrotic tumors
  + Small to large atypical lymphocytes in an inflammatory and necrotic background
  + Angiocentricity and angioinvasion
  + Positive: [CD2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1.html#cd2), [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html), **TIA1**, [granzyme B](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgranzymeb.html), [perforin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/perforin.html), [EBER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html)
  + Negative: [CD3ε](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html), [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html), [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html), [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html)
  + Poor prognosis

Which of the following is true about subcutaneous panniculitis-like T cell lymphoma?

1. Frequently presents with an aggressive clinical behavior
2. Localized or multiple subcutaneous nodules are the most common clinical presentation
3. Lymph nodes are usually involved at initial diagnosis
4. Somatic mutations of *HAVCR2* are common

Board review style answer #1

**B**. Localized or multiple subcutaneous nodules are the most common clinical presentation. Subcutaneous panniculitis-like T cell lymphoma usually presents as localized or multiple subcutaneous nodules, usually in the legs, arms or trunk and rarely with lymph node involvement. Germline mutations of *HAVCR2* are classically described as a predisposing factor in up to 85% of patients.
