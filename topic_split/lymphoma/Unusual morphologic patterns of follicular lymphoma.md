Definition / general

* Follicular lymphoma (FL) is a neoplasm composed of germinal center B cells (centrocytes and centroblasts in varying proportions), usually (but not always) distributed in a follicular pattern
* Distinct biologic variants of FL have identifiable clinical and histopathologic features that help their recognition in clinical practice; examples include [pediatric type FL](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomafollicularchildhood.html), [primary cutaneous follicular center cell lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomafollicularcutaneous.html), [primary testicular FL](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomafolliculartesticular.html) and [duodenal type FL](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomafolliculargi.html)
* Unusual morphologic patterns can also be seen in cases of FL and do not represent biologically distinct variants of FL; these FL may not be easily recognizable due to histopathologic overlap with other B cell lymphomas and are discussed in this chapter (see [Table 1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomafollicularunusual.html#lymphomafollicularunusualtable1))

Essential features

* FL with Castleman-like changes:
  + Castleman-like changes with B cell markers, BCL2 and germinal center markers
* FL with plasmacytic differentiation and IgG4 positive plasma cells:
  + FL with associated increased and monotypic plasma cells
* FL with marginal zone (MZ) differentiation involving mucosa associated lymphoid tissue (MALT) sites:
  + Marginal zone differentiation
  + Monocytoid B cells
  + Lymphoepithelial lesions
  + Plasmacytic differentiation
* FL negative for CD10, positive for MUM1 and with *BCL6* abnormalities:
  + More frequent diffuse proliferation and grade 3B compared with conventional FL
  + Usually lack *BCL2* rearrangement
  + MUM1 expression and *BCL6* abnormalities (translocation, amplification)
* EBV positive FL:
  + Morphology and immunophenotype similar to the conventional type of FL
  + EBER is positive in ~100% of cases
* Floral variant of FL:
  + Irregular neoplastic follicles with expanded, prominent mantle zone lymphocytes penetrating the neoplastic follicles

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9690/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=95) - follicular lymphoma, NOS
* ICD-10: [C82.90](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C81-C96/C82-/C82.90) - follicular lymphoma, unspecified, unspecified site

Epidemiology

* FL with Castleman-like changes:
  + Incidence: infrequent
  + Probably more common in practice than reported in the literature
  + Essential to be familiar with this variant as Castleman-like changes may mask the underlying lymphoma ([Hum Pathol 2017;68:136](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28873356))
* FL with plasmacytic differentiation and IgG4 positive plasma cells:
  + ∼3.5% of FL show plasmacytic differentiation ([Am J Clin Pathol 1985;84:283](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4036858), [Virchows Arch A Pathol Anat Histol 1981;394:119](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6801847), [Mod Pathol 2010;23:71](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19838161))
* FL with marginal zone differentiation involving MALT sites:
  + FL with marginal zone differentiation seen in ~9% of cases ([Leuk Lymphoma 2011;52:804](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21338287))
* FL negative for CD10 and expressing MUM1 ([Blood 2007;109:3076](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17138820)):
  + Elderly patients
  + M > F
* EBV positive FL ([Am J Hematol 2019;94:E62](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30474261), [Mod Pathol 2017;30:519](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27982024)):
  + Uncommon, prevalence of ~2.5% in an unselected cohort of FL
  + No association with prior history of immunosuppression, gender or age
* Floral variant of FL:
  + First described by Osborne and Butler ([Am J Clin Pathol 1987;88:264](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3307374))
  + In a study by Kojima et al., 13 cases were reported (1992 - April 2006) ([APMIS 2006;114:626](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16948815))

Clinical features

* Similar to those of [usual type FL](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomafollicularnodalusual.html)

Diagnosis

* Constitutional symptoms: fatigue, fever or night sweats, weight loss or recurrent infections
* Diagnostic imaging: enlarged lymph nodes, nodules / lesions / infiltration in extranodal sites
* Tissue biopsy / excision (lymph node / extranodal sites)
* Laboratory tests: peripheral blood exam / bone marrow biopsy (especially in staging) / lumbar puncture
* Reference: [Am J Hematol 2018;93:296](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29314206)

Prognostic factors

* FL negative for CD10 and expressing MUM1 ([Blood 2007;109:3076](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17138820)):
  + Relatively poor prognosis
* EBV positive FL ([Am J Hematol 2019;94:E62](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30474261)):
  + Inferior overall survival when adjusted for age or sex

Case reports

* 32 year old man presented with tonsillar hypertrophy in laryngoscopy and underwent a low temperature plasma tonsillectomy ([Diagn Pathol 2019;14:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31253137))
* 74 year old woman presented with a 4 year history of squamous cell carcinoma of the skin and regional lymphadenopathy; biopsy of the postauricular lymph node was performed ([Virchows Arch A Pathol Anat Histopathol 1985;405:473](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3920819))
* 77 year old woman presented with left foot swelling attributed to an insect bite; Doppler ultrasound was negative for deep venous thrombosis but the study revealed left inguinal lymphadenopathy ([Hum Pathol 2017;68:136](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28873356))

Treatment

* More studies are needed to evaluate the response to the standard therapy for FL

Microscopic (histologic) description

* FL with Castleman-like changes ([Hum Pathol 2017;68:136](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28873356)):
  + Small and atrophic neoplastic follicles
  + Atretic and hyalinized germinal centers
  + Concentric mantle zones
  + Increased interfollicular vascularity
  + Penetrating hyalinized blood vessels
* FL with plasmacytic differentiation and IgG4 positive plasma cells ([Mod Pathol 2010;23:71](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19838161)):
  + FL with associated increased and monotypic plasma cells
  + B cells and plasma cells show the same light chain expression, thus supporting a common clonal relationship
    - 2 distinct types:
      * Predominantly interfollicular distribution of plasma cells, these cases show *BCL2* rearrangements and are considered to be FL with plasmacytic differentiation
      * Predominantly intrafollicular plasma cell distribution; these cases tend not to show *BCL2* rearrangements and some evidence supports that these cases may not represent neoplastic FL with plasmacytic differentiation but show biologic overlap with marginal zone lymphomas
      * Can lack CD10 expression but retain expression of BCL6 and other germinal center markers such as LMO2, HGAL, etc.
* FL with marginal zone differentiation involving MALT sites:
  + Marginal zone differentiation variable, characterized by a perifollicular band of monocytoid B cells rimming the neoplastic follicles
  + Monocytoid B cells are represented by:
    - Round to slightly irregular nuclei
    - Moderate amount of clear cytoplasm
    - Pale appearance at low magnification
  + Lymphoepithelial lesions are prominent
  + Plasmacytic differentiation with monotypic interfollicular plasma cells
* FL negative for CD10, positive for MUM1 and with *BCL6* abnormalities ([Blood 2007;109:3076](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17138820)):
  + More frequent diffuse proliferation compared with conventional FL
  + More frequent grade 3B in follicular foci
  + More frequently shows component of diffuse large B cell lymphoma (DLBCL)
  + Usually lacks *BCL2* rearrangement
  + Frequently expresses MUM1 and cytoplasmic light chain
  + More frequent *BCL6* abnormalities (translocation, amplification)
* EBV positive FL:
  + Histologically and immunophenotypically indistinguishable from nodal FL
* Floral variant of FL:
  + Neoplastic follicles are irregular in shape
  + Expanded, prominent mantle zone lymphocytes surround follicles
  + Mantle zone lymphocytes penetrate neoplastic follicles

Cytology description

* Variable mixture of centrocytes and centroblasts
  + Centrocytes
    - Small to large
    - Angulated nuclei
    - Dense chromatin
    - Scant cytoplasm
  + Centroblasts
    - Large cells with oval nuclei
    - Vesicular chromatin
    - 1 - 3 nucleoli
    - Moderate cytoplasm
    - > 3 times the size of lymphocytes
    - Differentiate from follicular dendritic cells
      * Large round nuclei
      * Dispersed, nearly clear chromatin
      * Single eosinophilic nucleolus
      * Indistinct cytoplasm
* Absent / rare tingible body macrophages
* Reference: [Jaffe: Hematopathology, 2nd Edition, 2016](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0323296130/pathologyoutl-20)

Positive stains

* FL with Castleman-like changes:
  + B cell markers ([CD19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd19.html), [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html), [CD79a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd79a.html), [PAX5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax5.html))
  + [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html), [BCL6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl6.html) and [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html)
  + Follicular dendritic cells (FDCs) express typical FDC markers ([CD21](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd21.html), [CD23](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd23.html) and [CD35](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30to39.html#cd35))
    - FDC may be strongly positive for [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html); neoplastic lymphocytes may be weak for [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html) ([Hum Pathol 2017;68:136](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28873356))
* FL with plasmacytic differentiation and IgG4 positive plasma cells:
  + Immunophenotype similar to that of nodal FL
  + Plasmacytic differentiation: cytoplasmic light chain restriction among interfollicular neoplastic plasma cells
  + [IgG4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsigg4.html) positive plasma cells may be increased: > 100/high power field and IgG4:IgG ratio > 40%
* FL with marginal zone differentiation involving MALT sites:
  + B cell markers ([CD19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd19.html), [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html), [CD79a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd79a.html), [PAX5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax5.html))
  + [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html)
* FL negative for CD10 and expressing MUM1 ([Blood 2007;109:3076](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17138820)):
  + B cell markers
  + [MUM1 / IRF4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmum1.html)
  + [BCL6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl6.html) (54% of cases)
  + [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html) (variable)
  + [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html) (59% of cases)
    - Less frequent [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html) protein expression than conventional FL (~94% of cases)
  + [CD21](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd21.html), [CD23](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd23.html) and [CD35](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30to39.html#cd35) highlight the follicular dendritic cell meshwork in all [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html)- [MUM1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmum1.html)+ FL in follicular foci
* EBV positive FL ([Am J Hematol 2019;94:E62](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30474261)):
  + Immunophenotype similar to the conventional type of FL
  + [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html) (subset)
  + [LMP1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) (75 - 90% of cases)
  + [EBER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) (~100% of cases)
  + Most common latency pattern of EBV infection: type II (~92% of cases), followed by type I latency (~8% of cases)
* Floral variant of FL:
  + Immunophenotype similar to the conventional type of FL
  + B cell markers ([CD19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd19.html), [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html), [CD79a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd79a.html), [PAX5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax5.html))
  + [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html)
  + [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html)
  + [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html): in some cases ([Am J Surg Pathol 1994;18:37](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8279627))
  + Disrupted follicular dendritic cell network ([CD21](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd21.html), [CD23](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd23.html) or [CD35](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30to39.html#cd35))
  + [IgM / IgD](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsIgA.html): in the mantle zone

Negative stains

* FL with Castleman-like changes:

* FL negative for CD10 and expressing MUM1 ([Blood 2007;109:3076](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17138820)):
  + [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html)
* Floral variant of FL:
  + [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html), [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html), [CD43](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd43.html)
  + [Cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html)
  + [EBER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* FL with Castleman-like changes:
  + Positive for t(14;18)(q32;q21) or *BCL2* rearrangements ([Pathology 2017;49:544](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28689633))
* FL with plasmacytic differentiation and IgG4 positive plasma cells ([Mod Pathol 2010;23:71](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19838161)):
  + *BCL2* rearrangements
    - Present in monotypic plasma cells with an interfollicular distribution
    - Absent in monotypic plasma cells with prominent intrafollicular distribution
* FL with marginal zone differentiation involving MALT sites:
  + *BCL2* rearrangements seen in both the follicular and marginal zone components ([Mod Pathol 2001;14:191](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11266525))
    - Pitfall: FL involving MALT sites show *IGH-BCL2* rearrangements less frequently than typical (nodal) FL ([J Clin Pathol 2008;61:377](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17601964), [Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18830125))
* FL negative for CD10 and expressing MUM1 ([Blood 2007;109:3076](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17138820)):
  + Frequently lacks *IGH-BCL2* translocation
  + *BCL6* gene abnormalities (translocation or amplification) in the majority of cases
  + *BCL2* gene amplification (in ~50% of cases)
* EBV positive FL:
  + t(14;18)(q32;q21) translocation in ~85% of cases ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:519](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27982024))
* Floral variant of FL:
  + t(14;18)(q32;q21) translocation

Sample pathology report

* Lymph node, cervical, excision:
  + Follicular lymphoma, low grade, with Castleman-like changes (see comment)
  + Comment: Sections of the lymph node show a follicular lymphocytic proliferation, with a dense, atypical lymphocytic infiltrate. In most lymph node areas, sections reveal the presence of lymphoid follicles containing predominantly small, slightly angulated lymphocytes having irregular nuclei. In many of the follicles, the central areas of the follicle are nearly fully occupied by histiocytic / dendritic cells, with surrounding mantle zones prominent, giving an appearance of atretic follicles and imparting a distinctly Castleman-like appearance. The interfollicular regions of the lymph node show scattered plasma cells, in which a variable degree of plasma cell enlargement is present. Eosinophilic staining collagenous material is present around blood vessels and extensively in the lymph node capsule.
  + Immunohistochemical studies show positive staining for CD20, PAX5, BCL2 and BCL6 and are negative for CD3, CD5, cyclin D1 and HHV8. Cyclin D1 highlights endothelial cells, as well as histiocytic / dendritic cells. CD35 highlights numerous follicular dendritic cell meshworks in the areas of follicle formation and highlights the sclerotic centers. Few CD138 positive plasma cells are identified, which are polytypic by kappa and lambda immunohistochemical and in situ hybridization study. Congo red stain is negative for birefringent material.
  + By in situ hybridization analysis for Epstein-Barr virus encoded RNA (EBER), the neoplastic cells are negative for EBER.
  + Fluorescence in situ hybridization (FISH) reports *IGH-BCL2* fusion (25% nuclei), *BCL6* rearrangement (30% nuclei) and no evidence of *MYC* rearrangement.

Differential diagnosis

* **FL with Castleman-like changes** ([Hum Pathol 2017;68:136](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28873356), [Mod Pathol 2018;31:429](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29192651)):
  + Overlapping features with [Castleman disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodescastleman.html); however, features favoring FL include:
    - Increase in the number of follicles
    - Atypical follicles containing increased centrocytes
    - Presence of [BCL6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl6.html) positive lymphoid cells in atretic follicles
    - [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html) positive, even if weak
    - Presence of t(14;18)(q32;q21)
* **FL with plasmacytic differentiation and IgG4 positive plasma cells**:
  + Overlapping features with [marginal zone lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomamzlgeneral.html)
  + Immunophenotype in neoplastic cells is similar to that of [nodal FL](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomafollicularnodalusual.html)
  + Distinction of these entities typically requires extensive immunophenotyping and FISH studies to assess for *IGH-BCL2* translocation
* **FL with marginal zone differentiation involving MALT sites**:
  + Overlapping features with [marginal zone lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomamzlgeneral.html); features favoring FL include:
    - Expression of one of the germinal center markers ([CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html), [BCL6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl6.html), [LMO2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainslmo2.html)) in the neoplastic cells, few cases may show down expression of [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html); however, molecular signature similar to [nodal FL](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomafollicularnodalusual.html)
  + **t(14;18) negative follicular lymphoma**:
    - Shows aberrations more similar to [nodal marginal zone lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanodalMZL.html)
* **Floral variant of FL**:
  + [Progressive transformation of germinal centers (PTGC)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodesPTGC.html) ([APMIS 2006;114:626](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16948815)):
    - Absence of reactive germinal centers in FL
    - Presence of extranodal extension of germinal center in FL
    - Numerous mitotic figures in germinal centers in PTGC
    - [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html) positive cells in germinal centers in FL
    - [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html)+ cells in neoplastic germinal centers and interfollicular area in FL (floral variant)
  + **Marginal zone lymphoma (MZL) with follicular colonization** ([Leuk Lymphoma 2021;62:1116](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33283568)):
    - In FL, floral variant:
