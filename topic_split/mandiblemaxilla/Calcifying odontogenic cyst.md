Definition / general

* Benign cyst of odontogenic origin, characterized by an ameloblastoma-like epithelial lining containing ghost cells that may calcify
* Originally described by Gorlin and colleagues in 1962 as a possible oral analogue to pilomatrixoma of skin, owing to the presence of ghost cell keratinization in both lesions

Essential features

* Calcifying odontogenic cysts are located most commonly in the anterior regions of jaws
* Microscopically calcifying odontogenic cysts contain an ameloblastoma-like epithelial lining containing ghost cells that may calcify
* Calcifying odontogenic cysts are associated with β catenin (*CTNNB1*) mutations

Terminology

* Calcifying odontogenic cyst (COC) is the preferred terminology in the 2017 WHO classification
* Nomenclature has been continuously changing, due to debate as to whether COC is a neoplasm or a developmental cyst
  + In 1992, WHO classified this lesion as an odontogenic tumor but continued to use the term calcifying odontogenic cyst
  + In 2005, WHO redesignated the lesion as calcifying cystic odontogenic tumor (CCOT)
  + In 2017, the term calcifying odontogenic cyst was reapplied to this lesion and it was reclassified as a benign odontogenic cyst
* Other archaic names include Gorlin cyst and keratinizing and calcifying odontogenic cyst
* Given the diversity of the histopathologic features seen in COC and its tendency to coexist with other odontogenic lesions, complex classification systems have been proposed ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:372](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12604894), [J Oral Pathol Med 2008;37:302](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18221328), [Reichart: Odontogenic Tumors and Allied Lesions, Illustrated Edition, 2004](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1850970599/pathologyoutl-20))
* COC belongs to odontogenic ghost cell family of lesions ([J Oral Pathol Med 2008;37:302](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18221328))
  + From a practical standpoint, a spectrum of histopathologic patterns exists, ranging from a benign lesion that is primarily cystic, a benign lesion with a solid pattern of growth to a rare tumor with features of carcinoma
    - Solid lesions are designated [dentinogenic ghost cell tumors](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mandiblemaxilladentinogenicghost.html) in the 2017 WHO classification
    - Malignant counterpart is [ghost cell odontogenic carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mandiblemaxillaghostcellodontogeniccarcinoma.html)

Epidemiology

* 0.3% of odontogenic cysts ([J Oral Pathol Med 2006;35:500](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16918602))
* Mean age: 31 years (standard deviation: 21 years) range 5 - 92 years; however, this varies geographically ([J Oral Pathol Med 2018;47:721](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29738629))
* No consistent gender predominance
* Around 33% of cases are associated with odontogenic lesions, most commonly odontoma; odontoma associated COC occurs in younger patients on average (20.4 years) ([J Oral Pathol Med 2018;47:721](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29738629))

Sites

* Vast majority of lesions are central COC (within bone of jaw, intraosseous) ([Reichart: Odontogenic Tumors and Allied Lesions, Illustrated Edition, 2004](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1850970599/pathologyoutl-20))
* Less commonly, peripheral lesions may be seen (located on gingival tissues, extraosseous) most of which can be classified as [dentinogenic ghost cell tumors](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mandiblemaxilladentinogenicghost.html)
* Usually anterior regions of jaws, incisor / cuspid region
* Lesions associated with odontoma are most commonly found in the anterior maxilla ([J Oral Pathol Med 2008;37:302](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18221328))

Pathophysiology

* Cystic neoplasms with β catenin (*CTNNB1*) mutations in the Wnt signaling pathway
  + In one series, 91% (10/11) cases of CCOT / COC demonstrated *CTNNB1* point mutations COC ([PLoS One 2017;12:e0180224](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28658279))
  + Immunohistochemistry for LEF1 (a transcription factor of the Wnt pathway) is reported to be positive in 64% (7/11) of CCOT ([Hum Pathol 2015;46:255](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25497834))

Clinical features

* Either asymptomatic (incidental radiographic finding) or a painless swelling
* Extraosseous lesions present as a gingival swelling, which may be painful
* May be associated with other odontogenic pathology, most commonly odontoma ([J Oral Pathol Med 2018;47:721](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29738629))

Diagnosis

* Diagnosis can be made on an incisional biopsy or enucleation specimen

Radiology description

* Usually unilocular, well defined radiolucency with focal opacification but may rarely be multilocular ([Br J Radiol 2012;85:548](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21828147))
* Scattered radiopacities in 33 - 50%
* 33% associated with impacted tooth
* Usually 2 - 4 cm
* Adjacent tooth root displacement, resorption or root divergence may occur ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol Oral Radiol Endod 2006;101:356](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16504870))

Prognostic factors

* Prognosis excellent, few recurrences (< 5% documented)
* Rarely, malignant transformation has been reported ([Case Rep Pathol 2013;2013:853095](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23956907), [Korean J Pathol 2012;46:478](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23136575), [Ann Diagn Pathol 2009;13:394](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19917476))

Case reports

* 15 year old Japanese boy with pigmented calcifying cystic odontogenic tumor associated with compound odontoma
  ([Head Face Med 2007;3:35](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17892598))
* 23 year old man with ameloblastic fibro-odontoma arising from a calcifying odontogenic cyst
  ([Bull Tokyo Dent Coll 2001;42:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11484795))
* 24 year old woman with calcifying odontogenic cyst associated with an orthokeratinized odontogenic cyst ([Head Neck Pathol 2008;2:324](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20614303))
* 36 year old man with a 5.9 cm calcifying odontogenic cyst treated with 2 stage decompression ([J Oral Maxillofac Surg 2017;75:1915](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28390759))
* Hybrid odontogenic tumor with features of ameloblastic fibro-odontoma, calcifying odontogenic cyst and adenomatoid odontogenic tumor ([J Oral Maxillofac Surg 2010;68:470](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20116726))

Treatment

* Treated by enucleation and curettage
* For combined lesions, treat according to characteristics of more aggressive lesion
* Large cysts may be decompressed prior to surgical management in a 2 stage approach ([J Oral Maxillofac Surg 2017;75:1915](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28390759))

Gross description

* Unicystic, often 2 cm or less; no solid areas
* Grossly cystic with focal luminal thickenings

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Cyst lining is of odontogenic epithelium with a well defined layer of palisading basal cells, loosely arranged suprabasal epithelial cells resembling stellate reticulum, similar to ameloblastoma ([microscopic image #1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mandiblemaxillacalcifyingodontogenic.html#mandiblemaxillacalcifyingodontogenicmicro))
  + Unlike ameloblastoma, variable numbers of cells undergo ghost cell change in suprabasilar epithelium ([microscopic image #1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mandiblemaxillacalcifyingodontogenic.html#mandiblemaxillacalcifyingodontogenicmicro))
  + Pale, eosinophilic ghost cells are altered epithelial cells with preservation of basic cell outline and eosinophilic cytoplasm but loss of the nucleus; ghost cell change may be due to coagulative necrosis, accumulation of enamel protein, aberrant keratinization of odontogenic epithelium and these cells may calcify ([microscopic images #2 - 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mandiblemaxillacalcifyingodontogenic.html#mandiblemaxillacalcifyingodontogenicmicro))
  + Other variable findings may include:
    - Foreign body giant cell reaction
    - Proliferation of odontogenic epithelium into the cyst wall which can resemble strands of dental lamina ([microscopic image #4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mandiblemaxillacalcifyingodontogenic.html#mandiblemaxillacalcifyingodontogenicmicro))
    - Dystrophic calcifications
    - Dentinoid may be laid down next to basal cells:
      * Paucicellular, eosinophilic calcified material considered to represent dysplastic dentin ([microscopic image #4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mandiblemaxillacalcifyingodontogenic.html#mandiblemaxillacalcifyingodontogenicmicro))
      * May be present adjacent to epithelial component
      * Most likely formed due to an inductive effect of odontogenic epithelium on adjacent mesenchymal tissue
* Cyst wall consists of mature fibrous connective tissue containing scattered inflammatory cells (unless secondarily infected)
* Reference: [Reichart: Odontogenic Tumors and Allied Lesions, Illustrated Edition, 2004](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1850970599/pathologyoutl-20)

Negative stains

* Nothing useful or significant

Electron microscopy description

* Basal and suprabasilar cells contain tonofilaments and organelles
* Ghost cells contain coarse bundles of tonofilaments intermingled with dilated membranous organelles ([Med Electron Microsc 2002;35:109](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12181653), [Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1975;39:769](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1056580))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Aberrations of Wnt signaling pathway with β catenin overexpression ([APMIS 2008;116:206](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18377586))
* Mutations in *CTNNB1* but not *BRAF* ([PLoS One 2017;12:e0180224](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28658279))

Sample pathology report

* Anterior maxilla, right, excisional biopsy:
  + Calcifying odontogenic cyst (calcifying cystic odontogenic tumor) 1.2 cm

Differential diagnosis

* [Ameloblastoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mandiblemaxillaameloblastoma.html) **(conventional or unicystic)**:
  + Usually lacks ghost cells and calcifications
* [Dentinogenic ghost cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mandiblemaxilladentinogenicghost.html) / [carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mandiblemaxillaghostcellodontogeniccarcinoma.html)
  + Solid counterpart of COC
* [Ghost cell odontogenic carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mandiblemaxillaghostcellodontogeniccarcinoma.html):
  + Infiltrative
  + Exhibits mitotic activity, nuclear atypia or necrosis
* [Odontoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mandiblemaxillaodontoma.html):
  + Lacks the ameloblastic epithelium of COC
* [Ameloblastic fibro-odontoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mandiblemaxillaodontoma.html):
  + Exhibits primitive mesenchymal stroma
