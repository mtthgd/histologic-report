Definition / general

* Malignant biphasic mesenchymal neoplasm with a well differentiated hyaline cartilage component
* Most cases have *HEY-NCOA2* fusions; however *IRF2BP2-CDX1* fusion has also been described ([PLoS One 2012;7:e49705](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23185413))

Essential features

* Malignant biphasic neoplasm that most commonly involves soft tissue, bone and intracranial sites, with tendency for late local recurrences and distant metastases

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9240/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/%0D%0A9789241548496_eng.pdf#page=88) - mesenchymal chondrosarcoma
* ICD-10:
  + [C40](http://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C40-C41/C40-/C40) - malignant neoplasm of bone and articular cartilage of limbs
  + [C41](http://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C40-C41/C41-/C41) - malignant neoplasm of bone and articular cartilage of other and unspecified sites
* ICD-11: [2B50.Z & XH8X47](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f1625755389%2fmms%2funspecified?data=%7B%22dataType%22%3A%22pc%22%2C%22postcoordinationCodeSet%22%3A%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F1625755389%2Fmms%2Funspecified%22%2C%22axisToValueIds%22%3A%7B%22specificAnatomy%22%3A%5B%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F572669867%22%7D%5D%7D%7D%7D) - chondrosarcoma of bone and articular cartilage of unspecified sites and mesenchymal chondrosarcoma

Epidemiology

* Usually adolescents and young adults
* Slight female predominance

Sites

* Most commonly bone, soft tissue (head and neck, thigh), intracranial sites (meninges) and visceral organs
* Bone lesion: up to 50% in jaw ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2012;136:61](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22208489))

Pathophysiology

* Gene fusion driven

Etiology

* *HEY1-NCOA2* fusion evokes many different mechanisms, including direct DNA binding, protein - protein interaction and epigenetic modification
* Combination of these pathway dysregulations (including Notch signaling pathway, chromatin remodeling, apoptosis and transforming growth factor beta [TGFβ] signaling) results in a chimeric fusion protein that drives the biology of mesenchymal chondrosarcoma ([Curr Oncol Rep 2018;20:37](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29582189))

Clinical features

* Clinical symptoms depend on the tumor location and can last for years prior to diagnosis
* Enlarging, usually painful mass
* Neurologic symptoms If arising in spine / cranium ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2012;136:61](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22208489))

Diagnosis

* Diagnostic diagram includes radiology, histology (biopsy, resection specimen) with immunohistochemistry and molecular analysis

Radiology description

* CT scan:
  + Lobulated, destructive lytic lesion with granular, ring and arc or irregularly shaped calcifications ([Radiology 1993;186:819](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8430193), [Skeletal Radiol 2005;34:785](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16211384))
  + Bone lesion: usually erodes / destroys the cortex and involves the surrounding soft tissue
* MRI:
  + Lobulated, heterogeneous lesion with low signal intensity on T1 weighted images and high signal intensity on T2 ([Skeletal Radiol 2005;34:785](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16211384))

Prognostic factors

* In one study, 5, 10 and 20 year overall survival was 55.0%, 43.5% and 15.7%, respectively ([PLoS One 2015;10:e0122216](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25849226))
* With chemotherapy: 5 year and 10 year overall survival is 84% and 80%, respectively
* Without chemotherapy, under surveillance: 5 year and 10 year overall survival is 73% and 46%, respectively ([Eur J Cancer 2015;51:374](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25529371))

Case reports

* 30 year old man with dizziness, headache and imbalance ([Neurol India 2012;60:121](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22406804))
* 31 year old man with vascular lesion near knee ([Case #335](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case335.htm))
* 34 year old man with expansile, erosive tumor of mandible ([J Cancer Res Ther 2011;7:192](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21768711))
* 52 year old man with progressive proptosis of eye ([Case Rep Med 2012;2012:292147](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22761622))
* 64 year old man with renal mass ([Diagn Pathol 2012;7:125](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22999069))
* 64 year old woman with renal mass ([Oncol Lett 2020;19:885](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31897203))

Treatment

* Wide local excision with negative margins
* If tumor is not resected in toto, chemotherapy can be used (however, better survival is published in only one study) ([Cancer 2008;112:2424](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18438777), [PLoS One 2015;10:e0122216](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25849226))
* Radiotherapy

Gross description

* Lobulated, solid, firm, gray-tan, fleshy mass with scattered gritty white calcifications
* Bone lesions arise in the medullary cavity or on the bone surface, causing cortical destruction and extension into the soft tissue ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2012;136:61](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22208489))

Frozen section description

* Tumor composed of islands of well differentiated hyaline cartilage and primitive mesenchymal cells / undifferentiated small blue cells

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Solid sheets of undifferentiated small blue cells with a hemangiopericytoma-like vascular pattern mixed with islands of mature appearing, well differentiated hyaline cartilage
* Usually mitotic activity or necrosis present ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2018;142:1421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30407856), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2012;136:61](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22208489))

Cytology description

* Highly cellular smears composed of small round blue cells with high nuclear to cytoplasmic ratio infiltrating the fibrillary matrix ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2018;142:1421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30407856))

Positive stains

* Cartilaginous nodules: [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)
* Small round cell component:
  + [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkersCD99.html) (usually membranous), [NKX2.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsNKX2.html) (in up to 75%, [Mod Pathol 2016;29:370](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26847175))
  + [NKX3.1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnkx3.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2020;44:719](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31972596)) although in one study, negative in all cases ([Histopathology 2021;78:334](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32779239))
* [SOX9](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox9.html) (both components) ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2018;142:1421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30407856))
* Occasionally myogenic markers, such as [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* In most cases, recurrent *HEY1-NCOA2* gene fusion ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2012;51:127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22034177))
* In one case, *IRF2BP2-CDX1* fusion described ([PLoS One 2012;7:e49705](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23185413))

Sample pathology report

* Right thigh, excision:
  + Extraskeletal mesenchymal chondrosarcoma (see comment)
  + Comment: Tumor is composed of islands of well differentiated hyaline cartilage and primitive mesenchymal cells. Immunohistochemically, the tumor cells are positive for S100, focally positive for CD99 and SOX9 and are negative for CD20 and keratin. The morphology and immunoprofile strongly support the diagnosis of mesenchymal chondrosarcoma.

Differential diagnosis

* **Other small round cell neoplasms**:
  + Rule out metastatic [small cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorsmallcell.html) and [melanoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticmelanoma.html), especially when only the undifferentiated component is present in the biopsy
* [Ewing sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneewing.html):
  + Tumor composed of monotonous round blue cells
  + No hemangiopericytoma-like vascular pattern or cartilaginous component
  + [NKX2.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsNKX2.html)+, [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html) diffuse membranous staining
  + Harbors *EWSR1* negative and *FUS* rearrangements
* **Lymphoma**:
  + Lack of cartilaginous component; lymphoid antibodies positive
* [Rhabdomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueembryonalrhabdo.html):
  + Positive skeletal muscle markers (such as [myogenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html) and [MyoD1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyod1.html))
* [Synovial sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesynovialsarc.html):
  + No cartilaginous component
  + [Keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)+, [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)+, [TLE1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstle1.html)+, **SSX**+, **SS18-SSX**+
  + Harbors *SSX* rearrangements
* [Malignant solitary fibrous tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesft.html):
  + No cartilaginous component, hemangiopericytoma-like vascular pattern present
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)+, [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html)+
  + Harbors *NAB2-STAT6* fusion

Board review style question #1

Which of the following is true about mesenchymal chondrosarcoma?

1. Cut surface is white with myxoid areas
2. Cytology smears are paucicellular
3. On imaging, calcifications are rare
4. Tumor cells are typically negative for S100
5. Tumor is composed of undifferentiated small blue cells mixed with islands of mature appearing, well differentiated hyaline cartilage

Board review style answer #1

**E**. Tumor is composed of undifferentiated small blue cells mixed with islands of mature appearing, well differentiated hyaline cartilage
