Definition / general

* A benign developmental odontogenic cyst, mostly unilocular, with a fibrous tissue wall lined predominantly or entirely by orthokeratinized stratified squamous epithelium

Essential features

* Cystic bone lesion in mandible or maxilla with compatible radiological features
* Cyst has orthokeratinized stratified squamous epithelial lining
* Cyst wall is fibrous and uninflamed

Terminology

* Previously called orthokeratinized variant of odontogenic keratocyst (OKC) (no longer recommended to use by WHO)

Epidemiology

* Comprised 10.5% of cases in series of odontogenic keratocysts ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2010;134:271](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20121617))
* Comprises probably 1% of all odontogenic cysts
* Peak incidence during third and fourth decades
* More common in males (3:1) ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2010;134:271](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20121617))

Sites

* Most commonly (90.16%) involves mandible (mandibular molar and ramus) ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2010;134:271](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20121617))
* Maxilla involvement in 9.84% of cases
* Multiple and bilateral cases have been reported

Pathophysiology

* Thought to originate from dental lamina and its remnants; developmental in nature

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Most present as painless asymptomatic swelling ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2010;134:271](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20121617))
* Others discovered incidentally in orthodontic radiographs

Diagnosis

* Requires correlation of orthopantomogram (OPG) findings with histological features

Radiology description

* Orthopantomogram
  + Well demarcated unilocular radiolucency (93%) with a corticated margin ([Dentomaxillofac Radiol 2010;39:455](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21062939))
  + Associated with unerupted tooth in 50 - 75% of cases ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2010;134:271](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20121617), [Dentomaxillofac Radiol 2010;39:455](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21062939))

Prognostic factors

* Overall prognosis is good
* Recurrence seen in 2% ([Dentomaxillofac Radiol 2010;39:455](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21062939), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2010;134:271](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20121617))

Case reports

* 18 year old woman with posterior mandible calcification ([J Oral Maxillofac Pathol 2018;22:S20](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29491599))
* 20 and 23 year old men with multiple tumors of mandible and maxilla ([Head Neck Pathol 2020;14:381](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31119532))
* 41 year old man with tumor in maxilla ([Turk Patoloji Derg 2017;33:81](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25110243))
* 41 year old man with tumor of condylar head ([Rom J Morphol Embryol 2017;58:689](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28730263))
* 50 year old man with tumor of maxilla masquerading as dentigerous cyst ([Int J Appl Basic Med Res 2016;6:297](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27857903))
* 52 year old man with progression into squamous cell carcinoma of left mandible ([World J Clin Cases 2019;7:1686](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31367628))

Treatment

* Enucleation

Gross description

* If an intact cyst is enucleated:
  + Keratinous material filled cystic lesion
  + Usually unilocular but may be multilocular
  + Fibrous cyst membrane may envelope crown of molar tooth ([Int J Appl Basic Med Res 2016;6:297](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27857903))
* In curated specimens:
  + Multiple flattened, irregular, soft to firm and pearl white to gray-white to tan-brown cystic tissue fragments

Frozen section description

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Noninflamed fibrous cyst wall lined by thin uniform stratified squamous epithelium
* Prominent granular layer
* Thick luminal lamellated keratin (onion skin-like)
* Chevroning of lamellated keratin in some cases
* Cuboidal to flat basal layer
* No surface corrugation or basal palisading is seen
* Focal parakeratinized epithelium in case of inflammation
* Reference: [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2010;134:271](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20121617)

Positive stains

* Immunohistochemical stains are not usually used for diagnostic purpose
* Decreased [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) index and [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) staining when compared with odontogenic keratocyst ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2010;134:271](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20121617))
* Reduced expression of [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html), **bax**, [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) and **TGFα** as compared with odontogenic keratocyst ([Oral Oncol 2009;45:e41](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19362040), [Dent Res J (Isfahan) 2012;9:S39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23814560))
* [Cytokeratin 10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck10.html) positivity with variable expression of [cytokeratin 13](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck1.html#cytokeratin13ck13271412) and [14](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck14.html) ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol Oral Radiol Endod 2002;94:732](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12464899))
* Expression of [cytokeratin 1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck1.html), [2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck1.html#ck2), [10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck10.html) and **loricrin**
  + This profile suggests differentiation toward normal epidermis ([Hum Pathol 2010;41:1718](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20801488))

Sample pathology report

* Right posterior mandible, enucleation:
  + Orthokeratinized odontogenic cyst (see comment)
  + Comment: Histologic and radiologic features support the diagnosis.

Differential diagnosis

* [Odontogenic keratocyst (OKC)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mandiblemaxillakeratocyst.html):
  + Peak incidence in second and third decades
  + Mostly solitary, unilocular, well demarcated radiolucency
  + Multiple cysts in 10% of cases
  + Much more common
  + Associated with nevoid basal cell carcinoma syndrome
  + Associated with aggressive behavior (28 - 30% recurrence)
  + Thin uniform lining of parakeratinized squamous epithelium
  + Corrugated layer of parakeratin on its luminal surface
  + Palisaded layer of columnar basal cells (reverse polarity)
  + No keratohyalin granules
  + Lumen may contain keratinaceous debris
  + Frequent separation of the epithelial lining from underlying connective tissue
  + Dental lamina rests and microcysts occasionally present in capsule wall
* [Dentigerous cyst (DC)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mandiblemaxilladentigerous.html):
  + Radiologically similar as both are associated with unerupted molar tooth of mandible
  + Much more common
  + Peak incidence in second to fourth decades of life
  + Noninflamed lining is thin; 2 - 3 layers of regular nonkeratinizing stratified squamous epithelium
  + Inflamed lining is hyperplastic with elongated rete ridges
  + Mucus metaplasia and cilia are also seen
