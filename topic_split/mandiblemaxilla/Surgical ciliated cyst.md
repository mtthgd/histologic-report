Definition / general

* Benign cyst caused by the implantation of respiratory epithelium in gnathic bones
* Implantation is most commonly related to prior surgery in the region (sinonasal and maxilla / mandible)

Essential features

* Prior surgical procedure or trauma
* Cystic epithelial lining, most commonly respiratory epithelium with cilia
* Radiographically radiolucent (lytic)

Terminology

* Postoperative maxillary cyst (if maxillary location)
* Postoperative paranasal cyst (if paranasal location)
* Implantation (respiratory) cyst
* Postsurgical ciliated cyst ([Case Rep Dent 2024;2024:5584515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38798911))

ICD coding

* ICD-11: [DA05.Y](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#203165129%2Fother) - other specified cysts of oral or facial - neck region

Epidemiology

* Rare cyst ([Oral Maxillofac Surg 2019;23:281](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31093791))
* No definitive sex predilection
* Most common age range is the fifth to sixth decades of life ([Case Rep Dent 2024;2024:5584515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38798911))
* Latency period between the traumatic event and diagnosis ranges between 5 and 22 years

Sites

* Most common at posterior maxilla ([J Oral Surg 1981;39:191](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6936541), [J Oral Maxillofac Surg 2003;61:138](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12524623))
* Mandibular location rarely reported ([Oral Dis 2024;30:2858](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37884395))

Pathophysiology

* Implanted epithelium may form a cyst related to the persistent mucous secretion of respiratory epithelium with subsequent accumulation, similar to a mucous retention cyst of the sinus ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1986;62:544](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3466125))

Etiology

* Cysts develop in the jaw bone from implanted or entrapped sinonasal mucosa
* Factors reported to precede the development of surgical ciliated cyst include Caldwell-Luc procedure, LeFort 1 procedures, sinus surgery, maxillary fracture, traumatic maxillary tooth extraction ([J Oral Maxillofac Surg 2002;60:324](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11887151), [Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol Oral Radiol Endod 2005;100:36](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15953915), [J Oral Maxillofac Surg 2014;72:1736](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25109584))

Clinical features

* Most commonly presents with symptoms (pain, swelling vestibule or palate, discomfort in maxilla or adjacent teeth) but occasionally found incidentally ([Oral Dis 2024;30:2858](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37884395), [Int J Oral Maxillofac Surg 1988;17:282](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3143772), [Case Rep Dent 2016;2016:6263248](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27478654))

Diagnosis

* Histomorphology alone not entirely specific
* Final diagnosis of surgical ciliated cyst can be confidently rendered with a cyst exhibiting respiratory epithelium and a documented history of surgery in the area, along with supportive radiologic findings (radiolucent, well demarcated cyst)

Radiology description

* Well demarcated radiolucency, commonly unilocular with a sclerotic, corticated border
* Rarely, large lesions in the maxilla may appear multilocular ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1986;62:544](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3466125), [J Oral Surg 1981;39:191](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6936541), [Oral Dis 2024;30:2858](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37884395))

Prognostic factors

* Good prognosis
* Recurrence after treatment ranges between 6 and 20% ([J Oral Maxillofac Surg 1982;40:487](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6953207), [J Oral Maxillofac Surg 2014;72:1736](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25109584), [Yonsei Med J 2010;51:735](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20635449))

Case reports

* 40 year old man with a history of maxillary surgery later presented with pain and limited mouth opening ([Diagnostics (Basel) 2023;13:1825](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37296676))
* 41 year old woman with swelling and pain after LeFort 1 surgery ([Exp Ther Med 2023;26:343](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37383375))
* 42 year old man with a remote history of orthognathic surgery and genioplasty ([Maxillofac Plast Reconstr Surg 2022;44:26](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35909205))

Treatment

* Most commonly involves surgical enucleation and curettage ([Oral Dis 2024;30:2858](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37884395), [Bull Natl Res Cent 2022;46:235](https://doi.org/10.1186/s42269-022-00925-7))

Gross description

* Cysts, often received fragmented
* Mucus or mucinous material may be present

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Lined by pseudostratified ciliated columnar (respiratory type) epithelium
* Areas of squamous metaplasia, atrophic squamous (squamoid) or simple cuboidal cells may be seen ([Int J Oral Maxillofac Surg 1987;16:682](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3125266))
* Mucous (goblet) cells are not uncommon
* Cyst wall is composed of loose fibrous connective tissue, which may be inflamed
* Subepithelial fibrosis or hyalinization of cyst wall is common ([Int J Oral Maxillofac Surg 1988;17:282](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3143772), [Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1986;62:544](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3466125))
* Reactive bone formation may be present

Sample pathology report

* Right posterior maxilla, curettage:
  + Respiratory epithelial lined cyst, consistent with surgical ciliated cyst (see comment)
  + Comment: Microscopic examination reveals a cyst lined by benign ciliated respiratory type epithelium. The cyst wall is variably fibrotic with sparse, chronic inflammation. No cytologic atypia is seen. In view of the patient’s history of LeFort surgery and the current 3.0 cm unilocular maxillary lesion associated with reconstruction hardware, the findings are consistent with a surgical ciliated cyst.

Differential diagnosis

* [Glandular odontogenic cyst (GOC)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mandiblemaxillaglandularodontogenic.html):
  + Developmental odontogenic cyst
  + Cyst epithelium varies in thickness, often with whorled epithelial thickenings
  + May be composed of a thin layer of atrophic squamous to flattened cuboidal cells
  + Areas of stratified squamous epithelium possible
  + Surface hobnail cells composed of cuboidal or low columnar cells on the luminal surface
  + Intraepithelial microcysts, apocrine metaplasia, clear cells, papillary projections (tufting), cilia and mucous cells ([Oral Oncol 2005;41:895](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16043383), [Head Neck Pathol 2011;5:364](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21915706), [Oral Dis 2018;24:717](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28744957))
  + Not all features are present in every case and a higher number of features allows a more confident diagnosis ([Head Neck Pathol 2011;5:364](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21915706))
  + GOC is generally considered to be negative for *MAML2* gene rearrangements ([Head Neck Pathol 2014;8:287](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24647913))
  + High recurrence rate (up to 50%)
  + Radiographic appearance: radiolucent and often multilocular
  + Location: most common in mandible
* [Dentigerous cyst](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mandiblemaxilladentigerous.html):
  + Developmental odontogenic cyst associated with an impacted tooth
  + Composed of thin, nonkeratinized stratified squamous epithelial lining, may be bilaminar in uninflamed cysts
  + Hyaline (Rushton) bodies and focal keratinization may also occur ([J Pharm Bioallied Sci 2014;6:S110](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25210349), [J Clin Diagn Res 2015;9:ZE01](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25859536))
  + Cuboidal, columnar and ciliated cells may be present
  + Mucous cells may be present, rarely shows sebaceous metaplasia ([J Oral Sci 2005;47:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16050487), [J Oral Pathol Med 2013;42:462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23278191), [Mod Pathol 2017;30:S96](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28060370))
  + In the absence of inflammation, the cyst wall is often myxoid or fibromyxoid connective tissue ([J Oral Pathol Med 2013;42:462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23278191))
  + Dentigerous cysts with dense inflammation show squamous epithelial hyperplasia along with mixed inflammation, foamy macrophages, cholesterol clefts associated with foreign body giant cells ([J Oral Pathol Med 2013;42:462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23278191), [J Oral Pathol Med 2019;48:74](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30175860))
  + Radiographic appearance: well defined radiolucency associated with the crown of an unerupted tooth
* [Nasopalatine duct cyst](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mandiblemaxillanasopalatine.html):
  + Nonodontogenic cyst in the maxilla
  + Commonly lined by nonkeratinized stratified squamous epithelium, with focal cuboidal, columnar or ciliated areas
  + Respiratory epithelium may be present but it is unusual to be entirely lined by respiratory epithelium ([J Oral Maxillofac Surg 1991;49:268](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1995816), [J Craniomaxillofac Surg 2018;46:264](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29248496))
  + Cyst wall may contain neurovascular tissue
  + Cyst wall may contain lobules of mucous glands or rests of cartilage
  + Radiographic appearance: centered in anterior maxilla within the incisive canal
* [Radicular (periapical) cyst](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mandiblemaxillaperiapicalcyst.html) with metaplasia:
  + Inflammatory odontogenic cyst associated with the root of a nonvital tooth
  + Lined by nonkeratinized stratified squamous epithelium often with a characteristic arcading pattern
  + Limited areas of squamous keratinization are not uncommon
  + Hyaline (Rushton) bodies possible
  + While uncommon, radicular cysts may be partially lined by ciliated pseudostratified columnar epithelium and are hypothesized to represent either incorporation of regional sinus epithelial lining (through the process of inflammation and bone loss between the alveolus and maxillary sinus) or are the consequence of metaplasia, which has been reported in some mandibular cysts ([Case Rep Dent 2024;2024:5584515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38798911))
  + Cyst wall is composed of inflamed fibrous tissue

Board review style question #1

Which of the following clinical findings would support a diagnosis of a surgical ciliated cyst located in the left maxilla?

1. Cyst positioned around left impacted maxillary third molar
2. History of left maxillary sinus surgery
3. Located in the midline of the anterior maxilla
4. Prior history of radiation treatment to left neck

Board review style answer #1

**B**. History of left maxillary sinus surgery. History of maxillary sinus surgery ipsilateral to the current cyst is supportive of a diagnosis of maxillary surgical cyst; however, the prior surgical procedure may be remote, making this history more challenging to elicit. Answer A is incorrect because cysts positioned around an impacted maxillary third molar can occur in a number of gnathic cysts and are an essential feature of dentigerous cysts. This radiographic relationship has not been reported in maxillary surgical ciliated cysts. Answer C is incorrect because a cyst in the midline of the anterior maxilla can occur in several gnathic cysts and is an essential feature of nasopalatine duct cysts. This radiographic relationship has not been reported in maxillary surgical ciliated cysts. Answer D is incorrect because a prior history of radiation treatment to the neck is a feature to consider for osteoradionecrosis of the jaw but is not a supportive characteristic in surgical ciliated cysts.

Board review style question #2

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/mandiblemaxillasurgicalciliatedcystMagliocca08.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/mandiblemaxillasurgicalciliatedcystMagliocca08.jpg)

Which of the following is most frequently associated with surgical ciliated cyst?

1. Corrugated parakeratin
2. Intraepithelial microcysts
3. Respiratory epithelium
4. Reverse polarity of basal layer nuclei

Board review style answer #2

**C**. Respiratory epithelium. A surgical ciliated cyst is lined by respiratory epithelium. Answer A is incorrect because corrugated parakeratin is a microscopic feature of odontogenic keratocyst. Answer B is incorrect because intraepithelial microcyst formation is a feature of glandular odontogenic cyst. Answer D is incorrect because reverse polarity of basal layer nuclei occurs in ameloblastoma.
