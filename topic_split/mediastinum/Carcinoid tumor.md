Definition / general

* Neuroendocrine epithelial neoplasm of thymic origin
* Low grade nuclear features

Essential features

* Histologic examination showing neuroendocrine morphology and low grade nuclear features is required for diagnosis
* Demonstration of strong expression of keratin and neuroendocrine markers is desirable for diagnosis
* Typical carcinoid tumor has 2 and no necrosis; atypical carcinoid tumor has 2 - 10 mitoses/2 mm2 or foci of necrosis
* In contrast to the lung, atypical carcinoids are much more common than typical carcinoids in the thymus

Terminology

* Typical carcinoid tumor (well differentiated neuroendocrine tumor of the thymus, grade 1)
* Atypical carcinoid tumor (well differentiated neuroendocrine tumor of the thymus, grade 2)

ICD coding

* ICD-O
  + [8240/3](https://iris.who.int/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=68) - carcinoid tumor, NOS / neuroendocrine tumor, NOS
  + [8240/3](https://iris.who.int/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=68) - typical carcinoid / neuroendocrine tumor, grade 1
  + [8249/3](https://iris.who.int/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=69) - atypical carcinoid / neuroendocrine tumor, grade 2
* ICD-11
  + [2C27.1 & XH9LV8](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#381956131%20%26%201609609938) - carcinoid tumor or other neuroendocrine neoplasms of thymus & neuroendocrine tumor, grade 1
  + [2C27.1 & XH51K1](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#381956131%20%26%20522339131) - carcinoid tumor or other neuroendocrine neoplasms of thymus & neuroendocrine tumor, grade 2

Epidemiology

* Thymic carcinoid tumors are rare, representing 0.4% of all neuroendocrine tumors and J Thorac Oncol 2019;14:1472)
* Mean patient age for thymic typical carcinoids is 49 years, while atypical carcinoids occur at a slightly older age (48 - 55 years); both typical and atypical thymic carcinoids have a male predominance ([Ann Thorac Cardiovasc Surg 1999;5:285](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10550713), [Mod Pathol 2001;14:985](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11598168))
* Atypical carcinoids are much more common than typical carcinoids in the thymus ([Mod Pathol 2001;14:985](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11598168))

Sites

* Anterior mediastinum

Pathophysiology

* Unknown

Etiology

* Family history of *MEN1* is reported in 25% of patients with thymic carcinoid ([J Intern Med 1998;243:501](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9681849))
* Smoking is a risk factor for thymic carcinoid only in men with *MEN1* gene mutation ([World J Surg 2009;33:1197](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19294466))

Clinical features

* > 50% of patients present with clinical symptoms such as shortness of breath, cough, chest pain or superior vena cava syndrome ([Mod Pathol 2001;14:985](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11598168))
* Lymph node metastasis is common at presentation (40 - 50%) ([Ann Thorac Cardiovasc Surg 1999;5:285](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10550713))
* Paraneoplastic syndromes may occur due to associated hormone production
  + Cushing syndrome due to ectopic secretion of adrenocorticotropin (ACTH) ([Ann Thorac Cardiovasc Surg 1999;5:285](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10550713))
  + Hypercalcaemia / hypophosphatemia due to production of parathyroid hormone related protein (PTHrP) ([Surg Today 1994;24:544](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7919739))
  + Inappropriate production of antidiuretic hormone or atrial natriuretic peptide ([Ann Intern Med 1994;121:75](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8198356))
  + Acromegaly due to ectopic growth hormone releasing hormone (GHRH) ([Clin Endocrinol (Oxf) 1998;48:243](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9579239))
* Recurrence may occur late (> 5 years after diagnosis), including distant metastases to lung, brain, bone, liver, adrenals and soft tissue sites ([Mod Pathol 2001;14:985](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11598168), [Am J Clin Pathol 2000;114:100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10884805))

Diagnosis

* Tissue diagnosis obtained from a biopsy or cytology samples can be used to evaluate mediastinal lesions; however, definitive tumor classification can be difficult in small samples and primary resection may be considered

Laboratory

* Increased plasma chromogranin A, although it is not specific ([Hematol Oncol Clin North Am 2016;30:83](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26614370))

Radiology description

* Nonspecific features on imaging
* Computed tomography (CT): large, heterogenous enhancing mass and with irregular contours, with or without surrounding capsule
* Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI): heterogeneous T2 hyperintense signal
* Thymic carcinoids are typical FDG avid on positron emission tomography (PET) / CT ([Br J Radiol 2017;90:20150341](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28106503))

Prognostic factors

* Data on the prognosis of thymic carcinoids is sparse but suggests that there is no statistically significant difference in survival rates between typical and atypical thymic carcinoid tumors ([Ann Thorac Cardiovasc Surg 1999;5:285](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10550713))
* Surgery and radiotherapy seem to improve outcome ([Interact Cardiovasc Thorac Surg 2018;26:18](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29049806))
* 5 year survival is 50 - 70% ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2014;53:738](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24764238), [Am J Clin Pathol 2000;114:100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10884805))
  + Study of 58 patients showed a median survival of 126 months for thymic typical thyroid carcinoids and 52 months for thymic atypical carcinoids

Case reports

* 25 year old man presented with abnormal mental behavior due to ectopic adrenocorticotrophic hormone syndrome caused by thymic carcinoid ([Gland Surg 2022;11:767](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35531113))
* 32 year old man presented with cyclic Cushing syndrome due to ACTH secreting atypical thymic carcinoid ([Arch Endocrinol Metab 2021;65:512](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33844892))
* 33 year old man with atypical thymic carcinoid presented with ACTH ectopic syndrome ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2023;102:e33847](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37266641))
* 53 year old man with thymic carcinoid presented with gastrointestinal symptoms and pericardial effusion ([BMC Cardiovasc Disord 2021;21:54](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33509095))

Treatment

* Surgical resection
* Postoperative radiotherapy may be considered ([Interact Cardiovasc Thorac Surg 2018;26:18](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29049806))
* Systemic therapy using somatostatin analog for patients with advanced disease ([Cancer Med 2023;12:7893](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36560885))

Gross description

* Variable size; average of 8 - 10 cm ([Am J Clin Pathol 2000;114:100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10884805))
* May range from circumscribed to frankly invasive
* Firm, gray-white to tan cut surface; may be calcified
* Often lacks the prominent gross fibrous bands of thymoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Uniform, polygonal to spindle shaped tumor cells arranged in nests that commonly show classic neuroendocrine growth patterns including trabecular growth or rosettes
* Finely granular nuclear chromatin and scant to moderate pale eosinophilic cytoplasm
* Typical and atypical thymic carcinoid tumors are distinguished based on mitotic activity and necrosis, which are often patchy and can only be adequately assessed on resection specimens; therefore, definitive subclassification as typical or atypical usually cannot be made on small biopsy or cytology specimens
* Reference: [Semin Diagn Pathol 1991;8:35](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1646476)

Cytology description

* Definitive tumor classification as typical or atypical carcinoid cannot be performed based on the cytologic features
* Small to medium sized, round, elongated or plasmacytoid tumor cells arranged in clusters or rosettes with scant to moderate cytoplasm and salt and pepper chromatin ([Cibas: Cytology - Diagnostic Principles and Clinical Correlates, 4th Edition, 2014](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/145574462X/pathologyoutl-20))

Positive stains

* [Pankeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)
* Neuroendocrine markers ([synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html), [chromogranin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html), [INSM1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinsm1.html), [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html))
* [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html), polyclonal (50%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:850](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25871618))
* [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) is often 50%)

Negative stains

* [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html) (may help differentiate thymic carcinoid from pulmonary carcinoid which are positive in 50%, although thymic carcinoid can occasionally be positive in up to 14% of cases)
* [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html) (useful marker for neuroendocrine tumor of an intestinal origin) ([Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2007;15:407](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18091383))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Thymic carcinoids seem to demonstrate recurrent chromosomal gains and losses that appear different from those observed in pulmonary carcinoids ([Mod Pathol 2005;18:358](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15272285), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2014;53:738](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24764238))

Sample pathology report

* Thymus, mass, biopsy:
  + Carcinoid tumor (see comment)
  + Comment: In the current biopsy specimen, no increased mitotic activity or necrosis is identified; however, these features can be patchy and definitive classification as typical or atypical carcinoid tumor requires examination of a resection specimen.

Differential diagnosis

* [Paraganglioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorparaganglioma.html):
  + [Keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)-
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)+ sustentacular cells)
* [Thymoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mediastinumthymoma.html) ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2006;130:1612](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17076522)):
  + Variable proportions of epithelial cells and thymic lymphocytes (thymocytes)
  + [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html)+ and [p40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp40.html)+; may have [TdT](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstdt.html)+ thymocytes
  + Neuroendocrine markers ([synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html), [chromogranin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html)) are negative
* [Small cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorsmallcell.html):
  + High N:C ratio, scant cytoplasm, nuclear molding, inconspicuous nucleoli, marked necrosis and apoptotic bodies
  + Increased mitosis > 10 per 2 mm2
  + [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) proliferation index usually > 80%
* [Large cell neuroendocrine carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorlargecellNE.html):
  + Increased nuclear pleomorphism, prominent nucleoli, marked necrosis and apoptotic bodies
  + Increased mitosis > 10 mitoses/2 mm2
  + [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) proliferation index usually > 30%
* [Thymic carcinoma with neuroendocrine differentiation](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mediastinumneuroendocrinecarcinoma.html) ([Am J Clin Pathol 2016;145:393](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27124922)):
  + [CK5/6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5and6.html)+, [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html)+ and [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html)+
  + Neuroendocrine markers show focal and patchy staining

Board review style question #2

Which of the following criteria supports a diagnosis of thymic large cell neuroendocrine carcinoma over thymic carcinoid tumor?

1. 20 mitoses per 2 mm2
2. Ki67 proliferation index of 10%
3. Negative keratin stain
4. Tumor necrosis

Board review style answer #2

**A.** 20 mitoses per 2 mm2. Per WHO criteria, thymic carcinoid tumors cannot exceed a mitotic count of 10 per 2 mm2. Answers C and D are incorrect because both atypical thymic carcinoids and large cell neuroendocrine carcinoma can have tumor necrosis and should express keratin. Answer B is incorrect because Ki67 proliferation index is not currently one of the diagnostic criteria to differentiate thymic neuroendocrine tumors but a very high proliferation index can be helpful in supporting a diagnosis of large cell neuroendocrine carcinoma or small cell carcinoma over a carcinoid tumor.
