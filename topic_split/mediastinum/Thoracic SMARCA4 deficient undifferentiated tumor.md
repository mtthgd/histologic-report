Definition / general

* SMARCA4 deficient undifferentiated tumor (SMARCA4 UT) is a rare, aggressive thoracic malignancy, recently recognized as a distinct entity in the 2021 WHO Classification of Thoracic Tumors ([Jpn J Clin Oncol 2024;54:265](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38117955), [Int J Mol Sci 2024;25:3237](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38542211))
* It is characterized by inactivating mutations in the *SMARCA4* gene, resulting in the loss of BRG1 protein expression and typically presents as a high grade malignancy with an undifferentiated or rhabdoid phenotype ([Mediastinum 2021;5:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35118344), [Discov Oncol 2023;14:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37115343))

Essential features

* Characterized by loss of *SMARCA4*  (BRG1) expression due to inactivating mutation of the *SMARCA4* gene
* Predominantly occurs in the mediastinum of middle aged male smokers
* Aggressive clinical course with a median survival of ~4 - 7 months ([Mediastinum 2021;5:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35118344), [Nat Genet 2015;47:1200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26343384), [Mod Pathol 2017;30:797](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28256572), [Mod Pathol 2017;30:1422](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28643792), [J Thorac Oncol 2020;15:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31751681))
* Immunohistochemistry shows loss of BRG1, retention of INI1 and frequent expression of CD34 and SOX2 ([Mediastinum 2021;5:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35118344), [PeerJ 2024;12:e16923](https://doi.org/10.7717/peerj.16923/supp-1))

Terminology

* Not recommended: SMARCA4 deficient thoracic sarcoma; SMARCA4 deficient thoracic sarcomatoid tumor

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8044/3](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1556086421033165#tbl1) - thoracic SMARCA4 deficient undifferentiated tumor
* ICD-10: [C38.3](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C30-C39/C38-/C38.3) - malignant neoplasm of mediastinum, part unspecified
* ICD-11: [2C25.Y](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#316539081%2Fother) & XH0VV9 - other specified malignant neoplasms of bronchus or lung & SMARCA4 deficient thoracic sarcoma

Epidemiology

* Predominantly affects middle aged male smokers, often diagnosed in their mid to late 50s but a wide age range is observed (27 - 90 years) ([World J Clin Cases 2023;11:2521](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37123323), [Discov Oncol 2023;14:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37115343), [Nat Genet 2015;47:1200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26343384), [Mod Pathol 2017;30:797](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28256572), [Mod Pathol 2017;30:1422](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28643792), [Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:455](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30451731))
* ~100 cases reported in the literature ([World J Clin Cases 2023;11:2521](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37123323))
* No correlation with race or geography ([Discov Oncol 2023;14:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37115343))
* Striking male predominance

Sites

* Mediastinum (most common)
* Pulmonary hilum
* Lung
* Pleura
* Chest wall invasion may be present
* Lung is at least focally involved in most cases but it may be overshadowed by prominent mediastinal involvement
* Rare cases seem to lack pulmonary involvement ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:797](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28256572), [Pathology 2019;51:657](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31470991))

Pathophysiology

* Inactivating mutation of *SMARCA4* leads to loss of BRG1 protein expression
* BRG1 is a tumor suppressor and a catalytic subunit of the SWI / SNF chromatin remodeling complex, crucial for regulating gene expression ([Discov Oncol 2023;14:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37115343))
* SWI / SNF complex (BAF complex) is involved in various cellular processes, including development, differentiation, proliferation and apoptosis ([Discov Oncol 2023;14:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37115343))
* Tumorigenesis is driven by biallelic inactivation of *SMARCA4*, including mainly nonsense and frameshift mutations, with missense mutations, splice site mutations or deletions being less common; they are combined with loss of heterozygosity or uncommonly, deletion or a second mutation ([Nat Genet 2015;47:1200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26343384), [J Thorac Oncol 2020;15:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31751681))
* Multiple, mostly nonrecurrent copy number alterations are present across the tumor genome ([Nat Genet 2015;47:1200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26343384))
* Transcriptional profiles of these tumors are distinct from those of SMARCA4 deficient non-small cell lung carcinoma (NSCLC) but similar to those of malignant rhabdoid tumor ([Nat Genet 2015;47:1200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26343384))

Etiology

* Strong association with smoking; the vast majority of tumors affect heavy smokers and most harbor a genomic smoking signature ([Mediastinum 2021;5:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35118344), [Discov Oncol 2023;14:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37115343), [J Thorac Oncol 2020;15:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31751681))
* Smoking related bullous emphysema is present in more than half of cases, even in young patients, suggesting increased vulnerability to smoking related injury ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:797](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28256572), [Eur Radiol 2019;29:4730](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30762113), [J Thorac Oncol 2020;15:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31751681))
* ~10% of the patients are never smokers, who may also have bullous and interstitial lung diseases ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:797](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28256572), [J Thorac Oncol 2020;15:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31751681))
* No germline mutation of *SMARCA4* has been reported in these tumors ([Nat Genet 2015;47:1200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26343384))
* Exact etiology is unknown

Clinical features

* Presenting symptoms include dyspnea, pain, superior vena cava syndrome, weight loss and symptoms related to metastasis
* Dyspnea, chest pain, regurgitation, fatigue and weight loss ([Discov Oncol 2023;14:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37115343))
* Recurrent pleural effusion or pleural emphysema if pleura is involved ([Discov Oncol 2023;14:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37115343))
* Rapid progression to metastasis, often leading to death within a few months ([World J Clin Cases 2023;11:2521](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37123323))
* Imaging typically shows a large, ill defined and invasive PET avid mass that compresses adjacent structures, especially when centered in the mediastinum; however, primary lung tumors can sometimes be small (rarely) ([Nat Genet 2015;47:1200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26343384), [Mod Pathol 2017;30:1422](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28643792), [Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:455](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30451731), [Eur Radiol 2019;29:4730](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30762113))
* Tumor is aggressive, with frequent metastases at initial presentation, to lymph nodes, bones, adrenal glands, brain and abdominal cavity / pelvis ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:797](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28256572), [Mod Pathol 2017;30:1422](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28643792), [Eur Radiol 2019;29:4730](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30762113), [J Thorac Oncol 2020;15:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31751681))
* Coexisting bulky abdominal disease may make determination of primary site difficult ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:797](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28256572))

Diagnosis

* Imaging (CT, PET / CT) to identify mass
* Biopsy with histological and immunohistochemical evaluation
* Loss of BRG1 (*SMARCA4*) expression by immunohistochemistry is essential for diagnosis
* Endobronchial ultrasound guided transbronchial cryobiopsy (EBUS cryo) may be useful for obtaining tissue samples
* Contrast enhanced ultrasound (CEUS) can help identify internal necrosis and improve biopsy success rate

Prognostic factors

* Generally poor prognosis, with a median survival of ~4 - 7 months ([Mediastinum 2021;5:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35118344), [Nat Genet 2015;47:1200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26343384), [Mod Pathol 2017;30:797](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28256572), [Mod Pathol 2017;30:1422](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28643792), [J Thorac Oncol 2020;15:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31751681))
* They have a poorer outcome than SMARCA4 deficient conventional NSCLCs ([Nat Genet 2015;47:1200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26343384), [J Thorac Oncol 2020;15:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31751681))
* Patients receiving immunotherapy may have better overall survival (OS) and progression free survival (PFS) ([Lung Cancer 2024;189:107471](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38306886))
* Tumor size, stage and performance status may influence prognosis

Case reports

* 38 year old man with SMARCA4 deficient tumor treated with anti-PD-1 inhibitor ([Mediastinum 2021;5:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35118344))
* 46 year old woman with thoracic SMARCA4 deficient undifferentiated tumor treated with atezolizumab, carboplatin and nab-paclitaxel ([Thorac Cancer 2022;13:2264](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35778998))
* 63 year old man with advanced thoracic SMARCA4 deficient undifferentiated tumor treated with EZH2 inhibitor ([Discov Oncol 2023;14:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37115343))

Treatment

* No standardized treatment strategy due to rarity and aggressiveness
* Surgery may be considered for early stage disease but complete resection is often challenging ([Int J Mol Sci 2024;25:3237](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38542211))
* Chemotherapy has shown limited efficacy
* Cytotoxic chemotherapy regimens have been reported as generally ineffective and novel treatment strategies are under investigation, including immune checkpoint inhibition ([Ann Oncol 2019;30:1401](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31114851))
* Immunotherapy (anti-PD-1 and PDL1 antibodies) has shown promising results in some cases ([Mediastinum 2021;5:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35118344))
* Targeted therapy (EZH2 inhibitors) is under investigation ([Mediastinum 2021;5:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35118344), [Discov Oncol 2023;14:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37115343))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Sheets of variably discohesive, large round to epithelioid cells with vesicular chromatin and prominent nucleoli
  + Nuclei are relatively monotonous, with occasional cells displaying mild to moderate pleomorphism ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:797](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28256572), [Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:455](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30451731), [J Thorac Oncol 2020;15:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31751681))
* Undifferentiated or poorly differentiated morphology with occasional rhabdoid features
  + Rhabdoid cells may be present, although they are often a focal finding and may be absent in small specimens ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:797](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28256572), [Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:455](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30451731), [J Thorac Oncol 2020;15:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31751681))
* Markedly increased mitotic activity ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:797](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28256572), [Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:455](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30451731), [J Thorac Oncol 2020;15:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31751681))
* Extensive necrosis ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:797](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28256572), [Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:455](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30451731), [J Thorac Oncol 2020;15:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31751681))
* Rare findings include spindling, myxoid change, sclerosis, alveolar pattern and clear cell change ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:797](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28256572), [Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:455](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30451731), [J Thorac Oncol 2020;15:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31751681))
* Unequivocal evidence of epithelial differentiation (e.g., gland, papilla or keratinization) is absent in most cases but ~5% of cases show a combined histology with juxtaposed conventional NSCLC ([J Thorac Oncol 2020;15:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31751681))

Cytology description

* Smears are typically cellular in a necrotic background, with cells appearing singly or in loosely cohesive groups
* Highly atypical cells, singly and in loose clusters
* Cells are round to ovoid, with typically eccentric nuclei and prominent nucleoli
* High N:C ratio
* Despite overall monomorphism, isolated pleomorphic cells are common
* Rhabdoid features may be seen
* Hyaline inclusions are not well demonstrated in cytological smears but dense cytoplasm with indentation of nuclei is apparent in some cells ([Int J Surg Pathol 2020;28:109](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31448657), [Acta Cytol 2019;63:438](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31230044), [Acta Cytol 2018;62:456](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30286456))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Inactivating mutations in the *SMARCA4* gene ([Nat Genet 2015;47:1200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26343384), [J Thorac Oncol 2020;15:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31751681))
* High tumor mutation burden, comparable to SMARCA4 deficient non-small cell lung cancer
* Frequent smoking related mutations (e.g., *KRAS, STK11, KEAP1*) ([Nat Genet 2015;47:1200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26343384), [Mod Pathol 2017;30:797](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28256572), [J Thorac Oncol 2020;15:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31751681))
* *TP53* mutations are common ([Nat Genet 2015;47:1200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26343384))
* *NF1* mutation is present in a subset ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:797](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28256572), [J Thorac Oncol 2020;15:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31751681))
* Most tumors also have genomic smoking mutation signatures ([Nat Genet 2015;47:1200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26343384), [J Thorac Oncol 2020;15:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31751681))
* No specific recurrent chromosomal abnormalities
* Mutation may not be detectable, depending on the methods used, despite SMARCA4 immunohistochemical loss ([J Thorac Oncol 2020;15:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31751681))
* Because the second hit is often copy neutral loss of heterozygosity, SMARCA4 FISH assay is of limited utility ([J Thorac Oncol 2020;15:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31751681))

* Mediastinum, biopsy:
  + Thoracic SMARCA4 deficient undifferentiated tumor (see comment)
  + Comment: The tumor consists of sheets of high grade undifferentiated cells with focal rhabdoid features. Immunohistochemical stains show loss of BRG1 (*SMARCA4*) and intact INI1. The tumor cells are positive for CD34 and SOX2 and negative for TTF1, p40, desmin and S100. These findings are diagnostic of a thoracic SMARCA4 deficient undifferentiated tumor. This is an aggressive malignancy with a poor prognosis.

Differential diagnosis

* **SMARCA4 deficient** [non-small cell lung carcinoma (NSCLC)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumoradenocarcinoma.html):
  + Distinguish by epithelial architecture (e.g., glands), cellular cohesion and diffuse strong [keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) expression
  + Ancillary markers (**SMARCA2**, [claudin4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsclaudins.html#claudin4), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html) and [SOX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox2.html)) may also be helpful, although none is entirely sensitive or specific
* [Malignant rhabdoid tumor (MRT)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuerhabdoidtumor.html):
  + [INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html) loss
  + Typically occurs in infants and young children
* [Small cell carcinoma of the ovary, hypercalcemic type (SCCOHT)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorsmallcellhyper.html):
  + [SMARCA4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsSMARCA4BRG1.html) loss
  + Occurs in the ovary
* **Lymphoma**
* [NUT carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mediastinumnutcarcinoma.html)
* [Germ cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mediastinumgermcell.html)
* [Neuroendocrine carcinoma (NEC)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mediastinumneuroendocrinecarcinoma.html)
* [Large cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorlargecellne.html)
* [Melanoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticmelanoma.html)

* Because SMARCA4 UTs with a similar phenotype may [metastasize](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mediastinummetastaticcarcinoma.html) to the thorax from extraneous sites (e.g., uterus, ovary, stomach, kidney and pancreas), clinical correlation is mandatory

Board review style question #1

[![BRQ image](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/mediastinumthoracicSMARCA4deficientcecchini07.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/mediastinumthoracicSMARCA4deficientcecchini07.jpg)

A 56 year old male smoker presents with dyspnea, weight loss and a rapidly enlarging mediastinal mass. A biopsy of the mass reveals sheets of large round cells with vesicular chromatin, prominent nucleoli, areas of necrosis and focal keratin positivity. Immunohistochemical stains show loss of BRG1 expression (shown in image above), retained INI1 and positivity for SOX2. TTF1 and p40 are negative. What is the most likely diagnosis?

1. Germ cell tumor
2. Malignant rhabdoid tumor
3. SMARCA4 deficient non-small cell lung carcinoma
4. Thoracic SMARCA4 deficient undifferentiated tumor

Board review style answer #1

**D.** Thoracic SMARCA4 deficient undifferentiated tumor. The clinical and histologic features, along with the immunohistochemical profile (loss of BRG1, retained INI1, focal keratin positivity and SOX2 positivity), are diagnostic of thoracic SMARCA4 deficient undifferentiated tumor. This tumor is highly aggressive and typically affects middle aged male smokers.
Answer C is incorrect because SMARCA4 deficient non-small cell lung carcinoma typically shows diffuse keratin expression and epithelial architecture, including glandular differentiation.
Answer B is incorrect because malignant rhabdoid tumor usually shows loss of INI1 and primarily occurs in infants and children.
Answer A is incorrect because germ cell tumors typically express markers like OCT3/4 and PLAP, which are not mentioned in this case.

Board review style question #2

Which of the following features is most likely to distinguish thoracic SMARCA4 deficient undifferentiated tumor from SMARCA4 deficient non-small cell lung carcinoma?

1. Absence of glandular differentiation and diffuse keratin staining
2. Focal rhabdoid morphology
3. Loss of BRG1 expression
4. Retained INI1 expression

Board review style answer #2

**A.** Absence of glandular differentiation and diffuse keratin staining. Thoracic SMARCA4 deficient undifferentiated tumors lack glandular differentiation and typically have focal rather than diffuse keratin staining. This distinguishes them from SMARCA4 deficient non-small cell lung carcinoma, which commonly shows glandular differentiation and diffuse keratin staining.
Answer D is incorrect because retained INI1 expression is seen in both entities.
Answer C is incorrect because loss of BRG1 is common in both entities.
Answer B is incorrect because focal rhabdoid morphology is seen in SMARCA4 deficient undifferentiated tumors but is not a specific distinguishing feature from non-small cell lung carcinoma.
