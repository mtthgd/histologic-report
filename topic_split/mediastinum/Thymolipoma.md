Definition / general

* Increased thymic volume, due to lobules of mature adipose tissue mixed with unremarkable thymic tissue
* Benign
* May be neoplasm of thymic fat ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2009;13:185](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19433298))

Epidemiology

* Usually young to middle aged adults, found incidentally

Clinical features

* 10% associated with thymoma-like paraneoplastic symptoms

Case reports

* 26 year old woman with possible origin from thymic true hyperplasia ([Int J Surg Pathol 2010;18:526](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19233859))
* 36 year old woman with thymoma and thymic carcinoma arising in thymolipoma ([Int J Surg Pathol 2009;17:55](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18480395))
* 69 year old man with 570 g mediastinal mass ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2004;128:e159](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15504081))

Gross description

* Encapsulated, up to 20 cm
* Resembles lipoma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Thin fibrous capsule surrounding lobules of mature adipose tissue intimately associated with unremarkable thymic tissue containing cortex, medulla and Hassall corpuscles
* May have thymic epithelial proliferation, myoid cells, zones of dense fibrosis
