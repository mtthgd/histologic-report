Definition / general

* Macrophagic myofasciitis (MMF) is a localized macrophage aggregation associated with intramuscular injection of vaccines containing aluminum hydroxide, particularly DTaP, HepB, HepA and HPV
* Histologically characterized by aggregation of macrophages with basophilic granular cytoplasm that infiltrates the epimysium, perimysium, endomysium and subcutaneous tissue near the vaccine injection site, leading to fibrosis of those areas

Essential features

* First described by RK Gherardi et al. in 1998 and linked to vaccines containing aluminum salt in 2001 ([Lancet 1998;352:347](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9717921), [Brain 2001;124:1821](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11522584))
* Clinically characterized by the triad of myalgia, arthralgia and chronic fatigue; pathologically characterized by aggregations of large macrophages containing basophilic granular cytoplasm positive for PAS with diastase, usually without forming multinucleated giant cells ([Brain 2001;124:1821](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11522584))
* Diagnosis relies on demonstration of aluminum particles within macrophages; aluminum can be detected by the following methods
  + Atomic absorption spectrometry ([Brain 2001;124:1821](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11522584))
  + Electron microscopy as membrane bound spiculated inclusions within macrophages ([Brain 2001;124:1821](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11522584))
  + Morin special stain (see [Immunofluorescence images](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/musclemacrophagic.html#macrophagicIFimages)) ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2017;76:323](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28340105))
  + EBER in situ hybridization (cytoplasmic) (see [Microscopic (histologic) images - case #3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/musclemacrophagic.html#macrophagicmicroimages)) ([J Cutan Pathol 2021;48:625](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33496022))
* MMF is usually confined to the vaccine injection site; it is controversial whether the central nervous system (CNS) or diffuse systemic symptoms in some patients are caused by a reaction to the vaccine
* In young children, MMF is usually an incidental finding, not the primary cause of myopathy; the finding of MMF in a pediatric quadriceps muscle biopsy should not stop one from searching alternative etiologies of myopathy ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2017;76:323](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28340105))

Terminology

* Vaccination granuloma
* Cutaneous pseudolymphoma
* Autoimmune syndrome induced by adjuvants (ASIA)
* Gulf war syndrome
* Myalgic encephalomyelitis (ME) / chronic fatigue syndrome (CFS)

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [M79.1](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/M00-M99/M70-M79/M79-/M79.1) - myalgia

Epidemiology

* Adult
  + Single institution French study from 293 adult MMF patients ([Front Neurol 2014;5:230](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25506338))
  + Mean age: 52 years
  + M:F = 1:2.3 (88 male, 205 female)
  + Mean aluminum containing vaccines received: 5 (range: 1 - 12)
  + Mean persistence of time of aluminum: 70 months
* Pediatric
  + Most studies are small and come from single institutions with Age of onset: most under 3 years; range: Mean age: 1.7 years
  + M:F = 1:1.3
  + References: [J Child Neurol 2008;23:614](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18281624), [Pediatr Neurol 2005;33:350](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16243223), [Muscle Nerve 2017;56:71](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27859369), [Sci Rep 2020;10:11834](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32678281)
* True incidence is unknown

Sites

* Restricted to vaccine injection site
  + Quadriceps muscle in the pediatric population ([J Child Neurol 2008;23:614](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18281624))
  + Deltoid muscle in the adult population ([Front Neurol 2014;5:230](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25506338))

Pathophysiology

* Prolonged, localized macrophagic reaction to intramuscular injection of aluminum based vaccine, causing persistent symptoms years after vaccination ([Front Neurol 2014;5:230](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25506338))
* Emergence of MMF temporally coincides with transition from subcutaneous to intramuscular injection of vaccine; subcutaneous injection of aluminum based vaccine may cause more superficial vaccination granuloma (also called cutaneous pseudolymphomas) but not MMF ([Autoimmun Rev 2019;18:691](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31059838), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2005;52:623](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15793512))
* Pathophysiology for prolonged local macrophage aggregation is thought to be delayed clearing of aluminum adjuvant from macrophages due to genetic polymorphism ([Autoimmun Rev 2019;18:691](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31059838))
* Pathophysiology is unclear for the cognitive dysfunctions, chronic fatigue and diffuse arthromyalgias often associated with MMF and not restricted to vaccine injection site; a causal link between aluminum based vaccination and those symptoms is debated ([Cancer Sci 2023;114:1218](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36601818), [Autoimmun Rev 2019;18:691](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31059838))

Etiology

* Caused by vaccines with aluminum adjuvants
  + Higher association with diphtheria - tetanus - pertussis (DTaP), hepatitis B (HepB), hepatitis A (HepA) and human papillomavirus (HPV) vaccines
  + Less reported association with *Haemophilus influenzae* type b (Hib) and pneumococcal conjugate (PCV) vaccines
* Reference: [Front Neurol 2014;5:230](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25506338)

Clinical features

* Main clinical manifestations of prolonged MMF in adult patients are arthromyalgia, chronic fatigue and cognitive disorder ([Front Neurol 2014;5:230](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25506338))
* In patients with athromyalgia, those with MMF have more CNS dysfunction and less fibromyalgic tender points than non-MMF patients ([J Inorg Biochem 2013;128:262](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23921285))
* In the pediatric population, symptoms are more variable and often complicated by other underlining diseases, such as congenital myopathies or muscular dystrophy ([J Child Neurol 2008;23:614](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18281624), [Pediatr Neurol 2005;33:350](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16243223), [Muscle Nerve 2017;56:71](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27859369), [Sci Rep 2020;10:11834](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32678281))

Diagnosis

* Diagnosis relies on muscle biopsy demonstrating macrophagic aggregation with cytoplasmic aluminum particles
* Presence of macrophagic aggregation with cytoplasmic aluminum particles within a year of vaccination may not be pathologic ([Front Neurol 2014;5:230](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25506338))

Laboratory

* Serum creatine kinase elevation is seen in 33% of patients, usually at onset then returning to normal
* Baseline aluminum serum level remains within normal range
* Electromyograph (EMG) shows myopathic features in 33% of patients
* Reference: [Front Neurol 2014;5:230](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25506338)

Radiology description

* Gallium 67 scintigraphy shows bilateral, symmetric and homogeneous increased 67Ga uptake in the muscle and para-articular areas, predominantly in the legs ([Arthritis Rheum 2000;43:1520](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10902755))

Case reports

* 17 day old boy and 5 month old girl with macrophagic myofasciitis ([J Pediatr Neurosci 2020;15:279](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33531946))
* 7 children with macrophagic myofasciitis and subcutaneous pseudolymphoma caused by aluminum adjuvants ([Sci Rep 2020;10:11834](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32678281))
* 25 year old man with generalized muscle pain ([BMJ Case Rep 2018;2018:bcr2018224602](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29973411))

Treatment

* Currently no standardized treatment
* Patients may receive variable combination of anti-inflammatory drugs and antibiotics
* Reference: [Lancet 1998;352:347](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9717921)

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Focal infiltration of the epimysium, perimysium and perifascicular endomysium by well circumscribed and cohesive sheets of large mononuleated cells of the monocyte and macrophage lineage, usually intermingled with a minor lymphocytic population
* Macrophages contain conspicuous basophilic granular cytoplasm that is strongly positive for PAS with diastase, CD68, nonspecific esterase and acid phosphatase
* Macrophages aggregate but usually do not form multinucleated giant cells or granuloma
* Macrophage infiltrate may cause fibrosis of epimysium, perimysium and perifascicular endomysium
* Usually mild to minimal active myofiber damage and only focal weak MHC1 upregulation in myofibers
* Reference: [J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2017;76:323](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28340105)

Sample pathology report

* Skeletal muscle, right thigh, biopsy:
  + Macrophagic myofasciitis (see comment)
  + Comment: The muscle biopsy shows aggregation of macrophages with intense basophilic granular cytoplasm that infiltrates the epimysium, perimysium and perifascicular endomysium. The macrophages are strongly positive for esterase, acid phosphatase and Morin stains. Morin is a flavonoid that forms a complex with aluminum salt and emits green-blue fluorescence detectable by fluorescence scope. The presence of Morin positive macrophages in a muscle at a previous vaccine injection site is supportive of the diagnosis of macrophagic myofasciitis.

Differential diagnosis

* **Granulomatous myositis** ([Neuromuscul Disord 2019;29:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30578101), [J Neuroimmunol 2022;373:577975](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36228383)):
  + Demonstrates epithelioid histiocytes with formation of granuloma or multinucleated giant cells
  + Macrophage cytoplasm lacks basophilic granules typical of MMF
  + Usually has myofiber damage
  + Macrophages are negative on **Morin** stain
  + No spiculated inclusions on electron microscopy
  + Clinically associated with [sarcoidosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorsarcoidosis.html), [sporadic inclusion body myositis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/muscleinclusionbody.html) or infections and not restricted to vaccine injection sites
* **Inflammatory myositis** with abundant macrophages ([J Neuropathol Exp Neurol 2003;62:464](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12769186)):
  + May demonstrate prominent macrophage infiltration in epimysium, perimysium and endomysium; macrophages are more discohesive and diffuse and lack basophilic cytoplasmic granules
  + Negative on **Morin** stain
  + No spiculated inclusions on electron microscopy
  + Clinically associated with [dermatomyositis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumordermatomyositis.html) or **cytophagic histiocytic panniculitis**

A 2 year old with developmental delay and hypotonia presented with elevated creatine kinase in the 1,000s. There is a strong suspicion of a genetic cause, although whole exome sequencing was unrevealing. A quadriceps muscle biopsy was performed and showed aggregation of macrophages with basophilic granular cytoplasm mixed with lymphocytes. EBV in situ hybridization (EBER) shows the findings in the images above. What is the most likely diagnosis?

1. Hodgkin lymphoma
2. Inflammatory myositis with abundant macrophages
3. Langerhans cell histiocytosis
4. Macrophagic myofasciitis
5. Sarcoidosis

Board review style question #1

**D**. Macrophagic myofasciitis. The section shows an aggregate of CD68+ macrophages intermixed with lymphocytes in the epimysial connective tissue. EBER stain shows a unique strong cytoplasmic granular positivity that supports the diagnosis of macrophagic myofasciitis (MMF). This is due to the presence of aluminum particles within the lysosomes of the macrophages of MMF, which has nonspecific high affinity to RNA probes in the EBV in situ hybridization staining kit. This staining pattern is different from the typical nuclear staining in EBV positive cases. Answers A, B, C and E are incorrect because none of these choices contain aluminum, which gives such a strong cytoplasmic EBER reactivity ([J Cutan Pathol 2021;48:625](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33496022)).

A 2 year old with developmental delay and hypotonia presented with elevated creatine kinase in the 1,000s. There is a strong suspicion of a genetic cause, although whole exome sequencing was unrevealing. A quadriceps muscle biopsy was performed and showed aggregation of macrophages with basophilic granular cytoplasm mixed with lymphocytes. EBV in situ hybridization (EBER) shows the findings in the images above. Which of the following additional stains on muscle biopsy is most helpful in reaching the diagnosis of macrophagic myfasciitis (MMF)?

1. MHC class 1
2. Morin stain
3. Nonspecific esterase
4. PAS with and without diastase
5. Von Kossa stain

Board review style answer #2

**B**. Morin stain. The diagnosis of MMF relies on demonstration of aluminum particles within the macrophages. Morin (2-[2,4-dihydroxyphenyl]-3,5,7-trihydroxychromen-4-one) is a flavonoid that forms a green-blue fluorescent complex with aluminum visible under fluorescence microscopy. Positive Morin stain in macrophage cytoplasm is diagnostic for MMF on muscle biopsy. Answers C and D are incorrect because while nonspecific esterase and PAS with and without diastase are both strongly positive in the macrophages, these stains do not confirm the presence of aluminum. Answer A is incorrect because MHC class 1 is an inflammatory marker and does not detect aluminum. Answer E is incorrect because von Kossa stain is used in detecting calcium and does not stain aluminum.
