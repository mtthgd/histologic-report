Definition / general

* Aggressive high grade carcinoma defined by translocations involving the *NUTM1* gene with frequent foci of squamous differentiation

Essential features

* Highly aggressive malignancy with median survival of ~1 year
* Histologically defined by primitive but monotonous round cells, classically showing areas of abrupt keratinization
* Caused by translocations involving the *NUTM1* gene (15q14), most commonly with *BRD4* (19p13)
* Speckled nuclear positivity for NUT immunohistochemistry is sensitive and specific for diagnosis

Terminology

* NUT stands for nuclear protein in testis
* Formerly called NUT midline carcinoma due to proclivity for midline sites but midline was removed from name because it can occur at multiple sites

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8023/3](https://seer.cancer.gov/icd-o-3/sitetype.icdo3.20220429.pdf#page=117) - NUT associated carcinoma
* ICD-10: [C80.1](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C76-C80/C80-/C80.1) - malignant (primary) neoplasm, unspecified
* ICD-11: [XH2855](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#1892513495) - nuclear protein in testis (NUT) associated carcinoma

Epidemiology

* Affects all ages but most commonly people in their early 20s ([JNCI Cancer Spectr 2019;4:pkz094](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32328562))
* No sex or geographic predilection ([JNCI Cancer Spectr 2019;4:pkz094](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32328562))
* No clear risk factors established to date ([Pathol Int 2018;68:583](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30362654))

Sites

* Common in other midline sites such as the mediastinum but can occur anywhere in the body ([JNCI Cancer Spectr 2019;4:pkz094](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32328562))
* ~41% involve the head and neck, most commonly in the sinonasal tract ([JNCI Cancer Spectr 2019;4:pkz094](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32328562), [Front Surg 2023;10:1098704](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36936659), [Head Neck 2024;46:29](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37853958), [Histopathology 2024;85:317](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38708903))
* Most common within sinonasal tract, nasal cavity and maxillary sinus and extending to multiple subsites ([Int J Surg Pathol 2011;19:808](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20034986), [Front Surg 2023;10:1098704](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36936659), [Head Neck 2024;46:29](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37853958))

Etiology

* *NUTM1* rearrangement alone is sufficient for disease ([Ann Oncol 2017;28:890](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28203693))

Clinical features

* Aggressive malignancy with median survival of ~1 year ([Head Neck 2024;46:29](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37853958))
* Presents as rapidly enlarging mass ([JNCI Cancer Spectr 2019;4:pkz094](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32328562))
* Regional lymph node metastasis seen in 33% of cases and distant metastasis in 13% of cases at presentation ([Cancer 2016;122:3632](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27509377))
* Multiorgan dissemination occurs later in disease course ([Hum Pathol 2018;71:157](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29079177))
* Somewhat better prognosis in sinonasal tract than thoracic NUT carcinoma ([JNCI Cancer Spectr 2019;4:pkz094](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32328562))

Diagnosis

* Evidence of *NUTM1* alterations should be demonstrated for diagnosis, via either molecular testing or NUT immunohistochemistry

Radiology description

* On computed tomography (CT) scan: near muscle density mass with faint mineralization and without dystrophic calcifications ([Head Neck Pathol 2021;15:1409](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33686584))
  + Bone window: demineralization of bones surrounding sinus (e.g., ethmoid, maxilla, turbinate and uncinate process)
  + Soft tissue window: opacification of sinus, mass in the nasal cavity
* On magnetic resonance imaging (MRI): heterogeneous lesion with predominant hypointense signal on TI and hyperintense on T2, with heterogeneous postcontrast enhancement

Prognostic factors

* Maxillary sinus tumors may show improved prognosis ([Head Neck 2024;46:29](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37853958))
* *BRD4::NUTM1* confers poorer overall survival than *BRD3::NUTM1* or *NSD3::NUTM1* ([JNCI Cancer Spectr 2019;4:pkz094](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32328562))

Case reports

* 16 year old girl with maxillary sinus mass ([Front Oncol 2024;13:1296862](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38239638))
* 18 year old man with nasal cavity mass ([Onco Targets Ther 2021;14:4621](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34475768))
* 39 year old man with sinus and ear pressure ([Clin Case Rep 2021;9:e04616](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34429997))
* 42 year old man with maxillary sinus mass ([J Med Case Rep 2019;13:279](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31492174))
* 56 year old woman with nasal cavity mass ([Head Neck Pathol 2021;15:1409](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33686584))

Treatment

* Tumors generally show minimal response to radiation and conventional chemotherapy ([Head Neck 2024;46:29](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37853958))
* Tumors treated with immune checkpoint inhibitor in combination with chemotherapy may have better outcomes ([Lung Cancer 2024;189:107496](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38301600))
* In clinical trials, some patients show rapid response to extraterminal bromodomain inhibitors, although prognosis remains poor ([JCO Precis Oncol 2023;7:e2200633](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37384867))

Gross description

* Highly infiltrative tumor frequently involving multiple anatomic subsites
* Nonspecific white-tan cut surface commonly demonstrating prominent necrosis

Frozen section description

* Small round blue cell tumor
* Specific diagnosis is generally not possible on frozen section

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Primitive small to medium sized cells with minimal indistinct to clear cytoplasm
* Monotonous round to oval nuclei with variably prominent nucleoli
* High mitotic rate with prominent tumor necrosis
* Foci of abrupt squamous differentiation with clear to eosinophilic cytoplasm and keratin pearl formation seen in 33% of cases ([JNCI Cancer Spectr 2019;4:pkz094](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32328562))
* Rare head and neck cases with more diffuse squamous differentiation and papillary architecture ([Histopathology 2024;85:317](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38708903), [Head Neck Pathol 2024;18:9](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38381200), [Head Neck Pathol 2023;17:803](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37118352))
* Areas of spindled cells are frequently present
* Dense neutrophil infiltrate is seen in a subset of cases

Electron microscopy description

* Large, irregular nuclei with prominent compact nucleoli ([Ultrastruct Pathol 2012;36:280](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22849529))
* Abundant cytoplasm with prominent tonofilament bundles, scattered pleomorphic granules and numerous desmosomal junctions ([Ultrastruct Pathol 2012;36:280](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22849529))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Defined by translocations involving the *NUTM1* gene on 15q14 ([Cancer Discov 2014;4:928](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24875858))
* *BRD4::NUTM1* (19p13) fusion is present in ~67% of cases ([Cancer Res 2003;63:304](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12543779))
* Other partners include *BRD3, NSD3, ZNF532* and *ZNF592* ([Oncogene 2008;27:2237](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17934517), [Cancer Discov 2014;4:928](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24875858), [Proc Natl Acad Sci U S A 2017;114:E4184](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28484033))
* Fusions block epithelial differentiation and maintain proliferation in tumor cells ([Oncogene 2008;27:2237](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17934517), [Cancer Res 2023;83:3846](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37819236))
* In situ hybridization for high risk [HPV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshpv.html) and [EBV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) is uniformly negative ([Int J Surg Pathol 2013;21:102](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22777717))

Sample pathology report

* Maxillary sinus, excisional biopsy:
  + NUT carcinoma (see comment)
  + Comment: Immunostains show that the tumor cells are positive for AE1 / AE3, p40 and NUT and negative for CD99 and synaptophysin with intact SMARCB1 (INI1), supporting the above diagnosis. NUT carcinoma is a rare, highly aggressive malignancy that is defined by translocations involving the *NUTM1* gene. Please see [nc-registry.org](https://nc-registry.org/) for information about enrolling the patient into the International NUT Carcinoma Registry and opportunities for clinical trials.

Differential diagnosis

* [Basaloid squamous cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/oralcavitybasaloidscc.html):
  + Can also show abrupt keratinization and [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) / [p40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdeltaNp63.html) positivity
  + Negative for [NUT](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnutm1.html)
* [Sinonasal undifferentiated carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/nasalsnuc.html):
  + Also a high grade tumor
  + Has more cytologic atypia
  + Lacks keratinization
  + Negative for [NUT](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnutm1.html) and [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) / [p40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdeltaNp63.html)
  + Frequent *IDH2* mutations
* [High grade neuroendocrine carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/nasalsmallcell.html):
  + Also a high grade tumor
  + Lacks keratinization
  + Negative for [NUT](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnutm1.html) and [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) / [p40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdeltaNp63.html) and positive for [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html), [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html) and [INSM1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinsm1.html)
* [Adamantinoma-like Ewing sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneewing.html):
  + Can also show abrupt keratinization and [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) / [p40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdeltaNp63.html) positivity
  + Negative for [NUT](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnutm1.html) and positive for [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html) and [NKX2.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsNKX2.html)
* [*SMARCB1* deficient sinonasal carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/nasalSMARCB1deficient.html):
  + Also high grade tumor that can be positive for [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) / [p40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdeltaNp63.html)
  + Lacks keratinization
  + Negative for [NUT](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnutm1.html) and shows loss of [INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html)

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/nasalnutcarcinomarooper09.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/nasalnutcarcinomarooper09.jpg)

An 18 year old woman presented with a destructive sinonasal tumor; biopsy findings are shown above. What immunohistochemical finding would you expect?

1. Cytoplasmic positivity for cytokeratin without significant reactivity for any other markers
2. Diffuse strong positivity for synaptophysin and INSM1
3. Loss of nuclear INI1 expression
4. Speckled nuclear positivity for NUT1

Board review style answer #1

**D.** Speckled nuclear positivity for NUT1. The picture depicts a NUT carcinoma, the diagnosis of which is suggested by the presence of monotonous primitive cells interspersed with areas of abrupt keratinization. The correct immunohistochemical finding for this tumor is speckled nuclear positivity for NUT1, a sensitive and specific marker of this tumor type. Answers A, B and C are incorrect because these other immunoprofiles would be more characteristic of tumors that lack the characteristic keratinization pattern depicted in this photomicrograph, including sinonasal undifferentiated carcinoma (answer A), small cell carcinoma (answer B) and *SMARCB1* deficient sinonasal carcinoma (answer C).

What is the most common genetic abnormality identified in NUT carcinoma?
