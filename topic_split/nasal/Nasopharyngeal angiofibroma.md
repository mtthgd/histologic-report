Definition / general

* Locally aggressive fibrovascular neoplasm of the nasopharynx developed almost exclusively in adolescent and young male patients

Essential features

* Nasopharyngeal location
* Affecting adolescent or young male patients
* Histologically composed of vasculature of various sizes and cellular fibrotic stroma with fibroblasts

Terminology

* Juvenile nasopharyngeal angiofibroma, juvenile fibroma, angiofibroma

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9160/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=87) - angiofibroma, NOS
* ICD-10: [D10.6](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D10-/D10.6) - benign neoplasm of nasopharynx

Epidemiology

* 0.05 - 0.5% of all head and neck tumors ([StatPearls: Nasopharyngeal Angiofibroma [Accessed 10 June 2020]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31424824))
* Occurs almost exclusively in adolescent and young males, with a median age of diagnosis of 15 years (range: 8 - 27 years) ([Acta Otorrinolaringol Esp 2019;70:279](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30093086), [Head Neck Pathol 2018;12:52](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28508272))
* Although a handful of case reports describe nasopharyngeal angiofibroma in female patients, the documentation of these cases is not convincing to represent this particular entity ([Mol Clin Oncol 2018;9:702](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30546905), [J Laryngol Otol 2018;132:184](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29143693), [Eur Arch Otorhinolaryngol 2006;263:657](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16625398), [Indian J Dent Res 2004;15:145](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16035644), [AMA Arch Otolaryngol 1951;54:620](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14877367))
* May occur in patients with familial adenomatous polyposis ([Gastroenterology 1993;105:1550](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8224661), [Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 1995;113:435](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7567017), [ANZ J Surg 2013;83:387](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23614886))

Sites

* Arises in the nasopharynx or posterolateral wall of nasal cavity ([Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 2019;161:352](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31012381))
* May be locally aggressive, show extension into paranasal sinus, pterygopalatine fossa, infratemporal fossa and orbit ([Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 2019;161:352](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31012381))
* 10 - 37% have intracranial extension, usually into the middle cranial fossa ([Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 2019;161:352](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31012381), [StatPearls: Nasopharyngeal Angiofibroma [Accessed 10 June 2020]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31424824))

Etiology

* Etiology remains unclear
* May be hormonally affected given the predilection for adolescent male

Clinical features

* Highly vascular lesion with one or more feeding arterials
  + The most common feeding vessel is the internal maxillary artery, a branch of the external carotid artery ([StatPearls: Nasopharyngeal Angiofibroma [Accessed 10 June 2020]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31424824))
* Most frequently presents with epistaxis followed by nasal obstruction and rhinorrhea ([Head Neck Pathol 2018;12:52](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28508272), [J Oral Maxillofac Pathol 2016;20:330](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27601836))

Diagnosis

* Diagnosis is typically rendered using angiography, which allows visualization of the feeding vessels and preoperative embolization
* Given the risk of massive hemorrhage, preoperative biopsy and fine needle aspiration is strongly discouraged

Radiology description

* Angiography allows identification of a highly vascularized mass with a central feeding vessel

Prognostic factors

* Although considered as a benign mesenchymal neoplasm, nasopharyngeal angiofibroma is locally aggressive with approximately 20% local recurrence ([Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 2019;161:352](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31012381), [J Laryngol Otol 2018;132:978](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30345941))
* Cranial or orbital involvement and postoperative residual vascularity on imaging study are associated with increased risk of local recurrence ([Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 2019;161:352](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31012381))

Treatment

* Complete surgical resection is the treatment of choice
* Preoperative embolization through angiography may be performed

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Benign fibrovascular lesion composed of 2 components
  + Vascular space of various sizes, ranging from dilated branching vessel of various thickness to slit-like capillaries
  + Fibrous or collagenous stroma with fibroblasts
* Central area of the tumor is typically cellular, composed of fibroblasts or myofibroblasts with spindle, round or stellate morphology
* Stroma can be fibrous, edematous or collagenized
* Fibrinous thrombi may be seen in dilated vessels
* Frequently contain (abundant) mast cells
* Mitotic figures are usually absent
* Reference: [Chan: WHO Classification of Head and Neck Tumours, 4th Edition, 2017](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/9283224388/pathologyoutl-20)

Cytology description

* Given the nasopharyngeal location and the highly vascular nature of the lesion, fine needle aspiration should be avoided

Positive stains

* [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [HHF35](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsactinmusclespecific.html) / [h-caldesmon](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscaldesmon.html) in smooth muscle wall of large vasculature ([Pathology 2008;40:396](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18446631), [Head Neck Pathol 2018;12:52](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28508272))
* [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) and [ERG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsERG.html) highlight endothelial cells of vasculature
* [AR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsAR.html) is positive in stroma cells in 40 - 75% cases; the expression may be focal and weak ([Acta Otolaryngol 2015;135:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25384380), [Am J Clin Pathol 2006;125:832](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16690481), [Mod Pathol 1998;11:1122](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9831211))
* Reported positive rate of [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) is highly variable, ranging from 0% to over 90% partially depending on the subunits stained ([Acta Otolaryngol 2015;135:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25384380), [Am J Clin Pathol 2006;125:832](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16690481))
* [Beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html): nuclear staining in stromal cells (fibroblasts) in 70 - 90% of cases, membranous / cytoplasmic staining in endothelial cells ([J Laryngol Otol 2016;130:907](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27572281))

Negative stains

* [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html) and [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html) ([Pathology 2008;40:396](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18446631), [Head Neck Pathol 2018;12:52](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28508272))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Somatic mutation of *CTNNB1* (beta catenin) gene in 75% of cases ([Ann Otol Rhinol Laryngol 2019;128:1061](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31148463))

Sample pathology report

* Nasopharynx, resection:
  + Nasopharyngeal angiofibroma, 1.8 cm, margin negative for tumor (see comment)
  + Comment: Immunostain shows that the tumor is focally positive for AR. There is abnormal nuclear accumulation of beta catenin. The immunoprofile supports the diagnosis.

Differential diagnosis

* [Hemangioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuehemangioma.html):
  + May affect both genders, any age and is not limited to nasopharynx
  + Lacks cellular stroma enriched with fibroblasts
  + Vessels are typically uniform in size, whereas angiofibroma usually contains central large caliber vessels and peripheral slit-like vascular space
  + Does not show [AR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsAR.html) immunopositivity or nuclear [beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html)
* [Inflammatory sinonasal polyp](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/nasalpolypinflammatory.html):
  + May contain fibrous to edematous stroma but usually hypocellular
  + Lack rich vasculature seen in angiofibroma
  + Does not show [AR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsAR.html) immunopositivity or nuclear [beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html)
  + Occurs in nasal cavity or paranasal sinuses, rather than nasopharynx
* [Nasal turbinate](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/nasalnormalanatomy.html):
  + Normal nasal turbinate is vascular rich, containing large caliber blood vessels with muscle wall
  + Lacks hypercellular stroma and slit-like vasculatures of angiofibroma

Board review style question #3

Which of the following modalities is most commonly used in the diagnosis and preoperative evaluation of sinonasal tract angiofibroma (STA)?

1. Fine needle aspiration (FNA)
2. Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI)
3. Positron emission tomography (PET) scan
4. Ultrasonography (US)

Board review style answer #3

**B**. Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI). MRI is a common imaging modality for evaluating STA due to its excellent soft tissue contrast and ability to delineate the extent of the tumor and its relationship to adjacent structures (including intracranial extension). Answer A is incorrect since FNA biopsy is not routinely recommended due to the highly vascular nature of the neoplasm. Occasionally, severe hemorrhage can occur with minor manipulation due to the number of vascular channels, the lack of a predictable muscular layer or lack of an elastic layer or paucity of elastic fibers within the dense stroma. Answer C is incorrect because PET scans are generally not used for primary diagnostic evaluation of STA. Answer D is incorrect because ultrasonography is not commonly used for assessing STA due to limited visualization of deep structures.
