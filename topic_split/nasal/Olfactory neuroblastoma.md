Definition / general

* Malignant neuroectodermal tumor commonly located at superior aspect of nasal cavity showing neuroblastic differentiation ([El-Naggar: WHO Classification of Head and Neck Tumours, 4th Edition, 2017](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/9283224388/pathologyoutl-20))
* Thought to arise from olfactory membrane or olfactory placode (plate-like thickening of embryonic ectoderm from which a nerve ganglion or sensory organ will develop) which extends from roof of nasal cavity in fetus to midnasal septum and superior turbinate
* Not related to neuroblastoma elsewhere in body

Essential features

* Originated and centered around cribriform plate of nasal cavity
* Small blue round cell tumor with lobulated growth pattern and abundant neuropil
* Homer Wright pseudorosettes and Flexner-Wintersteiner rosettes may be seen
* The most important prognostic factors are Hyams grade and Kadish stage

Terminology

* Also called esthesioneuroblastoma, esthesioneuroepithelioma or olfactory placode tumor

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9522/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=91) - Olfactory neuroblastoma
* ICD-10: [C30.0](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C30-C39/C30-/C30) - Malignant neoplasm of nasal cavity and middle ear

Epidemiology

* Patients of all ages, 2 to 90 years
* No obvious gender, ethnic or familial predilection
* Annual incidence of 0.4 per million ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:S1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28060373), [Head Neck Pathol 2009;3:252](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20596981))
* 2% of all sinonasal neoplasms

Sites

* Usually confined to the cribriform plate and upper nasal vault; rarely in nasopharynx, maxillary or ethmoid sinus ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:S1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28060373), [Head Neck Pathol 2009;3:252](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20596981))
* May become locally invasive into paranasal sinuses, nasopharynx, palate, orbit, skull base, brain

Pathophysiology

* Theorized to originate from specialized sensory neuroepithelium located in superior nasal cavity, including cribriform plate of ethmoid, nasal roof, superior nasal concha and superior nasal septum ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:S1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28060373), [Head Neck Pathol 2009;3:252](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20596981))

Etiology

* None identified

Clinical features

* Usually presents with nonspecific syndromes of nasal obstruction, epistaxis, headache and rhinorrhea ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:S1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28060373), [Head Neck Pathol 2009;3:252](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20596981))
* Rare symptoms include anosmia, visual disturbance, paraneoplastic syndromes (e.g. syndrome of inappropriate antidiuretic hormone secretion (SIADH))

Radiology description

* Typical finding is a dumbbell shaped mass centered at cribriform plate

Prognostic factors

* 20% develop regional or distant metastases, usually to cervical lymph nodes and lung; late recurrence (after 10 years) is common
* Grade and stage are the most important prognostic factors
* Hyams histologic grade (Table 1) is the most widely used grading system which encompasses six histologic features and is an independent prognostic factor ([Head Neck Pathol 2015;9:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24806334), Hyams VJ. Olfactory neuroblastoma (Case 6) In: Batsakis JG, Hyams VJ, Morales AR, editors, Special tumors of the head and neck, Chicago: ASCP Press; 1982. pp. 24-29)
* Kadish staging system ([Cancer 1976;37:1571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1260676), [Head Neck 2017;39:1962](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28815831), [Head Neck Pathol 2015;9:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24806334)) and Morita modification (Table 2) ([Head Neck Pathol 2015;9:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24806334), [Neurosurgery 1993;32:706](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8492845)) are the staging systems that are most commonly used: the 5 year survival for stages A, B and C are 75%, 68% and 41%, respectively ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:S1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28060373), [Head Neck Pathol 2009;3:252](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20596981))

Treatment

* Complete surgical excision (may require craniofacial resection), with radiation therapy or chemotherapy

Gross description

* Red-gray, highly vascular, polypoid mass

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Low grade olfactory neuroblastoma usually contains nests and lobules of monotonous tumor cells with round nuclei, indistinct nucleoli and scanty cytoplasm in association with a vascular-rich to hyalinized stroma; fibrillary neural matrix may be present
* High grade tumors may show solid growth, marked mitotic activity, nuclear pleomorphism, necrosis and no neuropil
* Homer Wright pseudorosettes: neoplastic cells palisading around a central zone of fibrillar neural matrix; their presence in a nasal tumor is characteristic for olfactory neuroblastoma
* Flexner-Wintersteiner rosettes: palisading tumor cells surrounding a true central lumen
* Perivascular pseudorosettes may be present but are non-specific
* Other findings: melanin pigment, neurons, divergent differentiation (e.g. rhabdomyoblastic, glandular and squamous differentiation)

Positive stains

* [Synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html), [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html), [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html), [neurofilament](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsneurofilament.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) in sustentacular cells at periphery of cell nests
* [Keratins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), e.g. [cytokeratin AE1/AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [CAM5.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscam52.html) and [CK18](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck18.html), are commonly negative, but up to a third may show focal positivity
* [INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html)
* Area of rhabdomyoblastic differentiation if present is positive for [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) and [myogenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html)
* [Calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:1786](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22020045))

Negative stains

* [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html), [FLI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfli1.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html), [p40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdeltaNp63.html), [EBER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html), [NUT](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnutm1.html), lymphoid markers (e.g. [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html))
* With the exception of olfactory neuroblastoma with rhabdomyoblastic differentiation, the tumor is negative for [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) and [myogenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html)

Electron microscopy description

* Dense core neurosecretory granules, microtubules, neuritic processes, neurofilaments ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:S1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28060373), [Head Neck Pathol 2009;3:252](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20596981))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Recent next generation sequencing studies have shown that olfactory neuroblastoma has high level but heterogenous chromosomal instability and copy number alterations ([Oncotarget 2016;7:52584](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27256979))
* Absence of characteristic fusions of other small blue round cell tumors that may occur in the sinonasal tract, e.g. Ewing sarcoma *EWSR1* fusion and alveolar rhabdomyosarcoma *PAX3* or *PAX7* fusion

Sample pathology report

* Nasal cavity, left; biopsy:
  + Olfactory neuroblastoma, Hyams grade II (see comment)
  + Comment: Immunohistochemistry studies show that the tumor is positive for synaptophysin and chromogranin, whereas negative for cytokeratin. S100 highlighted the sustentacular network. The overall immunoprofile supports the diagnosis.

Differential diagnosis

Quite broad; includes all sinonasal small blue round cell tumors ([Surg Pathol Clin 2017;10:103](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28153129))* [Lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBnasal.html): positive for [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html) and other lymphoid markers
* [Melanoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticmucosalmelanoma.html): positive for [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html), [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html), [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html)
* Small round cell sarcoma:
  + [Ewing sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneewing.html): positive for [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html), [FLI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfli1.html), t(11,22) / *FLI1::EWSR1* fusion
  + [Embryonal rhabdomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueembryonalrhabdo.html): diffusely positive for [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) and [myogenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html)
  + [Alveolar rhabdomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuealvrhabdo.html): diffusely positive for [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) and [myogenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html), *PAX3* or *PAX7* fusion
  + [Mesenchymal chondrosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueeskchondrosarcomamesenchymal.html): *NCOA2* fusion
* [Carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/nasalcarcinomageneral.html):
  + Small cell carcinoma / poorly differentiated neuroendocrine carcinoma: diffusely positive for [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), negative for [neurofilament](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsneurofilament.html) and [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)
  + [Sinonasal undifferentiated carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/nasalsnuc.html): negative for [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html) and [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html)
  + [NUT midline carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/nasalnutcarcinoma.html): positive for [NUT](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnutm1.html)
  + [Adenoid cystic carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/nasaladenocarcinoma.html): negative for [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html) and [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html); positive for myoepithelial markers [calponin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalponin.html), [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) and [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) and *MYB::NFIB* fusion
  + SMARCB1-deficient sinonasal carcinoma: loss of [INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html)
