Definition / general

* Aggressive carcinoma defined by *SMARCB1* gene mutations and associated loss of immunohistochemical expression
* Provisional entity in [El-Naggar: WHO Classification of Head and Neck Tumors, 4th Edition, 2017](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/9283224388/pathologyoutl-20)

Essential features

* Sinonasal carcinoma defined by inactivation of *SMARCB1* gene
* Loss of *SMARCB1* (INI1) immunohistochemical expression is highly sensitive and specific for diagnosis in this site
* Tumors are high grade but demonstrate cytological uniformity
* Tumors have variable proportions of basaloid and rhabdoid / plasmacytoid cells
* Highly aggressive clinical behavior

Terminology

* *SMARCB1* stands for SWI/SNF related, matrix associated, actin dependent regulator of chromatin, subfamily B, member 1
* The protein product of the *SMARCB1* gene is also sometimes known as INI1, which stands for integrase interactor 1

ICD coding

* ICD-10:
  + [C30.0](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C30-C39/C30-/C30.0) - malignant neoplasm of nasal cavity
  + [C31.1](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C30-C39/C31-/C31.1) - malignant neoplasm of ethmoidal sinus

Epidemiology

* Described in patients from 16 to 89 years old ([Virchows Arch 2015;467:649](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26407663), [Pathol Res Pract 2017;213:133](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28069272))
* M:F = 3:2 ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:458](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28291122))
* No clear clinical risk factors documented to date

Sites

* Exclusively occurs in sinonasal tract
* Most commonly arises in nasal cavity or ethmoid sinus but frequently involves multiple sites ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:458](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28291122))

Clinical features

* Most present with symptoms of mass effect such as nasal obstruction, headache, epistaxis, proptosis or visual deficits ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:458](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28291122))
* More than 80% present with stage T4 disease with bone, skull base or periorbital invasion

Radiology description

* Avid contrast enhancement with intermediate to low T2 signal and fluorodeoxyglucose (FDG) avidity
* Frequent calcification with occasional hair on end periosteal reaction ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:1274](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24832165))

Treatment

* Best outcomes with trimodality therapy including surgical resection, external beam radiation and systemic chemotherapy ([Head Neck Pathol 2017;11:256](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27644951), [Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:458](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28291122))

Gross description

* Highly infiltrative tumor that frequently invades multiple sinonasal sites ([Head Neck Pathol 2017;11:256](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27644951))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Although tumors show high grade histologic features including prominent necrosis and mitoses, cytology is relatively uniform
* Nuclei are round to oval with smooth nuclear contours
* Two common morphologic variants: plasmacytoid / rhabdoid (40% of cases) and basaloid (60% of cases) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:458](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28291122))
* Plasmacytoid / rhabdoid variant composed entirely of sheets of plasmacytoid / rhabdoid cells with abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm and eccentric nuclei
* Basaloid variant composed of nests and lobules of basaloid cells with high nuclear cytoplasmic ratio and peripheral palisading and scattered plasmacytoid / rhabdoid cells
* No overt squamous differentiation should be identified ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:1282](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25007146))
* Frequent formation of nonspecific vacuoles and occasional overt glandular differentiation ([Hum Pathol 2019;83:59](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30120966))
* Subset shows papillary / exophytic growth ([Virchows Arch 2015;467:649](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26407663))
* No associated surface dysplasia but pagetoid spread in overlying epithelium can be seen ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:1274](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24832165))

Loss of *SMARCB1* (INI1)

Cytology description

* Hypercellular smears with clusters of cohesive polygonal cells
* Variable proportion of individual rhabdoid / plasmacytoid cells with abundant cytoplasm
* Uniform nuclei with fine chromatin and small nucleoli
* No overt keratinization or other evidence of squamous differentiation ([Diagn Cytopathol 2016;44:700](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27177850))
* Apoptotic bodies common with abundant background necrotic debris ([Cancer Cytopathol 2018;126:567](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29797680))

Positive stains

* Virtually always diffusely positive for [pancytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html)
* Squamous markers [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) / [p40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdeltaNp63.html) positive in > 50% of cases
* Neuroendocrine markers [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html) / [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html) positive in 30% of cases
* Small subset of cases express diffuse [p16](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp16.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:458](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28291122))

Negative stains

* Defined by loss of [*SMARCB1* (INI1)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html) expression with retained positivity in stroma
* Myoepithelial markers such as [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) and [calponin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalponin.html) are usually negative
* Consistently negative for [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html) / [NKX2.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsNKX2.html) and [NUT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnutm1.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Defined by inactivating mutations of *SMARCB1* gene, a tumor suppressor gene located on chromosome 22q11.2 ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:1274](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24832165), [Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:1282](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25007146))
* FISH less sensitive for diagnosis than immunohistochemistry, likely due to heterozygous deletions
* Next generation sequencing has not identified other driver mutations within the limited number of cases evaluated ([Pathol Res Pract 2017;213:133](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28069272))
* In situ hybridization for high risk HPV and EBV is consistently negative ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:458](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28291122))

Sample pathology report

* Nasal mass, biopsy:
  + *SMARCB1* deficient sinonasal carcinoma (see comment)
  + Comment: Immunostains show that the tumor cells are positive for AE1/AE3 and p40, focally positive for synaptophysin and INSM1 and negative for NUT1, CD99, p16, S100 and calponin with loss of *SMARCB1* (INI1) expression. *SMARCB1* deficient sinonasal carcinoma is an aggressive tumor unique to the sinonasal tract that is defined by loss of *SMARCB1* (INI1) expression ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:458](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28291122)).

Differential diagnosis

* [Sinonasal undifferentiated carcinoma:](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/nasalsnuc.html)
  + Also high grade but shows more prominent cytologic atypia and lacks rhabdoid /
    plasmacytoid cells
  + Negative for [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) / [p40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdeltaNp63.html) and [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html) / [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html) with frequent *IDH2* mutations
* [NUT carcinoma:](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/nasalnutcarcinoma.html)
  + Also high grade and frequently positive for [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) / [p40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdeltaNp63.html)
  + Shows abrupt keratinization and positivity for [NUT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnutm1.html) with retained [*SMARCB1* (INI1)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html)
* [Myoepithelial carcinoma:](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuemyoepithelialca.html)
  + Also demonstrates rhabdoid / plasmacytoid morphology and a subset show loss of [*SMARCB1* (INI1)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html)
  + Positive for myoepithelial markers [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) and [calponin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalponin.html)
* [Adamantinoma-like Ewing sarcoma:](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneewing.html)
  + Also high grade and frequently positive for [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) / [p40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdeltaNp63.html) and [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html) / [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html)
  + Positive for [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html) / **NKX2.1** with retained [*SMARCB1* (INI1)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html)
* [High grade neuroendocrine carcinoma:](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/esophagusneuroendocrine.html)
  + Also high grade and positive for [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html) / [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html)
  + Speckled chromatin and retained [*SMARCB1* (INI1)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html)
* [HPV related multiphenotypic sinonasal carcinoma:](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/HPVrelatedmultiphenotypiccarcinoma.html)
  + Also high grade with basaloid features and positivity for [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) / [p40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdeltaNp63.html)
  + Distinct biphasic basal and ductal populations, positive for HPV and
    retained [*SMARCB1* (INI1)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html)

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/nasalSMARCB1deficientRooperBRQ.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/nasalSMARCB1deficientRooperBRQ.jpg)

A 30 year old man presented with a destructive sinonasal tumor. Biopsy findings are shown in the
photomicrograph above. What molecular alteration is thought to be the driver of this tumor?

1. Mutations in *IDH2*
2. *EWSR1* translocations
3. *NUT1* translocations
4. Inactivation of *SMARCB1*

Board review style answer #1

**D.** Inactivation of *SMARCB1*. This photomicrograph depicts a *SMARCB1* deficient sinonasal carcinoma, an aggressive sinonasal tumor that is defined by genetic inactivation of the *SMARCB1* gene with corresponding immunohistochemical loss of *SMARCB1* (INI1) protein expression. The other genetic abnormalities listed here are characteristic of other high grade sinonasal tumors that fall into the differential diagnosis of *SMARCB1* deficient sinonasal carcinoma, including IDH2 mutations in sinonasal undifferentiated carcinoma, EWSR1 translocation in adamantinoma-like Ewing sarcoma and NUT1 translocation in NUT carcinoma; these other tumor types all demonstrate retained immunohistochemical expression of SMARCB1.

Board review style question #2

What feature would essentially exclude the diagnosis of *SMARCB1* deficient sinonasal carcinoma?

1. Basaloid cell nests with peripheral palisading and strong p40 positivity
2. Diffuse positivity for p16 immunohistochemistry and high risk HPV RNA in situ hybridization
3. Formation of glandular lumina with mucin production
4. Retained *SMARCB1* signals by FISH

Board review style answer #2

**B.** Diffuse positivity for p16 immunohistochemistry and high risk HPV RNA in situ hybridization. *SMARCB1* deficient sinonasal carcinoma can show a variety of morphologic features and a highly variable immunohistochemical profile. Histologically, *SMARCB1* deficient sinonasal carcinoma is composed of various proportions of basaloid and plasmacytoid / rhabdoid cells; a subset of cases can show glandular differentiation. The most sensitive and specific feature for diagnosis is consistent loss of *SMARCB1* (INI1) expression by immunohistochemistry; while FISH generally shows loss as well, signals can be retained in rare cases due to heterozygous mutations. Otherwise *SMARCB1* deficient sinonasal carcinoma is immunohistochemically heterogeneous with variable positivity for p63 / p40 and synaptophysin / chromogranin; a subset of cases can even demonstrate diffuse p16 positivity. However, in situ hybridization for high risk HPV should be consistently negative.
