Definition / general

* Uncommon benign entity first described in 1971 ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1991;71:583](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2047099))

Terminology

* Also called acinar adenomatoid hyperplasia ([Barnes: Surgical Pathology of the Head and Neck, 3rd Edition, 2008 [p500]](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0849390230/pathologyoutl-20))

Epidemiology

* Most cases reported in Caucasians, uncommon in Asians ([Barnes: Surgical Pathology of the Head and Neck, 3rd Edition, 2008 [p500]](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0849390230/pathologyoutl-20))
* Ages 9 to 79 years; 58% male ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1991;71:583](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2047099))

Sites

* 80% on hard and soft palate

Etiology

* Chronic, local trauma (smokers, denture wearers) may be contributing factor ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol Oral Radiol Endod 1995;79:482](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7614210))

Clinical features

* Presents as painless, sessile swelling at junction of hard and soft palate; may mimic a salivary gland tumor ([Barnes: Surgical Pathology of the Head and Neck, 3rd Edition, 2008 [p500]](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0849390230/pathologyoutl-20))

Prognostic factors

* Does not appear to predispose to benign or malignant salivary gland neoplasia

Case reports

* 48 year old Asian man with painless swelling in palate ([J Laryngol Otol 1996;110:167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8729505))

Treatment

* Excision is curative ([Barnes: Surgical Pathology of the Head and Neck, 3rd Edition, 2008 [p500]](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0849390230/pathologyoutl-20))

Gross description

* Localized swelling resembling a neoplasm, 0.5 - 2.7 cm

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Multiple aggregates, varying in size, of normal appearing mucous acini surrounded by fibrous connective tissue stroma
* Normal ductal structures
* Overlying palatal epithelium is usually normal but may have lichenoid reaction or pseudoepitheliomatous hyperplasia
* Occasionally, areas of interstitial mucous extravasation with associated inflammatory reaction ([Barnes: Surgical Pathology of the Head and Neck, 3rd Edition, 2008 [p500]](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0849390230/pathologyoutl-20)) but typically no significant inflammatory cell infiltrates ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1991;71:583](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2047099))

Differential diagnosis

* Idiopathic hyperplasia of the sublingual gland or "pouting" sublingual glands is seen as an **enlargement in the floor of the mouth** in totally or partially edentulous patients; sublingual glands may be otherwise normal or show nonspecific chronic inflammation
