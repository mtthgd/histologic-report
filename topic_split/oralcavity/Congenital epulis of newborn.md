Definition / general

* Congenital granular cell tumor of alveolar mucosa of jaw in newborns
* 90% girls; 10% present as multiple lesions
* Doesn't recur, even if incompletely excised
* 0.5 - 2 cm soft, pedunculated mucosal mass
* Spontaneously regresses over first 8 months of life ([Am J Surg Pathol 1981;5:37](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6264813))

Terminology

* Congenital granular cell myoblastoma, granular cell epulis of infancy and granular cell fibroblastoma

Sites

* Exclusively on anterior alveolar ridge of maxilla and mandible, usually overlying future canine and lateral incisor teeth

Case reports

* Newborn girl with 2 cm tumor ([Pediatrics 2001;107:E22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11158496))
* 2 day old girl with 4 x 2 cm exophytic growth ([Pan Afr Med J 2012;12:105](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23133705))
* 3 week old girl with spontaneous regression ([J Med Case Rep 2010;4:331](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20964807))
* 3 week old girl had a pedunculated lesion of the anterior maxillary alveolar process, which was excised ([Case of the Month #524](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case524.htm))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Confluent to nodular submucosal growth of tumors composed of large polygonal cells with eosinophilic granular cytoplasm and small central nuclei
* Overlying epithelium is atrophic; does NOT show pseudoepitheliomatous hyperplasia (common with granular cell tumor of adults)

Positive stains

* [Vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html), [acid phosphatase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsacidphosphatase.html)

Negative stains

* [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) (in contrast to granular cell tumor of adults), **laminin**, [Leu7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd57.html), [NSE](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsNSE.html)

Differential diagnosis

* [Granular cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuegranularcell.html):
  + Adults, infiltrates deep tissues, overlying epithelial hyperplasia
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)+
* [Verruciform xanthoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/oralcavityverruciformxanthoma.html):
  + Adults, overlying papillomatosis

Board review style question #1

Which of the following is a feature of congenital granular cell epulis?

1. Clinical presentation at ages 5 - 15
2. Detection within first trimester
3. Female predilection
4. Local recurrence after excision
5. Pseudoepitheliomatous hyperplasia

Board review style answer #1

**C.** Female predilection. The characteristic presentation of CGCE is the presence of a solitary, soft pedunculated mass on the anterior maxillary alveolar ridge in a female infant. The female to male ratio is striking at nearly 8:1. Pseudoepitheliomatous hyperplasia is not present (the overlying epithelium is attenuated) and recurrence has not been reported ([J Neonatal Surg 2015;4:17](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26034711/)).
