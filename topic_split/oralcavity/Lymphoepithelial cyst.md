Definition / general

* Lymphoepithelial cysts are uncommon benign oral cavity cysts

Essential features

* Oral lymphoepithelial cysts are most common in the lateral tongue and floor of mouth
* Histologic features include a squamous epithelial lined cyst filled with keratin debris and stromal lymphoid tissue
* Treatment is conservative surgical excision with no recurrence

Terminology

* Oral lymphoepithelial cyst
* Intraoral lymphoepithelial cyst
* Benign lymphoepithelial cyst

ICD coding

* ICD-10:
  + [K09.8](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/K00-K95/K00-K14/K09-/K09.8) - other cysts of oral region, not elsewhere classified
  + [K09.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/K00-K95/K00-K14/K09-/K09.9) - cyst of oral region, unspecified

Epidemiology

* < 1% of all lesions in oral cavity
* Occurs in adults
  + Most common in fifth to sixth decade
  + F > M ([Head Neck Pathol 2021 Jun 29 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34185247))

Sites

* Oral cavity ([Head Neck Pathol 2021 Jun 29 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34185247), [J Oral Pathol Med 2020;49:219](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31782199))
  + Most common site: ventral and posterolateral tongue
  + Second most common site: floor of mouth
  + Other sites in the oral cavity: buccal mucosa, palate, tonsillar pillar, lip

Etiology

* Unknown:
  + Leading theory: pseudocysts develop from obstruction of tonsil crypt of oral tonsil tissue ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1970;29:295](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/5263531))
  + Another theory postulates traumatic implantation of epithelium into deeper tissues, forming cyst ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1963;16:1214](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14079991))
  + A third theory postulates lymphoid tissue with ectopic foci of entrapped glandular epithelium ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1966;21:120](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/5215902))
* Oral lymphoepithelial cyst is not associated with human immunodeficiency virus (HIV), unlike a subset of lymphoepithelial cysts of the major salivary glands

Clinical features

* Small, white-yellow submucosal nodule
* < 1 cm
* Often painless and asymptomatic
* References: [Head Neck Pathol 2021 Jun 29 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34185247), [J Oral Pathol Med 2020;49:219](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31782199)

Diagnosis

* Combination of clinical and histologic findings

Prognostic factors

* Good prognosis
* No recurrence after excision

Case reports

* 21 year old man with lymphoepithelial cyst in the palatine tonsil ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2015;8:4264](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26097623))
* 42 year old woman with multiple lymphoepithelial cysts ([Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2013;56:473](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24441255))
* 55 and 57 year old women with oral lymphoepithelial cysts ([Bull Tokyo Dent Coll 2012;53:17](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22452887))

Treatment

* Conservative surgical excision

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Well circumscribed cyst lined by parakeratinized stratified squamous epithelium with lymphoid stroma
  + May have connection to overlying epithelium but not always identified
  + Other cyst linings: nonkeratinized squamous epithelium, respiratory metaplasia or ciliated pseudostratified epithelium (or any combination)
  + Cyst contents: predominantly keratin debris, amorphous eosinophilic material and inflammatory cells
  + Lymphoid stroma may or may not have germinal centers
  + Rarely subgemmal neurogenous plaques have been reported near the cyst ([Head Neck Pathol 2021 Jun 29 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34185247), [J Oral Pathol Med 2020;49:219](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31782199))

Positive stains

* Cyst lining: [p40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdeltaNp63.html)+
* Lymphoid stroma: [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html)+ cells predominant, rare [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html)+ ([J Oral Pathol Med 2020;49:219](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31782199))

Sample pathology report

* Floor of mouth, biopsy:
  + Oral lymphoepithelial cyst

Differential diagnosis

* [Epidermal inclusion cyst (epidermoid cyst)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocytickeratinouscystepidermal.html):
  + Squamous lined cyst without lymphoid stroma
* **Sebaceous cyst**:
  + Squamous lined cyst with adnexal structures, lacking lymphoid stroma
* [Fordyce granules](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/oralcavityfordyce.html):
  + Clusters of sebaceous glands
* [Sialolith](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandssialolithiasis.html):
  + Calcified material with surrounding ductal epithelium (with squamous metaplasia)
* [Mucocele](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/oralcavitymucocele.html):
  + Pseudocyst filled with mucin, lined by histiocytes and lymphocytes
* [Foreign body reaction](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorforeignbodyreaction.html):
  + Numerous multinucleated giant cells with poorly formed granulomas with foreign material
* [Squamous cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/oralcavityscc.html):
  + Invasive proliferation of squamous nests
