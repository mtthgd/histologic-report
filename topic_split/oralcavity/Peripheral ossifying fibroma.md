Definition / general

* Reactive gingival proliferation characterized by spindle cell proliferation with calcification or ossification

Essential features

* Reactive gingival lesion; thought to be associated with chronic irritation
* Spindle cell proliferation with calcification or ossification within gingival connective tissue

Terminology

* Other terms include peripheral cementifying fibroma, peripheral cemento-ossifying fibroma, ossifying fibrous epulis, calcifying fibrous epulis ([J Indian Soc Periodontol 2014;18:527](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25210274))
* Should not be confused with odontogenic / central bone tumors, such as ossifying fibroma and cemento-ossifying fibroma

ICD coding

* ICD-10:
  + [K06.8](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/K00-K95/K00-K14/K06-/K06.8) - other specified disorders of gingiva and edentulous alveolar ridge
  + [K13.6](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/K00-K95/K00-K14/K13-/K13.6) - irritative hyperplasia of oral mucosa

Epidemiology

* More common in females (F:M = ~2:1) ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1987;63:452](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3472146), [Med Oral Patol Oral Cir Bucal 2022;27:e460](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35717619), [J Indian Soc Periodontol 2015;19:83](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25810599), [Pediatr Dent 2001;23:245](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11447957))
* Second to fourth decades (children and young adults) ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1987;63:452](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3472146), [Med Oral Patol Oral Cir Bucal 2022;27:e460](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35717619), [Br J Oral Maxillofac Surg 2019;57:1081](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31601435))
* Can occur at any age, reports range from 2 months to 87 years ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol Oral Radiol 2020;130:e74](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32107185), [Med Oral Patol Oral Cir Bucal 2022;27:e460](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35717619))
* ~5% of oral hyperplastic lesions ([Braz J Otorhinolaryngol 2019;85:399](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29705120), [Indian J Dent Res 2018;29:61](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29442089), [Acta Odontol Latinoam 2015;28:103](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26355878))
* ~20% of gingival hyperplastic lesions ([Quintessence Int 2007;38:103](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17263149), [J Oral Pathol Med 2010;39:631](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20456619))

Sites

* Arise exclusively on the gingiva
* Maxilla more common than mandible ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1987;63:452](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3472146), [Pediatr Dent 2001;23:245](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11447957))
* ~67% in incisor cuspid region ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1987;63:452](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3472146), [Med Oral Patol Oral Cir Bucal 2022;27:e460](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35717619), [J Indian Soc Periodontol 2015;19:83](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25810599))

Pathophysiology

* Unknown, likely a reaction to chronic irritation ([J Indian Soc Periodontol 2015;19:83](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25810599))
* Hormonal influence has been proposed since the lesion is common in adolescents and young women ([Niger Med J 2011;52:35](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21968923))
  + However, lesional cells do not express estrogen or progesterone receptors (ER / PR) ([J Oral Sci 2010;52:95](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20339239))

Etiology

* Associated with chronic irritation, poor dentition and dental prosthetics, especially if poorly fitted ([J Indian Soc Periodontol 2015;19:83](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25810599))
* Thought to arise from the periodontal ligament ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2020;46:151510](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32252013))

Clinical features

* Slow growing nodule with a mean time to presentation of 16 months ([Med Oral Patol Oral Cir Bucal 2022;27:e460](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35717619))
* Generally painless or causing mild discomfort ([J Indian Soc Periodontol 2015;19:83](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25810599))
* May ulcerate ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1987;63:452](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3472146))
* Indolent clinical course but in some cases, the size and location may interfere with normal oral function, displace teeth and distort local anatomy ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2021;84:106127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34153696), [Ann Maxillofac Surg 2016;6:300](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28299276), [Head Neck Pathol 2013;7:356](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23857548))
* Recurrence in ~15% despite surgical excision ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1987;63:452](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3472146), [Br J Oral Maxillofac Surg 2019;57:1081](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31601435), [J Indian Soc Periodontol 2015;19:83](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25810599))
* Multifocal disease is rare ([Indian J Dent Res 2014;25:220](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24992855))

Diagnosis

* Difficult to distinguish clinically from other entities (pyogenic granuloma, oral irritation fibroma, giant cell fibroma, peripheral giant cell granuloma, metastatic lesions)
* Readily diagnosed histologically with high interobserver concordance ([Med Oral Patol Oral Cir Bucal 2022;27:e460](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35717619))

Radiology description

* May show irregular radiopacity due to calcification / ossification or show mild alveolar bone resorption ([Dentomaxillofac Radiol 2007;36:180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17463105), [Med Oral Patol Oral Cir Bucal 2022;27:e460](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35717619), [Head Neck Pathol 2013;7:356](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23857548))

Prognostic factors

* Benign but may recur
* Recurrence is more likely if offending irritant is not removed ([J Indian Soc Periodontol 2015;19:83](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25810599))

Case reports

* 2 month old girl with peripheral ossifying fibroma ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol Oral Radiol 2020;130:e74](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32107185))
* 24 year old man with multifocal disease ([Indian J Dent Res 2014;25:220](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24992855))
* 25 year old woman with 4 cm tumor interfering with oral function ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2021;84:106127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34153696))

Treatment

* Surgical excision ([J Indian Soc Periodontol 2015;19:83](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25810599))
* Removal of irritants including plaque / calculus or poorly fitted prosthetics ([J Indian Soc Periodontol 2015;19:83](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25810599))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Variably cellular fibroblastic spindle cell proliferation in subepithelial connective tissue in variably collagenous background ([J Indian Soc Periodontol 2015;19:83](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25810599))
  + Collagen deposition is more prominent in the deep aspect ([J Indian Soc Periodontol 2015;19:83](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25810599))
* Dystrophic calcification, lamellar or woven bone formation is essential for diagnosis ([J Indian Soc Periodontol 2015;19:83](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25810599))
* Overlying hyperplastic epithelium with hyperkeratosis ([J Indian Soc Periodontol 2015;19:83](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25810599))
  + Surface epithelium may be ulcerated ([J Indian Soc Periodontol 2015;19:83](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25810599), [Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1987;63:452](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3472146))
* Background contains variable chronic inflammatory infiltrate, predominantly lymphoplasmacytic and proliferating small capillary sized vessels ([J Indian Soc Periodontol 2015;19:83](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25810599), [Med Oral Patol Oral Cir Bucal 2022;27:e460](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35717619))

Positive stains

* Not used in routine diagnosis
* [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) in 73% ([Br J Oral Maxillofac Surg 2019;57:1081](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31601435))
* [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html) in 54% ([Br J Oral Maxillofac Surg 2019;57:1081](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31601435))
* [SATB2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssatb2.html) in 100% ([Head Neck Pathol 2022;16:339](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34224081), [Ann Diagn Pathol 2020;46:151510](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32252013))
* Positive for osteoblastic / cementoblastic proteins **RUNX2**, **BMP2**, **CAP** in one study and **osterix (OSX)** in another ([J Oral Pathol Med 2015;44:628](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25359431), [Braz Oral Res 2017;31:e53](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28678972))

* [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) / [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html) ([J Oral Sci 2010;52:95](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20339239))

Sample pathology report

* Gingiva, excision:
  + Peripheral ossifying fibroma (see comment)
  + Comment: Sections include oral squamous mucosa with underlying connective tissues and show features of a peripheral ossifying fibroma. The mucosa shows focal ulceration with organizing granulation tissue. The submucosal tissues show a well demarcated but unencapsulated lesion composed of areas of fibrosis and metaplastic ossification. There is no dysplasia or malignancy.

Differential diagnosis

* [Oral irritation fibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/oralcavityirritationfibroma.html):
  + Clinically difficult to distinguish
  + Histologically lacks calcification or bone formation
* [Peripheral giant cell granuloma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/oralcavityperipheralgiantcellgranuloma.html):
  + Clinically difficult to distinguish
  + Stromal multinucleated giant cells are seen on histology
* [Pyogenic granuloma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/oralcavitypyogenicgranuloma.html):
  + Clinically difficult to distinguish
  + Lobulated vascular proliferation on histology
* [Giant cell fibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/oralcavitygiantcellfibroma.html):
  + Clinically difficult to distinguish
  + Lacks bone formation

Board review style question #1

Which of the following is true regarding peripheral ossifying fibroma?

1. It is a reactive gingival proliferation thought to occur in response to irritation or trauma
2. It is histologically characterized by lobulated growth of capillary sized vessels with a central feeder vessel
3. It is molecularly characterized by recurrent internal tandem duplication of *BCOR*
4. It is most common in males over 50

Board review style answer #1

**A.** It is a reactive gingival proliferation thought to occur in response to irritation or trauma
