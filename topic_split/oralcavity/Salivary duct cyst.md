Definition / general

* Reactive lesion to salivary gland duct obstruction and buildup of trapped mucin within

Essential features

* Mucus trapped within lumen secondary to salivary gland duct obstruction
* Severe obstruction can lead to squamous or oncocytic metaplasia of duct epithelial bilayer
* Found on oral mucosal sites with minor salivary glands or parotid gland
* May become secondarily infected around sialolith, which acts as a nidus

Terminology

* Salivary retention cyst
* Mucus retention cyst
* Mucus duct cyst
* Sialocyst
* Reactive oncocytoid cyst (if epithelial lining has oncocytic metaplasia)
* Not to be confused with mucocele / mucus extravasation phenomenon / ranula / plunging rannula / mucus escape reaction

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [K11.8](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/K00-K95/K00-K14/K11-/K11.8)  - other diseases of salivary glands

Epidemiology

* M = F ([Arch Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 1987;113:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3790285))
* Some reports have found a slight female predilection ([Arch Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 1987;113:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3790285))
* Adults with median age of 56 years from 177 cases ([Head Neck Pathol 2017;11:469](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28349371))
* Rarer than the oral cavity mucocele ([Pediatr Dermatol 2008;25:308](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18577033))

Sites

* Minor salivary glands of the floor of mouth, vestibule (mucobuccal fold), buccal mucosa, upper labial mucosa ([Head Neck Pathol 2017;11:469](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28349371))
* Parotid is the most commonly associated major salivary gland ([J Oral Sci 2001;43:9](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11383640))
* Rare in the lower lip ([Pediatr Dermatol 2008;25:308](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18577033))

Pathophysiology

* Salivary duct occlusion leading to reactive ductal ectasia ([Diagn Cytopathol 2015;43:495](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25614439))
* True developmental cyst with epithelial lining unlike the oral cavity mucocele ([Head Neck Pathol 2017;11:469](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28349371))

Etiology

* May be associated with sialoliths, mucus plugs, postoperative or postinflammatory stricture ([J Oral Sci 2001;43:9](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11383640))
* Distal obstruction of a salivary duct leads to retention of secretion within the duct ([Diagn Cytopathol 2015;43:495](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25614439))

Clinical features

* Dome shaped, sessile, slow growing but asymptomatic nodule ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1984;58:692](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6594658))
* 1 - 3 cm but have been reported to grow up to 10 cm ([J Oral Sci 2001;43:9](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11383640))
* Fluctuant to palpation but usually painless, some may feel firmer especially if a sialolith is present ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1964;18:191](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14176606))
* Mucus or pus may be expressed from dilated ductal orifices when palpated if secondarily infected ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1984;58:692](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6594658))
* Blue tinge due to Tyndall effect
* Do not typically wax and wane over time ([Arch Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 1987;113:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3790285))

Diagnosis

* Based on patient reported history and clinical examination, definitive diagnosis made by histopathologic evaluation under light microscopy

Prognostic factors

* Benign with good prognosis

Case reports

* 15 year old boy with a 3 month history of a left lower labial mucosa swelling ([J Oral Maxillofac Pathol 2014;18:S151](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25364168))
* 30 year old man with a left submandibular swelling only noticeable on swallowing and nose blowing ([J Laryngol Otol. 2000;114:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10845052))
* 56 year old man with an obstructive mass at the vallecula ([Arch Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 1989;115:1254](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2789785))
* 63 year old man presenting with an enlarging left preauricular swelling ([J Clin Imaging Sci 2013;3:3](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23878772))
* 78 year old man with a firm swelling at the left upper labial mucosal ([Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2013;56:163](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24056658))

Treatment

* Complete excision along with the feeding minor salivary gland is curative ([Arch Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 1987;113:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3790285))
  + Iatrogenic intraoperative damage to neighboring salivary gland parenchyma may contribute to the development of postoperative mucocele ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1964;18:191](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14176606))
* Partial or total removal of feeding major salivary glands may be required ([Pediatr Dermatol 2008;25:308](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18577033))
* Chlorhexidine mouth rinse or oral antibiotics for secondarily infected salivary duct cysts ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1984;58:692](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6594658))
* Sialagogues may decrease the risk of salivary stasis within dilated ducts by stimulating salivary flow

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Mucoid secretion surrounded by a bilayer of cuboidal or columnar stratified salivary duct epithelium ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1964;18:191](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14176606))
  + Cyst lining may have papillary folds into the lumen ([Head Neck Pathol 2017;11:469](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28349371))
* May have squamous or oncocytic metaplasia of cyst lining, especially with more severe obstruction ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1964;18:191](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14176606))
* Sialolith and bacteria causing secondary infection may be present among the submitted tissue ([Head Neck Pathol 2017;11:469](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28349371))
* Feeding salivary gland, if included, has obstructive changes with variable ductal dilation, lymphocytic infiltrate with periductal pattern and fibrosis ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1984;58:692](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6594658))

  + Salivary duct cyst (mucus retention cyst)

Differential diagnosis

* [Mucocele/mucus extravasation phenomenon/ranula/plunging rannula/mucus escape reaction](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/oralcavitymucocele.html)
  + There is no true salivary gland duct epithelial lining and instead this is mimicked by epithelioid macrophages (muciphages) at the periphery of the extravasated mucin
* [Warthin tumor (papillary cystadenoma lymphomatosum)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandswarthin.html)
  + Lymphoid stroma in the cyst wall and multiple papillary infoldings with a bilayer of columnar and oncocytic epithelial lining
* **Papillary cystadenoma**
  + The lining epithelium has an adenomatous proliferation forming multilayered plaques of columnar or oncocytic salivary duct lining with papillary infoldings
* [Gingival cyst of the adult](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mandiblemaxillagingivalcystadult.html)
  + These occur only on the gingiva which has no minor salivary glands
