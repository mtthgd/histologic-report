Definition / general

* Oral varicosities are tortuous and aberrantly dilated venous structures that most often present on the lips and ventral tongue of older individuals
* They represent a developmental anomaly of unknown etiology with minimal clinical implications

Essential features

* Abnormally dilated venous structures
* The most common oral mucosal lesion in older adults ([J Dent Res Dent Clin Dent Prospects 2019;13:24](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31217915))
* Most often found under the tongue and around the lips / labial mucosa

Terminology

* Varicosity / varicosities
* Varix
* Varices
* Caviar tongue
* Phlebectasia linguae

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [I86.0](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/I00-I99/I80-I89/I86-/I86.0) - Sublingual varices
* IDC-10: [I86.8](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/I00-I99/I80-I89/I86-/I86.8) - Varices of unspecified locations

Epidemiology

* Older individuals (67% of patients are > 60 years) ([Clin Dermatol 2016;34:458](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27343960))

Sites

* Sublingual varices are most common
* May also be found on skin of lip, labial mucosa or buccal mucosa

Etiology

* Unknown etiology
* Loosely associated risk factors include smoking, cardiovascular disease (e.g. hypertension), female gender, denture wearing, chronic liver disease and old age ([Clin Dermatol 2016;34:458](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27343960), [Folia Morphol (Warsz) 2019;78:325](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30371931), [Medicine (Baltimore) 2019;98:e16987](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31626079), [Saudi Med J 2015;36:310](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25737173), [BMC Oral Health 2015;15:78](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26163474))

Clinical features

* Presents as a chain of compressible blue-purple nodules on either side of the lingual frenum or as a single elevated or flat submucosal nodule in other locations
* May be firm when thrombosed
* Ventral tongue is the most common site, although also found on labial mucosa, skin of lip, buccal mucosa or other rare oral sites
* Painless and often inconsequential ([Gerodontology 2015;32:82](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23992044))

Diagnosis

* Diagnosis often is made on clinical presentation, especially when in the sublingual location; biopsy often is not required
* Diascopy may prove useful in differential diagnosis, as varicosities demonstrate blanching ([Gerodontology 2015;32:82](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23992044))
* A single varicosity may be diagnosed upon histologic examination when the presentation is not clinically diagnostic

Radiology description

* Occasionally, oral varicosities may demonstrate small opacifications on radiographic imaging due to phlebolith formation within a thrombus ([Gerodontology 2015;32:82](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23992044))

Prognostic factors

* Varicosities are of little clinical consequence
* Patients have a good prognosis, although rare cases may be associated with bleeding ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2019;98:e16987](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31626079))

Case reports

* 42 year old woman with bleeding varicosity at base of tongue in association with liver cirrhosis ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2019;98:e16987](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31626079))
* 60 year old white woman with oral varicosity treated with sclerotherapy ([Med Oral Patol Oral Cir Bucal 2006;11:E44](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16388293))
* 62 year old woman with lingual varicosities visualized via dermoscopy / mucoscopy ([Dermatol Pract Concept 2018;8:54](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29445577))

Treatment

* Varicosities generally are not treated unless thrombosed or displeasing to the patient in form or function
  + In such cases, conservative surgical excision or sclerotherapy is sufficient ([Med Oral Patol Oral Cir Bucal 2018;23:e180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29476682))
* Safe and effective treatment has been performed with diode and Nd:YAG lasers ([J Contemp Dent Pract 2015;16:723](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26522597))

Gross description

* Tan surface with raised or flat blue-purple-black ovoid structure, which may be firm or depressible
* Cut surface shows dilated or collapsed vascular space that may demonstrate luminal accumulation of brown erythrocytes

Microscopic (histologic) description

* 1 - 3 tortuous, enlarged venous structures lined by a thin layer of endothelium ([Gerodontology 2015;32:82](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23992044))
* Thrombosis or organizing thrombosis is often present, which is depicted by concentric layers of erythrocytes and platelets, referred to as lines of Zahn
  + Aggregates of granulation tissue may also be present during formation
* Concentric rings of eosinophilic calcifications may be present within the lumen

* [GLUT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglut1.html) ([Oral Dis 2007;13:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17241430))

Sample pathology report

* Lower lip, excision:
  + Vascular anomaly consistent with varicosity

Differential diagnosis

* Sublingual varicosities are clinically diagnostic and do not require biopsy
* Clinical differential diagnosis for a single, blue oral varix may include the following:
  + [Hemangioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/oralcavityhobnailhemangioma.html):
    - An abnormal collection of more than 3 dilated vessels, seen on light microscopy
    - Congenital
    - Often [GLUT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglut1.html) positive ([Oral Dis 2007;13:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17241430))
  + [Vascular leiomyoma (angioleiomyoma)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueangioleiomyoma.html):
    - Histopathologic examination reveals a well defined proliferation of smooth muscle emanating from vascular walls
    - Positive for [alpha smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html)
  + **Salivary tumors / reactive processes**, differentiated upon biopsy:
    - [Mucoepidermoid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/oralcavitymucoep.html)
    - [Mucocele](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/oralcavitymucocele.html)
    - [Salivary duct cyst](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/oralcavitysalivaryductcyst.html)
  + **Amalgam tattoo**:
    - May demonstrate opacification on radiograph
    - Flat, blue-black pigmentation
    - Histopathologic examination reveals foreign particles within connective tissue
  + **Melanocytic nevus** (especially [blue nevus](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticbluenevus.html)):
    - Histopathologic examination reveals focal collection of melanocytes
  + [Melanoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/oralcavitymelanoma.html):
    - Often irregular borders
    - Histopathologic examination reveals abnormal increase in melanocytes
    - Most common on the maxillary gingiva and hard palate
  + [Focal melanosis / melanocytic macule](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/oralcavitymelanotic.html):
    - Flat, never raised
    - Focal accumulation of melanin without increase in melanocytes, seen on light microscopy
