Definition / general

* Carcinoid tumors involving the ovary can be primary (see [Ovary tumor > germ cell tumors > Carcinoid tumors](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorcarcinoid.html)) or metastatic
* Primary ovarian carcinoid tumors are frequently seen in the context of a teratoma
  + Robboy et al. reported 35/48 (73%) insular carcinoids were associated with a mature teratoma component
  + Also, 2/48 tumors (4%) had a mucinous cystadenoma component ([Cancer 1975;36:404](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1157010))
* Metastatic carcinoid tumors originate from the GI tract
  + Most common primary site is distal ileum: 20/30 (67%) as reported by Robboy et al. ([Cancer 1974;33:798](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4815582)), and 15/17 (88%) as reported by Strosberg et al. ([Gynecol Oncol 2007;106:65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17475313))
  + Less frequent locations are cecum, appendix, jejunum and pancreas
  + Synchronous metastases are frequent at time of diagnosis (88 - 100% of cases) ([Cancer 1974;33:798](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4815582), [Gynecol Oncol 2007;106:65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17475313))
    - Involved sites include pelvis (uterine and tubal serosa), abdominal cavity (peritoneum, intestinal serosa, liver) and retroperitoneum (periaortic lymph nodes)

Epidemiology

* Average age 57 to 62 years, range 21 to 82 years ([Cancer 1974;33:798](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4815582), [Gynecol Oncol 2007;106:65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17475313), [Hum Pathol 2013;44:2536](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24029704), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2008;28:41](http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19047909))
* Most patients (>90%) are Caucasian; a minority are Black and Hispanic ([Cancer 1974;33:798](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4815582), [Gynecol Oncol 2007;106:65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17475313))

Clinical features

* Symptoms of carcinoid syndrome are seen in 30 - 53% of cases, and include intermittent diarrhea, flushing, ankle edema, involuntary weight loss and cardiac murmurs
* Other symptoms are due to mass effect, including abdominal/pelvic pain and bowel obstruction
* Rarely, tumors are diagnosed incidentally during routine gynecologic examination

Laboratory

* Elevated urinary levels of 5-hydroxyindole acetic acid and serum serotonin are consistently found, as observed by Strosberg et al. (average peak of 24h 5-HIAA urine levels was 60 mg, reference range 0 - 6 mg) ([Gynecol Oncol 2007;106:65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17475313))

Radiology description

* Pelvic ultrasound confirms the presence of an ovarian mass (and usually detects bilaterality)
* Most patients have abnormal radiotracer uptake on indium-111-pentetreotid scintigraphy

Prognostic factors

* In 1974, the first and largest case series of carcinoid tumors metastatic to the ovary, Robboy et al. reported an overall poor prognosis, with a survival rate of 66% after the first year and 33% at 4 years post-diagnosis ([Cancer 1974;33:798](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4815582))
* A 2007 case series by Strosberg et al. reported an excellent response to long-term Ocreotide treatment and cytoreductive surgery, with only 2 deaths out of 17 patients and a projected 5 year survival rate of 94% ([Gynecol Oncol 2007;106:65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17475313))

Case reports

* Goblet cell carcinoid of vermiform appendix metastatic to ovary, mimicking primary ovarian mucinous cystadenocarcinoma ([Acta Pathol Jpn 1991;41:455](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1950561))

Gross description

* Tumor size ranges from 4 to 32 cm with an average of 10.2 cm ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2008;28:41](http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19047909))
* Most tumors are purely solid (80% in case series by Rabban et al.) ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2008;28:41](http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19047909)); a minority may display a cystic component
* Multinodular growth is frequently observed (60% in case series by Rabban et al.) ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2008;28:41](http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19047909))
* Bilateral ovarian involvement is seen in most cases (60 - 95%) ([Cancer 1974;33:798](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4815582))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Insular pattern is the most frequently observed; mucinous and trabecular patterns have also been reported ([Cancer 1975;36:157](http://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/1097-0142%28197507%2936%3A1%3C157%3A%3AAID-CNCR2820360113%3E3.0.CO%3B2-B/abstract), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2008;28:41](http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19047909))
* Insular architecture is characterized by sharply demarcated nests of tumor cells of varying sizes and shapes in a variable fibromatous stroma
* Acinar configurations and calcifications can also be observed
* Neoplastic cells are typically small and uniform in size, with a central round nucleus containing coarsely and evenly clumped chromatin ("salt and pepper" pattern)
* Cytoplasm is moderate to abundant and eosinophilic, occasionally granular
* Mitotic activity is absent to low (up to 2 mitoses / 10 HPFs)
* Tumor cell necrosis is absent

Positive stains

* [Chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html), [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html), [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html), [NSE](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsNSE.html), [pan-cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) (commonly with a perinuclear staining pattern)
* [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html): positive in 90 - 100% of carcinoids metastatic to ovary; positivity is frequent in tumors arising in small intestine (95%), appendix (92%) and duodenum (80%), but rare in tumors of colorectum (12%) and stomoach (0%) ([Hum Pathol 2013;44:2536](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24029704))

Negative stains

* [CA125](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsCA125.html) , [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html)
* [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html), [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html) and [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html) ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2008;28:41](http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19047909))

Differential diagnosis

* [Primary ovarian carcinoid tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorcarcinoid.html): distinction relies on conventional clinicopathologic features, as outlined by Rabban et al. ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2008;28:41](http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19047909)):
  + **Laterality:** Frequent in metastatic carcinoid; but all confirmed primary ovarian carcinoids reported have been unilateral
  + **Multinodular growth:** Frequent in metastases, but not seen in primary ovarian carcinoids
  + **Size:** Primary ovarian carcinoids average 3.4 cm in size (excluding any teratomatous element) versus 10.2 cm for metastatic carcinoids (range: 4-32 cm); 75% of primary ovarian carcinoids were 3 cm or less, compared to 0% of metastatic carcinoids to ovary
  + **Presence of teratomatous elements:** supports primary ovarian origin
  + **Metastates in other sites:** as mentioned above, very common in metastatic carcinoid to the ovary
    - Nonetheless, primary ovarian carcinoid tumors can rarely present with metastases ([J Obstet Gynaecol Res 2010;36:567](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20598039), [Case Rep Obstet Gynecol 2012;2012:961087](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23326740), [Gynecol Oncol 1994;54:222](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8063251))
  + Immunohistochemistry has controversial value in this differential
    - Rabban et al. reported 37.5% expression of CDX2 in primary ovarian carcinoids; importantly, most (4/6) insular primary carcinoids expressed this marker and recommended against the use of immunohistochemistry ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2008;28:41](http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19047909))
    - In contrast, Desouki et al. found that all 30 primary ovarian carcinoids tested were negative for CDX2, and therefore suggested this marker as sensitive and specific for small intestinal or appendiceal origin ([Hum Pathol 2013;44:2536](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24029704))
* [Brenner tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorb9brenner.html): nests composed of epithelioid, urothelial type cells with oval, pale, grooved nuclei
* [Granulosa cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorgctadult.html): Call-Exner bodies, inhibin and calretinin expression
