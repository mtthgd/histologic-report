Definition / general

* Cervical cancers only rarely metastasize to ovaries
  + Proportion of cases presenting with ovarian metastases at the time of surgery ranges from 0.66 to 1.5% ([Int J Gynecol Oncol 1997;57:173](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9184955), [Gynecol Oncol 2006;101:234](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16300819))
* Adenocarcinomas are more likely to metastasize to the ovaries than squamous cell carcinomas
  + In different series, 5.3 – 8.2% of cervical adenocarcinomas vs 0.4 - 1.3% of squamous cell carcinomas metastasized to ovary ([Gynecol Oncol 2001;82:312](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11531285), [Gynecol Oncol 2006;101:234](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16300819), [Gynecol Oncol 2001;82:504](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11520147))
* Independent risk factors for ovarian spread include histologic type (adenocarcinoma > squamous cell carcinoma) and uterine corpus involvement
* Other risk factors include vaginal involvement, lymphovascular space invasion and lymph node metastases ([J Gynecol Oncol 2008;19:181](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19471574), [Gynecol Oncol 2001;82:312](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11531285))
* Among HPV negative endocervical adenocarcinomas, risk is low for clear cell carcinomas ([Onco Targets Ther 2014;7:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24470762)), but high (up to 35%) for gastric type adenocarcinoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1449](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26457350))

- Risk also increases with tumor stage, ranging from 0% in stage IA squamous cell carcinomas to 9.8% in stage IIB adenocarcinomas ([Gynecol Oncol 1987;28:255](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3678976), [Gynecol Oncol 2006;101:234](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16300819))

Epidemiology

* Mean age of presentation is 57.4 years for squamous cell carcinoma and 50.2 years for adenocarcinoma ([Gynecol Oncol 1991;41:46](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2026359))

Pathophysiology

* Carcinomatous spread from the cervix to the ovaries is hematogenous or lymphatic ([Cancer 2000;88:2578](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10861436))
* Cervical and ovarian tumors are grossly discontinuous with no direct invasion from cervix to ovary reported to date, which also supports the hematogenous spread theory ([Diagn Pathol 2014;9:109](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24899394), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2015;34:551](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26166722))
* Based on the strong association between uterine corpus involvement and ovarian metastasis, transtubal implantation has also been postulated as a mechanism ([Int J Gynaecol Obstet 1997;57:173](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9184955))

Clinical features

* Mean age of presentation is 45 - 50 years ([Gynecol Oncol 2001;82:312](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11531285), [Gynecol Oncol 2006;101:234](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16300819), [Gynecol Oncol 2001;82:504](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11520147))
* Signs and symptoms are usually related to the cervical lesion (vaginal bleeding, pelvic pain, abnormal cytology)
* Rarely, the presence of an ovarian mass is the only finding at presentation and a cervical primary is initially unsuspected

Radiology description

* USG: enlarged ovaries

Prognostic factors

* Very poor outcome with 5 year overall survival close to 0% ([Int J Gynecol Oncol 1997;57:173](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9184955))
* Outcome not related to tumor stage or histological type ([Gynecol Oncol 2006;101:234](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16300819))

Treatment

* In patients with cervical cancer, ovarian preservation and transposition is recommended in:
  + Women 44 years old or younger with squamous cell carcinoma stage IB-II
  + Clinically normal ovaries and no history of breast cancer or family history of ovarian cancer ([Gynecol Oncol 2001;82:312](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11531285))
* Oophorectomy is recommended in patients that do not fulfill these criteria, in particular those with adenocarcinoma or locally advanced tumors ([Int J Gynaecol Obstet 2007;99:64](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17445818))

Gross description

* Most ovaries appear grossly normal
* When abnormal, ovaries have a nodular surface and cystic areas ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2015;34:551](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26166722), [Int J Gynecol Oncol 1997;57:173](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9184955))
* Bilateral involvement and size Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:128, [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2015;34:551-63](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26166722))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* **Squamous cell carcinoma:**
  + Nodular growth of malignant squamous epithelium (keratinizing or non-keratinizing) with confluent and destructive growth ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2015;34:551](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26166722))
* **Adenocarcinoma:**
  + Malignant glandular proliferation with confluent glandular, cribriform and papillary architecture
  + Stromal invasion may be expansile or infiltrative ([J Clin Pathol 2012;65:591](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22075188))
  + **Usual (endocervical) type:**
    - Glandular epithelium with mucinous or endometrioid differentiation
    - Neoplastic cells have pleomorphic, hyperchromatic, round to elongated nuclei with stratification and conspicuous apical mitoses
  + **Gastric type:**
    - Neoplastic cells have columnar shape and granular eosinophilic apical cytoplasm
    - Nuclei are round in shape and display significant atypia and pleomorphism
  + Importantly, malignant areas usually coexist with regions showing a benign and borderline appearance (architectural and cytologic)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Detection of HPV ribonucleic acid by PCR or in situ hybridization is a reliable method to differentiate metastatic HPV related cervical carcinoma (squamous cell and usual type adenocarcinoma) from primary ovarian tumors and other secondary lesions ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2004;23:7](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14668543))

Differential diagnosis

* Primary [mucinous](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumormucinouscarcinoma.html) and [endometrioid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorendometrioidcarcinoma.html) of ovary:
  + There is significant morphologic and immunophenotypic overlap
  + Correlation with clinical history and presentation (laterality, size) is important
  + Primary ovarian adenocarcinoma usually has bland cytology; cervical tumors display hyperchromasia, nuclear overlapping and apical mitoses, at least focally
  + "Block" positivity for p16 favors a primary HPV+ endocervical adenocarcinoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:653](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17460447))
* Metastatic [adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumoradenocarcinoma.html) from gastrointestinal tract origin:
  + Signet ring cell differentiation (uncommon in endocervical primaries), CK20+ and CDX2+ [strong and diffuse staining favors appendiceal and colorectal origin ([Histopathology 2015 Nov 6 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26542609), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2015 Nov 3 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/%2026535987))], SATB2+ ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2015 Nov 3 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/%2026535987), [Am J Surg Pathol 2015 Nov 5 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26551622)), p16- (although gastric type endocervical adenocarcinoma is also p16-)
* Primary [squamous cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixscc.html):
  + Exceedingly rare and seen in a background of mature teratoma
  + Absence of teratomatous elements and "block" positivity for p16 favor a cervical primary
* [Metastatic squamous cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticscc.html) from skin: p16-
