Definition / general

* Malignant epithelial tumor composed of clear, eosinophilic or hobnail cells with tubulocystic, papillary and solid growth patterns

Essential features

* Admixture of tubulocystic, papillary and solid growth patterns comprised of cells with uniform nuclear atypia without brisk mitotic figures
* Usually positive for CK7, PAX8, napsin A and HNF1β and negative for ER, PR and WT1, with aberrant p53 expression in up to 24% of cases
* May be associated with endometriosis or Lynch syndrome
* Stage is the most important prognostic factor

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8310/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=70) - clear cell adenocarcinoma, NOS
* ICD-10: [C56](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C51-C58/C56-/C56) - malignant neoplasm of ovary
* ICD-11: [2C73.00](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f315825558) - clear cell adenocarcinoma of ovary

Epidemiology

* Accounts for 10 - 12% of ovarian carcinomas in North America ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2010;29:203](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20407318), [J Natl Cancer Inst 2019;111:60](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29718305))
* Racial distribution in United States: 4.8% white, 3.1% black, 11.1% Asian ([Gynecol Oncol 2011;121:407](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21276610))
* Higher incidence in Asia (up to 27% in Japan) ([J Obstet Gynaecol Res 2014;40:338](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24472052), [Gynecol Oncol 2019;153:589](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30905436))
* Mean age is 55 - 56 years ([Gynecol Oncol 2008;109:370](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18395777), [J Pathol Clin Res 2018;4:250](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30062862))
* Increased risk associated with:
  + Endometriosis, including endometriotic cysts (50 - 74%) ([Lancet Oncol 2012;13:385](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22361336), [Int J Gynecol Cancer 2012;22:1310](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22976498))
  + Less frequently, Lynch syndrome ([Eur J Cancer 2016;55:65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26773421), [Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:1029](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18469706), [Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:1173](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25025451), [Clin Cancer Res 2019;25:3962](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30967419), [Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2008;1:502](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18787632))
    - Mismatch repair (MMR) deficiency in 6 - 43.75% of cases
    - Younger patients
    - 10% develop endometriosis associated carcinomas, including clear cell carcinoma
  + Family history of ovarian cancer in a first degree relative ([PLoS One 2018;13:e0205000](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30281663))
* Decreased risk associated with:
  + Late menarche (> 15 years), early menopause (< 40 years), tubal ligation, parity, hysterectomy, smoking, oral contraceptives ([J Clin Oncol 2016;34:2888](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27325851))
  + Expression of the genetic susceptibility locus (HNF1β) via epigenetic mechanisms ([Nat Commun 2013;4:1628](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23535649))

Sites

* Ovary
* Any extraovarian site with endometriosis

Pathophysiology

* Genetic alterations due to various mutations (see [Molecular / cytogenetics description](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorclearcell.html#ovarytumorclearcellmol))

Etiology

* Endometriosis ([J Pathol 2018;245:265](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29604057), [Int J Gynecol Cancer 2012;22:1310](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22976498))
* Mutation signatures: age related (40%) and APOBEC mediated (apolipoprotein B mRNA editing enzyme, catalytic polypeptide-like (26%), a family of evolutionarily conserved cytidine deaminases) ([Nat Genet 2017;49:856](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28436987))

Clinical features

* Symptoms related to endometriosis or pelvic mass ([Gynecol Oncol 2010;116:374](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19922988))
* Most common ovarian epithelial neoplasm associated with paraneoplastic syndromes, such as hypercalcemia, thromboembolism, subacute cerebellar degeneration or bilateral diffuse uveal melanocytic proliferation ([Onkologie 2009;32:517](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19745599), [Gynecol Oncol 2007;104:406](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17014897), [Semin Diagn Pathol 2019;36:246](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30772079))
* May be incidental (asymptomatic)

Diagnosis

* Microscopic examination

Laboratory

* May be associated with mildly elevated CA-125 (usually ≤ 200 U/mL) ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2018;97:e10881](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29794794))

Radiology description

* Large unilocular, mainly cystic, smooth marginated mass with 1 or more solid nodular protrusions into the cavity on all imaging modalities
* High attenuated cystic portion on computed tomography ([J Comput Assist Tomogr 2006;30:875](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17082688))
* Variable T1 signal, depending on hemorrhagic components on magnetic resonance imaging
* Blood flow in solid nodules on Doppler ultrasound examination
* In postmenopausal women, ground glass appearance in a cyst ultrasonographically should be evaluated carefully to rule out a component of clear cell carcinoma ([Ultrasonography 2015;34:173](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25797108))

Prognostic factors

* Negative prognostic factors:
  + Advanced stage (most important) ([Histopathology 2015;66:808](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25065903), [Medicine (Baltimore) 2018;97:e10881](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29794794))
    - Most cases are FIGO stage I ([BMC Cancer 2018;18:347](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29587661), [J Natl Cancer Inst 2019;111:60](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29718305))
    - Worse prognosis in stage IC disease compared with stage IA
    - Overall 5 year cause specific survival of 66%, including 85% for stage I, 71% for stage II, 35% for stage III and 16% for stage IV ([CA Cancer J Clin 2018;68:284](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29809280))
    - Better prognosis than high grade serous carcinoma when confined to the pelvis but worse prognosis if advanced stage except for some MMR deficient tumors ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:36](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21164285))
  + Positive lymph nodes ([Histopathology 2015;66:808](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25065903))
  + Lymphovascular invasion
  + Immunohistochemically, loss of ARID1A, aberrant p53 and block-like p16 staining, expression of IMP3, nuclear expression of beta catenin, fibroblast growth factor receptor 2, chromobox homolog 7 (CBX7), Emi1, CXCR4, HOXA10, glypican 3 and Rsf-1 (HBXAP) ([Surg Pathol Clin 2019;12:529](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31097114))
  + Genetic alterations, including amplification of *MET* or *MDM2* (in *TP53* wild type cases), *CCNE1* copy number gain, somatic copy number variations such as chr20q13.2 harboring the *ZNF217* gene ([Surg Pathol Clin 2019;12:529](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31097114))
* Positive prognostic factors:
  + *PIK3CA* mutations ([Hum Pathol 2012;43:2197](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22705003))

Case reports

* 28 year old woman with Cowden syndrome diagnosed with PTEN negative ovarian clear cell carcinoma ([J Natl Compr Canc Netw 2019;17:7](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30659124))
* 50 year old woman with cerebellar metastasis of ovarian clear cell carcinoma ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2020;39:68](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30252727))
* 55 year old woman with primary diaphragmatic clear cell carcinoma associated with endometriosis ([Gynecol Oncol Rep 2021;36:100733](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33665296))
* 61 year old woman with bilateral breast metastases of ovarian clear cell carcinoma ([Case Rep Oncol Med 2019;2019:8013913](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31183233))
* 62 year old woman with ovarian clear cell carcinoma associated with low grade endometrial stromal sarcoma ([Gynecol Obstet Invest 2020;85:371](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32570258))

Treatment

* Hysterectomy, bilateral salpingo-oophorectomy, omentectomy and staging biopsies
* Adjuvant chemoradiation in some patients ([Ann Oncol 2016;27:i50](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27141072))
  + Often platinum resistant ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:36](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21164285))

Gross description

* Usually unilateral
* Mean size 13 cm (range 0.8 - 35 cm) ([Histopathology 2015;66:808](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25065903))
* Variable appearance on cut surface:
  + Mainly cystic, with fleshy nodules protruding into the cyst lumen
  + Solid and cystic (spongy appearance due to small cysts)
  + Solid (may be arising from clear cell adenofibroma or borderline clear cell tumor)
  + Cyst may contain chocolate brown fluid
  + Pale yellow to brown appearance
  + Necrosis or hemorrhage
* Ovarian surface involvement in 11% of cases ([Histopathology 2015;66:808](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25065903))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* 3 classic growth patterns, frequently admixed but 1 pattern (usually papillary or tubulocystic) may predominate ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:36](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21164285), [Adv Anat Pathol 2012;19:296](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22885379), [Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:656](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26813747), [Histopathology 2015;66:808](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25065903)):
  + Papillary:
    - Most frequent (70%)
    - Usually small, round, simple, nonbranching papillae with fibrous / hyalinized, edematous / myxoid or empty (open tumor rings) stromal cores
    - Lined by 1 - 2 layers of cuboidal, flattened or hobnail cells
    - Micropapillary tufts may be seen
    - Rarely irregular, broad papillae with or without hierarchical branching and micropapillae ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:269](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18223330))
  + Tubulocystic:
    - Frequent (65%)
    - Variably sized tubules and cysts, with or without intraluminal dense eosinophilic secretions and outpouchings
    - Lined by a single layer of hobnail, cuboidal or flattened cells
    - Often associated with an adenofibromatous background
  + Solid:
    - Least common (62%)
    - Diffuse sheets or nested clusters of polyhedral cells separated by delicate septa exhibiting clear to eosinophilic cytoplasm
* General features:
  + Low cuboidal, polyhedral, hobnail or flattened cells with uniform nuclear atypia, prominent nucleoli and variable mitotic activity (usually low at < 6 per 10 high power fields)
  + Hobnail cells have hyperchromatic nuclei protruding into lumina of tubulocystic structures or lining papillae
  + Focal nuclear pleomorphism may be present in 40%
  + Eosinophilic hyaline globules ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:36](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21164285), [Adv Anat Pathol 2012;19:296](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22885379))
  + Nuclear pseudoinclusions
  + Peritumoural or diffuse lymphoplasmacytic infiltrate may be present
  + May be associated with clear cell adenofibroma, clear cell borderline tumor, endometriosis or endometrioid adenocarcinoma
  + Not graded (high grade by definition)
  + Oxyphilic variant of clear cell carcinoma is composed of tumor cells with variable amounts of clear to granular eosinophilic cytoplasm (oxyphil cells), especially in nested, solid or trabecular growths ([Am J Surg Pathol 1987;11:661](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2443026))
* Rare features:
  + Multinucleated cells
  + Psammoma bodies
  + Diastase resistant intracytoplasmic material imparting a signet ring appearance (so called targetoid cells)
  + Intraglandular cellular sloughing mimicking high grade serous carcinoma
  + Uncommon growth patterns include glandular, nested, trabecular and reticular-like (myxoid stroma)

Napsin A

Cytology description

* Round to oval nuclei with fine chromatin
* Abundant, pale, finely granular to vacuolated cytoplasm and indistinct cytoplasmic membranes
* Hyaline extracellular material forming so called raspberry bodies or globule-like structures ([Cytopathology 2016;27:427](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26932246))
* Psammoma bodies in ascitic fluid ([Acta Cytol 2000;44:1005](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11127728))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Loss of function mutations in *ARID1A*, a tumor suppressor gene from the SWI / SNF chromatin remodelling complex (50%) ([N Engl J Med 2010;363:1532](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20942669), [Science 2010;330:228](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20826764))
* *PIK3CA* mutations (40%), often associated with *ARID1A* mutations ([Cancer Res 2004;64:7678](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15520168), [Am J Pathol 2009;174:1597](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19349352), [Mod Pathol 2012;25:615](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22157930), [Int J Gynecol Cancer 2016;26:648](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26937756))
* *TERT* promoter mutations (16%) ([J Pathol 2014;232:473](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24338723))
* *KRAS* mutations (10%) ([Nat Genet 2017;49:856](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28436987))
* *TP53* mutations (< 10%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:1591](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31335355))
* DNA mismatch repair deficiency (up to 6%), most commonly germline mutations in *MSH2* ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2012;31:524](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23018216), [Histopathology 2016;69:288](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26799366), [Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:1591](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31335355), [Clin Cancer Res 2019;25:3962](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30967419), [Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:656](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26813747))
* *PTEN* mutations (5%) ([Cancer Res 2000;60:7052](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11156411))
* Rarely *MET, ARID1B, PIK3R1, SMARCA4* and *SMARCD3* mutations

Sample pathology report

* Uterus with cervix, fallopian tubes and ovaries, total hysterectomy and bilateral salpingo-oophorectomy:
  + Right ovary: clear cell carcinoma, positive for ovarian surface involvement (see synoptic report)
  + Left ovary and fallopian tubes: benign
  + Endometrium: inactive
  + Myometrium: leiomyomata
  + Uterine serosa and cervix: benign

Differential diagnosis

* [Clear cell adenofibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorclearcelladenofibroma.html):
  + Extremely rare
  + Scattered tubulocystic structures lined by bland flat to low cuboidal cells, with clear to eosinophilic cytoplasm in a fibromatous background
  + Associated with clear cell carcinoma in up to 33% of cases; therefore, extensive sampling is important to rule out clear cell carcinoma ([J Cancer 2011;2:94](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21479128))
* [Borderline clear cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovaryclearcellborderlinetumor.html):
  + Very rare
  + Growth pattern similar to clear cell adenofibroma but with cytologic atypia
  + Lacks stromal invasion by back to back glands, papillary or solid architecture or solid growth within cysts
* [Serous borderline tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorserousborderline.html) or [low grade serous carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorserouscarcinomalg.html):
  + Clear cell carcinoma with broad papillae; may mimic serous borderline tumor or low grade serous carcinoma
  + Hierarchical branching, cellular stratification and cellular budding usually present
  + Positive for [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html) and [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html)
  + Negative for [HNF1β](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsHNF1B.html) and [napsin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnapsina.html)
* [High grade serous carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorserouscarcinomahg.html) **with clear cell features**:
  + Usually diagnosed at advanced stage
  + May be associated with serous tubal intraepithelial carcinoma
  + Typical papillary growth pattern
  + Lack of small rounded papillae and tubulocystic structures
  + Greater cytologic atypia with brisk mitoses
  + Usually shows aberrant [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) expression, diffuse [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html), patchy to diffuse [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) and [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html); lacks [HNF1β](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsHNF1B.html) and [napsin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnapsina.html)
  + Panel of [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html), [napsin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnapsina.html), [HNF1β](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsHNF1B.html) and [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) recommended for distinction of clear cell carcinoma from high grade serous carcinoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:14](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18830127), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2016;35:430](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26974996))
* [Endometrioid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorendometrioidcarcinoma.html) **with clear cell features**:
  + Endometrioid morphology with or without squamous or mucinous differentiation and cribriform architecture
  + Positive for [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) and [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html)
  + Negative for [HNF1β](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsHNF1B.html) and [napsin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnapsina.html)
  + Panel of [napsin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnapsina.html) and [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html) recommended for distinction of clear cell carcinoma from endometrioid carcinoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1061](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25871622))
* **Metastatic [clear cell renal carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantrccclear.html)**:
  + Prior history or concurrent renal mass
  + Nested alveolar pattern with interalveolar stromal vascularity
  + Composed of tumor cells with mostly clear cytoplasm
  + Lack of tubulocystic architecture, small rounded papillae and hobnail cells
  + Diffuse expression of [RCC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsrcc.html), [carbonic anhydrase IX](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscarbonicanhydraseix.html) and [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) and no reactivity for [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html), [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html), [napsin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnapsina.html) and [AMACR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsamacr.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:377](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18300814))
* [Dysgerminoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumordysgerminoma.html):
  + Nests of primitive cells separated by fibrous septa containing abundant lymphocytes
  + Positive for [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html) and [OCT4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct34.html)
  + Negative for [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html)
* [Yolk sac tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumoryolksac.html):
  + Young patients
  + Elevated serum [α fetoprotein](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsafp.html)
  + Variable growth patterns with brisk mitoses
  + Schiller-Duval bodies may be seen
  + Diffusely positive for [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html), [AFP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsafp.html) and [glypican 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglypican3.html)
  + Negative or focally positive for [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html) and [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)
