Definition / general

* Malignant primitive germ cell tumor with no specific type of differentiation

Essential features

* Most common malignant ovarian germ cell tumor; female counterpart to testicular seminoma
* Most common in children and young women
* Excellent prognosis with chemotherapy
* Composed of sheets or nests of uniform cells with clear or eosinophilic cytoplasm and distinct cell membranes; nuclei are rounded, often with angulated edges; tumor often separated by fibrous septae containing cytotoxic T cells and epithelioid histiocytes that can extend into tumor
* Positive for SALL4, OCT3/4, D2-40, CD117, PLAP

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [C56](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C51-C58/C56-/C56) - malignant neoplasm of ovary
* ICD-11: [2C73.1](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f208782658) - dysgerminoma of ovary

Epidemiology

* Constitutes 1 - 2% of all malignant ovarian tumors ([Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:1009](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33577182))
* Most common malignant germ cell tumor of ovary (50%)
* Most common in children and young women

Sites

* Ovary
* Rarely, extraovarian sites, including abdomen, fallopian tube and uterus ([J Pediatr Adolesc Gynecol 2021 May 11 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33989803), [Curr Probl Cancer 2021;45:100667](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33129566), [Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2018;61:590](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30303159))

Pathophysiology

* Unknown

Etiology

* Arises from primordial germ cells of the ovary

Clinical features

* Most common in children and young women
* May present in pure form or as a component of a malignant mixed germ cell tumor
* Present with abdominal pain or distention
* Bilateral ovarian involvement in 10 - 15% of patients; involvement of contralateral ovary may be microscopic ([Cancer Treat Rev 2008;34:427](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18378402))
* Extraovarian spread in 33% of cases
* One of the most common neoplasms in pregnancy ([Cancer Treat Rev 2008;34:427](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18378402))
* Rare association with gonadal dysgenesis and chromosome Y abnormalities; may arise from gonadoblastoma
* Rare estrogenic manifestations

Diagnosis

* Diagnosis informed by patient's age, radiology and laboratory values (e.g. elevated lactate dehydrogenase [LDH] levels) but ultimately made based on histologic examination of surgical specimen

Laboratory

* Serum LDH often elevated (95%)
* 3 - 5% show low level hCG production related to the presence of multinucleated syncytiotrophoblastic giant cells that are not associated with cytotrophoblasts ([Cancer Treat Rev 2008;34:427](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18378402))
* Serum placental alkaline phosphatase (PLAP) may be elevated ([Cancer Treat Rev 2008;34:427](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18378402))
* Hypercalcemia may develop as a paraneoplastic syndrome, due to tumor production of 1,25-dihydroxyvitamin D3 (active form of vitamin D) ([Cureus 2019;11:e6097](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31886038))

Radiology description

* On ultrasound, tends to appear as a highly vascularized, large, solid, lobulated adnexal mass with irregular internal echogenicity ([Ultrasound Obstet Gynecol 2011;37:596](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21305635))
* Enhancing septa and areas of cystic change that may represent necrosis or hemorrhage may be seen; calcifications may manifest as a speckled pattern ([Radiographics 2014;34:777](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24819795))

Prognostic factors

* Most tumors are stage I at diagnosis
* Recurrence rate is low
* *KIT* mutations associated with advanced stage at presentation ([Cancer 2011;117:2096](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21523721))
* With treatment, excellent prognosis and overall survival > 90%
* Dysgerminoma patients have the most favorable outcomes compared with those with other malignant germ cell tumors; young age, low grade and surgery are significant predictors for improved survival ([Oncol Res Treat 2021;44:145](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33706324))

Case reports

* 16 year old girl with Swyer syndrome underwent prophylactic bilateral gonadectomy and salpingectomies and was found to have dysgerminoma solely within the fallopian tube ([J Pediatr Adolesc Gynecol 2021 May 11 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33989803))
* 19 year old woman with 29 cm dysgerminoma and pseudo-Meigs syndrome ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2021;100:e26319](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34115045))
* 22 year old woman with uncommon vaginal metastasis by dysgerminoma ([Medicina (Kaunas) 2021;57:534](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34071828))
* 26 year old pregnant woman with extraovarian (abdominal) dysgerminoma ([Curr Probl Cancer 2021;45:100667](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33129566))
* 27 year old woman with Swyer syndrome and stage IIA dysgerminoma in streak gonad ([Gynecol Endocrinol 2018;34:464](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29084462))

Treatment

* Radiosensitive but responds very well to cisplatin based chemotherapy, with overall survival > 90% ([Cancer Treat Rev 2008;34:427](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18378402))
* Fertility sparing surgery in patients with pure ovarian dysgerminoma with additional staging surgery, including retroperitoneal lymph node dissection to determine stage IA patients exempt from adjuvant chemotherapy ([J Adolesc Young Adult Oncol 2021;10:303](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32762565))

Gross description

* Usually > 10 cm
* Solid and lobulated with fleshy tan-white cut surface ([Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:1009](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33577182))
* Hemorrhage and necrosis with cystic degeneration may be present
* Presence of calcifications may suggest an associated component of gonadoblastoma but not specific

Frozen section description

* May favor diagnosis of high grade malignant germ cell tumor or malignant epithelioid neoplasm on frozen section; however, final diagnosis should be deferred to permanent sections
* Clear cytoplasm or distinct cell membranes may not be appreciated on frozen section due to artifact
* May mimic large cell lymphoma, both of which share similar gross appearance, large neoplastic nuclei and infiltrating background lymphocytes ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2020;39:79](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31815893), [Int J Surg Pathol 2019;27:387](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30474462))
  + Features favoring dysgerminoma include presence of fibrous septae and squared off nuclei
  + Thickening of the fallopian tube is more suggestive of lymphoma ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2008;27:353](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18580313))
* May mimic small cell carcinoma, hypercalcemic type, both of which typically occur in young women and show overlapping features, including paraneoplastic hypercalcemia ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2019;36:246](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30772079))
  + Features favoring dysgerminoma include elevated LDH levels, moderately abundant cytoplasm
  + In small cell carcinoma, hypercalcemic type, tumor cells usually have scant cytoplasm unless admixed component of large cell variant present

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Histomorphology identical to that of testicular seminoma
* In a well preserved specimen, characteristic appearance of nests of large, uniform polygonal cells with clear or eosinophilic cytoplasm and distinct cell membranes (alveolar pattern)
* May also show sheets, cords, macronodules, insular growth, microcysts, tubules, pseudoglandular spaces or trabeculae ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2014;138:351](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24576031), [Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:1009](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33577182))
* Rarely may show pseudopapillary or macrofollicular growth pattern ([Iran J Pathol 2018;13:94](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29731802))
* Tumor separated by fibrous septae containing cytotoxic T cells and epithelioid histiocytes, often with spillover into tumor; plasma cells, eosinophils, Langhans type giant cells, syncytiotrophoblasts, noncaseating granulomas or lymphoid follicles with germinal centers may be present ([Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:1009](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33577182))
* Stroma usually loose and delicate but in some cases may be hyalinized, myxoid or luteinized
* Numerous mitotic figures
* Rare cases show syncytiotrophoblastic cells, singly or in clusters, without cytotrophoblastic cells; sample tumor thoroughly to exclude choriocarcinoma
* Extensive hemorrhage and necrosis may be present with dystrophic calcification; if large areas of dystrophic calcification seen in the absence of necrosis, may be a gonadoblastoma
  + As gonadoblastomas give rise to dysgerminomas, thorough sampling indicated to rule out its presence

Cytology description

* Uniform population of medium to large sized cells ([Diagn Cytopathol 2020;48:356](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31886634))
* Centrally located, round to oval nuclei, often with angulated, squared off borders
* Vesicular, finely granular or coarse chromatin
* Prominent single or multiple nucleoli
* With poor fixation, cells may show loss of cohesion, nuclei can appear hyperchromatic and cytoplasm may appear scant and eosinophilic, mimicking cells of embryonal carcinoma

Positive stains

* [OCT3/4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct34.html) (nuclear), [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html) (KIT; membranous), [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html) (membranous and cytoplasmic) ([Histopathology 2013;62:71](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23240671))
* **NANOG** (nuclear) and [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html) (nuclear) stem cell markers
* [PLAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsplap.html) (membranous and cytoplasmic)
* May focally express keratins with cytoplasmic dot or rim-like staining: [CAM 5.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscam52.html) (positive in up to 10% of tumor cells in < 50% of dysgerminomas); [AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html) and [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html) (positive in up to 10% of tumor cells in < 25% of dysgerminomas)
* [hCG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshcg.html), [inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html), [glypican 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglypican3.html) and [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html) can highlight syncytiotrophoblastic giant cells; can search for these cells in patients with moderate serum hCG elevation ([Histopathology 2013;62:71](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23240671))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Isochromosome 12p in 81% ([Pathol Res Pract 2012;208:628](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22906432), [Mod Pathol 2006;19:611](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16554737))
* *KIT* mutations in exon 17 codon 816 or *KIT* amplification detected in approximately 33% of cases and associated with advanced stage at presentation ([Cancer 2011;117:2096](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21523721))
  + As this *KIT* mutation involves exon 17, not exon 11 as in gastrointestinal stromal tumors, these cases are not responsive to *KIT* receptor inhibitors

Sample pathology report

* Left ovary, left oophorectomy:
  + Dysgerminoma (11 cm) (see synoptic report)
  + No ovarian surface involvement identified

Differential diagnosis

* Important to rule out other types of mixed germ cell tumor
  + [Embryonal carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorembryonal.html):
    - Mimicker, especially in poorly fixed specimens
    - Nuclei are more pleomorphic, with amphophilic cytoplasm and frequent apoptotic cells
    - Lack fibrous septae containing lymphocytes
    - [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html)+, [SOX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox2.html)+, **NANOG**+, [OCT3/4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct34.html)+, [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)-, [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html)-
  + [Yolk sac tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumoryolksac.html):
    - Classically variable morphologic patterns (commonly reticular / microcystic)
    - Lacks fibrous septae containing lymphocytes
    - [Glypican 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglypican3.html)+, [AFP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsafp.html)+, [OCT3/4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct34.html)-, [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)-, [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html)-

* [Clear cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorclearcell.html):
  + Tubulocystic or papillary architecture
  + Usually older patients
  + [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)+, [Napsin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnapsina.html)+, [racemase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsamacr.html)+, [HNF-1B](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsHNF1B.html)+,
    [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html)+, [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html)-, [OCT3/4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct34.html)-, [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)-, [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html)-

* **Lymphoma**:
  + Lacks fibrous septae
  + May be associated with thickened fallopian tube
  + Positive lymphoid markers and *IgH* clonality

* [Small cell carcinoma, hypercalcemic type](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorsmallcellhyper.html):
  + Tumor cells are usually small with scant cytoplasm; may see larger eosinophilic cells with rhabdoid inclusions in the large cell variant
  + Pseudofollicular spaces
  + [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)+, loss of [SMARCA4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsSMARCA4BRG1.html), [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html)-

Board review style question #2

Which of the following immunostains are typically positive in dysgerminoma and can confirm the diagnosis?

1. AFP, SALL4, OCT3/4, hCG
2. D2-40, CD117, OCT3/4, SALL4
3. OCT3/4, EMA, hCG, SALL4
4. PLAP, AFP, hCG, AE1 / AE3

Board review style answer #2

**B**. D2-40, CD117, OCT3/4, SALL4. SALL4 is positive in germ cell tumors. OCT3/4 positivity narrows the differential of germ cell tumors to dysgerminomas and embryonal carcinomas. Dysgerminomas are positive for CD117 and D2-40.
