Definition / general

* Presence of ectopic decidualized uterine stromal cells in the ovary

Essential features

* Presence of ectopic decidualized uterine stromal cells in the ovary
* Usually an incidental finding associated with pregnancy
* Typically transient, will regress 4 - 6 weeks postpartum

Terminology

* Ectopic decidua or ovarian deciduosis

Epidemiology

* Associated with pregnancy
* In a study of 307 consecutive caesarean sections, macroscopic deciduosis was found in 31 (10.1%) cases ([Eur J Obstet Gynecol Reprod Biol 2016;197:54](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26717495))
* Can also develop as a result of exogenous progesterone effect

Sites

* Ectopic decidua have been described in the [cervix](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixdecidual.html), ovary and fallopian tube; also peritoneal surface, appendix, bladder, small and large intestine, mesentery, [lymph nodes](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodesdecidualreaction.html)

Pathophysiology

* High levels of estrogen and progesterone during pregnancy induce mesenchymal fibroblast differentiation into decidual cells ([Eur J Obstet Gynecol Reprod Biol 2016;197:54](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26717495), [Pathol Res Pract 1993;189:352](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8332577), [Korean J Obstet Gynecol 2011;54:373](https://doi.org/10.5468/KJOG.2011.54.7.373))

Diagnosis

* Often an incidental finding in surgical specimens or a discrete nodule / mass discovered during caesarean section

Radiology description

* When the ovarian deciduosis is mass forming, it may be difficult to differentiate from a neoplastic process ([Case Rep Obstet Gynecol 2015;2015:217367](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25960900))

Case reports

* 31 year old woman with decidualized ovarian mass mimicking malignancy ([Case Rep Obstet Gynecol 2015;2015:217367](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25960900))
* 32 year old woman with ovarian deciduosis in pregnancy ([Korean J Obstet Gynecol 2011;54:373](https://doi.org/10.5468/KJOG.2011.54.7.373))
* 33 year old woman with ovarian and peritoneal deciduosis in pregnancy ([Diagn Histopathol 2011;17:313](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.mpdhp.2011.05.001))

Treatment

* Benign entity, no further treatment necessary; usually regresses 4 - 6 weeks postpartum
* If mass forming and showing worrisome features for malignancy on imaging, the lesion is excised to establish the correct diagnosis
* Reference: [Case Rep Obstet Gynecol 2015;2015:217367](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25960900)

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Large polygonal cells with abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm, bland nuclei and visible nucleoli
* No glands present (to differentiate it from decidualized endometriosis)
* Reference: [Case Rep Obstet Gynecol 2015;2015:217367](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25960900)

Positive stains

* [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html) ([Case Rep Obstet Gynecol 2015;2015:217367](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25960900))
* [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) ([Eur J Obstet Gynecol Reprod Biol 2016;197:54](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26717495))

Negative stains

* [Cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)
* Mesothelial markers ([WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html), [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html))

Sample pathology report

* Ovary, oophorectomy:
  + Benign ovary with focal deciduosis

Differential diagnosis

* [Endometriosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarynontumorendometriosis.html):
  + Can also undergo decidualization during pregnancy
  + Main difference is that ectopic decidua have no glands ([Gynecol Obstet Invest 2008;66:241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18645258))
* [Epithelial ovarian neoplasm](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorsurfacegeneral.html) when mass forming:
  + Histologic features are bland and mitotic activity is low in deciduosis
  + Absence of glands in deciduosis
  + Epithelial proliferation with variable atypia and architectural complexity in epithelial neoplasms
* [Peritoneal mesothelioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pleuraperitmesotheliomageneral.html) or **peritoneal carcinomatosis** when there is additional involvement of peritoneal surface:
  + Histologic features are bland in deciduosis; also, context of pregnancy is helpful
  + Increased atypia, proliferation and architectural complexity in carcinomatosis and mesothelioma
* [Metastatic](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumormetastatic.html) **squamous cell carcinoma** with keratinization:
  + More atypical
  + Presence of necrosis
  + Mitotically active
  + [Cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)+

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/ovarynontumorectopicdecidual_busca03.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/ovarynontumorectopicdecidual_busca03.jpg)

Which of the following is true about the ovarian change illustrated in the figure shown above?

1. It is a malignant process
2. It is only seen in the ovary
3. It requires lymph node sampling and full staging for management
4. More sampling is required to demonstrate the presence of glands
5. Typically associated with pregnancy

Board review style answer #1

**E.** Typically associated with pregnancy. The image shows ovarian deciduosis, which is associated with pregnancy, is typically transient and resolves postpartum. Answer A is incorrect because ovarian deciduosis is a benign process typically associated with pregnancy. Answer D is incorrect because it consists of stromal tissue, without glands. Answer C is incorrect because it typically regresses after pregnancy and does not require treatment. Answer B is incorrect because deciduosis can be seen at other sites (cervix, peritoneal cavity, etc.).

Board review style question #2

Which of the following is true about ovarian deciduosis?

1. It can be mistaken for endometriosis
2. It is CD10 negative by immunohistochemistry
3. Presence of nuclear atypia indicates malignant potential
4. Responds to chemotherapy
5. Treatment includes resection with or without chemotherapy

Board review style answer #2

**A.** It can be mistaken for endometriosis with stromal decidualization but unlike endometriosis, it does not contain glands. Answer B is incorrect because both have CD10 positive stroma. Answers C, D and E are incorrect because ovarian deciduosis is a benign incidental process and does not require treatment.
