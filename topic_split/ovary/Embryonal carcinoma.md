Definition / general

* Primitive appearing ovarian tumor that is histologically similar to embryonal carcinoma of the testis
* Often a component in a malignant mixed germ cell tumor of the ovary; the pure form is exceedingly rare

Essential features

* Median age is 14 - 15 years; patients often present with precocious puberty, vaginal bleeding, infertility, amenorrhea and mild hirsutism ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2023;40:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36088223))
* Both serum alpha fetoprotein (AFP) and beta human chorionic gonadotropin (βhCG) can be elevated (which can result in a positive pregnancy test) ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2023;40:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36088223))
* Positive IHC expression with OCT 3/4, CD30, SOX2, SALL4 and βhCG (if syncytiotrophoblast is present); negative for glypican and CD117
* Worst prognosis among germ cell tumors, even at low stage ([Cancer 1976;38:2420](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/63319))
* Large, primitive pleomorphic cells with abundant cytoplasm arranged in sheets and nests

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9070/3](https://iris.who.int/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=85) - embryonal carcinoma, NOS
* ICD-11: [2C73.Y & XH8MB9](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f685124533%2fmms%2fother?data=%7B%22dataType%22%3A%22pc%22%2C%22postcoordinationCodeSet%22%3A%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F685124533%2Fmms%2Fother%22%2C%22axisToValueIds%22%3A%7B%22histopathology%22%3A%5B%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F234074139%22%7D%5D%7D%7D%7D) - other specified malignant neoplasms of the ovary & embryonal carcinoma, NOS

Epidemiology

* Primarily in adolescents and young women with average age of 14 - 15 years ([Cancer 1976;38:2420](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/63319), [Semin Diagn Pathol 2023;40:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36088223))
* First described in 1976 by Kurman and Norris as a distinct type of malignant germ cell neoplasm to distinguish from endodermal sinus tumor of ovary ([Cancer 1976;38:2420](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/63319))
* Estimated to account for Cancer 1976;38:2420)

Sites

* Ovary
* Metastasis is possible to liver, lungs, retroperitoneal lymph nodes, peritoneal surfaces in up to half of all cases ([Cancer 1976;38:2420](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/63319))

Pathophysiology

* Chromosome 12 abnormalities in 5 of 6 cases analyzed by FISH (iscochromosome 12p or 12p amplification) ([Hum Pathol 2010;41:716](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20096442))

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Hormonal manifestation, including precocious puberty, vaginal bleeding, infertility, amenorrhea and mild hirsutism, in approximately half of patients ([Cancer 1976;38:2420](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/63319), [Semin Diagn Pathol 2023;40:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36088223))
* May show serum elevation of AFP or βhCG with positive pregnancy test
* Palpable unilateral abdominal or pelvic mass in 80% of patients ([Cancer 1976;38:2420](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/63319))
* May present with abdominal pain, fever, menstrual irregularities or weight loss ([Cancer 1976;38:2420](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/63319))
* Clinical evidence of an endocrinopathy is seen in approximately half of patients ([Cancer 1976;38:2420](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/63319))

Diagnosis

* Large, unilateral abdominal or adnexal mass on imaging ([Ultrasound Obstet Gynecol 2021;57:987](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33142349), [Cancer 1976;38:2420](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/63319))
* Pregnancy test may be positive (increased βhCG) ([Cancer 1976;38:2420](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/63319), [Semin Diagn Pathol 2023;40:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36088223))
* AFP may be increased ([Cancer 1976;38:2420](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/63319), [Semin Diagn Pathol 2023;40:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36088223))
* Definitive diagnosis made by histologic examination of surgical specimen ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2023;40:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36088223))

Laboratory

* Serum βhCG elevation resulting in positive pregnancy test ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2023;40:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36088223))
* Serum AFP may be elevated

Radiology description

* Unilateral, solid mass with cystic areas, irregular margins, rich vascularization ([Radiographics 2014;34:777](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24819795), [Ultrasound Obstet Gynecol 2021;57:987](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33142349))
* No reported preference for right or left ovary ([Cancer 1976;38:2420](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/63319))
* 1 embryonal carcinoma of the ovary measured at 216 mm ([Ultrasound Obstet Gynecol 2021;57:987](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33142349))

Prognostic factors

* Worst prognosis among germ cell tumors
* 5 year survival rate for stage I cases: 50% ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2023;40:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36088223), [Cancer 1976;38:2420](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/63319))

Case reports

* 11 year old girl with large abdominal mass and ascites ([Med Arch 2018;72:371](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30524172))
* 13 year old girl with abdominal pain and 25 cm mass ([Iran J Pathol 2016;11:66](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26870146))
* 19 year old woman with abdominal pain and massive ascites ([J Pediatr Adolesc Gynecol 2011;24:e1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20869284))
* 53 year old woman with irregular menses, pelvic pain and positive pregnancy test ([Gynecol Oncol 1996;63:133](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8898183))

Treatment

* Surgery with adjuvant chemotherapy
  + Bleomycin, etoposide and cisplatin (BEP) most common chemotherapy regimen ([Ann Oncol 2018;29:iv1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29697741))

Gross description

* Large, with median size of 17 cm ([Cancer 1976;38:2420](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/63319))
* Cut surface is gray-white or yellow ([Cancer 1976;38:2420](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/63319))
* With or without cysts containing hemorrhage or necrosis ([Cancer 1976;38:2420](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/63319))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Similar to embryonal carcinoma of the testis ([Cancer 1976;38:2420](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/63319))
* Often mixed with other malignant germ cell tumor components ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2023;40:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36088223), [Cancer 1976;38:2420](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/63319))
* Sheets and nests of large, primitive pleomorphic cells with abundant cytoplasm ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2023;40:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36088223), [Cancer 1976;38:2420](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/63319))
* Round nuclei with coarse membranes, at least 1 prominent nucleolus and brisk mitotic activity ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2023;40:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36088223))
* Occasional papillae and gland morphology ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2023;40:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36088223))
* Syncytiotrophoblast-like tumor cells are seen (hCG+) around sheets of tumor cells or within stroma ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2023;40:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36088223), [Cancer 1976;38:2420](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/63319))
* Hyaline globules, similar to yolk sac tumor, may also be present ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2023;40:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36088223))
* Glandular pattern may mimic endometrial carcinoma or glandular morphology of a yolk sac tumor ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2023;40:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36088223))

Immunofluorescence description

* Chromosome 12p overrepresentation
* Isochromosome 12p

Positive stains

* [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html), [OCT 3/4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct34.html) and [SOX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox2.html) nuclear positivity ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:894](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19295406), [Histopathology 2013;62:71](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23240671))
* [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html) membranous positivity ([Hum Pathol 2010;41:716](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20096442))
  + [OCT 3/4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct34.html) more sensitive than [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html) but can also be expressed in dysgerminoma ([Histopathology 2013;62:71](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23240671))
* [AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html) ([Histopathology 2013;62:71](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23240671))
* [βhCG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshcg.html) positive in syncytiotrophoblastic cells ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2023;40:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36088223))
* [AFP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsafp.html) may be expressed in both tumor cells and hyaline globules ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2023;40:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36088223))
* [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html) expression might decrease after chemotherapy ([Hum Pathol 2006;37:662](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16733205))

* [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) ([Cancer 1988;62:1113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2457424))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Chromosome 12p FISH can be helpful in establishing a diagnosis in conjunction with IHC ([Hum Pathol 2010;41:716](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20096442))

Sample pathology report

* Left ovary and fallopian tube, left salpingo-oophorectomy:
  + Malignant mixed germ cell tumor (see comment)
  + Comment: The ovarian tumor is compatible with a malignant mixed germ cell tumor composed of embryonal carcinoma (\_\_%) (e.g., yolk sac tumor \_\_%; immature teratoma \_\_%; with or without choriocarcinoma \_\_%). Sheets of pleomorphic, primitive cells with brisk mitotic activity, tumor cell necrosis and positive expression of OCT 3/4 and CD30 are consistent with a component of embryonal carcinoma. Syncytiotrophoblastic cells are present (hCG+), which can raise concern for a component of choriocarcinoma; however, the presence of isolated syncytiotrophoblastic cells has been reported in embryonal carcinoma and there is no evidence of choriocarcinoma in examined sections of this extensively sampled ovarian tumor.

Differential diagnosis

* [Yolk sac tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumoryolksac.html):
  + Microcystic / reticular, polyvesicular vitelline and festoon-like patterns
  + Less nuclear pleomorphism
  + Positive expression with [AFP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsafp.html) and [glypican 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglypican3.html)
  + Negative for [OCT 3/4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct34.html) or [SOX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox2.html)
* [Dysgerminoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumordysgerminoma.html):
  + Lack of glandular and papillary architecture
  + Nuclei smaller / more monotonous and less pleomorphic
  + Presence of lymphocytic or granulomatous stromal infiltrate
  + [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html) negative
  + [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html) and [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html) positive
  + No diffuse keratin expression
* [Choriocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorchorio.html):
  + Mixture of multinucleated syncytiotrophoblast and mononucleated cytotrophoblast
  + More extensive hemorrhage with dilated sinusoids
  + [hCG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshcg.html), [inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html) and [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgata3.html) positive
  + Negative for [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html) and [OCT 3/4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct34.html)
* [Poorly or undifferentiated carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusundifferentiated.html):
  + Late reproductive age and postmenopausal patients
  + Usually has a glandular component
  + Typically [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html), [AFP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsafp.html) and [hCG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshcg.html) negative
* [Juvenile granulosa cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorgctjuv.html):
  + Follicle-like spaces with luteinized tumor cells
  + Lack of syncytiotrophoblast
  + Positive for [inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html), [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html) and negative for [AFP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsafp.html)
* [Poorly differentiated Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor (SLCT)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorsertolileydig.html):
  + Portion of the tumor will contain a better differentiated component of SLCT
  + Positive for [inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html) and [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html)

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/ovaryembryonalcarcinomabentz11.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/ovaryembryonalcarcinomabentz11.jpg)

What is the most specific immunohistochemical stain for an embryonal carcinoma of the ovary?

1. AFP
2. βhCG
3. CD30
4. CD117
5. OCT 3/4

Board review style answer #1

**C**. CD30. CD30 is the most specific marker for embryonal carcinoma of the ovary. Answer A is incorrect because AFP is a marker for yolk sac tumor. Answer D is incorrect because CD117 highlights dysgerminoma. Answer B is incorrect because βhCG is a marker for syncytiotrophoblast and choriocarcinoma. Answer E is incorrect because while OCT 3/4 can stain embryonal carcinoma of the ovary, it also stains dysgerminoma, whereas dysgerminoma should be negative for CD30.

[Comment Here](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/)

Reference: [Embryonal carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorembryonal.html)

Board review style question #2

The glandular pattern of embryonal carcinoma, particularly if present with hyaline globules, can mimic which of the following tumors?

1. Choriocarcinoma
2. Dysgerminoma
3. Leydig cell tumor
4. Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor
5. Yolk sac tumor

Board review style answer #2

**E**. Yolk sac tumor. The glandular pattern of embryonal carcinoma can mimic a yolk sac tumor, particularly in the presence of hyaline globules. Glandular architecture can also mimic a poorly differentiated carcinoma. Answer A is incorrect because choriocarcinoma will have a mixture of syncytiotrophoblast and cytotrophoblast without a prominent glandular architecture. Answer B is incorrect because dysgerminoma will not show a glandular architecture and will have fibrous septa with lymphocytic or granulomatous infiltrate. Answer D is incorrect because Sertoli-Leydig cell tumors will not show a prominent glandular architecture and stain with inhibin / calretinin. Answer C is incorrect because Leydig cell tumors are rare and arise in the hilum and have Reinke crystals.

[Comment Here](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/)

Reference: [Embryonal carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorembryonal.html)
