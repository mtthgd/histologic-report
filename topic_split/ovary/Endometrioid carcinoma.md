Definition / general

* Ovarian carcinoma resembling endometrioid adenocarcinoma of the endometrium

Essential features

* Most common ovarian endometrioid tumor
* Usually low grade and diagnosed at early stages
* May be associated with endometriosis and adenofibroma

Terminology

* Seromucinous carcinoma is included as a subtype of endometrioid ovarian carcinoma in the 2020 WHO blue book for female genital tumors

ICD coding

* ICD-11: [2C73.01](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f996484353) - endometrioid adenocarcinoma of ovary

Epidemiology

* 10% of primary ovarian carcinomas ([J Natl Cancer Inst 2019;111:60](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29718305))
* Mean patient age is 55 years
* Associated with endometriosis / endometriotic cyst (15%), endometrioid adenofibroma, synchronous endometrial endometrioid adenocarcinoma or endometrial hyperplasia (15 - 30%) ([Eur J Gynaecol Oncol 2015;36:21](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25872329))
* Risk factors: endometriosis, hormone replacement therapy, first degree family history of breast carcinoma ([Lancet Oncol 2012;13:385](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22361336), [J Clin Oncol 2016;34:2888](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27325851))
* May occur in the setting of Lynch syndrome ([Gynecol Oncol 2018;150:92](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29716739), [Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:1173](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25025451))

Sites

* Ovaries

Pathophysiology

* Most common molecular alterations: WNT / beta catenin signaling pathway (*CTNNB1* - 53%), PI3K pathway (*PIK3CA* - 40% and *PTEN* - 17%) ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:128](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23765252))

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Most common symptoms are abdominal distention and pain
* Background endometriosis is not associated with survival ([J Gynecol Oncol 2018;29:e18](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29400011))

Laboratory

* Elevated CA-125 ([Asian Pac J Cancer Prev 2013;14:4545](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24083699))

Radiology description

* Most common appearance is a cystic lesion ([Acta Radiol 2016;57:758](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26307063))

Prognostic factors

* Stage is the most important prognostic factor
* Survival rates: > 95% for stage IA and IB and 51% for stage III and IV ([J Natl Cancer Inst 2019;111:60](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29718305))
* Most tumors are confined to the ovary at diagnosis
* *CTNNB1* (beta catenin) mutation and low tumor grade are associated with a favorable outcome ([Histopathology 2019;74:452](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30326146), [Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:68](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32769429))
* Synchronous endometrioid carcinomas of the endometrium and ovaries are often clonally related but typically have indolent clinical behavior ([J Natl Cancer Inst 2016;108:djv428](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26832771), [J Natl Cancer Inst 2016;108:djv427](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26832770), [Mod Pathol 2021;34:994](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33328602))

Case reports

* 39 year old woman with Cowden syndrome incidentally detected from a metachronous ovarian endometrioid carcinoma ([BMC Cancer 2019;19:1014](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31664961))
* 41 year old woman with mixed adenocarcinoma and yolk sac tumor ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2019;12:3549](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31934203))
* 45 year old woman with endometrial endometrioid and synchronous bilateral endometrioid ovarian cancer ([Anticancer Res 2017;37:969](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28314254))
* 52 year old woman with ovarian mass on computed tomography imaging and a raised CA-125 ([Case of the Month #500](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case500.htm))
* 65 year old postmenopausal woman with ovarian endometrioid carcinoma presenting as an abdominal wall abscess ([J Midlife Health 2018;9:222](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30692822))

Treatment

* Surgery for early stage cancers
* Adjuvant chemotherapy is associated with survival benefit for patients with inadequately staged and grade 2 stage I cancers ([Gynecol Oncol 2020;156:315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31839340))
* Patients with advanced stage disease (FIGO III and IV) might benefit from platinum based chemotherapy ([Future Oncol 2018;14:123](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29098867))

Gross description

* Usually unilateral; only 5% bilateral
* Cystic with solid component and areas of hemorrhage
* With or without polypoid nodule in endometriotic cyst
* Mean tumor size: 11 cm (range: 3 - 22 cm)
* Reference: [Arch Gynecol Obstet 2008;278:209](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18259768)

Frozen section description

* Depending on histologic grade, a combination of glandular and solid areas may be seen
* Squamous differentiation may be present
* Differential diagnosis depends on histologic grade but includes metastatic carcinoma, well differentiated Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor and serous carcinoma ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2019;143:47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30785337))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Most common morphologic pattern is confluent (back to back) glands
* Stromal invasion is usually by expansion; rarely, destructive stromal invasion can be observed
* Squamous metaplasia (morules or keratin pearls), cytoplasmic mucin, intracytoplasmic vacuoles, oncocytic changes, clear cell changes and cilia and sex cord-like elements (sertoliform) can be observed; none of these morphologic features affect the histologic grade ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1203](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17667544))
* Histologic grading: same as for endometrial endometrioid adenocarcinoma
  + FIGO grade 1: less than 5% solid component
  + FIGO grade 2: 6 - 50% solid component
  + FIGO grade 3: more than 50% solid component
* Endometriosis or adenofibroma may be present in the background
* Vascular invasion is rare
* Might be associated with serous, undifferentiated carcinoma and yolk sac tumor (mixed carcinoma) ([Am J Surg Pathol 1994;18:687](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8017563), [Am J Surg Pathol 1996;20:1056](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8764742))
* Morphologic features in favor of synchronous endometrial and ovarian tumors:
  + Both tumors are low grade
  + No myometrial invasion or less than 50% myometrial inivasion
  + There is no involvement in any other anatomical sites
  + No lymphovascular involvement in either tumor
  + Background atypical endometrial hyperplasia in the endometrium
  + Unilateral ovarian involvement and unifocal parenchymal distribution of tumor
  + Lack of ovarian capsular, multifocal or hilar involvement
  + Presence of endometriosis or adenofibroma in the ovary ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2019;38:S75](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30550485))

FIGO grade 1, background endometriosis

Cytology description

* Large cohesive cell clusters
* Mild nuclear membrane irregularities
* May see squamous differentiation
* Detection in pelvic washing: sensitivity 58%, specificity 89% [Cancer 2004;102:150](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15211473)

Positive stains

* [Keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) cocktails, [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html), [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html) (15% negative), [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) and [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html) ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2005;22:3](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16512597), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2016;35:430](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26974996))
* [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html): wild / normal type expression (most tumors) ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2016;35:430](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26974996), [Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:534](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29309296))
  + High grade endometrioid cancers might demonstrate mutational pattern (nuclear staining in > 80% of cells, complete absence of staining, diffuse cytoplasmic staining)
* Mismatch repair protein deficiency has been reported in up to 23% of tumors ([Gynecol Oncol 2020;156:669](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31924330), [Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:235](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30256257))

Negative stains

* [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html) (10 - 14% can express) ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2019;38:353](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29901523))
* [Napsin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnapsina.html)
* [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html)
* [SATB2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssatb2.html) (15% positive in squamous morules)
* [Inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Ovarian endometrioid cancers and associated endometriosis share mutations in majority of cases (85 - 90%) ([J Pathol 2018;245:265](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29604057))
* *CTNNB1* (53%), *PIK3CA* (40%), *KRAS* (33%), *ARID1A* (30%) and *PTEN* (17%) are common mutations ([Cancer Cell 2007;11:321](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17418409), [Mod Pathol 2014;27:128](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23765252))
* The Cancer Genome Atlas (TCGA) molecular classifiers for endometrial carcinoma has categorized ovarian endometrioid carcinoma into prognostically significant groups ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:1748](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28776572))

Sample pathology report

* Left ovary and fallopian tube, salpingo-oophorectomy:
  + Endometrioid adenocarcinoma, FIGO grade 1 (see synoptic report)
  + Tumor size: 8.5 cm
  + Ovarian surface: not involved by carcinoma
  + Fallopian tube: not involved by carcinoma
  + Additional findings: background endometriotic cyst

Differential diagnosis

* [Adult granulosa cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorgctadult.html):
  + Presence of other patterns throughout the tumor
  + Squamous differentiation in endometroid carcinoma
  + Nuclear grooves in adult granulosa cell tumor
  + Adult granulosa cell tumor: [inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html)+ and [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)-
* [Sertoli cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorsertoli.html) / [Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorsertolileydig.html):
  + Presence of other architectural patterns
  + Leydig cells
  + Heterologous elements of Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor (if present)
  + Presence of squamous differentiation in endometrioid carcinoma
  + Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor: [inhibin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html)+ and [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)-
* [High grade serous carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorserouscarcinomahg.html):
  + Architecture: solid, papillary, labyrinthine, glandular
  + Nuclei are large
  + Nuclear size variability > 3 fold
  + High mitotic activity (> 12 mitoses per 10 high power fields)
* [Metastatic colonic carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorcolorectaladeno.html):
  + Multinodular growth
  + Presence of glands with dirty necrosis
  + Metastatic colonic carcinoma: [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html)+, [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html)+
* [Metastatic endometrial endometrioid adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusendometrioid.html):
  + Metastatic endometrial endometrioid adenocarcinoma is usually high grade (FIGO grade 3)
  + Bilateral involvement
  + Multinodular growth
  + No endometriosis / adenofibroma background
* [Clear cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorclearcell.html):
  + Papillary, tubular and solid architectural patterns
  + Uniformly atypical nuclei hobnail cells
  + [Napsin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnapsina.html)+, [HNF1β](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsHNF1B.html)+
