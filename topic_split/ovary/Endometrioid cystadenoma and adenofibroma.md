Definition / general

* Benign tumors with endometrioid type glands, sometimes associated with endometriosis

Essential features

* Benign biphasic tumor with endometrioid type glands and fibromatous stroma
* Sometimes associated with endometriosis
* Squamous or mucinous metaplasia or dystrophic stromal calcifications can be seen

ICD coding

* ICD-O:
  + [8380/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=71) - endometrioid cystadenoma, NOS
  + [8381/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=71) - endometrioid adenofibroma, NOS
* ICD-11:
  + [2F32.Y & XH1CX5](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f181365103%2fmms%2fother?data=%7B%22dataType%22%3A%22pc%22%2C%22postcoordinationCodeSet%22%3A%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F181365103%2Fmms%2Fother%22%2C%22axisToValueIds%22%3A%7B%22histopathology%22%3A%5B%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F1024578755%22%7D%5D%7D%7D%7D) - other specified benign neoplasm of ovary & endometrioid adenofibroma, NOS

Epidemiology

* Uncommon tumors, most often seen in postmenopausal women ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2000;19:276](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10907178))

Sites

* Ovary

Pathophysiology

* In some cases, endometrioid cystadenomas may represent endometriotic cysts without obvious endometrial type stroma

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Can be an incidental finding
* Symptoms may include abnormal vaginal bleeding, abdominal pain or pelvic mass ([J Obstet Gynaecol 2011;31:352](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21534769))

Diagnosis

* Detected on pelvic imaging (ultrasound, CT, MRI)
* Cystectomy / oophorectomy

Radiology description

* Low T2 weighted signal intensity of solid components ([Radiographics 2021;41:289](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33186060))

Prognostic factors

* These tumors are benign

Case reports

* 46 year old woman with pelvic pain, postmenopausal bleeding and 6.3 cm complex solid and cystic right adnexal mass ([J Obstet Gynaecol 2011;31:352](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21534769))
* 60 year old woman with vaginal bleeding and 14 cm pelvic mass ([Diagn Cytopathol 2004;31:38](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15236263))
* 64 year old woman with solid pelvic tumor ([J Obstet Gynaecol Res 1998;24:161](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9631607))

Treatment

* Not applicable; these tumors are benign

Gross description

* Cystadenoma: usually unilocular cyst
* Adenofibroma: firm, white nodule with honeycomb appearance to cut surface, sometimes in the wall of an endometriotic cyst ([J Obstet Gynaecol 2011;31:352](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21534769))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Cystadenoma:
  + Cyst lined by benign endometrioid epithelium without endometrial stroma
  + Endometriosis may be present
* Adenofibroma:
  + Widely spaced benign endometrioid glands associated with fibromatous stroma
  + Endometriosis may be present
* Areas of mucinous or serous differentiation can be seen ([Histopathology 2021;78:445](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32810322))

Cytology description

* Bland endometrioid cells (epithelial cells with moderate amounts of cytoplasm and bland, eccentrically placed nuclei) and fragments of ovarian type stroma ([Diagn Cytopathol 2004 Jul;31:38](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15236263))

Positive stains

* [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html), [estrogen receptor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html), [progesterone receptor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html)
* Nuclear [beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html) may be seen rarely; [beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html) alterations are better described in endometrioid borderline tumors ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2022 Feb 11 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35149615), [J Pathol 2006;208:708](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16429393))

Negative stains

* [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html)

Sample pathology report

* Ovary, right, oophorectomy:
  + Right ovary with endometrioid adenofibroma arising in a background of endometriosis

Differential diagnosis

* [Endometriosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarynontumorendometriosis.html):
  + Has associated endometrial type stroma with or without hemosiderin laden macrophages
* [Serous cystadenoma / adenofibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorserousbenign.html):
  + Glands show only tubal type differentiation
  + [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html), [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) diffusely positive
  + Membranous [beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html) staining
* [Seromucinous cystadenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovaryseromucinouscystadenoma.html):
  + Glands show admixture of Müllerian epithelia with bland cytological features
    - Ciliated, endocervical type, mucinous, endometrioid (which may have squamous or mucinous differentiation)
  + Also associated with endometriosis
  + Some authors believe that a subset of seromucinous cystadenomas represent benign endometrioid neoplasms with foci of mucinous or serous differentiation ([Histopathology 2021;78:445](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32810322))
