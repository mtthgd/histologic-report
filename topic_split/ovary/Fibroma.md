Definition / general

* Benign stromal tumor composed of fibroblastic cells within a variably collagenous stroma

Essential features

* Benign stromal tumor composed of spindled, ovoid to round cells within a variably collagenous stroma
* Variants include fibroma with minor sex cord elements, cellular fibroma and mitotically active cellular fibroma
* Important differential diagnoses for cellular fibroma include diffuse adult type granulosa cell tumor and fibrosarcoma
* Gorlin syndrome should be suspected in young patients with bilateral ovarian fibroma
* Long term follow up is recommended for cellular fibromas associated with rupture and adhesions due to increased risk of recurrence

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8810/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=80) - fibroma, NOS
* ICD-10: [D27](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D27-/D27) - benign neoplasm of ovary
* ICD-11: [2F32.1](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f871413134) - ovarian fibroma

Epidemiology

* Most common ovarian stromal tumor
  + ~4% of all ovarian tumors ([Cancer 1981;47:2663](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7260859))
  + Cellular fibromas represent ~10% of all ovarian fibromas
* Mean age 48 years, uncommon before 30
* Occurs in ~25% of patients with nevoid basal cell carcinoma syndrome (Gorlin syndrome) ([J Med Genet 1993;30:460](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8326488))
  + Autosomal dominant disease due to mutations of the human homologue of the Drosophila gene *PTCH* (patched)

Sites

* Ovary

Pathophysiology

* Neoplastic transformation of ovarian stromal cells due to hereditary or sporadic genetic abnormalities (see [Molecular / cytogenetics description](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorfibroma.html#ovarytumorfibromamolecular))

Clinical features

* Most commonly symptoms related to an ovarian mass, such as abdominal pain or distension and increased urinary frequency or may be incidental
* Can be associated with ovarian torsion ([BMC Surg 2014;14:38](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24962423))
* Rarely hormonal manifestations
* Ascites in large (> 10 cm) tumors in 10% of cases ([Am J Obstet Gynecol 1970;107:538](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4316407))
* Syndromic ovarian fibromas:
  + Often diagnosed before the age of 30 years, including children
  + Usually bilateral (75%), calcified and nodular, often overlapping medially (leading to misdiagnosis as calcified uterine leiomyoma) ([Genet Med 2004;6:530](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15545751))
  + May be renin secreting, leading to gestational hypertension ([Br J Obstet Gynaecol 1994;101:1015](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7999713))
  + May recur ([Hum Reprod 2001;16:1261](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11387302), [Obstet Gynecol 1983;61:95S](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6823402))
* Meigs syndrome occurs in ~1% of cases ([Am J Obstet Gynecol 1937;33:249](https://www.ajog.org/article/S0002-9378%2837%2980015-0/abstract), [Eur J Obstet Gynecol Reprod Biol 2000;92:199](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10996681))
  + Characterized by the triad of benign adnexal mass, ascites and pleural effusion
  + Due to seepage of fluid from ovary into peritoneal cavity and then into 1 or both pleural cavities either via lymphatics or through a communication between abdominal and pleural cavity (lumbocostal triangle)

Diagnosis

* Histologic examination for definitive diagnosis
* Tentative diagnosis can be made grossly and at frozen section evaluation ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2002;19:237](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12469791))

Laboratory

* Can lead to nonspecific increase in serum CA125 ([Gynecol Oncol 1995;59:405](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8522265))

Radiology description

* Ultrasonography:
  + Usually solid, homogeneous and hypoechoic mass with posterior acoustic shadowing, similar to a pedunculated subserosal uterine leiomyoma ([AJR Am J Roentgenol 1985;144:1239](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3890488))
  + Heterogeneous echogenicity in tumors with necrosis, hemorrhage or cystic degeneration
* Computed tomography:
  + Slightly hypoattenuating solid mass with poor, very slow contrast enhancement ([Radiographics 2002;22:1305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12432104))
  + Rarely calcifications
* Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI):
  + T1: homogeneous low signal intensity
  + T2:
    - Well circumscribed mass with low signal intensity ([J Magn Reson Imaging 1997;7:465](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9170028))
    - Hyperintense areas due to edema or cystic degeneration
    - Characteristic feature is a band of T2 hypointensity separating the tumor from the uterus on all imaging planes
  + Gadolinium: heterogeneous enhancement
  + May mimic malignancy ([Radiographics 2002;22:1305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12432104))

Prognostic factors

* Excellent in most cases
* Ovarian surface involvement and extraovarian adhesions occur in 6% of cellular fibromas and 10% of mitotically active cellular fibromas ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:929](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16861962))
* Extraovarian spread at surgery in 11% of cellular fibromas and 13% of mitotically active cellular fibromas ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:929](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16861962))
* Cellular fibromas may recur, often after a long interval, warranting long term follow up ([Cancer 1981;47:2663](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7260859))

Case reports

* 15 year old girl with bilateral ovarian fibroma associated with Gorlin syndrome ([J Pediatr Adolesc Gynecol 2011;24:e5](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20817576))
* 19 year old woman with large ovarian fibroma presenting with spontaneous abdominal bleeding ([Case Rep Obstet Gynecol 2019;2019:9834915](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31380131))
* 34 year old woman with mitotically active cellular fibroma that recurred 16 years later ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2020 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33252401))
* 40 year old woman with bilateral ovarian fibroma presenting as Meigs syndrome ([J Obstet Gynaecol 2013;33:636](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23919874))
* 66 year old woman with extraovarian fibroma with minor sex cord elements ([Int J Surg Pathol 2017;25:472](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28351194))

Treatment

* Surgical excision (salpingo-oophorectomy, oophorectomy or ovarian sparing procedure with or without hysterectomy depending on patient’s age)
* Cellular fibromas require long term follow up, particularly in the setting of ovarian surface involvement, intraoperative rupture or extraovarian spread ([Cancer 1981;47:2663](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7260859))

Gross description

* Well circumscribed mass with smooth, lobulated surface
* Firm, chalky, solid, white to yellow-white to tan-yellow cut surface that may be whorled
* Frequent edema resulting in softer consistency
* Mean size 6 cm (range 1 - 21.5)
* Usually unilateral (< 10% bilateral)
* Cystic degeneration in ~25% of cases
* Calcifications in ~10% of cases
* ~20% present as pedunculated or polypoid growths on ovarian surface
* With or without hemorrhage or necrosis
* Cellular fibroma:
  + Mean size 8 - 9 cm (range 1 - 19)
  + Tan-yellow, soft and fleshy cut surface ([Cancer 1981;47:2663](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7260859))
  + With or without surface adhesions
* Fibroma associated with Gorlin syndrome:
  + Bilateral, multinodular and calcified ([J Pediatr Adolesc Gynecol 2011;24:e5](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20817576))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Conventional fibroma:
  + Recapitulates ovarian cortex
  + Usually well circumscribed but nonencapsulated
  + Variably cellular fascicular or less frequently, storiform growth of tumor cells within a variably collagenous stroma, sometimes with hyaline plaques ([Cancer 1981;47:2663](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7260859))
  + Bland spindled to ovoid nuclei with pointy ends and scant eosinophilic cytoplasm blending with surrounding stroma
  + Occasional mitoses (usually up to 3 mitoses per 10 high power fields)
  + With or without Verocay-like areas (slightly wavy, parallel arrays of spindled nuclei), calcification, edema, hemorrhage, infarct type necrosis, rare groups of luteinized cells
  + Rarely intracytoplasmic lipid (may be diagnosed as thecoma), eosinophilic hyaline globules, melanin pigment or bizarre nuclei ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2009;28:356](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19483628), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2019;38:92](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29140884), [Int J Surg Pathol 2020 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32390474))
* Cellular fibroma (10% of cases):
  + Resembles diffuse type of adult granulosa cell tumor
  + Densely cellular with little intercellular collagen
  + Bland spindled nuclei with up to 3 mitoses per 10 high power fields ([Cancer 1981;47:2663](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7260859))
  + "Mitotically active cellular fibroma" has been proposed for cellular fibromas with ≥ 4 mitoses per 10 high power fields ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:929](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16861962))
* Fibroma with minor sex cord elements:
  + Sex cord component accounting for < 10% of overall tumor volume
  + No prognostic significance

Electron microscopy description

* Numerous thin, elongated cells with intercommunicating cytoplasmic processes admixed with abundant collagen fibrils ([Cancer 1971;27:438](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/5100406))
  + Fibrils appear grouped in bundles with a crossbanding pattern composed of a light band bound by two thin dark bands (recurring at intervals of 600 Å)
  + Cells have indistinct cell borders and are compressed by collagenous stroma
  + Elongated and thin nuclei with marked peripheral heterochromatin condensation
  + Scant cytoplasm containing few mitochondria and scant endoplasmic reticulum

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Trisomy or tetrasomy 12 ([Ann Pathol 2001;21:393](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11852357), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 1990;2:48](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2177641), [Gynecol Oncol 1990;38:28](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2354823))
* Rarely, *IDH1* mutations ([Histopathology 2013;62:667](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23347143))
* Loss of heterozygosity at 9q22.3 (*PTCH1*) and 19p13.3 (*STK11*) in cellular fibromas ([Hum Pathol 2005;36:792](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16084949))
* No or rare *FOXL2* mutations ([Am J Surg Pathol 2013;37:1450](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23774170), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2018;37:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28700438))

Sample pathology report

* Right ovary and fallopian tube, salpingo-oophorectomy:
  + Ovary: fibroma
  + Fallopian tube: unremarkable

Differential diagnosis

* [Diffuse adult granulosa cell tumor (GCT)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorgctadult.html):
  + Typically see the other patterns of granulosa cell tumor
  + Diffusely positive for [inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html) and [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html)
  + Nested [reticulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsreticulin.html) staining pattern ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2018;37:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28700438))
  + *FOXL2* mutations ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:484](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21378549), [N Engl J Med 2009;360:2719](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19516027))
* [Thecoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorthecoma.html):
  + May present with estrogenic manifestations, including endometrial carcinoma
  + Diffuse or rarely, lobulated or nested growth
  + Uniform tumor cells with abundant pale gray cytoplasm and indistinct cell membranes imparting a syncytial appearance
  + Ovoid to round nuclei, with or without nucleoli or nuclear grooves
  + None to minimal mitotic activity
  + With or without hyaline plaques, calcifications, adipose metaplasia, small clusters of steroid type cells with eosinophilic to clear cytoplasm ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:1023](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25025365))
  + Rarely, scattered pleomorphic bizarre nuclei due to degenerative changes ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 1983;1:325](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6309684), [Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:1023](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25025365))
  + Usually positive for [inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html), [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html) and other sex cord markers
* [Sclerosing stromal tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorsclerosingstroma.html):
  + Often occurs in women in their third to fourth decades
  + Alternating cellular and paucicellular areas with prominent staghorn vasculature
  + Pseudolobulation and biphasic population of cells (luteinized and spindled)
  + Usually positive for [inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html) and [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html)
* [Leiomyoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorleiomyoma.html):
  + More prominent fascicular growth pattern with intersecting long fascicles of spindle cells with cigar shaped nuclei, eosinophilic cytoplasm and paranuclear vacuoles
  + Positive for [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) and [caldesmon](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscaldesmon.html)
* [Endometrial stromal tumor, low grade](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumoress.html):
  + Typical features of low grade endometrial stromal neoplasia, including permeative growth and arteriole-like vessels
  + Diffusely positive for [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html)
  + With or without characteristic gene fusions such as *JAZF1-SUZ12* ([Orphanet J Rare Dis 2016;11:15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26879382))
* [Metastatic gastrointestinal stromal tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumormetastatic.html):
  + Prior history or concomitant extraovarian disease
  + With or without epithelioid or spindled to epithelioid morphology
  + Pale cytoplasm, ill defined cell membranes, with or without skenoid fibers
  + Positive for [KIT](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html) and [DOG1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsDOG1.html)
* [Ovarian stromal hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarynontumorhyperthecosis.html):
  + Bilateral
  + Diffuse or multinodular proliferation of stromal cells
  + Lacking collagenous stroma and hyaline plaques
* [Massive edema](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarymassiveedema.html):
  + Unilateral or bilateral
  + No well defined mass
  + Proliferation of stromal cells with marked intercellular edema
  + Entrapment of preexisting ovarian structures (follicles, corpora lutea or albicantia)
* [Fibromatosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuefibromatosisdeep.html):
  + Unilateral or bilateral
  + No well defined mass
  + Proliferation of stromal cells with abundant dense collagen
  + Entrapment of preexisting ovarian structures
* [Fibrosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorfibrosarcoma.html):
  + Exceptionally rare and no longer considered a distinct WHO entity
  + Hypercellular, composed of disorderly fascicles of spindle cells with scant cytoplasm and moderate to marked nuclear atypia, elevated mitotic activity including atypical forms ([Cancer 1981;47:2663](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7260859))
  + Presence of ≥ 4 mitoses per 10 high power fields is not sufficient to diagnose fibrosarcoma in the absence of significant cytologic atypia ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:929](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16861962))
  + With or without necrosis and hemorrhage
  + Focally positive for [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html) or [inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html), negative for [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2002;26:1477](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12409724), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2007;26:359](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17885484))

Microscopic examination of an 8 cm unilateral ovarian mass demonstrates a densely cellular neoplasm composed of intersecting fascicles of spindle cells with mild cytologic atypia and approximately 9 mitotic figures per 10 high power fields. Reticulin staining shows pericellular staining pattern and molecular testing is negative for *FOXL2* mutation.

What is the most likely diagnosis?

1. Fibroma
2. Fibrosarcoma
3. Leiomyoma
4. Leiomyosarcoma

Board review style question #2

You receive an intraoperative consultation for bilateral multinodular ovarian masses in a 16 year old patient. Microscopic examination demonstrates bland, monomorphic spindled cells within a collagenous stroma.

Mutation of which of the following genes is most likely associated with this entity?

1. *FOXL2- HMGA2
   - JAZF1
   - PTCH*
2. Vimentin

Board review style answer #2

**D.** *PTCH*. Bilateral ovarian fibroma in a young patient is most likely associated with Gorlin syndrome.
