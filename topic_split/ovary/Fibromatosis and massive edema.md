Definition / general

* Tumor-like enlargement of ovary due to edema fluid
* First described in 1969 ([Obstet Gynecol 1969;34:564](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/5822682))
* Patients present with pain, abdominal mass, menstrual irregularities, virilization, precocious puberty, Meigs syndrome (with ascites and pleural effusion, [eMedicine: Meigs Syndrome](http://emedicine.medscape.com/article/255450-overview))
* May be due to partial torsion of mesovarium leading to interference of venous / lymphatic drainage
* Usually young women
* Right sided involvement is more common

Gross description

* Marked ovarian enlargement, watery cut surface, no necrosis

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Marked edema of stroma surrounding follicles and clusters of luteinized cells
* Stroma around vessels and in superficial cortical zone is normal
* Variable stromal luteinization

Differential diagnosis

* [Fibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorfibroma.html): circumscribed, tumor cells replace ovarian architecture
* [Krukenberg tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorappendiceal.html): usually bilateral, signet ring cells present
