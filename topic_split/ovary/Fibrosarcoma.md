Definition / general

* Very rare (More common in [bone](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonefibrosarcoma.html)

Epidemiology

* Median age 49 years, range 20 - 73 years

Clinical features

* Pelvic pain, abdominal enlargement, awareness of abdominal mass ([Cancer 1981;47:2663](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7260859))

Laboratory

* CA125 and CEA in normal range

Radiology description

* MRI: solid mass ([Anticancer Res 2007;27:4365](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18214045))

Prognostic factors

* FIGO stage ([BMC Cancer 2010;10:585](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20979607))

Case reports

* 8 year old girl with nevoid basal cell carcinoma syndrome ([Am J Surg Pathol 1984;8:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6703200))
* 32 year old woman ([Anticancer Res 2007;27:4365](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18214045))
* 52 year old woman ([Int J Gynecol Cancer 2005;15:1142](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16343198))

Treatment

* Surgical resection with or without adjuvant chemotherapy or radiation

Gross description

* 5 - 23 cm solid mass, tan-yellow discoloration and partial necrosis ([BMC Cancer 2010;10:585](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20979607))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Spindle cells arranged in sheets and intersecting fascicles creating a diffuse herringbone appearance
* Mild to moderate nuclear atypia
* Reticulin stain separates individual neoplastic cells
* Mitotic activity > 4/10 HPF is most important criteria for diagnosing fibrosarcoma

Cytology description

* Pleomorphic oval to spindle shaped cells with indistinct cell borders and atypical mitotic figures
