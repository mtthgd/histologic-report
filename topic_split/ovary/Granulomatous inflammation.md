Definition / general

* Necrotizing or nonnecrotizing granulomatous inflammation of the ovary

Epidemiology

* Cortical granulomas are common incidental findings with uncertain clinical significance
* Primary ovarian tuberculosis and other primary ovarian granulomas are uncommon
* Frequency of secondary ovarian granulomas depends on primary cause

Etiology

* Tuberculosis: hematogenous spread to ovary or secondary to tuberculous salpingitis ([Arch Gynecol Obstet 2008;278:359](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18273629)); may clinically resemble malignancy ([Arch Gynecol Obstet 2010;282:643](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19949807))
* Actinomyces: associated with IUD ([Am J Clin Pathol 1977;68:622](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/920661))
* Ascaris lumbricoides: [J Coll Physicians Surg Pak 2009;19:663](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19811722)
* Bowel contents via colo-ovarian fistula: [Obstet Gynecol 1987;69:533](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3808543)
* Crohn disease: usually extension from bowel ([Am J Obstet Gynecol 1975;122:527](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1146908))
* Dysgerminoma: granulomatous reaction
* Enterobius vermicularis: [Eur J Gynaecol Oncol 2007;28:513](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18179151)
* Foreign material: often postsurgical, including suture, talc, starch, lubricant and carbon ([Obstet Gynecol 1985;66:701](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3903583), [Hum Pathol 1996;27:1008](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8892582))
* Keratin from cystic teratomas and squamous elements of endometrial and ovarian endometrioid adenocarcinomas
* Malakoplakia: [Obstet Gynecol 1987;69:537](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3808544)
* Necrobiotic (palisading) granuloma: often with history of surgery or cauterization months to years previous
* Sarcoidosis: rare ([Am J Obstet Gynecol 1990;162:1284](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2339730))
* Schistosomiasis

Clinical features

* Often an incidental finding or part of a systemic granulomatous disorder

Laboratory

* May be associated with elevated CA 125, raising concern for cancer ([Rom J Intern Med 2009;47:297](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20446446))

Case reports

* Due to microfibrillar collagen hemostat (avitene) used to control bleeding ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1995;119:1161](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7503666))

Treatment

* In secondary cases, treat primary cause

Gross description

* May present as a mass and simulate a tumor

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Tuberculosis is typically confined to the cortex
