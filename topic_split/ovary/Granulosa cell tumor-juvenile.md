Definition / general

* Sex cord stromal tumor composed of primitive appearing granulosa cells with follicular and solid growth patterns

Essential features

* Sex cord stromal tumor with primitive granulosa cell differentiation
* Solid and follicular growth
* Almost always occurs in patients younger than 30 years
* Lacks the *FOXL2* somatic mutation seen in [adult granulosa cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorgctadult.html)
* Usually stage IA and associated with a favorable prognosis

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8622/1](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=78) - granulosa cell tumor, juvenile
* ICD-10:
  + [C56.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C51-C58/C56-/C56.9) - malignant neoplasm of unspecified ovary
  + [D39.1](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D37-D48/D39-/D39.1) - neoplasm of uncertain behavior of ovary
* ICD-11: [2F76 & XH2KH2](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f2029680113?data=%7B%22dataType%22%3A%22pc%22%2C%22postcoordinationCodeSet%22%3A%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F2029680113%22%2C%22axisToValueIds%22%3A%7B%22specificAnatomy%22%3A%5B%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F1648716952%22%7D%5D%7D%7D%7D) - granulosa cell tumor, juvenile

Epidemiology

* Mean age 13 years; range 0 - 67 years
* 80% occur before age 20 and 97% before age 30 ([Am J Surg Pathol 1984;8:575](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6465418))

Sites

* Ovary
* Rarely extraovarian ([Pediatr Hematol Oncol 2021;38:272](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33621150))

Pathophysiology

* Unknown

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Most prepubertal patients present with sexual precocity due to excessive estrogen production and rarely produce androgens; older patients have nonspecific abdominal swelling and pain ([J Endocrinol Invest 2006;29:653](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16957416), [Am J Surg Pathol 1984;8:575](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6465418))
* Rarely associated with enchondromatosis (Ollier disease), Maffucci syndrome, abnormal karyotype / ambiguous genitalia ([Am J Surg Pathol 1984;8:575](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6465418), [Am J Surg Pathol 1985;9:737](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4061731))

Diagnosis

* Diagnosis is made at the time of removal of an adnexal mass
* Possibility of juvenile granulosa cell tumor may be suspected if there are estrogenic manifestations in a prepubertal patient but histopathological examination is required for diagnosis

Radiology description

* Typically appears on imaging as a large, unilateral, multicystic mass with occasional septae; most contain both solid and cystic components
* Can have a sponge-like appearance on imaging ([Radiographics 2014;34:2039](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25384300))

Prognostic factors

* Of low malignant potential, with a very favorable prognosis for patients with stage IA tumors (which account for a large majority of cases) but a guarded prognosis if there is spread beyond the ovary

Case reports

* Fetus with an androgenic ovarian mass in the third trimester ([J Obstet Gynaecol Res 2021;47:2220](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33754426))
* 2 year old girl with precocious pseudopuberty ([J Clin Res Pediatr Endocrinol 2021 Apr 14 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33849266))
* 17 year old girl with gender dysphoria ([Case Rep Oncol 2020;13:1330](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33362514))
* 33 year old woman with adnexal mass and Maffucci syndrome ([Clin Imaging 2019;56:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30965205))
* 40 year old Japanese woman with late onset juvenile granulosa cell tumor ([Acta Med Okayama 2020;74:159](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32341591))

Treatment

* Large majority of tumors are stage IA and surgical removal, with preservation of fertility if desired, is all that is required for treatment ([Acta Obstet Gynecol Scand 2021 May 24 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34027996))
* With recurrent or advanced stage tumors, platinum based chemotherapy is typically offered ([Acta Obstet Gynecol Scand 2021 May 24 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34027996))

Gross description

* Usually unilateral with a smooth surface
* Mean size 12.5 cm ([Am J Surg Pathol 1984;8:575](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6465418))
* Multiloculated, cystic and solid tumor with yellow-white solid areas
* May have hemorrhage and necrosis

Frozen section description

* Smooth surfaced ovarian mass in a young patient
* Presence of follicular differentiation is helpful but definitive diagnosis may have to be deferred to permanent sections

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Diffuse or nodular appearance at low power
* Macrofollicle and microfollicle formation containing eosinophilic secretions
* Tumor cells are often luteinized
* Round / oval hyperchromatic nuclei with small nucleoli, irregular nuclear contours
* No / rare nuclear grooves; high mitotic rate (mean 11/10 high power fields) ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1989;113:40](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2910225))
* Bizarre / atypical nuclei are rarely present
* May have pseudopapillary architecture ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:581](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18301054))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Most have *AKT1* mutations ([EBioMedicine 2015;2:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26137586))
* *GNAS* mutations reported in 30% of cases ([J Clin Endocrinol Metab 2006;91:1842](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16507630))
* Consistently negative for *FOXL2* (402C > G) mutation, while 5 - 10% have a mutation in *DICER1*; the *DICER1* mutations may be germline, i.e. associated with DICER1 syndrome ([Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:223](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32910017))
* Somatic mutations in *IDH1* or *IDH2* are associated with Ollier disease or Maffucci syndrome; juvenile granulosa cell tumor is an uncommon manifestation of both diseases ([Am J Med Genet A 2020;182:1093](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32144835))

Sample pathology report

* Right ovary, oophorectomy:
  + Juvenile granulosa cell tumor, negative for ovarian surface involvement or extraovarian spread

Differential diagnosis

* [Adult granulosa cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorgctadult.html):
  + Older age at presentation (but there is some overlap in the age ranges)
  + Small uniform cells resembling normal granulosa cells
  + *FOXL2* mutation in 95% of cases ([N Engl J Med 2009;360:2719](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19516027))
* [Small cell carcinoma, hypercalcemic type](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorsmallcellhyper.html):
  + More primitive appearing cells, with scant cytoplasm or rhabdoid cells
  + Loss of [BRG1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsSMARCA4BRG1.html) expression (protein encoded by *SMARCA4*) ([J Pathol 2016;238:389](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26356327))
  + Mutation in *SMARCA4* ([Nat Genet 2014;46:427](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24658001))
* [Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorsertolileydig.html):
  + Tubules (in well differentiated tumors), cords (intermediate differentiation), sarcomatoid growth (poorly differentiated) or retiform architecture
  + Follicle-like spaces occasionally seen admixed with typical sertoliform differentiation ([Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:59](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32815828))
* [Gynandroblastoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorgynandro.html):
  + Typical Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor component coexisting with separate typical juvenile granulosa cell tumor ([Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:59](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32815828))
* [Yolk sac tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumoryolksac.html):
  + Schiller-Duval bodies
  + Enteric differentiation
  + Hyaline globules
  + Expresses [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html) and [AFP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsafp.html)
* [Clear cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorclearcell.html):
  + Older age
  + Background endometriosis or adenofibroma
  + Expression of [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html) and [Napsin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnapsina.html)
  + Negative for [inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html), [SF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssf1.html) and [FOXL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfoxl2.html)
