Definition / general

* Rare tumor of teratomatous or nonteratomatous origin
* "True" leiomyosarcoma if of nonteratomatous origin

Sites

* Omentum, reteroperitoneum, mesentery

Pathophysiology

* Arises from: malignant degeneration of ovarian leiomyoma; smooth muscle in blood vessel walls in cortical stroma and corpus luteum, muscular attachments of ovarian ligament, wolfian duct remnants, or totipotential ovarian mesenchyme; or from a teratoma

Clinical features

* Mostly postmenopausal patients
* Presents with abdominal pain and mass
* 62% die of disease within mean 24 months ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1436](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15489647))

Prognostic factors

* Tumor stage, tumor size, grade, mitotic index ([Obstet Gynecol Surv 2007;62:480](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17572920))

Case reports

* 42 year old woman with epithelioid tumor ([Gynecol Oncol 2005;97:697](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15863185))
* 58 year old woman ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1991;115:941](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1929791))
* 60 year old women ([World J Oncol 2001;2:265](http://www.wjon.org/index.php/wjon/article/view/362/294), [Online J Health Allied Scs 2009;8:16](http://www.ojhas.org/issue31/2009-3-16.htm))

Treatment

* Multimodality treatment: surgical debulking, postoperative radiotherapy or chemotherapy
* FIGO staging and treatment of ovarian sarcoma is same as for epithelial ovarian carcinoma ([Obstet Gynecol Surv 2007;62:480](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17572920))

Gross description

* Solitary, lobular, soft fleshy solid mass with hemorrhage and cystic degeneration

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Usually 2 of 3: moderate / severe cytologic atypia, 10+ MF/10 HPF, tumor cell necrosis
* Varies from well differentiated to highly pleomorphic sarcoma

Differential diagnosis

* Cellular [fibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorfibroma.html): low mitotic activity **Krukenberg tumors** have stromal reaction, history of GI primary, pan CK+
* [Mixed Müllerian tumors](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumormmt.html): pan CK+ areas
* **Sarcomatoid form of sex cord stromal tumors**: sex cord elements present
* [Undifferentiated carcinomas](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorundifferentiated.html): pan CK+, H caldesmon-
