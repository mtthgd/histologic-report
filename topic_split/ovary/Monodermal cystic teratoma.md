Definition / general

* Benign cystic tumor comprising mature tissues derived from a single germ layer (ectoderm or endoderm), except for struma ovarii, carcinoid and neuroectodermal type tumors (malignant tumors of central neuroectodermal derivation)

Essential features

* Cystic tumor lined by benign keratinizing squamous epithelium or neuroectodermal tissues
* May contain pituitary type adenomas
* Benign with excellent prognosis

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9080/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/%0D%0A9789241548496_eng.pdf#page=176) - cystic teratoma NOS
* ICD-11: [2F32.0](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#882254343) - cystic teratoma

Epidemiology

* Monodermal teratomas are rare ([Pediatr Dev Pathol 2015;18:341](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26261871))
* Neuroectodermal cysts have been reported in children and young women
* Epidermoid cysts occur across a wide age range

Sites

* Ovary

Pathophysiology

* Unknown

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Abdominal distension or pain
* Amenorrhea due to lactotroph adenoma ([Obstet Gynecol 1990;75:540](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2304732))
* Central obesity and hirsutism due to corticotroph adenoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 1987;11:218](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3548446))

Diagnosis

* Histologic examination

Laboratory

* Elevated prolactin in lactotroph adenoma ([JAMA 1990;263:2472](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2329635))
* Increased cortisol and lack of suppression of either low dose or high dose dexamethasone suppression test in corticotroph adenoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 1987;11:218](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3548446))

Radiology description

* Wide spectrum of radiologic presentation ranging from a purely cystic to a complex cystic mass with a considerable solid component

Prognostic factors

* Benign with excellent prognosis

Case reports

* 2.5 year old girl with ovarian epidermoid cyst torsion associated with appendicitis ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2022;100:107731](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36242891))
* 10 year old girl with neurogenic ovarian cyst ([Pediatr Dev Pathol 2015;18:341](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26261871))
* 36 year old woman with epidermoid cyst treated with laparoscopic cystectomy ([Gynecol Minim Invasive Ther 2018;7:40](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30254935))
* 50 year old woman with a giant (17 cm) epidermoid cyst ([J Clin Diagn Res 2012;6:1584](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23285468))
* 65 year old woman with epidermoid cyst associated with stromal hyperplasia ([J Cancer Res Ther 2015;11:1046](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26881688))

Treatment

* Ovarian cystectomy ([Pediatr Dev Pathol 2015;18:341](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26261871))
* Oophorectomy

Gross description

* Neuroectodermal cysts are thin walled, simple to multilocular cysts filled with clear yellow fluid, ranging from 8 - 15 cm ([J Obstet Gynaecol Res 2001;27:21](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11330726))
* Epidermoid cysts are simple thin walled cysts, with white-grey cheesy material, ranging from 1 - 15 cm ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2009;28:193](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19188813))

Frozen section description

* Rarely performed but usually shows a cystic lesion lined by benign squamous epithelium or neuroectodermal tissues

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Neuroectodermal cyst
  + Lined by ependymal cells
  + May show choroid plexus-like epithelium along the cyst lining
  + Astrocytes, oligodendrocytes, microglia and ganglion cells may be present adjacent to the cyst lining ([Histopathology 1994;24:477](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8088721))
* Epidermoid cyst
  + Lined by mature, often keratinizing, stratified squamous epithelium
  + Surrounded by a rim of collagenous stroma containing fibroblasts
  + Keratinaceous debris fills the cyst lumen, with no evidence of hair or a Rokitansky tubercle ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 1996;15:69](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8852449))
* Rarely, cysts are lined by respiratory type epithelium ([Cancer 1979;43:383](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/761172))
* Lactotroph adenoma
  + Closely packed nests of small cells with eosinophilic cytoplasm as seen in the anterior hypophysis ([Obstet Gynecol 1990;75:540](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2304732))
* Corticotroph adenoma
  + Monomorphic cells with round nuclei and vacuolated or eosinophilic cytoplasm, growing in nests or cords ([Am J Surg Pathol 1987;11:218](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3548446))

Positive stains

* Immunohistochemistry has a limited role in diagnosis of monodermal cystic teratoma
* Prolactin in lactotroph adenoma ([Obstet Gynecol 1990;75:540](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2304732))
* [ACTH](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsACTH.html) in corticotroph adenoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 1987;11:218](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3548446))
* [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html) in neuroectodermal cyst ([Histopathology 1994;24:477](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8088721))

Sample pathology report

* Ovary, right, cystectomy:
  + Epidermoid cyst (monodermal cystic teratoma)

Differential diagnosis

* [Mature teratoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorteratomamature.html):
  + Mature tissue representing at least 2 germ layers ([BMC Cancer 2019;19:217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30866852))
  + Malignant transformation occurs rarely (most commonly squamous cell carcinoma)
* [Immature teratoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorteratomaimmature.html):
  + Malignant germ cell tumor ([Obstet Gynecol 2006;107:1075](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16648414))
  + Contains variable amounts of immature tissue (typically immature neuroepithelium comprising primitive appearing cells with scant cytoplasm, hyperchromatic nuclei and frequent mitoses)
* [Steroid cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorsteroidcelltumorNOS.html):
  + Pituitary type adenomas may be mistaken for steroid cell tumors with eosinophilic (lipid poor) cells
  + Association with ependymal lined neuroectodermal cyst as well as **prolactin** expression in lactotroph adenoma and [ACTH](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsACTH.html) expression in corticotroph adenoma along with negative sex cord stromal markers ([inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html), [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html), [SF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssf1.html)) are helpful clues

Board review style question #1

[![Monodermal cystic teratoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/ovarymonodermalcysticteratomaMashayekhiTurashvili005.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/ovarymonodermalcysticteratomaMashayekhiTurashvili005.jpg)

The image above is a representative section of an ovarian cyst from a 40 year old woman. What is the most likely diagnosis?

1. Epidermoid cyst
2. Immature cystic teratoma
3. Monodermal cystic teratoma with respiratory epithelium
4. Struma ovarii

Board review style answer #1

**A**. Epidermoid cyst. This ovarian cyst is lined by benign keratinizing squamous epithelium, consistent with epidermoid cyst. Answer D is incorrect because struma ovarii is exclusively composed of benign thyroid tissue.
Answer C is incorrect because some monodermal cystic teratomas are lined by respiratory epithelium.
Answer B is incorrect because immature cystic teratoma exhibits immature neuroepithelium in addition to endodermal, mesodermal and ectodermal elements.
