Definition / general

* Rare, benign ovarian epithelial tumors exhibiting admixture of 2 or more Müllerian type epithelia (mucinous, serous and endometrioid), each accounting for at least 10% of the epithelium

Essential features

* Benign ovarian neoplasms with admixture of 2 or more Müllerian type epithelia, each accounting for 10% or more
* Mucinous, serous and endometrioid cell types
* Mean age 62 years ([Histopathology 2021;78:445](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32810322))
* Association with [endometriosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarynontumorendometriosis.html)

Terminology

* Müllerian mucinous cystadenoma (preferred by some authors to reflect association with endometriosis, endometrioid and clear cell tumors rather than serous tumors of the ovary)

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [D27](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D27-/D27) - benign neoplasm of ovary

Epidemiology

* Rare tumors

Clinical features

* Detected incidentally during work up of other gynecological diseases
* May present with an abdominal mass, abdominal pain or swelling
* Unilateral / bilateral (bilateral in 40% of cases) ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2022;41:68](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33577227))
* Mean size is 9 cm, generally smaller than intestinal type neoplasms ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2022;41:68](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33577227))

Diagnosis

* Microscopic examination

Radiology description

* Unilocular / multilocular cystic masses ([J Comput Assist Tomogr 2019;43:119](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30052618))

Treatment

* Surgery (cystectomy or oophorectomy)

Gross description

* Cystic masses (unilocular or multilocular)
* Seromucinous cystadenofibroma are solid with a fibrous cut surface

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Single layered epithelial lining with occasional stratification
* Cell types: mucinous (endocervical in appearance), serous (ciliated), endometrioid (nonserous, nonciliated), hybrid morphology (serous and mucinous) ([Histopathology 2021;78:445](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32810322))
* Variable degree of papillae (epithelial papillary tufting or broader papillae with fibrovascular core) ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2022;41:68](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33577227))
* Neutrophilic infiltrate may be present
* Endometrioid epithelium may have squamous metaplasia
* Some tumors have a prominent fibrous stromal component (classified as seromucinous adenofibroma)
* Associated with [endometriosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarynontumorendometriosis.html) in 27 - 36% of cases ([Histopathology 2021;78:445](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32810322), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2022;41:68](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33577227))

Sample pathology report

* Right ovary, cystectomy / oophorectomy:
  + Seromucinous cystadenoma

Differential diagnosis

* [Serous cystadenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorserousbenign.html):
  + Lined by nonstratified tubal type epithelium
* [Mucinous cystadenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumormucinousbenign.html):
  + Lined by nonstratified mucinous epithelium with intestinal differentiation (goblet cells)
* [Seromucinous borderline tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovaryseromucinousborderline.html):
  + Epithelial proliferation > 10%, complex architecture, papillae with hierarchical branching, edematous papillae with neutrophils
* **Metastatic tumors:**
  + [Low grade appendiceal mucinous neoplasms](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/appendixmucinousneoplasm.html), [pancreatic adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pancreasductal.html), [endocervical adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixhpvadenocarcinoma.html), etc.
    - Morphology and immunohistochemical panels are helpful

3. Isochromosome 12p
4. Peutz-Jeghers syndrome

Board review style answer #1

**A.** Endometriosis
