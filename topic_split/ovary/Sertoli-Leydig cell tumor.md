Definition / general

* Rare ovarian tumor composed of sex cord (Sertoli cells) and stromal (Leydig cells) elements, accounting for < 0.5% of all ovarian neoplasms

Essential features

* Rare ovarian tumor composed of sex cord (Sertoli cells) and stromal (Leydig cells) elements
* May occur sporadically or in patients with DICER1 syndrome
* 3 molecular subtypes ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:628](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30986800)):
  + *DICER1* mutant: younger age, moderately / poorly differentiated, retiform or heterologous elements
  + *FOXL2* c.402C>G (p.Cys134Trp) mutant: postmenopausal patients, moderately / poorly differentiated, no retiform or heterologous elements
  + *DICER1 / FOXL2* wildtype: intermediate age, no retiform or heterologous elements, including all well differentiated tumors
* Express general sex cord markers (inhibin, calretinin, SF1, FOXL2 and CD56); CK7 and EMA immunostains are negative
* Behavior correlates with tumor grade and histologic subtype

Terminology

* Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor
  + Well differentiated
  + Moderately differentiated / intermediate grade
  + Poorly differentiated
* Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor with heterologous elements
* Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor, retiform variant
* Histologic grade and subtype correlates with clinical behavior (see [Prognostic factors](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorsertolileydig.html#ovarytumorsertolileydigprogfact))

Epidemiology

* Rare, accounting for < 0.5% of all ovarian neoplasms and 1 - 2% of pediatric ovarian cancers
* Most common in young patients with a mean age of 25 years ([Am J Surg Pathol 1985;9:543](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3911780))
* May also present after menopause ([Eur J Gynaecol Oncol 2017;38:214](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29953783))
* Tumors with a retiform pattern or germline *DICER1* mutation occur at a younger age ([Gynecol Oncol 2017;147:521](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29037807))
* Nearly all pediatric Sertoli-Leydig cell tumors are moderately or poorly differentiated, are DICER1 syndrome associated and often show a retiform pattern or heterologous elements

Sites

* Ovary

Pathophysiology

* May occur sporadically or in patients with DICER1 syndrome ([OMIM: Pleuropulmonary Blastoma; PPB [Accessed 16 August 2021]](https://www.omim.org/entry/601200))
  + Rare tumor predisposition syndrome caused by germline mutations in *DICER1*, a gene encoding the RNase III enzyme in the microRNA maturation pathway ([N Engl J Med 2012;366:234](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22187960), [Br J Cancer 2013;109:2744](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24136150))
  + Other tumors and disorders associated with DICER1 syndrome include [pleuropulmonary blastoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorPPB.html), [cystic nephroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumorcysticnephroma.html), [multinodular goiter](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidnodular.html) and [botryoid embryonal rhabdomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixrhabdomyosarcoma.html), among others ([Nat Rev Cancer 2014;14:662](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25176334))
  + Germline mutation is generally a truncating mutation; may occur anywhere in the gene
  + Second hit somatic mutation is a focused hotspot missense mutation affecting the RNase IIIb domain of *DICER1* ([Histopathology 2016;69:903](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27504996))
  + Sporadic Sertoli-Leydig cell tumors (moderately and poorly differentiated) harbor somatic mutations at the same hotspot of *DICER1* gene ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:1178](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28654427))
  + Somatic hotspot *DICER1* mutations are present in approximately half of all cases (range: 15 - 97%), 61 - 69% of which also have germline *DICER1* mutations ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:1178](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28654427), [Gynecol Oncol 2017;147:521](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29037807), [Histopathology 2016;68:279](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26033501))

Etiology

* May occur sporadically or in patients with DICER1 syndrome (see [Pathophysiology](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorsertolileydig.html#ovarytumorsertolileydigpatho)) ([OMIM: Pleuropulmonary Blastoma; PPB [Accessed 16 August 2021]](https://www.omim.org/entry/601200))

Clinical features

* Clinical presentation may include pelvic pain or a pelvic mass, rarely with ascites and tumor rupture
* Androgenic hormonal symptoms (virilization) are common and present in approximately 50% of patients; they include hirsutism, clitoromegaly, breast atrophy and menstrual irregularity or amenorrhea ([Am J Surg Pathol 1985;9:543](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3911780))
* Estrogenic hormonal manifestations are rare

Diagnosis

* May be suspected clinically in a young patient presenting with a combination of virilization, elevated testosterone levels and ovarian / pelvic mass on imaging studies
* Clinical presentation often overlaps with other, more common entities, as nearly half of patients with Sertoli-Leydig cell tumors lack the characteristic androgenic symptoms and may be older (peri or postmenopausal, expanding the clinical differential diagnosis) ([Eur J Gynaecol Oncol 2017;38:214](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29953783))
* Intraoperative frozen section evaluation is helpful to confirm the clinical suspicion and guide the surgical management

Laboratory

* May have elevated testosterone ([Eur J Gynaecol Oncol 2017;38:214](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29953783))

Radiology description

* Ultrasound, pelvic CT or MRI can show a solid or solid and cystic adnexal mass

Prognostic factors

* Behavior correlates with tumor grade and histologic subtype
* Well differentiated Sertoli-Leydig cell tumors are essentially benign
* Approximately 10% of moderately differentiated and 13 - 59% of poorly differentiated tumors have malignant behavior ([Gynecol Oncol 2012;125:673](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22446621), [Gynecol Oncol 2012;127:384](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22850410), [J Obstet Gynaecol Res 2013;39:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22691037))
* Heterologous elements, retiform pattern, tumor rupture and spread outside the ovary (stage II or higher tumor stage) are adverse prognostic factors ([J Obstet Gynaecol Res 2013;39:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22691037), [Gynecol Oncol 2012;125:673](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22446621))
* Patients with germline *DICER1* mutations have a favorable prognosis compared with those with somatic *DICER1* mutations only ([Gynecol Oncol 2017;147:521](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29037807))

Case reports

* 15 year old girl with Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor and *DICER1* mutation ([Case Rep Obstet Gynecol 2018;2018:7927362](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30356399))
* 20 and 21 year old sisters with Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor and DICER1 syndrome ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2020;99:e20806](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32629665))
* 68 year old woman with ovarian Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor with heterologous hepatocytes and a hepatocellular carcinomatous element ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2019;38:247](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29620585))
* 3 cases of bilateral ovarian Sertoli-Leydig cell tumors in patients with DICER1 syndrome ([Histopathology 2020;77:223](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32333409))
* 9 of 16 cases of familial multinodular goiter and Sertoli-Leydig cell tumors are associated with a large intragenic in frame *DICER1* deletion ([Eur J Endocrinol 2018;178:K11](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29187512))

Treatment

* Conservative fertility sparing surgery (unilateral salpingo-oophorectomy) and staging procedure (with or without lymphadenectomy) is usually performed in young patients with stage I tumors ([Gynecol Oncol 2012;125:673](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22446621), [Gynecol Oncol 2012;127:384](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22850410), [J Obstet Gynaecol Res 2013;39:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22691037))
* Older patients who do not wish to preserve fertility typically undergo bilateral salpingo-oophorectomy, total hysterectomy and complete surgical staging
* Platinum based adjuvant chemotherapy has also been reported in patients with moderately and poorly differentiated tumors, heterologous mesenchymal elements, advanced stage or rupture ([J Obstet Gynaecol Res 2013;39:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22691037), [Eur J Gynaecol Oncol 2017;38:214](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29953783))
* Genetic counseling and germline *DICER1* mutation testing is recommended ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:1178](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28654427))

Gross description

* Almost always unilateral ([Am J Surg Pathol 1985;9:543](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3911780))
* Tumor size ranges widely from < 1 cm to 35 cm (mean 12 - 14 cm) ([Am J Surg Pathol 1985;9:543](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3911780))
* Cut surface is typically solid, tan-yellow
* Cystic component may also be seen, especially in tumors with heterologous elements or with a retiform morphologic pattern
* Poorly differentiated tumor may show grossly identifiable foci of necrosis

Intermediate grade tumor

Frozen section description

* Most critical issue is differentiation from epithelial ovarian malignancies
  + Unlike epithelial malignancies, Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor is almost always unilateral and typically occurs in younger patients
  + Clinical history of hormonal (androgenic) symptoms is helpful and should be actively sought from the surgical team
  + Key microscopic features on frozen section include admixture of Sertoli cell tubules or compressed cords with variable amount of Leydig cell clusters
  + Intracytoplasmic Reinke crystals may be better preserved compared to formalin fixed paraffin embedded permanent sections
  + Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor: fertility sparing surgery is typically performed in younger patients
  + Epithelial ovarian malignancies: generally requires complete surgical staging and the role of fertility preserving surgery is more controversial ([Hui: Atlas of Intraoperative Frozen Section Diagnosis in Gynecologic Pathology, 1st Edition, 2015](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/3319218069/pathologyoutl-20), [Mod Pathol 1999;12:933](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10530556), [J Obstet Gynaecol Res 2013;39:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22691037), [Gynecol Oncol 2012;125:673](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22446621))

Heterologous elements

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Difficulty of histologic diagnosis increases with tumor grade
* While Sertoli and Leydig components can be readily identified in well differentiated tumors, less differentiated tumors lack well formed Sertoli cell tubules and have fewer Leydig cells
* Well differentiated
  + Open or compressed Sertoli cell tubules, admixed with clusters of Leydig cells in the intervening stroma
  + Sertoli cells are low columnar to cuboidal with oval to round nuclei, which often show nuclear grooves and small nucleoli
  + Leydig cells can be recognized by their round nuclei and abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm with characteristic Reinke crystals and lipofuscin pigment
  + No significant atypia or mitotic activity
* Moderately differentiated
  + Typically has a diffuse or lobulated architectural pattern and may show alternating hypo and hypercellularity on low magnification
  + Sertoli cells form compressed tubules, cords or diffuse sheets and have hyperchromatic, oval or spindled nuclei with mild to moderate atypia and occasional mitotic figures (average 5 per 10 high power fields)
  + Rare small clusters of Leydig cells are admixed with the Sertoli cell component
  + Follicular differentiation may be seen in moderately and poorly differentiated tumors and may resemble juvenile granulosa cell tumor ([Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:59](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32815828))
* Poorly differentiated
  + Diffuse sheets of immature, sarcomatoid Sertoli cells with moderate to marked nuclear atypia and only rare foci of vague cord formation
  + Increased mitotic activity, up to 20 mitoses per 10 high power fields
  + Leydig cells are difficult to find; a few small clusters are typically located at the periphery of tumor nodules
* Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor with heterologous elements
  + Heterologous (epithelial or mesenchymal) elements may be seen in approximately 20% of moderately or poorly differentiated tumors and in retiform Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor ([Am J Surg Pathol 1985;9:543](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3911780))
  + Most common heterologous element is mucinous (intestinal or gastric type) epithelium, which may be benign, borderline or malignant ([Cancer 1982;50:2448](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7139537))
  + Carcinoid tumor (trabecular or goblet cell) has also been described arising from heterologous mucinous epithelial elements ([Cancer 1982;50:2448](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7139537))
  + Heterologous mesenchymal (cartilage or skeletal muscle) elements are less common ([Cancer 1982;50:2465](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7139538))
  + Rare focal hepatocyte differentiation has also been reported and may be associated with increased serum AFP ([Hum Pathol 1999;30:611](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10374766))
* Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor, retiform variant
  + Approximately 15% of Sertoli-Leydig cell tumors demonstrate focal or diffuse retiform pattern, forming anastomosing, slit-like, irregular spaces or multicystic, sieve-like or papillary architecture ([Am J Surg Pathol 1985;9:543](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3911780), [Am J Surg Pathol 1983;7:755](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6660351))

Heterologous elements

Positive stains

* General sex cord proteins, such as [inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html), [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html), [SF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssf1.html), [FOXL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfoxl2.html), [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html), [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html) and [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:583](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9158684), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2017;141:1052](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28745567))
* [Vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html) and [pancytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:592](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17414107))
* [MelanA / MART1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html) in Leydig cells ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:484](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21378549))
* [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html) and [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html) in heterologous intestinal type mucinous epithelium
* [AFP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsafp.html), [arginase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsarginase1.html) and [HepPar1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsheppar1.html) in hepatoid elements
* [Reticulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsreticulin.html) histochemical staining may be helpful in differentiating Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor (completely absent or nested pattern) from fibromas and thecomas (individual pericellular reticulin network)
  + Absence of [reticulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsreticulin.html) staining is not specific for Sertoli-Leydig cell tumors, as adult granulosa cell tumors and epithelial ovarian tumors also show the same staining pattern ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2018;37:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28700438))

Negative stains

* [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html) and [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) immunostains are negative; helpful in distinguishing Sertoli-Leydig cell tumors from ovarian epithelial tumors ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2017;141:1052](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28745567))
* [Reticulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsreticulin.html) is absent (highlights the individual pericellular reticulin network in ovarian fibromas / thecomas; also absent in adult granulosa cell tumors and epithelial ovarian tumors) ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2018;37:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28700438))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* 3 molecular subtypes ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:628](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30986800)):
  + *DICER1* mutant: younger age, moderately / poorly differentiated, retiform or heterologous elements
  + *FOXL2* c.402C>G (p.Cys134Trp) mutant: postmenopausal patients, moderately / poorly differentiated, no retiform or heterologous elements
  + *DICER1 / FOXL2* wildtype: intermediate age, no retiform or heterologous elements, including all well differentiated tumors
* Somatic hotspot *DICER1* mutations are present in approximately half of all cases (range: 15 - 97%), 61 - 69% of which also have germline *DICER1* mutations ([Gynecol Oncol 2017;147:521](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29037807), [Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:1178](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28654427), [Histopathology 2016;68:279](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26033501), [Mod Pathol 2015;28:1603](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26428316))
* More recent study identified *DICER1* mutations in all of their moderately and poorly differentiated tumors, whereas none of the well differentiated tumors had *DICER1* mutations, raising the possibility that well differentiated Sertoli-Leydig cell tumors may represent a different pathogenetic entity ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:1178](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28654427))
* *FOXL2* mutation has been reported in up to 22% of ovarian Sertoli-Leydig cell tumors and is mutually exclusive with *DICER1* mutations ([Histopathology 2016;68:279](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26033501), [J Pathol 2010;221:147](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20198651), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2018;37:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28700438))

Sample pathology report

* Ovary and fallopian tube, right, salpingo-oophorectomy:
  + Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor of the ovary, poorly differentiated, with heterologous elements (embryonal rhabdomyosarcoma) (see comment)
  + Tumor size: 28 cm
  + Tumor ruptured intraoperatively (see surgeon's operative report)
  + Ovarian surface involvement: not definitely identified
  + Fallopian tube: negative for tumor
  + TNM stage (AJCC 8th edition): pT1c1 Nx, at least stage IC1
  + Comment: Pelvic wash cytology is negative. Referral for genetic counseling is recommended.

Differential diagnosis

* Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor:
  + [Endometrioid adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorendometrioidcarcinoma.html), especially **sertoliform variant** ([Mod Pathol 1999;12:933](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10530556)):
    - Positive for [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html) and [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) and negative for sex cord markers ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2017;141:1052](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28745567))
    - Presence of squamous differentiation is helpful
  + [Adult granulosa cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorgctadult.html):
    - Lacks Leydig cell component
    - Majority of tumors have *FOXL2* mutations ([Histopathology 2016;68:279](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26033501), [J Pathol 2010;221:147](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20198651), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2018;37:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28700438))
  + [Fibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorfibroma.html):
    - Lacks Leydig cell component
    - [Reticulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsreticulin.html) stain shows individual pericellular network ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2018;37:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28700438))
  + **Tubular Krukenberg tumor** (i.e. tubular variant of [metastatic signet ring cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorupperGItract.html)):
    - Bilaterality, presence of signet ring cells and positive [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) favor metastasis
    - Depending on the primary site, the tumor is also positive for [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html) or [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html)
    - May be positive for [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html)
* Retiform Sertoli-Leydig cell tumors:
  + [Yolk sac tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumoryolksac.html):
    - Lacks Leydig cell component
    - Positive for [AFP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsafp.html) and [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html) ([Histopathology 2014;65:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24444105))
  + **Low grade serous tumors** ([borderline tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorserousborderline.html) and [low grade serous carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorserouscarcinomalg.html)):
    - Positive for [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html)
    - Lacks Leydig cell component
    - Often bilateral
* Sertoli-Leydig cell tumors with heterologous elements:
  + [Carcinosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumormmt.html):
    - Marked nuclear atypicality in both components
    - Positive for epithelial markers ([EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html), [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html)), at least in the carcinoma component
  + **Mucinous tumors** ([primary ovarian](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumormucinouscarcinoma.html) and [metastatic](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumormetmucinous.html)):
    - Lack of the surrounding atypical sex cord (Sertoli cell) and Leydig cell component
  + [Teratoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorteratomamature.html):
    - Lack of atypical sex cord (Sertoli cell) and Leydig cell component
    - Usually predominant ectodermal component

Board review style answer #1

**B**. *DICER1*. Somatic hotspot missense mutation affecting the RNase IIIb domain of *DICER1* gene are common in Sertoli-Leydig cell tumors. In a recent large case series, all moderately and poorly differentiated Sertoli-Leydig cell tumors harbored *DICER1* mutations.

Board review style question #2

Sertoli-Leydig cell tumors of the ovary typically show which of the following immunoprofiles?

1. CK7+ / EMA+ / inhibin- / calretinin+
2. CK7- / EMA+ / inhibin+ / calretinin-
3. CK7- / EMA- / inhibin+ / calretinin+
4. CK7- / EMA- / inhibin+ / calretinin-

Board review style answer #2

**C**. CK7- / EMA- / inhibin+ / calretinin+. Sertoli-Leydig cell tumors express general sex cord immunohistochemical markers, such as inhibin and calretinin. CK7 and EMA immunostains are negative and can help differentiate Sertoli-Leydig cell tumors from sertoliform endometrioid adenocarcinoma of the ovary, which is typically positive for both CK7 and EMA.
