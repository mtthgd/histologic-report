Definition / general

* Ovarian tumor that is morphologically, immunohistochemically and genetically identical to [pancreatic solid pseudopapillary neoplasm](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pancreassolidpseudo.html)
* Categorized as miscellaneous tumor of ovary in WHO classification ([Kurman: WHO Classification of Tumours of the Female Reproductive Organs, 4th Edition, 2014](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/9283224353/pathologyoutl-20))

Essential features

* Rare tumor of reproductive age group; 10 reported cases
* Possible origin from genital ridge related cells, presumed epithelial / neuroendocrine nature
* Solid nests with pseudopapillary architecture, cysts with colloid-like material, papillae with myxoid stroma and tumor cell nuclei oriented away from the papillae
* Abnormal nuclear β catenin immunohistochemical staining and E-cadherin (negative NCH-38 clone)
* Limited cases with followup, presumed to be of low malignant potential, like pancreatic solid pseudopapillary neoplasm

Terminology

* Primary ovarian solid pseudopapillary neoplasm
* Extrapancreatic solid pseudopapillary neoplasm
* Miscellaneous ovarian tumors ([Kurman: WHO Classification of Tumours of the Female Reproductive Organs, 4th Edition, 2014](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/9283224353/pathologyoutl-20))

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [C56.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C51-C58/C56-/C56.9) - Malignant neoplasm of unspecified ovary

Epidemiology

* Rare tumor
* Females of reproductive age, range 17 - 57 years
* 10 reported cases: 6 from U.S., 2 from China, 1 each from India and Japan ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:1514](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20871224), [Hum Pathol 2012;43:1339](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22534259), [Ann Diagn Pathol 2012;16:498](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21778097), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2018;37:110](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28463908), [Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2015;8:8645](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26339451), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2011;30:539](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21979589), [Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2016;59:348](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27510674), [Pathol Int 2014;64:460](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25186079))

Sites

* Ovary

Clinical features

* Abdominal mass, fullness, swelling or pain (6 cases)
* Pelvic pain (2 cases)
* Incidental radiologic finding (2 cases)
* Postmenopausal bleeding (1 case)

Diagnosis

* Exclude metastasis of solid pseudopapillary neoplasm from extraovarian site

Radiology description

* Ultrasound: solid and cystic complex adnexal mass with high density in cyst ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2015;8:8645](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26339451))
* Multiloculated heterogeneous ovarian cystic mass ([Hum Pathol 2012;43:1339](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22534259))

Prognostic factors

* Unknown due to limited number of cases
* Only patient who died of disease was stage IV
  + Tumor showed high mitotic rate (62/50 high powered fields), necrosis and lymphovascular invasion ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2012;16:498](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21778097))
* 6 patients had no evidence of disease at followup, including a stage IIIC case at 18 months followup

Case reports

* 17, 21 and 57 year old women; first reported cases ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:1514](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20871224))
* 22 and 47 year old women with metastasis from pancreas to ovary ([Mol Clin Oncol 2016;4:845](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27123293))
* 47 year old woman with overlap of microcystic stromal tumor and SPN ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2015;8:11792](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26617928))
* 48 year old woman with clinically aggressive tumor ([Hum Pathol 2012;43:1339](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22534259))
* 49 year old woman with *CTNNB1* point mutation ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2018;37:110](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28463908))

Treatment

* Surgery is mainstay of treatment

Gross description

* Circumscribed ovarian / adnexal mass with solid and cystic hemorrhagic cut surface

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Well circumscribed tumor
* Tumor cell sheets, nests and bands surrounded by fibrous septa
* Thin delicate capillary sized vessels forming myxohyaline pseudopapillary cores; nuclei located away from the papillary core
* Moderate amount of pale eosinophilic cytoplasm with central / eccentric round to oval nuclei with uniform chromatin
* Inconspicuous mitoses, usually no necrosis, no significant nuclear atypia
* Abundant foamy cytoplasm, paranuclear vacuoles, nuclear grooves and intra / extracellular PAS+ diastase resistant eosinophilic globules can be present
* Microcysts with colloid-like material
* Infarction can be present

Positive stains

* [β catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html): nuclear and cytoplasmic
* [Keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html): variable for [CAM5.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscam52.html) and [AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html); can be negative
* [Vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html)
* [Synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html): cytoplasmic
* [Progesterone receptor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html): nuclear, can be focal or negative
* [Cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html), [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html), [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html), [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html), [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html), [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html) (cytoplasmic) and [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html)

Negative stains

* [E-cadherin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsecadherin.html): no staining with NCH-38 clone
* [Chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html): usually negative
* [Calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html), [inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html), [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html) and [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html)

Electron microscopy description

* Tumor cells with numerous pleomorphic mitochondria interspersed among short strands of rough endoplasmic reticulum ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2012;16:498](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21778097))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* c.110C > T point mutation in *CTNNB1* exon 3 ([Pathol Int 2014;64:460](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25186079))
* c.98C > G point mutation in *CTNNB1* exon 3 ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2018;37:110](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28463908))

Differential diagnosis

* [Granulosa cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorgctadult.html):
  + Cuboidal to polygonal cells with Call-Exner bodies arranged in macrofollicular, trabecular, solid and insular patterns
  + [Inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html)+ and [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html)+
* [Steroid cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorsteroidcelltumorNOS.html):
  + Sheets and nests of large vacuolated cells with clear or eosinophilic cytoplasm; minimal to no nuclear atypia
  + [Inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html)+ and [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html)+
* [Metastatic pancreatic solid pseudopapillary neoplasm](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pancreassolidpseudo.html) ([Mol Clin Oncol 2016;4:845](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27123293)):
  + Similar morphologically
  + Clinical correlation and pancreatic / abdominal imaging required
* [Metastatic neuroendocrine tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumormetastatic.html):
  + Cells arranged in nests, sheets and ribbons separated by thin walled vessels
  + Salt and pepper chromatin and moderate granular cytoplasm
  + [Keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)+, [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html)+ and [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html)+
* [Struma ovarii](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorstruma.html):
  + Mature thyroid tissue with variable sized follicles lined by cuboidal cells
  + [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html)+ and [thyroglobulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsthyroglobulin.html)+
* [Paraganglioma / pheochromocytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/adrenalparaganglioma.html):
  + Zellballen pattern with supporting vessels and spindle sustentacular cells
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)+ sustentacular cells, [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html)+ and [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html)+
* [Melanoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticmelanoma.html):
  + Variable histology, noncohesive epithelioid to spindle cells with abundant cytoplasm; prominent nucleoli
  + Melanin pigment may be present
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)+, [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html)+ and [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html)+
* [Gastrointestinal stromal tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stomachGIST.html):
  + Spindle or epithelioid cells in fascicles and sheets; mitotically active lesion, with cystic change and hemorrhage
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)+, [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)+ and [DOG1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsDOG1.html)+
* **Ependymoma**:
  + Monomorphic bland cells with coarse chromatin, perivascular and ependymal rosettes
  + [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html)+ and [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)+

Board review style question #1

The most characteristic immunohistochemical staining pattern of solid pseudopapillary neoplasm of the ovary is

1. c-kit-
2. Consistent keratin expression
3. Consistent positive expression of both chromogranin and synaptophysin
4. Nuclear β catenin+ and E-cadherin-
5. S100+ and HMB45+

Board review style answer #1

**D.** Nuclear β catenin+ and E-cadherin-
