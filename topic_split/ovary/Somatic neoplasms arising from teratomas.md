Definition / general

* Any benign or malignant neoplasm arising from any tissue within teratomas, either mature or immature

Essential features

* Any benign or malignant neoplasm arising from any tissue within teratomas, either mature or immature
* Wide variety of somatic malignancies can arise (2% of all teratomas)
* The most common carcinoma is squamous cell carcinoma
* Sarcomas account for 8%
* Other malignancies are exceedingly rare
* Immunohistochemical features differ depending on the type of somatic neoplasm

Terminology

* Teratoma with malignant transformation
* Malignant teratoma of ovary

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9084/3](https://iris.who.int/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=85) - teratoma with malignant transformation
* ICD-11: [2C73.3 & XH33E8](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#551209361%20%26%201507806648) - malignant teratoma of ovary & teratoma with malignant transformation

Epidemiology

* Wide age range: 21 - 75 years (mean: 53 years)
* Mature teratomas account for 20% of all ovarian neoplasms in pathological studies
* Somatic neoplasms may be encountered in up to 2% of mature cystic teratomas (extremely rare in immature teratomas) ([J Obstet Gynaecol Res 2022;48:3068](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36053141))
* Cutaneous neoplasms appear in up to 2% of dermoid cysts, with squamous cell carcinoma representing the majority; ~80% of malignant tumors in teratomas ([BMC Cancer 2019;19:217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30866852))
* Adenocarcinomas, melanomas, melanocytic nevi, basal cell carcinomas and sebaceous neoplasms are much less common ([BMC Womens Health 2019;19:149](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31775731), [Prz Menopauzalny 2018;17:63](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30150913))
* Sarcomas may rarely occur
* Other benign and malignant tumors have also been reported, including low grade malignant mucinous neoplasms, choroid plexus papilloma, Paget disease, glomus tumor, benign soft tissue tumors and lymphomas

Sites

* Tumors arising in ovarian teratomas are typically unilateral

Pathophysiology

* Incompletely understood
* Ovarian squamous cell carcinoma arising in mature cystic teratoma has a high mutational burden, with *TP53* mutations being the most common abnormality ([Clin Cancer Res 2017;23:7633](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28954785))
* *BRAF* mutations and *TERT* promoter alterations have been described in thyroid neoplasms arising from struma ovarii ([Histopathology 2024;84:291](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37771077))

Etiology

* Unknown at this time

Clinical features

* Typically, unilateral and occur in older patients on average compared to uncomplicated teratomas (about 20 years later) ([Arch Gynecol Obstet 2007;275:179](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16957908))
* Large or malignant tumors can show signs and symptoms associated with a pelvic mass
* Diameter > 10 cm and elevated CEA levels in patients ages 45 and above indicate a potential malignancy ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2024;103:e38793](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/39287252))
* Sarcomas are more frequently seen in younger patients compared to carcinomas

Diagnosis

* Often an incidental finding within a teratoma
* Somatic neoplasms may overgrow the underlying teratomatous elements, which may be seen only focally
* Mucinous tumors arising in association with teratomas may closely resemble gastrointestinal adenocarcinomas; metastasis should be excluded

Laboratory

* May present with elevated CEA

Radiology description

* Absence of clinical and radiological specificity of malignant transformation (the diagnosis remains purely histological) ([Case Rep Obstet Gynecol 2021;2021:5527467](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34336320))
* Computed tomography (CT): solid cystic septate pelvic mass
* Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI): well defined solid mass, lobulated
* Ultrasound (US): lobulated ovarian mass, solid ([J Cancer Res Ther 2023;19:S623](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38384029))

Prognostic factors

* Prognosis largely relies on the stage ([J Formos Med Assoc 2008;107:857](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18971155))
* Patients with tumors confined to the ovary tend to have a favorable prognosis
* Overall 5 year survival rate for all stages is between 15% and 52%, up to nearly 80% for stage I tumors ([Front Oncol 2022;12:842703](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35615156))
* Advanced disease (extraovarian spread) has a poor prognosis

Case reports

* 29 year old woman with a history of back pain and amenorrhea ([Gynecol Oncol Rep 2022;44:101100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36388754))
* 43 year old woman with squamous cell carcinoma transformation in a mature cystic teratoma of the ovary ([J Med Case Rep 2024;18:145](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38523295))
* 45 year old woman with abdominal pain ([Radiol Case Rep 2021;16:3275](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34484530))
* 47 year old woman investigated for diffuse pelvic pain ([In Vivo 2020;34:2141](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32606195))
* 51 year old premenopausal woman with left lower abdomen pain ([Fukushima J Med Sci 2020;66:44](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32161199))
* Middle aged woman with lower abdominal pain ([Cureus 2023;15:e44159](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37753035))

Treatment

* For benign tumors, oophorectomy is the treatment of choice
* In malignant cases, bilateral salpingo-oophorectomy + hysterectomy + eventual debulking
* Chemotherapy or radiotherapy in advanced stage disease
* Reference: [J Obstet Gynaecol Res 2022;48:3068](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36053141)

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Microscopic features of typical [mature cystic teratoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorteratomamature.html) or rarely, [immature teratoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorteratomaimmature.html), are definitionally present in the background
  + May contain histologic elements from all 3 germ layers
* Somatic neoplasm appears as a confluent growth of a particular tumor type within the teratomatous background; appearance differs according to the specific somatic tumor type
  + Essentially any histologic tumor type may occur
* Squamous cell carcinoma is the most common somatic malignancy and is defined by infiltrative nests of keratinizing or nonkeratinizing atypical squamous cells
  + They may have cystic degeneration or adjacent areas of dysplasia or carcinoma in situ ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2021;100:e24726](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33787574))
* Other benign and malignant cutaneous neoplasms are relatively common, including sebaceoma / sebaceous carcinoma, other skin adnexal tumors and melanocytic nevi / melanoma
* Mucinous tumors arising in teratomas may be morphologically and immunohistochemically identical to gastrointestinal adenocarcinomas
  + May resemble low grade mucinous neoplasms (LAMN) of the appendix
  + May be associated with mucin extravasation in the ovarian stroma and other peritoneal sites (pseudomyxoma ovarii) ([Case Rep Oncol 2016;9:331](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27462234))
* Thyroid carcinomas may arise within components of [struma ovarii](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorstruma.html) and are sometimes called malignant struma ovarii
  + Papillary thyroid carcinoma is most common and is defined as a confluent papillary or follicular growth of atypical cells with overlapping, optically clear nuclei with longitudinal grooves
  + Follicular thyroid carcinomas may be difficult to diagnose due to the typically disorganized nature of standard struma ovarii; features diagnostic of malignancy include confluent infiltrative growth of atypical cells arranged in follicles, vascular invasion and metastasis
* Other carcinomas of essentially any type may occur (pulmonary / bronchial, neuroendocrine, adenosquamous, etc.)
* A variety of sarcomas have been described including leiomyosarcoma, rhabdomyosarcoma, osteosarcoma, chondrosarcoma, liposarcoma, angiosarcoma and others ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2018;42:90](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29227858))
* Other unusual tumor types include paraganglioma, rhabdomyoma, glomus tumor, glioblastoma and choroid plexus papilloma, among others

Positive stains

* Variable depending on the neoplasia encountered
  + Adenocarcinoma arising from gastrointestinal epithelium: [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html), [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html), [SATB2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssatb2.html) ([Cureus 2023;15:e44159](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37753035), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2016;35:352](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26937866))
  + Squamous cell carcinoma: [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html), [p40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp40.html), [CK8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck8.html) / [18](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck18.html), [CK5/6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5and6.html) (variable), [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) mutated pattern ([BMC Cancer 2019;19:217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30866852))
  + Melanomas: [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html), [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html) ([BMC Womens Health 2019;19:149](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31775731))

Negative stains

* Differ depending on the type of neoplasia encountered
* Squamous cell carcinoma is not HPV associated and is therefore typically [p16](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp16.html) negative

Sample pathology report

* Ovary, ovarian cyst, ovariectomy:
  + Low grade mucinous adenocarcinoma arising from mature cystic teratoma (see comment)
  + Comment: The presence of an underlying mature cystic teratoma suggests a primary ovarian mucinous tumor. However, metastasis from a gastrointestinal site, particularly appendix, should be clinically excluded.

Differential diagnosis

* Differential diagnosis varies depending on the neoplasia encountered
* **Squamous cell carcinoma**:
  + [Metastatic squamous cell carcinoma from other sites](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumormetastatic.html):
    - Thoroughly check for prior history
    - More frequently bilateral
    - Not associated with dermoid cyst
    - Typically [p16](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp16.html) / [HPV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshpv.html) positive if from the uterine cervix
  + [Endometrioid adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorendometrioidcarcinoma.html) with extensive squamous differentiation:
    - May be suggested by the presence of endometriosis or an underlying endometrioid adenofibroma
    - Lack of underlying teratomatous elements
    - Typically positive for [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html), [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html)
* [Metastatic appendiceal mucinous tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorappendiceal.html):
  + May be microscopically and immunohistochemically identical to mucinous tumors arising in teratoma
  + Appendiceal tumor should be clinically excluded
  + Often associated with [pseudomyxoma peritonei](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/appendixpseudomyxoma.html)
* [Metastatic melanoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumormetastatic.html):
  + It is important to differentiate primary malignant melanomas of the ovaries from metastatic ovarian melanoma, as most ovarian malignant melanomas arise from metastasis of tumors located in other areas ([BMC Womens Health 2019;19:149](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31775731))
    - Teratoma component must be present
    - Prior history of malignant melanoma; rule out any other possible primary tumor sites ([Obstet Gynecol Sci 2018;61:282](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29564322))
    - Metastatic malignant melanoma is usually bilateral (as most metastatic malignancies to the ovary)
    - [CA 19.9](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsca19-9.html) can be elevated ([BMC Womens Health 2019;19:149](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31775731))
    - Genetic profiling might be helpful ([Int J Mol Sci 2021;22:2436](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33670958))

Board review style question #1

[![Mucinous low grade adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/ovarysomaticneoplasmsteratomasgiacometti04.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/ovarysomaticneoplasmsteratomasgiacometti04.jpg)

[![Dermoid cyst](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/ovarysomaticneoplasmsteratomasgiacometti03.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/ovarysomaticneoplasmsteratomasgiacometti03.jpg)

The solid nodule shown above is found in a cystic teratoma from a 45 year old woman. What is the diagnosis?

1. Appendiceal mucinous tumor
2. Immature teratoma
3. Metastatic colorectal carcinoma
4. Mucinous low grade carcinoma arising from teratoma

Board review style answer #1

**D.** Mucinous low grade carcinoma arising from teratoma. The images are from a mature cystic teratoma with a solid component. The lesion is well differentiated (low grade) mucinous neoplasia. The glands are embedded in extravasated mucin, which dissects the ovarian stroma, forming what is referred to as pseudomyxoma ovarii.
Answer A is incorrect because mucinous low grade tumors from the appendix usually are accompanied by pseudomyxoma peritonei. However, this is the top differential diagnosis. Appendiceal neoplasms must be ruled out.
Answer B is incorrect because immature teratoma contains immature tissue (mostly of neuroectodermal origin), not carcinomatous.
Answer C is incorrect because metastatic carcinoma is usually bilateral, not associated with pseudomyxoma ovarii and has a prior history of colorectal cancer.

Board review style question #2

Which of the following immunohistochemistry profiles would be expected in squamous cell carcinoma arising from teratoma?

1. CK7 positive
2. ER, PR positive
3. HMB45 positive
4. p63, p40 positive

Board review style answer #2

**D.** p63, p40 positive. The immunohistochemistry is expressed accordingly to the neoplasm encountered. Somatic neoplasms arising in teratomas, including squamous cell carcinoma, express the classical immunohistochemical markers of identical extraovarian tumors.
Answer A is incorrect because squamous cell carcinoma does not express CK7.
Answer B is incorrect because ER and PR are not typically expressed in squamous cell carcinoma.
Answer C is incorrect because HMB45 is a marker for melanoma and not for squamous cell carcinoma.
