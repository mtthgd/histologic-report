Definition / general

* Monodermal ovarian teratoma primarily (> 50%) or exclusively composed of benign thyroid tissue
* Any [mature teratoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorteratomamature.html) with malignant thyroid tissue

Essential features

* Ovarian teratoma either composed predominantly or exclusively of benign thyroid tissue or with any amount of malignant thyroid tissue
* Most common thyroid malignancy to occur in struma ovarii is [papillary thyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidpapillary.html), followed by [follicular carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidfollicular.html)

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [D27.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D27-/D27.9) - benign neoplasm of unspecified ovary
* ICD-10: [C56](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C51-C58/C56-/C56) - malignant neoplasm of ovary

Epidemiology

* Most common type of monodermal teratoma
* Accounts for 3% of ovarian teratomas ([Pathol 2007;39:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17365830))
* Usually presents in the fifth decade

Sites

* Ovary

Pathophysiology / etiology

* Unknown at this time

Clinical features

* Most often an incidental and asymptomatic finding but may present as a pelvic mass with abdominal pain ([Pathology 2007;39:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17365830))
* Uncommonly presents as [hyperthyroidism](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidhyper.html) or pseudo-[Meigs syndrome](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorfibroma.html#pathophysiology117514) (with ascites and pleural effusion) ([Pathology 2007;39:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17365830))
* Seen in association with [Brenner tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorb9brenner.html), [serous](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorserousbenign.html) and [mucinous cystadenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumormucinousbenign.html) and [fibrothecoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorfibroma.html) ([Endocr Pathol 2015;26:342](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26374222))

Diagnosis

* Diagnosis is made by microscopic examination of resected tissue

Laboratory

* Mildly increased CA125 levels in up to 30% of cases ([J Gynecol Oncol 2008;19:135](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19471561))

Radiology description

* Typically a lobulated and multiloculated solid cystic lesion on imaging ([Zhongguo Yi Xue Ke Xue Yuan Xue Bao 2015;37:309](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26149143), [Abdom Imaging 2012;37:904](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22052450))
* MRI is more specific, showing high and low signal intensity on T1 and T2 weighted images from the colloid ([J Med Imaging Radiat Oncol 2009;53:480](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19788483))

Prognostic factors

* Most cases have a good prognosis, even when malignancy is present

Case reports

* 10 year old girl with tachycardia, normal thyroid and thyrotoxicosis with papillary thyroid carcinoma arising from a struma ovarii ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2018;51:218](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30196119))
* 48 year old woman with malignant struma ovarii presenting 14 years after oophorectomy and chemotherapy ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2018;97:e13867](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30572559))
* 52 year old woman with clinical features of advanced ovarian carcinoma found to have a struma ovarii ([Oncol Lett 2015;9:1739](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25789033))
* 61 year old woman with insular carcinoma (90%) and adjacent papillary thyroid carcinoma arising in a struma ovarii ([Gynecol Oncol Rep 2016;18:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29607388))
* 62 year old woman with 2 different types of *RAS* mutations in a papillary thyroid carcinoma from a struma ovarii and papillary thyroid microcarcinoma of the thyroid ([J Endocr Soc 2018;2:944](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30087949))
* 74 year old woman with a mixed ovarian tumor composed of elements of Brenner tumor, mucinous cystadenoma and struma ovarii ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2019;38:576](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30134344))

Treatment

* Oophorectomy
* Surgery for malignant struma ovarii may include total abdominal hysterectomy and bilateral salpingo-oophorectomy with complete staging ([Gynecol Oncol 2004;94:835](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15350384))

Gross description

* Typically unilateral and solid with a gelatinous, red-brown to green cut surface; may show goiter-like multinodular or cystic change ([Am J Surg Pathol 1994;18:785](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8037292))

Frozen section description

* Frozen section sample usually shows a [mature teratoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorteratomamature.html) or benign thyroid tissue; rarely, a possible malignant component may be detected

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Variably sized macro and microfollicles often containing colloid
* Other architectural patterns include solid areas composed of cells with clear to oxyphilic cytoplasm, trabeculae, cords and pseudotubular structures
* Rarely, stroma in between follicles may be fibrotic or edematous; peripheral stromal leutinization may also be seen
* Adenomatous hyperplasia or proliferative changes may be seen, such as areas of densely packed follicles or papillary formations lacking nuclear features of [papillary thyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidpapillary.html)
* Birefringent [calcium oxalate crystals](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidcrystals.html) may be seen in colloid
* Most commonly associated malignancy is [papillary thyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidpapillary.html), cytologically characterized by crowded and overlapping elongated nuclei with irregular contours and chromatin clearing, usually with papillary or follicular architecture
* [Follicular carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidfollicular.html) is the second most common malignancy; since ovarian lesions typically lack capsule, demonstration of tumor invasion into surrounding ovarian tissue, vascular invasion or metastases is needed as evidence of malignancy
* Uncommonly associated with the more recently described highly differentiated follicular carcinoma of ovarian origin, characterized by extraovarian spread of thyroid elements histologically resembling nonneoplastic thyroid tissue ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2008;27:213](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18317221))
* [Undifferentiated (anaplastic) carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorundifferentiated.html) and [medullary carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidmedullary.html) have also been described in association with struma ovarii

Cytology description

* Flattened to cuboidal / columnar cells with small round to oval nuclei, even chromatin and pale to eosinophilic cytoplasm ([Crum: Gynecologic and Obstetric Pathology, 3rd Edition, 2017](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0323447325/pathologyoutl-20))
* Clear cell change may occasionally be seen

Positive stains

* [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html), [thyroglobulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsthyroglobulin.html), [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html), [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html) ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2005;22:3](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16512597))

Negative stains

* **HNF1β**, [Napsin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnapsina.html), [AMACR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsamacr.html), [inhibin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html), [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html), [SF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssf1.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html), [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* *BRAF* mutations and *RET / PTC* rearrangements may be seen in [papillary thyroid carcinomas](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidpapillary.html) arising from a struma ovarii ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1337](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17721188), [Endocr Pathol 2007;18:182](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18058267))

Differential diagnosis

* [Metastatic thyroid carcinoma to the ovary](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumormetastatic.html):
  + Clinical history of primary [thyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidthyroidca.html)
* [Strumal carcinoid](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorcarcinoid.html):
  + Corded, trabecular or follicular architecture with salt and pepper nuclear chromatin pattern and occasional eosinophilic granules
  + Positive staining for [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html), [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html), [thyroglobulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsthyroglobulin.html) (in follicular component) and neuroendocrine markers [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html), [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html), [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html) and [neuron specific enolase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsNSE.html)
* [Ovarian clear cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorclearcell.html):
  + Tubulocystic, papillary and solid growth patterns with cytologically atypical cells, often hobnailing into cysts
  + Positive staining for **HNF1β**, [Napsin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnapsina.html) and [AMACR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsamacr.html)
  + Negative for [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html), [thyroglobulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsthyroglobulin.html)
* [Sex cord stromal tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorsexcordannular.html):
  + Tubular, trabecular or corded growth without eosinophilic secretions
  + Positive staining for [inhibin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html), [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html) and [SF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssf1.html)
  + Negative for [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html), [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html) (usually negative, [Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2011;19:293](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21285870)), [thyroglobulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsthyroglobulin.html)
* [Melanoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticmelanoma.html):
  + Nested growth with eosinophilic cytoplasm
  + Positive staining for typical melanoma markers such as [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html) and [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html)
  + Negative for keratin ([AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html))

Board review style question #2

Which laboratory marker may be elevated with struma ovarii?

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case465image7.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case465image7.jpg)

1. Alpha-fetoprotein (AFP)
2. CA125
3. CEA
4. hCG
5. PLAP

Board review style answer #2

**B.** CA125 is a widely accepted biomarker for ovarian cancers that can also be elevated in many other conditions, such as menstruation, endometriosis, pregnancy, pelvic inflammatory disease, fibroids, liver disease and tumors of the endometrium, breasts and lungs. It can be increased in up to 30% of struma ovarii.
