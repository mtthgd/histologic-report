Definition / general

* Ovarian stromal neoplasm, almost always benign, composed of cells resembling theca cells

Essential features

* Almost always benign
* Usually occurs in postmenopausal women who present with uterine bleeding
* Histology shows a predominant population of cells with ovoid to round nuclei and pale gray cytoplasm
* Reticulin stain and molecular testing for *FOXL2* mutation help distinguish thecoma from adult granulosa cell tumor

Terminology

* Theca cell tumor

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8600/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=77) - thecoma, NOS
* ICD-10: [D27](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D27-/D27) - benign neoplasm of ovary
* ICD-11: [2F32.Y & XH34A0](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f181365103%2fmms%2fother?data=%7B%22dataType%22%3A%22pc%22%2C%22postcoordinationCodeSet%22%3A%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F181365103%2Fmms%2Fother%22%2C%22axisToValueIds%22%3A%7B%22histopathology%22%3A%5B%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F1500755340%22%7D%5D%7D%7D%7D) - other specified benign neoplasm of ovary & thecoma, NOS

Epidemiology

* Uncommon
* Usually postmenopausal women
* Mean age = 59 years ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2008;12:12](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18164409))
* Age ranges from 16 to 81 ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:1023](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25025365))

Sites

* Ovary

Pathophysiology

* Molecular alterations, such as trisomy 12 and loss of heterozygosity (LOH) at the *PTCH* gene (see [Molecular / cytogenetics description](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorthecoma.html#moleculardescription))

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* May be discovered incidentally
* Most common symptom is postmenopausal bleeding ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:1023](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25025365))
* Symptoms can be related to mass: pelvic pain / pressure
* May present with estrogenic or androgenic manifestations
* Associated with endometrial hyperplasia and malignancy

Diagnosis

* Histologic examination

Laboratory

* Occasionally elevated serum inhibin A and inhibin B ([Gynecol Oncol Rep 2020;34:100658](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33204793), [Gynecol Endocrinol 2016;32:872](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27689825))

Radiology description

* Sonographic features:
  + Adnexal hypoechoic mass with clear border and acoustic attenuation
  + Minimal Doppler flow signal ([J Ovarian Res 2016;9:81](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27876070))
* Magnetic resonance imaging features:
  + Isointense or slightly hyperintense
  + Pelvic fluid accumulation ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2020;99:e20358](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32481327))
* Computed tomography features:
  + Isodense or hypodense ([J Comput Assist Tomogr 2012;36:46](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22261769))
* Occasionally thecomas have positive F-FDG uptake on PET scan ([J Obstet Gynaecol Res 2017;43:599](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27987341))

Prognostic factors

* Thecomas are almost always benign but may be associated with endometrial malignancy

Case reports

* 57 year old woman with postmenopausal bleeding and elevated serum inhibin B level ([Gynecol Oncol Rep 2020;34:100658](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33204793))
* 58 year old woman with progressive scalp hair loss ([Skin Appendage Disord 2019;5:259](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31367608))
* 61 year old woman with Meigs syndrome and elevated CA-125 level ([J Menopausal Med 2015;21:56](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26046039))

Treatment

* Oophorectomy if fertility sparing is desired
* Total hysterectomy with bilateral salpingo-oophorectomy is indicated in postmenopausal patients
* References: [Gynecol Oncol 1994;55:S62](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7530680), [Acta Radiol Oncol 1980;19:241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6257044), [Gynecol Oncol 1985;21:135](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2985476)

Frozen section description

* Sheets of oval to round cells with moderate to abundant pale gray cytoplasm
* Bilaterality should raise suspicion for metastasis
  + Signet ring cells may be missed on frozen section and misinterpreted as fibrothecoma ([Yeungnam Univ J Med 2019;36:163](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31620631))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Predominant population of cells showing ovoid to round nuclei and pale gray cytoplasm, which can be abundant
* Minor component of the tumor may have spindled nuclei, reflecting overlap between fibroma and thecoma
* Indistinct cell membranes impart a syncytial appearance
* Diffuse or nodular growth pattern
* Absent or minimal nuclear atypia
* Mitotic rate usually < 5/10 high power fields
* Hyaline plaques
* Cytoplasmic lipid vacuoles may be present but are not essential
* May show aggregates of cells with brightly eosinophilic cytoplasm (lutein cells)
* Calcification is more common in young patients ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 1988;7:343](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2852649))
* Uncommon features include keloid-like sclerosis, nuclear grooves, bizarre nuclear atypia ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:1023](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25025365))
* Rarely contains a minor component of sex cord elements ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 1983;2:227](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6315609))
* Malignant thecoma: very rare, diagnosis requires diffuse moderate to severe nuclear atypia and high mitotic rate (> 4/10 high power fields) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:e15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21317704))

Electron microscopy description

* Cytoplasmic lipid ([Ultrastruct Pathol 1992;16:363](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1316660))
* Type I cells: dispersed chromatin, basal lamina investment of each cell, coiled / branching rough endoplasmic reticulum, sparse smooth endoplasmic reticulum, irregular mitochondria
* Type II cells: degenerative changes, large round mitochondria with incomplete cristae and centers displaced by microfilaments ([Hum Pathol 1984;15:153](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6321320))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Trisomy 12 or tetrasomy 12 ([Gynecol Oncol 1990;36:413](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2156766), [Cancer Genet Cytogenet 1993;71:180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8281525))
* Trisomy 4 ([Gynecol Oncol 1992;45:66](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1318257))
* Loss of heterozygosity (LOH) at 9q22.3 (*PTCH1*) and 19p13.3 ([Hum Pathol 2005;36:792](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16084949))
* *FOXL2* mutation has been reported in a subset of thecomas (3 out of 14 tumors in one series) ([N Engl J Med 2009;360:2719](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19516027))
  + However these may represent misdiagnosed granulosa cell tumors ([PLoS One 2009;4:e7988](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19956657))
  + Pericellular reticulin staining is 100% specific for absence of *FOXL2* mutation ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2018;37:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28700438))

Sample pathology report

  + Ovarian thecoma

Differential diagnosis

* [Adult granulosa cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorgctadult.html):
  + [Reticulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsreticulin.html) stain shows nested pattern ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2019;38:143](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29708950))
  + Harbors *FOXL2* mutation ([Mod Pathol 2013;26:860](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23348906), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2018;37:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28700438))
* [Fibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorfibroma.html):
  + Intersecting bundles of spindle cells producing collagen
  + A morphologic spectrum between fibroma and thecoma exists
  + Indeterminate tumors have been called fibrothecoma
* **Luteinized thecoma associated with [sclerosing peritonitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelsclerosingperitonitis.html)**:
  + Younger age at presentation (median age: 28)
  + Symptoms include abdominal pain, ascites and bowel obstruction
  + Cutaneous involvement has been reported ([Ann N Y Acad Sci 1985;448:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2862825))
  + Bland spindle cell proliferation circumferentially involving the ovarian cortex with sparing of the medulla
  + Almost always bilateral ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:1273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18636018))
* [Sclerosing stromal tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorsclerosingstroma.html):
  + Pseudolobular growth pattern with cellular nodules separated by dense collagenous or edematous connective tissue ([Cancer 1973;31:664](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4348335))
  + Staghorn vessels
* [Microcystic stromal tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumormicrocysticstromal.html):
  + Small, anastomosing cysts
  + [Inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html)-, [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html)-
  + Nuclear [beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html)+
  + [Cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html)+
  + [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html)+ ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1420](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26200099))
* [Steroid cell tumor, NOS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorsteroidcelltumornos.html):
  + [FOXL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfoxl2.html)- ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:484](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21378549))
  + Polygonal cells with abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm
  + Typically has a component of lipid rich cells (pale and vacuolated) ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2018;142:1459](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30500284))
* [Leydig cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorleydig.html):
  + Tumor centered in ovarian hilus
  + Cytoplasmic Reinke crystals
  + Nuclei tend to cluster, forming eosinophilic nuclei free zones ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 1989;8:299](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2553628))
  + Fibrinoid necrosis of blood vessel walls
