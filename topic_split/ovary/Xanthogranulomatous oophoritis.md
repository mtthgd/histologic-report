Definition / general

* Xanthogranulomatous oophoritis is an uncommon, nonneoplastic, chronic process ([Iran J Pathol 2018;13:372](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30636962))
* Destruction by massive cellular infiltration of foamy histiocytes admixed with multinucleated giant cells, plasma cells, fibroblasts, neutrophils and foci of necrosis ([Iran J Pathol 2018;13:372](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30636962))

Essential features

* Xanthogranulomatous oophoritis is a rare form of chronic oophoritis ([J Indian Med Assoc 2012;110:653](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23741842))
* Affects fallopian tubes or ovaries focally or entirely, forming a mass-like lesion in the pelvic cavity that invades the surrounding tissues ([J Cancer 2012;3:100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22393333))

Terminology

* Xanthogranulomatous oophoritis
* Xanthogranulomatous inflammation of the ovary
* Xanthogranulomatous inflammation of the female genital tract
* Ovarian fibroxanthoma

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [N70.92](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/N00-N99/N70-N77/N70-/N70.92) - oophoritis, unspecified

Epidemiology

* Most frequent in females of reproductive age (range: 23 - 72 years)
* Average age of patients with affected ovaries is 31 years ([AJR Am J Roentgenol 2002;178:749](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11856712))
* Only 13 related cases of xanthogranulomatous inflammation involving fallopian tube or ovary have been described through 2012 ([J Cancer 2012;3:100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22393333))

Sites

* Xanthogranulomatous inflammation of the female genital tract is not common and usually involves the endometrium; however, xanthogranulomatous inflammation of the ovaries is a rare entity ([J Ayub Med Coll Abbottabad 2017;29:162](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28712201))

Pathophysiology

* Exact pathophysiology is unknown
* Associated with infection, ineffective antibiotic therapy
* Ineffective clearance of bacteria by phagocytes, endometriosis, intrauterine contraceptive device, pelvic inflammatory disease and drugs (antibiotics) is suggested ([Iran J Pathol 2018;13:372](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30636962))

Etiology

* Development can be of multiple predisposing factors, for example, pelvic inflammatory diseases, intrauterine device use, uterine leiomyoma, endometriosis and inappropriate antibiotic intake ([BMJ Case Rep 2015 Jun 25;2015](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26113594))

Clinical features

* Longstanding history of pelvic inflammatory disease
* Symptoms such as anorexia, fever, suprapubic pain, menorrhagia or vaginal bleeding, adnexal tenderness and a pelvic mass are the usual chief complaints ([Iran J Pathol 2018;13:372](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30636962))

Diagnosis

* Careful histopathology by experienced pathologists aided by immunohistochemistry can lead to a definitive diagnosis ([J Ayub Med Coll Abbottabad 2017;29:162](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28712201))

Radiology description

* Ultrasound of the abdomen reveals a thick walled, cystic hyperechoic ovarian mass ([Iran J Pathol 2018;13:372](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30636962))
* Pelvic CT scan shows a peripherally enhancing ovarian cystic lesion, which represents either an inflammatory process or ovarian neoplasm ([Iran J Pathol 2018;13:372](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30636962))
* Presence of nonenhancing intramural nodules in the thickened wall of an ovarian cystic mass may be a unique MRI indicator of xanthogranulomatous oophoritis ([AJR Am J Roentgenol 2002;178:749](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11856712))

Prognostic factors

* Since xanthogranulomatous oophoritis is usually associated with pelvic inflammatory disease, endometriosis, intrauterine death, etc., these patients should be followed up closely ([Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2010;53:197](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20090279))

Case reports

* 20 year old woman presenting with a tubo-ovarian mass ([Iran J Pathol 2018;13:372](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30636962))
* 24 year old woman who was misdiagnosed with an ovarian neoplasm ([J Midlife Health 2018;9:41](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29628729))
* 25 year old woman presented with intermittent fever and lower abdominal pain ([Indian J Pathol Microbiol 1999;42:89](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10420690))
* 26 year old woman with a 10 year history of pelvic inflammatory disease ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 1984;3:398](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6511165))
* 31 year old Lebanese woman, 8 years following an open appendectomy as a reaction to talcum powder present on surgical gloves ([J Med Liban 2012;60:169](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23198459))
* 45 year old woman with heavy vaginal bleeding for 20 days ([BMJ Case Rep 2015 Jun 25;2015](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26113594))
* 47 year old woman with a several month history of intermittent abdominal pain localized to the left suprapubic region ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2001;125:260](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11175647))
* 48 year old woman with a 3 day history of lower abdominal pain and fever ([AJR Am J Roentgenol 2002;178:749](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11856712))

Treatment

* Treatment of choice is oophorectomy ([J Midlife Health 2018;9:41](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29628729))

Gross description

* Ovarian lesion usually has a solid mass with rugged outer surface
* Serial sectioning mostly reveals tan, grayish white and necrotic cut surfaces ([J Cancer 2012;3:100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22393333))

Positive stains

* [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html) in foamy histiocytes
* [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html) in T cells
* [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html) in B cells ([J Cancer 2012;3:100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22393333))

Negative stains

* Pankeratin ([AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html)), [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html)

Sample pathology report

* Left ovary and fallopian tube, left salpingo-oophorectomy:
  + Cystic ovary with endometriosis
  + Extensive xanthogranulomatous oophoritis
  + Fallopian tube with no significant pathologic change

Differential diagnosis

* [Ovarian carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorovariangen.html):
  + May be difficult to differentiate with imaging
  + Gross and microscopic pathologic findings will easily differentiate malignant tumors from inflammatory lesions
* [Endometrioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarynontumorendometriosis.html):
  + Usually filled with chocolate colored fluid
  + Histologic sections of cystic wall show hemosiderin laden macrophages without abundant foamy histiocytes
* [Tubo-ovarian mass](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarynontumorabscess.html):
  + Benign tumors can occur in the tubo-ovarian area, i.e. [leiomyoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorleiomyoma.html)
  + Gross and histologic examinations reveal smooth muscle neoplasm rather than inflammatory process / lesion
