Definition / general

* Primitive germ cell tumor with a variety of morphologic patterns, ranging from endodermal extraembryonic structures (secondary yolk sac, allantois) to, less commonly, endodermal somatic tissues (intestine, liver, mesenchyme)

Essential features

* Most common before the age of 30
* Usually occurs as a pure form or rarely as a component of a mixed germ cell tumor
* Numerous morphologic patterns, with the hallmark Schiller-Duval bodies in some cases and immunohistochemical expression of SALL4, glypican 3 and AFP
* Often associated with elevated serum [alpha fetoprotein (AFP)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsafp.html)
* Usually favorable clinical outcomes due to chemosensitivity

Terminology

* Primitive endodermal tumor (not recommended)
* Endodermal sinus tumor (not recommended)

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9071/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=85) - yolk sac tumor
* ICD-10: [C56](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C51-C58/C56-/C56) - malignant neoplasm of ovary
* ICD-11:
  + [2C73.5](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f455713450) - endodermal sinus tumor, unspecified site, female
  + [2C73.Y & XH09W7](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f685124533%2fmms%2fother?data=%7B%22dataType%22%3A%22pc%22%2C%22postcoordinationCodeSet%22%3A%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F685124533%2Fmms%2Fother%22%2C%22axisToValueIds%22%3A%7B%22histopathology%22%3A%5B%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F1084215767%22%7D%5D%7D%7D%7D) - other specified malignant neoplasms of the ovary, yolk sac tumor

Epidemiology

* ~20% of malignant germ cell tumors of the ovary ([Obstet Gynecol 2006;107:1075](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16648414))
* Second most common malignant ovarian germ cell tumor after dysgerminoma
* Mean age is 19 years; most common in the second and third decades of life ([Int J Surg Pathol 2014;22:677](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25395492))

Sites

* Usually ovary
* Less common in uterus, vagina, vulva and peritoneum ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27631522))

Pathophysiology

* Chromosome 12 abnormalities, usually isochromosome 12p, in ~75% of patients ([Cancer Res 1998;58:3105](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9679978))

Clinical features

* Abdominal enlargement or pain
* Lower abdominal or pelvic mass ([Int J Surg Pathol 2014;22:677](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25395492))
* Rarely hormonal manifestations

Diagnosis

* Microscopic examination

Laboratory

* Elevated serum levels of AFP (may be used diagnostically and in monitoring therapy)

Radiology description

* Ultrasonography:
  + Both echogenic and hypoechoic components ([AJR Am J Roentgenol 1996;167:791](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8751702))
* Computed tomography:
  + Usually appears as a unilateral large complex mass with solid and cystic components, heterogeneous enhancement and enlarged intratumoral vessels with hemorrhage and capsular tear ([Acta Radiol 2016;57:197](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25492969))
  + Helpful features for differentiating yolk sac tumor from other ovarian tumors include a mixed solid / cystic nature, intratumoral hemorrhage, marked enhancement and dilated intratumoral vessels ([Sci Rep 2015;5:11000](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26074455))
  + Intratumoral calcification and fatty tissue if associated with teratoma
* Magnetic resonance imaging:
  + Prominent signal voids ([J Comput Assist Tomogr 2000;24:605](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10966195))
  + Often with areas of hemorrhage

Prognostic factors

* Usually favorable prognosis due to chemosensitivity, with complete cure in > 80% of cases
* Stage dependent, with 5 year survival rates of > 95% for stage I - II, 70% for stage III and 50% for stage IV ([Gynecol Oncol 2017;147:296](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28803748), [Int J Gynecol Cancer 2018;28:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29194189))
* Prominent polyvesicular vitelline pattern associated with more indolent behavior ([Am J Surg Pathol 2013;37:393](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23348213))
* Pure hepatoid and glandular intestinal type yolk sac tumors associated with poorer prognosis

Case reports

* 12 year old girl with ovarian yolk sac tumor presenting with acute abdominal pain and elevated serum AFP ([Acta Biomed 2019;90:599](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31910193))
* 17 year old girl with bilateral metachronous ovarian yolk sac tumors ([J Pediatr Adolesc Gynecol 2017;30:259](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27818263))
* 19 year old woman with mixed germ cell tumor (yolk sac tumor and choriocarcinoma) arising in gonadoblastoma ([Int J Surg Pathol 2018;26:287](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29183227))
* 20 year old woman with fertility sparing surgery for ovarian yolk sac tumor ([J Clin Diagn Res 2017;11:QD12](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28969219))
* 21 year old woman with ovarian yolk sac tumor associated with granulomatous reaction resembling tuberculosis ([Turk Patoloji Derg 2016;32:126](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24585354))

Treatment

* Unilateral salpingo-oophorectomy ([Eur J Surg Oncol 2006;32:1063](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16996238))
* Adjuvant multiagent combination chemotherapy

Large ovarian tumor

Gross description

* Usually unilateral ovarian mass with predilection for right ovary
* Encapsulated with smooth and glistening surface, round, oval or globular, may be firm or somewhat lobulated
* Rupture in 25% of cases ([Int J Surg Pathol 2014;22:677](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25395492))
* Mean size 15 cm (range 3 - 30)
* Fleshy, gray-yellow to gray-tan, solid and cystic friable cut surface, often with gelatinous changes and areas of hemorrhage and necrosis ([Cancer 1976;38:2404](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/63318))
* May form adhesions to surrounding structures
* When part of a [mixed germ cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumormixed.html), other components, such as a [mature cystic teratoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorteratomamature.html) or [dysgerminoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumordysgerminoma.html), may be grossly recognizable
* Honeycomb appearance (multiple small cysts) on cut surface if polyvesicular vitelline component is present ([Am J Surg Pathol 2013;37:393](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23348213))

Cytologic atypia

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Multiple histologic patterns with predominance of 1 or 2 patterns
* Reticular / microcystic pattern:
  + Most common pattern
  + Loose meshwork of anastomosing channels and variably sized cysts (macro or microcysts) lined by primitive tumor cells with varying amounts of clear to eosinophilic cytoplasm ([Cancer 1976;38:2404](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/63318), [Histopathology 2012;60:1023](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22008025), [Int J Surg Pathol 2014;22:677](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25395492))
  + Lining cells may be flattened and deceptively bland
  + Tumor cells occasionally contain lipid and have a signet ring-like morphology
  + Cysts may contain eosinophilic hyaline globules and amorphous, eosinophilic acellular basement membrane-like material
  + Loose, hypocellular or myxoid stroma
* Endodermal sinus pattern:
  + Anastomosing network of labyrinthine-like spaces lined by tumor cells
  + Formation of vaguely glomeruloid perivascular structures (Schiller-Duval bodies)
    - Hallmark of yolk sac tumor but their absence does not rule out the diagnosis
    - Variably present, ranging from 20 - 75% of cases ([Surg Pathol Clin 2019;12:621](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31097117))
    - Rounded to elongated papillary structures containing a central fibrovascular core with a single central vessel, surrounded by tumor cells projecting into a cystic / sinusoidal space (resembling immature glomeruli) ([Histopathology 2012;60:1023](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22008025))
* Papillary pattern:
  + Papillae containing fibrovascular cores lined by pleomorphic tumor cells with brisk mitoses
  + Fibrovascular cores may be hyalinized
  + May contain tumor giant cells (mono or multinucleated)
* Solid pattern:
  + Sheets of polygonal tumor cells with large vesicular or pyknotic nuclei with prominent nucleoli, brisk mitoses, clear to eosinophilic cytoplasm and well defined borders, sometimes with prominent hyaline globules
  + Cells may be smaller and more blastema-like with scant cytoplasm
  + May contain tumor giant cells (mono or multinucleated)
* Festoon pattern:
  + Complex ribbons and undulating cords
  + Occasionally with a drape-like arrangement
* Glandular pattern (forming endodermal somatic derivatives):
  + Endometrioid type areas with glandular or villoglandular structures lined by single or multiple layers of tall columnar cells containing subnuclear or supranuclear vacuoles resembling secretory endometrium
  + Intestinal type areas with glandular structures lined by mucinous columnar or low columnar glands, ranging from primitive cribriform structures to well differentiated glands with goblet cells and rarely Paneth cells
  + Both types may occur in pure form and may contain tumor giant cells (mono or multinucleated)
* Polyvesicular vitelline pattern ([Am J Surg Pathol 2013;37:393](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23348213)):
  + May occur in pure form
  + Variably sized cysts or vesicles lined by flat to cuboidal to columnar cells, sometimes with basal or paraluminal vacuolation
  + Variably cellular stroma, occasionally with eccentric constriction (resembling subdivision of primary yolk sac vesicle)
* Parietal pattern:
  + Tumor cells embedded in extracellular linear bands of PAS positive basement membrane-like material
* Hepatoid pattern:
  + Tends to occur in pure form
  + Aggregates, clusters or cords of large polygonal cells with abundant uniform or granular eosinophilic cytoplasm, round nuclei and prominent nucleoli, separated by thin fibrous bands ([Cancer 1982;50:2355](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7139531))
* Mesenchyme-like pattern:
  + Cords, tubules and gland-like structures of tumor cells scattered in edematous to myxoid stroma
  + May be markedly myxoid (magma reticulare)
* General features:
  + Variable cytologic atypia and mitotic activity
  + Pale eosinophilic to clear cytoplasm
  + Prominent nucleoli
  + Intracellular hyaline globules
  + Tumor cells lining cystic structures can be deceptively bland
  + Rarely stromal luteinization
  + May show areas of extramedullary hematopoiesis
  + May be admixed with other malignant germ cell tumor, usually with dysgerminoma or gonadoblastoma in patients with gonadal dysgenesis
  + May be associated with synchronous or metachronous ipsilateral or contralateral mature cystic teratoma

Electron microscopy description

* Glandular intestinal type yolk sac tumor shows large nuclei with prominent nucleolonema, numerous intracytoplasmic ribosomes, rough endoplasmic reticulum, mitochondria and dense amorphous intracellular material ([Pathol Res Pract 1987;182:609](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2446292))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Usually isochromosome 12p ([Mod Pathol 2006;19:766](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16547466))

Sample pathology report

* Right fallopian tube and ovary, salpingo-oophorectomy:
  + Ovary: yolk sac tumor (see synoptic report)
  + Fallopian tube: benign

Differential diagnosis

* [Clear cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorclearcell.html):
  + Typical architectural patterns (tubulocystic, papillary, solid) with or without hobnail cells
  + Lack of microcysts and Schiller-Duval bodies
  + Discordance between mitotic activity and cytologic atypia
  + Stromal hyalinization
  + Background adenofibroma or endometriosis
  + Positive for [napsin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnapsina.html), [HNF1β](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsHNF1B.html), [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html), [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)
  + Negative for [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html) and [AFP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsafp.html)
  + [Glypican 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglypican3.html) is not helpful in this differential diagnosis
* [Endometrioid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorendometrioidcarcinoma.html):
  + Usually older patients
  + Typical cytology (columnar cells with moderately atypical pseudostratified nuclei)
  + Lack of histologic patterns of yolk sac tumor, Schiller-Duval bodies and primitive appearing nuclei ([Int J Surg Pathol 2014;22:677](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25395492))
  + Squamous differentiation
  + May be associated with endometriosis
  + Positive for [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html), [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html), [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html), [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)
  + Negative for [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html) and [AFP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsafp.html)
  + [Glypican 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglypican3.html) is not helpful in this differential diagnosis
* [Dysgerminoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumordysgerminoma.html):
  + Monomorphic architecture composed of cells with ample pale cytoplasm and large round nuclei
  + Lack of histologic patterns of yolk sac tumor, Schiller-Duval bodies and hyaline bodies
  + Associated with lymphocytic and granulomatous reactions
  + Positive for [OCT 3/4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct34.html) and [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html)
  + Negative for [AFP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsafp.html) and [glypican 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglypican3.html)
* [Embryonal carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorembryonal.html):
  + Very rare in pure form in the ovary
  + Lack of histologic patterns of yolk sac tumor and Schiller-Duval bodies
  + Aggregated of primitive cells with more marked nuclear atypia and more granular cytoplasm
  + Positive for [OCT 3/4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct34.html), [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html) and [SOX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox2.html)
  + Negative for [AFP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsafp.html) and [glypican 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglypican3.html)
* [Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorsertolileydig.html):
  + Androgenic manifestations
  + Morphologic patterns of Sertoli cell tumor or Leydig cells
  + Positive for [inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html), [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html), [FOXL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfoxl2.html) and [SF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssf1.html)
  + Negative for [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html), [AFP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsafp.html) and [glypican 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglypican3.html)
* [Juvenile granulosa cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorgctjuv.html):
  + Solid or follicular growth composed of polygonal cells with eosinophilic to clear cytoplasm and hyperchromatic nuclei without grooves
  + Lack of histologic patterns of yolk sac tumor and Schiller-Duval bodies
  + Positive for [inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html), [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html), [FOXL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfoxl2.html) and [SF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssf1.html)
  + Negative for [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html), [AFP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsafp.html) and [glypican 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglypican3.html)
* [Metastatic hepatocellular carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/livertumorHCC.html):
  + Clinical history
  + Diffuse, corded or trabecular patterns
  + With or without bile within canaliculi
  + Positive for [arginase1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsarginase1.html), [HepPar1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsheppar1.html), [albumin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalbumin.html) in situ hybridization
  + Negative for [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html)
* [Immature teratoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorteratomaimmature.html):
  + Admixture of endodermal, mesodermal and ectodermal elements
* **Somatic yolk sac tumor**:
  + Yolk sac tumor associated with a somatic epithelial neoplasm, usually high grade ([Histopathology 2016;69:739](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27334714))
  + Thought to be due to transdifferentiation of high grade carcinoma to yolk sac tumor, rather than a true mixed or collision tumor (i.e. somatic rather than germ cell origin) ([Histopathology 2016;69:739](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27334714), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2011;30:442](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21804392))
  + Typically displays a reticular pattern ([Histopathology 2017;71:562](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28581124))
  + Occurs in postmenopausal women and exhibits poor prognosis ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2011;30:442](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21804392))
