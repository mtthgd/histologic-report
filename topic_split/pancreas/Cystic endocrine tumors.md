Definition / general

* Distinctive subgroup of pancreatic neuroendocrine tumors that are well differentiated with a low mitotic rate and low Ki67 proliferative index as well as have a cystic component ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:1666](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23073325))

Essential features

* Histologically resembles typical pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor but with cystic component
* Improved prognosis
* Occurs more frequently in patients with MEN1

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [C25.4](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C15-C26/C25-/C25.4) - malignant neoplasm of endocrine pancreas

Epidemiology

* Up to 17% of pancreatic neuroendocrine tumors are purely or partially cystic, due to cystic degeneration of originally solid tumor ([J Am Coll Surg 2008;206:1154](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18501813), [Rev Esp Enferm Dig 2018;110:130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29313698))

Sites

* More common in tail of pancreas

Pathophysiology

* 10 - 25% are associated with MEN1, occur in younger patients and are more often multiple and functional ([Rev Esp Enferm Dig 2017;109:778](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29072081))

Clinical features

* Usually (85%) nonfunctional ([Rev Esp Enferm Dig 2017;109:778](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29072081))
* If functional, usually insulinoma
* Often (45%) diagnosed incidentally ([Rev Esp Enferm Dig 2017;109:778](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29072081))

Diagnosis

* Can be confirmed by octreoscan scintigraphy

Radiology description

* Cystic, making diagnosis difficult; may be confused with other pancreatic cystic lesions (including intraductal papillary mucinous neoplasm and mucinous cystic neoplasm)
* Usually shows peripheral enhancement ([AJR Am J Roentgenol 2013;200:W283](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23436873))

Prognostic factors

* Better prognosis than noncystic neuroendocrine tumors of pancreas
* 60% disease free survival at 10 years ([Rev Esp Enferm Dig 2017;109:778](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29072081))

Case reports

* 20 year old woman with incidental tumor ([J Cancer Res Ther 2012;8:289](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22842378))
* 42 year old woman with cystic mass and tumorlets ([World J Gastroenterol 2017;23:6911](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29085235))
* 51 year old woman with multicystic pancreas mass ([Clin J Gastroenterol 2018;11:87](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29270782))
* 66 year old woman with lesion mimicking mucinous cystic neoplasm ([Clin J Gastroenterol 2018;11:333](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29536430))
* 78 year old man with epigastric pain ([BMC Res Notes 2014;7:510](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25106541))

Treatment

* Surgical excision

Gross description

* Mean 5 cm
* Generally unilocular cystic lesion filled with straw colored fluid

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Typically appears as a thick fibrous wall with neuroendocrine cells lining the central cystic space and sometimes forming nests or vesicular structures within the fibrosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2001;25:752](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11395552))
* Necrosis and lymphovascular invasion are uncommon

Cytology description

* Loosely cohesive aggregates and single cells
* Cells are small and plasmacytoid with occasional cytoplasmic vacuoles; nuclei are round / oval and uniform with finely and evenly distributed chromatin ([Cancer 2009;117:203](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19365841))

Positive stains (IHC and special stains)

* Neuroendocrine markers ([synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html)
  / [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html))
* Often express [glucagon](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/glucagon.html) but do not behave clinically as [glucagonomas](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pancreasglucagonoma.html)

Sample pathology report

* Pancreas, tail, resection:
  + Pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor with cystic degeneration, WHO grade 1 (see synoptic report and comment)
  + Comment: Immunohistochemical stains for synaptophysin and chromogranin are positive and the Ki67 index is approximately 1.1%.

Differential diagnosis

* [Acinar cell cystadenoma / cystadenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pancreasacinarcellcystadenoma.html):
  + Cysts are lined by acinar cells without associated acinar cell nests
  + Negative for [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html)
    and [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html)

Board review style question #1

A patient is found to have an incidental cystic pancreatic mass on CT. Fine needle aspiration is performed and shows loosely cohesive tumor cells with a plasmacytoid appearance. Immunostains performed on the cell block show the cells are positive for synaptophysin and chromogranin. What is true about this lesion?

1. Almost never incidental, despite this case
2. More common in patients with MEN1 syndrome
3. Probably arose in the head of the pancreas
4. Very poor prognosis

Board review style answer #1

**B**. More common in patients with MEN1 syndrome. This is a cystic pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor.
