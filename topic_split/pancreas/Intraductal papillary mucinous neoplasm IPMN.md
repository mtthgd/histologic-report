Definition / general

* Grossly visible noninvasive mucinous epithelial neoplasm arising from main pancreatic duct or branch ducts, usually > 5 mm, composed of various cell types with various cytologic and architectural atypia ([Virchows Arch 2005;447:794](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16088402), [Pancreatology 2017;17:738](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28735806))
  + Further classified as:
    - Low grade (encompasses low or intermediate grade dysplasia) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1730](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26559377))
    - High grade ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1730](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26559377))
    - With an associated invasive carcinoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1730](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26559377))
* 1 of 3 precursor lesions of pancreatic adenocarcinoma (see also [mucinous cystic neoplasm](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pancreasmcn.html))

Essential features

* Grossly visible (> 5 mm) cystic pancreatic neoplasm, usually in head of pancreas
* > 90% 5 year survival with complete resection
* Roughly 33% of cases have an associated invasive carcinoma
* Further subtyped into gastric, intestinal and pancreaticobiliary types based on epithelium (note: [intraductal oncocytic papillary neoplasm (IOPN)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pancreasiopn.html) is now considered a distinct and separate entity)

Terminology

* Low grade to intermediate grade dysplasia previously termed: intraductal papillary mucinous adenoma
* High grade dysplasia previously termed: intraductal papillary mucinous carcinoma, noninvasive
* With an associated invasive carcinoma previously termed: intraductal papillary mucinous carcinoma, invasive
* Other previous terms include: mucin producing tumor, mucinous duct ectasia, ductectatic mucinous cystadenoma / cystadenocarcinoma, villous adenoma or papillary adenoma / carcinoma

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [D13.6](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D13-/D13.6) - benign neoplasm of pancreas

Epidemiology

* Rising incidence likely secondary to better characterization of this entity along with use of radiologic imaging studies ([Clin Gastroenterol Hepatol 2010;8:213](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19835989))
* More common in men, usually ages 60 - 70 years ([Hum Pathol 2012;43:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21777948))

Sites

* Main duct intraductal papillary mucinous neoplasm (IPMN) mostly involves head of pancreas, 33% in body and tail ([Hum Pathol 2012;43:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21777948))
* Branch duct IPMN mostly involves head of pancreas or uncinate process, with multiple distinct lesions seen in ~33% of cases ([Hum Pathol 2012;43:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21777948))

Pathophysiology

* See [Molecular / cytogenetics description](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pancreasipmn.html#pancreasipmnmolec)

Etiology

* No well established factors; cigarette smoking implicated in one series ([Adv Anat Pathol 1999;6:65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10331069))
* Reported in Peutz-Jeghers syndrome and familial adenomatous polyposis ([Gut 2002;51:446](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12171972), [Am J Pathol 1999;154:1835](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10362809))

Indications for surgery

Clinical features

* Clinically divided into main duct IPMN, branch duct IPMN and mixed IPMN (most mixed type IPMN present and behave as main duct IPMN) ([Hum Pathol 2012;43:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21777948))
* Main duct IPMN tends to be symptomatic, with symptoms related to duct obstruction (pancreatitis) ([Hum Pathol 2012;43:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21777948))
  + Signs and symptoms include epigastric pain, weight loss, jaundice, diabetes, pancreatitis ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1996;120:981](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12046614))
* Branch duct IPMN tends to be asymptomatic and is generally discovered incidentally on imaging ([Hum Pathol 2012;43:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21777948))
* [Peutz-Jeghers syndrome](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorPeutz.html) (autosomal dominant inherited syndrome, mutations in *STK11* / *LKB1I*) is also associated with IPMN ([Hum Pathol 2012;43:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21777948))

Diagnosis

* Radiographic / endoscopic findings
* Fine needle aspiration
* Surgical specimen

Laboratory

* Pancreatic cyst fluid analysis can assist in determining type of cyst ([Am J Gastroenterol 2008;103:2871](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18775021), [Gastrointest Endosc 2009;69:1095](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19152896), [Gastrointest Endosc 2016;83:140](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26253016))
  + Elevated CEA and identification of *KRAS / GNAS* mutations supports diagnosis of mucinous cyst

Radiology description

* CT:
  + Main duct IPMN causes distention of main pancreatic duct
  + Branch duct IPMN produces multilocular grape-like cystic appearance
* ERCP (endoscopic retrograde cholangiopancreatography): pancreatic ductal filling defects may be seen / ductal dilation
* MRCP (magnetic resonance cholangiopancreatography): additional imaging option which does not produce radiation
* EUS (endoscopic ultrasound): can also allow FNA and cyst fluid analysis
* References: [Diagn Interv Imaging 2016;97:1275](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27840080), [World J Gastroenterol 2016;22:9562](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27920477)

Prognostic factors

* Without an invasive carcinoma, has > 90% 5 year survival; those associated with an invasive carcinoma carry a worse prognosis (about half die of the disease) ([Ann Surg 2016;263:162](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25775066))
* Main duct IPMN: 60% have high grade dysplasia and 45% are associated with an invasive carcinoma ([Hum Pathol 2012;43:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21777948))
* Branch duct IPMN: most are low grade, 25% have high grade dysplasia and 20% are associated with an invasive carcinoma ([Hum Pathol 2012;43:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21777948))
* Invasive carcinoma associated with IPMN includes:
  + Tubular (ductal) adenocarcinoma: seen in about half of cases, with slightly better prognosis than non IPMN associated pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma
  + Colloid carcinoma: seen in half of cases, with much better prognosis than pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma ([Ann Surg 2016;263:162](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25775066))

Treatment

* Main duct IPMN: surgical resection indicated if main pancreatic duct > 10 mm, jaundice or presence of mural nodules ([Pancreatology 2017;17:738](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28735806))
  + Branch duct IPMN: surgical resection indicated if symptomatic, presence of mural nodule ≥ 5 mm, suspicious / positive cytology, obstructive jaundice or main pancreatic duct ≥ 10 mm ([Pancreatology 2017;17:738](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28735806))

Gross description

* Main duct involvement:
  + Usually diffusely dilated, tortuous and irregular, filled with mucin
  + Typically arises in head and progresses along path of main duct, may involve entire pancreas; may involve major or minor papillae leading to mucin extrusion from ampulla
  + Uninvolved pancreas is often pale and firm, reflecting extensive chronic obstructive pancreatitis
* Branch duct involvement:
  + Often in uncinate process
  + Forms multicystic, grape-like structures; cystically dilated ducts, filled with tenacious mucin; cyst walls are usually thin with flat or papillary lining
  + Cysts separated by normal pancreas, suggesting that cysts are separate on cut sections
* Multicentricity seen in up to 40% of cases ([Am J Gastroenterol 2007;102:1759](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17686073))
* Extensive sampling / complete submission of cyst for microscopic evaluation important to rule out an associated invasive carcinoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:480](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24451278), [Ann Surg 2016;263:162](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25775066))
* Greater than 5 mm in diameter
  + Incipient IPMN is a term that can be used for lesions 0.5 - 1.0 cm in size

Frozen section description

* Typically pancreatic resection margin status will be evaluated to exclude IPMN; additional margins will be submitted if invasive carcinoma is present or possibly for the presence of high grade dysplasia ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1730](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26559377), [Pancreatology 2017;17:738](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28735806))
* If low grade dysplasia is seen, typically no additional resection done; this may represent pancreatic intraepithelial neoplasia (PanIN) versus IPMN ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1730](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26559377), [Pancreatology 2017;17:738](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28735806))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Mucin producing epithelial cells with varied degrees of dysplasia ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1730](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26559377))
  + Low grade dysplasia is characterized by a flat epithelial lining with basal located nuclei without significant pleomorphism, while intermediate dysplasia has features between those of low and high grade dysplasia (note: low and intermediate dysplasia are now both grouped as low grade dysplasia) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1730](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26559377))
  + High grade dysplasia is characterized by complex architectural features (i.e. irregular branching, cribiforming) with loss of nuclear polarity along with increased nuclear hyperchromasia and nuclear irregularities ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1730](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26559377))
* Epithelial cells show variable differentiation and can be subclassified into: intestinal, gastric and pancreaticobiliary subtypes (oncocytic lining suggests intraductal oncocytic papillary neoplasm)
* Associated with pancreatic intraepithelial neoplasia (PanIN), chronic pancreatitis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1184](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15316318))
* No ovarian type stroma
* Assess presence or absence of invasive carcinoma (most important prognostic factor) ([Hum Pathol 2012;43:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21777948))
* Type of invasion is associated with MUC1 / MUC2 pattern; see [below](https://pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pancreasipmn.html#pancreasipmnpositivestainRB) ([Mod Pathol 2002;15:1087](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12379756))
* Gastric type IPMN:
  + Cells resemble gastric foveolae
  + Intestinal metaplasia may be seen, usually with low grade dysplasia and branch duct IPMN
  + If associated invasive carcinoma present, typically ductal (tubular) adenocarcinoma ([Ann Surg 2016;263:162](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25775066), [Virchows Arch 2005;447:794](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16088402))
* Intestinal type IPMN:
  + Cells with tall columnar epithelium (resembling intestinal villous adenomas)
  + Usually low or high grade dysplasia and main duct IPMN
  + If associated invasive carcinoma present, typically mucinous (colloid) carcinoma
* Pancreaticobiliary type IPMN:
  + Complex, thin branching papillae resembling cholangiopapillary neoplasms
  + Cuboidal cells with prominent nucleoli
  + Usually high grade dysplasia and main duct IPMN
  + If associated invasive carcinoma present, typically ductal (tubular) adenocarcinoma

Cytology description

* Cannot distinguish IPMN from mucinous cystic neoplasm on cytology (neoplastic mucinous cyst)
* Disordered mucinous epithelial clusters with nuclear overlap and variable cytologic atypia
* Distinguishing a low grade mucinous neoplasm from gastrointestinal tract contaminant (particularly gastric mucosa) can be challenging

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* *KRAS*, *GNAS* and *RNF43* mutations seen in decreasing frequency in noninvasive IPMNs and IPMNs with an associated invasive carcinoma ([J Am Coll Surg 2015;220:845](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25840541))
* *GNAS* mutations more commonly associated with IPMNs, which harbor an invasive colloid carcinoma
* *KRAS* mutations more commonly associated with IPMNs, which harbor an invasive tubular (ductal) adenocarcinoma
* With increasing grades of dysplasia, increased mutations in *KRAS*, *TP53*, *CDKN2A* (p16); also hypermethylation and reduced BRG1 protein ([Hum Pathol 2012;43:585](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21940037))
* Loss of programmed cell death 4 (PDCD4) and CD24 expression associated with tumor progression and proliferation ([Hum Pathol 2010;41:1507](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20656320), [Hum Pathol 2010;41:1466](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20619441))
* Cyst fluid shows mutations in *KRAS2* and *GNAS*

Sample pathology report

* Pancreas, small bowel and stomach, pancreaticoduodenectomy (Whipple resection):
  + Intraductal papillary mucinous neoplasm (IPMN), low grade, gastric phenotype, branch duct type, 3.0 cm (see comment)
  + Negative for high grade dysplasia or malignancy.
  + Margins are negative for IPMN.
  + 23 lymph nodes with no significant histologic abnormality.
  + Comment: The entire cyst is submitted for histologic examination.

Differential diagnosis

* [Intraductal oncocytic papillary neoplasm](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pancreasiopn.html):
  + Lined by oncocytic cells with complex growth patterns and arborizing papillae
  + Distinct molecular pathway with *ARHGAP26*, *ASXL1*, *EPHA8* and *ERBB4* genetic alterations ([Mod Pathol 2016;29:1058](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27282351))
  + *DNAJB1-PRKACA* fusions ([Mod Pathol 2020;33:648](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31676785))
  + [MUC1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)+, [MUC6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmuc2.html)+
* [Intraductal tubulopapillary neoplasm](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pancreasitpn.html):
  + Recently recognized subtype with potential origin from peribiliary cysts ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:1164](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19440145))
  + Confluent epithelial cells with tubular and papillary architectural patterns and usually high grade dysplasia
  + Necrotic foci, more solid growth without visible mucin or scanty cytoplasmic mucin, no *KRAS2* gene mutations
  + [MUC1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)+, [MUC2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmuc2.html)-, [MUC6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmuc2.html)+
* [Mucinous cystic neoplasm](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pancreasmcn.html):
  + Epithelial mucinous lining with ovarian type stroma
  + No communication to pancreatic ductal system
* [Pancreatic intraepithelial neoplasia (PanIN)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pancreaspanin.html):
  + Only detectable microscopically
  + No mass lesion, unlike IPMN
  + Almost always has gastric foveolar differentiation
* [Simple mucinous cyst](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pancreasmucnoncyst.html):
  + Cyst > 1 cm with flat (not papillary) gastric mucinous lining and no significant atypia ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1730](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26559377), [Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:121](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27740966))
* **Retention cyst**:
  + Cyst > 1 cm with flat (not papillary) ductal lining and no significant atypia in a background of obstruction ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1730](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26559377))

Board review style question #2

Which of the following clinical scenarios would most likely represent an intraductal papillary mucinous neoplasm (IPMN)?

1. 29 year old woman with 8 cm solid and cystic pancreatic tail mass
2. 45 year old woman with 3 cm cystic pancreatic tail mass
3. 58 year old man with 3 cm solid pancreatic head mass
4. 66 year old man with 2 cm cystic pancreatic head mass

Board review style answer #2

**D**. 66 year old man with 2 cm cystic pancreatic head mass. IPMNs are more often seen in older men and are cystic; thus choices A - C are incorrect (choice A would be more typical of a solid pseudopapillary neoplasm; choice B would more typical of a mucinous cystic neoplasm; choice C would be more typical of pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma or a pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor).
