Definition / general

* Benign or potentially low grade malignant cystic epithelial neoplasm composed of cells which contain intracytoplasmic mucin ([ICD-O: 8470/0 [Accessed 18 February 2021]](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=73))
* WHO classification:
  + MCN with low grade dysplasia (adenoma)
  + MCN with high grade dysplasia (carcinoma in situ)
  + MCN with invasive carcinoma

Essential features

* Cystic neoplastic lesion that is a precursor to pancreatic adenocarcinoma
* May harbor invasive carcinoma
* Presence of associated ovarian type stroma

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [C25](http://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C15-C26/C25-/C25) - Malignant neoplasm of pancreas (if malignant)
* ICD-10: [D13.6](http://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D13-/D13.6) - Benign neoplasm of pancreas (if benign)

Epidemiology

* Almost always women (> 95%); mean age of 45 years ([Ann Surg 2008;247:571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18362619))

Sites

* Distal pancreas (> 95%) ([Ann Surg 2008;247:571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18362619))
* Can also occur in the liver and gallbladder
* Metastases usually restricted to abdominal cavity; metastases to ovary may simulate primary ovarian tumors

Pathophysiology

* Ectopic ovarian stroma is thought to be seeded from primordial ovarian cells at early stages of embryonic development
* Cysts are later formed by hormones and growth factors released by the ovarian stroma ([Am J Surg Pathol 1999;23:410](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10199470))

Etiology

* No known etiology

Clinical features

* Usually a single lesion ([Gut Liver 2015;9:571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26343068))
* May present as abdominal pain or acute pancreatitis ([Ann Surg 2008;247:571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18362619))
* Majority are slow growing and asymptomatic ([Gastroenterology Res 2014;7:44](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27785269))

Diagnosis

* Cytology and lab analysis of pancreatic cyst fluid from endoscopic ultrasound guided FNA
* Histology of pancreatic resection

Laboratory

* Elevated carcinoembryonic antigen (CEA) and presence of *KRAS* mutation in cyst fluid supports a mucinous cyst (includes MCN and intraductal papillary mucinous neoplasm) ([Ann Gastroenterol;26:122](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24714589))

Radiology description

* Thick walled, single, septated cyst in the body or tail of the pancreas ([Gut Liver 2015;9:571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26343068))
* May have nodules or calcifications

Prognostic factors

* Prognosis is excellent unless there is invasive carcinoma with extracapsular or diffuse intracapsular infiltration ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:179](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25517958), [Ann Surg 2008;247:571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18362619))
* Features associated with invasive carcinoma include increased cyst size (> 5 cm), intracystic papillary nodules > 1 cm in size and elevated serum CA19-9 ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:179](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25517958))
* Less than 20% of cases have invasive carcinoma ([Gut Liver 2015;9:571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26343068), [Ann Surg 2008;247:571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18362619))

Case reports

* 38 year old woman with anaplastic carcinoma and mucinous cystic neoplasm of the pancreas during pregnancy ([World J Gastroenterol 2008;14:132](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18176976))
* 46 year old man with pancreatic mucinous cystic neoplasm with sarcomatous stroma metastasizing to liver ([World J Surg Oncol 2013;11:100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23688355))
* 52 year old Japanese woman with anaplastic carcinoma combined with mucinous cystadenocarcinoma of the pancreas ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1997;121:1104](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9341593))
* 65 year old man ([JOP 2012;13:687](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23183402))

Treatment

* Surgical resection is indicated for all MCNs ([Gut Liver 2015;9:571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26343068), [Gastroenterology Res 2014;7:44](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27785269))

Gross description

* Large (mean 10 cm)
* Typically unilocular megacysts that do not communicate with ductal system, though up to 15% communicate with main pancreatic duct ([Gut Liver 2015;9:571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26343068))
* Cyst wall is papillary, trabecular or thickened
* Has mucoid / watery cyst contents
* Must sample solid areas within the cyst

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Large cyst lined by intestinal, pseudopyloric or gastric foveolar type epithelium that often form papillae, surrounded by characteristic dense ovarian type stroma ([Gut Liver 2015;9:571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26343068))
* Epithelial lining has variable atypia (none, low grade, high grade); scattered neuroendocrine cells may be present
* Invasive adenocarcinoma may or may not be present; must sample extensively to rule out an invasive component ([Gut Liver 2015;9:571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26343068), [Am J Surg Pathol 1999;23:1320](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10555000))
* Calcifications are common
* May have mural nodules with features of giant cell tumor, malignant fibrous histiocytoma or anaplastic
  carcinoma

Cytology description

* Cyst aspirates are usually acellular with thick, gelatinous mucus
* Clusters of 3 dimensional atypical glandular cells with hyperchromasia predict at least moderate dysplasia ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2009;133:388](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19260745))

* Invasive component stains [MUC1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)
* Cyst fluid may stain positive for [Alcian blue](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalcianblue.html) and [mucicarmine](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmucins.html)

* [SF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssf1.html) in stroma ([Pathol Int 2016;66:281](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27060902))

Negative stains

* [MUC1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) (except in invasive components) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2002;26:466](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11914624), [Gut Liver 2015;9:571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26343068))
* [MUC2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmuc2.html) (except for faint staining of goblet cells)
* [DPC4 (MADH4, SMAD4)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdpc4.html) staining is lost in invasive MCNs ([Gut Liver 2015;9:571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26343068))
* [pVHL](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvhl.html) ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2015;139:24](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25549142))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* *KRAS* mutations noted in in situ or invasive areas, inactivating *SMAD4* and *TP53* mutations in more advanced MCNs ([Gut Liver 2015;9:571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26343068))
* Negative for *GNAS* mutations

Sample pathology report

* Pancreas and duodenum, Whipple resection:
  + Mucinous cystic neoplasm with low grade intraepithelial neoplasia (8.3 cm) (see comment)
  + Negative for high grade intraepithelial neoplasia or malignancy.
  + Focal background chronic pancreatitis
  + Margins of resection unremarkable.
  + Seven benign lymph nodes.
  + Comment: The gross cystic lesion was entirely submitted for microscopic analysis.
* Pancreas and duodenum, Whipple resection:
  + Focal adenocarcinoma arising from a mucinous cystic neoplasm (see synoptic report)

Differential diagnosis

* [Intraductal papillary mucinous neoplasm](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pancreasipmn.html):
  + Usually at the head of pancreas and communicates with the duct system (MCNs usually do not communicate with the main pancreatic duct)
  + IPMNs may be positive for *GNAS* mutations while MCNs will be negative ([Gut Liver 2015;9:571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26343068))
  + Both have elevated CEA and *KRAS* mutation in cyst fluid
* [Ovarian mucinous tumors](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumormucinouscarcinoma.html):
  + Similar clinical and histologic appearance
* [Pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma, large duct variant](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pancreasductal.html):
  + Will have smaller cysts, clustering of ducts and myxoid stroma ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2009;133:423](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19260748))
* [Pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pancreasductal.html):
  + Usually not cystic, shows irregular infiltrative glands with nuclear atypia, no ovarian type stroma
* [Pancreatic pseudocyst](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pancreaspseudocyst.html):
  + Mimics MCN when MCN has denuded cyst lining
  + MCN cystic fluid has high CEA content and viscosity, high expression of microRNAs, lower amylase (< 250 U/L) and elastase I than pseudocyst, although values may vary within different loculi of same neoplasm ([Am J Clin Pathol 1993;100:425](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7692722), [Ann Gastroenterol 2013;26:122](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24714589), [Gastroenterology Res 2014;7:44](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27785269))
* [Serous cystadenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pancreasserous.html):
  + Has low levels of CEA

Board review style question #1

Which of the following findings on fine needle aspiration of a pancreatic cyst are most consistent with a mucinous cystic neoplasm?

1. Decreased CEA, decreased amylase, no *KRAS* mutation, no *GNAS* mutation
2. Elevated CEA, elevated amylase, *KRAS* mutation, *GNAS* mutated
3. Elevated CEA, highly elevated amylase, no *KRAS* mutation, no *GNAS* mutation
4. Elevated CEA, variable amylase, *KRAS* mutation, no *GNAS* mutation

Board review style answer #1

**D.** Mucinous cystic neoplasms have elevated CEA, variable amylase, *KRAS* mutation and no *GNAS* mutation. If a *GNAS* mutation is present, then the findings will favor an IPMN (answer B). Decreased CEA and amylase with no *KRAS* or *GNAS* mutations favor a serous cystadenoma (answer A). An elevated CEA and amylase without *KRAS* or *GNAS* mutations will favor a pancreatic pseudocyst (answer C).
