Congenital / dysgenetic cysts

Definition / general

* By definition are intrapancreatic, do not communicate with duct system, lined by a single layer of cuboidal, columnar or flattened atrophic epithelium with a fibrous wall
* Most congenital pancreatic cysts are multiple, almost all are associated with underlying congenital diseases that primarily affect other organ systems ([AJR Am J Roentgenol 2002;179:1375](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12438020))
* Often associated with polycystic disease affecting [kidney](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumoradultpkd.html) and liver, [von Hippel-Lindau syndrome](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantrccclear.html) or oral-facial-digital syndrome type I ([GeneReviews: Oral-Facial-Digital Syndrome Type I [Accessed 11 December 2017]](http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK1188/))
* Single cysts may be due to abnormal duct development
* Other syndromes: Elejalde syndrome ([eMedicine: Elejalde Syndrome [Accessed 11 December 2017]](http://emedicine.medscape.com/article/1069594-overview)), glutaric aciduria II ([NIH: Glutaric Acidemia Type II [Accessed 11 December 2017]](https://rarediseases.info.nih.gov/diseases/6523/glutaric-acidemia-type-ii)), Ivemark syndrome ([NORD: Ivemark Syndrome [Accessed 11 December 2017]](https://rarediseases.org/rare-diseases/ivemark-syndrome/)), Jeune syndrome ([NIH: Jeune Syndrome [Accessed 11 December 2017]](https://rarediseases.info.nih.gov/diseases/3049/jeune-syndrome)), Meckel-Gruber syndrome ([eMedicine: Meckel-Gruber Syndrome [Accessed 11 December 2017]](http://emedicine.medscape.com/article/946672-overview)), short rib polydactyly syndrome type 1 ([OMIM: Short Rib Thoracic Dysplasia 3 With or Without Polydactyly [Accessed 11 December 2017]](http://omim.org/entry/613091)), trisomy 13, 14, 15, [tuberous sclerosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumorangiomyolipoma.html)

Case reports

* Multilocular cysts associated with choledochal cyst ([Hum Pathol 2003;34:99](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12605374))

Epidermoid cysts

Definition / general

* May be present within an intrapancreatic spleen ([Pancreas 2011;40:956](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21562442))
* In tail, unilocular, typically contain concentric enzymatic concretions, may communicate with acinar system
* Squamoid cyst of pancreatic ducts; has thin wall lined by transitional / squamous cells without keratinization or granular layer

Case reports

* 62 year old man with intrapancreatic spleen containing epidermoid cyst ([JOP 2011;12:279](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21546709))

Foregut cysts attached to pancreas

Definition / general

* Rare; unilocular, smooth surface with clear mucoid material, ciliated epithelium resembles bronchogenic cyst but no respiratory glands, no cartilage and two smooth muscle layers ([Am J Surg Pathol 1996;20:476](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8604815))

Case reports

* 34 year old woman with pancreatic mass ([JSLS 2008;12:183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18435895))
* 37 year old woman with acute cholangitis ([JOP 2011;12:420](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21737908))

Nonepithelial cysts

Definition / general

* Includes endometrial cyst, lymphangioma, [pseudocysts](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pancreaspseudocyst.html), parasitic cyst

Pancreatic tissue derived cysts

Definition / general

* May appear in thorax or mediastinum, as components of gastroenteric duplication cysts, intralobar pulmonary sequestrations, teratomas or rarely from ectopic pancreatic tissue ([Mod Pathol 1996;9:210](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8685216))
* Paraduodenal wall cyst (cystic dystrophy): patients with paraduodenal (groove) pancreatitis, cysts may enlarge and adhere to duodenal wall, mimicking duodenal duplication; lined by granulation tissue, may be partly lined by ductal epithelium ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2004;21:247](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16273943))
