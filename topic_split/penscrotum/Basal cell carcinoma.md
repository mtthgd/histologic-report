Definition / general

* Similar to counterpart in skin (see [skin - nonmelanocytic tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticbcc.html) chapter)
* **ICD-0:** [8090 / 3](http://codes.iarc.fr/code/4325)

Epidemiology

* Very rare

Sites

* Most cases arise in skin of shaft, may be multicentric

Etiology

* No evidence of HPV infection
* Typically develops on sun exposed skin and is closely associated with ultraviolet radiation

Clinical features

* Usually ages 40+ years ([J Urol 1994;152:1557](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7933201))
* Slowly growing hyperpigmented tumor
* Risk factors include ultraviolet and ionizing radiation, arsenic ingestion, immunosuppression and inherited syndromes, such as nevoid basal cell carcinoma syndrome and xeroderma pigmentosum
* Extremely low metastatic potential ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2001;45:68](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11423837))

Prognostic factors

* Multicentric and large tumors may present more aggressive behavior

Case reports

* 75 year old man with multicentric tumor and skin metastases ([Scand J Plast Reconstr Surg Hand Surg 2002;36:180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12141208))

Treatment

* Excision

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Regular neoplastic nests with evident peripheral palisading
* No areas of central comedonecrosis
* Frequent myxoid stromal changes

Differential diagnosis

* [Basaloid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/penscrotumHPVassociatedSCC.html): involves glans not shaft, more pleomorphic neoplastic cells, frequent central comedonecrosis and absence of peripheral palisading
