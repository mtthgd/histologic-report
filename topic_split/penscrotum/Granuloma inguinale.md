Definition / general

* Initially described in India by McLeod (1882) and Donovan (1905)
* Sexually transmitted disease caused by *Klebsiella granulomatis*, formerly *Calymmatobacterium granulomatis*, a gram negative rod
* Initially a small painful nodule at infection site that ulcerates; may have satellite lesions

Terminology

* Also called donovanosis

Epidemiology

* Rare in U.S. (100 cases/year)
* More common in African Americans, in individuals with a lower socioeconomic status and among those untrained in hygiene
* Endemic in tropical and subtropical climates such as Papua New Guinea, parts of South Africa, parts of India, Indonesia and Australian aborigines ([Braz J Infect Dis 2008;12:521](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19287842))

Sites

* Can affect foreskin, glans, penile shaft or scrotum

Etiology

* Caused by *Klebsiella granulomatis*, formerly *Calymmatobacterium granulomatis*, a gram negative rod ([Int J Syst Bacteriol 1999;49:1695](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10555350))

Case reports

* 21 year old HIV+ man with coexisting squamous cell carcinoma ([Dermatol Online J 2008;14:8](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19061590))
* 48 year old man ([Dermatol Online J 2006;12:14](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17459300), [free full text](https://escholarship.org/uc/item/87q57161))

Treatment

* Three weeks of treatment with erythromycin, streptomycin or tetracycline or 12 weeks of treatment with ampicillin
* Usually clinical improvement within 1 week

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Massive plasma cell infiltrate without lymphocytes in granulation tissue
* Diffuse infiltration by neutrophils forming microabscesses
* Large mononuclear cells (also called Pund cells) with Donovan bodies (large intracytoplasmic encapsulated bipolar bodies, highlighted with Warthin-Starry or Wright-Giemsa stain)

Positive stains

* [Wright-Giemsa](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswright.html) or [Warthin-Starry](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswarthin.html) stains show Donovan bodies in tissue sample

Electron microscopy description

* Bacteria residing inside phagosomes of macrophages

Differential diagnosis

* [Chancroid](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/penscrotumchancroid.html)
* [Herpes simplex virus](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/penscrotumHSV.html)
* [Lymphogranuloma venereum](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/penscrotumLGV.html)
* [Syphilis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/penscrotumsyphilis.html)
