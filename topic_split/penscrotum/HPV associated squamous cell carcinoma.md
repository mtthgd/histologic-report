Basaloid carcinoma

Definition / general

* Squamous cell carcinoma composed of uniform, small to intermediate cells in solid sheets or nests and often with central comedo-like necrosis

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8083/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf#page=64) - basaloid squamous cell carcinoma

Epidemiology

* 4 - 10% of all penile carcinomas ([Anal Quant Cytol Histol 2007;29:185](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17879626))
* Median age 52 years (range 33 - 84 years)

Sites

* Glans is the preferred site but extension to coronal sulcus and inner foreskin is common

Etiology

* HPV detected in 71 - 81% of all cases (HPV 16 in 81% of all positive tumors) ([Am J Pathol 2001;159:1211](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11583947))

Clinical features

* Aggressive, high grade and deeply invasive penile tumor
* Inguinal nodal metastases in 50 - 100% and local recurrence in 36% ([J Urol 2006;176:1431](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16952652))
* High mortality rate (21 - 67%)

Prognostic factors

* Regional metastasis and mortality associated with tumor thickness > 10 mm and infiltration of corpus cavernosum ([Am J Surg Pathol 1998;22:755](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9630184))

Treatment

* May require total penectomy, bilateral groin dissection and adjuvant chemotherapy from the onset ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:1299](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19471153))

Gross description

* Flat, ulcerated, irregular mass with solid tan tissue replacing corpus spongiosum and invading tunica albuginea and corpus cavernosa
* Mean tumor size of 3 - 4.5 cm

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Closely attached nests of tumor cells, often with central comedo-like necrosis
* Vertical growth pattern is typical
* Composed of small to intermediate basophilic cells with scant cytoplasm, indistinctive cell borders and high mitotic / apoptotic rate
* Occasionally peripheral palisading and focal central abrupt keratinization
* May have peripheral clefts due to retraction artifact
* Frequently associated with basaloid or warty penile intraepithelial neoplasia ([Int J Surg Pathol 2004;12:351](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15494861))
* Prominent perineural and vascular invasion

Positive stains

* [Cytokeratin 34bE12](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck34be12.html)
* Also [p16 INK4a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp16.html), [topoisomerase II alpha](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstop2a.html) ([BJU Int 2008;102:1040](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18489530))

Differential diagnosis

* [Basal cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/penscrotumbcc.html):
  + Neoplastic cells have less atypia, tumor occurs usually in skin of shaft, nests have prominent peripheral palisading and characteristic myxoid stromal changes
* [Neuroendocrine carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostateneuroendo.html):
  + Similar morphology
  + Immunohistochemistry for neuroendocrine differentiation may be useful
* [Poorly differentiated usual squamous cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/penscrotumSCC.html):
  + More irregular nests
  + Neoplastic cells with eosinophilic cytoplasm and distinct cellular boundaries
  + Gradual (not abrupt) keratinization and clear tendency towards squamous differentiation
* [Urothelial carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderurothelialinvasivegen.html):
  + More pleomorphism, features of urothelial differentiation usually evident in the invasive or in situ component
  + Positive for [uroplakin III](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsuroplakiniii.html) and [thrombomodulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsthrombomodulin.html)

Clear cell carcinoma

Definition / general

* Recently described aggressive variant of penile carcinoma

Epidemiology

* Middle aged men

Sites

* Foreskin inner mucosa

Etiology

* Most likely of sweat gland origin
* HPV 16 DNA found in all reported cases

Clinical features

* All reported patients presented with inguinal nodal metastases with clear cells and sclerotic basement membrane material

Case reports

* Series of 5 cases of middle aged men is only report to date ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1513](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15489656))

Treatment

* Excision with wide surgical margin

Gross description

* Large, exophytic and partially ulcerated
* Size 2.5 - 5.5 cm

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Solid proliferation of large neoplastic clear cells
* Marked nuclear atypia
* Extensive geographical necrosis
* Angiolymphatic invasion is common
* Warty PeIN may be found at surface

Positive stains

* [MUC1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmucins.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html), [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html) (most cells) and [pancytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html)
* [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html)+ and [d-PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html)+ intracytoplasmic material

Differential diagnosis

* [Clear cell urothelial carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladderclearcell.html):
  + Previous / concurrent history of urothelial carcinoma elsewhere ([Int J Urol 2003;10:348](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12757608))
  + Preferential central location, concurrent areas of urothelial carcinoma in situ
  + Positive for urothelial markers ([uroplakin III](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsuroplakiniii.html), [thrombomodulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsthrombomodulin.html))
* [Metastatic](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/penscrotummetastases.html) [renal cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantrcc.html):
  + Concurrent / previous history of renal carcinoma, deep seated, shaft location
  + Positive for [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html), [RCC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsrcc.html), [PAX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax2.html), [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html) and [CA9](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscarbonicanhydraseix.html) ([Actas Urol Esp 2005;29:593](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16092684))
* [Sebaceous carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticsebaceouscarcinoma.html):
  + Foreskin outer skin, smaller nuclei
  + [HPV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshpv.html)-, [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html)- ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2007;131:1655](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17979483))
* [Warty carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/penscrotumHPVassociatedSCC.html#wartycarcinoma):
  + Less aggressive behavior, prominent papillae with condylomatous feature, conspicuous koilocytosis, necrosis uncommon
  + [MUC1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmucins.html)-
* [Well differentiated squamous cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/penscrotumcarcinomagen.html) with glycogenated (clear) cells:
  + Clear glycogenated cells are usually focal and not prominent, retained squamous maturation, pyknotic nuclei, superficially located
  + [MUC1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmucins.html)-

Warty carcinoma

Definition / general

* HPV related low grade verruciform tumor, identical to vulvar, [cervical](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixscc.html) or anal counterparts ([J Urol 2009;182:528](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19524964))

Terminology

* Also called condylomatous carcinoma

Epidemiology

* 7 - 10% of penile carcinomas, 34 - 36% of verruciform carcinomas ([Anal Quant Cytol Histol 2007;29:185](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17879626))
* Mean age 48 - 55 years

Sites

* Affected sites include glans, foreskin and coronal sulcus
* Usually affects multiple anatomical compartments
* Tends to multicentricity

Clinical features

* Slow growing
* Lymph node metastasis in 17 - 18% of cases; associated with deep invasion
* Intermediate behavior between low grade verrucous or papillary carcinomas and usual squamous cell carcinomas of penis
* May recur due to inadequate excision or multicentric disease not identified at time of surgery
* Low mortality rate (0 - 9%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2001;25:673](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11342782), [Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:505](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10757397))

Prognostic factors

* Poor: invasion of corpora cavernosa; high grade areas; presence of vascular / perineural invasion

Treatment

* Partial or total penectomy; circumcision
* Groin dissection according to risk group stratification

Gross description

* Typical lesion is exophytic mass arising from glans; also coronal sulcus or foreskin
* Verruciform, white-tan, cauliflower-like and up to 5 cm
* May have cobblestone surface
* Endophytic cut surface
* May penetrate deep into corpus spongiosum or corpora cavernosa with broad or irregular contours

Exophytic neoplasm

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Low grade verruciform tumor with acanthosis, hyperkeratosis and parakeratosis
* Identical to warty carcinomas of vulva, uterine cervix or anus
* Arborescent papillary pattern with long, rounded or spiky papillae with prominent fibrovascular cores
* Conspicuous koilocytosis (increased nuclear size with hyperchromasia, wrinkling and bi or multinucleation, perinuclear halos and individual cell necrosis) throughout entire tumor (not just surface)
* May have intraepithelial abscesses
* Early: sharply delineated interface between tumor and stroma with no invasion (noninvasive warty carcinoma)
* Later: jagged boundary between tumor and stroma (invasive warty carcinoma)

Positive stains

* [HPV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshpv.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* 22 - 100% are associated with HPV, usually HPV 16 ([Am J Pathol 2001;159:1211](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11583947))

Differential diagnosis

* [Carcinoma cuniculatum](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/penscrotumscccuniculatum.html):
  + Deep tumoral invaginations forming irregular, narrow and elongated neoplastic sinus tracts connecting surface to deep anatomic structures
* [Giant condyloma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/penscrotumgiantcondy.html):
  + Benign, HPV changes only in superficial layers, no pleomorphism and no invasion
* [Papillary carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/penscrotumpenispap.html):
  + No HPV changes, irregular fibrovascular cores with complex papillae and invasive jagged border
  + More likely to have inguinal metastases
* [Verrucous carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/penscrotumpenisverr.html):
  + No HPV changes
  + Inconspicuous fibrovascular cores
  + Broad based invasive front
  + No regional or distant metastases
* [Warty basaloid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/penscrotumHPVassociatedSCC.html#basaloidcarcinoma):
  + Warty carcinoma mixed with basaloid squamous cell carcinoma
  + Basaloid cells present in bottom layers of papillae or in deeply infiltrative nests
  + More aggressive than pure warty carcinoma
