Definition / general

* Benign myointimal proliferation with predilection to the corpus spongiosum of the glans penis

Essential features

* Benign mesenchymal neoplasm in the corpus spongiosum of the glans penis
* Intravascular proliferation of the vascular intimal cells
* Lesional cells stain with α smooth muscle actin but not desmin

Terminology

* Penile myointimoma
* Myointimoma of the penis

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9137/0](https://www.kegg.jp/kegg-bin/get_htext?htext=br08420.keg&query=%22;%22#A29) - myointimoma

Epidemiology

* Rare tumor with ~28 cases reported in the literature ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:1524](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11075854), [Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1622](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17895766), [Int J Surg Pathol 2022 Aug 9 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35946081))
* Previously described as intravascular leiomyoma, leiomyomatosis, late stage intravascular fasciitis, leiomyoma of the glans penis, solitary cutaneous myofibroma of the glans penis ([Cancer 1970;25:1431](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4316326), [J Urol 2000;164:791](https://doi.org/10.1016/S0022-5347%2805%2967314-4), [Am J Dermatopathol 1996;18:317](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8806969))
* Wide age range (2 - 74 years old) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:1524](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11075854), [Actas Dermosifiliogr 2009;100:511](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19709559))

Sites

* Corpus spongiosum of the glans penis

Pathophysiology

* Intravascular proliferation from the intimal cells of the inner layer of the corpus spongiosum vasculature in the glans penis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:1524](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11075854))

Etiology

* Mesenchymal tumor unrelated to the status of circumcision, history of trauma or the presence of disease ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:1524](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11075854), [Actas Dermosifiliogr 2009;100:511](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19709559))

Clinical features

* Small, distinct, nonmobile, firm, painless nodule on the glans penis ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2005;53:1084](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16310076))
* Benign, rapidly growing lesions that remain stable for years, even after incomplete excision ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:1524](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11075854))

Diagnosis

* Laboratory tests, including urine analysis and ultrasonic evaluation of the abdomen and the scrotum to exclude other potential causes ([Pathol Int 2007;57:158](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17295649), [J Pediatr Surg Case Rep 2019;44:101189](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.epsc.2019.101189))
* Punch, incisional or excisional biopsy ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:1524](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11075854))

Prognostic factors

* Benign outcome with no recurrence; spontaneous regression may occur ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:1524](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11075854), [Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1622](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17895766), [Int J Surg Pathol 2022 Aug 9 [Epub ahead of print](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35946081))

Case reports

* 14 year old boy with a 1 cm nodule on the right side of his glans penis ([J Cutan Pathol 2009;36:817](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19519617))
* 49 year old man with a 12 month history of a palpable lesion involving the glans penis ([Int J Impot Res 2021;33:583](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32488208))
* 50 year old man with a nodule located in the glans penis ([Pathol Int 2007;57:158](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17295649))

Treatment

* Simple excision

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Multinodular or plexiform pattern; composed of occlusive intravascular myointimal proliferation ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:1524](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11075854))
* Nodules contain spindle or stellate shaped cells embedded in abundant fibromyxoid matrix or sometimes chondroid matrix ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1622](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17895766))
* Cells have long eosinophilic cytoplasmic processes, blunt ended nuclei, fine chromatin and juxtanuclear vacuoles
* Foci of degenerative changes appear as ghost cells ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:1524](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11075854))
* No cytologic atypia, nuclear pleomorphism, prominent nucleoli or mitoses
* Residual smooth muscle bundles surrounding the tumor ([Actas Dermosifiliogr 2009;100:511](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19709559))
* Overlying skin may show slight hyperkeratosis ([J Cutan Pathol 2009;36:817](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19519617))
* No necrosis or significant inflammation

Sample pathology report

* Glans penis, excisional biopsy:
  + Penile myointimoma (see comment)
  + Comment: Sections show a plexiform vascular proliferation composed of spindle cells in a fibromyxoid background with occluded lumens. The neoplastic cells are α smooth muscle actin positive and desmin negative.

Differential diagnosis

* [Plexiform fibrohistiocytic tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueplexiformfh.html):
  + Not intravascular
  + Common in extremities, trunk, head and neck
  + Dimorphic population of cells:
    - Fascicles of spindle cells with no [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) reactive collarettes of residual native smooth muscles, which are seen in myointimoma
    - Nodules of histiocytes, including osteoclast-like giant cells ([CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html)+ and [CD163](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd160.html#cd163274983)+)
* [Epithelioid hemangioendothelioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuehemangioendotheliomaepithelioid.html):
  + Neoplastic cells are more epithelioid with intraluminal vacuoles instead of intracytoplasmic juxtanuclear vacuoles
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)+, [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html)+ and [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html)-
* [Myofibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuemyopericytoma.html):
  + Biphasic pattern of myoid nodules similar to myointimoma; however, there are hemangiopericytoma-like areas
  + Can involve vessels but is not limited to intravascular lumina
* **Intravascular fasciitis**:
  + May overlap
  + Intralesional inflammatory cells, mucoid pools
* **Nerve sheath tumor**:
  + Diffuse nuclear [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)
* [Leiomyoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueleiomyoma.html):
  + Not intravascular
  + Fascicular architecture not plexiform or multinodular pattern
  + Fibromyxoid stroma is not a typical feature
  + [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html)+ and [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html)+
