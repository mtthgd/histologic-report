Definition / general

* Heterotopic bone in penis ([Eur Urol 1984;10:420](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6529961)), most commonly in elderly and also children
* **Note:** male mammals except chimpanzees and humans have an intrapenile bone (bacula); humans have an equivalent strong distal ligament composed of type I collagen in the glans ([J Androl 2005;26:624](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16088040))

Terminology

* Also called baculum, penis / penile bone

Etiology

* Penile ossification may occur after trauma ([Urology 1990;35:349](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2108520))
* Also associated with diabetes, gout, venereal disease, Peyronie disease and neoplasia
* Rarely congenital ([J Urol 1964;91:663](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14172255))
