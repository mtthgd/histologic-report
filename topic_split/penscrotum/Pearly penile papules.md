Definition / general

* Benign condition of 20 - 30% of normal young and middle aged males, asymptomatic

Terminology

* Also called hirsutoid papillomas, papillomatosis of glans corona

Epidemiology

* Prevalence of 38% in men < 25 years old and 11% in men > 50 years old ([Int J STD AIDS 2009;20:768](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19833692))

Etiology

* Hyperplastic, reactive
* Not related to HPV ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2003;49:50](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12833007))

Clinical features

* Multiple pearly gray-white fibroepithelial papillomas, 1 - 2 mm and in dorsal glans corona
* Usually arranged in 2 - 3 rows
* Rarely covers most of glans
* Reduced prevalence in circumcised men
* Has been confused with Tyson glands, which don't exist in humans ([Wikipedia: Preputial Gland [Accessed 2 April 2018]](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Preputial_gland))

Treatment

* None required; disappears with age; CO2 laser if patients request removal ([Dermatol Surg 2002;28:617](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12135521))
* Also cryotherapy, electrodesiccation, podophyllin or curettage

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Hyperkeratosis associated with a fibrovascular stroma, simulating an angiofibroma
* No koilocytosis, no significant inflammation

Differential diagnosis

* [Condyloma acuminatum](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/penscrotumcondyloma.html): nonuniform, not arranged in rows and has HPV related changes
* [Syphilis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/penscrotumsyphilis.html): ulcer with indurated and punched out base, marked plasmacytic inflammation and serologic evidence of syphilis
