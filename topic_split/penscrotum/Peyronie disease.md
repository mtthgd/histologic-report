Definition / general

* Fibrous thickening of dermis and Buck’s fascia between corpora cavernosa and tunica albuginea, causing curvature towards side of lesion and restricting movement of these structures during erection

Essential features

* Proliferation of fibroblasts and myofibroblasts on or around the tunica albuginea of the penis, most often the dorsal aspect, that may lead to plaques with curvature of the penis towards the plaque

Terminology

* Induratio penis plastica, penile fibromatosis

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [N48.6](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/N00-N99/N40-N53/N48-/N48.6) - Induration penis plastica
* ICD-11: [GB06.2](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f339086298) - Penile fibromatosis

Epidemiology

* Usually 40+ years, rare in younger population ([J Androl 2003;24:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12514077))
* Prevalence 3 - 9% ([Int J Impot Res 2002;14:379](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12454689))

Sites

* Tunica albuginea, most commonly the dorsal aspect

Etiology

* May be related to microtrauma (coital, urethral instrumentation), which releases fibroblast related cytokines
* May arise secondary to urethritis as a sclerosing inflammatory process
* Etiology may be related to Parc protein ([BJU Int 2010;106:1706](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21078039)) or Wnt2 ([J Sex Med 2012;9:1430](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22489561))

Clinical features

* May be associated with carcinoid syndrome, Dupuytren contracture
* May be associated with beta blockers, thiazides ([J Sex Med 2010;7:1529](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19912489)), hypertension, diabetes and immune reactions
* Fibrosis produces an abnormal curvature of the penis, which may cause pain during erection and intercourse
* In some cases, nodules adherent to tunica albuginea are noted over the mid dorsal line
* May be clinically mimicked by epithelioid sarcoma ([Int J Impot Res 2003;15:378](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14562141))

Diagnosis

* Usually clinical, however, occasionally may be a histologic confirmation

Radiology description

* Ultrasound
  + Echogenic plaques, frequently calcifications
* MRI
  + Thickened and hypointense plaques on T1 and T2 in and around tunica albuginea
  + T2 is superior ([Radiographics 2009;29:477](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19325060))

Case reports

* 59 year old man with penile ossification ([Sao Paulo Med J 2007;125:124](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17625713))

Treatment

* May need surgical treatment ([Adv Urol 2008;2008:263450](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19009027), [Eur Urol 2009;55:1469](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19084325), [Curr Urol Rep 2011;12:444](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21818660))
* Some nonsurgical options may stabilize disease ([Asian J Androl 2008;10:79](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18087647))
* Can respond to laser therapy and small amounts of irradiation and injected steroids ([J Androl 2009;30:397](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18974422), [J Androl 2010;31:445](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19684339)) and interferon
* Prosthesis may be useful ([Adv Urol 2008;2008:646052](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19081839))
* May spontaneously regress ([J Androl 2009;30:397](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18974422))

Tunica albuginea

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Dense fibrous nodules, similar to those in Dupuytren contracture, fibromatosis and other desmoplastic conditions involving myofibroblasts
* More dense and less cellular than other types of superficial fibromatosis
* Disorganization of collagen of tunica albuginea with formation of nodules, often hyalinizing fibrosis
* Perivascular lymphoid infiltrate in early stages of disease in 1/3
* Fibrotic tunica albuginea with extension of fibrosis to corpus cavernosum
* Abnormal vessels with venous leakages
* Calcification or ossification may occur, linear band of calcification in 1/4 ([J Urol 1997;157:282](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8976280))

Negative stains

* [Keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [EMA,](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)

Electron microscopy description

* Penile plaques are composed of collagen fibrils, amorphous particulate material and fibroblasts ([Int J Urol 1997;4:274](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9255666))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* No somatic mutations of beta catenin genes unlike desmoid fibromatosis ([Mod Pathol 2001;14:695](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11455002))

Differential diagnosis

* **Spindle cell sarcomas** (i.e. [synovial sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesynovialsarc.html), [malignant peripheral nerve sheath tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuempnst.html), etc.)
* [Epithelioid hemangioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueepithelioidhemangioma.html) ([J Med Case Rep 2011;5:260](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21718503))
* [Epithelioid sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueepithelioidsarcoma.html): may clinically appear similar ([Int J Impot Res 2003;15:378](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14562141))
