Definition / general

* Most placental pathologic findings can be organized into 1 of 4 major categories of placental injury ([Mod Pathol 2021;34:1074](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33558658))
* This framework is used by the 2016 Amsterdam Consensus Classification ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2016;140:698](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27223167))
* These categories are also used as top line diagnoses in pathology reporting, with supporting histopathologic findings listed underneath (see [Sample pathology report](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/placenta4categories.html#placenta4categoriessamplepathreport))
* Existing placenta articles in this chapter are organized below within the 4 category framework

Sample pathology report

* Placenta:
  + Relatively small, mature placenta (285 g, less than third percentile for 38 weeks)
  + Findings consistent with maternal vascular malperfusion:
    - Accelerated villous maturation
    - Increased syncytial knots and perivillous fibrin
    - Multifocal villous infarction
