# Chronic histiocytic intervillositis

Chronic histiocytic intervillositis

Definition / general

* Diffuse histiocytic infiltrate in intervillous space
* Associated with perinatal mortality of 80%, making it an important (although uncommon) cause of recurrent spontaneous abortion ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1993;117:1032](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8215826), [Hum Pathol 2000;31:1389](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11112214))
* Maternal risk factors: diabetes, hypertension, intravenous drug abuse, preeclampsia and systemic lupus erythematosus; may have immunologic origin (IgM and complement deposits are seen in vascular lesions)
* High recurrence rate (67%)
* Associated with recurrent abortion ([Hum Pathol 1995;26:1245](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7590700)), growth restriction ([Am J Surg Pathol 1998;22:1006](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9706981), [Hum Pathol 2001;32:1022](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11567235)) and fetal death

Gross description

* Small placenta

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Diffuse infiltrate of histiocytes within intervillous space with villous fibrinoid deposits
* Other associated lesions may include atherosis, acute chorioamnionitis and rarely chronic villitis
* Malaria: associated with abundant fibrin, black pigment, occasional neutrophils and parasite laden red blood cells

Positive stains

* [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html) (histiocytic marker)

Negative stains

* [hCG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshcg.html) (may be present in syncytiotrophoblasts but marked reduction compared to usual)
