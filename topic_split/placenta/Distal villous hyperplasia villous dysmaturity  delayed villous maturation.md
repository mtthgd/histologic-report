Definition / general

* Increased presence of immature villi (at least 10 villi) with decreased vasculosyncytial membranes and centrally placed capillaries comprising at least 30% of 1 full thickness parenchymal section

Essential features

* Delayed villous maturation is defined as the increased presence of immature villi for gestational age (at least 10 villi), comprising at least 30% of 1 full thickness parenchymal section
* Associated with maternal diabetes / prediabetes, obesity, fetal vascular malperfusion and chromosomal abnormalities
* Diagnosis is not recommended prior to 34 weeks gestation

Terminology

* Distal villous immaturity, villous dysmaturity, villous maturation defect

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [O43.89](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/O00-O9A/O30-O48/O43-/O43.89) - other placental disorders

Epidemiology

* Occurs in 1 - 6% of pregnancies
* Maternal diabetes or prediabetes, obesity and excessive weight gain in pregnancy ([Placenta 2003;24:819](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/13129678))
* Fetal vascular malperfusion
* Chromosomal abnormalities ([J Clin Ultrasound 2024 Apr 8 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38587238))
* Opioid maintenance therapy (buprenorphine or methadone) ([Am J Obstet Gynecol 2017;216:418.e1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28024988), [J Pregnancy 2021;2021:2175026](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34659831))

Sites

* Placenta

Pathophysiology

* Exact mechanism is unclear; hypotheses include
  + Excessive maternal glucose leads to increased fetal insulin, which promotes excessive placental growth at the expense of villous maturation
  + Alterations in fetal blood flow causes stromal vascular remodeling
  + Underlying genetic abnormalities resulting in abnormal villous development
* References: [Diagn Histopathol 2012;18:189](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.mpdhp.2012.02.002), [Heerema-McKenney: Diagnostic Pathology - Placenta, 2nd Edition, 2018](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0323609716/pathologyoutl-20)

Etiology

* Associated with biochemical abnormalities (maternal diabetes, obesity and excessive weight gain in pregnancy) and circulatory abnormalities (fetal vascular malperfusion), opioid maintenance therapy (buprenorphine or methadone) and chromosomal abnormalities

Clinical features

* May be associated with altered fetal growth; either high or low fetal weight for gestational age
* Associated with increased perinatal mortality ([Pediatr Dev Pathol 2011;14:273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21329421))

Diagnosis

* Diagnosed by microscopic examination

Radiology description

* No specific radiologic findings

Prognostic factors

* Associated with increased perinatal mortality ([Pediatr Dev Pathol 2011;14:273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21329421))
* Increased recurrence risk of fetal death in subsequent pregnancies ([Obstet Gynecol 2001;97:505](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11275018))

Case reports

* Male fetus delivered at 34 weeks gestation with neonatal onset glutaric acidemia type II ([Pediatr Dev Pathol 2002;5:315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12007026))
* Term male neonate exposed to methadone maintenance therapy in utero ([Pediatr Dev Pathol 2021;24:56](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32970505))
* 30 year old pregnant woman with COVID-19; placenta showing signs of delayed maturation ([Medicina (Kaunas) 2020;56:306](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32580461))
* 35 year old pregnant woman with primary syphilis; placenta showing delayed villous maturation ([Microorganisms 2022;10:1497](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35893555))

Gross description

* Placenta may be large for gestational age or pale in appearance
* Decreased fetoplacental weight ratio

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Difficult to diagnose prior to 34 weeks gestation
* Increased presence of immature villi (at least 10 villi), involving at least 30% of 1 full thickness parenchymal section
* Distal villi with increased diameter for gestational age
* Continuous cytotrophoblast layer and decreased syncytial knots
* Variable capillary density with centrally placed capillaries and poor vasculosyncytial membrane formation
* Increased stromal cellularity
* Associated features may include increased nucleated fetal red blood cells, other villous vascular lesions such as chorangiosis, chorangiomatosis and avascular villi
* Grading schema proposed in Amsterdam Placental Workshop Consensus Statement ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2016;140:698](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27223167))
  + Focal: lesion in 1 full thickness parenchymal section only
  + Diffuse: lesion present in 2 or more full thickness parenchymal sections

Positive stains

* [CD15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd15.html) staining has been reported in immature endothelium, indicating villous immaturity ([Eur J Obstet Gynecol Reprod Biol 2014;180:172](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25043745), [J Perinat Med 2020;48:516](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32396141))

Sample pathology report

* Placenta, vaginal delivery:
  + Third trimester placenta (450 grams) with delayed villous maturation
  + Fetal membranes, free of inflammation
  + 3 vessel umbilical cord, free of inflammation

Differential diagnosis

* [Villous edema](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/placentaplaedema.html):
  + Increased villous diameter due to increased stromal fluid
  + Predominantly affects basal villi
  + No increase in stromal cellularity
  + Appropriate vasculosyncytial membrane formation
* [Chorangiosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/placentachorangiosis.html):
  + Increased villous diameter due to increased capillary density
  + Appropriate vasculosyncytial membrane formation
* **Age appropriate villous immaturity**:
  + Requires clinical correlation for accurate gestational dating

Which of the following conditions is associated with the depicted placental finding seen at 39 weeks gestation?

1. Chorioamnionitis
2. Gestational diabetes
3. Meconium stained fluid
4. Placenta accreta
5. Preeclampsia

Board review style answer #1

**B**. Gestational diabetes. The image depicts delayed villous maturation with larger villous diameter for gestational age, centrally placed capillaries, continuous cytotrophoblast layer and poor vasculosyncytial membrane formation. Delayed villous maturation is associated with gestational diabetes. Answer E is incorrect because accelerated villous maturation is more often seen in preeclampsia versus delayed villous maturation. Answers A, C and D are incorrect because chorioamnionitis, meconium staining and placenta accreta are not associated with delayed villous maturation.

Board review style question #2

Which of the following microscopic findings is a feature of delayed villous maturation?

1. Decidual hemorrhage
2. Decreased villous diameter for gestational age
3. Distal villous hypoplasia
4. Increased syncytial knots
5. Poor vasculosyncytial membrane formation

Board review style answer #2

**E**. Poor vasculosyncytial membrane formation. Histologic features of delayed villous maturation includes increased villous diameter for gestational age, centrally placed capillaries, a continuous cytotrophoblast layer, decreased syncytial knots and poor vasculosyncytial membrane formation. Answers B, C and D are incorrect because decreased villous diameter for gestational age, distal villous hypoplasia and increased syncytial knots are features of accelerated villous maturation. Answer A is incorrect because decidual hemorrhage is a finding that can be seen in placental abruption.
