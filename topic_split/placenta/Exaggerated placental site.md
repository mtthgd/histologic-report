Definition / general

* Exuberant infiltration of the endometrium and myometrium by implantation site intermediate trophoblast

Essential features

* Exaggeration of normal physiologic process, with infiltration of the endometrium and superficial third of myometrium by implantation site intermediate trophoblast
* Can be associated with normal pregnancy or abortions, molar pregnancy and sometimes presents as postpartum bleeding
* Ki67 labeling index is near 0%
* Ki67 labeling index can be 0 - 5 % in exaggerated placental site (EPS) of molar pregnancies ([Hum Pathol 1998;29:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9445130))

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [O43.899](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/O00-O9A/O30-O48/O43-/O43.899) - other placental disorders, unspecified trimester

Epidemiology

* Incidence is 1.6% of first trimester spontaneous and elective abortions ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2014;33:339](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24901393%20), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2001;20:31](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11192071))

Sites

* Uterus

Pathophysiology

* Exaggeration of normal physiologic process
* Intermediate trophoblasts (IT) invade through endometrium into the superficial third of myometrium ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2001;20:31](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11192071))
* Normal structure of endometrial glands, myometrium and vessels is usually maintained
* Ki67 indices of near zero indicate increased number of intermediate trophoblasts in EPS is not a result of de novo proliferation of IT in the implantation site ([Hum Pathol 1998;29:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9445130))
* Increased Ki67 in molar pregnancy may be a spillover effect IT in the trophoblastic columns of hydatiform moles ([Hum Pathol 1998;29:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9445130))
* Although histologically the same, increased Ki67 labelling index in complete molar implantation site represents related but different lesions ([Hum Pathol 1998;29:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9445130))

Clinical features

* Can occur in normal pregnancy or following an abortion ([J Obstet Gynaecol Res 2008;34:609](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18840164))
* Causes postpartum bleeding, uterine atony ([J Reprod Med 2013;58:448](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24050037), [Tohoku J Exp Med 2014;234:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25186195))

Diagnosis

* Histologic examination

Radiology description

* May present as echogenic lesion in the uterine cavity ([J Obstet Gynaecol Res 2008;34:609](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18840164))

Prognostic factors

* Excellent prognosis following curettage

Case reports

* 26 year old woman presented with complete mole and coexistent exaggerated placental site reaction ([Niger Med J 2014;55:180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24791057))
* 34 year old woman primigravida with intrauterine fetal demise and suspected placenta previa ([Taiwan J Obstet Gynecol 2012;51:440](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23040933))
* 35 year old woman with intractable massive postpartum hemorrhage after removal of retained placenta ([Tohoku J Exp Med 2014;234:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25186195))
* 39 year old woman with an echogenic mass in uterine cavity following elective abortion ([J Obstet Gynaecol Res 2008;34:609](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18840164))

Treatment

* Curettage is curative; no follow up is required ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2001;20:31](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11192071))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Extensive infiltration of endometrium and myometrium by implantation site intermediate trophoblasts which are often multinucleate ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2001;20:31](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11192071))
* Endometrial glands and spiral arterioles can show invasion by trophoblasts
* Smooth muscle cells of myometrium are separated by cords, nests and individual trophoblastic cells
* Despite diffuse infiltration, there is not necrosis or mitoses
* Trophoblastic cells share similar morphologic features of normal implantation site intermediate trophoblasts; these cells contain abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm with hyperchromatic and irregular nuclei ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2001;20:31](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11192071))
* Chorionic villi are morphologically unremarkable

Exaggertated placental site

Positive stains

* [HPL](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshpl.html) (diffuse), [MUC4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmuc2.html), [CK18](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck18.html), **HLA-G** ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2014;33:339](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24901393%20))
* [MEL-CAM / CD146](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd140.html), [inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html), [PLAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsplap.html) (weak) ([Hum Pathol 1998;29:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9445130))
* Double staining of [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) and **HLA-G** is a useful method in the differential diagnosis of exaggerated placental site versus placental site trophoblastic tumor and placental site trophoblastic tumor versus choriocarcinoma ([Hum Immunol 2007;68:272](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17400063))

Negative stains

* [hCG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshcg.html), [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html)
* [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) (near zero)

Sample pathology report

* Uterus, endometrium, dilatation and curettage:
  + Exaggerated placental site

Differential diagnosis

* [Choriocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/placentachoriocarcinoma.html):
  + Comprised of syncytiotrophoblasts and cytotrophoblasts
  + Extensive hemorrhage and necrosis
  + [hCG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshcg.html) positive
* [Epithelioid trophoblastic tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/placentaETT.html):
  + Well circumscribed nodular expansile masses with necrosis
  + Mononucleate trophoblastic cells arranged in nests and cords ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2001;20:31](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11192071))
  + Strongly positive for [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html), focally positive for [HPL](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshpl.html) and [MEL-CAM / CD146](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd140.html); negative for [MUC4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmuc2.html)
* [Placental site trophoblastic tumor (PSTT)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/placentaPSTT.html):
  + Confluent sheets of trophoblastic cells
  + Increased mitoses ([Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) is 10 - 15%) ([Hum Pathol 1998;29:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9445130))
  + PSTT can be confused with molar implantation site; Ki67 labelling index of molar implantation site is 3 - 5% ([Hum Pathol 1998;29:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9445130))
  + **HLA-G** immunostaining highlights growth patterns of intermediate trophoblasts and can distinguish placental site trophoblastic tumor and an exaggerated placental site
  + In a placental site trophoblastic tumor, the cells form confluent masses, whereas in an exaggerated placental site, the IT cells infiltrate the endomyometrium as single cells and cords of cells ([Am J Surg Pathol 2002;26:914](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12131159))
* [Placental site nodule](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/placentaplacentalsitenodule.html):
  + Nodular with well circumscribed, surrounding thin rim of chronic inflammatory cells and occasionally decidual cells
  + [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html): trophoblast nuclear staining Hum Pathol 1998;29:27)
  + [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) positive

Exaggerated placental site (shown in the image above) is characterized by which of the following features?

1. Absence of villi
2. Presence of necrosis and mitoses
3. Proliferation of chorionic type intermediate trophoblasts with invasion of myometrium
4. Proliferation of implantation type intermediate trophoblasts with invasion of myometrium

Board review style answer #1

**D**. Proliferation of implantation type intermediate trophoblasts with invasion of myometrium
