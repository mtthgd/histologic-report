Definition / general
  • All gestational trophoblastic neoplasms (GTN) are covered by this staging system
  • These topics are not covered: nongestational trophoblastic tumors (e.g., ovarian choriocarcinoma), benign trophoblastic tumors (e.g., placental site nodule, complete and partial hydatidiform moles)
Essential features
  • American Joint Committee on Cancer (AJCC) 7th edition staging was sunset on December 31, 2017; as of January 1, 2018, use of the 8th edition is mandatory
Terminology
  • Gestational trophoblastic neoplasm, gestational trophoblastic disease, placental neoplasm
ICD coding
  • ICD-10: C58 - malignant neoplasm of placenta
Primary tumor (pT)
  • pTX: primary tumor cannot be assessed
  • pT0: no evidence of primary tumor
  • pT1: tumor confined to uterus
  • pT2: tumor extends to other genital structures (ovary, fallopian tube, vagina, broad ligaments) by metastasis or direct extension
Regional lymph nodes (pN)
  • Not applicable

Notes:
  • N classification is not used in the staging of gestational trophoblastic neoplasm, as nodal involvement is rare and portends an extremely poor prognosis
  • Any lymph node metastasis should be classified as metastatic (M1b) disease
Distant metastasis (pM)
  • pM0: no distant metastasis
  • pM1: distant metastasis
    • pM1a: lung metastasis
    • pM1b: all other distant metastasis

Notes:
  • Any lymph node metastasis should be classified as metastatic (M1b) disease
  • Lung is the most common site of metastasis and is not considered an adverse prognostic factor
Prefixes
  • y: posttreatment
  • r: recurrent
FIGO stage (2018 FIGO Cancer Report)
  • I: disease confined to the uterus
  • II: gestational trophoblastic tumor extends outside of the uterus but limited to the genital structures (adnexa, vagina, broad ligament)
  • III: gestational trophoblastic tumor extends to the lungs with or without known genital tract involvement
  • IV: all other metastatic sites

  • Histologic grade is not applicable to gestational trophoblastic neoplasms

Board review style question #1


Which of the following is the pTNM stage (per the AJCC 8th edition) of the gestational trophoblastic neoplasm in the image above? The tumor involves the uterus and bilateral ovaries with metastases to the lung.

  1. pT1 M0, FIGO stage I
  2. pT1 M1a, FIGO stage III
  3. pT2 M0, FIGO stage II
  4. pT2 M1a, FIGO stage III
  5. pT2 M1b, FIGO stage IV
Board review style answer #1
D. pT2 M1a, FIGO stage III. The figure shows a trophoblastic tumor with geographic necrosis, consistent with an epithelioid trophoblastic tumor. Any tumor with M1a, regardless of the pT status, is staged as FIGO stage III. Answers A and B are incorrect because metastasis to other genital structures is present, which makes this case a pT2 tumor. Answer C is incorrect because this case shows metastasis to the lung, classifying it as a M1a tumor. Answer E is incorrect because the tumor shown would be classified as stage III not IV as it does not extend to other sites outside of the reproductive organs or lungs.
