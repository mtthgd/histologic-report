Definition / general

* Reactive proliferation of mesothelial cells with no or minimal cytologic atypia and without invasion

Essential features

* Common response to inflammation occurring in any process that leads to irritation of peritoneal surface
* Reactive proliferation of mesothelial cells with minor degrees of nuclear atypia
* Without invasion, positive for mesothelial markers (CK5/6, calretinin and WT1)
* BAP1 and MTAP retained

Terminology

* Simple mesothelial hyperplasia
* Florid mesothelial peritoneal hyperplasia

Epidemiology

* Exact incidence is unknown but mesothelial cell hyperplasia is not an uncommon phenomenon
* May occur at any age, whatever the sex ([J Clin Pathol 2011;64:313](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21421699))

Sites

* May occur at any site of the peritoneum

Pathophysiology

* Common response to inflammation occurring in any process that leads to irritation of peritoneal surface

Etiology

* Chronic effusions (ascites)
* Inflammatory processes
* Endometriosis
* Hernias
* Neoplasms (e.g. ovarian tumor, colorectal tumor)
* Reference: [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2014;33:393](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24901399)

Diagnosis

* Exploratory laparoscopy with tissue sampling
* Often incidentally identified in peritoneal tissue obtained for other purposes

Radiology description

* No gross evidence of disease on imaging

Case reports

* 32 year old woman with a left ovarian microinvasive serous borderline tumor ([Eur J Gynaecol Oncol 2004;25:236](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15032291))
* 35, 53 and 59 year old women with tubal ectopic pregnancy, ovarian serous cystadenoma and ovarian adenosquamous carcinoma, respectively ([Histopathology 2013;63:598](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23855881))
* 36 and 37 year old women with dysmenorrhea and secondary infertility and endometriosis, respectively ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2004;8:115](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15185256))

Gross description

* Not commonly visible upon gross examination (incidental findings on microscopic examination)
* Occasionally observed as small nodules or flat plaques from the peritoneum ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2014;33:393](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24901399))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Variety of architectural patterns (solid sheets, nests, discrete small papillary or tubulopapillary growths, gland-like structures, cord-like linear arrays or single cells) ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2014;33:393](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24901399))
* Minor degrees of nuclear atypia (small, regular, round or oval and exhibit central nucleoli) without invasion; more common to be uniform in appearance
* Reactive changes may be present that are worrisome for malignancy (enlarged vesicular nuclei, multinucleation, conspicuous nucleoli and rare mitotic figures)
* Usually accompanied with inflammatory cells
* Sometimes florid mesothelial peritoneal hyperplasia or psammoma bodies may be seen ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 1993;12:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8418078))
* Rare findings:
  + Eosinophilic strap-like cells resembling rhabdomyoblasts ([Cancer 1975;35:165](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1109769))
  + Deciduoid morphology with glassy eosinophilic cytoplasm ([Histopathology 2013;63:598](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23855881))

Cytology description

* Usually mesothelial cells may be numerous or not, dispersed or present in small clusters
* Binucleation, multinucleation, mitosis, prominent nucleolus can be present in benign proliferations ([Cytojournal 2013;10:7](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23858317))
* 2 or more mesothelial cells are often separated by window or a narrow space ([Cytopathology 2004;15:131](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15165269))
* Benign mesothelial cells usually have recognizable halo at outer rim of cell
* Complex papillary or branching clusters should not be present ([Cytojournal 2013;10:7](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23858317))

Positive stains

* Positive immunostains in mesothelial cells ([CK AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [CK5/6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5and6.html), [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html), [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html), [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsd240.html))
* Reported positivity for [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) but nonspecific (more common in reactive mesothelial cells [84%] than mesothelioma [8%] or carcinoma [2%]) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2001;25:1405](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11684957))
* [BAP1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbap1.html) and **MTAP** are retained (positive)

Negative stains

* Glycoprotein markers ([CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html), [LeuM1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd15.html), [MOC31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsepcam.html), [B72.3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsb723.html), [BerEP4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsepcam.html), [claudin4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsclaudins.html#claudin4)) ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2018;142:89](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28686500))

Sample pathology report

* Peritoneal biopsy:
  + Mesothelial hyperplasia of the peritoneum (see comment)
  + Comment: There are small papillary structures containing myxoid stroma, which are lined by a single layer of uniform cuboidal mesothelial cells without any infiltrative cells or necrosis. Immunohistochemically, the mesothelial cells are positive for calretinin and WT1. BAP1 and MTAP are retained.

Differential diagnosis

* [Mesothelioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pleuraperitmesotheliomageneral.html):
  + Generally, diffuse involvement of the peritoneum
  + Infiltration of underlying pre-existing tissue is classical
  + At least moderate cytologic atypia in most cases
  + Solid architecture may be present
  + [BAP1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsBAP1.html) loss by immunostaining or *CDKN2A* homozygous loss is required for diagnosis ([Virchows Arch 2021;478:59](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33475835))
* [Mesothelioma in situ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pleuramesothelioma.html):
  + Similar morphology
  + [BAP1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsBAP1.html) loss by immunostaining or *CDKN2A* homozygous loss is required for diagnosis ([Virchows Arch 2021;478:59](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33475835))
* [Well differentiated papillary mesothelioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/peritoneumwelldiffpapillarymeso.html):
  + Papillae larger with myxoid cores, each lined by a single mesothelial cell layer
  + Invasion is rarely observed ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:990](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24618613))
  + Recurrent mutations in *TRAF7* or *CDC42* ([Mod Pathol 2019;32:88](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30171198))
    - Found by positive **L1CAM** immunostain
* [Adenomatoid tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisepidadenomatoid.html):
  + Organized in vascular-like, gland-like, complex slit-like and cystic branching spaces; also tubules or combination of above
  + Positive immunostain for **L1CAM**
* [Sertoli cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisSertoliNOS.html):
  + Usually well circumscribed
  + Different architecture to reactive mesothelial proliferations: often mixed patterns of tubular, cords, macro or microcystic, nested, trabecular, whorled, solid, retiform, pseudopapillary
  + Often presence of cells with cytoplasmic vacuoles
* [Metastatic carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pleurametastases.html):
  + Negative for [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html)
  + Positive for [claudin4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsclaudins.html#claudin4), [MOC31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsepcam.html), [BerEP4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsepcam.html) and other epithelial lineage markers

Board review style question #1

Which of the following statements regarding mesothelial hyperplasia of the peritoneum is true?

1. Approximately 10% of patients progress to malignant mesothelioma over 10 years
2. BAP1 immunostain can be lost
3. Common response to inflammation occurring in any process that leads to irritation of peritoneal surface
4. Lesions harbor consistently mutation of *TRAF7*
5. Psammoma bodies are never seen

Board review style answer #1

**C.** Common response to inflammation occurring in any process that leads to irritation of peritoneal surface
