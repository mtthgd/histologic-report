Definition / general

* Mesothelioma is a neoplasm arising from mesothelial cells that line serous cavities, such as the pleura and peritoneum
* Pleural mesothelioma is much more common than peritoneal mesothelioma
* Epithelioid mesothelioma is the most frequent histologic type of malignant mesothelioma; sarcomatoid and biphasic subtypes are less common
* 20 - 33% of malignant mesothelioma arises in the peritoneum ([Semin Oncol 2002;29:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11836669))

Essential features

* Hallmark of epithelioid mesothelioma is the epithelioid cells which are polygonal cells with moderate to abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm, vesicular round nuclei and prominent nucleolus; often mimic nonneoplastic, reactive mesothelial cells ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2018;142:89](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28686500))
* The most common histologic patterns of epithelioid mesothelioma are tubulopapillary, adenomatoid, solid well differentiated, solid poorly differentiated and acinar ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2012;136:241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22372900))
* Myxoid variant of peritoneal epithelioid mesothelioma is extremely rare with only 5 reported cases

Epidemiology

* Often due to asbestos exposure, whether in the pleura, peritoneum and pericardium; cumulative asbestos exposure is directly proportional to risk of cancer ([J Med Case Rep 2008;2:121](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18439258))

Pathophysiology

* Asbestos fibers lead to chronic inflammation, which causes the release of free radicals
* Latent period between asbestos exposure and disease averages 20 - 30 years ([Cancer Treat Rev 2012;38:605](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22104079))

Clinical features

* No distinctive symptoms, causing difficulties in diagnosis and treatment
* When symptomatic, usually present with abdominal pain, ascites and abdominal distention

Prognostic factors

* Prognosis is poor
* Survival rate of myxoid variant appears to be better than epithelioid mesothelioma in general ([Virchows Arch 2005;447:828](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16021506))
* Suggested favorable prognostic factors are small nuclear size and low Ki67 labeling index ([World J Gastroenterol 2009;15:4856](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19824125), [Med Mol Morphol 2010;43:53](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20340007))

Case reports

* 34 year old woman with epithelioid mesothelioma after radiation for cervical cancer ([Mol Clin Oncol 2018;8:302](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29435293))
* 44 year old woman with extensive myxoid change in well differentiated papillary mesothelioma ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2002;6:164](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12089727))
* 59 year old man with abdominal bloating and vague abdominal pain ([Case #441](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case441.htm))
* 60 year old woman with myxoid variant of epithelioid malignant mesothelioma ([Cesk Patol 2014;50:149](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25186596))
* 76 year old woman with small bowel obstruction secondary to carcinomatosis caused by primary peritoneal mesothelioma ([Am J Case Rep 2015;16:496](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26222965))

Treatment

* Systemic chemotherapy
* Cytoreductive and palliative surgery

Gross description

* Diffuse thickening or multiple nodules on the peritoneum
* Myxoid variant is gelatinous

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Invasive epithelioid cells are arranged in different patterns (which form the basis of the different subtypes of epithelioid mesothelioma)
* Form tubules and papillae with / without psammoma bodies (tubulopapillary variant), gland-like structures (acinar variant) and are in solid sheets, nests or cords (solid variant)
* Myxoid variant:
  + Dyscohesive medium to large epithelioid cells with a moderate to abundant amount of eosinophilic cytoplasm dispersed in a myxoid background
  + Some cells can have intracytoplasmic clear vacuoles
  + Nuclei with coarse chromatin and prominent nucleoli
  + Mitotic figures are usually inconspicuous
  + Difficulty to differentiate from other myxoid lesions of the peritoneum (e.g. adenocarcinoma) thus panel of immunohistochemical markers is generally required

* [AE1 / 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html)

Negative stains

* [MOC31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsepcam.html), [BerEP4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsepcam.html), [B72.3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsb723.html) and [claudin4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsclaudins.html)

Electron microscopy description

* Very long, thin apical microvilli and the absence of glycocalyx (compared to [adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pleuramesovsadeno.html), which has shorter villi)

Sample pathology report

* Peritoneum, resection:
  + Multifocal epithelioid mesothelioma (largest focus 4.5 cm) (see comment)
  + Margins of resection unremarkable.
  + Comment: There is not currently an AJCC TNM cancer staging system for peritoneal mesothelioma. Immunohistochemical stains for calretinin and D2-40 are positive in the tumor.

Differential diagnosis

* **Mucinous adenocarcinoma**
  + [Papillary serous carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarytumorserouscarcinomahg.html) involving the peritoneum ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17667535))
* [Pleura - mesothelioma versus adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pleuramesovsadeno.html)
* [Pseudomyxoma peritonei](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/appendixpseudomyxoma.html)

Additional references

* [Am J Clin Pathol 2005;123:724](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15981812)

Board review style question #1

Which of the following stains is positive in the myxoid variant of epithelioid mesothelioma and helps to differentiate it from mucinous adenocarcinoma?

1. B72.3
2. Claudin4
3. D2-40
4. MOC31

Board review style answer #1

**C.** D2-40
