Definition / general

* Tumor that originates from the serosal lining of the peritoneal cavity

Essential features

* Peritoneal mesothelioma shows weaker association with asbestos exposure and more likely involves women / young patients than pleural mesothelioma
* Histologic variants (epithelioid, biphasic and sarcomatoid) impart prognostic information with treatment implications
* Loss of BAP1 expression is seen in 40 - 60% of peritoneal mesothelioma, not sensitive but fairly specific for the diagnosis of mesothelioma in the appropriate histologic context

Terminology

* Peritoneal mesothelioma
* Malignant peritoneal mesothelioma
* Diffuse malignant peritoneal mesothelioma
* Abdominal mesothelioma

ICD coding

* ICD-O:
  + [9050/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=84) - mesothelioma
  + [9051/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=85) - sarcomatoid / desmoplastic mesothelioma
  + [9052/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=85) - epithelioid mesothelioma
  + [9053/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=85) - biphasic mesothelioma
* ICD-10: [C45.1](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C45-C49/C45-/C45.1) - mesothelioma of peritoneum

Epidemiology

* Accounts for ~10% of all mesotheliomas
* Incidence: ~300 new cases annually in the United States
* Presents with a wide age range and no apparent sex predilection ([Cancer Causes Control 2009;20:935](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19294523), [Bull World Health Organ 2011;89:716](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22084509), [Clin Epidemiol 2016;8:743](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27822122))

Sites

* Involves the serosal lining in the peritoneal cavity, often of multiple intra-abdominal organs
* Generally diffuse, rarely solitary / localized ([Am J Surg Pathol 2005;29:866](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15958850))

Etiology

* Associated with exposure to asbestos fibers in a subset of patients, typically with a long latency (median ~32 years); the association is weaker than in pleural mesothelioma ([J Clin Oncol 1983;1:386](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6668506), [J Occup Med 1992;34:718](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1494965), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2018;142:753](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29480760))
* Rarely associated with exposure to non asbestos mineral fibers: erionite, fluoro-edenite and others ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2018;142:753](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29480760))
* Rarely associated with prior exposure to therapeutic radiation for other malignancy with a latency of years to decades ([J Clin Oncol 1983;1:695](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6668488), [Cancer 1988;61:2019](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3282640), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2018;142:753](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29480760))
* Rarely associated with recurrent peritonitis / chronic serosal inflammation secondary to Crohn disease, endometriosis or Familial Mediterranean Fever ([J Clin Pathol 2017;70:228](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27484913), [J Clin Pathol 2018;71:971](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29794065), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2018;142:753](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29480760))
* Rarely associated with germline mutations and characteristic gene rearrangements (see molecular/cytogenetics section below)

Clinical features

* More likely to affect women and young patients as compared to pleural mesothelioma ([Cancer Causes Control 2009;20:935](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19294523))
* Symptoms can be nonspecific and depend on the extent of involvement
* Presentation includes most commonly abdominal pain, distension or ascites; rarely incidental or with new hernia, bowel obstruction or perforation ([Tumori 2003;89:269](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12908781), [Ann Gastroenterol 2018;31:659](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30386115))
* Morbidity / mortality primarily due to locoregional spread with extra-abdominal metastasis rare
* Median overall survival of 3 - 7 years with a 5 year survival rate of 40 - 60% with treatment ([Ann Gastroenterol 2018;31:659](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30386115))

Diagnosis

* Radiologic assessment of disease extent by computed tomography (CT) or magnetic resonance imaging (MRI)
* Cytologic analysis of peritoneal fluid, though this is not entirely sensitive
* Definitive diagnosis is most commonly based on histologic analysis of surgical specimen from laparoscopic / open or core needle biopsy
* Peritoneal mesothelioma, particularly the sarcomatoid variant, is difficult to diagnose and requires multiple immunohistochemical markers to exclude mimics
* Since no single immunohistochemical marker is entirely sensitive and specific for the diagnosis, a panel of at least 2 positive markers and 2 negative markers is recommended ([Hum Pathol 2017;67:160](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28782639))

Radiology description

* Multiple nodular lesions involving omentum and the mesentery, peritoneal thickening and accumulation of ascites ([Anticancer Res 2016;36:1067](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26976999))

Prognostic factors

* Improved survival has been associated with the following:
  + Age J Clin Oncol 2003;21:4560, [Ann Surg Oncol 2018;25:2159](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29423664), [Ann Surg Oncol 2018;25:2018](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29721724))
  + Female gender ([Ann Surg Oncol 2018;25:2159](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29423664), [Ann Surg Oncol 2018;25:2018](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29721724))
  + Epithelioid variant ([J Clin Oncol 2009;27:6237](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19917862), [Pathology 2014;46:604](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25393250), [Ann Surg Oncol 2018;25:2018](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29721724))
  + Complete cytoreduction ([J Clin Oncol 2003;21:4560](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14673042), [J Clin Oncol 2009;27:6237](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19917862), [Ann Surg Oncol 2015;22:1686](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25124472), [J Gastrointest Oncol 2017;8:915](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29184697))
  + Lack of lymph node metastasis ([J Clin Oncol 2009;27:6237](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19917862), [J Gastrointest Oncol 2017;8:915](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29184697))
  + Low peritoneal cancer index (PCI) ([Cancer 2011;117:1855](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21509762), [J Gastrointest Oncol 2017;8:915](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29184697))
  + Absence of loss of chromosomal region 9p21 / *CDKN2A* ([Mod Pathol 2010;23:531](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20081810), [JAMA Oncol 2018;4:235](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28910456))
* Worse survival has been noted to be associated with the following, although more data is needed for definitive conclusion:
  + BAP1 molecular or expression status ([J Thorac Oncol 2017;12:724](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28034829))
  + Elevated mitotic count ([Clin Cancer Res 2005;11:3303](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15867227), [Histopathology 2016;68:729](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26272336))
  + High nuclear grade ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1243](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27438989))
  + Solid pattern in epithelioid variant ([Histopathology 2016;68:729](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26272336))

Treatment

* Cytoreductive surgery, often combined with hyperthermic intraoperative chemotherapy, generally recommended for treating epithelioid variant (and some biphasic) but not sarcomatoid variant ([Ann Surg Oncol 2015;22:1686](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25124472), [Ann Surg Oncol 2018;25:667](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29260418))
* Chemotherapy types: hyperthermic intraoperative chemotherapy (HIPEC), early postoperative intraperitoneal chemotherapy (EPIC), long term intraperitoneal (IP) chemotherapy and systemic chemotherapy ([Eur J Cancer 2016;65:69](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27472649), [Eur J Surg Oncol 2017;43:1228](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28189456))
* Radiation
* Immunotherapy: clinical trial on using tremelimumab (anti-CTLA4 monoclonal antibody) and durvalumab (PD-L1 blockade) ongoing ([Lancet Respir Med 2018;6:451](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29773326))

Gross description

* Multiple omental / serosal nodules and thickened peritoneum

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Unequivocal indicator of malignancy: invasion into adipose tissue or stromal invasion ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10976692), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2018;142:89](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28686500))
* Histologically classified into epithelioid, biphasic and sarcomatoid variants with implications on prognosis and treatment planning
* Epithelioid mesothelioma is characterized by epithelioid to round tumor cells, which are often more monotonous than what are seen in most carcinomas
* Sarcomatoid mesothelioma is characterized by spindled tumor cells
* Biphasic mesothelioma is characterized by the presence of both epithelioid and sarcomatoid components, each comprising at least 10% of the tumor
* In epithelioid mesothelioma, architectural / cytologic features that can be seen are diverse; histologic patterns most commonly seen are tubular, papillary and solid and more rarely micropapillary, trabecular, acinar, adenomatoid-like, clear cell, deciduoid, adenoid cystic-like, signet ring cell, small cell and rhabdoid ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2013;137:647](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22929121))
* In sarcomatoid mesothelioma, histologic patterns include conventional, desmoplastic, lymphohistiocytoid and those with heterologous differentiation ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2013;137:647](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22929121), [Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1568](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26371785))

AE1 / AE3

Cytology description

* Large clusters to sheets of fairly monotonous mesothelial tumor cells
* Limitation of cytologic diagnosis: rarely definitive, since tissue invasion is difficult to assess

Positive stains

* Mesothelial markers: [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html), [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html) and [D2-40 (podoplanin)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html), [CAIX](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscarbonicanhydraseix.html) ([J Clin Pathol 2016;69:706](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26729015), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2018;142:236](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29048219), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2018;142:89](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28686500))
* Keratins such as [AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [CAM 5.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscam52.html) and broad spectrum [pankeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html)
* [Keratin 5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5.html) / [6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck6.html): expressed in most epithelioid mesotheliomas (rarely adenocarcinomas)
* **Mesothelin**, [thrombomodulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsthrombomodulin.html), [HBME1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshbme.html)
* [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgata3.html): expressed in a large subset of sarcomatoid mesothelioma (also in breast and urothelial carcinoma)

Negative stains

* Epithelial markers: claudin4, [BerEP4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsepcam.html), [MOC31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsepcam.html), [B72.3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsb723.html), [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html)
* [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html)
* [BAP1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsBAP1.html): complete loss of [BAP1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsBAP1.html) expression in 40 - 60% (lost in Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:977, [Hum Pathol 2016;51:9](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27067777), [J Thorac Oncol 2017;12:724](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28034829))
* [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html): expression in 10 - 20% ([Hum Pathol 2018;72:160](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29241740), [Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:1675](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28877056), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2018;142:89](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28686500))

Electron microscopy description

* Microvilli and desmosomes

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* *BAP1* somatic mutations in 40 - 70% ([J Transl Med 2015;13:122](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25889843), [Mod Pathol 2017;30:246](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27813512), [J Thorac Oncol 2017;12:724](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28034829))
* Copy number loss of *BAP1* in 3p21, *CDKN2A* in 9p21 and NF2 in 22q12 in 20 - 40% ([Mod Pathol 2010;23:531](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20081810), [Hum Pathol 2016;55:72](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27184482))
* Somatic mutations in *SETD2, DDX3X* and others in a subset
  ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:246](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27813512))
* Germline mutations in *BAP1, ATM* or other regulators in DNA repair in a subset ([J Clin Oncol 2018;36:2863](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30113886), [J Pediatr Hematol Oncol 2018;40:e511](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30124550))
* *EWSR1-ATF1* or *FUS-ATF1* gene fusion in rare cases ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:980](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28505004))
* *ALK* gene rearrangement in rare cases but enriched in young patients ([JAMA Oncol 2018;4:235](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28910456))

Sample pathology report

* Peritoneum and omentum, resection:
  + Mesothelioma, epithelioid variant, 5.0 cm in greatest dimension
  + Surgical margins, negative for tumor
  + Comment: The tumor cells are positive for WT1, calretinin and D2-40, and are negative for claudin4, PAX8, MOC31 and CEA.

Differential diagnosis

* **Metastatic adenocarcinoma**
  + Variable histologic overlap
  + Typically expresses other epithelial markers: claudin4, [BerEP4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsepcam.html), polyclonal [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html),
    [MOC31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsepcam.html) and [B72.3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsb723.html)
  + Rarely express [keratin 5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5.html) / [6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck6.html) and mesothelial markers ([calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html) and [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html)), though [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html) is commonly expressed in carcinomas of Müllerian primary and [CAIX](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscarbonicanhydraseix.html) is expressed in renal cell carcinoma, clear cell type
  + Expression of [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html) favors renal or gynecologic origin and [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html) favors lung origin
* **Sclerosing peritonitis**
  + Reactive proliferation of stromal myofibroblasts, often associated with adjacent linear arrangement of mesothelial cells, chronic inflammation, surface fibrin deposition and occasionally entrapped fat
  + Associated with various conditions including luteinized thecomas ([Am J Surg Pathol 1994;18:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8279621))
  + Distinction can be challenging, particularly in small biopsies, cases with tangential sectioning or fat entrapment ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10976692))
* **Well differentiated papillary mesothelial tumor**
  + Most commonly in the peritoneum, rarely pleura and other sites
  + Histology: papillae with myxoid cores, each lined by a single mesothelial cell layer
  + Invasion is typically not present ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:990](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24618613))
  + Overall more indolent than peritoneal mesothelioma ([Ann Surg Oncol 2019;26:852](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30635798))
  + Recurrent mutations in *TRAF7* or *CDC42* ([Mod Pathol 2019;32:88](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30171198))
* **Adenomatoid tumor of genital type**
  + Benign mesothelial tumor that arises mostly commonly near the genital tract
  + Histology: a microcystic low power appearance with poor margination and tubules / cords of epithelioid cells with characteristic cytoplasmic vacuolation
  + Associated with immunosuppressive state in some cases ([Histopathology 2018;73:1013](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30099776))
  + Recurrent mutations in *TRAF7* ([Mod Pathol 2018;31:660](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29148537))
* **Peritoneal inclusion cyst**
  + Also known as multilocular inclusion cyst
  + Histology: multiple cysts of various sizes, each with thin fibrous walls lined by flattened mesothelial cells ([Cancer 1989;64:1336](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2766227))
  + *TNS3-MAP3K3* or *ZFPM2-ELF5* gene fusion in rare cases ([Cancer Lett 2015;357:502](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25484136))
* **Epithelioid hemangioendothelioma**
  + Rare distinctive malignant vascular tumor
  + Most commonly involves liver, lung / pleura, bone or soft tissue
  + Histology: cord-like pattern, myxohyaline matrix, epithelioid cells with intracytoplasmic vacuoles
  + Immunohistochemistry: typically positive for [CAMTA1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscamta1.html) and endothelial markers ([ERG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainserg.html), [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) and [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html)) but negative for [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html) and [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:94](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26414223))
  + *WWTR1-CAMTA1* gene fusion in most cases, *YAP1-TFE3* fusion in rare cases ([Sci Transl Med 2011;3:98ra82](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21885404), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2011;50:644](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21584898), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2013;52:775](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23737213))
