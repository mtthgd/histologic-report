Definition / general

* > 50% of tumor shows desmoplastic pattern
* 5 - 10% of malignant mesotheliomas
* Note: DMM may refer to desmoplastic or diffuse malignant mesothelioma

Case reports

* 2 cases with rapid decline ([Med Electron Microsc 2003;36:173](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14505061))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Characteristic dense collagenized tissue separated by atypical cells arranged in a storiform or haphazard pattern ([J Clin Pathol 1992;45:295](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1577967)), comprising > 50% of tumor
* Presence of sarcomatoid areas, bland collagen, necrosis, stromal / fat invasion help differentiate from organizing pleuritis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:1823](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21959310))
* Paucicellularity of lesion may lead to misdiagnosis

Positive stains

* [Keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) stains:
  + [Pancytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html) is positive in > 70% of sarcomatoid mesotheliomas (including desmoplastic)
  + Use multiple [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) antibodies ([AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [CAM 5.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscam52.html) and [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html))
  + If only focal [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) staining, additional blocks should be stained along with [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html) to check for tissue antigen integrity
* [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html), [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html)
* [GLUT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglut1.html): 53% sensitivity, 98% specific

Negative stains

* [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html) (positive in angiosarcoma), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) (positive in solitary fibrous tumor, [Hum Pathol 1995;26:428](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7535740))
* [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) (positive in neurogenic tumors, mesotheliomas may show focal staining)
* [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html)
* Some muscle markers are focally positive

Electron microscopy description

* Sarcomatous mesotheliomas including desmoplastic type have no specific ultrastructural features

Differential diagnosis

* [Calcifying fibrous pseudotumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuecalcifyingfibrous.html) **of pleura / localized fibrous tumor**:
  + Whorls and haphazard arrays of keloidal collagen
  + Also psammomatous and dystrophic calcification
* [Fibrous pleurisy / pleuritis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pleurapleuritis.html):
  + [Cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) highlights malignant cells of mesothelioma; if present in fat, skeletal muscle or connective tissue, is an important clue to desmoplastic mesothelioma
  + "Fake fat" may be seen in organizing pleuritis due to artefactual changes in dense fibrous connective tissue
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), **laminin** and [collagen](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscollagentypeIV.html) are negative in fake fat ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2013;137:647](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22929121))
* [Solitary fibrous tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pleurasolitaryfibrous.html): [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)+
