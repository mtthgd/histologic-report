Definition / general

* Defined as > 90% spindle shaped tumor cells
* Termed desmoplastic if > 50% of the tumor shows desmoplastic pattern

Epidemiology

* 10 - 20% of all mesotheliomas

Case reports

* 61 year old woman with shortness of breath ([Intern Med 2013;52:249](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23318857))
* Osteoblastic variant ([Br J Radiol 2011;84:e106](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21511743))

Treatment

* Sarcomatoid histology (pure or mixed) is poor prognostic factors after extrapleural pneumonectomy
* Chemotherapy is recommended according to NCCN guidelines ([National Comprehensive Cancer Network: NCNN Guidelines for Treatment of Cancer by Site [Accessed 23 March 2018]](https://www.nccn.org/professionals/physician_gls/default.aspx#site))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Conventional, spindle cell type: spindle cells arranged in fascicles or having a haphazard distribution
* [Desmoplastic variant](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pleuramesotheliomadesmoplastic.html)
* Heterologous differentiation: osteosarcoma, chondrosarcoma or other sarcoma-like areas
* Lymphohistiocytoid: diffuse large histiocyte-like cells with dense infiltrate of lymphocytes and plasma cells; behaves similar to epithelioid type, some prefer classifying as such ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:711](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17460454))

Cytology description

* Tumor cells not shed in pleural fluid, which typically contains only reactive mesothelial cells

Positive stains

* [Keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html): [pancytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html) positive in > 70%; use multiple antibodies ([AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [CAM 5.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscam52.html) and [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html)); if only focal [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) staining is present, stain additional blocks with [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html) to check tissue antigen integrity
* [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html), [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html)
* [GLUT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglut1.html): overall sensitivity and specificity in mesothelioma is 53% and 98% respectively ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2012;136:804](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22742553))

Negative stains

* [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html) (positive in angiosarcoma), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) (solitary fibrous tumor), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) (neurogenic tumors but mesotheliomas may show focal staining)
* [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html)
* May have focal positive muscle markers

Electron microscopy description

* No specific ultrastructural features

Differential diagnosis

* Other **biphasic tumors**:
  + [Carcinosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorcarcinosarcoma.html)
  + [Pleomorphic carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorpleomorphic.html)
  + [Synovial sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesynovialsarc.html)
* [Desmoid tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuefibromatosisdeep.html)
* [Fibrous pleuritis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pleurapleuritis.html)
* **Sarcoma**
* **Sarcomatoid carcinoma**:
  + [Kidney](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantrccsarcoma.html) may be difficult to differentiate
  + **Lung**
* [Solitary fibrous tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pleurasolitaryfibrous.html): [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)+, [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html)+, [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html)+, [keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)-
