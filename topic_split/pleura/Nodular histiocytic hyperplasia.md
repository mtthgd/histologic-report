Definition / general

* Small nodular aggregates of histiocytes with mesothelial cells scattered in the aggregates or in clusters

Terminology

* Nodular mesothelial hyperplasia, mesothelial / monoytc incidental cardiac excrescences (MICE), nodular histiocytic / mesothelial hyperplasia

Sites

* Lesions have been described in lung, pleura, hernia sac, pericardium, peritoneum

Pathophysiology

* **Hypothesis:** trauma, tumor or inflammation lead to histiocyte / mesothelial expression of CD34 and adhesion molecules, which lead to aggregates of histiocytes and mesothelial cells through cell-cell interactions

Clinical features

* Almost always an incidental finding

Case reports

* 2 year old boy, 23 and 78 year old women, 74 year old man ([Am J Surg Pathol 1998;22:285](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9580050))
* 5 cases: peritoneal, pulmonary, pericardial ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2004;8:115](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15185256))

Treatment

* None required; an incidental finding

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Compact nodular collections of polygonal to oval cells with indistinct cell borders and moderate cytoplasm
* Nuclei are oval to angulated with grooves (usually) and inconspicuous nucleoli
* Mitoses may be present but no atypical mitoses
* Hemosiderin laden macrophages may be present
* Also present are scattered indistinguishable bland cuboidal to polygonal cells with moderate cytoplasm, which show cytokeratin and calretinin staining, indicating their mesothelial origin

Cytology description

* Similar nodular aggregates have also been described in cell block material from serous effusions - this is the most important pitfall in cytodiagnosis of serous effusions ([Diagn Cytopathol 2002;26:68](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11782092))

Positive stains

* Two populations: histiocytes ([CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html)+), mesothelial cells ([cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)+, [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html)+)

Negative stains

* [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html), [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html), [B72.3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsb723.html)

Differential diagnosis

* [Endometriosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarynontumorendometriosis.html)
* **Leukemia / lymphoma**
* [Metastatic adenocarcinoma / carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pleurametastases.html)
* [Mesothelioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pleuramesothelioma.html)
* **Reactive / florid** [mesothelial hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pleuraatypicalmesohyper.html): generalized, in contrast to the localized nodular histiocytic proliferation in this lesion
