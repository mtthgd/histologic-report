Definition / general

* Adenosquamous carcinoma of the prostate is characterized by presence of both glandular / acinar and squamous components

Essential features

* Adenosquamous prostatic carcinoma is a very rare aggressive tumor
* Critical to exclude other sources of the squamous component through clinical history, examinations, endoscopy and immunostains
* 67% of cases are associated with prior androgen deprivation therapy or radiotherapy
* Diagnosis requires proper morphology and immunostaining with prostate specific markers (prostate specific antigen [PSA], prostate specific membrane antigen [PSMA], prostatic specific acid phosphatase [PSAP] and prostatic acid phosphatase [PAP]), which should be positive in the glandular and negative in the squamous component

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [C61.9](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=55) - prostate, NOS
* ICD-10: [C61](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C60-C63/C61-/C61) - malignant neoplasm of the prostate

Epidemiology

* First described by Thompson, 1942; 58 cases described as of 2023 ([JAMA 1942;120:1105](https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jama/article-abstract/258826))
* Less prevalent than pure squamous prostatic carcinoma ([Clin Genitourin Cancer 2014;12:e29](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24145000))
* Men, mean age in 70s
* Many cases are associated with having received hormonal or radiotherapy, although some cases are not associated with prior therapy ([Rare Tumors 2010;2:e47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21139962), [Clin Genitourin Cancer 2014;12:e29](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24145000), [Urol Case Rep 2019;29:101084](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31867218), [Int J Urol 2005;12:319](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15828965), [Scientific World Journal 2006;6:2491](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17619723), [Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:67](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25025445), [Urology 1983;22:73](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6346632))

Sites

* Prostate, often with extraprostatic extension and seminal vesicle involvement

Pathophysiology

* Theories
  + Metaplastic transformation of adenocarcinoma frequently after treatment; this is supported by a case of adenosquamous carcinoma in which the squamous component rose from 5% before adjuvant radiotherapy to predominantly squamous after radiotherapy ([Eur J Cancer 2018;95:109](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29477368))
  + Collision tumor: intermingling of squamous and glandular components with no abrupt transition argues against this, as does the observation that squamous component may be PSAP reactive ([Hum Pathol 1984;15:87](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6319269), [Int J Urol 2005;12:319](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15828965), [Scientific World Journal 2006;6:2491](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17619723))
  + Arises from pluripotent stem cells capable of multidirectional differentiation ([Urol Case Rep 2019;29:101084](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31867218))

Etiology

* Often association with status postandrogen ablation or prostatic radiotherapy

Clinical features

* Up to 20% of patients presented with metastases in a SEER survey ([Rare Tumors 2010;2:e47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21139962))
* Patients frequently have urinary obstruction from a large tumor or rarely a rectal bleed ([Case Rep Urol 2022;2022:7613482](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35782655), [Clin Nucl Med 2024;49:180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38049966), [Int Urol Nephrol 2023;55:613](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36538273), [Clin Genitourin Cancer 2014;12:e29](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24145000), [Oncol Lett 2014;8:2325](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25295118), [Eur J Cancer 2018;95:109](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29477368), [Case Rep Urol 2022;2022:7613482](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35782655))

Diagnosis

* Histologic findings are established on transurethral resection (consistent with frequent obstructive uropathy) or biopsy
* Imaging cannot distinguish it from generic prostate cancer

Laboratory

* Serum PSA is usually elevated but not always, particularly if the squamous component predominates ([Int Urol Nephrol 2023;55:613](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36538273), [Prostate Cancer Prostatic Dis 2000;3:53](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12497163))

Radiology description

* Notably, F18 PSMA PET / CT scan demonstrated low PSMA uptake ([Int Urol Nephrol 2023;55:613](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36538273))
* F18 FDG PET / CT scan showing rectal mass and lung metastases ([Eur J Cancer 2018;95:109](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29477368))
* CT scan showing heterogeneous enhancement and rectal / bladder invasion ([Clin Genitourin Cancer 2014;12:e29](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24145000))
* CT scan showing rectal invasion, hydronephrosis, metastases ([Oncol Lett 2014;8:2325](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25295118))
* T2 weighted MRI scan showing tumor abutting rectum / anus ([Case Rep Urol 2022;2022:7613482](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35782655))

Prognostic factors

* 30% 5 year survival ([Rare Tumors 2010;2:e47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21139962))
* For patients undergoing prostatectomy it is 63%, whereas for those not undergoing prostatectomy 1 year survival is 39%

Case reports

* 54 year old man with rectal invasion ([Clin Nucl Med 2024;49:180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38049966))
* 56 year old man with no prior therapy, with metastasis to testis and a sarcomatoid component ([Scientific World Journal 2006;6:2491](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17619723))
* 62 year old man presented with metastases after hormonal treatment for prostate cancer ([Oncol Lett 2014;8:2325](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25295118))
* 65 and 73 year old men both after radiation, with rectal invasion by tumors that were predominantly squamous with a minimal gland component and with normal serum PSA ([Prostate Cancer Prostatic Dis 2000;3:53](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12497163))
* Man in his 70s with metastasis to penis in 1 case ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:67](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25025445))

Treatment

* No standard therapy and it depends on details
  + Radical prostatectomy ([Rare Tumors 2010;2:e47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21139962))
  + Radical cystoprostatectomy due to bladder neck involvement, androgen deprivation therapy (ADT) and radiation with survival ([Urol Case Rep 2019;29:101084](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31867218))
  + Radiation for inoperable cases, followed by nivolumab for a PDL1 positive case ([Rare Tumors 2010;2:e47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21139962), [Eur J Cancer 2018;95:109](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29477368), [Case Rep Urol 2022;2022:7613482](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35782655), [Scientific World Journal 2006;6:2491](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17619723), [Eur J Cancer 2018;95:109](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29477368))
  + Radiation plus androgen deprivation with survival in 1 of 2 cases ([Scand J Urol Nephrol 1994;28:425](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7886421))
  + Enzalutamide then carboplatin ([Int Urol Nephrol 2023;55:613](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36538273))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* In addition to the usual glandular prostate cancer component, there is a component of cells with eosinophilic cytoplasm
* Keratin pearls and intercellular bridges are required to diagnose squamous cell carcinoma
* Additional sarcomatoid transformation has been rarely reported ([Histopathology 2020;77:890](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32639612), [Scientific World Journal 2006;6:2491](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17619723))

Positive stains

* [PSA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspsa.html), [PSAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspap.html) and [PSMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspsma.html) in the glandular component ([Int Urol Nephrol 2023;55:613](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36538273))
* [ERG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainserg.html) in about half of cases ([Histopathology 2020;77:890](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32639612), [Int Urol Nephrol 2023;55:613](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36538273), [Eur J Cancer 2018;95:109](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29477368))
* [Cytokeratin 34 beta E12](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck34be12.html) in squamous component ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:67](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25025445))

Negative stains

* [PSA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspsa.html), [PSAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspap.html) and [PSMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspsma.html) usually negative in the squamous component ([Int Urol Nephrol 2023;55:613](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36538273), [Clin Genitourin Cancer 2014;12:e29](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24145000), [Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2017;25:e71](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27753662), [Eur J Cancer 2018;95:109](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29477368), [Scientific World Journal 2006;6:2491](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17619723), [Hum Pathol 1991;22:1046](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1842377))
* In one study, only 12% of squamous carcinomas were positive for [PSA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspsa.html) or [PSAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspap.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:651](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15105655))
* Another case had a squamous component positive for [PSAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspap.html) (however it is typically negative) ([Hum Pathol 1984;15:87](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6319269))
* [Cytokeratin 34 beta E12](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck34be12.html) negative in glandular component ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:67](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25025445))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* *TMPRSS2::BRAF* fusion ([Histopathology 2020;77:890](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32639612), [Int Urol Nephrol 2023;55:613](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36538273))
* *FAM131A::BRAF* fusion ([Histopathology 2020;77:890](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32639612))
* *TMPRSS2::ERG* fusion noted in half of cases ([Histopathology 2020;77:890](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32639612), [Int Urol Nephrol 2023;55:613](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36538273))

Sample pathology report

* Prostate, left apex, biopsies:
  + Prostatic adenosquamous carcinoma, Gleason 4 + 4 (score = 8) (see comment)
  + Comment: 30% of the tumor is a squamous component with cells showing intracellular and extracellular keratin and intercellular bridges. This finding correlates with the documented antiandrogen dosing the patient underwent after diagnosis of a Gleason 3+3 carcinoma 4 years ago. Adenosquamous carcinoma is an aggressive tumor.

Differential diagnosis

* Secondary involvement by **squamous neoplasms** arising in bladder, urethra or anorectal region:
  + Clinical endoscopic findings negative in these sites
  + Normal serum PSA
  + Limited use for immunostains
* [Pure squamous carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostatesquamous.html):
  + Absence of a contiguous or separate focus of glandular cancer in the specimen
* Admixed prostate cancer with **urothelial neoplasms** arising in bladder or urethra:
  + Keratin pearls and intercellular bridges are required to diagnose squamous cell carcinoma
  + If these findings are absent and if [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgata3.html) or [uroplakin II](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsUroplakinii.html) staining are present, the lesion is urothelial and not prostatic
* [Basal cell hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostatebch.html) with squamous features ([Hum Pathol 2005;36:531](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15948120)):
  + Lack of atypia of squamous cells
  + Absent invasive glandular component
  + Cytologic features of basal cells including nuclear vacuoles

Board review style question #1

[![Same, with obvious keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/prostateadenosquamouscarcinomamicro02.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/prostateadenosquamouscarcinomamicro02.jpg)

Which of the following findings would exclude a definite diagnosis of prostatic adenosquamous carcinoma in a biopsy?

1. Close intermingling of squamous and glandular tumor components
2. Lack of a history of hormone deprivation or radiotherapy for prostate cancer
3. Prior history of muscle invasive urothelial carcinoma with squamous features
4. PSA staining usually negative in the squamous component
5. Staining of the squamous tumor with cytokeratin 34 beta E12

Board review style answer #1

**C**. Prior history of muscle invasive urothelial carcinoma with squamous features. Prior history of a squamous cancer in the vicinity of the prostate is critical. A urothelial carcinoma, including that with squamous features, can easily invade the prostate. Answer D is incorrect because indeed the squamous component does lose reactivity for PSA in most adenosquamous tumors. Answer E is incorrect because the squamous component of adenosquamous carcinoma should stain with cytokeratin 34 beta E12. Answer B is incorrect because while many cases of adenosquamous carcinoma do have an association with hormonal or radiotherapy, quite a few reported cases do not. Answer A is incorrect because indeed there often is close intermingling of squamous and glandular components of the tumor.
