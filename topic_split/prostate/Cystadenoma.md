Definition / general

* Rare, benign epithelial tumor composed of variably sized cysts lined by bland cuboidal cells

Essential features

* Distinguished from mixed epithelial and stromal tumor of the seminal vesicle by the absence of stromal hypercellularity
* Should be distinguished from prostatic stromal tumor of uncertain malignant potential entrapping glands
  + Factors that favor seminal vesicle cystadenoma:
    - Cystic tumor centered in the seminal vesicle
    - No expression of prostatic differentiation markers

Terminology

* Some authors consider it as the expression of lowest grade (benign) of mixed epithelial and stromal tumor of the seminal vesicle ([Adv Anat Pathol 2015;22:113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25664946))

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8440/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf#page=72) - cystadenoma, NOS
* ICD-11: [2F34 & XH5RJ2](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f317567417?data=%7B%22dataType%22%3A%22pc%22%2C%22postcoordinationCodeSet%22%3A%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F317567417%22%2C%22axisToValueIds%22%3A%7B%22histopathology%22%3A%5B%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F1035495682%22%7D%5D%7D%7D%7D) - benign neoplasm of male genital organs & cystadenoma, NOS

Epidemiology

* Rare (< 30 described cases)
* Wide age distribution (23 - 66 years old)

Sites

* Centered within seminal vesicles

Pathophysiology

* Unknown

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Obstructive urinary symptoms
* Some patients present with an asymptomatic mass or the masses are incidentally detected in imaging studies

Diagnosis

* Cystic or solid and cystic masses detected in imaging studies

Radiology description

* Cystic or solid and cystic masses centered within the seminal vesicles

Prognostic factors

* Benign neoplasm; local resection is considered curative

* 48 year old man with a 3.5 cm mass in the right seminal vesicle ([J Endourol Case Rep 2015;1:62](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27579393))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Glandular spaces of varying sizes are lined by cuboidal cells without atypia ([Adv Anat Pathol 2015;22:113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25664946))
* Lobular pattern, forming branching lumina and cysts that contain granular intraluminal secretions
* No hypercellularity or atypia in stromal cells

Positive stains

* Epithelial lining stains for [pancytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html) and [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html)
* Basal cells express [high weight molecular keratins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck34be12.html)
* Stromal cells may stain for [smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html)
* Normal epithelial lining of a seminal vesicle usually strongly expresses [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsGATA3.html); however this marker is also positive in the prostate and should be interpreted with caution ([Actas Urol Esp 2017;41:577](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28461097))
* Normal and neoplastic seminal vesicle epithelium is strongly positive for [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsPAX8.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:1837](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21989345), [Hum Pathol 2017;69:123](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28506732))

Negative stains

* Epithelial cells usually do not express [PSA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspsa.html), [prostein / P501S](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprostein.html) or [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html)
* Stromal cells are negative for [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)

Sample pathology report

* Prostate and seminal vesicles, radical prostatectomy:
  + Cystadenoma of the seminal vesicle (see comment)
  + Comment: This is a benign neoplasia. Surgical resection is considered curative.

Differential diagnosis

* [Mixed epithelial and stromal tumor of the seminal vesicle](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostatemixedepithelialandstromal.html):
  + Stromal hypercellularity
* [Prostatic stromal tumor of uncertain malignant potential entrapping glands](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostatestromalprolif.html):
  + Centered in the prostate, entrapped prostatic glands express [PSA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspsa.html) and other markers of prostatic differentiation
* [Adenomatoid tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisepidadenomatoid.html):
  + Expresses mesothelial markers such as [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html)
* [Mesothelial cysts](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testiscysts.html):
  + Express mesothelial markers such as [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html)
* [Cystic ductal adenocarcinoma of the prostate](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostateprostaticduct.html):
  + Pseudostratified and atypical epithelial neoplastic cells
* [Primary seminal vesicle carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostatesemca.html):
  + Atypical cells
  + Tumor centered in a seminal vesicle
  + Adenocarcinomas usually show papillary glandular and trabecular growth patterns
  + Exclusion of other primary sites and immunophenotype excluding primary prostate adenocarcinoma

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/prostatecystadenomaAthanaziomicro04.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/prostatecystadenomaAthanaziomicro04.jpg)

What major criterion favors the diagnosis of mixed epithelial and stromal tumor of the seminal vesicle over cystadenoma of the seminal vesicle?

1. Expression of PSA in epithelial cells
2. Expression of smooth muscle actin in stromal cells
3. Stromal proliferation and hypercellularity
4. Tumor grossly centered in the seminal vesicle

Board review style answer #1

**C.** Stromal proliferation and hypercellularity. Seminal vesicle cystadenoma shows no stromal hypercellularity (image shown above).
