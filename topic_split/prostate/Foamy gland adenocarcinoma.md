Definition / general

* Rare variant with abundant foamy cytoplasm and minimal cytologic atypia ([Am J Surg Pathol 2001;25:618](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11342773), [Am J Surg Pathol 1996;20:419](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8604808), [Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:616](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9158690))
* Usually large volume, bilateral, extraprostatic extension
* Foamy appearance due to intracytoplasmic vesicles, not lipid or neutral mucin
* Aggressive behavior despite its benign histologic appearance

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Abundant xanthomatous cytoplasm, small hyperchromatic nuclei, minimal / no atypia, pink luminal secretions
* Hyperchromatic nuclei may make nucleoli difficult to see
* Cytoplasm differs between malignant and benign glands
* No obvious basal layer compared to normal glands
* Foamy morphology comprises most of cancer
* Usually Gleason score 3+3=6, occasionally Gleason 7 or higher ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:583](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19033862))
* Needle biopsies may have only a few atypical foamy glands ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2008;12:349](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18774498))

Positive stains

* Colloidal iron
* Alcian blue
* P504S ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:772](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12766580))

Negative stains

* Mucicarmine
* PAS
* Lipid

Electron microscopy description

* Intracytoplasmic vesicles
* Polyribosomes

Differential diagnosis

* [Clear cell cribriform hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostateclearcellcribhyper.html): basal cells readily identified
* [Cowper's glands](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostatecowpers.html): ducts often embedded in skeletal muscle
* **Gleason hypernephroid pattern 4**: cytoplasm is optically clear but not foamy
* [Mucinous metaplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostatemucousglandhyper.html): focal, cells positive for mucicarmine, PAS
