Definition / general

* Putative precursor of prostatic adenocarcinoma

Essential features

* Nucleoli visible at 200x magnification ([Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:873](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7611534))
* Surrogate marker for missed cancer in negative prostate biopsies; less useful in contemporary practice as lower incidence of unsampled prostate cancer with extended biopsy protocols
* No clinical utility in radical prostatectomies and biopsies containing foci suspicious or diagnostic of prostate cancer
* Would not account for raised serum PSA or radiological abnormality

Terminology

* Formerly known as severe dysplasia, PIN 2 / PIN 3 and carcinoma in situ
* PIN = high grade prostatic intraepithelial neoplasia (HGPIN); [low grade PIN](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostatelowgradePIN.html) is not reported due to wide interobserver variability ([Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:873](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7611534))

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8148/2](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/%0D%0A9789241548496_eng.pdf#page=199) - glandular intraepithelial
  neoplasia, high grade
* ICD-10: [D07.5](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D00-D09/D07-/D07.5) - carcinoma in situ of prostate
* ICD-11: [2E67.5](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f825422779) - carcinoma in situ of prostate

Epidemiology

* Needle biopsy prevalence: 0 - 25%, mean 7.7% ([Korean J Urol 2012;53:297](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22670187))
* Cystoprostatectomy prevalence: 11 - 34% ([BJU Int 2007;99:780](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17378841), [Urology 2007;70:1100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18158026), [Hum Pathol 2016;55:117](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27189342))
* Earlier age and more diffuse in African Americans, lower incidence in Asians ([Pathol Res Pract 1995;191:838](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8606862))

Sites

* Prostate gland

Clinical features

* Would not account for elevated serum PSA or abnormal digital examination findings or radiological abnormality

Diagnosis

* Diagnosis is made on histologic examination of biopsied prostate tissue, typically from a needle core biopsy

Radiology description

* No radiological abnormality

Prognostic factors

* Contemporary value of HGPIN as predictor of missed cancer is contentious
  + Risk of finding cancer on rebiopsy following unifocal HGPIN diagnosis is similar to benign diagnosis ([Pathology 2010;42:325](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20438403), [J Urol 2009;182:485](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19524976))
  + Risk of finding cancer on rebiopsy following multifocal HGPIN (2 or more cores) diagnosis is substantially higher at 30 - 40% ([Pathology 2010;42:325](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20438403), [J Urol 2009;182:485](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19524976), [Urol Oncol 2020;S1078-1439:30488](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33160846))
  + Cancers associated with HGPIN diagnosis on initial biopsy are more likely to be low grade and organ confined as compared to cancers diagnosed in the first biopsy without preexisting HGPIN ([Can J Urol 2015;22:8056](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26688133), [Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:1165](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21716083))
* Prostate cancer detected at rebiopsy following diagnosis of HGPIN generally low grade and organ confined ([Can J Urol 2015;22:8056](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26688133), [J Urol 2009;181:1069](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19150546), [Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:1165](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21716083))
  + Rebiopsy Gleason ≥ 7 rate following diagnosis of HGPIN in contemporary practice similar to that following an initial benign biopsy (17% versus 14%) ([Hum Pathol 2018;79:116](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29803813))

Case reports

* 60 year old man with foamy gland HGPIN ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:140](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10632499))
* 69 year old man with foamy gland HGPIN ([Human Pathology Case Reports 2017;10:32](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2214330017300093))

Treatment

* Follow up PSA
* Repeat biopsy if PSA remains elevated
* NNCCN guidelines (2021) recommendations for isolated HGPIN:
  + Focal (HGPIN in 1 site): follow-up as following benign biopsy
  + Multifocal (HGPIN in > 2 sites): If extended biopsies performed initially then rebiopsy only those at high risk of more aggressive prostate cancer ([NNCCN.org: National Comprehensive Cancer Network Clinical Guidelines in Oncology - Prostate Cancer Early Detection, 2021 [Accessed 19 February 2021]](https://www.nccn.org/professionals/physician_gls/default.aspx))
* No therapy is needed for HGPIN as an isolated finding in needle biopsy ([Mod Pathol 2018;31:S71](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29297491))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Diagnostic feature: prominent nucleoli visible at 200x or lower magnification
* Medium to large ducts and acini with enlarged hyperchromatic nuclei and amphophilic cytoplasm
* Common architectural patterns:
  + Flat: 1 - 2 layers of simple epithelium
  + Tufted: stratified epithelium with small luminal protrusions
  + Micropapillary: filliform structures lacking true fibrovascular core
  + Cribriform: epithelial proliferation with punched out spaces
    - In contemporary practice, most experts report such proliferations as atypical intraductal proliferation suspicious for intraductal carcinoma of the prostate (IDC-P), particularly on needle biopsy ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2020 Jun 26 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32589068))
* Less common patterns: signet ring cell, foamy gland, mucinous, inverted and small cell neuroendocrine ([Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:1215](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9331295), [Rev Urol 2004;6:171](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16985598), [Oncol Lett 2015;10:2395](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26622858))

Positive stains

* Basal cells: high molecular weight cytokeratin ([34 beta E12 / CK903](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck34be12.html)), [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html); may be discontinuous
* Acinar cells: [P504S / AMACR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsamacr.html)
* [PTEN](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspten.html): normal retained pattern ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:169](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25517949))

Negative stains

* ERG could be positive in rare cases of isolated HGPIN (see [ERG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsERG.html), [Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:608](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21383613))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Deletions of 8p most common allelic loss; telomere shortening activity similar to prostatic adenocarcinoma
* Other chromosomal abnormalities in both HGPIN and carcinoma include gains of chromosomes 7, 8, 10 and 12 ([Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:506](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7537024), [Rev Urol 2004;6:171](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16985598))
* *TMPRSS* - *ERG fusion* ([Science 2005;310:644](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16254181), [Clin Cancer Res 2008;14:3380](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18519767))
  + Found in approximately 50% of prostate carcinoma
  + Present in approximately 20% of HGPIN admixed with adenocarcinoma
  + Less common in HGPIN not associated with invasive adenocarcinoma
* Hypermethylation *GSPT1*, the most common epigenetic change in prostate carcinoma, is also seen in HGPIN
* Other genetic features of HGPIN
  + Aneuploidy (present in approximately 50%)
  + Increased expression of CDKN2A, TP53, tumor suppressor genes involved in cell cycle regulation and *MYC* oncogene amplification ([Clin Cancer Res 2001;7:544](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11297246), [Mod Pathol 1997;10:1113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9388062))

Sample pathology report

* Prostate, left lateral mid, needle core biopsy:
  + High grade prostatic intraepithelial neoplasia

Differential diagnosis

* **Prostatic central zone glands**:
  + Increased architectural complexity (cribriform / micropapillary)
  + Cytologically bland with inconspicuous nucleoli
  + Central / basal location
* [Ejaculatory duct epithelium / seminal vesicle epithelium](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostateseminalves.html):
  + May show cribriform architecture
  + Increased nuclear pleomorphism and cytoplasmic golden lipofuscin pigment (degenerative phenomena)
  + Usually immunonegative for prostatic markers
* [Urothelial metaplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostateurothelialmet.html):
  + No architectural complexity
  + No prominent nucleoli
* **Inflammatory reactive atypia**:
  + No architectural complexity
  + Inflammatory cells including polymorphs
  + Reactive nuclear features
* [Radiation atypia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixradiationatypia.html):
  + No architectural complexity
  + Nuclear pleomorphism but no macronucleoli
  + Basal cells prominent
* [Clear cell cribriform hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostateclearcellcribhyper.html):
  + Clear cytoplasm
  + Inconspicuous nucleoli and lacks nucleomegaly
* [Basal cell hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostatebch.html):
  + Proliferation of peripheral basal cells showing prominent nucleoli
  + Central secretory cells lack nucleoli
  + Basal cell markers demonstrate basal cell predominance
* **PIN-like carcinoma**:
  + Architecture resembling HGPIN
  + Greater crowding / dilated glands lined by columnar pseudostratified cells
  + May show cystically dilated glands lined by ductal type epithelium
  + Immunonegative for basal cell markers ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:1693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30138215))
* [Pattern 4 cribriform acinar adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostategrading.html):
  + Confluent, increased architectural complexity
  + Immunonegative for basal cell markers
* [Ductal carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostateprostaticduct.html):
  + Peri-urethral or peripheral prostatic ductal location
  + Expansive, complex cribriform / papillary / solid architecture
  + Tall columnar cells, oval pleomorphic nuclei
  + Immunonegative for basal cell markers ([Med Princ Pract 2010;19:82](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19996627))
* [Intraductal carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostateidc.html):
  + Solid or dense cribriform architecture
  + Loose cribriform or micropapillary architecture with either marked nuclear atypia or comedonecrosis ([Virchows Arch 2019;474:525](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30825003), [Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:e67](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26848801))
  + Markedly distended glands ([Mod Pathol 2018;31:S71](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29297491),  [Mod Pathol 2006;19:1528](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16980940))
* [HGPIN with adjacent atypical small glands (PINATYP)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostatePINATYP.html):
  + Unresolved differential of tangential sectioning / budding of HGPIN and invasive adenocarcinoma ([Hum Pathol 2001;32:389](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11331955))
  + [AMACR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsamacr.html) immunohistochemistry unhelpful: generally positive in both scenarios ([Histopathology 2005;47:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15982318))
