# Mucinous colloid adenocarcinoma

Mucinous (colloid) adenocarcinoma

Definition / general

* Uncommon ( Intra and extracellular mucin must comprise 25%+ of tumor
* May be more hormone independent, less responsive to radiation therapy
* Aggressive biologic behavior ([Am J Surg Pathol 1985;9:299](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2409826), [Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:468](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2409826), [Mod Pathol 2004;17:316](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14976541))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Tumor cells float in pools of mucin
* Resembles mucinous (colloid) carcinoma of breast
* Has microglandular, cribriform, comedo, solid and hypernephroid patterns
* Signet ring cells are rare

Positive stains

* PSA, PAP, MUC2 ([Mod Pathol 2008;21:789](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18487999))

Differential diagnosis

* Extension of large bowel carcinoma, Cowpers gland carcinoma
* Mucinous adenocarcinoma of bladder (PSA-, PAP+)
* Post-hormone treatment changes which leave behind acellular pools of mucin ([Am J Surg Pathol 1998;22:347](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9500777))
