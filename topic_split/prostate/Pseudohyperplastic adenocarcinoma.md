Definition / general

* Rare carcinoma that resembles benign hyperplastic glands
* Difficult to grade
* Epstein recommends deferring to the grade of the associated usual type adenocarcinoma in the radical prostatectomy specimen, which is often Gleason score 5-7
* False negative diagnostic rate was 1.3% for TURP specimens ([Pseudo Oncol Res 2003;9:232](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14688829))
* 60% of tumor has benign architectural but malignant nuclear features ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:1039](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10935644))

Case reports

* 70 year old man ( [Case #117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case2008117.htm))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Papillary infoldings (100%), crowded glands, large atypical glands (95%), nuclear enlargement (95%), pink amorphous secretions (70%), occasional to frequent nucleoli (45%), branching (45%), crystalloids (45%), corpora amylacea (20%); at low power, transition to typical, small acinar adenocarcinoma may be helpful ([Am J Surg Pathol 1998;22:1239](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9777986))
* With core needle biopsy, only diagnostic clue may be a subtle disruption of the normal glandular - stromal relationship ([Mod Pathol 2004;17:307](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14739905), [Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:35](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19935058))
* Despite its benign appearance, may be associated with intermediate grade cancer and can exhibit aggressive behavior

Positive stains

* P504S in 70% of cases ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:772](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12766580), [Histopathology 2006;48:668](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16681682))

Negative stains

* High molecular weight keratin and p63 show absence of basal cells in hyperplastic glands (normal glands serve as positive control)

Differential diagnosis

* Adenosis, high grade PIN (not as crowded or infiltrative), benign hyperplasia
