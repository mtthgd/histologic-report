Definition / general

* Salivary gland carcinoma, NOS (not otherwise specified) represents a heterogeneous spectrum of carcinomas forming epithelial, ductal or glandular structures; it is a diagnosis of exclusion, specifically exclusive of otherwise defined salivary gland carcinoma entities ([Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:753](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33284193))

Essential features

* Nonspecific heterogeneous spectrum of carcinomas forming epithelial, ductal or glandular structures
* Diagnosis of exclusion, specifically exclusive of otherwise defined salivary gland carcinoma entities
* 2 recognized subtypes: oncocytic adenocarcinoma and intestinal type adenocarcinoma
  + Oncocytic adenocarcinoma: oncocytic aspect with RA-
  + Intestinal type adenocarcinoma: intestinal aspect with CDX2+ / CK20+
* Prognostic factors: histological grade (3 grades), histological subtype and clinical stage

Terminology

* Not recommended: unclassified adenocarcinoma, ductal ​adenocarcinoma, (papillary) cystadenocarcinoma
* Subtypes: oncocytic adenocarcinoma, intestinal type adenocarcinoma

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8140/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=65) - adenocarcinoma, NOS
* ICD-11

  + [2B68.Z](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#480928417%2Funspecified) - malignant neoplasms of submandibular or sublingual glands, unspecified
  + [2B60.Z](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#304997495%2Funspecified) - malignant neoplasms of lip, unspecified
  + [2B65.Z](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#1881533064%2Funspecified) - malignant neoplasms of palate, unspecified
  + [2B66.Z](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#76069495%2Funspecified) - malignant neoplasms of other or unspecified parts of mouth, unspecified

Epidemiology

* Incidence is only 5 - 10% due to identification of new types of salivary adenocarcinoma ([Oral Oncol 2011;47:677](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21612974), [Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:753](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33284193))
* Average age: 58 years with a wide age range; extremely rare in children ([Oral Oncol 2002;38:706](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12167424), [Oral Oncol 2011;47:677](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21612974), [Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:753](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33284193))

Sites

* > 50% arise in the parotid gland
* 40% arise in minor glands, most often in the hard palate, buccal mucosa and lips
* References: [Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol Oral Radiol Endod 2007;104:94](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17577550), [Oral Oncol 2002;38:706](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12167424), [Oral Oncol 2011;47:677](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21612974), [Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:753](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33284193)

Pathophysiology

* Unknown

Etiology

* Unknown

Diagnosis

* Salivary carcinoma lacking morphological, immunohistochemical and molecular features of other salivary carcinomas
* Exclusion of metastasis is mandatory ([ORL J Otorhinolaryngol Relat Spec 2001;63:233](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11408820))

Prognostic factors

* Histological grade: 15 year survival rates for low, intermediate and high grade tumors of 54%, 31% and 3%, respectively ([Am J Surg 1982;144:423](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7125074), [Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:753](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33284193))
* Histological subtype
  + Significant cystic component is more favorable ([Am J Surg Pathol 1996;20:1440](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8944036))
  + Intestinal type adenocarcinoma shows more aggressive behavior ([Head Neck 2013;35:E234](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23108630), [Hum Pathol 2009;40:1798](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19695679))
* Clinical stage ([Head Neck 2016;38:1208](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27166875), [Cancer 2022;128:509](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34661906))

Case reports

* 32 year old woman with adenocarcinoma of accessory parotid, metastasis to lung and complete response to nivolumab treatment ([In Vivo 2022;36:2840](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36309400))
* 53 year old woman with an anterior tongue mass of salivary carcinoma, NOS, intestinal type adenocarcinoma ([Head Neck Pathol 2017;11:234](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27357134))
* 54 year old woman with rapid enlargement of the right parotid gland ([Autops Case Rep 2022;12:e2021357](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35252049))
* 54 year old man presented with symptoms of fullness in his tongue, diagnosed as salivary carcinoma, NOS, intestinal type adenocarcinoma ([Head Neck Pathol 2017;11:186](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27752836))
* 61 year old man presented with a swelling in the left lower cheek, diagnosed as oncocytic adenocarcinoma of parotid gland ([Acta Cytol 2003;47:1099](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14674089))
* 66 year old woman with a case of oncocytic adenocarcinoma arising in parotid gland ([World J Surg Oncol 2006;4:54](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16923179))
* 68 year old woman with previous history of high grade adenocarcinoma of the parotid and metastasis to uterine leiomyoma ([Cureus 2020;12:e6789](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32140349))

Treatment

* Surgical procedure for salivary carcinoma, NOS ([Cancer 2022;128:509](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34661906), [J Clin Oncol 2021;39:1909](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33900808), [ESMO Open 2022;7:100602](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36567082))
  + Complete removal in resectable case
  + In cN0 patients with high grade or in cT3 - T4: elective treatment of the neck with either elective nodal dissection or elective neck irradiation since salivary carcinoma, NOS has a > 20% rate of clinically positive nodal involvement at presentation ([Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 2016;154:121](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26419838))
* Postradiotherapy ([Cancer 2022;128:509](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34661906), [J Clin Oncol 2021;39:1909](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33900808))
  + In high grade tumors: positive margins, perineural invasion, lymph node metastases, lymphatic or vascular invasion, T3 - T4 tumors or those that are not candidates for surgical resection
  + Can be considered in cases of tumors with close margins or intermediate grade tumors
  + Elective neck irradiation in patients with cN0 disease for the following indications: T3 - T4 cancers or high grade malignancies or those that are not candidates for surgical resection
* Chemotherapy ([Cancer 2022;128:509](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34661906), [J Clin Oncol 2021;39:1909](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33900808), [Head Neck 2016;38:1208](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27166875), [ESMO Open 2022;7:100602](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36567082))
  + May be considered for initiation of systemic therapy in the following circumstances
    - Metastatic deposits are symptomatic and not amenable to palliative local therapy
    - Growth has the potential to compromise organ function
    - Lesions have increased in size > 20% in the preceding 6 months
* Immunotherapy ([Cancer 2022;128:509](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34661906),[J Clin Oncol 2021;39:1909](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33900808), [In Vivo 2022;36:2840](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36309400))
  + Not routinely offered at this time except for patients with selected molecular alteration (high tumor mutational burden, MSI-H)

Gross description

* Tumors are partly circumscribed but usually irregular with infiltrative appearance
* Cut surface of tumors: commonly tan or yellow, sometimes with areas of necrosis and hemorrhage ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2004;128:1385](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15578883))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Lacks the characteristic histomorphology of other salivary adenocarcinomas
* Wide range of ductal or glandular proliferation with or without a cystic component or necrosis ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2004;128:1385](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15578883))
* Architectural variety of growth patterns: glandular, papillary, cystic, cribriform, solid, lobular, nest-like, strand-like differentiation and small confluent nests or cords, large islands ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2004;128:1385](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15578883))
* Variety of tumor cells: cuboidal, columnar, polygonal, clear, mucinous, oncocytoid or plasmacytoid ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2004;128:1385](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15578883))
* Proof of an intraductal component favors salivary origin ([Virchows Arch 2006;449:159](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16673121))
* Subtypes
  + Oncocytic ([Am J Surg Pathol 1996;20:1440](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8944036))
  + Intestinal type ([Head Neck Pathol 2012;6:250](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21948243), [Head Neck 2013;35:E234](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23108630))

Cytology description

* Smears show adenocarcinoma usually with high grade nuclei in a necrotic background and lacking features of other salivary gland carcinomas, resulting in a differential diagnosis of metastases from colon and lung
* Oncocytic and intestinal types are recognized ([Am J Surg 1982;144:423](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7125074))

Positive stains

* [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html)+ / [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html)- ([Mod Pathol 2004;17:407](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14976534))
* [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html)+ / [p40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp40.html)+ in oncocytic subtype: predominately in basal cell type distribution ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:409](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18971778), [Head Neck Pathol 2007;1:123](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20614263))
* [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html)+ / [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html)+ in intestinal type subtype ([Head Neck 2013;35:E234](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23108630), [Head Neck Pathol 2012;6:250](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21948243))
* **MMR** proficient

Negative stains

* IHC is helpful in distinguishing from
  + Acinic cell carcinoma: positive for [NR4A3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnr4a3.html) and [DOG1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsDOG1.html) ([Pathol Res Pract 2002;198:777](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12608654%22))
  + Salivary duct carcinoma: positive for [androgen receptor (AR)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsAR.html) ([Clin Cancer Res 2014;20:6570](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25316813), [Head Neck Pathol 2013;7:S48](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23821208), [Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1070](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12883239))
  + Context of metastasis: positive for site specific markers ([Mod Pathol 2004;17:407](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14976534))

Electron microscopy description

* For oncocytic subtype: abundant mitochondria to the exclusion of most other organelles
* Mitochondria are varied in shape from spherical to ovoid, with elongated, lamelliform cristae
* Rough endoplasmic reticulum and free ribosomes are rare ([Acta Cytol 2003;47:1099](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14674089))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* No specific molecular alterations
* Not needed for diagnosis ([Clin Cancer Res 2016;22:6061](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27334835))

Sample pathology report

* Parotid gland, left, inferior, partial parotidectomy:
  + Adenocarcinoma carcinoma, NOS, 2 cm (see comment)
  + Comment: There is a lobulated and unencapsulated biphasic primary neoplasm. The tumor shows a nodular growth pattern. The tumor is composed of a bilayered arrangement of small to large luminal cells with eosinophilic cytoplasm and outer myoepithelial cells. Immunohistochemically, the inner epithelial cells are positive for AE1 / AE3 and EMA, while the outer myoepithelial layer shows staining for p63, smooth muscle actin and S100. The rest of the staining is negative for CK20, DOG1, AR and PLAG1 / HMGA2. There is no evidence of necrosis or high grade transformation. The molecular analysis is negative for *PLAG1, HMGA2, MAML2, PRKD* fusions and *PRKD1* mutation.

Differential diagnosis

* [Salivary duct carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandssalivaryductcarcinoma.html) ([Clin Cancer Res 2014;20:6570](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25316813), [Head Neck Pathol 2013;7:S48](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23821208), [Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1070](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12883239)):
  + Generally high grade morphology with comedonecrosis
  + Oncocytic variant pure or not: less atypical aspect
  + Mucin rich variant: rarely prominent
  + [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html)- in invasion component, [AR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsAR.html)+
* [Mucoepidermoid carcinoma, oncocytic variant](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsMEC.html):
  + Few mucocytes seen
  + [p40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp40.html)+ / [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html)+, [AR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsAR.html)-, **PS100**-, commonly [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html)-
  + Commonly *MAML2* gene fusion
* [Polymorphous adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsPLGA.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1526](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27454943), [Cancer Cytopathol 2018;126:275](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29266837)):
  + Mostly in minor salivary glands or seromucous glands of the upper aerodigestive tract
  + Tumor proliferation with cytological uniformity, histological diversity and an infiltrative growth pattern
  + Foci of oncocytic, clear, squamous, apocrine or mucous cells
  + [p40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp40.html)- / [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html)+
  + *PRKD1* p.Glu710Asp hotspot mutation or translocation of one of the *PRKD1, PRKD2* or *PRKD3* genes
* **High grade transformation** of other types of **salivary carcinoma** ([Mod Pathol 2004;17:407](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14976534)):
  + High grade morphology
  + Specific molecular alteration of other types of salivary carcinoma (*PLAG1, HMGA2, MAML2*, etc.)
* [Mucinous adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsmucinousadeno.html) ([Histopathology 2010;57:395](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20738418), [Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:1337](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33739781)):
  + Most common in intraoral minor salivary glands
  + Significant mucinous differentiation with abundant intracellular or extracellular mucin
  + [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html)+, [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html)-
  + Commonly *AKT1* p.E17K mutation
* [Metastasis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsmetastases.html) from other body sites (e.g., lung, colon) ([StatPearls: Malignant Salivary Gland Tumors [Accessed 28 March 2025]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33079504)):
  + [Colorectal carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumoradenocarcinoma.html) is positive for [villin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvillin.html), [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html) and [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html) and negative for [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html) and [MYB](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyb.html) ([World J Clin Cases 2021;9:5238](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34307573))

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/salivaryglandsadenocarcinomaNOSleon02.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/salivaryglandsadenocarcinomaNOSleon02.jpg)

Which of the following statements is true regarding salivary carcinoma, NOS?

1. It is defined by its peculiar morphological, immunohistochemical and molecular features
2. It is the most common differential diagnosis of mucoepidermoid carcinoma
3. Nodal dissection is systematic regardless of the imaging
4. Oncocytic subtype is the most aggressive
5. *TP53* mutation is never identified

Board review style answer #1

**B.** It is the most common differential diagnosis of mucoepidermoid carcinoma. Answer C is incorrect because elective nodal dissection is performed if the imaging study identifies lymph nodes suspected of metastasis in low grade carcinoma. Answer D is incorrect because the oncocytic subtype is commonly associated with low grade carcinomas. Answer A is incorrect because the oncocytic subtype is defined by its lack of peculiar morphological, immunohistochemical and molecular features. Answer E is incorrect because *TP53* mutation can be identified, along with others such as *PI3KCA, NRAS* and *HRAS*.

Board review style question #2

Which of the following statements is true regarding salivary carcinoma, NOS?

1. Abundant intracellular or extracellular mucin can be observed
2. CK20 is not consistently found
3. Lymph node metastases never occur
4. Principal differential diagnosis of metastatic colorectal carcinoma must be addressed
5. This histological subtype is specific for MMR deficiency

Board review style answer #2

**D.** The principal differential diagnosis of metastatic colorectal carcinoma must be addressed. Answer B is incorrect because CK20, like CDX2, is commonly observed as positive in immunostaining. Answer A is incorrect because only mucinous adenocarcinoma exhibits these features with significant mucinous differentiation, abundant intracellular or extracellular mucin, CK7+ / CK20- and commonly the *AKT1* p.E17K mutation. Answer C is incorrect because lymph node metastases can occur. Answer E is incorrect because MMR proficiency is identified, in contrast to the GI tract.
