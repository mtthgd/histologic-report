Definition / general

* Many recent advances in salivary gland molecular alterations
* Molecular testing is not generally needed for diagnosis but may be helpful in histologically difficult cases, small biopsies and cytology specimens ([Cancer Cytopathol 2018;126:627](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30156767))

Essential features

* Genetic alterations include rearrangements, fusions and amplifications
* Most are of diagnostic value
* *ERB2* amplification in salivary duct carcinoma is a treatment marker for HER2 therapy ([Ther Adv Med Oncol 2022;14:17588359221119538](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36090801))
* Secretory carcinoma may be treated with tyrosine receptor kinase (TRK) inhibitors ([Head Neck 2023;45:1315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36859797))

Treatment

* Trastuzumab given to patients with recurrent or metastatic salivary duct carcinoma that overexpresses HER2 with demonstrated survival benefit ([Ther Adv Med Oncol 2022;14:17588359221119538](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36090801))
  + This treatment may be supplemented with androgen deprivation therapy if the tumor is androgen receptor positive by immunohistochemistry
* Secretory carcinoma may be treated with TRK inhibitors including larotrectinib and entrectinib ([Head Neck 2023;45:1315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36859797))
* NOTCH1 inhibitors for adenoid cystic carcinoma are in the clinical trial phase

Immunohistochemistry

* Immunostains commercially available as surrogate markers ([Acta Histochem Cytochem 2012;45:269](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23209336), [CA Cancer J Clin 2023;73:597](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37490348))
  + [PLAG1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsplag1.html), [HMGA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsHMGA2.html) for pleomorphic adenoma
  + [MYB](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyb.html) for adenoid cystic carcinoma (must be diffuse positivity)
    - In situ hybridization is also available
  + [NOTCH1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/molecularnotch1.html) positive in aggressive adenoid cystic carcinoma
  + [Androgen receptor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsAR.html) and [HER2 / neu](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbreasther2.html) for salivary duct carcinoma
  + [NR4A3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnr4a3.html) for acinic cell carcinoma
  + [Beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html) aberrant nuclear staining sometimes seen in basal cell neoplasms
  + [PTEN](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspten.html) loss of expression (mutant phenotype) in sclerosing polycystic adenoma
  + [Pan-TRK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsntrk.html) nuclear positivity in secretory carcinoma
    - May not show strong expression

Molecular / cytogenetics description

**Benign neoplasms**

* [Sclerosing polycystic adenoma (SPA)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandssclerosingpolycysticadenoma.html)
  + PI3K / PTEN / AKT pathway alterations
    - *PIK3CA* activating mutations stimulate AKT signaling and growth factor independent proliferation
    - Loss of *PTEN* tumor suppressor expression leads to cell growth
* [Sialadenoma papilliferum (SP)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandssialadenoma.html)
  + *BRAF* V600E mutation
    - Part of mitogen activated protein kinase (MAPK) pathway; activation leads to cell proliferation

**Benign and malignant neoplasms**

* [Basal cell adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsbasalcelladenoma.html) / [basal cell adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsbasalcelladenocarcinoma.html)
  + *CTNNB1* mutations
    - Mutation leads to aberrant translocation to nucleus and gene transcription
  + *CYLD* mutations in membranous variant
* [Pleomorphic adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandspleomorphicadenoma.html), atypical adenoma, [carcinoma ex pleomorphic adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsmalignantmixedtumor.html) ([Genet Med 2018;20:250](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28796236))
  + Pleomorphic adenoma gene 1 (*PLAG1*) rearrangements (8q12)
    - Binds insulin-like growth factor 2 (IGF2) promoter, leading to gene transcription
  + High mobility group AT hook 2 (*HMGA2*) rearrangements (12q14-15)
    - Oncogenic transcription factor that upregulates *PLAG1*
  + *TP53* (17p13.1) mutations in carcinoma ex pleomorphic adenoma
* [Myoepithelioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsmyoepithelioma.html) / [myoepithelial carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsmyoepithelialcarcinoma.html)
  + 8q12 *PLAG1* fusions
  + t(12,22)(q21;q12) *EWSR1::ATF1* fusions

**Malignant neoplasms**

* [Acinic cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsaciniccell.html)
  + 9q31 nuclear receptor subfamily 4 group A member 3 (*NR4A3*) fusion / activation
    - Mutation leads to gene transcription
  + 9q31.1 *MSANTD3* fusion / amplification
* [Adenoid cystic carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsadenoidcystic.html)
  + *MYB* translocation: t(6;9) → *MYB::NFIB*
    - *MYB* acts as coactivator to increase *NR4A3* transcription
    - Promotes proliferation genes such as *MYC*
    - Promotes expression of microRNAs that repress expression of tumor suppressor genes
  + Subset with t(8;9) → *MYBL1::NFIB*
  + Activating *NOTCH1* mutations (9q34.3)
    - Notch1 into active fragment, ICN1
    - ICN1 is transferred into the nucleus and promotes gene transcription of Hes1, c-Myc, interleukin 7 receptor α chain (IL7Rα) and insulin-like growth factor 1 receptor (IGF1R)
    - This leads to cell proliferation
* [Clear cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsclearcellcarcinoma.html)
  + t(12;22) → *EWSR1::ATF1*
* [Cribriform adenocarcinoma of the minor salivary gland](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsPLGA.html)
  + *PRKD1, PRKD2* and *PRKD3* rearrangements
* [Epithelial / myoepithelial carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsepimyocarcinoma.html)
  + 11p15.5 *HRAS* mutations
  + 8q12 *PLAG1* fusion
  + 12q14 *HMGA2* fusion
* [Intraductal carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsintraductalsalivary.html)
  + Most have *RET* gene rearrangements, including *NCOA4::RET* and *TRIM27::RET*
  + *BRAF* V600E mutations identified in oncocytic subtype
* [Microsecretory adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsmicrosecretoryadeno.html)
  + t(5q14.3)(18q11.2) *MEF2C::SS18* fusion
* [Mucoepidermoid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsMEC.html)
  + *MAML2* (mastermind-like protein) rearrangements
    - t(11;19) → *CRTC1::MAML2* or t(11;15) → *CRTC3::MAML2*
    - Help distinguish this entity from histologic mimickers including [Warthin tumor with mucinous metaplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandswarthin.html) and [squamous cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsSCC.html)
    - Fusion encodes chimeric protein that leads to ligand independent NOTCH signaling and transcription of genes leading to proliferation
  + *CDKN2A* deletion (9p21.3)
* [Mucinous adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsmucinousadeno.html)
  + *AKT1* E17K mutation
* [Polymorphous adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsPLGA.html)
  + *PRKD1* (14q12) point mutations, especially E710D hotspot mutations
  + In cases with extensive cribriform features, *PRKD2* (19q13.2), *PRKD3* (2p22.2) are also noted
* [Salivary duct carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandssalivaryductcarcinoma.html)
  + *HER2* (17q21.1) amplification in about 30 - 40% of cases
    - May be identified by HER2 immunohistochemistry, fluorescence in situ hybridization or chromogenic in situ hybridization
    - Interpretation guidelines same as for breast carcinoma ([Ther Adv Med Oncol 2022;14:17588359221119538](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36090801))
  + 17p13.1 *TP53* mutation most common
  + Xq12 androgen receptor copy gain, especially with oncocytic morphology
  + 3q26.32 *NCOA4::RET*
  + inv(10)(q11.21q11.22) *PIK3CA* mutation
  + 10q23.31 *PTEN* loss
  + 9p21.3 *CDKN2A* loss
* [Secretory carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandssecretory.html) (formerly mammary analogue secretory carcinoma)
  + *ETV6* fusions
    - t(12;15) → *ETV6::NTRK3* fusion most common
      * Leads to downstream activation of mitogen activated protein kinase (MAPK), cell differentiation and survival
    - Also t(10;12) → *ETV6::RET* and t(12;7) → *ETV6::MET* and t(10;10) → *VIM::RET* fusion

Board review style question #1

Which of the following is characteristic of mucoepidermoid carcinoma and not squamous cell carcinoma?

1. *ETV6* gene rearrangements
2. *EWSR* fusions
3. *HER2* amplification
4. *MAML2* gene rearrangements

Board review style answer #1

**D.** *MAML2* gene rearrangements are seen in mucoepidermoid carcinoma but not squamous cell carcinoma. Answer C is incorrect because *HER2* amplification is seen in salivary duct carcinoma. Answer B is incorrect because *EWSR* fusions are seen in clear cell carcinoma. Answer A is incorrect because *ETV6* gene rearrangements are seen in secretory carcinoma.
