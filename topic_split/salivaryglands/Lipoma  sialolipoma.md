Definition / general

* Lipoma:
  + Benign tumor; rare in salivary glands but usually involves parotid gland ([Laryngol Rhinol Otol 1986;65:485](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3784727))
  + More common than lipomatosis
  + Clinical findings are nonspecific ([J Oral Maxillofac Surg 2006;64:1583](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17052582))
* Sialolipoma:
  + Uncommon lipoma variant composed of mature adipose tissue mixed with acinar, ductal, basal and myoepithelial cells of normal salivary gland
  + First described in 2001 by Nagao ([Histopathology 2001;38:30](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11135044))
* Lipoadenoma:
  + Slow growing tumor with glandular structures with sertoliform features and adipose tissue; oncocytic and sebaceous differentiation
  + Initially described by Yau in 1997 ([Mod Pathol 1997;10:242](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9071732))

Clinical features

* Lipoma:
  + 3% of parotid tumors - #2 most common benign mesenchymal neoplasms of major salivary glands (#1 is schwannoma)
  + Often incidental ([Rev Stomatol Chir Maxillofac 1988;89:117](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3165545))
  + Ages 40+, usually men, occasionally children
* Sialolipoma:
  + Mean age 61 years but wide age range at presentation
  + Female gender predominance for minor salivary gland location
  + Parotid and submandibular glands, hard and soft palate
  + May be due to entrapment of salivary gland elements by lipoma
  + Benign behavior, no recurrences reported

Treatment

* Simple excision, usually do not recur ([J Korean Med Sci 1996;11:522](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9008102), [Arch Otolaryngol 1976;102:230](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1267707), [Ann Diagn Pathol 2011;15:6](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20952301))

Gross description

* Well circumscribed, resembles lipoma at other sites
* Median 2 cm (range, 1 - 4 cm)

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Lipoma: bland appearing adipose tissue; osteolipoma or angiolipoma variants exist
* Lipoadenoma: mature adipose cells (> 90% mass) and proliferated glandular tissue (sharply demarcated, duct - acinar units or proliferated glands, may resemble sertoliform tubules), oncocytic change, sebaceous differentiation, squamous metaplasia
* Sialolipoma:
  + Mature adipose tissue mixed with acinar, ductal, basal and myoepithelial cells of normal salivary gland
  + Also duct ectasia with fibrosis, prominent lymphoid infiltrates with nodular aggregates in stroma, oncocytic changes, sebaceous differentiation
  + Vascular variant is sialoangiolipoma

Cytology description

* Nonspecific findings ([Eur Arch Otorhinolaryngol 2008;265:S47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17934743)); spindle cell lipomas have bland appearing spindle cells in myxoid background, CD34+ mature fat cells; S100 may be negative

Immunohistochemistry & special stains

* Oncocytic lipoadenoma:
  + Positive for [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html), [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), [CK19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck19.html), [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [CK14](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck14.html), [CK5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5.html) / [6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck6.html), [alpha-1-antichymotrypsin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalpha1antichymo.html) ([Virchows Arch 2006;449:722](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17091251))
  + Negative for [calponin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalponin.html), [actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsactin.html)
  + Dual epithelial population with ductal ([CK19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck19.html), [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html)) and basal cell ([p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html), [CK14](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck14.html), [CK5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5.html) / [6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck6.html)) differentiation in oncocytic areas

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* t(12,14) / *HMGA* rearrangements

Differential diagnosis

* [Pleomorphic adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandspleomorphicadenoma.html): may have extensive lipometaplasia / lipomatosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2005;29:1389](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16160483))
