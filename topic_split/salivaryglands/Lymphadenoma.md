Definition / general

* Rare benign tumor with nests and islands of bland epithelium composed in part of sebaceous elements, with lymphoid stroma
* Called sebaceous lymphadenoma if lymphoid stroma is prominent
* Also called benign lymphoepithelial cyst with sebaceous differentiation

Clinical features

* 0.1% of salivary gland neoplasms, < 0.5% of salivary adenomas
* Over 90% occur in or near the parotid gland or in minor salivary glands
* May arise from salivary duct inclusions within parotid lymph node, similar to Warthin tumor ([Am J Clin Pathol 1980;74:683](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7446474))
* Fine needle aspiration cytology identifies a benign process but usually not diagnosed prior to excision ([Acta Otorhinolaryngol Ital 2007;27:144](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17883193))

Case reports

* 53 year old woman with progressively enlarging, painless parotid mass ([University of Pittsburgh: Parotid Gland Mass [Accessed 19 March 2018]](http://path.upmc.edu/cases/case281.html))
* 57 year old man with neck mass ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2005;129:e171](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15974831))
* 67 year old woman with parotid mass ([Acta Otorhinolaryngol Ital 2007;27:147](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17883194))
* 68 year old woman with synchromous squamous cell carcinoma ([World J Surg Oncol 2003;1:30](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14693033))
* 86 year old man with stable parotid mass ([Case #103](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case2007103.htm))
* Benign tumor with transition to sebaceous adenocarcinoma ([Eur Arch Otorhinolaryngol 2006;263:940](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16786364))

Treatment

* Excision is curative, no / rare recurrences, rare malignant transformation ([Eur Arch Otorhinolaryngol 2006;263:940](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16786364))

Gross description

* Solid or cystic, well circumscribed, tan-yellow mass, up to 3 cm, with variable encapsulation

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Benign, encapsulated, solid and cystic ([Mod Pathol 2012;25:26](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21892186))
* Nests and islands of benign squamous cells, often lining a cyst; epithelial nests have focal sebaceous differentiation
* Background is prominent lymphoid infiltrate, often with germinal centers
* May be associated foreign body reaction, histiocytes, oncocytic change
* Rarely malignant change to sebaceous carcinoma
* May be combined / hybrid / synchronous tumors with Warthin tumor, acinic cell carcinoma, adenoid cystic carcinoma, squamous cell carcinoma ([Eur J Cancer B Oral Oncol 1996;32B:251](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8776422), [Pathol Res Pract 1993;189:577](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8378181), [J Oral Surg 1979;37:826](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/226666))

Cytology description

* Mixed population of large and small lymphocytes, plasma cells and occasional tingible body macrophages
* 3 dimensional, cohesive aggregates of epithelial cells, often with cytoplasmic vacuoles characteristic of sebaceous differentiation, surrounded by layers of basaloid cells ([Acta Cytol 2004;48:551](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15296345))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* May have trisomy 9 ([Oncol Rep 1994;1:561](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21607404))

Differential diagnosis

* [Low grade mucoepidermoid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsMEC.html): epithelial islands, ducts and cysts tend to be haphazardly distributed with variable shapes and sizes; usually infiltration of connective tissue or parenchyma; cells have some atypia, cells are [mucin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmucins.html)+
* **Normal sebaceous glands**: present in 10% of parotid glands but no mass
* [Warthin tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandswarthin.html): prominent cysts and lymphoid stroma, cysts have bilayered oncocytic epithelium
