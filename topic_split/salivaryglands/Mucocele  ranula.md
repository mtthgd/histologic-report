Definition / general

* Nonneoplastic cyst-like, mucus accumulation of the salivary gland ([Acta Cytol 2022;66:359](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35717936))
* Note: this chapter is referring specifically to extravasation type mucoceles; retention type mucoceles are covered under the separate [salivary duct cyst](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/oralcavitysalivaryductcyst.html) chapter

Essential features

* Mucocele, sialocele or ranula is extravasation of mucin ([Otolaryngol Clin North Am 2021;54:543](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34024482))
* Cyst-like structure surrounded by granulation tissue with no cyst lining epithelium consists of abundant mucin, scattered macrophages and rare inflammatory cells ([Otolaryngol Clin North Am 2021;54:543](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34024482))

Terminology

* Mucocele, sialocele, ranula ([Otolaryngol Clin North Am 2021;54:543](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34024482))

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [K11.6](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/K00-K95/K00-K14/K11-/K11.6) - mucocele of salivary gland

Epidemiology

* Mainly young adults and children ([Otolaryngol Clin North Am 2021;54:543](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34024482))
* Slight female prevalence (M:F = 1:1.3) ([Am J Otolaryngol 2015;36:114](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25459318))

Sites

* Minor salivary glands located in the lower lip, tongue, floor of mouth and buccal mucosa; major salivary glands including parotid and submandibular ([J Pharm Bioallied Sci 2012;4:S180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23066247), [Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol Oral Radiol Endod 2000;89:159](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10673650))

Pathophysiology

* Ruptured salivary gland duct and subsequent leakage of the mucin into the soft tissue surrounding salivary gland ([J Oral Maxillofac Surg 2002;60:237](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11815931), [Med Oral Patol Oral Cir Bucal 2005;10:180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15735552))

Etiology

* History of trauma to the mouth or face
* Habit of biting the lip
* History of previous sialolith removal ([Am J Otolaryngol 2015;36:114](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25459318))
* Obstruction of the salivary ducts ([Am J Otolaryngol 2015;36:114](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25459318))

Clinical features

* Painless, gradually increasing in size, unilateral mass near or within the salivary gland or well defined, dome shaped, fluctuant nodule of buccal mucosa ([J Pharm Bioallied Sci 2012;4:S180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23066247))

Diagnosis

* Pseudocyst with a granulation wall (histology)
* Abundant mucin, scattered macrophages and inflammatory cells (cytology)

Laboratory

* Increased protein content and amylase in aspirated material by chemical analysis ([J Pharm Bioallied Sci 2012;4:S180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23066247))

Radiology description

* Well defined, hypoechogenic cystic mass with sharp borders and no echoes within the mass ([J Pharm Bioallied Sci 2012;4:S180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23066247))
* Hourglass shaped cystic mass on CT scan ([Am J Otolaryngol 2015;36:114](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25459318))
* Tail sign, diagnostic of sublingual ranula, communication between the sublingual and submandibular components of the cystic mass located behind the posterior free edge of the mylohyoid muscle ([Am J Otolaryngol 2015;36:114](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25459318))

Prognostic factors

* Excellent
* Low to null recurrence rate ([Am J Otolaryngol 2015;36:114](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25459318))

Case reports

* 3 year old girl with an intraparotid mucocele ([Br J Oral Maxillofac Surg 2020;58:1049](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32532573))

Treatment

* Surgical excision such as cystectomy, en bloc removal of the cyst and the submandibular gland, or en bloc removal of the mass, marsupialization or uproofing the large lesions to prevent significant loss of tissue, laser ablation, electrocautery, sclerotherapy and cryosurgery ([J Pharm Bioallied Sci 2012;4:S180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23066247), [Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol Oral Radiol Endod 2000;89:159](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10673650), [Am J Otolaryngol 2015;36:114](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25459318))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Pseudocyst with a fibrous tissue wall ([Am J Otolaryngol 2015;36:114](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25459318))
* Mucous cyst (mucocele)
  + Extravasation mucocele
    - Pool of mucin surrounded by granulation tissue due to traumatically injured minor salivary glands
    - More common in young individuals and lower the lip
  + Ranula: blue domed cyst in sublingual area
  + Plunging ranula: a mucocele extending into the neck and above the hyoid bone
  + Retention mucocele
    - Mucous filled cyst lined by epithelial cells such as cuboidal
    - Older patients, the floor of mouth, inside of the cheek
* Reference: [Rosai: Rosai and Ackerman's Surgical Pathology, 9th Edition, 2004](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/9996000427/pathologyoutl-20)

Sample pathology report

* Surgical pathology
  + Parotid mass, right, resection:
    - Fragments of fibroconnective tissue with fibrosis and chronic inflammation consistent with mucocele; no epithelial component identified (see comment)
    - Comment: The imaging of the mass showed a well defined cystic mass.
* Cytopathology
  + Parotid, left, fine needle aspiration:
    - The Milan System for Reporting Salivary Gland Cytopathology: atypia of undetermined significance (AUS) (see comment) ([Faquin: The Milan System for Reporting Salivary Gland Cytopathology, 2nd Edition, 2023](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/3031266617/pathologyoutl-20))
    - Comment: The specimen consists of predominantly mucin and rare macrophages. The differential diagnosis includes mucocele, low grade mucoepidermoid carcinoma, Warthin tumor and less likely, pleomorphic adenoma.
    - Recommend: Clinical and radiographic correlation.

Differential diagnosis

* [Low grade mucoepidermoid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsMEC.html):
  + Abundant mucin
  + Rare mucin containing epithelial cells
  + Rare clusters of malignant epithelial cells ([Acta Cytol 2022;66:359](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35717936), [J Oral Maxillofac Surg 2010;68:2146](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20580145))
* [Retention cyst / salivary duct cyst](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/oralcavitysalivaryductcyst.html):
  + Mucinous cystic contents
  + Lacks the extensive surrounding chronic inflammation
  + Surrounded by epithelial lining ([J Pharm Bioallied Sci 2012;4:S180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23066247))
* [Warthin tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandswarthin.html):
  + Double layer of oncocytic epithelium
  + Dense lymphoid aggregate underneath the epithelium

**Differential diagnosis in cytology**

* [Warthin tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandswarthin.html):
  + Polymorphous lymphocytes and fragments of oncocytic epithelium in a dirty mucoid background ([Diagn Cytopathol 2002;27:197](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12357495))
* [Low grade mucoepidermoid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsMEC.html):
  + Fragments of malignant epithelial cells and scattered squamous cells in a background of abundant mucin ([Diagn Cytopathol 2002;27:197](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12357495))
* [Pleomorphic adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandspleomorphicadenoma.html):
  + Cystic change and abundant mucoid / myxoid background or dilated ducts ([Diagn Cytopathol 2002;27:197](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12357495))

Board review style question #1

[![BRQ image](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/salivaryglandsmucocelemaleki02.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/salivaryglandsmucocelemaleki02.jpg)

Fine needle aspirate (FNA) of a cystic sublingual mass in a 23 year old woman is shown above. The patient noticed a small bump 2 weeks ago. The aspirated material looks like the image shown. What is the most likely diagnosis?

1. Mucocele
2. Mucoepidermoid carcinoma
3. Squamous cell carcinoma
4. Warthin tumor

Board review style answer #1

**A.** Mucocele. Mucocele consists of mucin and scattered macrophages. Answer B is incorrect because there are no atypical epithelial cells or mucin producing cells. Mucoepidermoid carcinoma is more cellular than the above case. Answer C is incorrect because there are no malignant squamous cells. Squamous cell carcinoma consists of malignant squamous cells with variable degree of maturation and intracellular keratin. Anisonucleaosis and pleomorphism are common features of squamous cell carcinoma in cytology specimens. Answer D is incorrect because there are no oncocytes or lymphocytes. Warthin tumor consists of fragments of oncocytes and lymphocytes in a cystic background.

Board review style question #2

[![BRQ image](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/salivaryglandsmucocelemaleki03.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/salivaryglandsmucocelemaleki03.jpg)

Fine needle aspirate (FNA) of a submandibular gland is shown above. 2 passes were performed and all slides displayed the same cytomorphology. The mass completely disappeared after the aspiration. What is the most correct diagnosis when applying the Milan System for Reporting Salivary Gland Cytology?

1. Atypia of undetermined significance
2. Malignant
3. Nondiagnostic
4. Nonneoplastic

Board review style answer #2

**A.** Atypia of undetermined significance. Abundant mucin even without any epithelial cells is considered atypical in the Milan system due to a risk of malignancy and potential overlap with low grade mucoepidermoid carcinoma. Answer C is incorrect because there is abundant mucin in the background. Answer D is incorrect because there is no salivary gland epithelium and there are no salivary gland acini. Answer B is incorrect because there is no epithelial component indicating a malignant process such as acinic cell carcinoma.
