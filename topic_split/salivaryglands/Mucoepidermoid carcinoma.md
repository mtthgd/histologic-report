Definition / general

* Malignant glandular epithelial neoplasm characterized by mucous, intermediate and epidermoid cells, with columnar, clear cell or oncocytoid features

Essential features

* Malignant epithelial neoplasm
* Characterized by mucous, intermediate and epidermoid cells, with columnar, clear cell or oncocytoid features
* Most common malignant salivary gland neoplasm in both adults and children
* Prognosis influenced by tumor stage, tumor site, surgical margins (WHO 2017)
* Associated with a specific translocation t(11;19)(q14-21;p12-13) with *CRTC1(MECT1)-MAML2* fusion

Terminology

* Mucoepidermoid carcinoma (MEC)
* Not recommended: mucoepidermoid tumor

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8430/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf#page=72) - mucoepidermoid carcinoma
* ICD-10 (depends on site):
  + [C08.0](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C00-C14/C08-/C08.0) - malignant neoplasm of submandibular gland
  + [D48.7](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D37-D48/D48-/D48.7) - neoplasm of uncertain behavior of other specified sites
  + [C07](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C00-C14/C07-/C07) - malignant neoplasm of parotid gland
  + [C08.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C00-C14/C08-/C08.9) - malignant neoplasm of major salivary gland, unspecified

Epidemiology

* Most common malignant salivary gland tumor (adults and children) ([Br J Oral Maxillofac Surg 2013;51:399](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23103239))
* Represents 10 - 15% of all salivary gland tumors ([Br J Oral Maxillofac Surg 2013;51:399](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23103239))
* Adults: wide age range (15 - 86 years, median 49 years) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2001;25:835](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11420454))
* Children: 11 - 15 years (median 13 years) ([Int J Pediatr Otorhinolaryngol 2019;120:93](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30772619))
* F > M (slight predominance) ([Br J Oral Maxillofac Surg 2013;51:399](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23103239))

Sites

* Occurs in major and minor salivary glands (predominance of major > minor) ([Eur J Surg Oncol 2021;47:1376](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33248902))
* Major: parotid > submandibular > sublingual ([Eur J Surg Oncol 2021;47:1376](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33248902))
* Minor: palate, buccal mucosa, retromolar region ([Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2018;61:397](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30004063))
* Rarely occurs in sinonasal tract, nasopharynx, lungs and intraosseous mandible ([Int Forum Allergy Rhinol 2019;9:1046](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31314958), [Expert Rev Respir Med 2018;12:249](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29338644), [Case Rep Dent 2018;2018:9348540](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30647976))

Etiology

* Possible association with ionizing radiation ([Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2018;61:397](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30004063))

Clinical features

* In one study, 33% of patients are asymptomatic; however, varies with tumor site and size ([J Stomatol Oral Maxillofac Surg 2020;121:713](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32565266))
* Most common presentation is painless swelling with pressure and discomfort
* Can be variably fixed, rubbery or soft mass ([J Stomatol Oral Maxillofac Surg 2020;121:713](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32565266))

Diagnosis

* Preoperative assessment (radiology) may consist of:
  + Ultrasonography (USG) for small tumors in major salivary glands
  + Larger recurrent tumors: CT imaging for bone involvement, MRI for soft tissue delineation ([Semin Radiat Oncol 2012;22:245](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22687949))
  + Evidence of (18)-fluorodeoxyglucose positron emission tomography CT in locoregionally advanced tumors ([Nucl Med Commun 2013;34:211](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23353886))
* Preoperative assessment (fine needle aspiration)

Radiology description

* Influenced by tumor size, location and possibly tumor grade
* Ultrasonography:
  + Typically a well circumscribed hypoechoic lesion, with a partial or completely cystic appearance against a relatively hyperechoic normal parotid gland
* CT:
  + Lower grade tumors are well circumscribed with cystic component (see [Radiology images](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/#radio))
  + Enhancing solid component and calcifications occasionally seen
  + Higher grade tumors are solid and show poorly defined, infiltrative margins
* MRI:
  + Low grade tumors are similar in appearance to a [pleomorphic adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandspleomorphicadenoma.html)
  + Higher grade tumors are solid, with a lower signal on T2 and poorly defined margins
* Reference: [Radiopaedia: Mucoepidermoid Carcinoma of Salivary Glands [Accessed 8 January 2021]](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/mucoepidermoid-carcinoma-of-salivary-glands)

Prognostic factors

* Adults:
  + Excellent prognosis, with approximately 98.8% 5 year survival rate in low grade and 97.4% in intermediate grade tumors
  + About 67% 5 year survival rate for high grade tumors
* Children:
  + 5 year survival of 98%, as they predominantly present with low to intermediate grade tumors
* Negative prognostic variables
  + Tumor arising in the submandibular gland: lymph node metastases common
  + Positive surgical margins
  + Extraparenchymal extension
  + Nodal / distant metastases
  + Increased expression of MUC1
* References: [J Stomatol Oral Maxillofac Surg 2020;121:713](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32565266), [Wenig: Atlas of Head and Neck Pathology, 3rd Edition, 2016](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1455733822/pathologyoutl-20)

Case reports

* 16 year old boy with a mandibular radiolucent lesion ([Case Rep Dent 2018;2018:9348540](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30647976))
* 36 year old man with a left parotid gland mass ([Anticancer Res 2019;39:3213](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31177170))
* 69 year old woman with a right submental mass ([Eur Ann Otorhinolaryngol Head Neck Dis 2017;134:355](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28330594))

Treatment

* Complete surgical resection
  + Conservative approach for stage I and stage II tumors
  + Wide excision with wide margin for high grade tumors or tumors with positive margins
* Neck lymph node dissection (depends on nodal status and histologic grade)
* Adjuvant radiotherapy and chemotherapy might be considered for higher grade tumors
* Reference: [J Stomatol Oral Maxillofac Surg 2020;121:713](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32565266)

Gross description

* Incompletely encapsulated or unencapsulated
* Well circumscribed
* Low grade: often cystic
* High grade: often solid, necrotic, tan-pink
* References: [J Stomatol Oral Maxillofac Surg 2020;121:713](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32565266), [Wenig: Atlas of Head and Neck Pathology, 3rd Edition, 2016](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1455733822/pathologyoutl-20)

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Solid, cystic or mixed growth patterns
* Can show papillary or glandular growth pattern
* Areas suggesting a capsule can be seen in low grade mucoepidermoid carcinoma
* Varying proportions of epidermoid cells, intermediate cells and mucocytes
* Solid nests, sheets or cords of epidermoid cells
* Overt keratinization is rare
* Mucous cells embedded in epidermoid cell nests or lining cystic spaces
* Intermediate cells found within epidermoid cell nests or forming separate nests
* Background stromal sclerosis
* Luminal or extracellular pools of mucin may be present
* Mitosis, necrosis and pleomorphism more commonly seen in high grade tumors
* Limited number of mucocytes and cystic spaces in high grade tumors
* Perineural and lymphovascular invasion possible ([Wenig: Atlas of Head and Neck Pathology, 3rd Edition, 2016](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1455733822/pathologyoutl-20))
* Focal clear cell changes can occur due to accumulation of glycogen
* Histologic variants: clear cell, oncocytic, sclerosing, Warthin-like, ciliated, spindle cell and mucoacinar  ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26457352), [Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28877061), [Int J Surg Pathol 2017;25:705](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28637362), [Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:1028](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34091485), [Am J Surg Pathol 1999;23:523](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10328083))
* Reference: [J Stomatol Oral Maxillofac Surg 2020;121:713](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32565266)

**Histopathologic grading:**

* 4 histologic grading systems (2 quantitative and 2 qualitative)
* 2 different morphologic quantitative point systems, although histologic grading using any grading system appears to be inconsistent ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:885](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31021855))
* Significant grading disparity between pathologists ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:885](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31021855))
* Armed Forces Institute of Pathology (AFIP) grading scheme (quantitative) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:885](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31021855))
  + Criteria:
    - Intracystic component less than 20% (2)
    - Neural invasion (2)
    - Necrosis (3)
    - 4 or more mitoses (3)
    - Anaplasia (4)
  + Grade:
    - Low grade (0 - 4)
    - Intermediate grade (5 - 6)
    - High grade (7 or more)
* Brandwein et al. grading scheme (quantitative) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2001;25:835](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11420454))
  + Criteria:
    - Intracystic component less than 25% (2)
    - Tumor front invades in small nests and islands (2)
    - Pronounced nuclear atypia (2)
    - Lymphovascular invasion (3)
    - Bony invasion (3)
    - 4 or more mitoses (3)
    - Perineural invasion (3)
    - Necrosis (3)
  + Grade:
    - Low grade (0)
    - Intermediate grade (2 - 3)
    - High grade (4 or more)
* Modified Healy grading system (qualitative)
  + Low grade
    - Macro and microcysts
    - Rare intermediate cells
    - Rare mitotic figures
    - Absent or minimal nuclear pleomorphism
    - Well circumscribed tumor with broad edges
    - Extravasated mucin and fibrotic stroma present
  + Intermediate grade
    - Microcysts and solid component
    - More intermediate cells
    - Few mitotic figures
    - Slight nuclear pleomorphism
    - Uncircumscribed tumor
    - Fibrotic stroma separating tumor nests
  + High grade
    - Predominantly solid, with or without microcysts
    - Perineural invasion present
    - Lymphovascular invasion present
    - Surrounding soft tissue invasion present
    - Many mitotic figures
    - Nuclear pleomorphism, including presence of prominent nucleoli
    - Predominance of intermediate cells
    - Desmoplastic stoma
* Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center (MSKCC) grading system (qualitative)
  + Low grade
    - Predominantly cystic growth pattern (> 80%)
    - 0 - 1 mitotic figures/10 high power fields (HPF)
    - Well circumscribed
    - No necrosis
  + Intermediate grade
    - Predominantly solid growth pattern
    - 2 - 3 mitotic figures/10 high power fields (HPF)
    - Well circumscribed or infiltrative
    - No necrosis
  + High grade
    - Any growth pattern but usually solid
    - ≥ 4 mitotic figures/10 high power fields (HPF)
    - Usually infiltrative
    - Necrosis is present

Cytology description

* Most often graded as low grade or high grade on FNA
* Low to intermediate grade:
  + Can be acellular or hypocellular smears
  + Extracellular mucin may be the prominent feature
  + Cystic background
  + Aggregates of epidermoid cells, intermediate cells and mucocytes
  + Epidermoid cells appear as bland cohesive flat sheets with squamoid / dense cytoplasm and well defined cellular borders
  + Predominantly mucus cells floating in extracellular mucin (low grade)
  + No keratinization seen
  + Lympocytes present in about 20% of cases and are abundant
* High grade:
  + Highly cellular aspirates
  + High grade nuclear features with pleomorphic nuclei, prominent nucleoli
  + Intermediate cells and mucous cells are rare
  + Increased mitosis
  + Necrotic background
* References: [J Clin Diagn Res 2017;11:ER04](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28658793), [Cibas: Cytology - Diagnostic Principles and Clinical Correlates, 5th Edition, 2020](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0323636365/pathologyoutl-20)

* [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgata3.html): occasional cells may be positive ([Hum Pathol 2016;56:134](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27327192))

Electron microscopy description

* Epidermoid cells attached to each other with desmosomes and mucus cells showing mucous globules with stellate mucoid material
  ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1979;47:247](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/216963))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* t(11;19)(q14-21;p12-13) with *CRTC1(MECT1)-MAML2* fusion ([Nat Genet 2003;33:208](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12539049))
  + Present in 80% of mucoepidermoid carcinoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1151](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27299797))
  + Low to intermediate grade (78%), high grade (84%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1151](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27299797))
  + Detected by fluorescence in situ hybridization (FISH) or RT-PCR ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:885](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31021855))

Differential diagnosis

* For low to intermediate grade tumors:
  + [Necrotizing sialometaplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsnecrotizingsialo.html) ([Acta Otorrinolaringol Esp 2020;71:328](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31582173)):
    - Lobular pattern, lacks cystic pattern, no intermediate cells
  + [Pleomorphic adenoma with squamous and mucinous cell metaplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandspleomorphicadenoma.html) ([J Oral Maxillofac Pathol 2018;22:S69](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29491610)):
    - Composed of epithelial and myoepithelial cells and extracellular matrix material
  + [Warthin tumor with squamous metaplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandswarthin.html) ([J Med Case Rep 2019;13:12](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30636634)):
    - Lacks the translocations seen in mucoepidermoid carcinoma
    - Shows bilayered oncocytic epithelium
  + [Acinic cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsaciniccell.html) ([Eur Arch Otorhinolaryngol 2016;273:3511](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26685679)):
    - [DOG1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsDOG1.html) and [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html) positive
  + [Mammary analogue secretory carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidMASC.html) (MASC) ([Bosn J Basic Med Sci 2016;16:237](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27131022)):
    - [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) positive
* For high grade tumors:
  + [Squamous cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsSCC.html) (primary or secondary) ([J Cancer Res Ther 2015;11:664](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26458694)):
    - Shows keratinization and intercellular bridges
    - Lacks intermediate cells and mucocytes
  + [Salivary duct carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandssalivaryductcarcinoma.html) ([Head Neck Pathol 2017;11:288](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28321773)):
    - [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgata3.html), [HER2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsher2GI.html) and [androgen receptor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsAR.html) positive
    - Shows a Roman bridge pattern, comedonecrosis
