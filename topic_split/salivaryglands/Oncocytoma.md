Definition / general

* Benign tumor composed of oncocytes
* Also called oxyphilic adenoma
* 1 - 2% of salivary gland neoplasms; more frequent than oncocytosis and oncocytic carcinoma

Clinical features

* Usually parotid gland mass, also submandibular gland or minor salivary glands
* Mean age 60 years
* 20% associated with radiation therapy or radiation exposure
* Rarely bilateral, multiple
* Rarely synchronous with Warthin tumor or carcinoma ex pleomorphic adenoma
* May occur in trisomy 7 or in BHD syndrome

Radiology description

* Specific MRI features: T1 hypointense, isointense to normal gland on fat saturated T2 and postcontrast T1; CT features: enhancing tumor and nonenhancing cystic component ([AJNR Am J Neuroradiol 2011;32:1703](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21757520), [AJNR Am J Neuroradiol 2010;31:1413](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20395389))

Case reports

* 68 year old man with parotid mass ([J Cytol 2012;29:80](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22438628))
* 79 year old woman with parotid tumor with multinodular oncocytic hyperplasia ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2003;127:e53](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12562301))

Treatment

* Local excision
* Excellent prognosis but may have late recurrence

Gross description

* Well circumscribed with fibrous capsule, solid, tan-red-brown, lobulated, often small, may have cystic spaces

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Eosinophilic or clear cell (glycogen) with sheets, trabeculae, acini or follicular patterns of monotonous large polygonal cells with well defined cell borders, deeply eosinophilic, granular cytoplasm, small round nuclei
* Vascular stroma, may have clear cell change, background of oncocytic nodular hyperplasia, psammoma bodies, tyrosine rich crystals
* No mitotic figures, no elastosis

Cytology description

* Cellular oncocytic aspirates with very clean background
* Oncocytes show abundant finely granular cytoplasm, centrally placed or eccentric nuclei and occasional distinct nucleoli
* Cellular arrangement can be irregular sheets, small clusters or single cells

Negative stains

Electron microscopy description

* Packed with mitochondria with partitions

Differential diagnosis

* [Metastases](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsmetastases.html): prostate carcinoma is [PSA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspsa.html)+, renal cell carcinoma is [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html)+, [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html)+, [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html)+
* **Oncocytic carcinoma**: malignant features, invasive, high [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html), regional nodal or distant metastases
* **Oncocytic metaplasia**: no mass
* **Oncocytoid artifact**: electrocautery
* [Oncocytosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsoncocytosis.html)
* [Warthin tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandswarthin.html): FNA has lymphoid component, mucus, necrosis
