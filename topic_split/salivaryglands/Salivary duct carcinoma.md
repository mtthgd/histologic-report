Definition / general

* First described by Kleinsasser in 1968 ([Arch Klin Exp Ohren Nasen Kehlkopfheilkd 1968;192:100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4301301))
* High grade malignant salivary gland neoplasm with in situ and invasive components
* Morphologically resembles [invasive ductal carcinoma of the breast](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantductalNOS.html)

Essential features

* High grade salivary gland malignancy
* Histology is similar to invasive ductal carcinoma of the breast (specifically luminal androgen receptor [AR]+ subtype)
* In situ areas frequently have comedo necrosis
* Nearly all cases (> 90%) are AR positive
* Poor prognosis

Terminology

* Also known as high grade ductal carcinoma

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8500/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/%0D%0A9789241548496_eng.pdf#page=74) - infiltrating duct adenocarcinoma, salivary glands
* ICD-10: [C07 - C08](https://www.icd10data.com/search?s=C07-C08) - malignant neoplasm of salivary gland

Epidemiology

* Patients are usually in their sixth to seventh decade of life
  + Mean age: 67.4 years
  + Range: 33 - 92 years ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:705](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25871467))
* M:F = 2.4:1 ([AMA Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 2016;142:489](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26939990), [Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:705](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25871467))
* Represents ~10% of all salivary glands malignancies

Sites

* Most commonly affects the parotid gland
* Other sites include the submandibular gland and minor salivary glands

Pathophysiology

* Several tumor suppressor genes may play a role in development, including *TP53, HRAS, PIK3CA, PTEN* and *BRAF* ([Cancer Cytopathol 2020;128:693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32421944))

Etiology

* Unknown
* May arise de novo (60%) or from ex pleomorphic adenoma (40%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:705](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25871467))

Clinical features

* Rapidly growing neck mass, typically in the vicinity of the parotid gland
* Facial pain
* Facial weakness or paralysis
* Regional or distant metastasis (lung, bone, brain or other sites)
* References: [Cancer Cytopathol 2020;128:693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32421944), [Oral Oncol 2017;74:40](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29103750), [Zhonghua Kou Qiang Yi Xue Za Zhi 2019;54:416](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31177683)

Diagnosis

* Neck mass that typically leads to imaging studies and fine needle aspiration
* Patients may have a long standing history of pleomorphic adenoma that suddenly started to grow rapidly
* References: [Head Neck Pathol 2017;11:288](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28321773), [Ear Nose Throat J 2012;91:356](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22996706)

Radiology description

* CT ([Neuroradiology 2012;54:631](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22307272)):
  + Typically shows a mass with heterogonous enhancement
  + Can show foci of calcifications
* MRI ([AJNR Am J Neuroradiol 2005;26:1201](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15891184)):
  + Invasive or ill defined margins
  + Can appear well circumscribed in a minority of cases (~15%) ([Neuroradiology 2012;54:631](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22307272))
  + On T1 weighted images, tumors appear hypointense compared with the surrounding salivary gland and isointense compared with the skeletal muscle
  + On T2 weighted images, tumors are hyperintense compared with contralateral parotid
  + Cellular components of the tumor enhance early with a high washout ratio
  + Fibrotic and cellular poor areas show gradual upward enhancement
  + Cellular areas with necrosis enhance early and poorly washout

Prognostic factors

* Prognosis is poor, with overall survival rate of ~35%
* Median overall survival is 3.1 years ([AMA Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 2016;142:489](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26939990))
* Poor prognostic factors:
  + Higher tumor stage (especially nodal category N2 or higher), 5 year survival rates for stage I is 42% and 23% for stage IV ([Cancer 2005;103:2526](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15900577))
  + Perineural invasion (especially if the facial nerve is involved and is sacrificed during surgery)
  + Extranodal extension
  + 50 years or older ([Head Neck 2014;36:694](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23606370))

Case reports

* 39 year old man complaining of decreased mobility of lower left eyelid and painless swelling of the left preauricular area ([Folia Histochem Cytobiol 2015;53:342](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26691893))
* 45 year old man with cytology and followup surgical pathology ([J Cytol 2017;34:107](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28469320))
* 50 year old patient with progressive facial paralysis and left parotid mass ([Mod Pathol 2012;25:919](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22434951))
* 59 year old man with progressive difficulty in swallowing and a muffled voice for 2 months ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2018;97:e0095](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29538201))
* 60 year old patient with salivary duct carcinoma of the sinonasal cavity ([Head Neck 2016;38:E2464](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27080391))

Treatment

* Total parotidectomy with neck dissection
* Adjuvant radiotherapy should be considered, as salivary duct carcinoma is high grade by definition ([NCCN: NCCN Guidelines - Head and Neck Cancers [Accessed 7 June 2021]](https://www.nccn.org/professionals/physician_gls/default.aspx#head-and-neck))
* Chemotherapy (antiandrogens, trastuzumab, anti-BRAF and anti-PIK3CA) for advanced disease as indicated

Gross description

* Mean gross greatest dimension is 3.2 cm and ranges from 0.5 - 9 cm ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:705](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25871467))
* Tumors typically have ill defined borders but may appear well circumscribed
* Cut surface is heterogeneous and can show gross evidence of necrosis
* Regional lymphadenopathy is common

Frozen section description

* Typically not indicated as cytology is usually diagnostic of a high grade malignancy
* High grade malignancy with necrosis and cellular pleomorphism

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Invasive tumor cells with ample eosinophilic (oncocytic) cytoplasm forming cords, nests and cribriform glands in a desmoplastic stroma
* Nuclear cytology is high grade with nuclear pleomorphism
* In situ component frequently shows central necrosis
* Secondary salivary duct carcinomas show pleomorphic adenoma intimately associated with the malignant component (~40% of salivary duct carcinomas are secondary) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:705](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25871467))
* Histological variants show at least focal areas with conventional histology ([Head Neck Pathol 2017;11:288](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28321773))
  + Mucin rich ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1070](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12883239))
  + Micropapillary ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:319](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15104294), [Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol Oral Radiol 2016;121:e162](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26725719))
  + Sarcomatoid ([Am J Clin Pathol 2004;122:222](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15323139))
  + Rhabdoid ([Histopathology 2017;70:164](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27079821))
  + Basal-like ([Histopathology 2012;61:629](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22882517), [Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:705](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25871467))

Cytology description

* Cellular smears with dirty necrotic background
* Clusters of epithelial cells with high grade pleomorphic nuclei and ample cytoplasm
* Mitotic figures and apoptotic debris
* Immunohistochemical stains can be done on cell block slides to confirm the diagnosis (AR positive) and rule out metastasis / other primary high grade neoplasms of the salivary gland (see [IHC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandssalivaryductcarcinoma.html#RBsalivaryglandssalivaryductcarcinomaposstn) section)
* Reference: [Cancer Cytopathol 2020;128:693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32421944)

Positive stains

* Nearly all are positive for [AR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantAR.html) (> 90%), [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgata3.html), [AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [34βE12](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck34be12.html) and [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2015;139:55](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25549144))
* Most (~80%) are positive for [GCDFP-15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgcdfp15.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:705](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25871467), [Am J Clin Pathol 1998;109:75](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9426521))
* [PSA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspsa.html) positive in ~57% ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:579](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10757407))
* [HER2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbreasther2.html) (3+) in 10 - 35% ([Head Neck Pathol 2018;12:488](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29302899), [Head Neck Pathol 2014;8:133](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24065449), [Am J Clin Pathol 1998;109:75](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9426521))
* [Mucicarmine](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmucins.html) in mucin rich variant

Negative stains

* Multifocal [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) positivity in 41% ([Am J Clin Pathol 1998;109:75](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9426521))
* [DOG1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsDOG1.html) and [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html) ([Mod Pathol 2012;25:919](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22460810), [Mod Pathol 2013;26:1041](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23558573))
* Most are negative for [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html), [CK5/6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5.html), [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) and [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html) ([Head Neck Pathol 2018;12:488](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29302899), [Head Neck Pathol 2014;8:133](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24065449), [Am J Clin Pathol 1998;109:75](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9426521))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* *PLAG1*, *HMGA2*, *PIK3CA*, *HRAS*, *TP53*, *ERBB2*, *BRAF* alterations may be present ([Cancer 2016;122:3136](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27379604))

Sample pathology report

* Parotid, right, parotidectomy:
  + Salivary duct carcinoma, 2 cm, with lymphatic, vascular and perineural invasion, within 0.1 cm from deep resection margin (see comment)
  + Comment: Metastatic carcinoma in 5 of 40 lymph nodes (5/40), largest metastatic focus measures 2.3 cm, with extranodal extension.

Differential diagnosis

* Metastatic adenocarcinoma:
  + [Breast invasive ductal carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantductalNOS.html):
    - If [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) and [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html)+, easy to differentiate; otherwise morphological, clinical and radiological correlations are necessary
  + [Lung adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumoradenocarcinoma.html):
    - [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html) and [napsin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnapsina.html)+
  + [Prostatic adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostateadenoNOS.html):
    - [NKX3.1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsnkx3.html)+; [AR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantAR.html) and [PSA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspsa.html) do not differentiate
    - Other sites should be ruled out, based on clinical, radiological and histological findings
  + [Metastatic squamous cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsSCC.html):
    - Histologic overlap, especially if nonkeratinizing; [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) and [CK5/6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5.html)+, [AR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantAR.html)-
* Primary salivary gland neoplasms with high grade transformation (well differentiated areas may be focal):
  + [High grade mucoepidermoid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsMEC.html):
    - Can be challenging to differentiate from mucinous variant
    - [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) and [CK5/6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5.html) positivity favors mucoepidermoid carcinoma ([Head Neck Pathol 2014;8:133](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24065449))
  + [Epithelial myoepithelial carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsepimyocarcinoma.html):
    - Dual population with myoepithelial component highlighted by [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html)
  + [Myoepithelial carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsmyoepithelioma.html):
    - Plasmacytoid morphology
    - Diffuse [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) and [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)
  + [Adenoid cystic carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsadenoidcystic.html):
    - Dual population and cribriform growth pattern in areas with conventional histology
  + [Adenosquamous carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticscc.html):
    - Diffuse [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) and [CK5/6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5.html) positivity in squamous areas
  + [Intraductal carcinoma (cribriform cystadenocarcinoma)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsintraductalsalivary.html):
    - Entirely in situ and usually has low grade cytology
    - Although [AR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantAR.html) can be positive, it is typically negative ([Head Neck Pathol 2017;11:288](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28321773), [Histopathology 2018;73:314](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29574881), [Head Neck Pathol 2013;7:S59](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23821212))

Board review style question #1

Which of the following immunohistochemical stains is characteristic of salivary duct carcinoma?

1. Androgen receptor
2. DOG1
3. Estrogen receptor
4. GATA3
5. SOX10

Board review style answer #1

**A**. Androgen receptor. Although GATA3 is frequently positive in salivary duct carcinoma, it is also positive in other salivary and nonsalivary gland malignancies. DOG1, SOX10 and estrogen receptor are usually negative.
