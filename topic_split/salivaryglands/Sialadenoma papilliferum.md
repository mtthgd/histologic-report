Definition / general

* Rare benign biphasic tumor with exophytic squamous component and endophytic glandular component
* Well differentiated papillary hyperplastic squamous epithelium covering ductal component of cleft-like cystic spaces lined by cuboidal or columnar epithelium with occasional goblet cells
* First described by Abrams in 1969 ([Cancer 1969;24:1057](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/5353939%20))

Clinical features

* Usually hard palate ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2001;125:1595](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11735698)) or parotid gland of men over 40 years; also children ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol Oral Radiol Endod 2007;103:e51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17321439))
* Painless
* Tends to recur after excision

Case reports

* 65 year old man with parotid tumor ([Head Neck 2013;35:E74](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22076703))
* 79 year old woman with malignant transformation of sialadenoma papilliferum of the palate ([Virchows Arch 2004;445:641](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15455228))
* 82 year old woman with mucoepidermoid carcinoma arising in a background of sialadenoma papilliferum ([Head Neck Pathol 2009;3:59](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20596992))
* Recurrence in buccal mucosa 3 years after excision ([J Laryngol Otol 1995;109:787](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7561511))

Gross description

* Well circumscribed, round / oval, papillary tumor of mucosal surface

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Biphasic, with well differentiated papillary hyperplastic squamous epithelium covering ductal component of cleft-like cystic spaces lined by cuboidal or columnar epithelium with occasional goblet cells
* Variable oncocytes and squamous metaplasia, dysplasia and in situ carcinoma in exophytic component ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol Oral Radiol Endod 2007;104:e27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17630095))
* Has malignant counterpart or evolves to mucoepidermoid carcinoma or epithelial myoepithelial carcinoma with high grade carcinoma

Positive stains

* **Squamous epithelium and ductal structures:** [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)
* **Ductal structures:** also [CAM 5.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscam52.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)
* Also [CK8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck8.html), [CK19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck19.html) ([Int J Oral Maxillofac Surg 2004;33:621](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15308267))
* One study identified 2 subsets of basally located cells:
  1. Positive for [CK14](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck14.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html), [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html) and [smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) - similar to myoepithelial cells
  2. [CK13](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck1.html#ck13)+ and [CK14](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck14.html)+ only ([J Oral Pathol Med 1996;25:336](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8887080))

Negative stains

* [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [muscle specific actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsactinmusclespecific.html), [HPV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshpv.html) ([J Cutan Pathol 1999;26:259](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10408352))

Electron microscopy description

* Oncocyte is predominant cell; contains numerous mitochondria, parallel filaments within cell cytoplasm attached by desmosomes ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1986;110:523](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3754726))
