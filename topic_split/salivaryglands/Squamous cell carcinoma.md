Definition / general

* True salivary gland primaries of squamous cell carcinoma are very rare; most squamous cell carcinomas are metastases to intraparotid lymph nodes from primaries in oral cavity, upper aerodigestive tract or skin
* Squamous cell carcinomas represent 16 - 54% of malignant salivary gland tumors, about 11% of malignant tumors of major salivary glands

Clinical features

* Squamous cell carcinomas (primary or secondary) are most frequent parotid malignant tumor (30 - 37%), represent 4% of submandibular gland tumors
* Usually men (when considering major salivary gland tumors); also associated with AIDS
* May occur in Stensen duct
* Rapid painless growth with infiltration of surrounding structures, regardless of origin; may cause acute facial paralysis
* Aggressive behavior, with 50% 5 year survival ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2001;125:740](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11371224))

Treatment

* Radical surgery, radiation therapy

Gross description

* Large, poorly encapsulated mass

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Classic features of squamous cell carcinoma
* May have perineural invasion, clear cell change
* Often component or coexisting with other tumors: carcinosarcoma, epidermoid cyst, epithelial myoepithelial carcinoma, malignant mixed tumor, mucoepidermoid carcinoma, sarcomatoid carcinoma, salivary duct carcinoma, sebaceous lymphadenoma, Warthin tumor ([Mod Pathol 2002;15:724](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12118110))

Positive stains

* [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html) (variable, [Mod Pathol 2004;17:407](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14976534)), [CK13](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck1.html#ck13), [CK14](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck14.html), [CK19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck19.html); also [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html), [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html), [cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html), [MDM2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmdm2.html), [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) diffuse (basaloid type)

Negative stains

* [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html), [COX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclooxygenase2.html), [CK8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck8.html), [factor XIIIa](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfactorxiiia.html), [p16](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp16.html), [CDK4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdk4.html), [Rb](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsrb.html), **E2F1**
* Loss of [E-cadherin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsecadherin.html)

Differential diagnosis

* [Adenoid cystic carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsadenoidcystic.html): [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html)+, resembles basaloid squamous cell carcinoma ([Mod Pathol 2005;18:645](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15529180))
* [High grade mucoepidermoid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsMEC.html): intermediate cells, mucus cells, [COX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclooxygenase2.html)+
