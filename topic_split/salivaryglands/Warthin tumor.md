Definition / general

* Benign salivary gland tumor that is composed of oncocytic epithelial cells lining papillary and cystic structures in a lymphoid stroma
* Second most frequent benign tumor of the parotid gland (after pleomorphic adenoma) ([Head Neck Pathol 2020;14:412](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31228167))
* Infarcted / metaplastic subtype may occur following FNA biopsy ([Clin Otolaryngol Allied Sci 1989;14:205](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2743609))

Essential features

* Benign salivary gland tumor
* Bilayered papillae and cysts lined by oncocytic cells and flattened basal cells
* Lymphoid stroma

Terminology

* Papillary cystadenoma lymphomatosum, adenolymphoma, cystadenolymphoma

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [D11.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D11-/D11.9#:~:text=Benign%20neoplasm%20of%20major%20salivary%20gland%2C%20unspecified,-2016%202017%202018&text=Billable%2FSpecific%20Code-,D11.,effective%20on%20October%201%2C%202022.) - benign neoplasm of major salivary gland, unspecified

Epidemiology

* Accounts for 5 - 20% of all salivary tumors ([J Clin Exp Dent 2013;5:e218](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24455085))
* Mean age at diagnosis is 62 years (range: 12 - 92 years) ([Laryngoscope 1983;93:695](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6304434))
* M ≈ F
  + Historically M > F ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1986;61:256](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3458128))
* More common in smokers ([Am J Epidemiol 1996;144:183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8678050))

Sites

* Almost exclusively involves the parotid gland, especially the inferior pole and periparotid lymph nodes ([Br J Oral Maxillofac Surg 1998;36:52](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9578258), [Br J Oral Maxillofac Surg 1993;31:43](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8431414))
* Multifocality is detected in 12 - 20% of patients with bilaterality in 5 - 17% of patients ([Oral Oncol 2002;38:35](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11755819), [Acta Otolaryngol 2006;126:1213](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17050316))

Pathophysiology

* Probably arises from salivary ductal inclusions in parotid lymph nodes ([Head Neck Pathol 2014;8:73](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23868565), [Head Neck Pathol 2021;15:438](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32865726))
* Clonality studies have suggested a nonneoplastic nature ([Hum Pathol 2000;31:1377](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11112212), [Mod Pathol 2005;18:964](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15861216))

Etiology

* Strong link to cigarette smoking (especially when bilateral) ([Am J Epidemiol 1996;144:183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8678050))
* Other suggested risk factors include radiation exposure in atomic bomb survivors and autoimmune diseases (especially thyroiditis) ([Cancer 1997;79:1465](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9118025), [Acta Otolaryngol 1997;117:623](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9288224), [J Craniomaxillofac Surg 2015;43:427](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25814196))

Clinical features

* Presents with a painless, slow growing and fluctuant mass in the lower pole of the parotid gland ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol 1986;61:256](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3458128))
* Glucose avid on PET and often incidentally detected during PET work up ([Am J Otolaryngol 2015;36:259](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25523505))

Radiology description

* Parotid tail location
* Cystic change, multifocality
* Avidity on PET CT ([Am J Otolaryngol 2015;36:259](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25523505))

Prognostic factors

* Favorable prognosis ([Diagn Interv Imaging 2016;97:37](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25543869))
* Recurrence is usually due to inadequate excision ([Int J Oral Maxillofac Surg 2008;37:831](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18550337))
* Malignant transformation has rarely been reported but is increasingly thought to represent Warthin-like carcinomas rather than true Warthin tumors ([J Oral Pathol Med 1994;23:330](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7965888), [Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26457352))

Case reports

* 50 year old man presented with painless swelling over the right lower side of the face ([J Clin Diagn Res 2014;8:ZD37](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25386545))
* 69 year old man presented with right parotid tumor of 1 year duration ([J Med Case Rep 2019;13:12](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30636634))
* 71 year old man with left parotid mass, slowly growing over a period of 12 years ([Surgeries 2020;1:46](https://doi.org/10.3390/surgeries1020006))

Treatment

* Surgical excision with adequate margins is usually curative ([Diagn Interv Imaging 2016;97:37](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25543869))

Gross description

* Most are well circumscribed oval masses, 20 - 50 mm in diameter ([Indian J Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 2019;71:839](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31742077))
* Solid areas and multiple cysts with papillary projections are apparent on the cut surface

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Varying proportions of papillary cystic structures lined by bilayered oncocytic epithelial cells and surrounded by a lymphoid stroma including germinal centers
* Epithelial component is comprised of inner columnar and outer cuboidal cells
* Limited foci of squamous, mucous, ciliated and sebaceous cells can be present
* Infarcted / metaplastic subtype ([Am J Surg Pathol 2013;37:1743](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24121173), [Histopathology 1999;35:432](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10583558)):
  + Bilayered epithelium is replaced by squamous metaplastic epithelium with no atypia
  + Mucinous metaplasia may also be present
  + Biopsy site reaction may be present with necrosis, squamous metaplasia and a stromal reaction (including granuloma formation)
* Stroma poor tumors have bilayered oncocytic lining but minimal lymphoid stroma ([Oral Oncol 2002;38:163](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11854064))

Cytology description

* Small cohesive sheets of oncocytes with abundant granular cytoplasm
* Central round nucleus with prominent nucleolus
* Numerous lymphocytes with granular debris in the background

Positive stains

* All epithelial cells are positive for [pancytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html)
  + [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsCK7.html), [CK8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsCK8.html) / [CK18](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsCK18.html) and [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsEMA.html) are positive in luminal (or inner) epithelial cells
  + [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) and [p40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp40.html) show heterogenous nuclear staining of the basal cells (see [Microscopic (histologic) images](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandswarthin.html#microimages)) ([Interv Med Appl Sci 2016;8:41](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28386459))
* Lymphoid cells are positive for: B cell markers ([CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html)) and T cell markers ([CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html), [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html), [CD8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd8.html))

Negative stains

* [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [calponin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalponin.html), [actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsGFAP.html)

Electron microscopy description

* Oncocytic epithelial cells show cytoplasm filled with mitochondria on electron microscopy ([Acta Otorhinolaryngol Ital 2005;25:150](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16450769))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Absent *MAML2* fusion (positive in mucoepidermoid carcinoma)

Sample pathology report

* Salivary gland, parotidectomy:
  + Warthin tumor (see comment)
  + Comment: Sections show a well circumscribed tumor with a papillary architecture. The papillae are lined by bilayered oncocytic epithelial cells with a surrounding lymphoid stroma, containing germinal centers.

Differential diagnosis

* [Lymphoepithelial cyst](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsb9lymphoepithelialcyst.html):
  + Dilated cystic structure lined by squamous / columnar epithelium with lymphoid follicles in the cyst wall
  + Lacks papillary architecture
* [Mucoepidermoid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsMEC.html):
  + Critical consideration in Warthin tumors with metaplastic squamous or mucinous cells
  + Mucoepidermoid carcinoma can show Warthin-like variant ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26457352))
  + [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) and [CK5/6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5and6.html) are diffusely positive in mucoepidermoid carcinoma ([Head Neck Pathol 2013;7:64](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23054955))
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) is negative in mucoepidermoid carcinoma
  + *MAML2* fusions are present in mucoepidermoid carcinoma, absent in Warthin tumor
  + Warthin-like mucoepidermoid carcinoma lacks the classic oncocytic bilayer of Warthin tumor and tends to show more complex epithelial organization ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26457352), [Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28877061))

A 49 year old man presents with bilateral, slow growing parotid masses. An H&E section of the tumor is pictured above. What is the most likely diagnosis?

1. Classic Warthin tumor
2. Lymphoepithelial cyst
3. Oncocytoma
4. Warthin-like variant of mucoepidermoid carcinoma

Board review style answer #1

**A.** Classic Warthin tumor. The photograph shows features of a classic Warthin tumor with papillary structures lined by bilayered oncocytic epithelium. The other entities on the list do not match the entity shown on the H&E photograph provided.

Board review style question #2

Which of the following is a risk factor for developing a Warthin tumor?

1. Alcohol abuse
2. Cigarette smoking
3. Intravenous drug use
4. *MAML2* translocation

Board review style answer #2

**B.** Cigarette smoking. The other risk factors listed have not been proven to be associated with Warthin tumor.
