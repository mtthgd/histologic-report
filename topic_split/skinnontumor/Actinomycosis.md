Definition / general

* Rare suppurative and granulomatous infectious disease ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2015;16:99](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26439419))
* Caused by a group of anaerobic, gram positive, filamentous bacteria, which are normal flora in the oral cavity, gastrointestinal tract and female genital tract ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2015;16:99](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26439419))
* Clinically presents with abscess formation, tissue fibrosis, sinus drainage and occasionally, a soft tissue mass mimicking a tumor ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2015;16:99](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26439419))

Essential features

* Causative agent *Actinomyces israelli*
* Treated with high dose penicillin
* Splendore-Hoeppli phenomenon is a histologic feature

Terminology

* Most common causative agent is *Actinomyces israelii*
* Sulfur granules: small yellow granules found within the abscesses formed by *Actinomyces* infection ([Ophthal Plast Reconstr Surg 1992;8:237](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1476972))
* Lumpy jaw syndrome: large abscesses located on the head and neck, usually following dental disease and mandibular osteomyelitis ([BMJ Case Rep 2015;2015:bcr2014206557](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25657195) )
* Splendore-Hoeppli phenomenon (asteroid bodies): pink rim at the periphery of the colony, due to immunoglobulin and cell debris, which occurs around colonies of fungi, bacteria and parasites ([J Oral Maxillofac Pathol 2018;22:161](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30158765))

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [A42.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/A00-B99/A30-A49/A42-/A42.9) - actinomycosis, unspecified

Epidemiology

* Higher prevalence within areas of low socioeconomic status ([BMC Infect Dis 2018;18:686](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30572823))
* Use of intrauterine device contraceptive devices has increased the incidence of actinomycosis ([Contraception 2007;75:S48](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17531616))

Sites

* Cervicofacial (post dental infection), skin (posttraumatic injury creating an anaerobic environment), pelvic (post intrauterine device placement), abdominal (status post ruptured appendix or bowel perforation) and pulmonary (smokers with poor dental hygiene, aspiration of infective material) ([Infect Drug Resist 2014;7:183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25045274))

Pathophysiology

* Predisposing factors include poor oral hygiene, trauma, male gender, diabetes mellitus, immunosuppression, alcoholism and malnutrition ([Infect Drug Resist 2014;7:183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25045274))
* Filamentous bacteria are a normal commensal inhabitant of the oral and buccal cavities, gastrointestinal tract and female genitalia
* Etiology of infection in skin is commonly linked with a traumatic injury, human bite or a perforating injury, which creates an anaerobic environment for *Actinomyces israelii* to grow in ([J Clin Diagn Res 2014;8:YD03](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25177623))

Etiology

* Caused by *Actinomyces israelii* in humans and *Actinomyces bovis* in animals

Clinical features

* Localized pain, swelling and draining fistulas

Diagnosis

* Culture on chocolate agar media at 37 °C
* Gram stain is more sensitive than culture, especially if patient is on antibiotics
* Filamentous bacteria of actinomycosis can be identified on H&E and are highlighted by Gram stain ([Infect Drug Resist 2014;7:183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25045274))
* Polymerase chain reaction (PCR) and nucleic acid probes are being developed for faster and more accurate identification

Case reports

* 16 year old boy with scalp actinomycosis presenting as a soft tissue tumor ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2015;16:99](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26439419))
* 32 year old woman with actinomycosis of the back and left axilla ([J Clin Diagn Res 2014;8:YD03](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25177623))
* 36 year old man with renal actinomycosis presenting as a urocutaneous fistula (​[Urol Case Rep 2019;28:101054](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31709154)​)
* 42 year old man with primary cutaneous actinomycosis ([AIDS Res Ther 2019;16:16](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31362755%E2%80%8B))
* 55 year old woman with primary cutaneous actinomycosis of upper extremity ([Trop Doct 2012;42:58](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22290111))
* 82 year old man with actinomycosis of the upper lip ([Ann Dermatol 2011;23:S131](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22028560))

Treatment

* High dose penicillin is necessary to penetrate areas of fibrosis and suppuration / granules ([Clin Infect Dis 2004;38:444](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14727221))
* Drainage of abscesses or radical excision of sinus tracts

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Lymphocytes, neutrophils, giant cells and fibroblasts
* Bacterial colonies (sulphur granules) found at the center of the inflammatory reaction, composed of basophilic radiating filaments

Gram stain

Positive stains

* [Gram](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgramstain.html) positive
* [GMS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgms.html) stain demonstrates filamentous bacteria, which do not stain with [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html) but are visible on H&E
* Modified [Ziehl-Neelsen](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsacidfast.html) stains (e.g., [Fite-Faraco](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsacidfast.html)) ([ScienceDirect: Nocardia [Accessed 24 June 2022]](https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/medicine-and-dentistry/nocardia))

Negative stains

* [Acid fast](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsacidfast.html) stains

Sample pathology report

* Skin, biopsy:
  + Primary cutaneous actinomycosis

Differential diagnosis

* [Nocardiosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungnontumornocardiosis.html):
  + Branching filamentous bacteria are partially [acid fast](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsacidfast.html) positive
  + Histologic appearance of *Nocardia* is similar to other actinomycetes family members; culture and biochemical testing is necessary for diagnosis / identification
  + [Fite-Faraco](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsacidfast.html) stain is helpful since positive in nocardiosis
* [Tuberculosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumortb.html):
  + Characteristic histologic findings include caseating granulomas
  + Detected by [Ziehl-Neelsen](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsacidfast.html) stain and *Mycobacterium* culture
* **Sporotrichosis**:
  + Characteristic histologic findings include mixed acute and chronic inflammatory response
  + Organisms "sporothrix asteroids" can be detected via [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html), [GMS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgms.html) stains
* **Paracoccidioidomycosis**:
  + Characteristic histologic findings include mixed acute and chronic inflammatory response
  + Organisms referred to as captain wheel on morphology can be detected via [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html), [GMS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgms.html) stains
