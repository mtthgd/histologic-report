Definition / general

* *Demodex* mites are commensal organisms that are occasionally implicated in inflammatory skin disease

Essential features

* Commensal mite found in folliculosebaceous units
* Increased incidence with age
* Pathogenic with overgrowth or immune reaction causing rosacea or folliculitis

Terminology

* *Demodex* mites, demodicosis, demodicidosis, *Demodex folliculorum*, *Demodex brevis*, face mite, follicle mite

ICD coding

* ICD-10:
  + [B88.0](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/A00-B99/B85-B89/B88-/B88.0) - other acariasis (inclusion term dermatitis due to *Demodex* species)
  + [L71.8](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/L00-L99/L60-L75/L71-/L71.8) - other rosacea
  + [L71.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/L00-L99/L60-L75/L71-/L71.9) - rosacea, unspecified
  + [L73.8](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/L00-L99/L60-L75/L73-/L73.8) - other specified follicular disorder
  + [L73.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/L00-L99/L60-L75/L73-/L73.9) - follicular disorder, unspecified

Epidemiology

* Acquired after birth ([Clin Dermatol 2014;32:739](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25441466))
* Prevalence increases with age ([Cutis 2005;76:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16422462))
* M > F ([Cutis 2005;76:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16422462))
* Worsened with immunosuppression
* Worsened with topical or systemic corticosteroids
* Demodectic mange well established in dogs

Sites

* *D. folliculorum*
  + Follicular infundibulum ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2009;60:453](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19231642))
* *D. brevis*
  + Sebaceous duct and meibomian glands ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2009;60:453](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19231642))
* Face (most common)
* Eyelids
* Scalp
* Upper aspect of chest
* Neck
* Trunk

Pathophysiology

* Unclear
* 4 proposed mechanisms for how *Demodex* cause disease ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2009;60:453](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19231642)):
  + Blockage of hair follicle and sebaceous ducts
  + Humoral or cell mediated immune reaction to mite components
  + Foreign body granulomatous reaction to mite’s exoskeleton
  + Vector role for bacteria
* *Wolbachia* within *Demodex* may incite inflammation ([Cutis 2005;76:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16422462))
* Upregulation of TNF alpha and IL1β in papulopustular rosacea ([Clin Dermatol 2014;32:739](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25441466))
* Papulopustular reaction associated with HLA type ([Cutis 2005;76:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16422462), [Clin Dermatol 2014;32:739](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25441466))
  + Lack of HLA-A2 phenotype
  + HLA-Cw2 phenotype

Etiology

* Unclear why it is pathogenic in some ([Am J Dermatopathol 1996;18:589](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8989930))
  + Demodex mites in 42% of 388 follicles with inflammation
  + Demodex mites in 10% of noninflamed follicles
  + 83% of follicles with *Demodex* with inflammation

Clinical features

* 2 species on humans: *D. folliculorum* and *D. brevis* ([WebMD: What are Demodex Mites? [Accessed 25 February 2022]](https://www.webmd.com/skin-problems-and-treatments/what-are-demodex-mites))
* Folliculocentric erythematous papules and pustules:
  + Pityriasis folliculorum (spinulate demodicosis)
  + Papulopustular rosacea
  + Acne rosacea-like demodicosis
  + Granulomatous rosacea-like (demodicosis gravis)
  + Perioral dermatitis-like
  + Blepharitis
* Otitis externa / pruritus ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2009;60:453](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19231642))
* Mimics acute graft versus host disease (GVHD) ([Biol Blood Marrow Transplant 2020;26:S342](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bbmt.2019.12.374))
* Pigmented demodicosis ([Int J Dermatol 2021 Dec 13 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34897670)):
  + Brown-gray facial pigmentation
  + Dark skinned individuals
* Demodectic frost of the ear ([JAMA Dermatol 2017;153:356](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28030652)):
  + Sandpaper-like follicular based scale on ear
* Vulvar demodicosis ([J Cutan Pathol 2020;47:1063](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33448447)):
  + Erythematous pruritic papules
  + Fordyce spots colonized with mites
* Possible association with neutrophilic sebaceous adenitis ([Am J Dermatopathol 2015;37:315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25229566)):
  + Erythematous annular facial plaques

Diagnosis

* Skin scraping:
  + Potassium hydroxide (KOH) examination
  + Density > 5 mites/follicle or 5 mites/cm2 ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2009;60:453](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19231642))
* Skin biopsy

Case reports

* 12 and 15 year old boys with acneiform eruption following dupilumab ([Pediatrics 2021;147:e2020029520](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33879520))
* 36 year old woman with vulvar demodicosis ([J Cutan Pathol 2020;47:1063](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33448447))
* 46 year old woman with demodex folliculitis mimicking acute GVHD ([JAMA Dermatol 2013;149:1407](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24132559))
* 48 year old woman with pruritic face and neck eruption ([Dermatol Online J 2007;13:9](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18319006))
* 61 year old white man with neutrophilic sebaceous adenitis colonized by demodex mites ([Am J Dermatopathol 2015;37:315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25229566))

Treatment

* Topical sulfur compounds ([Cutis 2005;76:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16422462), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2009;60:453](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19231642))
* Permethrin
* Ivermectin
* Topical metronidazole ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2009;60:453](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19231642))
* Topical dilute camphor oil with oral metronidazole ([Cutis 2005;76:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16422462), [JAMA Dermatol 2017;153:356](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28030652))
* Benzyl benzoate (not available in US)
* Lindane, crotamiton
* Demodectic frost treatment ([JAMA Dermatol 2017;153:356](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28030652)):
  + Selenium sulfide wash and low potency topical corticosteroid with or without salicylic acid

Microscopic (histologic) description

* 8 legged mites in folliculosebaceous units
* Incidental identification in 10% of skin specimens ([Clin Dermatol 2014;32:739](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25441466))
* *D. folliculorum*: 0.3 - 0.4 mm, head and neck ([WebMD: What are Demodex Mites? [Accessed 25 February 2022]](https://www.webmd.com/skin-problems-and-treatments/what-are-demodex-mites))
* *D. brevis*: 0.15 - 0.2 mm, upper trunk ([WebMD: What are Demodex Mites? [Accessed 25 February 2022]](https://www.webmd.com/skin-problems-and-treatments/what-are-demodex-mites))
* If pathogenic:
  + *Demodex* mites in spongiotic follicular infundibulum or sebaceous gland with perifollicular and perivascular lymphohistiocytic infiltrate ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2009;60:453](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19231642))
  + With or without neutrophils and multinucleated histiocytes
  + Infundibular pustules containing mites
* Demodicosis gravis:
  + Dermal granulomas with central caseation necrosis and phagocytized mite fragments in foreign body giant cells ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2009;60:453](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19231642))
* Pigmented demodicosis ([Int J Dermatol 2021 Dec 13 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34897670)):
  + Numerous mites in a follicular infundibulum with interface dermatitis involving the follicle
  + Numerous melanophages
* Vulvar demodicosis ([J Cutan Pathol 2020;47:1063](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33448447)):
  + Demodex mites in Fordyce spots with surrounding neutrophilic and histiocytic infiltrate

Sample pathology report

* Skin, face:
  + Suppurative folliculitis with *Demodex* (see comment)
  + Comment: In the correct clinical setting, these findings are consistent with *Demodex* folliculitis. Similar findings can be seen in rosacea or other acneiform process.

Differential diagnosis

* [Acne](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumoracne.html):
  + Usually teens
  + Comedones
  + Pustules, cysts, nodules
* [Bacterial folliculitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorfolliculitis.html):
  + Swab or tissue culture
* [Fungal folliculitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorfolliculitis.html):
  + Often in setting of tinea corporis
  + Fungal hyphae and spores within involved hair follicle
  + Tissue culture
* **Pityrosporum folliculitis** ([Cutis 2021;107:E40](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34096861)):
  + Caused by *Malassezia furfur*
  + Pruritic monomorphic folliculocentric pustules
  + KOH shows short hyphae and spores
  + Biopsy shows hyphae and spores in inflamed follicular infundibula
* [Eosinophilic folliculitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumoreosinophilicpustularfolliculitis.html) ([Cutis 2021;107:E40](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34096861)):
  + Pruritic facial papules and pustules
  + 3 variants: Ofuji disease, immunosuppression associated and infancy associated
  + Peripheral eosinophilia
  + Histopathology shows follicular spongiosis with exocytosis of eosinophils
* [Miliaria](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumormiliaria.html) ([Cutis 2021;107:E40](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34096861)):
  + Due to occlusion of eccrine glands
  + 2 - 4 mm erythematous vesicular papules or pustules in area of occlusion
  + Not folliculocentric
