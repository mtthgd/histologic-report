Definition / general

* Idiopathic, self limited dermatosis typically characterized by erythematous papules and plaques and characterized histologically by palisaded or interstitial granulomatous inflammation

Essential features

* Benign, self limited dermatosis characterized by erythematous papules and plaques, classically in an arciform or annular configuration
* 2 classic morphologic patterns
  + Palisaded granulomas with central necrobiosis (collagen degradation) with peripheral histiocytes and admixed lymphocytes
  + Histiocytes intercalating among collagen bundles with interstitial mucin
* Associations with hyperlipidemia and diabetes mellitus
* No proven treatment for this recurring, idiopathic disease

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [L92.0](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/L00-L99/L80-L99/L92-/L92.0) - granuloma annulare

Epidemiology

* F:M = 2:1 ([G Ital Dermatol Venereol 2008;143:359](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19169208))
* Favors children (especially subcutaneous granuloma annulare / pseudorheumatoid nodule) and young adults ([Ann Acad Med Singap 2000;29:714](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11269975))
* Usually asymptomatic

Sites

* Classically extremities but virtually any site possible ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2016;75:457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27543209))
* Pseudorheumatoid nodule (subcutaneous variant)
  + Classically periarticular ([J Pediatr Orthop 1993;13:582](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8376556))
  + Distal lower extremity, scalp ([Pediatr Dev Pathol 1998;1:300](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10463292))

Pathophysiology

* Delayed type hypersensitivity reaction plausible based on T cell subpopulations ([Arch Dermatol 1983;119:125](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6218788))
* Th1 cytokines (e.g. IFNγ, TNFα) ([Arch Dermatol Res 2000;292:384](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10994772))
* Sequestration of macrophages in dermis via macrophage inhibitory factor ([Br J Dermatol 1976;95:481](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/791335))

Etiology

* Numerous inciting factors reported
  + Viral infections ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2000;43:918](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11044822))
  + Arthropod bites ([Biochem Int 1991;23:507](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1877989))
  + Ultraviolet radiation ([Arch Dermatol 1990;126:830](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2346333))
  + *Borrelia* infection ([J Cutan Pathol 2008;35:1050](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18616764))
  + HIV ([Int J STD AIDS 2019;30:206](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30486766))
  + Medications (e.g. TNFα inhibitors) ([Curr Health Sci J 2020;46:203](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32874695))
  + Paraneoplastic ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2018;79:913](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29920319))

Clinical features

* Variants
  + Classic: localized, annularly arranged pink to red nonscaly macules or papules, sometimes forming arciform to annular plaques, favoring extremities
    - Approximately 75% of cases ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2016;75:457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27543209))
  + Generalized: macules, papules, patches, plaques or nodules widely distributed over trunk and extremities
    - Annular or nonannular
    - Mean age: 51.7 years ([J Am Acad Dermatol 1989;20:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2913080))
    - Associated with diabetes mellitus in 21% of cases (versus 9.7% in localized) ([J Am Acad Dermatol 1989;20:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2913080))
    - 45% have lipid abnormalities ([J Am Acad Dermatol 1989;20:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2913080))
    - Possible association with HLA-Bw35 ([Br J Dermatol 1978;98:425](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/638048))
  + Deep variants
    - Subcutaneous
    - Pseudorheumatoid nodule / juxta-articular nodular
      * Predominantly disease of childhood ([J Pediatr Orthop 1993;13:582](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8376556))
      * Periarticular or scalp
      * Not associated with connective tissue disease ([Pediatrics 2001;107:E42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11230623))
  + Patch
    - Trunk and proximal extremities
    - May be mistaken for mycosis fungoides, morphea or contact dermatitis ([J Cutan Pathol 2020;47:785](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32279342))
    - F > M
  + Rare
    - Perforating ([Case Rep Dermatol 2019;11:233](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31543770))
    - Palmoplantar ([An Bras Dermatol 2018;93:878](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30484534))
    - Linear ([J Dtsch Dermatol Ges 2012;10:434](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22435723))
* F:M = approximately 2:1
* Favors children and young adults
* Usually asymptomatic

Diagnosis

* Histopathologic evaluation confirms diagnosis

Laboratory

* Associated with dyslipidemia and diabetes ([Arch Dermatol 2012;148:1131](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22710282))

Prognostic factors

* Self limited with 40 - 50% chance of recurrence ([Lippincotts Prim Care Pract 1997;1:33](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9166607))
* Generalized form may be more resistant to treatment ([Dermatology 2020;236:369](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32403113))
* Benign ([Dermatology 2020;236:369](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32403113))

Case reports

* 58 year old woman with generalized granuloma annulare treated unsuccessfully with tildrakizumab ([JAAD Case Rep 2020;7:3](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33318989))
* 59 year old man with photoinduced granuloma annulare confirmed by experimental exposure to UVA light ([Actas Dermosifiliogr 2020 Oct 17 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33075292))
* 62 year old man with granuloma annulare arising under systemic psoriasis therapy; successfully treated with adalimumab ([JAAD Case Rep 2020;6:832](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32875031))
* 74 year old woman with recalcitrant generalized granuloma annulare treated successfully with dupilumab ([JAAD Case Rep 2020;7:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33318988))

Treatment

* Largely ineffective ([J Eur Acad Dermatol Venereol 2010;24:103](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19548976))
* Topical or intralesional corticosteroids
* Cryotherapy
* Phototherapy ([Photodermatol Photoimmunol Photomed 2005;21:68](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15752123))
* Laser therapy
* Antimalarial medications
* TNFα inhibitors (also reported to incite disease) ([J Drugs Dermatol 2011;10:1466](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22134573))
* Fumaric acid esters
* Oral antibiotics
  + Rifamicin, ofloxacin and minocycline ([Indian J Dermatol Venereol Leprol 2015;81:35](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25566894))
* Oral retinoids ([Australas J Dermatol 2016;57:327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29896835))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Palisaded or interstitial granulomatous inflammation with mucin unites all forms ([Arch Dermatol 1977;113:1681](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/596898))
* Interstitial may be most common pattern ([Indian Dermatol Online J 2018;9:409](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30505780), [Arch Dermatol 1977;113:1681](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/596898))
* Variable histopathology
  + Interstitial pattern may be associated with patch (rather than papular / plaque) presentation ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2000;42:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10688710), [Br J Dermatol 2019;181:198](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30609014))
* Necrobiotic (collagenolytic) granulomas
  + Granulomas characterized by central areas of degraded collagen surrounded by lymphocytes and histiocytes, occasionally palisaded, are present in dermis
  + Occasional multinucleate giant cells
  + Deep variants
    - Subcutaneous
      * May extend from deep dermis through subcutis to fascia and synovium ([Clin Exp Dermatol 1991;16:218](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1934578))
    - Pseudorheumatoid nodule
      * Nodules composed of palisaded granulomas with central eosinophilic material (degraded collagen) are separated by thickened collagen bundles
      * Minimal or absent mucin
* Interstitial (or incomplete)
  + Busy dermis (interstitial lymphohistiocytic inflammation)
  + Histiocytes and lymphocytes arranged around vessels and intercalating between collagen bundles with conspicuous interstitial mucin
  + Lacks necrobiosis
  + Patch granuloma annulare more likely to be only or predominantly interstitial pattern (without palisaded granulomas) ([J Cutan Pathol 2020;47:785](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32279342))
* Nonnecrobiotic (sarcoidal or tuberculoid)
  + Mimics sarcoidosis or tuberculous granulomas ([Am J Dermatopathol 2015;37:547](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25140662))
  + Increased mucin or eosinophils may suggest granuloma annulare over mimics
* Eosinophils in ~40% of cases ([J Cutan Pathol 1985;12:13](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3973184))
* In generalized granuloma annulare, interstitial and palisading patterns roughly equal in frequency ([Ann Dermatol 2009;21:113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20523767))
* Transepithelial excretion of altered collagen in perforating granuloma annulare
* Minimal epidermal changes
* Potential overlap with other granulomatous disorders

Electron microscopy description

* Cytoplasm of histiocytes contains dense cup-like structures in plasma membrane, pinocytic vacuoles, vesicular structures and large intracellular organelles ([Arch Dermatol 1977;113:1681](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/596898))
* Few secondary lysosomes within histiocytes ([Arch Dermatol 1977;113:1681](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/596898))
* Large amount of cytoplasmic vesicles and debris ([Arch Dermatol 1977;113:1681](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/596898))
* Granular material coating blood vessels ([Arch Dermatol 1977;113:1681](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/596898))
* Degenerating collagen and elastin ([Arch Dermatol 1977;113:1681](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/596898))
* Fibrin within areas of necrobiosis ([Arch Dermatol 1977;113:1681](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/596898))

Sample pathology report

* Skin, left forearm, punch biopsy:
  + Consistent with granuloma annulare (see comment)
  + Comment: Sections show a punch biopsy specimen with a normal epidermis. Within the reticular dermis is a palisaded array of histiocytes associated with central collagen degeneration and mucin deposition.

Differential diagnosis

* [Annular elastolytic giant cell granuloma (actinic granuloma)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorannularelastolytic.html):
  + May be variant of granuloma annulare ([Am J Dermatopathol 1979;1:43](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/398673))
  + Mucin often absent ([Cutis 1998;62:181](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9798106))
  + Elastophagocytosis may be seen in both granuloma annulare and annular elastolytic giant cell granuloma ([J Am Acad Dermatol 1986;14:975](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3722492))
  + Central atrophy can occasionally be noted
  + Minimal or absent necrobiosis
* [Interstitial granulomatous dermatitis (with arthritis)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorinterstitialgranulomatous.html):
  + Histologically (and often clinically) similar to interstitial form of granuloma annulare
    - Interstitial histiocytic infiltrate with variable degeneration of collagen and elastin
  + May be more diffuse ([Br J Dermatol 2012;166:775](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22059717))
  + Mucin infrequently observed
  + Clusters of histiocytes rimming collagen (floating sign) ([Br J Dermatol 2012;166:775](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22059717))
  + Interstitial granulomatous dermatitis may be best differentiated from granuloma annulare based on clinical context
    - Interstitial granulomatous dermatitis usually reported in association with autoimmune disease (especially rheumatoid arthritis) / autoantibodies (especially anti-DNA antibodies) or as an adverse drug reaction (most commonly calcium channel blockers, statins) ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2001;45:286](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11464193), [Dermatol Clin 2015;33:373](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26143420))
  + When reported in association with drug, may be termed interstitial granulomatous drug reaction (IGDR)
    - Interstitial granulomatous drug reaction may have an accompanying interface dermatitis and has been reported as having pseudolymphomatous features ([J Cutan Pathol 1998;25:72](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9521495), [Am J Dermatopathol 2001;23:295](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11481519))
    - Eosinophils may be seen in both connective tissue disease related interstitial granulomatous disease and IGDR ([Am J Case Rep 2014;15:94](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24624249), [Am J Dermatopathol 2010;32:408](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19940750))
    - Overlap with drug induced granuloma annulare and IGDR ([JAAD Case Rep 2018;4:152](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29387770))
* [Necrobiosis lipoidica](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumornecrobiosislipoidica.html) ([J Dtsch Dermatol Ges 2015;13:211](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25721629)):
  + Layer cake appearance
  + Minimal mucin
  + Involves the entire dermis and extends into subcutis, often producing a septal panniculitis
  + Plasma cells, giant cells
* [Rheumatoid nodule](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorrheumatoidnodules.html) ([J Dtsch Dermatol Ges 2015;13:211](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25721629)):
  + Histologically very similar to pseudorheumatoid nodule variant of deep granuloma annulare
  + Palisaded histiocytes with fibrinoid necrosis
  + Mucin typically sparse or absent
  + Typically subcutis rather than dermal (versus deep granuloma annulare)
* **Palisaded neutrophilic granulomatous dermatitis** ([G Ital Dermatol Venereol 2018;153:243](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29368864)):
  + Prominent neutrophils and frequent leukocytoclasis
  + Deep basophilic degeneration
  + Associated with connective tissue disease, rheumatoid arthritis and lymphoproliferative disease
* **Acne agminata** ([Skinmed 2005;4:234](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16015073)):
  + Ruptured folliculosebaceous units
  + Tuberculoid granulomas with caseous (coagulative) necrosis
  + Perifollicular fibrosis
