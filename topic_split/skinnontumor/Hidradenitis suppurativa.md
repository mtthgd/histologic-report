Definition / general

* Chronic inflammatory disorder of pilosebaceous apocrine unit characterized by nodules, abscesses, fistulae and sinus tracts, with scarring
* Most commonly affects the axillae and groin, among other areas of young adults

Essential features

* Inflammatory disorder of terminal hair bearing skin, most commonly in the axillae and groin, manifesting with nodules, open comedones and draining sinuses with prominent scarring
* Risk factors include female sex, obesity, smoking and family history
* Associated with metabolic syndrome, adverse cardiovascular events and other inflammatory disorders, e.g. psoriasis
* Follicular occlusion is the first step in pathogenesis, followed by epithelial rupture with ensuing inflammation, foreign body reaction, occasionally apocrine involvement and bacterial colonization
* Effective treatment (may involve antibiotics, biologic or surgical measures) is often challenging

Terminology

* Acne inversa
* Verneuil disease
* Note that hidradenitis suppurativa has been described as a misnomer ([Int J Dermatol 2005;44:535](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15985019))

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [L73.2](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/L00-L99/L60-L75/L73-/L73.2) - hidradenitis suppurativa
* ICD-11: [ED92.0](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f1621794154) - hidradenitis suppurativa

Epidemiology

* Estimated prevalence between 0.00033% and 4.10% ([Exp Dermatol 2019;28:769](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30924968))
* Prevalence in U.S. population recently estimated to be 0.10% ([JAMA Dermatol 2017;153:760](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28492923))
  + More than twice as common in women
  + Highest among patients aged 30 - 39 years
  + Prevalence is more than 3 times greater in African American patients than Caucasian patients
* Onset before 11 years of age estimated to account for 2% of cases ([Pediatr Dermatol 1999;16:292](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10469415))

Sites

* Axillae, inguinal area, inframammary folds, perineum, buttocks, mons pubis, scalp, postauricular area, back ([J Cutan Med Surg 2016;20:52](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26318545), [Dermatology 2015;231:184](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26139027))
* Predilection for apocrine gland bearing areas ([Exp Dermatol 2010;19:533](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19659829))

Pathophysiology

* Historically, the inflammation was thought to originate in the apocrine gland ([Br J Dermatol 1990;122:763](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2369556))
* More recent evidence suggests that follicular occlusion is the primary inciting event ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2015;73:S8](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26470623))
* Occlusion of the follicle is caused by infundibular hyperkeratosis and hyperplasia of the follicular epithelium ([Exp Dermatol 2010;19:533](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19659829))
* Subsequent accumulation of cellular debris, leading to cyst formation ([Exp Dermatol 2010;19:533](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19659829))
* Eventual follicular rupture is met with local immune response and subsequent sinus tract formation ([Exp Dermatol 2010;19:533](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19659829))
* Not a result of inflammation in the apocrine sweat glands or infection ([Br J Dermatol 1990;122:763](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2369556))
* Sebaceous gland volume is reduced but unclear if this has a primary or secondary role in pathogenesis ([Br J Dermatol 2011;164:1017](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21250966))
* Elevated levels of TNF alpha, IL1β, IL10 and IL36 cytokines, as well as Th17 cytokines (e.g. IL17, GM-CSF, IL22, TNF alpha and IFN gamma) ([Br J Dermatol 2011;164:1292](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21332464), [Arch Dermatol Res 2017;309:673](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28852851), [J Invest Dermatol 2017;137:2389](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28652108))
* High Th17 to regulator T cell ratio in lesional HS skin ([J Invest Dermatol 2017;137:2389](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28652108))
* Decreased levels of anti-inflammatory adiponectin ([Br J Dermatol 2018;178:792](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28834543))
* Upregulation of Toll-like receptors, NOD-like receptor protein 3 (NLRP3) inflammasome activity and matrix metalloproteinases ([Inflamm Res 2017;66:931](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28656364))
* Androgens may have deleterious role ([J Clin Aesthet Dermatol 2016;9:44](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27386051))
* Disturbances in cutaneous microbiome and biofilm production may play a role ([Br J Dermatol 2017;176:993](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27564400), [JAMA Dermatol 2017;153:897](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28538949), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2015;73:S12](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26470608))
  + Microbiome profiles of lesional skin may have greater proportions of *Corynebacterium*, *Porphyromonas* and  *Peptoniphilus* species, as compared with healthy controls ([JAMA Dermatol 2017;153:897](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28538949))
  + *Propionibacterium* more abundant in healthy controls' skin than lesional HS skin ([JAMA Dermatol 2017;153:897](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28538949))

Etiology

* Cause unknown (see [Pathophysiology](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorhidradenitis.html#skinnontumorhidradenitispatho) and [Clinical features](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorhidradenitis.html#skinnontumorhidradenitisclin))

Clinical features

* Characteristic lesions are deep seated painful nodules that expand to form abscesses, which may subsequently rupture and drain ([Semin Cutan Med Surg 2017;36:42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28538742))
* Draining sinuses, sinus tracts and open comedones with eventual scarring ([G Ital Dermatol Venereol 2017;152:46](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27854113))
* Heat, sweating, physical activity, shaving and friction may exacerbate symptoms ([F1000Res 2017;6:1272](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28794864))
* Alternating between exacerbations and quiescence is typical ([G Ital Dermatol Venereol 2017;152:46](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27854113))
* Axillae, inguinal area, perineum, buttocks and mons pubis most commonly affected but other sites, e.g. scalp, may be affected as well ([Semin Cutan Med Surg 2017;36:42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28538742), [Dermatology 2015;231:184](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26139027))
* Numerous associations, including obesity, hypertension, thyroid disease, dyslipidemia, psychiatric disease and type VI pityriasis rubra pilaris associated follicular occlusion disease ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2014;71:1144](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25440440), [Clin Exp Dermatol 2004;29:480](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15347329))
* Significant adverse impact on quality of life ([J Eur Acad Dermatol Venereol 2018;32:467](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29125658))
* Increased risk of major adverse cardiovascular events, including myocardial infarction and stroke, as well as all-cause mortality ([JAMA Dermatol 2016;152:429](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26885728))
* Strong association with smoking, i.e. 70 - 90% of HS patients smoke ([Br J Dermatol 2018;178:587](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29595225))
  + Odds of developing HS among smokers compared with nonsmokers was 1.9 in one study ([Br J Dermatol 2018;178:709](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28960235))
* Associated with inflammatory disorders, e.g. psoriasis, Crohn's disease, inflammatory arthritis ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2018 Nov 28 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30502412), [Int J Dermatol 2018;57:547](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29431201), [J Rheumatol 2014;41:490](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24429166))
* A component of several autoinflammatory syndromes, including PASH (pyoderma gangrenosum, acne and hidradenitis suppurativa) and PAPASH (pyoderma gangrenosum, acne, pyogenic arthritis and hidradenitis suppurativa) ([Int J Dermatol 2017;56:811](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28345207))
* Overlap, both in clinical appearance and in pathogenesis, with other follicular occlusion tetrad diseases: acne conglobata, dissecting cellulitis and pilonidal cyst ([Indian Dermatol Online J 2014;5:491](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25396138))

Diagnosis

* 3 criteria for diagnosis: characteristic lesions, predilection for flexural sites and lesion recurrence ([Australas J Dermatol 2018;59:267](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29355905))
* Hurley 3 tiered staging system used to describe disease severity and help select treatment ([J Eur Acad Dermatol Venereol 2015;29:619](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25640693))
* Newer systems (e.g. Sartorius, HSSI, HiSCR) designed to quantify disease intensity and response to treatment ([Br J Dermatol 2003;149:211](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12890229), [Int J Dermatol 2010;49:950](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21128923), [Br J Dermatol 2014;171:1434](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25040429))
* Frequent delay in diagnosis ([Br J Dermatol 2015;173:1546](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26198191))
* Ultrasound may aid in assessment of disease severity ([Dermatol Surg 2013;39:1835](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24118433), [Ital J Dermatol Venerol 2021;156:235](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30298709))

Laboratory

* Decreased ferritin, transferrin saturation and hepcidin ([Dermatology 2020;236:52](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31927542))
* Consider screening for glucose intolerance, hypertension and dyslipidemia ([J Eur Acad Dermatol Venereol 2017;31:e411](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28300335))

Radiology description

* Ultrasound ([Skin Res Technol 2020;26:11](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31423654))
  + Thickened dermis
  + Widened hair follicles
  + Tracts
  + Hypoechoic fluid pockets
  + Decreased surrounding tissue echogenicity
  + Abscess

Microscopic (histologic) description

* In early lesions: follicular hyperkeratosis, hyperplasia of follicular epithelium and perifolliculitis ([Br J Dermatol 2011;164:367](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20831631))
* Dermal mixed inflammatory cell infiltrate, sometimes extending into the subcutis ([Br J Dermatol 1990;122:763](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2369556))
* Neutrophilic abscesses, which may connect with squamous epithelium lined cysts and sinus tracts that extend to the skin surface ([Br J Dermatol 1990;122:763](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2369556))
* Cysts and sinus tracts contain laminated keratin and occasional hair follicles ([Br J Dermatol 1990;122:763](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2369556))
* Granulation tissue with occasional foreign body giant cells present in ~25% of cases ([Histopathology 1993;23:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8406382))
* Inflammation involves apocrine glands in a minority of cases ([J Am Acad Dermatol 1996;34:994](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8647993))
* Dense fibrosis surrounding areas of follicular rupture

Hidradenitis suppurativa

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Loss of function mutations of 3 genes, *NCSTN*, *PSENEN* and *PSEN1*, which are components of the gamma secretase multiprotein complex ([Science 2010;330:1065](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20929727))
* Upregulation of several cytokines, including tumor necrosis factor alpha, IL17 and IL23 ([Front Immunol 2018;9:2965](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30619323))
* Decreased levels of Drosha and DGRC8 miRNA ([J Dermatol Sci 2016;82:166](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26917346))
* Single nucleotide polymorphisms in tumor necrosis factor gene promoter ([Br J Dermatol 2013;168:311](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23106544))

Sample pathology report

* Right proximal thigh, excision:
  + Dermal epithelial lined sinus tract with cyst rupture and surrounding acute and chronic inflammation, consistent with hidradenitis suppurativa

Differential diagnosis

* Other entities within the follicular occlusion tetrad (**acne conglobata**, **pilonidal cyst** and **dissecting cellulitis**) have similar histopathology
* Clinicopathologic correlation, especially with anatomic site, is necessary
* **Ruptured follicular cyst** / [folliculitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorfolliculitis.html):
  + Similar histopathology, except dissecting tracts would not be expected
* **Soft tissue infection**:
  + Special stains for microorganisms, as well as tissue cultures, help to exclude this possibility
