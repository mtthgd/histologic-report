Definition / general

* Autosomal dominant genodermatosis
* Clinical: greasy keratotic papules in a seborrheic distribution, nail changes and mucosal findings
* Histologic: acantholytic dyskeratosis

Essential features

* Autosomal dominant genodermatosis due to mutations in *ATP2A2* gene
* Clinical features: family history, greasy hyperkeratotic papules in a seborrheic / flexural / acral distribution, mucosal lesions and nail changes
* Histologic features: acantholytic dyskeratosis with corp rond and grain formation
* Clinical correlation is crucial to distinguish from mimickers with identical histopathologic features

Terminology

* Darier-White disease, keratosis follicularis and dyskeratosis follicularis

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [L11.8](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/L00-L99/L10-L14/L11-/L11.8) - other specified acantholytic disorders
* ICD-11: [EC20.2](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f248560941) - hereditary acantholytic dermatoses

Epidemiology

* Worldwide distribution ([Br J Dermatol 2002;146:107](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11841374))
* Prevalence varies regionally between 1:30,000 and 1:100,000 ([Br J Dermatol 2002;146:107](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11841374), [Br J Dermatol 1992;127:126](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1390140))
* M = F

Sites

* Seborrheic distribution: central chest, back, marginal scalp and face
* Dorsal hands
* Palms and soles
* Oral cavity
* Nails
* Intertriginous skin

Pathophysiology

* Autosomal dominant mutations in *ATP2A2* gene ([Hum Mutat 2017;38:343](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28035777))
* *ATP2A2* encodes the sarco / endoplasmic reticulum Ca2+ ATPase isoform 2 (SERCA2) ([Hum Mol Genet 1999;8:1611](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10441323))
* Abnormal SERCA2 results in endoplasmic reticulum stress and impaired formation of keratinocyte adhesion proteins ([J Invest Dermatol 2014;134:1961](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24390139))
* Resultant lack of epidermal integrity with acantholysis and impaired keratinization ([Biochim Biophys Acta 2011;1813:1111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21167218))

Etiology

* Autosomal dominant mutations in *ATP2A2* gene ([Hum Mutat 2017;38:343](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28035777))

Clinical features

* Onset 6 - 20 years ([J Am Acad Dermatol 1992;27:40](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1619075))
* Keratotic, red-brown papules on the trunk, face, neck and intertriginous areas; often malodorous
* Hypopigmented macules may predominate in darker skin types ([JAAD Case Rep 2018;4:262](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29687067))
* Flat topped acral papules and palmoplantar pits are common
* Nail changes: longitudinal erythro / leukonychia, subungual hyperkeratosis and V shaped nicking of the free edge of the nail ([J Am Acad Dermatol 1992;27:40](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1619075))
* White papules involving the hard palate, buccal mucosa or tongue
  + Mucosal disease may be associated with salivary gland swelling ([Br Dent J 1991;171:133](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1931295))
* Less common presentations include acral hemorrhagic lesions, segmental lesions and comedonal variants ([Actas Dermosifiliogr 2017;108:e49](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28407871), [Clin Case Rep 2019;7:1362](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31360488), [J Dermatol 2019;46:e211](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30663125))
* Associated with neuropsychiatric conditions, particularly mood disorders ([Br J Dermatol 2010 Sep;163:515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20456342))

Diagnosis

* Family history
* Mucocutaneous findings
* Skin biopsy
* PCR DNA amplification to detect *ATP2A2* mutations ([Mol Med Rep 2015;12:1845](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25872913))

Prognostic factors

* Chronic, with intermittent exacerbations and no tendency for resolution ([J Dermatol 2016;43:275](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26945535))
* Prone to secondary bacterial, viral and fungal colonization / infections ([J Eur Acad Dermatol Venereol 2013;27:1405](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23078077), [Am J Dermatopathol 2017;39:370](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28426487), [Dermatol Ther 2020;33:e14500](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33145898), [Br J Dermatol 2015;172:837](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25142759), [Acta Derm Venereol 2017;97:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27171353))
* Increased morbidity from associated mood disorders ([Br J Dermatol 2010;163:515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20456342))
* Increased risk of squamous cell carcinoma within affected skin ([Br J Dermatol 2008;159:1378](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18808417), [Clin Exp Dermatol 2009;34:e1015](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20055824))

Case reports

* 28 year old woman, 50 year old man and 60 year old woman presenting with acral hemorrhagic lesions ([Actas Dermosifiliogr 2017;108:e49](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28407871))
* 31 year old woman with asymptomatic acneiform papules on the face ([Ann Dermatol 2011;23:S398](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22346288))
* 48 year old woman with worsening of Darier disease after interferon alpha treatment ([Case Rep Dermatol 2016;8:218](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27721754))
* Man in his 50s with leonine face and keratotic papules all over his body, a condition present since childhood ([Case of the Month #517](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case517.htm))
* 67 year old woman with segmental Darier disease treated with doxycycline ([Dermatol Online J 2018;24:13030](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29634885))

Treatment

* Mild disease: topical agents, including retinoids, vitamin D analogues and calcineurin inhibitors ([Pediatr Dermatol 2011;28:197](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20403120), [J Dermatol 2010;37:718](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20649714), [Eur J Dermatol 2011;21:301](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21489907))
* Systemic retinoids ([Indian J Dermatol Venereol Leprol 2021;87:14](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33580925))
* Laser ablation ([Arch Dermatol 1999;135:423](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10206049))
* Botulinum toxin injections ([Orphanet J Rare Dis 2021;16:93](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33602313))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Parakeratosis
* Variable epidermal thickness
* Acantholysis with characteristic dyskeratosis forming corp ronds and grains
  + Corp rond: rounded keratinocyte in superficial spiny and granular layer with basophilic / pyknotic nucleus, perinuclear halo and often a rim of eosinophilic cytoplasm ([J Dermatol 2017;44:232](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28256765))
  + Grain: elongated keratinocyte in the stratum corneum with small basophilic nuclei and intensely pink cytoplasm; appears as plump parakeratosis; may form tiers ([J Dermatol 2017;44:232](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28256765))
  + Corp rond and grain type dyskeratosis is classical but not specific for Darier disease (see [Differential diagnosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumordariersdisease.html#skinnontumordariersdiseaseddx))
* Suprabasal acantholysis and clefting with retained single layer of basal keratinocytes overlying dermal papillae which appear to project into the acantholytic cavity (villi) ([J Dermatol 2016;43:275](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26945535))
* Frank bullae may occur in cases with extensive acantholysis and large clefts ([Arch Dermatol 1982;118:278](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7065684))
* Superimposed fungal, bacterial and herpetic infections may be seen ([J Eur Acad Dermatol Venereol 2013;27:1405](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23078077), [Am J Dermatopathol 2017;39:370](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28426487), [Dermatol Ther 2020;33:e14500](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33145898), [Br J Dermatol 2015;172:837](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25142759), [Acta Derm Venereol 2017;97:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27171353))
* Variable mild perivascular inflammatory cell infiltrate
* Variants: extensive pseudoepitheliomatous hyperplasia, comedonal lesions with prominent villus formation and hemorrhagic bullous lesions ([Am J Dermatopathol 2015;37:323](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25238450), [J Dermatol 2006;33:477](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16848820), [J Cutan Med Surg 2016;20:478](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26992421))
* Flat topped acral papules demonstrate orthokeratosis (may be church spire type), hypergranulosis and papillomatous epidermal hyperplasia; acantholytic dyskeratosis is often subtle or absent

Mild perivascular superficial lymphocytic inflammation, intraepidermal acantholysis and dyskeratosis

Positive stains

* [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html) stain - should be performed to rule out superimposed fungal infections but may not always be positive

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* > 270 unique *ATP2A2* mutations described (missense / nonsense > deletion / splice site / insertion mutations) ([PLoS One 2017;12:e0186356](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29028823), [Hum Mutat 2017;38:343](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28035777))

Sample pathology report

* Skin of chest, punch biopsy:
  + Darier disease (see comment)
  + Comment: The specimen exhibits parakeratosis, epidermal hyperplasia, acantholytic dyskeratosis with prominent corp rond and grain formation, suprabasal clefting with formation of villi and a mild superficial perivascular lymphocytic infiltrate. Although similar acantholytic dyskeratosis may be seen in a number of entities, given the clinical history of greasy papules in a seborrheic distribution, a positive family history and persistence of lesions, the findings are most consistent with Darier disease.

Differential diagnosis

* [Hailey-Hailey disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorhaileyhailey.html):
  + Intraspinous to full thickness acantholysis
  + Less prominent dyskeratosis
  + Dilapidated brick wall appearance
* **Acrokeratosis verruciformis of Hopf**:
  + Church spire hyperkeratosis, hypergranulosis and papillomatous epidermal hyperplasia
  + Lesions may be identical to flat topped acral papules of Darier disease ([J Dermatol 2016;43:275](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26945535), [J Invest Dermatol 2003;120:229](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12542527))
  + No acantholytic dyskeratosis seen
* [Pemphigus vulgaris](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorpemphigus.html):
  + Suprabasal and intraspinous acantholysis without corp rond and grain formation
  + Intraepidermal, intercellular deposition of IgG/C3
* [Transient acantholytic dermatosis (Grover disease)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorGrover.html):
  + May have identical histopathologic features
  + Distinction is easy based on clinical features (relapsing remitting pruritic papular eruption in middle aged to elderly males)
* [Warty dyskeratoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticwartydyskeratoma.html):
  + Identical Darier type acantholytic dyskeratosis but solitary lesion
  + Tends to have distinct cup shaped epidermal invagination and very prominent villus formation ([J Dermatol 2017;44:232](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28256765))
* **Acantholytic dyskeratotic acanthoma**:
  + Darier or pemphigus type acantholytic dyskeratosis but solitary papule or nodule; rarely erythronychia ([J Cutan Pathol 2007;34:494](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17518779))
  + Regular acanthosis without a cup shaped invagination ([Dermatol Pract Concept 2014;4:25](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25126453))
* **Acantholytic dermatosis of the genitocrural area**:
  + Darier or Hailey-Hailey type histopathologic appearance
  + Limited to the genitocrural area
  + No other clinical features of heritable acantholytic disease
* **Focal acantholytic dyskeratosis**:
  + Incidental acantholytic dyskeratosis without a clinical correlate of an acantholytic condition ([J Am Acad Dermatol 1998;38:243](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9486681))
  + May be seen in normal skin or adjacent to a variety of benign or malignant tumors ([J Am Acad Dermatol 1998;38:243](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9486681))

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/skinnontumordariersdiseasebrq1Ho1.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/skinnontumordariersdiseasebrq1Ho1.jpg)

A 35 year old man presents with a 10 year history of scaly papules on the face and chest. Biopsy reveals the findings shown in the image above. What is the most likely diagnosis?

1. Darier disease
2. Hailey-Hailey disease
3. Herpes simplex virus infection
4. Pemphigus vulgaris
5. Seborrheic dermatitis

Board review style answer #1

**A.** Darier disease. The photomicrograph shows acantholysis with dyskeratosis (corp ronds and grains) as well as the formation of villi classically seen in Darier disease. While Hailey-Hailey disease may have acantholysis with dyskeratosis, prominent corp ronds and grains are lacking. Pemphigus has bland acantholysis and herpes simplex shows distinct viral cytopathic change. Seborrheic dermatitis is a spongiotic dermatitis.

Board review style question #2

Which of the following diseases may demonstrate histopathologic features identical to Darier disease?

1. Grover disease
2. Hailey-Hailey disease
3. Inflammatory and linear verrucous epidermal nevus
4. Pemphigus foliaceus
5. Pemphigus vulgaris

Board review style answer #2

**A.** Grover disease. Grover disease has multiple histopathologic patterns including those with Darier type histology. All other options lack typical corp rond and grain formation.

Board review style question #3

Where is the abnormal protein located in Darier disease?

1. Cytoplasm
2. Endoplasmic reticulum
3. Golgi apparatus
4. Mitochondria
5. Nucleus

Board review style answer #3

**B.** Endoplasmic reticulum. An abnormal SERCA2 protein is located in the endoplasmic reticulum and plays a role in the Ca2+ signaling pathway regulating cell to cell adhesion and differentiation of the epidermis ([Nat Genet 1999;21:271](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10080178)).
