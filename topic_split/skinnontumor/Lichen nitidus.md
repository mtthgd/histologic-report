Definition / general

* Uncommon, idiopathic, lichenoid disorder of the skin with characteristic clinical and histopathologic features

Essential features

* Papular eruption most commonly seen in the young
* Discrete lymphohistiocytic infiltrate expanding 1 - 4 dermal papillae
* Epidermal collarette gives ball and claw appearance
* Vacuolar interface change may be prominent
* Multinucleated giant cells often present
* Atypical clinical variants have classical histopathologic findings

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [L44.1](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/L00-L99/L40-L45/L44-/L44.1) - Lichen nitidus
* ICD-11: [EA92](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f157647942) - Lichenoid dermatoses

Epidemiology

* More common in children and young adults ([Clin Dermatol 2015;33:631](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26686015), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2004;51:606](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15389198))
* No consistent sex or ethnic predilection ([Cutis 1978;21:634](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/648164))

Sites

* Skin of upper limbs, dorsal hands, trunk and genitalia ([Pediatr Dermatol 2019;36:189](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30746752))
* Less commonly involves oral mucosa, nails and skin of the lower limbs, palms / soles or face ([Indian J Dermatol 2019;64:62](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30745638), [Pediatr Dermatol 2013;30:e100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23627703))

Pathophysiology

* Largely unknown at this time
* CD4+ T helper cell driven disease ([Clin Exp Dermatol 1990;15:273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2145098))

Etiology

* Generally unknown at this time
* Rarely drug and vaccination associated ([JAMA Dermatol 2018;154:367](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29344609), [Int J Dermatol 2004;43:956](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15569031))

Clinical features

* Localized > generalized disease ([J Dermatol 2012;39:185](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21954905))
* 1 - 2 mm flesh colored or hypopigmented, pinpoint papules
* Koebnerization common
* Typically asymptomatic but occasionally pruritic
* White / gray / yellow papules and plaques if oral mucosa involved
* Sometimes onychodystrophy ([Pediatr Dermatol 2015;32:386](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25440319))
* Unusual presentations: purpuric lesions, palmar involvement, vesicles, hypertrophic / hyperkeratotic lesions, perifollicular papules and perforating lesions ([Acta Derm Venereol 2011;91:108](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21264455), [Pediatr Dermatol 2013;30:e100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23627703), [Arch Dermatol 1972;105:430](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/5012147), [Dermatol Online J 2017;23:15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29469791), [Am J Dermatopathol 2018;40:694](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29649008), [Am J Dermatopathol 2015;37:406](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25803725))
* Rare familial cases and associations with trisomy 21 and [Crohn's disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/coloncrohns.html) reported ([Case Rep Dermatol Med 2012;2012:982084](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23213579), [Pediatr Dermatol 2009;26:109](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19250429), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2012;67:e218](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23062923))

Diagnosis

* Classical clinical presentation
* Histopathologic findings are diagnostic even in atypical variants

Prognostic factors

* Majority resolve spontaneously

Case reports

* 8 and 12 year old boys with papules on skin and nail dystrophy ([Skin Appendage Disord 2019;5:158](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31049338))
* 11 year old boy with linear papules on the thumb and nail involvement ([Indian J Dermatol 2019;64:62](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30745638))
* 24 year old woman with white plaques on the tongue ([Ear Nose Throat J 2016;95:143](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27140009))
* 35 year old man with a 10 year history of hyperkeratotic plaques on the hands ([Dermatol Online J 2017;23:15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29469791))
* 42 year old man with right sided blaschkolinear papules ([JAAD Case Rep 2016;2:102](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27051843))

Treatment

* Benign neglect
* Topical steroids
* Topical calcineurin inhibitors ([J Dermatol 2013;40:499](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23551296))
* Phototherapy ([Photodermatol Photoimmunol Photomed 2013;29:215](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23815355))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Discrete inflammatory cell infiltrate obscuring the dermoepidermal junction
* Lichenoid infiltrate expands 1 - 4 dermal papillae
* Epidermal collarette gives a ball and claw appearance
* Variable admixture of lymphocytes and histiocytes with occasional giant cells
* Basal layer vacuolation and colloid bodies may be prominent
* Overlying hyperkeratosis with or without parakeratosis frequent
* Epidermal atrophy may be noted
* Melanophages prominent in richly pigmented individuals
* Extravasated erythrocytes in purpuric variant ([Dermatol Online J 2007;13:5](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17498424))
* Rarely, may have perifollicular distribution ([Pediatr Dermatol 2013;30:e20](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22775120))

Classical lichen nitidus

Immunofluorescence description

* Immunofluorescence not used for diagnosis
* Immunoglobulins typically absent
* May have fibrinogen in a linear pattern at the dermoepidermal junction ([Arch Dermatol 1973;107:200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4119572))

Positive stains

* [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html) highlights papillary dermal colloid bodies

Sample pathology report

* Left knee, punch biopsy:
  + Lichen nitidus (see comment)
  + Comment: The specimen exhibits focal parakeratosis, focal epidermal atrophy, basal layer vacuolation with associated apoptotic keratinocytes and a subjacent lichenoid lymphohistiocytic infiltrate expanding 1 - 2 dermal papillae. There are scattered multinucleated giant cells, colloid bodies and occasional melanophages. These features are diagnostic of lichen nitidus.

Differential diagnosis

* [Lichen planus](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorlichenplanus.html):
  + Wedge shaped hypergranulosis
  + Sawtooth appearance of rete ridges
  + Broader lichenoid infiltrate
  + Fewer histiocytes in infiltrate
* [Lichen scrofulosorum](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumortb.html):
  + Tuberculid
  + Prominent epithelioid granulomas centered around adnexal structures ([Dermatology 2000;201:272](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11096205))

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/skinnontumorlichennitidusho08.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/skinnontumorlichennitidusho08.jpg)

A 9 year old boy presents with an asymptomatic eruption of hypopigmented pinpoint papules involving the skin of his elbows, knees and penile shaft. The child is otherwise well. A punch biopsy reveals focal hyperkeratosis, epidermal atrophy, basal layer vacuolation and a discrete lymphohistiocytic infiltrate expanding a single dermal papilla and obscuring the dermoepidermal junction. What is the most likely diagnosis?

1. Lichen nitidus
2. Lichen planus
3. Lichen scrofulosorum
4. Lichen simplex chronicus
5. Lichen striatus

Board review style answer #1

**A.** Lichen nitidus

Board review style question #2

Which of the following histopathologic features favors a diagnosis of lichen nitidus over lichen planus?

1. Basal layer vacuolar change
2. Broad band-like lymphocytic infiltrate with scattered colloid bodies
3. Multinucleated giant cells within a focal lichenoid infiltrate
4. Scattered melanophages
5. Wedge shaped hypergranulosis

Board review style answer #2

**C.** Multinucleated giant cells within a focal lichenoid infiltrate
