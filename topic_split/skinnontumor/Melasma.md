Definition / general

* Acquired large areas of darkened skin, usually due to hormonal changes (birth control pills, pregnancy) and usually on both sides of face ([eMedicine](http://emedicine.medscape.com/article/1068640-overview))
* Clinical patterns are centrofacial, malar and mandibular
* Classification (based on level of increased melanin in skin determined by Wood's light examination, [J Am Acad Dermatol 1981;4:698](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6787100)):
  + Epidermal: 70% of cases, increased melanin in basal and suprabasal epidermis; skin pigmentation enhanced under Wood's light; responds best to bleaching agents
  + Dermal: 10% of cases, increase in melanophages in upper dermis; no enhancement of skin pigmentation under Wood's light, responds poorly to bleaching agents
  + Mixed: 20% of cases, mixture of epidermal and dermal features; patchy enhancement of skin pigmentation under Wood's light
  + Indeterminate: 2% of cases; not possible to characterize pigmentation pattern

Terminology

* Also called chloasma

Clinical features

* 90% women; more common in dark skinned individuals
* In pregnant women, hormones cause "mask of pregnancy" on face and darkened skin on abdomen and elsewhere
* Associated with freckles, lentigines and 4+ nevi ([BMC Dermatol 2008;8:3](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18680608))
* Some cases associated with phenytoin, autoimmune thyroid disease, UV light in men ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2012;66:642](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22285674))
* May be due to increase in stem cell factor and c-kit expression ([Br J Dermatol 2006;154:1094](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16704639))

Treatment

* Avoid sunlight, broad spectrum (UVA + UBV) sunscreen
* Also topical hydroquinone, tretinoin, azelaic acid and Vitamin C
* Also laser ([J Cosmet Laser Ther 2008;10:167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18788035)), pidobenzone 4% ([Dermatol Ther 2008;21:S18](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18727811)), chemical peels ([Dermatol Surg 2008;34:1032](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18430172)), ellagic acid containing products ([J Dermatol 2008;35:570](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18837701)) or Mequinol 2% / tretinoin 0.01% topical solution for men ([Cutis 2008;81:179](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18441773))
* Often relapses when treatment stops

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Mild lymphohistiocytic infiltrate in 75%
* Increase in epidermal melanin but no increase in number of melanocytes
* Melanocytes are larger with prominent dendrites ([Am J Dermatopathol 2005;27:96](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15798432))

Electron microscopy description

* More melanosomes in keratinocytes, melanocytes and dendrites

Differential diagnosis

* Broad clinical differential ([Dermatol Clin 2007;25:321](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17662897))
* [Actinic lichen planus](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorlichenplanus.html)
* **Cutaneous mercury deposits**
* **Erythema dyschromicum perstans**: ashy dermatosis
* [Exogenous ochronosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorochronosis.html): history of hydroquinone use
* [Hori nevus](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticdermalmelanocytosis.html): acquired bilateral nevus of Ota-like macules
* [Lichen planus pigmentosus](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorlichenplanus.html): uncommon variant of lichen planus characterized by hyperpigmented, dark brown macules in sun exposed areas and flexural folds
* **Pigmented contact dermatitis (Riehl melanosis)**
* **Post inflammation pigmentation**
