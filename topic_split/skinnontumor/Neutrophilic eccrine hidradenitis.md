Definition / general

* A benign, self limiting chemotherapy associated neutrophilic dermatosis characterized by neutrophilic infiltration of eccrine sweat glands ([Case Rep Dermatol 2013;5:340](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24474918))

Essential features

* Most commonly associated with induction chemotherapy for lymphoproliferative disorders ([Case Rep Dermatol 2013;5:340](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24474918), [StatPearls: Neutrophilic Eccrine Hidradenitis [Accessed 28 April 2020]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28846275))
* Presents as a polymorphous eruption consisting of variably asymptomatic or tender erythematous to violaceous macules, papules, plaques, nodules and pustules ([Arch Dermatol 1986;122:809](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2425751), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2007;56:693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17109994))
* Characterized by neutrophils infiltrating and surrounding the eccrine secretory coils in the deeper reticular dermis or subcutaneous fat
* A benign, self limiting condition in which lesions desquamate and usually heal spontaneously within 1 - 3 weeks ([Case Rep Dermatol 2013;5:340](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24474918))

Sites

* Most commonly affecting the trunk and upper limbs but may affect the face and bilateral ears ([Arch Dermatol 1986;122:809](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2425751), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2007;56:693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17109994))

Pathophysiology

* Postulated to arise secondary to a direct cytotoxic effect of a therapeutic agent secreted in the sweat on the eccrine glands or a form of paraneoplastic syndrome ([Case Rep Dermatol 2013;5:340](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24474918), [Indian J Dermatol 2016;61:683](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27904195))
* Damage of the glands may stimulate an inflammatory response and neutrophilic chemotaxis, which would lead to cellular damage and necrosis of the epithelial cells ([Case Rep Dermatol 2013;5:340](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24474918))

Etiology

* Most commonly associated with induction chemotherapy for lymphoproliferative disorders ([Case Rep Dermatol 2013;5:340](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24474918), [StatPearls: Neutrophilic Eccrine Hidradenitis [Accessed 28 April 2020]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28846275))
* Less commonly described as a prodromal manifestation of acute myelogenous leukemia in the absence of chemotherapy ([J Am Acad Dermatol 1984;11:584](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6238061))
* Reported in patients with HIV and other malignancies such as breast carcinoma, Wilms tumor, osteosarcoma and testicular carcinoma ([Case Rep Dermatol 2013;5:340](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24474918), [Int J Dermatol 2007;46:59](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17214722))
* May be caused by infectious agents such as *Serratia, Enterobacter* and *Staphylococcus aureus* ([Actas Dermosifiliogr 2014;105:e7](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24011437), [Case Rep Dermatol 2013;5:340](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24474918))
* Associated chemotherapeutic agents include cytarabine (most common), doxorubicin, bleomycin, chlorambucil, mitoxantrone, daunorubicin, dactinomycin, vincristine, lomustine, mitoxantrone, cisplatin, vinblastine, topotecan, cyclophosphamide and 5-fluorouracil ([Arch Dermatol 1986;122:809](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2425751), [Acta Dermatovenerol Alp Pannonica Adriat 2018;27:207](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30564834), [Indian J Pharmacol 2013;45:91](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23543588), [Clin Case Rep 2019;7:533](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30899488), [StatPearls: Neutrophilic Eccrine Hidradenitis [Accessed 28 April 2020]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28846275), [Acta Haematol 1992;87:167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1642100))

Clinical features

* Clinically manifests 2 days to 3 weeks following the induction of chemotherapy ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2007;56:693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17109994))
* Presents as a polymorphous eruption consisting of variably asymptomatic or tender erythematous to violaceous macules, papules, plaques, nodules and pustules ([Arch Dermatol 1986;122:809](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2425751), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2007;56:693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17109994))
* Fever or neutropenia commonly present ([Arch Dermatol 1986;122:809](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2425751), [Indian J Dermatol 2016;61:683](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27904195), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2007;56:693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17109994))

Diagnosis

* Skin biopsy

Prognostic factors

* Lesions desquamate and usually heal spontaneously within 1 - 3 weeks ([Case Rep Dermatol 2013;5:340](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24474918))

Case reports

* 35 year old HIV positive man on antiretroviral therapy ([Skinmed 2017;15:297](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28859744))
* 37 year old man receiving FOLFOX chemotherapy for colon cancer ([Acta Dermatovenerol Alp Pannonica Adriat 2018;27:207](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30564834))
* 47 year old man undergoing chemotherapy for acute myeloid leukemia ([Pan Afr Med J 2014;18:727](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25400839))
* 66 year old man receiving chemotherapy for acute myeloid leukemia ([JAAD Case Rep 2019;5:691](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31440558))

Treatment

* Treatment is symptomatic and mainly involves topical or systemic corticosteroids ([Case Rep Dermatol 2013;5:340](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24474918))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Neutrophils infiltrating and surrounding the eccrine secretory coils in the deeper reticular dermis or subcutaneous fat
* Neutrophils may be sparse or lacking when patients are neutropenic ([J Cutan Pathol 1987;14:272](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3479460))
* May be accompanied by dermal edema, syringosquamous metaplasia and necrosis of the eccrine coils and glands ([Dermatol Online J 2011;17:14](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21971279))
* Periadnexal fibroadipose tissue typically shows mucinous degeneration ([J Am Acad Dermatol 1984;11:584](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6238061))
* Epidermal spongiosis, basal vacuolization and focal necrotic keratinocytes may also be present ([Arch Dermatol 1982;118:263](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6950689))
* No evidence of vasculitis ([Dermatol Pract Concept 2016;6:55](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27648385), [J Am Acad Dermatol 1984;11:584](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6238061))

Sample pathology report

* Skin, upper back, biopsy:
  + Neutrophilic eccrine hidradenitis

Differential diagnosis

* **Idiopathic plantar hidradenitis** (neutrophilic eccrine hidradenitis in children):
  + Neutrophilic inflammation is centered on the coiled duct and proximal straight duct, with the secretory apparatus usually being spared or only minimally affected
  + Eccrine syringometaplasia is not seen
* [Sweet syndrome](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorsweetssyndrome.html):
  + Dense diffuse infiltration of neutrophils in the dermis
  + No eccrine involvement
* [Cellulitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/penscrotumcellulitis.html):
  + Diffuse infiltration of neutrophils
  + Positive culture or special stains
  + No eccrine involvement
* [Pyoderma gangrenosum](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorpyodermagangrenosum.html):
  + Nonspecific ulceration with dermal acute and chronic inflammation
  + No eccrine involvement
