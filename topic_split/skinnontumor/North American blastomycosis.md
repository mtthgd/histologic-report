Definition / general

* Skin lesions usually secondary to pulmonary blastomycosis when soil containing mycelia is disturbed and airborne conidia are inhaled ([Clin Microbiol Rev 2010;23:367](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20375357)); pulmonary disease may be subclinical
* Rarely occurs at site of penetrating injuries
* Due to *Blastomyces dermatitidis*, a spherical, double-contoured, 12 micron yeast that reproduces by budding
* Endemic areas are Mississippi, Missouri and Ohio river valleys (USA) and southern Canada
* Risk factors are immunosuppression, collagen vascular disease, being an outdoor worker, having a coworker with blastomycosis ([Can J Infect Dis Med Microbiol 2009;20:117](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21119803))
* May affect healthy patients via spore inhalation; usually men > women because they participate more in activities that disturb infected soil

Clinical features

* Slowly enlarging verrucous plaques containing multiple small abscesses

Diagnosis

* Histology, smears and culture using Sabouraud dextrose agar ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:256](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20090507))

Case reports

* 53 year old African American man with diabetes and knee mass ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2005;129:e132](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15859661))

Treatment

* Itraconazole or long term amphotericin

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Marked pseudoepitheliomatous hyperplasia of epidermis
* Granulomatous and neutrophilic infiltrate
* Fungi are within giant cells
* *Blastomyces dermatitidis* is a 12 micron, spherical, double-contoured yeast with broad based buds

Differential diagnosis

* **Alternaria species:** may have similar histologic features, but differentiate based on culture ([J Cutan Pathol 2011;38:923](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21752048))
* [Squamous cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticscc.html): may resemble pseudoepitheliomatous hyperplasia of fungal infection, but has marked atypia and invasion
