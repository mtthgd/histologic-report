Definition / general

* Spread of fungi to nail apparatus, caused by *Trichophyton rubrum* and *Trichophyton mentagrophytes*; also *Candida albicans* and *Scopulariopis brevicaulis*
* Often multiple fungal species
* Common in patients with diabetes; associated with NOT washing feet daily ([Int J Nurs Stud 2011;48:1101](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21367414))
* Proposed clinical classification: distal and lateral subungual (beneath the nail), superficial, endonyx (diffuse milky-white discolouration of affected nail but no nail bed hyperkeratosis and no onycholysis), proximal subungual, mixed, totally dystrophic and secondary onychomycosis ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2011;65:1219](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21501889))

Treatment

* Treatment recommended even with negative laboratory findings, because repeated testing is eventually positive in 94% ([Clin Exp Dermatol 2011;36:724](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21623878))
* Fungi are embedded in nail and are require months of treatment for success ([Wikipedia](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Onychomycosis))
* Systemic therapy includes terbinfine, itraconazole, griseofulvin and fluconazole or fluconazole 1% and urea 40% ([J Dermatolog Treat 2012;23:453](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21781012))
* Over the counter Vicks VapoRub may be effective ([J Am Board Fam Med 2011;24:69](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21209346))
* Initial trials suggest laser therapy may be useful ([J Cosmet Laser Ther 2011;13:2](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21250792))
