Definition / general

* Rare non-Langerhans histiocytic disease affecting primarily young adults ([Dermatol Clin 2015;33:465](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26143426))
* Part of a clinically heterogenous group of histiocytic disorders collectively called reticulohistiocytoses, which can be divided into
  + Solitary cutaneous reticulohistiocytoma, an indolent skin limited process
  + Multicentric reticulohistiocytosis, a disseminated process with involvement of skin, soft tissue (erosive arthritis), liver and potentially bone marrow

Essential features

* Usually presents as a single reddish brown dermal lesion
* Histologically identical to multicentric reticulohistiocytosis
* CD68 and CD163 positive
* Histiocytic cells containing eosinophilic hyalinized / ground glass cytoplasm is the histological hallmark

Terminology

* Synonymous with giant cell reticulohistiocytoma or solitary cutaneous reticulohistiocytoma

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [D76.3](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/D50-D89/D70-D77/D76-/D76.3) - other histiocytic syndromes
* ICD-11: [XH33Q1](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#1375097941) - reticulohistiocytoma

Epidemiology

* Primarily young adults ([Dermatol Clin 2015;33:465](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26143426))
* Has been reported in children and the elderly
* Slightly M > F ([J Eur Acad Dermatol Venereol 2020;34:1684](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31955466))

Sites

* Head, neck and trunk ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:521](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16625100), [J Eur Acad Dermatol Venereol 2020;34:1684](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31955466))
* Can theoretically present anywhere; rarely on the face, fingers, nails or near joints ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:521](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16625100), [J Eur Acad Dermatol Venereol 2020;34:1684](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31955466))

Etiology

* Unknown at this time

Clinical features

* Presents as a solitary nodule that can appear white, yellow, pink, brown or red ([Dermatol Clin 2015;33:46](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26143426), [J Eur Acad Dermatol Venereol 2020;34:1684](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31955466))
* Can involve mucosa
* Is not associated with widespread presentation or systemic symptoms

Diagnosis

* Based on both histologic and clinicopathologic correlation
* Clinical context is key in terms of distinguishing reticulohistiocytic granuloma from multicentric reticulohistiocytosis

Prognostic factors

* Some cases self resolve ([Dermatol Clin 2015;33:46](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26143426))
* Excision of lesion is curative ([Dermatol Clin 2015;33:46](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26143426))

Case reports

* 6 year old girl with an asymptomatic solitary thigh nodule ([Int J Dermatol 2022;61:e186](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34473862))
* 32 year old man with a reddish brown temple papule ([JAAD Case Rep 2018;4:1039](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30456281))
* 34 year old man with a nodule on the glans penis ([Int Urol Nephrol 2012;44:1345](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22777481))
* 56 year old woman with an enlarging right eyelid nodule ([Ophthalmic Plast Reconstr Surg 2015;31:e13](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24807799))
* 61 year old man with a sacrococcygeal mass ([Asian J Surg 2023;46:1315](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36114068))
* 71 year old man with a left cheek nodule ([J Cutan Pathol 2020;47:985](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32342514))

Treatment

* Can self resolve or lesion can be excised

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Usually a well circumscribed nodular dermal histiocytic infiltrate comprised of eosinophilic and ground glass cytoplasm within the epidermis displaying variable hyperplasia and hyperkeratosis
* Lesional cells show peripheral lacunar-like clearing and harbor predominantly round to oval nuclei with occasional cleaved forms
* Histiocytes can be multinucleated or have cytologic atypia
* Can have background inflammatory cells present; lymphocytes are also typically present with peripheral distribution
* Mixed inflammatory cell infiltrates may be observed; early lesions show sparse multinucleated giant cells, whereas later lesions can exhibit a diffuse giant cell response
* Foamy macrophages may be present
* Can see entrapped dermal collagen between clusters of large histiocytes
* Reference: [Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:521](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16625100)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Currently not well understood
* *RAF1* and *TSC2* somatic mutations reported in a single case ([J Cutan Pathol 2020;47:985](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32342514))
* Multicentric reticulohistiocytosis has been linked to mutations in the PERK pathway, *TET2* mutations and translocations involving *KIF5B* ([J Eur Acad Dermatol Venereol 2019;33:e195](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30710384), [Haematologica 2020;105:e61](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31171640))

Sample pathology report

* Skin, left shoulder, excision:
  + Reticulohistiocytic granuloma (solitary reticulohistiocytoma) (see comment)
  + Comment: Sections demonstrate numerous mononuclear and multinuclear histiocytes with an eosinophilic and ground glass cytoplasm within the dermis. The lesion stains positive for CD68 and CD163 and negative for S100, thus supporting the diagnosis of reticulohistiocytic granuloma. Reticulohistiocytic granulomas are histologically identical to multifocal reticulohistiocytosis but differ clinically as the former usually presents as a solitary lesion.

Differential diagnosis

* [Multicentric reticulohistiocytosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormultireticulo.html) ([Clin Case Rep 2023;11:e7846](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37645054), [J Eur Acad Dermatol Venereol 2020;34:1684](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31955466)):
  + F > M, with predilection for 40 - 50 years of age
  + Commonly presents with widespread lesions and severe arthritic symptoms
  + Coral bead lesional pattern around the fingernails are a hallmark finding
* [Juvenile xanthogranuloma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticjuvenilexantho.html) ([StatPearls: Juvenile Xanthogranuloma [Accessed 4 September 2024]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30252359)):
  + Non-Langerhans histiocytic disease in children
  + Presents commonly with multiple skin nodules
  + Foreign body giant cells, Touton giant cells and foamy histiocytes are often seen histologically
  + Although both can exhibit ground glass cytoplasm, it is less commonly observed in juvenile xanthogranuloma
* [Rosai-Dorfman disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodessinushistiocytosiswithmassivelymph.html):
  + Often presents with systemic symptoms such as lymphadenopathy
  + Consistently [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)+
  + Associated with autoimmune or lymphoproliferative disorders ([Dermatol Clin 2015;33:46](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26143426))
* [Melanoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticmelanoma.html):
  + Stains positive for melanocyte markers such as [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html), [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html), [MART1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html)
  + Malignant melanocytes do not typically resemble histiocytes
* [Epithelioid sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueepithelioidsarcoma.html) ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2009;133:814](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19415960)):
  + Malignant cells are spindled and have storiform collagen patterns
  + Stains consistently positive for [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) and [cytokeratins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)

Board review style question #1

[![Reticulohistiocytic granuloma (solitary reticulohistiocytoma)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/skinreticulohistiocyticgranDaiPrieto03.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/skinreticulohistiocyticgranDaiPrieto03.jpg)

Which of the following IHC stains is most likely to be useful in the diagnostic workup of a suspected reticulohistiocytoma?

1. CD8
2. CD34
3. CD56
4. CD68
5. S100

Board review style answer #1

**D**. CD68 is one of the few stains, in addition to CD163, which have been documented in the literature to stain positive in reticulohistiocytomas. Answer E is incorrect because S100 typically stains negative in reticulohistiocytomas. Answer A is incorrect because CD8 is a T cell surface marker that is not associated with reticulohistiocytoma staining patterns. Answer B is incorrect because CD34 is a marker of vascular endothelial cells and it is not associated with reticulohistiocytoma staining patterns. Answer C is incorrect because CD56 is a marker of NK cells and it is also not associated with reticulohistiocytoma staining patterns.

Board review style question #2

Which of the following clinical features is most characteristic of reticulohistiocytic granuloma (solitary reticulohistiocytoma)?

1. It is associated with chronic immunosuppression
2. It is associated with middle aged White women
3. It is associated with multiple site involvement
4. It is associated with severe arthritis
5. It usually presents as a single lesion

Board review style answer #2

**E**. It usually presents as a single lesion. Reticulohistiocytic granuloma clinically presents as a solitary lesion or nodule. Answer C is incorrect because multicentric reticulohistiocytosis has multiple site involvement, while reticulohistiocytic granuloma does not. Answer D is incorrect because severe arthritis can also be associated with multicentric retriculohistiocytosis (a sequelae of the condition). Answer B is incorrect because multicentric retriculohistiocytosis has been most often reported to affect middle aged White women. Answer A is incorrect because reticulohistiocytic granuloma has not been documented in the literature to be associated with chronic immunosuppression.
