Definition / general

* Historically there has been controversy as to whether Stevens-Johnson syndrome (SJS) is a distinct entity or at the center of the disease spectrum that includes [erythema multiforme](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorerythemamultiforme.html) (EM) and toxic epidermal necrolysis (TEN)
* Currently, there is an increasing trend for SJS and TEN as representing the ends of a spectrum of severe epidermolytic adverse cutaneous drug reactions ([Orphanet J Rare Dis 2010;5:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21162721))

Terminology

* **SJS:** keratinocyte necrosis; epidermal detachment Orphanet J Rare Dis 2010;5:39)
* **SJS / TEN:** considered point of overlap of two diseases in patients with 10 - 30% body surface area epidermal detachment ([N Engl J Med 1994;331:1272](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7794310))
* **TEN:** severe disease state and the end of a spectrum of epidermolytic adverse cutaneous drug reactions; full thickness epidermal necrosis; epidermal detachment > 30% body surface area ([Orphanet J Rare Dis 2010;5:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21162721))
* **Atypical SJS:** referred to by some as severe mucositis with *Mycoplasma pneumoniae* infection but without skin lesions ([Pediatrics 2007;119:e1002](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17353300)), while others argue that the classification of SJS requires cutaneous involvement ([Pediatr Dermatol 2006;23:546](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17155996))

Epidemiology

* Incidence: 1.2 - 6 per million persons per year ([Allergol Int 2006;55:9](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17075281))
* Increased incidence with HIV ([Orphanet J Rare Dis 2010;5:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21162721))

Sites

* Lesions usually begin on trunk and spread centrifugally

Pathophysiology

* Mechanism unknown but appears to be CD8+ T cell mediated immune reaction ([J Allergy Clin Immunol 2011;127:S74](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21354503))
* Granulysin, a mitochondrial protein, is most important factor in epidermal destruction; is found in blister fluid in increasing concentrations with increased severity of disease ([Nat Med 2008;14:1343](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19029983))

Etiology

* Cases in children are usually associated with infection, especially *Mycoplasma pneumonia* and herpes simplex virus
* In adults, most cases are due to medications, including:
  + Allopurinol (most common cause of SJS and TEN in study of 379 patients in Europe / Israel) ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2008;58:25](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17919772))
  + Antibiotics: sulfonamides, cephalosporins, quinolones, aminopenicillins ([Orphanet J Rare Dis 2010;5:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21162721))
  + Nonsteroidal anti inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs)
  + Anticonvulsants: carbamazepine, phenytoin, phenobarbital ([Orphanet J Rare Dis 2010;5:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21162721))
  + Corticosteroids
* ALDEN (algorithm for assessment of drug causality in SJS and TEN) provides a structured approach to determine the responsible drug ([Clin Pharmacol Ther 2010;88:60](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20375998)), although no identifiable cause in some cases ([Orphanet J Rare Dis 2010;5:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21162721))

Clinical features

* Drug associated cases typically present one to three weeks following initiation of therapy with the offending drug; occurs more rapidly with re-challenge ([N Engl J Med 1994;331:1272](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7794310))
* Fever, malaise, cutaneous and mucosal eruption
* Cutaneous and mucosal lesions are tender
* Nikolsky sign positive for epidermal detachment with application of tangential mechanical pressure ([Orphanet J Rare Dis 2010;5:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21162721))
* Eruption consists of either "flat atypical target" lesions or erythematous to purpuric macules, many with central epidermal necrosis or blister formation
* Epidermal detachment Variable GI and respiratory tract involvement ([Orphanet J Rare Dis 2010;5:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21162721))
* Potentially fatal, a medical emergency

Diagnosis

* Based on clinical picture plus confirmatory skin biopsy showing vacuolar interface alteration, often with epidermal necrolysis ([Orphanet J Rare Dis 2010;5:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21162721))

Prognostic factors

* Mortality rate 1 - 5%; rises with increasing surface area of epidermal detachment; higher in elderly ([Orphanet J Rare Dis 2010;5:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21162721))
* SCORTEN scoring system is used to rapidly evaluate risk and prognosis ([J Invest Dermatol 2000;115:149](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10951229))
* One study of 27 patients suggested Mycoplasma related disease has a less severe course than drug related cases ([Mayo Clin Proc 2010;85:131](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20118388))

Case reports

* 3.5 year old girl with severe gynecologic sequelae of Stevens-Johnson syndrome and toxic epidermal necrolysis ([J Reprod Med 2013;58:354](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23947089))
* 12 year old boy with Mycoplasma pneumoniae induced recurrent Stevens-Johnson syndrome ([Pediatr Dermatol 2013;30:624](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24016288))
* 19 year old woman with photo induced Stevens-Johnson syndrome ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2014;71:e82](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25128136))
* 19 and 36 year old women with Stevens-Johnson syndrome associated with sulfasalazine ([J Crohns Colitis 2011;5:457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21939920))
* 28 year old man with Stevens-Johnson syndrome associated with furosemide ([J Pharm Pract 2010;23:367](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21507837))
* 49 year old man with doxycycline-induced Stevens-Johnson syndrome ([Cornea 2011;30:595](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21099409))
* 53 year old woman with Stevens-Johnson syndrome associated with adalimumab ([Rheumatol Int 2013;33:1351](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22187054))
* Associated with sorafenib for metastatic renal cell carcinoma ([Urol Int 2013;91:482](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23969404))
* Colonic involvement in Stevens-Johnson syndrome ([Dig Endosc 2012;24:382](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22925298))
* 14 adult cases ([Ann Allergy Asthma Immunol 2013;110:207](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23548536))

Treatment

* Withdrawal of all potential medication triggers, especially medications which have been initiated within the past four weeks ([Semin Cutan Med Surg 2014;33:10](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25037254))
* Primarily supportive and symptom targeted ([Semin Cutan Med Surg 2014;33:10](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25037254))

Detached and necrotic epidermis

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Early lesions: apoptotic keratinocytes scattered in basal epidermis
* Later lesions: numerous necrotic keratinocytes, full thickness epidermal necrosis and subepidermal bullae
* Epidermal changes are often accompanied by a moderate or dense lymphocyte predominant dermal infiltrate
* Less commonly, neutrophils and eosinophils are present ([Mayo Clin Proc 2010;85:131](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20118388))
* Less common findings are red blood cell extravasation, pigment incontinence, regenerating epidermis, parakeratosis and necrosis of hair follicle ([Mayo Clin Proc 2010;85:131](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20118388))
* Clinical correlation is essential to distinguish erythema multiforme, SJS and TEN, as they may look nearly identical histologically
  + Cannot reliably distinguish based on full thickness epidermal necrosis / necrolysis, because EM may have it and SJS / TEN may not, depending on the site of the biopsy

Differential diagnosis

* Other entities with vacuolar interface dermatitis (with individual keratinocyte necrosis):
  + [Acute graft-versus-host disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorGVHD.html) (GVHD)
  + **Connective tissue disease** (including lupus and dermatomyositis)
  + [Fixed drug eruption](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumordrugreaction.html)
  + [Pityriasis lichenoides (PLEVA or PLC)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorpleva.html)
  + [Subacute radiation dermatitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorradiationassociated.html)
  + **Viral exanthems**
* Other entities with desquamation and epidermal detachment:
  + [Bullous pemphigoid](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorbullouspemphigoid.html)
  + Congenital bullous diseases, such as [epidermolysis bullosa](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorepidermolysisbullosa.html)
  + **Other autoimmune blistering diseases**
  + [Pemphigus](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorpemphigus.html)
  + **Staphylococcal scalded skin syndrome**
  + **TEN**
