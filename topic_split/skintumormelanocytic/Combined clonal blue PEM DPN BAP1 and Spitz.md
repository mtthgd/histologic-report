Definition / general

* Uncommon; two types of melanocytic proliferation in same pigmented lesion ([Am J Surg Pathol 1991;15:1111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1746678))
* Usually common blue nevus of penetrating type and acquired nevus ([Pathology 2004;36:419](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15370111))
* Rarely includes Spitz nevus, which are difficult to classify by dermoscopy ([J Cutan Pathol 2004;31:600](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15330991))

Terminology

* Also called melanocytic nevus with phenotypic heterogeneity

Epidemiology

* Mean age 30 years

Sites

* Usually trunk, head and neck and upper extremity

Clinical features

* May resemble melanoma, particularly at dermoscopy ([Dermatology 2007;214:174](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17341869), [Dermatol Surg 2006;32:1176](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16970701))

Case reports

* Common blue nevus and pigmented epithelioid melanocytoma ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2008;58:1021](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18485984))
* Compound nevus and spindle cell Spitz nevus ([J Dermatol 2000;27:233](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10824486))
* Cellular blue nevus in reticular dermis, overlying compound melanocytic nevus with transition to melanoma in situ ([Am J Dermatopathol 1991;13:169](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2029090))
