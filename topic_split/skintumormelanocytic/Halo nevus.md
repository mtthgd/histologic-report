Definition / general

* Nevus surrounded by zone of hypopigmented skin ([eMedicine: Halo nevus](http://emedicine.medscape.com/article/1057446))

Terminology

* Also called leukoderma acquisitum centrifugum, Sutton nevus ([Am J Dermatopathol 2003;25:349](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12876497))

Epidemiology

* 1% of population before adulthood, no racial or gender predilection ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2012;67:582](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22387032))

Sites

* Back, followed by head / neck are most common sites ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2012;67:582](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22387032))

Clinical features

* Single or multiple
* Usually due to regression caused by cell mediated immunity, or less commonly humoral immunity or granulomatous inflammation ([Am J Dermatopathol 2008;30:233](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18496423))
* No fibrosis, in contrast to melanoma ([J Cutan Pathol 2007;34:301](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17381800))
* Seen in 18% of Turner syndrome patients ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2004;51:354](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15337976))
* May persist for decades ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2012;67:582](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22387032))
* Note: classify based on nevus cell population because halo phenomenon occurs in various nevus types ([J Cutan Pathol 1995;22:342](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7499574))

Treatment

* Observation, excision (for cosmetic reasons) or laser for facial lesions to induce repigmentation ([J Cosmet Laser Ther 2007;9:245](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18236244))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Residual melanocytes with heavy infiltration by lymphocytes and histiocytes that destroy pigment containing cells
