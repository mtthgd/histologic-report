Definition / general

* Often only part of lesion is excised and pathologist report includes recommendation for complete excision ([J Clin Pathol 2004;57:1121](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15509670))

Patterns of Benign Behavior

* Lentiginous hyperplasia (single cell melanocytic growth along dermoepidermal junction)
* Nested proliferation
* Melanocyte nuclei are smaller than adjacent keratinocytes
* Pagetoid proliferation (discohesive single cell growth throughout entire epidermis) is nonspecific; present in Spitz nevi, acral nevi, melanoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:792](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7793477))

Other Benign Features

* Symmetric pattern of growth and involution
* Lateral dimension of dermal component should roughly equal that of original epidermal component ("shoulder" = when epidermal component extends laterally beyond dermal)

Maturation

* Deeper portion of lesion has smaller cells with less pigment, less atypia
* Deep cells grow in smaller sized nests or single cells
* May resemble neural tissue
* Maturation is a morphologic term; does not truly reflective biologic maturation ([Am J Dermatopathol 1988;10:20](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3177822))

Case reports

* 7 month old girl with lentiginous hyperplasia associated with hamartoma ([Am J Dermatopathol 2008;30:488](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18806496))
* Lentiginous melanocytic hyperplasia overyling dermatofibroma ([J Dermatol 1996;23:840](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8990711))
