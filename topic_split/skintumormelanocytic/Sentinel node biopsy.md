Definition / general

* Sentinel lymph node is defined as first extracutaneous target of lymphogenous tumor cell spread and potential source of subsequent lymph node metastases and distant metastases ([eMedicine](http://emedicine.medscape.com/article/854424-overview))
* Interval sentinel nodes: nodes receiving direct lymphatic drainage from primary site but lying between tumor and a recognized node field ([Ann Surg Oncol 2011;18:3292](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21913021))
* Lymphatic mapping and sentinel lymph node biopsy are widely used for staging

Recommendations

* Recommended for patients with intermediate thickness melanomas (Breslow thickness, 1 to 4 mm) of any anatomic site ([J Clin Oncol 2012;30:2912](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22778321))
* Tumor in sentinel node by H&E predicts recurrence ([Mod Pathol 2007;20:427](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17334354)), usually leads to dissection of lymph nodes in affected nodal basin ([Ann Surg Oncol 2008;15:1566](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18414952)), which reduces recurrence ([Curr Treat Options Oncol 2008;9:243](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18998214))
* Measuring antimony (originating from antimony sulfide colloid) can confirm sentinel nature of node ([Mod Pathol 2004;17:1191](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15372052))

Prognostic factors

* **Predictors of positive sentinel nodes**: Breslow thickness < 1 mm ([Int J Surg 2008;6:205](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18417434)); lymphatic invasion using D2-40, ulceration ([Arch Dermatol 2008;144:462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18427039)), younger age ([Ann Surg Oncol 2008;15:630](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18080717)), low density of tumor infiltrating lymphocytes (TILs,  [J Clin Oncol 2012;30:2678](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22711850)), absence of regression ([Ann Surg Oncol 2011;18:3593](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21647761))
* **Predictors of positive non sentinel nodes in sentinel node positive patients (occurs in 20 - 35%)**: amount of tumor in sentinel node, Breslow thickness of primary melanoma and density of dendritic WBCs in sentinel node paracortex ([Mod Pathol 2004;17:747](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15098011)); also perinodal intralymphatic tumor ([Ann Surg Oncol 2008;15:1723](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18389323)), depth of invasion in sentinel node ([Ann Surg Oncol 2008;15:1202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18165880))
* Intraoperative touch imprints may be accurate ([Anticancer Res 2008;28:465](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18383886))
* Patients with triple negative (H&E, S100 / HMB45, RT-PCR) nodes have a markedly improved survival ([Mod Pathol 2008;21:438](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18223554))
* **Micrometastasis**: single metastasis < 2 mm are NOT associated with metastases in non sentinel nodes ([J Surg Oncol 2008;98:46](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18452214))

**Selected protocols for sentinel nodes**:

* 3 levels at 250 micrometer intervals, each level has 1 section stained with H&E, S100 and HMB45 ([Am J Surg Pathol 2005;29:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15725798)) OR
* 2 H&E sections, S100 and HMB45 ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1197](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12960803))
* 4 sequential sections of both halves of each sentinel node examined - first and fourth sections stained with H&E, second section stained for S100 and third section for HMB45 ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2008;25:100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18697713))
* Complete step sectioning of nodes with high gamma counts ([Ann Surg Oncol 2008;15:1492](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18340492))

Positive stains

* [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) and
  [NKI-C3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd63.html) are most sensitive stains for nodal metastases but are nonspecific; [MART1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html) is most specific ([Am J Surg Pathol 2001;25:1039](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11474288))
* S100 and MART1 together are recommended ([Hum Pathol 2004;35:217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14991540))
* Pattern of [reticulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsreticulin.html) staining may help distinguish positive node from nodal nevus ([Am J Dermatopathol 2013;35:452](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23694823))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* RT-PCR: prognostic significance of melanocytic mRNA in histologically negative nodes is controversial (significant - [APMIS 2008;116:199](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18377585), not significant - [Mod Pathol 2007;20:427](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17334354))
* RT-PCR detects significant sentinel node metastases that are missed by histopathology but overestimates number of patients with clinically significant metastases ([Melanoma Res 2003;13:313](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12777989))
* FISH detects chromosomal aberrations in 83% of nodal metastatic melanoma vs. 6% of nodal nevi ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20087158))

Differential diagnosis

* **Antigen presenting interdigitating dendritic cells**
* [Benign nevus cells](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticnevigeneral.html): usually in capsule, also parenchyma, no atypia, MART1+, S100+, HMB45- and Ki67- ([Am J Surg Pathol 2002;26:1351](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12360050), [Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:673](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12717252))
* **Histiocytes**: no atypia
* [Tattoo](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumortattoo.html) pigment, see [Dermatol Online J 2005;11:14](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15748555)
