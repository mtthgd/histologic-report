Definition / general

* Subtype of cutaneous melanoma that arises from the nail apparatus
* Variant of acral melanoma ([Cancers (Basel) 2023;15:4417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37686691), [StatPearls: Subungual Melanoma [Accessed 23 December 2024]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29494087))

Essential features

* Melanoma that involves the nail apparatus
* Largely considered a variant of acral melanoma, with a predominantly ultraviolet (UV) radiation mutagenic signature and different somatic mutations compared to acral melanoma ([Cancers (Basel) 2023;15:4417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37686691), [Melanoma Res 2020;30:562](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33156595), [Nat Commun 2020;11:5259](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33067454), [Oncotarget 2020;11:2404](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32637031), [Pigment Cell Melanoma Res 2013;26:758](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23782496))

Terminology

* Subungual melanoma (SUM)
* Nail (unit) melanoma (NM)

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [C43.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C43-C44/C43-/C43.9) - malignant melanoma of skin, unspecified

Epidemiology

* Represents 0.7 - 3.5% of all malignant melanomas reported internationally ([StatPearls: Subungual Melanoma [Accessed 23 December 2024]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29494087))
* Most common variant of melanoma among Black, Asian and Hispanic populations
* Rare in White populations (1 - 3%) ([Orthop Clin North Am 2019;50:357](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31084838))
* 75% of melanomas in African populations, 25% in Chinese populations and 10% in Japanese populations ([StatPearls: Subungual Melanoma [Accessed 23 December 2024]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29494087), [Australas J Dermatol 2001;42:71](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11309027))
* Most common in the seventh decade of life for men and sixth for women ([StatPearls: Subungual Melanoma [Accessed 23 December 2024]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29494087), [Dermatol Surg 2017;43:798](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28296790), [J Med Assoc Thai 2015;98:820](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26437541))

Sites

* Nail unit
* Great toe and thumb are the most common digits affected (75 - 90% of cases) ([Dermatol Surg 2017;43:798](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28296790), [J Med Assoc Thai 2015;98:820](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26437541))
* Fingernails more likely to be affected than toenails ([Cancers (Basel) 2023;15:4417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37686691))

Pathophysiology

* More likely to demonstrate mutational signature related to UV radiation versus acral melanoma (AM) ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2018;79:559](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29438763), [Cancers (Basel) 2023;15:4417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37686691))
* Several somatic mutations have been reported in SUM, including *KIT, GNAQ, NF1, BRAF* and *NRAS* mutations ([Melanoma Res 2020;30:562](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33156595), [Nat Commun 2020;11:5259](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33067454), [Oncotarget 2020;11:2404](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32637031), [Pigment Cell Melanoma Res 2013;26:758](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23782496), [J Eur Acad Dermatol Venereol 2022;36:651](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35098589), [Nature 2017;545:175](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28467829), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2018;79:559](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29438763), [Cancers (Basel) 2023;15:4417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37686691))
* Tends to be triple negative (*BRAF, RAS, NF1*) with mutations involving *GNAQ* R183 and *KIT* in the majority of SUM cases reported, followed by mutations in *RAS* and *BRAF* ([Cancers (Basel) 2023;15:4417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37686691))
* *BRAF* and *RAS* (*NRAS* and *KRAS*) mutations are more common in AM than SUM ([Cancers (Basel) 2023;15:4417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37686691))
* *BRAF* mutations are seen in a minority of SUM cases ([J Invest Dermatol 2018;138:384](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28870692), [Cancers (Basel) 2023;15:4417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37686691))
* SUM had an increased number of single nucleotide variations (SNVs), versus copy number alterations (CNAs) in AM ([Cancers (Basel) 2023;15:4417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37686691))
* Increased frequency of CNAs and *CDK4* mutations in SUM, with *BRAF* mutations in the minority of SUM cases, has been reported ([J Invest Dermatol 2018;138:384](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28870692))

Etiology

* Stress, trauma and melanocyte activation may play a role ([StatPearls: Subungual Melanoma [Accessed 23 December 2024]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29494087))
* UV radiation may be implicated ([Cancers (Basel) 2023;15:4417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37686691))

Clinical features

* Dark black, vertical band affecting the nail
* Extension to periungual or lateral nail folds surrounding skin (Hutchison sign) may be seen ([StatPearls: Subungual Melanoma [Accessed 23 December 2024]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29494087), [Dermatol Clin 2015;33:185](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25828711), [Actas Dermosifiliogr (Engl Ed) 2018;109:801](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30082026))
* Diagnosis is often delayed because the lesion is attributed to trauma; most (73%) cases are AJCC stage II / III, acral lentiginous subtype (66%) and Clark level IV / V (79%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1902](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18043047))
* Sentinel node metastases in 24% of cases
* Width > 3 mm or > 28% involvement of the nail plate and nail plate dystrophy
* 25% of SUM cases are amelanocytic

Diagnosis

* Clinical presentation (see [Clinical features](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticsubungualmelanoma.html#skintumormelanocyticsubungualmelanoma_clinical))
* Definite diagnosis made through histologic examination
* Lack of dermal epidermal junction of the nail matrix can make biopsy inadequate or suboptimal for evaluation of NUM

Prognostic factors

* Poor prognosis related to late presentation / detection ([Cancers (Basel) 2023;15:4417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37686691), [StatPearls: Subungual Melanoma [Accessed 23 December 2024]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29494087))
* Early diagnosis good prognostic features ([Cancers (Basel) 2023;15:4417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37686691))
* Breslow thickness, ulceration, high mitotic rate, microsatellites, sentinel lymph node status ([Br J Dermatol 2007;157:311](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17596173), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2021;145:943](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33290520))
* 5 year survival for patients with SUM reported to be 87.1% in one study, with subungual versus acral location as an independent prognostic factor for improved survival (OS) ([Cancers (Basel) 2023;15:4417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37686691))
* Subungual location does not appear to confer a worse prognosis ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2021;145:943](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33290520))

Case reports

* 13 year old Chinese girl with subungual melanoma ([Dermatol Ther 2020;33:e13918](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32594641))
* 31 year old man with amelanotic subungual melanoma ([J Foot Ankle Surg 2009;48:220](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19232976))
* 41 year old man with subungual melanoma, originally thought to be infectious entity, in left thumb ([Asian J Surg 2023;46:3101](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36878796))
* 46 year old man with subungual melanoma and circumferential osteoinvasive disease ([SAGE Open Med Case Rep 2024;12:2050313X241257698](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38828380))
* 51 year old Indian man with subungual melanoma in left thumb nail ([Indian J Dermatol 2015;60:188](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25814711))
* 50 year old man with osteocartilaginous differentiation ([Skeletal Radiol 2003;32:724](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14534752))
* 55 year old and 62 year old women with regressed subungual melanoma simulating cellular blue nevus ([Dermatol Surg 2006;32:577](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16681670))
* 81 year old woman with amelanotic tumor resembling pyogenic granuloma ([J R Coll Surg Edinb 2002;47:638](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12363192))
* 86 year old man with posttraumatic amelanotic tumor ([Dermatol Online J 2008;14:13](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18319030))

Treatment

* Wide, local excision; may require amputation although conservative approach for thumb lesions has been advocated ([Am J Surg 2008;195:244](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18086464), [J Plast Reconstr Aesthet Surg 2007;60:635](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17485051))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Abundant melanin deposition in nail plate
* Presence (remnants) of melanocytes within nail plate is a helpful clue ([J Cutan Pathol 2023;50:580](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36749314))
* Nail bed and matrix will show an irregular and atypical melanocytic proliferation
* Increased number and confluence of atypical junctional melanocytes will be seen as well as florid pagetoid spread of melanocytes; even though the density count is not the most practical or reliable criteria for evaluation melanocyte count, density 40 or higher per millimeter raises the suspicion for SUM
* Melanocytes demonstrate increased nuclear pleomorphism
* Melanocytes may be angulated and hyperchromatic ([J Cutan Pathol 2023;50:580](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36749314))
* Invasive melanoma demonstrates atypical melanocytes within dermis / nail matrix
* Most are of acral lentiginous melanoma subtype (67%), with superficial spreading subtype as second most common; there may be overlap of both subtypes ([StatPearls: Subungual Melanoma [Accessed 23 December 2024]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29494087))
* Inflammation (lymphocyte predominant) is a helpful feature to distinguish melanoma from melanonychia ([StatPearls: Subungual Melanoma [Accessed 23 December 2024]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29494087))
* Increased melanocytic density ([J Cutan Pathol 2023;50:580](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36749314))
  + Melanocyte density in white individuals reportedly 6.5 - 7.7 cells per millimeter along basal layer of epidermis
  + Benign melanocytic hyperplasia demonstrates on average between 15 and 31 melanocytes per millimeter
  + 40 or greater melanocytes per millimeter highly suspicious for melanoma ([J Cutan Pathol 2023;50:580](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36749314))

Positive stains

* Melanocytic markers are typically positive including [MITF](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmitf.html), [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html) and [MART1 / MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html)
* [PRAME](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprame.html) and [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsHMB45.html) are also typically positive ([J Cutan Pathol 2022;49:859](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35794643), [J Cutan Pathol 2023;50:580](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36749314))
* [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) is less sensitive for intraepidermal nail unit melanocytes but higher sensitivity in dermal involvement; can also identify melanocyte remnant within the nail plate
* Of note, [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsHMB45.html) and [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html) may stain pigment and overestimate melanocyte density ([J Cutan Pathol 2023;50:580](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36749314))

Negative stains

* Nonmelanocytic markers

Sample pathology report

* Right great toe nail, excisional biopsy:
  + Invasive melanoma, acral lentiginous type (see comment)
    - Tumor thickness (Breslow): at least 0.4 mm
    - Clark level: at least IV
    - Ulceration: not identified
    - Regression: not identified
    - Mitotic count: 3/mm2
    - Tumor infiltrating lymphocytes: present, nonbrisk
    - Lymphovascular invasion: not identified
    - Perineural invasion: not identified
    - Tissue edges: melanoma present at deep and peripheral tissue edges
  + Comment: Sections show skin, nail bed and matrix with a cellular and atypical compound melanocytic proliferation that extends to deep and peripheral tissue edges. A confluence of atypical melanocytes is appreciated best at the dermoepidermal junction available for review. Pagetoid spread is also readily seen. Abundant pigment within nail plate is also appreciated. Cytologically, the melanocytes are epithelioid to spindled. These melanocytes of interest have atypical and enlarged nuclei with readily identifiable mitotic figures within the dermal component. By immunohistochemistry, the tumor cells are positive for MelanA, HMB45, S100, SOX10 and PRAME. Overall, the findings are supportive of the above interpretation. Correlation with the subsequent excisional specimen is recommended for further definitive staging information and classification.

Differential diagnosis

* [Benign subungual lentigo](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticsolarlentigo.html):
  + Benign subungual lentigo as either benign melanocyte hyperplasia or hyperpigmentation (melanocyte activation) with no increase in melanocytes
  + [Benign melanocytic hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticatypicalmelanocytichyperplasia.html):
    - Increased cell density when it is > 4 - 5 times the normal or higher is in favor of MIS
    - Lack of confluence of melanocytes, multinucleation, multifocal pagetoid spread, moderately dense lichenoid inflammatory infiltrate, cytologic atypia, florid pagetoid spread
    - However, focal pagetoid spread should not be overinterpreted as it can commonly be seen in benign nail lesions
  + **Melanocytic activation**:
    - Increase in melanin pigment within basal epidermis; no definite increase in number of melanocytes
* **Fungal melanonychia**:
  + Clinically can give nails a darkened appearance; however, there is no increase in melanocytes appreciated in the majority of cases
* **Minocycline induced pigmentation**:
  + Clinically can give the nail a darkened appearance with no definite increase in melanocytes appreciated
* **Subungual hemorrhage**:
  + Can give the nail a darkened appearance but hemorrhagic change is appreciated within nail tissue
  + Dermoscopy can be used to help distinguish SUM from subungual hemorrhage
    - Features of SUM include Hutchinson sign, longitudinal irregular lines, triangular shape of bands, vascular pattern and ulcerations ([Br J Dermatol 2013;168:1224](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23302009))
* [Nevus (of nail matrix)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticnevisitesacral.html):
  + Increased number of melanocytes appearing singly and in nests
  + Melanocytes lack pleomorphism and mitotic activity
  + Confluence of melanocytes (particularly at the periphery of the lesion) and pagetoid spread not readily seen ([J Cutan Pathol 2020;47:1159](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32640104))
* **Pediatric melanonychia**:
  + Histologically, can be indistinguishable from subungual melanoma in situ
  + Clinical information including patient age and clinical presentation and course important for distinguishing this entity from melanoma in situ ([J Cutan Pathol 2023;50:580](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36749314))

A 51 year old man presents with a longitudinal black marking on his great toe with involvement of the lateral nail folds. Based on the findings shown in the images above, what is the best diagnosis?

1. Fungal melanonychia
2. Lentigo
3. Subungual hemorrhage
4. Subungual melanoma

Board review style answer #1

**D**. Subungual melanoma. The images show an atypical melanocytic proliferation with increased confluence of atypical melanocytes with nuclear pleomorphism and pagetoid spread. Answer B is incorrect because lentigo does not show an increase in melanocytic proliferation, only increased melanin pigment. Answer A is incorrect because there is an absence of fungal elements in the image and fungal melanonychia lacks an increase in melanocytes. Answer C is incorrect because the clinical findings are not caused by a hemorrhage within the nail but an increase in atypical melanocytes.

Answer A is incorrect because SOX10 would be expected to be positive in melanocytic proliferations.
