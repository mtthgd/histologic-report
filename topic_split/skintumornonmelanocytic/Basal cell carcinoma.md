Definition / general

* Basal cell carcinoma (BCC) arises from the interfollicular or follicular epithelium
* Most common malignant tumor type in humans
* Local aggressive course
* Low disease associated death rate; metastases to lung and bone exceptionally rare
* When multiple, associated with a number of genetic conditions, including basal cell nevus (Gorlin), Bazex-Dupré-Christol, Rombo syndromes and xeroderma pigmentosum

Essential features

* Nests of basaloid cells with peripheral palisading associated with a fibromyxoid stroma

Terminology

* Basal cell epithelioma
* Rodent ulcer
* Basalioma
* Fibroepithelioma of Pinkus

Epidemiology

* Most common cancer type in fair skinned people ([Br J Dermatol 2017;177:359](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28220485))
* Highest rate is observed in Australia (1,000/100,000 person years) ([Br J Dermatol 2012;166:1069](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22251204))
* More frequent:
  + Middle aged adults with increasing incidence with age
  + People with I - II Fitzpatrick skin phototype ([Cancer Epidemiol 2013;37:534](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23849507))
  + Male (M:F = 1.5:1)
* Number of cases is increasing every year worldwide, especially among young age group, with a predilection for women ([Br J Dermatol 2012;166:1069](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22251204), [Pediatrics 2014;134:e4](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24958589))
* Tumors that occur in younger age group tend to behave in an aggressive fashion ([Br J Plast Surg 2000;53:393](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10876275))

Sites

* Sun exposed skin ([G Ital Dermatol Venereol 2014;149:423](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25068230))
* 64% on the head region (most often nodular variant)
* 24% on the trunk (most often superficial variant)
* Rare areas such as anogenital region, nail unit, palm and sole ([J Cutan Pathol 2018 Jun 19 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29920730))

Pathophysiology

* UV radiation induced carcinogenesis
* Mutations in *TP53* gene
* *PTCH1* gene mutations
* Activating mutations in *SMO* gene
* Other genes including mutations in the *CDKN2A* gene and *RAS* genes
* Reference: [Calonje: McKee's Pathology of the Skin, 5th Edition, 2019](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0702069833/pathologyoutl-20)

Etiology

* UV radiation (sun damage and indoor tanning) ([Cancer 1990;65:2811](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2340474), [Am J Epidemiol 1999;150:459](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10472945), [Pediatrics 2014;134:e4](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24958589))
* Ionizing radiation ([J Natl Cancer Inst 1996;88:1848](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8961975))
* Arsenic exposure ([J Dermatol 2017;44:1374](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28815697))
* Genetic conditions
  + Nevoid basal cell carcinoma syndrome (Gorlin syndrome) ([Lancet 1992;339:581](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1347096))
  + Xeroderma pigmentosum ([J Invest Dermatol 2012;132:785](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22217736))
  + Bazex-Dupré-Christol syndrome ([NIH: Bazex-Dupre-Christol syndrome [Accessed 15 December 2020]](https://rarediseases.info.nih.gov/diseases/838/bazex-dupre-christol-syndrome))
  + Oculocutaneous albinism ([BMC Cancer 2014;14:157](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24597988))
  + [Muir-Torre syndrome](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorMuirTorre.html)
* Smoking ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2002;46:706](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12004311))
* [Nevus sebaceus](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticnevussebaceus.html) ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2000;42:263](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10642683))
* Immunosuppression ([Lancet 1998;351:623](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9500317), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2003;49:397](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12963901), [Arch Dermatol 2001;137:459](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11295926))

Clinical features

* Nodular variant usually presents as a pearly pink or flesh colored papule or nodule with arborizing and branching vessels ([Br J Dermatol 2002;147:41](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12100183))
* In the past, ulceration of a nodular BCC gave rise to the term rodent ulcer
* Tumor with ulceration has characteristic rolled borders
* Superficial variant presents with scaly macules, patches or plaques with an erythematous surface
* Pigmented variant resembles a nodular or superficial BCC but has a pigmented surface and can be clinically mistaken for a [melanoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticmelanoma.html)
* Neglected tumors may result in massive skin, soft tissue and bone destruction with severe disfiguration

Diagnosis

* Clinical features
* Dermatoscopy
* Histological features

Prognostic factors

* Histological type associated with risk of local recurrence
  + Lower risk: nodular, superficial, pigmented, infundibulocystic, fibroepithelial
  + Higher risk: basosquamous, sclerosing / morpheaform, keloidal, infiltrating, BCC with sarcomatoid differentiation and micronodular variants
* Circumscription of tumor margins
* Presence of perineural and lymphovascular invasion
* Status of surgical margins
* Tumor size
* Localization (part of skin affected, e.g. head and neck, trunk, etc.)
* History of nonmelanoma skin cancer
* Radiation therapy
* PUVA therapy
* Immunosuppression
* Reference: [NCCN: Basal Cell Skin Cancer Evidence Blocks [Accessed 15 December 2020]](https://www.nccn.org/professionals/physician_gls/default.aspx#nmsc)

Case reports

* 55 year old Caucasian man with BCC on the left forearm presenting with ulcerative axillary lymph node and pulmonary metastases ([Case Rep Oncol Med 2018;2018:3485326](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29854516))
* 65 year old man with clear cell BCC on upper chest ([Patholog Res Int 2011;2011:386921](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21559201))
* 65 year old man with neglected BCC on posterior neck presenting with diffuse skeletal metastases ([JAAD Case Rep 2018;4:678](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30112453))
* 76 year old Caucasian man with basosquamous carcinoma with lymphovascular invasion on left lateral shoulder ([Cureus 2018;10:e3401](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30533335))
* 79 year old woman with massive basosquamous carcinoma of back ([Oxf Med Case Reports 2017;2017:omw095](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28058108))
* 90 year old woman with infiltrative BCC on left shin that was clinically diagnosed as tinea corporis ([Dermatol Online J 2018;24:13030](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29634889))

Treatment

* Surgery
* Mohs micrographic surgery
* Curettage and electrodesiccation for tumors with lower risk of local recurrence
* Radiation therapy
* Topical treatment (5-fluorouracil, imiquimod)
* Photodynamic therapy with aminolevulinic acid or porfimer sodium
* Nicotinamide (can be used for prophylactic treatment)
* Systemic therapy (sonidegib and vismodegib are hedgehog pathway inhibitors [HhIs])
* Reference: [NCCN: Basal Cell Skin Cancer Evidence Blocks [Accessed 15 December 2020]](https://www.nccn.org/professionals/physician_gls/default.aspx#nmsc)

Microscopic (histologic) description

**Common variants** ([Calonje: McKee's Pathology of the Skin, 5th Edition, 2019](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0702069833/pathologyoutl-20))

* Nodular and nodulocystic BCC
  + Relatively circumscribed mass
  + Epidermal or follicular attachment variably present
  + Large basaloid lobules with peripheral nuclear palisade
  + Lobules may be solid or show central cyst formation due to excessive mucin production
  + Fibromyxoid stroma
  + Cleft formation between tumor lobules and stroma
  + Pleomorphism is generally mild
  + Variable mitotic activity and apoptosis
  + Sometimes necrosis en masse
* Adenoid BCC
  + Reticulate pseudoglandular pattern of basaloid cells
  + Mucinous stroma
  + Can mimic true gland formation, resulting in diagnostic confusion with a sweat gland adenocarcinoma, e.g. [adenoid cystic carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticACC.html)
* Micronodular BCC
  + Small basaloid nests
  + Peripheral palisading less prominent
  + Retraction artifact usually absent
  + Can diffusely infiltrate the dermis and extend into the subcutis
* Infiltrative BCC
  + Small irregular clumps of basaloid cells
  + Limited peripheral palisading
  + Stroma loose and mucin may be prominent
  + Extensive spread
  + Perineural invasion can be seen
* Morpheaform (sclerosing, morphoeic) BCC
  + Thin strands and nests of basaloid cells
  + Limited peripheral palisading
  + Stroma is dense and sclerotic
  + Extensive spread
  + Perineural invasion can be seen
* Keratotic BCC
  + Horn cyst formation
* Basosquamous (metatypical) BCC
  + Biphasic tumor
  + Foci of neoplastic squamous differentiation
* Pigmented BCC
  + Nodular and superficial variants can be pigmented
  + Colonization of tumor's complexes with melanocytes
  + Stromal melanophages
* Superficial BCC
  + Isolated basaloid lobules projecting from the lower margin of the epidermis
* Ulcerative BCC
  + Ulceration
  + Highly infiltrative growth pattern
  + Less commonly nodulocystic variant with ulceration
* BCC, fibroepitheliomatous type (fibroepithelioma of Pinkus)
  + Anastomosing strands and cords of basaloid cells connected to the epidermis
  + Peripheral nuclear palisading with a formation of follicular germ-like structures
  + Rarely, isthmic differentiation is present
  + Fibrotic stroma that can differentiate towards follicular papillae in the areas of germ-like structure formation

**Rare variants**

* Pleomorphic (giant cell, BCC with monster cells) BCC
  + Mitotic activity
  + Apoptosis
  + Сellular pleomorphism and giant cell formation
  + Atypia has no prognostic significance
* Clear cell BCC
  + Focal or total clear cell change with clear to finely granular eosinophilic cytoplasm due to lysosomal degeneration
  + Peripheral palisading is usually preserved
* Signet ring cell BCC
  + Tumor cells with laterally displaced nuclei
  + May show positivity for S100 protein, glial fibrillary acidic protein and smooth muscle actin, suggesting myoepithelial differentiation
  + Referred to as BCC with myoepithelial differentiation
* Granular BCC
  + Tumor cells with abundant granular eosinophilic cytoplasm
* BCC with differentiation towards adnexal structures
  + Tumor complexes can show differentiation towards sebaceous, follicular, eccrine or apocrine structures
* Infundibulocystic BCC
  + Synonym: BCC with follicular differentiation
  + Superficial dermal proliferation of basaloid cells arranged in anastomosing cords and strands
  + Peripheral palisading is present
  + Stroma is typically scanty
  + Infundibular cysts are present
* Metaplastic BCC
  + Stromal malignant metaplastic features (carcinosarcoma)
  + Chondroid, osteoid and smooth muscle differentiation may be seen
* BCC with matricial differentiation (shadow cell BCC)
  + Tumor cells show differentiation towards matricial cells of the hair follicle displaying shadow cells and trichohyaline granules
* Keloidal BCC
  + Stroma shows thick, sclerotic collagen bundles
* BCC with thickened basement membrane
  + Tumor complexes surround by thickened basement membrane
* Basomelanocytic tumor
  + Combined and intermixed basal cell and melanomatous elements
* BCC with neuroid type nuclear palisading
  + Central nuclear palisading reminiscent of that seen in [schwannoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueschwannoma.html)

Cytology description

* Sensitivity and specificity of exfoliative cytology estimate 97.5% (95% CI 94.5% to 98.9%) and 90.1% (95% CI 81.1% to 95.1%) respectively and can result in wrong or false positive result ([Cochrane Database Syst Rev 2018;12:CD013187](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30521689))
* Complexes of tightly packed basaloid cells with palisading in the periphery
* High nuclear/cytoplasmic ratio, minimal atypia and mitotic activity

Positive stains

* [CK AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html) (100%), [BerEP4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsepcam.html) (80 - 100%), [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) (100%), [CAM 5.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscam52.html) (20 - 95%), [androgen receptor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsAR.html) (33 - 66%), [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) (74.5 - 83%), [34 beta E12](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck34be12.html) (high molecular weight CK), [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html) (diffuse pattern), [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) (positive in tumor cells, negative in stroma) ([Dermatopathology (Basel) 2015;2:15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27047932), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2017;141:1490](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29072946), [Rom J Morphol Embryol 2018;59:1115](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30845292), [Am J Pathol 1992;141:25](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1632467))

Differential diagnosis

* **Follicular induction** (also known as follicular basal cell hyperplasia, epidermal basaloid cell hyperplasia and basaloid epidermal proliferation) versus superficial BCC:
  + Usually seen above [dermatofibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticdermatomyofibroma.html), less common above [nevi](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticnevigeneral.html)
  + Follicular differentiation (germinative buds and papillary mesenchymal bodies) shows clear cell hyperplasia, epidermal hyperplasia between sites of follicular induction
  + No atypical nuclei, increased mitotic ﬁgures or apoptotic bodies
  + [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html)+ colonizing Merkel cells
* [Trichoepithelioma / trichoblastoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocytictrichoepithelioma.html) versus nodular BCC:
  + Delicate perifollicular-like stroma with small spindled cells
  + Papillary mesenchymal bodies
  + No prominent cleft retraction, myxoinﬂammatory stroma, signiﬁcant mitotic activity (is sometimes marked in trichoblastoma and should not be misconstrued as implying malignant trichoblastoma), cytologic atypia or tumor necrosis
* **Desmoplastic** [trichoepithelioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocytictrichoepithelioma.html) versus morpheaform BCC:
  + Does not extend into the deep dermis
  + Architectural symmetry, horn cysts with calciﬁcation, granulomatous inflammation and follicular / sebaceous / infundibular differentiation
  + No mitotic ﬁgures, ductal differentiation, cytologic atypia, tumor to stroma clefting or perineural infiltration
  + [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html)+ colonizing Merkel cells, [androgen receptor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsAR.html)-
  + **PHLDA1**+ (highly speciﬁc for trichoepithelioma / trichoblastoma, although expression in micronodular BCC has been reported)
  + Note: no single IHC marker can differentiate trichoepithelioma and BCC
* [Basaloid follicular hamartoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticbasaloidfollicularhamartoma.html) versus infundibulocystic BCC:
  + Cystic structures (uncommon in BCC, except infundibulocystic type)
  + No tumor necrosis, mitotic activity, cytologic atypia, myxoinflammatory stroma or peripheral clefting
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)+ (outlines tumor islands)
  + [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html)+ (Merkel cells): utility is uncertain for distinction from infundibulocystic BCC
* [Tumor of follicular infundibulum](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocytictumorfollicularinfundibulum.html) versus superficial BCC:
  + Cytoplasmic pallor (due to glycogen) and conspicuous basement membrane
  + No cytologic atypia, mitotic activity, no myxoid inflammatory stroma or stroma to tumor clefting
  + Colonizing [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html)+ Merkel cells, [BerEP4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsepcam.html)-
  + [Elastin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainselastic.html) may demonstrate an elastin ﬁber network at the base of the lesion
* [Syringoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticsyringoma.html) versus morpheaform BCC:
  + Well circumscribed, superficial
  + Ductal differentiation (reminiscent of tadpoles)
  + No tumor necrosis, mitotic activity, cytologic atypia, myxoinflammatory stroma, peripheral clefting or perineural invasion
  + [CD200](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd200.html)+, [claudin 4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsclaudins.html)+, [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)+, [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html)+ (highlight lumina)
* [Squamous cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticscc.html) (basaloid, clear cell, sarcomatoid):
  + Shows an intraepidermal component, pagetoid spread
  + No peripheral palisading, clefting or myxoinflammatory stroma
  + Areas with conventional features like keratinization, keratin pearls
  + [BerEP4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsepcam.html)- (also absent in squamous areas of basosquamous carcinoma)
  + [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)+ (may be expressed in squamous areas of BCC), [CD44](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd44.html)+
* [Microcystic adnexal carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticsclerosingsweatductcarcinoma.html) versus morpheaform BCC:
  + Superficial keratocysts (infundibular or isthmic), ductal differentiation with eosinophilic luminal secretions
  + Frequently invades nerves (can't discriminate)
  + No peripheral palisading, mitotic activity, tumor to stroma retraction or myxoinﬂammatory stroma
  + [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html)+, [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)+ in ductal structures
  + [CK15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck1.html#ck15)+, [BerEP4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsepcam.html) variable (mixed reports)
  + [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) highlights ductal myoepithelial cells
* [Sebaceous carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticsebaceouscarcinoma.html) versus BCC (nodular, clear cell):
  + Intraepidermal pagetoid spread
  + No peripheral clefting
  + May have evidence of sebaceous, lobular architecture
  + Often relatively greater cytologic atypia and less basaloid morphology
  + Diffuse [androgen receptor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsAR.html)+ (versus focally positive in BCC)
  + [Low molecular weight CK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)+, [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)+
  + [BerEP4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsepcam.html)-, **adipophilin**+, **perilipin**+
* [Merkel cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticmerkelcell.html):
  + Rarely may show intraepidermal with pagetoid spread
  + No / focal peripheral palisading
  + Homogenous salt and pepper chromatin
  + Nuclear molding
  + Abundant mitotic activity and necrosis
  + [MCPyV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsMCPyV.html) 70 - 80% (highly speciﬁc marker)
  + [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html)+ (paranuclear, dot-like pattern)
  + Neuroendocrine markers expressed but rarely also positive in BCC
* [Adenoid cystic carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticACC.html) versus adenoid BCC:
  + Well defined cribriform patterns
  + No peripheral palisading, continuity with the epidermis or adjacent hair follicle or retraction artifacts between the tumor islands and stroma
  + Typically shows hyaline material lining the lumina of the ducts and sometimes also present in the stroma
  + [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)+ (20% of BCC [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)+), [CD43](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd43.html)+ (40%), [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html)+, [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html)+, [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)+ (in ductal structures)
  + [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html)+, [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html)+, [calponin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalponin.html)+ (myoepithelial cell population at periphery of tumor islands)
* [Clear cell melanoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticmelanomaunusual.html) (balloon cell melanoma) versus clear cell BCC:
  + Intraepidermal pagetoid spread, variable melanin pigment
  + No peripheral palisading, stroma to tumor clefting or myxoinflammatory stroma
  + [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html)+, [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)+, [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html)+, [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html)+, [tyrosinase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstyrosinase.html)+, [BerEP4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsepcam.html)-
* [Clear cell eccrine porocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticeccrineporoma.html) versus clear cell BCC:
  + No myxoinflammatory stroma (sometimes may be present), peripheral clefting or conventional poroma-like areas
  + [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html)+, [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)+, [BerEP4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsepcam.html)-
* **Clear cell hidradenocarcinoma** versus clear cell BCC:
  + No peripheral palisading, clefting or myxoinflammatory stroma
  + [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html)+, [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)+, [BerEP4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsepcam.html)-
* **Trichilemmal carcinoma** versus clear cell BCC:
  + No conventional areas of BCC
  + Contains abundant glycogen ([PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html)+), [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html)-, [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)-
* **Pilomatrix carcinoma** versus BCC with matricial differentiation:
  + No conventional areas of BCC
* [Pigmented reticulated seborrheic keratosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticSK.html) versus BCC with follicular differentiation:
  + No peripheral palisading, myxoinflammatory stroma, peripheral clefting, atypical nuclei, increased mitotic ﬁgures or apoptotic bodies
  + [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html)+ colonizing Merkel cells may be present
* [Cylindroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticeccrinecylindroma.html) versus BCC with thick basement membrane:
  + No conventional areas of BCC
  + [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html)+, [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)+
