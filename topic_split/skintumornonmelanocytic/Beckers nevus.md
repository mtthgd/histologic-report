Definition / general

* Sharply demarcated pigmented macule, often with localized hypertrichosis ([eMedicine](http://emedicine.medscape.com/article/1068257))
* Dermoscopy: network, focal hypopigmentation, skin furrow hypopigmentation, hair follicles, perifollicular hypopigmentation and vessels ([Dermatology 2006;212:354](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16707885))

Terminology

* Also called pigmented hairy epidermal nevus, Becker's melanosis
* Becker's nevus syndrome: Becker's nevus with congenital abnormalities ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2004;51:965](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15583590))

Epidemiology

* First described by Becker in 1948 (**Arch Dermatol 1948;60:155**)
* Present in 0.5% of young men ([Ann Dermatol Venereol 1981;108:41](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7235503))
* Sex ratio close to 1:1 despite apparent increase in women due to association with breast hypoplasia ([An Bras Dermatol 2010;85:379](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20676475))
* Associated with high localized androgen receptor expression ([J Am Acad Dermatol 1984;10:235](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6715592), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2008;59:834](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19119099))
* May be associated with hypoplasia of ipsilateral breast ([Arch Iran Med 2006;9:68](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16649383)), aplasia of ipsilateral pectoralis major muscle, limb shortening, lipoatrophy ([Clin Exp Dermatol 2002;27:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11952664)), spina bifida and scoliosis
* Occasionally associated with melanoma, but overall no known increased risk ([Dermatologica 1991;182:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2050238))
* Possible association with Kabuki Syndrome ([Dermatol Online J 2011;17:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21906481))

Sites

* Usually back, shoulder or chest

Clinical features

* Sharply demarcated, unilateral, hyperpigmented tan macule on back, shoulder or chest
* Hypertrichosis in 50%

Case reports

* 12 year old girl with coexisting segmental nevus depigmentosus ([Australas J Dermatol 2007;48:224](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17956480))
* 18 year old man with bilateral nevus ([Indian J Dermatol Venereol Leprol 2008;74:73](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18187838))
* 24 year old man with hypopigmented pityriasis versicolor developing on pre-existing Becker's nevus ([Indian J Dermatol Venereol Leprol 2002;68:43](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17656869))

Treatment

* Laser ([Br J Dermatol 2005;152:308](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15727644))
* Spironoactone may improve associated breast hypoplasia ([J Dermatol 2003;30:154](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12692385))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Increased basal layer pigmentation, mild acanthosis, hyperkeratosis and regular elongation of rete ridges
* Variable hypertrichosis
* Areas associated with smooth muscle hamartoma have more pronounced smooth muscle bundles irregularly dispersed within the dermis and unrelated to either hair follicles or vascular channels
* Does not actually contain nevus cells

Differential diagnosis

* Congenital smooth muscle hamartoma
