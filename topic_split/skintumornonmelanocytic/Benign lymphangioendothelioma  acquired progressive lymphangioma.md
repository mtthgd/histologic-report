Definition / general

* Rare benign lymphovascular proliferation of unknown etiology, initially described in 1990 ([J Am Acad Dermatol 1990;23:229](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2212118))

Essential features

* Delicate, thin walled, endothelium lined dilated vascular spaces involving the superficial dermis
* Intravascular papillary stromal projections resemble [papillary endothelial hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticintravascularpapillary.html)
* Deeper in dermis, vascular spaces collapse and dissect dermal collagen in angiosarcoma-like pattern

Terminology

* Also called acquired progressive lymphangioma

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [D18.1](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D18-/D18.1) - lymphangioma, any site

Epidemiology

* Uncommon; around 50 reported cases in English literature
* Wide age distribution
* No sex predilection

Sites

* Trunk / limbs usually; other sites have been described

Pathophysiology

* Favored to be a benign vascular malformation rather than a true neoplasm

Etiology

* Some cases possibly related to trauma

Clinical features

* Bruise-like red-brown patch or plaque with indolent growth
* May be nodular, fluctuant and rubbery
* Drainage of serous fluid has been reported ([Clin Exp Dermatol 2009;34:e341](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19489864), [Ann Acad Med Singapore 2011;40:106](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21468468), [J Cutan Pathol 2015;42:217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25410589))

Case reports

* 1 year old boy with extensive acquired progressive lymphangioma ([Pediatr Dermatol 2018;35:486](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29633311))
* 2 year old patient with alopecia lesions in scalp ([Cir Pediatr 2000;13:170](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12601956))
* 32 year old man with tumor in hypogastric region ([Actas Dermosifiliogr 2010;101:792](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21034710))
* 75 year old man with giant benign lymphangioendothelioma ([J Cutan Pathol 2012;39:950](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22834950))
* HIV+ man with acquired progressive lymphangioma ([J Cutan Pathol 2007;34:882](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17944731))
* 4 cases of benign lymphangioendothelioma ([J Cutan Pathol 2013;40:945](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24102654))

Treatment

* Complete excision is usually curative but not necessary
* May recur
* Reports of regression with sirolimus ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2014;71:e221](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25438003)), pulsed dye laser ([Dermatol Surg 2014;40:218](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24237619)), 5% imiquimod ([Pediatr Dermatol 2017;34:e302](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28940760))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Delicate, thin walled, endothelium lined dilated vascular spaces involving the superficial dermis
* Intravascular papillary stromal projections resemble papillary endothelial hyperplasia
* Deeper portion of lesions have vascular space collapse and dissect collagen bundles, mimicking patch stage [Kaposi sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuekaposi.html)
* Preexisting vessels and adnexal structures of the dermis also appear dissected by newly formed vascular channels
* Smooth muscle often focally present around vascular spaces
* Endothelial cells may hobnail, may form morula resembling giant cells
* Crowding of endothelial cells present but no endothelial atypia
* Vascular spaces lack erythrocytes and hemosiderin deposits
* No mitotic figures

Positive stains

* [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html), [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [ERG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsERG.html)

Negative stains

* [HHV8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshhv8.html)
* Most cases are [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html)-; however, rare [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html) positivity has been reported

Differential diagnosis

* [Kaposi sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuekaposi.html): [HHV8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshhv8.html)+; usually demonstrates slit-like vasculature without marked dilation of vessels and plasma cell infiltrate
* **Atypical vascular lesion**: demonstrates more endothelial prominence and atypia; associated with [radiation](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorradiationassociated.html)
* [Angiosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticangiosarcoma.html): dissects through dermal collagen with significant atypia, atypical intraluminal cells, endothelial stacking (multilayering) and mitoses
* [Superficial lymphangioma (lymphangioma circumscriptum)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticlymphangioma.html): congenital hamartoma often associated with cytogenetic abnormalities; typically does not dissect dermal collagen but may show dilated superficial dermal vessels
* [Targetoid hemosiderotic hemangioma (hobnail hemangioma)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocytichemangiomahobnail.html): may have similar infiltration pattern to benign lymphangioendothelioma; endothelial cells protruding into vascular lumen (hobnail cells); deep stromal hemosiderin deposition
