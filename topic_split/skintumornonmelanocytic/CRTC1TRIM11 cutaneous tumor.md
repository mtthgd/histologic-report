Definition / general

* Malignant dermal tumor with epithelioid and spindled cells of uncertain origin with partial melanocytic differentiation and *CRTC1::TRIM11* translocation

Essential features

* Histological features include a dermal / subcutaneous circumscribed tumor composed of fascicles and nests of monomorphic epithelioid and spindle cells with prominent nucleoli divided by delicate collagen septa, no / very rare pigment
* Immunohistochemical features include consistent diffuse SOX10 and MITF staining; S100, MelanA and HMB45 stains are variable
* Defined by identifying *CRTC1::TRIM11* translocation
* No evidence of *EWSR1* rearrangement
* Appears to have at least some malignant potential, although cases and follow up are limited

Terminology

* Cutaneous melanocytic tumor with *CRTC1::TRIM11* fusion
* Not recommended: cutaneous melanocytoma with *CRTC1::TRIM11* fusion

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9044/3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnonmelanocyticWHO.html) - *CRTC1:TRIM11* cutaneous tumor
* ICD-11: [2C35](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f1833969739) - cutaneous sarcoma

Epidemiology

* Age: broad range (5 - 87); median age of 42 ([Histopathology 2023;82:368](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36177516))
* No clear gender bias ([Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:1457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35993578))

Sites

* Broad anatomic distribution
* Limbs (64%) appear to be involved more often than trunk (20%) and head (2%) based on current case reports ([Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:1457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35993578))
* 2 cases of oral and nasal mucosal involvement ([Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:1457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35993578))

Pathophysiology

* Exact mechanism of how the *CRTC1::TRIM11* fusion transcript causes neoplasia is not known; hypotheses include
  + *CRTC1::TRIM11* fusion deletes a protein - protein interaction site on *TRIM11* that is involved in directing misfolded proteins towards proteasomes for degradation ([Cureus 2022;14:e33179](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36726909))
  + *CRTC1::TRIM11* fusion retains an interaction site with *CREB1*, which in turn may drive expression of MITF; MITF acts like an oncogene and can drive MITF synthesis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:1457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35993578))

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Discrete, slowly growing, skin colored nodule without pigmentation ([Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:1457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35993578))

Diagnosis

* Biopsy
* Defined by molecular findings

Radiology description

* Lobulated lesion on imaging
* May be positron emission tomography (PET) avid

Prognostic factors

* Limited case reports and follow up available but appears more indolent than melanoma and clear cell sarcoma at the time of writing
* 5 of 50 published cases reported have shown regional or distant metastasis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:1457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35993578), [Histopathology 2023;82:368](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36177516), [Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:861](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30676334), [J Cutan Pathol 2024;51:181](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37944913), [J Cutan Pathol 2024 Jun 15 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38877838))

Case reports

* 5 year old girl with right upper arm lesion ([J Cutan Pathol 2022;49:1025](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35751643))
* 27 year old man with fourth finger mass ([Cureus 2022;14:e33179](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36726909))
* 75 year old man with right toe lesion ([J Cutan Pathol 2024 Jun 15 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38877838))
* 77 year old man with right thigh lesion ([Pathol Int 2019;69:496](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31276279))

Treatment

* Other than complete surgical excision, optimal treatment is unknown

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Morphology is uniform and resembles clear cell sarcoma
* Circumscribed, unencapsulated dermal nodule, no / very rare pigment, with only rare cases showing epidermal involvement ([J Cutan Pathol 2022;49:1025](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35751643))
* Some cases show extension into subcutis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:861](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30676334))
* Characteristic pattern of nests and intersecting (occasionally herringbone to vaguely palisaded) fascicles of uniform epithelioid to spindled cells with intervening delicate collagen bands ([Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:1457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35993578))
* Abundant pale eosinophilic cytoplasm, monomorphic nuclei with vesicular chromatin and prominent nucleoli
* Mitotic figures present ([Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:1457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35993578))
* Necrosis reported but uncommon ([Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:1457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35993578))
* Lymphovascular and perineural invasion uncommon ([Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:1457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35993578))
* Pigment and multinucleate giant cells may very rarely be seen
* A recent case of CTCT with ulceration was reported ([J Cutan Pathol 2024 Jun 15 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38877838))

Positive stains

* [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html) diffuse positive in all cases ([Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:1457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35993578))
* [MITF](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmitf.html) diffuse positive in majority of cases ([J Cutan Pathol 2022;49:1025](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35751643))
* [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsS100.html) diffuse positive in 44%, partial positive in 36% ([Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:1457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35993578))
* [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html) diffuse positive in 9%, partial positive in 52% ([Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:1457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35993578))
* [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html) diffuse positive in 4%, partial positive in 52% ([Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:1457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35993578))
* [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) 5 - 20% ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:382](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29240581))
* [Pan-TRK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsntrk.html) diffuse cytoplasmic positive in 57%, partial cytoplasmic positive in 36% despite lack of *TRK* mutations ([Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:1457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35993578))
* **TRIM11** diffuse nuclear positive in 94% ([Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:1457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35993578))

* *CRTC1::TRIM11* fusion ([Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:1457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35993578))

*CRTC::TRIM11* cutaneous tumor

Sample pathology report

* Skin, amputation, right fifth toe:
  + *CRTC1::TRIM11* cutaneous tumor (see comment)
  + Comment: The neoplasm is composed of intersecting fascicles and nests of relatively uniform epithelioid and spindled cells with intervening bands of fibrocollagenous stroma. The neoplastic cells have abundant amounts of amphophilic to eosinophilic cytoplasm and moderately pleomorphic nuclei with prominent nucleoli. Mitotic figures including deep mitoses are identified. There is no evidence of necrosis, lymphovascular invasion or perineural invasion. The neoplastic cells are diffusely positive for SOX10, patchy positive for S100 and negative for MelanA and HMB45. Molecular analysis demonstrated the presence of *CRTC1::TRIM11* fusion. Overall, the features are compatible with the recently described *CRTC1::TRIM11* cutaneous tumor. Based on the limited reported cases and limited follow up data, the clinical course was indolent in most of the reported cases. However, 4 of the reported cases presented with regional nodal metastasis or distant metastasis as of January 2024. The available data at the time of this report suggest that these tumors have at least some malignant potential.

Differential diagnosis

* [Clear cell sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueclearcellsarcoma.html):
  + Predominantly affects adults ages 30 - 40s
  + Often deeper location (associated with tendons or aponeuroses)
  + Infiltrative growth
  + May have clear cells and melanin pigment
  + Wreath-like multinucleated tumor giant cells
  + More consistently expresses [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsS100.html), [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html) and [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html)
  + Has *ESWR1* translocations, most commonly *EWSR1::ATF1* or *EWSR1::CREB1*
  + Lacks *CRTC1::TRIM11* fusion
  + More aggressive behavior
* [Melanoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticmelanoma.html):
  + In situ component (may not be present for previously excised melanoma, metastatic melanoma or primary dermal melanoma)
  + Infiltrative growth
  + Pleomorphic cytology
  + Pigment production
  + More consistently expresses [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsS100.html), [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html) and [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html) (other than in desmoplastic melanoma)
  + [PRAME](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsPRAME.html) positive
  + Has melanoma driver mutations
  + Lacks *CRTC1::TRIM11* fusion
  + More aggressive behavior
* [Spitz tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticspitzoidmelanoma.html):
  + Junctional component of vertically oriented nests and Kamino bodies may be present
  + More consistently expresses [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsS100.html), [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html) and [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html)
  + Molecular analysis shows *HRAS* mutation or kinase fusions
  + Lacks *CRTC::TRIM11* fusion
* [Cellular blue nevus](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticbluenevus.html):
  + Component of dendritic blue nevus is often present
  + Pigmented melanophages present
  + Necrosis is absent
  + Mitotic activity is usually low
  + Consistently positive for [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html) and [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html)
  + Molecular shows *GNAQ* or *GNA11* mutations
  + Lacks *CRTC1::TRIM11* fusion
* [Myoepithelial tumors](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuemyoepithelialca.html):
  + Myxoid stroma may be present
  + [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) positive
  + [Keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html), [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) and [calponin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalponin.html) may be positive
  + [MITF](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmitf.html) negative
  + *EWSR1* rearrangements in syncytial variant
  + Lacks *CRTC1::TRIM11* fusion
* [Epithelioid schwannoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueschwannoma.html):
  + [MITF](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmitf.html) negative
  + 40% show loss of [INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:1013](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28368924))
  + Lacks *CRTC1::TRIM11* fusion
* [Epithelioid MPNST](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuempnst.html):
  + Increased pleomorphism
  + [MITF](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmitf.html) negative
  + Subset shows loss of [INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html)
  + Lacks *CRTC1::TRIM11* fusion
* [PEComa](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusPEComa.html):
  + Irregular interface with dermal collagen
  + Mitotic figures sparse or absent
  + Express [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html), [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html) as well as [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) and [caldesmon](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscaldesmon.html)
  + [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html) negative ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:826](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25724000))
  + Deletion of *TSC2* gene
  + Lacks *CRTC1::TRIM11* fusion
* **MITF pathway activated melanocytic tumors**:
  + Infiltrative growth
  + Prominent clear cytoplasm
  + May have areas of higher grade morphology
  + Can have mitotic activity and perineural invasion
  + Usually expresses either [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html) or [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html)
