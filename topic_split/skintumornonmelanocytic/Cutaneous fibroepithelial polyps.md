Definition / general

* Benign nonepithelial tumors arising from mesodermal tissue

Essential features

* Common benign cutaneous tumor
* On eyelids, flexural areas
* F = M
* Associated with trauma, obesity, diabetes mellitus, pregnancy

Terminology

* Acrochordon, soft fibroma, fibroma molle, skin tag

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [L91.8](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/L00-L99/L80-L99/L91-/L91.8) - other hypertrophic disorders of the skin
* ICD-11: [EK71](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http://id.who.int/icd/entity/1834683622) - skin tags or polyps
* SNOMED:
  + [201091002](https://phinvads.cdc.gov/vads/ViewCodeSystemConcept.action?oid=2.16.840.1.113883.6.96&code=201091002) - skin tag (disorder)
  + [31069005](https://phinvads.cdc.gov/vads/ViewCodeSystemConcept.action?oid=2.16.840.1.113883.6.96&code=31069005) - fibroepithelial polyp (morphologic abnormality)

Epidemiology

* 50+ years of age ([Dermatologica 1987;174:180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3582706))
* Incidence: 46 - 64% in people > 50 years ([Dermatologica 1987;174:180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3582706))
* F = M ([Dermatologica 1987;174:180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3582706))
* Causal connections: obesity ([Eur J Dermatol 2012;22:106](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22063265))

Sites

* Eyelids
* Flexural areas: axillae, groin
* Lateral neck
* Inframammary

Pathophysiology

* Trauma → mast cell recruitment → upregulation of TNF-α → fibroblast proliferation and mitogenicity ([Indian J Dermatol 2011;56:641](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22345761))
* Growth factors → keratinocyte and fibroblast proliferation ([Clin Cosmet Investig Dermatol 2019;12:255](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31118729))
* Loss / downregulation of hamartin and tuberin (inactivating tumor suppressor proteins) ([J Cutan Pathol 2004;31:383](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15059224))
* Human papillomavirus (HPV) ([Iran J Basic Med Sci 2012;15:840](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23493098))

Etiology

* The etiology is not clear nor the same for every FEP; reported associations include:
  + Trauma ([Indian J Dermatol 2011;56:641](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22345761))
  + Diabetes mellitus, metabolic syndrome ([Eur J Dermatol 2012;22:106](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22063265))
  + Polycystic ovary syndrome ([Australas J Dermatol 2019;60:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30009441))
  + Hormonal replacement therapy ([Australas J Dermatol 2019;60:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30009441))
  + Acromegaly ([Acta Dermatovenerol Croat 2013;21:224](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24476608))
  + HPV 6 and 11 ([Indian J Dermatol Venereol Leprol 2008;74:222](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18583787))
  + Lymphedema ([APMIS 2008;116:215](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18377588))

Clinical features

* Clinical appearance:
  + Small soft papules, pedunculated or filiform, 2 - 5 mm, rough surface
  + Pendulous fibromas can be large (several centimeters) ([Australas J Dermatol 2019;60:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30009441))
  + May be multiple ([Dermatologica 1987;174:180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3582706))
* Look for associated disease / physiologic states including:
  + Diabetes mellitus / insulin resistance ([Eur J Dermatol 2012;22:106](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22063265))
  + Hyperlipidemia
  + Familial predisposition
  + Pregnancy ([J Am Acad Dermatol 1984;10:929](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6376552))
  + Lymphedema ([APMIS 2008;116:215](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18377588))

Diagnosis

* Characteristic clinical features and histopathology

Prognostic factors

* Benign

* Surgical removal / snip excision ([Aesthetic Plast Surg 2015;39:644](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26044394))
* Cryotherapy ([Aesthetic Plast Surg 2015;39:644](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26044394))

Gross description

* Sessile or pedunculated polyp
* 2 - 5 mm to several centimeters ([Australas J Dermatol 2019;60:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30009441))
* Surface smooth or filiform
* Smaller ones may be pigmented
* Larger ones are skin colored

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Epidermis may be hyperplastic and papillomatous and may have keratotic cysts and pigment in basal epidermal keratinocytes
* Loose fibrocollagenous stroma, abundant vessels
* Usually no adnexa
* Variable adipose tissue in larger ones - lipofibroma
* Traumatic changes: lichen simplex chronicus, epidermal necrosis, ulceration, pagetoid dyskeratosis, lichen sclerosus-like change ([Am J Dermatopathol 2006;28:478](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17122491), [Am J Dermatopathol 2019;41:e64](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30946102))
* Pseudosarcomatous change: pleomorphic stellate stromal cells, multinucleated stromal cells, myxoid to collagenous stroma ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2008;12:440](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18995210))
* On IHC, the pleomorphic cells stain diffusely for vimentin; there is variable staining for CD34 and factor 13a and the cells do not stain for SMA or desmin ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2008;12:440](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18995210))
* Some of the lesions with pseudosarcomatous change overlap with [pleomorphic fibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuepleomorphicfibroma.html)
* A subset of cellular pseudosarcomatous fibroepithelial polyps occurs in the female genital tract including the vulva ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10680891))

Trauma / inflammation

Sample pathology report

* Left neck, shave biopsy:
  + Fibroepithelial polyp / acrochordon (see comment)
  + Comment: A polyp covered by mildly acanthotic epidermis with mild hyperkeratosis. There is a mildly cellular fibrovascular core with scattered dilated small thin walled vessels and a sparse lymphocytic infiltrate. Dysplasia or malignancy is not seen.

Differential diagnosis

* Polypoid / papillary variants of:
  + [Seborrheic keratosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticSK.html) ([Arch Dermatol 1996;132:1459](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8961875)):
    - Generally lacks the fibrovascular core of a fibroepithelial polyp
    - **Dermatosis papulosa nigra**:
      * Considered to be a seborrheic keratosis; however, it has a well developed fibrous stroma
      * It is a clinically distinct entity ([Clin Dermatol 2017;35:491](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28916031))
  + [Verruca vulgaris / squamous cell papilloma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorwarts.html) ([Australas J Dermatol 2019;60:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30009441)):
    - Epidermal papillomatosis with coarse hypergranulosis
    - May have koilocytosis
    - Hyperkeratosis with columns of parakeratosis
  + [Melanocytic nevus](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticnevigeneral.html) ([Arch Dermatol 1996;132:1459](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8961875)):
    - Melanocytic proliferation within the polyp
    - Commonly [intradermal](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticnevigeneral.html#dermal) or [compound](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticnevigeneral.html#compound)
  + [Neurofibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueneurofibroma.html) ([Australas J Dermatol 2019;60:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30009441)):
    - Cellular stromal proliferation of spindle cells with wavy nuclei
    - Mast cells within the lesion
  + [Hemangioma and pyogenic granuloma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticpyogenicgranuloma.html) ([Arch Dermatol 1996;132:1459](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8961875)):
    - Large number of vessels in the stroma
  + [Fibroepithelial tumor of Pinkus](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticbcc.html):
    - Thin anastomosing strands of basaloid or squamous keratinocytes projecting downward from the epidermis in a fenestrated pattern
    - Abundant fibrous stroma ([Am J Dermatopathol 2005;27:149](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15798442))
  + [Polypoid trichodiscomas and fibrofolliculomas in Birt-Hogg-Dubé syndrome](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticfolliculofibroma.html):
    - Infundibular structures in the stroma ([Am J Dermatopathol 1999;21:369](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10446780))
  + [Accessory tragus](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/earaccessorytragi.html):
    - Most commonly preauricular
    - Vellus hairs, eccrine glands, adipose tissue and cartilage in the stroma ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2007;56:AB54](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jaad.2006.10.285))
  + [Supernumerary digit](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticsupernumerarydigit.html):
    - Congenital
    - Increased peripheral nerves in the stroma ([Arch Dermatol 1973;108:223](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4724126))
  + [Acquired digital fibrokeratoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticacquireddigitalfibrokeratoma.html):
    - Acral skin
    - Marked epidermal acanthosis and hyperkeratosis
    - Thickened stromal collagen parallel to the long axis of the lesion ([J Am Acad Dermatol 1985;12:816](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4008685))

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/skintumornonmelanocyticfibroepithelialpolypClinAN1.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/skintumornonmelanocyticfibroepithelialpolypClinAN1.jpg)

A 62 year old woman presents with multiple lesions on the neck (shown above) with pathological diagnosis of fibroepithelial polyps, NOS. Which clinical association should you consider?

1. Anorexia
2. Birt-Hogg-Dubé syndrome
3. Diabetes mellitus
4. Dysplastic nevus syndrome
5. Neurofibromatosis

Board review style answer #1

**C.** Diabetes mellitus. Skin tags may be associated with diabetes mellitus / metabolic syndrome as well as obesity. The other listed conditions have not been shown to have increased incidence of fibroepithelial polyps but may have other cutaneous polypoid lesions. Birt-Hogg-Dubé syndrome was initially reported as having increased incidence of skin tags, however, the lesions are polypoid trichodiscomas and fibrofolliculomas.

Board review style question #2

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/skintumornonmelanocyticfibroepithelialpolypMicroAN7.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/skintumornonmelanocyticfibroepithelialpolypMicroAN7.jpg)

A clinician excises a skin tag from the groin of a 73 year old woman (microscopic image shown above). The best diagnosis is

1. Cutaneous myxoma
2. Neurofibroma
3. Pseudosarcomatous fibroepithelial polyp
4. Pyogenic granuloma
5. Traumatized fibroepithelial polyp

Board review style answer #2

**E.** Traumatized fibroepithelial polyp. This is a fibroepithelial polyp with features of trauma, including an area of epidermal necrosis, stromal edema and stromal inflammatory cells. The stroma is loose and has low cellularity of mesenchymal cells, unlike a neurofibroma. The stroma is edematous rather than myxoid. It lacks the pleomorphic stellate and multinucleated stromal cells of a pseudosarcomatous fibroepithelial polyp. The lesion has dilated vessels, however, it lacks the lobular proliferation of capillaries that characterizes a pyogenic granuloma.
