Definition / general

* Dermatofibromas (also known as fibrous histiocytomas) are a spectrum of benign dermal based lesions with fibroblastic and histiocytic differentiation
* There is debate as to whether dermatofibroma is a reactive or neoplastic process

Essential features

* Benign dermal based, nodular proliferation of fibroblasts and histiocytes
* Collagen trapping at periphery and follicular induction commonly seen
* IHC is nonspecific for dermatofibroma

Terminology

* Benign fibrous histiocytoma and fibrous histiocytoma

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [D23](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D23-/D23) - benign neoplasm of skin
* ICD-11: [2F23.0](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f425706508) - dermatofibroma

Epidemiology

* Common
* All races
* All ages; most common in third and fourth decades
* F > M
* Reference: [Am J Surg Pathol 1990;14:801](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2167613)

Sites

* Typically occurs on distal extremities (legs > arms > trunk)
* May present on any part of the skin surface

Pathophysiology

* Cause unknown
* No evidence associates it with insect bites or trauma

Etiology

* Unclear whether a reactive or neoplastic process
* Cytogenetic data has shown clonality, arguing in favor of a neoplastic process ([J Cutan Pathol 2000;27:36](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10660130), [Histopathology 2000;37:212](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10971696))
* Multiple dermatofibromas may occur in immunosuppressed populations ([Eur J Dermatol 1999;9:45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9920988))

Clinical features

* Painless
* 5 mm - 2 cm
* Variable color: skin colored to brown to purple
* Variable shape: plaques, nodules or polyps
* Covered by intact skin
* Suspected by pinching the nodule between the fingers and observing that the tumor is fixed within the dermis
* Pinch sign: overlying skin dimples on pinching the lesion ([An Bras Dermatol 2014;89:472](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24937822))

Diagnosis

* Combination of clinical and histologic findings

Prognostic factors

* Excellent prognosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 1990;14:801](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2167613))
* Cellular, atypical, aneurysmal and deep subtypes associated with higher risks of local recurrence ([Am J Surg Pathol 1994;18:668](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8017561), [Eur J Dermatol 1998;8:122](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9649666))
* Very rarely metastasize ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:354](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18300816),
  [Am J Surg Pathol 2002;26:35](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11756767),
  [Mod Pathol 2013;26:256](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22996372))

Treatment

* Complete excision is curative
* Wide local excision is adequate to prevent recurrence
* Spontaneous regression has been reported

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Symmetric and predominantly dermal based
* Relatively circumscribed but have irregular and unencapsulated borders
* Patterns range from diffuse to reticular to hemangioma-like to keloid-like
* Classic pattern: storiform, pinwheel or curlicue pattern
  + Made up of spindled fibroblasts or histiocytes
* Some areas densely cellular, while others are sclerotic and hypocellular
  + Early lesions: more cellular
  + Later lesions: more sclerotic
* Spindled cells: thin, elongated nuclei with pointed ends and eosinophilic cytoplasm
* Histiocytic cells: epithelioid shaped cells with abundant pale cytoplasm
* May see varying amounts of inflammatory cells
* Cytologic atypia and pleomorphism are variable
* Mitotic activity is variable
* Touton giant cells and ringed lipidized siderophages may be present
* Collagen trapping at periphery
  + Spheres of eosinophilic collagen (collagen balls) surrounded by the fibroblast proliferation
* Grenz zone (sparing of the superficial papillary dermis) commonly seen
* Follicular or sebaceous induction and basilar hyperpigmentation
* Tumor may extend into superficial fat
* Numerous histological subtypes ([An Bras Dermatol 2014;89:472](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24937822)):
  + Aneurysmal and hemosiderotic variants are both characterized by numerous multinucleate cells and prominent hemosiderin deposition due to red blood cell extravasation; distinction between these two is the pseudovascular spaces with blood in aneurysmal type ([Histopathology 1995;26:323](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7607620))
  + Hemosiderotic frequently has extracellular hemosiderin pigmentation present
  + Deep fibrous histiocytoma: may extend into hypodermis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:354](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18300816))
  + Cellular: highly cellular and look blue at low power with thick collagen bundles ([Am J Surg Pathol 1994;18:668](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8017561))
  + Lipidized: also known as 'ankle type' [Am J Dermatopathol 2000;22:126](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10770432)
  + Atypical: also has prominent pleomorphism, mitotic activity including atypical forms ([Am J Dermatopathol. 1987 Oct;9(5):380](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2825558))
  + Epithelioid fibrous histiocytoma: epidermal collarette, binucleate cells ([Mod Pathol 2015;28:904](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25857825))

Positive stains

* Most useful stain based on evidence is [CD163](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd163.html); [factor XIIIa](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfactorxiiia.html) is variable in dermatofibroma; [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html) and [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) are less specific for fibrohistiocytic lineage
* [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) and [PHH3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsphosphohistoneh3.html) can also be used to show increased proliferation index in dermatofibroma (compared to low index in dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans) ([Am J Dermatopathol 2017;39:504](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28609344))
* [ALK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html) nuclear and cytoplasmic reactivity in epithelioid fibrous histiocytoma due to [ALK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html) rearrangement and overexpression
* Staining pattern is highly variable and nonspecific but the following stains may be positive:
  + [Factor XIIIa](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfactorxiiia.html), [CD163](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd163.html), [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html)
    - [Factor XIIIa](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfactorxiiia.html) is usually negative in dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans
  + [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html) may highlight histiocytic cells ([Patterson: Weedon's Skin Pathology, 5th Edition, 2020](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0702075825/pathologyoutl-20))

Negative stains

* [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) shows a characteristic staining pattern:
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) is usually positive in the periphery of dermatofibroma, and negative in the middle
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) is positive in dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) peripheral staining in dermatofibroma is observed in cellular dermatofibroma and deep FH and is weak and patchy
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) is strong and diffuse throughout dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans
* [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html), [MelanA / MART1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html), **nestin** ([Patterson: Weedon's Skin Pathology, 5th Edition, 2020](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0702075825/pathologyoutl-20))

Differential diagnosis

* [Dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans (DFSP)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticdfsp.html):
  + Lower mitotic activity
  + Greater cellularity
  + Absence of inflammation
  + Involvement of subcutis is infiltrative and extensive rather than pushing and focal in deep and cellular variants of fibrous histiocytoma
  + [CD163](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd163.html) is negative
  + Monotonous cells
  + Usually deeper, with adnexal structures and fat within
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)+ and [factor XIIIa](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfactorxiiia.html)-
  + *COL1A1::PDGFB* fusion
* [Melanoma, especially desmoplastic type](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticdesmoplasticmelanoma.html):
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)+
  + Prominent lymphoid aggregates
* [Dermatomyofibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticdermatomyofibroma.html):
  + Corkscrew nuclei running parallel to epidermis

  + Poor circumscription
