Definition / general

* Also called desmoplastic fibroblastoma
* Rare paucicellular tumor consisting of dense collagen fibers with scattered stellate and spindled fibroblasts involving the subcutis or skeletal muscles
* Benign clinical behavior with no reported tumor recurrence

Essential features

* Most frequently found in the subcutis, with up to 25% found in the skeletal muscles ([Hum Pathol 1998;29:676](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9670823))
* Essential to distinguish from other more aggressive fibroblastic and myofibroblastic neoplasms
* Treatment is simple excision

Terminology

* Desmoplastic fibroblastoma

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8810/0](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnonmelanocyticWHO.html) - desmoplastic fibroblastoma
* ICD-11: [EE6Y & XH2ZF3](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#831995767%2Fother%20%26%202142449293) - other specified fibromatous disorders of skin and soft tissue & desmoplastic fibroblastoma

Epidemiology

* Rare (largest series is 63 cases) ([Hum Pathol 1998;29:676](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9670823))
* M:F = 4:1
* Median age of 50 - 60 years

Sites

* Most common in upper extremities ([Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:1077](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7661281))
* Followed by lower extremities ([Hum Pathol 1998;29:676](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9670823))
* Rarely head and neck ([Clin Case Rep 2022;10:e6029](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35846929))

Pathophysiology

* Translocation involving the long arm of chromosome 11, particularly 11q12
* Reported translocations include t(2;11)(q31;q12) and t(11;17)(q12;p11.2) ([Cancer Genet Cytogenet 2004;149:161](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15036892), [Cancer Genet Cytogenet 2009;192:73](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19596257), [Cancer Genet 2011;204:569](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22137488))
* 11q12 rearrangement results in the overexpression of *FOSL1* ([Histopathology 2016;69:1012](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27442992), [Lab Invest 2012;92:735](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22411068), [J Pathol 2023;259:119](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36426824))

Etiology

* Unknown at this time

Clinical features

* Solitary, painless, slow growing nodule ([Hum Pathol 1998;29:676](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9670823), [In Vivo 2021;35:69](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33402451))
* Size ranges from 1 to 20 cm (median size: 3 cm) ([Hum Pathol 1998;29:676](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9670823), [In Vivo 2021;35:69](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33402451))

Diagnosis

* Histopathology is the gold standard for definitive diagnosis

Radiology description

* Relatively well defined, lobulated, increased soft tissue density ([Skeletal Radiol 2019;48:637](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30229348))

Prognostic factors

* Simple excision is curative
* No recurrence or metastasis reported ([Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:1077](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7661281), [Hum Pathol 1998;29:676](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9670823))

Frozen section description

* Intraoperative frozen section analysis is usually not performed

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Well circumscribed but may infiltrate fat or less commonly skeletal muscles ([Hum Pathol 1998;29:676](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9670823))
* Hypocellular with fibromyxoid or collagenous stroma
* Bland spindled to stellate shaped fibroblasts
* Minimal vasculature
* Minimal to absent mitotic activity

Cytology description

* Cytology is usually not performed

Positive stains

* Immunohistochemical (IHC) stains are usually not needed for diagnosis
* Most IHC stains are nonspecific
* Diffuse and strong nuclear reactivity with **FOSL1** ([In Vivo 2021;35:69](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33402451))
* Variable [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) positive
* Rare focal [keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) expression

Sample pathology report

* Skin, chest, biopsy:
  + Collagenous fibroma (desmoplastic fibroblastoma) (see comment)
  + Comment: Sections demonstrate a well circumscribed hypocellular proliferation of stellate fibroblasts in a collagenous stroma, consistent with a collagenous fibroma (desmoplastic fibroblastoma).

Differential diagnosis

* [Desmoid type fibromatosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuefibromatosisdeep.html):
  + Predominantly affects young adults (mean age: 41)
  + Deep seated soft tissue tumors, subdivided into extra-abdominal, abdominal and intra-abdominal
  + May be associated with [Gardner syndrome](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorgardner.html)
  + Locally aggressive, nonmetastasizing
  + Long fascicles of bland fibroblasts and myofibroblasts
  + Usually more cellular than collagenous fibroma, except for the hypocellular variant
  + Conspicuous thin walled blood vessels with perivascular edema and lymphoid aggregates
  + [Beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html) nuclear staining
  + Mutations *CTNNB1* (sporadic) and *APC* (familial)
* [Low grade fibromyxoid sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuelgfibromyxoid.html):
  + Predominantly affects young adults (mean age: 35 - 45)
  + Deep seated soft tissue tumors of proximal extremities and trunk
  + Protracted clinical course with high risk of local recurrence and late metastasis
  + Short fascicles of bland spindle fibroblasts in alternating myxoid and collagenous stroma
  + Curvilinear blood vessels ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2018;11:5860](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31949672))
  + [MUC4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsMUC2.html#muc4) positive
  + *FUS::CREB3L2*, *FUS::CREB3L1* and rarely *EWSR1::CREB3L1* fusions
* [Fibroma of tendon sheath](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuefibromatendon.html):
  + Predominantly affects young to middle aged adults (median age: 31)
  + Arises from synovium of tendon sheath in small distal joints
  + 5 - 10% risk of recurrence
  + Well circumscribed paucicellular tumor with bland fibroblasts, collagenous stroma and slit-like vessels
  + [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) positive; may be focal
  + [Desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) and nuclear [beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html) negative
  + *USP6* gene rearrangement by FISH

Board review style question #1

What molecular findings are associated with collagenous fibroma?

1. t(2;11)(q31;q12)
2. t(7;16)(q32-34;p11) *FUS::CREB3L2* fusion
3. t(17;22)
4. *USP6* gene rearrangement

Board review style answer #1

**A**. t(2;11)(q31;q12). Collagenous fibromas (desmoplastic fibroblastomas) have been reported to have translocations involving 11q12, including t(2;11)(q31;q12) and t(11;17)(q12;p11.2). Answer D is incorrect because *USP6* gene rearrangement is associated with fibroma of tendon sheath. Answer C is incorrect because t(17;22) is associated with dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans. Answer B is incorrect because t(7;16)(q32-34;p11) *FUS::CREB3L2* fusion is associated with low grade fibromyxoid sarcoma.

A 50 year old woman presents with a solitary, firm nodule in the left upper arm. It is excised and the histology is shown in the image above. What is the most likely diagnosis?

1. Collagenous fibroma
2. Desmoid type fibromatosis
3. Fibroma of tendon sheath
4. Low grade fibromyxoid sarcoma

Board review style answer #2

**A**. Collagenous fibroma. Collagenous fibroma is a hypocellular tumor with bland spindled to stellate shaped fibroblasts and a fibromyxoid to collagenous background. Answer C is incorrect because fibroma of tendon sheath shows bland fibroblasts and collagenous stroma with slit-like vessels. Answer B is incorrect because desmoid type fibromatosis shows long fascicles of bland fibroblasts and myofibroblasts, conspicuous thin walled blood vessels with perivascular edema, lymphoid aggregates and more cellularity. Answer D is incorrect because low grade fibromyxoid sarcoma shows short fascicles of bland spindled fibroblasts in alternating myxoid and collagenous stroma as well as curvilinear blood vessels.
