Definition / general

* Diffuse dermal angiomatosis (DDA) is a rare, benign cutaneous reactive angiomatosis that presents with erythematous lesions that are often painful and ulcerated

Essential features

* Diffuse dermal CD31 positive vascular proliferation ([Case Rep Dermatol 2017;9:194](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29282392), [Acta Derm Venereol 2020;100:adv00202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32511742))
* Endothelial cells have a diffuse dissecting growth pattern but lack cytologic and nuclear atypia or mitoses ([Case Rep Dermatol 2017;9:194](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29282392))
* Diffuse dermal angiomatosis is a rare, acquired reactive vascular disorder that clinically appears with poorly circumscribed erythematous to violaceous plaques, often with ulceration and necrosis ([Acta Derm Venereol 2020;100:adv00202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32511742))
* Develops in response to tissue ischemia; associated with severe atherosclerosis, obesity, macromastia, heavy smoking history, arteriovenous fistulae ([Case Rep Dermatol 2017;9:194](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29282392))

Terminology

* Diffuse reactive angioendotheliomatosis ([Case Rep Dermatol 2017;9:194](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29282392))

ICD coding

* ICD-10
  + [L98.8](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/L00-L99/L80-L99/L98-/L98.8) - other specified disorders of the skin and subcutaneous tissue
  + [D18.01](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D18-/D18.01) - hemangioma of skin and subcutaneous tissue
* ICD-11
  + [LC50.Y](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#727340642%2Fother) - other specified cutaneous capillary vascular malformation
  + [2F2Y & XH5AW4](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#1435238626%2Fother%20%26%20134504304) - other specified benign cutaneous neoplasms & hemangioma, NOS

Epidemiology

* Most often presents in middle aged women ([Cutis 2019;103:181](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31039226))
* Almost exclusively occurs in patients with underlying comorbidities ([Cutis 2019;103:181](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31039226))

Sites

* Breasts
  + Specifically in cases of macromastia ([Arch Dermatol 2006;142:343](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16549710), [JAAD Case Rep 2022;31:49](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36505035))
  + Usually bilateral breasts
  + Exclusively reported in women
  + Most common location ([Case Rep Dermatol 2017;9:194](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29282392))
* Lower extremities ([Cutis 2019;103:181](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31039226))
  + Often secondary to peripheral artery disease
  + Several cases of critical limb ischemia with initial presentation of diffuse dermal angiomatosis ([J Vasc Surg Cases Innov Tech 2023;9:101303](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37767346), [Eur J Vasc Endovasc Surg 2011;42:381](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21641837))
* Trunk ([Case Rep Dermatol 2017;9:194](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29282392))
* Forearms
  + Can be secondary to trauma from arteriovenous shunts for chronic hemodialysis ([Cutis 2019;103:181](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31039226), [J Cutan Pathol 1999;26:159](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10235383))

Pathophysiology

* Ischemic events or inflammation may cause hypoxia at the tissue site, inducing an inflammatory response
  + Subsequent production of angiogenic factors, such as vascular endothelial growth factor (VEGFR) in response to diminished tissue oxygenation
  + Endothelial cells proliferate at these sites of ischemia, causing extravascular endothelial cell hyperplasia ([Case Rep Dermatol 2017;9:194](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29282392), [Acta Derm Venereol 2020;100:adv00202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32511742))
* Some propose that microthrombi are created at sites of vessel occlusion or inflammation, resulting in tissue hypoxia and subsequent endothelial cell hyperplasia ([Cutis 2019;103:181](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31039226), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2003;49:887](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14576670))
* In cases with breast involvement, the weight of large, pendulous breasts is thought to cause compression and increased venous hydrostatic pressure
  + Subclinical trauma to breast tissue causes angiogenesis
* Other risk factors, such as insulin resistance, smoking and hypercoagulability, may contribute to endothelial cell dysfunction and vascular inflammation, precipitating local tissue ischemia ([Case Rep Dermatol 2017;9:194](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29282392))

Etiology

* Ischemia has been described most often secondary to
  + Severe atherosclerotic vascular disease ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2016;95:e4212](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27442644), [J Vasc Surg Cases Innov Tech 2023;9:101303](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37767346), [Eur J Vasc Endovasc Surg 2011;42:381](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21641837))
  + Macromastia ([Clin Dermatol 2021;39:271](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34272021), [Arch Dermatol 2006;142:343](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16549710), [JAAD Case Rep 2022;31:49](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36505035))
  + Tobacco use (current and former smokers affected) ([Clin Dermatol 2021;39:271](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34272021), [Acta Derm Venereol 2020;100:adv00202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32511742), [Cutis 2019;103:181](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31039226))
  + Obesity ([Clin Dermatol 2021;39:271](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34272021), [Cutis 2019;103:181](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31039226))
  + Hypertension ([Cutis 2019;103:181](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31039226))
  + Trauma (arteriovenous fistula) ([Cutis 2019;103:181](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31039226), [J Cutan Pathol 1999;26:159](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10235383))

Clinical features

* Erythematous to violaceous purpuric papules and plaques
* Often painful
* Advanced lesions may progress to have central necrosis or ulceration
* Lesser described associations with
  + Diabetes ([Cutis 2019;103:181](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31039226))
  + Other cardiovascular risk factors such as hyperlipidemia, atrial fibrillation ([Cutis 2019;103:181](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31039226))
  + End stage renal disease (ESRD), dialysis ([Cutis 2019;103:181](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31039226))
* Diffuse dermal angiomatosis can occur in the dermis adjacent to necrotizing ulcers of calciphylaxis ([Am J Dermatopathol 2009;31:653](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19652584), [J Cutan Pathol 2013;40:829](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23782317), [G Ital Dermatol Venereol 2015;150:115](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25502367), [JAAD Case Rep 2022;24:8](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35518276))
* Other causes of relative ischemia contributing to diffuse dermal angiomatosis have been reported in cases with
  + Cutis marmorata telangiectatica ([Arch Dermatol 2010;146:1311](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21079080))
  + Granulomatosis with polyangiitis ([Acta Derm Venereol 2013;93:93](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22855132))
  + Anticardiolipin antibodies ([Cutis 2019;103:181](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31039226))
  + Monoclonal gammopathy ([G Ital Dermatol Venereol 2015;150:115](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25502367))

Diagnosis

* Skin biopsy of the lesion for microscopic examination is diagnostic

Radiology description

* Breast fluorescent angiography shows a network of dense, regular capillary vessels through the dermis ([Gland Surg 2015;4:554](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26645009))
* Xray / CT images with calcification of the large arteries of pelvis or lower extremities in cases with severe atherosclerotic occlusive disease contributing to presentation of diffuse dermal angiomatosis ([J Vasc Surg Cases Innov Tech 2023;9:101303](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37767346), [Eur J Vasc Endovasc Surg 2011;42:381](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21641837))

Prognostic factors

* Resolution reported with improvement in local blood flow via revascularization procedures, surgical resection (e.g., breast mastectomy), lifestyle changes (smoking cessation) ([Case Rep Dermatol 2017;9:194](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29282392), [Acta Derm Venereol 2020;100:adv00202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32511742))

Case reports

* 33 year old woman with diffuse dermal angiomatosis of the bilateral breasts ([Proc (Bayl Univ Med Cent) 2020;33:273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32313486))
* 53 year old woman with diffuse dermal angiomatosis of the breast and subclavian artery occlusion ([Arch Dermatol 2006;142:343](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16549710))
* 61 year old woman with multifocal diffuse dermal angiomatosis of the breast and abdomen ([Acta Derm Venereol 2020;100:adv00202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32511742))
* 63 year old woman with diffuse dermal angiomatosis of the thigh and critical limb ischemia ([J Vasc Surg Cases Innov Tech 2023;9:101303](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37767346))
* 65 year old man with diffuse dermal angiomatosis on the lower extremity and severe peripheral vascular atherosclerosis ([Eur J Vasc Endovasc Surg 2011;42:381](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21641837))

Treatment

* Treatment of underlying hypoxia with lifestyle changes (e.g., weight loss, smoking cessation, etc.) ([Case Rep Dermatol 2017;9:194](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29282392))
* Isotretinoin used due to antiangiogenic properties ([Gland Surg 2012;1:132](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24353983))
* Pentoxifylline ([Gland Surg 2015;4:554](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26645009))
* Oral and topical corticosteroids ([Gland Surg 2015;4:554](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26645009), [Acta Derm Venereol 2020;100:adv00202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32511742))
* Surgical resection (breast reduction mammoplasty or mastectomy) ([JAAD Case Rep 2024;48:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38778895), [Arch Dermatol 2008;144:693](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18490610))
* Revascularization / stenting procedures for cases with vascular occlusion identified on angiography (such as in cases of critical limb ischemia and breast cases with subclavian artery stenosis) ([J Vasc Surg Cases Innov Tech 2023;9:101303](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37767346), [Arch Dermatol 2006;142:343](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16549710))
* Treatment of other underlying etiologies (calciphylaxis, etc.) ([Am J Dermatopathol 2009;31:653](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19652584), [J Cutan Pathol 2013;40:829](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23782317), [G Ital Dermatol Venereol 2015;150:115](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25502367), [JAAD Case Rep 2022;24:8](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35518276))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Diffuse proliferation of endothelial cells and surrounding pericytes in the interstitia of the papillary and reticular dermis, dissecting the dermal collagen bundles
* Endothelial cells form small capillary vessels with regular, small lumina
* Endothelial cells may be spindle shaped, vacuolated or haphazardly arranged in the collagen but there is no cytologic or nuclear atypia since this is a benign proliferation of capillary vessels
* Erythrocytes may be present within vessels or extravasated, as these are functional blood capillaries
* There may be thrombi in vessels, reflecting possible tissue hypoxia or underlying hypercoagulability
* There is no intravascular proliferation of endothelial cells within the vascular lumina as is seen in other cutaneous reactive angiomatosis
* References: [Case Rep Dermatol 2017;9:194](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29282392), [Acta Derm Venereol 2020;100:adv00202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32511742)

Positive stains

* Expresses endothelial cell markers [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [ERG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsERG.html)

* References: [Case Rep Dermatol 2017;9:194](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29282392), [Semin Diagn Pathol 2023;40:284](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37121782)

Sample pathology report

* Skin, punch biopsy:
  + Increased banal appearing dermal vessels, suggestive of reactive angiomatosis (see comment)
  + Comment: The sections demonstrate a superficial to deep proliferation of small well formed vessels throughout the dermis that dissect through the dermal collagen. Vascular cleft-like spaces are lined by banal appearing endothelial cells and uniform pericytes. Immunohistochemical staining reveals a proliferation of cells positive with CD31 and CD34. HHV8 is negative. The microscopic differential diagnosis includes reactive angiomatosis such as diffuse dermal angiomatosis. This may arise in instances of tissue hypoxia and clinical correlation with the patient’s comorbid conditions is recommended.

Differential diagnosis

* **Acroangiodermatitis** (also known as **pseudo-Kaposi sarcoma**, [severe vascular stasis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorstasisdermatitis.html)) ([Case Rep Dermatol 2017;9:194](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29282392)):
  + Lobular proliferation of thick walled capillary vessels
  + Lumina are mostly wide open
  + Occurs within the superficial dermis
  + Background features of chronic venous insufficiency (such as dermal fibrosis, hemosiderin laden macrophages)
* [Intravascular reactive angioendotheliomatosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticreactiveangioendotheliomatosis.html) ([Case Rep Dermatol 2017;9:194](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29282392)):
  + Proliferation of endothelial cells within the lumina of dilated blood vessels
  + Positive for endothelial markers [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [ERG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsERG.html)
* [Kaposi sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuekaposi.html) ([Dermatol Online J 2018;24:13030](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30142739), [Case Rep Dermatol 2017;9:194](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29282392)):
  + Spindling of endothelial cells
  + Diffuse slit-like lumen formation with promontory sign
  + Commonly mitoses with minimal pleomorphism
  + Inflammatory cellular infiltrate with plasma cells
  + [HHV8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsHHV8.html) positive
  + [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsd240.html) positive
  + Positive for endothelial markers [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [ERG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsERG.html)
* [Well differentiated angiosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticangiosarcoma.html) ([Dermatol Online J 2018;24:13030](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30142739), [Case Rep Dermatol 2017;9:194](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29282392)):
  + Infiltrative vascular channels that are irregularly shaped
  + Atypical endothelial cells with cytologic and nuclear atypia, mitotic figures
  + [HHV8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsHHV8.html) negative
  + Lack of an outer layer of [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) positive pericytes
  + May have positive [D2-40 / podoplanin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsd240.html) suggestive of focal lymphatic tumor differentiation
  + High [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) activity showing high cell proliferative activity
  + Positive for endothelial markers [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [ERG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsERG.html)
* [Calciphylaxis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorcalciphylaxis.html) ([JAAD Case Rep 2020;6:826](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32875030), [Case Rep Dermatol 2017;9:194](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29282392)):
  + Can clinically resemble diffuse dermal angiomatosis with painful ulcerative and necrotic lesions in ESRD patients
  + Histologically has calcification, fibrointimal hyperplasia and microthrombi in dermal / subcutaneous vessels
  + [von Kossa](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalcium.html) stain positive
* Lymphatic vessel proliferation disorders:
  + [Postradiation atypical vascular lesion](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuelymphangiomaradiation.html), [lymphangioendothelioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuelymphangioendothelioma.html), [lymphangiomatosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuelymphangioma.html) ([Case Rep Dermatol 2017;9:194](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29282392)):
    - [D2-40 / podoplanin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsd240.html) expression of endothelial cells
    - Lack of an outer layer of [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) positive pericytes

A 55 year old woman with a past medical history of end stage renal disease (ESRD) (on dialysis), coronary cardiovascular disease (history of coronary artery bypass grafts) and obesity presents with a 6 month history of painful, ulcerative lesions on the bilateral breasts. A skin punch biopsy is shown. HHV8 is negative. What is the diagnosis?

1. Angiosarcoma
2. Calciphylaxis
3. Diffuse dermal angiomatosis
4. Hemangioma
5. Kaposi sarcoma

Board review style answer #1

**C**. Diffuse dermal angiomatosis. Diffuse dermal angiomatosis is a benign reactive angiomatosis to tissue ischemia that can present as painful, ulcerative and necrotic erythematous lesions often on the breast. It commonly affects middle aged women with comorbid conditions such as obesity, macromastia, smoking, atherosclerotic disease and ESRD. The slide above shows a proliferation of endothelial cells (confirmed by CD31 positivity) with regular, small vascular lumina interspersed in dermal collagen. Answer A is incorrect because angiosarcoma would show cytologic / nuclear atypia with mitotic figures and irregular vascular channels. Answer E is incorrect because Kaposi sarcoma would be HHV8 positive with spindled endothelial cells, inflammatory cell infiltrate and slit-like vascular lumina. Answer B is incorrect because calciphylaxis clinically resembles diffuse dermal angiomatosis but it would have subcutaneous calcifications, fibrointimal hyperplasia and thrombi in vessels. Answer D is incorrect because hemangioma is a benign proliferation of capillaries but it would not be expected to dissect collagen diffusely through the papillary and reticular dermis and would be a circumscribed lesion.

Board review style question #2

Which stain helps differentiate Kaposi sarcoma from a benign reactive angiomatosis such as diffuse dermal angiomatosis?

1. CD31
2. CD34
3. ERG
4. HHV8
5. Ki67

Board review style answer #2

**D**. HHV8. Kaposi sarcoma is HHV8 positive as it is caused by the human herpesvirus 8. Diffuse dermal angiomatosis is not associated with HHV8. Answers A, B and C are incorrect because both Kaposi sarcoma and diffuse dermal angiomatosis stain positive for endothelial markers ERG, CD34 and CD31. Answer E is incorrect because Ki67 is a marker of cell proliferation that can be increased in angiosarcoma.
