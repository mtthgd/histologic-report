Definition / general

* Cutaneous acanthotic benign lesion with epidermolytic hyperkeratosis spanning more than 50% of its surface

Essential features

* Solitary or multiple lesions with acanthosis and epidermolytic hyperkeratosis
* Epidermolytic hyperkeratosis spanning > 50% of the lesion surface

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [D23.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D23-/D23.9) - other benign neoplasm of skin, unspecified
* ICD-11: [2F21.Y](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f1791550158%2fmms%2fother) - other specified benign keratinocytic acanthomas

Epidemiology

* M = F ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30663082))
* Mean age: 58 years old ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30663082))

Sites

* Extragenital (67% of cases): head and neck, upper limbs, trunk or back, lower limbs (uncommon), oral (uncommon)
* Genital (33% of cases): vulvar, penile, perianal, perineal, scrotal

Pathophysiology

* Somatic hotspot mutations in the Arg156 position of *KRT10*, also known to cause epidermolytic ichthyosis (germline) ([J Cutan Pathol 2020;47:524](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32045015))
* Not HPV related ([J Cutan Pathol 2020;47:524](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32045015))

Clinical features

* Most frequently a single lesion but may be multiple (5% of instances) ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30663082))
* Erythematous, skin colored or brown papules or nodules with scale
* Multiple epidermolytic acanthomas are more likely to be found on genital sites
  + Can be confused with condyloma ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30663082))
* May be pruritic
* Dermoscopy may show pearly white areas, irregular pigmented grooves and peripheral pigmented radial streak-like areas ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2017;77:e37](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28711100))

Diagnosis

* H&E stain with light microscopy

Prognostic factors

* Benign

Case reports

* 40 year old man with multiple epidermolytic acathomas on the fingers, mimicking flat warts ([Case Rep Dermatol 2017;9:98](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28868008))
* 49 year old man with multiple pruritic epidermolytic acanthomas on the scrotum ([Actas Dermosifiliogr 2018;109:81](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28705521))
* 56 year old man with epidermolytic acanthoma on the lower back and related dermoscopy findings ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2017;77:e37](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28711100))
* 57 year old man with solitary penile pidermolytic acanthoma mimicking condyloma ([Dermatopathology (Basel) 2019;6:23](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31700841))
* 58 year old woman with a solitary vulvar epidermolytic acanthoma mimicking condyloma ([Int Angiol 1988;7:19](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3290356))
* 71 year old Caucasian man with an oral solitary epidermolytic acanthoma ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol Oral Radiol 2019;128:e208](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31078506))

Treatment

* Surgical excision
* Liquid nitrogen ([Case Rep Dermatol 2017;9:98](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28868008))
* Topical imiquimod ([J Dermatol 2007;34:267](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17352727))
* Topical calcipotriol ([J Dermatol 2013;40:144](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23083445))

* See [clinical features](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticepidermolyticacanthoma.html#clinicalfeatures)

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Acanthosis and epidermolytic hyperkeratosis (covering > 50% of the lesion surface) ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30663082))
* Low power architecture generally resembles seborrheic keratosis ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30663082))
* Epidermolytic hyperkeratosis consists of lace-like degeneration of the midspinous epithelial layer, perinuclear vacuolization, epithelial eosinophilia and finely speckled keratohyaline granules ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30663082))
* 3 architectural patterns of solitary epidermolytic acanthoma have been described: papillomatous, cup shaped and acanthotic ([J Eur Acad Dermatol Venereol 2011;25:175](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20553361))
* Solitary and multiple epidermolytic acanthomas display similar histopathological findings ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30663082))

Differential diagnosis

* [Seborrheic keratosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticSK.html):
  + Acanthosis and papillomatosis but no epidermolytic hyperkeratosis
  + Focal incidental epidermolytic hyperkeratosis may be present
    - Still classified as seborrheic keratosis if covering < 50% of the surface
* [Verruca or condyloma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorwarts.html):
  + Hypergranulosis and finely speckled keratohyaline granules
  + No lace-like reticular degeneration
  + No dense cytoplasmic eosinophilia
  + HPV induced
* [Incidental epidermolytic hyperkeratosis in another acanthotic lesion](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorepidermolytichyperkeratosis.html):
  + Epidermolytic hyperkeratosis is found spanning < 50% of the lesion surface
* **Epidermolytic ichthyosis (formerly bullous congenital icthyosiform erythroderma)**:
  + Generalized desquamating plaques rather than a solitary lesion
  + Hereditary ichthyosiform condition due to alterations in keratin genes
  + Overlapping histology with solitary epidermolytic acanthoma, clinicopathologic correlation is crucial
* [Clear cell acanthoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticclearcellacanthoma.html):
  + Pale epithelial cells with acanthosis but no epidermolytic hyperkeratosis
* **Solitary keratosis with hypergranulotic dyscornification**:
  + Dense keratohyaline granules rather than finely speckled
  + Both may display eosinophilic cytoplasm but perinuclear vacuolization is more characteristic of epidermolytic acanthoma
  + Hypergranulotic dyscornification may be a focal incidental finding in other lesions

What is the best diagnosis based on the images above, considering this is a solitary lesion?

1. Clear cell acanthoma
2. Epidermolytic acanthoma
3. Epidermolytic ichthyosis
4. Seborrheic keratosis

Board review style answer #1

**B.** Epidermolytic acanthoma. Epidermolytic acanthoma is an acanthotic benign lesion that displays epidermolytic hyperkeratosis on more than 50% of its surface. Clear cell acanthoma and seborrheic keratosis do not show epidermolytic hyperkeratosis on their surface. Epidermolytic ichthyosis does not present as a solitary lesion but rather as a hereditary generalized desquamative eruption.

Board review style question #2

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/skinnonmelanocyticepidermolyticacanthomaRoy4B.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/skinnonmelanocyticepidermolyticacanthomaRoy4B.jpg)

What is the histopathological epidermal reaction pattern seen in the image above?

1. Acantholysis
2. Dyskeratotic acantholysis
3. Epidermal necrosis
4. Epidermolytic hyperkeratosis

Board review style answer #2

**D.** Epidermolytic hyperkeratosis. Epidermolytic hyperkeratosis is the epidermal reaction pattern displayed here, with reticular (lace-like) clearing of the epidermis, dense eosinophilia of the keratinocytes and coarse keratohyalin granules. Acantholysis is rather a rounding of keratinocytes with detached intercellular attachments (desmosomes). Dyskeratotic acantholysis shows acantholysis in addition to dense eosinophilia of keratinocytes but no reticular degeneration of finely speckled keratohyalin granules.
