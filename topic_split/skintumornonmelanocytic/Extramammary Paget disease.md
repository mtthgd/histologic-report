Definition / general

* Extramammary Paget disease (EMPD) is defined as intraepidermal adenocarcinoma

Essential features

* Paget cells in small groups or singly in the basal layer of the epidermis near apocrine sweat glands ([Dermatol Surg 2020;46:151](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31356440))
* May differentiate into glandular elements
* May originate from intraepithelial portion of sweat glands or primitive basal cells ([Dermatol Surg 2020;46:151](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31356440))

Terminology

* Extramammary Paget disease
* EMPD

ICD coding

* ICD-11
  + [2E64.1](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#1796624917) - extramammary Paget disease of skin
  + [2E67.11](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#1913185137) - vulvar Paget disease

Epidemiology

* F > M
* Presents in the sixth to eighth decades of life ([Dermatol Surg 2020;46:151](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31356440))
* White populations are most commonly affected
* M > F in Asian populations ([Curr Oncol 2021;28:2969](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34436026))

Sites

* Areas where there are abundant apocrine sweat glands
* Anogenital region followed by the axilla are the most commonly involved sites ([Dermatol Surg 2020;46:151](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31356440))
* Rarely can be found in the external auditory canal, eyelid or other sites ([Dermatol Surg 2020;46:151](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31356440))
* Can be uni or multifocal

Pathophysiology

* Exact pathogenesis is unknown
* Associated with Toker cells ([Am J Dermatopathol 2005;27:185](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15900119))
* Likely does not have a uniform histogenesis ([Dermatol Surg 2020;46:151](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31356440))
* 25% of cases have a primary cutaneous adnexal carcinoma ([StatPearls: Extramammary Paget Disease [Accessed 1 May 2024]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29630276))
* 10 - 15% of cases have bladder, rectum, cervix, prostate or urethra carcinomas
* Early stages are characterized by slow, indolent growth
* Later stages are characterized by deeper invasion and possible metastasis

Etiology

* Primary EMPD likely has an apocrine origin
* Due to underlying carcinoma in 10 - 20% of cases of vulvar disease, secondary EMPD may be due to cervical, prostatic, ovarian or endometrial carcinoma
* Secondary EMPD is an extension of underlying adnexal adenocarcinoma or visceral malignancy, such as urothelial or anorectal malignancies
* Underlying malignancy is associated with 10 - 30% of all EMPD cases

Clinical features

* Erythematous, well demarcated plaques that may grow to several centimeters ([Curr Oncol 2021;28:2969](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34436026))
* Lesions may become ulcerated, scaly, eczematous or erosive
* Lesion may have crusting, weeping or ulcerations at later stages
* Erosions with a white scale can give a strawberries and cream appearance ([StatPearls: Extramammary Paget Disease [Accessed 1 May 2024]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29630276))
* Typically, lesions show slow expansion with sharp demarcation between uninvolved skin
* Lesions can be hyper or hypopigmented ([Curr Oncol 2021;28:2969](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34436026))
* EMPD may be misdiagnosed as eczema, psoriasis or candidiasis for several months to years
* Median delay of 2 years in diagnosis is reported
* Most common presenting symptoms are pruritis, burning, tenderness and edema ([StatPearls: Extramammary Paget Disease [Accessed 1 May 2024]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29630276))
* Underpants pattern erythema can be seen in those with underlying carcinoma and is associated with a worse prognosis due to lymphatic invasion ([StatPearls: Extramammary Paget Disease [Accessed 1 May 2024]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29630276))
* Spontaneous regression is possible ([Br J Dermatol 2005;153:676](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16120170))

Diagnosis

* Definitive diagnosis is by biopsy
* Accurate diagnosis is necessary due to the differing treatment strategies for primary and secondary EMPD ([Curr Oncol 2021;28:2969](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34436026))

Laboratory

* Elevated CEA levels are associated with a worse prognosis ([Br J Dermatol 2008;158:313](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18028492))

Prognostic factors

* Favorable prognosis in general
* Worse prognosis is associated with visceral or adnexal carcinomas
* Worse prognosis if there are elevated levels of CEA in the blood, high tumor invasion levels, the presence of nodules in the primary lesion and lymph node metastasis ([Br J Dermatol 2008;158:313](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18028492))
* Worse prognosis is associated with underpants pattern erythema due to lymphatic invasion
* EMPD with HER2 overexpression shows a more aggressive behavior ([Int J Gynecol Cancer 2020;30:1672](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32998859))

Case reports

* HIV+ man in his late 30s with scrotal EMPD ([JAAD Case Rep 2023;43:24](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38162408))
* 64 year old woman with EMPD and brain metastasis ([Dermatol Reports 2020;12:8841](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33408837))
* 64 year old man with EMPD and concomitant herpes simplex ([Am J Dermatopathol 2019;41:590](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31335413))
* 74 year old woman with pigmented EMPD ([Am J Dermatopathol 2019;41:45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29894358))
* 75 year old man with EMPD secondary to recurrence of bladder carcinoma ([IJU Case Rep 2019;2:202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32743413))
* 78 year old woman with perianal rash ([Dis Colon Rectum 2021;64:511](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33661234))
* 83 year old man with inguinal EMPD ([Cureus 2019;11:e5937](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31788394))

Treatment

* Complete surgical resection with 2 cm margins (1 cm if clinically clear margins) ([Curr Oncol 2021;28:2969](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34436026))
* Lower recurrence rate with Mohs micrographic surgery (MMS) ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2004;51:767](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15523356))
* Treatment of underlying malignancy
* Adjuvant therapy with surgery to reduce recurrence
* Systemic chemotherapy if there are contraindications for surgery or radiation

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Paget cells are large, clear to pale cells with abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm and moderate pleomorphism within the epidermis
* May have prominent nucleoli with occasional mitosis
* Rarely, EMPD can have a signet ring appearance with eccentric nuclei ([Dermatol Surg 2020;46:151](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31356440))
* Paget cells may contain ample mucin ([StatPearls: Extramammary Paget Disease [Accessed 1 May 2024]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29630276))
* EMPD is a poorly circumscribed proliferation of large epithelial cells singly or in small clusters between normal keratinocytes in the epidermis
* Paget cells may have cleft-like spaces between Paget cells and neighboring keratinocytes
* Hyperkeratosis and parakeratosis may overlie aggregates of Paget cells ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2010;29:69](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19952933))
* No intracellular bridges or dyskeratosis are present in the overlying epidermis
* Dermis may have a chronic inflammatory infiltrate ([StatPearls: Extramammary Paget Disease [Accessed 1 May 2024]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29630276))
* Early lesions
  + Single cells or small groups of cells
  + Possibly have glandular formation in the epidermal basal layer
  + Highest concentration of Paget cells is most often found in the basal layer but lesions can be the full thickness of the epidermis
  + Generally, Paget cells spread into contiguous eccrine and follicular epithelium
  + Uncommonly, EMPD may involve the dermis
* In later lesions, Paget cells expand to fill the entire epidermis ([Dermatol Surg 2020;46:151](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31356440))
* May mimic Paget disease of the breast ([An Bras Dermatol 2015;90:225](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25830993))
* Dermal involvement is rare in vulva, more common in perianal region

Sample pathology report

* Skin, right vulva, punch biopsy:
  + Extramammary Paget disease, present at the peripheral and deep specimen edges

* Skin, perianal, excision:
  + Extramammary Paget disease, present at the peripheral specimen margin (see comment)
  + Comment: Sections reveal atypical epithelial cells involving the entire thickness of the epidermis. The cells have pleomorphic nuclear detail and ample pale cytoplasm. The dermis also shows a variable mostly perivascular lymphohistiocytic infiltrate. Immunohistochemistry is positive for CK7 and negative for SOX10. The overall changes are consistent with extramammary Paget disease.

Differential diagnosis

* Clinically, may resemble [psoriasis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorpsoriasis.html), [eczema](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumoracuteeczematousdermatitis.html), [candidiasis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorfungicandida.html), [contact dermatitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorallergiccontact.html)
* [Anogenital intraepithelial neoplasia (vulva)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/vulvadifferentiatedVIN.html):
  + Both may have cytological atypia and increased mitotic activity, which may be confined to the epithelium
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) negative, [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html) negative, [p16](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp16.html) positive if related to HPV, increased [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) ([StatPearls: Extramammary Paget Disease [Accessed 1 May 2024]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29630276))
* [Clear cell papulosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticclearcellpapulosis.html):
  + Both can have large clear cells that are usually confined in the epidermis; distinction is usually based on the clinical information
  + Negative or minimal staining for [mucin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmucins.html) and neuroendocrine markers
* **Melanocytic lesions**:
  + Melanocytes within the epidermis may resemble Paget cells but EMPD lacks classic features of melanocytes
  + Melanocytic markers such as [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html), [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsHMB45.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) and [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html) are positive, [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) markers are negative or minimal ([Curr Oncol 2021;28:2969](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34436026))
* [Melanoma in situ (MIS)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticmelanoma.html):
  + Clinical information and lack of melanocytic lesion in the dermis; however, some melanomas can be amelanotic
    - Melanoma commonly present in sun exposed skin with solar elastosis
  + MIS presents with poorly circumscribed proliferation of melanocytes with cytologic atypia
    - Single melanocytes frequently form a confluent proliferation along the basal layer and involve adnexal structures
    - Melanocytes usually show pleomorphism, melanin and nuclear atypia
  + Melanoma is negative for [cytokeratins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) and [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html)
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsHMB45.html) and [MART1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html) are usually negative in EMPD and positive in melanoma ([Curr Oncol 2021;28:2969](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34436026))
* [Metastatic epidermotropic carcinomas](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticmetastaticcarcinoma.html):
  + May express [cytokeratins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) or other epithelial markers as well as markers for the primary site of malignancy (e.g., [mammaglobin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmammaglobin.html) for breast cancer)
  + Usually negative for apocrine markers
* [Pagetoid actinic keratosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticactinickeratosis.html) / [pagetoid Bowen disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticcisgeneral.html):
  + Pagetoid actinic keratosis is typically found in sun damaged areas such as the face, scalp and extremities, whereas EMPD is usually found in the anogenital region
  + Pagetoid actinic keratosis may have atypical keratinocytes with cytologic atypia within the epidermis and frequently, parakeratosis and absence of granular layer
  + Often demonstrates [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) overexpression and negative [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsHMB45.html), [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html), [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html) and [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html)
* [Sebaceous carcinoma in situ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticsebaceouscarcinoma.html):
  + Sebaceous carcinoma in situ typically occurs in the periocular region
  + Both may exhibit atypical pale cells with pagetoid spread within the epidermis; the sebocytes usually have foamy and multilocular cytoplasm

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/wick/skin/pagetdiseaseextramammary2.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/wick/skin/pagetdiseaseextramammary2.jpg)

A 67 year old woman presents with pruritic, eczematous plaques involving the vulva that have repeatedly failed to respond to topical steroids. A biopsy reveals large, pale cells with abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm scattered within the epidermis. Immunohistochemical staining reveals positive cytokeratin, EMA and PAS. What is the most likely diagnosis and which other IHC stain would support your diagnosis?

1. Basal cell carcinoma, CK20
2. Bowen disease, p16
3. Extramammary Paget disease, CK7
4. Melanoma in situ, S100
5. Sebaceous carcinoma, EMA

Board review style answer #1

**C**. Extramammary Paget disease, CK7. The lesion shows redness and involves the vulva, which is a common site for extramammary Paget disease. CK7 is often expressed in Paget cells. Answer A is incorrect because even though basal cell carcinoma can involve the vulva, it is not a very common site. Basal cell carcinoma shows basaloid cells, which are usually nested and invade the dermis. The presented staining pattern does not fit basal cell carcinoma. Answer B is incorrect because while Bowen disease with clear cell change can stain positive for PAS, the epidermis is associated with a hyperkeratotic change and full thickness cellular atypia, rather than scattered atypical cells. Therefore, answer C is the better answer. Answer D is incorrect because melanoma is not usually pruritic and presents as a pigmented lesion. Even though amelanotic melanoma exists, most melanomas show pigmentation. The cells in melanoma show atypia and do not stain with cytokeratin, EMA and PAS. Answer E is incorrect because sebaceous carcinoma presents as sheets or lobules separated by fibrovascular stroma, in contrast to the scattered cells throughout the epidermis. The cytoplasm shows multivacuolation. Even though sebaceous carcinoma is positive for EMA and keratin, the presented histology with PAS positivity is not consistent with this diagnosis.

Board review style question #2

A 73 year old man presents to his primary care physician with a pruritic, well demarcated erythematous plaque on his scrotum. Biopsy of the lesion reveals large, pale cells with eosinophilic cytoplasm spread throughout the epidermis. Immunohistochemical staining is positive for CEA, EMA, PAS and cytokeratin. The patient denies any urinary or bowel symptoms. What is the most likely diagnosis and what additional investigation must be performed to confirm the diagnosis?

1. Bowen disease, Ki67 immunostaining
2. Extramammary Paget disease, imaging for underlying adenocarcinoma
3. Melanoma in situ, PRAME immunostain
4. Pagetoid actinic keratosis, p16 immunostain
5. Sebaceous carcinoma in situ, S100

Board review style answer #2

**B**. Extramammary Paget disease, imaging for underlying adenocarcinoma. The location and presence of epidermal cells with light eosinophilic cytoplasm that are positive for EMA and PAS are consistent with EMPD diagnosis. Considering the location and possibility of associated malignancy (in 10% of cases) or metastatic epidermotropic carcinomas, workup for adenocarcinoma is recommended. Answer A is incorrect because Bowen disease is more likely to display atypical cells throughout the full thickness of the epidermis, while EMPD is more likely to exhibit single or small clusters of atypical cells. Also, Ki67 is not routinely done to differentiate Bowen disease from EMPD. Answer D is incorrect because pagetoid squamous cell carcinoma in situ would not stain with PAS and typically shows prominent full thickness atypia. Answer E is incorrect because sebaceous carcinoma presents as sheets or lobules separated by fibrovascular stroma, in contrast to scattered cells throughout the epidermis. The cytoplasm typically shows multivacuolation. Even though sebaceous carcinoma is positive for EMA and keratin, the presented histology with PAS positivity is not consistent with this diagnosis. Answer C is incorrect because melanoma presents as a pigmented lesion. The melanocytes exhibit significant atypia and confluence of basal melanocytes is commonly present. The cells in melanoma do not stain with cytokeratin, EMA or PAS. Skin lymphoma can show epidermotropism and involvement of the epidermis by atypical lymphocytes. The cells are usually T cells and express T cell markers. They are typically smaller with fusiform nuclei and do not express keratin markers or PAS.
