Definition / general

* Benign dermal nodule of eccrine / apocrine gland derivation ([Middle East Afr J Ophthalmol 2010;17:374](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21180442))

Essential features

* Presents as a slow growing, nodular, solid or cystic cutaneous mass usually measuring up to 3 cm in diameter ([Dermatology 2016;232:78](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26606241))
* Characterized by variably sized nests and nodules of epithelial cells within the upper or mid dermis, typically with no overlying connection to the epidermis ([J Clin Pathol 2007;60:145](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16882695))

Terminology

* Also known as nodular hidradenoma, eccrine acrospiroma, clear cell hidradenoma, eccrine sweat gland adenoma and solid cystic hidradenoma

Epidemiology

* Frequently occurs in the third to fifth decade of life with a slight predominance in females ([Case Rep Oncol 2018;11:298](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29928207))

Sites

* Typically located on the head, neck and limbs (although any site may be affected) ([Case Rep Oncol 2018;11:298](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29928207))

Clinical features

* Presents as a slow growing, nodular, solid or cystic cutaneous mass usually measuring up to 3 cm in diameter ([Dermatology 2016;232:78](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26606241))
* Overlying skin can be skin colored, erythematous or blue and may exhibit superficial ulceration or serous discharge ([Indian Dermatol Online J 2016;7:410](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27730041))

Case reports

* 13 year old boy with nodular hidradenoma on the upper arm ([Australas J Dermatol 2017;58:e135](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27523405))
* 20 year old woman with nodular hidradenoma on the breast ([Lab Med 2019;50:320](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30722042))
* 61 year old woman with clear cell hidradenoma on the chin ([J Craniofac Surg 2017;28:e454](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28570408))

Treatment

* Recurrence rate of 12% if not completely excised ([J Clin Pathol 2007;60:145](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16882695))

Gross description

* Solitary well circumscribed dermal nodule ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2008;27:457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18580328))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Well circumscribed but unencapsulated, lobulated / cystic mass with variably sized nests and nodules of epithelial cells within the upper or mid dermis, typically with no overlying connection to the epidermis ([J Clin Pathol 2007;60:145](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16882695))
* Shows both solid and cystic components ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2005;129:e113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15859654))
  + Solid portion composed of 2 types of cells: polyhedral cells with basophilic cytoplasm and glycogen containing clear cells with eccentric round nucleus
  + Cystic areas secondary to degeneration of tumor cells
* Tubular lumina are lined by cuboidal or columnar secretory cells ([J Oral Maxillofac Pathol 2013;17:136](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23798849))
* Fibrovascular, collagenous or hyalinized stroma ([J Cutan Pathol 2007;34:497](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17518780), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2005;129:e113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15859654))
* Focal apocrine decapitation secretion, squamous differentiation, squamous eddy formation, keratinization, mucinous change or sebaceous differentiation can be seen ([Am J Dermatopathol 2012;34:461](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21993337), [J Cutan Pathol 2007;34:801](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17880588), [J Cutan Pathol 2007;34:497](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17518780))
* Malignant hidradenocarcinoma presents with infiltrative growth pattern, nuclear atypia and pleomorphism, predominantly solid cell islands, angiolymphatic invasion, necrosis and ≥ 4 mitoses per 10 high power fields ([Middle East Afr J Ophthalmol 2010;17:374](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21180442), [Mod Pathol 2009;22:600](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19252473))
* Variants:
  + Clear cell hidradenoma ([Cutis 2014;94:268](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25566574))
    - Has a biphasic cellular population: (1) round, fusiform, or
      polygonal cells with vesicular nuclei and eosinophilic cytoplasm
      and (2) cells with clear cytoplasm and often eccentrically located
      nuclei
  + Solid cystic hidradenoma ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2005;129:e113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15859654))
    - Shows both solid and cystic components
    - Cysts probably represent cystic degeneration
  + Mucinous hidradenoma ([J Cutan Pathol 2007;34:497](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17518780))
    - Demonstrates diffuse and prominent mucinous cell proliferation
  + Poroid hidradenoma ([Ann Dermatol 2021;33:289](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34079192))
    - Has architectural features of the apocrine hidradenoma and
      cytological features of poroid and cuticular cells
    - Poroid cells are uniform, small cuboidal cells with an oval to round nuclei
    - Cuticular cells have an abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm with a
      larger nucleus that shows occasional multinucleation
  + Pigmented nodular hidradenoma ([Arch Dermatol 1971;104:117](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4328525))
    - Shows melanin pigmentation

Positive stains

* [AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html) and other low molecular weight cytokeratins, [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html), [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html) ([J Cytol 2011;28:235](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22090707), [J Clin Pathol 2007;60:145](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16882695))
* Some cases show partial staining with [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [p63](https://pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html), [actin-muscle specific](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsactinmusclespecific.html) ([J Cytol 2011;28:235](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22090707))
* Stains are not particularly helpful in the diagnosis of this entity and can be misleading

Negative stains

* Some cases show partial staining with [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html), [actin - muscle specific](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsactinmusclespecific.html) ([J Cytol 2011;28:235](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22090707))
* [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) is typically negative

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* t(11;19) translocation has been reported ([Am J Dermatopathol 2007;29:457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17890914))

Sample pathology report

* Skin, neck, excision:
  + Hidradenoma, extending to the deep margin

Differential diagnosis

* [Metastatic renal cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticmetastaticcarcinoma.html)
  + Absence of ductal differentiation/sweat production
  + Positive for PAX8 and CD10
  + Negative for p63
* [Basal cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticbcc.html) **with eccrine differentiation**:
  + Peripheral palisading and no cystic areas
  + Stromal epithelial retraction
  + Epidermal connection
* **Hidradenocarcinoma**:
  + Greater cytologic atypia, mitoses and infiltrative
* [Squamous cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticscc.html):
  + Epidermal connection, keratin pearls and infiltrative
  + Absence of secretory differentiation
* [Trichilemmoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocytictrichilemmoma.html):
  + Broad connection with the epidermis
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), thickened hyalinized basement membrane and peripheral cell palisading
* [Poroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticeccrineporoma.html):
  + Epidermal connection and broad, anastomosing cords
  + Cells are more basaloid while cells of hidradenoma are more eosinophilic
  + Recurrent *YAP1-MAML2* and *YAP1-NUTM1* fusions identified in a subset
* [Cylindroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticeccrinecylindroma.html):
  + Jigsaw puzzle pattern
  + Hyaline globules and thickened basement membranes
