Definition / general

* Rare, benign, cystic tumor of apocrine glands
* Affects middle aged adults with no gender predilection

Essential features

* Most commonly affects eyelids
* Usually solitary; multiple hidrocystomas have syndromic association
* Intradermal, dome shaped, translucent, bluish nodule up to 1 cm, lined by dual cuboidal or columnar layer histologically

Terminology

* Cystadenomas (more complex papillary projections), sudoriferous cysts, Moll’s gland cysts

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8404/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=72) - hidrocystoma
* ICD-10
  + [D23.11](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D23-/D23.11) - other benign neoplasm of skin of right eyelid, including canthus
  + [D23.12](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D23-/D23.12) - other benign neoplasm of skin of left eyelid, including canthus
  + [H02.82](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/H00-H59/H00-H05/H02-/H02.82) - cysts of eyelid
* ICD-11: [EK70.3](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#2040265542%20%26%201303709853) - hidrocystoma

Epidemiology

* Commonly affects adults between the ages of 30 and 70 years ([MedGenMed 2006;8:57](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17406184))
* No gender predilection
* Usually solitary, painless
* Solitary lesions are not familial but multiple hidrocystomas are associated with Goltz-Gorlin syndrome and Schopf-Schulz-Passarge syndrome (a form of ectodermal dysplasia) ([Acta Derm Venereol 2008;88:607](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19002348), [MedGenMed 2006;8:57](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17406184))

Sites

* Predilection for head and neck region, most common site being the eyelids
* Despite its apocrine derivation, rare at sites rich in apocrine glands (groin, axilla, anogenital region, eyelids [Moll glands], ears [ceruminous glands])

Pathophysiology

* Exact pathogenesis is unknown
* Regarded as a proliferative tumor arising from the secretory part of the apocrine sweat glands, unlike earlier belief that it is a simple retention cyst
* References: [Eplasty 2022;22:ic13](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36072057), [StatPearls: Apocrine hidrocystoma [Accessed 4 April 2025]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28846245)

Etiology

* Sun exposure may be considered a potential risk factor ([StatPearls: Apocrine hidrocystoma [Accessed 4 April 2025]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28846245))

Clinical features

* Presents as an intradermal, moderately firm, dome shaped, translucent, bluish or purplish cystic nodule up to 1 cm ([Acta Dermatovenerol Alp Pannonica Adriat 2021;30:53](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34169699))
* Cysts are gradually progressive until they reach a certain size ([Acta Dermatovenerol Alp Pannonica Adriat 2021;30:53](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34169699))
* Multiple lesions on face, also called Robinson variant, usually in middle aged women ([Clin Exp Dermatol 1995;20:323](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8548991))
* May be precursor to apocrine carcinoma ([Acta Dermatovenerol Alp Pannonica Adriat 2021;30:53](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34169699))
* Do not exhibit seasonal variations in size or number ([Acta Dermatovenerol Alp Pannonica Adriat 2021;30:53](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34169699))

Diagnosis

* Clinical diagnosis by examination of a translucent cystic lesion with a bluish hue on an eyelid ([MedGenMed 2006;8:57](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17406184))
* Dermoscopy findings: smooth surface with a translucent appearance ([Dermatol Pract Concept 2022;12:e2022090](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35646469))
* Color may vary depending on the cyst contents and surrounding tissue; vascular structures may be visible, especially in cases of inflammation or irritation
* Biopsy is confirmatory

Treatment

* No treatment required
* Surgical excision with narrow margins ([Dermatol Surg 2001;27:382](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11298711))
  + Prognosis following excision is excellent due to the benign nature
* Needle puncture: less effective, with higher recurrence rates ([Dermatol Surg 2016;42:134](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26716715), [Eplasty 2022;22:ic13](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36072057))
* Sclerotherapy: cyst puncture followed by injection of hypertonic glucose or trichloroacetic acid
* Botulinum toxin A: emerging as a potential option for multiple lesions ([Br J Dermatol 2017;176:488](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27233901))
* Laser treatments: carbon dioxide laser vaporization ([J Dermatolog Treat 2001;12:97](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12243666))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Large unilocular or multilocular cystic space within dermis
* Fibrous pseudocapsule is often present
* Typically, cystic spaces are lined by a double layer of epithelial cells: an outer layer of flattened vacuolated myoepithelial cells and an inner layer of cuboidal or columnar cells with eosinophilic cytoplasm and basally located, round or oval vesicular nuclei (secretory cells)
* Decapitation secretions and apical snouts are usually present
* Small papillary projections into the lumen can be occasionally seen: lesion described as cystadenoma ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2024;114:109085](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38086129))
  + WHO states that the terms hidrocystoma and cutaneous cystadenoma may be used interchangeably; while hidrocystoma denotes a simpler cyst, cystadenoma refers to relatively complex lesions

* Often adjacent to hyperplastic apocrine glands

- Malignant transformation is extremely rare ([Dermatol Online J 2019;25:13030](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31329389))

Sample pathology report

* Skin, eyelid, excision:
  + Hidrocystoma (see comment)
  + Comment: Histologic sections examined show skin with unremarkable epidermis. Dermis shows a cyst lined by dual layer composed of outer myoepithelial cells and inner columnar cells with decapitation secretions and apical snouting. Intraluminal homogenous, eosinophilic secretions are also seen.

Differential diagnosis

* [Eccrine hidrocystoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/eyeeyelidcysts.html) ([Acta Dermatovenerol Alp Pannonica Adriat 2021;30:53](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34169699)):
  + Considered to be a retention cyst of eccrine duct rather than a proliferative lesion
  + Common site is head and neck region, more commonly eyelids and periorbital area
  + Clinically, translucent bluish papules, rarely > 5 mm in size
  + Eccrine hidrocystomas are known to increase in size during summers or after heat exposure and decrease during winters
  + Cyst is lined by bland bilayer
  + Inner lining with basally located round nuclei and moderate pale cytoplasm; no decapitation secretions or snouts
  + Outer layer is composed of myoepithelial layer
  + Negative for [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [CK18](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck18.html), [GCDFP-15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgcdfp15.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsS100.html) and [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html) ([Acta Dermatovenerol Alp Pannonica Adriat 2021;30:53](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34169699))
* **Lacrimal gland cyst** ([Am J Ophthalmol 2013;155:380](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23114708)):
  + Clinically characterized by rubbery, mobile nodule on the eyelid; may have a bluish hue
  + Histologically, fluid filled cyst in association with normal lacrimal tissue
  + Cyst lined by single or double layer of epithelium; may be cuboidal or stratified squamous epithelium
  + Present in proximity to normal lacrimal gland structure with surrounding chronic inflammatory infiltrate

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/skintumornonmelanocyticapocrinecystadenomaKhurana02.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/skintumornonmelanocyticapocrinecystadenomaKhurana02.jpg)

Which of the following is true about the intradermal cystic lesion shown above?

1. Associated with Goltz-Gorlin syndrome
2. Common at sites rich in apocrine glands like groin and axilla
3. Luminal cells are negative for GCDFP-15
4. Malignant transformation is common

Board review style answer #1

**A.** Associated with Goltz-Gorlin syndrome. Despite its apocrine derivation, hidrocystomas are rare at sites rich in apocrine glands. Answer B is incorrect because it has predilection for head and neck region, the most common site being the eyelids. Answer C is incorrect because luminal cells are positive for GCDFP-15. Answer D is incorrect because malignant transformation is extremely rare.

Board review style question #2

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/skintumornonmelanocyticapocrinecystadenomaKhurana04.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/skintumornonmelanocyticapocrinecystadenomaKhurana04.jpg)

A 38 year old woman presented with a 0.8 cm diameter cystic bluish, painless lesion on the upper eyelid. What is the likely diagnosis?

1. Epidermal inclusion cyst
2. Hidrocystoma
3. Milium
4. Steatocystoma

Board review style answer #2

**B.** Hidrocystoma. Hidrocystoma most commonly presents as a bluish, painless nodule on an eyelid. The microscopy picture shows a cystic space lined by double layer of epithelial cells. Answer A is incorrect because an epidermal inclusion cyst is lined by stratified squamous epithelium. Answer C is incorrect because a milium cyst is lined by stratified squamous epithelium. Answer D is incorrect because steatocystoma is lined by squamous epithelium with sebaceous glands in the cyst wall.
