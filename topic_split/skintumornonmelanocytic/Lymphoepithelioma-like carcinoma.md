Definition / general

* Exceptionally rare and poorly differentiated cutaneous carcinoma with prominent reactive inflammatory infiltrate
  + Mimics undifferentiated nasopharyngeal carcinoma ([Rare Tumors 2013;5:e47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24179659))
  + WHO defines these tumors (in nasopharynx/sinonasal cavity) as lymphoepithelial carcinoma
  + Importantly, cutaneous LELC is NOT associated with Epstein Barr virus (EBV) infection

Epidemiology

* Older to elderly adult patients ([Dermatol Online J 2008;14:12](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19061572))
* Approximately equal incidence of females and males

Sites

* Head and neck most common site

Pathophysiology

* In skin, no apparent relationship with EBV ([Dermatol Online J 2008;14:12](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19061572), [Am J Dermatopathol 1996;18:478](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8902094))
* This is in contrast to consistent EBV association with similar tumors from [lung](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumorLEL.html), [salivary glands](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandsLEL.html), [stomach](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stomachLEL.html), [thymus](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mediastinumLEL.html), [nasopharynx and sinonasal cavity](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/nasalsinonasalpapilloma.html)

Etiology

* Uncertain origin ([Dermatol Online J 2008;14:12](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19061572))
* Unclear whether a poorly / undifferentiated squamous cell carcinoma versus poorly differentiated adnexal neoplasm

Clinical features

* Usually firm, variably colored, face or neck plaque, papule or nodule ([Dermatol Online J 2008;14:12](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19061572))

Case reports

* 73 year old woman with vulvar tumor ([Diagn Pathol 2011 Jan 10;6:4](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21219641))
* 85 and 87 year old men ([Dermatol Online J 2012;18:7](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22398228), [Rare Tumors 2013;5:e47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24179659))
* 89 year old woman ([Dermatol Online J 2008;14:12](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19061572))

Treatment

* Wide local excision or Moh's microsurgery to ensure complete removal
* Radiation reserved for recurrence or lymph node involvement ([Case Rep Oncol Med 2012;2012:241816](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23133769))
* Generally low recurrence and metastatic potential with complete excision (in contrast to nasopharyngeal counterpart)
* Rare lymph node metastasis ([Am J Dermatopathol 2006;28:211](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16778488)) or death from disease

Microscopic (histologic) description

* With Diagnostic criteria:
  + Well circumscribed lobules, nests or small aggregates of large, cohesive, epithelioid cells closely associated with a dense, mixed T and B lymphocytic and plasmacytic infiltrate ([Rare Tumors 2013;5:e47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24179659))
    - Biphasic nature may be more apparent at high magnification
  + The epithelioid component generally lacks connection with the epidermis
    - Cells are often polygonal and display poorly defined eosinophilic cytoplasm, vesicular nuclei with prominent nucleoli and increased mitotic activity, including atypical mitotic figures ([Dermatol Online J 2008;14:12](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19061572))
    - Focal ductular differentiation or trichilemmal keratinization rarely reported ([J Cutan Pathol 1991;18:93](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1649848))
  + Mandatory dense inflammatory infiltrate (polymorphous and polytypic) surrounding and intermingling with epithelial tumor cells
    - Lymphoid infiltrate may obscure the epithelial component
    - When inflammation predominates, may mimic lymphoproliferative disorder

Positive stains

* CK [AE1/AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html) (pancytokeratin), [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html), +/- [CK5/6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5.html)
* Intraepithelial lymphocytes are typically a mixture of [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html)+ B cells and [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html)+ T cells with no loss of T cell antigens (i.e. preserved [CD2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1.html#cd2), [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html) and [CD7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1.html#cd7)), polytypic plasma cells (both kappa and lambda expressing subsets)

Negative stains

* Melanoma markers: [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) and [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html)
* Neuroendocrine markers: [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html), [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html) and [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html)
* [EBER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) (EBV encoded RNA in situ hybridization)
