Definition / general

* Cutaneous metastases from many internal organ sites or soft tissue sarcomas are uncommon ([J Surg Case Rep 2014 Sep 9;2014](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25210135))
* **Men:** usually from lung (25%), colon, melanoma, kidney, upper aerodigestive tract
* **Women:** usually from breast (69%), lung, melanoma, kidney, ovary
* Metastasis is often close to site of primary

Sites

* Local recurrence / regional metastasis most commonly occurs upon anterior chest wall
* Scalp is tmost commonly observed distant metastatic site

Case reports

* 73 year old woman with cutaneous metastases as only sign of extranodal tumor spread ([ISRN Surg 2011;2011:902971](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22084786))
* 83 year old woman with herpetiform cutaneous metastases from transitional cell carcinoma of bladder ([J Clin Pathol 2006;59:1331](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17142578))
* 91 year old woman with seven month history of redness and swelling around her right eye ([BMJ 2007;334:1228](http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC1889966/))

Treatment

* Treat underlying malignancy, possible excision of metastasis

Cutaneous metastases

Gross description

* Often multiple, firm, nonulcerated nodules, but may be solitary

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Poorly differentiated adenocarcinoma within dermis and often underlying subcutaneous tissue
* Tumor cells in sheets or clusters or as single detached cells
* Either abortive gland formation or "signet ring" cells (with central, cleared mucin-filled space which compresses and marginalizes the cell nucleus)
* Tumor cells may show a "single file" appearance (metastatic lobular breast carcinoma)
* Cytologic atypia in small to large epithelioid cells, often with striking nuclear pleomorphism
* Tumor cells may produce mucin, or mucinous debris may separate collagen bundles
* Variable desmoplasia or sclerosis of dermal collagen

Positive stains

* Immunohistochemical studies are often essential for diagnosis of cutaneous metastasis by breast carcinoma
  + Tumor cells are usually positive for [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), mammoglobin, or [GCDFP-15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgcdfp15.html)
  + May be positive for [estrogen receptor (ER)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html), [progesterone receptor (PR)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html), and [HER2/neu](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsher2GI.html)
* [Carcinoembryonic antigen (CEA)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd66.html#cd66e) may highlight gland or signet ring cell lumina
* [CK5/6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5.html) or [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) may distinguish primary apocrine or eccrine adnexal neoplasms (positive) from metastatic breast adenocarcinoma (generally negative)

Negative stains

* Negative immunostains for metastatic breast adenocarcinoma are [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) (positive in melanoma), [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html) (positive in lung adenocarcinoma), [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html) and [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html) (positive in colon adenocarcinoma)

Differential diagnosis

* [Dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticdfsp.html): may resemble metastatic signet ring cell carcinoma
* [Merkel cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticmerkelcell.html): resembles neuroendocrine metastases
* [Sweat gland tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticsweatglandcarcinoma.html): may resemble metastatic renal cell carcinoma
