Definition / general

* Rare disorder of women ages 40 - 50, with widespread cutaneous papules and nodules ([eMedicine](http://emedicine.medscape.com/article/283885-overview))
* Often associated with a destructive arthritis and internal malignancy
* Tumor cells are histiocytes and multinucleated giant cells containing abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm with a "ground glass" appearance

Terminology

* Formerly called lipoid dermatoarthritis

Epidemiology

* Usually women 40 - 50 years

Etiology

* Histiocytic origin ([Am J Surg Pathol 1990;14:687](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2192569))

Clinical features

* Skin lesions on the hands, especially at the base of the nails
* Lesions may also be on the face, ears, arms, scalp or mucosal surfaces
* "Coral beads" and vermicular erythematous lesions bordering nostrils are pathognomonic ([J Eur Acad Dermatol Venereol 2001;15:524](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11843211))
* Lesions vary from small papules to lesions several centimeters across, and are usually skin colored, yellow or reddish brown
* Recommended to screen patients for malignancy ([Rheumatology 2008;47:1102](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18445627)), since accompanied by neoplasm in 28% of cases
* May be a paraneoplastic process ([J Am Acad Dermatol 1998;39:864](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9810917)), or association with neoplasm may be due to reporting bias ([eMedicine](http://emedicine.medscape.com/article/283885-overview))
* Associated with destructive arthritis

Case reports

* 6 year old girl ([J Rheumatol 1998;25:794](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9558188))
* 33 year old woman with generalized systemic involvement ([Clin Exp Dermatol 2004;29:373](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15245533))
* 34 year old woman with papular lesions on hands and face ([Case of the Week #153](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case153.htm))
* 61 year old man with associated liver carcinoma ([Case Rep Dermatol 2012;4:163](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22949898))
* Relapse of multicentric reticulohistiocytosis before relapse of associated neoplasm ([Med Pediatr Oncol 1985;13:273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2412096))

Treatment

* No consistently reliable treatment ([Ryumachi 1993;33:68](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8493587))
* TNF inhibitors ([Arch Dermatol 2008;144:1360](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18936400)), aminobisphosphonates ([Arthritis Rheum 2003;48:3538](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14674006)) and immunosuppressive drugs ([Dermatol Online J 2009;15:2](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19281707)) have been effective only in individual cases

Differential diagnosis

* **Solitary reticulohistiocytoma**: younger age, solitary lesions usually not on digits or face ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:521](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16625100))
* [Epithelioid fibrous histiocytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuebfh.html): usually [Epithelioid sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueepithelioidsarcoma.html): deep seated tumor with markedly atypical cells that form granuloma-like clusters with central necrosis; tumor cells are EMA+, keratin+, CD68-
* **Granulomatous inflammation**: well formed granulomas and prominent lymphocytes, no large epithelioid histiocytes with eosinophilic glassy cytoplasm
* [Histiocytic sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodeshistiocyticsarcoma.html): typically forms a large mass of epithelioid histiocytes with significant nuclear atypia and mitotic activity
* [Juvenile xanthogranuloma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticjuvenilexantho.html): usually children, has scattered Touton type histiocytic giant cells and numerous eosinophils, but large epithelioid histiocytes are not prominent
* [Rosai-Dorfman disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodessinushistiocytosiswithmassivelymph.html): associated with adenopathy; histiocytes are pleomorphic and S100+
