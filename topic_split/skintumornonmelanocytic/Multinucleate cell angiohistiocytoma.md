Definition / general

* Uncommon cutaneous lesion
* Vascular and fibrohistiocytic lineage are hypothesized
* Characterized by odd multinucleated fibroblasts, superficial fibrosis and thickening of superficial papillary dermal vessels

Essential features

* Presence of odd multinucleated fibroblasts
* Presence of superficial parallel fibrosis
* Presence and thickening of superficial papillary dermal vessels
* Absence of perifollicular fibrosis

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [D23.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D23-/D23.9) - other benign neoplasm of skin, unspecified
* ICD-11: [2F23](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f466688507) - benign dermal fibrous or fibrohistiocytic neoplasms

Epidemiology

* M = F according to the largest series with histopathological review ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:563](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30927287))
  + But F > M according to prior literature review ([Am J Dermatopathol 2015;37:222](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25140659))
* Mean age 56 years for both sexes ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:563](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30927287))
* 2 cases associated with chronic hepatitis B and C ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:563](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30927287), [J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:678](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31070801))
* Certain authors postulate that MCAH-like changes can exist, although not as a distinct solitary papule clinically and more as a focal histologic finding aside or within another dermatologic process ([Am J Dermatopathol 2010;32:415](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20216199))
* Generalized form may be associated with systemic disease or abnormal immune states ([J Cutan Pathol 2017;44:125](https://doi.org/10.1111/cup.12853))

Sites

* Dorsal hands or fingers most frequent; when multiple lesions are found, they are most likely to affect the hands or fingers ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:563](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30927287))
* Lower extremities
* Trunk or back
* Head and neck (rare, most often these are fibrous papules with multinucleated stromal cells, not multinucleate cell angiohistiocytoma)

Pathophysiology

* Currently unknown
* Role for increased mast cells had been postulated but when mast cell counts were compared with counts of normal skin, no difference was found ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:563](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30927287))
* Role for fibroblasts, given the increased intralesional elastic fibers in generalized multinucleate cell angiohistiocytoma, has been postulated ([J Dermatol 2021;48:114](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33180332))
* Role for estrogen receptor alpha overexpression has been proposed ([Am J Dermatopathol 2010;32:655](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20644465))

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Dull red papules and nodules
* Lesions may be single, multiple or generalized (rare)
* Reference: [J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:563](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30927287)

Diagnosis

* Made on H&E stain with light microscopy in conjunction with clinical findings

Prognostic factors

* Benign
* May recur ([Am J Dermatopathol 2010;32:655](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20644465))

Case reports

* 28 year old woman with congenital chronic hepatitis B with multinucleate cell angiohistiocytoma and with cutaneous plasmacytosis ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:678](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31070801))
* 42 year old woman with generalized multinucleate cell angiohistiocytoma ([J Cutan Pathol 2017;44:125](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27813137))
* 42 year old man with generalized multinucleate cell angiohistiocytoma with involvement of palms and soles ([Indian J Dermatol Venereol Leprol 2018;84:468](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29873310))
* 42 year old man with Kaposi sarcoma-like multinucleate cell angiohistiocytoma complicating an iatrogenic arteriovenous fistula ([Eur J Dermatol 2010;20:642](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20621852))

Treatment

* No standard first line therapy; without treatment, course is indolent and progressive and spontaneous remission is rare
* Numerous treatment modalities have shown success:
  + Intralesional corticosteroids ([An Bras Dermatol 2020;95:480](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32471758))
  + Surgical excision ([An Bras Dermatol 2020;95:480](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32471758))
  + Potassium-titanyl-phosphate laser in combination with intralesional corticosteroids ([JAAD Case Rep 2019;5:880](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31681826))
  + Fractionated ablative carbon dioxide (CO2) laser ([JAAD Case Rep 2019;5:297](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30963081))
  + Pulsed dye laser ([Clin Exp Dermatol 2016;41:312](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26269389))
  + Intense pulsed light ([Dermatol Surg 2009;35:1141](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19438680))
  + Argon laser ([Br J Dermatol 1995;133:308](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7547405))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Presence of odd multinucleated, stellate fibroblasts with stretched out cytoplasmic extensions; multinucleated cells may be scant and may require additional tissue levels ([Dermatol Online J 2009;15:4](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19336021))
* Presence of superficial parallel fibrosis ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:563](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30927287))
* Presence and thickening of superficial papillary dermal vessels ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:563](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30927287))
* May show moderately increased number of blood vessels
* Absence of perifollicular fibrosis (found in the fibrous papule of the nose) ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:563](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30927287))
* May be acanthotic or flat architectural profile, with generally a normal basket woven stratum corneum ([Dermatol Online J 2009;15:4](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19336021))
* Eosinophils are very rarely encountered ([Cutis 2017;100:429](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29360886))

Positive stains

* Immunohistochemistry is seldom contributory for the diagnosis ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:563](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30927287))
* Multinucleated cells: variably [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html), [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html) and [factor XIIIa](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfactorxiiia.html) positive ([Dermatol Online J 2009;15:4](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19336021))
* Mast cells, plasma cells and prominent vessels may be highlighted with their respective markers ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:563](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30927287))

Negative stains

* Multinucleated cells are generally [CD163](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd160.html#cd163), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) and [factor VIII](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfactorviii.html) negative ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:563](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30927287))

Sample pathology report

* Skin, left dorsal hand, punch biopsy:
  + Multinucleate cell angiohistiocytoma (see comment)
  + Comment: Sections show superficial parallel fibrosis, thickened and abundant papillary dermal vessels and stellate multinucleated cells consistent with multinucleate cell angiohistiocytoma.

Differential diagnosis

* [Fibrous papule of the nose](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticangiofibroma.html):
  + Can also show prominent superficial papillary dermal vessels and multinucleated cells but the fibrosis is not parallel to the epidermis
  + Shows concentric perifollicular fibrosis in most instances, unlike multinucleate cell angiohistiocytoma
  + Much more commonly seen on the face / nose ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:563](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30927287))
* [Dermatofibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuebfh.html):
  + More common on lower extremities
  + Atrophic vascular variant of dermatofibroma may mimic multinucleate cell angiohistiocytoma
  + Fibrosis in dermatofibroma is more storiform, rather than parallel to the epidermis
  + May also show multinucleated cells and [factor XIIIa](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfactorxiiia.html) positivity
  + Follicular induction and [beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html) positivity in underlying dermal fibroblasts is frequent in dermatofibroma but absent in multinucleate cell angiohistiocytoma ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:563](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30927287))
* [Telangiectasia macularis eruptive perstans](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticmastcell.html):
  + Eosinophils are frequent in the inflammatory infiltrate of telangiectasia macularis eruptive perstans but very rare in multinucleate cell angiohistiocytoma
  + Multinucleated cells in telangiectasia macularis eruptive perstans are slightly different, showing < 3 nuclei, contrarily to multinucleate cell angiohistiocytoma
  + Mast cell counts are elevated in telangiectasia macularis eruptive perstans compared with multinucleate cell angiohistiocytoma or normal skin ([J Cutan Pathol 2019;46:563](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30927287))

Which histologic feature is absent in multinucleate cell angiohistiocytoma?

1. Concentric perifollicular fibrosis
2. Multinucleated stromal cells
3. Prominent and thickened superficial papillary dermal vessels

Board review style answer #2

**A.** Concentric perifollicular fibrosis. Concentric perifollicular fibrosis is rather a feature of fibrous papule of the nose and is the main histopathological criteria to distinguish these entities, along with knowledge of the anatomical site.
