Definition / general

* Benign neoplasm of the nail matrix characterized by localized subungual keratosis

Essential features

* Clinical features include subungual hyperkeratosis, distal fissures, erythronychia, longitudinal ridge, onycholysis, leukonychia and splinter hemorrhages
* Most characteristic morphologic feature is the longitudinal red band within the nail, known as longitudinal erythronychia
* Associated with *BAP1* tumor predisposition syndrome in people more than 30 years old

Terminology

* Localized multinucleate distal keratosis (not recommended per WHO)
* Subungual hyperkeratosis

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [L60.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/L00-L99/L60-L75/L60-/L60.9) - nail disorder, unspecified

Epidemiology

* Most common in middle aged adults
* Affects both men and women
* Affects all races and ethnicities
* Reference: [Ann Dermatol 2016;28:655](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27746654)

Sites

* Thumb (56%) > middle finger (14%) > first toe (10%) > ring finger (8%) ([Australas J Dermatol 2022;63:e350](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35904503))

Pathophysiology

* 3 hypotheses are suggested
  + Neoplastic hyperplasia of nail bed
  + Reactive hyperplasia of the onychopapilloma nail bed epithelium due to chronic irritation or trauma
  + Concomitant response with other inflammatory nail diseases, such as lichen planus ([Ann Dermatol 2016;28:655](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27746654))

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Longitudinal streaks: red streak (erythronychia), white streak (leukonychia), brown streak (melanonychia)
* Splinter hemorrhages: linear streaks of blood under the nail
* Subungual mass: warty growth under the nail
* Distal fissure: split or lift at the distal tip of nail
* Onycholysis: distal tip of nail may split or lift
* Chromonychia: yellow-brown discoloration
* Reference: [J Eur Acad Dermatol Venereol 2022;36:2235](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35869667)

Prognostic factors

* Prognosis of onychopapilloma is good; the recurrence rate after surgical treatment is 0 - 20% ([J Dermatolog Treat 2023;34:2213363](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37260138))

Case reports

* 50 year old man with 1 mm longitudinal erythematous streak on left thumb nail ([Ann Dermatol 2020;32:74](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33911713))
* 56 year old man with a lesion on left third toenail ([Actas Dermosifiliogr 2024;115:1067](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38307171))
* 71 year old woman with white streak on nail ([Skin Appendage Disord 2017;2:89](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28232912))
* 72 year old man with yellow longitudinal streak on left fourth toenail ([Actas Dermosifiliogr 2024;115:1067](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38307171))

Treatment

* Watchful waiting with transverse nail clipping for histology and surgical excision

Microscopic (histologic) description

* On microscopic examination, onychopapilloma is characterized by
  + Papillomatous proliferation of epithelial cells (82.4% of cases), ovoid bland nuclei
  + Acanthosis (82.4%) which is partially due to the increased thickness of stratum spinosum
  + Matrix metaplasia of nail bed epithelium (41.2%)
  + Hyperkeratosis (29.4%)
  + Distal premature keratinization (70.6%)
  + Dilated vessels resulting in splinter hemorrhage (23.5%) ([J Cutan Pathol 2022;49:147](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34396563))

Sample pathology report

* Skin, biopsy:
  + Onychopapilloma (see comment)
  + Comment: The epithelial cells are arranged in a papillomatous pattern. The stratum corneum is hyperkeratotic and parakeratotic. These findings are consistent with onychopapilloma.

Differential diagnosis

* [Glomus tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueglomus.html):
  + Usually occurs at subungual sites
  + Characterized by a mix of glomus cells, vasculature and smooth muscle cells
* [Darier disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumordariersdisease.html):
  + Usually affects multiple nails
  + Characterized by parakeratosis and variable epidermal thickness
  + Acantholysis with characteristic dyskeratosis forming corps ronds and grains
* [Lichen planus](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorlichenplanus.html):
  + Classic lichenoid dermatitis
  + Characterized by a band of lymphohistiocytic infiltrate obscuring the dermoepidermal junction
  + Lichen planus affects multiple nails where onychopapilloma occurs as solitary lesion
* **Traumatic splinter hemorrhages**:
  + Clinically resembles erythronychia; both appear as red linear streaks under the nail plate
  + Splinter hemorrhage appears as extravasated blood within the longitudinal vessels of nail bed
* [Melanoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticmelanoma.html):
  + Onychopapilloma with longitudinal melanonychia simulates melanoma
  + Melanoma appears irregular with uneven color and spreads to surrounding tissue
  + Morphologically characterized by pleomorphic melanocytes with irregular nuclei
* **Malignant onychopapilloma**:
  + Malignant onychopapilloma reveals large irregular abnormal onchocytes
  + Nail bed epithelium shows a high [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) proliferation index

A 17 year old boy presents with a lesion on his left toenail. Microscopic examination revealed papillomatous proliferation. Which of the following is true regarding this lesion?

1. Etiology is unknown
2. More common in men
3. Most common feature is splinter hemorrhage
4. Ring finger is the most common site

Board review style answer #1

**A**. Etiology is unknown. The lesion is onychopapilloma. Answer D is incorrect because the most common site of onychopapilloma is the thumb. Answer C is incorrect because the most common feature is subungual hyperkeratosis. Answer B is incorrect because onychopapilloma is common in both men and women.

Board review style answer #2

**D**. Treatment consists of watchful waiting with transverse nail clipping for histology and surgical excision. The lesion is onychopapilloma.
