Definition / general

* Low grade B cell lymphoma arising in skin composed of follicular center B cells without evidence of systemic / nodal involvement at the time of diagnosis

Essential features

* Mainly centrocytes; predominantly large centrocytes
* Follicular, diffuse or follicular and diffuse growth pattern
* Presence of monoclonal B cells in the right clinical and pathologic context can support a diagnosis of primary cutaneous follicle center lymphoma (PCFCL)
* Diffuse growth of centroblasts and immunoblasts excludes this diagnosis
* Clinically and genetically distinct from nodal follicular lymphoma

Terminology

* Obsolete names include reticulohistiocytoma of the dorsum and Crosti lymphoma ([J Am Acad Dermatol 1988;19:259](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3049688))

ICD coding

* ICD-O:  [9597/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=93) - primary cutaneous follicle centre lymphoma
* ICD-10:  [C86.6](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C81-C96/C86-/C86.6) - primary cutaneous CD30 positive T cell proliferations

Epidemiology

* ~10% of all primary cutaneous lymphomas
* Most common (~50%) type of primary cutaneous B cell lymphomas
* Middle aged adults (i.e. 50 - 60 years)
* M:F = ~1.5:1
* Reference: [Blood 2005;105:3768](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15692063)

Sites

* Mainly head or trunk ([Int J Radiat Oncol Biol Phys 2013;87:719](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24001373), [J Clin Oncol 2007;25:1581](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17353548))

Pathophysiology

* No definitive risk factors identified and no identified hereditary predisposition ([StatPearls: Primary Cutaneous Follicle Center Lymphoma [Accessed 7 June 2021]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33620820))

Etiology

* Low grade lymphoma of follicle center B cells
* No systemic or nodal involvement at diagnosis
* Reference: [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2018;142:1313](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30407851)

Clinical features

* Single, raised nodule or plaque
  + Satellite lesions in a localized area are common
  + ~15% of patients have multifocal lesions
* Commonly scalp, forehead or trunk
  + ~5% of patients present with lesions on the leg
* Intact epidermis
* Mildly erythematous
* References: [J Clin Oncol 1999;17:2471](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10561311), [StatPearls: Primary Cutaneous Follicle Center Lymphoma [Accessed 7 June 2021]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33620820), [Blood 2000;95:3922](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10845929)

Diagnosis

* Diagnosis made by:
  + Histology and immunohistochemical evaluation of a skin biopsy
    - Lymphoma involving the dermis, with follicular to diffuse growth pattern
    - Mixture of centrocytes and centroblasts
* Diagnosis is supported by:
  + Flow cytometry immunophenotyping, if performed
  + Immunoglobulin heavy / light chain gene rearrangement studies, if performed
* Reference: [StatPearls: Primary Cutaneous Follicle Center Lymphoma [Accessed 7 June 2021]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33620820)

Radiology description

* PET / CT: negative for lymphadenopathy
* Dissemination to extracutaneous sites is uncommon (J Clin Oncol 2006;24:1376)

Prognostic factors

* Excellent prognosis
* 5 year survival rate of > 95%
* Cutaneous relapses after initial treatment: ~30% of patients
* If left untreated, the skin lesions gradually increase in size over years but dissemination to extracutaneous sites is uncommon ( Prognosis is good regardless of:
  + Growth pattern (follicular or diffuse)
  + Number of centroblasts / immunoblasts
  + Presence or absence of t(14;18) and BCL2 expression
  + Presence of either localized or multifocal skin disease
  + Cutaneous relapse ([J Clin Oncol 2007;25:1581](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17353548), [Blood 2008;11:1600](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18567836), [Int J Radiat Oncol Biol Phys 2015;92:32](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25863751))
* Additional references: [J Clin Oncol 2006;24:1376](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16492713), [J Clin Oncol 2007;25:1581](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17353548)

Case reports

* 16 year old girl with primary cutaneous follicle center lymphoma ([J Cutan Pathol 2021;48:663](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33326136))
* 40 year old man who presented with a scalp mass ([Am J Case Rep 2019;20:1273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31462626))
* 77 year old woman favored to represent primary cutaneous marginal zone lymphoma on an initial punch biopsy and PCFCL with extensive plasmacytic differentiation ([J Cutan Pathol 2021 Mar 31 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33786907))

Treatment

* Single cutaneous lesion
  + Local therapy with:
    - Either surgical excision or local radiation
    - In case of cutaneous relapse: radiotherapy
  + Generalized skin lesions:
    - Systemic intralesional administration of rituximab or systemic rituximab
* Reference: [Blood 2008;11:1600](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18567836)

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Infiltrate is based in the superficial dermis:
  + Lymphoma may involve the entire dermis
  + Often follicles of the tumor extending into the subcutis
  + Perivascular, periadnexal, nodular and diffuse infiltrates
  + Follicles are ill defined; lack tingible body macrophages and mantle zone
* Intact grenz zone
* Growth pattern varies:
  + Follicular (mainly head and neck)
  + Follicular and diffuse
  + Purely diffuse (primarily trunk)
* Mixture of centrocytes and centroblasts
* Large cells are variable and can be polylobated
* Predominance of large centrocytes (large cleaved cells), may be spindle shaped
* Variable numbers of centroblasts
* T cells are abundant
* No WHO grading of lymphoma required as in nodal / systemic follicular lymphoma
* Reference: [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2018;142:1313](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30407851)

Cytology description

* Cells resembling centrocytes and centroblasts ([Jaffe: Hematopathology, 2nd Edition, 2016](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0323296130/pathologyoutl-20))
* Large centrocytes: dispersed chromatin and small nucleoli
* Large noncleaved cells: prominent and paracentral nucleoli

Positive stains

* B cell markers ([CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html), [CD19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd19.html), [CD22](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20to29.html), [CD79a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd79a.html) or [PAX5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax5.html))
* Germinal center markers: [BCL6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl6.html), [LMO2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainslmo2.html), [HGAL](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsHGAL.html), **MEF2B**

Negative stains

* [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) is positive in
* Positive in follicular pattern and usually negative in diffuse pattern ([J Clin Oncol 2007;25:1581](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17353548))

- [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html) (usually negative but may show faint staining in a minority of neoplastic cells) ([J Clin Oncol 2007;25:1581](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17353548))
  * Expression of [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html) in > 50% of the neoplastic B cells: 11% of cases
  * Combination of positive staining for both [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html) and [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) highly suggests secondary skin involvement by follicular lymphoma ([Mod Pathol 2020;33:96](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31653979))
- [CD5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd5.html) and T cell markers
- [IRF4 / MUM1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmum1.html)
- **FOXP1**
- [CD43](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd43.html)

Flow cytometry description

* Restricted light chain expression in ~75% of cases ([Am J Dermatopathol 2014;36:781](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24335516))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Similar gene expression profile as germinal center B cell subtype DLBCL
* Clonally rearranged immunoglobulin heavy and light chain genes
* *BCL2* rearrangement or t(14;18): generally absent but can be positive in 10 - 40% of cases ([Blood Adv 2021;5:649](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33560380), [Hum Pathol 2020;106:93](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33045225))
* A subset of PCFCLs have 1p36 deletion
* Amplification of *REL* is often seen
* Deletion of chromosome 14q32.33 is rare
* References: [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2018;142:1313](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30407851), [J Clin Oncol 2006;24:296](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16330669)

Sample pathology report

* Skin, left cheek, biopsy
  + Low grade follicular lymphoma involving skin (see comment)
  + Comment: Histologic sections demonstrate skin involved by lymphoma. The lymphoma has a vague nodular pattern and is composed of a mixture of large noncleaved and small cleaved lymphoid cells with predominance of small lymphoid cells. The epidermis is unremarkable. No epidermotropism noted. Plasma cells are not increased. Immunohistochemical studies performed demonstrate that the neoplastic cells are positive for CD10, CD20, PAX5, BCL6 and are negative for BCL2, cyclin D1 and MUM1. CD3 and CD5 highlight small reactive lymphocytes in the background. Ki67 demonstrates a proliferation index of ~20%.
  + In the appropriate clinical setting, this neoplasm involving the skin is consistent with primary cutaneous follicle center cell lymphoma. Clinical correlation and staging studies may help to confirm this diagnosis. If the neoplasm is systemic, this follicular lymphoma can be classified as low grade.

Differential diagnosis

* [Reactive follicular hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodesfollicularhyperplasia.html):
  + Follicles are well defined
  + Polymorphous infiltrate
  + Presence of tingible body macrophages
  + Intact mantle zone
  + Polyclonal with molecular studies and flow cytometry
  + Spontaneous resolution
* Secondary involvement by [systemic / nodal follicular lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomafollicularnodalusual.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26658664)):
  + Clinical features: lymphadenopathy or B symptoms present
  + Strong [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) and [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html) expression
  + t(14;18) identified in most follicular lymphoma (> 75% of cases)
  + Rearrangements of [BCL6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl6.html): more common in the systemic type of follicular lymphoma than PCFCL
  + Common mutations in ≥ 1 of chromatin modifying genes including *CREBBP, KMT2D, EZH2, EP300* and *TNFRSF14* (immune modulation) genes
  + Recent study identified 3 criteria (i.e., presence of *BCL2* rearrangement, mutations in ≥ 2 of the chromatin modifying genes [*CREBBP, KMT2D, EZH2* and *EP300*] and proliferation index [Ki67 Blood Adv 2021;5:649)
* [Primary cutaneous marginal zone lymphoma (PCMZL)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticMALT.html):
  + With or without monocytoid lymphocytes in histopathology
  + With or without Dutcher bodies
  + Inside out appearance of follicles
  + Germinal center cells are at the periphery of a B cell aggregate
  + [BCL6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl6.html) and [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) negative, [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html) positive
  + [CD43](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd43.html)+
  + Monotypic light chain expression (especially in plasma cells)
  + Mutations: *FAS* (63%), *SLAMF1* (24%), *SPEN* (18%) and *NCOR2* genes (13%)
* [Primary cutaneous diffuse large B cell lymphoma, leg type](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticdiffuselargeBcellleg.html):
  + Most frequently located on lower extremities
  + Sheets of the large cell (centroblasts and immunoblasts)
  + Positive for [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html), [MUM1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmum1.html), **IgM** and **FOXP1**
  + *MYD88* mutations (~70%)
  + Amplification of [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html)
  + Rearrangements in *MYC* (30%) and *BCL6* (rare cases)
  + Loss in 9p21.3 (*CDKN2A* gene) in a large subset of cases (66% of cases)
* References: [J Clin Oncol 2007;25:1581](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17353548), [Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:1043](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20551823), [J Invest Dermatol 2017;137:2450](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28684330), [J Clin Oncol 2006;24:296](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16330669), [Blood 2019;133:1703](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30635287)
