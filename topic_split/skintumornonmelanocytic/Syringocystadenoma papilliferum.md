Definition / general

* Benign, hamartomatous, adnexal tumor ([J Clin Diagn Res 2014;8:QD03](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24995228))
* Originates from the apocrine or the eccrine sweat glands ([J Clin Diagn Res 2013;7:742](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23730665))

Essential features

* Benign adnexal tumor, most commonly occurring in the head and neck in early childhood ([J Dermatol 2004;31:939](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15729871))
* Most are solitary papules but variable clinical presentation
* Macroscopic: pink, hairless plaque or nodule
* Microscopic: cystic invaginations of the infundibular epithelium extending into the dermis with a double cell layer of inner columnar and outer cuboidal cells

Terminology

* Syringocystadenoma papilliferum (SCAP), also known as papillary syringadenoma ([J Clin Diagn Res 2014;8:QD03](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24995228))

Epidemiology

* M = F ([Indian J Dermatol Venereol Leprol 2009;75:170](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19293506))
* 50% of cases present at birth or in early childhood ([Onco Targets Ther 2013;6:233](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23569386))
* 15 - 30% of cases develop during puberty ([Onco Targets Ther 2013;6:233](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23569386))

Sites

* 75% of cases are reported in the head and neck region ([Indian J Dermatol Venereol Leprol 2009;75:170](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19293506))
* Unusual locations: buttock, vulva and scrotum, pinna, eyelid, outer ear canal, postoperative scar, scalp, nipple, thigh, axilla, back and right lower abdomen (3 cases) ([J Clin Diagn Res 2014;8:QD03](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24995228))

Pathophysiology

* Benign hamartomatous adnexal tumor ([J Clin Diagn Res 2014;8:QD03](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24995228))
* Origin is uncertain:
  + Derived from apocrine glands, eccrine glands or both:
    - Immunohistochemical studies support an apocrine origin ([J Clin Diagn Res 2014;8:QD03](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24995228))
    - Ultrastructural analysis favors an eccrine derivation ([J Clin Diagn Res 2014;8:QD03](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24995228))
    - May arise from pluripotent stem cells ([J Clin Diagn Res 2014;8:QD03](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24995228))
* During puberty, may increase in size and develop a crusted and papillomatous texture ([Br J Dermatol 2000;142:543](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10735969))
* Although benign, co-occurrence of basal cell carcinoma, verrucous carcinoma metastatic adenocarcinoma and ductal carcinoma may be observed ([Onco Targets Ther 2013;6:233](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23569386), [OrphaNet: Syringocystadenoma Papilliferum [Accessed 5 November 2021]](https://www.orpha.net/data/patho/GB/uk-Syringocystadenoma-papilliferum.pdf))

Etiology

* Often observed in association with other benign adnexal neoplasms, such as nevus sebaceus (8 - 19% of patients), apocrine nevus, tubular apocrine adenoma, apocrine hidrocystoma, apocrine cystadenoma and clear cell syringoma ([Pathologica 2006;98:178](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17036946))

Clinical features

* Measures between 1 - 3 cm and < 4 cm in diameter ([J Clin Diagn Res 2013;7:742](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23730665))
* Variable clinical presentation:
  + Solitary papule
  + Linear arrangement of several papules ([Onco Targets Ther 2013;6:233](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23569386))
* Most patients present with solitary lesions in the head and neck region at birth or in early childhood ([J Clin Diagn Res 2014;8:QD03](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24995228))
* Variable presentations:
  + Warty plaques on the head and neck ([J Clin Diagn Res 2014;8:QD03](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24995228))
  + Papule or a smooth hairless plaque on the scalp and forehead ([Indian J Dermatol Venereol Leprol 2009;75:170](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19293506))
  + Often in the setting of nevus sebaceus ([J Clin Diagn Res 2014;8:QD03](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24995228))
  + Nodular or verrucous transformation is noted at puberty ([Indian J Dermatol Venereol Leprol 2009;75:170](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19293506))
* Multiple lesions are rarely seen and those that arise outside the head and neck region are even more uncommon ([J Clin Diagn Res 2014;8:QD03](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24995228))
* With increasing size:
  + More prominent papillary configuration develops ([Onco Targets Ther 2013;6:233](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23569386))
  + Surface can develop hemorrhagic crust ([Onco Targets Ther 2013;6:233](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23569386))

Diagnosis

* Skin biopsy

Case reports

* 6 year old boy with progressive growth lesion on left flank ([An Bras Dermatol 2020;95:112](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31980353))
* 25 year old man with a 10 year history of an asymptomatic, slowly growing skin lesion on face ([Case Rep Dermatol 2019;11:36](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32355486))
* 45 year old woman with a right supraclavicular lesion since childhood ([J Nippon Med Sch 2017;84:79](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28502963))
* 56 year old woman with a swelling lesion on right temporal scalp ([Malays J Pathol 2019;41:47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31025637))
* 64 year old man with a growing mass on left occipital scalp ([Case Rep Pathol 2019;2019:1783758](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31772805))

Treatment

* Surgical excision ([J Cutan Aesthet Surg 2016;9:204](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27761094))

Gross description

* Macroscopic:
  + Skin colored to pink, hairless, firm plaque of grouped nodules or as a solitary nodule
    - Verrucous, papillary, hyperkeratotic, moist or fleshy excrescences have also been described ([Onco Targets Ther 2013;6:233](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23569386))
  + Some tumors may show central umbilication through which small fistulae may discharge fluid ([Onco Targets Ther 2013;6:233](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23569386))
  + Mature lesions:
    - Clusters of pinkish brown nodules ([Onco Targets Ther 2013;6:233](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23569386))
    - 2 - 10 mm in diameter ([Onco Targets Ther 2013;6:233](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23569386))
    - Occasional central opening ([Onco Targets Ther 2013;6:233](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23569386))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Cystic invaginations of the infundibular epithelium projecting into the dermis, covered by a double cell layer ([An Bras Dermatol 2017;92:721](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29166517), [J Clin Diagn Res 2014;8:QD03](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24995228)):
  + Innermost layer is composed of columnar cells with decapitation secretion
  + Outermost layer is composed of cuboidal cells with papillary projections
* Verrucous (papillomatous) epidermal hyperplasia with hyperkeratosis and hypergranulosis ([Pathologica 2006;98:178](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17036946))
* Exoendophytic configuration with a gradual transition from stratified squamous epithelium at the epidermal surface to a bilayered ductal epithelium ([Pathologica 2006;98:178](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17036946))
* Papillary fronds extend upward from the base and plasma cells are common in the stroma of each frond ([Pathologica 2006;98:178](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17036946))
* Many irregular duct-like structures and cystic spaces ([Pathologica 2006;98:178](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17036946))
* Ducts, containing papillary processes and lined by 2 epithelial cell layers, connect to the surface ([Indian J Dermatol Venereol Leprol 2009;75:170](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19293506))
* Exoendophytic architecture, verrucous epidermal hyperplasia, sometimes pseudoepitheliomatous hyperplasia and connection of glands to epidermis ([An Bras Dermatol 2017;92:721](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29166517))
* Background features of nevus sebaceus may be observed ([An Bras Dermatol 2017;92:721](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29166517))

Positive stains

* IHC may help in favoring eccrine or apocrine lineage but is not required for diagnosis ([Indian J Dermatol 2013;58:409](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24082219))
* Positive stains:
  + [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html), [AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html) and [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) highlight ductal epithelium ([Indian J Dermatol 2013;58:409](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24082219))
* Luminal columnar cells:
  + Positive for [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html) ([Br J Dermatol 2002;147:936](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12410704))
  + > 70% positive for [CK19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck19.html) ([Br J Dermatol 2002;147:936](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12410704))
  + [CK1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck1.html), [CK5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5and6.html), [CK8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck8.html), [CK10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck10.html), [CK14](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck14.html): heterogeneous expression ([Br J Dermatol 2002;147:936](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12410704))
* Basal cuboidal cells:
  + Almost constantly express [CK1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck1.html), [CK5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5and6.html), [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [CK8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck8.html), [CK10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck10.html), [CK14](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck14.html) ([Br J Dermatol 2002;147:936](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12410704))
  + Basal tumor cells express [CK19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck19.html) and [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html) heterogeneously and [alpha smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) focally ([Br J Dermatol 2002;147:936](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12410704))
  + Positive for [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [CK5/6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5and6.html) and [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html), consistent with myoepithelial differentiation

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Some cases have mutations in:
  + Patched gene (*PTCH*) ([Indian J Dermatol 2013;58:409](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24082219))
  + *p16* tumor suppressor gene ([Indian J Dermatol 2013;58:409](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24082219))

Sample pathology report

* Scalp, biopsy:
  + Syringocystadenoma papilliferum (see comment)
  + Comment: Several cystic invaginations arise from a papillomatous epidermis. These invaginations demonstrate papillae lined by 2 rows of cuboidal to columnar epithelial cells, with oval nuclei and a pale eosinophilic cytoplasm. The deep dermis contains tubular glands with apocrine decapitation secretion. The stroma contains a dense mononuclear infiltrate, which is comprised predominantly of plasma cells.

Differential diagnosis

* [Hidradenoma papilliferum](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocytichidradenomapap.html):
  + Almost exclusively occurs in the anogenital region of women; diagnosis outside of this site should be exceptional ([J Clin Diagn Res 2014;8:QD03](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24995228))
  + Unlike syringocystadenoma papilliferum, there is no epidermal connection (it hides in the dermis) ([J Clin Diagn Res 2014;8:QD03](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24995228))
  + Dermal nodule that consists of arborizing ducts that produce maze-like patterns ([J Clin Diagn Res 2014;8:QD03](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24995228))
* [Papillary eccrine adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticpapillaryeccrineadenoma.html):
  + Composed of multiple, dermal, dilated, duct-like spaces containing papillary projections ([J Clin Diagn Res 2014;8:QD03](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24995228))
  + No exoendophytic architecture, glands appear cystic, no epidermal connection and lacks plasmacellular stroma
* [Warty dyskeratoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticwartydyskeratoma.html):
  + Exoendophytic architecture and verrucous epidermal hyperplasia but lacks ductal epithelium
  + Lined by elongated dermal papillae with suprabasilar acantholysis of keratinocytes, some of which are dyskeratotic ([J Clin Diagn Res 2014;8:QD03](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24995228))
* [Tubular apocrine adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticapocrinetubularadenoma.html):
  + Predominantly cuboidal and columnar cells with or without secretions ([J Cytol 2015;32:130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26229253))
  + No epidermal connection
  + Glands contain papillary projections and form cysts
* **Syringocystadenocarcinoma papilliferum** ([J Korean Med Sci 2007;22:762](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17728526)):
  + Asymmetric and poorly circumscribed
  + Extends into the deep dermis
  + Marked cytologic atypia and mitotic activity
  + Positive reaction to [GCDFP-15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgcdfp15.html) but negative reaction to [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html) and **HMFG1**
* [Nipple adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastnipplead.html) (and variant [erosive adenomatosis of the nipple](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastnipplead.html)):
  + Anatomic site permits differentiation
