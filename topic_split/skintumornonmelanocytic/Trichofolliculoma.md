Definition / general

* Benign adnexal hamartomatous follicular tumor
* Histologically shows multiple follicles in various stages spreading from a central cystic follicle

Essential features

* Solitary papule in adults in the head and neck region
* Central dilated primary follicle with secondary follicles budding from the primary follicle
* Can show a spectrum of morphology depending on the hair follicle cycle

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [839 - 842](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf#page=72) - adnexal and skin appendage neoplasms
* ICD-10: [D23.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D23-/D23.9) - other benign neoplasm of skin, unspecified

Epidemiology

* Mostly solitary papule or nodule
* Occurs in adulthood with no definitive racial or gender predilection ([An Bras Dermatol 2015;90:519](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26375221))
* Rarely occurs as a congenital lesion ([Int J Mol Sci 2021;22:4759](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33946233))
* No proven association with any dermatological or systemic diseases ([Am J Pathol 1976;85:479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/793411))

Sites

* Face, with nose as the most common site ([Am J Dermatopathol 2010;32:35](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19730084))

Pathophysiology

* Repeated development of hair follicles with disordered hair cycle; defective sonic hedgehog polarization ([J Dermatol 2017;44:1050](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28370423), [Am J Dermatopathol 2009;31:248](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19384065))
* Distorted ability to control the size of hair follicles
* Trichofolliculoma with sebaceous differentiation: follicular and sebaceous components have independent cycles
* Primary follicle: ([J Dermatol 2017;44:1050](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28370423))
  + Primary infundibular cystic structure which shows a thin wall with radiating secondary follicles
* Secondary follicles:
  + Follicles radiating from the primary follicle
  + Most follicles are in anagen phase
* Tertiary follicles:
  + Regression of secondary follicles to tertiary follicles
  + Shift of anagen hair to catagen phase
  + Variation in size of hair from vellus hair to thick terminal hair
* Quaternary follicles:
  + Regression of tertiary follicles to quaternary follicles

Clinical features

* Solitary, skin colored papule or nodule (approximately 0.2 - 1.5 cm in diameter) with central depression ([An Bras Dermatol 2015;90:780](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26734858), [Arch Dermatol 1960;81:922](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14409842))
* Multiple tufts of vellus and thin hairs emerging from the central section

Diagnosis

* Clinical: central primary follicle with multiple tufts of vellus hair ([Am J Pathol 1976;85:479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/793411))
* If the hair is plucked, trichofolliculoma can be clinically misdiagnosed as basal cell carcinoma, molluscum contagiosum, keratoacanthoma, milium, trichoepithelioma, syringoma or sebaceous hyperplasia ([J Eur Acad Dermatol Venereol 2017;31:e123](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27504964))
* Dermoscopy: shows troll hair sign - tight plumes of white and thin hairs, similar to children's troll dolls ([Australas J Dermatol 2021;62:90](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32914875))
* Biopsy and histological examination

Prognostic factors

* Excellent prognosis ([Dermatol Online J 2013;19:19264](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24021443))
* Rarely recurs at the primary site ([Arch Dermatol 1979;115:1003](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/464613))
* Rarely coexists with basal cell carcinoma ([Australas J Dermatol 2007;48:127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17535204))
* Malignant transformation with perineural invasion has been reported in a single case report ([Arch Dermatol 1979;115:1003](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/464613))

Case reports

* 15 year old girl with a hairy papule on nose since birth ([Dermatol Online J 2020;26:13030](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32898407))
* 21 year old woman with multiple skin colored lesions on face ([Indian J Dermatol 2015;60:214](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25814743))
* 52 year old woman with a collision tumor of trichofolliculoma and basal cell carcinoma ([Australas J Dermatol 2007;48:127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17535204))
* 54 year old woman with multiple soft lesions in zosteriform distribution ([Indian J Dermatol 2013;58:330](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23919042))
* 57 year old man with a tender nodule on canthus ([Arch Dermatol 1979;115:1003](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/464613))

Treatment

* Typically no need for treatment but rarely excision is recommended ([Int J Mol Sci 2021;22:4759](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33946233))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Dilated central cystic follicle with surrounding multiple fully formed vellus or terminal follicles
* The central cystic follicle shows connection / opening to epidermis
* Early lesion: a mildly dilated infundibulum and radiating secondary curved vellus follicles; cystic dilatation may be absent
* Late lesion: thin walled primary infundibular cystic structure and radiating vellus or terminal follicles that are mostly in the anagen phase
* Trichofolliculoma demonstrates outer root sheath differentiation
* Central follicle shows stratified squamous cell epithelium with a granular layer with dilation or cystic changes and contains keratinous material and may have vellus hairs
* Primary follicle has keratinized stratified epithelium with keratohyaline granules
* Branched follicles may show varying degree of maturation, including rudimentary structures or epithelial cords and anagen, catagen or telogen hair in older lesions
* Secondary follicles are small with many epithelial strands and abortive pilar formation
* Sebaceous differentiation may be present
* Trichofolliculoma is usually surrounded by well developed connective tissue, which is frequently cellular
* Each follicle is surrounded by an individual perifollicular sheath
* Late stage can show a solid pattern as the regressing secondary follicles and developing tertiary follicles coalesce
* Sebaceous trichofolliculoma variant: sebaceous gland attached to radiating follicles ([J Cutan Pathol 1980;7:394](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7451702))

Positive stains

* Immunohistochemistry is not usually needed for diagnosis
* [CK15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck1.html) in the basal cells of the secondary follicles ([Br J Dermatol 2003;148:597](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12653763))
* [CK16](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck1.html) and [CK17](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck17.html) in the suprabasal cells of the immature secondary hair follicles ([Br J Dermatol 2003;148:597](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12653763))
* [BerEP4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsepcam.html) expression in basaloid germ-like structures but weak staining in the secondary or tertiary follicles compared to the normal hair ([Am J Dermatopathol 2010;32:35](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19730084))
* [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) in basal cells of the outer root sheath in the thick terminal follicles ([Am J Dermatopathol 2010;32:35](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19730084))

Sample pathology report

* Forehead skin, shave biopsy:
  + Trichofolliculoma (see comment)
  + Comment: Atypia is not identified.
  + Microscopic description: Sections demonstrate a centrally dilated follicular unit with multiple radiating follicular units. At the center of the cystic structure there is keratotic debris and immature hair shafts (trichoids). Surrounding the structure is a mantle of well organized connective tissue. Atypical features were not noted.

Differential diagnosis

* **Trichoadenoma**:
  + Multiple multilayered squamous epithelial islands with a central cystic cavity containing keratinous material
  + Does not contain hair shafts
* [Solitary trichoepithelioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocytictrichoepithelioma.html):
  + Islands of basaloid cells are present, which is not a feature of trichofolliculoma
* [Fibrofolliculoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticfolliculofibroma.html):
  + Both show a large central follicle with multiple arising epithelial attachments
  + Fibrofolliculoma only has strands of follicular epithelium and does not contain any hair shafts
* [Dilated pore of Winer](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticdilatedporewiner.html):
  + Widened follicular infundibulum
  + Unlike trichofolliculoma, it does not show the radiating secondary follicles
* [Hair follicle nevus](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocytichairfolliclenevus.html):
  + Both lesions show multiple hair follicles with vellus hairs but trichofolliculoma has a central cystic component
* [Follicular infundibulum cyst](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocytickeratinouscystepidermal.html):
  + Does not contain the secondary or tertiary follicular structures
* **Pilar sheath acanthoma**:
  + Less florid pattern than trichofolliculoma
  + Dilated central follicle with cystic features and radiating acanthotic epithelium
  + Unlike trichofolliculoma, pilar sheath acanthoma does not have hair shafts in the peripheral buds
* **Folliculosebaceous cystic hamartoma**:
  + Folliculosebaceous structures with surrounding stroma with various mesenchymal elements
  + Sebaceous component is the more prominent component
  + Although it has been suggested that this is a late stage of sebaceous trichofolliculoma, reports of congenital folliculosebaceous cystic hamartoma suggest that this idea is incorrect ([Am J Dermatopathol 2008;30:500](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18806500), [J Cutan Pathol 2008;35:843](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18494824))
