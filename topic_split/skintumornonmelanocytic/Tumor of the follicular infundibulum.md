Definition / general

* Rare, benign neoplasm, first described in 1961 as a plate-like subepidermal epithelial tumor with a fenestrated growth pattern, cells with light staining nuclei and vesiculated cytoplasm ([Arch Dermatol 1961;83:924](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/13769000))

Essential features

* Horizontal plate-like organization of epithelial cells with abundant cytoplasm under the epidermis between adjacent infundibulum
* Dense elastic network beneath the tumor

Terminology

* Tumor of the follicular infundibulum (TFI)
* Infundibular tumor
* Tumor of the follicular isthmus
* Infundibuloma

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [D23.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D23-/D23.9) - other benign neoplasm of skin, unspecified

Epidemiology

* 3 - 17/100,000 skin biopsies
* 60 - 70 year old adults
* Associated with Cowden syndrome ([J Am Acad Dermatol 1995;33:979](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7490369))
* Associated with Schöpf-Schulz-Passarge syndrome ([Dermatology 1998;196:463](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9669133))
* Associated with basal cell carcinoma (BCC), squamous cell carcinoma (SCC), nevi, acne scars, actinic keratosis, Hansen disease, vitiligo, discoid lupus erythematosus and lichen planus ([J Cutan Pathol 2013;40:532](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23531053))
* There is a slight female predominance but some report equal distribution ([Case Rep Pathol 2019;2019:4606493](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30809409), [J Cutan Pathol 2013;40:532](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23531053), [Int J Dermatol 1996;35:282](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8786188))

Sites

* Head, neck, trunk
* Other unusual reported areas include the vulva, other genital areas and extremities ([Case Rep Pathol 2019;2019:4606493](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30809409), [An Bras Dermatol 2014;89:964](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25387502), [J Cutan Pathol 2013;40:532](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23531053))

Pathophysiology

* Adnexal follicular proliferation with isthmic differentiation of the follicular epidermis
* Multiple infundibulomas accounts for ~7% of cases ([J Cutan Pathol 2013;40:532](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23531053))
* Currently debated among researchers; there are few case reports of tumor of the follicular infundibulum with sebaceous differentiation ([Ann Dermatol 2011;23:198](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21747619))

Etiology

* Etiology is not fully understood at this time
* Predominantly isthmic differentiation but sebaceous differentiation has been described ([J Cutan Pathol 2001;28:314](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11401679), [Ann Dermatol 2011;23:198](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21747619))
* Part of a nevus sebaceous ([Korean J Pathol 2013;47:569](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24421851), [J Cutan Pathol 2015;42:645](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25761672))
* Epidermal reaction to dermal fibrosis ([Am J Dermatopathol 2015;37:535](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25051108))
* Spectrum of basal cell carcinoma ([Am J Dermatopathol 2009;31:634](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19652582))

Clinical features

* Variable clinical presentation, although typically presents as hypopigmented scar-like macules or flat papule on the head, neck or trunk
* Can present as a solitary papule / plaque or multiple eruptive macules / papules, both hyper and hypopigmented lesions have been described

Diagnosis

* Typically diagnosed on biopsy given their variable clinical presentation and overlapping features with other entities including basal cell carcinoma, benign lichenoid keratosis and vitiligo
* Only histology with critical features can make the diagnosis ([Int J Dermatol 1996;35:282](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8786188))
* Critical biopsy features include
  + Plate-like horizontal growth
  + Interconnected with elastin fibers
  + Pale abundant cytoplasm without cytologic atypia

Prognostic factors

* Benign
* Tumors of the follicular infundibulum or infundibulomas can arise in conjunction with other lesions and although not fully elucidated, have been reported to be associated with many entities across the spectrum from benign to malignant lesions including: basal cell carcinoma, actinic keratosis (AK), desmoplastic malignant melanoma, junctional melanocytic nevus, epidermal inclusion cyst, among many others; extensively documented by Dempsey et al. ([JAAD Case Rep 2018;5:15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30547076))

Case reports

* 53 year old man and 54 year old woman with TFIs that presented as flat erythematous macules, both with chrysalis on dermoscopy ([Skin Res Technol 2019;25:761](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31102284))
* 69 year old man with atypical brown macule and an associated dysplastic nevus biopsied to rule out melanoma ([JAAD Case Rep 2018;5:15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30547076))
* 70 year old woman with a vulvar ulcerated lesion with lichenoid change ([Case Rep Pathol 2019;2019:4606493](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30809409))
* 28 patients with TFI features ([J Dtsch Dermatol Ges 2024;22:223](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38332423))

Treatment

* Surgical excision typically curative
* Topical therapies including keratolytics and corticosteroids have been reported but were ineffective ([Dermatol Online J 2010;16:14](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20137756))
* Single case report of patient on etretinate showing slight improvement ([Ann Dermatol Venereol 1987;114:55](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3619301))
* Cryotherapy has shown some success ([Int J Dermatol 1996;35:282](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8786188))
* Long term follow up may be recommended due to (rare) potential for malignant transformation ([JAAD Case Rep 2018;4:330](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29693061))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Plate-like superficial, horizontal proliferation of pale isthmic cells with eosinophilic cytoplasm, emanating from the epidermis
  + Grow as strands in a fenestrated pattern
  + Base can have basophilic palisading cells, with scant and fibrotic stroma
  + No atypia
* Plate-like proliferation of pale staining epithelial cells
* Large, bland appearing, abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm
* Differentiation: isthmus, sebaceous

Positive stains

* Staining is typically performed to rule out malignancy with diagnostic emphasis placed on the lack of staining for markers indicative of malignant processes
* Single cell positivity for [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html), whereas basal cell carcinoma is [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html) negative ([J Cutan Pathol 2013;40:532](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23531053))
* [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html) positive ([An Bras Dermatol 2014;89:964](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25387502))

Negative stains

* [BerEP4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsepcam.html), [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html), [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html) ([J Cutan Pathol 2013;40:532](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23531053))

Sample pathology report

* Skin, right upper cutaneous lip, shave biopsy:
  + Tumor of follicular infundibulum

* Skin, nose, shave biopsy:
  + Tumor of follicular infundibulum (see comment)
  + Comment: Tumor of the follicular infundibulum is favored, especially in superficial biopsy where we cannot see the full lesion. These features somewhat resemble the superficial portion of a tumor of follicular infundibulum. BerEP4 stain is negative. As the lesion extends to the deep margins and little dermis is seen, clinical follow up is recommended, with additional sampling if the lesion persists or recurs.

* Skin, right posterior shoulder, punch biopsy:
  + Benign adnexal neoplasm / benign follicular neoplasm (see comment)
  + Comment: The overall architecture of this tumor is most typical of tumor of follicular infundibulum, although there are also some focal features of trichilemmoma. Clinical correlation and follow up are recommended.

Differential diagnosis

* [Superficial basal cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticbcc.html):
  + Often exhibits multicentric pattern characterized by nests of basaloid cells within the superficial dermis
  + These nests typically lack pale cells, show frequent cytologic atypia and are associated with clefting between tumor islands and a mucinous stromal background
  + [BerEP4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsepcam.html) and [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html) positive
* [Trichilemmoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocytictrichilemmoma.html):
  + Lobular folliculocentric pattern
  + Hyaline basement membrane
* **Epidermal panfolliculoma**:
  + Typically pale cells with plaques limited to focal area

Board review style question #1

A 54 year old woman presents with a well circumscribed, skin colored plaque on her cheek that has been present for years. She denies spontaneous bleeding from the lesion but she reports a personal history of skin cancer that was removed. A 6 mm punch biopsy was performed. Histopathology reveals interconnected cords of pale staining keratinocytes extending from the epidermis. There is no evidence of acanthosis, peripheral palisading or significant cytologic atypia. Which of the following is the most likely diagnosis?

1. Basal cell carcinoma
2. Clear cell acanthoma
3. Desmoplastic trichoepithelioma
4. Tumor of the follicular infundibulum

Board review style answer #1

**D.** Tumor of the follicular infundibulum is a benign lesion that can be present for years characterized by dense elastic network and abundant pale keratinocytes with notable absence of characteristic features of basal cell carcinoma (BCC).
Answer A is incorrect because there is a notable lack of peripheral palisading and cytologic atypia on histology. It is important to note that both tumor of the follicular infundibulum and basal cell carcinoma can have peripheral palisading; however, tumor of the follicular infundibulum does not have basaloid cells but rather pale cells due to glycogen.
Answer B is incorrect because although benign lesions, clear cell acanthoma typically presents on the legs and has a pink to brown color or more commonly, is red and shiny. On histology, although clear cell acanthomas have abundant glycogen containing cells, acanthosis is a typical feature.
Answer C is incorrect because desmoplastic trichoepithelioma is typically characterized by cords of basaloid cells, often in a dense fibrous stroma. Peripheral palisading is also a common feature on histology.

Board review style question #2

Which of the following immunohistochemistry markers would be most helpful in differentiating tumor of the follicular infundibulum with its most common differential?

1. BerEP4
2. CD34
3. CK5/6
4. PDL1

Board review style answer #2

**A.** BerEP4 is positive in basal cell carcinoma, which is the most common typical differential diagnosis for these lesions. Answer B is incorrect because CD34 is seen is other adnexal tumors and trichoblastoma; however, CD34 would not help to differentiate a basal cell carcinoma from a tumor of the follicular infundibulum. Answer C is incorrect because CK5/6 is positive in squamous cell carcinoma (SCC). Answer D is incorrect because PDL1 is typically used for tumor sensitivities in melanoma, not for differentiating a basal cell carcinoma.

A patient presenting with multiple eruptive tumors displaying the histologic features shown above should be evaluated for which of the following?

1. Bowen disease
2. Cowden syndrome
3. Grover disease

Board review style answer #3

**B.** Cowden syndrome is associated with the presentation of infundibulomas. Answer A is incorrect because there is no known association between Bowen disease and infundibulomas. The image shows plate-like, reticulated proliferation of keratinocytes that are branching from the epidermis and anastomosing in the reticular dermis which is characteristic of TFI. Answer C is incorrect as there is no known association of Grover disease with TFIs. Additionally, the image does not show focal acantholysis which is characteristic of Grover disease. Answer D is incorrect because tuberous sclerosis is not associated with TFIs. While tuberous sclerosis may present as hypopigmented macules on the skin similar to TFIs, they are the result of simple depigmentation rather than benign neoplastic growths arising from the hair follicle infundibulum.
