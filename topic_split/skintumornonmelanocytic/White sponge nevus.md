Definition / general

* Not a melanocytic entity; benign keratinization defect leading to white plaques on mucosa
* Primarily affects nonkeratinized stratified squamous epithelium
* First described by Cannon in 1935 ([Arch Dermatol Syphilol 1935;31:365](http://jamanetwork.com/journals/jamadermatology/article-abstract/514508))

Terminology

* Synonyms: familial white folded mucosal dysplasia, leukoderma exfoliativum mucosae oris, hereditary leukokeratosis

Epidemiology

* Rare, typically autosomal dominant disorder due to mutation in mucosal keratins CK4 ([Br J Dermatol 2003;148:1125](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12828738)) and CK13 ([J Dent Res 2001;80:919](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11379896))

Sites

* Buccal, gingival, esophageal, rectal or genital mucosal surfaces

Clinical features

* White to gray, diffuse, painless, spongy and folded plaques on buccal mucosa

Case reports

* 33 year old man with buccal lesion, no family history ([Dermatol Online J 2008;14:16](http://escholarship.org/uc/item/768308n1))

Treatment

* No standard treatment; although reports of tetracycline, chlorhexidine
* Progression usually stops at puberty
* No malignant transformation

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Parakeratosis, acanthosis with formation of large blunt rete ridges and spongiosis
* Extensive vacuolation of suprabasal keratinocytes
* Dyskeratotic cells exhibit dense peri and paranuclear eosinophilic condensations, which correspond to tonofilament aggregates
* Abundant Odland bodies (keratinosome, membrane bound granule in upper stratum spinosum) within keratinocytes but few are present in the intercellular spaces

Differential diagnosis

* [Candidiasis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorfungicandida.html)
* [Hereditary benign intraepithelial dyskeratosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/eyehereditarybenign.html)
* [Lupus erythematosus](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorlupuschronic.html)
* **Morsicatio buccarum**: chronic cheek biting
* **Morsicatio linguorum**: chronic tongue biting
* [Mucosal lichen planus](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/oralcavitylichenplanus.html)
* [Oral submucous fibrosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/oralcavityoralsubmucosalfibrosis.html)
* **Pachyonychia congenita**
* Tobacco induced **keratotic lesions**
