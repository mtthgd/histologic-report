Definition / general

* Dysplastic, premalignant lesion of ampulla of Vater

Essential features

* Essentially the ampullary counterpart to [colonic tubular](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumortubularadenoma.html) / [tubulovillous adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumortvadenoma.html)
* Often occurs in the setting of familial adenomatous polyposis
* Generally asymptomatic, with good prognosis

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [D37.6](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D37-D48/D37-/D37.6) - neoplasm of uncertain behavior of ampulla of Vater

Epidemiology

* < 10% of periampullary neoplasms
* Age range: 32 - 86 years ([Am J Clin Pathol 2009;132:506](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19762527))
* Incidence 1:1,000 - 2,000 based on autopsy studies
* Generally occurs in older patients
* Increased incidence in [familial adenomatous polyposis syndrome](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorFAP.html)

Clinical features

* Usually asymptomatic but can cause gastric outlet obstruction or bleeding and rarely acute pancreatitis, biliary obstruction or intussusception
* Excellent prognosis if completely excised
* Often associated with concurrent [pancreatic intraepithelial neoplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pancreaspanin.html) ([Mod Pathol 2001;14:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11266517))

Diagnosis

* Endoscopic impression, confirmed on biopsy
* Interobserver variability for distinguishing low grade dysplasia / indefinite for dysplasia / reactive atypia is high on small biopsy samples ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:1095](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29738360))

Case reports

* 40 year old woman with jaundice ([Surg Case Rep 2024;10:25](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38252200))
* 49 year old woman with intussusception ([Case Rep Gastroenterol 2016;10:545](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27920640))
* 53 year old man with common bile duct stricture ([Cytojournal 2017;14:19](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28900465))
* 63 year old man who underwent robotic transduodenal ampullectomy ([Ann Hepatobiliary Pancreat Surg 2021;25:150](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33649269))
* 72 year old woman with giant ampullary adenoma ([Rev Esp Enferm Dig 2023;115:467](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36426861))

Treatment

* Early or relatively confined lesions may be excised by endoscopic polypectomy or ampullectomy ([Am J Clin Pathol 2009;132:506](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19762527))
* Otherwise, requires pancreatoduodenectomy

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Tubular, tubulovillous or villous, similar to [adenomas in colon](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumoradenoma.html), with approximately half tubular and half villous ([Am J Clin Pathol 2009;132:506](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19762527))
* Low grade dysplasia demonstrates hyperchromatic, pencil shaped nuclei confined to the basal layer of cells, with limited architectural changes ([Mod Pathol 2019;32:1291](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30976103))
* High grade dysplasia demonstrates large, rounded nuclei with loss of polarity, along with architectural abnormalities such as cribriforming ([Mod Pathol 2019;32:1291](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30976103))
* Dysplastic epithelium may have only subtle changes of mild cellular stratification and fine chromatin pattern
* Often contain prominent Paneth cells (with coarse, large, red-pink, refractile granules in supranuclear cytoplasm), endocrine cells (dark, red-purple, fine, small granules in basal cytoplasm) and goblet cells
* May show high grade dysplasia or give rise to [adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ampullaadenocarcinoma.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Sporadic and [familial adenomatous polyposis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorFAP.html) related adenomas show similar molecular features to [colorectal adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumoradenoma.html), with presence of *APC* and *KRAS* mutations
* *BRAF* mutations, *TP53* alterations and DNA mismatch repair abnormalities are rare ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:1388](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18670349))

Sample pathology report

* Ampulla, polypectomy:
  + Ampullary adenoma (low grade dysplasia)
  + Negative for high grade dysplasia or malignancy
  + Margins of resection negative for dysplasia

Differential diagnosis

* **Duodenal (nonampullary) adenoma**:
  + Anatomic site is only distinction
* [Intra-ampullary papillary tubular neoplasm (IAPN)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ampullaiapn.html):
  + Occurs deeper within the ampulla
  + May have pancreatobiliary type or intestinal type epithelium ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:1731](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21084962))
  + IAPNs are predominantly nonintestinal and very often harbor an infiltrating component ([Am J Surg Pathol 2024;48:1093](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38938087))

Board review style question #1

Which of the following is true about adenomas of the ampulla of Vater?

1. *BRAF* mutations are rarely seen
2. Cases must be managed with pancreatoduodenectomy
3. Patients with Lynch syndrome are at significantly increased risk
4. Progression to adenocarcinoma is unusual

Board review style answer #1

**A.** *BRAF* mutations are rarely seen. *KRAS* and *APC* mutations are more common, both in sporadic cases and in those from patients with familial adenomatous polyposis (FAP). Answer B is incorrect because some cases can be managed endoscopically. Answer C is incorrect because patients with FAP, not Lynch syndrome, are at increased risk. Answer D is incorrect because progression to adenocarcinoma is an important risk.

Board review style question #2

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/ampullaadenomagonzalez01new.jpeg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/ampullaadenomagonzalez01new.jpeg)

A polypoid lesion of the ampulla is biopsied and shows the features above. Which of the following molecular mutations is most likely to be present?

1. *BRAF*
2. *KRAS*
3. *MLH1*
4. *STK11*

Board review style answer #2

**B.** *KRAS* mutations are most commonly seen in ampullary adenomas, along with *APC* mutations. Answers A and C are incorrect because those genes are rarely mutated in such adenomas. Answer D is incorrect because *STK11* mutations suggest a Peutz-Jeghers polyp.
