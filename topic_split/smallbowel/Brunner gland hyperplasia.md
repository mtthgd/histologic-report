Definition / general

* Brunner gland hyperplasia (BGH) is defined as benign hyperplastic proliferation of mature Brunner glands, commonly in the duodenal bulb

Essential features

* BGH clinical findings can be nonspecific; endoscopies show a polypoid / pedunculated mass that varies in size
* Histologic features show proliferation of Brunner glands that extends to the submucosa
* Smooth muscle proliferation and mature adipose tissue favors Brunner gland hamartoma

Terminology

* Brunner gland hamartoma, Brunner gland adenoma and Brunneroma: > 0.5 cm with dilated glands and smooth muscle proliferation ([Adv Ther 2021;38:2779](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33914269), [Rev Esp Enferm Dig 2022;114:124](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34607439))

ICD coding

* ICD-11: [DA53.Y](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f569977267%2fmms%2fother) - other specified duodenal polyp

Epidemiology

* BGH represents < 1% of all gastrointestinal tumors and ~5% of benign duodenal tumors ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2015;8:7565](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26261670))
* More prevalent from 50 - 70 years old ([World J Gastroenterol 2004;10:2616](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15300922))
* Risk factors ([World J Gastroenterol 2004;10:2616](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15300922))
  + High gastric acid secretion
  + *Helicobacter pylori* infection
  + Chronic pancreatitis

Sites

* Most common in proximal duodenum (57% in duodenal bulb) ([Gastrointest Endosc 1998;47:403](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9609436))

Pathophysiology

* Poorly understood
* Hypothesized to be embryonic dysplasia of the duodenum (Brunner gland hamartoma) ([JNMA J Nepal Med Assoc 2019;57:50](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31080246))
* High gastric acid secretion can cause BGH, increasing alkaline mucous secretion ([Scand J Gastroenterol 1990;25:165](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2305213))
* Relationship between *H. pylori* infection and BHG is not clearly understood

Etiology

* Majority of cases have unclear etiology
* High acid environment in the duodenum, *H. pylori* and chronic pancreatitis may cause duodenal mucosal injury and trigger a repair process that includes foveolar metaplasia and BGH ([BMC Gastroenterol 2014;14:14](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24422755))

Clinical features

* Majority of patients are asymptomatic
* Nonspecific symptoms ([J Surg Case Rep 2018;2018:rjy305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30515293))
  + Dyspepsia, nausea and vomiting
  + Abdominal pain and distension
* Rare cases present with gastrointestinal bleeding and iron deficiency anemia (especially in hamartomas and large lesions) ([Endosc Ultrasound 2015;4:266](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26374589))
* Few cases present with intestinal obstructions (lesions > 2 cm) ([Am J Gastroenterol 1995;90:290](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7847303))
* Ampullary lesions can present with biliary obstruction ([Endoscopy 2000;32:998](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11147953))

Diagnosis

* Upper GI endoscopy (EGD)
  + Some lesions (especially in posterior wall of duodenum) can be missed on EGD
  + Sensitivity of EGD is 72 - 89% ([Eur J Radiol 1993;16:115](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8462575))
  + Evaluates extent, size and origin of the lesion
  + Duodenal nodule that can be covered by normal mucosa ([Gastrointest Endosc 2006;64:464](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16923507))
  + Can mimic lipoma, endocrine tumor or gastrointestinal stromal tumor (GIST) ([Trop Gastroenterol 2010;31:121](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20862991))
* Radiology (barium Xray and abdominal CT)
  + More sensitive for larger lesions ([J Comput Assist Tomogr 2010;34:543](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20657222))
  + See [Radiology description](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelbrunnersgland.html#radio)

Radiology description

* Barium Xray
  + Sessile or pedunculated polypoid filling lesions (large Brunner gland hamartomas) ([Gastroenterol Hepatol (N Y) 2008;4:473](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21960922))
  + Multiple small filling defects and cobblestone pattern (diffuse nodular BGH) ([AJR Am J Roentgenol 2006;187:715](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16928936))
* CT ([Dig Liver Dis 2021;53:134](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33168417))
  + Mass in the duodenum with central low attenuation
  + Shows the relationship with adjacent organs

Prognostic factors

* Overall good prognosis for most lesions
* Very rare cases of BGH have been reported showing dysplasia or invasive carcinoma (2% and 0.3%, respectively) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2005;29:1442](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16224210), [J Gastroenterol 2002;37:293](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11993514))

Case reports

* 22 year old woman presented with duodenal intussusception due to BGH ([Ann Surg 1959;150:160](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/13661844))
* 33 year old woman with large BGH presented with bleeding ([Case Rep Surg 2021;2021:8861308](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34194867))
* 57 year old man with pyloric obstruction due to Brunner gland hamartoma ([J Surg Case Rep 2020;2020:rjaa191](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32874536))
* 60 year old man with BGH mimicking malignant pathology ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2021;81:105827](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33887840))

Treatment

* Endoscopic or surgical resection
* Pancreaticoduodenectomy has been reported for giant hamartomas and diffuse nodular BGH ([J Korean Med Sci 2008;23:540](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18583897))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Brunner gland hyperplasia
  + Closely packed clusters of cuboidal cells with basal round to flat nuclei and foamy cytoplasm with neutral mucin
  + Features of peptic duodenitis, foveolar metaplasia or mucosal injury can be seen ([Scand J Gastroenterol 1990;25:165](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2305213))
* Brunner gland hamartoma
  + Typically, larger size: > 0.5 cm
  + Smooth muscle and adipose tissue, intermixed with proliferating Brunner glands and cystically dilated ducts
  + Brunner glands can be intermingled with mature adipose tissue / adipocytes
* Brunner gland adenoma
  + Similar morphology to Brunner gland hyperplasia or hamartoma, with cytological atypia, nuclear enlargement and mitosis ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2015;8:7565](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26261670))

Positive stains

Sample pathology report

* Duodenum, polyp, biopsy:
  + Polypoid duodenal mucosa with prominent Brunner glands consistent with Brunner gland hyperplasia
  + Negative for dysplasia or malignancy

Differential diagnosis

* [Gastric heterotopia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelheterotopicgastric.html):
  + Commonly in first and second parts of duodenum
  + Gastric oxyntic glands with foveolar epithelium on the surface
* [Pancreatic heterotopia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelheterotopicpancreas.html):
  + Variable mixture of elements of normal pancreatic tissue including acini, ducts or islet cells
* [Neuroendocrine tumors](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelcarcinoidtumor.html):
  + Commonly in first and second parts of duodenum, including the ampulla
  + Nests of uniform cells with round to oval nuclei with salt and pepper chromatin
  + Tumor cells are positive for [chromogranin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html) and [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html)
* **Duodenal** [adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ampullaadenoma.html):
  + Common in the ampullary area
  + Tubular or villous architecture with dysplasia of the surface epithelium
* [Gastrointestinal stromal tumor (GIST)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelGIST.html):
  + Rare in the duodenum
  + Monotonous spindle or epithelioid cells with pale cytoplasm and vesicular chromatin
  + Tumor cells are positive for [DOG1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsDOG1.html) and [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)
* [Leiomyoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelleiomyoma.html):
  + Incidental finding
  + Can be merged with muscularis mucosa
  + Positive for [smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) and [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html)
* [Lipoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowellipoma.html):
  + Rare, submucosal
  + May be ulcerated
  + Needs to be distinguished from fat rich Brunner gland hamartoma

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/smallbowelbrunnersglandSharma04.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/smallbowelbrunnersglandSharma04.jpg)

A 52 year old man with history of dyspepsia and abdominal pain. The upper GI endoscopy showed a 0.5 cm polyp in the duodenal bulb. Which of the following is a possible etiology of the findings depicted in the image above?

1. Gastric atrophy
2. Immunosuppression
3. Increased duodenal acidic environment
4. Inflammatory bowel disease

Board review style answer #1

**C.** Increased duodenal acidic environment. The biopsy findings show Brunner gland hyperplasia with focal foveolar metaplasia. A possible etiology of Brunner gland hyperplasia is increased gastric acidity with compensatory increased alkaline secretion by Brunner glands in the duodenum. Answer D is incorrect due to a lack of chronic inflammation and mucosal injury. Answer B is incorrect due to lack of additional findings associated with immunosuppression like infectious agents or inflammation. Answer A is incorrect as Brunner gland hyperplasia is usually associated with gastric increased acid secretion, not gastric atrophy.

Board review style question #2

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/smallbowelbrunnersglandSharma03.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/smallbowelbrunnersglandSharma03.jpg)

The figure above is from a duodenal biopsy in a patient with duodenal polyp on upper GI endoscopy. Which of the following, if present, favors a diagnosis of Brunner gland hamartoma over Brunner gland hyperplasia?

1. Lesion size < 0.5 cm
2. Presence of foveolar metaplasia
3. Smooth muscle proliferation
4. Superficial lesion limited to the mucosa

Board review style answer #2

**C.** Smooth muscle proliferation. Brunner gland hamartoma is typically a larger lesion (> 0.5 cm), composed of proliferation of Brunner glands, smooth muscle and mature adipose tissue. Brunner gland hyperplasia usually presents as a small lesion (< 0.5 cm) showing only hyperplastic Brunner glands with or without foveolar metaplasia. Answer D is incorrect as both Brunner gland hamartoma and hyperplasia can extend to the submucosa. Answer A is incorrect as Brunner gland hamartoma is usually > 0.5 cm in size. Answer B is incorrect as foveolar metaplasia is mostly associated with Brunner gland hyperplasia.
