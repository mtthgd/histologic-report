Definition / general

* Idiopathic chronic inflammatory condition that may involve any part of the upper and lower gastrointestinal tract
* Tends to involve the distal ileum and proximal large intestine

Essential features

* Diagnostic criterion: segmental disease, transmural inflammation, noncaseating granulomas, deep fissuring ulcers, ileal involvement
* Distal ileum is the most commonly involved part of the small intestine
* Risk of colorectal carcinoma increases with duration and extent of disease

Terminology

* Inflammatory bowel disease (IBD), Crohn's disease (CD), Crohn's

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [K50](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/K00-K95/K50-K52/K50-/K50) - Crohn's disease of the small intestine

Epidemiology

* Western counties, with the highest reported prevalence in Europe (322 per 100,000 in Germany) and North America (319 per 100,000 in Canada) ([Lancet 2017;390:2769](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29050646))
* Rising incidence in newly industrialized countries in Africa, Asia and South America ([Lancet 2017;390:2769](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29050646))
* Genetic predisposition through population and family based studies showed Crohn's disease with higher incidence among relatives with Crohn's disease, twins and Jewish population from middle European origin ([Gastroenterology 2011;140:1704](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21530736), [Gastroenterology 1989;97:900](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2777043), [Med Clin North Am 1990;74:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2404170))
* Bimodal age distribution, first peak between 15 - 30 years, where the majority of patients are diagnosed; the second peak is between 50 - 80 years of age ([Am J Gastroenterol 2006;101:1559](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16863561), [Gastroenterology 1991;100:350](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1985033), [Med Clin North Am 1990;74:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2404170))
* Slightly more common in women (F:M = 1.3:1)

Sites

* May occur anywhere in the GI tract, from oral cavity to perianal area
* Terminal ileum is the most commonly affected site (80%), with 33% having ileitis alone
* 50% of patients have ileocolitis (involvement of both terminal ileum and colon)
* About 20% of patients have limited colon disease, of which only half will have rectal sparing
* 33% of patients have perianal disease
* 5 - 15% can have oral gastroduodenal involvement, with significantly fewer patients developing esophageal and proximal small intestinal involvement
* Reference: [UpToDate: Clinical Manifestations, Diagnosis, and Prognosis of Crohn Disease in Adults [Accessed 22 October 2021]](https://www.uptodate.com/contents/clinical-manifestations-diagnosis-and-prognosis-of-crohn-disease-in-adults)

Pathophysiology

* Unclear, as overall pathogenesis remains poorly understood
* Inappropriate immune response has been implicated, with vast literature delineating the role of both host and microbial factors in its pathogenesis ([Nat Rev Immunol 2008;8:458](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18500230))
* Dysregulation in epithelial barriers, immune cells and secreted mediators have been shown to play a role ([J Clin Invest 1983;72:142](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6192143), [Gut 2013;62:1734](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23013725), [Gastroenterology 2009;136:1261](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19230854))
* Microbiota may induce inflammatory bowel disease if a concurrent underlying genetic defect is present ([Nature 2012;491:119](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23128233))
* More than 200 loci have been identified, many of which are shared among both Crohn's disease and ulcerative colitis (UC), indicating the overlap between these 2 entities; these loci lead to modulation of protein expression, rather than amino acid change, which supports that each locus confers an increased risk of developing Crohn's disease ([Nature 2012;491:119](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23128233), [Inflamm Bowel Dis 2015;21:1166](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25895007))

Etiology

* Idiopathic
* Some associations have been demonstrated:
  + Smoking, as shown in meta analysis studies ([Mayo Clin Proc 2006;81:1462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17120402), [Am J Gastroenterol 2012;107:1399](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22777340))
  + Physical activity is inversely related to developing Crohn's disease, with some limited data suggesting exercise can even reduce disease activity ([BMJ 2013;347:f6633](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24231178), [Prev Med Rep 2016;3:177](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27419012), [Inflamm Bowel Dis 2015;21:1063](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25723616))
  + Sleep deprivation ([Inflamm Bowel Dis 2013;19:2440](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23945186), [Sleep Breath 2020;24:971](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31512092))
  + Infection and immune response with *Salmonella* and *Campylobacter* gastroenteritis, as shown in population based cohort studies ([Nat Rev Immunol 2008;8:458](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18500230), [Gastroenterology 2009;137:495](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19361507))
  + *Mycobacterium paratuberculosis* ([Expert Opin Biol Ther 2019;19:79](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30574820))
  + Unclear medication association ([Am J Gastroenterol 2011;106:2133](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21912437), [Am J Gastroenterol 2014;109:1728](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25223575), [Am J Gastroenterol 2010;105:2610](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20700115), [Lancet Gastroenterol Hepatol 2020;5:986](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32818437))

Clinical features

* Abdominal pain, diarrhea (bloody or nonbloody), fatigue and weight loss are classic symptoms ([Am J Gastroenterol 2000;95:3458](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11151877), [Clin Gastroenterol Hepatol 2006;4:614](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16630762), [Am J Gastroenterol 2018;113:481](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29610508))
* Patients with distal terminal ileal involvement can present with right lower quadrant abdominal pain
* Distal ileum is the most commonly involved site
* Given the transmural inflammation nature in Crohn's disease, some advanced conditions can present with bowel obstruction due to fibrotic stricture formation
* Extraintestinal manifestations and associations include ([Inflamm Bowel Dis 2011;17:471](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20725943), [J Crohns Colitis 2016;10:239](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26614685), [J Crohns Colitis 2019;13:541](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30445584)):
  + Joint and bones: arthropathies and osteoporosis
  + Eye: iritis, uveitis and episcleritis
  + Skin: erythema nodosum, pyoderma gangerosum, Sweet syndrome
  + Liver: primary sclerosing cholangitis, cholelithiasis
  + Kidney: nephrolithiasis
  + Lung: asthma, bronchiectasis, chronic bronchitis, interstitial lung disease, bronchiolitis obliterans with organizing pneumonia, sarcoidosis, necrobiotic lung nodules and pulmonary infiltrates

Diagnosis

* Multimodality approach with clinicopathologic correlation and exclusion of other differential diagnoses
* Colonoscopy with ileoscopy, esophagogastroduodenoscopy (EGD) and endoscopic ultrasound are helpful in evaluating Crohn's disease as well as differentiating Crohn's disease from ulcerative colitis ([Aliment Pharmacol Ther 2014;39:823](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24612000))
* Endoscopy also plays a pivotal role in evaluating the severity of the disease and surveillance of neoplasms
* Characteristic endoscopic findings of Crohn's disease include ([Med Clin North Am 1990;74:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2404181), [Gastrointest Endosc 1977;23:150](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/838244), [Gastrointest Endosc 1984;30:167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6735093)):
  + Aphthous ulcers
  + Linear and serpiginous ulcers (giving a cobblestone appearance)
  + Skip lesions
  + Fistulas and strictures
  + Isolated terminal ileum involvement, uninvolved rectum

Laboratory

* May be normal
* High white blood cells, anemia, elevated C reactive protein, electrolyte abnormalities, vitamin D deficiency and vitamin B12 deficiency may be found
* Elevated fecal calprotectin or lactoferrin (stool inflammatory markers), which can be used as a screening tool and determine the need for endoscopy ([BMJ 2010;341:c3369](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20634346))

Radiology description

* Not used as a primary diagnostic tool due to the wide availability of colonoscopy
* Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), computed tomography (CT) and ultrasonography have shown good diagnostic accuracy by prospective meta analysis studies ([Arch Dis Child 1996;74:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8660040), [Eur J Radiol 2006;58:140](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16337356), [Gut 2005;54:257](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15647191))

Prognostic factors

* Disease course and severity may vary but is usually chronic and intermittent
* 50% of patients experience intestinal complications (strictures, fistula and abscess) 20 years after diagnosis ([Intest Res 2015;13:19](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25691840))
* Involvement of terminal ileum, ileocolonic and upper GI have higher risk of developing complications ([Gastroenterology 2010;139:1147](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20637205))
* Smoking, age Gastroenterology 2006;130:650, [Gut 2012;61:1140](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22387526))
* Carcinoma may develop in longstanding disease with cumulative risk of 2.9% at 10 years, 5.6% at 20 years and 8.3% at 30 years after Crohn's disease diagnosis ([World J Gastroenterol 2014;20:9872](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25110418))
  + 2 most important risk factors: duration and extent of disease

Case reports

* 24 year old woman with Crohn's terminal ileitis in remission and recurrent ileal invagination ([Dtsch Arztebl Int 2020;117:146](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32234192))
* 41 year old man with Crohn's disease and mesenteric artery thrombosis ([Ann Vasc Surg 2019;58:382.e15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30802583))
* 62 year old man with duodenal Crohn's disease with associated ampullary stenosis ([Dig Dis Sci 2005;50:1118](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15986865))
* 77 year old man with unusual initial presentation of elderly onset Crohn's disease ([Cureus 2020;12:e10173](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33029453))

Treatment

* Multidisciplinary approach is recommended, including medications, surgery, nutritional, psychosocial support and cancer screening
* Assessing disease severity using Crohn's Disease Activity Index (CDAI) is helpful in guiding treatment approach ([Gastroenterology 1979;77:829](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38175), [J Pediatr Gastroenterol Nutr 1991;12:439](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1678008), [Gastroenterology 1999;116:527](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10029610))
* Mild to moderately severe disease / low risk disease: sulfasalazine (colonic CD), budesonide (ileocecal CD) ([Am J Gastroenterol 2018;113:481](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29610508))
* Moderate to severe disease / moderate to high risk disease: corticosteroids, immunomodulators (e.g. azathioprine, 6-mercaptourin, methotrexate), anti-TNF agents (e.g. infliximab, adalimumab, certolizumab pegol), agents targeting leukocyte trafficking, agents targeting IL12 / 23 (anti-p40 antibody) ([Am J Gastroenterol 2018;113:481](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29610508))
* Severe / fulminant disease: intravenous corticosteroids, anti-TNF agents ([Am J Gastroenterol 2018;113:481](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29610508))
* Surgery is reserved for patients with limited disease, failure to response to treatment and complications of Crohn's disease (fistula, stricture, perforation) ([Gastroenterology 1999;116:527](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16618401), [J Pediatr Surg 1997;32:1063](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9247235), [J Pediatr Gastroenterol Nutr 2015;60:347](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25373863), [Inflamm Bowel Dis 2013;19:7](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22573565), [Am J Gastroenterol 2018;113:481](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29610508))
* Surveillance colonoscopy for dysplasia and colorectal carcinoma at 8 - 10 years after onset of symptoms with subsequent surveillance intervals of 1 - 2 years ([Gastroenterol Hepatol (N Y) 2017;13:357](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28690452))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Features of activity: active inflammation with cryptitis, crypt abscess and ulceration
* Features of chronicity: pyloric gland metaplasia, architectural distortion, crypt loss, crypt atrophy, basal lymphoplasmacytosis, fibrosis and stromal hypertrophy
* Skip lesions (portions of normal appearing small intestine with scattered areas of disease)
* Transmural lymphoid aggregates (beading or rosary pattern)
* Noncaseating granulomas (not related to crypt injury)
* Deep fissuring ulcers
* Aphthous ulcers (small ulcers occurring over lymphoid aggregates)
* Obliterative muscularization of submucosa ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2001;125:1331](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11570909))
* Dysplasia (low or high grade) and carcinoma may be present in patients with longstanding disease
* Note: Some features of Crohn’s disease may also be seen in fulminant, untreated ulcerative colitis, such as aphthous ulcers, deep ulceration with transmural inflammation and superficial fissuring ulcers
* References: [Circulation 1965;32:332](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/5294316), [Histopathology 2012;60:1034](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22008086), [Virchows Arch 2014;464:511](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24487791), [Virchows Arch 2018;472:81](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29103205), [Mod Pathol 2015;28:S30](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25560598)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* 71 susceptibility loci on 17 chromosomes ([Nat Genet 2010;42:1118](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21102463))
* Nucleotide binding oligodimerization domain 2 (*NOD2*) or caspase recruitment domain 15 (*CARD15*) on chromosome 16 ([Nature 1996;379:821](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8587604), [Hum Mol Genet 1996;5:1679](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8894707), [Gastroenterology 2000;119:1483](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11113069), [Ann N Y Acad Sci 2006;1072:9](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17057186))
* Autophagy related 16-like 1 (*ATG16L1*) and immunity related guanosine triphosphatase family member M (*IRGM*) gene on chromosome 5 ([Nat Genet 2007;39:596](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17435756), [Gut 2008;57:717](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18272545))
* Genetic polymorphisms in tumor necrosis factor receptors (*TNFRSF1A / 1B*) with differential treatment response to TNFα inhibitors ([BMJ Open Gastroenterol 2019;6:e000246](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30815272))

Sample pathology report

* Terminal ileum and cecum, ileocecectomy:
  + Segment of terminal ileum with features consistent with history of Crohn's disease, including:
    - Moderately to markedly active chronic ileitis with patchy ulcerations
    - Fibromuscular stricture with decreased luminal circumference
    - Scattered noncaseating granulomas
  + No dysplasia identified
  + Immunostain for CMV is negative

Differential diagnosis

* [Ulcerative colitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelUC.html):
  + Small bowel typically not involved except in case of backwash ileitis
  + Absence of granulomas and segmental disease (these features are more specific for CD)
  + Typically superficial inflammation with lack of transmural lymphoid aggregates
    - Deep or serosal based lymphoid aggregates may be seen adjacent to ulceration in both UC and CD
  + Absence of deep fissuring ulceration
    - Superficial fissures may be seen in fulminant disease
* **Infectious enteritis**:
  + May show overlapping features of inflammatory bowel disease
  + Correlation with microbiologic studies is necessary for exclusion
* [Drug associated enteritis (e.g. NSAIDs)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colonnsaid.html):
  + Patchy active inflammation
  + Tends to lack significant features of chronicity
* [Behçet disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelbehcet.html):
  + Form of vasculitis
  + Recurrent aphthous stomatitis and genital ulcers may be present
* [Ischemia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelischemia.html):
  + Withering crypts and hyalinization of lamina propria
* References: [Ann Gastroenterol 2011;24:271](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24713761), [Curr Gastroenterol Rep 2010;12:249](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20532706)
