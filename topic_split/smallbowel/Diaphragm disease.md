Definition / general

* Small bowel pathology related to NSAID use, first described in 1988 ([J Clin Pathol 1988;41:516](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3384981))

Case reports

* 46 year old woman with chronic aspirin use ([Case of the Week #240](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case240.htm))

Treatment

* Cessation of NSAID use
* Often surgery ([Colorectal Dis 2012;14:804](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21812898)) or endoscopic balloon dilation ([Gastrointest Endosc 2006;64:1014](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17140921))

Gross description

* Segmentation of ileum by incomplete mucosal diaphragms, defined as thin circumferential membranes resembling the plica circularis, composed of mucosa and submucosa with accompanying fibrosis
* Also ulceration, strictures, perforation

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Thin circumferential membranes resembling plica circularis, composed of mucosa and submucosa with accompanying fibrosis
* Neuromuscular and vascular hamartoma-like changes, eosinophilic enteritis and acute inflammation ([Am J Clin Pathol 2008;130:518](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18794043))

Differential diagnosis

* **Cryptogenic multifocal ulcerous stenosing enteritis (CMUSE)**: small intestinal strictures and ulcerations of unknown origin ([World J Gastroenterol 2011;17:2873](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21734797)), characterized as atypical vasculitis presenting with unexplained stricture and ulceration of the small bowel in young and middle aged patients but without systemic inflammation; treated with steroids
