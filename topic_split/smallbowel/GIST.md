Definition / general

* Most common mesenchymal tumor of the gastrointestinal (GI) tract
* Derived from interstitial cells of Cajal

Essential features

* Small bowel is second most common site of GISTs (stomach is first)
* *NF1* and *BRAF* mutated GISTs are more common in small bowel versus other sites
* Prognosis depends on size, mitotic rate and location

Terminology

* Gastrointestinal stromal tumor (GIST)

ICD coding

* + ICD-10: [C49.A3](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C45-C49/C49-/C49.A3) - gastrointestinal stromal tumor of small intestine
  + ICD-11: [2B5B&XH9HQ1](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f819644414?data=%7B%22dataType%22%3A%22pc%22%2C%22postcoordinationCodeSet%22%3A%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F819644414%22%2C%22axisToValueIds%22%3A%7B%22specificAnatomy%22%3A%5B%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F2069696861%22%7D%5D%7D%7D%7D) - gastrointestinal stromal tumor
  + ICD-O: [8936/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=83) - gastrointestinal stromal tumor, malignant

Epidemiology

* M:F = 1
* Median age is 60 - 65 years; rare in children and young adults
* May present in younger patients in neurofibromatosis type 1 (NF1) lesions (mean age 49 years), Carney triad (childhood), familial (middle age)
* Annual incidence of GIST is between 11 and 14.5 cases per million ([Int J Cancer 2005;117:289](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15900576), [Cancer 2005;103:821](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15648083))
* GISTs account for 1 - 3% of all GI neoplasms

Sites

* Occurs anywhere in tubular GI tract
* Small bowel is second most common site
* Stomach (60%) > jejunum and ileum (30%) > duodenum (4 - 5%) > rectum (4%) > colon and appendix (1 - 2%) > esophagus (Semin Diagn Pathol 2006;23:70)
* Extraintestinal GISTs occur in mesentery, omentum, retroperitoneum ([Am J Surg Pathol 2005;29:52](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15613856), [Mod Pathol 2000;13:577](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10824931))
  + May represent a metastasis from an unrecognized primary or a detached mass from the GI tract

Pathophysiology

* Sporadic - vast majority
  + Somatic mutations in *KIT* (70 - 85%), *PDGFRA* (5 - 10%, mutually exclusive with *KIT* mutations), *NF1* and *BRAF* ([Science 2003;299:708](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12522257), [Cancer Res 2001;61:8118](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11719439))
* Sporadic tumor syndrome (nonhereditary)
  + Carney triad (GIST, paraganglioma, pulmonary chondroma)
    - SDH deficient but lacking *SDH* germline mutations
* Autosomal dominant hereditary syndromes (5 - 10% of all GISTs)
  + Neurofibromatosis type 1 (NF1) - commonly small bowel and often multifocal
  + Germline *KIT* mutations
  + Germline *PDGFRA* mutations
  + Carney-Stratakis syndrome
    - Germline mutations in *SDHB, SDHC* or *SDHD* subunit ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2020;144:655](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31169996))

Etiology

* Unknown, most cases are sporadic
* Small subset of cases can be familial (see [Pathophysiology](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelGIST.html#smallbowelGISTpath))

Guidelines for risk assessment of primary GIST

Clinical features

* Vague abdominal pain
* Symptoms related to mucosal ulceration, including bleeding (47%) ([Ther Clin Risk Manag 2018;14:1467](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30174429))
* Abdominal mass
* Smaller GISTs are discovered incidentally
* NF1 associated GISTs ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:90](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16330947))
  + Patients are more likely to develop multiple independent GISTs
  + NF1 tumors have a strong predilection to arise in small bowel
  + While NF1 associated GISTs have been estimated to account for only about 1 - 2% of tumors from all anatomic sites, they make up ~4 - 6% of small intestinal GISTs

Diagnosis

* Double balloon enteroscopy (89%), CT angiography (71%) and CT (55%) are best imaging modalities ([Ther Clin Risk Manag 2018;14:1467](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30174429))
* Endoscopy with biopsy or fine needle aspiration

Radiology description

* Radiologic findings are variable, depending on size and time of presentation
* CT usually shows a solid, heterogeneous mass (reflecting the presence of hemorrhage or cystic degeneration)
* Endoscopic ultrasound reveals a hypoechoic solid mass
* References: [Front Oncol 2021;11:582847](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33968714), [Front Oncol 2021;11:631927](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34041017)

Prognostic factors

* Prognosis depends on site, size, mitotic activity, molecular profile ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2006;23:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17193820))
* Small bowel GISTs are more likely to be malignant (35 - 40%) compared with gastric GISTs (25%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:477](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16625094), [Am J Surg Pathol 2005;29:52](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15613856))
* *NF1* mutated GISTs are usually grossly small and mitotically inactive, much like spontaneous low grade GISTs of the small intestine
  + Their histologically benign appearance is reflected in their commonly nonaggressive clinical behavior
  + NF1 associated tumors that do manifest as clinically malignant disease are generally bigger (> 5 cm in greatest dimension) and more proliferative (> 5 mitoses/5 mm2), adhering to the risk stratification provided by standard staging criteria

Case reports

* 58 year old woman with small bowel GIST presenting as intussusception ([GE Port J Gastroenterol 2016;23:279](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28868478))
* 68 year old woman with small bowel GIST with diffuse omental and mesenteric implants ([J Surg Case Rep 2020;2020:rjaa341](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32994921))
* 74 year old man with small bowel GIST presenting as strangulated inguinal hernia ([BMJ Case Rep 2017;2017:bcr2016217273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28104721))
* 75 year old woman with NF1 jejunal GIST ( [J Surg Case Rep 2018;2018:rjy017](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29492250))

Treatment

* Most GISTs are treated with surgical resection
* Imatinib mesylate (Gleevec): tyrosine kinase inhibitor of KIT and PDGFRα
  + Metastatic / recurrent / high risk GIST
* Sunitinib malate (Sutent): a tyrosine kinase inhibitor of KIT, PDGFRα, VEGFR
  + Used in imatinib resistant GIST
* *NF1* mutated GISTs - surgery is treatment of choice as these tumors do not respond to
  imatinib
* *BRAF* mutated GISTs do not respond to imatinib and BRAF inhibitors are used for
  therapy if indicated
* References: [Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:922](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25970686), [Surg Clin North Am 2017;97:437](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28325196)

Microscopic (histologic) description

* 3 morphologic types: spindle (70%), epithelioid (20%), mixed (10%)
* Monotonous, bland cells with spindled or epithelioid cytoarchitecture, lightly eosinophilic to pale cytoplasm and vesicular chromatin residing within uniformly ovoid or round nuclei
* In spindle cell GISTs, the pink cytoplasm often has a fibrillary texture with indistinct cell borders
* Can show a degree of nuclear palisading reminiscent of schwannoma
* Small intestinal GISTs are most often spindled
  + Epithelioid morphology in small intestinal GIST portends malignant behavior, in contrast to the stomach, where epithelioid morphology is not a significant prognostic feature
* Small intestine GISTs more commonly feature skeinoid fibers, stromal PAS positive globules of curved collagen fibrils, which, when present in a small intestinal tumor, correlate with less malignant clinical behavior
* Treated tumors can display a widely variable histomorphology that may significantly differ from treatment naive tumors
  + Tumor may be necrotic or hypocellular; the stromal component, which may be hyalinized or calcified, becomes prominent
  + Cytomorphology of the tumor cells can change, usually adopting a more epithelioid and even anaplastic or sarcomatoid appearance
* Histologic grade
  + G1: low grade, mitoses 2
  + G2: High grade, mitoses > 5/5 mm2
* References: [Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:577](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28288036), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2020;144:655](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31169996)

Cytology description

* Bland spindle and sometimes epithelioid cells
* EUS-FNA with IHC staining of cell block can lead to diagnosis
  + If not enough material and large tumor size, there might not be enough for genotyping to guide neoadjuvant therapy
* Without enough tissue to perform immunohistochemical stains, it might be hard to get to a definitive diagnosis
* Reference: [Diagn Cytopathol 2021 May 18 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34003599)

Sample pathology report

* Duodenum, resection:
  + Gastrointestinal stromal tumor (GIST), spindle cell type, 3.0 cm, grade 1 (see synoptic report for risk stratification as well as biomarker report)

Differential diagnosis

* Low grade spindle GISTs
  + [Leiomyoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelleiomyoma.html):
    - Eosinophilic cytoplasm, blunt ended cigar shaped nuclei
    - Cytoplasmic vacuolization present
    - Positive for [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) and negative for [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html) and [DOG1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsDOG1.html)
  + [Schwannoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueschwannoma.html):
    - Cuff of lymphocytes
    - Verocay bodies
    - Positive for [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) and negative for [DOG1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsDOG1.html) and [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)
  + [Solitary fibrous tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesft.html):
    - Patternless pattern
    - Collageneous stroma
    - [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html) positive and negative for [DOG1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsDOG1.html) and [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)
  + [Inflammatory myofibroblastic tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelIMT.html):
    - Prominent inflammatory infiltrate
    - [ALK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html) positive and negative for [DOG1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsDOG1.html) and [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)
* High grade spindle GISTs
  + [Leiomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueleiomyosarcoma.html):
    - Eosinophilic cytoplasm, blunt ended cigar shaped nuclei
    - Positive for [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) and negative for [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html) and [DOG1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsDOG1.html)
  + [Dedifferentiated liposarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuededifflipo.html):
    - Well differentiated component usually present
    - Positive for [MDM2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmdm2.html) amplification by FISH, negative for [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html) and [DOG1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsDOG1.html)
  + **Spindle cell carcinoma**:
    - Positive for [pancytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) and negative for [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html) and [DOG1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsDOG1.html)

in behavior
