Definition / general

* Rare tumor of periampullary region and second part of duodenum

Essential features

* Duodenal neoplasm that rarely metastasizes
* Triphasic histology (epithelioid, spindle, ganglion type cells) and immunoprofile are characteristic and distinctive

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [D13.2](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D13-/D13.2) - Benign neoplasm of duodenum

Sites

* Almost all arise in the duodenum or around ampulla of Vater
* May very rarely arise elsewhere (such as in the nasopharynx, [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2001;125:1098](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11473468))

Etiology

* May derive from endodermal neuroectodermal complexes in the embryonic ventral pancreas ([Am J Surg Pathol 1985;9:31](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2578747))

Clinical features

* 15 - 84 years (mean 52 years) ([BMC Cancer 2011;11:187](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21599949))
* M > F (1.5:1) ([BMC Cancer 2011;11:187](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21599949))
* Presents with GI bleeding or abdominal pain but may be incidental finding
* Usually benign and nonfunctional; very rarely produces hormones ([J Gastrointest Oncol 2016;7:S107](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27034808))
* Approximately 7% metastasize to lymph nodes ([BMC Cancer 2011;11:187](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21599949))
  + Very rarely metastasizes widely and may cause patient death ([World J Clin Cases 2017;5:222](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28685135))
* May recur if incompletely excised

Diagnosis

* Tissue diagnosis required

Prognostic factors

* Almost always benign but distant metastases can rarely occur

Case reports

* 47 year old man with widely metastatic gangliocytic paraganglioma ([World J Gastroenterol 2014;20:15454](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25386095))
* 55 year old woman with gangliocytic paraganglioma of the thymus ([J Pathol Transl Med 2016;50:165](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26447134))
* 68 year old woman with gangliocytic paraganglioma metastatic to peripancreatic lymph node ([Diagn Pathol 2017;12:57](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28764742))
* 71 year old man with gangliocytic paraganglioma of the minor papilla ([Intern Med 2017;56:1029](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28458307))
* 73 year old woman with gangliocytic paraganglioma and concurrent ampullary adenocarcinoma ([Intern Med 2018;57:2663](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29780117))

Treatment

* Endoscopic resection ([Gastroenterol Hepatol 2016;39:605](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26547609))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Unencapsulated submucosal lesion
* Triphasic, with epithelioid, spindle cell (Schwann cell-like) and ganglion type cells of varying proportions
  + May therefore resemble [well differentiated neuroendocrine tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelcarcinoidtumor.html), [paraganglioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/adrenalparaganglioma.html) or [ganglioneuroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorganglioneuroma.html)
* Variable stromal amyloid ([Am J Surg Pathol 1977;1:207](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/920868))

Cytology description

* All 3 cell types may be visible in aspirated material ([Diagn Cytopathol 2013;41:650](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22102547))

Positive stains

* Epithelioid cells: **pancreatic polypeptide**, [progesterone receptor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html), **somatostatin**, [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html), [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html), [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html) ([BMC Cancer 2015;15:269](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25886293))
* Ganglion cells: [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), **pancreatic polypeptide**, **somatostatin**, [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html), [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html), [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html)
* Spindle cells: [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html) (67%)

Negative stains

* Epithelioid cells: [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) ([BMC Cancer 2015;15:269](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25886293))
* Ganglion cells: [progesterone receptor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html), [pancytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [estrogen receptor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html), [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html)
* Spindle cells: **pancreatic polypeptide**, [progesterone receptor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html), **somatostatin**, [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html) (25%), [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html), [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html), [estrogen receptor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html), [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* May show *HIF2A* gain of function mutations ([Endocr Relat Cancer 2016;23:L13](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27130043))

Sample pathology report

* Ampulla, mass, resection:
  + Gangliocytic paraganglioma (2.2 cm) (see comment)
  + Margins of resection unremarkable.
  + Comment: Immunohistochemical stains for S100 and synaptophysin highlight various components of this uncommon triphasic neoplasm, which is typically indolent but can rarely metastasize.

Differential diagnosis

* [Ganglioneuroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorganglioneuroma.html):
  + Rare in the ampulla / duodenum
  + Difficult to distinguish if ganglion cells are predominant in gangliocytic paraganglioma
* [Paraganglioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/adrenalparaganglioma.html):
  + Rare in the ampulla / duodenum
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)+ sustentacular cells are arranged around nests of epithelioid cells, rather than forming regions of the tumor
* [Well differentiated neuroendocrine tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelcarcinoidtumor.html):
  + Can be difficult, depending on sampling
  + [Progesterone receptor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html) and **pancreatic polypeptide** staining may help ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2017;141:1309](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28968157))

Board review style question #1

Which of the following cell types are seen in gangliocytic paraganglioma?

1. Blister, ganglion and stem
2. Epithelioid, ganglion and spindle
3. Epithelioid, ganglion and sustentacular
4. Epithelioid, stem and spindle

Board review style answer #1

**B.** Epithelioid, ganglion and spindle

The following picture shows a lesion resected from the ampulla. Which of the following is true?

1. Similar tumors may rarely be seen outside the ampulla
2. The patient is at high risk for distant metastasis
3. The patient likely has MEN1 syndrome
4. This lesion usually shows translocations involving *EWS*

Board review style answer #2

**A.** Similar tumors may rarely be seen outside the ampulla. The picture shows a gangliocytic paraganglioma, which usually occurs in the ampulla but can rarely arise in other gastrointestinal or extragastrointestinal sites.
