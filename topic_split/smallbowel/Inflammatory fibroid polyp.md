Definition / general

* Uncommon, benign polypoid submucosal mass with CD34+ spindle cells and granulation tissue, usually in distal stomach or terminal ileum ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2011;135:1311](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21970486))
* Presents as obstructive mass or causing intussusception ([Radiographics 1999;19:539](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10194795))
* True neoplasms, usually with *PDGFRA* mutation ([Pathologe 2009;30:117](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19756614))

Case reports

* 35 year old woman with concurrent appearance of Crohn's disease and inflammatory fibroid polyp ([Ann Ital Chir 2005;76:395](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16550878))

Treatment

* Excision (does not recur)

Gross description

* Polypoid (without stalk), submucosal mass, dumbbell shape in small intestine due to intussuception, mucosal ulceration and splitting and atrophy of muscularis propria

Inflammatory fibroid polyp

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Submucosal mixture of prominent capillaries, spindle cells (occasionally concentrically arranged around vessels / onion skinning) and inflammatory cells (particularly eosinophils, plasma cells and mast cells) in granulation tissue-like stroma ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2010;134:378](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20196666))
* May be transmural
* May have lymphoid follicles
* Rare multinucleated giant cells, no atypia, no / rare mitotic figures
* **Histologic patterns:** classical, edematous, fibrovascular, nodular, sclerotic

Positive stains

* [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)+ spindle cells
* Variable [calponin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalponin.html), [cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html), [fascin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfascin.html), [smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html), [CD35](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30to39.html#cd35)

Negative stains

* [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html) (although mast cells are positive)

Differential diagnosis

* [GIST](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelGIST.html): malignant in 30 - 50%, plump spindle cells with eosinophilic cytoplasm, variable skenoid fibers and muscular infiltration, [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)+ ([Mod Pathol 2000;13:1134](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11048809), [Mod Pathol 2003;16:366](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12692202))
* [Inflammatory myofibroblastic tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelIMT.html): usually children, may recur, plasma cells and lymphocytes with few eosinophils, mitotic activity in cellular areas, less prominent blood vessels, [smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html)+, [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html)+, [ALK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html)±, [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)- ([Hum Pathol 2002;33:307](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11979371))
* [Inflammatory polyp](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelinflammatorypolyp.html) of [Crohn's](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelcrohns.html) or [ulcerative colitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelUC.html): similar histology but different clinical history and findings in remainder of bowel
