Definition / general

* Rare malignant mesenchymal neoplasm of the gastrointestinal tract

Essential features

* Mesenchymal malignancy that grows in sheets or cords and may have osteoclast-like giant cells
* Positive for [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) and [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html); negative for [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html) and [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html)
* Harbors *EWSR1* translocation

Terminology

* Also known as clear cell sarcoma-like tumor of the gastrointestinal tract

Sites

* Small bowel is most common site in gastrointestinal tract, followed by stomach ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:857](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22592145%0D%0A))

Clinical features

* Median age 35 years; no sex predilection ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2015;139:407](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25724038))
* Most common symptoms are abdominal pain and intestinal obstruction
* Tumor can spread to lymph nodes and liver and may cause death

Case reports

* 17 year old boy with stomach mass ([Oncol Lett 2014;8:2687](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25364450))
* 18 year old man with small bowel mass ([Diagn Pathol 2017;12:29](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28320420))

Treatment

* Surgery; possible adjuvant role for c-Met / ALK inhibitors and VEGF inhibitors ([Oncology 2016;91:348](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27764830))

Gross description

* Large mass (mean 5 cm)

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Sheets or nests of oval / spindled mesenchymal cells with clear or eosinophilic cytoplasm, visible nucleoli and often mitoses
* Osteoclast-like giant cells may be seen
* Tumor often spans the entire wall

Positive stains

* [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html), [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html), [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html)

Negative stains

* [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html), [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html),
  [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html), [DOG1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsDOG1.html), [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)

Electron microscopy description

* Secretory vesicles and dense core granules

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Translocation involving [*EWSR1*](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsewsr1.html) (most commonly *EWSR1-ATF1* [t(12;22)] or *EWSR1-CREB1*)

Sample pathology report

* Jejunum, resection:
  + Malignant gastrointestinal neuroectodermal tumor (6.1 cm) (see comment)
  + Lymphovascular invasion present.
  + Margins of resection unremarkable.
  + Three of eight lymph nodes involved by metastatic tumor (3/8).
  + Comment: Gastrointestinal neuroectodermal tumor is a rare malignancy that can metastasize distantly and has a poor prognosis. Immunohistochemical stains show the tumor is positive for S100 and synaptophysin.

Differential diagnosis

* [Alveolar rhabdomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuealvrhabdo.html):
  + Positive for [myogenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html) and [myoD1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyod1.html), harbors t(2;13) translocation
* [Clear cell sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueclearcellsarcoma.html):
  + Positive for [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html) and [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html)
  + May metastasize to gastrointestinal tract or possibly arise there
* [Gastrointestinal stromal tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelGIST.html):
  + Positive for [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html) and [DOG1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsDOG1.html)
* [Synovial sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesynovialsarc.html):
  + Positive for [keratins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), harbors t(X;18) translocation

Additional references

Board review style question #1

Which of the following is true about malignant gastrointestinal neuroectodermal tumor?

1. It can demonstrate a t(12;22) *EWSR1-ATF1* translocation
2. It most commonly occurs in the colon
3. Its preferred site of metastasis is the lungs
4. It stains positive for S100 and cytokeratin by immunohistochemistry
5. Most patients are in their sixth decade or older

Board review style answer #1

**A.** It can demonstrate a t(12;22) *EWSR1-ATF1* translocation
