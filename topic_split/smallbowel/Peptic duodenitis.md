Definition / general

* Histologic findings indicative of duodenal mucosa injury as a result of chronic exposure to increased gastric secretion

Essential features

* Primarily involves proximal duodenum
* Histologic features include gastric foveolar metaplasia and Brunner gland hyperplasia
* Usually mild and regresses with treatment

Terminology

* Also termed chronic nonspecific duodenitis

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [K29.80](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/K00-K95/K20-K31/K29-/K29.80) - duodenitis without bleeding

Epidemiology

* Median age: 58 years ([Hum Pathol 2010;41:1593](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20656325))
* Foveolar metaplasia can be seen in up to 26.3% of patient population ([Lab Invest 2016;96 Suppl 1:187A](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26813171))

Sites

* Duodenum, usually proximal segment

Pathophysiology

* Chronic exposure of the duodenal mucosa to excessive gastric acidity in proportion to duodenal bicarbonate contents, resulting in injury to the lining mucosa ([Monogr Pathol 1990;31:69](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2406581))

Etiology

* Medications (in particular NSAIDs) ([Aliment Pharmacol Ther 2012;36:48](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22554233))
* *Helicobacter pylori* infection (though recent data suggests *H. pylori* might not be implicated as much as it was believed) ([Hum Pathol 2010;41:1593](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20656325), [Lab Invest 2016;96 Suppl 1:187A](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26813171))
* Idiopathic ([Aliment Pharmacol Ther 2013;38:946](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23981105))

Clinical features

* May be asymptomatic (incidental finding) or cause dyspepsia, abdominal pain, hematemesis
* In advanced cases: symptoms related to gastric outlet obstruction or acute abdominal pain (clinical features of peritonitis) secondary to duodenal perforation

Diagnosis

* Endoscopy with biopsy

Laboratory

* Negative celiac sprue serology (antitissue transglutaminase, endomysial antibodies, deamidated gliadin peptide)

Endoscopic findings

* Mucosal erythema
* Mucosal nodularity ([Mod Pathol 2005;18:1134](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15803187))
* Mucosal erosion and ulceration

Prognostic factors

* Good prognosis as most cases regress with treatment

Case reports

* 56 year old man with foveolar gastric metaplasia of atypical appearance ([J Med Case Rep 2016;10:355](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27998304))
* 72 year old man with duodenal obstruction secondary to Brunner gland hyperplasia ([Pathologica 2017;109:414](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29449737))
* 80 year old man with carcinoma arising from gastric foveolar metaplasia in the duodenum after 9 years of observation ([Clin J Gastroenterol 2018;11:391](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29605945))

Treatment

* Stop the offending medication
* Proton pump inhibitors
* Treat underlying infection, like *H. pylori* ([Histopathology 2006;48:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16487363))
* Surgical intervention for perforated peptic duodenitis or gastric outlet obstruction

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Foveolar metaplasia of the surface duodenal epithelium
* Brunner gland hyperplasia (Brunner glands seen above the muscularis mucosae)
* Expansion of the lamina propria by mixed inflammatory cell infiltrate, including few neutrophils that usually do not infiltrate the epithelium
* Mildly increased intraepithelial lymphocytes, usually corresponding to Marsh 1 lesion ([Mod Pathol 2005;18:1134](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15803187))
* Mild villous blunting can be seen ([World J Gastroenterol 2005;11:686](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15655822))
* Severe cases may show mucosal erosion, ulceration or regenerative changes, like mucin depletion, nuclear hyperchromasia and increased mitotic activity
* *H. pylori* very rarely present in metaplastic epithelium

Peptic duodenitis

Sample pathology report

* Duodenum (D1 / D2), biopsy:
  + Duodenal mucosa with preserved villous architecture and gastric metaplasia, suggestive of peptic injury

Differential diagnosis

* [Celiac sprue](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelceliacsprue.html):
  + Positive celiac serology
  + Marked increased intraepithelial lymphocytes and prominent villous blunting
  + Improvement with gluten elimination
  + May also show concomitant peptic injury / gastric metaplasia
* [Crohn’s disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelcrohns.html):
  + Nonnecrotizing granulomas
  + Involvement of other locations in the gastrointestinal tract

Additional references

* [J Clin Pathol 1997;50:54](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9059358), [Can J Gastroenterol Hepatol 2019;2019:6340565](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31187029)

Board review style question #1

Which of the following histologic findings are most likely to be encountered in peptic duodenitis?

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/smallbowelduodenitisMostafa01.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/smallbowelduodenitisMostafa01.jpg)

1. Cryptitis and crypt abscess formation
2. Lamina propria expansion and nonnecrotizing granulomas
3. Marked intraepithelial lymphocytosis and moderate villous blunting
4. Scattered lymphoid follicles in the lamina propria
5. Surface foveolar metaplasia and Brunner gland hyperplasia

Board review style answer #1

**E.** Surface foveolar metaplasia and Brunner gland hyperplasia
