Definition / general
  • Well differentiated neuroendocrine tumors of the jejunum and ileum are covered by this staging system
  • Not covered by this staging system are well differentiated neuroendocrine tumors of the ampulla or duodenum (use ampulla staging) or poorly differentiated neuroendocrine carcinomas of the small intestine (use small bowel carcinoma staging)
Essential features
  • AJCC 7th edition staging was sunset on December 31, 2017; as of January 1, 2018, use of the 8th edition is mandatory
  • AJCC 9th edition was released in 2023, with minimal changes from the 8th edition
ICD coding
  • ICD-10
    • C7A.011 - malignant carcinoid tumor of the jejunum
    • C7A.012 - malignant carcinoid tumor of the ileum
Primary tumor (pT)
  • TX: primary tumor cannot be assessed
  • T0: no evidence of primary tumor
  • T1: tumor invades lamina propria or submucosa and is ≤ 1 cm in greatest dimension
  • T2: tumor invades muscularis propria or is > 1 cm in greatest dimension
  • T3: tumor invades through the muscularis propria into subserosal tissue without penetration of overlying serosa
  • T4: tumor invades visceral peritoneum (serosa) or other organs or adjacent structures
Regional lymph nodes (pN)
  • NX: regional lymph nodes cannot be assessed
  • N0: no tumor involvement of regional lymph node(s)
  • N1: tumor involvement of < 12 regional lymph nodes
  • N2: tumor involvement of large mesenteric masses (> 2 cm) or extensive nodal deposits (12 or greater), especially those that encase the superior mesenteric vessels

Notes:
  • Regional lymph nodes include superior mesenteric and mesenteric nodes; posterior cecal nodes also apply for terminal ileum lesions
  • Mesenteric masses ≤ 2 cm should be stated in the pathology report as being present and collected by registrars but do not affect stage
  • 2 cm cutoff for including mesenteric masses in N categorization may be suboptimal (Mod Pathol 2018;31:1560)
Distant metastasis (pM)
  • cM0: no distant metastasis
  • cM1: distant metastasis
    • cM1a: metastasis confined to liver
    • cM1b: metastasis in at least 1 extrahepatic site (e.g., lung, ovary, nonregional lymph node, peritoneum, bone)
    • cM1c: both hepatic and extrahepatic metastasis
  • pM1: microscopic confirmation of distant metastasis
    • pM1a: microscopic confirmation of metastasis confined to liver
    • pM1b: microscopic confirmation of metastasis in at least 1 extrahepatic site (e.g., lung, ovary, nonregional lymph node, peritoneum, bone)
    • pM1c: microscopic confirmation of both hepatic and extrahepatic metastasis
Prefixes
  • c: clinical
  • p: pathological
  • yc: posttherapy clinical
  • yp: posttherapy pathological
Primary tumor suffix
  • (m): multiple synchronous primary tumors
Regional lymph nodes suffix
  • (f): fine needle aspiration (FNA) or core needle biopsy
AJCC prognostic stage groups
Stage I:  T1  N0  M0
Stage II:  T2, T3  N0  M0
Stage III:  T4  N0  M0
Stage III:  any T  N1, N2  M0
Stage IV:  any T  any N  M1
Prognostic tumor characteristics
  • Ki67 index
  • Mitotic count
  • Echocardiogram (carcinoid heart disease)
  • Plasma or urinary 5-hydroxyindoleacetic acid (5-HIAA) level
  • Mesenteric fibrosis
Registry data collection variables
  • Procedure
  • Tumor site (jejunum or ileum or both or small intestine, NOS)
  • Size of tumor (value or unknown)
  • Tumor focality (unifocal or multifocal, number of tumors / unknown)
  • Depth of invasion
  • Lymphovascular invasion
  • Perineural invasion
  • Margin status
  • Lymph nodal status (including number of lymph nodes assessed and number of positive nodes)
  • Large mesenteric masses (> 2 cm)
  • Mesenteric tumor deposits (≤ 2 cm)
  • Mesenteric vascular elastosis
  • Sites of metastasis, if applicable
  • Grade (from Ki67 or mitotic count): G1, G2, G3, unknown
  • Ki67 index (value, unknown)
  • Mitotic count (value, unknown)
  • Tumor necrosis
  • Presence of somatostatin receptor expression (usually determined by using somatostatin receptor imaging such as PET)
Emerging factors for data collection
  • Somatostatin receptor type 2 (SSTR2) positivity
Histologic grade (G)
  • G1: mitotic rate 2 and Ki67 G2: mitotic rate 2 - 20 per 2 mm2 or Ki67 3 - 20%
  • G3: mitotic rate > 20 per 2 mm2 or Ki67 > 20%
Histopathologic type
  • Neuroendocrine tumor, NOS
  • Neuroendocrine tumor, grade 1
  • Neuroendocrine tumor, grade 2
  • Neuroendocrine tumor, grade 3
  • Enterochromaffin cell (EC cell) neuroendocrine tumor
Residual tumor (operative factor)
  • R0: complete resection, margins histologically negative, no residual tumor left after resection
  • R1: incomplete resection, margins histologically involved, microscopic tumor remains after resection of gross disease (relevant to resection margins that are microscopically involved by tumor)
  • R2: incomplete resection, margins involved or gross disease remains
Board review style question #1
A patient undergoes resection for jejunal neuroendocrine tumors. 3 tumors are present (one smaller than 1 cm; one larger than 1 cm and invading the muscularis propria; one larger than 1 cm and invading the subserosa). Using AJCC 9th edition criteria, how would the pathologic T category be reported for this patient?

  1. pT1 / T2 / T3
  2. pT1(1)T2(1)T2(1)
  3. pT3(m)
  4. Separate complete staging for each primary focus
Board review style answer #1
C. pT3(m). With 9th edition AJCC criteria, (m) is used as a suffix to denote multiple primary tumors. The pT category staging of the most advanced tumor should also be provided. Answers A, B and D are incorrect because they provide notation styles incompatible with current AJCC guidelines.

Board review style question #2
Which of the following is true regarding pN category staging of jejunal and ileal well differentiated neuroendocrine tumors?

  1. Mesenteric tumor deposits larger than 2 cm indicate pN1c disease
  2. Mesenteric tumor deposits smaller than 2 cm should not be mentioned in the signout report
  3. pN1 disease indicates involvement of fewer than 6 regional lymph nodes
  4. Upstaging a tumor from pN1 to pN2 does not affect the overall stage grouping
Board review style answer #2
D. Upstaging a tumor from pN1 to pN2 does not affect the overall stage grouping. pT(any)N1M0 and pT(any)N2M0 tumors are both stage 3 overall. Answer A is incorrect because mesenteric tumor deposits larger than 2 cm would indicate pN2 disease. pN1c disease is not a designation for these tumors but is used for tumor deposits without nodal metastases in colorectal adenocarcinomas. Answer B is incorrect because these smaller deposits should be included in the report, at least for registry purposes. Answer C is incorrect because the cutoff for pN1 disease is 12 regional lymph nodes, not 6.
