Definition / general

* Rare mesenchymal tumor of borderline malignant potential and uncertain histogenesis, characterized by solid nodules of epithelioid to spindled cells, blood filled pseudoangiomatous spaces, a fibrous pseudocapsule and a lymphoplasmacytic cuff toward the periphery

Essential features

* Syncytial-like sheets of epithelioid to ovoid cells
* Pseudoangiomatous blood filled spaces
* Peripheral fibrous pseudocapsule and lymphoid tissue

Terminology

* Originally described as angiomatoid malignant fibrous histiocytoma by Franz M. Enzinger in 1979 ([Cancer 1979;44:2147](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/228836))

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8836/1](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=81) - angiomatoid fibrous histiocytoma
* ICD-11: [2F7C & XH9362](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f1369250201?data=%7B%22dataType%22%3A%22pc%22%2C%22postcoordinationCodeSet%22%3A%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F1369250201%22%2C%22axisToValueIds%22%3A%7B%22specificAnatomy%22%3A%5B%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F875428322%22%7D%5D%7D%7D%7D) - neoplasms of uncertain behavior of connective or other soft tissue & angiomatoid fibrous histiocytoma

Epidemiology

* Relatively rare entity, comprising 0.3% of all soft tissue tumors
* More common in the first 2 decades of life without any sex predilection
* Has been reported in a wide age range, extending from congenital presentation to the ninth decade ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2015;139:674](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25927151))
* Patients with extrasomatic soft tissue sites show higher mean age

Sites

* Superficial soft tissues of extremities, followed by the trunk and the head and neck

Pathophysiology

* Not known

Etiology

* Originally described as a fibrohistiocytic tumor; however, WHO classification has kept it in the list of tumors of uncertain differentiation since no convincing ultrastructural or immunohistochemical evidence has been found to support this theory ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2015;139:674](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25927151))

Clinical features

* Predominantly presents as a slow growing, nontender, superficial, subcutaneous nodule in extremities, followed by the trunk and the head and neck
* In the extremities, almost 66% of these tumors affect lymph node bearing regions, such as popliteal fossa, antecubital fossa, axilla, inguinal area and neck
* Nonsomatic soft tissue sites of origin are increasingly being reported, including mediastinum, lung, bone, brain, gynecological tract, omentum and retroperitoneum ([Mod Pathol 2011;24:1560](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21822206), [Br J Neurosurg 2021;35:233](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29540076))
* Often clinically mistaken with a vascular neoplasm or a hematoma
* Constitutional symptoms are uncommon and are linked to cytokine production by tumor

Diagnosis

* Diagnosis can be challenging due to nonspecific clinical, histological and immunohistochemical features compounded by rarity of this tumor
* Key diagnostic histological features include:
  + Thick, fibrous pseudocapsule
  + Dense pericapsular cuff of lymphoplasmacytic cells
  + Presence of pseudoangiomatous / pseudovascular spaces
  + Syncytial proliferation of bland epithelioid to spindle shaped cells
  + Supportive immunohistochemical features: desmin+, CD99+, EMA+, CD68+
* Reference: [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2015;139:674](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25927151)

Radiology description

* Circumscribed, lobulated mass
* Cystic change with multiple areas of fluid - fluid levels
* Signal characteristics and enhancement are presumed to be variable subject to the cellular, myxoid, collagenous and vascular content of the lesion
* Heterogeneous intermediate to low signal intensity in T1 weighted image (T1WI) and heterogeneous high signal intensity in T2 weighted / short T1 inversion recovery (STIR) with the hemorrhagic component
* After administration of IV contrast, enhancement of the soft tissue component within the lesion is seen with no enhancement of cystic components ([Cureus 2021;13:e16465](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34422494))

Prognostic factors

* Morphological features of this tumor cannot predict the outcome
* Most behave in an indolent fashion
* Local recurrences have been reported in up to 15% of cases
* Most recurrent cases show positive margins, deeper presentations and extrasomatic locations
* BMC Musculoskelet Disord 2017;18:31)

Case reports

* 7 year old boy presented with angiomatoid fibrous histiocytoma and Castleman disease ([Pediatr Blood Cancer 2021;68:e28590](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32672859))
* 8 year old boy with left thigh angiomatoid fibrous histiocytoma having arterial vascular channels within the tumor and feeding branch from the left profunda femoris artery ([BJR Case Rep 2020;7:20190069](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33841896))
* 10 year old boy and 11 year old girl with intracranial angiomatoid fibrous histiocytoma presented with history of seizures ([World Neurosurg 2020;143:398](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32777394))
* 10 year old boy with subcutaneous pleomorphic variant of angiomatoid fibrous histiocytoma with multiplication of *EWSR1::CREB1* fusion gene ([Pathol Oncol Res 2012;18:545](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22038502))
* 13 year old girl with angiomatoid fibrous histiocytoma on left angle of mandible ([Int J Paediatr Dent 2006;16:363](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16879335))
* 20 year old man presented with angiomatoid fibrous histiocytoma in the cervical spinal canal ([Br J Neurosurg 2020 Dec 7 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33284054))
* 50 year old woman with a falcine angiomatoid fibrous histiocytoma having diffuse rhabdoid morphology and focal high mitotic activity mimicking rhabdoid meningioma ([Brain Tumor Pathol 2021;38:138](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33432531))

Treatment

* Wide local excision with follow up is the treatment of choice
* Irresectable and recurrent cases may be treated by adjuvant radiotherapy and or chemotherapy ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2015;139:674](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25927151))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Thick, fibrous pseudocapsule with a dense pericapsular cuff of lymphoplasmacytic cells (with or without lymphoid follicles), seen in up to 80% of cases
* Pseudocapsule imparts a low power resemblance to a lymph node involved by a metastatic tumor; absence of subcapsular sinus helps differentiate it from a lymph node
* Circumscribed, often lobulated with syncytial sheets, short fascicles or whorls of epithelioid (histiocyte-like) to spindle shaped cells with moderate amount of eosinophilic cytoplasm, mildly pleomorphic vesicular nuclei and inconspicuous nucleoli
* Presence of pseudoangiomatous / pseudovascular spaces or blood filled lakes lined by tumor cells is an important diagnostic feature but may be absent in up to 33% of cases showing a solid growth ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2015;139:674](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25927151))
* Mitotic activity is usually low
* Interspersed inflammatory cells are commonly seen
* Hemosiderin deposition may be prominent
* May have areas of nuclear pleomorphism and atypical mitoses
* Morphological variations:
  + Small round blue cell tumor-like morphology
  + Myxoid change particularly in intracranial ones ([World Neurosurg 2019;126:113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30831299))
  + Pulmonary edema-like pattern
  + Microcystic pattern
  + Myxoid chondrosarcoma-like pattern (cord-like arrangement against myxoid stroma)
  + Marked sclerosis
  + Amianthoid fibers
  + Rhabdoid features ([Brain Tumor Pathol 2021;38:138](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33432531))
  + Schwannoma-like features (nuclear palisading and hyalinized vessels)

Cytology description

* Cytological diagnosis of angiomatoid fibrous histiocytoma is challenging due to its rarity and nonspecific features
* Smears are usually moderately cellular and show spindle shaped to ovoid (histiocyte-like to fibroblast-like) cells in cohesive clusters or dispersed cells ([Diagn Cytopathol 2021;49:E36](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32749779))
* Nuclei show mild to moderate pleomorphism with vesicular chromatin
* Nuclear grooves or intranuclear inclusions may be present
* Background shows blood with lymphocytes and plasma cells

Positive stains

* No specific immunohistochemical features
* Focal or diffuse [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) (40 - 50%)
* [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html) (50%), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) (40%), [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html) (45%)
* [SOX9](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox9.html) (85%) ([Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2020;28:635](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31567275))
* [CD21](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd21.html) (focal, rarely)
* [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) is usually low (2 - 4%)

Negative stains

* [Smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) (14%), [calponin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalponin.html) (12%), [caldesmon](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscaldesmon.html) (3%) ([Hum Pathol 1999;30:1336](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10571514))
* [Myogenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html), [MyoD1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyod1.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [CD35](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30to39.html#cd35), [ERG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsERG.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2015;139:674](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25927151))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Most cases show *FET* family gene rearrangement (mostly *EWSR1* or less commonly *FUS* gene rearrangement)
* FISH for *EWSR1* rearrangement is useful diagnostically
* Partner gene is mostly from the *CREB* family (*CREB1, ATF1* or *CREM*)
* Any of the following 3 characteristic translocations can be seen:
  + t(2;22)(q33;q12) creating *EWSR1::CREB1* fusion gene (90% cases)
  + t(12;22)(q13;q12) creating *EWSR1::ATF1* fusion gene (more common in extrasomatic tumors); identical translocation also seen in clear cell sarcoma of soft tissue
  + t(12;16)(q13;p11) creating *FUS::ATF1* fusion gene (least common) ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2007;46:1051](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17724745), [Clin Cancer Res 2007;13:7322](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18094413), [Hum Pathol 2011;42:1359](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21411119))
* Some intracranial myxoid spindle cell tumors with *EWSR1::CREB* family gene fusion are described, which lack the features typical of angiomatoid fibrous histiocytoma; it is not clear whether they are subtypes of angiomatoid fibrous histiocytoma or belong to a novel entity ([Brain Pathol 2018;28:183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28281318))

Brief overview of morphology

Sample pathology report

* Right upper thigh, wide local excision and inguinal lymph node dissection:
  + Angiomatoid fibrous histiocytoma (see comment)
  + Tumor size: 5 x 4 x 2.5 cm
  + Lymphovascular invasion: absent
  + Margins of excision: all margins of excision are tumor free (give distances from each margin)
  + Skin: tumor free
  + Lymph nodes: all 06 lymph nodes are tumor free
  + Comment: Angiomatoid fibrous histiocytoma is considered as a borderline malignancy of uncertain histogenesis. It may recur in up to 15% cases and rarely can metastasize. Clinical follow up at adequate interval is advised.
  + FISH studies to detect *EWSR1* rearrangement may be helpful for confirmation of diagnosis (optional comment for difficult cases)

Differential diagnosis

* [Organized hematoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuehematoma.html):
  + No atypical spindle cells
  + No pseudocapsule or lymphoplasmacytic cuff
  + Negative for [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html)
* [Nodal metastasis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodesmetastases.html):
  + Metastatic melanoma in particular can mimic angiomatoid fibrous histiocytoma
  + Arises in regions populated by lymph nodes
  + True nodal architecture present
  + Subcapsular and medullary sinuses
  + History of malignancy may or may not be present
  + Relevant IHC may help
* [Granulomatous inflammation in a lymph node](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodesgranulomatous.html):
  + Lacks solid growth pattern of angiomatoid fibrous histiocytoma
  + Granulomas are usually dispersed and discrete or irregular and confluent
  + Large granulomas often show central necrosis and lack cystic hemorrhage
  + May contain foreign body type giant cells
  + Predisposing factors may be noted clinically (exposure to infectious agents, etc.)
  + [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html)+, [CD168](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd160.html#cd168)+
  + Negative for [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html)
* [Aneurysmal benign fibrous histiocytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuebfh.html):
  + Morphologic variant of benign fibrous histiocytoma (dermatofibroma)
  + Dermal localization, often with overlying epidermal changes
  + No pseudocapsule or lymphoplasmacytic cuff
  + Peripheral collagen trapping often present
  + Mixed cell population, including chronic inflammatory cells, siderophages and giant cells
  + Negative for [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html)
* [Follicular dendritic cell sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphnodesFDCsarcoma.html):
  + Most arise in lymph nodes
  + Cervical lymph nodes are more commonly affected
  + Infiltrating lymphocytes are common
  + Binucleation and multinucleation often seen
  + [CD21](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd21.html)+, [CD23](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd23.html)+, [CD35](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30to39.html#cd35)+, [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)+
  + Negative for [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html)
* [Kaposi sarcoma (nodular)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuekaposi.html):
  + May involve lymph nodes
  + Predisposing factors often present (AIDS history)
  + Moderately cellular neoplasm of spindled endothelial cells
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)+, [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html)+, [ERG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsERG.html)+, [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html)+, [HHV8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshhv8.html)+
  + Negative for [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html)
* [Spindle cell hemangioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocytichemangiomaspindle.html):
  + Distal extremities, particularly acral
  + No pseudocapsule or lymphoplasmacytic cuff
  + Resembles cavernous hemangioma with cellular septa composed of spindle cells
  + [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html)+, [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)+, [ERG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsERG.html)+, [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html)+
  + Negative for [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html)
* [Inflammatory myofibroblastic tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueinflammyofibro.html):
  + Mostly in abdominal soft tissue
  + No pseudocapsule or lymphoplasmacytic cuff
  + Lacks pseudovascular spaces
  + More commonly myxoid
  + Eosinophils and neutrophils are common
  + [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html)+, [ALK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html)+ in 50 - 60%
* [Intranodal palisaded myofibroblastoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueintranodalpalisaded.html):
  + Occurs in lymph nodes, predominantly in inguinal region
  + Common in males
  + Fascicles of spindle cells with nuclear palisading
  + Amianthoid fibers are characteristic
  + Lacks pseudovascular spaces
  + [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html)+; negative for [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html)
