Definition / general

* Clear cell sarcoma (CCS) is a malignant soft tissue sarcoma composed of monotonous epithelioid and spindle cells with clear to eosinophilic cytoplasm characterized by melanocytic differentiation and *EWSR1-ATF1 / CREB1* rearrangement

Essential features

* Young adult, M:F = 1, distal extremity, slow growing
* Not related to epidermis (hence, tendon and aponeuroses)
* Do not confuse with clear cell sarcoma of kidney
* Sheets and nests of monotonous epithelioid spindle cells with clear to eosinophilic cytoplasm separated by a delicate framework of fibrocollageneous stroma
* Scattered Touton type or wreath-like multinucleated giant cells are relatively common
* S100 / SOX10+, Melan A+, HMB45+
* *EWSR1-ATF1* or *EWSR1-CREB1*

Terminology

* Melanoma of soft parts (not recommended)
* Clear cell sarcoma of tendon and aponeuroses
* Clear cell sarcoma of soft parts

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9044/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=84) - clear cell sarcoma, NOS (except of kidney [M-8964/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=83))
* ICD-10: [C49.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C45-C49/C49-/C49.9) - malignant neoplasm of connective and soft tissue, unspecified
* ICD-11: [2B5K & XH77N6](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f1544901926?data=%7B%22dataType%22%3A%22pc%22%2C%22postcoordinationCodeSet%22%3A%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F1544901926%22%2C%22axisToValueIds%22%3A%7B%22specificAnatomy%22%3A%5B%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F994012326%22%7D%5D%7D%7D%7D) - unspecified malignant soft tissue tumors or sarcomas of bone or articular cartilage of other or unspecified sites & clear cell sarcoma of soft tissue

Epidemiology

* Young adults (third decade)
* No gender predilection

Sites

* Foot and ankle > hand > thigh > trunk > head & neck
* 75% distal extremities
* Rarely located in penis, mediastinum, lung, retroperitoneum, abdomen and bone
* References: [J Clin Pathol 2010;63:416](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20418233), [Dermatol Res Pract 2012;2012:984096](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22693489), [Eur J Surg Oncol 2014;40:505](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24560887)

Pathophysiology

* Microphthalmia associated transcription factor (MITF) seems to be critical oncogenic target of *EWSR1-ATF1* in CCS leading to melanocytic phenotype but not in angiomatoid fibrous histiocytoma with the same *EWSR1-ATF1* rearrangement ([Cancer Cell 2006;9:473](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16766266), [Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:e1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22510762))
* Regarding gene expression profile, CCSs cluster with melanomas, not soft tissue sarcomas ([J Clin Oncol 2003;21:1775](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12721254))
* Classified as tumor of uncertain differentiation in WHO 2020

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Not uncommon to get a long history (prebiopsy duration)
* Pain and mass are common presenting symptoms

Diagnosis

* Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI)
* Biopsy and molecular pathology

Radiology description

* Well defined, homogeneous and benign looking mass
* Magnetic resonance imaging
  + Homogeneous iso hyperintense on T1 and heterogeneous on T2 with variable contrast enhancement
* Reference: [Skeletal Radiol 2000;29:187](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10855466)

Prognostic factors

* High recurrence rate (up to 40%)
* Metastasis to lung and lymph nodes in 30 - 50% of patients ([1999 Sep;86:969](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10491522), [J Cancer Res Clin Oncol 2018;144:1711](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29961184), [Cancer 1983;52:1482](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6616410), [Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:452](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18300804), [Am J Surg Pathol 1992;16:1197](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1463095))
* 5 year survival 60%, 10 year survival 40%, 20 year survival 10% ([J Clin Pathol 2010;63416](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20418233), [Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:452](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18300804), [Dermatol Res Pract 2012;2012:984096](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22693489), [J Cancer Res Clin Oncol 2018;144:1711](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29961184))
* Poor prognostic factors: Tumor size > 5 cm, necrosis, recurrence and lymph node metastases ([Dermatol Res Pract 2012;2012:984096](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22693489), [1999 Sep;86:969](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10491522), [Cancer 1983;52:1482](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6616410), [Am J Surg Pathol 1992;16:1197](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1463095), [Cancer 1990;65:367](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2295060))

Case reports

* 32 year old man with penile mass metastatic to the lymph nodes and bone ([Am J Clin Exp Urol 2015;3:43](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26069887))
* 33 year old man and 37 year old woman with clear cell sarcoma in unusual sites mimicking metastatic melanoma ([World J Clin Oncol 2019;10:213](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31205866))
* 36 year old man with distal femoral clear cell sarcoma ([BMC Musculoskelet Disord 2021;22:99](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33478436))
* 43 year old woman with a neck mass ([Balkan Med J 2018;35:203](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29072181))
* 57 year old man with primary mediastinal CCS ([Diagn Pathol 2017;12:5](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28086809))
* 80 year old woman with mass in large toe ([Cureus 2020;12:e11719](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33391950))

Treatment

* Complete surgical excision with or without radiotherapy
* Chemotherapy / immunotherapy has no effect

Clear cell sarcoma of soft tissue

Frozen section description

* Sentinel lymph node can be encountered
* Melanoma is the main differential diagnosis and may be impossible to separate in this setting

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Essential: nests of epithelioid spindle cells with clear eosinophilic cytoplasm and prominent nucleoli plus melanocytic differentiation
* Morphological patterns ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:452](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18300804), [J Clin Pathol 2010;63:416](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20418233)):
  + Vaguely organoid (neuroendocrine-like) pattern: nests and short fascicles of epithelioid or spindle cells, surrounded by a delicate framework of fibrocollagenous tissue contiguous with the adjacent tendons and aponeurosis
  + Diffuse pattern: solid sheets of epithelioid to spindle cells
  + Pseudoalveolar pattern: reminiscent of alveolar soft part sarcoma
  + Myxoid / microcystic pattern
  + Inflammatory pattern: reminiscent of seminoma
* Cells:
  + Epithelioid to spindled
  + Monotonous round to oval nuclei with prominent nucleoli
  + Lightly eosinophilic to amphophilic cytoplasm
  + Clear cells typically comprise only a subset of the tumor
  + Scattered rhabdoid and pleomorphic cells
  + Scattered Touton type or wreath-like multinucleated giant cells are relatively common
  + Rare mitosis
* Necrosis usually focally in 33%
* Melanin pigment in scattered cells in half of the cases
* No involvement of epidermis or no epidermal melanocytic proliferation but longstanding lesions (especially at fingers) may enlarge and therefore tumors may ulcerate

Cytology description

* Hypercellular smears composed of monomorphic epithelioid / polygonal cells, including singly scattered or loosely clustered spindle cells, with prominent nucleoli
* Granular to vacuolated, well defined cytoplasm
* Binucleation / multinucleation (50%), abrupt anisonucleosis, racquet shaped cells (20%)
* Tigroid background (10%)
* Focal intracytoplasmic pigment (10%)
* If available, immunohistochemical stains and molecular testing are crucial in reinforcing a diagnosis ([Cytopathology 2020;31:280](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32356379))

Electron microscopy description

* Resembles synovial sarcoma given the presence of basement membrane material, a biphasic pattern of cells and the presence of pseudoglandular structures with filopodia
* Presence of melanosomes and interdigitating cellular processes wrapping around cells and extracellular structures are similar to melanoma and nerve sheath tumors
* May contain aggregates of glycogen and inclusions composed of multilayered membrane structures forming myelin-like figures
* References: [Am J Pathol 1984;114:264](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6320649), [Virchows Arch A Pathol Anat Histopathol 1983;401:109](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6412444)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* t(12;22)(q13;q12) for *EWSR1-ATF1* [4 splice variants including the most common *EWSR1*(ex8)-*ATF1*(ex4)] in 90 - 95%
* t(2;22)(q32.3;q12) for *EWSR1*(ex7)-*CREB1*(ex7) in 5 - 10% ([Mod Pathol 2009;22:1201](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19561568))
* Aberrations in chr 2, 3, 7, 8, 12, 13, 14, 15, 17, 21 and 22
* Rare *BRAF* V599E and *NRAS* codon 61 mutations ([Dermatol Res Pract 2012;2012:984096](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22693489))

Sample pathology report

* Right toe, incisional biopsy:
  + Clear cell sarcoma of soft tissue (see comment)
  + Comment: Tumor was composed of nests of clear cells with prominent nucleoli and melanocytic differentiation. FISH break apart study revealed the presence of an *EWSR1* rearrangement, which excluded the diagnosis of melanoma. These tumors are definitionally regarded as high grade - grade 3 sarcomas. Wide excision is recommended.

Differential diagnosis

* [Melanoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanoma.html):
  + More common in the elderly
  + With epidermal involvement / component
  + No giant cells
  + *[EWSR1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsewsr1.html)-ATF1 / CREB1* negative
* [Malignant gastrointestinal neuroectodermal tumor (GNET)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/smallbowelmaliggastroneuroectodermaltumor.html):
  + More prominent osteoclast-like giant cells
  + Frequent necrosis and mitosis
  + Typically express only [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) / [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html) and lack expression of mature melanocytic markers, such as [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html), [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html)
  + More common *[EWSR1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsewsr1.html)-CREB1* fusion
* [Epithelioid malignant peripheral nerve sheath tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuempnst.html):
  + [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html)-, [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html)-
  + 50% [INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html) loss
  + *[EWSR1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsewsr1.html)-ATF1 / CREB1* negative
* [Malignant melanotic nerve sheath tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuemmnst.html):
  + Carney syndrome
  + Paraspinal or nerve plexus related location
  + Psammomatous calcifications and abundant melanin pigment
  + Nonnested, syncytial growth pattern
  + *[EWSR1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsewsr1.html)-ATF1 / CREB1* negative
* [Perivascular epithelioid cell neoplasm (PEComa)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusPEComa.html):
  + Less prominent nucleoli
  + No giant cells
  + [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html)+, [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) variable, [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) / [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html)-
* [Cellular blue nevus](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticbluenevus.html):
  + No cytological atypia
  + Dumbbell shaped growth pattern
  + *[EWSR1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsewsr1.html)-ATF1 / CREB1* negative
* [Granular cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuegranularcell.html):
  + No cytological atypia
  + Abundant cytoplasm with coarse granules
  + [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html)-, [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html)-
* [Alveolar soft part sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuealveolarsoft.html):
  + [TFE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstfe3.html)+
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) / [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html)-, [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html)-, [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html)-
* **Paraganglioma**:
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) expression in sustentacular cells (focal)
  + [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html)-, [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html)-
* [Synovial sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesynovialsarc.html):
  + No nested growth
  + [PanCK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)+
* [Epithelioid sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueepithelioidsarcoma.html):
  + [PanCK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)+, [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)-, [INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html) loss
* **Carcinoma**:
  + [PanCK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)+, [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)- (usually)

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/softtissueclearcellsarcomabrqKosem1.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/softtissueclearcellsarcomabrqKosem1.jpg)

An 18 year old woman presented with a 1 cm mass in the right great toe. Morphological features are shown above. Which one of the following immunohistochemical panels is the most helpful for reaching the correct diagnosis?

1. EMA, SMA, INI1
2. HMB45, MelanA, desmin
3. PanCK, INI1, S100
4. PanCK, synaptophysin, CD31
5. SMA, desmin, SOX10

Board review style answer #1

**C.** PanCK, INI1, S100. If you order just HMB45, MelanA, and desmin and all of them turn out to be positive you may end up with the diagnosis of PEComa. PEComa is in the differential, even desmin is negative. So IHC panel should include an S100 or Sox10 and not necessarily HMB45 and MelanA at first. HMB45 and MelanA are better requested after S100/Sox10 positivity. panCK and INI1 are to exclude epithelioid sarcoma. INI1 could be replaced by HMB45 if requested.

Board review style question #2

An 18 year old woman presented with a 1 cm toe mass. There was diffuse S100 expression plus SOX10, HMB45 and MelanA positivity. What is the next step?

1. Diagnose as melanoma
2. Order PAX8 and TFE3
3. Order whole genome sequencing
4. Perform an *EWSR1* break apart FISH
5. Request PCR for *EWSR1-CREB1*

Board review style answer #2

**D.** Perform an *EWSR1* break apart FISH. The differential diagnosis is between melanoma and CCS and *EWSR1* FISH is less time consuming and cheaper than NGS to reach the correct diagnosis.
