Definition / general

* Desmoplastic small round cell tumor (DSRCT) is a malignant mesenchymal neoplasm composed of small round tumor cells associated with prominent stromal desmoplasia, polyphenotypic differentiation and *EWSR1-WT1* gene fusion

Essential features

* Nests of small round cells in a desmoplastic stroma
* Expression of cytokeratin, desmin and WT1 (antibody to carboxy terminus [C terminus])
* *EWSR1-WT1* fusion transcripts

Terminology

* Intra-abdominal desmoplastic round cell tumor

ICD coding

* ICD-0: [8806/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/%0D%0A9789241548496_eng.pdf#page=80) - desmoplastic small round cell tumor
* ICD-11: [2B5F.2 & XH5SN6](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f1132023553?data=%7B%22dataType%22%3A%22pc%22%2C%22postcoordinationCodeSet%22%3A%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F1132023553%22%2C%22axisToValueIds%22%3A%7B%22histopathology%22%3A%5B%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F2009079266%22%7D%5D%7D%7D%7D) - sarcoma, not elsewhere classified of other specified sites & desmoplastic small round cell tumor

Epidemiology

* DSRCT primarily affects children and young adults
* Striking male predominance
* Wide age range (first to fifth decades), with peak incidence in the third decade of life
* Reference: [Clin Cancer Res 2018;24:4865](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29871905),
  [Am J Surg Pathol 1991;15:499](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1709557)

Sites

* Vast majority of patients develop tumors in the abdominal cavity ([Int J Surg Pathol 2016;24:672](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27621277), [Int J Surg Pathol 2017;25:158](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27431755))
* Multiple serosal implants are common
* Clinical presentation outside the abdominal cavity is rare and mainly restricted to the thoracic cavity and paratesticular region ([Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:219](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9042290))
* Rare cases occur in the limbs, head and neck, kidney and brain ([Diagn Pathol 2019;14:43](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31103034), [Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:576](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17414105), [Am J Case Rep 2020;21:e922762](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32655125), [J Cutan Pathol 2020;47:768](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32249945), [J Neurosurg 2015;122:773](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25479120), [Int J Surg Pathol 2019;27:236](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30522375))

Pathophysiology

* Recurrent t(11;22)(p13;q12) translocation leading to formation of the *EWSR1-WT1* fusion gene, which generates a chimeric protein with transcriptional regulatory activity ([Int J Surg Pathol 2016;24:672](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27621277))

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Clinical symptoms are usually related to the primary site of presentation, such as pain, palpable mass, abdominal distention, obstruction and ascites ([J Gastrointest Cancer 2019;50:560](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29282625))
* At initial presentation, 90% of patients have multifocal disease, nodular disease, diffuse peritoneal and omental disease or a combination of these conditions
* Substantial number of patients have diaphragmatic involvement

Diagnosis

* Tissue sampling is the gold standard for a definitive diagnosis
* Fluorescence in situ hybridization (FISH) to detect *EWSR1* rearrangement and reverse transcription polymerase chain reaction (RT-PCR) to assess for *EWSR1-WT1* fusion transcripts are routine diagnostic ancillary tools ([Virchows Arch 2017;471:631](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28748349))
* Both methods (FISH and RT-PCR) complement each other by confirming cases that the other method may not ([Virchows Arch 2017;471:631](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28748349))

Radiology description

* On ultrasound, the DSRCTs appear as lobulated, hypoechoic and heterogeneous masses with increased vascularity ([J Comput Assist Tomogr 2002;26:579](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12218823), [Radiology 1999;210:633](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10207460), [Curr Probl Diagn Radiol 2013;42:26](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23146167))
* On unenhanced CT, DSRCTs appear as hypoattenuating masses involving the peritoneal surfaces, omentum and mesentery ([AJR Am J Roentgenol 2019;212:W45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30673334))
* On MRI, DSRCTs appear as heterogeneous isointense to hypointense masses on T weighted images, compared with skeletal muscle ([AJR Am J Roentgenol 2019;212:W45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30673334))
* Masses may show foci of calcification ([AJR Am J Roentgenol 2019;212:W45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30673334))
* PET / CT images show intense FDG hypermetabolic activity in soft tissue masses ([AJR Am J Roentgenol 2019;212:W45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30673334))

Prognostic factors

* Despite multimodal treatment, DSRCT is associated with dismal outcomes ([J Surg Oncol 2018;117:1759](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29878371))
* Approximately 60 - 70% of patients die due to disease progression, usually within 3 years after diagnosis ([Cancers (Basel) 2021;13:498](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33525546), [J Surg Oncol 2018;117:1759](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29878371))
* Median overall survival varies between 28 - 60 months, with a median disease free survival between 10 - 15 months ([Cancers (Basel) 2021;13:498](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33525546))
* Systemic chemotherapy and surgery are associated with reduced mortality ([J Surg Oncol 2018;117:1759](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29878371))
* Residual macroscopic disease after resection correlates with increased risk of mortality ([J Surg Oncol 2018;117:1759](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29878371))

Case reports

* 8 year old girl with tumor of the kidney ([Diagn Pathol 2020;15:95](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32703229))
* 23 year old woman with ovarian tumor ([Am J Case Rep 2019;20:1675](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31723117))
* 33 year old man with tumor of the tibia ([J Bone Oncol 2019;20:100272](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31871884))
* 35 year old man with tumor of the peritoneal cavity ([Ann R Coll Surg Engl 2020;102:e77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31973562))
* 42 year old man with a painless nodule in his left inguinal region ([World J Surg Oncol 2014;12:227](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25037705))

Treatment

* Current management of DSRCT includes a combination of chemotherapy, radiation and aggressive cytoreductive surgery, plus intraperitoneal hyperthermic chemotherapy ([Cancers (Basel) 2021;13:498](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33525546))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Well defined nests of small round cells separated by desmoplastic stroma ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2006;130:728](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16683894))
* In the more desmoplastic areas, tumor cells may be arranged as thin trabeculae or in a single file fashion ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2006;130:728](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16683894))
* Uniform cells with small hyperchromatic nuclei, inconspicuous nucleoli, scant cytoplasm and indistinct cytoplasmic borders
* Some tumors have larger cells with greater anisonucleosis and dispersed chromatin
* Peripheral palisading is common
* Rosette-like structures and pseudopapillae may be observed ([Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:219](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9042290), [Am J Surg Pathol 1991;15:499](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1709557))
* Rhabdoid cells or cells with clear cytoplasm ([Diagn Pathol 2020;15:95](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32703229))
* DSRCT with variant morphology:
  + Solid pattern
    - Entirely solid morphology, lacking evidence of desmoplastic stroma ([Int J Surg Pathol 2017;25:158](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27431755))
  + Spindle cell pattern
    - Nodules of spindle cells with scant cytoplasm and oval nuclei arranged in short, intersecting fascicles and set in a scant, nondesmoplastic stroma ([Diagnostics (Basel) 2021;11:545](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33803887))
  + Glandular differentiation
    - Extensive glandular differentiation with mucicarmine positive mucin content ([Korean J Pathol 2013;47:182](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23667380))
  + Large cell pattern
    - Predominantly large epithelioid cells with greater pleomorphism mimicking carcinoma ([Ultrastruct Pathol 2000;24:333](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11071572))

Cytology description

* Hypercellular smears of small round cells ([Cancer Cytopathol 2014;122:386](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24639098))

* Nuclear molding usually present
* Scant cytoplasm
* Paranuclear cytoplasmic densities

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Tumors associated with t(11;22)(p13;q12) ([Semin Cancer Biol 2005;15:197](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15826834))
* Chimeric *EWS-WT1* RNA and DNA detected by PCR ([J Clin Oncol 1998;16:3028](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9738572))

Sample pathology report

* Peritoneum, biopsy:
  + Desmoplastic small round cell tumor (see comment)
  + Comment: The tumor is characterized by sharply outlined nests of small round cells surrounded by a prominent desmoplastic stroma. The tumor cells are uniform, with small hyperchromatic nuclei, inconspicuous nucleoli, scant cytoplasm and indistinct cytoplasmic borders. Mitoses are frequent and focal tumor cell necrosis is present. Immunohistochemically, the tumor cells are immunoreactive for keratin AE1 / AE3 and desmin and show strong nuclear expression for WT1 (C terminus), while all of the following are negative: myogenin, MYOD1, S100 and WT1 (N terminus). The findings support the above diagnosis. Despite multimodal treatment, DSRCT is associated with dismal outcomes.

Differential diagnosis

* **Blastemal predominant Wilms tumor**:
  + Overlapping histologic features with DSRCT, including poorly differentiated morphology, desmoplastic stroma and immunoreactivity for desmin and cytokeratin ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:1220](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24832162))
  + Focal triphasic elements and glomeruloid bodies
  + [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html) immunoreactivity with antibodies to both the amino terminus and carboxy terminus
  + Negative for *EWSR1-WT1* rearrangements
* [Ewing sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneewing.html):
  + Classic Ewing sarcoma:
    - Composed of uniform small round cells with round nuclei containing finely stippled chromatin and inconspicuous nucleoli, and scant clear or eosinophilic cytoplasm
  + Atypical Ewing sarcoma:
    - Tumor cells are larger, with prominent nucleoli and irregular nuclear contours
  + [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html) and [NKX2.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsNKX2.html) positive ([Virchows Arch 2014;465:599](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25031013))
  + [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html) (C terminus) negative
  + *EWSR1-FLI1* and *EWSR1-ERG* fusions transcripts
  + Negative for *EWSR1-WT1* rearrangements
* **Rhabdomyosarcoma, [embryonal](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueembryonalrhabdo.html) and [alveolar](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuealvrhabdo.html) variants**:
  + Embryonal rhabdomyosarcoma
    - Composed of primitive mesenchymal cells that show variable degrees of skeletal muscle differentiation
  + Alveolar rhabdomyosarcoma
    - Classic subtype: nests of neoplastic cells arranged in alveolar spaces
    - Solid subtype: sheets of neoplastic cells; nests separated by thin fibrovascular septae but alveoli are not seen
  + [Mogenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html) and [MyoD1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyod1.html) positive
  + [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html) (C terminus) negative
  + *PAX3-FOXO1* and *PAX7-FOXO1* fusion transcripts (alveolar rhabdomyosarcoma) ([Mod Pathol 2021;34:748](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33299109))
  + Negative for *EWSR1-WT1* rearrangements
* [Lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonelymphomageneral.html):
  + Diffuse or nodular growth pattern, large atypical cells with abundant cytoplasm, nuclei with clumped chromatin and prominent nucleoli
  + Positive for [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html), B cell and T cell markers ([Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2013;21:116](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22820658))
  + [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html) (C terminus) negative
  + Negative for *EWSR1-WT1* rearrangements
* **Small cell carcinoma**:
  + Small to medium sized, round to oval cells with minimal cytoplasm, nuclei with finely dispersed chromatic, no distinct nucleoli, nuclear molding, high mitotic rate
  + [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html), [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html), [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html) and [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html) positive ([J Thorac Oncol 2019;14:377](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30572031))
  + [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html) (C terminus) negative
  + Negative for *EWSR1-WT1* rearrangements

Board review style question #1

[![Desmoplastic small round cell](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/testisdesmoplasticsmallAlexiev13.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/testisdesmoplasticsmallAlexiev13.jpg)

A 31 year old man presented with abdominal distention and multiple tumor nodules studding the peritoneal surface. Hematoxylin eosin stains demonstrate well defined nests of small round cells separated by desmoplastic stroma.
