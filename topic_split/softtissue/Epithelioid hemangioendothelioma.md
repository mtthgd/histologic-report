Definition / general

* Malignant endothelial neoplasm that most commonly involves soft tissue, bone, lung, skin and liver; can be locally aggressive and has metastatic potential
* 2 subtypes defined by *WWTR1-CAMTA1* or *YAP1-TFE3* rearrangement; former expressing CAMTA1 and latter TFE3 (less specific)

Essential features

* Endothelial neoplasm that most commonly involves soft tissue, bone, lung, skin and liver
* Locally aggressive tumor with metastatic potential
* *WWTR1-CAMTA1* rearranged tumors: composed of cords or small nests of large endothelial cells with abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm embedded in a myxohyaline stroma; CAMTA1 positive staining
* *YAP1-TFE3* rearranged tumors: composed of solid nests or pseudo alveolar formations of epithelioid cells enmeshed in a fibrous stroma; TFE3 positive staining

* ICD-O: [9133/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=86) - epithelioid hemangioendothelioma, malignant
* ICD-10: [D18.0](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D18-/D18.0) - hemangioma
* ICD-11: [2B5Y & XH9GF8](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f1965082709%2fmms%2fother?data=%7B%22dataType%22%3A%22pc%22%2C%22postcoordinationCodeSet%22%3A%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F1965082709%2Fmms%2Fother%22%2C%22axisToValueIds%22%3A%7B%22specificAnatomy%22%3A%5B%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F1014962038%22%7D%5D%7D%7D%7D) - epithelioid hemangioendothelioma, NOS

Epidemiology

* Wide age range (0 - 93 years)
* Usually adults, 60% women ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:924](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18551749))

Sites

* Any location
* Most commonly: soft tissue, bone, lung, skin and liver
* Often multifocal: bone and visceral lesions ([Diagn Pathol 2014;9:131](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24986479), [Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:363](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9130982))
* In up to 50% of cases, closely associated with or arising from a vein

Pathophysiology

* Gene fusion driven (see [Molecular / cytogenetics description](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/#softtissuehemangioendotheliomaepithelioidmolecular))

Etiology

* Unclear

Clinical features

* Clinical presentation depends on the tumor location, most frequently pain
* If arising from a vein, occurring symptoms caused by vascular occlusion, such as edema or thrombophlebitis
* Recurs locally, may metastasize (usually to lymph nodes and lungs) ([Diagn Pathol 2014;9:131](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24986479), [Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:363](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9130982))

Diagnosis

* Diagnostic workup includes radiology, histology (biopsy, resection) with immunohistochemistry and molecular analysis

Radiology description

* CT scan: poorly circumscribed lesion with ground glass appearance

Prognostic factors

* 13% recur, 20 - 30% metastasize (lung, lymph nodes), 13% die of disease; for lung, mortality is 65% ([Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:363](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9130982))
* High risk (> 3 mitotic figures per 50 high power fields and size > 3 cm) have 5 year disease specific survival of 59% versus 100% for low risk ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:924](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18551749))

Case reports

* 43 year old woman with a 6 year history of severe Crohn's disease ([Int J Surg Pathol 2019;27:423](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30238810))
* 46 year old woman with a 2 month history of right shoulder and arm pain ([Am J Case Rep 2019;20:864](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31209195))
* 55 year old man with thigh mass ([Case #77](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case200777.htm))
* 65 year old woman with multiple liver masses ([Case Rep Gastrointest Med 2019;2019:7530845](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31341686))

Treatment

* Wide local excision

Gross description

* Poorly circumscribed, firm, white-tan mass ([Diagn Pathol 2014;9:131](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24986479))
* Variable size, up to 18 cm

Frozen section description

* Hypercellular tumor composed of groups of epithelioid cells embedded in a myxohyaline stroma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* *WWTR1-CAMTA1* subtype (classic EHE):
  + Cords, strands or small nests of large endothelial cells with abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm embedded in a myxohyaline stroma
  + Tumor cells have vesicular, round to oval, sometimes indented nuclei
  + Some tumor cells have intracytoplasmic, round, clear vacuoles representing small vascular lumina, which may contain erythrocytes
* *YAP-TFE3* subtype:
  + Solid nests or pseudo alveolar arrangement of epithelioid cells enmeshed in a fibrous stroma
  + Tumor cells have abundant, densely eosinophilic cytoplasm and can form vascular spaces
  + Intracytoplasmic vacuoles are rare
* Usually minimal mitotic activity, atypia or necrosis
* Up to 10% of cases exhibit frank malignant features of prominent nuclear pleomorphism, increased mitotic activity, solid growth or necrosis; these tumors resemble epithelioid angiosarcoma and have a more aggressive behavior ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:924](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18551749))

Cytology description

* Clusters of polygonal cells with moderate nuclear atypia ([J Cytol 2012;29:89](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22438631))

Positive stains

* [ERG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsERG.html), [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) (can be negative), [podoplanin (D2-40)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html), [FLI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfli1.html), [von Willebrand factor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvwf.html) ([Diagn Pathol 2014;9:131](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24986479), [Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:363](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9130982))
* [CAMTA1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscamta1.html) in *WWTR1-CAMTA1* rearranged tumors ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:94](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26414223))
* [TFE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstfe3.html) in *YAP-TFE3* rearranged (less specific) ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2013;52:775](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23737213))

Negative stains

* [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) (usually negative) ([Diagn Pathol 2014;9:131](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24986479))
* [Keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) (positive up to 40%) ([Diagn Pathol 2014;9:131](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24986479), [Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:363](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9130982))
* [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) (positive up to 45%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:363](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9130982))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Defined by recurrent *WWTR1-CAMTA1* or *YAP1-TFE3* gene fusion ([Histopathology 2015;67:699](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25817592), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2013;52:775](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23737213))
* Translocations result in:
  + Fusion of *CAMTA1* on 1p36.23 to *WWTR1* on 3q25.1
  + Fusion of exon 1 of *YAP1* and exon 4 of *TFE3*

Sample pathology report

* Right thigh, excision:
  + *WWTR1-CAMTA1* rearranged epithelioid hemangioendothelioma (see comment)
  + Comment: Tumor is composed of cords and small nests of large endothelial cells with abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm embedded in a myxohyaline stroma. Immunohistochemically, the tumor cells are positive for ERG, CD31, CAMTA1, focally positive for keratin and are negative for CD34 and TFE3. The morphology and immunoprofile strongly support the diagnosis of *WWTR1-CAMTA1* rearranged epithelioid hemangioendothelioma.

Differential diagnosis

* [Epithelioid angiosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueangiosarcoma.html):
  + Irregular sinusoidal vascular channels
  + Solid sheets of cells with marked cytological atypia, necrosis and prominent mitotic activity
  + Lack of myxohyalinized stroma
  + [CAMTA1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscamta1.html)-, [TFE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstfe3.html)- by IHC
  + Lacks *WWTR1-CAMTA1* or *YAP1-TFE3* fusion
* [Epithelioid sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueepithelioidsarcoma.html):
  + Distal extremities of young adults
  + Composed of nodules of medium sized epithelioid cells with epithelioid cytoplasm that merge with collagenous stroma
  + Frequent mitosis, central necrosis and hemorrhage
  + [Keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)+ (strong), [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html)-, [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)-, [INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html) lost ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:542](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19033866))
* [Melanoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanoma.html):
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)+, [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html)+, [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html)+, [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html)+
  + [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html)-, [ERG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsERG.html)-
* [Carcinoma / metastatic carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticmetastaticcarcinoma.html):
  + More marked atypia, mitotic activity
  + Usually not angiocentric
  + [Keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)+
  + [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html)-, [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)-
