Definition / general

* Heterogeneous group of neoplasms unified by the presence of globular cytoplasmic inclusions, vesicular nuclei with prominent nucleoli and aggressive behavior
* Response to therapy is poor
* Metastasizes to lung, liver and lymph nodes early in the course of disease

Essential features

* Abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm (due to hyaline globular inclusions of intermediate filaments) with eccentric vesicular nuclei and prominent nucleoli
* Skeletal muscle markers and S100 negative
* Karyotypic rearrangements of 22q11.2 resulting in homozygous inactivation of *SMARCB1 (hSNF5 / INI1)*, with subsequent loss of INI1 nuclear expression by immunohistochemistry

Terminology

* Malignant rhabdoid tumor (synonym)

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8963/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf#page=83) - rhabdoid tumor, NOS
* ICD-11: [2B5F.2 & XH3RF3](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f1132023553?data=%7B%22dataType%22%3A%22pc%22%2C%22postcoordinationCodeSet%22%3A%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F1132023553%22%2C%22axisToValueIds%22%3A%7B%22histopathology%22%3A%5B%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F1850105733%22%7D%5D%7D%7D%7D) - sarcoma, not elsewhere classified of other specified sites & malignant rhabdoid tumor

Epidemiology

* Genuine rhabdoid tumors exclusively belong to the pediatric age group and are very rare
* Adult cases represent expression of a particular phenotype, which can occur in any type of sarcoma or even carcinoma but it is virtually universally associated with an aggressive course
* Mean age at diagnosis < 1 year for tumors with germ line *SMARCB1* alterations and 1.5 years for sporadic tumors ([Crit Rev Oncog 2015;20:199](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26349416))
* M = F

Sites

* Deep soft tissue including limb girdles, retroperitoneum, abdominal cavity, liver, pelvis, neck, paraspinal and perineal regions
* Rare unusual sites include:
  + Intraocular ([J Pediatr Ophthalmol Strabismus 2018;55:e7](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29684225), [J Pediatr Hematol Oncol 2020;42:228](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30608492))
  + Paratesticular ([Cureus 2020;12:e8273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32467817))
  + Intradural ([Childs Nerv Syst 2018;34:165](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28815340))

Pathophysiology

* Mutations in 22q11.2, which cause homozygous inactivation of the *SMARCB1* gene ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2011;50:379](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21412926))
* More recently, has been described as one of the many neoplasms belonging to a group of SW1 / SNF deficient tumors ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2018;35:193](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29397238))

Clinical features

* Clinical presentation is related to the site of involvement
* Intraocular tumors present with proptosis ([J Pediatr Hematol Oncol 2020;42:228](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30608492))
* Deep / visceral involvement causes local pain and pressure symptoms and may result in loss of function of an anatomic site if local neural bundle / plexus is compromised ([Brain Dev 2017;39:717](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28434767))

Diagnosis

* Rhabdoid cell morphology in a pediatric tumor along with loss of INI1
* Requires thorough sampling and immunohistochemical studies to rule out rhabdoid phenotype in another underlying tumor, particularly in adult cases ([Mod Pathol 2016;29:1232](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27338635))
* Demonstration of 22q11.2 mutations in a tumor with well developed rhabdoid morphology ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2011;50:379](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21412926))

Radiology description

* Heterogenous appearance on CT and MRI ([J Pediatr Surg 2018;53:567](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28966010))
* May show cystic areas

Prognostic factors

* Rhabdoid phenotype itself is a poor prognostic factor
* Advanced stage and presentation at < 1 year of age is associated with poorer survival ([Oncologist 2019;24:e551](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30914466))
* 5 year survival rate is < 15% ([Cancer 2007;110:2061](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17828773), [Pediatr Blood Cancer 2008;51:363](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18506766))
* Rhabdoid tumors of liver behave more aggressively ([Blood Cancer 2011;57:423](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21744471))

Case reports

* Infant 36 weeks with bulging parieto-occipital mass ([Head Neck Pathol 2017;11:224](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27632188))
* Infant 37 weeks with echogenic bladder mass ([Urology 2019;123:221](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30359705))
* 1 year old boy presenting with an immobile arm ([Brain Dev 2017;39:717](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28434767))
* 15 year old girl with inguinal mass ([Pediatr Blood Cancer 2019;66:e27784](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31034722))
* Teenager with primary intraocular tumor ([J Pediatr Ophthalmol Strabismus 2018;55:e7](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29684225))
* 65 year old man with tumor of colon and disease free survival ([J Med Case Rep 2018;12:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29452605))

Treatment

* No definite chemotherapy regimen is available; some cases report successful therapy using alkaloids, platinum agents and combinations of chemotherapy, surgery and radiation ([Crit Rev Oncog 2015;20:199](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26349416))
* Targeted therapy is under investigation, utilizing various epigenetic pathways including DNA and histone methylation, histone deacetylation, cell cycle arrest and antimitotic mechanisms ([Crit Rev Oncog 2015;20:199](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26349416))

Frozen section description

* Frozen sections are rarely performed and show sheets of cells with typical rhabdoid appearance and vesicular nuclei with nucleoli

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Tumor cells have classic rhabdoid or skeletal-like profile, comprised of abundant cytoplasm with eosinophilic hyaline globules and vesicular nuclei with prominent nucleoli ([Anticancer Res 2005;25:4573](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16334144), [Ann Diagn Pathol 2012;16:504](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21775180))
* Some cases may show areas of spindling of tumor cells
* Nuclear pleomorphism can be prominent
* Background stroma may be variably myxoid to fibromyxoid
* Tumor necrosis and mitotic activity can be variable

Cytology description

* Variably cellular smears with individual cells and structureless clusters of rhabdoid cells, spindle cells or round cells
  + Nuclei show either prominent nucleoli in rhabdoid cells or nuclei with homogenous chromatin in round cells ([Cancer Cytopathol 2011;119:49](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21319312))
* Cytoplasmic inclusions are eosinophilic on Giemsa and pale gray on Papanicolaou stain ([Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2011;54:819](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22234121))
* Differentials on cytology include extrarenal Wilms tumor, rhabdomyosarcoma, spindle cell sarcoma and round blue cell tumors including lymphoma ([Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2011;54:819](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22234121))

Sample pathology report

* Right arm, excision:
  + Extrarenal rhabdoid tumor (see comment)
  + Comment: Tumor cells are rhabdoid in appearance with eccentric nuclei. Positive cytokeratin, negative skeletal markers and loss of INI1 are seen. 22q11.2 mutation confirms the diagnosis.

Differential diagnosis

**In children:**

* [Rhabdomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuerhabdomyosarcoma.html):
  + [Embryonal rhabdomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueembryonalrhabdo.html):
    - Common in children < 10 years old
    - M > F
    - Most common sites: head and neck region, genitourinary region
    - May have rhabdomyoblasts / strap cells with cross striations
    - May show eccentric cytoplasm similar to extrarenal rhabdoid tumor
    - Shows positive staining for markers of skeletal differentiation ([desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [myogenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html) and [MyoD1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyod1.html))
    - Retained nuclear [INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html)
  + [Alveolar rhabdomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuealvrhabdo.html):
    - Occurs in adolescents and young adults
    - Grows as alveoli-like spaces formed by loss of cohesion centrally
    - Monomorphic round cells with hyperchromatic nuclei and scant cytoplasm
    - Strong and diffuse [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) and [myogenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html) positivity
    - May express focal / weak [cytokeratins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) (5 - 50% cases)
    - Intact nuclear [INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html)
* [Ewing sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneewing.html):
  + Shows sheet-like growth of round blue cells with scant cytoplasm and hyperchromatic nuclei
  + Diffuse [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html) and [NKX2.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsNKX2.html) expression
  + Retained nuclear [INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html)
  + May show [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) positivity in up to 20% cases
  + *EWSR1* mutations are diagnostic
* [Desmoplastic round blue cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuedsrct.html):
  + Mean age 25 years
  + M > F
  + Abdominal cavity is the most common site
  + Contains prominent desmoplastic stroma
  + Round cells grow in nodules separated by stroma
  + May show polyphenotypic differentiation with positive [keratins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html), [NSE](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsNSE.html) and nuclear [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html)
  + Retained nuclear [INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html)
  + *EWSR1-WT1* gene fusion is consistent

**In adults:**

* Extrarenal rhabdoid tumor should be considered only after other tumors with a rhabdoid appearance have been ruled out
* **Carcinoma**:
  + Age over 50, a history of carcinoma and areas of cohesive epithelial differentiation help in reaching correct diagnosis
* [Malignant melanoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticmelanoma.html):
  + Nonpigmented melanoma mimics rhabdoid tumor
  + Occurs in older age group
  + Positive melanocytic markers [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html), [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html) and negative [cytokeratins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)
* [Anaplastic large cell lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lymphomanonBanaplastic.html):
  + May occasionally exhibit rhabdoid features
  + Positive for [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html), weak / focal positive for [CD45 / LCA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html) and [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html) and negative for [keratins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)
* [Proximal type epithelioid sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueepithelioidsarcoma.html):
  + Positive for [keratins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) (50% cases) and may show loss of nuclear [INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html)
  + Harbors mutations of 22q11 with resultant abnormalities of *SMARCB1* but the mechanism of these mutations is different than that underlying extrarenal rhabdoid tumors
