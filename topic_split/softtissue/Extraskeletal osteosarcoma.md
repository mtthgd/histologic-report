Definition / general

* Adults, extremities
* May occur after Xray exposure
* 60% mortality, worse than chondrosarcoma
* Subtypes: osteoblastic, chondroblastic, fibroblastic, MFH-like, telangiectactic, well-differentiated (parosteal)

Case reports

* 30 year old man with chest wall mass ([BMC Cancer 2010;10:645](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21106072))
* 56 year old man with cutaneous tumor on scar of previous bone graft ([Ann Dermatol 2011;23:S160](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22148040))
* 62 year old woman with well-differentiated tumor arising from retroperitoneum that recurred as anaplastic spindle cell sarcoma ([Case Rep Med 2010;2010:327591](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20224645))
* 75 year old man with acute intestinal perforation and 55 year old woman with gluteal mass ([Case #370](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case370.htm))
* 79 year old man with upper arm tumor ([Oncol Lett 2011;2:75](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22870132))

Differential diagnosis

* [Myositis ossificans](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuemyositis.html): no nuclear atypia, zonal
* **Other sarcomas producing metaplastic bone:** [MFH](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuemfhpleo.html), [synovial sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesynovialsarc.html), [fibrosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuefibrosarcoma.html)
