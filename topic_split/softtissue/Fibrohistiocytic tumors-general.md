Definition / general

* Due to controversy over the precise lineage of these tumors, they are termed fibrohistiocytic tumors ([Fletcher: WHO Classification of Tumours of Soft Tissue and Bone, 4th Edition, 2013)](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/9283224345/pathologyoutl-20)
* Tumors previously called malignant fibrous histiocytoma (MFH) are now called undifferentiated or unclassified sarcoma; based on cell morphology, they are subclassified as undifferentiated pleomorphic sarcoma, undifferentiated spindle cell sarcoma, undifferentiated epithelioid cell sarcoma and undifferentiated round cell sarcoma
* Tumors currently within this category include [tenosynovial giant cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuegctlocal.html), [deep fibrous histiocytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticdeepbfh.html), [plexiform fibrohistiocytic tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueplexiformfh.html) and [giant cell tumor of soft tissue](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuegct.html) ([WHO Classification of Tumours Editorial Board: Soft Tissue and Bone Tumours, 5th Edition, 2020](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/9283245024/pathologyoutl-20))
