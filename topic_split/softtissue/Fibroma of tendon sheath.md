Definition / general

* Benign fibroblastic / myofibroblastic nodular proliferation usually attached to a tendon / tendon sheath

Essential features

* Benign nodular, paucicellular spindle cell lesion with slit-like spaces mostly on finger tendon sheath
* Cellularity may be higher at the periphery
* Has the propensity to recur in 5 - 10% cases

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8810/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=80) - fibroma, NOS
* ICD-11: [EE6Y](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#831995767%2Fother) & [XH0WB3](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#112951513) - other specified fibromatous disorders of skin & soft tissue and fibroma of tendon sheath

Epidemiology

* M > F
* 20 - 50 years
* Young individuals; see [Case reports](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuefibromatendon.html#fibromasheathtendoncasereports) ([BMC Musculoskelet Disord 2020;21:732](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33172434))

Sites

* Mostly on finger tendons
* Intra-articular, rarely

Etiology

* Not known at this time

Clinical features

* Slow growing, painless and firm mass
* Usually ≤ 3 cm
* Overlying skin is usually unremarkable
* Reference: [Geschickter: Tumors of Bone, 1949](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B0007EU0HW/pathologyoutl-20)

Diagnosis

* Diagnosis requires correlation of site with typical histological features

Radiology description

* Plain Xrays show a soft tissue shadow without calcification or bone involvement
* Ultrasound: well circumscribed hypoechoic mass
* MRI: iso signal intensity to muscle on T1 weighted images, low signal intensity to muscle on T2 weighted images ([BMC Musculoskelet Disord 2020;21:732](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33172434))

Case reports

* 3 year old boy with fibroma of tendon sheath of hand ([BMC Musculoskelet Disord 2020;21:732](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33172434))
* 14 year old boy with fibroma of tendon sheath of the hand with novel chromosomal translocation 4;10 ([Case Rep Orthop 2019;2019:3514013](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31637074))
* 35 year old Japanese man with fibroma of tendon sheath presenting limited flexion of the fingers ([Case Rep Orthop 2017;2017:4129714](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28593061))
* 42 year old woman, 54 year old woman and 63 year old man with fibroma of tendon sheath around large joints ([BMC Musculoskelet Disord 2017;18:376](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28854920))
* 54 year old man with fibroma of tendon sheath on medial side of knee ([J Med Invest 2017;64:173](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28373618))

Treatment

* Surgical excision (marginal excision) is warranted in all cases

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Well circumscribed tumor of variable cellularity
* Cellularity mostly higher at tumor edges
* Bland spindle cells in a collagenous background
* Tumor has characteristic thin walled slit-like vessels
* Degenerative changes like myxoid / cystic change, osseous / chondroid metaplasia can be seen
* Bizarre pleomorphic cells can also be present
* Mitotically inactive
* Necrosis not present
* Cellular variant of fibroma of tendon sheath also exists; it overlaps morphologically with nodular fasciitis and fibrous histiocytoma ([Cancer 1979;44:1945](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/91424))
* Reference: [Geschickter: Tumors of Bone, 1949](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B0007EU0HW/pathologyoutl-20)

Cytology description

* H&E stained slides ([J Cytol 2015;32:207](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26729987)):
  + Low cellularity
  + Few loose clusters and singly dispersed bland appearing fibrotic spindle cells and stellate cells admixed with hyalinized fibrocollagenous matrix
  + Necrosis and atypical mitoses not seen

Sample pathology report

* Finger nodule, excision:
  + Fibroma of tendon sheath (see comment)
  + Comment: Histology showed a well circumscribed, variably cellular lesion composed of bland spindle cells having regular nuclei arranged in sheets and fascicles. Thin walled vessels are present. The background is collagenous.
  + It is a benign condition with recurrence in 5 - 10% cases.

Differential diagnosis

* [Deep benign fibrous histiocytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticdeepbfh.html):
  + Involves extremities and head and neck region
  + Affects wide age range (i.e. 6 - 84 years)
  + Slight male predominance
  + Histologically well circumscribed and cellular lesion, prominent histiocyte-like cells, foam cells, giant cells and hemosiderin
  + IHC: [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) positive
* [Tenosynovial cell tumor, localized type](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuegctlocal.html):
  + Present in hands; located in close proximity to synovium of tendon sheath or interphalangeal joint
  + Age range of 30 - 35 years
  + Female predominance
  + Histologically cellular tumor exhibiting variable composition of mononuclear cells, multinucleated giant cells, foamy macrophages, inflammatory cells and hemosiderin
  + IHC: positive for [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html), [CD163](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd160.html#cd163) and [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html)
* [Inclusion body fibromatosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueinfantiledigitalfibromatosis.html):
  + Affects toes of children < 5 years of age
  + Ill defined, paucicellular, plump spindle cells exhibiting intracytoplasmic inclusion and bland nucleus with low mitotic activity
  + IHC: expresses [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html)
  + Recurrence rate is high

Board review style question #1

What is the most common location for fibroma of tendon sheath?

1. Face
2. Fingers
3. Oral cavity
4. Pelvis
5. Toes

Board review style answer #1

**B.** Fingers. The most common location for fibroma of tendon sheath is finger tendons (i.e. thumb, index finger and middle finger).
