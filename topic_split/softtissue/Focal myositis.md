Definition / general

* Inflammatory condition of skeletal muscle with degeneration and regeneration, inflammatory cells and fibrosis
* Not a WHO diagnosis

Epidemiology

* Mean age 41 years, range 7 - 94 years

Clinical features

* Usually healthy patients with no history of trauma
* Evolves over weeks to a localized, painful soft tissue swelling, usually of lower extremity
* Solitary, self limited, may be related to denervation
* May be due to statins ([Int J Cardiol 2009;133:e33](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18155787), [Cleve Clin J Med 2011;78:393](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21632911))

Case reports

* 16 year old girl ([J R Coll Surg Edinb 2000;45:339](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11077785))
* 27 year old man post bCG vaccination ([Rheumatology (Oxford) 2002;41:1074](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12209048))
* 32 year old pregnant woman ([Rheumatology (Oxford) 2000;39:211](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10725076))
* 36 year old man with recurrent disease of peroneal muscles ([Rheumatology (Oxford) 2002;41:1318](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12422007))
* 43 year old man with Lyme disease ([Arthritis Rheum 2006;54:2697](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16871548))

Treatment

* Usually none; spontaneously regresses

Gross description

* Pale, ill defined
* Mean 4 cm, range 1 - 20 cm

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Degeneration and regeneration of muscle fibers associated with interstitial inflammation and fibrosis
* Also focal neurogenic changes
* Occasionally prominent eosinophils
* Markedly inflamed cases have B cells or CD123+ dendritic plasma cells

Cytology description

* Inflammatory cells, skeletal muscle fibers with degenerative and regenerative changes, fibrous tissue ([Acta Cytol 2005;49:653](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16450907))

Positive stains

* [CD163](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd163.html)+ macrophages
* Lymphocytes are [CD3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd3.html)+ and [CD4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd4.html)+

Negative stains

* Macrophages are [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)- and [CD1a](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd1a.html)-
* Lymphocytes are negative for **TIA1**, [granzyme B](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgranzymeb.html), [EBV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainseber1.html) and [ALK1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:1016](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19363438))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* No B or T cell rearrangement

Differential diagnosis

* **Poliomyelitis**
