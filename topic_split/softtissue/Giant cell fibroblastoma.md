Definition / general

* Locally aggressive subcutaneous fibroblastic neoplasm, arising predominantly in pediatric population
* Considered to be in a spectrum of dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans (DFSP)

Essential features

* Rare fibroblastic neoplasm in dermis, usually affecting children and closely related to DFSP
* Histologically characterized by the presence of spindle to stellate cells, multinucleated / floret-like giant cells lining the ectatic pseudovascular spaces and diffuse CD34 positivity
* Chromosomal translocation t(17:22) resulting in *COL1A1::PDGFB* fusion
* Increased risk of recurrence if incompletely excised

Terminology

* Giant cell fibroblastoma (GCF)

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8834/1](https://iris.who.int/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=81) - giant cell fibroblastoma
* ICD-11: [FB51.Y & XH9AV8](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#765238319%2Fother%20%26%2087038938) - other specified fibroblastic disorders & giant cell fibroblastoma

Epidemiology

* Rare neoplasm
* M > F
* Median age of 6 years ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2007;11:81](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17349565))
* Infrequent in adults

Sites

* Predilection for trunk, back of the thigh, inguinal region and axilla region
* Rarely head and neck / extremities

Pathophysiology

* Chromosomal translocation t(17:22) resulting in *COL1A1::PDGFB* fusion; breakpoint in *COL1A1* is variable but occurs in region encoding alpha helical domain
* Breakpoint in *PDGFB* always occurs in exon 2 and encodes beta chain of the PDGF ligand, a tyrosine kinase receptor with mitogenic potential
  + Fusion of these genes results in overexpression of a chimeric protein or native PDGFB ([Cancer Genet Cytogenet 2003;140:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12550751))
* Closely related to DFSP in histogenesis, with studies showing DFSP recurring as giant cell fibroblastoma and hybrid tumor as well as giant cell fibroblastoma recurring as DFSP ([J Plast Reconstr Aesthet Surg 2010;63:e785](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20655288), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 1996;120:1052](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12049108))
* Amplification / gain of *COL1A1::PDGFB* is absent in giant cell fibroblastoma but present in DFSP component of hybrid forms, indicating association of genomic gains in the evolution of giant cell fibroblastoma to DFSP ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2008;47:260](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18069662))

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Superficial painless slow growing plaque to protuberant nodule attached to overlying skin

Diagnosis

* Diagnosis is based on location, morphological features and CD34 positivity by immunohistochemistry
* *COL1A1::PDGFB* fusion may be detected by different molecular methods; useful for diagnosis but not required

Radiology description

* Ultrasound
  + Hypoechoic with well defined margins and internal, irregular, septum-like echogenic structures ([Taehan Yongsang Uihakhoe Chi 2020;81:237](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36238106))
* Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI)
  + Different signal intensity in the center and the periphery on fluid sensitive T2 weighted images with a bright edge and lower signal in the center ([Taehan Yongsang Uihakhoe Chi 2020;81:237](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36238106))
  + Low T1 signal intensity and high T2 signal intensity with marked contrast enhancement ([Am J Dermatopathol 2006;28:228](https://journals.lww.com/amjdermatopathology/citation/2006/06000/giant_cell_fibroblastoma.11.aspx))

Prognostic factors

* Recurrence in 50% of cases, especially in cases of incomplete, marginal excision
* Excellent prognosis and low risk of recurrence with wide excision ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12502925))
* No metastasis reported

Case reports

* 1.5 year old boy with swelling in the occipital area of scalp and encephalocele ([BMJ Case Rep 2014;2014:bcr2013203420](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24728899))
* 3 year old boy with painless swelling at right ankle ([Oman Med J 2012;27:e037](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29181132))
* 3 year old boy with well circumscribed soft tissue mass at left lower abdomen ([Taehan Yongsang Uihakhoe Chi 2020;81:237](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36238106))
* 25 year old man with painless tumor in right lumbar with multiple recurrences ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2018;11:4052](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31949795))

Treatment

* Wide local excision with follow up
* Mohs surgery in recurrent cases ([Dermatol Surg 2010;36:417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20100251))
* DFSP can be treated with imatinib ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2016;25:64](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27806849), [Clin Exp Dermatol 2023;48:297](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36630365), [J Clin Med 2020;9:1752](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32516921))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Dermal tumor with infiltration into subcutaneous fat in honeycomb or parallel growth pattern; spared cutaneous adnexal structures
* Fibrous to myxoid stroma with variable cellularity
* Bland uniform spindled and stellate tumor cells
* Multinucleated / floret-like giant cells with wreath-like nuclei lining irregular pseudovascular spaces
* Perivascular lymphocytic infiltration in an onion skinning pattern; intralesional hemorrhage; myoid whorls in rare cases ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2007;11:81](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17349565))
* Tumors with DFSP components, fibrosarcomatous and pigmented Bednar tumor component reported ([Mod Pathol 1998;11:491](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9619604), [Cesk Patol 2002;38:173](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12629865), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 1996;120:1052](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12049108))
* Tumors may recur as DSFP and may then develop fibrosarcomatous change ([Histopathology 1994;24:197](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8181817))

Cytology description

* Singly scattered or occasional clusters of mononuclear cells with scant bipolar cytoplasm, vesicular nuclei and inconspicuous to single, small nucleoli ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2001;125:1091](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11473466))
* Nuclear membranes with frequent notches, creases or folds; rare or no mitotic figures ([Diagn Cytopathol 2002;26:398](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12112833))
* Metachromatic stromal fragments associated with small aggregates of cells in the background
* Absence of necrosis

Positive stains

* [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html): strong and diffusely positive ([Med Arh 2006;60:389](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17297857))

Negative stains

* [Smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html), [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) protein, [factor XIIIa](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfactorxiiia.html) ([J Cutan Pathol 1986;13:301](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3534028), [Histopathology 1988;13:499](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3215632))

Electron microscopy description

* Ultrastructural finding supports both mononuclear cells and multinucleated giant cells to be of fibroblastic origin ([Am J Clin Pathol 1985;83:165](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2982254), [Mod Pathol 1992;5:639](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1369800))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Balanced or unbalanced chromosomal translocation t(17;22) resulting in the fusion gene *COL1A1::PDGFB*
* Other less common translocations occurring in DFSP / giant cell fibroblastoma include *COL1A2::PDGF, COL6A3::PDGFD, EMILIN2::PDGFD* and *TNC::PDGFD* ([JAMA Dermatol 2015;151:1330](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26332510), [Mod Pathol 2018;31:1683](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29955147))

Giant cell fibroblastoma

Sample pathology report

* Skin and soft tissue, right thigh, excision:
  + Giant cell fibroblastoma with a dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans component (see comment)
  + Deep surgical margin free
  + Comment: Immunohistochemical stains using the modified avidin biotin technique are performed with appropriate controls. The neoplasm is CD34 positive and S100 protein negative. Controls stain appropriately.

Differential diagnosis

* [Fibrous hamartoma of infancy](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuefibroushamart.html):
  + Commonly occurs in infants and young children
  + Triphasic morphology with bland fibroblastic / myofibroblastic cell bundles, primitive mesenchyme nodules and mature adipose tissue arranged in an organoid pattern
  + Pseudoangiomatous areas lined by plump tumor cells in 33 - 50% of cases
  + Immunohistochemistry shows [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) positive fibroblastic areas, [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) and [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html) positive primitive mesenchymal cells and [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) positive adipocytic areas
  + *EGFR* exon 20 insertion / duplication mutations
* [Giant cell angiofibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesft.html):
  + Rare soft tissue tumor occurring predominantly in mesenchymal tissue of orbit and head and neck region
  + Well circumscribed tumor with patternless spindle cell proliferation, pseudovascular spaces and floret-like multinucleated giant cells
  + Prominent hyalinized blood vessels
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) and [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html): strong and diffusely positive
  + [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html), [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html): negative ([Case Rep Pathol 2012;2012:408575](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22934216), [Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:1286](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7573691))
* [Spindle cell / pleomorphic lipoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueadiposepleomorphicgeneral.html):
  + Arising mainly in men 45 - 60 years of age
  + Most common locations are posterior neck, shoulder and back
  + Solitary, circumscribed, slowly growing nodule in the subcutaneous tissue
  + Histologically characterized by the triad of mature adipocytes, bland spindle cells and thick ropy collagen in varying proportion
  + Multinucleated floret-like giant cells present in pleomorphic lipoma
  + Pseudoangiomatous variant is characterized by irregular branching spaces with well formed connective tissue projections
  + Strong and diffuse [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) positivity, loss of nuclear [RB1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsrb.html) protein expression
  + Loss of 16q or 13q
* [Pleomorphic fibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuepleomorphicfibroma.html):
  + Common in adults and presenting as a slow growing solitary skin colored papule
  + Well circumscribed tumor involving papillary and reticular dermis
  + Histologically characterized by predominant thick haphazardly arranged collagen and low cellularity
  + Presence of scattered spindle or stellate cells and pleomorphic multinucleated giant cells
  + Variable immunoreactivity for [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) and [actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsactin.html)
  + [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html), [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) and [factor XIII](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfactorxiiia.html): negative ([Cureus 2023;15:e37018](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37139038))
  + Loss of expression of [RB1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsrb.html) protein
  + Cytogenetic abnormality consists of loss of 13q

Board review style question #1

[![Ill defined dermal tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/softtissuegiantcellfibrodehner01.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/softtissuegiantcellfibrodehner01.jpg)

A 5 year old boy presents with a slow growing painless nodule on the back. Histological examination reveals the above given picture. What is the cytogenetic finding in this tumor?

1. t(12;15)(p13;q26)
2. t(12;16)(q13;p11)
3. t(17;22)(q22;q13)
4. t(21;22)(q12;q12)

Board review style answer #1

**C.** t(17;22)(q22;q13) is the cytogenetic finding in giant cell fibroblastoma. Answer A is incorrect because t(12;15)(p13;q26) is not seen in giant cell fibroblastoma but is present in infantile fibroblastoma and congenital mesoblastic nephroma. Answer D is incorrect because t(21;22)(q12;q12) is seen in Ewing sarcoma but not in giant cell fibroblastoma. Answer B is incorrect because giant cell fibroblastoma lacks t(12;16)(q13;p11) and is present in myxoid liposarcoma.

Board review style question #2

Which of the following immunoprofiles is consistent with giant cell fibroblastoma?

1. CD34-, CD31-, S100+, SMA-
2. CD34+, CD31+, S100-, SMA-
3. CD34+, CD31-, S100-, SMA-
4. CD34+, CD31-, S100+, SMA+

Board review style answer #2

**C.** CD34+, CD31-, S100-, SMA-. Giant cell fibroblastoma is diffuse and strongly positive for CD34. Both the spindle cells and multinucleated giant cells stain with CD34. Answer A is incorrect because giant cell fibroblastoma is negative for S100 and positive for CD34. Myxoid liposarcoma is positive for S100 but negative for CD34. Answer B is incorrect because giant cell fibroblastoma is positive for CD34 but negative for CD31. Vascular tumors are positive for both CD31 and CD34. Answer D is incorrect because giant cell fibroblastoma is negative for SMA. Fibrous hamartoma of infancy has CD34 positive primitive mesenchymal area along with SMA positive fibroblastic areas and S100 positive adipocytic area.
