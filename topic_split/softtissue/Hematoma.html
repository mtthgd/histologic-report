Definition / general
  • May clinically resemble a tumor if deep seated
  • Important to exclude a sarcoma with intratumoral hemorrhage
  • Pseudoaneurysm may occur after intravascular procedures
  • Postoperative seromas may persist for years and be confused with recurrent tumor
Sites
  • Usually near tensor fascia lata (upper thigh muscle, see drawing below)
Case reports
  • 67 year old woman with chronic expanding hematoma of the thorax (Yonsei Med J 2007;48:337)
  • A case of chronic expanding hematoma in the tensor fascia lata (Dermatol Online J 2001;7:6)
  • Spontaneous giant expanding thigh hematoma mimicking soft tissue neoplasm (Joint Bone Spine 2008;75:64)
Treatment
  • Complete surgical excision, including pseudocapsule
