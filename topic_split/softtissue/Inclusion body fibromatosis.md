Definition / general

* Dermal fibroblastic and myofibroblastic lesion with cytoplasmic eosinophilic inclusions, usually in digits of infants
* Also called infantile digital fibromatosis, infantile digital fibroma ([J Hand Surg Am 1995;20:1014](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8583050))
* Distinct lesion from classic fibromatosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18830128))

Sites

* Usually exterior surface of distal phalanges of fingers and toes, but not thumb or great toe, also oral cavity and breast
* 50% recur, do not metastasize
* Similar inclusions reported in breast fibroadenoma ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2007;131:1126](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17617003)), breast phyllodes tumor ([Am J Surg Pathol 1994;18:506](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8172324), [Breast J 2008;14:198](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18266786)), cervical polyp ([Pathology 1998;30:215](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9643509)), GI leiomyomas ([Cesk Patol 2006;42:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16955562))

Clinical features

* Rare; lesions usually present at birth or in first 2 years
* Similar lesions in adults
* Often are multiple

Case reports

* 6 month old girl ([Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2010;53:827](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21045432))
* 1 year old boy with spontaneous regression ([J Dermatol 1998;25:523](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9769598))
* 2 year old with post-surgical involvement of all 4 extremities ([Ann Plast Surg 2008;61:472](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18812724))

Treatment

* Conservative excision (preserve function because recurrences are not destructive and tumors do not metastasize) or watchful waiting ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18830128), [Pediatr Dermatol 2008;25:72](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18304159))

Gross description

* Nodules with stretched overlying skin, lesions are ill defined, white-tan, usually 2 cm or less
* No hemorrhage or necrosis

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Nonencapsulated, dermal proliferation of hypocellular sheets or fascicles of fibroblasts and myofibroblasts with variable collagen
* Some spindle cells have peculiar eosinophilic (hyaline) cytoplasmic inclusions the size of a lymphocyte nucleus
* Usually mitotic figures
* May infiltrate into adjacent tissue
* No atypia

Cytology description

* See [Acta Cytol 2011;55:481](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21986179)

Positive stains

* Inclusions: [trichrome](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstrichrome.html) (stain red), **PTAH**, variable staining for [actins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsactin.html)
* Spindle cells: [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html), [muscle actins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsactinmusclespecific.html) (tram track pattern), [calponin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalponin.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html); often [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)

Negative stains

* Inclusions: [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html)
* Spindle cells: [keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html), [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html), [beta-catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html)

Electron microscopy description

* Spindle cells are myofibroblasts with rough endoplasmic reticulum and free lying inclusions composed of compact masses of actin granules and filaments without a limiting membrane ([Am J Pathol 1979;94:19](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/216266))

Differential diagnosis

* [Infantile fibrosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuefibrosarcomainfantile.html):
  + Not digits, usually > 2 cm, more cellular, chromatin is denser and more irregular, more mitotic figures, no inclusions
* [Infantile desmoid fibromatosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuefibromatosisdeep.html):
  + Rare on hand, usually > 2 cm, more cellular, no inclusions
