Definition / general

* Rare hereditary pediatric disorder with extracellular hyaline material deposition in skin, soft tissue and bone

Terminology

* *Molluscum fibrosum in children* (original name given by Murray, 1873), *mesenchymal dysplasia* (Puretic et al, 1962), disseminated painful fibromatosis
* Also called fibromatosis hyalinica multiplex
* Called infantile systemic hyalinosis if organ involvement, debatable if this is same disease or a distinct entity ([Pediatr Dermatol 2004;21:154](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15078358), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2009;61:695](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19344977), [Hum Mutat 2009;30:583](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19191226))
* "Hyaline fibromatosis syndrome" proposed as an umbrella term for juvenile hyaline fibromatosis and infantile systemic hyalinosis since the two syndromes have significant clinicopathologic overlap ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2009;61:695](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19344977))

Epidemiology

* Infants or children under 5 years
* No ethnic predisposition ([Rheumatol Int 2011;31:273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20661739)), slight male predominance
* Occasionally presents in 4th - 5th decade

Sites

* Skin, soft tissue, bones

Etiology

* Autosomal recessive disorder; associated with consanguineous parents ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2009;61:695](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19344977))
* Due to mutations in capillary morphogenesis protein 2 (CMG2) gene on chromosome 4q21, which cause retention of CMG2 in endoplasmic reticulum ([Hum Mutat 2009;30:583](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19191226), [Am J Hum Genet 2003;73:791](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14508707), [Am J Hum Genet 2003;73:957](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12973667))

Clinical features

* Progressive, chronic, and often debilitating disease
* Pearly papules and plaques (face, posterior neck, perianal region)
* Large, subcutaneous nodules (most often on scalp)
* Gingival hypertrophy
* Flexure contractures (due to masses in periarticular soft tissue)
* Osteolytic bone lesions of skull, long bones, phalanges ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2009;61:695](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19344977))

Laboratory

* Anemia, hypogammaglobulinemia, hypoalbuminemia, electrolyte imbalance

Radiology description

* Fractures, osteolytic bone lesions, osteoporosis

Case reports

* 14 year old girl without prominent hyaline changes ([J Cutan Pathol 2005;32:235](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15701086))
* Disease in 2 siblings of first-degree consanguineous marriage ([Indian J Dermatol Venereol Leprol 2005;71:115](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16394387))

Treatment

* No curative treatment
* Resection of lesions for aesthetic purposes may be considered (varying results in literature)
* Intralesional steroid injections in early lesions
* Physiotherapy to prevent contractures ([Indian J Dermatol 2011;56:731](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22345782), [Eur J Dermatol 2010;20:631](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20627857))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Poorly circumscribed amorphous or nodular deposits of abundant, hyaline material with embedded fibroblasts in cords
* Can have retraction artifact around fibroblasts
* Early lesions are characterized by increased cellularity
* No atypia, no necrosis

Positive stains

* Matrix: [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html) (diastase resistant), [Alcian blue](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalcianblue.html)
* Fibroblasts: [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html) (faint), [smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) (focally, [Am J Dermatopathol 2007;29:99](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17284973))
* [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html) stains macrophages adjacent to fibroblasts

Negative stains

* Matrix: **toluidine blue**, [Congo red](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscongored.html) ([Indian J Dermatol 2011;56:731](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22345782))
* Fibroblasts: [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [muscle specific actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsactinmusclespecific.html) ([Am J Dermatopathol 2007;29:99](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17284973))

Electron microscopy description

* Fibroblasts with fibril-filled balls (dilated ER with numerous cisternae containing granular material) which displace nearly the entire cytoplasm
* The same granular material is present in extracellular spaces ([Weiss, Goldblum, Folpe: Enzinger and Weiss's Soft Tissue Tumors, 2007, 5th Edition](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0323046282/pathologyoutl-20), [Am J Dermatopathol 2007;29:99](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17284973))

Differential diagnosis

* **Gingival fibromatosis**:
  + Limited to gums, collagen rich fibrous tissue
* **Lipoid proteinosis**:
  + Erythematous papules and subcutaneous nodules over joints, tendons and pressure points ([Pediatr Dermatol 2004;21:154](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15078358))
* [Myofibromatosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuemyopericytoma.html), [neurofibromatosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueNF1.html) and [turban tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticeccrinecylindroma.html) syndrome (multiple cylindromas) are potential clinical mimics but have completely different histologic features
* **Winchester syndrome**:
  + Also has hypertrichosis, corneal opacities but may be related entity ([Pediatr Dermatol 2004;21:154](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15078358))
