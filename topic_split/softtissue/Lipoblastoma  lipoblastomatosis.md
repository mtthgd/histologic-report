Definition / general

* Benign, rapidly growing neoplasm composed of embryonal white fat with variable degrees of myxoid changes ([Mod Pathol 2021;34:584](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33097826), [J Clin Med 2022;11:1938](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35407546))
* Occurs predominantly in infancy, early childhood and very rarely in adulthood ([J Clin Med 2022;11:1938](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35407546))
* Lipoblastoma: superficial, localized, well circumscribed; complete excision easy; common type
* Lipoblastomatosis: deep, diffuse, irregular borders; complete excision difficult; rare type ([J Clin Med 2022;11:1938](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35407546))

Essential features

* Benign, rapidly growing neoplasm that is commonly superficial and well defined
* 75 - 90% are seen before the age of 3 years, with male predilection (M:F = 2.8:1)
* Mostly occurs in trunk and extremities
* Lobular architecture with intersecting fibrovascular septa, myxoid changes and a spectrum of fat cell maturation
* Positive stains are S100, CD34 (adipocytes and vascular network), desmin (mesenchymal cells)

Terminology

* Lipoblastoma: well circumscribed and superficial ([J Clin Med 2022;11:1938](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35407546))
* Lipoblastomatosis: deep seated with ill defined borders ([J Clin Med 2022;11:1938](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35407546))
* Not recommended: fetal lipoma, fetal fat tumor, fetocellular lipoma, embryonal lipoma, congenital lipomatoid tumor, lipoblastic tumor of childhood

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8881/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=82) - lipoblastomatosis
* ICD-10: [D17.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D17-/D17.9) - benign lipomatous neoplasm, unspecified
* ICD-11: [2E80.1 & XH8L55](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f581420938?data=%7B%22dataType%22%3A%22pc%22%2C%22postcoordinationCodeSet%22%3A%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F581420938%22%2C%22axisToValueIds%22%3A%7B%22specificAnatomy%22%3A%5B%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F1532146135%22%7D%5D%7D%7D%7D) - lipoblastoma / lipoblastomatosis

Epidemiology

* 18 - 20% of adipocytic tumors ([J Int Med Res 2021;49:300060520981362](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33730887))
* 75 - 90% are seen before 3 years of age ([J Clin Med 2022;11:1938](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35407546))
* Rarely in adulthood ([J Clin Med 2022;11:1938](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35407546))
* Most common in males (M:F = 2.8:1) ([BMC Res Notes 2018;11:42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29343278))
* Can be associated with cleft lip, cleft palate, cephalic malformations and neurologic disorders ([Exp Ther Med 2021;22:903](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34257716))

Sites

* Common in trunk and extremities ([Exp Ther Med 2021;22:903](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34257716), [BMC Res Notes 2018;11:42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29343278))
* Less common sites:
  + Head and neck
  + Abdomen and mesentery
  + Retroperitoneum and pelvis
  + Inguinoscrotal or labial region
  + Perineum and mediastinum ([Exp Ther Med 2021;22:903](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34257716))
* Very rare sites: lung, liver, heart, colon and parotid gland ([J Clin Med 2022;11:1938](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35407546))

Pathophysiology

* Exhibits pseudodiploid or hyperdiploid karyotypes combined with structural alteration of 8q11-q13, which leads to *PLAG1* rearrangement ([J Clin Med 2022;11:1938](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35407546))
* Numerical change in one or more extra copies of chromosome 8, with or without concurrent rearrangement of 8q11-q13 ([J Clin Med 2022;11:1938](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35407546))

Etiology

* Unknown at this time ([Pediatr Hematol Oncol 2017;34:212](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29035641))

Clinical features

* Rapidly growing, painless, soft tissue mass with swelling ([BMC Res Notes 2018;11:42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29343278))
* Usually, the rapid growth of the mass causes the symptoms
  + Colonic and mesenteric cases have been associated with intussusception and volvulus ([BMC Res Notes 2018;11:42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29343278))
  + Mediastinal or cervical tumors may appear with stridor ([Cancer Imaging 2012;12:484](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23114460))
* No metastasis recorded ([Exp Ther Med 2021;22:903](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34257716), [J Clin Med 2022;11:1938](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35407546))

Diagnosis

* Standard diagnostic approach is histopathologic examination with immunohistochemistry as an adjunct ([Exp Ther Med 2021;22:903](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34257716))
* FISH and RNA sequencing are also useful ([Mod Pathol 2021;34:584](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33097826))
* Radiologic findings alone cannot rule out malignancy ([Turk Patoloji Derg 2020;36:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31282548))

Radiology description

* Radiologic findings alone cannot rule out malignancy ([Turk Patoloji Derg 2020;36:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31282548))
* Ultrasound:
  + Usually is the first imaging examination ([Korean J Radiol 2020;21:341](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32090527))
  + Fluid collection, vascularity detection in color Doppler ([Indian J Radiol Imaging 2021;31:710](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34790320))
  + Hyperechoic, admixed with the surrounding muscle tissue and parallel to the skin, as an elliptical or rounded mass lesion ([Turk Patoloji Derg 2020;36:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31282548))
* Xray (plan radiography):
  + Only as a shadow of soft tissue
  + Without calcification or specific features ([J Med Case Rep 2017;11:338](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29202860))
* Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI):
  + Lobulated fatty mass with a variable amount of nonenhancing cystic area
  + Internal fibrous partitions intermixed with enhancing soft tissue mass
  + Difficult to differentiate from myxoid liposarcoma ([J Med Case Rep 2017;11:338](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29202860))
* Computed tomography (CT scan):
  + Well limited lobulated mass, showing greasy density similar to that of subcutaneous fat
  + Crossed by internal septa ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2021;84:106050](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34139421))

Prognostic factors

* Favorable: well circumscribed, encapsulated ([J Clin Med 2022;11:1938](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35407546))
* Up to 80% chance of recurrence for incompletely resected tumors ([BMC Res Notes 2018;11:42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29343278))
* Excellent prognosis after complete excision ([J Clin Med 2022;11:1938](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35407546))

Case reports

* 12 month old boy with bilateral inguinal masses ([Urol Case Rep 2019;26:100961](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31360638))
* 23 month old girl with a painless mass on popliteal side of her thigh ([J Med Case Rep 2017;11:338](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29202860))
* 3 year old boy with a right hemithorax mass and respiratory problems ([Respir Med Case Rep 2019;26:200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30723667))
* 37 year old man with a right side intrascrotal mass ([BMJ Case Rep 2019;12:e231320](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31826903))
* 80 year old woman with a left kidney mass ([Ann Transl Med 2019;7:150](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31157271))

Treatment

* Complete surgical excision ([BMC Res Notes 2018;11:42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29343278), [J Clin Med 2022;11:1938](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35407546))
* 5 year follow up is recommended ([BMC Res Notes 2018;11:42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29343278))
* No metastasis has been reported ([J Clin Med 2022;11:1938](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35407546))

Gross description

* Typically 2 - 5 cm in diameter but can be > 10 cm ([Pediatr Hematol Oncol 2017;34:212](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29035641))
* Soft, lobulated, yellow, white or tan mass ([J Clin Med 2022;11:1938](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35407546))
* May display myxoid nodules, cystic spaces or adipose nodules separated by fine white fibrous septa on the cut surface ([BMC Res Notes 2018;11:42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29343278))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Consists of lobulated sheets of adipocytes ([J Clin Med 2022;11:1938](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35407546), [BMC Res Notes 2018;11:42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29343278))
* Myxoid areas with plexiform vascular network and primitive mesenchymal cells ([J Clin Med 2022;11:1938](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35407546), [BMC Res Notes 2018;11:42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29343278), [Pediatr Blood Cancer 2009;52:132](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18798558))
* Fat cells revealing a spectrum of maturation, ranging from primitive stellate or spindled mesenchymal cells to multivacuolated or small signet ring cell lipoblasts ([J Clin Med 2022;11:1938](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35407546), [BMC Res Notes 2018;11:42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29343278))
* Lobule zonation is defined by arrangement of immature myxoid cells at periphery near fibrous septa and mature adipocytes at center of the lobule ([BMC Res Notes 2018;11:42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29343278), [J Clin Med 2022;11:1938](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35407546))
* Myxoid areas may rarely mimic myxoid liposarcoma due to pooling of myxoid matrix ([Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:1131](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9331284))
* Mature parts are similar to lipoma or fibrolipoma ([Mod Pathol 2021;34:584](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33097826))
* Tumor with late resection may show fibrolipomatous changes with no presence of lipoblasts ([Mod Pathol 2021;34:584](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33097826))
* Other histological findings are chondroid metaplasia, extramedullary hematopoiesis, chronic inflammation and sparsely multinucleated or floret cells ([J Clin Med 2022;11:1938](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35407546))

Cytology description

* Mixed population of lipoblasts and mature adipocytes ([Cancer Cytopathol 2017;125:934](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28902468))
* Myxoid background with a rich network of capillary vessels ([Cancer Cytopathol 2017;125:934](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28902468))
* May also show spindle cells and naked nuclei ([Cancer Cytopathol 2017;125:934](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28902468))
* Mostly no cytological atypia ([Cancer Cytopathol 2017;125:934](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28902468))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Clonal rearrangements involving chromosomal region 8q11 > q13 (8q12) is the hallmark ([BMC Res Notes 2018;11:42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29343278))
* Oncogene *PLAG1* (pleomorphic adenoma gene 1) is located on band 8q12 ([BMC Res Notes 2018;11:42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29343278))
* Other rare fusion partners of *PLAG1* are *COL1A2* (7q21.3), *HAS2* (8q24.13), *RAD51B* (14q24.1), *COL3A1* (2q32.2), *RAB2A* (8q12.1-q12.2) and *BOC* (3q13.2) ([Mod Pathol 2021;34:584](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33097826))
* Novel reported fusions are *HNRNPC::PLAG1*, *SRSF3::PLAG1*, *PCMTD1::PLAG1*, *YWHAZ::PLAG1*, *CTDSP2::PLAG1* and *PPP2R2A* ([Mod Pathol 2021;34:584](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33097826))

Sample pathology report

* Soft tissue, thigh, left, excisional biopsy:
  + Benign adipocytic neoplasm with features favoring lipoblastoma (see comment)
  + Tumor size: 7 x 6 x 4 cm
  + Margins are free of tumor
  + Comment: Histological examination shows an adipocytic neoplasm with a lobular architecture by intersecting fibrous septa and zonation along with myxoid areas. A spectrum of fat cell maturation, from primitive stellate to spindled mesenchymal cells, multivacuolated lipoblasts to mature fat cells are seen. Immunohistochemically, the tumor cells are showing positivity for S100 and CD34 and are negative for MDM2, CKD4 and p16. This constellation of morphological and immunohistochemical features strongly supports the diagnosis of lipoblastoma. This is a benign, rapidly growing soft tissue neoplasm prone to recur unless completely excised.

Differential diagnosis

* Pediatric [lipoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueadiposelipoma.html):
  + Does not show lipoblasts or primitive mesenchymal cells
  + Not common in extremities
  + Does not show chromosome 8 abnormalities ([BMC Res Notes 2018;11:42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29343278))
* [Hibernoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueadiposehibernoma.html):
  + Rare in children
  + Brown fat cells that have a central nucleus and abundant finely granular cytoplasm
  + 11q13 abnormalities ([BMC Res Notes 2018;11:42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29343278))
* Pediatric [myxoid liposarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuemyxoidliposarcoma.html):
  + Very rare in children Shows pools of stromal mucin (pulmonary edema pattern)
  + Has hypercellular areas
  + Shows nuclear atypia with no presence of lobulated growth
  + Maturation of adipocytes occurs at periphery
  + Does not show chromosome 8 abnormalities
  + t(12;16) or t(12;22) ([BMC Res Notes 2018;11:42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29343278))
* [Atypical lipomatous tumor / well differentiated liposarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuewdliposarcoma.html):
  + Rare in children
  + Atypical stromal cells and lipoblasts
  + [MDM2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmdm2.html) and [CDK4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdk4.html) are positive
  + Majority are positive for [p16](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp16.html) ([J Clin Med 2022;11:1938](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35407546))
