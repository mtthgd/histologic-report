Definition / general

* Benign tumor composed of mature adipocytes

Essential features

* Most common soft tissue tumor
* Mostly subcutaneous, < 5 cm
* Radiologically, grossly and microscopically same as normal fat
* In large (> 10 cm) and deep seated / retroperitoneal tumors, exclusion of *MDM2* amplification is required for diagnosis

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8850/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=81) - lipoma, NOS
* ICD-11: [2E80.0Y](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http://id.who.int/icd/entity/1090000716/mms/other) - lipoma, other specified site

Epidemiology

* Most common mesenchymal tumor in adults ([Acta Orthop Scand 1983;54:929](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6670522))
* M = F (slightly more common in males)
* Rare in children

Sites

* Superficial (subcutaneous) soft tissue
* Upper back, proximal extremities and abdominal region
* Subset deep seated (intramuscular, parosteal, visceral)

Pathophysiology

* Reactivated expression of HMGA2 protein ([J Biol Chem 2000;275:14394](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10747931), [Am J Hum Genet 2005;76:340](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15593017))

Etiology

* Unknown at this time

Clinical features

* Painless, subcutaneous mass
* In special site, might have specific symptoms (i.e. gastrointestinal → melena)
* 5% multiple ([Acta Orthop Scand 1983;54:929](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6670522))
  + Cowden syndrome ([J Med Genet 1999;36:360](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10353779))
  + Bannayan-Riley-Ruvalcaba syndrome ([GeneReviews: *PTEN* Hamartoma Tumor Syndrome [Accessed 18 November 2021]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20301661))
* Associated with obesity

Diagnosis

* Clinical features ([Eur J Surg Oncol 2021 Oct 18 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34688512))
  + < 5 cm
  + Soft
  + Superficial
* Imaging
  + Ultrasonography
  + Computed tomography (deep seated)
  + Magnetic resonance (deep seated)

Radiology description

* Ultrasonography
  + Variably echogenic mass ([Radiographics 2004;24:1433](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15371618))
  + Mostly isoechoic ([Radiology 2004;233:763](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15486212))
  + No shadowing
  + No flow with color Doppler
* CT ([Radiographics 2004;24:1433](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15371618))
  + Superficial
  + Circumscribed
  + Low attenuation
  + Calcification might be present
* MRI
  + Same signal as subcutaneous fat in all sequences

Prognostic factors

* Recurrence < 5%
* Incomplete surgery, higher local recurrence

Case reports

* 37 year old woman with giant infiltrative tumor of face ([AJNR Am J Neuroradiol 2003;24:283](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12591650))
* 58 year old and 63 year old women with tumors of pectoralis major muscle simulating a breast mass ([Ann Acad Med Singapore 2005;34:275](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15902350), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2011;135:1061](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21810001))
* Well circumscribed tumor of sternocleidomastoid muscle ([Auris Nasus Larynx 2004;31:283](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15364365))

Treatment

* Simple excision

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Proliferation of mature adipocytes
* Paucicellular fibrous septa can be present
* Fat necrosis is often found in larger tumor
* Skeletal muscle fibers are infiltrated in intramuscular lipoma
* Subtypes
  + Intramuscular lipoma
  + Chondrolipoma
* Visceral sites
  + Submucosal lipoma of the colon
  + Endobronchial lipoma

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Structural rearrangement of chromosome bands12q13-q15 or *HMGA2* gene
* Structural rearrangement of chromosome band 6p21 or *HMGA1* gene
* Absence of *MDM2* amplification ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;3:1476](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17895748), [Histopathology 2021 Sep 14 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34523152))
* Absence of *CDK4* amplification ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;3:1476](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17895748))
* Absence of giant marker / ring chromosome ([Cancer Genet Cytogenet 2004;150:93](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15066317))
* Absence of 13p loss ([Cancer Genet Cytogenet 2004;150:93](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15066317))

Lipoma and its differential diagnosis

Sample pathology report

* Soft tissue, subcutaneous trunk, excision:
  + Lipoma
* Soft tissue, deep (deltoid), excision:
  + Lipoma, intramuscular (see comment)
  + Comment: Sections show a mature adipocytic neoplasm. No cytologic atypia or mitotic activity is identified. Immunohistochemical stain shows negativity for MDM2. Fluorescence in situ hybridization does not show amplification of *MDM2* gene.

Differential diagnosis

* [Angiolipoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueadiposeangiolipoma.html):
  + Typically composed of two elements: mature adipocytes and branching capillary sized vessels, which often contain fibrin thrombi
  + Relative proportions of adipocytes and vessels varies and some lesions are almost completely composed of vascular channels
* [Spindle cell / pleomorphic lipoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueadiposepleomorphicgeneral.html):
  + Head, neck, back
  + Male
  + Spindle and pleomorphic cells
  + Ropy collagen
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) strong IHC positivity
  + [Rb](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsrb.html) loss by IHC (positive internal control of blood vessels)
* [Atypical lipomatous tumor / well differentiated liposarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuewdliposarcoma.html):
  + Size > 10 cm
  + Deep seated
  + Atypical cells
  + [MDM2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmdm2.html) IHC positivity
  + *MDM2* gene FISH cluster amplification
* **Mobile encapsulated adipose tissue (MEAT)**

Board review style question #2

Which of the following is true about a lipoma?

1. Diagnosis is often made without immunohistochemistry
2. Highly infiltrative lesion, which leads the majority to recur
3. It is the most common benign neoplasm
4. It is usually large (> 10 cm) and located in deep soft tissue or retroperitoneum
5. Patient always reports a history of trauma

Board review style answer #2

**A**. Diagnosis is often made without immunohistochemistry. Diagnosis can be done on H&E in most cases and in the appropriate clinicopathological setting (i.e. small, superficial, homogeneous lesion, composed of uniform adipocytic cells without atypia).
