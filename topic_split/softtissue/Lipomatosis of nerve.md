Definition / general

* Infiltration of epineurium of a major nerve by adipose and fibrous tissue

Terminology

* Also called fibrolipoma of nerve, fibrolipomatous hamartoma of nerve, macrodystrophia lipomatosa

Epidemiology

* May be noted at birth
* Almost always age 30 years or less
* Associated with macrodactyly (abnormal enlargement of digits) innervated by affected nerve in 30 - 67%

Sites

* 85% have involvement of median nerve and its digital branches in hand, wrist and forearm ([Histopathology 1994;24:391](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8045530))
* Also ulnar nerve

Radiology description

* T1 weighted images on MR imaging reveal a fatty mass that is evenly distributed between nerve bundles and is seen running along individual nerves
* Often described as having a "coaxial cable-like" appearance on axial scans ([Acta Radiol 2003;44:326](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12752006))

Case reports

* 26 year old man with sciatic nerve involvement ([Microsurgery 2009;29:66](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18942654))
* 26 year old man with mass on palm of hand ([Case of the Week #158](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case158.htm))
* 32 year old woman ([J Orthop Surg (Hong Kong) 2011;19:123](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21519094))
* 46 year old man with bilateral involvement ([Muscle Nerve 1998;21:656](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9572249))

Treatment

* Benign but often no effective treatment as resection causes sensory and motor deficits ([J Neurosurg 1998;89:683](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9761069))
* Carpal tunnel release may relieve symptoms of median nerve involvement
* Amputation if severe deformity
* May recur in 33 - 60% if incomplete resection

Median nerve

Gross description

* Fusiform enlargement of nerve by yellow adipose tissue, confined within epineurium

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Infiltration of epineurium and perineurium by adipose and fibrous tissue (collagen), causing enlargement of nerve
* Concentric perineurial fibrous tissue and pseudo-onion bulb formation
* Occasionally metaplastic bone

Differential diagnosis

* [Diffuse lipomatosis:](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueadiposelipomatosis.html) not confined to epineurium
* [Intraneural lipoma:](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueadiposelipoma.html) mass of fatty tissue displaces nerve bundles, but does not separate them
* [Traumatic neuroma:](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/softtissue.html) onion bulb formation, usually lacks concentric perineural fibrosis, has high T2 signal density on magnetic resonance imaging
* **Differential diagnosis of macrodactyly**:
  + **Angiomatosis**
  + **Klippel-Trenaunay-Weber syndrome** ([eMedicine](http://reference.medscape.com/article/1084257-overview))
  + [Neurofibromatosis type 1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueNF1.html)
  + **Proteus syndrome** ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proteus_syndrome))
