Definition / general

* Rare tumor composed predominantly of malignant myofibroblasts

Essential features

* Deep intramuscular tumor of head and neck (tongue and oral cavity) and extremities (1.5 - 17 cm), rarely abdominopelvic ([J Clin Pathol 2008;61:301](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17513510))
* Commonly recurs, only rarely metastasizes; recurrences may be higher grade
* No corresponding high grade myofibroblastic sarcoma; such a tumor would fit under the designation of undifferentiated pleomorphic sarcoma
* No uniformly accepted criteria for diagnosis; some authors suggest electron microscopy appearance is the gold standard ([Int J Surg Pathol 2013;21:29](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22843641))

Terminology

* Also called myofibrosarcoma

Epidemiology

* Any age but predominantly patients in their 30s or 40s; slight male predominance

Case reports

* 49 year old woman with laryngeal mass ([Case #310](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case310.htm))

Treatment

* Complete excision

Gross description

* Firm, pale, fibrous cut surface, ill defined margins

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Circumscribed to diffusely infiltrative with fascicles or storiform growth of spindled tumor cells
* Cells have ill defined pale eosinophilic cytoplasm, fusiform nuclei that are elongated or wavy with evenly distributed chromatin or round and vesicular with indentations and small nucleoli
* At least focal moderate nuclear atypia with hyperchromasia and irregular nuclear membranes
* Collagenous matrix with prominent hyalinization
* May have numerous thin walled capillaries
* 1 - 6 mitoses/10 high power fields
* No histiocytic giant cells or prominent inflammation

Positive stains

* At least 1 myogenic marker ([desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [calponin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalponin.html), tram track pattern for [smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) or [muscle specific actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsactinmusclespecific.html))

Negative stains

* [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html), [h-caldesmon](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscaldesmon.html), [ALK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)

Electron microscopy description

* Myofibroblastic features of indented and clefted nuclei, variable rough endoplasmic reticulum, discontinuous basal lamina, fibronexus junctions ([Ultrastruct Pathol 2008;32:97](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18570154))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Gains at 1p11 → p36.3 (66%), 12p12.2 → p13.2 (45%), 5p13.2 → p15.3 (31%), +22 (28%), loss at 15q25 → q26.2 (24%) ([Am J Clin Pathol 2009;131:701](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19369631))

Sample pathology report

* Tongue, resection:
  + Low grade myofibroblastic sarcoma (3.8 cm) (see comment)
  + Margins of resection unremarkable.
  + Comment: Immunohistochemical stains for SMA and desmin are positive. The overall histologic and immunohistochemical findings are most consistent with low grade myofibroblastic sarcoma.

Differential diagnosis

* [Fibromatosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuefibromatosisdeep.html):
  + Minimal nuclear atypia, usually negative for myogenic markers (but may sometimes express them focally)
* [Fibrosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuefibrosarcoma.html):
  + May have focal (not diffuse) myofibroblastic differentiation; herringbone fascicular pattern, a diagnosis of exclusion that should be made with much hesitation ([Histopathology 2006;49:152](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16879392))
* [Inflammatory myofibroblastic tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueinflammyofibro.html):
  + Tumor of myofibroblasts with prominent lymphoplasmacytic infiltrate scattered among tumor cells, usually less atypia than myofibroblastic sarcoma, often [ALK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html)+ ([Hum Pathol 2008;39:846](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18400254))
* [Leiomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueleiomyosarcoma.html):
  + Alternating fascicular pattern, more eosinophilic cytoplasm, more strong and diffuse staining for myogenic makers, usually dense cytoplasmic [actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsactin.html) staining rather than tram track pattern
* [Myofibroma / myofibromatosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuemyopericytoma.html):
  + Often multiple and often in children (although solitary lesions and lesions in adults are both common as well), usually zonated with peripheral myoid nodules with intervening central cellular spindle areas with ectatic "hemangiopericytic" vessels; more diffuse or atypical forms of myofibroma may be very difficult to distinguish from myofibroblastic sarcoma (zonation is helpful feature)
* [Nodular fasciitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuenf.html):
  + Not infiltrative, not deep, < 3 cm usually, lacks chromosomal anomalies seen in myofibroblastic sarcoma (although recently a recurrent translocation resulting in *MYH9-USP6* gene fusion has been described in many cases of nodular fasciitis) ([Lab Invest 2011;91:1427](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21826056))
* [Solitary fibrous tumor (SFT)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesft.html):
  + Diffusely positive for [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), usually patternless haphazard arrangement of cells, usually no atypia (except in malignant SFT)
