Definition / general

* Myoepithelioma, myoepithelial carcinoma and mixed tumor of soft tissue are a group of uncommon neoplasms that share morphological, immunophenotypic and genetic features with their counterparts in salivary gland and skin

Essential features

* Trabecular, reticular, nested or solid growth of variably spindled or epithelioid cells
* Frequent myxoid or hyalinized stroma
* Mixed tumors show ductal differentiation
* Myoepithelial carcinoma shows moderate to severe nuclear atypia, increased mitotic activity and necrosis
* Positivity for S100, EMA, keratin or GFAP

Terminology

* Not recommended: parachordoma

Epidemiology

* Equal distribution between the sexes and a wide age range (median age: 40) ([Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:13](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8990137), [Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12960802), [Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1813](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18043035))
* About 20% of tumors arise in the pediatric population
* Myoepithelial carcinoma seems to be disproportionately common among children ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1813](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18043035))

Sites

* Most myoepithelial tumors of soft tissue arise on the limbs and limb girdles ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12960802), [Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1813](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18043035), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2010;49:1114](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20815032))
* Others arise on the trunk and the head and neck ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12960802))
* Rarely, tumors arise in bone, visceral organs or skin ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2010;49:1114](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20815032), [Am J Surg Pathol 2013;37:960](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23681076), [Mod Pathol 2011;24:1444](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21725291))

Pathophysiology

* Some cases are driven by fusions genes (see [Molecular / cytogenetics description](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/#softtissuemyoepithelialcamolec))

Etiology

* Tumors appear to be sporadic and of unknown etiology

Clinical features

* Myoepithelioma usually presents as a painless nodule of variable size and gradually progressing growth that generally causes no clinical symptomatology ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2018;97:e11209](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29979383), [Case Rep Pathol 2020;2020:3747013](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32607268))

Diagnosis

* Tissue sampling is the gold standard for a definitive diagnosis
* Up to 45% of myoepithelial tumors arising in skin, soft tissue and bone harbor *EWSR1* gene rearrangement (and rarely alternate *FUS* rearrangement) ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2010;49:1114](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20815032), [Head Neck Pathol 2015;9:32](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25804378))

Radiology description

* Computed tomography (CT) and magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) show well circumscribed lobulated tumor with heterogenous moderate enhancement ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2018;97:e11209](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29979383))
* 18F-FDG PET shows increased 18F-FDG accumulation ([AJR Am J Roentgenol 2006;186:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16357423))

Prognostic factors

* Although the majority of morphologically benign myoepithelial neoplasms of soft tissue behave in a benign fashion, there is an approximate 20% risk for local recurrence ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12960802))
* Distant metastasis of morphologically benign myoepithelial neoplasms is rare ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12960802))
* Myoepithelial carcinomas show more aggressive behavior, with a recurrence rate of 39 - 42% and distant metastasis in 32 - 52% of affected patients ([Head Neck Pathol 2015;9:32](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25804378))
* Common sites of metastasis include lungs, lymph nodes, bone and soft tissue ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12960802), [Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1813](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18043035))

Case reports

* 4 year old girl and 6 year old girl with molecularly confirmed primary renal myoepithelial carcinoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:386](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26523541))
* 36 year old woman with a molecularly confirmed myoepithelioma of the metatarsal bone ([Pathol Int 2019;69:42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30605259))
* 40 year old man with a molecularly confirmed myoepithelial carcinoma with a rhabdoid morphology arising in the neck ([Head Neck Pathol 2015;9:273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24993038))
* 45 year old woman with a molecularly confirmed soft tissue myoepithelial carcinoma of the abdominal wall diagnosed on fine needle aspirate ([Diagn Cytopathol 2015;43:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25693574))
* 54 year old man with molecularly confirmed myoepithelioma arising in the sacrum ([Histopathology 2014;65:917](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25066216))

Treatment

* Mainstay of management of localized soft tissue myoepithelioma is surgical resection with clear margins, with pre / postoperative radiation of malignant tumors ([Med Oncol 2019;37:13](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31879796), [Am J Clin Oncol 2018;41:357](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27139621))

Gross description

* Most benign myoepithelial tumors are grossly nodular and well circumscribed ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12960802))
* Malignant tumors typically have infiltrative margins
* Cut surface ranges from gelatinous and glistening to firm and fleshy ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12960802))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Wide range of cytologic and architectural features, both within a given lesion and between different tumors
* Though often well circumscribed grossly, both benign and malignant tumors are unencapsulated and frequently have infiltrative margins
* Multinodular or lobular growth ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12960802))
* Spindled, ovoid, plasmacytoid or rhabdoid cells with eosinophilic or clear cytoplasm ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12960802), [Head Neck Pathol 2015;9:273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24993038))
* Other morphologic appearances include tumor cells with copious vacuolated cytoplasm (formerly identified in so called parachordoma) ([Head Neck Pathol 2015;9:32](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25804378))
* Nuclei are monomorphic, ovoid or round, with minimal atypia; mitotic activity is variable ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12960802))
* Nuclear pleomorphism, macronucleoli and necrosis tend to be associated with malignancy ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12960802))
* Mixed tumors contain ductal elements ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12960802))
* Metaplastic differentiation may include bone, cartilage, squamous epithelium or adipocytes ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12960802))
* Variably myxoid, hyalinized or chondroid stroma ([Head Neck Pathol 2015;9:32](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25804378))
* Myoepithelial tumors with *EWSR1*-*POUSF1* fusion demonstrate distinctive morphological features (nested growth of epithelioid cells with clear cytoplasm) ([Head Neck Pathol 2015;9:32](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25804378))
* Myoepithelial carcinomas have moderate to severe atypia with vesicular nuclei and prominent nucleoli, often together with a high mitotic count and necrosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12960802), [Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1813](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18043035), [Head Neck Pathol 2015;9:32](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25804378))
* There are no clearly defined or validated criteria for grading of myoepithelial carcinoma ([Head Neck Pathol 2015;9:32](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25804378))
* Cytologic atypia has been shown to be the sole predictor of aggressive behavior in soft tissue ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12960802))
* Morphologic variants:
  + Cutaneous syncytial myoepithelioma
    - Microscopically, tumors show a solid sheet-like growth of uniformly sized ovoid to spindled or histiocytoid cells with palely eosinophilic syncytial cytoplasm ([Am J Surg Pathol 2013;37:710](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23588365))
* Mixed tumor
  + Tubuloductal differentiation, appearing similar to their salivary gland counterparts ([Head Neck Pathol 2015;9:32](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25804378))
* Myoepithelial carcinomas with round cell undifferentiated component
  + Round cell undifferentiated component is observed in up to 10% of myoepithelial carcinomas that arise in pediatric patients ([Am J Surg Pathol 2013;37:710](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23588365))

Cytology description

* Moderately cellular smears showing loosely arranged clusters, sheets, isolated cells, rosette-like arrangement with fibrillary material within ([Cytojournal 2017;14:14](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28694835))
* Round to oval, spindle, epithelioid and plasmacytoid cells in myxoid background ([Diagn Cytopathol 2016;44:152](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26644362))
* Bland uniform, round to ovoid nuclei with finely distributed chromatin and eosinophilic or pale cytoplasm ([Diagn Cytopathol 2016;44:152](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26644362))

Positive stains

* Keratin [AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [calponin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalponin.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12960802), [Am J Surg Pathol 2013;37:710](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23588365), [Head Neck Pathol 2015;9:273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24993038))
* [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html), [smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html), [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) tend to be more variable ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:1183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12960802), [Head Neck Pathol 2015;9:273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24993038))
* Cutaneous syncytial myoepithelioma has a distinct immunophenotype of [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)
  and [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) positivity but infrequent cytokeratin staining ([Am J Surg Pathol 2013;37:710](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23588365))
* [PLAG1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsplag1.html) immunostaining is positive in 58 - 100% of mixed tumors but is negative in myoepitheliomas lacking ductal differentiation ([Head Neck Pathol 2015;9:32](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25804378))
* A panel including keratin [AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html),
  [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) and [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html) will identify myoepithelial differentiation in the majority of cases ([Head Neck Pathol 2015;9:32](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25804378))

Negative stains

* [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [ERG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsERG.html)

Electron microscopy description

* Intercellular desmosome-like junctions and basal lamina ([Pathol Int 2005;55:510](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15998380))
* Subplasmalemmal densities and cytoplasmic myofilaments ([Pathology 2002;34:451](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12408345))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* *EWSR1* is most commonly rearranged, followed by *FUS* ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2010;49:1114](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20815032), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2015;54:267](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25706482), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020;59:348](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31994243))
* Fusion partners include: *ATF1, KLF15, KLF17, PBX1, PBX3, POU5F1, VGLL1* and *ZNF444* ([Hum Pathol 2012;43:764](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22154050), [Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:386](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26523541), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2008;47:558](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18383210), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2015;54:63](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25231231), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2010;49:1114](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20815032), [Histopathology 2014;65:917](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25066216), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020;59:249](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31697442), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2009;48:1051](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19760602))
* *EWSR1*-*POU5F1* fusions are associated with malignant myoepithelial tumor with nested epithelioid growth and clear cell morphology ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020;59:348](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31994243))
* Myoepithelial tumors with *EWSR1*-*PBX1/3* fusions are often benign and associated with bland spindle cell and sclerotic morphology ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020;59:348](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31994243))
* *FUS-KLF17* fusions are associated with chordoma-like morphology ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020;59:348](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31994243))
* Superficial tumors and those with ductal differentiation tend to genetically approximate mixed tumors / pleomorphic adenomas of salivary gland with *PLAG1* rearrangement ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2013;52:675](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23630011))
* Novel *SS18*-*POU5F1* has been reported in a malignant round cell neoplasm with myoepithelial phenotype ([Histopathology 2020;77:681](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32516451))

Sample pathology report

* Left leg, biopsy:
  + Myoepithelioma (see comment)
  + Comment: The neoplasm is composed of epithelioid and spindle cells with eosinophilic cytoplasm arranged in anastomosing cords and nests. Nuclei are monomorphic, ovoid or round, with minimal atypia. Interspersed between the cells there is abundant myxohyaline stroma. Occasional mitotic figures are identified (2 mitoses/10 high power fields). The cells are positive for SMA, S100, keratin AE1 / AE3 and GFAP. The findings support the above diagnosis. Although the majority of morphologically benign myoepithelial neoplasms of soft tissue behave in a benign fashion, there is an approximate 20% risk for local recurrence.

Differential diagnosis

* [Extraskeletal myxoid chondrosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueeskchondrosarcomamyxoid.html):
  + [INSM1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinsm1.html) expression in 90% of cases; however, [INSM1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinsm1.html) expression was also observed in a small subset (1 of 20) of soft tissue myoepithelioma ([Mod Pathol 2018;31:744](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29327709))
  + Negative epithelial markers ([AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [CAM5.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscam52.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)) ([Hum Pathol 2001;32:1116](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11679947))
  + Characteristic translocations, typically t(9;22)(q22;q12.2), fusing *EWSR1* to *NR4A3* genes ([Cancer 2008;113:3364](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18951519))
  + Smaller proportion of cases show *NR4A3* fused to other gene partners including *TAF15, TCF12* and *TFG* ([Hum Pathol 2014;45:1084](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24746215))
* [Ossifying fibromyxoid tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueossifyingfibromyxoid.html):
  + Most cases show a peripheral shell of lamellar bone ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2014;53:183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24285434))
  + [Desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) expression in 70% of cases ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2014;53:183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24285434))
  + *PHF1* gene rearrangement has been observed in 80% of OFMTs, including benign, atypical and malignant subtypes, with fusion to *EP400* in 44% of cases ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2016;140:371](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27028395), [Hum Pathol 2013;44:2603](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23806526))
  + *ZC3H7B*-*BCOR* and *MEAF6*-*PHF1* fusions occur predominantly in [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) negative and malignant OFMT ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2014;53:183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24285434))
* [Soft tissue chordoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonechordoma.html):
  + [Brachyury](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbrachyury.html) expression ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:572](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18301055), [Am J Surg Pathol 2013;37:719](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23588366))
  + No *EWSR1* rearrangement
* [Myxofibrosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuemyxofibrosarcoma.html), epithelioid variant:
  + **c-MET** overexpression ([Diagn Pathol 2018;13:56](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30126419))
  + Negative for [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [pankeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html) and [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:99](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17197925), [World J Surg Oncol 2012;10:245](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23152982))
  + No *EWSR1* rearrangement
* Myxoid [leiomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueleiomyosarcoma.html):
  + [HMGA2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsHMGA2.html), [h-caldesmon](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscaldesmon.html) and [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) expression ([Hum Pathol 2017;59:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27717885), [Hum Pathol 1999;30:392](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10208459), [Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:927](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10895815))
  + Negative for [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) and [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html) ([Hum Pathol 1991;22:569](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1864587), [Histochem J 1983;15:691](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6350235))
  + No *EWSR1* rearrangement
* [Malignant melanoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticmelanoma.html):
  + [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html) expression ([J Biomed Sci 2010;17:87](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21080952))
  + Epithelial markers negative in majority of cases; however, [pankeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html) expression was observed in undifferentiated / dedifferentiated melanoma ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1987;111:447](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2436593), [Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:181](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26448190))
  + [*BRAF*](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbraf.html) (V600E) mutation ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:181](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26448190))
* [Mucinous / colloid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticmucinouscarcinoma.html):
  + Primary cutaneous mucinous carcinoma of the skin express [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) and [GCDFP-15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgcdfp15.html) ([Am J Dermatopathol 2004;26:353](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15365364))
  + [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html), [SATB2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssatb2.html) and nuclear [beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html) expression is seen in metastasis from lower gastrointestinal (GI) tract adenocarcinoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:1830](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21881489))
  + [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html) and [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html) expression is seen in metastasis from gynecologic adenocarcinoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:1830](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21881489))
  + [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html), [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgata3.html), [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html) and [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html) expression is seen in metastasis from breast carcinoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:1830](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21881489))
  + Upper GI tract adenocarcinomas are frequently positive for [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html) and [MUC6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmuc2.html#muc6) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:1830](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21881489))
  + Lung mucinous adenocarcinomas are frequently positive for [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html) and [MUC1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) negative ([Am J Dermatopathol 2004;26:353](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15365364))
  + *KRAS* mutation ([Cancers (Basel) 2018;10:108](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29621151))
  + No *EWSR1* rearrangement
* [Epithelioid schwannoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueschwannoma.html):
  + Majority of tumors are negative for [keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html); focal keratin expression in rare cases has been described ([Diagn Pathol 2017;12:11](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28103910), [Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:1013](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28368924))
  + [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) and [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) negative ([Diagn Pathol 2017;12:11](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28103910), [Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:1013](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28368924))
  + Loss of [SMARCB1 / INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html) in approximately 40% of cases ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:1013](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28368924))
  + No *EWSR1* rearrangement
* [Epithelioid malignant peripheral nerve sheath tumor (MPNST)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuempnst.html):
  + 67% show [SMARCB1 / INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html) loss ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:673](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25602794))
  + Majority of tumors are negative for [keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) and [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html); focal keratin expression was observed in 5% of cases ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:673](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25602794), [Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:1013](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28368924))
  + Loss of [H3K27me3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsh3k27me3.html) expression has good sensitivity and robust specificity for the diagnosis of MPNST; however, [H3K27me3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsh3k27me3.html) expression is retained in epithelioid MPNST ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26645727), [Histopathology 2017;70:385](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27580378))
