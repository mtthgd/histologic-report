Definition / general

* Iatrogenic benign mass composed of fungi-like spherules that are actually erythrocytes damaged by endogenous and exogenous fat

Etiology

* Due to erythrocyte damage from endogenous and exogenous fat
* Also due to endogenous membranocystic degeneration of fat that occurs in lupus erythematosus and in membranous lipodystrophy with dermal atrophy due to local application of steroid ointment ([Arch Dermatol 1991;127:88](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1986712))
* In the gluteal region, this entity is described in relation to old injections of petrolatum based hormones and penicillin ([Diagn Cytopathol 1988;4:137](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3243159), [J Am Acad Dermatol 1989;21:400](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2666470))

**History**:

* First described by McClatchie ([E Afr Med J 1969;46:625](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/5369143)), who reported 7 patients from Kenya with unusual soft tissue nodules in arm, legs and subcutaneous tissue of buttock
* Called myospherulosis due to the involvement of skeletal muscle in some patients ([Am J Clin Pathol 1969;51:699](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/5770668))
* Five patients were subsequently reported by Hutt in Uganda ([Trans R Soc Trop Med Hyg 1971;65:182](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/5557351))
* Initially these structures were thought to be a fungus, but the usual stains for fungi were negative
* Kyriakos ([Am J Clin Pathol 1977;67:118](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/836618)) reported non African cases in paranasal sinuses, nasal cavity and middle ear; most patients had undergone surgery and the surgical wound was packed with gauze impregnated with petrolatum and tetracycline ointment, suggesting an iatrogenic etiology
* De Schriver and Kyriakos confirmed this etiology by inducing similar lesions in experimental animals ([Am J Pathol 1977;87:33](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/851165))
* Rosai ([Am J Clin Pathol 1978;69:475](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/350035)) and Wheeler ([Arch Otolaryngol 1980;106:272](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6989350)) demonstrated that the spherules were erythrocytes damaged by endogenous and exogenous fat
* Travis ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1986;110:763](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2425778)) and Shimada ([Am J Surg Pathol 1988;12:427](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3287958)) confirmed the presence of damaged erythrocytes by immunostaining for hemoglobin
* Kakizaki ([Am J Clin Pathol 1993;99:249](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8447286)) demonstrated that the wall of the spherules was due to the physical emulsion phenomenon that occurs between lipid containing materials and blood
* The damaged erythrocytes are enclosed by a lipid membrane and later phagocytosed by histiocytes as part of the lipogranulomatous reaction that takes place in adipose tissue

Clinical features

* May occur with aspergillosis of the maxillary sinus ([Oral Sur Oral Med Oral Pathol 1987;63:582](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3295652), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2005;129:e84](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15737057))

Case reports

* 39 year old man with large buttock tumor ([Case of the Week #173](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case173.htm))
* 60 year old man with retroperitoneal tumor ([Asian J Neurosurg 2010;5:91](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22028750))

Treatment

**Treatment and prognosis:**

* Benign process; no treatment needed other than for symptomatic relief

Gross description

* Large saccular cyst-like lesion, surrounded by fat
* May contain oily substance with a yellow color

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Cyst composed of spherules which are damaged erythrocytes
* Wall of the cyst made of fibrous tissue, accompanied by a lipogranulomatous reaction
* Many eosinophilic spherules containing red blood cells are within histiocytes lining the cyst wall
* Some larger spherules resemble a bag of marbles
* Dermal nodules are cystic cavities with a fibrous wall lined by histiocytes and multinucleated foreign body giant cells, with lipogranulomatous inflammation in the adipose tissue adjacent to the cavities
