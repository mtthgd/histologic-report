Definition / general

* *NTRK* rearranged mesenchymal neoplasms show variable morphologic patterns, including lipofibromatosis-like neural tumor, solitary fibrous tumor-like, inflammatory myofibroblastic tumor-like, infantile fibrosarcoma, dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans-like and malignant peripheral nerve sheath tumor-like spindle cell neoplasms and various histologic grades from low to high grade, as well as benign forms ([Mod Pathol 2021;34:396](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32860002))
* Pan-TRK IHC is a reliable diagnostic marker that can be used as a surrogate marker for identification of *NTRK* fusion in the appropriate setting (young age, morphology) to help discover these rare tumors
* Expensive RNA based next generation sequencing (NGS) to detect / confirm specific fusions needs to be performed if patients are candidates for targeted therapy
* Name may change in the future to kinase rearranged spindle cell neoplasm
* May also have mutations in *BRAF, RAF, RET, ALK* ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:28](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28877062), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2018;57:611](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30276917), [Mod Pathol 2021;34:1710](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34088997))
