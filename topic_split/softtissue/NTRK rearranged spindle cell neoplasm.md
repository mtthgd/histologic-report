Definition / general

* *NTRK* rearranged spindle cell neoplasms are a heterogeneous, emerging group of soft tissue tumor entities ([NPJ Precis Oncol 2021;5:69](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34285332))

Essential features

* Though *NTRK* rearranged spindle cell neoplasms may have a wide spectrum of morphologic patterns, these entities may be summarized as follows ([Front Oncol 2022;11:740676](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35070960))
  + Monomorphic appearing spindle cell population with or without fat involvement
  + Often infiltrative or pushing growth pattern on radiology
  + Frequently immunopositive for both S100 and CD34 and negative for SOX10
  + Identification of *NTRK* gene rearrangement events (most commonly *NTRK1* fusions) via molecular testing
  + Positive pan-TRK immunostaining is suggestive but is not entirely specific
  + Response to NTRK treatments after therapy initiation (e.g., via tumor shrinkage on FDG PET / CT scan)

Terminology

* *NTRK* associated lipofibromatosis-like neural tumor ([Pathology 2018;50:519](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29958731))
* NTRK positive tumor resembling peripheral nerve sheath tumor ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:1384](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31219820))

Epidemiology

* *NTRK* rearranged spindle cell neoplasms may occur in all age groups at any location throughout the body, though they are more common in pediatric than adult patients and most frequently occur within the first 2 decades of life ([Pathologica 2021;113:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33179614), [JCO Precis Oncol 2021;1:PO.20.00250](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34036219))
* Whether present in children / adolescents or adults, tumors tend to share similar behavior and genetic patterns ([Histopathology 2023;82:596](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36413100))
* There may be a female to male predominance (as much as 82% of cases), though additional studies are needed to better determine this ratio ([Histopathology 2023;82:596](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36413100))
* Patients of Asian heritage have been noted to have a higher incidence of *NTRK* fusion neoplasms compared to individuals of other ethnicities (0.4% Asian, 0.37% Central / South American, 0.34% African and 0.28% European with P value NPJ Precis Oncol 2021;5:69)
* Patients with Central and South American ancestry also have an increased prevalence of *NTRK* fusion positive soft tissue sarcomas (24% versus 10 - 13% in other ancestries) ([NPJ Precis Oncol 2021;5:69](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34285332))
  + Further studies specific to spindle cell neoplasms need to be pursued for varying patient demographics
* Although *NTRK* fusions occur in Ann Med Surg (Lond) 2022;79:103893)
  + Appendiceal adenocarcinoma
  + Colorectal cancer (1.5%)
  + Congenital mesoblastic nephroma (83%)
  + Cutaneous melanomas (0.3%)
  + Glioblastomas (including multiforme)
  + Gliomas (low grade)
  + Head and neck neoplasms (e.g., squamous cell carcinoma; ~0.2%)
  + Infantile fibrosarcoma (up to 100%)
  + Invasive cervical cancer
  + Mammary analog secretory carcinoma (90 - 100%)
  + Non-small cell lung cancer (e.g., adenocarcinoma, large cell neuroendocrine; 0.2 - 0.3%)
  + Pilocytic astrocytoma
  + Sarcoma (1%)
  + Secretory breast carcinoma (> 90%)
  + Spitzoid tumors (16%)
  + Thyroid cancer
    - Irradiation associated cancer (14.5%)
    - Papillary thyroid cancer (26%)
* Because of the increased tendency of *NTRK* related tumors to occur in pediatric versus adult patients, a potential emphasis on screening for *NTRK* fusions as part of tumor genomic profiling for certain children and adolescent patients has been recommended ([JCO Precis Oncol 2021;1:PO.20.00250](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34036219))
  + It must be noted that this group of entities is emerging and continues to evolve as more literature is produced; therefore, the distinction between *NTRK* fusion neoplasms and *NTRK* rearranged spindle cell neoplasms may not be entirely clear ([Histopathology 2022;80:698](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34843129), [Oncol Lett 2022;24:452](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36380878))

Sites

* Although *NTRK* rearranged spindle cell neoplasms can occur anywhere throughout the body, they predominantly occur in the extremities, head and trunk regions and may present as either superficial or deep tumors ([Mod Pathol 2022;35:911](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35149769))

Pathophysiology

* NTRK stands for neurotrophic tropomyosin kinase receptor, which is a family of transmembrane tyrosine kinase receptors responsible for neural development and, thus, primarily expressed in central and peripheral nervous system tissues (like neurons and ganglia) ([Ann Med Surg (Lond) 2022;79:103893](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35860155))
* There are 3 major members of this receptor family, summarized below (responsible gene in parenthesis) ([Ann Oncol 2019;30:viii5](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31738427), [Eur J Hum Genet 1997;5:102](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9195161))
  + TRKA (*NTRK1* on chromosome 1q21-q22)
  + TRKB (*NTRK2* on chromosome 9q22.1)
  + TRKC (*NTRK3* chromosome 15q25)
* Each receptor has 3 domains with specific functional roles ([Ann Oncol 2019;30:viii5](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31738427))
  + Extracellular, which has 3 leucine-rich 24-residue repeats (LRR1-3) specific to TRK proteins
  + Transmembrane, which contains tyrosine residues necessary for transducing activation signals from the extracellular domain downstream to the intracellular domain via autophosphorylation
  + Intracellular, which docks cytoplasmic adaptors and enzymes that continue downstream signaling once activated
* TRK signaling begins with neurotrophins (ligands) binding to the extracellular domain, causing the formation of TRK dimers ([Sheng Wu Hua Xue Yu Sheng Wu Wu Li Xue Bao (Shanghai) 2001;33:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12053180))
  + According to Chen et al. (2021), activation of these receptors through specific neurotrophins may result in cell proliferation, differentiation, apoptosis and survival through 1 or more cytoplasmic signal transduction pathways (such as PI3K, RAS / MAPK / ERK and phospholipase C gamma) depending on the ligand and receptor ([J Transl Med 2021;19:73](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33593392))
* Mouse studies have shown that other soft and reproductive tissues, like blood vessels, immune system and ovaries, may also express NTRKs for angiogenesis, lymphocyte mediation and follicle assembly / development, respectively; more investigation on human tissues is needed ([Trends Cardiovasc Med 2007;17:140](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17482097), [J Clin Invest 2005;115:653](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15765148), [Endocrinology 1996;137:198](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8536613), [Reproduction 2009;138:131](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19357131), [Development 2004;131:5185](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15459109))
* In aberrancy or malignancy, *NTRK* genes undergo fusion or rearrangement events resulting in constitutive, ligand independent (oncogenic) tyrosine kinase activation and continuous downstream message signaling ([Ann Oncol 2019;30:viii5](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31738427), [Cancer Discov 2015;5:25](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25527197))
* The list of neoplasms with *NTRK* fusions in both adult and pediatric patients is continuously expanding as they are increasingly being detected via genomic analysis methods like next generation sequencing (NGS) ([Ann Oncol 2019;30:viii5](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31738427))
* Though the pathophysiology of *NTRK* rearranged spindle cell neoplasms is not entirely clear at this time and more studies are needed, fusions may result in distinct DNA methylation profiles that drive a common myofibroblastic (spindle cell appearing) differentiation pattern shared among this group of entities ([Histopathology 2023;82:596](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36413100))
  + Tumors with *NTRK1* / *NTRK2* gene fusions may also harbor *RAF1* or *BRAF* aberrancies ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2018;57:611](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30276917))
  + H3K27me3 expression is frequently retained; this is in contrast to malignant peripheral nerve sheath tumors (MPNST), which are on the differential diagnosis ([Eur J Radiol 2021;143:109937](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34547634))
  + Additionally, these neoplasms may also harbor a simultaneous *CDKN2A* deletion, suggesting that deleterious mutations in p16 and p14 may play a role in tumorigenesis ([Mod Pathol 2022;35:911](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35149769))

Etiology

* Etiology of these neoplasms is not completely known at this time
* In general, the most frequently observed fusion pairs for tumors with known *NTRK* events are noted below ([Nat Rev Clin Oncol 2018;15:731](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30333516), [NPJ Precis Oncol 2021;5:69](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34285332), [Histopathology 2023;82:596](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36413100), [Mod Pathol 2022;35:911](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35149769))
  + *ETV6::NTRK3*
    - Most common for both adult (26.4%) and pediatric (20 - 32.7%) patients
    - Most common tumor type with this fusion is from the salivary gland (36.8%)
    - Histologic and genetic similarities with infantile fibrosarcoma and a subset of inflammatory myofibroblastic tumors
  + *TPM3::NTRK1*
    - Second most common (21.5%)
    - Most common tumor type with this fusion is colorectal cancer (29.3%)
  + *LMNA::NTRK1*
    - Third most common (9.5%)
    - Most common tumor type with this fusion is also colorectal cancer (39.4%)
  + *SQSTM1::NTRK3*
    - This fusion was observed in an adult patient with spindle cell neoplasm of the heart
    - *SQSTM1* gene is responsible for generating p63, a known regulator of autophagy, apoptosis and other immune and inflammatory processes
    - It could be postulated that this gene fusion leads to cell cycle disruption and consequent pathogenesis of *NTRK1* spindle cell neoplasms, though this relationship needs to be explored further ([Mod Pathol 2022;35:911](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35149769), [Hum Mol Genet 2002;11:2735](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12374763))
  + *NTRK1::RET*
    - This occurred up to 8% of the time in one study population and is a common fusion observed in papillary thyroid cancer ([Nat Rev Clin Oncol 2018;15:731](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30333516), [Nat Rev Clin Oncol 2017;14:735](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28857077))
  + *NTRK1::IRF2BP2*
    - This was reported in 2 out of 8 cases in a recently published case series; IRF2BP2 is a known regulator of apoptosis, cell differentiation, macrophage regulation and lymphocyte activation and its dysregulation is thought to contribute to oncogenesis ([Histopathology 2023;82:596](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36413100), [J Leukoc Biol 2019;106:717](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31022319))
  + *NTRK1::TPR*
    - This was reported in 1 out of 8 cases in a recently published case series for *NTRK1* spindle cell neoplasms and is also observed in pediatric papillary thyroid carcinoma ([Histopathology 2023;82:596](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36413100), [Cancer 2016;122:1097](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26784937))

Clinical features

* *NTRK* spindle cell neoplasms have wide clinicopathologic presentations, with the following subgroups noted ([Front Oncol 2023;13:1252359](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37876963))
  + Subgroup 1
    - Predominantly affects children
    - Histological appearance includes monomorphic spindle cells locally invading surrounding adipose tissue in a reticular pattern
    - Locally aggressive with rare instances of metastasis
  + Subgroup 2
    - Equal incidence was reported in all age groups
    - Moderate to high grade proliferation of spindle cells, stomal band prominence and perivascular hyaline collagen reminiscent of keloid tissue, though the histological grade may vary from low to high
    - Metastasis is more common than other subgroups and is commonly observed in the lung
    - Varying morphology that can mimic MPNST or fibrosarcoma

Diagnosis

* According to guidance by the current WHO edition, *NTRK* rearranged spindle cell neoplasms have varying morphologic patterns and histologic grades, including haphazard monomorphic appearing spindle cells, infiltrative growth and stromal / perivascular keloidal collagen production
  + In most cases (up to 100%), tumors are positive for S100 and CD34 (focal or diffuse) and negative for SOX10; highlighting this immunostaining profile is essential for diagnosis ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2018;57:611](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30276917))
  + A 2021 study found that pan-TRK immunostaining may also have up to 100% sensitivity for both *NTRK1* (96%) and *NTRK2* (100%) implicated tumors, while *NTRK3* tumors may have slightly less sensitivity (79%) and other cases may have weak / focal staining, increasing the chance for false negatives ([Virchows Arch 2021;478:283](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32915263))
  + Detection of *NTRK* fusions using molecular techniques is also desired for the determination of therapy

Radiology description

* Though radiologic depictions are not entirely clear at this time, a recent study by Kobayashi et al. described the following radiographic findings in 6 patients (3 women, 3 men; mean age of 22 years, range of 2 - 43 years) ([Mol Clin Oncol 2023;18:14](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36798466))
  + Well circumscribed or poorly infiltrative subcutaneous tissue growth depending on tumor behavior
  + Homogenous or heterogenous intense tissue enhancement with gadolinium on magnetic resonance imaging (MRI)
    - Areas of nonenhancement may suggest necrotic regions
  + Intratumoral or peritumoral flow void on axial T2 weighted imaging
  + Areas of pushing or compressive growth on nearby structures may depend on tumor size
* A 2024 retrospective, clinicopathologic correlation imaging study by Spano et al. of 8 women and 4 men (median age of 35.5 years, range of 0 - 66 years) also found that ([Clin Imaging 2024;110:110134](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38631176))
  + Primary tumors may have linear hypointense signal and enhancement patterns without clear fatty components (though fat infiltration is commonly seen on histology)
  + Lungs were frequent sites for metastasis and can be detected as fluorodeoxyglucose (FDG) avid masses on positron emission tomography (PET) imaging
  + Most patients have an initial treatment response to NTRK inhibitors, which can be followed by imaging studies

Prognostic factors

* Cases are limited, therefore complete prognostic criteria cannot be rendered but the current WHO edition offers several pearls of guidance
* In adults, tumor behavior appears to be related to their histologic grade (i.e., the higher the histologic grade, the greater the propensity to metastasize to distant tissues like lung or bone [e.g., MPNST-like morphology])
* Lipofibromatosis-like neural tumor (LPFNT) patterns may locally recur after excision (similar to other fibromatosis entities, such as desmoid) particularly if resection margins are positive (~42% of cases) but are not shown to metastasize ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1407](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27259011))
  + In children, most cases are clinically benign or exhibit this local recurrence behavior, though cases with increased cellularity or mitotic rate may be more aggressive ([Histopathology 2021;78:644](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33438273))

- Tumors with aberrantly expressed tyrosine kinase receptors may be therapeutically targetable, resulting in potentially improved patient outcomes; this supports the importance of identifying these mutations when possible (see [Treatment](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuentrkrearranged.html#ntrkrearrangedtreatment))

Treatment

* Treatment strategy for *NTRK* neoplasms is hotly debated due to their heterogeneous nature and lack of research investigating treatment efficacy
* However, the current preferred treatment of *NTRK* rearranged spindle cell neoplasms is surgical resection followed by adjuvant chemoradiotherapy and tyrosine kinase inhibitor therapy; cases with metastasis may not be amendable with resection ([Nat Rev Clin Oncol 2018;15:731](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30333516), [Mod Pathol 2022;35:911](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35149769))
* Larotrectinib is a pan-TRK inhibitor that is currently in clinical trials and has shown efficacy in treating many *NTRK* fusion spindle cell neoplasms, including those with locally aggressive disease ([Nat Rev Clin Oncol 2018;15:731](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30333516), [Mod Pathol 2022;35:911](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35149769), [Front Oncol 2022;11:740676](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35070960))
* Entrectinib is a pan-TRK, pan-ROS1 and pan-ALK inhibitor that is in clinical trials and is showing varying efficacy against *NTRK* fusion neoplasms ([Nat Rev Clin Oncol 2018;15:731](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30333516))
* *NTRK* fusion spindle cell neoplasms often are the result of a somatic mutation resulting in an amino acid substitution, often rendering lesions immune to first generation TRK inhibitors ([Nat Rev Clin Oncol 2018;15:731](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30333516))
* Second generation TRK inhibitors are in clinical trials and show efficacy in treating *NTRK* neoplasms resistant to first generation TRK inhibitor therapy ([Nat Rev Clin Oncol 2018;15:731](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30333516))
* Because the *NTRK* gene is largely responsible for the synthesis of neurotrophins, on target adverse effects result in various neuropathies and neurological symptoms
  + Other adverse effects reported from high frequency, high dose tyrosine kinase inhibitor therapy include fatigue, gastrointestinal upset and transaminitis ([Nat Rev Clin Oncol 2018;15:731](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30333516))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* According to the current WHO edition, *NTRK* rearranged spindle cell neoplasms occur on a morphologic spectrum of 1 or more possible morphologic presentation types
  + Lipofibromatosis-like neural tumor pattern ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1407](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27259011))
    - Highly infiltrative, monomorphic spindle cell population with haphazard arrangement involving subcutaneous fat (like lipofibromatosis)
    - Tapering nuclei, indistinct cytoplasm, with or without increased cellularity and cytologic atypia
      * Mitotic counts and necrosis are minimal to absent
  + Solid growth, patternless architecture ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2018;57:611](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30276917))
    - Moderate to high cellular proliferation
    - Prominent stromal hyaline bands
    - Perivascular rings of keloidal collagen
  + Zoned appearance with paucicellular or myxomatous stromal areas ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:435](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30585824))
    - Histologic grade may be low or high
    - High grade tumors exhibit increased cellularity arranged in fascicles, high mitotic count and may have necrosis
  + Myopericytoma / hemangiopericytoma-like pattern ([J Pathol 2016;238:700](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26863915))
    - Thick walled dysplastic vessels may be seen
    - Segmental or diffuse myxohyaline changes with a monotonous proliferation of myointimal cells with smooth muscle actin positivity (with CD34) may be present
    - Negative immunostaining of other mesenchymal markers (e.g., STAT6)

*NTRK* rearranged spindle cell neoplasm

Sample pathology report

* Soft tissue, left forearm mass, core needle biopsy:
  + *NTRK* rearranged spindle cell neoplasm (see comment)
  + Comment: The histological sections demonstrate a variably cellular spindle cell neoplasm with fat entrapment. The neoplasm shows streaming and fascicular arrangements with focal collagenous background. Admixed there are thick walled vessels with focal hyalinization. Only focal nuclear pleomorphism is observed with few mitotic figures (mitotic count 3 per 10 high power fields). No necrosis is identified. Immunohistochemical stains demonstrate that the neoplasm is positive for pan-TRK and CD34 and focally positive for S100. The tumor cells are negative for SOX10, desmin, MyoD1, smooth muscle actin, ALK and STAT6. H3K27me3 expression is retained. The overall findings are most diagnostic of *NTRK* rearranged spindle cell neoplasm.

Differential diagnosis

* **Lipofibromatosis-like neural tumors** / [peripheral nerve sheath-like tumors](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuempnst.html) ([Histopathology 2022;80:4](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34958503), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020;59:575](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32506523)):
  + Also express [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsS100.html) and lack [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html)
  + Variable [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) coexpression
  + Variable mitotic activity (anywhere from 10 mitoses per 10 high power fields may be seen) and necrosis
  + Retention of [H3K27me3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsh3k27me3.html) (as opposed to MPNSTs)
  + Up to two - thirds of cases may show hybrid morphologies with both LPFNT and spindle to ovoid cells within the same neoplasm, complicating the differential diagnosis between the entities
* **Spindle cell sarcoma, not otherwise specified** ([Mod Pathol 2021;34:396](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32860002)):
  + Often older individuals (> 50 years)
  + Negative [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsS100.html), [pan-TRK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsntrk.html) immunostaining or *NTRK* fusions
* [Undifferentiated pleomorphic sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneMFH.html) ([Mod Pathol 2021;34:396](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32860002)):
  + Minor subset of cases show spindle cell morphology
  + Negative [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsS100.html), [pan-TRK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsntrk.html) immunostaining or *NTRK* fusions
* [Infantile fibrosarcoma / congenital mesoblastic nephroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuefibrosarcomainfantile.html) ([Histopathology 2022;80:4](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34958503)):
  + Analogous tumors in pediatric patients (typically within the first 2 years of life, ~80% of cases; the latter occurring in the kidney)
    - *NTRK* rearranged spindle cell neoplasms more commonly occur after infancy, affecting older children and young adults (median age is 13.5 years); though this is not definitive, as up to half of reported cases occur before the age of 1 year old
  + Primitive monotonous ovoid to spindle cells with scant cytoplasm, angulated nuclei, arranged haphazardly or in fascicles
  + Dilated and ectatic vessels are not uncommon
  + May occur more so along the spectrum of *NTRK* rearranged spindle cell neoplasms as this entity continues to evolve and has overlapping features
    - Particularly the presence of perivascular stromal hyalinization and more frequent [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) and [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsS100.html) coexpression may favor a diagnosis of *NTRK* rearranged spindle cell neoplasm but the significance of this distinction is currently unclear ([Histopathology 2022;80:4](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34958503))
  + Variable expression of [smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsS100.html), [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html) and [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) (rare)
  + If *NTRK* gene expression is present, [pan-TRK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsntrk.html) is likely
* [Adult type fibrosarcomas](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuefibrosarcoma.html) ([Histopathology 2022;80:4](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34958503)):
  + Rare *NTRK* fusions have been reported (e.g., in the cervix and uterus)
  + May lack [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsS100.html) and [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) expression
  + Overlapping nuclear / histologic features to high grade MPNSTs
* [Solitary fibrous tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesft.html) ([Cancers (Basel) 2021;13:2913](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34200924)):
  + May also have patternless, monotonous spindle cell pattern with hemangiopericytoma-like blood vessels (or staghorn vessels)
  + Frequently positive for [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html), though dedifferentiated cases may have negative immunostaining
  + *NAB2::STAT6* gene fusion

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/softtissuentrkrearrangedHeider16.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/softtissuentrkrearrangedHeider16.jpg)

A 6 year old patient presented with enlarging subcutaneous soft tissue mass. A core biopsy was obtained and an H&E photomicrograph is provided above. What positive immunostaining pattern would confirm your suspicion of *NTRK* rearranged spindle cell tumor?

1. Pan-TRK, CD34, CD31, ERG
2. Pan-TRK, CD34, S100, H3K27me3
3. Pan-TRK, CD34, S100, SOX10
4. Pan-TRK, CD34, STAT6, S100
5. Pan-TRK, S100, CD34, MyoD1

Board review style answer #1

**B**. Pan-TRK, CD34, S100, H3K27me3. *NTRK* rearranged spindle cell neoplasm has a distinctive immunophenotype, including coexpression of CD34 and S100 along with pan-TRK. H3K27me3 is retained and should also be positive.
Answer D is incorrect because these tumors lack STAT6 staining, which is seen in the differential diagnostic entity of solitary fibrous tumors.
Answer C is incorrect because although S100 is positive, these tumors are negative for SOX10, another important stain typically expressed in peripheral nerve sheath tumors.
Answer A is incorrect because it includes the vascular neoplasm phenotype.
Answer E is incorrect because MyoD1, an immunostain that is positive in spindle cell sclerosing rhabdomyosarcoma (another spindle cell soft tissue neoplasm) is negative in *NTRK* rearranged spindle cell neoplasms.

Board review style question #2

Which of the following is true regarding *NTRK* rearranged spindle cell neoplasm?

1. Aggressive malignant neoplasm with neural and smooth muscle differentiation
2. Demonstrates only *ETV6::NTRK3* gene fusion on a molecular level
3. Evolving, molecularly defined benign neoplasm
4. Has a spectrum of morphological patterns, including lipofibromatosis-like and peripheral nerve sheath tumor (PNST)
5. Surgical excision is the only modality of treatment

Board review style answer #2

**D**. Has a spectrum of morphological patterns, including lipofibromatosis-like and peripheral nerve sheath tumor (PNST)-like. Other patterns include solid growth / patternless, zoned appearance and myopericytoma / hemangiopericytoma-like, according to the current WHO edition. Answers A and C are incorrect because tumor clinical behavior and categorization depend on morphological features and histological grades. Answer E is incorrect because although surgery is the main treatment, it is not the only modality. There are promising clinical trials for the use of pan-TRK and tyrosine kinase inhibitors. Answer B is incorrect because while a subset of NTRK rearranged spindle cell neoplasms harbor the *ETV6::NTRK3* gene fusion, other fusions have been reported.
