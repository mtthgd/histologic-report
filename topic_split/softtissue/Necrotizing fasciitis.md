Definition / general

* Uncommon fulminant soft tissue infection characterized by extensive fascial necrosis
* Usually due to group A streptococci, also community-acquired MRSA ([Surg Infect (Larchmt) 2008;9:469](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18399783))
* May be due to *Clostridium perfringens* and cause gas gangrene ([Cases J 2008;1:252](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18937845))

Clinical features

* Fatal if untreated
* 15% mortality even if treated ([Epidemiol Infect 2009;137:1609](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19351432))
* Particularly serious in patients with badly controlled diabetes ([Diabetes Res Clin Pract 2008;80:218](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18190995))

Case reports

* 85 year old man with necrotizing fasciitis caused by Pseudomonas aeruginosa ([Intern Med 2008;47:553](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18344645))

Treatment

* Promptly start antibiotics and aggressive debridement ([World J Emerg Surg 2007;2:19](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17683625), [J Am Acad Orthop Surg 2009;17:174](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19264710))
