Definition / general

* Ossifying fibromyxoid tumor (OFMT) is a distinctive mesenchymal neoplasm of uncertain differentiation, with cords, nests, clusters and sheets of uniform ovoid cells embedded in a variable myxoid, fibromyxoid or hyalinized stroma, often with an incomplete peripheral shell of bone ([Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:817](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2476942))
* Has potential for local recurrence and metastasis (especially when showing malignant features) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12657926))

Essential features

* Lobulated or nodular growth pattern with frequent incomplete / partial rim of metaplastic bone ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12657926))
* Low to moderate cellular proliferation of uniform, oval to round tumor cells embedded in myxoid, fibromyxoid or hyalinized stroma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:996](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18469710))
* Frequent positivity for CD10, S100 and desmin ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:996](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18469710), [Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12657926))
* OFMTs, including typical, atypical and malignant lesions, display a gene fusion, most commonly involving the *PHF1* gene

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8842/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=81) - Ossifying fibromyxoid tumor, NOS
* ICD-11: [2F7C & XH1DA7](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f1369250201?data=%7B%22dataType%22%3A%22pc%22%2C%22postcoordinationCodeSet%22%3A%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F1369250201%22%2C%22axisToValueIds%22%3A%7B%22histopathology%22%3A%5B%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F1592290487%22%7D%5D%7D%7D%7D) - Neoplasms of uncertain behavior of connective or other soft tissue and ossifying fibromyxoid tumor

Epidemiology

* Occurs over a wide age range (14 - 83 years) with a median age of about 50 years ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12657926), [Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:996](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18469710))
* Men are more often affected than women (M:F = 1.5:1) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:996](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18469710))

Sites

* May occur in all parts of the body
* Lower extremities are the most commonly involved region ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:996](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18469710))
* Most frequently arise in the subcutis but also not uncommonly within skeletal muscle of the extremities ([J Cutan Pathol 2015;42:622](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25950586), [Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12657926))

Pathophysiology

* Majority of cases are driven by fusions genes (see [Molecular / cytogenetics description](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/#softtissueossifyingfibromyxoidmolecular))

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Slow growing, painless mass ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:996](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18469710))
* Longstanding clinical course, ranging from 1 to 20 years or even of longer duration (median, 4 years) ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2016;140:371](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27028395))

Diagnosis

* Tissue sampling is the gold standard for a definitive diagnosis
* At least 85% harbor *PHF1* gene rearrangement ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2010;49:1114](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20815032), [Head Neck Pathol 2015;9:32](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25804378))

Radiology description

* Computed tomography (CT) images demonstrate an incompletely ossified shell in the lesions ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2016;140:371](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27028395), [Oncol Lett 2013;5:1301](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23599783))
* Low to intermediate signal intensity on magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) T1 weighted sequences and heterogeneous high signal intensity, with foci of low signal intensity on T2 weighted sequences ([Oncol Lett 2013;5:1301](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23599783))

Prognostic factors

* Has unpredictable potential for local recurrence and metastasis
* Local recurrence rates for typical, atypical and malignant OFMTs, respectively, are reported as 0 - 12%, 0 - 13% and 0 - 60% ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12657926), [J Cutan Pathol 2015;42:622](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25950586), [Hum Pathol 2017;60:174](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27816723), [Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:1615](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21997683))
* Metastatic rates for typical, atypical and malignant OFMTs, respectively, are reported as 0 - 4%, 0 - 6% and 20 - 60% ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12657926), [J Cutan Pathol 2015;42:622](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25950586), [Hum Pathol 2017;60:174](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27816723), [Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:1615](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21997683))
* Mitotic count of > 2 mitotic figures/50 high power fields is a risk factor for local recurrence but necrosis, tumor size, the presence of satellite nodules and positive margins are not ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:996](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18469710))
* Common metastatic sites are lung and soft tissue

Case reports

* 26 year old man with painless mass in the tongue ([Head Face Med 2013;9:16](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23800162))
* 36 year old man with left thigh mass ([Oncol Lett 2013;5:1301](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23599783))
* 43 year old woman with mediastinal mass ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2015;8:2139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25973116))
* 46 year old man with intracranial mass ([Cureus 2020;12:e10662](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33014664))
* 52 year old man with subcutaneous, supraclavicular mass ([J Cutan Pathol 2006;33:749](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17083695))

Treatment

* Tumor resection and clinical follow up are considered to be the appropriate management of the tumor at present ([Eur J Echocardiogr 2007;8:384](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16762602), [Auris Nasus Larynx 2006;33:325](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16600550))
* Early follow up to discover early local recurrences or metastasis is mandatory ([BMJ Case Rep 2011;2011:bcr0820103263](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22699474))
* Adjuvant radiotherapy does not seem to be indicated, except for the management of distant metastasis or unresectable disease ([Diagn Pathol 2018;13:21](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29587781))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Well circumscribed with a fibrous capsule / pseudocapsule and fibrous septa producing a lobular appearance ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12657926))
* Complete or incomplete peripheral shell of woven or lamellar bone is present in many cases ([Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:817](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2476942), [Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:996](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18469710))
* Tumor may invade through the capsule and form satellite nodules in adjacent tissues ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:996](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18469710))
* Uniform round, ovoid or spindle shaped cells with bland round to oval nuclei, inconspicuous nucleoli and scant eosinophilic cytoplasm ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12657926), [Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:996](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18469710))
* Tumor cells are arranged in cords, nests and sheets ([Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:817](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2476942), [Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12657926))
* Cellularity varies from low to moderate to high
* Variably fibrous or myxoid stroma ([Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:817](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2476942), [Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12657926))
* The following morphologic subtypes have been proposed ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12657926)):
  + Typical:
    - Low cellularity, low nuclear grade and mitotic activity of < 2 mitoses/50 high power fields
  + Atypical:
    - Deviating from typical OFMT but falling short of proposed criteria for malignant OFMT
  + Malignant:
    - High nuclear grade or high cellularity and > 2 mitoses/50 high power fields
* Cellularity:
  + Low cellularity: abundant fibromyxoid matrix between and around individual tumor cell and nests ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12657926))
  + Moderate and high cellularity: progressively less intercellular matrix and in cases with high cellularity, frequent nuclear overlapping ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12657926))
* Nuclear grade
  + Low: small round nuclei with fine chromatin and indistinct nucleoli ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12657926))
  + Moderate: significant nuclear enlargement and occasional small nucleoli but lack the irregular nuclear contours, coarse chromatin and macronucleoli that characterize cells with high nuclear grade ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12657926))
  + High: significant nuclear enlargement, irregular nuclear contours, coarse chromatin and macronucleoli ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12657926))

Cytology description

* Paucicellular to moderately cellular smears ([Diagn Cytopathol 2020;48:396](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31925994))
* Clusters, cords or small aggregates of round, polygonal or spindle cells in myxoid background with osteoid-like or calcified material ([Diagn Cytopathol 2004;30:41](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14696144), [J Cytol 2012;29:205](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23112466))
* Round to oval nuclei with fine granular chromatin and inconspicuous nucleoli
* Scant cytoplasm
* Malignant cases may show significant nuclear pleomorphism with coarse chromatin, irregular contours and 1 - 2 distinct nucleoli ([Acta Cytol 2001;45:745](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11575654))

Electron microscopy description

* Scanty cytoplasmic organelles with areas of condensed intermediate filaments ([Ultrastruct Pathol 2005;29:535](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16316954))
* Primitive intercellular junctions between cytoplasmic processes forming intercellular bridges ([Ultrastruct Pathol 2005;29:535](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16316954))
* Reduplicated basal laminas ([Ultrastruct Pathol 2007;31:233](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17614002))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* *PHF1* gene rearrangement has been observed in 80%, including benign, atypical and malignant subtypes with fusion to *EP400* in 44% of cases ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2016;140:371](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27028395), [Hum Pathol 2013;44:2603](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23806526))
* *ZC3H7B-BCOR* and *MEAF6-PHF1* fusions occur predominantly in S100 negative and malignant OFMT ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2014;53:183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24285434))
* Recurrent *PHF1-TFE3* fusions in a subset of OFMTs with aggressive clinical behavior ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2019;58:643](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30920708))

Sample pathology report

* Soft tissue, excision:
  + Ossifying fibromyxoid tumor (OFMT), atypical subtype (see comment)
  + Comment: The neoplasm is well circumscribed, multinodular, with thick fibrous capsule and incomplete peripheral shell of woven and lamellar bone. Invasion through the capsule and satellite nodules in the surrounding fat are noted. The neoplasm is composed of uniform, round to spindle shaped cells with bland round to oval nuclei with fine chromatin and indistinct nucleoli and scant, pale eosinophilic cytoplasm. Neoplastic cells are arranged in cords, nests and sheets in fibromyxoid stroma. The neoplasm demonstrates moderate cellularity, low nuclear grade and increased mitotic activity (9 mitoses/10 high power fields). The neoplastic cells are positive for CD10, desmin and keratin AE1 / AE3 (focal) and negative for S100, SOX10, MUC4 and CD34. The diagnosis of OFMT is supported by a fusion between *MEAF6* and *PHF1* gene. OFMT has potential for local recurrence and metastasis.

Differential diagnosis

* [Extraskeletal myxoid chondrosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueeskchondrosarcomamyxoid.html):
  + [Desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) negative
  + Characteristic translocations, typically t(9;22(q22;q12.2), fusing *EWSR1* to *NR4A3* genes ([Cancer 2008;113:3364](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18951519))
* [Soft tissue myoepithelioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuemyoepithelialca.html):
  + Consistently expresses keratins and [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html) positivity is relatively common ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:996](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18469710))
  + [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html) positive in most myoepitheliomas (about 80%) and a subset of myoepithelial carcinomas (30%) ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2019;143:1504](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31765250))
  + [Desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) usually negative
  + No *PHF1* gene rearrangements
* [Low grade fibromyxoid sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuelgfibromyxoid.html):
  + Alternating fibrous and myxoid areas ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2017;28:60](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28648941))
  + Bland spindle cells in a whorled or fascicular growth pattern ([Head Neck Pathol 2016;10:161](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26276044))
  + [MUC4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmuc2.html#muc4) positive, diffuse and strong ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2017;28:60](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28648941), [Head Neck Pathol 2016;10:161](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26276044))
  + [Desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) negative
  + Recurrent (7;16)(q34;p11) translocation resulting in the *FUS-CREB3L2* fusion gene, with less common fusion events including *FUS-CREB3L1* or *EWSR1-CREB3L1* ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2017;28:60](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28648941), [Lab Invest 2005;85:408](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15640831), [Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:1077](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16931951))
* [Sclerosing epithelioid fibrosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesclerosingepithelioidfibrosarcoma.html):
  + [MUC4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmuc2.html#muc4) positive, diffuse and strong ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2017;56:695](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28569045))
