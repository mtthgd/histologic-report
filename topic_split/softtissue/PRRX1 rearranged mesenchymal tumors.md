Definition / general

* First formally described in 2019 in a series of 4 cases by Lacambra et al. and at least 20 cases have been described to date ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2019;58:705](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31008539))
* Distinctive mesenchymal neoplasm, likely of fibroblastic origin with the possibility of partial neural or neuroectodermal differentiation, with gene fusions involving *PRRX1* and various fusion partners including *NCOA1*, *NCOA2* and *KMT2D*

Essential features

* Lobulated hypocellular growth of bland spindle to stellate cells in a myxocollagenous stroma
* Often has peripheral staghorn-like vessels with perivascular hyalinization
* Has gene fusions involving *PRRX1* and either *NCOA1/2* or *KMT2D*

Terminology

* *PRRX::NCOAx* rearranged fibroblastic tumor

Epidemiology

* Adults with a median age of 40 years (range: 20 - 76 years) ([Virchows Arch 2023;483:207](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37338620))
* Roughly equal sex distribution ([J Cutan Pathol 2022;49:802](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35583270), [Virchows Arch 2023;483:207](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37338620))

Sites

* Often involves superficial subcutaneous tissues of middle aged adults ([J Cutan Pathol 2022;49:802](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35583270))
* Develops in diverse anatomic sites including the abdominal wall, head and neck, groin, shoulder, thigh, axilla and hip ([J Cutan Pathol 2022;49:802](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35583270))
* Rare cases can be intramuscular and involve deeper soft tissue ([Virchows Arch 2023;483:207](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37338620))

Pathophysiology

* When *PRRX1* is fused to *NCOA1*, the nuclear receptor binding protein of *NCOA1* is lost and is replaced by exon 1 of *PRRX1*, whereas the transcriptional activation domains of *NCOA1* remain intact ([Virchows Arch 2023;483:207](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37338620))

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Frequently presents as a slow growing subcutaneous nodule ([Virchows Arch 2023;483:207](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37338620), [Histopathology 2021;79:997](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34272753))
* Duration of lesion varies from weeks to years, with 1 patient reporting a 4 - 5 year history of a slow growing mass prior to presenting for excision ([Virchows Arch 2023;483:207](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37338620))

Diagnosis

* Molecular testing to confirm presence of *PRRX1* gene rearrangement

Radiology description

* Computed tomography (CT) scan may reveal heterogeneous enhancement ([Virchows Arch 2022;481:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34647172))
* Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) shows an ovoid, superficial mass with low to intermediate T1 signal intensity and heterogeneous T2 signal intensity ([Histopathology 2021;79:997](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34272753))

Prognostic factors

* Best considered to be a benign mesenchymal tumor ([J Cutan Pathol 2022;49:802](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35583270))
* None of these tumors are associated with local recurrence of metastasis ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2019;58:705](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31008539))

Case reports

* 23 year old man with a 2.5 cm pigmented subcutaneous nodule on the shoulder ([J Cutan Pathol 2022;49:802](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35583270))
* 46 year old man with a tender left groin mass ([Virchows Arch 2022;481:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34647172))
* 65 year old woman with a soft tissue mass involving the left posterior chest wall ([Virchows Arch 2023;483:207](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37338620))

Treatment

* Simple excision ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2019;58:705](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31008539))

Gross description

* Encapsulated nodule with lobulated cut surface ([Histopathology 2021;79:997](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34272753))
* Grayish white cut surface, fibromyxoid to rubbery in consistency ([Virchows Arch 2022;481:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34647172))
* Size ranges from 2.3 to 14.0 cm (average: 5.8 cm) ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2019;58:705](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31008539))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Well circumscribed with a nodular or multinodular growth pattern
* Predominantly hypocellular with focal areas of increased cellularity ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2019;58:705](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31008539))
* Monomorphic spindle to stellate cells with bland ovoid nuclei and scant eosinophilic cytoplasm
* Only rare mitotic activity (0 - 1 per 10 high power field [HPF])
* Prominent collagenous or hyalinized to myxoid stroma with rope-like collagen bundles
* Occasional multinucleation may be seen
* Necrosis is not identified
* Rarely contains pigmented melanocytes ([J Cutan Pathol 2022;49:802](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35583270))
* Dilated staghorn-like vessels with variable perivascular hyalinization are more prominent at the periphery of tumor nodules ([J Cutan Pathol 2022;49:802](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35583270))
* Minimal to mild cytologic atypia
* Rare mast cells and rare cases with entrapped benign adipose tissue ([Virchows Arch 2023;483:207](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37338620))
* May have collagenous rosettes ([Virchows Arch 2022;481:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34647172))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Most common fusion partner for *PRRX1* is *NCOA1*, followed by *KMT2D* and *NCOA2*
* *PRRX1::NCOA1* resulting from t(1;2)(q24.2;p23.3)
* *PRRX1::NCOA2* resulting from t(1;8)(q24.2;q13.3)
* *PRRX1* breakpoint is usually located at exon 1 ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2019;58:705](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31008539))
* *NCOA1* breakpoint is often located at exon 13 and the *NCOA2* breakpoint at exon 15 ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2019;58:705](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31008539))
* Corresponding reciprocal fusions may be identified
* Targeted sarcoma fusion panels may not identify the fusion ([Virchows Arch 2023;483:207](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37338620))
* Unsupervised clustering using RNA sequencing data shows that cases appear to cluster closely to solitary fibrous tumor ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2019;58:705](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31008539))

Sample pathology report

* Soft tissue, shoulder mass, excision:
  + *PRRX1* rearranged mesenchymal tumor, 3.2 cm, completely excised (see comment)
  + Comment: Sections show a well circumscribed multinodular spindle cell neoplasm composed of bland spindle cells in collagenous stroma with rope-like collagen bundles. Scattered staghorn-like vessels with perivascular hyalinization are present at the periphery of the lesion. No marked cytologic atypia, no necrosis and no significant mitotic activity is identified. Immunohistochemical stains show that the lesional cells are focally positive for S100 protein and SOX10, while they are essentially negative for pancytokeratin, SMA, desmin and CD34. Next generation sequencing revealed a *PRRX1::NCOA2* gene fusion, supporting the above diagnosis. All control slides stained appropriately.

Differential diagnosis

* [Soft tissue angiofibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueangiofibroma.html):
  + Broad anatomic distribution
  + Tends to occur in middle age adults
  + May be superficial or deep
  + Characteristic vasculature with innumerable branching vessels
  + Positive for [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)
  + Subset shows staining with [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) and [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html)
  + Characterized by *NCOA2* gene fusions (e.g., *AHRR::NCOA2* and *GTF2I::NCOA2*)
* [Solitary fibrous tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesft.html):
  + Occurs throughout the body
  + Patternless pattern of spindle cells
  + Thin walled branching vasculature
  + Positive for [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) and [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html)
  + Recurrent *NAB2::STAT6* gene fusion
* [Low grade fibromyxoid sarcoma (LGFMS)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuelgfibromyxoid.html):
  + Positive for [MUC4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmuc2.html#muc4)
  + Often has *FUS::CREB3L2* gene fusion
  + May have giant collagen rosettes
  + Collagenous stroma transitioning to myxoid areas
* ***YAP1::KMT2A* rearranged sarcoma**:
  + Negative for [MUC4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmuc2.html#muc4)
  + Morphology can mimic sclerosing epithelioid fibrosarcoma (SEF)
  + Presence of *YAP1::KMT2A* and *KMT2A::YAP1* gene fusions
* [Ossifying fibromyxoid tumor (OFMT)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueossifyingfibromyxoid.html):
  + Often has expression of [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) and [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) protein
  + *PHF1* gene rearrangements
  + Does not have expression of [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html)
* [Dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans (DFSP)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticdfsp.html):
  + Spindle cells with a storiform to whorled pattern
  + Centered in the dermis or subcutis
  + Diffusely positive for [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)
  + Presence of *COL1A1::PDGFB* gene fusions
  + May have pigmented cells (i.e., Bednar tumor)
* [Spindle cell melanoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticmelanoma.html):
  + Positive for [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html) and [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) protein
  + May have a component of conventional melanoma
  + May have *NF1* loss of function mutations and *NFKBIE* promoter mutations
* [*NTRK* rearranged spindle cell neoplasm](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuentrkrearranged.html):
  + Positive for [pan-TRK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsntrk.html) IHC
  + Can show expression of [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) protein and [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)
  + Often has an infiltrative growth pattern
  + Usually does not express [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html)
  + Rarely has ropy collagen bundles
* [Fat poor spindle cell lipoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueadiposepleomorphicgeneral.html):
  + Ropy collagen bundles
  + Scattered mast cells
  + Positive for [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) with loss of [RB1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsrb.html) expression
* [Schwannoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueschwannoma.html):
  + Verocay bodies
  + Distinct Antoni A and B areas
  + Strong and diffuse staining for [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) protein and [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html)

Board review style question #1

[![S100 protein](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/softtissueprrx1rearrangedwarmke11.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/softtissueprrx1rearrangedwarmke11.jpg)

Which of the following immunohistochemical stains can be focally positive in *PRRX1* rearranged mesenchymal tumor?

1. CD34 and STAT6
2. DOG1 and CD117
3. S100 protein and SOX10
4. SMA and desmin

Board review style answer #1

**C.** S100 protein and SOX10. Both S100 protein and SOX10 have been reported to be focally positive in *PRRX1* rearranged mesenchymal tumor, raising the possibility of partial neural or neuroectodermal differentiation. Answer A is incorrect because expression of CD34 and STAT6 are usually seen in solitary fibrous tumor. Answer B is incorrect because both DOG1 and CD117 are typically positive in gastrointestinal stromal tumor (GIST). Answer D is incorrect because SMA and desmin are positive in smooth muscle tumors.

Reference: [*PRRX1* rearranged mesenchymal tumors](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueprrx1rearranged.html)

Which of the following fusion partners have been reported in gene fusions involving *PRRX1* in *PRRX1* rearranged mesenchymal tumor?

1. *AHRR* and *GTF2I*
2. *NAB2* and *STAT6*
3. *NCOA1/2* and *KMT2D*
4. *YAP1* and *KMT2A*

Board review style answer #2

**C.** *NCOA1/2* and *KMT2D*. Both *PRRX1::NCOA1/2* and *PRRX1::KMT2D* fusions have been reported in *PRRX1* rearranged mesenchymal tumor. Answer D is incorrect because *YAP1::KMT2A* fusions have been reported in *YAP1::KMT2A* rearranged sarcomas with a sclerosing epithelioid fibrosarcoma-like morphology. Answer A is incorrect because *AHRR::NCOA2* and *GTF2I::NCOA2* fusions have been reported in soft tissue angiofibroma. Answer B is incorrect because *NAB2::STAT6* fusions are associated with solitary fibrous tumor.
