Definition / general

* High grade pleomorphic sarcoma composed of bizarre eosinophilic, round and spindle cells with skeletal muscle differentiation
* Exceedingly rare category of rhabdomyosarcoma (RMS) in adults
* Highly aggressive tumor associated with poor prognosis ([Anticancer Res 2015;35:6213](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26504053))

Essential features

* High grade sarcoma composed of atypical cells that display skeletal muscle differentiation, confirmed with immunohistochemistry for myogenin or myoD1

Terminology

* Pleomorphic rhabdomyosarcoma

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8901/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=82) - pleomorphic rhabdomyosarcoma, NOS
* ICD-11: [2B55 & XH5SX9](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#800945476%20%26%201912206559) - rhabdomyosarcoma, primary site & pleomorphic rhabdomyosarcoma, NOS

Epidemiology

* Usually in the sixth to seventh decade
* Male predominance (1.8:1) ([Anticancer Res 2015;35:6213](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26504053))

Sites

* Deep soft tissues
* Most common in extremities ([Anticancer Res 2015;35:6213](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26504053))
* Less common locations include the pelvis, abdomen, thorax and head and neck ([Anticancer Res 2015;35:6213](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26504053))

Pathophysiology

* Complex karyotype but no signature genetic alteration

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Rapidly growing, painful deep mass, most commonly of lower extremity
* Nodal involvement uncommon ( Majority of patients (70%) with localized disease at presentation ([Anticancer Res 2015;35:6213](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26504053))

Diagnosis

* Pleomorphic cells with brightly eosinophilic cytoplasm ([Mod Pathol 2001;14:595](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11406662))
* Skeletal muscle differentiation as confirmed by desmin, myogenin or MyoD1 ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2018;36:50](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30098515))
* Thorough histologic sampling and evaluation are necessary for diagnostic confirmation

Radiology description

* In MRI T1 weighted sequences, the tumor appears iso or hypointense to muscle
* In T2 weighted and STIR sequences, the tumor is hyperintense with surrounding edema
* In postcontrast sequences, the mass shows heterogeneous enhancement; necrosis is usually present within
* Reference: [AJR Am J Roentgenol 2007;189:371](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17646463)

Prognostic factors

* High propensity for metastatic spread
* Lungs are the most common site for metastases (77%)
* Positive margins associated with disease relapse ([Anticancer Res 2015;35:6213](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26504053))
* Tumors with superficial location (~20%) have a more favorable outcome

Case reports

* 28 year old man with pleomorphic rhabdomyosarcoma of the thigh ([Oncol Lett 2016;12:1921](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27588141))
* 50 year old man with pleomorphic rhabdomyosarcoma in subcutaneous tissue of the trunk ([J Dermatol 2017;44:59](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27569112))
* 53 year old woman with 3 synchronous lung metastasis from pleomorphic rhabdomyosarcoma ([Cancer Treat Res Commun 2021;26:100282](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33360328))
* 57 year old woman with pleomorphic rhabdomyosarcoma of the chest wall ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2020;75:380](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32987291))
* 60 year old man with pleomorphic rhabdomyosarcoma of the thigh ([J Community Hosp Intern Med Perspect 2020;10:287](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32850081))
* 73 year old woman with pleomorphic rhabdomyosarcoma of the uterus ([Anticancer Res 2017;37:2509](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28476820))

Treatment

* Clinical behavior and responsiveness to chemotherapy are similar to adult high grade soft tissue sarcomas
* Poor prognosis with median survival of 7 months
* Surgical resection with wide margins is mainstay of treatment
* Stereotactic body radiation therapy (SBRT) has been used for lung oligometastases ([Cancer Treat Res Commun 2021;26:100282](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33360328))

Gross description

* Can be variable in size (median 8 cm, range from 1 - 30 cm) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:1850](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19898221))
* Cut surface with variable hemorrhage and necrosis
* Appears circumscribed, often in deep soft tissue and involving striated muscle ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:1850](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19898221))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Sheets of large, atypical and frequently multinucleated polygonal, spindled or rhabdoid eosinophilic cells ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:1850](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19898221))
* Cross striations are seldom detected

Positive stains

* Expresses [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [MyoD1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyod1.html), **skeletal muscle (fast) myosin** and [myogenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html) ([Mod Pathol 2001;14:595](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11406662))
  + [Desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) expression is usually strong, while [myogenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html) and [MyoD1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyod1.html) staining can be focal
* Variable [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) and may have focal [keratin AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html) and [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) positivity ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:1850](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19898221))

Electron microscopy description

* Skeletal muscle differentiation with rudimentary sarcomeres containing Z bands or Z band material with thick and thin filaments ([Mod Pathol 2001;14:595](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11406662))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Complex karyotype ([Cancer Genet Cytogenet 2009;192:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19480930))
* Comparative genomic in situ hybridization (CGH) reveals 8 highly amplified regions at 1p36.1-p36.2, 1p31-p32, 1q21-q31, 8q12-q21, 8q24-qter, 11q12-q13, 12q13-q14 and 18q12-q22 ([Am J Cancer Res 2012;2:141](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22432055))

Sample pathology report

* Soft tissue, biopsy:
  + Pleomorphic sarcoma with rhabdomyoblastic differentiation (see comment)
  + Comment: These findings may represent a pleomorphic rhabdomyosarcoma, although heterologous differentiation cannot be excluded.

Differential diagnosis

* [Embryonal](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueembryonalrhabdo.html) or [alveolar rhabdomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuealvrhabdo.html) with anaplastic features:
  + No evidence of embryonal or alveolar component which may have scattered pleomorphic cells or solid foci of pleomorphic cells ([Am J Surg Pathol 1993;17:601](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8333559))
    - Embryonal RMS has ovoid to spindle cells, typically occurs in the pediatric population but can be in adults, and has a predilection for the head and neck and genitourinary system ([Pediatr Blood Cancer 2021;68:e28798](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33306276))
  + Alveolar RMS tumor cells are arranged in nests separated by fibrovascular septa, which frequently exhibit loss of cellular cohesion in the center, conferring a pattern of irregular alveolar spaces; the presence of either a *PAX3::FOXO1* or a *PAX7::FOXO1* fusion gene is detected in the majority of alveolar RMS cases
  + Pleomorphic RMS prefers older adults and occurs in the extremities ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:1850](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19898221))
* [Undifferentiated pleomorphic sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuemfhpleo.html):
  + Diagnosis of exclusion
  + No evidence of skeletal muscle differentiation
* [Pleomorphic liposarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueadiposepleomorphiclipo.html):
  + No evidence of skeletal muscle differentiation
* Carcinomas with rhabdomyosarcomatous differentiation, such as [malignant mixed Müllerian tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusmmmt.html):
  + Carcinomatous component present
* [Melanoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticmelanoma.html) with rhabdomyosarcomatous dedifferentiation ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:181](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26448190)):
  + Presence of *BRAF* mutation, history of melanoma with current presentation of tumor in a characteristic site of metastasis (lymph node, lung, etc.)
* Sarcomas with heterologous rhabdomyoblastic differentiation, such as [dedifferentiated liposarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuededifflipo.html) or [MPNST (malignant Triton tumor)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuempnst.html):
  + Histologic or molecular evidence of another tumor (i.e., *MDM2* amplification present for dedifferentiated liposarcoma or a well differentiated liposarcoma component present), loss of [H3K27me3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsh3k27me3.html) in MPNST or areas of more classic appearing MPNST arising from a nerve

1. A t(2;13)(q36;q14) translocation is found in the majority of alveolar rhabdomyosarcoma

3. May express pankeratin

**A.** A t(2;13)(q36;q14) translocation is found in the majority of alveolar rhabdomyosarcoma

Board review style question #2

Which rhabdomyosarcoma type is more predominant in adults in the sixth to seventh decades and is characterized by the presence of pleomorphic cells with a significant amount of bright eosinophilic cytoplasm?

1. Alveolar rhabdomyosarcoma
2. Embryonal rhabdomyosarcoma
3. Pleomorphic rhabdomyosarcoma
4. Spindle cell / sclerosing rhabdomyosarcoma

Board review style answer #2

**C.** Pleomorphic rhabdomyosarcoma
