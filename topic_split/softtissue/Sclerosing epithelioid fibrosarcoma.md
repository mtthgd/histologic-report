Definition / general

* Sclerosing epithelioid fibrosarcoma (SEF) is a rare, malignant mesenchymal tumor with unique architectural features consisting of cords, nests or sheets of monotonous epithelioid cells within a dense collagenous background ([Virchows Arch 2020 Oct 21 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33084978))
* A subset is related morphologically and molecularly to low grade fibromyxoid sarcoma (LGFMS) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:801](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24441665))

Essential features

* Rare aggressive fibroblastic neoplasm composed of cords, nests or sheets of uniform epithelioid cells embedded in a dense collagenous stroma
* Some cases show morphologic and molecular overlap with low grade fibromyxoid sarcoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:1444](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22982887))
* MUC4 expression ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:1444](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22982887))
* Recurrent *EWSR1-CREB3L1* gene fusions ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:801](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24441665))
* A minority of cases show *FUS-CREB3L2* fusions ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2015;54:28](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25231134))

ICD coding

* ICD-O:
  + [8840/3](https://portal.ct.gov/-/media/Departments-and-Agencies/DPH/Tumor-Registry/ICD-O-3-numeric-table_Jan-10-2018.pdf#page=9) - sclerosing epithelioid fibrosarcoma
* ICD-11:
  + [2B5F.2](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f1132023553) - sarcoma, not elsewhere classified of other specified sites
  + [XH4BT2](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f410892652) - sclerosing epithelioid fibrosarcoma

Epidemiology

* M:F = 1.3:1 with a median age of 45 years ([Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:979](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7661286))

Sites

* Tumors are deep seated and arise most often in the lower extremities and limb girdles, followed by the trunk, upper limb girdles and the head and neck ([Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:979](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7661286))
* Rarely, tumors arise in the kidneys, intestinal tract, mesentery and bone ([Diagn Pathol 2015;10:186](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26449317), [Int J Surg Pathol 2020 Sep 23 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32964769), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2017;56:695](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28569045), [Virchows Arch 2020 Oct 21 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33084978))

Pathophysiology

* Most cases are driven by fusion genes (see [Molecular description](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/#softtissuesclerosingepithelioidfibrosarcoma_molecular))

Etiology

* Tumors appear to be sporadic and of unknown etiology

Clinical features

* Most patients present with a mass of variable duration, with 33% reporting recent enlargement or pain ([Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:979](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7661286))

Diagnosis

* Tissue sampling is the gold standard for a definitive diagnosis
* MUC4 expression ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:1444](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22982887))
* *EWSR1-CREB3L1* gene fusions ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:801](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24441665))

Radiology description

* Zonal architecture on MRI, with a central core of very low signal intensity and a peripheral rim of intermediate to high signal intensity on T1 and T2 weighted images ([Skeletal Radiol 1997;26:619](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9361360), [Virchows Arch 2015;467:339](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26209924))
* Radiographs of bone tumor show osteolytic lesion associated with destruction of the cortex and soft tissue extension ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020;59:217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31675134))
* Positron emission tomography (PET) show increased uptake ([Virchows Arch 2015;467:339](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26209924))

Prognostic factors

* Distinct sarcoma that behaves more aggressively than low grade fibromyxoid sarcoma with a shorter survival, higher metastatic rate and greater propensity to involve deep soft tissue and bone ([Am J Surg Pathol 2020 Aug 4 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32769431))
* Poor prognostic features include proximal location and large tumor size ([Am J Surg Pathol 2001;25:699](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11395547), [Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:979](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7661286))
* Recurrences and metastases, mainly to lung and bone, are common, occurring in about 50% of cases ([Am J Surg Pathol 2020 Aug 4 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32769431))

Case reports

* 16 year old girl and 57 year old woman with renal mass ([Diagn Pathol 2015;10:186](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26449317))
* 18 year old man with intraabdominal tumor ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2017;56:695](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28569045))
* 35 year old woman with gastric mass ([Int J Surg Pathol 2020 Sep 23 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32964769))
* 55 year old woman with right mandible mass ([Am J Surg Pathol 2020;44:594](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31913156))
* 55 year old woman with a mass in the soft tissue of the thigh ([Wien Med Wochenschr 2017;167:120](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27631873))

Treatment

* Radical resection for localized disease ([Med Oncol 2018;35:138](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30187231))
* Pre or postoperative radiotherapy for optimal local disease control ([Med Oncol 2018;35:138](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30187231))
* Limited responsiveness to conventional chemotherapy ([Med Oncol 2018;35:138](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30187231))
* Recurrent genomic alterations (CD24 and DMD) constitute potential therapeutic targets ([Clin Cancer Res 2017;23:7426](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28939748))

Gross description

* Most lesions are grossly circumscribed ([Am J Surg Pathol 2001;25:699](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11395547))
* Cut surface is typically homogeneously white or white-tan, lobulated and very hard ([Am J Surg Pathol 2001;25:699](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11395547), [Virchows Arch 2020 Oct 21 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33084978))

Frozen section description

* Cords, nests or sheets of monotonous epithelioid cells within a dense collagenous background ([Virchows Arch 2020 Oct 21 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33084978))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Proliferation of uniform, small, round to ovoid epithelioid cells with sparse, often clear cytoplasm and round to oval nuclei with inconspicuous nucleoli ([Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:979](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7661286), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020;59:217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31675134))
* Tumor cells are arranged in nests, cords or sheets ([Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:979](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7661286), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020;59:217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31675134))
* Areas with pseudoalveolar pattern may be present
* Prominent hyalinized sclerotic collagenous stroma, sometimes reminiscent of osteoid or cartilage ([Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:979](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7661286), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020;59:217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31675134))
* Occasional myxoid zones with cyst formation and foci of hyaline cartilage, calcification and metaplastic bone ([Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:979](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7661286))
* Focal ectatic staghorn vasculature
* Mitotic figures are generally scarce ([Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:979](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7661286))
* Necrosis is uncommon
* Variant morphology:
  + Complete absence of the defining collagen (nonfibrosing variant) ([Histopathology 2016;68:760](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26212576))
  + Areas of increased cellularity, reminiscent of a round to undifferentiated neoplasm ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020;59:217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31675134))
  + Fibroma-like and conventional fibrosarcoma-like areas ([Am J Surg Pathol 2001;25:699](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11395547))
  + Low grade fibromixoid sarcoma-like areas (so called hybrid LGFMS / SEF) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:1444](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22982887), [Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:801](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24441665), [Pediatr Dev Pathol 2018;21:574](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29426275))
  + Tumor cells with eccentric nuclei and eosinophilic cytoplasm (plasma cell-like) ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020;59:217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31675134))
  + Foci of increased nuclear pleomorphism and atypia

Cytology description

* Diagnosis based solely upon cytologic features remains challenging ([J Am Soc Cytopathol 2020;9:513](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32624384))
* Tumor cells range from small round, medium sized ovoid / short spindle, to epithelioid / plasmacytoid cells
* Sclerotic, fibrous to myxoid stroma
* Cellular variants can mimic plasmacytoma / myeloma and myoepithelioma ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2020;68:228](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32193142))

Electron microscopy description

* Features of fibroblasts, with an abundant, rough endoplasmic reticulum, mitochondria and a well developed Golgi apparatus with dense bodies and collagen secretion granules ([Pathol Int 2002;52:135](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11940218), [Histopathology 1998;33:354](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9822926))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Predominantly *EWSR1-CREB3L1* fusion and complex secondary genomic alterations ([Am J Surg Pathol 2020 Aug 4 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32769431))
* A subset of SEF with composite histologic features of SEF and low grade fibromyxoid sarcoma (hybrid tumors) is more likely to harbor the *FUS-CREB3L2* fusion, which is also seen in most low grade fibromyxoid sarcoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2020;44:594](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31913156))
* Recurrent *YAP1* and *KMT2A* gene rearrangements in a subset of MUC4 negative, *EWSR1 / FUS* fusion negative SEF ([Am J Surg Pathol 2020;44:594](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31913156), [Am J Surg Pathol 2020;44:368](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31592798))
* Rare cases with *FUS-CREM* fusion ([Int J Surg Pathol 2020 Sep 23 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32964769))

Sample pathology report

* Right maxillary mass, biopsy:
  + Sclerosing epithelioid fibrosarcoma (see comment)
  + Comment: The neoplasm is composed of cords and strands of uniform epithelioid cells with scant pale cytoplasm and round to oval nuclei in a dense sclerotic stroma. Occasional mitotic figures are identified (2 mitoses/10 high power fields). Immunohistochemical stains for MUC4, CD56, FLI1, ERG and EMA (focal) are positive in tumor cells while all of the following are negative: pankeratin AE1 / AE3, S100, CD34, SMA, desmin and p63. SMARCB1 / INI1 expression is preserved. Next generation sequencing is positive for *EWSR1-CREB3L1* fusion. The findings support the above diagnosis. Sclerosing epithelioid fibrosarcoma behaves more aggressively than the related low grade fibromyxoid sarcoma with a shorter survival, higher metastatic rate and greater propensity to involve deep soft tissue and bone. Recurrences and metastases, mainly to lung and bone, are common, occurring in about 50% of cases.

Differential diagnosis

* [Low grade fibromyxoid sarcoma (LGFMS)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuelgfibromyxoid.html):
  + May contain areas resembling SEF, either in the primary tumor (so called hybrid LGFMS / SEF) or in recurrences
  + Classic admixture of hypocellular fibrous zones and cellular myxoid areas ([Head Neck Pathol 2016;10:161](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26276044))
  + Majority of cases characterized by t(7;16)(q33;p11) resulting in *FUS-CREB3L2* fusion ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2017;28:60](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28648941))
* [Ossifying fibromyxoid tumor (OFMT)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueossifyingfibromyxoid.html):
  + Most cases show a peripheral shell of lamellar bone ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2014;53:183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24285434))
  + [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) expression ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:996](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18469710))
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) expression ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:996](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18469710), [Am J Surg Pathol 2013;37:1751](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887158))
  + [Desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) expression in 70% of cases ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2014;53:183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24285434))
  + *PHF1* gene rearrangement in 80% of cases, including benign, atypical and malignant subtypes, with fusion to *EP400* in 44% of cases ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2016;140:371](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27028395), [Hum Pathol 2013;44:2603](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23806526))
  + *ZC3H7B-BCOR* and *MEAF6-PHF1* fusions occur predominantly in [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) negative and malignant OFMT ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2014;53:183](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24285434))
* [Metastatic carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticmetastaticcarcinoma.html):
  + Cord, nests and chains of small to medium sized epithelial cells in some breast and gastric carcinomas
  + Densely sclerotic stroma uncommon
  + Clinical evidence of primary tumor
  + Strong keratin expression ([AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [OSCAR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoscarkeratin.html), [CAM 5.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscam52.html)) ([Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2020](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32858538))
  + No *EWSR1* and *CREB3L1* rearrangements
* [Sclerosing rhabdomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesclerosingrhabdo.html):
  + Occasional rhabdomyoblasts
  + Strong [MyoD1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyod1.html) expression ([Surg Pathol Clin 2020;13:729](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33183730), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2014;53:779](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24824843))
  + Variable desmin and myogenin expression ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2014;53:779](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24824843))
  + Negative for [MUC4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmuc2.html#muc4)
  + *MyoD1* mutations ([Surg Pathol Clin 2020;13:729](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33183730), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2014;53:779](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24824843))
  + No *EWSR1* and *CREB3L1* rearrangements
* [Osteosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneosteosarcomageneral.html):
  + Identification of malignant osteoid matrix formation and the integration of clinical and radiological data remain the cornerstone of osteosarcoma diagnosis ([Pathol Res Pract 2016;212:811](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27465835))
  + Significant nuclear atypia
  + Mitoses common and often abnormal
  + [SATB2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssatb2.html) expression in > 90% of osteosarcomas ([Pathol Res Pract 2016;212:811](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27465835))
    - However, a recent study demonstrated [SATB2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssatb2.html) positivity in SEFs of bone ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020;59:217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31675134))
