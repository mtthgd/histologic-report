Definition / general

* This topic contains characteristic molecular alterations for topics in our [Soft tissue](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/softtissue.html) and [Bone & joints](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/bone.html) chapters
* The intent is not to catalog every alteration identified, just those that are considered characteristic
* More information is listed in the linked topics
* Most sarcomas are cytogenetically complex neoplasms
* Recurrent genetic alterations are seen in a subset of bone and soft tissue neoplasms
  + Point mutations: detection may aid in diagnosis or allow targeted therapy
  + Chromosomal rearrangements: recurrent gene fusions can be useful for diagnosis
  + Copy number alterations

Acral fibromyxoma

* Tumor shows deletion of the *RB1* gene ([Hum Pathol 2017;60:192](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27825811))
* See [Acral fibromyxoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesuperficialacral.html)

Alveolar soft part sarcoma

* t(X;17)(p11.2;q25) - *ASPSCR1::TFE3* fusion gene
* Note: *ASPSCR1* is also known as *ASPL*
* See [Alveolar soft part sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuealveolarsoft.html)
* Reference: [Liddy Shriver Sarcoma Initiative: Chromosomal Translocations in Sarcomas [Accessed 19 February 2021]](http://sarcomahelp.org/articles/chromosomal-translocations.html)

Aneurysmal bone cyst, primary

* t(17;17)(p13;q12)
* Up to 75% have *USP6* rearrangements
* See [Aneurysmal bone cyst](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneabc.html)

Angiofibroma of soft tissue

* Detection of *NCOA2* gene rearrangements (*AHRR::NCOA2* gene fusion) supports the diagnosis of angiofibroma of soft tissue ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2017;56:750](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28639284))
* See [Angiofibroma of soft tissue](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueangiofibroma.html)

Angiomatoid fibrous histiocytoma

* t(2;22)(q33;q12) - *EWSR1 / FUS::CREB1* fusion gene ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2007;46:1051](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17724745), [Clin Cancer Res 2007;13:7314](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18094412))
* t(12;22)(q13;q12) - *EWSR1::ATF1* (also present in [clear cell sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueclearcellsarcoma.html))
* t(12;16)(q13;p11) - *FUS::ATF1* fusion gene
* See [Angiomatoid fibrous histiocytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuefhangiomatoid.html)
* Reference: [Liddy Shriver Sarcoma Initiative: Chromosomal Translocations in Sarcomas [Accessed 19 February 2021]](http://sarcomahelp.org/articles/chromosomal-translocations.html)

Angiosarcoma, radiation associated

* Amplification of *MYC* is consistently seen in radiation associated angiosarcoma ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2011;50:25](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20949568))
  + Useful for distinguishing from sporadic tumors and [radiation associated atypical vascular lesions](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuelymphangiomaradiation.html)
* See [Angiosarcoma, radiation associated](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueangiosarcoma.html)

Cellular angiofibroma

* Tumor shows deletion of the *RB1* gene ([Mod Pathol 2011;24:82](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20852591))
* See [Cellular angiofibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuecellularangiofibroma.html)

Chondroblastoma

* *H3F3B* K36M point mutation has been described in 95% of chondroblastomas ([Nat Genet 2013;45:1479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24162739))
  + Mutation specific immunohistochemical stain for H3.3 K36M is helpful to support this diagnosis ([Histopathology 2016;69:121](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26844533))
* See [Chondroblastoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonechondroblastoma.html)

Chondroid lipoma of soft tissue

* t(11,16)(q13;p12-13) - *ZFTA* (formerly *C11orf95)::MRTFB* (formerly *MKL2*) fusion; also in [hibernoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueadiposehibernoma.html) ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2010;49:810](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20607705), [Histopathology 2013;62:925](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23672313))
* See [Chondroid lipoma of soft tissue](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueadiposechondroid.html)

Chondromyxoid fibroma

* *GRM1* rearrangements ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2019;58:88](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30582658))
* See [Chondromyxoid fibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonechondromyxoidfibroma.html)

Chondrosarcoma, myxoid, extraskeletal (EMC)

* t(9;22)(q22;q12) - *EWSR1::NR4A3* fusion gene seen in up to 75% of cases
* Other less common rearrangements include t(9;15)(q22;q21) (*TFC12::NR4A3*) and t(9;17)(q22;q11) (*TAF15::NR4A3*)
* See [Chondrosarcoma, myxoid, extraskeletal](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueeskchondrosarcomamyxoid.html)
* Reference: [Liddy Shriver Sarcoma Initiative: Chromosomal Translocations in Sarcomas [Accessed 19 February 2021]](http://sarcomahelp.org/articles/chromosomal-translocations.html)

Chordoma, poorly differentiated

* Molecularly different from conventional chordoma and characterized for *SMARCB1* deletion and subsequent loss of INI1 expression ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2019;58:804](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31135077))
* See [Chordoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonechordoma.html)

*CIC* rearranged sarcoma

* t(4;19)(q35;q13) - *CIC::DUX4* fusion gene
* t(X;19)(q13;q13) - *CIC::FOXO4* fusion gene
* *CIC::NUTM1* ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:268](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30407212))
* See [*CIC* rearranged sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneCICDUX4.html)
* Reference: [Int J Mol Sci 2018;19:3784](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30487384), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2012;51:207](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22072439), [Hum Mol Genet 2006;15:2125](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16717057)

Clear cell sarcoma

* t(12;22)(q13;q12) - *EWSR1::ATF1* fusion gene
* See [Clear cell sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueclearcellsarcoma.html)
* Reference: [Int J Mol Sci 2018;19:3784](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30487384)

Dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans / giant cell fibroblastoma

* t(17;22)(q22;q13) - *COL1A1::PDGFB* fusion gene
* *COL6A3::PDGFD* and *EMILIN2::PDGFD* ([Mod Pathol 2018;31:1683](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29955147))
* See [Dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticdfsp.html) / [giant cell fibroblastoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuegiantcellfibro.html)
* Reference: [Liddy Shriver Sarcoma Initiative: Chromosomal Translocations in Sarcomas [Accessed 19 February 2021]](http://sarcomahelp.org/articles/chromosomal-translocations.html)

Desmoid type fibromatosis

* *CTNNB1*: point mutations in approximately 90% of cases ([Virchows Arch 2015;467:203](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25838078))
  + Cases with the S45F mutation may be associated with higher risk of recurrence, compared with wild type and other mutations ([Future Oncol 2021;17:435](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33397129))
* Germline mutations in *APC* in patients with [familial adenomatous polyposis (FAP)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumorFAP.html) ([Eur J Surg Oncol 2009;35:3](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18722078))
* See [Desmoid type fibromatosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuefibromatosisdeep.html)

Desmoplastic fibroblastoma

* Occasionally t(2;11)(q31;q12) or 11q12 abnormalities
* See [Desmoplastic fibroblastoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuecollagenousfibroma.html)

Desmoplastic small round cell tumor

* t(11;22)(p13;q12) - *EWSR1::WT1* fusion gene
* See [Desmoplastic small round cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuedsrct.html)
* Reference: [Int J Mol Sci 2018;19:3784](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30487384)

Endometrial stromal sarcoma

* [Low grade endometrial stromal sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusESSlowgrade.html): most common rearrangement is t(7;17)(p15;q21) - *JAZF1::SUZ12 (JJAZ1)*
  + However, multiple other fusions, most commonly involving members of the polycomb repressive complex (PRC), have been reported
* [High grade endometrial stromal sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusESShighgrade.html): most common rearrangement is t(10;17)(q22;p13), resulting in *YWHAE::NUTM2A/B* (formerly known as *FAM22A/B*) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:641](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22456610))
  + *ZC3H7B::BCOR, BCOR* ITD, *JAZF1::PHF1* ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2013;52:610](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23580382), [Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:335](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29200103), [PLoS One 2012;7:e39354](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22761769))
* See [Endometrial stromal sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusessgeneral.html)
* Reference: [Liddy Shriver Sarcoma Initiative: Chromosomal Translocations in Sarcomas [Accessed 19 February 2021]](http://sarcomahelp.org/articles/chromosomal-translocations.html)

Epithelioid hemangioendothelioma

* *WWTR1 (TAZ)::CAMTA1* and *YAP1::TFE3* ([Sci Transl Med 2011;3:98ra82](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21885404), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2011;50:644](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21584898), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2013;52:775](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23737213), [Mod Pathol 2021;34:2211](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34381186))
* See [Epithelioid hemangioendothelioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuehemangioendotheliomaepithelioid.html)

Epithelioid hemangioma

* *FOSB* gene rearrangements ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1313](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26135557), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2014;53:951](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25043949))
* See [Epithelioid hemangioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueepithelioidhemangioma.html)

Epithelioid sarcoma

* Deletions of *SMARCB1* are seen in the vast majority of cases ([Adv Anat Pathol 2014;21:394](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25299309))
  + Immunohistochemical loss of nuclear INI1 expression is important for supporting this diagnosis
* See [Epithelioid sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueepithelioidsarcoma.html)

Ewing sarcoma / PNET, extraosseous

* t(11;22)(q24;q12) - *EWSR1::FLI1* fusion gene
* t(2;22)(q33;q12) - *EWSR1::FEV* fusion gene
* t(7,22)(p22;q12) - *EWSR1::ETV1* fusion gene
* t(17;22)(q12;q12) - *EWSR1::ETV4* fusion gene
* t(21;22)(q22;q12) - *EWSR1::ERG* fusion gene
* t(16;21)(p11;q22) - *FUS::ERG* fusion gene
* See [Ewing sarcoma / PNET, extraosseous](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneewing.html)
* Reference: [Int J Mol Sci 2018;19:3784](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30487384)

*EWSR1::SMAD3* positive fibroblastic tumor

* *EWSR1::SMAD3* ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:522](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29309308), [Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:1325](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29957732))
* See [*EWSR1::SMAD3* positive fibroblastic tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueesft.html)

Fibrous hamartoma of infancy

* *EGFR* exon 20 insertion / duplication ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1713](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27631514))
* See [Fibrous hamartoma of infancy](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuefibroushamart.html)

Giant cell fibroblastoma

* *COL1A1::PDFGB* fusion may be detected by various molecular approaches ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2008;47:260](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18069662))
* See [Giant cell fibroblastoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuegiantcellfibro.html)

Giant cell tumor of bone

* Point mutations in p.Gly34 of *H3F3A* have been reported in 92% of cases ([Nat Genet 2013;45:1479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24162739))
  + G34W substitution in 90% of tumors
  + Mutation specific immunohistochemical stain for H3.3 G34W is helpful to support the diagnosis of giant cell tumor ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:1059](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28505000))
* See [Giant cell tumor of bone](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bonegiantcelltumor.html)

Glomus tumor

* *MIR143::NOTCH* fusions or *BRAF* mutations in benign and malignant glomus tumors ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:1532](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28834810))
* See [Glomus tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueglomus.html)

Infantile fibrosarcoma / mesoblastic nephroma

* t(12;15)(p13;q25) - *ETV6::NTRK3* fusion gene
* See [Infantile fibrosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuefibrosarcomainfantile.html) / [mesoblastic nephroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormesoblastic.html)

Inflammatory myofibroblastic tumor

* Translocations at 2p23 involving *ALK* gene
* t(1;2)(q21;p23) - *TMP3::ALK* fusion gene
* t(2;19)(p23;p13) - *TMP4::ALK* fusion gene
* t(2;17)(p23;q23) - *CLTC::ALK* fusion gene
* *ROS1* and *NTRK3* rearrangements ([Cancer Discov 2014;4:889](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24875859), [Mod Pathol 2015;28:732](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25612511), [Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1051](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27259007))
* See [Inflammatory myofibroblastic tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueinflammyofibro.html)
* Reference: [Int J Mol Sci 2018;19:3784](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30487384)

Lipoma

* t(12;14)(q13-15;q23-24) or related changes involving *HMGA2 / HMGIC* at 12q13-15
* See [Lipoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueadiposelipoma.html)

Lipoma, spindle cell / pleomorphic

* Deletion in 13q has been described in most cases of pleomorphic / spindle cell lipoma ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2011;50:619](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21563233))
* Loss of *RB1* (13q14) can be detected by FISH or IHC to support this diagnosis
* This alteration is shared by a spectrum of similar neoplasms, including [mammary type myofibroblastoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmyofibroblastoma.html) and [cellular angiofibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuecellularangiofibroma.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:1119](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22790852))
* Atypical spindle cell / pleomorphic lipomatous tumor (similar alterations) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:234](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27879515), [Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:1443](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28877053))
* See [Lipoma, spindle cell / pleomorphic](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueadiposepleomorphicgeneral.html)

Liposarcoma, myxoid / round cell variants

* t(12;16)(q13;p11) or t(12;22)(q13;q12) - *FUS / EWSR1::DDIT3* (formerly *CHOP*) fusion gene

* Detection of *MDM2* amplification by FISH (or IHC expression) is useful for diagnosis
* Potential targeted therapy with CDK4 inhibitors ([Crit Rev Oncol Hematol 2020;153:103029](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32593094))

* Inactivating mutations of *NF1, CDKN2A / CDKN2B* and *PRC2* components (*EED, SUZ12*) and associated H3K27me3 loss ([Nat Genet 2014;46:1227](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25240281), [Nat Genet 2014;46:1170](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25305755), [Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:479](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26645727))

Myoepithelioma of soft tissue

* *EWSR1* is most commonly rearranged, followed by *FUS* ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2010;49:1114](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20815032), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2015;54:267](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25706482), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020;59:348](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31994243))
* Fusion partners include *ATF1, KLF15, KLF17, PBX1, PBX3, POU5F1, VGLL1* and *ZNF444* ([Hum Pathol 2012;43:764](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22154050), [Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:386](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26523541), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2008;47:558](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18383210), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2015;54:63](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25231231), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2010;49:1114](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20815032), [Histopathology 2014;65:917](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25066216), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020;59:249](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31697442), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2009;48:1051](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19760602))
* *EWSR1::POU5F1* fusions are associated with malignant myoepithelial tumor with nested epithelioid growth and clear cell morphology ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020;59:348](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31994243))
* Myoepithelial tumors with *EWSR1::PBX1/3* fusions are often benign and associated with bland spindle cell and sclerotic morphology ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020;59:348](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31994243))
* *FUS::KLF17* fusions are associated with chordoma-like morphology ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020;59:348](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31994243))
* Superficial tumors and those with ductal differentiation tend to genetically approximate mixed tumors / pleomorphic adenomas of salivary gland with *PLAG1* rearrangement ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2013;52:675](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23630011))
* See [Myoepithelioma / myoepithelial carcinoma / mixed tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuemyoepithelialca.html)

Round cell sarcoma with *EWSR1*-non-ETS fusions

* Tumors with non-ETS gene partners were previously designated Ewing-like sarcoma but are now classified as round cell sarcoma with *EWSR1*-non-ETS fusions in the latest 2020 WHO classification
* There may be significant correlations between fusion subtype and age at presentation, primary tumor sites and overall survival in these patients ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020;59:525](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32362012))
* Includes *EWSR1 / FUS::NFATC2* and *EWSR1::PATZ1* ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:1112](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30994538), [Hum Pathol 2019;90:45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31078563), [Mod Pathol 2019;32:1593](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31189996), [Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:220](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30379650))

Sarcoma with *BCOR* genetic alterations

* *BCOR::CCNB3* sarcoma (BCS) is a recently defined genetic entity among undifferentiated round cell sarcomas, initially classified as and treated similarly to the Ewing sarcoma (ES) family of tumors
* In contrast to ES, BCS shows consistent BCOR overexpression and preliminary evidence suggests that these tumors share morphologic features with other tumors harboring *BCOR* genetic alterations, including *BCOR* internal tandem duplication (ITD) and *BCOR::MAML3* ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:604](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29300189))
* *BCOR* - BCL6 transcriptional corepressor is located at Xp11.4 locus ([Genes Dev 2000;14:1810](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10898795), [J Clin Pathol 2020;73:314](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32161069))
