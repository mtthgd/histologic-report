Definition / general

* Solitary circumscribed neuroma (SCN) is a benign, slow growing proliferation of Schwann cells and nerve axons within the perineurium

Essential features

* Benign, slow growing proliferation of peripheral nerve cells
* Small (< 1 cm), solitary, skin colored papulonodule on the face of a middle aged adult
* Surgical excision is usually curative and SCNs rarely recur (even with incomplete excision) ([Int J Surg Pathol 2019;27:506](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30834800))

Terminology

* Also known as palisaded encapsulated neuroma (PEN)

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [D36.10](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D36-/D36.10) - benign neoplasm of peripheral nerves and autonomic nervous system, unspecified

Epidemiology

* Typically found in middle aged adults
* No difference in incidence between men and women
* Rarely, multiple SCNs can be associated with MEN2B syndrome ([Am J Dermatopathol 2014;36:562](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24247580))
* Rarely, multiple acral SCNs can be associated with *PTEN* mutations ([Am J Dermatopathol 2014;36:562](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24247580), [Pediatr Dermatol 2017;34:e219](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28543709))

Sites

* Commonly found on the head and neck (especially near mucocutaneous junctions) and oral cavity ([Int J Surg Pathol 2019;27:506](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30834800), [J Yeungnam Med Sci 2022;39:168](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34237212))
* Less often found on trunk, extremities and genitalia ([Cureus 2016;8:e726](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27630799), [J Dent Sci 2021;16:1041](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34141127), [Dermatol Online J 2020;26:13030/qt3b10r3zk](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33423423))
* Multiple acral PENs can be associated with *PTEN* mutations ([Pediatr Dermatol 2017;34:e219](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28543709), [J Cutan Pathol 2022;49:82](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34310726))

Pathophysiology

* Poorly understood at this time
* Thought to be reactive but are often reported without any history of inciting trauma ([Cutis 2013;92:167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24195095))
* Has not been found to be associated with malignancies

Etiology

* Largely unknown
* Benign neoplasm that is thought to be idiopathic or reactive in nature ([Cutis 2013;92:167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24195095), [J Yeungnam Med Sci 2022;39:168](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34237212))
* Rarely, multiple SCNs can be associated with MEN2B ([Am J Dermatopathol 2014;36:562](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24247580))
* Rarely, multiple acral SCNs can be associated with *PTEN* mutations ([Pediatr Dermatol 2017;34:e219](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28543709), [J Cutan Pathol 2022;49:82](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34310726))

Clinical features

* Usually solitary
* Small (< 1 cm), firm, rubbery, skin colored papulonodules, often near mucocutaneous junctions
* Generally asymptomatic
* Reference: [VisualDx: Palisaded Encapsulated Neuroma [Accessed 21 August 2024]](https://www.visualdx.com/visualdx/diagnosis/palisaded%2Bencapsulated%2Bneuroma?diagnosisId=54651&moduleId=101)

Diagnosis

* Definitive diagnosis requires skin biopsy with histopathologic evaluation

Prognostic factors

* Benign tumor but can be cosmetically bothersome
* Surgical excision is curative
* Rarely recurs, even with incomplete excision ([J Yeungnam Med Sci 2022;39:168](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34237212))
* Acral plexiform lesions and multiple lesions can rarely be associated with underlying genetic mutations ([Am J Dermatopathol 2014;36:562](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24247580), [Pediatr Dermatol 2017;34:e219](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28543709), [J Cutan Pathol 2022;49:82](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34310726))

Case reports

* 30 year old woman with painful nodule on the right cheek ([Dermatol Online J 2020;26:13030/qt11r6714r](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32621706))
* 30 year old man with exophytic mass on the hard palate ([Clin Case Rep 2023;11:e8212](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38028074))
* 50 year old man with dome shaped papule on the upper lip ([JAAD Case Rep 2024;49:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38873248))
* 52 year old man with solitary papule on the glans penis ([Dermatol Online J 2020;26:13030/qt3b10r3zk](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33423423))
* 63 year old woman with painless cystic nodule on the lower lip ([J Yeungnam Med Sci 2022;39:168](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34237212))

Treatment

* Surgical excision is curative and recurrence is rare (even with incomplete excision) ([J Yeungnam Med Sci 2022;39:168](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34237212))

Gross description

* Firm, well circumscribed, gray-white, sphere to ovoid papule ([Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:574](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2660609))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Well circumscribed dermal tumor
* Thin to incomplete perineural connective tissue capsule surrounding the tumor
* Cells are arranged in fascicles or small nodules
* Distinct artificial cleft and cracks between the fascicles
* Compositional cells are narrow, elongated and wavy with tapered ends
* Multinodular or plexiform growth pattern is uncommon ([J Cutan Pathol 1993;20:34](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8468415))
* Rarely, epithelioid cells are present ([Histopathology 1992;20:439](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1587496))

Palisaded encapsulated neuroma (solitary circumscribed neuroma)

Sample pathology report

* Skin, left cheek, shave biopsy:
  + Solitary circumscribed neuroma (palisaded encapsulated neuroma) (see comment)
  + Comment: Sections show a well circumscribed dermal nodule with peripheral thin connective tissue capsule. The proliferation is composed of wavy spindle cells in nests and fascicles with intermittent cleft-like spaces.

Differential diagnosis

* [Schwannoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueschwannoma.html):
  + More defined and developed capsule
  + Displays a varied cell density (Antoni A and Antoni B)
  + Lacks the clefting artifact of solitary circumscribed neuroma
* [Neurofibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueneurofibroma.html):
  + Lacks capsule
  + Heterogeneous appearance due to mixed composition of Schwann cells, fibroblasts, collagen fibers and mast cells
  + Weaker [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) staining
* [Traumatic neuroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueneuroma.html):
  + Occurs at site of previous trauma or surgery
  + Disorganized organization of nerve bundles
  + Also has clefting artifacts
  + Has a background fibrotic stroma
* [Leiomyoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueleiomyoma.html):
  + Spindle cell proliferation with fascicular growth pattern
  + Composed of cigar shaped smooth muscle cells rather than wavy buckled Schwann cells
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)-
  + [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html)+ and [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html)+

Board review style question #1

[![Distinct clefting artifact](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/softtissuesolitarycircneuromahuang02.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/softtissuesolitarycircneuromahuang02.jpg)

Which of the following is true of the entity shown in the image above?

1. It is a well circumscribed proliferation composed of cigar shaped spindle cells
2. It is commonly seen in the oral cavity
3. It is often associated with underlying neurofibromatosis
4. It often recurs after surgical excision

Board review style answer #1

**B.** It is commonly seen in the oral cavity. The image above shows a solitary circumscribed neuroma (palisaded encapsulated neuroma). They are commonly seen on the head and neck and oral cavity. Answer A is incorrect because this is a well circumscribed proliferation composed of wavy spindled Schwannian cells (cigar shaped spindle cells would refer to smooth muscle cells). Answer D is incorrect because they are benign neoplasms and excision is curative, with low risk of recurrence. Answer C is incorrect because rarely, multiple solitary circumscribed neuromas are associated with underlying neurofibromatosis.

Board review style question #2

Which of the following stains is diffusely positive in solitary circumscribed neuroma (SCN)?

1. Cytokeratin
2. EMA
3. S100
4. SMA

Board review style answer #2

**C.** S100. Solitary circumscribed neuroma is diffusely positive for S100. Answer B is incorrect because there is only focal positivity in the peripheral perineural cells of the thin capsule. Answers A and D are incorrect because SCN is negative for cytokeratin and SMA.
