Definition / general

* Fibroblastic tumor characterized by haphazardly arranged spindled to ovoid cells, prominent staghorn vasculature and *NAB2-STAT6* gene rearrangement

Essential features

* Haphazard arrangement of spindled to ovoid cells arranged around branching and dilated vasculature within variably collagenous stroma
* Occurs at any anatomical site with a wide range of histological patterns
* CD34 usually strong and diffuse but nonspecific
* STAT6 immunohistochemistry highly sensitive and specific surrogate for *NAB2-STAT6* gene fusion ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:390](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24030747))
* Risk stratification preferred over anatomic staging ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:1433](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28731041))

Terminology

* Hemangiopericytoma (no longer preferred) ([Cancer 1975;36:2232](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1203874))
* Giant cell angiofibroma (no longer preferred)

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [D48.1](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D37-D48/D48-/D48.1) - neoplasm of uncertain behavior of connective and other soft tissue

Epidemiology

* M = F
* Adults, peak incidence 40 - 70 years old ([Cancer 1975;36:2232](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1203874))

Sites

* Any anatomic site: superficial and deep soft tissues, visceral organs and bone ([Cancer 2002;94:1057](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11920476))
* Deep > superficial ([Eur J Surg Oncol 2016;42:1064](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26924782))
* Extrapleural > pleural locations (see [pleural solitary fibrous tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pleurasolitaryfibrous.html)) ([Mod Pathol 2012;25:1298](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22575866))
* Extremities, abdominal cavity, pelvis, retroperitoneum > head and neck (sinonasal, orbit), trunk

Pathophysiology

* Paracentric inversion involving chromosome 12q, resulting in *NAB2-STAT6* gene fusion ([Nat Genet 2013;45:131](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23313954), [Nat Genet 2013;45:180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23313952))
* *NAB2-STAT6* fusions converts NAB2 from transcriptional repressor to activator
* Altered NAB2 function leads to constitutive (deregulated) expression of early growth response 1 (EGR1) target genes, including *IGF2, FGFR1* ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2013;52:873](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23761323), [J Pathol 2010;221:300](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20527023))
* Other mechanisms include overexpression of:
  + *ALDH1, EGFR, JAK2* ([PLoS One 2013;8:e64497](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23734203))
  + *TERT* promoter mutations ([Histopathology 2018;73:843](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29985536))

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Symptoms associated with anatomic location
* Somatic soft tissue tumors present as slow growing, painless mass
* Abdominopelvic tumors produce symptoms due to organ impingement ([Eur Urol 2008;54:1188](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18692954))
* Pleural tumors often discovered incidentally but could grow exophytically into lungs
* Paraneoplastic syndrome (Doege-Potter syndrome) extremely rare; due to IGF2 production by tumor ([J Endocr Soc 2019;3:537](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30788455))

Diagnosis

* Imaging features are nonspecific and diagnosis is dependent on histologic findings

Radiology description

* CT: well defined, sometimes lobulated mass that is isodense to skeletal muscle with heterogeneous contrast enhancement ([Clin Imaging 2018;48:48](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29028514))
* MRI: T1 intermediate and T2 hypointense (cellular / fibrous areas) to hyperintense (myxoid areas) signals ([Radiol Clin North Am 2016;54:565](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27153789))

Prognostic factors

* 10 - 30% (local or distant) recurrence rate ([Cancer 1975;36:2232](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1203874), [Ann Oncol 2017;28:1979](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28838212), [Mod Pathol 2012;25:1298](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22575866), [Eur J Surg Oncol 2016;42:1064](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26924782))
* 5 and 10 year disease specific survival rates 89 and 73%, respectively ([Mod Pathol 2012;25:1298](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22575866))
* Adverse prognostic factors: mitotic count > 4 per 10 high power fields, tumor size > 10 cm, necrosis, hypercellularity, cellular atypia, dedifferentiation, location in chest, abdomen or pelvis, *TERT* promoter mutations ([Eur J Surg Oncol 2016;42:1064](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26924782), [Am J Surg Pathol 1998;22:1501](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9850176), [Ann Oncol 2017;28:1979](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28838212), [Ann Surg Oncol 2017;24:3865](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29039030), [Mod Pathol 2012;25:1298](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22575866), [Mod Pathol 2016;29:1511](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27562490), [Virchows Arch 2019;475:467](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31392468))
* Numerous risk stratification models exist, the most widely used one classifies tumors into groups of low, intermediate and high risk for metastasis using mitotic count, age, tumor size and necrosis ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:1433](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28731041), [J Thorac Oncol 2018;13:1349](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29935303))
* French Federation of Cancer Centers Sarcoma Group (FNCLCC) grading system is not applicable

Treatment

* Surgical resection remains gold standard of treatment ([Brain Res 1998;809:297](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19853123), [Ann Surg Oncol 2013;20:4090](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24052313))
* Long term surveillance following surgery recommended ([ERJ Open Res 2020;6:00055](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32832532))
* Combined surgery and radiation therapy may improve local control ([Am J Clin Oncol 2018;41:81](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26270446))
* Role of chemotherapy controversial and limited ([J Thorac Oncol 2015;10:309](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25319181))

Gross description

* Usually unencapsulated but well circumscribed mass, sometimes lobulated
* Exophytic lesions on serosal surfaces
* 5 - 15 cm in greatest diameter
* Gray-white, yellow to red-brown color
* Fibrous and firm cut surface
* Cystic, hemorrhagic or myxoid degeneration may be present
* Tumor necrosis and hemorrhage in malignant tumors
* Reference: [Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:1257](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7573687)

Frozen section description

* Circumscribed spindled cell tumor
* Presence of staghorn vasculature helpful but not entirely specific

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Ovoid to fusiform spindle cells with indistinct cell borders arranged haphazardly or in short, ill defined fascicles
* Dilated, branching, hyalinized staghorn-like (hemangiopericytoma-like) vasculature
* Hyalinized to collagenous stroma, sometimes with streaming of cells between collagen
* Myxoid changes can be prominent ([Mod Pathol 1999;12:463](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10349983))
* Lipomatous variant contains variable amount of fat ([Am J Surg Pathol 1999;23:1201](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10524520))
* Giant cell rich variant, formerly known as giant cell angiofibroma, shows admixed multinucleated giant cells lining pseudovascular spaces ([Am J Surg Pathol 1995;19:1286](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7573691))
* Tumors with high mitotic count, hypercellularity, atypia (round cell and anaplastic morphology, nuclear pleomorphism) and necrosis have been termed malignant ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:1314](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19718788), [Am J Surg Pathol 1998;22:1501](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9850176))
* Dedifferentiated (anaplastic) solitary fibrous tumor shows transition to high grade sarcomatous areas with or without heterologous components ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:1314](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19718788), [Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:1202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22613995), [Ann Diagn Pathol 2013;17:457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23040384))

Positive stains

* [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html): diffuse to focal but nonspecific, present in 85 - 95% of cases, may be lost in malignant or dedifferentiated cases ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:1202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22613995), [Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:1314](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19718788))
* [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html) (nuclear): highly sensitive and specific marker (close to 100%) but could be lost in dedifferentiated cases ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:390](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24030747), [Acta Neuropathol 2013;125:651](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23575898), [Mod Pathol 2015;28:1074](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26022454), [Int J Surg Pathol 2017;25:58](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27189111))
* May have focal [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) (30%) and [actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsactinmusclespecific.html) (20%) ([Hum Pathol 1998;29:636](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9635686))
* [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html) (30%) and [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html) (70%): nonspecific and not diagnostically useful

Electron microscopy description

* Fibroblast-like cells with well developed rough endoplasmic reticulum and surrounded by collagen fibers ([Med Mol Morphol 2009;42:239](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20033371))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* *NAB2-STAT6* gene fusion best detected by multiplexed massively parallel sequencing assays ([Nat Genet 2013;45:131](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23313954), [Nat Genet 2013;45:180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23313952))
* Detection of gene fusion difficult by conventional cytogenetic methods due to close proximity of *NAB2* and *STAT6* on chromosome 12q
* Diverse fusion variants makes PCR based detection methods difficult
* *NAB2* exon 4 *STAT6* exon 2 / 4: most common genotype, associated with thoracic location and older age ([Hum Pathol 2015;46:347](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25582503), [Mod Pathol 2015;28:1324](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26226844), [Cancer Med 2016;5:159](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26686340))
* *NAB2* exon 6 *STAT6* exon 16 / 18: associated with extrapleural location and aggressive phenotype ([Hum Pathol 2015;46:347](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25582503), [Mod Pathol 2015;28:1324](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26226844))

Sample pathology report

* Soft tissue, pelvis, excision:
  + Solitary fibrous tumor (5.6 cm), completely excised (see comment)
  + Comment: Histologic sections demonstrate a moderately cellular spindled cell tumor in a collagenous stroma with prominent dilated staghorn type vasculature. Mitotic rate is approximately 1 - 2 per 10 high power fields. Overt cytologic atypia and tumor necrosis are not identified. Immunohistochemical studies show that the tumor cells are diffusely and strongly positive for CD34 and STAT6 and are negative for SOX10, S100 and AE1 / AE3, supporting the above diagnosis. According to the 2017 risk stratification criteria by Demicco et al, this should be classified as a low risk solitary fibrous tumor.

Differential diagnosis

* [Synovial sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesynovialsarc.html):
  + Can exhibit hemangiopericytoma-like vasculature
  + Negative for [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) and [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html)
  + *SS18-SSX* gene fusions
* [Mesenchymal chondrosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueeskchondrosarcomamesenchymal.html):
  + Frequently exhibits hemangiopericytoma-like vasculature
  + Islands of well differentiated cartilage
  + Negative for [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html)
  + *HEY1-NCOA2* gene fusions
* [Deep fibrous histiocytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticdeepbfh.html):
  + Polymorphous spindled cells arranged in short fascicles and storiform pattern
  + Peripheral collagen trapping
  + Negative for [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html)
* [Mammary type myofibroblastoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmyofibroblastoma.html):
  + Plump spindled cells arranged in short vascicles with interspersed thick collagen bands
  + Usually coexpress [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) and [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html)
  + Negative for [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html)
* [Myopericytoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuemyopericytoma.html):
  + Bland ovoid to spindled cells growing concentrically around small vessels
  + Hemangiopericytoma-like blood vessels may be present
  + Strong and diffuse expression for [smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html)
  + Negative for [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html)
* [Dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticdfsp.html):
  + Subset of solitary fibrous tumor can lack hemangiopericytoma-like vasculature and appear densely cellular and monomorphic
  + Positive for [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)
  + Negative for [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html)
  + *COLIA1-PDGFB* gene fusions
* [Gastrointestinal stromal tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stomachGIST.html):
  + Wide histologic spectrum
  + Arise from anywhere in the gastrointestinal tract
  + Usually [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) positive
  + Negative for [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html)
  + Majority positive for [DOG1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsDOG1.html) and [CD117 (c-KIT)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)
* [Well differentiated](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuewdliposarcoma.html) or [dedifferentiated liposarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuededifflipo.html):
  + Lipomatous solitary fibrous tumor can be mistaken for well differentiated or dedifferentiated liposarcoma
  + Rare cases of dedifferentiated liposarcoma may show weak expression of [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html) ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:390](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24030747))
  + *MDM2* gene amplification by FISH
