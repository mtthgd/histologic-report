Definition / general

* Spindle cell lipoma and pleomorphic lipoma are benign adipocytic tumors, currently regarded as morphologic variants of a single neoplasm
* Compose approximately 1.5% of all lipomatous tumors ([Histopathology 1987;11:803](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3623439))
* First described in 1975 by Enzinger and Harvey ([Cancer 1975;36:1852](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1192370))

Essential features

* Spindle cell lipoma contains a varying mixture of mature adipocytes, bland spindle cells and ropy collagen fibers ([Cancer 1975;36:1852](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1192370))
* Pleomorphic lipoma also contains pleomorphic and multinucleated floret-like giant cells

Terminology

* Dendritic fibromyxolipoma (not recommended)

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8857/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=81) - spindle cell lipoma
* ICD-11:
  + [2E80.0Z & XH30M7](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#1090000716%2Funspecified%20%26%201257531697) - lipoma, unspecified site & pleomorphic lipoma
  + [2E80.0Z & XH4E98](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#1090000716%2Funspecified%20%26%20236799898) - lipoma, unspecified site & spindle cell lipoma

Epidemiology

* Commonly affects middle aged to elderly men (45 - 70 years) ([Cancer 1975;36:1852](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1192370))
* Very rare in patients Uncommon compared with conventional lipoma (ratio = 1:60)

Sites

* Mostly superficial, extremely rare in deep soft tissue
* 80% arise in the subcutaneous tissue of the posterior neck, back and shoulders ([Cancer 1975;36:1852](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1192370), [Cancer 1981;47:126](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7459800))
* 20% involve different sites, including face, oral cavity and trunk / extremities ([Acta Pathol Microbiol Scand A 1976;84:477](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/998247), [Histopathology 1987;11:803](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3623439), [Histopathology 2015;66:430](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25219904), [Am J Clin Pathol 2016;146:487](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27686175))
* Cases in women more likely to arise in atypical locations ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:1267](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28719462))
* Intramuscular and dermal based lesions rarely seen ([Am J Clin Pathol 2016;146:487](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27686175), [Am J Dermatopathol 2000;22:496](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11190440))

Pathophysiology

* Characterized by partial or whole chromosome 13 or 16 deletions ([Histopathology 1997;31:222](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9354891))
* Breakpoints cluster around 13q14 where the *RB1* gene resides ([Int J Cancer 2003;103:616](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12494468))
* Part of the so called 13q / *RB1* family of tumors, which includes myofibroblastoma and cellular angiofibroma ([Hum Pathol 2012;43:1887](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22575260), [Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:3617](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26523539), [Mod Pathol 2011;24:82](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20852591))

Etiology

* Familial cases with multiple lesions have been described ([Am J Surg Pathol 1998;22:40](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9422314), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2003;48:82](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12522375))

Clinical features

* Asymptomatic
* Longstanding, mobile lesion in subcutaneous tissue
* Usually solitary
* Shawl region of posterior neck / shoulder / upper back ([Cancer 1975;36:1852](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1192370))
* Small size (most

Diagnosis

* Superficial, well circumscribed adipocytic lesion involving characteristic shawl region in a middle aged to elderly man ([Cancer 1975;36:1852](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1192370))
* Demonstration of CD34 positivity and loss of RB1 helpful

Radiology description

* MRI
  + Appearance can vary from completely nonfatty to lipoma-like ([Clin Radiol 2020;75:396.e15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31932047))
  + Findings nonspecific
  + Helpful in determining extent of lesion
  + Fat free variant may be misdiagnosed as sarcoma

Prognostic factors

* Benign lesion, no risk of metastasis
* Local recurrence is rare even with incomplete excision ([Cancer 1975;36:1852](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1192370))

Treatment

* Conservative surgical excision only

Gross description

* Well circumscribed, oval mass in subcutaneous tissue ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2019;36:105](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30850230))
* Yellow-tan cut surface with grey-white and myxoid foci
* Texture firmer than ordinary lipoma
* Dermal and intramuscular tumors may have an infiltrative appearance

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Triad of mature adipocytes, bland spindle cells and hyalinized rope-like collagen fibers
* Multinucleated stromal giant cells and floret cells in pleomorphic lipoma
* Myxoid background with mast cells frequent
* Short and stubby spindle cells show occasional palisading
* Rare lipoblasts present in half of cases ([Hum Pathol 2017;65:140](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28546131))
* Infiltrative growth, necrosis, atypical spindle cells, pleomorphic lipoblasts and significant mitotic activity usually absent ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2019;36:105](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30850230))
* Fat poor and fat free subtype: contains little (Am J Dermatopathol 2007;29:437, [Am J Dermatopathol 2009;31:423](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19542913))
* Myxoid subtype: contains abundant myxoid stroma ([Pathol Int 2014;64:346](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25047505))
* (Pseudo) angiomatous subtype: contains dilated endothelial lined channels and nodules of floating tumor ([Histopathology 1994;24:565](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8063285), [Pathol Int 2007;57:26](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17199739))
* Plexiform variant has been described ([Histopathology 1995;27:533](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8838333))
* Focal cartilaginous and osseous metaplasia rarely present ([Histopathology 1987;11:803](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3623439))
* Extramedullary hematopoiesis may occur ([Histopathology 2001;39:215](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11493343))

Spindle cell / pleomorphic lipoma

Cytology description

* Mixture of mature adipocytes, uniform spindle cells and collagen fibers ([Cancer 2001;93:381](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11748578))
* Spindle cell nuclei fusiform to ovoid with poorly defined, bipolar cytoplasmic processes
* Nuclear grooves may be present
* Myxoid background with mast cells common
* Mitotic activity and nuclear pleomorphism typically absent
* Multinucleated giant cells in pleomorphic lipoma

Sample pathology report

* Soft tissue, back, excision:
  + Spindle cell lipoma (see comment)
  + Comment: Sections show a benign adipocytic neoplasm with bland spindle cells and eosinophilic collagen bundles in a myxoid background with scattered mast cells. No necrosis, marked cytologic atypia or significant mitotic activity is identified. Immunohistochemical stains show that the spindle cells are diffusely positive for CD34, while demonstrating loss of RB1. These results support the above diagnosis.

Differential diagnosis

* [Fibrolipoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueadiposelipoma.html#fibrolipoma301969):
  + Ordinary lipoma with fibrous connective tissue
  + Lacks uniform short and stubby cells of spindle cell lipoma
* [Atypical lipomatous tumor (ALT) / well differentiated liposarcoma (WDLS)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuewdliposarcoma.html):
  + Deep seated (subfascial)
  + Large tumor size
  + Positive for [MDM2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmdm2.html) and [CDK4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdk4.html) by IHC
  + *MDM2* amplification by FISH
  + Cytologically atypical stromal cells
  + Infiltrative growth pattern
* [Pleomorphic liposarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueadiposepleomorphiclipo.html):
  + Pleomorphic lipoblasts
  + Infiltrative border
  + Atypical mitotic figures and necrosis common
  + Complex chromosome losses and gains
* [Myxoid liposarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuemyxoidliposarcoma.html):
  + Younger patient population
  + Characteristic plexiform capillary network
  + Small signet ring lipoblasts common
  + *FUS-DDIT3* gene rearrangement
* [Low grade myxofibrosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuemyxofibrosarcoma.html):
  + Frequently in limbs and limb girdles
  + Elongated, curvilinear blood vessels
  + Pseudolipoblasts may be present
  + Varying cellularity and histologic grade
* [Solitary fibrous tumor (especially fat forming / lipomatous variant)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesft.html):
  + Positive for [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) and [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html)
  + Prominent staghorn vasculature
  + *NAB2-STAT6* gene fusion
* [Mammary type myofibroblastoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmyofibroblastoma.html):
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) positive
  + [Desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) positive
  + *RB1* (13q14) deletion / loss of nuclear Rb expression
  + Short fascicles of monomorphic spindle cells
  + Thickened, coarse collagen bundles
  + Vulvovaginal / inguinoscrotal region
* [Cellular angiofibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuecellularangiofibroma.html):
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) positive
  + *RB1* (13q14) deletion / loss of nuclear Rb expression
  + Hyalinized vessels common
  + Vulvovaginal / inguinoscrotal region
* [Dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans (DFSP)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticdfsp.html):
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) positive
  + Prominent storiform growth pattern
  + Infiltrative growth pattern (honeycomb infiltration)
  + Can show myxoid change
  + *COL1A1-PDGFB* gene fusion
* [Schwannoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueschwannoma.html):
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) positive
  + Hyalinized blood vessels
  + More extensive nuclear palisading
* [Neurofibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueneurofibroma.html):
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) positive fibroblasts
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) positive Schwann cells
  + Wavy nuclei
  + Variable myxoid to collagenous background

What 2 immunohistochemical stains may be helpful in confirming the diagnosis of spindle cell lipoma?

1. CD34 positive and RB1 loss
2. CD34 positive and STAT6 positive
3. Desmin positive and RB1 loss
4. MDM2 positive and CDK4 positive

Board review style answer #2

**A**. Spindle cell lipoma is diffusely positive for CD34 and demonstrates loss of expression of RB1. Atypical lipomatous tumor / well differentiated liposarcoma is positive for both MDM2 and CDK4. Solitary fibrous tumor is positive for both CD34 and STAT6. Desmin positivity and loss of expression of RB1 can be seen in mammary type myofibroblastoma.
