Definition / general

* Spindle cell / sclerosing rhabdomyosarcoma (RMS) is a rare type of rhabdomyosarcoma characterized by fascicular spindle cell morphology
* Subclassification depends on the presence of genetic alterations associated with prognosis
* Predilection for the head and neck / extremities
* 5 - 10% of RMS ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2015;139:1281](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25989287))

Essential features

* Rhabdomyosarcoma with prominent fascicular spindle cell or sclerosing features
* Relatively similar morphology but a genetically and clinically heterogeneous disease, especially in the pediatric population
* Tumors with *VGLL2*, *NCOA2* and *CITED2* gene rearrangements typically arise in infants and have a favorable prognosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:224](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26501226))
* Tumors with *MYOD1* mutations usually arise in adolescents and adults and have an unfavorable prognosis ([Mod Pathol 2019;32:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30181563))
* Tumors with *TFCP2::NCOA2* gene rearrangements can be intraosseous ([Mod Pathol 2020;33:404](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31383960))

Terminology

* Spindle cell / sclerosing are considered the same diagnostic variant of rhabdomyosarcoma

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8912/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf#page=82) - spindle cell rhabdomyosarcoma
* ICD-11: [2B55.Z & XH7NM2](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f800945476%2fmms%2funspecified?data=%7B%22dataType%22%3A%22pc%22%2C%22postcoordinationCodeSet%22%3A%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F800945476%2Fmms%2Funspecified%22%2C%22axisToValueIds%22%3A%7B%22histopathology%22%3A%5B%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F1373994611%22%2C%22axisToValueIds%22%3A%7B%7D%7D%5D%7D%7D%7D) - rhabdomyosarcoma, unspecified primary site & spindle cell rhabdomyosarcoma

Epidemiology

* M > F

Sites

* Sclerosing is more common in adults in the extremities and head and neck regions ([Am J Surg Pathol 2002;26:1175](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12218574))
* Spindle cell is most common in the paratestis, followed by head and neck
* More common in the paratesticular region in the pediatric population ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2015;139:1281](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25989287))

Pathophysiology

* Multiple genetic abnormalities have been identified
* Congenital / infantile spindle cell type: *VGLL2*, *SRF*, *TEAD1*, *NCOA2*, *CITED2* gene fusions ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:224](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26501226))
* Spindle cell / sclerosing type in adolescents / adults: *MYOD1* mutation ([J Pathol 2014;232:300](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24272621))
* Intraosseous spindle cell / epithelioid type: *EWSR1* or *FUS* fused with *TFCP2* or *MEIS1::NCOA2* gene fusions ([Mod Pathol 2020;33:404](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31383960), [Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:695](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30720533))

Clinical features

* Infantile spindle cell RMS with *VGLL2::NCOA2* associated gene fusions has a very good prognosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:224](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26501226))
* Spindle cell / sclerosing RMS with no identified genetic abnormality has a good prognosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:224](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26501226))
* *MYOD1* mutant and *TFCP2* associated gene fusion RMS have a poor prognosis ([Mod Pathol 2019;32:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30181563), [Mod Pathol 2020;33:404](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31383960))

Diagnosis

* Cellular spindle cell fascicles or tumor cells in sclerotic / collagenous background with positivity for desmin, myogenin and MyoD1 ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2018;36:50](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30098515))
* Demonstration of a *MYOD1* mutation or various gene rearrangements may be helpful for subclassification ([Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol Oral Radiol 2022;134:354](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35840496))

Radiology description

* Hypointense to isointense on T1 weighted images and hypointense to hyperintense on T2 weighted images ([Pathol Res Pract 2019;215:152399](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30948206))
* Heterogeneously enhancing soft tissue mass ([J Oral Maxillofac Pathol 2022;26:S103](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35450245))

Prognostic factors

* In adults, recurrence and metastasis rate is 40 - 50% ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2015;139:1281](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25989287))
* Congenital / infantile spindle cell variant with gene fusions has a favorable prognosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:224](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26501226))
* *MYOD1* mutant variants have a poor prognosis ([Mod Pathol 2019;32:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30181563))

Case reports

* 7 year old girl with a spindle cell rhabdomyosarcoma in the parotid gland ([Ann Pathol 2021;41:123](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32713625))
* 15 year old girl with aggressive head tumor and involvement of the optic nerve ([Cureus 2022;14:e21062](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35155025))
* 16 year old girl presented with aggressive intraosseous tumor ([Cold Spring Harb Mol Case Stud 2022;8:a006209](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35768243))
* 27 year old woman with facial spindle cell rhabdomyosarcoma ([J Oral Maxillofac Pathol 2022;26:S103](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35450245))
* 29 year old man presented with tongue tumor ([J Maxillofac Oral Surg 2021;20:464](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34408375))

Treatment

* No consensus for optimal treatment
* In localized congenital type, complete surgical resection was associated with favorable prognosis ([Eur J Cancer 2022;168:56](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35452896))
* Most cases are treated with combination of surgery, radiotherapy and chemotherapy ([J Maxillofac Oral Surg 2021;20:464](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34408375))
* Recurrence and progression are common ([J Maxillofac Oral Surg 2021;20:464](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34408375))

Cystic mass

Gross description

* Variably circumscribed tumor with a tan, sometimes whorled cut surface

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Fascicles of spindle cells with a herringbone growth pattern resembling leiomyosarcoma or fibrosarcoma
* Primitive undifferentiated areas with round cells and hyperchromatic nuclei may focally be present
* Tadpole or strap cells and rhabdomyoblasts can be seen in some cases
* Sclerosing RMS shows prominent hyalinization / sclerosis; tumor cells in cords, nests or trabeculae in a pseudovascular growth pattern
* In bone, there can be epithelioid cells in sheets and fascicles in addition to the typical spindle cell morphology ([Histopathology 2018;73:514](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29758589))

Positive stains

* Variable [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) and focal [myogenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html) expression
* Prominent [MyoD1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyod1.html) in *MYOD1* mutated cases
* [Keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) and [ALK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html) expression in intraosseous cases ([Mod Pathol 2020;33:404](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31383960))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Should be *PAX* fusion negative
* Congenital / infantile spindle cell type: *VGLL2*, *SRF*, *TEAD1*, *NCOA2*, *CITED2* gene fusions ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:224](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26501226))
* Spindle cell / sclerosing type in adolescents / adults: *MYOD1* mutation
* Intraosseous spindle cell / epithelioid type: *EWSR1::FUS* fused with *TFCP2* or *MEIS1::NCOA2* gene fusions ([Mod Pathol 2020;33:404](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31383960), [Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:695](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30720533))

Sample pathology report

* Soft tissue, neck, biopsy:
  + Rhabdomyosarcoma with spindle cell features (see comment)
  + Comment: The biopsy contains cellular fascicles of tumor cells that are strongly positive for desmin, myogenin and MyoD1. Molecular testing will be performed for further subtyping and reported in an addendum.

Differential diagnosis

* [Alveolar rhabdomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuealvrhabdo.html):
  + Different morphology; has *PAX3* and *PAX7::FOXO1* fusion transcripts ([Pediatr Dev Pathol 2004;7:583](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15630526))
  + [Myogenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html) is generally seen as weak, focal and nuclear
* [Angiosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueangiosarcoma.html):
  + Lacks characteristic hyalinizing matrix of sclerosing RMS
  + Negative for [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [myogenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html) and [MyoD1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyod1.html)
  + [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkersCD31.html) and [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkersCD34.html) positive ([Head Neck Pathol 2015;9:147](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24710732))
* [Mesenchymal chondrosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueeskchondrosarcomamesenchymal.html):
  + Characterized by a distinctive biphasic histology composed of round to spindled tumor cells admixed with areas of hyaline cartilage
  + Spindle cell areas may even show limited expression of [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [myogenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html) and [MyoD1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyod1.html) ([Hum Pathol 2018;77:28](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29559236))
* **Metastatic carcinoma**:
  + Positive expression of [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) is seen in metastatic carcinoma
  + [Desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) and [MyoD1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyod1.html) are negative
* [Osteosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueeskosteo.html):
  + Has osteoid associated with cytologically malignant spindled cells
* [Sclerosing epithelioid fibrosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesclerosingepithelioidfibrosarcoma.html):
  + Composed of epithelioid cells arranged in nests and cords and deposited in a densely hyalinized collagenous matrix
  + However, in almost all cases the tumor also shows foci of spindle shaped sarcoma similar to conventional fibrosarcoma and exhibits [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsEMA.html) or [MUC4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsMUC2.html#muc4) positivity ([Head Neck Pathol 2015;9:147](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24710732))
* Also [synovial sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesynovialsarc.html), [leiomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueleiomyosarcoma.html), **spindle cell carcinoma**, [spindle cell melanoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticmelanoma.html), [fibrosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuefibrosarcoma.html), [malignant peripheral nerve sheath tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuempnst.html) and [malignant triton tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuempnst.html)
