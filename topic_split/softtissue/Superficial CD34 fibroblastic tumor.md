Definition / general

* Superficial CD34+ fibroblastic tumor is a distinctive low grade neoplasm of the skin and subcutis, characterized by a fascicular proliferation of spindled cells with abundant, eosinophilic, granular to glassy cytoplasm, marked nuclear pleomorphism and low mitotic count ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887307))
* Invariably express CD34 and are focally immunoreactive for keratin in close to 70% of cases ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887307), [Histopathology 2017;70:394](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27636918))

Essential features

* Superficial location ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887307))
* Spindle cells with eosinophilic granular to glassy cytoplasm
* Nuclear pleomorphism but extremely low mitotic rate ([Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2018;61:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30004071))
* Diffuse CD34 expression and frequent keratin (AE1 / AE3) immunoreactivity

Terminology

* *PRDM10* rearranged soft tissue tumor

ICD coding

* ICD-0: [8810/1](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/%0D%0A9789241548496_eng.pdf#page=140) - superficial CD34+ fibroblastic tumor
* ICD-11: [2B53.Y](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f1100526834%2fmms%2fother) - other specified fibroblastic and myofibroblastic tumor, primary site

Epidemiology

* Fewer than 40 cases have been reported ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887307), [Histopathology 2017;70:394](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27636918))
* Most tumors occurred in middle aged adults (median age 38 years, range 20 - 76 years) with slight male predominance ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887307), [Histopathology 2017;70:394](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27636918))

Sites

* Tumor may occur anywhere in the body but most lesions appear on the lower limbs, particularly the thighs ([Am J Dermatopathol 2020;42:68](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30702454), [Mod Pathol 2014;27:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887307), [Histopathology 2017;70:394](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27636918))

Etiology

* Unknown at this time

Clinical features

* Tumor typically presents as a slowly enlarging, firm, painless mass of the superficial soft tissue ([J Dermatol 2016;43:934](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26946226), [Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2018;61:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30004071))

Diagnosis

* Tissue sampling with microscopic examination is the gold standard for a definitive diagnosis

Radiology description

* Well marginated tumor without calcification in the subcutaneous adipose tissue on CT / MRI scans ([Oncol Lett 2017;14:3395](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28927093))
* Tumor demonstrates abnormal uptake on 2-(18F) fluoro-2-deoxy-D-glucose (18F-FDG) (PET) ([Oncol Lett 2017;14:3395](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28927093))

Prognostic factors

* Indolent clinical behavior ([Histopathology 2017;70:394](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27636918))
* Majority of patients have no disease recurrence ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887307))
* One patient had regional lymph node metastases 7 years after incomplete excision of the primary tumor ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887307))

Case reports

* 18 year old man with a painless mass in the medial aspect of his right distal thigh ([Oncol Lett 2017;14:3395](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28927093))
* 48 year old man presented with a gradually increasing soft tissue mass in his right forearm of 2 years' duration ([Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2018;61:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30004071))
* 48 year old woman presented with a painless, slow growing, brown nodule on the left thigh ([J Dermatol 2016;43:934](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26946226))
* 50 year old woman presented with a four month history of a slowly enlarging, firm, painless mass at the medial aspect of her right knee below unremarkable overlying skin ([Diagn Cytopathol 2016;44:926](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27432164))
* 55 year old woman with a slowly enlarging mass on the ventral antebrachium ([Dermatol Surg 2017;43:1489](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28263196))
* 75 year old woman with no significant medical history presented with a firm erythematous asymptomatic nodule on the left posterior thigh ([Dermatol Surg 2018;44:313](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28498203))

Treatment

* Surgery ([Dermatol Surg 2018;44:313](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28498203), [Dermatol Surg 2017;43:1489](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28263196))

Frozen section description

* Low grade spindle cell neoplasm with pleomorphism and low mitotic activity ([Am J Dermatopathol 2020;42:68](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30702454), [Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2020;13:38](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32055270))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Tumor involving dermis and subcutaneous fat ([Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2018;61:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30004071))
* Generally circumscribed growth ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887307))
* Fascicles or sheets of epithelioid to spindle cells with abundant granular or glassy eosinophilic cytoplasm ([Am J Dermatopathol 2020;42:68](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30702454))
* Pleomorphic, hyperchromatic nuclei
* Intranuclear cytoplasmic pseudoinclusions ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2020;13:38](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32055270))
* Variably prominent nucleoli ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887307), [Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2018;61:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30004071))
* Xanthomatous change in a subpopulation of tumor cells ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887307))
* Extremely low mitotic rate ([Am J Dermatopathol 2020;42:68](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30702454), [Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2020;13:38](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32055270))
* Necrosis is uncommon ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887307))
* Mixed chronic inflammatory infiltrate of lymphocytes, plasma cells, mast cells and often eosinophils ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887307), [Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:504](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30570551), [Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2018;61:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30004071))
* Arborizing capillary sized vasculature is frequently present ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887307))

Sample pathology report

* Thigh, mass, excision:
  + Superficial CD34+ fibroblastic tumor (see comment)
  + Comment: Microscopic sections show a circumscribed tumor in the dermis and subcutaneous fat composed of spindle cells containing moderate to abundant glassy eosinophilic cytoplasm, vesicular nuclei with distinct nucleoli and intranuclear pseudoinclusions, along with interspersed pleomorphic giant cells. Tumor cells are arranged in fascicular or sheet-like patterns. Neither necrosis nor mitotic activity is identified. Also present is a chronic inflammatory infiltrate of lymphocytes, plasma cells, mast cells and eosinophils.
  + Immunohistochemically, neoplastic cells show diffuse and strong expression of CD34 and focal staining of cytokeratin while are negative for myogenin, S100 and STAT6. INI1 is preserved. Fluorescence in situ hybridization studies for *TGFBR3* and *MGEA5* are negative.
  + This constellation of morphological, immunohistochemical and molecular features strongly supports the diagnosis of superficial CD34+ fibroblastic tumor. It is a distinctive low grade neoplasm of the skin and subcutis. The majority of patients have no disease recurrence.

Differential diagnosis

* [Myxoinflammatory fibroblastic sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuemyxoinflammatory.html):
  + Multilobulated architecture with prominent paucicellular myxoid zones ([Am J Dermatopathol 2020;42:68](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30702454))
  + Increased mitotic activity or atypical mitotic figures ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24121178))
  + t(1;10)(p22;q24) *TGFBR3* / *MGEA5* translocation ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2019;143:212](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29979612))
  + [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html) (86%), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) (50%), [keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)(s) (33%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24121178))
* [Myxofibrosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuemyxofibrosarcoma.html):
  + Myxoid multinodular growth pattern
  + Curvilinear thin walled blood vessels
  + Perivascular tumor cell condensation
  + Increased mitotic activity ([Am J Dermatopathol 2020;42:68](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30702454))
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) focally expressed ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:1698](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28776571))
  + Lack of expression for [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:1698](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28776571))
* [Dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticdfsp.html):
  + Cytologically bland, lacking pleomorphism ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887307))
  + Several variants: pigmented (Bednar tumor), myxoid, myoid, granular cell, sclerotic, atrophic, [giant cell fibroblastoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuegiantcellfibro.html) and [DFSP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticdfsp.html) with fibrosarcomatous areas ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2013;30:13](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23327727))
  + Loss of [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) expression in fibrosarcomatous areas ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887307))
  + Lack of expression for [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) ([Oncol Lett 2016;11:661](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26870263))
  + Specific translocation t(17;22)(q22;q13) with *COL1A1-PDGFB* fusion transcripts ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2016;25:64](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27806849))
* [Undifferentiated pleomorphic sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantmfh.html):
  + Great majority occur in subfascial location ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887307))
  + Typically a high grade lesion ([Clin Cancer Res 2015;21:864](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25516889))
  + No identifiable line of differentiation
  + High mitotic rate ([Am J Dermatopathol 2020;42:68](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30702454))
  + Necrosis is common
  + Lacks diffuse [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) expression ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887307))
* [Epithelioid sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueepithelioidsarcoma.html):
  + Granuloma-like pattern, conventional type ([Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9042279), [Cancer 1970;26:1029](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/5476785))
  + Rhabdoid features, marked cytologic atypia, proximal type ([Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9042279))
  + High mitotic rate ([Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9042279))
  + Necrosis is common
  + Loss of [INI1 / SMARCB1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html) expression ([Adv Anat Pathol 2016;23:41](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26645461))
* [Pleomorphic hyalinizing angiectatic tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuephat.html):
  + Hemosiderin rich spindle cell proliferation ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1417](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15489645))
  + Ecstatic blood vessels surrounded by perivascular hyaline material ([Am J Surg Pathol 1996;20:21](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8540605))
  + Lacks [keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) expression ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887307))
  + t(1;10)(p22;q24) *TGFBR3* / *MGEA5* translocation ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2019;143:212](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29979612))
* Malignant [granular cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuegranularcell.html):
  + Mitotic figures and tumor necrosis ([Case Rep Pathol 2019;2019:9576487](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31080684))
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)+ ([Am J Dermatopathol 2020;42:68](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30702454))
  + [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html)+ ([Case Rep Pathol 2019;2019:9576487](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31080684))
  + [TFE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstfe3.html)+ ([Hum Pathol 2014;45:1039](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24746209))
  + Lacks [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) and [keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) expression ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887307), [Case Rep Pathol 2019;2019:9576487](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31080684))
* [Solitary fibrous tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesft.html):
  + Irregular fascicles of spindled cells, staghorn-like blood vessels and variable amounts of collagen ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:778](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29438169))
  + [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html) and [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html)+ ([J Dermatol 2019;46:267](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30614065))
  + [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html)+ ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:778](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29438169))
  + [Keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) expression in dedifferentiated solitary fibrous tumor ([Int J Surg Pathol 2018;26:423](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29482421))
* [Low grade fibromyxoid sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuelgfibromyxoid.html):
  + Bland spindle cells with small, angulated nuclei and scant, wispy cytoplasm ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2017;28:60](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28648941), [Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2018;11:5860](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31949672))
  + Whorled growth pattern with abrupt transition from myxoid to fibrous areas ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2017;28:60](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28648941))
  + Giant rosettes may be observed in tumors with variant morphology
  + [MUC4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmuc2.html#muc4)+ ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2017;28:60](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28648941))
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)- ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2018;11:5860](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31949672))
  + Recurrent (7;16)(q34;p11) translocation, resulting in *FUS-CREB3L2* fusion gene ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2017;28:60](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28648941))

Hematoxylin eosin stains demonstrate a circumscribed tumor in the dermis and subcutaneous fat composed of spindle cells containing moderate to abundant glassy eosinophilic cytoplasm, vesicular nuclei with distinct nucleoli and intranuclear pseudoinclusions, along with interspersed pleomorphic giant cells. Immunohistochemical stains for CD34 and keratin AE1 / AE3 are positive in tumor cells while all of the following are negative: MUC4, SMA, Desmin, S100 and STAT6. INI1 is retained. Which of the following is most likely the correct diagnosis?

3. Myxofibrosarcoma
4. Solitary fibrous tumor
5. Superficial CD34+ fibroblastic tumor
