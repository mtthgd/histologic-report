Definition / general

* Benign neoplasm of fibroblastic origin, with tendency for affecting periungual regions of the digits on acral sites
* Lesions are prone to local recurrence

Essential features

* Acral region especially around the digits are favored sites
* Lobulated dermal and subcutaneous spindle cell lesion with myxoid and collagenous stroma
* Stroma and prominent vessels
* *RB1* gene deletions are observed
* Surgical excision with adequate margins is treatment of choice

Terminology

* Acceptable: digital fibromyxoma, superficial acral fibromyxoma
* Not recommended: cellular digital fibroma

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8811/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/%0D%0A9789241548496_eng.pdf#page=80) - fibromyxoma
* ICD-11: [EE6Y](https://icd.who.int/browse/2024-01/mms/en#831995767%2Fother) & [XH8173](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f1320360493) - other specified fibromatous disorders of skin and soft tissue & acral fibromyxoma

Epidemiology

* Occurs in mid adult life, with a wide age range of 4 - 86 years ([Hum Pathol 2001;32:704](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11486169))
* Especially males, M:F = 1.3:1 ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:789](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22367301))

Sites

* Most commonly periungual or subungual region of fingers and toes ([Hum Pathol 2001;32:704](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11486169))
* Great toe is the most frequent one among digits ([Cutis 2015;95:E15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25750972))
* Regions under the matrix are unusual ([Ann Dermatol Venereol 2014;141:94](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24507203))
* Hands and feet (other than digits such as wrists and heels) can be affected ([J Cutan Pathol 2008;35:1020](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18537858))
* Ankles and leg are rarely involved ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:789](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22367301))

Pathophysiology

* *RB1* gene deletions at chromosome 13q14.2 ([Diagnostics (Basel) 2021;11:430](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33802620))
* Variable co-loss of the corresponding 13q12 signal (monosomy at the 13q region) ([Hum Pathol 2017;60:192](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27825811))

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Slow growing, single and often painful tumors
* Nail erosion was reported ([Arch Orthop Trauma Surg 2008;128:271](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17203283))
* Ulceration may also occur ([An Bras Dermatol 2014;89:147](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24626661))
* Preceding trauma has been noticed in few of the cases ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2011;135:1064](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21810002))
* Pseudoclubbing, onychogryphosis (ram horn nail) or triangular macrolunula were presenting features of rare lesions under the matrix ([Ann Dermatol Venereol 2014;141:94](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24507203))
* Erosive or lytic lesion of underlying bone may be seen on imaging ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:789](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22367301))

Diagnosis

* Acral soft tissue lesion affecting digits with or without cortical erosion
* Typical location around digits should raise clinical suspicion
* Radiological features are not characteristic
* Histological examination of the lesion is diagnostic
* Reference: [J Cutan Pathol 2008;35:1020](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18537858)

Radiology description

* Plain radiograph ([Skeletal Radiol 2008;37:499](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18327578))
  + Well circumscribed soft tissue mass with secondary saucer-like erosion of bone cortex
  + Calcification is absent
  + There may be sclerosis and buttressed margin of cortex in longstanding cases
* Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI): soft tissue lesion, T2 hyperintense with focal thinning of the cortex ([Skeletal Radiol 2008;37:499](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18327578))

Prognostic factors

* Local recurrences developed in 3 of 14 patients (22%) in a study ([J Cutan Pathol 2008;35:1020](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18537858))
* In a study of 18 patients (mean follow up interval of 10.1 years), there was 1 recurrence after local excision and 2 cases persisted / progressed after partial excision ([Hum Pathol 2001;32:704](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11486169))
* Local recurrence was seen in 24% of cases in another study (all near the nail unit of the fingers or toes) after a mean interval of 27 months ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:789](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22367301))
  + 1 tumor recurred twice
  + Margins on initial biopsy or subsequent excision had been involved
  + No other clinical or pathologic features were predictive of recurrence / persistence
* Metastasis has not been reported

Case reports

* 18 year old woman with painless subungual nodule of great toe ([Indian Dermatol Online J 2017;8:45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28217473))
* 28 year old woman with a painless, slow growing mass on right distal aspect of the phalanx ([JAAD Case Rep 2023;33:109](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36911794))
* 54 year old man with 2 lobed mass lesion in the fifth finger ([Int J Surg Case Rep 2020;77:531](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33395839))
* 64 year old man presented to the emergency room for an ingrown toenail associated lesion ([J Surg Case Rep 2023;2023:rjad027](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36751671))
* 70 year old woman with ulcerated firm nodule on sole of foot ([BMJ Case Rep 2022;15:e247565](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35750432))

Treatment

* Surgical excision with adequate margin is recommended
* Locally recurrent lesions can undergo re-excision

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Poorly circumscribed lesion in dermis composed of spindle to stellate shaped fibroblastic cells arranged in a loose storiform / fascicular pattern with alternating myxoid and collagenous stroma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:789](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22367301))
* Microvasculature is prominent ([Hum Pathol 2001;32:704](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11486169))
* Multinucleated stromal cells may be present and mast cells are frequent ([Hum Pathol 2001;32:704](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11486169))
* Bland nuclei lacking significant mitoses or necrosis; occasionally degenerative atypia may be encountered
* Infiltration in adipose tissue can occur
* Cellular variants are also encountered
* Cartilaginous and osseous metaplasia may be observed

Superficial acral fibromyxoma

Sample pathology report

* Left big toe, excision biopsy:
  + Acral fibromyxoma (see comment)
  + Comment: There is a well circumscribed dermal based spindle cell neoplasm comprised of collagenous and myxoid areas. The stellate cells are bland, lacking atypia. Mitoses are rare. There is prominent thin vasculature. Scattered mast cells are noticed. Immunohistochemical stains for CD34 and CD99 are positive in spindle cells; features are consistent with superficial acral fibromyxoma, a benign fibroblastic tumor. The tumor is completely excised. Local recurrence is uncommon following complete excision.

Differential diagnosis

* [Onychomatricoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticonychomatricoma.html):
  + Rarely affects nail matrix
  + Spindle shaped stromal cells with epithelial component
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) is positive
* **Myxoid** [neurofibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueneurofibroma.html):
  + Spindle cells with wavy nuclei in abundant myxoid stroma
  + Prominent vasculature is lacking
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) and [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) positive
* [Superficial angiomyxoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticsuperficialangio.html):
  + Occurs anywhere in the body, mostly affecting the head and neck region
  + Multilobulated tumor in dermis and subcutis with infiltrative growth
  + Myxoid matrix is abundant
  + Arborizing vessels are prominent at edges
  + Intralesional neutrophils
  + [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) is negative
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) is negative
* [Acral fibrokeratoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticacquireddigitalfibrokeratoma.html):
  + Polypoid dome shaped
  + Dense collagenous stroma, mature spindle cells and small vessels
  + Vertical orientation of collagen fibers
* [Dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans (DFSP)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticdfsp.html):
  + Affecting trunk and extremities
  + Myxoid DFSP is rare in digits
  + Storiform pattern and deep infiltration in the subcutaneous fat
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) is positive
* [Fibroma of tendon sheath](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuefibromatendon.html):
  + Well circumscribed, attached to tendon
  + Sparsely cellular
  + Spindle cells in fibrous stroma
  + Vessels are slit-like
  + [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) is positive
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) is negative
* [Giant cell tumor of tendon sheath](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuegct.html):
  + Lobulated, gray-brown
  + Attached to tendon sheath
  + Spindle, epithelioid and giant cells in fibrous stroma
  + Hemosiderin and foamy cells along with inflammatory cells in varying proportion
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) is negative
  + [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html) is positive
* [Glomus tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueglomus.html):
  + Solitary painful mass of digits involving subcutaneous
  + Uniform round to polygonal cells arranged around vessels
  + [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) is positive
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) is negative

Board review style question #1

[![Superficial acral fibromyxoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/softtissuesuperficialacralUdDin01.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/softtissuesuperficialacralUdDin01.jpg)

A 30 year old woman presented with a slow growing painless subungual mass. Plain Xray revealed uninvolved bone. Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) confirmed a soft tissue confined lesion without bone erosion. Excision specimen revealed histological features as shown in the given image. Which positive IHC stain helps confirm the diagnosis?

1. CD34
2. Desmin
3. MUC4
4. S100
5. SMA

Board review style answer #1

**A**. CD34. In superficial acral fibromyxoma (SAF), only CD34 will be diffuse and strongly positive.
Answer E is incorrect because SMA is only focally positive and is not diagnostic, as it can be positive in many other spindle cell lesions. Answer D is incorrect because S100 is negative in SAF and will exclude nerve sheath tumor. Answer B is incorrect because desmin negativity excludes rhabdoid differentiation. Answer C is incorrect because MUC4 will be negative in SAF, which is a positive marker in low grade fibromyxoid sarcoma.

A 30 year old woman presented with a slow growing painless subungual mass. Plain Xray revealed erosion of underlying bone. Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) confirmed a soft tissue lesion and bone erosion. Excision specimen revealed histological features as shown in the given H&E image. The provided IHC stain is CD34. What is the treatment of choice?

1. Local excision
2. Moh's microsurgery
3. Neoadjuvant chemotherapy
4. Radiation
5. Wide excision

Board review style answer #2

**A**. Local excision. The current diagnosis of superficial acral fibromyxoma warrants only local excision with adequate margins. Answer B is incorrect because Moh's microsurgery is a procedure done for skin cancers, including basal cell carcinoma, squamous cell carcinoma and melanoma at critical sites like eyes, ear, nose and lips where excision margins are sent for frozen section to secure clear margins with minimal tissue defect. Answers C, D and E are incorrect because a benign diagnosis does not merit chemotherapy, radiation or wide excision.
