Definition / general

**Bannayan Zonana syndrome:**

* Multiple lipomas and hemangiomas
* [OMIM #153480](http://omim.org/entry/153480)

[**Beckwith-Wiedemann syndrome:**](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/syndromesbeckwith.html)

* Congenital overgrowth syndrome associated with alveolar rhabdomyosarcoma ([Pediatr Dev Pathol 2001;4:550](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11826361))
* [OMIM #130650](http://omim.org/entry/130650)

**Blue rubber bleb nevus syndrome:**

* Autosomal dominant or sporadic syndrome of skin or visceral hemangiomas
* Elevated expression of c-kit has been noted in small venous malformations
* 14 year old girl with multiple recurrent hemangiomas of skin and GI tract ([Korean J Intern Med 2008;23:208](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19119258))
* 19 year old man with severe syndrome ([Rare Tumors 2010;2:e36](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21139838))

**Carney syndrome / complex:**

* Autosomal dominant multiple neoplasia syndrome with myxomas and pigmented lesions of skin and mucosa, due to mutation in PRKAR1A gene ([OMIM #160980](http://omim.org/entry/160980)) or 2p16 abnormalities ([OMIM #605244](http://omim.org/entry/605244))

**Carney triad:**

* Gastric GIST, pulmonary chondroma and extra-adrenal paraganglioma ([Mayo Clinic Proceedings 1999;74:543](http://www.gistsupport.org/media/mcpCarney.pdf), [Mayo Clinic Proceedings;1999;74:638](http://www.gistsupport.org/media/mcpAppelman.pdf))
* [OMIM # 604287](http://omim.org/entry/604287)

**Gardner syndrome:**

* Fibromatosis, familial adenomatous polyposis and osteomas
* [OMIM #175100](http://omim.org/entry/175100)

**Kasabach-Merritt syndrome:**

* Microangiopathic hemolytic anemia associated with childhood hemangioma or kaposiform hemangioendothelioma
* [OMIM #141000](http://omim.org/entry/141000)

**Klippel-Trenaunay-Weber syndrome:**

* Hemangiomas with hypertrophy of associated bone and soft tissue
* [OMIM #149000](http://omim.org/entry/149000)

**Launois-Bensaude syndrome:**

* Painless symmetrical diffuse deposits of fat beneath the skin of the neck, upper trunk, arms and legs
* Also known as multiple symmetrical lipomatosis, cephalothoracic lipodystrophy and Madelung disease

**Li Fraumeni syndrome:**

* Various soft tissue sarcomas, osteosarcoma, breast cancer, brain tumors, leukemia and adrenocortical carcinoma
* Usually due to p53 mutation
* [OMIM #151623](http://omim.org/entry/151623)

**Lipomatosis, multiple syndrome:**

* [OMIM #151800](http://omim.org/entry/151800)

**Maffucci syndrome:**

* Hemangiomas, enchondroma, chondrosarcoma
* [OMIM #166000](http://omim.org/entry/166000)

**Mazabraud's syndrome:**

* Fibrous dysplasia and intramuscular myxoma ([J Cancer Res Clin Oncol 1998;124:401](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9719504))

**McCune-Albright syndrome:**

* Major features are polyostotic fibrous dysplasia, cafe-au-lait skin pigmentation and precocious puberty
* Myxomas are minor feature (see Mazabrauds syndrome)
* [OMIM #174800](http://omim.org/entry/174800)

**Multiple endocrine neoplasia 1:**

* Multiple lipomas may be a minor feature
* [OMIM #131100](http://omim.org/entry/131100)

**Neurofibromatosis type 1:**

* Neurofibroma, MPNST
* [OMIM #162200](http://omim.org/entry/162200)

**Neurofibromatosis type 2:**

* Schwannoma, neurofibroma, meningioma, astrocytoma, rarely perineurioma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:1624](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17122521))
* [OMIM #101000](http://omim.org/entry/101000)

**Osler-Weber-Rendu syndrome:**

* Telangiectasias
* [OMIM #187300](http://omim.org/entry/187300)

**Proteus syndrome:**

* Various cutaneous and subcutaneous lesions, including vascular malformations, lipomas, hyperpigmentation and nevi ([eMedicine](http://emedicine.medscape.com/article/948174-overview))

**Stewart-Treves syndrome:**

* Cutaneous angiosarcoma that develops in long-standing chronic lymphedema ([eMedicine](http://emedicine.medscape.com/article/1102114-overview))

**Sturge-Weber syndrome:**

* Port-wine spot of face
* [OMIM #185300](http://omim.org/entry/185300)

**Turner syndrome:**

* 45 X0, associated with cystic hygroma ([eMedicine](http://emedicine.medscape.com/article/949681-overview)), rarely familial desmoids or GIST

**Sarcomas associated with hereditary nonpolyposis colorectal cancer:**

* Uterine sarcomas, liposarcoma, leiomyosarcoma, and carcinosarcoma ([Fam Cancer 2009;8:209](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19130300))
