Definition / general

* WHO classification of soft tissue tumors serves as a guide to clarify diagnoses among a multidisciplinary team composed of pathologists, radiologists and clinicians ([Adv Anat Pathol 2021;28:44](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32960834))

Major updates

* Adipocytic tumors
  + 2 new entities are considered in the new classification: atypical spindle cell / pleomorphic lipomatous tumor and myxoid pleomorphic liposarcoma
  + Benign
    - Atypical spindle cell / pleomorphic lipomatous tumor (ASPLT) ([Adv Anat Pathol 2021;28:44](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32960834), [Pathologica 2021;113:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33179614))
  + Malignant
    - Myxoid pleomorphic liposarcoma ([Adv Anat Pathol 2021;28:44](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32960834), [Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:645](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19194281))
  + Extrarenal angiomyolipoma was deleted in this category and reclassified in the category of tumors of uncertain differentiation and is now referred to as angiomyolipoma ([Adv Anat Pathol 2021;28:44](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32960834))
  + Extra-adrenal myelolipoma was removed in this edition
* Fibroblastic and myofibroblastic tumors
  + 3 new benign entities are considered in the new classification
  + Benign
    - Angiofibroma of soft tissue ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:500](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22301504))
    - *EWSR1-SMAD3* fibroblastic tumor ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:522](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29309308))
    - Superficial CD34 positive fibroblastic tumor ([Mod Pathol 2014;27:294](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23887307))
* So called fibrohistiocytic tumors
  + Most relevant entity that has disappeared since 2013 is represented by the family of so called malignant fibrous histiocytoma (MFH) ([Pathologica 2021;113:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33179614))
    - Undifferentiated pleomorphic sarcoma currently represents the correct designation for the storiform and pleomorphic variant of MFH
    - Giant cell MFH is currently replaced by 3 distinct tumor types: giant cell tumor of soft tissues, extraskeletal osteosarcoma and giant cell rich osteosarcoma ([Pathologica 2021;113:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33179614))
    - Myxoid MFH is currently recognized as a purely fibroblastic tumor, identified with the original name myxofibrosarcoma ([Pathologica 2021;113:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33179614))
    - Inflammatory MFH overlaps entirely with the inflammatory variant of dedifferentiated liposarcomas ([Pathologica 2021;113:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33179614))
    - Angiomatoid MFH (an indolent lesion most often harboring a *EWSR1-CREB1* fusion gene and more rarely a *EWSR1-ATF1* or *FUS-ATF1* fusion gene) is currently listed within the group of soft tissue lesion of unknown
      differentiation ([Pathologica 2021;113:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33179614), [Clin Cancer Res 2007;13:7322](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18094413))
* Vascular tumors
  + Benign
    - Single new entity appearing among vascular lesions is named anastomosing hemangioma ([Pathologica 2021;113:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33179614), [Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:1364](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19606014))
* Smooth muscle tumors
  + 2 new entities are considered in the new classification: EBV associated smooth muscle tumors and inflammatory leiomyosarcoma ([Pathologica 2021;113:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33179614))
  + Intermediate
    - EBV associated smooth muscle tumor / smooth muscle tumor of uncertain malignant potential ([Front Immunol 2018;9:368](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29535735))
  + Malignant
    - Inflammatory leiomyosarcoma ([Mod Pathol 2018;31:93](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28884746))
* Peripheral nerve sheath tumors
  + Single major change introduced by the 2020 WHO classification is the recognition that so called melanotic schwannoma actually represents a clinically aggressive neoplasm (no longer belonging to the intermediate category) and is consequently relabeled as malignant melanotic nerve sheath tumor ([Pathologica 2021;113:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33179614))
  + Malignant
    - Malignant melanotic nerve sheath tumor ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:94](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24145644))
* Tumors of uncertain differentiation
  + *NTRK* rearranged spindle cell neoplasms (excluding infantile fibrosarcoma that represent a distinct clinicopathologic entity defined molecularly by the presence of *NTRK3-ETV6* fusion gene) represent an emerging group of molecularly defined rare soft tissue tumors ([Pathologica 2021;113:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33179614))
  + Prognosis of *NTRK* rearranged adult tumors appear to be related to histological grade
  + *NTRK* rearranged spindle cell neoplasm
    - This provisional category includes the recently described lipofibromatosis-like neural tumor and *NTRK* rearranged neoplasm resembling peripheral nerve sheath tumors ([Am J Surg Pathol 2016;40:1407](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27259011), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2018;57:611](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30276917), [Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:435](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30585824), [J Pathol 2016;238:700](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26863915))
* Undifferentiated small round cell sarcoma of bone and soft tissue
  + This new section contains not only the prototypical round cell sarcoma named Ewing sarcoma but also 3 distinct subsets that differs from Ewing sarcoma clinically, pathologically and molecularly ([Pathologica 2021;113:70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33179614)):
    - Round cell sarcomas with *EWSR1* gene fusion with non-ETS family members ([Virchows Arch 2020;476:109](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31802230), [Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:1112](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30994538), [Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:220](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30379650), [Mod Pathol 2019;32:1593](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31189996))
    - *CIC* rearranged sarcomas ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2012;51:207](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22072439), [Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:941](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28346326))
    - *BCOR* rearranged sarcomas ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:604](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29300189))

Board review style question #1

The single new entity appearing among vascular lesions in the WHO 2020 classification is named anastomosing hemangioma. What is the most common site of anastomosing hemangioma?

1. Head and neck
2. Intestinal tract
3. Kidney and retroperitoneal adipose tissue
4. Limbs
5. Lungs and uterus

Board review style answer #1

**C.** Kidney and retroperitoneal adipose tissue

Board review style question #2

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/softtissuewhoclassificationAlexievBRQ2.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/softtissuewhoclassificationAlexievBRQ2.jpg)

The histologic pattern shown above would be most commonly expected in which of the following soft tissue tumors listed in WHO 2020?

1. Cellular angiofibroma
2. Low grade fibromyxoid sarcoma
3. Myofibroblastoma
4. Perineurioma
5. Solitary fibrous tumor

Board review style answer #2

**E.** Solitary fibrous tumor. Solitary fibrous tumor is characterized by haphazardly arranged spindled to ovoid cells with indistinct, pale eosinophilic cytoplasm within a variably collagenous stroma and branching staghorn vascular pattern (hemangiopericytomatous vessels).
