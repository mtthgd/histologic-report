Definition / general

* Very rare juvenile polyposis disorder with diffuse GI polyposis, alopecia, hyperpigmentation, dystrophic changes in fingernails and toenails
* Nonhereditary, unknown etiology
* Onset usually in middle age adults
* Patients may have diarrhea, weight loss, abdominal pain, anorexia, weakness, hematochezia
* Occasionally gastric adenocarcinoma, adenocarcinoma anywhere in GI tract ([Am J Surg Pathol 1985;9:65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3970299))

Case reports

* With alopecia and CMV infection ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1977;101:432](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/195553))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Polyp with broad sessile base, expanded edematous lamina propria, cystic glands ([Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:940](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2552848))

Differential diagnosis

* [Juvenile polyps](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stomachjuvenile.html)
* [Ménétrier's disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stomachmenetriers.html): need endoscopic findings and clinical history
