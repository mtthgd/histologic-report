Definition / general

* High grade B-cell tumor ([Am J Surg Pathol 2003;27:790](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12766583))
* May arise from transformed extranodal marginal zone (MALT) lymphoma
* Usually > age 50 years
* 5 year survival is 65% (related to stage)
* Coexistence of MALT lymphoma and diffuse large B-cell lymphoma: do not report as "high grade MALT" (MALT is usually indolent); report instead as diffuse large B-cell lymphoma with coexisting MALT lymphoma

Prognostic factors

* Cases associated with extranodal marginal zone (MALT) lymphoma have 84% 5 year cause specific survival ([Am J Surg Pathol 2001;25:95](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11145257)), better than without coexisting MALT
* Germinal center origin (CD10+, bcl6+, [Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:1641](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11117785))

Case reports

* 61 year old man with coexisting early gastric carcinoma ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1989;113:419](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2705873))

Gross description

* Large polypoid or lobulated mass with superficial or deep ulceration; often in distal stomach, but sparing pylorus

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Infiltrative pattern of high grade, centroblast like cells; multinucleated forms may resemble Reed-Sternberg cells

Positive stains

* [bcl6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl6.html) (variable), [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) (variable), [CD35](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30to39.html#cd35) (variable), [keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) (rare, [Am J Surg Pathol 1996;20:346](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8772789))

Differential diagnosis

* **Undifferentiated carcinoma**: continuity between tumor cells and epithelium, acinar pattern, muscularis mucosa destruction, positive for keratin and mucin
