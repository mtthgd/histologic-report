Definition / general

* Benign neoplastic polyp that occurs anywhere in gastrointestinal tract but most commonly in stomach and small intestine

Essential features

* Benign mesenchymal gastrointestinal polyp that arises in the submucosa
* Associated with *PDGFRA* mutation

Terminology

* Also known as Vaněk polyps ([Am J Pathol 1949;25:397](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18127133))
* Formerly called eosinophilic granuloma

Sites

* Usually arises in pylorus or distal antrum in stomach

Etiology

* May be familial (Devon polyposis syndrome, [Gut 1992;33:1004](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1644320))

Clinical features

* Peak incidence in sixth or seventh decade of life
* No endoscopic surveillance is required after the histological diagnosis is confirmed
* Rarely associated with adenocarcinoma or adenoma ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1988;112:829](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3395220))

Case reports

* 79 year old woman with chronic gastritis and a 2 cm sessile gastric polyp ([Case #421](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case421.htm))

Treatment

* Local excision

Gross description

* Typically solitary, small and sessile
* Median size 1.5 cm but can measure up to 9 cm

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Submucosal lesion composed of spindle and stellate stromal cells
* Loose edematous stroma containing thin walled blood vessels with characteristic onion skin arrangement of spindled cells around vessels
* Inflammatory infiltrate rich in eosinophils
* Can infiltrate lamina propria
* Minimal mitotic activity

Positive stains

* [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), variable [smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html)

Negative stains

* [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html) (although mast cells are CD117+), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [DOG1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsDOG1.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Associated with activating mutation in the platelet derived growth factor receptor alpha (*PDGFRA*) gene, supporting a neoplastic origin ([Histopathology 2012;61:59](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22394371))

Sample pathology report

* Stomach, antrum, polypectomy:
  + Inflammatory fibroid polyp (3.1 cm), focally extending to deep margin (see comment)
  + Lateral margins unremarkable.
  + Comment: An immunohistochemical stain for CD34 is positive.

Differential diagnosis

* [Gastrointestinal stromal tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stomachGIST.html):
  + Centered in muscularis propria
  + More hypercellular
  + [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)+ and [DOG1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsDOG1.html)+
* [Plexiform fibromyxoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stomachplexiformfibromyxoma.html):
  + Multinodular, centered on muscularis propria
  + Lacks concentric vessels
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)-
* [Schwannoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stomachschwannoma.html):
  + Usually arise from muscularis propria
  + Peripheral lymphoid cuffing
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)+

1. Associated with activating mutation in the platelet derived growth factor receptor alpha gene

Board review style answer #1

**A.** They are associated with activating mutation in the platelet derived growth factor receptor alpha gene
