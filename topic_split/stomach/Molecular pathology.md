Definition / general

* 2 main gastric cancer molecular classifications have been proposed by comprehensive molecular profiling, with partial overlap ([Nature 2014;513:202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25079317), [Nat Med 2015;21:449](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25894828))
* Routine immunohistochemical analysis and in situ hybridization may be useful for tumor molecular classification ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:106](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27819872), [Virchows Arch 2018;472:369](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29046940), [PLoS One 2019;14:e0224812](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31790410))

Essential features

* PDL1 overexpression in microsatellite instability (MSI) and Epstein-Barr virus (EBV) associated tumors ([Nat Med 2018;24:1449](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30013197))
* MSI tumors are susceptible to immune checkpoint blockade ([World J Gastroenterol 2019;25:5773](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31636471))
* EBV tumors may be identified by Epstein-Barr encoding region (EBER) in situ hybridization ([Gut 2014;63:236](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23580779))
* MSI tumors may be identified by mismatch repair (MMR) immunohistochemical protein analysis or microsatellite molecular testing ([Ann Oncol 2019;30:1232](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31056702))
* MSI tumors are frequently characterized by the hypermethylation of the *MLH1* gene promoter ([Cell Mol Life Sci 2018;75:4151](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30173350))

The Cancer Genome Atlas (TCGA) network classification

* EBV positive tumors (~9%)
  + Gastric fundus or body
  + Male prevalence
  + Extensive DNA promoter hypermethylation
  + *CDKN2A* (p16INK4A) promoter hypermethylation
  + *PIK3CA*, *ARID1A* and *BCOR* mutations
  + Overexpression of PDL1 and PDL2 (assessable with IHC)
* Tumors with MSI (~22%)
  + Preferred localization in the gastric antrum
  + Associated with intestinal histotype
  + Elderly age of onset
  + More favorable prognosis than the other molecular subtypes
  + *MLH1* gene promoter hypermethylation
  + High mutational burden
  + In contrast to MSI colorectal adenocarcinoma, there is no association with *BRAF* mutations
  + Can be part of the spectrum of inherited malignancies such as Lynch syndrome
* Genomically stable tumors (~20%)
  + Distal localization
  + Poorly cohesive histotype
  + Younger patients in comparison to other molecular subtypes
  + Worst prognosis among the 4 TCGA subtypes
  + Low copy number alterations
  + Low mutational burden
  + *ARID1, *RHOA** and *CDH1* mutations
  + *CLDN18-ARHGAP26* fusions in 15%
* Tumors with chromosomal instability (CIN) (~50%)
  + Frequently located at the gastroesophageal junction / cardia
  + Most are classified as intestinal type
  + DNA aneuploidy and highly variable chromosomal copy numbers
  + Frequent mutations of the tumor suppressor *TP53*
  + Frequent genomic amplifications of receptor tyrosine kinases (RTKs) / RAS pathway, including epidermal growth factor receptor (*EGFR*), *ERBB2* (*HER2*), *ERBB3*, MET proto-oncogene (*MET*), fibroblast growth factor receptor 2 (*FGFR2*), vascular endothelial growth factor A (*VEGFA*) and *KRAS*
* Reference: [Nature 2014;513:202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25079317)

The Asian Cancer Research Group (ACRG) classification

* Microsatellite unstable (MSI) tumors (~23%)
  + Preferred location in the gastric antrum
  + Intestinal histology
  + Often diagnosed at an early stage
  + Best overall prognosis
  + Silencing of *MLH1* gene
  + DNA methylation signature
  + Presence of hypermutation
  + Mutations of *ARID1A*, *KRAS* and *ALK*
  + Alterations affecting the PI3K-PTEN-mTOR pathway
  + Overexpression of PDL1 (assessable with IHC)
* Epithelial to mesenchymal-like type tumors (microsatellite stable [MSS] / EMT) (~15%)
  + Poorly cohesive histotype
  + Younger age at presentation compared to all other subtypes
  + Higher TNM stages at presentation (III / IV)
  + Higher frequency of peritoneal metastases
  + Worst prognosis
  + *CDH1* loss of expression
  + Lower number of mutation events when compared to the other MSS groups
* MSS / TP53 positive tumors (~26%)
  + Intestinal histology
  + Male predominance
  + Frequent EBV infection
  + Frequent mutations in *ARID1A*, *PIK3CA*, *SMAD4* and *APC*
* MSS / TP53 negative tumors (~36%)
  + Intestinal histology
  + Male predominance
  + Highest prevalence of *TP53* and *RHOA* mutations ("TP53 negative" refers to loss of function, not presence of mutation)
  + Mutations in *APC*, *ARID1A*, *KRAS*, *PIK3CA* and *SMAD4*
* Reference: [Nat Med 2015;21:449](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25894828)

Sites

* EBV tumors are more common in the fundus or body ([Gastroenterology 2009;137:824](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19445939))
* MSI tumors are more common in the antrum ([Nature 2014;513:202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25079317))
* Tumors with chromosomal instability are frequently located at the gastroesophageal junction / cardia ([Nature 2014;513:202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25079317))

Clinical features

* EBV tumors are more common in men ([Gastroenterology 2009;137:824](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19445939))
* MSI tumors have an elderly age of onset ([Cell Mol Life Sci 2018;75:4151](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30173350))
* MSI tumors can be part of the spectrum of inherited malignancies such as Lynch syndrome ([Cell Mol Life Sci 2018;75:4151](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30173350))
* MSI tumors are often diagnosed at an early stage ([Cell Mol Life Sci 2018;75:4151](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30173350))
* Genomically stable tumors are characterized by an earlier age of onset in comparison to other molecular subtypes ([Nature 2014;513:202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25079317))

Diagnosis

* EBV tumors may be identified by EBER in situ hybridization ([Gut 2014;63:236](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23580779))
* MSI tumors may be identified by MMR immunohistochemical protein analysis or microsatellite molecular testing ([Ann Oncol 2019;30:1232](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31056702))
* MSI tumors are frequently characterized by the hypermethylation of the *MLH1* gene promoter ([Cell Mol Life Sci 2018;75:4151](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30173350))
* Complete loss of or clonal p53 overexpression (i.e. strong and with a diffuse pattern) is frequently observed in tumors with chromosomal instability ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:106](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27819872), [Virchows Arch 2018;472:369](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29046940), [PLoS One 2019;14:e0224812](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31790410))

Prognostic factors

* MSI tumors have a more favorable prognosis ([Cell Mol Life Sci 2018;75:4151](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30173350))
* Genomically stable tumors have the worst prognosis among the 4 TCGA subtypes ([Nature 2014;513:202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25079317))

Treatment

* MSI tumors are susceptible to immune checkpoint blockade ([World J Gastroenterol 2019;25:5773](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31636471))
* EBV positive tumors seem to benefit significantly from immune checkpoint blockade ([World J Gastroenterol 2019;25:5773](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31636471), [Nat Med 2018;24:1449](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30013197))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Genomically stable tumors have a poorly cohesive histotype ([Nature 2014;513:202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25079317))
* MSI tumors and tumors with chromosomal instability are usually characterized by an intestinal histotype ([Nature 2014;513:202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25079317))
* Gastric adenocarcinoma with lymphoid stroma is usually associated with EBV infection or MSI status ([Am J Surg Pathol
  2018;42:453](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29438172), [Int J Mol Sci 2018;19:2079](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30018250))

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/stomachmolecularfassan03.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/stomachmolecularfassan03.jpg)

What is an important molecular characteristic associated with microsatellite unstable gastric carcinomas?

1. Frequent amplification of the *ERBB2* (*HER2*) gene
2. High frequency of *BRAF* mutations
3. High mutational burden
4. Poor prognosis
5. Poorly cohesive histotype

Board review style answer #1

**C.** High mutational burden. Alterations in the DNA mismatch repair machinery results in the accumulation of frame shift mutations (either through insertions or deletions) with a subsequent increased mutational burden.
