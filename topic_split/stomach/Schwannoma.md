Definition / general

* Well circumscribed, benign nerve sheath tumor (also called neurilemmoma), much less common (~ 1 / 50) in stomach than GIST

Case reports

* 37 year old man with incidentally detected gastric mass ([Case #455](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case455.htm))
* 46 year old woman with stomach mass ([Case #347](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case347.htm))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Well circumscribed but not encapsulated, with interlacing bundles of spindle cells and collagen
* Often nuclear pallisading, Verocay bodies, hyalinized vessels
* May have nuclear atypia, inflammatory cells, peripheral cuff of lymphoid aggregates
* No mitotic figures, no skenoid fibers, no epithelioid features

Positive stains

* [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) (strong), [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html)
* Often [PAS+](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html) needle shaped crystalloids ([Am J Surg Pathol 1999;23:431](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10199472)); variable [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html)

Negative stains

* [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) (may be focally positive), [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html) ([Mod Pathol 2003;16:366](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12692202)), [DOG1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsDOG1.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsactin.html)

Board review style question #1

Which of the following describes the majority of schwannomas arising in the gastrointestinal tract?

1. Both encapsulated and circumscribed
2. Circumscribed but not encapsulated
3. Encapsulated but not circumscribed
4. Neither encapsulated nor circumscribed

Board review style answer #1

**B.** Circumscribed but not encapsulated
