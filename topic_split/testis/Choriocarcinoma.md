Definition / general

* Choriocarcinoma is a malignant nonseminomatous germ cell tumor (GCT) composed of trophoblastic cells with associated hemorrhage

Essential features

* Tumor that arises from trophoblastic tissue and consists of syncytiotrophoblasts, cytotrophoblasts and intermediate trophoblasts with associated hemorrhage
* Increased serum beta hCG
* Early hematogenous metastasis
* Mixed germ cell tumor with choriocarcinoma component has better outcome than pure choriocarcinoma

Terminology

* Nonseminomatous germ cell tumor (NSGCT)
* Trophoblastic malignant teratoma, chorionepithelioma (not recommended)

Epidemiology

* Testicular GCT are the most common solid tumors in young men ([Expert Rev Anticancer Ther 2020;20:75](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31951790))
* 95% of testicular tumors are GCT
* Testicular GCT with choriocarcinoma affects men aged 20 - 39, with a median age of 29 ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24145647))
* 7 - 8% of testicular GCT have choriocarcinoma as a component, usually admixed with other GCT types ([J Urol 2014;192:934](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24949806))
* 0.9% of testicular mixed GCT have choriocarcinoma as a main component ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24145647))
* 0.2 - 0.6% of testicular GCT are pure choriocarcinoma ([J Urol 2014;192:934](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24949806), [Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24145647))

Sites

* Testicular parenchyma
* Metastases to lung, brain, liver, retroperitoneum, gastrointestinal tract, lymph node, bone ([Int J Health Sci (Qassim) 2017;11:81](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28539868), [World Neurosurg 2017;106:536](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28712904), [Cureus 2018;10:e3083](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30324039), [J Pak Med Assoc 2013;63:1544](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24397105), [Curr Oncol Rep 2015;17:2](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25645112))

Pathophysiology

* Unknown

Etiology

* No specific known cause
* Precursor lesion: germ cell neoplasia in situ (GCNIS)
* Testicular GCT risk factors include cryptorchidism and a personal or family history of testicular cancer ([Am Fam Physician 2018;97:261](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29671528))
* Testicular GCT are likely the result of genetic and environmental factors during fetal development and subsequent changes in premalignant germ cells following puberty ([Expert Rev Anticancer Ther 2020;20:75](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31951790))

Clinical features

* Presentation with elevated serum beta hCG, scrotal mass with or without pain, possible hyperthyroidism and gynecomastia ([Curr Oncol Rep 2015;17:2](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25645112), [Am Fam Physician 2018;97:261](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29671528))
* Can present with hemoptysis, seizures, confusion ([Radiol Case Rep 2021;16:923](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33613805), [Curr Oncol Rep 2015;17:2](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25645112))
* Patients with pure or predominant choriocarcinoma have distant metastasis at presentation (100% in 1 study), usually to the lungs (67%), liver (60%) and less frequently brain (20%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24145647))
* Cases with minimal choriocarcinoma component (≤ 5%) have regional lymph node metastases in 10% and distant metastases in 7% at presentation ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:1113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29794869))
* Choriocarcinoma syndrome is a rare, life threatening complication in patients with substantially increased serum beta hCG (usually > 50,000 IU/L) due to high choriocarcinoma tumor burden at metastatic sites, often following chemotherapy induction, yielding fatal bleeding and pulmonary hemorrhage leading to acute respiratory failure ([Cureus 2021;13:e14286](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33968500))

Diagnosis

* Initial scrotal ultrasonography followed by orchiectomy histopathology ([Am Fam Physician 2018;97:261](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29671528))
* CT, MRI, Xray for staging

Laboratory

* Higher levels of serum beta hCG indicate heavier choriocarcinoma burden at presentation ([Case Rep Oncol Med 2019;2019:4092941](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30918732)):
  + Pure choriocarcinoma: 65,000 - 899,000 IU/L ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24145647))
  + Predominant choriocarcinoma: 55,000 - 666,000 IU/L ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24145647))
  + Pure or predominant choriocarcinoma: median 199,000 IU/L ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24145647))
  + Minimal choriocarcinoma component (≤ 5%): 0.1 - 60,715 IU/L (median 485 IU/L) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:1113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29794869))
* Serum beta hCG in circulation reflects residual tumor ([Curr Oncol Rep 2015;17:2](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25645112))

Radiology description

* Ultrasonography of testicle can reach 100% sensitivity in combination with physical exam for testicular tumors ([AJR Am J Roentgenol 2013;200:1215](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23701056))
* Choriocarcinoma appears as hypoechoic lesion on ultrasound ([Radiol Case Rep 2021;16:923](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33613805))
* CT, MRI, Xray to identify metastasis and for staging
* Metastatic choriocarcinoma forms hemorrhagic masses and in lungs has cannonball appearance on Xray and CT ([Radiol Case Rep 2021;16:923](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33613805))

Prognostic factors

* Patients with beta hCG > 50,000 IU/L have a 5 year survival of 48% ([Int J Health Sci (Qassim) 2017;11:81](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28539868))
* Pure or predominant choriocarcinoma has a median survival of 13 months ([Lancet 2006;367:754](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16517276))
* 79% of patients with pure or predominant choriocarcinoma (> 50%) treated with orchiectomy and chemotherapy died within 3 years ([Am J Surg Pathol 1993;17:1075](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8214253))
* Minimal choriocarcinoma component (≤ 5%) does not seem to negatively impact prognosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24145647))

Case reports

* 17 year old boy with thyroid storm ([Eur J Case Rep Intern Med 2020;7:001754](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33083353))
* 18 year old man with metastases to skin, lungs, retroperitoneum ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2018;97:e10736](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29851778))
* 28 year old man with choriocarcinoma syndrome ([Cureus 2021;13:e18681](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34786260))
* 30 year old man with upper gastrointestinal bleed ([Cureus 2019;11:e5243](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31565641))
* 37 year old man with metastases to skin, brain, gastrointestinal tract ([Cureus 2018;10:e3083](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30324039))

Treatment

* Unlike other testicular GCT, chemotherapy may be indicated prior to a tissue diagnosis in patients with radiologic findings and a serum hCG > 50,000 mIU/mL ([Curr Oncol Rep 2015;17:2](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25645112))
* Orchiectomy is primary treatment for nonmetastatic tumors
* Retroperitoneal lymph node dissection or chemotherapy for pT2 or higher
* Chemotherapy for pN2 - 3
* Treatment regimens:
  + Bleomycin, etoposide, cisplatin (BEP) ([Curr Oncol Rep 2015;17:2](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25645112), [World Neurosurg 2017;106:536](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28712904))
  + Bleomycin might be held until after induction therapy to avoid pulmonary fibrosis ([Front Endocrinol (Lausanne) 2019;10:218](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31031704))
  + 4 cycles with hCG monitoring is often needed ([Curr Oncol Rep 2015;17:2](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25645112))
  + Delay can be fatal ([Curr Oncol Rep 2015;17:2](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25645112))
* Goal of treatment is tumor marker normalization

Gross description

* Central hemorrhage and necrosis, surrounded by viable tumor
* Tumor size has a wide range:
  + Pure or predominant choriocarcinoma: 1.5 - 10 cm, median 7 cm ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24145647))
  + Minimal choriocarcinoma component (≤ 5%): 1.1 - 8.0 cm, median 4.5 cm ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:1113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29794869))

Frozen section description

* Intraoperative definitive diagnosis of the type of testicular GCT is not needed because it does not impact intraoperative management
* Determination of benign versus malignant testicular tumor for small masses via frozen section is highly accurate ([Eur Urol 2003;44:458](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14499681))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Solid nests and sheets of syncytial cells (syncytiotrophoblasts) and mononucleated trophoblasts (cytotrophoblasts and intermediate trophoblasts)
* Syncytiotrophoblasts:
  + Large cells
  + Abundant, dense, eosinophilic cytoplasm
  + Indistinct cell border
  + Multinucleated
  + Pleomorphic, hyperchromatic nuclei which can have a degenerated, smudged appearance
  + Grow over mononucleated cells and can form a thin compressed rim or a thicker cap
* Mononucleated trophoblasts (cytotrophoblasts and intermediate trophoblasts):
  + Medium sized cells
  + Pale, eosinophilic cytoplasm
  + Distinct cell border
  + Mitotically active
* Hemorrhage, cyst formation and necrosis are common ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24145647))
* Lymphovascular invasion is often present in tumors with pure or predominant choriocarcinoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24145647))
* Germ cell neoplasia in situ may be present
* Remaining nonneoplastic testicular parenchyma may show findings seen in other germ cell tumors, including atrophy, maturation arrest, Leydig cell hyperplasia, microlithiasis, angiopathy, Sertoli cell only tubules and Sertoli cell nodules ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:1260](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17001157))

Cytology description

* Dual cell population composed of mononucleated trophoblasts and syncytiotrophoblasts ([Ann Saudi Med 1997;17:432](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17353596))
* Mononucleated trophoblasts:
  + Intermediate size, round
  + Single round nuclei, coarse chromatin and visible single nucleoli
  + Clear to slightly basophilic cytoplasm with few small vacuoles ([Ann Saudi Med 1997;17:432](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17353596))
* Syncytiotrophoblasts:
  + Large cells
  + Multiple, pleomorphic nuclei
  + Abundant cytoplasm with variable numbers of small vacuoles ([Ann Saudi Med 1997;17:432](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17353596))
* Background of necrosis and hemorrhage which may limit cellularity of specimen ([Ann Saudi Med 1997;17:432](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17353596))

Positive stains

* [AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html)
* [hCG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshcg.html): 96% (caveat that tumors containing choriocarcinoma can have expression that diffuses into all GCT types, requiring careful interpretation and use of more specific markers) ([Hum Pathol 2019;84:254](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30359635))

* [Glypican 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglypican3.html): 30 - 100% (strong in syncytiotrophoblasts, mononucleated trophoblasts) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2020;44:e66](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32205480), [Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:1570](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17122513), [Mod Pathol 2020;33:2354](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32561848), [Urol Ann 2022;14:21](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35197698))

  + [Inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html): 89% ([Hum Pathol 2019;84:254](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30359635))
  + [HPL](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshpl.html): 100% ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24145647))

  + [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html): 100%, but variable percentage of cells ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:1065](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19390421))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* 12p abnormalities are found in testicular GCT ([Am J Surg Pathol 2020;44:e66](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32205480), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 1990;1:289](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2177638))

Sample pathology report

* Right testis, radical orchiectomy:
  + Mixed germ cell tumor, teratoma (35%), seminoma (20%), embryonal carcinoma (20%), yolk sac tumor (20%) and choriocarcinoma (5%) types (see synoptic report)

Differential diagnosis

* [Embryonal carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisembryonal.html):
  + Sheet-like, glandular and papillary growth
  + Can have scattered, rare syncytiotrophoblasts
  + Darker cytoplasm, lacks prominent cell membranes throughout
  + Positive [OCT 3/4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct34.html), [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html); negative [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html), [beta hCG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshcg.html)
* [Seminoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisseminomas.html):
  + Patients tend to be 10 years older at diagnosis ([Andrology 2015;3:4](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25331326))
  + Expansile nodules separated by fibrous bands containing lymphocytes
  + Monotonous, smaller cells with distinct cell borders throughout
  + Can have scattered, occasional clusters of syncytiotrophoblasts
  + Usually lacks hemorrhage and necrosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24145647))
  + Positive [OCT 3/4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct34.html), [CD117 / KIT](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html), [D2-40 / podoplanin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html); negative [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html), [beta hCG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshcg.html)
* [Squamous cell carcinoma somatic malignancy arising from teratoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisteratocarcinoma.html):
  + Lacks 2 different cell types
  + Distinct cell membranes throughout
  + Can have keratin formation
  + Diffuse positive [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html), [CK5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5and6.html); negative [inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html), [beta hCG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshcg.html)
* **Nonchoriocarcinomatous trophoblastic tumors** ([cystic trophoblastic tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisctt.html), [epithelioid trophoblastic tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisepithelioidtrophoblastictumor.html), [placental site trophoblastic tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisptt.html)):
  + Very rare
  + Usually seen posttreatment, particularly in metastatic sites
  + Only mild serum beta hCG elevation (unless choriocarcinoma elsewhere)
  + Lacks 2 different cell types
* **Carcinoma of nongerm cell origin**, especially [squamous cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/penscrotumcarcinomagen.html):
  + No history of germ cell tumor, no elevation of serum beta hCG
  + Lacks 2 different cell types
  + Lack 12p abnormalities
  + Negative [beta hCG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshcg.html), [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html)
