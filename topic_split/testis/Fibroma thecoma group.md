Definition / general

* Sex cord stromal tumors composed of spindle, oval or round cells with varying amounts of collagen present

Essential features

* Hemorrhage and necrosis absent
* Variable cell density, variable collagen
* Usually no Sertoli or granulosa elements present but may have minor aggregates of other sex cord cells ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1999;123:391](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10235496))
* Usually scanty mitoses (≤ 5 mitoses/10 high power fields), can be as high as 10 mitoses/10 high power fields

Terminology

* Synonyms:
  + Fibrothecoma
  + Thecofibroma
  + Thecoma / fibroma group
  + Testicular fibroma

ICD coding

* ICD-O:
  + [8600/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=77) - thecoma, NOS
  + [8810/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=80) - fibroma, NOS
* ICD-10: [D29.20](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D29-/D29.20) - benign neoplasm of unspecified testis
* ICD-11: [2F34 & XH34A0](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f317567417?data=%7B%22dataType%22%3A%22pc%22%2C%22postcoordinationCodeSet%22%3A%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F317567417%22%2C%22axisToValueIds%22%3A%7B%22histopathology%22%3A%5B%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F1500755340%22%7D%5D%7D%7D%7D) - benign neoplasm of skin of male genital organs & thecoma, NOS

Epidemiology

* Rare tumor
* Age range: 16 - 69 years; mean age: 44 years ([Am J Surg Pathol 2013;37:1208](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23715159))
* Often occurs in third or fourth decade of life

Sites

* Testis
* Paratestis

Pathophysiology

* Unknown
* May be associated with Gorlin syndrome (nevoid basal cell carcinoma syndrome), abnormalities of *PTCH* gene ([J Pediatr Surg 2010;45:E1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20223301))

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Commonly presents as slowly increasing unilateral testicular mass ([Urol Ann 2021;13:308](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34421271))
* Hormonal changes are extremely uncommon
* Clinically benign behavior with no reported recurrences or metastases ([Urol Ann 2021;13:308](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34421271))

Diagnosis

* Ultrasound
* Orchiectomy

Laboratory

* Hormone levels are typically within normal range
* Serum testosterone and estrogen may be elevated in some secreting tumors
* Tumor markers (beta human chorionic gonadotropin [hCG], alpha fetoprotein [AFP], lactate dehydrogenase [LDH]) are within normal range ([Urol Case Rep 2020;33:101368](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33102067), [Urol Ann 2021;13:308](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34421271))

Radiology description

* Well defined, hypoechoic, solid mass
* Can appear as a heterogeneous mass

Prognostic factors

* Benign behavior
* Even 10 mitoses per 10 high power fields show no adverse prognosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2013;37:1208](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23715159))

Case reports

* 31 year old man with a firm nodule in the right testis and a 14 year old boy with a nontender mass in the left testis ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1999;123:391](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10235496))
* 33 year old man with an incidental nodular mass in the left testis ([Hum Pathol 2009;40:584](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18835626))
* 37 year old man with a firm right testicular mass ([Urol Case Rep 2020;33:101368](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33102067))
* 51 year old man with a 2 year history of a small painless testicular mass, with recent size increase ([Urol Ann 2021;13:308](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34421271))

Treatment

* Diagnostic orchidectomy is curative; observation after orchidectomy is the only necessary treatment

Gross description

* Well defined, firm tumors ranging from 0.5 to 7.6 cm in greatest dimension ([Am J Surg Pathol 2013;37:1208](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23715159))
* Cut surface is tan, yellow or white and solid to cystic; can have focally hemorrhagic areas

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Circumscribed but unencapsulated; encapsulated forms are rare
* Arranged in fascicles, storiform or a combination pattern in collagenized stroma with small blood vessels
* Herringbone pattern may be seen
* Variable cell density, variable collagen
* Usually scanty mitoses (≤ 5 mitoses/10 high power fields), can be as high as 10 mitoses/10 high power fields
* May be infiltrative and entrap seminiferous tubules; this feature has no impact on tumor behavior
* Usually no Sertoli or granulosa components are present but may have minor aggregates of other sex cord cells ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1999;123:391](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10235496))
* If > 1 microscopic focus of other sex cord stromal cells, classify as mixed or unclassified since these tumors have metastatic potential unlike the classical fibroma

Positive stains

* [Inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html), [SF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssf1.html), [smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html), [SOX9](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox9.html), [FOXL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfoxl2.html), [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html), [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html)

Negative stains

* [MIC2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [KIT](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html), [keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) (occasionally positive), [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html), [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)
* [Desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) is typically negative but may be focally positive ([Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:296](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9060599))

Electron microscopy description

* Fibroma cells resemble both fibroblasts and myofibroblasts

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Although loss of heterozygosity at 9q22.3 (*PTCH* locus) is observed in 40% of sporadic ovarian thecoma fibromas, only one case in testis has been reported ([J Pediatr Surg 2010;45:E1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20223301))

Sample pathology report

* Right testis, orchiectomy:
  + Fibrothecoma (see comment)
  + Comment: The neoplastic cells showed positivity for inhibin, smooth muscle actin and SOX9 while MelanA, KIT and desmin were negative. This is consistent with the diagnosis above.

Differential diagnosis

* [Fibrosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuefibrosarcoma.html):
  + Malignant tumor with infiltration, cytological atypia, frequent mitoses, necrosis
* [Leiomyoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueleiomyoma.html):
  + Positive for muscle markers; negative for [inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html) and other sex cord stromal markers
* [Neurofibroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueneurofibroma.html):
  + Patchy [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) positive; negative for sex cord stromal markers
* **Fibrous pseudotumors**:
  + Less cellular, more inflammatory cells
* [Solitary fibrous tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesft.html):
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) positive; tends to arise from extratesticular tissues like tunica albuginea, tunica vaginalis, gubernaculum, not testicular stromal cells
* [Unclassified sex cord stromal tumors](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testismixedscst.html):
  + Show more than focal incomplete Sertoli cell differentiation; strong positive [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) is useful stain
* [Myoid gonadal stromal tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testismyoidgonadalstromaltumor.html):
  + Rare, usually well circumscribed tumor composed of densely packed spindle cells with variable intervening collagen deposits; the nuclei are tapered with inconspicuous nucleoli
  + Positive for [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [FOXL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfoxl2.html) and [SF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssf1.html) and negative for
    [SOX9](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox9.html)
* [Granulosa cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisgranulosa.html):
  + Nodular growth pattern with cells showing nuclear grooves; luteinization of cells and mitotic figures are rare
  + *FOXL2* mutations can be seen
  + [Reticulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsreticulin.html) staining shows fibers surrounding aggregates of cells instead of individual cells as in fibrothecoma
