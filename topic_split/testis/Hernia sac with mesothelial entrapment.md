Definition / general

* Entrapment of mesothelial cells within hernia sac fibroadipose tissue

Essential features

* Small glands, clusters, cords or individual mesothelial cells within the hernia sac
* Usually no fat, muscle, stromal invasion or necrosis
* Lacks grossly visible proliferation ([Virchows Arch A Pathol Anat Histopathol 1989;415:283](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2503929))

Epidemiology

* 6% of pediatric hernia sacs contained mesothelial proliferation ([J Urol 2011;186:1620](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21855930))
* Mean age in adults is 44.5 years ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:54](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24061516))
* Incidence in adults unknown

Pathophysiology

* Persistent serosal injury causes reactive hyperplasia of the mesothelial lining and submesothelial fibrosis, entrapping mesothelial cells beneath the mesothelial lined surface ([Mod Pathol 2005;18:S131](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15502808), [Diagn Pathol 2011;6:78](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21867523))

Clinical features

* Similar clinical presentation as a patient with a hernia sac without mesothelial entrapment (usually hydrocele, also hematocele, cyst adjacent to epididymis, hemorrhagic epididymal cyst, pyocele) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:54](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24061516))

Prognostic factors

* No aggressive behavior has been reported ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:54](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24061516))

Treatment

* Similar treatment as a hernia without mesothelial entrapment, i.e. excision of hernia sac ([J Urol 2011;186:1620](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21855930))

Gross description

* No grossly visible proliferation; same findings as hernia sac without mesothelial proliferation ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:54](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24061516))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* No surface proliferation; surface often partially or completely denuded ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:54](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24061516))
* Mesothelial proliferation within fibrotic stroma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:54](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24061516))
* Widely spaced proliferation of mesothelial cells predominately oriented parallel to the surface mesothelium ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:54](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24061516))
* Most commonly form small glands and cords; also small nests and individual appearing cells ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:54](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24061516))
* Proliferation has a well defined edge of pseudoinvasion within the fibrous stroma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:54](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24061516))
* Usually does not penetrate into muscle ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2003;20:272](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14694980))
* Reactive appearing mesothelial cells with large nuclei and prominent nucleoli ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2003;20:272](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14694980))
* No to low mitotic activity ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:54](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24061516))
* Background of fibrosis and chronic inflammation ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:54](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24061516))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* 9p21 not deleted ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:54](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24061516))

Differential diagnosis

* [Mesothelioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testismesotheliomamalignant.html)
  + Grossly visible lesion, usually exophytic in this location ([Diagn Pathol 2011;6:78](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21867523))
  + Forms a mass with more tightly packed glands
  + True invasion
  + Haphazard arrangement of glands rather than in a linear arrangement
  + More nuclear atypia and mitotic activity
* [Adenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisepidadenocarcinoma.html):
  + Forms a mass
  + Desmoplastic reaction
  + Back to back glands
  + Mitotic activity ([Mod Pathol 2005;18:S131](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15502808))
  + Mesothelial markers usually negative
* [Adenomatoid tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisepidadenomatoid.html):
  + Forms a mass lesion near epididymis
  + Positive for mesothelial markers ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2003;20:272](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14694980))
* **Histiocytic inflammation**:
  + Keratin negative ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:54](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24061516))

Which of the following is a feature of a hernia sac with mesothelial entrapment?

1. Glands parallel to surface
2. GLUT1 and p53 positivity
3. Homozygous for 9p21 deletion
