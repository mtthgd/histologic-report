Definition / general

* Most common sex cord stromal tumor of the testis; comprised of cells resembling nonneoplastic Leydig cells
* A small minority (< 10%) of cases are clinically malignant ([World J Urol 2020;38:2857](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31960106), [Int J Urol 2010;17:886](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20812939))

Essential features

* Most common sex cord stromal tumor of the testis
* Histology: diffuse / nodular growth of polygonal cells with abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm, uniform round nuclei and prominent central nucleoli; Reinke crystals may be present
* Immunohistochemistry: inhibin A+, calretinin+, MelanA+, SF1+, AR+
* Features associated with malignant potential include: size > 5 cm, infiltrative borders, cytological atypia, frequent mitoses (> 3/10 high power fields), vascular invasion and necrosis
* Treatment: surgical resection, curative for nonmetastasizing tumors
* Prognosis: overall 5 year survival after orchiectomy > 90%

Terminology

* Interstitial cell tumor - obsolete term

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [D40.10](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D37-D48/D40-/D40.10) - Leydig cell tumor of testis

Epidemiology

* 1 - 2% of testicular tumors in adults and 3 - 6% of testicular tumors in prepubertal males ([J Urol 2009;181:2299](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19303105), [J Pediatr Hematol Oncol 2019;41:74](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29554024))
* Mostly sporadic, rarely associated with hereditary leiomyomatous and renal cell carcinoma syndrome ([J Clin Endocrinol Metab 2006;91:3071](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16757530))
* Occurs at any age with 2 peaks: 5 - 10 years and 30 - 60 years ([J Urol 2009;181:2299](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19303105))

Sites

* Testicular parenchyma
* Rarely in ectopic rests of Leydig cells in the epididymis ([Andrologia 2013;45:430](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23194277))

Pathophysiology

* Produces androgen, mainly testosterone, which can cause symptoms described [below](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/#testisleydigclinical)
* Can also produce estrogen by either direct production of estradiol or by peripheral aromatization of testosterone ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2007;131:311](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17284120))

Etiology

* Little is known
* Rare association with germline fumarate hydratase mutations in patients with hereditary leiomyomatosis and renal cell carcinoma syndrome and activating mutations of the luteinizing hormone receptor ([N Engl J Med 1999;341:1731](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10580072))

Clinical features

* > 90% benign
* Usually unilateral, rarely bilateral
* Painless testicular enlargement
* Children
  + Precocious puberty caused by androgen production
  + Gynecomastia and breast tenderness due to estrogen production ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2007;131:311](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17284120))
* Adults
  + Gynecomastia
  + Infertility, loss of libido and erectile dysfunction ([Hum Reprod 2019;34:1389](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31532522))
  + Rarely, Cushing syndrome ([Urology 2000;56:153](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10869651))
* Malignant Leydig cell tumors may rarely present with metastases, usually to the retroperitoneal lymph nodes or lungs ([Am J Surg Pathol 1985;9:177](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3993830), [J Urol 2020;203:949](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31845841))

Diagnosis

* Tumor histology and immunohistochemistry

Laboratory

* Serum testosterone and estrogen levels may be elevated
* Lower sperm concentration, lower total sperm count and motility ([Hum Reprod 2019;34:1389](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31532522))

Radiology description

* Nonspecific
* On ultrasound, tumors are generally well defined, homogeneous hypoechoic, small solid masses with hypervascularity ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2007;131:311](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17284120))
* May show cystic areas

Prognostic factors

* Benign Leydig cell tumors: excellent prognosis, curative by surgery
* Malignant Leydig cell tumors: poor survival, most develop metastatic disease leading to death ([J Urol 2020;203:949](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31845841))

Case reports

* 6 year old boy with precocious puberty and a left testicular mass ([J Indian Assoc Pediatr Surg 2017;22:181](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28694581))
* 32 year old man with gynecomastia and a small mass in the right testis ([Case Rep Urol 2018;2018:7202560](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30112247))
* 45 year old man with a large painless mass in the right testis ([Urol Case Rep 2019;28:101064](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31754603))
* 62 year old man with a huge painless mass in the right testis ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2018;97:e11158](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29924022))
* 91 year old man with a malignant Leydig cell tumor in the left testis ([Int Braz J Urol 2019;45:1260](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31808415))

Treatment

* Benign Leydig cell tumors
  + Orchidectomy
  + Testis sparing surgery in small tumors is a safe alternative ([Hum Reprod 2019;34:1389](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31532522), [World J Urol 2020;38:2857](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31960106))
* Malignant Leydig cell tumors
  + Orchiectomy with retroperitoneal lymph node dissection can be curative
  + No significant response to radiation or chemotherapy ([Oncology (Williston Park) 2014;28:211,](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24855728), [World J Urol 2020;38:2857](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31960106))

Gross description

* Well circumscribed, solid homogeneous mass
* Usually < 5 cm in size
* Golden brown or brownish green cut surface
* Hyalinization and calcification may be present
* Gross features suggestive of malignancy ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2007;131:311](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17284120))
  + Large size: > 5 cm
  + Infiltrative margins
  + Hemorrhage and necrosis
  + Extratesticular extension

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Architecture:
  + Diffuse or nodular with fibrous bands
  + Uncommon patterns: insular, trabecular, pseudotubular, ribbon-like, trabecular, spindled and microcystic ([Surg Pathol Clin 2018;11:739](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30447839))
* Cytologic features:
  + Polygonal cells with abundant eosinophilic granular cytoplasm, uniform round nuclei and prominent central nucleoli; rarely, nuclei may have a ground glass appearance
  + Uncommon cell types: scant cytoplasm, foamy cytoplasm and spindling ([Am J Surg Pathol 2002;26:1424](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12409718))
  + Lipofuscin pigment maybe present: golden yellow on H&E stain, red-purple granular appearance on PAS stain
  + Binucleated and multinucleated cells may be present
  + Reinke crystals: pathognomonic; identified in only up to 30% (degradation / dissolution by formalin fixation); intracytoplasmic, rarely extracellular
  + Mitosis: rare
  + Mild nuclear atypia permissible
  + Occasionally, psammoma bodies, calcification, osseous and adipocytic metaplasia may be identified ([Am J Surg Pathol 2002;26:1424](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12409718))
* Microscopic features suggestive of malignancy (most malignant tumors will have more than 2 of these features) ([Am J Surg Pathol 1985;9:177](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3993830)):
  + > 5 cm
  + Infiltrative borders
  + Cytological atypia
  + Frequent mitoses (> 3/10 high power fields)
  + Vascular invasion
  + Necrosis

Cytology description

* Fine needle aspiration is rarely performed unless in a metastatic lymph node
* Cellular smears with discohesive cells having eccentric round nuclei, evenly distributed chromatin, prominent nucleoli and abundant eosinophilic granular cytoplasm
* Naked nuclei are common
* Cytoplasm may be vacuolated due to lipid accumulation
* Nuclear grooves, binucleation and multinucleation may be identified
* Nuclear pseudoinclusions and Reinke crystals can be seen
* No cytological features to differentiate Leydig cell tumors from nodular Leydig cell hyperplasia ([J Am Soc Cytopathol 2019;8:220](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31272604))

Positive stains

* [Inhibin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html), [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html), [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html), [androgen receptor (AR)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsandrogenreceptor.html) and [steroidogenic factor 1 (SF1)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssf1.html)
* Insulin-like 3 (**INSL3**) variably reported to be positive
* [CD99 (MIC2)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html) membranous staining ([Histopathology 2021;78:290](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32757426))

Negative stains

* [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html), [OCT4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct34.html) and [beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1390](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26034868))
* [Cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html), [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) and [PLAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsplap.html) (rarely focally positive)

Electron microscopy description

* Reinke crystals are diagnostic
  + Appearance depends on plane of sectioning: prismatics, hexagonal lattices or hexagonal microtubules with parallel lines
  + Located in cytoplasm but can be seen in nucleus or interstitium
* Abundant smooth endoplasmic reticulum, mitochondria with tubulovesicular cristae, numerous lipid droplets and lipofuscin granules ([Case Reports Histol Histopathol 1989;4:247](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2520461))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* DNA aneuploidy is associated with malignant Leydig cell tumors, benign Leydig cell tumors are diploid
* Gain of chromosome X, 19 or 19p and loss on chromosome 8 and 16 are most frequent findings ([Oncol Rep 2007;17:585](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17273737))
* Somatic *GNAS* (guanine nucleotide binding protein, alpha stimulating activity polypeptide 1)
  + Activating mutation (R201S) has been reported ([J Androl 2012;33:578](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22016347))

Sample pathology report

* Right testicle, radical orchiectomy:
  + Leydig cell tumor, 2 cm in greatest dimension (see synoptic report)
  + Tumor limited to testis
  + Resection margins uninvolved by tumor
* Note that benign tumors are not staged

Differential diagnosis

* [Testicular tumor of adrenogenital syndrome or testicular adrenal rest tumors](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisadrenogenital.html):
  + Arise in males with congenital adrenal hyperplasia
  + Hormonal profile with high 17-hydroxyprogesterone and adrenal androgen levels and frequent regression following dexamethasone treatment
  + Usually bilateral, dark brown nodules
  + Spotty cytological atypia, abundant cytoplasmic lipofuscin pigment but no Reinke crystals, broad bands of hyalinized collagenous stroma
  + [Androgen receptor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsandrogenreceptor.html) and **INSL3** negative
  + More frequently positive for neuroendocrine markers ([synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html) and [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html))
  + Reference: [Histopathology 2017;70:513](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27801954)
* **Leydig cell hyperplasia:**
  + Interstitial growth pattern with small nodules < 0.5 cm; usually bilateral and multifocal
  + Diffuse positivity for **INSL3**
  + Reference: [Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2019;27:203](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29084059)
* [Granular cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuegranularcell.html):
  + Abundant granular cytoplasm, no Reinke crystals
  + Positive for [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html) and [PASD](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html); negative for [inhibin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html), [SF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssf1.html) and [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html)
* [Large cell calcifying Sertoli cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testissertolilargecell.html):
  + Associated with Carney complex
  + Sertoli cells with abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm and extensive calcification, variable tubular or intratubular growth, stroma more myxoid and neutrophil rich, no Reinke crystals
  + Positive for [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) and [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), more diffuse [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) positivity
  + Reference: [Hum Pathol 2010;41:552](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20004940)
* [Malakoplakia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladdermalakoplakia.html):
  + Involves tubules and interstitium with xanthogranulomatous inflammation and Michaelis-Gutmann bodies
* [Seminoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisseminomas.html):
  + Is in the differential diagnosis of Leydig cell tumor with clear cytoplasm
  + Seminoma is associated with [germ cell neoplasia in situ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisitgcn.html), fibrous septae, lymphocytic infiltrate and granulomas
  + Positive for [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html) and [OCT4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct34.html)
  + Negative for [inhibin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html), [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html) and [SF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssf1.html)
