Definition / general

* A rare benign tumor of neuroectodermal origin with biphasic population of neuroblastic cells and pigmented epithelial cells

Terminology

* First described by Krompecher in 1918 as congenital melanocarcinoma of alveolar process of maxilla ([BMJ Case Rep 2010 Nov 23;2010](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22797196))
* Previously known as: melanotic adamantinoma, melanotic hamartoma, melanotic progonoma, pigmented congenital epulis, pigmented neuroectodermal tumor, retinal anlage tumor, retinoblastic teratoma

Epidemiology

* Rare
* Usually young infants (mean 7 months, range 3 months to 8 years) but also reported in adults
* Most common in maxilla of infants with 2:1 female preponderance (see [Mandible - Maxilla Chapter](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mandiblemaxillaPigNEtumor.html))
* Also reported in long bones ([Cancer 1983;52:661](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6305482)), uterus, skin and brain
* Epididymis is uncommon site

Etiology

* Precise origin is unknown but suggested to be of neural crest origin, due to a developmental error in evolution of mesonephric ducts, where neural crest cells migrate ([J Pathol Bacteriol 1967;93:549](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6054056))
* A dysembryogenetic neoplasm that recapitulates embryonic retinal development ([Am J Surg Pathol 1991;15:233](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1847607))
* Relationship with other neural crest tumors such as PNET, neuroblastoma, etc. is not clear

Clinical features

* Firm, rapidly enlarging mass, sometimes associated with hydrocele
* Usually noticed at birth or within the first year of life
* Lymphatic space invasion is associated with hydrocele formation

Diagnosis

* Suspected clinically and confirmed histologically

Laboratory

* Urinary vanillylmandelic acid (VMA) / homovanillic acid (HVA) elevated in some cases

Prognostic factors

* Good prognosis, rarely metastasizes ([Cancer 1983;52:661](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6305482)), does recur ([Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2005;48:363](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16761753))
* Local recurrence rate in jaw tumors is ~15%
* No reliable histological features predict recurrence or metastasis

Case reports

* Infant with tumor of epididymis and testis ([Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2005;48:363](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16761753))
* 4 month old boy with tumor in epididymis ([Urol Int 1996;57:262](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8961502))
* 6 month old boys ([Urology 1995;46:415](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7660523), [Zentralbl Pathol 1994;140:181](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7947625))

Treatment

* Benign behavior ([Hum Pathol 1985;16:416](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2579889))
* Simple orchiectomy is usually curative

Gross description

* Round to oval, circumscribed, firm epididymal mass, usually < 4 cm
* Cut surface is grey white with areas of pigmentation; color depends on amount of pigmentation

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Appears to recapitulate retina at 5 weeks of gestation
* Contains dual population of cells: neuroblastic cells with high nuclear cytoplasmic ratio and epithelioid cells containing melanin
* Mitoses may be present, usually in neuroblastic component

Maxillary tumor: H&E and stains

Cytology description

* Dual cell population with dyscohesive primitive neuroblast like cells in fibrillary background, admixed with clusters of larger cells having melanin pigment ([Acta Cytol 2006;50:460](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16901015))

Positive stains

* [NSE](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsNSE.html), [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html), [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html), [keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)
* [Vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html), [GFAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgfap.html), [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html), **dopamine β hydroxylase**
* [Fontana-Masson](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfontana.html) stain highlights the melanin

Negative stains

* [AFP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsafp.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) and [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html)

Electron microscopy description

* Large cells contain melanosomes and premelanosomes with varied maturation while the neuroblastic cells contain neurosecretory granules and neurofilaments

Differential diagnosis

* Other round cell tumors
  + [Ewings / PNET](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneewing.html)
  + **Leukemic infiltrate**
  + Metastatic [neuroblastoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/adrenalneuroblastoma.html)
  + [Rhabdomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisrms.html): desmin, myogenin+, HMB45-
