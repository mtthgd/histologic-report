Definition / general

* Tumors secondarily involving testis and paratesticular structures from distant sites
* Excludes hematopoietic tumors

Essential features

* Most have known history of primary but rarely the initial presentation
* Histologic features recapitulate their site of origin
* Should be differentiated from germ cell tumor, sex cord stromal tumor, malignant mesothelioma and adenocarcinoma of the rete testis or epididymis

Terminology

* Also called secondary tumors of the testis

Epidemiology

* Occur in 0.7% of patients with solid organ malignancies in autopsy studies ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2000;4:59](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10760317))
* Majority are > 50 years old ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:1683](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18769334))

Sites

* Most common primary sites include the prostate, lung, skin, colon and kidney ([Am J Surg Pathol 2008;32:1683](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18769334))

Clinical features

* Most nonsymptomatic or incidental finding
* Most unilateral and solitary
* Rarely present as carcinoma of unknown primary

Diagnosis

* Clinical information and ancillary investigations can be more helpful than special histological techniques in the differential diagnosis

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Histologic features recapitulate their site of origin
* Metastatic prostatic adenocarcinoma tends to involve the tubular system
* Other metastatic carcinomas tend to infiltrate the testicular interstitium while sparing the seminiferous tubules
* Prominent lymphovascular invasion

Positive stains

* [Mucin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmucins.html) (teratomatous elements and yolk sac tumors may also be positive)
* [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)

Negative stains

* [PLAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsplap.html), [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Detection of isochromosome 12 by fluorescence in situ hybridization would suggest a primary germ cell tumor rather than a metastases

Sample pathology report

* Right testis and spermatic cord, radical orchiectomy:
  + Metastatic renal cell carcinoma, clear cell type, 2 cm, confined to the testicular parenchyma
  + Margins of resection, negative for malignancy

Differential diagnosis

* [Germ cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisgctgeneral.html):
  + Relatively younger age (typically 30s and 40s)
  + Background of germ cell neoplasm in situ
  + Positive for [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html) (all germ cell tumors), [OCT 3/4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct34.html) and [PLAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsplap.html) (seminoma and embryonal carcinoma), [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html) (embryonal carcinoma), [AFP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsafp.html) and [glypican 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglypican3.html) (yolk sac tumor)
  + Presence of isochromosome 12p ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:1529](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19574883), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2012;136:435](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22458906), [Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:689](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24503753))
* [Sex cord stromal tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testissexcordgeneral.html):
  + Positive for [inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html) and [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html), negative for [CK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) ([Mod Pathol 1998;11:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9720507), [Am J Surg Pathol 1998;22:615](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9591732))
* [Malignant mesothelioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testismesotheliomamalignant.html):
  + Usually extensively and diffusely involves tunica
  + Typically positive for mesothelial markers such as [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html), [CK5/6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5and6.html), [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html) and [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html) ([Mod Pathol 2005;18:S131](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15502808), [Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16330935), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2012;136:113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22208496))
* [Adenocarcinoma of the rete testis or epididymis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisreteadeno.html):
  + Has a morphologic transition from nonneoplastic rete or epididymis to adenocarcinoma (including in situ changes)
  + Positive for [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html) ([Mod Pathol 2005;18:S131](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15502808), [Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:1473](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21934480))

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/testismetastasesZyngergross01.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/testismetastasesZyngergross01.jpg)

Which of the following is the most common primary site of metastatic carcinoma in the testis?

1. Bladder
2. Kidney
3. Prostate
4. Lung
5. Colon

Board review style answer #1

**C.** The most common primary site of metastatic carcinoma in the testis is the prostate

As shown in the figure, there is an adenocarcinoma in the testis. Which of the following markers is the best for differentiation between this metastatic adenocarcinoma in the testis and primary adenocarcinoma of the rete testis or epididymis?

1. Calretinin
2. CDX2
3. CK7
4. CK20
5. PAX8

Board review style answer #2

**E.** Primary adenocarcinomas of the rete testis or epididymis are positive for PAX8 while metastatic adenocarcinomas from other sites are usually negative for PAX8 (except kidney and thyroid)
