Definition / general

* Ovarian type surface epithelial carcinoma of the testis with mucinous differentiation

Epidemiology

* Extremely rare testicular tumor reported in middle age and elderly men (mean age 62)

Pathophysiology

* The histogenesis of the ovarian type surface epithelial tumors of the testis and paratestis remains unclear
* These tumors are possibly derived from Müllerian remnants or by metaplasia of the mesothelium of the tunica vaginalis ([Hum Pathol 2011;42:1343](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21396680))

Diagnosis

* Clinical evaluation is necessary to rule out metastatic spread from a primary adenocarcinoma of the appendix, colon, stomach, pancreas, lung or prostate

Case reports

* 55 year old man with primary testicular mucinous neoplasms ([Can Urol Assoc J 2010;4:E112](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20694089))
* 59 year old man with bilateral mucinous cystadenocarcinoma of the testis and epididymis ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1992;116:1360](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1456884))
* 71 year old man with mucinous cystadenocarcinoma of the testis ([Hum Pathol 2011;42:1343](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21396680))

Treatment

* Radical orchiectomy

Gross description

* Mucinous, cystic mass replacing the testicular parenchyma

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Well to moderately differentiated malignant cystic neoplasm with mucinous differentiation
* Back to back irregular glands, elongated fine complex papillary structures lined with alternating goblet cells and ciliated cells, with little intervening stroma
* Papillary structures have atypical cells with hyperchromatic overlapping elongated nuclei with coarse chromatin and increased nuclear to cytoplasmic ratio
* Cystic spaces filled with mucin, fibrinopurulent material, degenerative cellular debris
* Psammomatous calcifications can be present as well as mucin extravasation

Differential diagnosis

* Adenocarcinoma of the [rete](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisreteadeno.html) or [epididymis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisepidadenocarcinoma.html)
* **Metastatic mucinous adenocarcinoma** ([appendix](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/appendixadenocarcinoma.html), [colon](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/colontumoradenocarcinoma.html), [stomach](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stomachcarcinomageneral.html), **pancreas**, [lung](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungtumoradenoclass.html), [prostate](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostatemucinous.html))
* [Teratoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisteratoma.html) with malignant transformation
