Definition / general

* Benign tumor of epididymis characterized by prominent papillary architecture
* Often associated with [von Hippel-Lindau disease](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Von_Hippel%E2%80%93Lindau_disease) ([Hum Pathol 1995;26:1341](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8522307))
* First described by Sherrick ([Cancer 1956;9:403](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/13304856))

Terminology

* Also called papillary cystadenofibroma

Epidemiology

* Second most common benign neoplasm of epididymis after [adenomatoid tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisepidadenomatoid.html)
* Sporadic or familial
* Unilateral or bilateral (40%)
* Mean age 36 years
* Associated with von Hippel-Lindau (VHL) disease; 65% with bilateral tumors have VHL vs. 18 - 20% with unilateral lesions ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2010;134:630](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20367315))

Sites

* In male GU, occurs in epididymis (develops within efferent ductules of head of epididymis)
* In female GU, occurs in broad ligament, peritoneum; also fallopian tube and uterus

Clinical features

* Benign, usually asymptomatic
* If symptomatic, typically is slow growing swelling in scrotum, with only rare pain or tenderness
* Rarely discovered during infertility workup ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2010;134:630](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20367315))

Laboratory

* No specific laboratory features
* Negative for germ cell tumor markers

Case reports

* 46 year old man ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2011;4:629](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21904638))
* Vascular endothelial growth factor in von Hippel-Lindau syndrome - associated papillary cystadenoma of epididymis ([Hum Pathol 1998;29:1322](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9824115))
* 3 cases ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1990;114:672](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1694653))
* Solid variant ([Histopathology 2015;67:138](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25406742))

Treatment

* Surgical excision; does not recur
* If bilateral, patient should be investigated for VHL syndrome

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Papillary in foldings project into cystic spaces covered by single to double layered cuboidal / columnar cells
* Tubules common, colloid type cystic material common
* Tumor cells have lightly eosinophilic to clear cytoplasm
* May see dilated efferent ductules
* May have clear cells resembling metastastic renal cell carcinoma but no mitoses, no necrosis, no pleomorphism
* Papillary cystadenofibroma: prominent stroma

Cytology description

* Simple and complex papillary clusters
* Tumor cells are monomorphic, with moderate to abundant cytoplasm, well defined cytoplasmic borders; round to oval nuclei, finely dispersed chromatin, inconspicuous nucleoli
* Vacuolations may be present
* No features of malignancy - i.e. no mitoses, no necrosis, no atypia
* Usually no psammoma bodies, but see [Cytopathology 2014;25:279](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23901807), [Acta Cytol 2004;48:467](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15192977)

Positive stains

* [AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [alpha-1-antitrypsin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsa1a.html), [alpha-1-antichymotrypsin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalpha1antichymo.html), [CAM5.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscam52.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html), [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html), vascular endothelial growth factor
* [Lectin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainslectins.html)
* [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html), [carbonic anhydrase IX](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscarbonicanhydraseix.html)
* Variable [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:713](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24441657))

Negative stains

* Useful negative stains include [AMACR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsamacr.html) and [RCC antigen](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsrcc.html), to differentiate from clear cell papillary renal cell carcinoma
* [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) is usually negative but focal staining may be noted ([Am J Surg Pathol 2005;29:520](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15767808))

Differential diagnosis

* [Metastatic renal cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testismetastases.html)
  + Other features of malignancy
  + CD10, RCC antigen are strongly positive
  + Lectin and CK7 are negative
* [Serous borderline tumors](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisserousborder.html) and [serous adenocarcinoma of paratestis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisserouspapca.html): atypia, mitotic activity, stratification and detached cell clusters
