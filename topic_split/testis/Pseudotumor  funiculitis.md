Definition / general

* Paratesticular (fibrous) pseudotumor (PFP) / funiculitis is a rare tumor-like lesion of unknown etiology
* Variably cellular proliferation of bland fibroblasts and myofibroblasts, fibrous stroma and chronic inflammation

Essential features

* Rare, nonneoplastic, paratesticular lesion, involving epididymis, tunica or spermatic cord
* Unknown etiology; associated with hydrocele, trauma or increased number of IgG4 positive plasma cells
* Bland spindle cell proliferation within dense fibrous tissue, associated with variable chronic inflammation
* Imaging studies, particularly scrotum magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), might show findings suggestive of fibrous pseudotumor
* Scrotum exploration with biopsy and intraoperative consultation with frozen section may help differentiate this tumor from a malignant mimic and avoid orchiectomy

Terminology

* Paratesticular fibrous pseudotumor, inflammatory pseudotumor, nodular pseudotumor, chronic proliferative periorchitis, fibrous periorchitis, reactive periorchitis, pseudofibromatous periorchitis, nodular and diffuse fibrous proliferation, nonspecific peritesticular fibrosis, fibroma, benign fibrous paratesticular tumor, pseudosarcomatous myofibroblastic proliferation of the spermatic cord, proliferative funiculitis, fibrous mesothelioma ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2003;127:742](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12741903), [Pathol Annu 1990;25:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2202966), [Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:296](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9060599), [Urology 2000;56:670](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11018636), [Semin Diagn Pathol 2000;17:340](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11202549), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 1992;116:277](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1536613), [J Pediatr Surg 2001;36:1057](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11431777), [J Urol 1989;141:376](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2492352), [J Pak Med Assoc 2009;59:47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19213380), [Urology 2006;67:623.e15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16527591), [Am J Surg Pathol 1992;16:448](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1599023), [Tunis Med 2012;90:333](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22535350), [Ulbright: Tumors of the Testis, Adnexa, Spermatic Cord, and Scrotum, 1st Edition, 1999](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1881041468/pathologyoutl-20))
* Mostofi and Price proposed the term fibrous pseudotumor to encompass all reactive fibroinflammatory lesions of the testicular tunics ([Mostofi: Tumors of the Male Genital System, 1st Edition, 1973](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B002GFCWH6/pathologyoutl-20))

Epidemiology

* Can occur across all age groups (e.g., 16 - 76); mean age at presentation is 42 years ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:569](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20216379), [Am J Surg Pathol 1992;16:448](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1599023), [Ann Surg 1904;39:396](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17861428))
* Rarely seen in children ([J Urol 1993;150:1886](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8230526), [Pediatr Dev Pathol 2016;19:460](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27575254))

Sites

* Paratesticular tissue and spermatic cord (involving epididymis, tunica or spermatic cord) ([Front Med 2014;8:484](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24627288), [Urology 2000;56:670](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11018636), [Am J Surg Pathol 1992;16:448](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1599023), [J Urol 2004;171:1625](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15017237))

Etiology

* Nonneoplastic, rare lesion of unknown etiology
* May be due to ischemia, torsion, secondary to trauma, hydrocele, infections or inflammation ([J Pediatr Surg 2001;36:1057](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11431777), [J Pak Med Assoc 2009;59:47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19213380), [Virchows Arch 2011;458:109](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20957491), [Front Immunol 2021;12:717902](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34447384), [APMIS 2018;126:459](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29924455), [J Pediatr Surg 2001;36:1057](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11431777), [J Urol 1992;148:1254](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1404648), [J Urol 1989;141:376](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2492352), [Pediatr Dev Pathol 2016;19:460](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27575254))
* Abundance of IgG4 expressing plasma cells in many of these lesions suggests that paratesticular fibrous tumor may be part of the IgG4 related disease family; correlation with clinical findings is essential in the diagnosis of IgG4 related diseases and its distinction from nonspecific inflammatory reactions, particularly in unusual anatomic sites ([J Med Case Rep 2013;7:225](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24025610), [Gland Surg 2023;12:426](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37057041), [Virchows Arch 2011;458:109](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20957491))
* Some have been associated with testicular infarct, Gorlin syndrome (the nevoid basal cell carcinoma syndrome) and *Schistosoma haematobium* infection ([J Pediatr Surg 2001;36:1057](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11431777), [J Urol 1992;148:1254](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1404648), [J Urol 1989;141:376](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2492352))

Clinical features

* Most patients present with a palpable, painless scrotal mass simulating a testicular tumor ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2003;127:742](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12741903))
* Can be an incidental finding at herniorrhaphy ([Am J Surg Pathol 1992;16:448](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1599023))
* May present as a solitary lesion or multifocal ([J Urol 2004;171:1625](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15017237), [Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:569](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20216379))

Diagnosis

* Imaging studies, particularly scrotum MRI, might show findings suggestive of fibrous pseudotumor
* Scrotum exploration with biopsy and intraoperative consultation with frozen section will help differentiate from a malignant mimic and avoid orchiectomy

Laboratory

* Testicular tumor marker levels within normal limits (normal levels of alpha fetoprotein, beta human chorionic gonadotropin) ([Am J Mens Health 2023;17:15579883231181571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37381639), [J Urol 2004;171:1625](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15017237), [Rare Tumors 2016;8:6288](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27441080))
* Some patients show elevated serum levels of IgG4 ([Front Immunol 2021;12:717902](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34447384))

Radiology description

* Ultrasonographic findings are largely unspecific: the lesion is usually heterogeneous and hypoechoic ([Pol J Radiol 2016;81:10](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26834864))
* Computed tomography (CT) imaging studies show a hypodense scrotal mass ([Pol J Radiol 2016;81:10](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26834864))
* Scrotal magnetic resonance imaging may help to differentiate between malignant tumors and paratesticular (fibrous) pseudotumors, which in general appear hypointense compared to testicular tissue on T1 and T2 weighted MRI and might also show focal enhancement after intravenous injection of contrast material ([AJR Am J Roentgenol 1997;168:1586](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9168731), [Pol J Radiol 2016;81:10](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26834864))

Prognostic factors

* Can recur locally
* Overall excellent prognosis

Case reports

* 16 year old boy with painless enlargement of the scrotum ([Ann Surg 1904;39:396](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17861428))
* 32 year old man with a painless left scrotal mass ([Cureus 2023;15:e46768](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37954714))
* 36 year old man with a small, painless right scrotal mass ([Urol Case Rep 2021;36:101584](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33552918))
* 38 year old man with palpable left scrotal masses ([Am J Mens Health 2023;17:15579883231181571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37381639))

Treatment

* Surgical resection (frozen section assessment and local excision / testicular sparing surgery), whenever technically feasible, is the preferred treatment ([Front Med 2014;8:484](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24627288), [Case Rep Urol 2018;2018:6904827](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29593931), [J Med Case Rep 2013;7:225](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24025610), [Can Urol Assoc J 2011;5:E47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21806893))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Variably cellular proliferation of bland fibroblasts and myofibroblasts with oval to fusiform nuclei, small nucleoli and amphophilic cytoplasm
* In some cases, there is also a minor population of myofibroblasts / fibroblasts with abundant basophilic cytoplasm, resembling the ganglion-like cells of proliferative fasciitis ([Am J Surg Pathol 1992;16:448](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1599023))
* No nuclear atypia or atypical mitoses
* Necrosis might be present ([Int J Surg Pathol 2008;16:48](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18203784))
* Dense fibrous stroma with variable, mixed inflammatory infiltrate mainly composed of plasma cells and lymphocytes (mixed small B and T cells) but also histiocytes and scattered eosinophils or even uncommonly, mast cells ([Gland Surg 2023;12:426](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37057041), [Pathol Int 2003;53:897](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14629758))
* May have focal dystrophic calcifications and ossification
* One study showed the presence of malignant appearing glandular structures composed of keratin positive mesothelial cells within paratesticular pseudotumor of the spermatic cord ([Int J Surg Pathol 2008;16:48](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18203784))

Sample pathology report

* Soft tissue, right paratestis, excision:
  + Dense fibrous tissue with chronic inflammation, consistent with fibrous pseudotumor
  + Negative for malignancy
* Right testicle, radical orchiectomy:
  + Fibrous pseudotumor of spermatic cord (see comment)
  + Comment: Histologic sections show reactive appearing fibroblasts / myofibroblasts, dense fibrous stroma and scattered chronic inflammatory cells. These features are consistent with a nonneoplastic reactive lesion that has been described under a variety of names, including fibrous pseudotumor. Due to the location of the mass, *MDM2* FISH was performed and was negative for *MDM2* amplification, which argues against a subtle sclerosing / inflammatory variant of well differentiated liposarcoma. In addition, given the increased plasma cells, an IgG4 immunostain was performed and demonstrated increased IgG4 positive plasma cells (up to 50/HPF). While recent studies have suggested that paratesticular fibrous pseudotumor might belong to the growing list of IgG4 related diseases, correlation with clinical findings is essential in the diagnosis of IgG4 related diseases and its distinction from nonspecific inflammatory reactions, particularly in unusual anatomic sites ([APMIS 2018;126:459](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29924455)).

Differential diagnosis

* [Dedifferentiated liposarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuededifflipo.html):
  + Much more atypical
  + Adjacent well differentiated liposarcoma component ([Am J Surg Pathol 1992;16:448](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1599023))
  + *MDM2* amplification
* [Well differentiated liposarcoma (sclerosing or inflammatory subtypes)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuewdliposarcoma.html):
  + Rare, atypical, hyperchromatic spindle cells in background of dense fibrosis or a prominent inflammatory infiltrate
  + *MDM2* amplification
* [Embryonal rhabdomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueembryonalrhabdo.html):
  + Younger population (children and adolescents)
  + More cellular and atypical ([Am J Surg Pathol 1992;16:448](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1599023))
  + Positive for [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [myogenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html), [MyoD1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyod1.html)
* [Myxoid leiomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueleiomyosarcoma.html):
  + Intracytoplasmic glycogen
  + Nuclear pleomorphism
  + Possible necrosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 1992;16:448](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1599023), [Pathol Int 2006;56:625](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16984620))
* [Fibromatosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuefibromatosisdeep.html):
  + Long, sweeping fascicles
  + [Beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html) positive (nuclear staining)
* [Inflammatory myofibroblastic tumor (IMT)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueinflammyofibro.html):
  + *ALK* gene rearrangements and [ALK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html) overexpression in ~50% of cases ([Curr Opin Oncol 2012;24:409](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22664824))

The spindle cells in the paratesticular pseudotumor shown in the photomicrograph are believed to pertain to which line of differentiation?

1. Adipocytic
2. Histiocytic
3. Myofibroblastic
4. Nerve sheath
5. Vascular

Board review style answer #1

**C**. Myofibroblastic. Paratesticular pseudotumor is thought to be a reactive lesion composed of myofibroblasts / fibroblasts set in a dense fibrous stroma with a variable, mixed inflammatory infiltrate. Answer A is incorrect because while the reactive spindle cells and chronic inflammation can be found infiltrating into adipose tissue, this lesion is not related to an adipocytic proliferation / neoplasm. Answer B is incorrect because while histiocytes are present within the lesion, the main reactive, proliferative population is represented by myofibroblasts / fibroblasts. Answer D is incorrect because this lesion is not related to a nerve sheath proliferation / neoplasm. Answer E is incorrect because this lesion is not related to a vascular proliferation / neoplasm.
