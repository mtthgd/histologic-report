Definition / general

* Testicular germ cell tumor presenting with spontaneous complete or partial regression ([J Ultrasound Med 2003;22:515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12751863), [J Urol 2009;182:2303](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19762049), [Case Rep Oncol 2017;10:846](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29071000))

Essential features

* Testicular germ cell tumor presenting with spontaneous complete or partial regression ([J Ultrasound Med 2003;22:515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12751863), [J Urol 2009;182:2303](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19762049), [Case Rep Oncol 2017;10:846](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29071000))
* Presents as metastasis, residual testicular germ cell tumor or both ([BJU Int 2004;94:74](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15217435), [Case Rep Oncol 2017;10:846](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29071000), [Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:858](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16819328))
* Histologic features of testicular tumor regression include fibrous scar and [germ cell neoplasia in situ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisitgcn.html) ([J Ultrasound Med 2003;22:515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12751863), [Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:858](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16819328))
* Most commonly associated with pure [seminoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisseminomas.html) in metastasis and residual testicular tumor ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:858](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16819328), [J Urol 2009;182:2303](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19762049))

Terminology

* Regressed germ cell tumor
* Burned out germ cell tumor
* Burnt out germ cell tumor

Epidemiology

* 3% of all testicular tumors ([J Ultrasound Med 2003;22:515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12751863))
* Median age of diagnosis is between 28 and 32 (range 17 - 67 years) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:858](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16819328), [J Urol 2009;182:2303](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19762049))
* 10% of retroperitoneal germ cell tumors have a regressed testicular primary ([J Ultrasound Med 2003;22:515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12751863))

Sites

* Retroperitoneal metastasis is most common ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:858](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16819328))
* Metastasis to liver, lung, bone and brain has been reported ([Case Rep Urol 2015;2015:835962](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25705542))

Pathophysiology

* Poorly understood ([J Ultrasound Med 2003;22:515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12751863))
* Proposed mechanisms ([J Ultrasound Med 2003;22:515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12751863), [BJU Int 2004;94:74](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15217435), [Case Rep Oncol 2017;10:846](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29071000)):
  + Immune modulated regression
  + Ischemic regression

Clinical features

* Metastatic symptoms are the most common initial presentation: extragonadal mass, abdominal pain, back pain, lymphadenopathy, weight loss and fever ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:858](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16819328), [J Ultrasound Med 2003;22:515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12751863), [J Urol 1996;156:85](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8648846))
* Less common presentations include: testicular mass, elevated serum tumor markers (AFP, beta hCG and LDH) and testicular pain ([J Ultrasound Med 2003;22:515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12751863), [Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:858](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16819328))

Diagnosis

* Discovered most commonly as metastatic disease in the absence of palpable testicular mass ([J Ultrasound Med 2003;22:515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12751863), [J Urol 1996;156:85](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8648846))
* Sonographic evidence of testicular abnormality ([J Ultrasound Med 2003;22:515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12751863), [J Urol 2009;182:2303](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19762049))
* Extragonadal germ cell tumor identified ([J Ultrasound Med 2003;22:515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12751863), [J Urol 2009;182:2303](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19762049))
* Histopathologic features of tumor regression within testis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:858](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16819328), [Am J Pathol 1961;38:207](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/13685483))

Radiology description

* Sonographic features of tumor regression ([J Ultrasound Med 2003;22:515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12751863), [Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:858](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16819328), [J Urol 2009;182:2303](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19762049)):
  + Macrolithiasis (> 0.2 cm) or microlithiasis (< 0.2 cm)
  + Loss of testicular homogeneity
  + Nonspecific hyperechoic or hypoechoic lesions
  + Testicular atrophy
* Computed tomographic (CT) features of metastasis ([J Ultrasound Med 2003;22:515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12751863)):
  + Lymphadenopathy
  + Extragonadal mass

Prognostic factors

* Posttreatment disease free survival rate: 29% at 5 years and 12% at 10 years ([J Urol 2009;182:2303](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19762049))
* No difference in prognosis between complete and partial regression ([J Urol 2009;182:2303](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19762049))
* In context of regressed germ cell tumor with metastasis, pure seminoma has a more favorable prognosis ([J Urol 2009;182:2303](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19762049))
* No data is available comparing prognosis of regressed versus nonregressed testicular germ cell tumors

Case reports

* 24 year old man with brain metastasis ([Cureus 2016;8:e551](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27182465))
* 43 year old man with retroperitoneal seminoma ([Rom J Morphol Embryol 2011;52:193](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21424055))
* 45 year old man with retroperitoneal metastasis ([Int J Health Sci (Qassim) 2018;12:94](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29896078))
* 54 year old man with pancreatic choriocarcinoma ([Mod Pathol 2004;17:1573](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15545958))
* 54 year old man with pulmonary choriocarcinoma ([Intern Med 2016;55:1481](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27250057))

Treatment

* Combination systemic chemotherapy is mainstay of treatment for metastasis ([J Ultrasound Med 2003;22:515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12751863), [J Urol 2009;182:2303](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19762049))
  + Can be combined with radiotherapy for seminoma
* Radical orchiectomy for abnormal sonographic features ([J Ultrasound Med 2003;22:515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12751863), [J Urol 2009;182:2303](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19762049))

Gross description

* Fibrous scar displacing normal testicular parenchyma ([J Ultrasound Med 2003;22:515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12751863), [J Urol 2009;182:2303](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19762049))
* Scars appear white to tan in color ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:858](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16819328))
* Scars can appear as well demarcated nodular foci or stellate lesions ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:858](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16819328))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Intratesticular fibrous scar ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:858](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16819328), [Am J Pathol 1961;38:207](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/13685483)):
  + Acellular collagenous tissue with numerous small interspersed vessels
  + Foamy or hemosiderin laden macrophages
  + Lymphoplasmacytic infiltrate
  + May have necrosis or intratubular calcification (microlithiasis)
* Residual germ cell tumor may be present if regression is incomplete
  + Pure [seminoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisseminomas.html) most common ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:858](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16819328), [J Urol 2009;182:2303](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19762049))
* Testicular parenchyma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:858](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16819328)):
  + [Germ cell neoplasia in situ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisitgcn.html) (50% of cases)
  + Remaining parenchyma may be atrophic with:
    - Shrunken seminiferous tubules with decreased spermatogenesis
    - Sertoli cell only lined tubules
    - Thickened peritubular basement membrane

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Identification of **isochromosome 12p** by FISH can confirm a testicular germ cell tumor metastasis although immunohistochemistry is more commonly used and has wider availability

Sample pathology report

* Left testicle and spermatic cord, radical inguinal orchiectomy:
  + Scar (1.5 cm) and germ cell neoplasia in situ, consistent with regressed germ cell tumor (see synoptic report, pTis NX)
  + Comment: the germ cell neoplasia in situ expresses OCT3/4, D2-40, CD117 and PLAP

Differential diagnosis

* **Nonneoplastic scar**:
  + Normal serum tumor markers, no extragonadal metastasis
  + Usually multifocal
  + No [germ cell neoplasia in situ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisitgcn.html) (negative for [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html), [OCT3 / 4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct4.html), [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html), [PLAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsplap.html) and [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html))
  + Intratubular calcification less common
* [Testicular atrophy](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisatrophy.html):
  + Normal serum tumor markers, no extragonadal metastasis
  + Usually diffuse, no discrete scar
  + No [germ cell neoplasia in situ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisitgcn.html) (negative for [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html), [OCT3 / 4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct4.html), [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html), [PLAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsplap.html) and [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html))
  + Intratubular calcification less common
* **Treatment effect**:
  + History of chemotherapy for germ cell tumor
