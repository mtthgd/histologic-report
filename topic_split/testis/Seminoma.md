Definition / general

* Most common type of testicular germ cell tumor (up to 50%) and may occur as component of mixed germ cell tumor
* Derived from transformed gonocytes
* Consists of cells with well defined borders, (usually) clear cytoplasm, round to polygonal nuclei and prominent nucleolus

Essential features

* Most frequent germ cell tumor of the testis
* Presents in young men (30 - 49) with unilateral palpable mass
* Typically a well demarcated, uniform neoplasm with characteristic cytological features and background of fibrous septae and lymphocytes
* Germ cell markers (OCT 3/4, CD117) are useful in the diagnosis
* Prognosis is stage dependent with excellent 5 year survival when clinical stage 1

Terminology

* Seminoma: testicular primary (or mediastinal primary)
* Dysgerminoma: same tumor but primary to the ovary
* Germinoma: same tumor but primary to extragonadal sites (such as pineal gland)

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9061/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf#page=85) - seminoma, NOS
* ICD-10: [C62.90](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C60-C63/C62-/C62.90) - malignant neoplasm of unspecified testis, unspecified whether descended or undescended

Epidemiology

* Male, age 30 - 49
* Rare in > 70 or

Sites

* Testicle

Pathophysiology

* Arises from germ cell neoplasia in situ (GCNIS)
* GCNIS cells are proposed to arise from delayed maturation of primordial germ cells / gonocytes with polyploidization resulting in a transformed germ cell
* This progresses post puberty into seminoma (most likely GCNIS evolves into intratubular seminoma and then invasive seminoma) ([J Urol 1996;155:1938](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27747907))

Etiology

* Risk factors include: family history, previous germ cell tumor, cryptorchidism, subfertility, immunodeficiency disorders ([Br J Urol 1989;64:78](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2569902), [J Urol 1996;155:1938](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8618292))

Clinical features

* Usual presentation is with a mass
* Paraneoplastic symptoms are rare (limbic encephalopathy, hypercalcemia, polycythemia, exophthalmus) ([BMJ Case Rep 2014;2014:bcr2014206893](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25378115))
* Metastases are initially retroperitoneal and then progress to mediastinal and cervical nodes; visceral metastases develop late

Diagnosis

* Testicular ultrasound
* Serum LDH, AFP and hCG performed
* Chest Xray or CT of abdomen and chest ([Cancer Treat Res 2018;175:273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30168127))

Laboratory

* Serum LDH and PLAP may be elevated
* hCG levels are increased in up to 20% of cases but elevation is modest ([Pathol Annu 1991;26:59](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1650448))
* AFP should not be increased, if elevated think liver disease or nonseminoma component

Radiology description

* Uniform, well delineated and hypoechoic mass on ultrasound
* May be microlithiasis

Prognostic factors

* Clinical stage 1 has 95 - 98% 5 year survival
  + > 40 years and advanced stage are poor prognostic features (nonpulmonary metastases)

Case reports

* 27 year old man with neoplastic meningitis from testicular seminoma ([Oncologist 2018;23:1385](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30012875))
* 30 year old man with hemoperitoneum from ruptures intra-abdominal testis ([BMJ Case Rep 2018;2018:224898](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29674406))
* 34 year old man with mediastinal seminoma presenting as a neck mass ([Diagn Cytopathol 2019;47:334](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30468316))
* 40 year old man with intra-abdominal seminoma arising in cryptorchid testis ([J Med Case Rep 2019;13:2](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30609931))
* 44 year old man with limbic encephalitis from metastatic “burned out” seminoma ([Front Neurol 2018;9:20](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29441039))

Treatment

* Surgery (radical orchiectomy) for primary
* Surveillance protocols used for stage 1 disease
* Radiation (very radiosensitive tumor) and chemotherapy (platinum based) used in higher stage disease
* Retroperitoneal lymph node dissection used for residual post chemotherapy disease ([J Natl Compr Canc Netw 2012;10:502](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22491049))

Gross description

* Well demarcated, homogeneous, solid cream or grey tumors; surface nodularity and lobulation; may be multiple nodules
* Necrosis or hemorrhage usually minimal
* If regressed, only a scar may be visible
* Usually confined to testis (90%)

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Sheets or lobular configuration of tumor with fibrous septae
* Cells are typically pale (glycogen) but may be eosinophilic
* Cell membranes are well defined with distinct cell boundaries
* Nuclei are polygonal and may have a flat edge giving a squared off appearance; they contain one or more prominent central nucleoli; no nuclear overlap if well fixed tissue
* A lymphocytic infiltrate is present (T lymphocytes) with plasma cells; germinal centers may occur
* Granulomas noted in up to 50% of cases
* Intercellular edema with microcystic spaces and coagulative type necrosis can be present
* Multinucleated syncytiotrophoblasts can be seen in 20% of tumors and can produce hCG
* Intratubular seminoma may be adjacent to invasive seminoma (30% cases)
* Less frequent growth patterns include intertubular pattern (may not form a mass), corded growth, microcystic, tubular, signet ring appearance
* Anaplasia referring to increased mitoses and pleomorphism within a seminoma is no longer reported (no correlation with outcome) ([J Urol 1996;155:1938](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27747907))
* Fibrosis and sclerosis can indicate [regressed or burnt out seminoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisregressedgct.html)
* Lymphovascular invasion is uncommon, especially in tumors under 4 cm, and should be differentiated from artifactual carry over ([Am J Clin Pathol 2016;145:341](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27124916))
* Invasion of rete testis can be direct stromal invasion or pagetoid invasion; direct stromal invasion carries prognostic significance in seminoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:e22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28368923))

Cytology description

* Discohesive population of large cells with moderate cytoplasm, round slightly irregular nuclei and one or more prominent nucleoli
* Background population of small, mature lymphocytes
* Classic “tigroid smear” seen mostly in hypercellular aspirates ([J Cytol 2011;28:39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21552408))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Isochromosome from the short arm of chromosome 12, i(12p) present
* *KIT* mutations ([Cancer Res 2005;65:8085](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16166280))

Sample pathology report

* Right testis, mass, radical orchiectomy:
  + Seminoma, 3.6 cm (see synoptic report)
  + Comment: Immunohistochemistry was performed and the tumor expresses OCT 3/4 and CD117. The cells are negative with CD30 and AFP. The immunoprofile supports the morphologic interpretation of seminoma.

Differential diagnosis

* [Embryonal carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisembryonal.html), solid pattern:
  + Significantly greater atypia with lack of distinct cell borders
  + Nuclei overlap and increased mitotic activity
  + May have additional growth patterns present
  + [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html) and [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) positive
* [Spermatocytic tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisspermseminoma.html):
  + Older patients (> 50)
  + Lack of lymphocytes and fibrous septae, three distinct cell populations
  + Absence of [GCNIS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisitgcn.html)
  + [OCT 3/4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct34.html), [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html) and [PLAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsplap.html) negative (may express [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html) and [CD30](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd30.html))
* [Yolk sac](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisyst.html), solid pattern:
  + Usually some variability in growth patterns present
  + Lacks distinct fibrous septae and abundant lymphocytes
  + May have Schiller-Duval bodies and hyaline globules
  + [Glypican 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglypican3.html) and [alpha-fetoprotein (AFP)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsafp.html) positive
* [Sertoli cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisSertoliNOS.html):
  + Lack fibrous bands, lymphocytes and granulomas
  + May have more typical tubular areas in conjunction with sheet like growth
  + Absence of [GCNIS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisitgcn.html)
  + [Inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html) and [keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) can be positive; negative for [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html), [PLAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsplap.html) and [OCT 3/4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct34.html)
* [Lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testislymphoma.html):
  + Older patients (> 60)
  + Predominantly interstitial involvement between seminiferous tubules, may be bilateral (rare for seminoma)
  + Absence of [GCNIS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisitgcn.html)
  + [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html) and [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html) positive; [OCT 3/4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct34.html) and [PLAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsplap.html) negative
* [Granulomatous orchitis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisgranulomatousorchitis.html):
  + Predominantly intratubular involvement, granulomas may be poorly formed
  + Absence of [GCNIS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisitgcn.html)
  + [OCT 3/4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct34.html), [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html) and [PLAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsplap.html) negative
