Definition / general

* Sex cord stromal tumor of testis composed of cells showing features of fetal, prepubertal, adult or atrophic Sertoli cells, at least focally

Essential features

* Second most common type of pure sex cord stromal tumor after Leydig cell tumor
* Most are small, unilateral tumors in adults and the vast majority are benign
* Characterized by tubular or cord pattern, at least focally
* Positive for SF1 and nuclear beta catenin
* Variable and inconsistent expression of calretinin, inhibin A, androgen receptor, CD56
* Negative for SALL4, OCT4 and other germ cell markers
* Poor response to therapy when metastatic

Terminology

* Androblastoma (obsolete)

ICD coding

* ICD-O:
  + [8640/1](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=78) - Sertoli cell tumor
  + [8640/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=78) - malignant Sertoli cell tumor

Epidemiology

* < 1% of testicular tumors
* Rare before age 20; mean age 45 years

Etiology

* Risk factors: cryptorchidism, familial adenomatous polyposis ([Virchows Arch 2012;461:713](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23090627))
* No known environmental factors

Clinical features

* Slowly enlarging unilateral testicular mass
* Testicular discomfort, pain or incidental finding
* Hormonal manifestations very rare; more common in malignant tumors
* Exceptionally, metastases are initial manifestation
* References: [Histopathology 2017;70:513](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27801954), [Ulbright: Tumors of the Testis and Adjacent Structures (AFIP Atlas of Tumor Pathology, Series 4), 1st Edition, 2014](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1933477210/pathologyoutl-20), [Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1390](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26034868)

Diagnosis

* For suspected testicular mass, the first test is scrotal ultrasound (US) (sensitivity 95%, specificity 97%)
* If US confirms a lesion, the patient should be immediately referred to a urologist for evaluation and management
* Men 15 to 44 years old with retroperitoneal mass / metastases should undergo testicular examination and scrotal US
* Evaluate serum markers
* Biopsy of testicular tumors is in general not performed, to prevent tumor seeding
* Staging:
  + In clinically stable patients, imaging may be performed before or after orchiectomy
  + Imaging: computed tomography (CT) scan of the abdomen / pelvis with intravenous contrast, chest radiograph (CXR)
  + Noncontrast chest CT should be performed if CXR is abnormal
* Reference: [Med Clin North Am 2018;102:251](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29406056)

Laboratory

* Serum tumor markers:
  + Beta human chorionic gonadotropin (hCG), alpha fetoprotein (AFP) should be normal and are used to exclude nonseminomatous germ cell tumors
  + Lactate dehydrogenase (LDH) to assess global tumor burden

Radiology description

* Ultrasound: solitary hypoechoic lesion; not helpful in distinguishing from germ cell tumors

Prognostic factors

* Vast majority are benign, ~ 5% cases are malignant
* Tumors with sclerosis > 50% almost invariably benign ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:510](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24552667))
* Features associated with malignancy: extratesticular spread, size > 5 cm, lymphovascular invasion, high grade cytological atypia, tumor necrosis, mitotic index > 5/10 high power fields ([Am J Surg Pathol 1998;22:709](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9630178))
* Very rarely tumors without malignant histology metastasize

Case reports

* 23 year old man with small, solid, well demarcated intraparenchymal tumor ([Case #18](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case200518.htm))
* 23 year old man with sclerosing Sertoli cell tumor ([Rev Urol 2014;16:191](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25548547))

* 13 examples of malignant Sertoli cell tumors ([Am J Surg Pathol 2002;26:541](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11979085))

Treatment

* Orchiectomy (total or partial) is treatment of choice
* Retroperitoneal lymph node dissection for malignant Sertoli cell tumors
* No specific therapy; poor response to chemotherapy or radiotherapy
* Reference: [Med Clin North Am 2018;102:251](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29406056)

Gross description

* Cut surface usually homogenous white or yellowish
* Cystic component present in ~ 30% of cases ([Am J Surg Pathol 1998;22:709](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9630178))
* Benign: well circumscribed, most between 2 - 5 cm ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:510](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24552667))
* Malignant: > 5 cm, poor circumscription, extratesticular extension, necrosis, hemorrhage

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Architecture: tubular (lumen), cords (no lumen), tubulopapillary present at least focally
* Other patterns: macro or microcystic, nested, trabecular, whorled, solid, retiform, pseudopapillary; most tumors show mixed patterns
* Nuclei: bland, uniform, round to ovoid in benign tumors; small hyperchromatic or large with prominent nucleoli in malignant tumors
* Cytoplasm: usually clear and abundant but extremely variable, ranging from scant, foamy, eosinophilic to markedly lipidized; hyaline globules are common
* Stroma: variable, basement membrane-like material around tubules, sclerotic, myxoid, edematous, angiomatous
* If sclerosis > 50% → sclerotic variant (now considered subtype of Sertoli cell tumor, NOS)
* Inflammatory cells usually absent, rarely prominent
* References: [Histopathology 2017;70:513](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27801954), [Ulbright: Tumors of the Testis and Adjacent Structures (AFIP Atlas of Tumor Pathology, Series 4), 1st Edition, 2014](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1933477210/pathologyoutl-20), [Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1390](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26034868)

Cytology description

* Cytologic samples are from metastatic (malignant) Sertoli cell tumors
* Aspirates show branching tubules, microacinar and papillary groups
* Numerous individual cells are present in the background
* Cells are small, ovoid to plasmacytoid, relatively uniform
* Nuclei show smooth cell membranes and granular chromatin pattern
* Malignancy evident by nuclear overlap, discordant large nuclei and mitotic activity
* Aspirates resemble neuroendocrine tumors or well differentiated adenocarcinomas
* References: [Histopathology 2017;70:513](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27801954), [Ulbright: Tumors of the Testis and Adjacent Structures (AFIP Atlas of Tumor Pathology, Series 4), 1st Edition, 2014](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1933477210/pathologyoutl-20), [Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1390](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26034868)

Electron microscopy description

* Prominent Golgi complex, variable smooth endoplasmic reticulum, lipid droplets, glycogen
* Cells interconnected by desmosomes, peripheral basement membrane
* References: [Histopathology 2017;70:513](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27801954), [Ulbright: Tumors of the Testis and Adjacent Structures (AFIP Atlas of Tumor Pathology, Series 4), 1st Edition, 2014](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1933477210/pathologyoutl-20), [Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1390](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26034868)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* *CTNNB1* gene mutations ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:66](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24061522%20))
* Negative for isochromosome 12p
* Cytogenetics: gains of X chromosome (~ 40% cases), loss of chromosomes 2 and 19 (less common) ([Virchows Arch 2007;450:425](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17333264))

Sample pathology report

* Left testis, orchiectomy:
  + Sertoli cell tumor, not otherwise specified (see comment and synoptic report)
  + Comment: The morphologic features are diagnostic of Sertoli cell tumor, not otherwise specified. No features of malignancy are identified in this tumor (i.e. extratesticular spread, size > 5 cm, high grade cytologic atypia, > 5 mitotic figures per 10 high power fields, necrosis or lymphovascular invasion).

Differential diagnosis

* [Rete testis adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisreteadenomatoushyper.html):
  + Located at the rete testis
  + Strong diffuse positivity for [PAX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax2.html), [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html) and [pankeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html)
* [Adenomatoid tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisepidadenomatoid.html):
  + Paratesticular location
  + Uniformly positive mesothelial markers: [pankeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html), [CK5/6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5and6.html), [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html)
  + Negative for [SF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssf1.html) or nuclear [beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html)
* [Leydig cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisleydig.html):
  + Abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm, absent tubules / cords
  + Consistent strong expression of [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html), [inhibin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html), [SF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssf1.html)
  + Negative [beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1390](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26034868))
* [Yolk sac tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisyst.html):
  + Positive for [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html), [glypican 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglypican3.html), [alpha fetoprotein](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsafp.html), [pankeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html)
  + Negative for [OCT4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct34.html), [SF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssf1.html), nuclear [beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html), [inhibin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html), [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html)
* [Seminoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisseminomas.html):
  + Positive for [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html), [OCT4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsoct34.html), [KIT](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html), [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html), [PLAP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsplap.html)
  + Negative for [SF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssf1.html), nuclear [beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html), [inhibin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html), [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html)
* [Juvenile granulosa cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisgranulosajuvenile.html):
  + Most common in infants; 90% occur before age 10
  + Follicles with [mucicarmine](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmucins.html)+ basophilic or eosinophilic secretions
  + Variable solid pattern, frequent brisk mitotic / apoptotic activity
  + Consistent expression of [inhibin A](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html), [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html), [FOXL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfoxl2.html)
* [Large cell calcifying Sertoli cell tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testissertolilargecell.html):
  + Large epithelioid cells, lamellated calcifications
  + Associated with Carney complex
* [Sertoli cell nodule (Pick adenoma)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testissertolihyperplasia.html):
  + Small size, multifocal, monotonous tubular / cord architecture, associated with androgen insensitivity syndrome and cryptorchidism
