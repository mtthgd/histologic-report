Definition / general

* Testicular germ cell tumor with an area of pure somatic type malignancy that arises from germ cell tumor progenitor cells and occupies ≥ 0.5 cm (approximately ≥ 1 microscopic field using 4x objective)

Essential features

* Identified within primary testicular tumor or at a metastatic site
* Rhabdomyosarcoma, adenocarcinoma, embryonic type neuroectodermal tumor and sarcoma, NOS are the most frequent histologies ([Histopathology 2022;81:84](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35438203), [Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:11](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34334690))
* Varying morphologies, including neuroglial, neuroendocrine carcinoma and neuroblastoma, may make diagnosis difficult ([Histopathology 2022;81:84](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35438203))
* Must be distinguished from sarcomatoid or glandular yolk sac tumor, which represent up to 60% of postchemotherapy sarcomatous tumors ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:251](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25229769))

Terminology

* Current terminology: teratoma with somatic type malignancy
* Previous terminology: teratoma with malignant transformation, teratoma with somatic malignancy
* Currently recommended that embryonic type neuroectodermal tumor should replace primitive neuroectodermal tumor (PNET) when referring to a teratoma with this type of somatic malignancy because although it resembles CNS PNET, it lacks *EWSR1* rearrangement ([Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:1299](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34232605))

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [9084/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=85) - teratoma with malignant transformation
* ICD-11: [2C80.Y & XH33E8](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f673566475%2fmms%2fother?data=%7B%22dataType%22%3A%22pc%22%2C%22postcoordinationCodeSet%22%3A%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F673566475%2Fmms%2Fother%22%2C%22axisToValueIds%22%3A%7B%22histopathology%22%3A%5B%7B%22stemId%22%3A%22http%3A%2F%2Fid.who.int%2Ficd%2Fentity%2F1507806648%22%7D%5D%7D%7D%7D) - other specified malignant neoplasms of testis & teratoma with malignant transformation

Epidemiology

* 3 - 6% of testicular germ cell tumors ([Cancer 1985;56:860](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2990657), [Cancer 1984;54:1824](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6090001), [J Urol 1994;152:1144](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8072083), [Histopathology 2022;81:84](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35438203))
* Median age of diagnosis within testis: 26 years (range: 17 - 55) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:11](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34334690))
* Median age of diagnosis at metastatic site: 39 years (range: 23 - 62) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:11](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34334690))

Sites

* Testis
* Metastatic locations (e.g., retroperitoneum, lungs)

Pathophysiology

* 3 proposed origins of somatic type malignancy ([Eur Urol 2007;51:1306](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17081678)):
  + Mature teratoma
  + Embryonal carcinoma
  + Yolk sac tumor ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:251](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25229769), [Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:463](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33136584))

Clinical features

* More commonly found as metastasis, rather than at initial diagnosis confined to the testis
* Most frequently identified after chemotherapy ([J Urol 2014;192:1403](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24952240), [Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:11](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34334690))

Diagnosis

* Found as metastatic disease, within the testis or both ([J Urol 1998;159:133](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9400455))
* Histologic features and immunohistochemistry determine type of somatic malignancy

Radiology description

* Imaging may suggest specific somatic malignancy in germ cell tumors ([Eur J Radiol 2009;69:230](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19056194))
  + Ossified lymph nodes and soft tissue reported in osteosarcoma
  + Peritoneal carcinoma seen in adenocarcinoma
  + Septal thickening and opacifications reported in a case of bronchoalveolar carcinoma

Prognostic factors

* If confined to the testis without synchronous metastasis, somatic malignancy does not appear to confer a worse prognosis [Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:11](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34334690))
* Presence in a metastatic site is associated with poor survival (median survival: 44 months) [Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:11](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34334690))
* Median time from initial diagnosis of testicular germ cell tumor and diagnosis of somatic malignancy at metastatic site: 70 months (range: 0 - 384 months) [Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:11](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34334690))

Case reports

* 32 year old man with angiosarcoma ([Int J Surg Pathol 2022;30:462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34955058))
* 37 year old man with neuroendocrine carcinoma ([Case Rep Oncol 2022;15:191](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35431867))
* 40 year old man with rhabdomyosarcoma ([Urol Case Rep 2020;33:101385](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33102083))
* 50 year old man with sarcoma, NOS ([Front Oncol 2021;11:633543](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33796464))

Treatment

* Surgical resection is the mainstay of treatment
* Chemotherapy is based on histologic subtype of malignant transformation ([J Clin Oncol 2003;21:4285](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14645417))
* Radical surgery is often utilized for advanced disease ([J Urol 1998;159:133](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9400455))

Gross description

* No gross findings have been reported in large cases series ([Histopathology 2022;81:84](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35438203), [Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:11](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34334690))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Expansile and infiltrative pure area of atypical mesenchymal or epithelial cells that occupies ≥ 0.5 cm
* 4 most common histologies ([Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:11](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34334690), [Histopathology 2022;81:84](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35438203)):
  + Rhabdomyosarcoma
  + Adenocarcinoma
  + Embryonic type neuroectodermal tumor
  + Sarcoma, NOS
* Embryonic type neuroectodermal tumor appears as immature neuroectodermal elements
* Neuroglial tumors have CNS-like morphology similar to astrocytoma, glioma, glioblastoma and gliosarcoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:422](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30557172))
* Nephroblastoma-like histology has been described

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* 12p gain or i(12p) is usually present ([Histopathology 2022;81:84](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35438203), [Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:1849](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23154771))
* Clonal relationship to the associated teratoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:1849](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23154771))

Sample pathology report

* Right testis, radial orchiectomy:
  + Mixed germ cell tumor, embryonal carcinoma (75%), yolk sac tumor (10%), teratoma (10%), teratoma with somatic malignancy composed of embryonic type neuroectodermal tumor (5%) (see synoptic report)
* Left lung, wedge resection:
  + Metastatic rhabdomyosarcoma, 3.2 cm (see comment)
  + Comment: The patient has a history of a mixed testicular germ cell tumor. The current tumor is consistent with testicular origin (teratoma with somatic malignancy).

Differential diagnosis

* **Metastasis of non-germ cell tumor (primary sarcoma or carcinoma)**:
  + History of primary sarcoma or carcinoma
  + Both primary sarcoma / carcinoma and somatic malignancies of sarcoma / carcinoma are usually [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html) negative so this cannot be used to discriminate
  + Lack 12p abnormality
* [Teratoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisteratoma.html) without somatic type malignancy:
  + Malignant appearing somatic cells occupy
  + [Yolk sac tumor (glandular or sarcomatoid)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisyst.html):
    - Mimics adenocarcinoma and sarcoma
    - Should be considered before a postchemotherapy diagnosis of sarcoma as the somatic type is rendered ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:251](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25229769))
    - Sarcomatoid yolk sac tumor: [GPC3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglypican3.html) (100%), [AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html) (100%), [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html) (60%), [AFP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsafp.html) (0%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:1396](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24921638))
    - Glandular yolk sac tumor: [GPC3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsglypican3.html) (86%), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) (71%), [SALL4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssall4.html) (71%), [AFP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsafp.html) 71%, [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html) (14%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:1396](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24921638))
  + [Choriocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testischorio.html):
    - Cytotrophoblasts mimic squamous cell carcinoma
    - [βhCG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshcg.html) positive
  + [Embryonal tumor with multilayered rosettes, *C19MC* altered](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cnstumorETMR.html):
    - Mimics embryonic type neuroectodermal tumor
    - *C19MC* amplification
    - Lack 12p abnormality
  + [Ewing sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/boneewing.html)
    - Mimics undifferentiated areas of embryonic type neuroectodermal tumor
    - [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html) membranous; [FLI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsfli1.html) and [NKX2.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsNKX2.html) nuclear positive
    - *EWSR1* fusion
    - Lack 12p abnormality
