Definition / general

* Mass of tortuous and dilated veins of pampiniform plexus and internal testicular vein of spermatic cord
* Occurs posterior and superior to the testis, may extend into the inguinal ring

Essential features

* Probably due to incompetent valves of left internal spermatic vein as it empties into renal vein
* 90% on left
* 10% bilateral
* Right internal spermatic vein is less often involved since it empties directly into the inferior vena cava and tends not to have incompetent valves

Epidemiology

* Affects 15% of adults ([Ultrastruct Pathol 2010;34:260](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20929307)) and 13% ([Fertil Steril 2017;107:74](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27793385)) to 40% ([J Assist Reprod Genet 2017;34:839](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28523408)) of men seeking fertility treatment
* Only 20% of men with a varicocele will have lowered fertility ([Asian J Androl 2016;18:276](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26698233))
* Associated with decreased sperm count and motility ([J Assist Reprod Genet 2017;34:839](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28523408))
* Testicular atrophy is the main indication for surgical repair

Sites

* Right sided varicocele is unusual; isolated right sided varicocele is associated with situs inversus, venous thrombosis or inferior vena cava compression from space occupying lesion such as soft tissue sarcoma; less commonly paraganglioma ([Ann Saudi Med 2016;36:148](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26997533))
* It suggests the presence of a vascular abnormality ([Urology 2015;85:e39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25799175))

Etiology

* Most cases are idiopathic
* Renal tumor may invade the left renal vein and block the drainage of the spermatic vein
* Possible association with maternal exposure to diethylstilbestrol ([Am J Obstet Gynecol 1981;140:186](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7234914))
* Rare association with sertoliform cystadenoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:141](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29240582))
* Kidney donation usually involves a left kidney; a study showed left pampiniform venous plexus diameter increased after kidney donation in men, suggesting that this can predispose to varicocele ([Transplant Proc 2009;41:2738](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19765422))
* Can be caused by May-Thurner syndrome, which is reflux in the left spermatic vein caused by iliac vein compression between the right iliac artery and the spine [[Ann Vasc Surg 2017;42:305](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28258018))
* Certain genetic and epigenetic changes are associated with varicocele ([J Assist Reprod Genet 2017;34:839](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28523408))

Clinical features

* Often associated with infertility; after treatment, 40 - 55% recover fertility
* Testicular pain may be associated with sexual activity
* If longstanding, testicular atrophy and infertility of the affected testis may result

Diagnosis

* Based on palpation while patient is at rest compared to during Valsalva
* Graded based on physical exam findings:
  + **Grade 1**: palpable only during Valsalva
  + **Grade 2**: palpable at rest but not visible
  + **Grade 3**: visible and palpable at rest
* Ultrasound if physical exam is inconclusive ([J Ultrason 2016;16:359](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28138407))

Laboratory

* May decrease Leydig cell function, causing a lower serum testosterone, which can be improved after varicocelectomy ([Urology 2013;81:1213](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23561709))

Radiology description

* Ultrasound can demonstrate vessel size, reversal of blood flow and testicular volume

Case reports

* 64 year old Asian man with varicocele due to renal arteriovenous malformation ([J Med Case Rep 2018;12:2](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29304872))
* 68 year old man with thrombosed varicocele ([BMC Urol 2018;18:34](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29739377))
* Fighter pilot with an asymptomatic varicocele precipitated by centrifuge training ([Aerosp Med Hum Perform 2015;86:1063](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26630055))

Treatment

* Varicocelectomy
* Some patients have improved fertility after microsurgery, perhaps because DNA fragmentation is reduced / DNA integrity is improved ([Syst Biol Reprod Med 2012;58:274](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22979920))
* Ligation or occlusion of left spermatic vein at the internal inguinal ring
* Motile sperm were found in 11 of 19 patients after varicocele repair; testicular histology was also improved ([Biomed Res Int. 2015;2015:709452](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26601110))

Gross description

* Thin walled cystically dilated membrane

Microscopic (histologic) description

* From inside to outside, changes include ([Ultrastruct Pathol 2010;34:260](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20929307))
  + Narrowing or obliteration of the vein lumens
  + Segmental obliteration and occasional thrombi
  + Internal elastic lamina fragmentation and invagination of intima
  + Variable thickening of vein wall; the media contains hypertrophied smooth muscle fibers and deposition of collagen bundles ([Ultrastruct Pathol 2012;36:201](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22849521))
* If a testis is included: decreased spermatogenesis in tubules with germ cell degeneration and increased Leydig cells
* Adolescent varicocele: pathologic changes found at or soon after puberty, consisting of tubular sclerosis, premature germ cell sloughing, small vessel sclerosis and variable hypospermatogenesis ([Am J Clin Pathol 1988;89:321](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3348167))

Positive stains

* [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html) (stains endothelial layer)

Negative stains

* [Calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html)

Electron microscopy description

* Endothelial cell damage and loss of internal elastic lamina ([Ultrastruct Pathol 2010;34:260](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20929307))

Differential diagnosis

* [Hydrocele](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testishydrocele.html) is lined by tunica vaginalis mesothelium (calretinin+, CD31-) whereas varicocele is lined by endothelium (CD31+, calretinin-)

Board review style question #1

Which of the following is not associated with varicocele?

1. Destruction of vessel's external elastic lamina
2. Endothelial cell damage
3. Narrowing or obliteration of the lumen of the vein
4. Thickening of the media by collagen deposition

Board review style answer #1

**A.** Destruction of vessel's external elastic lamina. It is the internal elastic lamina that is destroyed
