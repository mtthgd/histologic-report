Definition / general

* Well differentiated papillary mesothelial tumor is a neoplasm of uncertain malignant potential that originates from the mesothelium of the pleura, peritoneum or tunica vaginalis

Essential features

* Mesothelial tumor without significant cytologic atypia ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2019;38:43](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30419426), [Ann Surg Oncol 2019;26:852](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30635798))
* Composed entirely of papillary structures and tubules lined by a single layer of bland cuboidal cells with no identifiable invasion
* Positive staining for mesothelial markers (calretinin, WT1)
* BAP1 and MTAP immunohistochemical staining should be retained

Terminology

* Well differentiated papillary mesothelioma ([Eur Urol 2022;82:458](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35853783))

Epidemiology

* Tumors arising from tunica vaginalis are rare and comprise Mod Pathol 2022;35:1327)
* Age range is 18 - 70 years

Sites

* Peritoneum
* Tunica vaginalis
* Pleura
* Pericardium
* References: [Mod Pathol 2022;35:1327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35440764)

Pathophysiology

* Unknown

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Pleural cases are often symptomatic and associated with a pleural effusion ([Mod Pathol 2022;35:1327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35440764))
* Peritoneal cases are typically identified incidentally during surgery ([Mod Pathol 2022;35:1327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35440764))
* Cases involving the tunica vaginalis are associated with hydrocele and testicular pain ([Mod Pathol 2022;35:1327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35440764), [Clin Genitourin Cancer 2016;14:e435](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27067374), [Can Urol Assoc J 2018;12:E425](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29787369))

Diagnosis

* Histologic diagnosis is required

Radiology description

* No specific imaging findings ([Radiographics 2023;43:e220128](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36757881), [Adv Anat Pathol 2023;30:280](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36395181))
* There may be no imaging abnormalities ([Radiographics 2023;43:e220128](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36757881))
* Sometimes there may be small peritoneal nodules, a single nodule or scattered areas of peritoneal thickening ([Radiographics 2023;43:e220128](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36757881))
* Calcifications may be present ([Radiographics 2023;43:e220128](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36757881))

Prognostic factors

* This tumor is likely benign in nature but is often morphologically identical to mesothelioma in situ, which may explain the rare reports of recurrence as mesothelioma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2023;47:611](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36876759))

Case reports

* 18 year old man with hydrocele ([J Clin Pathol 1992;45:1029](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1452778))
* 35 year old man with bilateral hydroceles ([Urol Oncol 2006;24:36](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16414491))
* 47 year old man with a testicular tumor found after an episode of acute epididymitis ([Int J Surg Pathol 2023;31:1126](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36624609))
* 48 year old man without asbestos exposure ([Korean J Pathol 2014;48:225](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25013421))
* 55 year old man with chronic testicular pain, epididymitis and a hydrocele ([Int J Surg Pathol 2023;31:1126](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36624609))

Treatment

* Complete excision

Gross description

* Peritoneal surfaces with small nodules or papillary excrescences, single to innumerable ([Mod Pathol 2022;35:1327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35440764))

Frozen section description

* These tumors are often found incidentally during other procedures, prompting frozen section evaluation
* Expanded papillary structures with edema or myxoid change, lined by a single layer of bland mesothelial cells
* Invasion should be excluded
* Reference: [Int J Surg Pathol 2021;29:844](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33978505%20)

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Papillary structures with expansile cores showing myxoid features
* Papillary structures are lined by a bland simple layer of flattened to cuboidal cells
* Fibrovascular cores generally lack inflammation and psammoma bodies
* Occasional gland-like or tubulocystic areas may be seen
* There should be a lack of stromal invasion, significant atypia and mitotic activity ([Mod Pathol 2022;35:1327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35440764))

Differential diagnosis

* [Mesothelioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testismesotheliomamalignant.html):
  + Cytologic atypia, mitoses and invasion
  + Loss of [BAP1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsBAP1.html)
* [Mesothelioma in situ](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/pleuramesothelioma.html):
  + Cytologic atypia and mitoses
  + Loss of [BAP1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsBAP1.html)
  + Invasion will not be present
* **Serous cystadenoma**:
  + May have similar morphologic features
  + Negative for [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html), [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html)
* [Serous borderline tumors](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisserousborder.html):
  + Architectural complexity of papillae or epithelial proliferation
  + Negative for [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html), [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html)
* [Low grade serous cystadenocarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisserouspapca.html):
  + Invasion is present
  + Negative for [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html), [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html)
* [Adenomatoid tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testisepidadenomatoid.html):
  + Tubules, cysts and cords without papillae
  + Infiltrative growth is common
  + Also mesothelial in origin; both express [CK5/6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5and6.html), [WT1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainswt1.html), [calretinin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalretinin.html), [D2-40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsD240.html)

The following tumor is incidentally found on the peritoneum during a cholecystectomy. This tumor should show which immunophenotype?

1. BAP1 loss, calretinin+, WT1+
2. BAP1 retained, MTAP retained, calretinin+, WT1+
3. Cytokeratin 5/6-, D2-40+
4. p53 null, AE1 / AE3+, PAX8+

Board review style answer #1

**B**. BAP1 retained, MTAP retained, calretinin+, WT1+. These images show a well differentiated mesothelial tumor, which should have retained BAP1 and MTAP. Mesothelial markers will be positive.
Answer A is incorrect because while a well differentiated mesothelial tumor will express mesothelial markers, BAP1 should be retained.
Answer C is incorrect because mesothelial tumors will express both CK5/6 and D2-40.
Answer D is incorrect because this immunophenotype is more in keeping with an ovarian type serous tumor.

A patient presents with scrotal swelling that has been persistent over the last few years. An orchiectomy is done. Which of the following immunophenotypes would favor a mesothelioma over a well differentiated papillary mesothelial tumor?

1. BAP1 loss, calretinin+, WT1+
2. BAP1 retained, MTAP retained, calretinin+, WT1+
3. Cytokeratin 5/6-, D2-40+
4. p53 null, AE1 / AE3+, PAX8+

Board review style answer #2

**A**. BAP1 loss, calretinin+, WT1+. These images show a tumor with significant cytologic atypia and invasion into the testis. This is a mesothelioma of the tunica vaginalis. Mesotheliomas will express mesothelial markers and have BAP1 loss.
Answer B is incorrect because this immunophenotype is in keeping with a benign mesothelial lesion.
Answer C is incorrect because mesothelial tumors will express both CK5/6 and D2-40.
Answer D is incorrect because this immunophenotype is more in keeping with an ovarian type serous tumor.
