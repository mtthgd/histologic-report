Definition / general

* Nonneoplastic thyroid disorders with antithyroid antibodies and associated with specific HLA haplotypes
* #1 cause of hypothyroidism in iodine sufficient areas
* Includes Graves' disease, Hashimoto's thyroiditis and most forms of thyroiditis in this section except infectious
* Rosai differentiates based on follicular epithelium: lymphocytic thyroiditis if relatively normal follicles, Hashimoto's thyroiditis if oncocytic change, Graves' disease if diffusely hyperplastic follicles

Epidemiology

* Children often present with asymptomatic goiter of short duration ([J Pediatr Endocrinol Metab 2007;20:961](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18038704), [Arch Dis Child 2009;94:33](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18703546))
* Associated with female children of bipolar parents ([Eur Neuropsychopharmacol 2007;17:394](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17140771)), radioactive iodine ([Epidemiology 2006;17:604](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17028502), but see [JAMA 2006;295:1011](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16507802)), iodine supplementation ([Hormones (Athens) 2007;6:25](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17324915)), history of pregnancy ([Autoimmunity 2008;41:174](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18324487))

Clinical features

* May be due to disturbance in suppressor T cells
