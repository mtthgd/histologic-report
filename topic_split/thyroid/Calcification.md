Definition / general

* Deposition of calcium salts in thyroid gland
* More important to radiologists than pathologists

Epidemiology

* Prevalence in thyroidectomy specimens is 15% ([Head Neck 2002;24:651](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12112538))
* Found by ultrasound in 8% of benign (multinodular goiter) and 26% of malignant nodules ([Head Neck 2002;24:651](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12112538))
* Increases with advancing age

Sites

* Retrosternal thyroid / goiter tends to be calcified more heavily

Pathophysiology

* Dystrophic calcification of thyroid results from degenerative changes (calcified colloid and degenerated epithelium, psammoma bodies, old hemorrhage, vessel wall, etc.)
* Metastatic calcification is caused by elevated blood calcium / phosphate
* Stromal calcification may progress to bone formation ([Mod Pathol 2009;22:887](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19305382))

Diagnosis

* Core needle biopsy is superior to FNA for thyroid nodules with macrocalcification ([Thyroid 2015;25:657](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25851539))

Laboratory

* Hypercalcemia due to hyperparathyroidism, in rare cases of metastatic calcification

Radiology description

* Microcalcifications (< 2 mm and without acoustic shadow by ultrasound) in thyroid nodules are usually psammoma bodies
* Macrocalcifications (≥ 2 mm and with acoustic shadow) are secondary to tumor necrosis and can be seen in both benign and malignant nodules
* Peripheral (eggshell) calcifications surrounding the nodule are secondary to chronic degenerative changes
* Various patterns of calcification may be observed on Xray: nodular, flat, curvilinear, cloudy and a mixed type ([Clin Radiol 1981;32:571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7273652))

Prognostic factors

* Microcalcification due to psammoma bodies is a strong predictor of thyroid carcinoma ([Head Neck 2002;24:651](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12112538), [J Int Med Res 2012;40:350](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22429375)), but the association of macrocalcifications with malignancy is controversial ([Thyroid 2013;23:1106](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23311668))

Case reports

* 41, 49 and 72 year old women with osseous metaplasia and mature bone formation ([Oncol Lett 2013;6:977](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24137448))
* 49 year old woman with long standing goiter ([Minerva Endocrinol 2000;25:81](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11338400))
* 67 year old man with eggshell calcification ([Cases J 2008;1:11](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18577251))
* 74 year old man with metastatic calcification ([J Nucl Med 1986;27:373](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3712056))
* Unusual calcification in mixed papillary and follicular carcinoma ([Radiology 1976;119:554](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/935387))
* Association with hypothyroidism ([Clin Radiol 1992;45:209](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1555379))

Differential diagnosis

* Psammoma bodies are highly specific for [papillary thyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidpapillary.html), but should be differentiated from dystrophic stromal calcifications and inspissated colloid, both of which lack concentric laminations
* [Thyroid calcium oxalate crystals](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidcrystals.html)
