Definition / general

* Unusual morphologic subtype of papillary thyroid carcinoma (PTC) with retained papillary nuclear features and clear cytoplasm
* Clear cell features are likely due to the mitochondrial expansion, abundant glycogen, lipid or thyroglobulin in the tumor cells ([Am J Surg Pathol 1985;9:705](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4061729))

Essential features

* Rare papillary thyroid carcinoma subtype which comprises < 2% of all papillary thyroid carcinomas ([Thyroid 2017;27:819](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28293986))
* Not associated with a worse prognosis
* Needs to be distinguished from clear cell metastatic tumors to the thyroid

Terminology

* Papillary thyroid carcinoma, clear cell variant

Epidemiology

* Clear cell features are seen in < 2% of papillary thyroid carcinoma subtypes ([Thyroid 2017;27:819](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28293986))
* M = F

Sites

* Thyroid

Etiology

* May be related to thyroid stimulating hormone (TSH) overstimulation ([Thyroid 2017;27:819](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28293986))

Clinical features

* Neck mass
* May have obstructive symptoms

Diagnosis

* Neck ultrasound
* Fine needle aspiration
* Hemithyroidectomy versus complete thyroidectomy

Prognostic factors

* Similar to conventional / [classic papillary thyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidpapillaryclassic.html)
* Angiolymphatic invasion, extrathyroidal extension and lymph node metastasis are associated with a worse outcome ([Thyroid 2017;27:819](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28293986))
* Rare case associated with [anaplastic thyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidundiff.html) ([Int J Surg Pathol 2019;27:658](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30931661))

Case reports

* 49 year old woman with euthyroid status after total thyroidectomy due to functioning lung metastases from a clear cell variant of papillary thyroid carcinoma ([Thyroid 2012;22:1084](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22873179))
* 64 year old woman diagnosed with clear cell variant of papillary thyroid carcinoma ([J Med Cases 2019;10:113](https://doi.org/10.14740/jmc3278))
* 79 year old woman with clear cell variant of papillary thyroid carcinoma with associated anaplastic thyroid carcinoma ([Int J Surg Pathol 2019;27:658](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30931661))

Treatment

* Similar to conventional / [classic papillary thyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidpapillaryclassic.html) ([Thyroid 2017;27:819](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28293986))
* Surgical resection
* Lymph node dissection may be indicated
* Radioactive iodine may be used on case to case basis

Gross description

* Solitary or multifocal masses with average size of 1.9 cm (range: 0.2 - 5.5 cm) ([Thyroid 2017;27:819](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28293986))
* Solid in consistency
* Ranges from pale to white in color
* Contour may be well defined to infiltrative

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Architecture may be follicular, solid or papillary
* Tumor cell cytoplasm may be entirely clear to somewhat granular
* Nuclear size and shape range from largely round to irregular
* Classic nuclear features of papillary thyroid carcinoma: enlarged overlapped nuclei, chromatin clearing, irregular contours, grooves and intranuclear cytoplasmic pseudoinclusions are frequently observed throughout the tumor
* Tumor proportion with clear cell features may vary and can be only focal
* Psammoma bodies may be seen
* Necrosis, increased mitotic activity and cytological pleomorphism are worrisome features of dedifferentiation ([Int J Surg Pathol 2019;27:658](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30931661))

Cytology description

* Cells show cytoplasmic clearing ([Clin Nucl Med 2015;40:885](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26018685))
* Range from watery clear to granular in appearance
* Cells are cuboidal to columnar in shape
* Typical nuclear features of papillary thyroid carcinoma are present but may only be focal

Positive stains

* [Thyroglobulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsthyroglobulin.html) ([Int J Surg Pathol 2019;27:658](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30931661))
* [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html) ([Int J Surg Pathol 2019;27:658](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30931661))

Electron microscopy description

* Reveals dilated empty mitochondria, which can explain the clear cell feature
* Characteristic findings of conventional papillary thyroid carcinoma were observed as well ([Am J Surg Pathol 1980;4:501](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7435777))
  + Microvilli
  + Infolded nuclei
  + Nuclear bodies

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Few described molecular alterations ([Thyroid 2017;27:819](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28293986)):
  + *RAS* mutation
  + *PAX8::PPARy* translocation
  + *EML4::ALK* translocation
  + *TFG::MET* translocation

Sample pathology report

* Thyroid, total thyroidectomy:
  + Papillary thyroid carcinoma, clear cell subtype, 1.5 cm, isthmus (see synoptic report)
  + Negative for lymphatic invasion and extrathyroidal extension; resection margin negative

Differential diagnosis

* [Follicular thyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidfollicular.html) with clear cell features:
  + Does not have papillary thyroid carcinoma nuclear features
  + Usually [HBME1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshbme.html) negative
* Clear cell [medullary carcinoma of the thyroid](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidmedullary.html):
  + Has C cell differentiation
    - Positive for [calcitonin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalcitonin.html), [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html) and [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html) but negative for [HBME1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshbme.html)
  + Stroma may have amyloid deposits
* [Intrathyroidal parathyroid tumors](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidparathyroidtissue.html) ([Surg Pathol Clin 2019;12:1007](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31672291)):
  + Most are composed of round, finely granular chief cells and occasionally oxyphilic / oncocytic cells
  + Has [PTH](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/PTH.html) immunoreactivity
  + Does not express [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsTTF1.html) and [thyroglobulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsthyroglobulin.html)
* [Metastatic clear cell renal cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneytumormalignantrccclear.html):
  + Does not have papillary thyroid carcinoma nuclear features
  + [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html), [CAIX](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscarbonicanhydraseix.html) and [RCC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsrcc.html) positive
