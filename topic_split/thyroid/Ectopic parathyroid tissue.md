Definition / general

* Estimated incidence of 35%; due to aberrant migration during early stages of development
* Common etiology of persistent or recurrent hyperparathyroidism when missed at initial diagnosis
  ([Exp Clin Endocrinol Diabetes 2012;120:604](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23174995))
* Often symptomatic due to hyperplasia associated with secondary hyperparathyroidism
* Often symmetrical from side to side, even when ectopic, making localization somewhat easier
  ([eMedicine: Embryology of the Thyroid and Parathyroids [Accessed 24 July 2017]](http://emedicine.medscape.com/article/845125-overview))
* Can undergo adenomatous change and cause primary hyperparathyroidism, hypercalcemia and acute pancreatitis
  ([World J Surg Oncol 2004;2:41](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15571624))
* Inability to identify may lead to failure of parathyroid surgery

Sites

* Axilla ([Int Surg 2004;89:6](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15085990))
* Mediastinum ([Ann Thorac Surg 1997;64:238](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9236371))
* Pyriform sinus ([Arch Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 2002;128:71](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11784259))
* Thyroid gland ([Nihon Rinsho 1995;53:920](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7752485))
* Vagus nerves of children ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1988;112:304](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3345129))
* Ectopic inferior parathyroids are most frequently found in anterior mediastinum, in association with thymus or thyroid gland; ectopic superior parathyroids are usually at tracheoesophageal groove or retroesophageal region ([Exp Clin Endocrinol Diabetes 2012;120:604](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23174995))
