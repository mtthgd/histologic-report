Definition / general

* Papillary carcinoma totally surrounded by a capsule
* 8 - 13% of papillary carcinomas

Prognostic factors

* Excellent prognosis ([Eur J Surg 1996;162:177](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8695730), [Cancer 1984;54:90](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6722747))
* May have nodal metastases, but only rarely angiolymphatic invasion or distant metastases

Case reports

* 22 year old woman with encapsulated macrofollicular variant ([Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2006;49:24](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16625968))

Gross description

* Thick capsule, resembles follicular adenoma

Cytology description

* Scattered clusters of follicular cells with focal Hürthle cell changes and prominent nuclear pleomorphism, mixed with colloid and normal appearing follicular cells
* May not show classic papillary nuclear features ([Acta Cytol 2002;46:555](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12040653))

Differential diagnosis

* [Hyperplastic nodule with central cystic degeneration](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidpapillaryhypernod.html): papillary formations face lumen of cystically dilated cavity, "hot" on scan, pale and vacuolated colloid, no papillary nuclear features, negative for high molecular weight keratin
