Definition / general

* Click [here](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastfrozenprocedure.html) for the frozen section procedure topic
* Communication with surgeon important
* Weigh, measure and freeze largest gland
* Algorithm: presumed adenoma if rim of normal tissue, diffuse chief cell growth, no fat, bizarre nuclei; if these criteria not met, examine at least one more gland
* Oil Red O fat stain may be helpful if little fat in suspect gland and abundant fat in normal appearing gland
  ([Am J Surg Pathol 1981;5:381](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6168205),
  [Hum Pathol 1985;16:1255](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2415441))
* Density gradient measurements: denser tissue sinks in 25% mannitol solution, implies more parenchymal cells / abnormal glands
* Parathyroid tissue usually lacks birefringent calcium oxalate crystals detectable by polarized light microscopy that are present in thyroid tissue ([Am J Surg Pathol 2002;26:813](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12023590))
* Fewer thyroid frozen sections are now performed ([Virchows Arch 2008;453:433](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18830623))

Cytology description

* In touch preparations, parathyroid tissue appears as clusters of cells vs. discohesive lymphocytes and histiocytes in lymph nodes mistaken as parathyroid gland
  ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:158](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10632504))
* Parathyroid: moderately cellular, small uniform cells in isolation / small groups with round / oval nuclei, salt and pepper chromatin, occasional naked nuclei, delicate vacuoles in cytoplasm and in background ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2003;127:64](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12521369))
* Cytology plus frozen section is more accurate than either alone
  ([Am J Clin Pathol 2002;118:895](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12472283))
* Rapid intraoperative parathyroid hormone assay of needle aspirates is an accurate method of distinguishing parathyroid from nonparathyroid tissues during FNA as well as parathyroidectomy ([Am J Otolaryngol 2011;32:574](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21315486), [World J Surg 2010;34:538](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20052470))

Differential diagnosis

* Coexisting **parathyroid and nodular thyroid disease**
* [Intrathyroidal parathyroid glands](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidparathyroidtissue.html) with conspicuous follicle formations or abundant oncocytic cells mimics thyroid tissue
* [Solitary thyroid nodules with fatty stroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidsolitary.html): see [Am J Surg Pathol 1998;22:538](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9591722)
* **Thyroid tissue with parathyroid pattern**: see [Mod Pathol 2000;13:210](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10697280)
