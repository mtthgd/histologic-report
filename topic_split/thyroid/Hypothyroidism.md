Definition / general

* Hypothyroidism is a common endocrine disorder resulting from a deficiency of thyroid hormone ([Rev Endocr Metab Disord 2016 May 7 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27154040))
* Presents as clinical or subclinical disease based on presence of symptoms and levels of serum TSH and free thyroxine / T4
* Further classified as primary, secondary and tertiary ([Thyroid 2012;22:1200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22954017))
* Central hypothyroidism refers to decreased production of TSH / TRH due to pituitary or hypothalamic lesions ([Thyroid 2012;22:1200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22954017))
* Complications include myxedema, characterized by thickened, nonpitting edematous soft tissue in markedly hypothyroid patients ([J Clin Diagn Res 2014;8:YD01](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25121051), [An Bras Dermatol 2016;91:100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26982789))
  + Myxedema can also occur in hyperthyroidism, such as Graves disease ([J Clin Diagn Res 2014;8:YD01](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25121051))
  + Myxedema crisis / coma; a medical emergency and life threatening condition associated with a high mortality rate, which usually occurs following severe hypothyroidism decompensation ([J Thyroid Res 2011;2011:493462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21941682))
* See also [Hypothyroidism-lab diagnosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/chemistryhypothyroidism.html)

Essential features

* Hypothyroidism is a common endocrine disorder resulting from a deficiency in thyroid hormone ([Rev Endocr Metab Disord 2016 May 7 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27154040))
* It is imperative to distinguish primary hypothyroidism from secondary / tertiary hypothyroidism ([Indian J Endocrinol Metab 2011;15:S99](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21966662)), as the causes are very different
* In severe hypothyroidism decompensation, myxedema crisis / coma, a life threatening condition, can occur and prompt medical treatment is recommended ([J Thyroid Res 2011;2011:493462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21941682))
* Myxedema describes thickened, nonpitting edema of soft tissue that may develop in patients with hypothyroidism ([J Clin Diagn Res 2014;8:YD01](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25121051), [An Bras Dermatol 2016;91:100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26982789))
  + Histologically, mucin accumulation is found between collagen fibers of the reticular dermis
  + Other findings include perivascular lymphocytes, large fibroblasts and hemosiderin deposits ([An Bras Dermatol 2016;91:100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26982789))
  + Diagnosis relies primarily on clinical findings, biopsy and laboratory findings suggestive of hypothyroidism ([Endotext - Severe Hypothyroidism in the Elderly](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25905221))

Epidemiology

* Prevalence: clinical primary hypothyroidism, 0.3%; subclinical disease, 4.3% ([Thyroid 2012;22:1200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22954017))
* Myxedema crisis / coma has a higher incidence among older females, affects about 220,000 people per year ([J Thyroid Res 2011;2011:493462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21941682))

Sites

* Thyroid gland
* Involvement of pituitary gland or hypothalamus are usually observed in central hypothyroidism ([Hippokratia 2010;14:82](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20596261), [J Clin Endocrinol Metab 2012;97:3068](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22851492))
* Myxedema involves primarily bilateral pretibial areas ([Am J Clin Dermatol 2005;6:295](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16252929))
  + Other sites include: cheeks, hands, supraclavicular fossae and soft tissue around the eyes ([Ophthal Plast Reconstr Surg 2006;22:457](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17117102) , [Med J Aust 2003;179:211](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/13678044))

Pathophysiology

* Secretion of thyroid hormones are regulated by the hypothalamic pituitary thyroid axis
* In primary hypothyroidism, the following changes occur:
  + Destruction of the thyroid gland leads to decreased secretion of thyroid hormones T3 and T4
  + In response, TSH secretion increases ([Neth J Med 2009;67:332](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19915227), [Hippokratia 2010;14:82](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20596261))
* Myxedema: dermal mucinosis is caused by increased deposition of connective tissue components (glycosaminoglycans, hyaluronic acid and mucopolysaccharides) within the reticular dermis ([Wikipedia - Myxedema](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Myxedema))
  + Protein mucopolysaccharide complex binds water, resulting in nonpitting edema
* Myxedema coma: patients with longstanding hypothyroidism often develop adaptive mechanisms, including chronic peripheral vasoconstriction, diastolic hypertension and diminished blood blood volume to preserve a normal body core temperature
* Myxedema coma occurs when a precipitating event disrupts this homeostasis ([Endotext - Myxedema and Coma (Severe Hypothyroidism)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25905238))

Etiology

* **Primary hypothyroidism**:
  + Environmental iodine deficiency (most common etiology worldwide, [Thyroid 2012;22:1200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22954017))
  + Destruction or ablation of thyroid gland (surgery, radiation, developmental)
  + In the United States, Hashimoto thyroiditis is the most common etiology ([Thyroid 2012;22:1200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22954017))
  + Interference with thyroid hormone synthesis (idiopathic, genetic [[J Med Genet 2005;42:379](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15863666)], drugs [lithium, iodide, methimazole, PTU])
  + Supraphysiologic Iodine exposure, especially in those with preexisting thyroid disease ([Nat Rev Endocrinol 2014;10:136](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24342882))
  + Chronic renal failure (normal TSH, low T3 and T4) ([Nucl Recept 2005;3:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15807894))
* **Secondary hypothyroidism**:
  + Any pituitary disorder that causes reduced TRF or TSH secretion
  + Include genetics, tumors (e.g., pituitary adenoma), infection, autoimmune disorders, drugs, pituitary surgery or radiotherapy, trauma or pituitary apoplexy ([Indian J Endocrinol Metab 2011;15:S99](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21966662), [Hippokratia 2010;14:82](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20596261))
* **Tertiary hypothyroidism**:
  + Any hypothalamic disorder that causes reduced TRF secretion
  + Includes tumors, surgery or radiotherapy ([Indian J Endocrinol Metab 2011;15:S99](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21966662), [Hippokratia 2010;14:82](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20596261))
* **Subclinical hypothyroidism**:
  + High TSH, normal T3 and T4
  + No clinical symptoms of hypothyroidism ([Am Fam Physician 2005;71:1763](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15887455)) but may have sensory neuropathy ([Neurol Sci 2009;30:149](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19214379))

Clinical features

* Most common symptoms are dry skin, fatigue, muscle cramps, cold sensitivity, voice changes and constipation
  + In advanced disease: carpal tunnel syndrome, sleep apnea and pituitary hyperplasia ([Thyroid 2012;22:1200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22954017))
* Rarely presents with myxedema pseudovolvulus ([J Surg Case Rep 2016 Apr 22;2016(4)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27106615)) or severe hyponatremia ([Eur J Endocrinol 2017;176:R15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27484454))
* Myxedema coma is characterized by unconsciousness, respiratory failure, bradycardia, hypothermia ([Case Rep Endocrinol 2015;2015:169194](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26618010)) and altered mental status ([N Engl J Med 2015;372:764](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25693016)), including "myxedema madness" (psychosis due to marked hypothyroidism)
* Recently, arrythmias and coagulation disorders have been recognized as part of myxedema crisis ([J Thyroid Res 2011;2011:493462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21941682))

Diagnosis

* For most outpatients with primary thyroid disease, serum thyrotropin (TSH) is the best screening test, although it may not be adequate for hospitalized patients ([Thyroid 2012;22:1200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22954017))
* Primary hypothyroidism is suspected when TSH is elevated, a goitrous thyroid is present, associated pituitary hormone deficiencies are absent and the thyrotropin releasing hormone stimulation test is normal ([Indian J Endocrinol Metab 2011;15:S99](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21966662))
* Secondary / tertiary hypothyroidism (central hypothyroidism) is suggested by a normal to low normal TSH and low normal thyroid hormone, confirmed by the thyrotropin releasing hormone stimuation test ([Indian J Endocrinol Metab 2011;15:S99](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21966662))

Laboratory

* Low free T3 / T4, high TSH ([Endotext - Severe Hypothyroidism in the Elderly](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25905221))
* Elevated serum TPO antibody titers may indicate autoimmune thyroiditis, and provide prognostic information on the risk of developing hypothyroidism ([Thyroid 2012;22:1200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22954017))
* In myxedema, CEA and CA125 may be elevated ([Clin Ther 2007;29:2710](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18201588))
  + May have hyponatremia ([Eur J Endocrinol 2017;176:R15](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27484454))
* See also [Hypothyroidism-lab diagnosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/chemistryhypothyroidism.html)

Radiology description

* Primary hypothyroidism:
  + Ultrasound reveals a hyperechoic multinodular thyroid ([Med J Aust 2016;205:179](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27510349)), with a halo around nodules and no calcifications; benign nodules typically have ill defined margins ([AJR Am J Roentgenol 2010;195:216](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20566819))
* Pretibial myxedema:
  + Multiple imaging modalities may be helpful in diagnosis
  + Echo-Doppler of the lower legs may show venous and lymphatic insufficiency ([An Bras Dermatol 2016;91:100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26982789))
  + Digital infrared thermal imaging, which detects surface temperature ([Eur J Endocrinol 2011;164:605](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21266550)), shows an abnormally low focal temperature over the lower legs
  + High resolution ultrasonography shows composition changes in pretibial soft tissue ([Eur J Endocrinol 2011;164:605](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21266550))
* Increased skin thickness is demonstrated by hypoechoic substance deposition in the cutaneous tissue, and blurred boundary lines between dermal and subcutaneous tissue

Prognostic factors

* Poor compliance with thyroid hormone replacement may lead to myxedema coma ([Case Rep Endocrinol 2015;2015:169194](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26618010))
* Predictors of mortality in myxedema crisis or coma: bradycardia, hypotension, hypothermia, respiratory failure requiring mechanical ventilation, sepsis, intake of sedative drugs, high APACHE II score, SOFA (Sequential Organ Failure Assessment) scores greater than 6, and no response to treatment ([J Thyroid Res 2011;2011:493462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21941682))

Case reports

* 22 year old woman with hypothyroidism due to bromide intoxication ([Am J Clin Pathol 1988;89:802](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3369375))
* 30 year old man with Graves disease ([Dermatol Online J 2008;14:8](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19061607))
* 37 year old man with HIV, hemophilia and *Pneumocystis carinii* infection presenting as thyroid mass with hypothyroidism ([Am J Clin Pathol 1991;95:489](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2014774))
* 38 year old woman with myxedema coma and cardiac ischemia ([Clin Ther 2007;29:2710](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18201588))
* A man in his late thirties with undiagnosed hypothyroidism presenting with sigmoid volvulus ([J Surg Case Rep 2016 Apr 22;2016(4)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27106615))
* 43 year old woman with cardiac death due to cardiovascular myxoedema and endocardial fibroelastosis ([Int J Cardiol 2015;182:281](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25585372))
* 54 year old woman with fatal cardiac tamponade due to myxedema pericarditis ([Am J Clin Pathol 1986;86:113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3728379))
* 82 year old woman with myxoedema secondary to hypothyroidism treated with intramuscular injection of Levothyroxine ([Case Rep Endocrinol 2015;2015:169194](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26618010))

Treatment

* Thyroid hormone replacement therapy (L-thyroxine), tailored for each patient ([Drugs Aging 2005;22:23](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15663347), [Thyroid 2012;22:1200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22954017))
* Pretibial myxedema usually resolves spontaneously
* In some cases, topical steroids, intralesional steroids, gamma globulin, pentoxifylline, surgery and radiotherapy may be required ([J Clin Diagn Res 2014;8:YD01](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25121051))
* Myxoedema coma can be successfully managed with levothyroxine and hydrocortisone ([Case Rep Endocrinol 2015;2015:169194](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26618010))
* Weekly administration of thyroxine may be useful for patients with cognitive deficits, chronic disabilities and poor medical compliance ([Case Rep Endocrinol 2015;2015:169194](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26618010), [Postgrad Med J 2007;83:e3](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17916865))

Gross description

* Diffusely enlarged, firm goiter ([Thyroid 2012;22:1200](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22954017))
* Pretibial myxedema: well defined, indurated, erythematous skin with large urticarial plaques often involving both legs and sparing the toes ([J Clin Diagn Res 2014;8:YD01](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25121051))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Primary hypothyroidism ([J Clin Endocrinol Metab 1993;76:466](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8432791)):
  + Hyperplastic follicles, some with papillary folding
  + Follicle cells range from cuboidal to columnar with clear cytoplasm and round nuclei
  + Scant colloid material
  + Lymphocytic infiltrate may be present
* Skin myxedema is usually characterised by ([J Clin Diagn Res 2014;8:YD01](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25121051)):
  + Collagen fibers in reticular dermis are separated by mucin accumulation
  + Cutaneous mucinosis ([Am J Dermatopathol 2010;32:196](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19940747))
  + Mild perivascular lymphocyte infiltrate
  + Epidermis is spared
  + Prominent fibroblasts ([An Bras Dermatol 2016;91:100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26982789))
  + Presence of hemosiderin deposits ([An Bras Dermatol 2016;91:100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26982789))

Positive stains

* Pretibial myxedema: [Alcian blue](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalcianblue.html) reveals mucin deposition across the entire dermal thickness ([An Bras Dermatol 2016;91:100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26982789))

Electron microscopy description

* Primary hypothyroidism: severe interference with thyroid hormone biosynthesis within the follicular cells ([J Clin Endocrinol Metab 1993;76:466](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8432791))
