Definition / general

* Prognostically distinct entity from counterpart [NIFTP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidglandNIFTP.html) (noninvasive follicular thyroid neoplasm with papillary-like nuclear features)

Essential features

* Same nuclear features and follicular architecture as NIFTP but with vascular or tumor capsule invasion present

Terminology

* Encapsulated follicular variant of papillary thyroid carcinoma with invasion

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [C73](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C73-C75/C73-/C73) - malignant neoplasm of thyroid gland

Epidemiology

* Women are affected more than men (F:M = 3.6:1)
* Mean age of patients 44 years ([Diagn Histopathol 2016;22:171](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.mpdhp.2016.04.009))

Sites

* Thyroid

Pathophysiology

* Thyroid parenchymal follicular cells follow well defined tumor progression model ([Clin Adv Hematol Oncol 2015;13:3](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26430868))

Etiology

* Exposure to radiation implicated in etiology of thyroid cancer ([Clin Adv Hematol Oncol 2015;13:3](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26430868))

Clinical features

* May be found incidentally on imaging or present as a visible or palpable tumor in the thyroid

Diagnosis

* Diagnosis made on histology of surgical specimens following submission and examination of entire tumor capsule ([Am J Clin Pathol 2002;117:143](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11789719))

Laboratory

* Follicular variants of papillary thyroid carcinoma may be associated with elevated serum thyroglobulin ([Thyroid 2016;26:872](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27214083))

Radiology description

* Ultrasound features include solid composition, smooth margins, parallel orientation, round to oval shape and presence of a halo (hypoechoic rim surrounding thyroid nodule) ([Thyroid 2017;27:1177](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28699414))

Prognostic factors

* Good prognosis
* Based on an international, multidisciplinary, retrospective study in which 101 participants had invasive EFVPTC, 12 patients experienced an adverse event (including 5 patients developing distant metastases and 2 patients dying of the disease) by a median follow up time of 13 years ([JAMA Oncol 2016;2:1023](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27078145))

Case reports

* 35 year old man with invasive encapsulated follicular variant of papillary thyroid carcinoma metastatic to lung at presentation ([Mod Pathol 2010;23:1191](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20526288))
* 43 year old patient with encapsulated follicular variant of papillary thyroid carcinoma showing capsular and vascular invasion on histology ([ARS Medica Tomitana 2018;24:15](https://doi.org/10.2478/arsm-2018-0004))
* 3 cases of encapsulated follicular variant of papillary thyroid carcinoma with focal vascular invasion and bone metastases ([Mod Pathol 2000;13:861](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10955452))

Treatment

* Hemithyroidectomy with or without isthmusectomy, near total thyroidectomy or total thyroidectomy
* Depending on size and stage, may consider lymph node dissection and radioactive iodine ablation for subtotal thyroidectomies ([Thyroid 2009;19:1167](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19860577))

Gross description

* Well circumscribed encapsulated solid nodules ([Head Neck Pathol 2011;5:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21221869))
* Capsular invasion may or may not be grossly evident ([Head Neck Pathol 2009;3:86](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20596997))

Frozen section description

* Frozen sections generally not recommended on primary thyroid tumors, particularly ones with preoperative cytology diagnoses of follicular patterned lesions, as diagnosis of these requires evaluation of the entire capsule

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Tumor invades capsule or extends into adjacent thyroid tissue or has evidence of lymphovascular invasion
* Nuclear features of papillary thyroid carcinoma:
  + Size and shape: nuclear enlargement / overlapping / crowding, elongation
  + Nuclear membrane irregularities: irregular contours, grooves, pseudoinclusions
  + Chromatin characteristics: clearing with margination / glassy nuclei
* Follicular growth pattern may be microfollicular, normofollicular or macrofollicular with abundant colloid

Cytology description

* FNA samples are usually hypercellular with neoplastic cells containing nuclear features of papillary thyroid carcinoma arranged in microfollicles
* Nuclear features are more subtle than conventional papillary thyroid carcinoma ([Endocr Pathol 2014;25:257](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24696132))
* Colloid may be present
* Cannot distinguish invasive EFVPTC from NIFTP on cytology, as cannot evaluate capsule on FNA

Positive stains

* [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html), [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html), [thyroglobulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsthyroglobulin.html)
* [HBME-1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshbme.html), [CK19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck19.html), [Galectin3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgalectin3.html) ([Cancer Sci 2011;102:288](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21070478))

Negative stains

* [Calcitonin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalcitonin.html), [parathyroid hormone](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/chemistryPTH.html)
* [BRAF V600E](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbraf.html) not generally seen in EFVPTC ([J Korean Med Sci 2018;33:e75](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29962924))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Shares molecular features (*RAS* type mutations) with follicular adenoma, follicular carcinoma ([Mod Pathol 2010;23:1191](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20526288))
* Follicular variants of papillary thyroid carcinoma more commonly harbor *RAS* type mutations than classic variant of papillary thyroid carcinoma (more *BRAF* type mutations); however, invasive EFVPTC is associated with more *BRAF* type mutations than noninvasive EFVPTC (which is associated with more *RAS* type mutations) ([Cell 2014;159:676](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25417114), [Mod Pathol 2010;23:1191](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20526288))

Sample pathology report

* Right lobe, partial thyroidectomy:
  + Invasive encapsulated follicular variant of papillary thyroid carcinoma

Differential diagnosis

* [Encapsulated follicular variant of papillary thyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidencapsulatedfollicularvariant.html):
  + Tumor lacks evidence of capsular or lymphovascular invasion
* [Noninvasive follicular thyroid neoplasm with papillary-like nuclear features](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidglandNIFTP.html):
  + Same nuclear features and follicular architecture but lacks vascular or tumor capsular invasion
* [Follicular carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidfollicular.html):
  + Thyroid carcinoma with capsular or lymphovascular invasion, similarly composed of follicles but lacks papillary nuclear features
* [Follicular adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidfollicularadenoma.html):
  + Thyroid neoplasm without capsular or lymphovascular invasion, similarly composed of follicles but lacks papillary nuclear features
