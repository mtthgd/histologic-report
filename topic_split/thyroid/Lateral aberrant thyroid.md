Definition / general

* Outdated, imprecise term ([Surgery 1993;114:1103](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8256214)); should not be used by pathologists
* Term "lateral aberrant thyroid" has been known since 1779 ([Eur Arch Otorhinolaryngol 2016;273:2867](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/%2026459007)); today it is rarely found in surgical textbooks
* Aberrant thyroid is a mass of tissue having the structure of a normal or pathological thyroid gland, but situated at some definite distance from the normal thyroid, with which it has no connection (Schrager, 1906, cited from [Ann Surg 1942;115:161](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17857960)) - or, more strictly, lateral to the jugular vein ([Wenig: Atlas of Head and Neck Pathology, 3rd Edition, 2015](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1455733822/pathologyoutl-20))
* Collective ("umbrella") term, largely represented by metastatic carcinoma or benign parasitic nodule
* Conditions formerly called "lateral aberrant thyroid" are indicated below:
* Malignant
  + Nodal metastasis of [thyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidpapillary.html) accounts for most of these cases; see also [Thyroid inclusions in cervical lymph nodes](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidlymphnodes.html)
  + [Branchial cleft](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidbranchial.html) with thyroid cancer ([BMJ Case Rep 2012;2012](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22778453))
* Benign
  + Many cases are actually [parasitic nodule](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidparasiticnodule.html)
  + True [ectopic thyroid](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidheterotopic.html) of lateral location is extremely rare compared to midline location
  + Displaced thyroid tissue after trauma / surgery ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2004;8:61](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15060882))
