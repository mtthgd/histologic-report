Definition / general

* Rare lesion which may arise from intrathyroidal remnants of branchial apparatus
* Originally reported by Louis et al. in 1989 ([Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2909197))
* Branchial cleft remnants in thyroid become prominent in [Hashimoto thyroiditis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidhashimotosthyroiditis.html)

Terminology

* "Branchial cleft-like cyst": used for unusually located cervical lymphoepithelial cysts / [branchial cleft cysts](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidbranchial.html) (i.e. not in soft tissue of anterolateral neck)
* Also called branchial-like cleft cyst

Epidemiology

* F:M = 2:1
* Mainly adults (mean age 45 years, range 1 day to 73 years), 2 pediatric cases (see Case Reports section below)
* < 40 cases published

Sites

* Lateral thyroid
* One case had unusual isthmic location ([Thyroid 2010;20:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20017618))

Pathophysiology / etiology

* Most authors link the origin of thyroid lymphoepithelial cysts to [solid cell nests](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidsolidcellnests.html), which are the remnants of the [ultimobranchial body](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidsolidcellnests.html)
* The most likely mechanism is squamous metaplasia and cystic degeneration of solid cell nests of the thyroid ([Pathol Res Pract 1997;193:777](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9521510))
  + This has been confirmed by CEA expression ([Surg Today 2001;31:477](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11428596))
* The theory that intrathyroidal lymphoepithelial cysts arise from branchial remnants is supported by the occasional intrathyroidal presence of other branchial derived structures, such as [thymic](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidthymictissue.html) and [parathyroid tissue](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidparathyroidtissue.html) ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2003;127:e205](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12683903))
* An association with chronic lymphocytic thyroiditis is strong; immunological mechanisms responsible for the autoimmune thyroiditis may act on metaplastic epithelium or ultimobranchial rests to form the cysts and may explain the associated lymphoid infiltrate ([Hum Pathol 1994;25:1238](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7959670))
* Alternatively, lymphoid tissue may be attracted by the presence of solid cell nests rather than by a primary autoimmune thyroidal process, because of their endodermal origin and inherited ability to form lymphoepithelial lesions ([Pathol Int 2006;56:150](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16497248))

Clinical features

* Either incidental finding of minimal clinical significance or presenting as a neck mass ([Endo Pathol 1993;4:115](http://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/BF02915299), [Hum Pathol 1994;25:1238](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7959670))
* Only a few cases (all pediatric) were not associated with concomitant thyroid disease, such as Hashimoto thyroiditis / chronic lymphocytic thyroiditis or nodular goiter
* May coexist with thyroid cancer, but not related pathogenetically ([Thyroid 2010;20:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20017618))
* Rarely causes hoarseness due to vocal cord palsy ([Ann Acad Med Singapore 2010;39:68](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20126821))

Diagnosis

* Diagnosis occurs only after surgery ([AJNR Am J Neuroradiol 2000;21:1340](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10954292))
* Workup includes radiological and pathological examination

Radiology description

* TBS / total body scan: cold nodule(s), rarely a hot nodule ([Am J Surg Pathol 1990;14:1165](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2078246))
* US: hypoechoic or echogenic with pseudosolid appearance ([AJNR Am J Neuroradiol 2000;21:1340](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10954292)), rarely solid ([J Clin Ultrasound 2006;34:298](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16788962))
* MRI: multiloculated cystic nodule

Case reports

* Infant with intrathyroidal branchial cleft ([Korean J Pediatr 2006;49:1005](http://synapse.koreamed.org/Synapse/Data/PDFData/0052KJP/kjp-49-1005.pdf))
* 7 year old girl with intrathyroidal branchial cleft-like cyst with heterotopic salivary gland-type tissue ([Pediatr Dev Pathol 2004;7:262](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15037945))
* 36 year old woman with multiple branchial cleft-like cysts with Hashimoto thyroiditis ([Endocr J 2000;47:303](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11036874))
* 42 year old man with multilocular lymphoepithelial cyst in the thyroid accompanied by a minute thyroid carcinoma ([Pathol Int 1995;45:965](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8808303))
* 47 year old man with bilateral intrathyroidal lymphoepithelial cysts ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2003;127:251](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12562250))
* 50 year old woman with eosinophilic replacement infiltrates in cystic Hashimoto thyroiditis ([Endocr Pathol 2014;25:332](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24639138))
* 50 year old man with branchial cleft-like cysts ([Am J Otolaryngol 2006;27:146](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16500482))
* 55 year old woman with lymphoepithelial cysts of the thyroid gland ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2003;127:e205](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12683903))
* 54 year old woman with cytological features of a lymphoepithelial cyst ([Soonchunhyang Med Sci 2014;20:131](http://jsms.sch.ac.kr/journal/view.php?number=290))
* 65 year old man with branchial-like cysts of the thyroid associated with solid cell nests ([Pathol Int 2006;56:150](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16497248))
* Intrathyroidal lymphoepithelial cyst ([Pathol Res Pract 1997;193:777](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9521510))
* Amyloid goiter associated with intrathyroid parathyroid and lymphoepithelial cyst ([Endocr Pathol 2009;20:243](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19697162))
* Bilateral lymphoepithelial cysts of the thyroid gland ([Thyroid 2010;20:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20017618))

Treatment

* Surgical excision of the cyst; radical thyroid removal is not recommended ([Thyroid 2010;20:111](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20017618))

Gross description

* Lateral lobe(s) of the thyroid
* Solitary / multiple unilocular or multilocular cysts, usually 1 - 2 cm (few mm to 5 cm)
* Smooth external surface, 0.1 - 0.2 cm thick wall; light tan glistening lining with cobblestone appearance; clear or yellow viscous fluid ([Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2909197))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* The cyst structure is identical to branchial cleft cyst ([Branchial pouch / cleft anomalies](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidbranchial.html)) composed of lymphoid tissue with germinal centers and squamous lining
* Epithelial lining is attenuated stratified squamous (one or two cells to approximately seven cells in thickness) or focal respiratory-type epithelium with ciliated or goblet cells
  + Focally can be denuded
  + Cyst lumen contains keratin / mucin and debris with cholesterol clefts
* Abundant adjacent lymphoid tissue, lymphoid follicles with prominent germinal centers common in cyst wall ([Hum Pathol 1994;25:1238](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7959670))
  + Old cysts have a rim of fibrous tissue
* No thyroid parenchyma within the cyst, may have entrapped follicles in the outer side of cystic wall
* Signs of thyroiditis (often florid Hashimoto thyroiditis) in surrounding thyroid ([Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2909197))
* Solid cell nests, basaloid squamous nests and C cell hyperplasia often found in surrounding parenchyma ([Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:1072](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2596621), [Am J Surg Pathol 1990;14:1165](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2078246))

Cytology description

* Mature superficial squamous cells with intact nuclei, anucleate squames, clusters of neutrophils and lymphocytes, macrophages, amorphous debris in the background ([AJNR Am J Neuroradiol 2000;21:1340](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10954292))
* Some cases yield solely lymphocytes ([Endocr J 2000;47:303](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11036874)), squamous cells ([Am J Otolaryngol 2006;27:146](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16500482)) or respiratory epithelium ([J Clin Ultrasound 2006;34:298](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16788962))

Differential diagnosis

* [Intrathyroidal thyroglossal duct cyst](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidthyroglossal.html): midline location, thyroid remnants in cyst wall, no germinal centers ([Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2909197))
* Cystic tumors:
  + [Papillary carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidpapillary.html)
  + [Follicular carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidfollicular.html)
  + [Follicular adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidfollicularadenoma.html) ([Pathol Int 2000;50:897](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11107066))
  + [Cystic goiter](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidnodular.html)
  + [Cystic thymic remnants](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidthymictissue.html): basaloid cells with Hassall corpuscles ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2003;127:251](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12562250))
  + The distinction from [branchial cleft cyst](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidbranchial.html) rests on the finding of respiratory-type epithelium ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2003;127:251](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12562250))
* Cytology:
  + [Hashimoto thyroiditis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidhashimotosthyroiditis.html) **with squamous metaplasia**
  + [Mucoepidermoid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidMuco.html)
  + [Squamous cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidSCC.html)
  + [Warthin-like variant](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidwarthin.html) **of papillary thyroid carcinoma** ([Soonchunhyang Med Sci 2014;20:131](http://jsms.sch.ac.kr/journal/view.php?number=290))
