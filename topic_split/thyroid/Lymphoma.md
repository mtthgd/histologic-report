Definition / general

* Comprises 2.5% of extranodal lymphoma and 4 - 5% of thyroid malignancies
* Usually arise on top of Hashimoto thyroiditis or lymphocytic thyroiditis
* Most are diffuse large B cell lymphoma, marginal zone B cell / MALT lymphoma or mixtures of these two
  + Rarely follicular cell lymphoma
* Hong Kong / Chinese cases are only rarely EBV+ ([Am J Clin Pathol 1999;112:263](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10439808))
* Follicular lymphoma has two subgroups:
  + t(14;18) or bcl2 overexpression, usually CD10+ and WHO grade 1-2
  + No t(14;18) or bcl2 overexpression, often CD10- and WHO grade 3 ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18830125), [Mod Pathol 2005;18:1471](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15976812))
* Hodgkin lymphoma: very rare, favorable prognosis, female predominance ([Neuroimaging Clin N Am 2003;13:371](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14631680))

Epidemiology

* 75% women, usually adults or elderly ([Am J Surg Pathol 2000;24:623](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10800981))

Clinical features

* Rapidly growing neck mass
* Compression symptoms including dysphagia and hoarseness
* Can present with diffuse thyroid enlargement
* May be accidentally discovered
* Hypothyroid manifestations may develop
* Cold nodule
* Virtually all primary thyroid lymphomas are MALT-type arising after 20 - 30 years of lymphocytic thyroiditis in older patients (mean age 64 years)
* Sequence similarity in clonal IgH bands suggests lymphoma may arise from thyroiditis ([J Clin Pathol 2008;61:438](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18006670))
* Secondary involvement seen in 20% dying of generalized lymphoma, although usually does not produce clinical hypothyroidism
* Regional lymph node enlargement can be seen
* Hodgkin lymphoma: thyroid mass, cervical lymphadenopathy, patient is euthyroid but may be hypothyroid

Prognostic factors

* Overall 5 year survival is 80%
* **Poor prognostic factors**: diffuse B cell lymphoma subtype, perithyroidal soft tissue invasion, stage 2E or higher
* **Good prognostic factors**: marginal zone lymphoma subtype or stage IE

Case reports

* 19 year old woman with syncytial variant of nodular sclerosing Hodgkin lymphoma ([Acta Cytol 1995;39:543](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7762349))
* 22 and 29 year old women with thyroid nodule as a first manifestation of Hodgkin lymphoma ([Diagn Pathol 2013;8:116](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23856094))
* 32 year old man with primary T cell lymphoma of thyroid ([Med Oncol 2008;25:462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18363111))
* 37 year old woman with nodular sclerosing Hodgkin lymphoma ([Ann Endocrinol (Paris) 2012;73:492](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22841568))
* 48 year old woman with primary thyroid lymphoma ([Diagn Cytopathol 2012;40:444](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21681970))
* 54 year old man with primary mediastinal large B cell lymphoma ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2015;8:5944](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26191324))
* 62 year old woman with Hodgkin lymphoma presenting as abscess in thyroid gland ([Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2012;55:122](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22499323))
* 65 year old woman with thyroid MALT lymphoma ([Ann Thorac Surg 2015;100:700](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26234841))
* 70 year old man with diffuse large B cell lymphoma of thyroid ([Cytojournal 2006;3:23](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17052355))

Treatment

* Often curable by radiation or chemotherapy (particularly MALT), in contrast to anaplastic carcinoma
* Surgery is rare ([Eur J Surg Oncol 2008;34:576](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17604588))

Gross description

* Variable sized, rubbery / soft mass
* White cut surface with fish flesh appearance
* Necrosis could be found

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Varies by histologic type
* **Diffuse large B cell lymphoma**:
  + Diffuse infiltrate destroying thyroid follicles
  + Large cells with moderate amphophilic cytoplasm, vesicular nuclei, prominent nucleoli
  + Bizarre cells may be seen
* **MALT lymphoma**:
  + Infiltration of thyroid epithelium creates lymphoepithelial lesions (lymphocytes "stuff" glandular lumina, [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2007;131:1673](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17979485))
  + May have background lymphocytic thyroiditis
* **Follicular lymphoma**:
  + Usually prominent follicular pattern with prominent interfollicular neoplastic infiltrate, lymphoepithelial lesions are common
  + May arise on top of thyroiditis

Cytology description

* Monotonous population of large atypical lymphoid cells (scant cytoplasm, finely granular chromatin, prominent nucleoli), lymphoglandular bodies present (cytoplasmic fragmentation), karyorrhexis ([Cytojournal 2005;2:21](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16336672))
* MALT features: see [Acta Cytol 2015;59:26](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25531657)
* May be misdiagnosed as lymphocytic thyroiditis
* Hodgkin lymphoma: some atypical cells, may have marked fibrosis

Positive stains

* [CD20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd20.html) and [keratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) highlight lymphoepithelial lesions
* [Thyroglobulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsthyroglobulin.html) stains entrapped follicular epithelium
* Also [CD45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd45.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* t(11;18) is not present in thyroid MALT lymphomas ([Mod Pathol 2006;19:1578](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16980947))

Differential diagnosis

* [Anaplastic carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidUndiff.html)
* [Insular carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidinsular.html)
* [Small cell variant of medullary carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidmedullary.html)
* **Thyroiditis** ([Hashimoto](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidhashimotosthyroiditis.html) or [lymphocytic](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidfocallymphocytic.html)):
  + Diagnosis of lymphoma is supported by the presence of a dense clonal proliferation of lymphoid cells, lymphoepithelial lesions and CD20 positivity ([Acta Cytol 2012;56:352](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22846513))
* **Undifferentiated carcinoma** ([Eur Radiol 2016;26:1031](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26201291))
