Definition / general

* Macrofollicles (large dilated follicles) > 50% of cross sectional area, with papillary nuclear features
* Very rare; similar clinical features as classic papillary carcinoma (female predominant, occasional nodal metastases, few deaths)
* May have minor (< 5%) insular component that does not appear to affect prognosis ([Cancer 1997;80:1110](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9305712))

Gross description

* Mean 3 cm, often encapsulated

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Frequent macrofollicles (> 50% of cross sectional area) and foci of follicular variant of papillary carcinoma, all with prominent, large ground glass nuclei and nuclear grooves ([Hum Pathol 1991;22:1195](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1748427))
* Also cells with large but less pale nuclei with stippled chromatin, or cuboidal cells with hyperchromatic nuclei

Cytology description

* Moderate to highly cellular with micro- and macrofollicles with thick colloid
* Papillary nuclear changes often absent or focal
* May resemble goiter ([Diagn Cytopathol 2007;35:560](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17703452), [Cancer 1998;84:235](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9723599))

Differential diagnosis

* [Follicular adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidfollicularadenoma.html): lined by flat epithelial cells with hyperchromatic nuclei
* [Nodular hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidnodular.html)
