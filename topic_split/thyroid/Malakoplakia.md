Definition / general

* Rare chronic inflammatory / granulomatous disease that most commonly affects genitourinary tract (see topics in [Bladder](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/bladdermalakoplakia.html), [Kidney](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/kidneymalakoplakia.html), [Prostate](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/prostatemalako.html), [Testis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/testismalakoplakia.html) chapters)
* Usually in immunocompromised adult women due to inadequate phagocytosis / degradation of gram negative bacteria by macrophages
* Microscopic hallmarks are Hansemann cells and Michaelis-Gutmann bodies (see "Micro description" below)

Terminology

* Malakoplakia (or malacoplakia) = Greek "malakos" (soft) + "plakos" (plaque)

Epidemiology

* < 10 cases of thyroid involvement have been reported, all in women (see below)

Clinical features

* Difficulty swallowing, fever, asymmetrical enlargement of the gland and destruction of surrounding tissues (nonspecific)
* Diagnosis only after surgical excision

Radiology description

* Ultrasound: solid nodule
* I-131 scan: low uptake

Case reports

* 50, 54 and 60 year old women ([Am J Clin Pathol 1989;92:813](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2556021), [Int J Surg Pathol 2015;23:308](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25663335), [Acta Cytol 1996;40:970](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8842176))
* 64 year old woman with malacoplakia manifesting as a chronic inflammatory mass ([Ear Nose Throat J 2003;82:876](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14661439))
* 72 year old woman with thyroid gland malakoplakia with autoimmune thyroiditis ([Histopathology 1993;23:491](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8314230))

Treatment

* Antibiotics (quinolones, rifampicin and trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole)
* Mass excision

Gross description

* Unencapsulated nodule several cm in diameter, yellow to white, usually soft

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Sheets of histiocytes with eosinophilic granular cytoplasm containing characteristic basophilic or PAS+ diastase resistant inclusions / bodies loaded with calcium, iron and bacterial debris (Hanseman cells)
* Michaelis-Gutmann bodies (calcospherites) are round laminated mineralized concretions, 1 - 10 microns, intra- or extracellular; may be targetoid, with a dense central crystalline core
* Inflammatory (neutrophils, plasma cells, lymphocytes and granulation tissue) to fibrotic background

Cytology description

* Numerous histiocytes with abundant eosinophilic granular cytoplasm containing basophilic inclusions (4 - 10 microns) consistent with Michaelis-Gutmann bodies ([Acta Cytol 1996;40:970](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8842176))
* The background contains similar round basophilic bodies, blood and inflammatory cells ([Int J Surg Pathol 2015;23:308](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25663335))

Positive stains

* Inclusions: [calcium (von Kossa)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalcium.html), [iron (Prussian blue)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsiron.html), [PAS+ diastase resistant](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html)
* [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html) for macrophages contained inclusions

Electron microscopy description

* Macrophages with numerous phagolysosomes packed with undigested bacteria
* Concentric crystalline laminations with dense inner zone containing calcium salts crystals and thin outer zone of amorphous granular material ([Histopathology 1993;23:491](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8314230))

Differential diagnosis

* **Malignancy of thyroid or neck organs**
