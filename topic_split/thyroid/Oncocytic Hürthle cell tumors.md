Definition / general

* Thyroid tumors composed exclusively or almost exclusively of oncocytic cells

Essential features

* Follicular neoplasm with > 75% oncocytic tumor cells
* Oncocytic appearance is due to abundance of eosinophilic cytoplasm from the accumulation of dysfunctional mitochondria
* Malignant counterpart is characterized by the presence of capsular or vascular invasion
  + Malignant transformation is also associated with tumor size, marked nuclear atypia, multinucleation, pleomorphism, mitoses or histologic pattern of the lesion but are not determinants of malignancy ([Front Endocrinol (Lausanne) 2021;12:678119](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34012422))
* Frequent hematogenous metastases; 30% are to lymph node but these are rare in follicular carcinoma
* No known exogenous risk factors for developing oncocytic tumors

Terminology

* Synonym: oncocytic cell is also called oxyphilic cell
  + The term Hürthle cell is a misnomer and not used anymore; specifically, Dr. Hürthle originally described C cells instead of oncocytes
  + WHO 2022 allows the use of the term oncocytic

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [C73](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C73-C75/C73-/C73) - malignancy of the thyroid

Epidemiology

* Oncocytic carcinoma is more common in men
* Oncocytic carcinoma represents 5% of differentiated thyroid carcinoma in the U.S. ([Front Endocrinol (Lausanne) 2021;12:678119](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34012422))
* Especially adults (mean age: 57 years); 10 years later than the mean age of follicular carcinoma ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2008;132:1241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18684023))

Sites

* Thyroid gland, although oncocytic cells are present in many different body sites

Clinical features

* Detection of a thyroid mass by clinical or ultrasound (US) evaluation

Diagnosis

* Lesion is found with clinical or US evaluation and then frequently diagnosed as oncocytic neoplasm on fine needle aspiration, category IV Bethesda thyroid atlas
* Histologically the diagnosis is based on the evidence of an oncocytic lesion, which can be either a benign adenoma (no capsular or vascular invasion) or an oncocytic carcinoma (evidence of capsular or vascular invasion) ([Front Endocrinol (Lausanne) 2021;12:678119](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34012422))
* Oncocytic carcinoma is subclassified as
  + Minimally invasive carcinoma
  + Extensively invasive carcinoma
  + Vascular invasive carcinoma

Prognostic factors

* Oncocytic adenoma is encapsulated and benign, no evidence of recurrence after excision
* Overall, oncocytic carcinoma is more aggressive than conventional follicular carcinoma, with higher frequency of extrathyroidal extension, local recurrence and metastasis to lymph nodes ([Front Endocrinol (Lausanne) 2021;12:678119](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34012422))
* Mortality rate: 10 - 80%
* Worse prognosis: old age, tumor size > 4 cm and extensive vascular invasion

Case reports

* 30 year old woman with a 1.1 cm thyroid nodule underwent a fine needle aspiration biopsy showing a Bethesda IV Hürthle cell neoplasm ([Am J Otolaryngol 2020;41:102312](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31727331))
* 63 year old Caucasian woman was incidentally diagnosed with Hürthle cell thyroid carcinoma ([J Med Case Rep 2021;15:399](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34376229))
* 63 year old man with oncocytic carcinoma of the thyroid (OCA) with an aggressive clinical course, including widespread metastasis and resistance to radioiodine treatment ([J Clin Med 2023;12:6970](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/38002585))
* 81 year old man who had undergone total thyroidectomy for goiter in the past and presented with metastatic papillary thyroid carcinoma (PTC) to the neck after a gap of 16 years ([Am J Case Rep 2023;24:e939025](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37853680))
* 84 year old woman who presented with a neck mass ([Front Endocrinol (Lausanne) 2024;15:1349114](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/39220363))

Treatment

* Adenoma: lobectomy
* Carcinoma: total thyroidectomy or radiation ablation
* Radioactive iodine: resistant compared to conventional follicular carcinoma

Gross description

* Majority of tumors are > 2 cm ([Front Endocrinol (Lausanne) 2021;12:678119](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34012422))
* Prone to infarction or hemorrhage, especially after FNA or core biopsy
* Solitary, solid, bright brown to mahogany color, mostly encapsulated, lobulated, may have central scar
* Widely invasive tumors have irregular borders, may have satellite nodule / multinodular appearance

Microscopic (histologic) description

* At least 75% of tumor cells are oncocytes with large size, distinct cell borders, deeply eosinophilic and granular cytoplasm, large nucleus with prominent nucleolus, complete loss of cell polarity
* Follicular, trabecular, solid or papillary growth patterns
* Occasional nuclear grooves or nuclear pseudoinclusions
* May have psammomatous-like calcifications without lamination in the lumen of follicle (papillary carcinoma has laminated psammoma body in the stroma) ([Virchows Arch 2024;485:853](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/39349665))
* Random nuclear atypia is common but is not evidence for malignancy
* Carcinoma tends to have
  + Thicker capsule than that of adenoma
  + Solid / trabecular rather than follicular pattern of growth
  + Smaller cells with high N:C ratio, increased mitotic figures
* Can have clear cell changes due to dilated mitochondria
* Oncocytic follicular cell derived thyroid carcinoma includes
  + Oncocytic papillary thyroid carcinoma (PTC) (nuclear features of PTC)
  + Oncocytic encapsulated follicular subtype of PTC (follicular growth pattern and nuclear features of PTC)
  + Oncocytic poorly differentiated carcinoma: size > 4 cm, with tumor necrosis, numerous mitoses, foci of small tumor cells
  + Differentiated high grade oncocytic carcinoma: oncocytic cells organized in papillary, solid growth pattern, lack of any anaplastic features and 1 of these 2 features: mitotic count ≥ 5/2 mm2 and tumor necrosis
  + Oncocytic medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC): nuclear features of MTC, positivity for calcitonin, carcinoembryonic antigen (CEA monoclonal), insulinoma associated protein 1 (INSM1), negativity for thyroglobulin

Cytology description

* Highly cellular, 75% or more Hürthle cells (abundant granular cytoplasm, round nuclei, often prominent nucleoli), often discohesive cells, some enlarged and pleomorphic with intracytoplasmic lumens (empty vacuoles with magenta [Diff-Quik], green [Pap] or no material); transgressing vessels (capillaries in clusters of Hürthle cells) ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2001;125:1031](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11473452))
* According to the Bethesda Atlas, 2 different types of oncocytic cells: small oncocytes with high N:C ratio and large oncocytes with at least 2x variability in nuclear size
* No colloid, lymphocytes, histiocytes, plasma cells or ordinary follicular cells
* Cannot definitively diagnose malignancy based on cytologic material but malignant cases tend to have small or large cell dysplasia, nuclear crowding and discohesive cells ([J Clin Pathol 2023;76:671](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35701142), [Diagn Cytopathol 2025;53:102](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/39582472), [Oncologist 2011;16:1380](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21964000), [Diagn Cytopathol 2008;36:149](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18232004))
* Metastatic tumors may have bland cytologic features ([Diagn Cytopathol 2007;35:439](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17580356))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Aneuploidy is common in oncocytic tumors, including chromosome gains and losses
* Deletions or mutation of mitochondrial DNA (mtDNA) coding for oxidative phosphorylation (OXPHOS) proteins, which lead to energy production defects and compensatory mitochondrial proliferation
* *PTEN* and *TP53* mutations ([Endocr Pathol 2015;26:365](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26530486))
* *MEN1* loss of function mutations in 4% of patients diagnosed with oncocytic thyroid carcinoma ([J Clin Endocrinol Metab 2015;100:E611](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25625803))
* *RAS* mutations (lower rate than follicular thyroid carcinoma), *EIF1AX, TERT, CDKN1A* ([J Clin Endocrinol Metab 2015;100:E611](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25625803), [J Clin Endocrinol Metab 2013;98:E962](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23543667))

Sample pathology report

* Thyroid, thyroidectomy:
  + Oncocytic adenoma (see comment)
  + Comment: The slides include a capsulated nodule composed exclusively or almost exclusive of oncocytic cells. No evidence of capsular or vascular invasion.
* Thyroid, thyroidectomy:
  + Oncocytic carcinoma (see comment)
  + Comment: The slides include a capsulated nodule composed exclusively or almost exclusive of oncocytic cells. The slides show the evidence of capsular or vascular invasion.

Differential diagnosis

* [Hashimoto thyroiditis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidhashimotosthyroiditis.html):
  + Abundant lymphocytes without distinct nodule; evidence of lymphoepithelial clusters
* [Medullary carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidmedullary.html) with Hürthle cell-like growth pattern:
  + Specific ICC panel: [calcitonin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalcitonin.html)+, [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html)+, [thyroglobulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsthyroglobulin.html)-
* [Nodular goiter](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidnodular.html) with prominent oncocytic cells:
  + Abundant colloid, follicular cells and histiocytes mixed with oncocytic cells
* [Papillary thyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidpapillary.html), [oncocytic variant](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidoncocytic.html), [Warthin-like variant](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidwarthin.html), [tall cell variant](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidtallcellvariant.html):
  + With nuclear features of papillary carcinoma

Board review style question #1

What is the minimal percentage of oncocytic cells for a diagnosis of thyroid oncocytic lesion?

1. 10%
2. 25%
3. 50%
4. 75%

Board review style answer #1

**D.** 75%. According to the literature, the amount of oncocytic cells for a definition of oncocytic neoplasm is 75%.
Answers A, B and C are incorrect because 10%, 25% and 50% of oncocytic cells is insufficient for this diagnosis.

Board review style question #2

[![Oncocytic carcinoma, angioinvasive](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/thyroidhurthlerossi07.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/thyroidhurthlerossi07.jpg)

Which of the following is the criterion for a discrimination between benign versus malignant oncocytic neoplasm?

1. Capsular or vascular invasion
2. Mitotic activity
3. Nuclear atypia
4. Positivity for antimitochondrial stain

Board review style answer #2

**A.** Capsular or vascular invasion. The discrimination between adenoma and carcinoma is based on the detection of capsular or vascular invasion.
Answer C is incorrect because nuclear atypia can be seen in both benign and malignant oncocytic lesions.
Answer B is incorrect because increasing mitotic activity can be seen in either benign or malignant oncocytic lesions.
Answer D is incorrect because positivity for antimitochondrial stain is linked to the nature of the cells (i.e., oncocytic rather than to the malignant or benign nature).

[Comment Here](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/)
