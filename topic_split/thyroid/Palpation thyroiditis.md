Definition / general

* Common (85%+ of surgically resected thyroids) but clinically insignificant

Terminology

* Also called multifocal granulomatous thyroiditis

Clinical features

* More often associated with goiter than autoimmune thyroiditis ([J Korean Med Sci 1988;3:27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3079564))
* May be due to minor trauma, such as vigorous physical examinations ([Am J Clin Pathol 1975;64:639](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1242618))

Case reports

* 70 year old man with case causing new onset atrial fibrillation ([Thyroid 2008;18:571](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18407755))

Treatment

* Self limited, no treatment necessary

Gross description

* Normal, or small foci of hemorrhage

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Multiple small granulomas centered in disrupted follicles, composed of lymphocytes (usually T cells), macrophages (some foamy), occasional multinucleated giant cells associated with colloid breakdown, usually no necrosis, no neutrophils

Differential diagnosis

* **Fungi**: [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html)+ / [GMS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgms.html)+, necrosis usually present
* [Sarcoidosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidsarcoid.html): interstitial granulomas, systemic disease
* [Subacute thyroiditis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidgranuloma.html): acute inflammatory cells
