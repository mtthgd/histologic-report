Definition / general

* Classic variant of papillary thyroid carcinoma is characterized by 2 cardinal features:
  + The presence of true papillae defined as papillae with a central vascular core
  + Nuclear features in the overlying epithelial cells defined by nuclear enlargement, nuclear membrane irregularity and a distinct chromatin pattern

Essential features

* Defined by 2 cardinal features: true papillae with a fibrovascular core and nuclear features of papillary carcinoma
* One of the most common types of papillary carcinoma
* Tendency to spread to regional lymph nodes
* High frequency of *BRAF* V600E mutation

Terminology

* Papillary carcinoma, conventional type (variant)
* Papillary carcinoma, usual type (variant)

ICD coding

+ ICD-O: [8260/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=69) - papillary adenocarcinoma, NOS
+ ICD-10: [C73](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C73-C75/C73-/C73) - malignant neoplasm of thyroid gland

Epidemiology

* One of the most common variants of papillary thyroid carcinoma ([J Clin Endocrinol Metab 2014;99:E276](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24248188))
  + Previously was most common type of papillary thyroid carcinoma but now papillary microcarcinoma and papillary thyroid carcinoma, follicular variant are more common
* Female predominance; F:M ratio = ~3:1
* Median age of diagnosis in 50s

Sites

* Most commonly affects the thyroid gland
* May also occur in thyroglossal duct, lingual thyroid, ectopic thyroid and struma ovarii ([Endocr Pathol 2015;26:75](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25577275), [Endocrine 2016;51:189](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25987346), [Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1337](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17721188))

Etiology

* Ionizing radiation is the best established risk factor, including:
  + Iatrogenic (e.g., radiation for head and neck cancer)
  + Post Chernobyl nuclear accident ([Endocr Pathol 2006;17:307](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17525478))
  + Post atom bomb
* Can be familial in 4.5% of cases ([Surgery 2009;145:100](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19081481))

Clinical features

* Painless palpable thyroid mass

Diagnosis

* Typically, the diagnosis is first rendered on ultrasound guided preoperative fine needle aspiration cytology
* Surgical pathology report of a resected specimen provides further information about the subtyping (i.e., variant) and microstaging

Radiology description

* Diagnostic thyroid ultrasound with survey of cervical lymph nodes is the recommended imaging modality for patients with thyroid nodule(s) ([Thyroid 2016;26:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26462967))
* The following ultrasound findings are associated with thyroid carcinoma, in particular papillary thyroid carcinoma ([Thyroid 2016;26:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26462967)):
  + Hypoechogenicity compared with surrounding thyroid or strap muscles
  + Irregular border
  + Microcalcification
  + Tall shape (i.e., a nodule that is taller than wide measured on a transverse view)

Prognostic factors

* AJCC pathologic staging and the American Thyroid Association (ATA) initial risk stratification provide prognostic information ([Amin: AJCC Cancer Staging Manual, 8th Edition, 2018](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/3319406175/pathologyoutl-20), [Thyroid 2016;26:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26462967))
* Pathologic parameters included in the risk stratification for a classic variant of papillary thyroid carcinoma are:
  + ATA intermediate risk: microscopic invasion into perithyroidal soft tissue, lymphovascular invasion and > 5 pathologically positive lymph nodes with all involved lymph nodes < 3 cm in size
  + ATA high risk: gross extrathyroidal extension, incomplete resection, distant metastasis and pathologic nodal metastasis ≥ 3 cm in greatest dimension

Case reports

* 7 year old girl with sporadic papillary thyroid carcinoma, classic variant harboring *ETV6-NTRK3* fusion ([J Pediatr Endocrinol Metab 2018;31:461](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29427554))
* 12 and 16 year old boys with papillary thyroid carcinoma arising within thyroglossal duct cyst ([Head Neck Pathol 2017;11:442](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28293858))
* 48 year old woman with synchronous papillary thyroid carcinoma and non-Hodgkin lymphoma ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2018;97:e9831](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29419687))
* 61 year old man with papillary thyroid carcinoma metastatic to axillary lymph node ([J Med Case Rep 2015;9:181](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26307101))
* 72 year old man with metastatic papillary thyroid carcinoma into a clear cell renal cell carcinoma ([J Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 2017;46:17.](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28249616))

Treatment

* Commonly treated with surgical resection (e.g., total thyroidectomy, subtotal thyroidectomy, hemithyroidectomy or lobectomy)
* Post operative radioactive iodine treatment may be considered if the tumor exhibits aggressive features (ATA intermediate or high risk) ([Thyroid 2016;26:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26462967))

Frozen section description

* Frozen section of papillary thyroid carcinoma is strongly discouraged as frozen artifacts distort the nuclear features necessary for diagnosis
* The standard care is to perform preoperative fine needle aspiration to establish the diagnosis and to determine the most appropriate surgical procedure

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Characterized by 2 cardinal features:
  + The presence of true papillae defined as finger-like projection with a fibrovascular core
  + The lining cells show nuclear features of papillary carcinoma, defined as 1) nuclear enlargement, 2) nuclear membrane irregularity and 3) chromatin clearing
* Lack diagnostic features of other aggressive variants (e.g., tall cell variant, columnar variant and hobnail variant)
* Other histologic findings that may be present include:
  + Psammoma bodies: laminated microcalcification, common in classic variant
  + Prominent cystic degeneration / cystic change
  + Ossification or dystrophic calcification

Cytology description

* Cytology sample shows nuclear features of papillary thyroid carcinoma: oval nuclei, nuclear overlapping, nuclear membrane irregularity, powdery chromatin, chromatin margination, nuclear grooves and nuclear pseudoinclusions
* In classic variant, large papillary projections may be seen in cytology samples

Positive stains

* Thyroid follicular cell lineage markers: [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html), [thyroglobulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsthyroglobulin.html) and [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html)
* Mutation specific protein: [BRAF](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbraf.html) V600E (VE1 antibody) ([Thyroid 2019;29:1792](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31452453), [J Clin Endocrinol Metab 2013;98:E1414](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23775351))

Negative stains

* [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html), [calcitonin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalcitonin.html), [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html) and [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* 50 - 77% harbor *BRAF* mutation, in particular *BRAF* V600E ([J Clin Endocrinol Metab 2014;99:E276](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24248188), [Cell 2014;159:676](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25417114))
* Absence or low frequency (6.3%) of *RAS* mutation ([J Clin Endocrinol Metab 2014;99:E276](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24248188), [Cell 2014;159:676](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25417114))
* 8% with *RET-PTC* rearrangement ([Cell 2014;159:676](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25417114))
* Other rare fusions in classic variant include: *NTRK3* fusion in 2%, *NTRK1* fusion in 1.4%, *ALK* fusion in 1.1% and *PAX8-PPARgamma* fusion in 0.8% ([Cell 2014;159:676](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25417114))

* Thyroid, left lobe and isthmus, left hemithyroidectomy:
  + Papillary carcinoma, classic type, 2.3 cm (see synoptic report)

Differential diagnosis

* **Papillary foci of [Graves disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidgraves.html) or other papillary hyperplasia**
  + May have abundant papillary infoldings and the cytoplasm may be tall or columnar
  + However, the nuclei are small and round and lack nuclear clearing
* **[Medullary thyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidmedullary.html), papillary variant**
  + May occasionally contain true papillary architecture (so called papillary variant)
  + Lesional cells show nuclear features of neuroendocrine tumors with salt and pepper nuclei
  + Positive for [calcitonin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalcitonin.html), [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html) and [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html)

**A.** *BRAF* V600E mutation. This is papillary thyroid carcinoma, classic type.

Board review style question #2

Which of the following histologic findings is a feature of classic type of papillary thyroid carcinoma?

1. Composed entirely of follicles
2. Nuclei of the lesional cells are small and round without nuclear membrane irregularity
3. Contains well formed papillae with a fibrovascular core
4. Lesional cells have a cell height at least 2 - 3 times of the cell width

Board review style answer #2

**C.** Contains well formed papillae with a fibrovascular core
