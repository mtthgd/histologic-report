Definition / general

* Peripheral nodule of goiter that is anatomically separate from the main thyroid gland ([Arch Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 1996;122:1409](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8956762))
* Spontaneous detachment of thyroid tissue with subsequent implant in the lateral neck may occur in [nodular goiter](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidnodular.html) or [Hashimoto thyroiditis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidhashimotosthyroiditis.html)
* Diagnostic criteria ([Histopathology 2006;49:107](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16879388)):
  + Parasitic nodule should be in same fascial plane as thyroid gland and exhibit a similar histologic appearance as the main gland
  + No evidence of lymph node structures
* Similar separated nodules are described in [uterine myoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusleiomyoma.html) ([Obstet Gynecol 2009;114:611](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19701042))

Terminology

* Also called sequestered (i.e. sequestered goiter), detached or accessory thyroid nodule
* Recommended to use "parasitic nodule" for separated thyroid nodules in lateral neck, as opposed to midline ectopic thyroid tissue along the thyrothymic tract, which is mainly a developmental abnormality ([Virchows Arch 1999;434:241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10190304))
* [Lateral aberrant thyroid](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidlateral.html) often represents parasitic thyroid nodule

Epidemiology

* F:M = 4:1, median age is 51 years (range 15 to 83 years)
* ~100 cases have been reported; the largest series was from Dr. Rosai ([Lab Invest 2006;86:96A](https://www.google.com/url?sa=i&rct=j&q=&esrc=s&source=images&cd=&cad=rja&uact=8&ved=0ahUKEwj2lJz10fHQAhVJzlQKHcGvAkkQjRwIBw&url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.nature.com%2Flabinvest%2Fjournal%2Fv86%2Fn1s%2Fabs%2F3700610a.html%0D%0A))

Sites

* Perithyroidal, close to the gland (< 1 cm)
* Can be located in the lateral neck from the submandibular to the retroclavicular area, the sternocleidomastoid and sternohyoid muscles ([Lab Invest 2006;86:96A](https://www.google.com/url?sa=i&rct=j&q=&esrc=s&source=images&cd=&cad=rja&uact=8&ved=0ahUKEwj2lJz10fHQAhVJzlQKHcGvAkkQjRwIBw&url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.nature.com%2Flabinvest%2Fjournal%2Fv86%2Fn1s%2Fabs%2F3700610a.html%0D%0A))
* Rarely found in the mediastinum as part of a substernal nodular goiter ([Arch Intern Med 1983;143:1015](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6679211))

Pathophysiology / etiology

* Portion of goitrous thyroid extending through the fascia may be separated by the mechanical action of neck muscles, and remains connected to the main gland by a thin fibrous strand of vascular tissue ([Boston Med Surg J 1903;149:616](http://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJM190312031492302))
* Split from thyroid gland is due to ablation of pre-existing connection or lack of identification of connection to the main gland ([Wenig: Atlas of Head and Neck Pathology, 3rd Edition, 2015](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1455733822/pathologyoutl-20))
* Alternatively, parasitic nodule may represent concurrent hyperplastic changes in accessory thyroid tissue ([N Engl J Med 1964;270:927](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14114112))
* Blood supply may be obtained from thyroid via fibrovascular pedicle, or be autonomous, acquired from the surrounding tissues ([ISRN Surg 2011;2011:313626](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22084753))

Clinical features

* Palpated in the lateral neck ([N Engl J Med 1964;270:927](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14114112))
* The nodule is usually an expression of nodular hyperplasia or nodular Hashimoto thyroiditis, less commonly of Graves disease ([Histopathology 2006;49:107](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16879388))
* Benign condition. but some cases of metastatic thyroid carcinoma from occult primary may be initially misdiagnosed as parasitic nodules
  + Rodriguez found malignancy without evidence of tumor in the main gland in 10% of studied parasitic nodules, and suggested that parasitic nodule can originate in a primary tumor ([Lab Invest 2006;86:96A](http://www.nature.com/labinvest/journal/v86/n1s/abs/3700610a.html)), but microcarcinoma in the main thyroid cannot be excluded

Diagnosis

* On histopathology, after exclusion of metastatic cancer

Radiology description

* RAI uptake in a nodule separate from thyroid ([N Engl J Med 1964;270:927](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14114112))
* Sonography: the main gland is multinodular, but parasitic nodules imitate enlarged cervical lymph nodes ([Endocrinol Diabetes Metab Case Rep 2014;2014:140027](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24839549))

Case reports

* 40 year old man with parasitic thyroid nodules and nontoxic multinodular goiter ([J Med Case Rep 2014;8:66](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24555692))
* 53 year old woman with parasitic thyroid nodule and Hashimoto chronic thyroiditis ([Rev Hosp Clin Fac Med Sao Paulo 2000;55:65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10959126))
* 58 year old woman with parasitic thyroid nodules ([Endocrinol Diabetes Metab Case Rep 2014;2014:140027](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24839549))
* 67 year old woman with parasitic nodule of the thyroid and Graves disease ([Virchows Arch 1999;434:241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10190304))
* 80 year old woman with ectopic sequestered thyroid tissue ([ISRN Surg 2011;2011:313626](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22084753))
* Young woman with sequestered substernal goiter ([Arch Intern Med 1983;143:1015](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6679211))
* Parasitic nodule of thyroid in neck of patient with family history of papillary thyroid carcinoma ([Endocr Pathol 2015;26:273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25217334))
* Scintigraphic demonstration of sequestered nodular goiter ([Clin Nucl Med 1992;17:402](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1587049))
* Parasitic nodule of the right carotid triangle ([Arch Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 1996;122:1409](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8956762))

Treatment

* Usually removed surgically to rule out metastasis

Gross description

* 0.5 - 6.5 cm nodule, separate from thyroid gland, usually single (> 80%)
* Fibrovascular pedicle connecting to the main thyroid can be discovered after careful dissection at surgery
* Often nodular or shows changes similar to the main thyroid

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Benign appearing thyroid tissue with colloid filled or hyperplastic follicles
* Similar features are found in orthotopic gland
* Hashimoto thyroiditis in parasitic nodule may simulate lymph node tissue
  In addition, reactive atypia in Hashimoto follicular cells with ground glass nuclei mimics papillary thyroid carcinoma ([Rev Hosp Clin Fac Med Sao Paulo 2000;55:65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10959126))

Cytology description

* Thyroid follicular cells ([Endocr Pathol 2015;26:273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25217334))

Negative stains

* [CK19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck19.html), [Galectin3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgalectin3.html), [HBME1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshbme.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* No BRAF mutation ([Endocrinol Diabetes Metab Case Rep 2014;2014:140027](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24839549))
* Polyclonal on HUMARA assay ([Hum Pathol 1998;29:187](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9490281))

Differential diagnosis

* [Papillary thyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidpapillary.html) (nodal metastais) vs. parasitic nodule with Hashimoto thyroiditis:
  + Cytological and architectural features of carcinoma
  + Primary tumor in the main thyroid
  + Lymph node structures, subcapsular sinuses highlighted by reticulin stain ([Virchows Arch 1999;434:241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10190304))
* **Displaced / seeded thyroid tissue** due to prior surgery or accidental trauma ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2004;8:61](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15060882), [Endocrinol Diabetes Metab Case Rep 2014;2014:140027](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24839549)):
  + Often multiple and superficial / subcutaneous
  + May have suture material, talc crystals, reactive fibrosis
