Definition / general

* Benign neoplasm derived from parathyroid parenchymal cells
* Typically involves one gland

Essential features

* Incidence increasing due to biochemical testing
* Diagnosis confirmed by a drop in parathyroid hormone (PTH) after surgical removal
* Atypical parathyroid adenoma displays histology concerning for but not diagnostic of malignancy and requires clinical follow up after excision
* Can be mistaken for thyroid follicular neoplasm by fine needle aspiration

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [D35.1](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D35-/D35.1) - Benign neoplasm of parathyroid gland

Epidemiology

* Incidence has increased over the past 50 years due to routine biochemical testing
* Accounts for over 85% of cases of primary hyperparathyroidism worldwide ([N Engl J Med 2011;365:2389](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22187986))
* Women more frequently affected than men
* Age range is broad but commonly 30s - 60s ([Lloyd: WHO Classification of Tumours of Endocrine Organs, 4th Edition, 2017](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/9283244931/pathologyoutl-20))

Sites

* Parathyroid gland, inferior glands slightly more common than superior
* Can also occur where ectopic / supernumerary parathyroid tissue may be found (thyroid gland, thymus, retroesophageal area, mediastinum, vagus nerve, carotid sheath)
* Double adenomas can occur, usually involving both superior parathyroid glands ([Surg Pathol Clin 2019;12:1007](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31672291))

Etiology

* Most are sporadic cases of unknown etiology
* Associated syndromes: hyperparathyroidism jaw tumor syndrome (*HRPT2* gene germ line mutation), multiple endocrine neoplasia (MEN1 more likely to have adenomas than MEN2A and MEN2B) ([Surg Pathol Clin 2019;12:1007](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31672291))
* Risk factors: radiation exposure, long term lithium therapy ([Endocr Rev 2019;40:711](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30476004), [Front Oncol 2019;9:1092](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31750236))

Clinical features

* Often detected early in asymptomatic patients due to routine serologic testing (see [laboratory](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/#parathyroidpthadenomalab) findings below)
* Symptoms of hyperparathyroidism: nephrolithiasis, osteopenia, osteitis fibrosa cystica, weakness, fatigue and psychiatric disturbances can occur if not detected early
* Rarely presents as a palpable mass ([J Med Case Rep 2019;13:332](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31722742))

Diagnosis

* Various imaging techniques can identify parathyroid nodules, including CT, MRI and ultrasound
* Technetium 99 sestamibi scintigraphy (99mTc) (see [radiology description](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/#parathyroidpthadenomaradio) below)
* Intraoperative parathyroid hormone (PTH) rapidly decreases after the abnormal gland is removed

Laboratory

* Serum parathyroid hormone (PTH) and calcium elevated, though usually not as high as in parathyroid carcinoma
  + Needle washouts after FNA can be used for PTH measurements ([Diagn Cytopathol 2020 Aug 24 [Online ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32833315))
* Hypophosphatemia and hypophosphaturia ([J Med Case Rep 2019;13:332](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31722742))

Radiology description

* Nodule posterior to thyroid gland
* 99mTc sestamibi:
  + Sestamibi accumulates in the mitochondria rich oxyphil cells of the parathyroid
  + Increased focal uptake may indicate an adenoma ([J Nucl Med 1992;33:1801](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1328564))

Prognostic factors

* Normally can be cured by surgical removal but recurrences can happen if not properly localized and excised
* Atypical adenomas are considered tumors of uncertain malignant potential and should be followed up clinically ([Surg Pathol Clin 2019;12:1007](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31672291))

Gross description

* Variable size, from < 1 cm to > 10 cm ([Rosai: Tumors of the Thyroid and Parathyroid Glands, Series 4](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B01FIXG2FK/pathologyoutl-20))

* Weight > 40 mg ([Endocr Pathol 2018;29:113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29626276))
* Solid yellow / tan, well circumscribed ovoid nodule
* No invasion into adjacent structures

Frozen section description

* Identification of parathyroid tissue is usually sufficient for intraoperative management, rather than trying to distinguish adenoma from hyperplasia

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Well circumscribed, frequently with thin fibrous capsule
* Absent or reduced stromal adipocytes
* Compressed nonneoplastic parathyroid tissue may be seen at edge
* Most commonly composed of chief cells (round nucleus, little granular cytoplasm)
* Follicle formation is not rare
* Mitoses and bizarre nuclei (endocrine atypia) may be focally present
* Variants:
  + Oxyphilic / oncocytic adenomas: composed entirely of oncocytic cells with abundant, eosinophilic granular cytoplasm ([Surg Pathol Clin 2019;12:1007](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31672291))
  + Water clear cell adenoma: cells have clear, glycogen containing cytoplasm ([Surg Pathol Clin 2019;12:1007](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31672291))
  + Lipoadenoma (hamartoma): contains stromal (adipose) and parenchymal (usually chief cells) elements; most of the tumor is adipose tissue ([Surg Pathol Clin 2019;12:1007](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31672291))
  + Atypical adenoma: contains borderline features concerning for (but not diagnostic of) malignancy ([Surg Pathol Clin 2019;12:1007](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31672291))
    - Dense fibrous bands with hemosiderin
    - Prominent nuclear atypia with spindled nuclei
    - Notable mitotic activity
    - Adherence to adjacent tissue
    - Necrosis
    - Solid or trabecular growth
    - No evidence of lymphovascular invasion, perineural invasion, invasion into adjacent structures or metastasis

Cytology description

* Cellular aspirates with uniform small cells in sheets, 3D clusters and trabecular arrangements
* Round dark nuclei with smooth nuclear borders and without nucleoli
* Salt and pepper chromatin ([Diagn Cytopathol 2020 Aug 24 [Online ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32833315))
* No colloid unless adjacent thyroid tissue is also aspirated
* More monotony than normal thyroid tissue
* Can be mistaken for a thyroid follicular neoplasm ([Diagn Cytopathol 2017;45:526](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28371486))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* *CDC73 (HRPT2)* in patients with hyperparathyroidism jaw tumor syndrome ([Endocr Pathol 2018;29:113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29626276))
* *MEN1* in multiple endocrine neoplasia and sporadic adenomas

Differential diagnosis

* Histology:
  + [Parathyroid hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/parathyroidpthhyper.html):
    - Typically involves all 4 glands
    - Diffuse enlargement rather than nodular growth with compressed rim of normal tissue
    - Distinction between hyperplasia and adenoma may be difficult on routine histology and definitive diagnosis is based on intraoperative
      [PTH](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/chemistryPTH.html) findings
  + [Parathyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/parathyroidpthca.html):
    - Definitive evidence of invasion or metastasis (vascular invasion, perineural invasion, invasion into adjacent structure)
    - Atypical mitotic figures
  + [Paraganglioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/adrenalparaganglioma.html):
    - Nested growth wrapped by sustentacular cells with increased vascularity
    - Sustentacular cells identified by [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) immunostain
    - Negative for [PTH](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/chemistryPTH.html)
* Cytology:
  + [Normal thyroid tissue](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidhistology.html) and [follicular neoplasms](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidfollicularneoplasm.html):
    - Less defined cell membranes with no visible intracellular lipid droplets ([Endocr Pathol 2018;29:113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29626276))
    - Positive for [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html) and [thyroglobulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsthyroglobulin.html) and negative for [PTH](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/chemistryPTH.html) and neuroendocrine markers ([Endocr Pathol 2018;29:113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29626276))
    - Serum [calcium](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalcium.html) and [PTH](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/chemistryPTH.html) not elevated
