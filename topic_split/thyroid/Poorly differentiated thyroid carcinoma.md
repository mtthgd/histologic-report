Definition / general

* Malignant follicular cell neoplasm with limited evidence of follicular cell differentiation
* Intermediate clinical behavior between well differentiated (papillary and follicular carcinoma) and anaplastic carcinoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1256](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17667551), [Surg Pathol Clin 2014;7:475](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26837549), [Lloyd: WHO Classification of Tumours of Endocrine Organs, 4th Edition, 2017](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/9283244931/pathologyoutl-20))

Essential features

* Intermediate grade follicular cell carcinoma with limited evidence of follicular cell differentiation

Terminology

* Insular / trabecular carcinoma
* Primordial cell carcinoma
* Poorly differentiated follicular carcinoma
* Poorly differentiated papillary carcinoma
* Solid type follicular carcinoma
* High risk thyroid carcinoma of follicular cell origin

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [C73](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C73-C75/C73-/C73) - malignant neoplasm of thyroid gland

Epidemiology

* Older patients, mean age 55 - 63 years
* 0.3 - 6.7% of thyroid carcinomas
* More common in Europe and South America than U.S. ([Mod Pathol 2010;23:1269](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20562850))

Etiology

* Iodine deficiency may be a risk factor; no association with radiation exposure ([Clin Oncol (R Coll Radiol) 2011;23:261](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21333507))
* Some tumors are de novo; some arise from dedifferentiation of follicular or papillary carcinoma

Clinical features

* Large solitary thyroid mass. Patient may have a history of recent growth in a longstanding
  uninodular or multinodular thyroid ([Am J Surg Pathol 1984;8:655](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6476195))
* Intermediate behavior between well differentiated and anaplastic carcinoma ([World J Surg 2007;31:934](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17431717))
* Has nodal and hematogenous metastases and 3 year survival of 38% ([Langenbecks Arch Surg 2007;392:671](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17216284))
* Extends to perithyroidal soft tissue in 60 - 70% cases
* Vascular invasion in 60 - 90% cases
* Regional lymph node metastasis in 15 - 65%
* Distant metastasis in 40 - 70%

Radiology description

* Ultrasound shows inhomogeneous hyoechoic mass ([Cancer 2006;106:1286](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16470605))
* Cold on scintigraphy and positive on FDG PET

Prognostic factors

* Tumors with overall 5 year survival rate: 60 - 70% ([J Clin Endocrinol Metab 2014;99:1245](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24512493))
* Poor prognosis associated with high stage and older age (> 45 years)

Case reports

* 4 year old girl with metastatic disease ([Am J Otolaryngol 2009;30:61](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19027516))
* 26 year old African American woman with benign biopsy but compressive symptoms ([Case #435](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/caseofweek/case435.htm))
* 57 year old woman with unusual short term complete response to chemotherapy
  ([J Clin Endocrinol Metab 2012;97:3046](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22723320))
* 60 year old woman with metastatic poorly differentiated thyroid carcinoma within an intracranial meningioma ([J Clin Endocrinol Metab 2014;99:3513](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24978673))
* 64 year old woman with tumor arising from ectopic thyroid ([Am J Clin Pathol 1995;104:408](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7572790))
* 75 year old woman with superior vena cava metastasis
  ([J Clin Oncol 2015;33:e119](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24752048))
* 80 year old woman with neck mass ([University of Pittsburgh: Case 24 - Neck Mass](http://path.upmc.edu/cases/case24.html))
* 82 year old woman with extensive extracellular mucin in tumor ([Hum Pathol 2005;36:698](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16021578))

Treatment

* Total thyroidectomy, neck dissection, radioactive iodine and suppressive thyroxine

Gross description

* Large (median size: 5 cm), grayish white, some show soft pale areas of necrosis
* Pushing margins, may be partially encapsulated
* Can have satellite nodules ([Am J Surg Pathol 1984;8:655](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6476195))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* **Turin consensus diagnostic criteria**:
  + Solid / trabecular / insular growth pattern
  + No nuclear features of papillary carcinoma
  + Presence of at least one of following: convoluted nuclei, ≥ 3 mitotic figures/10 HPF, tumor necrosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1256](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17667551))
* **Other**:

  - Prototypical type insular carcinoma: solid nests (may contain microfollicules) composed of small uniform cell with round hyperchromatic nuclei or convoluted nuclei, increased mitotic figures, necrosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 1984;8:655](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6476195))
  - Others tumors: solid nests composed of larger more pleomorphic tumor cells; may have oncocytic cells, clear cells, signet ring cells or rhabdoid cells
  - Component of well differentiated tumor (papillary or follicular carcinoma) may also be present
  - As few as 10% of poorly differentiated carcinomas (in otherwise well differentiated carcinomas) may be associated with unfavorable prognosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2011;35:1866](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21989341))
  - May have peritheliomatous pattern (tumor cells around blood vessels with necrosis of tumor cells further away from vessels), vascular and capsular invasion ([Lloyd: WHO Classification of Tumours of Endocrine Organs, 4th Edition, 2017](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/9283244931/pathologyoutl-20))

Cytology description

* Highly cellular, crowded cell clusters with solid, trabecular or insular morphology ([Cancer 2009;117:185](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19365842), [Cytopathology 2016;27:176](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26662642))
* Background of single cells with high N:C ratio
* May have necrotic background and increased mitotic figures

Differential diagnosis

* [Anaplastic thyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidUndiff.html):
  + Completely lacks follicular differentiation, prominent nuclear pleomorphism and necrosis
  + Generally thyroglobulin- [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html)- ([Surg Pathol Clin 2014;7:475](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26837549))
* [Hürthle cell tumors](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidhurthle.html):
  + Poorly differentiated thyroid carcinoma can be predominantly composed of oncocytic cells (often with a small cell component) but also has necrosis and ≥ 3 mitoses/10 HPF
* [Metastatic carcinoma to thyroid](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidmetastatic.html):
  + Pertinent tumor history
* [Medullary thyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidmedullary.html):
  + Also has nesting pattern
  + In addition, has prominent vasculature, granular cytoplasm and finely stippled chromatin, [calcitonin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalcitonin.html)+ [thyroglobulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsthyroglobulin.html)- with amyloid
* [Parathyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/parathyroidpthca.html):
  + [PTH](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/PTH.html)+
* [Solid variant papillary thyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidsolidvariant.html):
  + Typical papillary nuclear features throughout

Board review style question #1

Which of the following features are not required for the diagnosis of poorly differentiated thyroid carcinoma?

1. Absence of conventional nuclear features of papillary thyroid carcinoma
2. Necrosis / convoluted nuclei / increased mitotic activity
3. Solid, trabecular or insular growth
4. Vascular invasion

Board review style answer #1

**D.** Vascular invasion. The Turin criteria specify solid / trabecular / insular growth, lack of conventional nuclear
features of papillary thyroid carcinoma and one of the following: necrosis, convoluted
nuclei or increased mitotic activity (3 or more mitoses/10 HPF). Vascular invasion
may be seen in a variety of thyroid carcinomas and is an adverse prognostic factor
regardless of histologic subtype or grade.
