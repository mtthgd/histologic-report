Definition / general

* Densely fibrotic inflammatory process involving thyroid gland and adjacent neck tissue
* Described in 1896 by German surgeon Bernhard Moritz Carl Ludwig Riedel ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bernhard_Moritz_Carl_Ludwig_Riedel))
* Rare (0.05% of thyroidectomy specimens)

Terminology

* Also called Riedel struma, fibrous thyroiditis

Epidemiology

* Slight female predominance, usually age 40 - 60 years

Etiology

* Etiology unclear; may be part of generalized fibroinflammatory process also involving other organs ([Am J Clin Pathol 2004;121:S50](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15298150))

Clinical features

* Associated with inflammatory fibrosclerosis / multifocal systemic fibrosclerosis (mediastinal or retroperitoneal fibrosis, sclerosing cholangitis, inflammatory pseudotumor of orbit)
* 65% have antithyroid antibodies
* Clinically resembles carcinoma
* In one study, 67% had antithyroid antibodies, supporting an autoimmune mechanism of injury ([Am J Clin Pathol 1988;90:715](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3057862))

Case reports

* 41 year old woman with case resembling anaplastic carcinoma ([Int J Surg 2008;6:e24](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19059128))
* 44 year old woman with bilateral orbital pseudotumors ([J Fr Ophtalmol 2008;31:715.e1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18971848))
* 51 year old woman with prior subacute thyroiditis ([Endocr J 2007;54:559](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17603227))
* Two cases of Riedel thyroiditis, one had coexisting retroperitoneal fibrosis ([Am J Clin Pathol 1976;65:274](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/943929))

Treatment

* Surgery to decompress, steroids or tamoxifen ([Endocr Pract 2004;10:483](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16033720))

Gross description

* Extensive stony hard fibrosis involving a goitrous thyroid gland and infiltration into adjacent muscle and other structures, obliterating tissue planes at surgery
* Binds soft tissues of neck in an "iron collar," may compress trachea
* Tan / gray, woody and avascular, no lobules apparent

Microscopic (histologic) description

* No normal lobular pattern
* Follicles are obliterated or compressed by extensive dense fibrous tissue, which also infiltrates adjacent skeletal muscle
* Patchy lymphocytes (B & T cells), plasma cells (IgA, lambda) and eosinophils, inflammation in walls of trapped veins
* 25% have adenoma centrally in fibrous mass
* No oncocytic cells, no giant cells

Cytology description

* Moderate cellularity with fragments of fibrous tissue containing bland spindle cells and myofibroblasts ([Diagn Cytopathol 2004;30:193](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14986301))

Differential diagnosis

* [Fibrous variant of Hashimoto thyroiditis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidhashimotosthyroiditis.html): limited to thyroid, noninfiltrative, less abundant fibrous reaction, oncocytic cells present, more plasma cells, no granulocytes, no monocytes, no eosinophils ([J Endocrinol Invest 2003;26:444](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12906372))
* [Sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidsarcoma.html): atypical spindle cells ([J Clin Pathol 2001;54:570](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11429435))
* [Subacute thyroiditis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidgranuloma.html): late phase ([Ann Pathol 2008;28:263](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18928863))
