Definition / general

* **S**pindle **E**pithelial **T**umor with **T**hymus-**L**ike Differentiation
* Rare initially indolent tumor of neck in young patients (4 - 59 years old, median age 18 years), with delayed (after 5 years) metastases to lymph nodes or lungs, indolent even with metastasis ([Head Neck 2015;37:746](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24677409))

Essential features

* Low grade biphasic tumor with metastatic potential which occurs in young patients

Terminology

* Terminology first used in 1991 ([Hum Pathol 1991;22:349](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2050369))
* Synonym: Thyroid spindle cell tumor with mucinous cysts

Case reports

* 6 year old boy with circumscribed tumor with slightly gritty whorled appearance ([Hum Pathol 2003;34:190](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12612889))
* 20 year old woman with enlarged thyroid ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2006;130:405](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16519576))
* 59 year old man with enlarged thyroid for adult life and recent rapid enlargement of thyroid ([Mod Pathol 2000;13:1150](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11048811))

Treatment

* Thyroidectomy, radiotherapy and chemotherapy are effective in cases with unresectable tumor or metastasis

Gross description

* Circumscribed or infiltrative, lobulated, white / tan cut surface

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Encapsulated or infiltrative tumor separated by sclerotic stroma
* Biphasic pattern: spindle cells and epithelial structures (cords, tubules, papillae or glandular formation)
* Rare monomorphic variant can have spindle cells or glandular only ([Histopathology 1998;33:71](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9726052))
* No or rare lymphocytes
* Mitotic activity or focal necrosis are rare

Cytology description

* Tumor with minimal epithelial component was moderately cellular with single and loosely grouped spindle cells in homogeneous metachromatic material resembling amyloid ([Diagn Cytopathol 2007;35:113](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17230570))

Positive stains

* [34betaE12](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck34be12.html) (diffuse in both spindle and epithelial cells), [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html), [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html), [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html), [INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html), [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html)
* [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) (focally), [CAM 5.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscam52.html) (focally), rarely [TLE1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstle1.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:1179](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19417583))

Negative stains

* [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html), [calcitonin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalcitonin.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [thyroglobulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsthyroglobulin.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)

Differential diagnosis

* **Synovial sarcoma**: translocation t(x;18) (SSX; SS18 / SYT), increased mitotic figures, no / weak cytokeratin staining, no sclerosing stroma, no epithelial component
* [Medullary carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidmedullary.html): not biphasic, amyloid+, calcitonin+, chromogranin+
* [Anaplastic carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidUndiff.html): pleomorphic spindle cells, increased mitotic figures, necrosis
* [Ectopic thymoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/mediastinumthymoma.html): with TdT+ lymphocytes
* [Malignant teratoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidteratomas.html): with other immature tissue
