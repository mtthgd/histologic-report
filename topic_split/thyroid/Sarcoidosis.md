Definition / general

* Idiopathic multisystemic disease with noncaseating granulomas and predominantly pulmonary involvement (see [Lung nontumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/lungnontumorsarcoidosis.html) chapter)

Epidemiology

* Clinically recognizable thyroid involvement occurs in < 1% of sarcoidosis patients
* Autopsy series reports thyroid involvement in 4% of sarcoidosis patients ([Am J Med 1963;35:67](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14046006))

Clinical features

* Hypo- and euthyroidism are more common than hyperthyroidism ([Thyroid 2006;16:1175](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17123346))
* May be manifested as or mimic thyroiditis, nodular and diffuse goiter, and thyroid cancer ([Arq Bras Endocrinol Metabol 2012;56:209](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22666738))

Diagnosis

**Diagnosis / Radiology**

* Presence of noncaseating granulomas in the gland and the evidence of generalized sarcoidosis
* Isolated sarcoidosis of thyroid is a diagnosis of exclusion - further radiographic evaluation for systemic disease is suggested
* Granulomas often occur as cold nodules

Laboratory

* Serum angiotensin converting enzyme (ACE) level is elevated and represents total body granuloma load

Case reports

* 23 year old woman with Graves' thyrotoxicosis and concomitant sarcoidosis ([Endocr Pract 2007;13:159](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17490930))
* 51 year old man - first report of thyroid sarcoidosis ([Arch Intern Med (Chic) 1938;62:285](http://jamanetwork.com/journals/jamainternalmedicine/article-abstract/543917))
* 59 year old man with granulomatous thyroiditis ([Acta Biomed 2004;75:69](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15315090))
* 65 year old woman with multinodular goiter as the initial presentation of systemic sarcoidosis ([Respir Care 2011;56:1029](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21352663))
* Hürthle cell hyperplasia and sarcoidosis of the thyroid ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1991;115:1044](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1898233))
* Two cases with Graves' disease ([Arq Bras Endocrinol Metabol 2012;56:209](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22666738))

**Isolated sarcoidosis of the thyroid**:

* 42 year old woman with sarcoidosis presenting as cold thyroid nodules ([Surg Today 2005;35:770](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16133673))
* 54 year old man with asymptomatic nontoxic thyromegaly ([Endocr Pract 2013;19:e40](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23337134))
* Sarcoidosis of the thyroid gland initially diagnosed as malignancy ([Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 2003;129:154](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12869935))
* Thyroid sarcoidosis as a unique localization ([Thyroid 2006;16:1175](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17123346))
* Two cases of thyroid sarcoidosis associated with hyperthyroidism ([J Endocrinol Invest 2006;29:834](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17114917))

**Thyroid sarcoidosis with thyroid cancer**:- 35 year old woman with papillary thyroid carcinoma with sarcoidosis ([Neth J Med 2007;65:185](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17519514))
- Thyroid cancer and sarcoidosis ([Sarcoidosis Vasc Diffuse Lung Dis 2014;31:239](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25363225))

Treatment

* In patients with compression symptoms, hyperthyroidism, resistance to antithyroid drugs and RAI treatment thyroidectomy is performed

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Multiple scattered noncaseating, epithelioid granulomas with Langhans giant cells and lymphocytes, nonspecific
* Interstitial (rather than intrafollicular) location, usually not surrounding colloid
* Associated sarcoid granulomas in the extrathyroidal tissue such as the lymph nodes, parathyroids and adjacent muscle ([Thyroid 2006;16:1175](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17123346))

Cytology description

* Granulomas with multinucleated cells, nonspecific ([Respir Care 2011;56:1029](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21352663))
* Epithelioid cells and lymphocytes

Negative stains

* [GMS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgms.html); [acid fast stains](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsacidfast.html)

Differential diagnosis

* Granulomatous diseases:
  + [Tuberculosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidinfectious.html#tb): AFB+, caseating necrosis
  + **Fungi**: positive for GMS, PAS
  + **Other rare lesions**: atypical mycobacteria, granulomatous vasculitis
* [Subacute](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidgranuloma.html) and [palpation](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidpalpation.html) thyroiditis with involvement of follicles and engulfment of colloid
* [Post FNA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidFNA.html) effects and foreign body granulomas
* **Sarcoid reaction in thyroid carcinoma**: a noncaseating granuloma found in the primary tumor, its vicinity or within the lymph nodes draining the neoplasm when evidence of systemic sarcoidosis does not exist ([Endocr J 1997;44:697](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9466325)); may have similar findings in autoimmune thyroiditis ([Endocr Pract 2013;19:e40](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23337134))
