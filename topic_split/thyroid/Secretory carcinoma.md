Definition / general

* Rare salivary gland type tumor arising in thyroid
* First described by Stevens et al. in 2015 ([Mod Pathol 2015;28:1084](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26089091))

Essential features

* Recently described rare cancer, originally reported in salivary glands, which is associated with ETV6-NTRK3 translocation
* Highly reminiscent of papillary thyroid carcinoma with oncocytic features, can be suspected by the presence of microcystic / cribriform pattern
* Important to differentiate from thyroid cancer because of likely aggressive course and ineffectiveness of radioactive iodine / RAI therapy
* Diagnosis: Mammaglobin+, Thyroglobulin-, TTF1-; also confirmed by molecular testing

Terminology

* MASC: mammary analog / analogue secretory carcinoma

Epidemiology

* Very rare; fewer than 10 cases described to date ([Head Neck Pathol 2016 Sep 15 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27638813))
* Middle age (range, 36 - 74 years old, mean 57 years old); F:M is 2.5

Sites

* MASC was originally described in salivary glands, and shares the same histology, immunophenotype and molecular background as secretory carcinoma of breast ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:599](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20410810))
* Reported in major salivary glands (parotid > submandibular), occasionally in other head / neck locations (lip, palate and thyroid), also in skin

Pathophysiology / etiology

* Origin is debated ([Head Neck Pathol 2016 Jul 11 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27400918), [Mod Pathol 2016;29:985](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27282352)):
  + Primary thyroid tumor from ectopic salivary gland remnants, intra- or perithyroidal
  + Metastatic from occult primary (salivary glands, breast)
  + May have the same origin with papillary thyroid cancer, some MASCs coexist with PTC and share molecular aberrations
* Initiating molecular event is a chromosomal translocation t(12;15)(p12;q25) resulting in the [ETV6-NTRK3 gene fusion](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsntrk.html)
* Chimeric protein is a constitutively active protein tyrosine kinase with transformation activity
* ETV6-NTRK3 fusion is found in several malignancies other than MASC, e.g., secretory carcinoma of the breast, congenital fibrosarcoma, cellular or mixed mesoblastic nephroma, myeloid leukemias and papillary thyroid carcinoma (2% sporadic, 14% radiation induced) ([Head Neck Pathol 2016;10:405](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27075025))

ETV6-NTRK3 fusion

Clinical features

* Invasive / locally aggressive growth with nodal metastasis, 2 out of 7 reported cases were disseminated and fatal (in contrast to the indolent MASC of salivary gland)
* Frequent recurrences

Diagnosis

* On histopathology, confirmed by IHC and molecular testing
* Occult primary (breast, salivary glands) should be considered

Laboratory

* Recurrence after total thyroidectomy produce no elevated serum thyroglobulin

Radiology description

* Non RAI avid, both primary tumor and relapses / metastases

Prognostic factors

* Probably more aggressive than follicular derived, well differentiated thyroid tumors

Case reports

* 36 year old woman with MASC apparently arising in thyroid which progressed to fatal disseminated disease ([Head Neck Pathol 2016;10;405](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27075025))

Treatment

* Surgical excision, usually total thyroidectomy with lymph node dissection
* Does not respond to RAI ([Head Neck Pathol 2016;10;405](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27075025))
* ETV6-NTRK3 is a "druggable" fusion, Trk inhibitors are currently used for MASC patients in clinical trials ([Mod Pathol 2016;29:985](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27282352))

Gross description

* Poorly circumscribed solid white tan nodules ([Head Neck Pathol 2016 Jul 11 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27400918))
* Large (range = 2.4 - 7.0 cm, mean = 4.3 cm)
* Often extrathyroidal extension ([Mod Pathol 2016;29:985](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27282352))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Invasive growth with multiple nodules in fibrotic stroma
* Complex architecture with microcystic / cribriform, solid, trabecular, tubular and papillary growth patterns
* Polygonal eosinophilic cells with vacuolated cytoplasm:
  + Prominent single centrally placed nucleolus
  + Abundant nuclear grooves (in all architectural patterns), rare pseudoinclusions
  + Focal vesicular nuclei with chromatin clearing
  + Sometimes apocrine-like capitations
  + Cellular atypia is mild to focally moderate, rarely may have high grade features (increased mitoses and necrosis)
* Intraluminar (sometimes intracytoplasmic) colloid-like mucin secretions, from slightly pink watery to purple dense
* Stroma:
  + Extensive fibrosis
  + Few psammoma bodies
  + Lymphoplasmacytic infiltrate and chronic lymphocytic thyroiditis in adjacent thyroid tissue
* Minor papillary thyroid carcinoma component can exist, either classic or follicular variant ([Mod Pathol 2016;29:985](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27282352))

Immunophenotype

Cytology description

* Hypercellular smear with sheets, caps, blunted papillary structures without fibrovascular cores ("tentacular nubbins") and loss of polarity ([Diagn Cytopathol 2017;45:45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27633961))
* Round to oval nuclei with prominent grooves, vesicular to powdery chromatin, single central nucleoli and rare nuclear pseudoinclusions
* Abundant granular cytoplasm with vacuolation
* No increased colloid

Positive stains

* [Mammaglobin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmammaglobin.html)
* [GATA3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgata3.html), [DOG1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsDOG1.html)
* [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) is consistently positive, [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html) variable, which is overlapped with papillary thyroid carcinoma (not helpful for differential diagnosis)
* [Mucicarmine](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmucins.html), [PAS and PASD](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html) for secretions (mucin)
* Low [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) (1 - 7%) ([Mod Pathol 2016;29:985](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27282352))

Negative stains

* [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html) and [Tg](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsthyroglobulin.html)

Electron microscopy description

* Large number of secretory granules with extrusion into the intercellular spaces, well developed endoplasmic reticulum, lipid laden vacuoles and well formed microvilli ([Int J Surg Pathol 2017;25:127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27670353))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* FISH (on paraffin sections) to detect chromosome translocation t(12;15)(p12;q25)
* RT-PCR or NGS (RNA template) to detect ETV6-NTRK3 gene fusion

Differential diagnosis

* Both histology and cytology closely mimics [papillary thyroid cancer](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidpapillary.html)
* [Oncocytic PTC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidoncocytic.html):
  + Histology rarely shows unusual patterns (cribriform / microcystic), no intracytoplasmic secretions and capitation
  + Absence of tentacular nubbins in smears
  + Immunophenotype is inconsistent with MASC, IHC panel with mammaglobin and Tg / TTF1 can be used on surgical samples and FNA (cell block)
* [Poorly differentiated thyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidinsular.html): high grade features like mitoses and necrosis, TTF1+ / Tg+ (evident or residual)
* Metastasis from [salivary MASC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/salivaryglandssecretory.html) and [secretory carcinoma of breast](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmalignantjuvenile.html) should be excluded clinically (history, imaging)
