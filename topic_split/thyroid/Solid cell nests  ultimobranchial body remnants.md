Definition / general

* Small (< 1 mm) interfollicular clusters of epithelial cells resembling squamous / transitional epithelium
* First described by Getzowa in 1907 ([Virchows Archiv 1907;188:181](http://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/BF01945893))
* Considered as normal thyroid gland components ([J Clin Endocrinol Metab 1997;82:4274](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9432519), [Acta Anat (Basel) 1992;143:79](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1374994))
* Remnants of the ultimobranchial body derived from branchial apparatus (fourth / fifth pharyngeal pouch complexes) and giving rise to C cells ([Am J Clin Pathol 1994;101:186](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7509563), [Lab Invest 2011;91:138](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20697379))

Terminology

* Also known as ultimobranchial body remnants, ultimobranchial body nests
* Solid cell nests are the ultimobranchial body remnants in postnatal life
* Rarely called compact cell nests, solid cell rests

Epidemiology

* Adults: from 3% of routinely examined thyroids to 60% of the glands subjected to serial blocking ([Acta Anat (Basel) 1986;127:262](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3811818))
* 30% to 90% of serially sectioned fetal thyroids ([J Pathol 1993;169:465](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7684778), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 1990;114:1049](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2222147))
* Male predominance ([Acta Anat (Basel) 1986;127:262](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3811818), [Acta Anat (Basel) 1992;143:79](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1374994))

Sites

* Located in the middle or occasionally in the upper third of the thyroid lateral lobes and placed in a central to paracentral and slightly dorsal longitudinal axis ([J Pathol 1988;155:191](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3045277))

Clinical features

* May play a stem cell role in adult thyroid gland ([Mod Pathol 2004;17:819](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15044923), [Endocr Pathol 2011;22:35](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21234707))
* May be site of origin of CASTLE tumors ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:994](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16861971), [Thyroid 2013;23:511](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23078135)), primary thyroid mucoepidermoid carcinoma ([Mod Pathol 2004;17:526](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15001991))
* Histogenetic link to thyroid cancer is debated ([Hum Pathol 2004;35:465](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15116328), [Hum Pathol 2005;36:590](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15948129), [Am J Clin Pathol 2012;137:612](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22431538))

Diagnosis

* Usually an incidental microscopic finding

Case reports

* 47 year old man with ultimobranchial body remnants ([J Clin Endocrinol Metab 2012;97:2209](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22549936))
* 48 year old man with BRAF mutation in solid cell nest hyperplasia ([Hum Pathol 2009;40:1029](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19269016))
* 58 year old man with carcinoma showing thymus-like elements arising in close association with solid cell nests ([Thyroid 2013;23:511](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23078135))
* 65 year old man with branchial-like cysts of the thyroid associated with solid cell nests ([Pathol Int 2006;56:150](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16497248))
* 70 year old man with absence of the BRAF and GRIM-19 mutations in oncocytic solid cell nests ([Am J Clin Pathol 2012;137:612](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22431538))
* 70 year old man with thyroid tumor-in-tumor with basaloid differentiation and phenotype similar to solid cell nests ([Int J Surg Pathol 2011;19:276](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21216781))
* Giant solid cell rest of thyroid ([Int J Surg Pathol 2009;17:268](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19443890))
* Solid cell nest in fine needle aspiration of goiter ([Diagn Cytopathol 2002;27:66](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12112820))
* Thyroid type solid cell nests in struma ovarii ([Int J Surg Pathol 2011;19:627](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20034983))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Small cells in solid structures (50 - 1000 μm) interspersed between follicles
* Usually surrounded by stroma and demarcated by adjacent thyroid follicles
* Lobulated, nested or irregular shape on low power
* Multiple foci (2 - 8 per section) are common ([Histopathology 2016;68:866](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26334919))
* Composed of two cell populations - predominant main cells and a minority of C cells
  + Sometimes with intermingled cystic structures containing mucin and mixed follicles
* **Main cells:**
  + Polygonal / elongated to round to spindle cells
  + Scant amphophilic or rarely deeply eosinophilic cytoplasm with squamoid features (but no intercellular bridges)
  + Nuclei are centrally located, oval to fusiform, with finely granular chromatin, uneven nuclear membrane, occasional nuclear grooves
* **C cells:** minor population of cells with clear cytoplasm and centrally located, small compact nuclei
* May contain ciliated columnar cells
* May also contain cells with follicular lumen-like pattern (mixed follicles)
* Usually have a tropism to lymphocytes because of the common embryologic derivation of SCNs and the thymus ([Pathol Int 2006;56:150](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16497248), [Hum Pathol 2009;40:1029](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19269016))
* C cells in small clusters are common in the vicinity of solid cell nests
* Cartilage and adipose tissue are rarely seen ([Hum Pathol 1994;25:684](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7517912))
* Asioli et al classified SCN based on the solid (types 1 - 2) or cystic (types 3 - 4) patterns ([Endocr Pathol 2009;20:197](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19809803)):
  + **Type 1** (floret-like) is composed of main cells, characterized by round to oval or elongated cells with scant cytoplasm, centrally located oval to fusiform nuclei, and occasional nuclear grooves
    - The cells form round to oval groups, surrounded by lymphocytes
  + **Type 2** SCN (epidermoid-like) are made of larger, polygonal cells with an epidermoid appearance
  + **Type 3** has cystic architecture lined by flattened or polygonal cells
  + **Type 4** SCNs (mixed follicles) have a follicular appearance and contain both follicular epithelium and small main cells

Cytology description

* Cohesive cellular group consisting of polygonal squamoid cells with lightly eosinophilic cytoplasm (no intercellular bridges), elongated to columnar shaped cells with clear cytoplasm, and scattered lymphocytes
* Nucleus is large and centrally located, slightly irregular with evenly distributed chromatin and chromocenter ([Hum Pathol 2004;35:465](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15116328))

Positive stains

* **Main cells:**
  + [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) ([Mod Pathol 2003;16:43](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12527712), [Hum Pathol 2004;35:465](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15116328)), [p40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdeltaNp63.html) ([Histopathology 2016;68:866](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26334919))
  + [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html) (variable)
  + [bcl2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html)
  + Cytokeratins ([AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [CK19](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck19.html), [CAM5.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscam52.html), [34βE12](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck34be12.html))
  + [Galectin 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgalectin3.html) ([J Clin Pathol 2003;56:142](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12560396))
* **C cells:**
  + Calcitonin (may leak into adjacent cells), calcitonin gene related peptide, [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html)
  + [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html)
* **Lumina:** [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html)+ colloid

Negative stains

* **Main cells:**
  + [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html), [thyroglobulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsthyroglobulin.html)
  + [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html)
  + [HBME1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshbme.html) (inconsistent across different studies, e.g. [Am J Clin Pathol 2010;134:169](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20551283), [Histopathology 2016;68:866](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26334919))
* **C cells:** [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html)

Electron microscopy description

* **Main cells:** desmosomes, intermediate filaments, intracytoplasmic vacuoles and projections ([Acta Anat (Basel) 1987;129:289](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2442951))
* **C cells:** neurosecretory granules; also ciliated cells, lymphocytes ([Ultrastruct Pathol 2000;24:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10721146))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* No *BRAF* mutations found ([Am J Clin Pathol 2012;137:612](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22431538), [Histopathology 2016;68:866](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26334919))

Differential diagnosis

* [C cell hyperplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidccell.html) and [medullary microcarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidmedullary.html): uniformly positive for calcitonin; p63 negative
* [Intrathyroidal thymic remnants](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidthymictissue.html): Hassall corpuscles
* [Papillary microcarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidmicrocarcinomavariant.html): TTF1 and thyroglobulin positive, p63 negative
  + Not recommended to rely on CK19 / galectin 3 (positive in both SCN and papillary microcarcinoma)
* [Primary or metastatic squamous carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidSCC.html): cellular atypia, mitotic activity, stromal desmoplasia
* Squamous metaplasia in [Hashimoto thyroiditis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidhashimotosthyroiditis.html): associated with chronic inflammation (p63 positive and TTF1 positive)
* **Tangential sections of normal follicles**
* [Thyroglossal duct cyst](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidthyroglossal.html): midline location (e.g. thyroid isthmus)
