Definition / general

* Solitary fibrous tumor (SFT) of the thyroid is a fibroblastic mesenchymal neoplasm characterized by *NAB2::STAT6* gene fusion
* Thyroid SFT is indistinguishable from pleural or other extrapleural solitary fibrous tumors

Essential features

* Fibroblastic tumor composed of collagen producing spindle cells arranged in a characteristic vascular pattern
* Immunohistochemical nuclear expression of STAT6 is highly sensitive and specific for the presence of the *NAB2::STAT6* gene fusion product; STAT6 is a marker for confirming the diagnosis of solitary fibrous tumor

Terminology

* Extrapleural solitary fibrous tumor
* Not recommended: hemangiopericytoma; giant cell angiofibroma; benign solitary fibrous tumor

ICD coding

* ICD-O
  + [8815/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=80) - solitary fibrous tumor, benign
  + [8815/1](https://seer.cancer.gov/tools/solidtumor/2023/STM_Combined.pdf#page=294) - solitary fibrous tumor, NOS
  + [8815/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=80) - solitary fibrous tumor, malignant
* ICD-10: [D48.7](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D37-D48/D48-/D48.7) - neoplasm of uncertain behavior of other specified sites

Epidemiology

* Rare tumor in thyroid; < 0.1% of SFTs arise in the head and neck area ([Thyroid 2010;20:435](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20373988))
* M = F
* Median age: 54.4 years (28 - 88 years) ([Head Neck Pathol 2019;13:597](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30758754))

Sites

* Entire thyroid

Etiology

* Unknown
* Not associated with a longstanding pre-existing goiter or thyroid tumor
* Possibly primitive mesenchymal cells capable of myofibroblastic, adipose and hemangiopericytic differentiation ([Nose: Diagnostic Pathology - Endocrine, 2nd Edition, 2018](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/032352480X/pathologyoutl-20))

Clinical features

* Painless, slow growing neck mass in euthyroid patients ([Nose: Diagnostic Pathology - Endocrine, 2nd Edition, 2018](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/032352480X/pathologyoutl-20))
* Tumor may extend inferiorly from the thyroid gland and present as a mediastinal mass ([Med J Islam Repub Iran 2014;28:51](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25405117))
* Compressive symptoms and respiratory failure may occur in some rare cases ([Ann Thorac Cardiovasc Surg 2014;20:427](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23445791), [Saudi Med J 2004;25:805](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15195217))

Diagnosis

* Based on histological findings and *NAB2::STAT6* gene fusion (immunohistochemical or molecular tests)

Laboratory

* Serum thyroid function tests such as thyroid stimulating hormone (TSH), triiodothyronine (T3), free thyroxine (T4) and thyroperoxidase (TPO) antibodies are normal ([Endocr Pathol 2011;22:165](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21818669))

Radiology description

* Ultrasonography: heterogeneous, mainly hyperechoic nodule ([World J Clin Cases 2020;8:782](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32149061))
* Computed tomography (CT): heterogeneous enhanced lesion ([Clinical Imaging 2019;53:105](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30317135))
* Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI): fibrous areas have intermediate intensity on T1 while cellular or myxoid zones present as variable hypointensity to hyperintensity on T2 ([Trans Gastroenterol Hepatol 2018;3:94](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30603730))

Prognostic factors

* High mitotic index, tumor cellularity, necrosis, nuclear pleomorphism correlated with metastatic or local recurrence potential
* Difficult to predict patient's clinical course if based solely on histologic parameters ([Ann Surg Oncol 2017;24:3865](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29039030))
* Age ≥ 55, tumor size ≥ 10 cm, mitotic count ≥ 4/10 high power fields, tumor necrosis ≥ 10% are unfavorable prognostic factors in SFTs of all anatomic sites ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:1433](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28731041))
* *TERT* promoter mutations may be associated with a poor prognosis ([Mod Pathol 2016;29:1511](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27562490), [Histopathology 2018;73:843](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29985536))

Treatment

* Total thyroidectomy or lobectomy ([Laryngoscope 2009;119:2306](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19806654), [Med Mol Morphol 2014;47:117](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24013381))
* Roles of chemotherapy or radiation therapy are controversial ([Diagn Cytopathol 2011;39:694](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21837658))
* Close follow up after surgery seems to be the most commonly used approach

Gross description

* Well circumscribed mass
* Relatively large (5 - 10 cm)
* White to gray-brown color, usually solid, sometimes with cystic spaces ([Head Neck Pathol 2019;13:597](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30758754))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Similar to SFT of any other organ
* Usually unencapsulated
* Proliferation of spindled cells with alternating hypo and hypercellular areas
  + Tumor cells are spindled with elongated, slender nuclei surrounded by scant cytoplasm; nuclear chromatin is delicate, fine to vesicular
  + Patternless architecture interspersed with loosely to densely collagenized or myxoid stroma
* Highly vascular, with dilated, branching, hyalinized staghorn-like (hemangiopericytoma-like), thin walled vessels
* Infiltration by inflammatory cells (mainly mast cells) can be seen
* Mitoses and nuclear atypia are rare; necrosis is absent or rare
* Uncommon: cysts, myxoid change, lipomatous features ([Thompson: Diagnostic Pathology - Head and Neck, 2nd Edition, 2016](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0323392555/pathologyoutl-20))
* High mitotic index (> 4 mitoses per 10 high power fields); hypercellularity, nuclear atypia; hemorrhage and necrosis are suggestive of malignant transformation ([Am J Surg Pathol 1998;22:1501](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9850176))

Cytology description

* Smears contain loosely cohesive clusters around blood vessels or dispersed cells with bare nuclei; the cells are oval, elongated or rounded with thin cytoplasm and eosinophilic collagenous stroma ([Virchows Arch 2023 Apr 20 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37079094))
* Adjacent follicular epithelium often shows features compatible with atypia of undetermined significance or follicular lesions of undetermined significance ([Diagn Cytopathol 2003;28:213](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12672098), [Med Mol Morphol 2014;47:117](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24013381))
* Liquid based cytology specimens show only dispersed monomorphic spindle cells with bland nuclear features ([Virchows Arch 2023 Apr 20 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37079094))

Positive stains

* [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html)
  + The most sensitive and specific marker for establishing a diagnosis of SFT ([Am J Clin Pathol 2015;143:672](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25873501), [Pathology 2014;46:389](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24977739))
  + Strong nuclear expression reflects underlying *STAT6* gene fusion
* [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [CD99](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd99.html), [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html): not specific
* [Actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsactinmusclespecific.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) may be focal

Negative stains

* Thyroid specific markers: [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html), [thyroglobulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsthyroglobulin.html), [calcitonin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalcitonin.html)
* [Cytokeratins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)
* [Desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html), [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html), [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Multiple *NAB2::STAT6* gene fusion variants detected by whole exome sequencing and RT-PCR ([Am J Pathol 2014;184:1209](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24513261))
* Due to the proximity of the 2 genes on chromosome 12, *NAB2::STAT6* gene fusion may be missed by fluorescence in situ ([J Clin Pathol 2017;70:508](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27802414))
* Moderate to strong and diffuse immunohistochemistry expression of STAT6 correlated with the *NAB2::STAT6* gene fusion ([Pathol Res Pract 2017;213:1404](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28869107))

Sample pathology report

* Thyroid, completion thyroidectomy:
  + Solitary fibrous tumor of thyroid, 35 mm, margins of resection are uninvolved (see comment)
  + Comment: The histological sections show a well circumscribed mass made of moderately cellular spindle cells in a collagenous stroma with prominent dilated staghorn type vasculature. No mitosis or necrosis is seen. Benign thyroid parenchyma is uninvolved and compressed at periphery of the tumor. Immunohistochemistry studies show that the tumor cells are positive for STAT6, CD34 and BCL2 and negative for TTF1, thyroglobulin, calcitonin, cytokeratins, desmin and S100. The overall findings favor a solitary fibrous tumor, likely thyroid primary. According to the 2017 risk stratification criteria by Demicco et. al., this should be classified as a low risk solitary fibrous tumor.

Differential diagnosis

* [Post-fine needle aspiration spindle cell nodules](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidpostop.html):
  + Localized phenomenon adjacent to fine needle aspirated thyroid nodule with hemosiderin, extravasated erythrocytes, reactive vascular pattern
* [Follicular thyroid adenoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidfollicularadenoma.html) with prominent spindle cells:
  + Lacks collagen while showing colloid production ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2012;5:143](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22400075))
  + Positive: [cytokeratins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html), [thyroglobulin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsthyroglobulin.html)
* [Hyalinizing trabecular tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidhtn.html):
  + Hyalinization is intra and intercellular
  + Trabecular arrangement of follicular epithelial cells: perpendicular nuclei, inclusions and yellow bodies
  + Positive: [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html), membranous [MIB1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html)
* **Smooth muscle tumors**:
  + Fascicular pattern of growth comprised of spindle shaped cells with blunt ended nuclei
  + Positive: [actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsactinmusclespecific.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html)
  + Negative: [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html), [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html), [BCL2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcl2.html)
* [Riedel thyroiditis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidriedel.html):
  + Follicles are compressed by extensive dense fibrous tissue, which also infiltrates adjacent skeletal muscle
  + Patchy lymphocytes, plasma cells, eosinophils
  + > 40% [IgG4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsigg4.html) / [IgG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsiga.html)+ cells and elevated [IgG4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsigg4.html)+ plasma cells
* [Schwannoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueschwannoma.html):
  + Antoni A and B areas, wavy nuclei and tapered cells
  + Positive: [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html)
* [Medullary thyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidmedullary.html):
  + Medullary thyroid carcinoma of spindle cell variant may be similar morphologically to SFT
  + Positive: [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html), [calcitonin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscalcitonin.html), [TTF1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsttf1.html), [cytokeratins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html)
  + Presence of amyloid
* [Spindle epithelial tumor with thymus-like differentiation](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidsettle.html):
  + Biphasic pattern: spindled epithelial cells merge into glandular structures
  + Rare monomorphic variant can have spindle cells or glandular only
  + Young patients
  + Positive: [34 beta E12](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck34be12.html), [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html)
  + Negative: [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html)
* [Paucicellular variant of anaplastic thyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidUndiff.html):
  + Acellular or necrotic fibrous tissue with hypocellular foci of mildly atypical spindle cells obliterating large blood vessels
  + Positive: [cytokeratins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html), [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html), [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html)

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/thyroidsftMicroNguyen10.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/thyroidsftMicroNguyen10.jpg)

A 50 year old woman presented with a 4.5 cm, well circumscribed, firm mass in the right lobe of thyroid. Histologic sections showed ovoid to spindled cells arranged haphazardly around prominent thin walled, hyalinized, dilated staghorn type vasculature (see image above). Which of the following is the diagnosis of this thyroid tumor?

1. Medullary carcinoma
2. Riedel thyroiditis
3. Solitary fibrous tumor
4. Spindle epithelial tumor with thymus-like differentiation
5. Anaplastic thyroid carcinoma, paucicellular variant

Board review style answer #1

**C.** Solitary fibrous tumor. This case has gross appearance and histological features consistent with solitary fibrous tumor of thyroid. Differential diagnosis includes other thyroid tumors with the spindle cell pattern.

Board review style question #2

Which of the following is the most sensitive and specific immunohistochemical marker for solitary fibrous tumor of the thyroid?

1. CD34
2. Cytokeratin CAM 5.2
3. STAT6
4. Thyroglobulin
5. TTF1

Board review style answer #2

**C.** STAT6. STAT6 is a highly sensitive and specific marker for the presence of the *NAB2::STAT6* gene fusion product, which is identified to be the pathognomonic molecular aberration in solitary fibrous tumor.
