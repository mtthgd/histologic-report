Definition / general

* Teratoma is a germ cell tumor characterized by the presence of 3 germ cell layers: ectoderm, endoderm and mesoderm
* Common in newborns and infants (usually benign), rare in adolescence, usually malignant in adults ([Cancer 2000;88:1149](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10699906))

Sites

* Only 3 - 6% of teratomas occur in head and neck region ([Endocrine 2006;30:231](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17322585))

Clinical features

* Thyroid mass, usually normal thyroid function

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Neuro / ectodermal tissue: squamous epithelium, glial tissue
* Endodermal tissue: GI epithelium, thyroid and salivary glands
* Mesodermal tissue: bone and cartilage
* Presence of immature tissue or neuroepithelium makes it a malignant immature teratoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2005;29:700](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15832097))
