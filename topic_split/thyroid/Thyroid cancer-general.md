Definition / general

* 1% of all cancer in U.S., 0.2% of all cancer deaths
* Estimated 56,000 new cases and 1800 deaths in U.S. in 2012 ([SEER](https://seer.cancer.gov/statfacts/html/thyro.html))
* Increasing incidence due to new diagnostic practices which detect smaller tumors ([Cancer Causes Control 2008;19:585](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18240001), [BMC Cancer 2006;6:284](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17156468), [BMC Cancer 2006 Apr 24;6:102](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16635261)), although increase may be abating ([Eur J Endocrinol 2009;160:71](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18952764))

Epidemiology

* Often estrogen receptor positive, which may explain female predominance of tumors in reproductive years (for other ages, incidence in males and females is similar, [Mod Pathol 2003;16:437](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12748250), [Arch Pathol Lab Med 1991;115:1203](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1768209))
* More common in U.S. whites than blacks, for unknown reasons ([Ann Surg Oncol 2008;15:1169](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18246401))
* Increased risk for children exposed to ionizing radiation ([Cancer Causes Control 2009;20:75](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18766448)), particularly under age 1 year ([Best Pract Res Clin Endocrinol Metab 2008;22:1061](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19041832)), women atomic bomb survivors in Japan ([Epidemiology 2009;20:181](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19177023))

Prognostic factors

* 20 year survival is 90%, because most are indolent papillary carcinomas
* **Good prognostic factors**:
  + Men under age 40 years or women under age 50 / 60
  + Favorable histologic types
* **Poor prognostic factors**:
  + Men age 41+ or women 51+
  + Large tumor size, nuclear pleomorphism, tumor necrosis, vascular invasion, increased mitotic activity, higher stage, unfavorable subtypes ([Am J Surg Pathol 2002;26:1007](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12170087))
* Death usually from undifferentiated, poorly differentiated, Hürthle cell or medullary carcinoma, due to distant metastases
* Poor survival in those with bone metastases (5 year: 29%, 10 year: 13%, [Arch Pathol Lab Med 2000;124:1440](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11035572))

Treatment

* See also subtypes - either lobectomy, subtotal thyroidectomy or total thyroidectomy; also postoperative radioiodine therapy, TSH suppressive therapy, new molecular therapies ([Ther Clin Risk Manag 2008;4:935](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19209276))
* Can assess for recurrence with serum thyroglobulin levels (different assays show good agreement between themselves, [Clin Biochem 2009;42:416](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19154731)), calcitonin (medullary carcinoma)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Rearrangements of RET-PTC in 40% of papillary carcinomas, rearrangements of PAX8-PPARy in 40% of follicular carcinomas, BRAF mutations in 40 - 60% of papillary carcinomas ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2008;132:164](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18251569))
