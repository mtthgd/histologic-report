Definition / general

* Multinodular goiter plus hyperthyroidism ("toxic")
* #2 cause of hyperthyroidism after Graves disease, #1 in elderly, particularly in iodine deficient areas ([J Endocrinol Invest 2002;25:16](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12508907))

Terminology

* Also called Plummer's disease (particularly if single nodule)
* **Toxic nodular goiter**: also called toxic adenoma, may arise in background of multinodular goiter

Epidemiology

* Usually older patients rather than those with nontoxic multinodular goiter, with long evolution time ([Surg Today 2005;35:901](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16249841))

Clinical features

* Associated with atrial fibrillation, tachycardia, weakness and muscle wasting, only rarely with eye disease

Laboratory

* Slight increase in T3 / T4 (less than Graves disease), reduced TSH

Treatment

* Radioactive iodine, total thyroidectomy if obstructive symptoms or large ([World J Surg 2008;32:1278](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18357484))

Gross description

* Enlarged thyroid gland with multiple nodules exhibiting fibrosis, old and new hemorrhage and dystrophic calcification

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Hyperplastic nodules with discrete fibrous capsule, composed of follicles with papillary hyperplasia and tall columnar cells
* Nonfunctioning nodules may appear inactive and have degenerative changes of fibrosis, calcification and hemorrhage (old and new)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Monoclonal or polyclonal

Differential diagnosis

* [Dyshormonogenetic goiter](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroiddyshormono.html): diffuse changes compared to focal changes in toxic multinodular goiter
* [Follicular carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidfollicular.html): vascular invasion or invasion beyond thyroid gland
* [Graves disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidgraves.html): uniformly enlarged gland with uniformly diffuse hyperplasia and no nodules
* [Nontoxic multinodular goiter](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidnodular.html): difficult to distinguish histologically, need imaging studies and clinical correlation
