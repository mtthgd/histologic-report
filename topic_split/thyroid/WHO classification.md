WHO (2017) - Parathyroid gland

* Tumours of the parathyroid glands **ICD 0 codes**
  + Parathyroid carcinoma 8140/3
  + Parathyroid adenoma 8140/0
  + Secondary, mesenchymal and other tumours

* **ICD 0 note**: the first four digits indicate the specific histological term; the fifth digit after the slash (/) is the behavior code,
  including /0 for benign tumors, /1 for unspecified, borderline or uncertain behavior, /2 for carcinoma in situ and grade III intraepithelial neoplasia (not used for adrenal tumors), and /3 for malignant tumors

WHO classification: thyroid tumors

Major Updates

* A group of borderline thyroid tumors has been introduced for the first time:
  1. FT - UMP (follicular tumor of uncertain malignant potential)
  2. [WDT - UMP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidwelldiffump.html) (well differentiated tumor of uncertain malignant potential)
  3. [NIFTP](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidglandNIFTP.html) (noninvasive follicular thyroid neoplasm with papillary nuclear features)Borderline tumors ("not cancer yet") are equivalent to carcinoma in situ in other organs; they are placed
  between follicular adenoma and follicular carcinoma or follicular variant of papillary carcinoma
* [Papillary thyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidpapillary.html) (PTC) variants:
  1. Conventional / classic variant
  2. [Papillary microcarcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidmicrocarcinomavariant.html)
  3. [Encapsulated PTC](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidencapsulated.html) – applicable to fully encapsulated classic PTC
  4. [Follicular variant](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidfollicularvariant.html) – PTC with an exclusively or almost exclusively follicular pattern, and no developed
     papillae
  5. [Diffuse sclerosing variant](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroiddiffusesclerosing.html)
  6. [Tall cell variant](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidtallcellvariant.html)
  7. [Columnar cell variant](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidcolumnarvariant.html)
  8. [Cribriform-morular variant](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidcribriformmorular.html)
  9. [Hobnail variant](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidpapillaryhobnail.html) – new entity
  10. PTC with [fibromatosis/fascitiis-like stroma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidnodularfasciitis.html)
  11. [Solid/trabecular](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidsolidvariant.html) variant
  12. [Oncocytic variant](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidoncocytic.html)
  13. Spindle cell variant
  14. Clear cell variant
  15. [Warthin like variant](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidwarthin.html)
* [Follicular thyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidfollicular.html) (FTC) groups:
  1. Minimally invasive FTC – capsular invasion only
  2. Angioinvasive FTC
  3. Widely invasive FTC – grossly invasive
* [Hürthle cell (oncocytic) tumors](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidhurthle.html) as a separate entity:
  + include Hürthle cell adenoma and Hürthle cell carcinoma
  + previously classified as oncocytic variant of follicular adenoma and FTC
  + term "Hürthle" is favored over "oncocytic"
* [Poorly differentiated thyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidinsular.html):
  + Turin criteria were adopted
* [Anaplastic thyroid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/thyroidUndiff.html):
  + "anaplastic" is favored over "undifferentiated"
