Definition / general

* Polypoid lesion composed of atypical, architecturally complex endometrial glands within benign myomatous / fibromyomatous stroma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1148](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25828387))

Essential features

* Biphasic neoplasm composed of crowded atypical endometrial glands (with or without squamous morules) in myomatous / fibromyomatous stroma
* Stroma typically positive for SATB2, in contrast to most examples of myoinvasive carcinoma
* Risk of recurrence or concurrent or subsequent endometrial endometrioid carcinoma

Terminology

* Atypical polypoid adenomyofibroma

ICD coding

* ICD-11: [XH7ZB1](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f1153202251) - atypical polypoid adenomyoma

Epidemiology

* Usually diagnosed in women of reproductive age; range of 25 - 73 years ([Am J Surg Pathol 1996;20:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8540600))
* Associated with obesity, infertility, nulliparity

Sites

* Lower uterine segment > uterine corpus

Pathophysiology

* Prolonged estrogenic stimulation plays a role in some cases ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 1987;6:104](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3692666))
* Progesterone may have a role in development of the stroma of atypical polypoid adenomyoma ([Hum Pathol 2014;45:33](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24182564))
* Overlapping molecular and immunohistochemical features with atypical hyperplasia ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1148](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25828387))

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Abnormal vaginal bleeding
* Incidental finding during evaluation of infertility
* May occur in the setting of Turner syndrome ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 1987;6:104](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3692666))
* Rare report of occurrence in patient with Cowden syndrome ([Gynecol Oncol Case Rep 2012;2:29](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24371612))
* Reference: [Pathol Res Pract 2019;215:766](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30661903)

Diagnosis

* Based on histologic features

Radiology description

* T2 weighted MRI images: slightly hyperintense polypoid mass mixed with markedly hyperintense foci corresponding to endometrial glands
* T1 weighted MRI images: isointense with myometrium, occasional hyperintense cystic foci
* Solid portions, other than cystic foci, show contrast enhancement in the arterial phase and washout or plateau pattern in the late phase
* Reference: [J Comput Assist Tomogr 2015;39:32](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25286280)

Prognostic factors

* Approximately 30 - 44% recur after conservative treatment; higher risk of recurrence if sampling is not under hysteroscopic visualization ([Int J Gynaecol Obstet 2021 Dec 18 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34921691), [Medicine (Baltimore) 2020;99:e20491](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32590732))
* Concurrent diagnosis of endometrial endometrioid carcinoma in ~11%; diagnosis of endometrial carcinoma during follow up in ~14% ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2020;99:e20491](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32590732))
* High likelihood of successful pregnancy after conservative treatment ([Medicine (Baltimore) 2020;99:e20491](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32590732))

Case reports

* 29 year old woman with low grade endometrioid adenocarcinoma arising from atypical polypoid adenomyoma; diagnosed in early pregnancy and managed conservatively ([Gynecol Oncol Rep 2019;28:101](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30993161))
* 35 year old woman with atypical polypoid adenomyoma treated by hysteroscopy with photodynamic diagnosis using 5-aminolevulinic acid ([Photodiagnosis Photodyn Ther 2019;27:295](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31252140))
* 36 year old woman with endometrioid adenocarcinoma developing 8 years after conservative management for atypical polypoid adenomyoma ([Gynecol Oncol Case Rep 2014;8:21](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24707458))
* 45 year old woman with atypical polypoid adenomyoma treated with local resection ([Pathologica 2020;112:214](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33393525))

Treatment

* Conservative management and close follow up can be considered for women that desire fertility preservation; addition of progestin does not seem to improve oncologic outcomes ([Int J Gynaecol Obstet 2021 Dec 18 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34921691))
  + One proposed strategy: transvaginal ultrasonography plus hysteroscopic biopsy every 3 months for 2 years, every 4 - 6 months for another 3 years and then once a year ([Arch Gynecol Obstet 2016;293:177](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26209972))
  + Small case series suggests a potential role for photodynamic therapy to ensure complete resection ([Photodiagnosis Photodyn Ther 2021;36:102583](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34673272))
* Hysterectomy, if peri or postmenopausal ([Int J Gynaecol Obstet 2021 Dec 18 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34921691))

Gross description

* Firm polypoid lesion
* Typically unifocal
* Average size of 2 cm
* Reference: [Am J Clin Pathol 1986;86:139](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3739968)

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Biphasic tumor:
  + Endometrioid glands, usually with complex architecture and sometimes with cytologic atypia
  + Glandular component often shows lobulated architecture
  + Benign fibromyomatous stroma, rarely with myxoid change
* Squamous morular metaplasia is frequently seen and may show central necrosis
* Well circumscribed or slightly irregular margin
* Reference: [Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1148](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25828387)

Stromal SATB2 positivity

Positive stains

* [Beta catenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbetacatenin.html) nuclear positivity (squamous morules > glands) ([Hum Pathol 2014;45:33](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24182564))
* [SATB2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssatb2.html) reactivity in the stromal component ([Histopathology 2021;79:96](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33459390), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2019;38:397](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30085940))
* [Pancytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [estrogen receptor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) and [progesterone receptor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html) typically positive in glands
* [Smooth muscle actin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) and [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) positive in stromal muscle ([Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2020;28:646](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31855579))
* [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html) and [p16](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp16.html) positive in squamous morules ([Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2020;28:646](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31855579), [Histopathology 2021;79:96](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33459390))

Negative stains

* [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) and [caldesmon](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscaldesmon.html) negative in stroma ([Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2020;28:646](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31855579))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* *CTNNB1* mutation, *PTEN* deletion and *KRAS* mutation ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:1148](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25828387), [Hum Pathol 2014;45:33](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24182564))
* *MLH1* promoter hypermethylation and microsatellite instability ([Hum Pathol 2003;34:784](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14506639))

Sample pathology report

* Uterus, total hysterectomy:
  + Atypical polypoid adenomyoma

Differential diagnosis

* [Endometrial atypical hyperplasia (complex atypical hyperplasia / endometrioid intraepithelial neoplasia)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusendometrialhyperplasiageneral.html):
  + Lacks muscular stroma
  + Involves flat endometrium or polyps without myomatous stroma
* [Myoinvasive endometrioid carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusendometrioid.html):
  + Fragments of myometrium with invasive carcinoma often admixed with separate fragments of endometrioid carcinoma
  + May show desmoplasia
  + Muscular stroma negative for [SATB2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssatb2.html) by immunohistochemistry
* [Endometrial polyp](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusendopolyp.html):
  + Lacks muscular stroma
  + Some cases may show endometrial hyperplasia with or without squamous morules
* [Adenosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusadenosarcoma.html):
  + Benign endometrial glands and malignant stroma
* [Carcinosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusmmmt.html):
  + Malignant endometrial glands and stroma
