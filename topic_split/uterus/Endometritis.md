Definition / general

* Inflammatory process involving the endometrium

Essential features

* Endometrial stromal plasma cells required for the diagnosis of chronic endometritis
* Chronic endometritis is implicated in infertility and recurrent pregnancy loss
* Acute endometritis typically represents ascending infection from lower genital tract
* Xanthogranulomatous endometritis usually associated with cervical stenosis

ICD coding

* ICD-10:
  + [N71.1](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/N00-N99/N70-N77/N71-/N71.1) - chronic inflammatory disease of uterus
  + [N71.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/N00-N99/N70-N77/N71-/N71.9) - inflammatory disease of uterus, unspecified

Epidemiology

* Typically premenopausal women

Sites

* Endometrium

Pathophysiology

* Unknown

Etiology

* Chronic endometritis
  + Unclear etiology
  + Causative organisms include *Streptococcus* species, *Escherichia coli*, *Enterococcus*, *Staphylococcus* species and *Mycoplasma* / *Ureaplasma* species ([Fertil Steril 2008;89:677](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17531993))
* Acute endometritis
  + Ascending infection from lower genital tract
    - Causative organisms include *Chlamydia trachomatis,* *Neisseria gonorrhoeae,* *Trichomonas vaginalis, Mycoplasma* species ([Sex Transm Infect 2020;96:436](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31719170))
    - Group A streptococcal infection rarely causes postpartum endometritis ([Postgrad Med 2020;132:526](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32379557))
    - May be secondary to retained products of conception
  + *Actinomyces*-like organisms are a rare cause of acute and chronic endometritis with prolonged intrauterine device use ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2019;38:138](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29369919))
* Xanthogranulomatous endometritis
  + Pyometra, cervical stenosis ([Clin Case Rep 2021;9:e04299](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34194800), [J Midlife Health 2019;10:206](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31942158))
* Granulomatous endometritis
  + Tuberculosis ([Am J Obstet Gynecol 2020;223:737.e1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32497612))
  + Sarcoidosis ([Int J Surg Pathol 2017;25:246](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27738097))
  + Coccidioidomycosis ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 1986;110:232](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/3753858))
  + Postendometrial ablation ([Br J Obstet Gynaecol 1992;99:928](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1450148))
  + Cytomegalovirus ([Am J Surg Pathol 1992;16:716](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/1326899))

Clinical features

* Chronic endometritis
  + Often asymptomatic ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2010;29:44](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19952932), [Am J Reprod Immunol 2011;66:410](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21749546))
  + Abnormal uterine bleeding, pelvic discomfort ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2010;29:44](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19952932), [Am J Reprod Immunol 2011;66:410](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21749546))
  + Associated with moderate to severe intrauterine adhesions ([Fertil Steril 2019;111:1002](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30922643))
  + May be associated with unexplained infertility, unexplained recurrent miscarriages and repeated implantation failure after in vitro fertilization embryo transfer ([Mod Pathol 2010;23:1136](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20495539), [Fertil Steril 2011;95:1156](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21030015), [Fertil Steril 2010;93:437](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19217098))
    - Somewhat controversial
* Acute endometritis
  + Fever, pelvic pain, vaginal discharge

Diagnosis

* Microscopic examination is the gold standard
* Hysteroscopic scoring systems have been proposed ([J Minim Invasive Gynecol 2020;27:1127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32240839))
  + Features: endometrial hyperemia (focal or diffuse), hemorrhagic spots, dilated endometrial vessels, micropolyps, endometrial polyp

Prognostic factors

* Women with cured chronic endometritis may have higher ongoing pregnancy rate / live birth rate, clinical pregnancy rate and implantation rate ([Fertil Steril 2018;110:103](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29908776))
* Untreated acute endometritis has a fatality rate ~17% ([StatPearls: Endometritis [Accessed 3 August 2021]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31985918))
* Acute endometritis is not associated with infertility ([Am J Obstet Gynecol 2003;188:141](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12548208))

Case reports

* 61 year old woman with pyometra and enlarged uterus ([J Midlife Health 2019;10:206](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31942158))
* 70 year old woman with intrauterine device in place for 42 years ([Acta Medica (Hradec Kralove) 2019;62:35](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30931895))
* 74 year old woman with endometrial fluid collection and clinical concern for endometrial cancer ([Radiol Case Rep 2018;14:121](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30386451))

Treatment

* Chronic endometritis
  + Antibiotic therapy (often empiric treatment with doxycycline [first line] or metronidazole / ciprofloxacin [second line]) ([Am J Reprod Immunol 2017;78:e12719](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28608596))
* Acute endometritis
  + Oral antibiotics for mild disease (doxycycline / metronidazole, levofloxacin / metronidazole or amoxicillin / clavulanate) ([StatPearls: Endometritis [Accessed 3 August 2021]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31985918))
  + IV antiobiotics (such as gentamicin or clindamycin) if more severe ([StatPearls: Endometritis [Accessed 3 August 2021]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31985918))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Chronic endometritis
  + Plasma cells (required) with or without lymphoid follicles
    - Presence of eosinophils associated with plasma cells ([Hum Pathol 2010;41:33](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19801162))
    - Currently no specific guidelines about required number of plasma cells or whether detection of rare plasma cells by IHC alone is sufficient ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2020 Dec 14 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33323860))
      * Plasma cells can be seen in disordered proliferative or breakdown endometrium in the absence of infection ([Hum Pathol 2007;38:581](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17188330))
  + Spindled stromal cells
  + Endometrial dating is unreliable due to frequent out of phase morphology ([Am J Reprod Immunol 2011;66:410](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21749546))
    - Higher prevalence in proliferative phase ([Reprod Biomed Online 2018;36:78](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29111313))
  + Note: structural abnormalities ([endometrial polyp](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusendopolyp.html), [submucosal leiomyoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusleiomyoma.html)) can be associated with plasma cells; such cases should not be diagnosed as chronic endometritis
* Acute endometritis
  + Neutrophils infiltrating and destroying endometrial epithelium
  + Neutrophils filling gland lumina
  + With or without microabscess formation
* Granulomatous endometritis
  + Clusters of epithelioid histiocytes rimmed by lymphocytes
  + Cytopathic effects if viral etiology (herpes simplex virus, cytomegalovirus)
  + Tuberculosis: necrotizing granulomas with multinucleated giant cells, usually in superficial functional endometrium ([Am J Obstet Gynecol 2020;223:737.e1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32497612))
  + Sarcoidosis: nonnecrotizing granulomatous inflammation, more commonly in myometrium
* Xanthogranulomatous endometritis
  + Abundant foamy histiocytes, siderophages, neutrophils, plasma cells, lymphocytes
  + With or without fibrosis, calcification

Positive stains

* Chronic endometritis
  + [CD138 / syndecan](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd138.html) in plasma cells ([Arch Pathol Lab Med 2004;128:1000](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15335255))
  + [MUM1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmum1.html) in plasma cells; higher sensitivity, cleaner background ([Int J Surg Pathol 2019;27:372](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30482071))

Sample pathology report

* Endometrium, biopsy:
  + Chronic endometritis

Differential diagnosis

* [Predecidualized endometrial stromal cells](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusnormal.html):
  + Lack "clock face" chromatin
  + Plasma cell markers negative
* [Lymphoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixlymphoma.html):
  + Atypical proliferation of clonal lymphoid cells
