Definition / general

* Malignant uterine mesenchymal tumor originating from the endometrial stroma

Essential features

* Cellular tumor with high grade round or spindle cells, sometimes with an associated low grade component
* Variable positivity for BCOR, cyclin D1 and CD10, depending on molecular alteration present; often negative or only focally positive for smooth muscle markers and hormone receptors
* Major subtypes include sarcomas with *YWHAE::NUTM2A/B* fusions, *BCOR* rearranged and *BCOR* internal tandem duplication (ITD)

Terminology

* Undifferentiated endometrial sarcoma, uniform type
* High grade endometrial stromal sarcoma with *YWHAE::FAM22* fusions

ICD coding

* ICD-O: [8930/3](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=82) - endometrial stromal sarcoma, high grade
* ICD-10: [C54.1](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C51-C58/C54-/C54.1) - malignant neoplasm of endometrium

Epidemiology

* Rare, wide age range
  + *YWHAE::NUTM2A/B* fusions: 28 - 67 (mean 50) years ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:641](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22456610))
  + *BCOR* rearranged: 28 - 71 (median 54) years ([Mod Pathol 2018;31:674](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29192652))
  + *BCOR* ITD: 14 - 55 (median 42) years ([Oncology 2019;96:101](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30380541))
  + High grade transformation: 45 - 74 (median 54) years ([Mod Pathol 2020;33:1861](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32317704))

Sites

* Uterine corpus
* Rarely vagina ([Mod Pathol 2020;33:1861](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32317704))

Pathophysiology

* Several alterations have been described (*YWHAE::NUTM2A/B* fusions, *BCOR* fusions, *BCOR* ITD) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:641](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22456610), [Mod Pathol 2018;31:674](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29192652), [Oncology 2019;96:101](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30380541))
* High grade transformation of low grade endometrial stromal sarcoma ([Mod Pathol 2020;33:1861](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32317704))

Etiology

* Unknown

Clinical features

* Pelvic pain, pelvic mass, abnormal uterine bleeding

Diagnosis

* Hysterectomy needed for definitive diagnosis
* Rarely diagnosed on endometrial biopsy / curettage

Radiology description

* MRI ([Cancer Imaging 2019;19:63](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31514752)):
  + Solid and occasionally cystic mass
  + Feather-like enhancement, hemorrhage and necrosis

Prognostic factors

* Intermediate prognosis between low grade endometrial stromal sarcoma and undifferentiated uterine sarcoma
* Limited number of cases reported with limited followup information to identify favorable / unfavorable prognostic factors

Case reports

* 40 year old woman with a 4 month history of recurrent right sided lower thoracic pain and previous hysterectomy ([BMC Cancer 2018;18:92](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29357824))
* 41 year old woman with a 6 month history of menorrhagia ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2019;38:420](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29901520))
* 51 year old woman with brown discharge and postcoital bleeding ([Virchows Arch 2020;476:615](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31938824))

Treatment

* Cytoreductive surgery and multimodality therapy ([Taiwan J Obstet Gynecol 2016;55:625](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27751406))
* Anthracycline based chemotherapy for *YWHAE::NUTM2A/B* sarcomas ([Gynecol Oncol 2017;145:531](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28390819))
* Potential role for MDM2 or CDK4 inhibitors in *BCOR* rearranged sarcomas with *MDM2* amplification or alterations of genes in the cyclin D1 / CDK4 kinase pathway ([Gynecol Oncol 2020;157:357](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32156473), [J Pathol Clin Res 2020;6:178](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32352245))
* Limited role for antihormonal therapy as most lack or have limited ER / PR expression

Gross description

* *YWHAE::NUTM2A/B* fusions ([Histopathology 2015;67:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25355621))
  + 1 - 12 cm
  + Gross mass with permeative myoinvasion
* *BCOR* rearranged ([Mod Pathol 2018;31:674](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29192652))
  + 1.5 - 12 (median 9.7) cm
  + Tan-pink to yellow
  + Soft, fleshy or rubbery
  + Can be polypoid involving the endometrium; rarely myometrial confined
* *BCOR* ITD ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:335](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29200103), [Oncology 2019;96:101](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30380541))
  + 2.5 - 14.5 (median 7) cm
  + Can be polypoid with additional intramural nodular masses

Microscopic (histologic) description

* *YWHAE::NUTM2A/B* fusions ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:641](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22456610), [Mod Pathol 2013;26:1390](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23599159), [Histopathology 2015;67:1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25355621), [Pathology 2018;50:162](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29275929)):
  + Permeative (tongue-like) invasion
  + Vaguely nested growth of round cells with scant (small round blue cell appearance) to moderate eosinophilic cytoplasm and uniform nuclear atypia
  + Brisk mitoses (> 10/10 high power fields)
  + Tumor cell necrosis and lymphovascular invasion common
  + Delicate network of arborizing or curvilinear vessels
  + 50% associated with a low grade spindle cell component resembling low grade endometrial stromal sarcoma
    - Admixed or occasionally well demarcated
    - Monomorphic bland spindle cells
    - Fibrous to fibromyxoid matrix
    - Low mitoses (≤ 3/10 high power fields)
    - Typically lacks necrosis
  + Variant features include pseudoglandular or pseudopapillary appearance, sex cord-like differentiation, rosette-like formations
  + Overt nuclear pleomorphism rare
* *BCOR* rearranged ([Mod Pathol 2018;31:674](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29192652), [Pathology 2018;50:162](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29275929)):
  + Often involves endometrium and myometrium
  + Tongue-like invasion or broad front with infiltrative borders
  + Haphazard fascicles of uniformly atypical spindle cells with scant to moderate and eosinophilic or abundant and blue-gray cytoplasm
  + Extensive myxoid stroma
  + Collagen plaques in ~50%
  + Brisk mitoses (≥ 10/10 high power fields)
  + Small arterioles without perivascular whorling of tumor cells
  + Lymphovascular invasion in ~50%
  + Infarct type necrosis more common than tumor cell necrosis
  + Overt nuclear pleomorphism rare
  + No low grade component present
* *BCOR* ITD ([Mod Pathol 2017;30:1251](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28621321), [Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:335](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29200103), [Oncology 2019;96:101](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30380541), [Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:662](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30789359)):
  + Tongue-like invasion
  + High grade round cell component with uniform atypia, high grade spindle cell component with uniform atypia, low grade fibromyxoid spindle cell component
  + Myxoid matrix
  + Brisk mitoses (≥ 10/10 high power fields)
  + Lymphovascular invasion and tumor cell necrosis
  + Overt nuclear pleomorphism rare
* High grade transformation of low grade endometrial stromal sarcoma ([Mod Pathol 2020;33:1861](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32317704)):
  + High grade transformation may occur in the primary, metastatic or recurrent tumor
  + High grade component usually easily recognized from low grade component on low power magnification but occasionally is a gradual transition
  + High grade component:
    - Comprises 10 - 90% of tumor
    - Sharply demarcated nodules often associated with sclerotic or less commonly myxoid stroma
    - Rounded epithelioid cells with increased atypia (often uniform) and brisk mitoses (median, 16/10 high power fields)
    - Delicate vasculature without perivascular tumor cell whorls
    - Tumor cell necrosis uncommon
  + Low grade component:
    - Conventional low grade endometrial stromal sarcoma
    - May show variant morphology (fibroblastic, smooth muscle differentiation, myxoid matrix, sex cord-like differentiation)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* *YWHAE::NUTM2A/B* fusions ([Proc Natl Acad Sci U S A 2012;109:929](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22223660), [Mod Pathol 2013;26:1390](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23599159)):
  + t(10;17)(q22;p13)
  + Produces the 14-3-3 oncoprotein
* *BCOR* rearranged ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:12](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27631520), [Gynecol Oncol 2020;157:357](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32156473), [J Pathol Clin Res 2020;6:178](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32352245)):
  + *ZC3H7B* is most common gene partner; t(X;22)(p11;q13)
    - Less frequent partners include *L3MBTL2*, *EP300*, *NUTM2G*, *RALGPS1*, *MAP7D2*, *RGAG1*, *ING3*, *NUGGC*, *KMT2D*, *CREBBP*
  + *MDM2* amplification (45 - 100%)
  + *FRS2* amplification (40%)
  + *CDK4* amplification (38 - 60%)
  + Homozygous deletion of *CDKN2A* (20 - 28%) and *CDKN2B* (18%)
* *BCOR* ITD ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:335](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29200103), [Oncology 2019;96:101](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30380541), [Gynecol Oncol 2020;157:357](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32156473)):
  + Internal tandem duplications of different lengths involving exon 15
  + *CDKN2A / 2B* homozygous deletion (20%)
  + No *MDM2* or *CDK4* amplifications
* High grade transformation of low grade endometrial stromal sarcoma ([Mod Pathol 2020;33:1861](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32317704)):
  + *JAZF1::SUZ12* fusions most common
  + Rarely or *JAZF1::PHF1*, *EPC1::PHF1* or *BRD8::PHF1* fusions
  + No *YWHAE* and *BCOR* alterations

Sample pathology report

* Uterus, fallopian tubes and ovaries, hysterectomy and bilateral salpingo-oophorectomy:
  + *ZC3H7B::BCOR* rearranged high grade endometrial stromal sarcoma (14 cm) (see comment and synoptic report)
  + Comment: The tumor shows tongue-like myometrial invasion and is composed of enlarged, hyperchromatic spindle cells percolating within a myxoid stroma. Mitoses number up to 15 per 10 high power fields. It is positive for BCOR, cyclin D1 and CD10 but negative for desmin, caldesmon and SMA. Molecular testing confirmed a *ZC3H7B::BCOR* fusion.

Differential diagnosis

* *YWHAE::NUTM2A/B* high grade endometrial stromal sarcoma:
  + [Undifferentiated carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusundifferentiated.html) ([Semin Diagn Pathol 2020 Nov 27 [Epub ahead of print]](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33323288)):
    - Sheets of noncohesive round and monomorphic cells lacking epithelial differentiation
    - Large areas of geographic necrosis and lymphovascular invasion
    - Rhabdoid cells in ~25%
    - Small foci of overt pleomorphism in 20%
    - Often positive, at least focally, for [cytokeratins](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) ([AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [CAM5.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscam52.html), [CK8/18](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/CK818.html)) and [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html)
    - Subset are positive for [synaptophysin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssynaptophysin.html), [chromogranin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainschromogranin.html) or [CD56](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd56.html)
    - May show mismatch repair protein (50%) or SWI / SNF protein (70%) deficiency
    - Molecular alterations common to endometrial carcinomas (*PTEN*, *PIK3CA*, *CTNNB1*, *TP53*)
* *BCOR* related high grade endometrial stromal sarcoma:
  + [Myxoid leiomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uteruslms.html) ([Histopathology2017;70:1138](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28130839), [Mod Pathol 2019;32:1688](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31189997), [Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:382](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30489320)):
    - May see conventional smooth muscle component
    - Often positive, at least focally, for [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [caldesmon](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscaldesmon.html) or [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html)
    - Aberrant [p16](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp16.html) (70%) or [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) (50%)
    - With or without *PLAG1* rearrangement
  + [Inflammatory myofibroblastic tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusinflammpseudo.html):
    - May be associated with pregnancy
    - Myxoid, compact / fascicular, hyalinized growth patterns
    - Lymphoplasmacytic infiltrate
    - [ALK](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalk.html) positive
    - Often positive, at least focally, for [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [caldesmon](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscaldesmon.html), [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html)
    - *ALK* fusions (rarely *ROS1*, *RET*, *NTRK*)
* High grade endometrial stromal sarcoma (in general):
  + [Low grade endometrial stromal sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusESSlowgrade.html):
    - No admixed high grade component
    - Bland spindle cells, typically with low mitoses
    - [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html), [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) and [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html) positive
    - [BCOR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsbcor.html) and [cyclin D1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscyclind1.html) negative
    - *JAZF1* or *PHF1* fusions
  + [Leiomyosarcoma (conventional / spindle cell type)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uteruslms.html):
    - Often shows overt nuclear pleomorphism
    - [Desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [caldesmon](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscaldesmon.html), [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) positive
    - [CD10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd10.html) often negative
    - *TP53*, *ATRX*, *RB1* alterations
  + [Undifferentiated uterine sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterussarcoma.html):
    - Complex karyotype
    - No known recurring genetic alterations
