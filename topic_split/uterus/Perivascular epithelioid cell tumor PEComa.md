Definition / general

* Rare mesenchymal neoplasm with myomelanocytic differentiation that is favored to arise from perivascular epithelioid cells

Essential features

* Best classified as a tumor of uncertain malignant potential since recurrences may arise years after initial diagnosis
* Characterized by sheets or nests of epithelioid cells with clear to eosinophilic and granular cytoplasm, surrounded by delicate vasculature
* Coexpresses melanoma and smooth muscle markers, with variable staining intensity and distribution
* 2 main molecular subgroups: *TSC1 / TSC2* alterations, *TFE3* fusions

ICD coding

* ICD-10:
  + [D39.0](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D37-D48/D39-/D39.0) - neoplasm of uncertain behavior of uterus
  + [C54.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/C51-C58/C54-/C54.9) - malignant neoplasm of corpus uteri, unspecified
* ICD-11
  + [2F76](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f2029680113) - neoplasms of uncertain behaviour of female genital organs
  + [2C76.Z](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f959493854%2fmms%2funspecified) - malignant neoplasms of corpus uteri, unspecified

Epidemiology

* Most commonly presents at 40 - 60 years (range: 6 - 79) ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2021;60:168](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33099813))

Sites

* Uterine corpus is most common in gynecologic tract
* Rare in cervix, vagina, ovary, broad ligament and vulva

Pathophysiology

* Somatic or germline mutations in *TSC1 / TSC2* inactivate the tuberous sclerosis complex protein, leading to activation of the mTOR kinase ([Nat Cell Biol 2019;21:63](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30602761), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2021;60:168](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33099813))

Etiology

* ~10% associated with tuberous sclerosis complex ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:1370](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30001237))

Clinical features

* Generally present with nonspecific gynecologic symptoms (abnormal uterine bleeding, abdominal / pelvic pain) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:1370](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30001237))
* May be discovered incidentally during evaluation for adnexal mass, leiomyomas, infertility or at cesarean section
* Rarely present with uterine rupture or hemoperitoneum, especially in pregnancy ([J Obstet Gynaecol Res 2019;45:709](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30548368), [Int J Gynecol Cancer 2020;30:2008](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32763950))

Diagnosis

* Rarely made on endometrial sampling
* Myomectomy or hysterectomy

Radiology description

* No defining features to distinguish from other uterine mesenchymal neoplasms on imaging

Prognostic factors

* 3 main algorithms have been proposed for predicting behavior
  + Nongynecologic specific criteria ([Am J Surg Pathol 2005;29:1558](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16327428)):
    - Benign: Uncertain malignant potential: nuclear pleomorphism / multinucleated giant cells or > 5 cm
    - Malignant: 2+ features (> 5 cm, noninfiltrative, high grade atypia, mitoses > 1/50 HPFs, necrosis, LVI)
  + Gynecologic specific criteria ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:176](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24418852)):
    - Benign / uncertain malignant potential: 1/50 HPFs, necrosis, LVI)
    - Malignant: ≥ 4 features
  + Modified gynecologic specific criteria (adopted by the WHO 2020) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:1370](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30001237))
    - Uncertain malignant potential: 5 cm, high grade atypia, mitoses > 1/50 HPFs, necrosis, LVI)
    - Malignant: ≥ 3 features
* Recurrences can occur years after original diagnosis

Case reports

* 21 year old woman with a subserosal uterine nodule identified at cesarean section ([Gynecol Oncol Rep 2022;40:100962](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35341106))
* 38 year old woman with adnexal mass and 62 year old woman with abnormal uterine bleeding ([Eur J Med Res 2017;22:7](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28270196))
* 65 year old woman with postmenopausal bleeding and abdominal pain ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2021;14:993](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34646418))

Treatment

* Surgery (hysterectomy, myomectomy) is first line therapy as many are presumed leiomyomas
* Chemotherapy and radiation may be administered in patients with metastatic or recurrent disease
* mTOR inhibitors have shown variable results ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2021;60:168](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33099813), [J Clin Oncol 2021;39:3660](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34637337))

Gross description

* Mean: 6.5 cm; range: 0.2 - 25 cm ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2021;60:168](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33099813))
* Typically intramural but may be polypoid
* Tan, white, pink or gray solid mass
* Subset show hemorrhage or necrosis

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Well circumscribed or infiltrative growth
* Numerous growth patterns, with sheets and nests being the most common
* Noncohesive epithelioid cells with clear to eosinophilic granular cytoplasm
  + May have a component of spindled cells (usually minor)
  + Subset have dense eosinophilic (rhabdoid) or foamy cytoplasm
* Variable cytologic atypia and mitotic index
* Melanoma-like nucleoli, intranuclear pseudoinclusions, multinucleated cells, Touton giant cells and melanin pigment (rare) may be present
* Characterized by thin and delicate vessels but may also have thick walled (generally peripherally located) and staghorn vessels
* Radial / perivascular distribution of tumor cells identified in Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:1370)
* Stromal hyalinization is common
  + Sclerosing PEComa is rare in the gynecologic tract ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2011;30:121](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21293289), [Pathol Int 2011;61:768](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22126387))
* *TFE3* translocation associated PEComas ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:1395](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20871214), [Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:394](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25517951)):
  + Typically epithelioid with clear cells in a nested / alveolar growth
  + Low grade atypia and rare mitoses
* Lymphangioleiomyomatosis (LAM)-like PEComas are rare, predominantly spindled with thick walled blood vessels, cleft / slit-like spaces, low grade atypia and infrequent mitoses ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:1370](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30001237))

PEComa

Positive stains

* Melanocytic markers - variable intensity and distribution ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2021;60:168](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33099813)):
  + [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html) (99%)
  + **PNL2** (86%)
  + [MiTF](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmitf.html) (83%)
  + [MelanA / MART1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html) (67%)
* Myogenic markers - variable intensity and distribution ([Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2021;60:168](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33099813)):
  + [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) (88%)
  + [Desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) (80%)
  + [Caldesmon](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscaldesmon.html) (76%)
* [Cathepsin K](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscathepsink.html): usually strong and diffuse
* [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) (53%), [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html) (85%) ([J Clin Pathol 2015;68:418](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25750268))
* [TFE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstfe3.html) may show focal staining but typically is nonspecific unless strong and diffuse ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:1395](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20871214))
* *TFE3* translocation associated PEComa: [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html) and [TFE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstfe3.html) strong and diffuse

Negative stains

* [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) (focally positive in 20%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:176](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24418852))
* [AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html) (focally positive in 11%) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2014;38:176](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24418852))
* [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html)
* *TFE3* translocation associated PEComa: [MelanA / MART1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html) and smooth muscle markers (focal / negative), [MiTF](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmitf.html) negative

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* 2 main molecular subgroups that are mutually exclusive:
  + *TSC1 / TSC2* alterations ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:813](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25651471), [Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32889887), [Oncology 2020;98:905](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32966992), [Mod Pathol 2022;35:515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34131293))
  + *TFE3* fusions ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:1395](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20871214), [Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:394](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25517951))
* *RAD51B* and *HTR4-ST3GAL1* fusions are rare ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:813](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25651471), [Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:1370](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30001237))
* Concurrent *TP53*, *ATRX* or *RB1* alterations in malignant PEComas ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:813](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25651471), [Am J Surg Pathol 2021;45:77](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32889887), [Oncology 2020;98:905](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32966992), [Mod Pathol 2022;35:515](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34131293))
* Tumors with hybrid PEComa and endometrial stromal sarcoma histologic features harbor *TSC2* mutations and *JAZF1-SUZ12* fusions ([Mod Pathol 2022;35:117](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34561551))

Sample pathology report

* Uterus, hysterectomy:
  + PEComa of uncertain malignant potential (4.5 cm) (see comment)
  + Chronic cervicitis
  + Proliferative endometrium
  + Leiomyomas
  + Comment: 1 of the myometrial nodules is comprised of sheets of epithelioid cells with clear to eosinophilic and granular cytoplasm. Cytologic atypia, mitoses and necrosis are not appreciated. This nodule is positive for HMB45 (50%, strong), MelanA (focal), desmin, caldesmon and SMA. The morphologic and immunoprofile supports the diagnosis of PEComa. Based on the WHO 2020 criteria for predicting behavior in PEComas, this tumor would classify as being of uncertain malignant potential. Most tumors in this category have a benign clinical course; however, as a small subset recur, close clinicoradiological follow up is recommended.

Differential diagnosis

* [Epithelioid smooth muscle tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusSTUMP.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2022;46:464](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34419987)):
  + No thin or delicate vessels
  + Perinuclear vacuoles and diffuse eosinophilic cytoplasm with cytoplasmic granularity
  + Typically negative or focally / weakly positive for melanocytic markers and [cathepsin K](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscathepsink.html)
  + No known association with *TSC1 / TSC2* alterations or *TFE3* fusions
  + Subset with *PGR* fusions ([Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:810](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30829727))
* [Malignant melanoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixmelanoma.html):
  + [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html) and [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) positive
  + Negative for smooth muscle markers
  + No *TSC1 / TSC2* alterations or *TFE3* fusions
* [Alveolar soft part sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixalveolarsoftpartssarcoma.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:622](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28009610)):
  + Rod shaped, [PAS](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspas.html) positive, diastase resistant intracytoplasmic crystals
  + Conspicuous vessels
  + Often [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html), [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) and [caldesmon](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscaldesmon.html) negative
  + *ASPSCR1-TFE3* fusion
* [Poorly differentiated endometrial carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusendometrialcarc.html):
  + Focal gland formation may be present
  + [Cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) and [PAX8](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspax8.html) positive
  + Negative for myomelanocytic markers
* [Placental site trophoblastic tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/placentaPSTT.html):
  + Fibrinoid material surrounds groups of tumor cells and replaces vessel walls
  + Endometrium may show decidual / Arias-Stella changes
  + [Inhibin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsinhibina.html), [hPL](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshpl.html), [cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) positive
  + Negative for myomelanocytic markers
  + No *TSC1 / TSC2* alterations or *TFE3* fusions
