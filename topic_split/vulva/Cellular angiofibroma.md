Definition / general

* Cellular angiofibroma is a benign, site specific soft tissue tumor of the lower genital tract

Essential features

* Benign lower genital tract tumor, occurring equally in women and men
* Bland spindle cell fascicles, abundant medium sized hyalinized vessels and wispy stromal collagen
* Characterized by deletion of *RB1* / *FOXO1* locus on chr 13q
* Local excision curative; recurrences exceptionally rare

ICD coding

* ICD-10
  + [D21.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D21-/D21.9) - benign neoplasm of soft tissue
  + [D23.9](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D23-/D23.9) - benign neoplasm of skin
  + [D28.0](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/C00-D49/D10-D36/D28-/D28.0) - benign neoplasm of vulva

Epidemiology

* F ~ M
* In women, age range 22 - 77 years (median ~ 47 years) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1426](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15489646), [J Cutan Pathol 2003;30:405](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12834491))
* Men typically older (32 - 88 years; median ~ 65 years) ([Mod Pathol 2011;24:82](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20852591), [Am J Surg Pathol 1998;22:6](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9422311))

Sites

* In women, labium majus most common, followed by vagina and perineum ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1426](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15489646), [Mod Pathol 2011;24:82](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20852591), [Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:636](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9199640))
* In men, scrotum and groin most common ([Am J Surg Pathol 1998;22:6](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9422311))
* Rare limb, chest wall and retroperitoneal cases ([Diagn Pathol 2015;10:114](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26187500))

Pathophysiology

* Histogenesis unknown

Clinical features

* Painless, slowly growing mass
* Present for weeks to years before diagnosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:636](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9199640), [Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1426](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15489646))
* Clinically mimics Bartholin cyst, lipoma or leiomyoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1426](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15489646))

Diagnosis

* Diagnosis typically follows complete local excision of a clinically benign appearing mass

Prognostic factors

* Local recurrence exceptionally rare, even with positive margins ([Mod Pathol 2011;24:82](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20852591), [J Clin Pathol 2002;55:477](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12037036))
* So called sarcomatous transformation does not increase risk of recurrence ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:707](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20305534))
* No reports of distant metastasis

Case reports

* 37 year old woman with a painless vulvar mass ([BMC Clin Pathol 2016;16:8](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27274709))
* 49 year old woman with a recurrent vulvar cellular angiofibroma ([J Clin Pathol 2002;55:477](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12037036))
* 77 year old man with a left inguinal mass ([IJU Case Rep 2020;3:69](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32743474))
* 79 year old man with a scrotal mass ([Diagn Pathol 2017;12:17](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28193293))

Treatment

* Simple excision considered curative ([Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:636](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9199640), [Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1426](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15489646))
* Re-excision for positive margins not mandatory

Gross description

* Well circumscribed, nodular or multilobulated, rubbery mass
* Cut surface white-tan to grey
* Gross hemorrhage and necrosis in < 5% ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1426](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15489646))
* In women, size 0.6 - 12 cm (mean ~ 3 cm) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1426](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15489646), [Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:636](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9199640), [Mod Pathol 2011;24:82](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20852591))
* In men, size 0.6 - 25 cm (mean ~ 7 cm) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1426](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15489646), [Am J Surg Pathol 1998;22:6](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9422311))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Predominantly based in subcutis; rarely in dermis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1426](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15489646))
* Usually well circumscribed; rare cases infiltrative ([Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:636](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9199640), [Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1426](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15489646))
* Fibrous pseudocapsule in a subset ([Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:636](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9199640), [Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1426](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15489646))
* Tumor cells:
  + Small, monotonous spindle cells with bland, ovoid to fusiform nuclei
  + Arranged in short intersecting fascicles
  + Bland multinucleated cells common ([Histopathology 2004;45:360](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15469474))
  + Mitoses typically rare (< 1 per 10 hpf) but occasionally brisk (> 10 per 10 hpf) ([Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:636](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9199640), [Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1426](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15489646))
  + Rare cases show focal or diffuse atypia or discrete areas of sarcomatous transformation ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:707](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20305534))
    - Sarcomatous transformation may resemble well differentiated liposarcoma, pleomorphic liposarcoma or undifferentiated pleomorphic sarcoma
* Tumor stroma and vasculature:
  + Myxoid, edematous, fibrous or hyalinized stroma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1426](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15489646))
  + Abundant medium sized, thick walled, hyalinized vessels
  + Short wispy collagen bundles
  + Minor adipocytic component in ~ 50%; rare cases show prominent adipocytic differentiation ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1426](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15489646))
  + Mast cells may be conspicuous
  + Necrosis and hemorrhage absent

Positive stains

* [Vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:636](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9199640), [Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1426](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15489646), [J Cutan Pathol 2003;30:405](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12834491), [Histopathology 2004;45:360](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15469474), [Mod Pathol 2011;24:82](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20852591))
* [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) (most), [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html) (most)
* [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) (~50%)
* Tumors with sarcomatous transformation typically show [p16](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp16.html) overexpression and rarely mutant [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2010;34:707](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20305534))

Negative stains

* [Rb](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsrb.html) (i.e., expression lost) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2012;36:1119](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22790852))
* Myogenic markers: [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) (rarely positive), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [caldesmon](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscaldesmon.html)
* [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) (rarely positive)
* [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:636](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9199640), [J Cutan Pathol 2003;30:405](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12834491), [Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1426](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15489646), [Histopathology 2004;45:360](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15469474))
* Cytokeratin ([AE1 / AE3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsae1ae3.html), [CAM5.2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscam52.html))
* [CD117](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd117.html)
* [MDM2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmdm2.html) and [CDK4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdk4.html) in those with sarcomatous transformation ([Diagnostics (Basel) 2020;10:35](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31936598))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Monoallelic *RB1* / *FOXO1* deletion (chr 13q12-22) in all tested cases ([Mod Pathol 2011;24:82](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20852591), [Histopathology 2007;51:410](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17727484), [Diagn Pathol 2017;12:17](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28193293))
* *TP53* mutations present in a subset of cases with sarcomatous transformation ([Diagnostics (Basel) 2020;10:35](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31936598))

Differential diagnosis

* [Angiomyofibroblastoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/vulvaangiomyofibroblastoma.html):
  + Alternating hypo and hypercellular foci
  + Spindled to epithelioid to plasmacytoid cells, clustering around vessels
  + Vascular hyalinization not prominent
* [Spindle cell lipoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueadiposepleomorphicgeneral.html):
  + Typically affects head and neck region in men
  + Thick ropy collagen bands
  + Hyalinized vessels not prominent
* [Mammary type myofibroblastoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/breastmyofibroblastoma.html):
  + More fascicular spindle cells
  + Less conspicuous hyalinized vessels
  + Thick ropy collagen bands
  + [Desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) and [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) consistently co-expressed
* [Solitary fibrous tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesft.html):
  + Characteristic staghorn / hemangiopericytoma-like vessels
  + More marked variation in cellularity
  + Thick bands of hyalinized collagen
  + [STAT6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsstat6.html) positive
* [Leiomyoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uterusleiomyoma.html):
  + Large cigar shaped nuclei
  + Prominent eosinophilic cytoplasm
  + Fewer and larger vessels, with less prominent hyalinization
  + [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html) and [caldesmon](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscaldesmon.html) positive
* [Perineurioma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueperineurioma.html):
  + Lacks vascular hyalinization
  + [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) positive

Board review style question #1

[![](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/vulvacellularAFchapelBRQ01.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/vulvacellularAFchapelBRQ01.jpg)

A 48 year old woman presented with a painless 3.5 cm vulvar mass, which had been slowly growing for 18 months. She underwent complete local excision of a rubbery, well circumscribed mass. A representative photomicrograph is shown. Which of the following is true about this lesion?

1. Complete local excision is considered curative
2. Immunohistochemistry for estrogen receptor and progesterone receptor is typically negative
3. Nuclear pleomorphism is associated with an increased risk of distant metastasis
4. Rb protein is typically overexpressed
5. This lesion occurs exclusively in women

Board review style answer #1

**A**. Complete local excision is considered curative. This is a cellular angiofibroma.
