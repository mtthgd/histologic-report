Definition / general

* Condyloma acuminatum is an HPV associated wart-like lesion of squamous epithelium

Essential features

* Benign HPV related lesion of the anogenital region with warty appearance and histologically bland papillomatous hyperplasia of squamous epithelium
* Giant condyloma (Buschke-Löwenstein tumor) develops over confluence of longstanding condyloma acuminata with the propensity to transform into invasive carcinoma

Terminology

* Acceptable: condylomatous low grade squamous intraepithelial lesion (LSIL), genital wart

ICD coding

* ICD-11: [1A95.1](https://icd.who.int/browse11/l-m/en#/http%3a%2f%2fid.who.int%2ficd%2fentity%2f144526718) - genital warts

Epidemiology

* Sexually active adults (1%), peak incidence between 20 - 29, HIV predisposing
* Buschke-Löwenstein tumor is observed mostly in the fifth decade after median condyloma diagnosis for 5 years

Sites

* Labia majora, vestibule, perineum, vagina, perianal, rare extragenital

Etiology

* Low risk HPV types 6 and 11 most common ([Sex Transm Dis 2013;40:123](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23324975), [Acta Derm Venereol 2013;93:223](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22688658))
* High risk HPV types in up to 42% ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:1513](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17122506))

Clinical features

* Range from only colposcopically visible lesions to small papules to large masses with cauliflower-like appearance, often multiple ([Best Pract Res Clin Obstet Gynaecol 2014;28:1063](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25155525))
* Buschke-Löwenstein tumor shows clinically expansive and destructive growth; there is no sharp criterion for size to differentiate it from large condyloma

Diagnosis

* Clinical appearance, punch biopsy

Prognostic factors

* Benign process, frequent recurrence, spontaneous regression possible ([Sex Transm Dis 2011;38:949](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21934571))
* Buschke-Löwenstein tumor shows focal development of invasive carcinoma in up to 50%, rarely metastasizes; mortality 20%; relapse in up to 50% after therapy ([Dis Colon Rectum 1994;37:950](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8076499))

Case reports

* 25 year old pregnant woman with large condyloma acuminatum in the vagina ([Int J STD AIDS 2011;22:534](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21890557))
* 29 year old woman with giant condyloma during pregnancy ([Acta Obstet Gynecol Scand 2007;86:762](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17520414))
* 32 year old pregnant woman with disseminated condyloma acuminata ([Indian J Pathol Microbiol 2019;62:310](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30971564))

Treatment

* Local excision or laser ablation, imiquimod

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Overall verrucous or warty architecture ([Adv Anat Pathol 2005;12:20](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15614161))
* Acanthosis, papillomatosis, hyperkeratosis and hypergranulosis of squamous epithelium
* Broad papillae with rounded ends which are fused at the base
* Viral cytopathic effects: variable koilocytosis (may be absent or inconspicuous), binuclear cells
* Usually no stromal inflammation
* Giant condyloma Buschke-Löwenstein shows the same histological changes with more endophytic growth but without invasion

Positive stains

* [HPV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshpv.html) detection by RNA in situ hybridization or immunohistochemical demonstration of L1 capsid protein

Negative stains

* [p16](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp16.html) stains in patchy / nonblock type pattern or is negative

Sample pathology report

* Labium minora, biopsy:
  + Condyloma acuminatum

Differential diagnosis

* [Seborrheic keratosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticSK.html):
  + Horn pearls, squamous eddies, minimal hypergranulosis
  + [HPV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshpv.html) negative
* **Vestibular papillomatosis**:
  + Branching delicate papillae without keratinization, no cytopathic viral effects
  + [HPV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshpv.html) ISH negative
* [Squamous papilloma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cervixsquamouspapilloma.html):
  + Minimal branching, fibrovascular core, no cytopathic viral effects
  + [HPV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshpv.html) ISH negative
* [Condyloma with HSIL](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/vulvaVIN.html):
  + [p16](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp16.html) positive / block type
  + Significant nuclear atypia up to upper epithelial layers, loss of maturation towards apical, no prominent koilocytosis
  + High risk [HPV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshpv.html) positive
* [Verrucous carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/vulvaverrucous.html):
  + Unequivocal pushing border invasion, stromal inflammation, no koilocytosis / no [HPV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshpv.html) association (as seen in giant condyloma Buschke Löwnstein)

Board review style question #1

[![Condyloma and HPV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/vulvacondylomaChoschzick002.jpg)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/imgau/vulvacondylomaChoschzick002.jpg)

Which virus type is most often associated with condyloma acuminatum?

1. HHV8
2. HIV
3. HPV16
4. HPV6
5. HSV

Board review style answer #1

**D.** HPV6

HPV6 is a low risk HPV and is most often found in condyloma as shown in several studies. The frequency of HPV16 is lower than that of HPV6. HIV is only predisposing, not found in the epithelium. There is no association with HHV8 and HSV. ([Am J Surg Pathol 2006;30:1513](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17122506))

Board review style question #2

Which diagnostic test is useful to differentiate between verrucous carcinoma and a large condyloma?

1. HPV IHC
2. HPV ISH
3. Ki67
4. p16
5. p53 IHC

Board review style answer #2

**B.** HPV ISH

HPV ISH has high sensitivity and specificity while HPV IHC has low sensitivity. Both entities are p53 wildtype, p16 patchy / negative and have elevated Ki67 indices.
