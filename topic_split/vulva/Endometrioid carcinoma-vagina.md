Definition / general

* Second most common subtype of primary vaginal adenocarcinoma with majority of cases associated and likely arising from endometriosis
* Metastatic endometrioid adenocarcinoma to vagina and local spread from a neoplasm arising in an adjacent organ needs to be excluded ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1490](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17895749))

Epidemiology

* Age range 45 to 81 years (mean age 60 years) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1490](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17895749))

Sites

* Most common site is vaginal apex
* Can also occur in posterior wall, lateral wall and anterior wall ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1490](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17895749))

Pathophysiology

* Prior hysterectomy and trauma due to surgery might predispose to development of endometriosis at vaginal apex and further development of carcinoma at the same site ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1490](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17895749))

Etiology

* Vaginal endometriosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1490](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17895749), [Pathol Int 2010;60:636](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20712651), [Gynecol Oncol 1989;34:232](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2666285), [Am Fam Physician 2000;62:734](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10969853), [Obstet Gynecol 1984;64:592](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6483304), [Gynecol Oncol 1982;14:271](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7129222))
* Patient can have history of prior hysterectomy for endometriosis
* Unrelated to exposure to DES that is usually seen in association with clear cell carcinoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1490](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17895749) but see [Cancer 1970;25:745](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/5443099))

Clinical features

* Most common symptoms are vaginal bleeding or vaginal discharge ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1490](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17895749))
* Also pelvic pain and constipation
* Can be discovered incidentally as pelvic mass on routine vaginal examination
* May have history of prior hysterectomy due to endometriosis or other benign disease ([Pathol Int 2010;60:636](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20712651))

Diagnosis

* Based on histologic examination of biopsy or resection specimen which shows pure or predominant component of typical endometrioid adenocarcinoma and excluding local spread or metastatic carcinoma to vagina

Prognostic factors

* May recur and can metastasize to distant sites including lungs, bowel
* Stage I and II do well without distant metastasis and have better 5 year survival ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1490](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17895749))

Case reports

* 34 year old woman with primary invasive vaginal cancer in setting of Mayer-Rokitansky-Küster-Hauser syndrome ([Gynecol Oncol 2002;85:384](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11972406))
* 42 year old woman ([Proc R Soc Med 1967;60:999](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6057988))
* 57 year old woman with endometrioid adenocarcinoma of the vagina with a microglandular pattern arising from endometriosis after hysterectomy ([Pathol Int 2010;60:636](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20712651))
* 68 year old woman with adenocarcinoma arising in endometriosis ([Am Fam Physician 2000;62:734](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10969853))
* Arising in vaginal endometriosis ([Gynecol Oncol 1989;34:232](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2666285))
* Primary vaginal endometrioid carcinoma following unopposed estrogen administration ([J Obstet Gynaecol 2003;23:316](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12918477))
* Malignant transformation of vaginal endometriosis ([Obstet Gynecol 1984;64:592](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6483304))
* Adenocarcinoma of vagina arising in endometriosis ([Gynecol Oncol 1982;14:271](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7129222))
* Vaginal adenocarcinoma developing in residual pelvic endometriosis ([Gynecol Oncol 1989;33:96](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2703173))
* Adenocarcinoma of vaginal vault following prolonged unopposed estrogen hormone replacement therapy ([J Obstet Gynaecol 2005;25:220](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15814421))

Treatment

* Radical resection of tumor; if tumor is small, conservative local resection can be attempted
* Post surgical radiotherapy, chemotherapy, hormonal therapy or a combination ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1490](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17895749))

Gross description

* Polypoid, papillary, rough, granular, fungating, exophytic or flat
* Can also be partially cystic
* Size ranges from 1.4 cm to 7.0 cm ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1490](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17895749))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Atypical glandular proliferation composed of tubular glands lined by columnar cells with moderate amount of eosinophilic cytoplasm and occasional intracytoplasmic mucin
* Nuclei are oval to elongated, large and stratified or pseudostratified
* Glands can show microcysts and numerous neutrophils within and around cysts, microglandular pattern ([Pathol Int 2010;60:636](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20712651), [Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1490](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17895749))
* Nuclear features are bland in microglandular pattern so careful histological examination for classic endometrioid adenocarcinoma and architectural complexity is required ([Pathol Int 2010;60:636](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20712651))
* Squamous metaplasia can also be seen with cytoplasmic clearing due to glycogen accumulation
* Rare cases have nonvillous papillary budding pattern ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1490](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17895749))
* **Grades:** vary from well differentiated to moderately to poorly differentiated ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1490](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17895749), [Am Fam Physician 2000;62:734](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10969853))

Endometroid adenocarcinoma

Cytology description

* Atypical glandular cells with hyperchromatic nuclei and high N:C ratio with prominent nucleolus
* Microglandular pattern has clusters of epithelial cells in papillary arrangement and microglandular structures; neutrophils are seen within and around cystic glands
* Cells have lacy and pale cytoplasm, round to oval small nuclei with fine chromatin and small but distinct nucleoli ([Pathol Int 2010;60:636](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20712651))

Positive stains

* Diffusely positive for [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [MUC1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmucins.html), [ER](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainser.html) and [PR](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsprog.html) and focally positive for [vimentin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsvimentin.html), [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html), [CK5](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5.html) / [6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck6.html), [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html), [p16 (INK4a)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp16.html) and [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) ([Pathol Int 2010;60:636](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20712651))

Negative stains

* [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html), [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1490](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17895749))

Differential diagnosis

* **Metastasis from adjacent organs including**: uterus, cervix, ovary, vulva, urinary tract and from distant sites such as lower GI tract
* **Other subtypes of primary vaginal adenocarcinoma:** serous adenocarcinoma, adenosarcoma, polypoid endometriosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 2007;31:1490](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17895749))
