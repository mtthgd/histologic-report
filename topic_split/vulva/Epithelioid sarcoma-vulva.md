Definition / general

* Epithelioid sarcoma of the vulva and deep pelvic soft tissue is a rare malignant neoplasm characterized by *SMARCB1* / *INI1* deletion
* Most vulvar and pelvic epithelioid sarcomas are of the proximal type and show more aggressive clinical behavior than distal type (classical) epithelioid sarcoma

Essential features

* In vulva and pelvic soft tissues, proximal type epithelioid sarcoma > distal type
* Proximal type epithelioid sarcoma: sheets or nests of epithelioid to rhabdoid cells with nuclear atypia
* Positive immunostains: CK, EMA, CD34 (~ 50%)
* *SMARCB1* / *INI1* deletion in > 90%, resulting in SMARCB1 / INI1 loss by IHC
* Frequent local recurrence, lymph node metastasis, distant metastasis (especially to lung) and death

Epidemiology

* Women, 17 - 80 years (most often in fourth and fifth decades) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:836](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25651469), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2010;29:600](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20881848), [Mod Pathol 2001;14:655](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11454997))
* Rarely diagnosed in pregnancy ([Gynecol Oncol 2002;85:218](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11925150), [Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2009;17:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19384081))

Sites

* Vulvar epithelioid sarcoma most often involves the labia majora and mons pubis ([Cancer 1983;52:1462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6352000))
* Superficial inguinal region and deep pelvic soft tissues also affected ([Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9042279), [Mod Pathol 2001;14:655](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11454997))

Pathophysiology

* Histogenesis uncertain

Clinical features

* Typically presents with painless, slow growing vulvar mass ([Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9042279), [Mod Pathol 2001;14:655](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11454997))
* Occasional lesions painful or pruritic ([Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:848](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2476944), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2010;29:600](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20881848))
* Present for weeks to years before diagnosis ([Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9042279))
* Rare cases ulcerated ([Mod Pathol 2001;14:655](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11454997))
* Clinically resemble lipoma or Bartholin cyst ([Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:848](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2476944), [Cancer 1983;52:1462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6352000))
* Clinical evaluation may underestimate lesion size ([Cancer 1983;52:1462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6352000))

Diagnosis

* Diagnosis typically established on examination of excision specimen
* Occasionally diagnosed on preoperative biopsy ([Mod Pathol 2001;14:655](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11454997), [BMJ Case Rep 2015;2015:bcr2014208488](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25858929))

Radiology description

* Ultrasound: may show local tumor infiltration or lymph node metastases ([BMJ Case Rep 2015;2015:bcr2014208488](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25858929))
* CT: may show local tumor infiltration or lymph node or distant metastases ([BMJ Case Rep 2015;2015:bcr2014208488](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25858929))
* T2 weighted MRI: signal intensity similar to fat ([Int J Clin Exp Pathol 2015;8:7526](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26261664))
* Diffusion weighted MRI: high signal intensity

Prognostic factors

* Proximal type epithelioid sarcoma (more common in vulva) has poorer prognosis than distal type (classical) epithelioid sarcoma (rare in vulva) ([Cancer 1983;52:1462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6352000), [Mod Pathol 2001;14:655](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11454997))
* Local recurrence in approximately 67% ([Mod Pathol 2001;14:655](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11454997))
* Metastases (most often to lymph nodes, lung, liver, bone and skin) in approximately 70%, often after one or more local recurrences ([Cancer 1983;52:1462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6352000), [Mod Pathol 2001;14:655](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11454997), [Gynecol Oncol 1980;9:237](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7372196))
* Approximately 50% die of disease, often within 2 years of diagnosis ([Cancer 1983;52:1462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6352000), [Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9042279))
* Large tumor size (> 7.8 cm) and early metastasis (< 28 months after diagnosis) associated with increased mortality ([Mod Pathol 2001;14:655](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11454997))

Treatment

* Early wide en bloc excision or radical vulvectomy with node dissection; local excision is inadequate ([Gynecol Oncol 1980;9:237](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7372196), [Obstet Gynecol 1976;48:14S](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/945875), [Obstet Gynecol 1972;40:839](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/4636913))
* Long term followup for late recurrence ([Mod Pathol 2001;14:655](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11454997))
* Chemotherapy and radiation administered at clinical discretion; robust trials lacking ([Gynecol Oncol 1980;9:237](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7372196), [Gynecol Oncol 2007;107:130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17692365), [Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2009;17:270](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19384081))
* EZH2 inhibitor tazemetostat FDA approved for epithelioid sarcoma ([Drugs 2020;80:513](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32166598))

Gross description

* Size range, 1 - 8 cm (average, ~ 3 - 5 cm) ([Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9042279), [Mod Pathol 2001;14:655](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11454997), [Gynecol Oncol Rep 2016;15:31](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26937486))
* Poorly circumscribed, multinodular, firm gray to fleshy white mass ([Cancer 1983;52:1462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6352000))
* Most often based in subcutis or deep pelvic soft tissues; occasionally dermal ([Cancer 1983;52:1462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6352000), [Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9042279), [Mod Pathol 2001;14:655](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11454997))
* Gross necrosis may be present ([Cancer 1983;52:1462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6352000))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* In vulva: proximal type > distal type
* Proximal type epithelioid sarcoma ([Cancer 1983;52:1462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6352000), [Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:848](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2476944), [Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9042279), [Mod Pathol 2001;14:655](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11454997), [Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:836](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25651469)):
  + Extensively infiltrates surrounding tissues
  + Multinodular or diffuse growth pattern in collagenous or myxoid stroma
  + Atypical polygonal epithelioid cells
  + Minor spindle cell population may be present peripherally or admixed
  + Coarse vesicular chromatin and 1 - 2 prominent nucleoli
  + Abundant eosinophilic cytoplasm with distinct borders
  + Cytoplasm may contain an inclusion displacing nucleus peripherally (rhabdoid morphology)
  + Subset show exclusively rhabdoid morphology
  + Mitoses highly variable (2 - 57 mitoses per 10 high power fields) ([Cancer 1983;52:1462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6352000))
  + Atypical mitoses, necrosis, hemorrhage and lymphovascular invasion common
* Distal type epithelioid sarcoma of the vulva ([Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:848](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2476944), [Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9042279)):
  + Pseudo granulomatous growth pattern: scattered small tumor nodules with central necrosis in a collagenous stroma with prominent lymphocytic inflammation
  + Monomorphic eosinophilic epithelioid or histiocytoid cells
  + Bland nuclei with inconspicuous nucleoli
  + Rhabdoid morphology not common
  + 8 mitoses per 10 high power fields in 1 vulvar case ([Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:848](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2476944))

Electron microscopy description

* Mononuclear cells
* Conspicuous intermediate filaments, sometimes as perinuclear whorls ([Cancer 1983;52:1462](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6352000), [Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9042279), [Am J Surg Pathol 1989;13:848](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2476944))
* Epithelial type cell - cell adhesions (tonofilaments or desmosomes) ([Am J Surg Pathol 1997;21:130](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9042279))
* Well developed rough endoplasmic reticulum, ribosomes and lysosomes
* Few mitochondria ([Gynecol Oncol 1980;9:237](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7372196))

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Homozygous deletions of *SMARCB1* / *INI1* in 80 - 90% of proximal type epithelioid sarcoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:542](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19033866), [Cancer Res 2005;65:4012](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15899790), [Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2014;53:475](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24585572), [Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:312](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29309303), [Mod Pathol 2013;26:385](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23060122))
* *SMARCB1* / *INI1* deletion correlates strongly with SMARCB1 / INI1 loss by IHC ([Eur J Cancer 2011;47:287](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20932739))
* *SMARCA4*, *SMARCC2* or *SMARCC1* alterations in rare cases ([Am J Surg Pathol 2018;42:312](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29309303))

Sample pathology report

* Vulva, mass, wide local excision:
  + Epithelioid sarcoma, proximal type (6.0 cm) (see comment)
  + Margins are negative for tumor
  + Comment: Microscopic examination reveals an infiltrative tumor composed of atypical epithelioid cells, some of which show prominent rhabdoid cytoplasmic inclusions. By immunohistochemistry, tumor cells are positive for cytokeratin AE1 / AE3, EMA and CD34 and show loss of SMARCB1 / INI1 expression. S100, CD31, ER and PR are negative. The findings are most consistent with proximal type epithelioid sarcoma. These are aggressive tumors with a high rate of local recurrence, lymph node metastasis and distant metastasis. Clinical and radiographic correlation are advised.

Differential diagnosis

* [Squamous cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/vaginascc.html):
  + Keratinization or intercellular bridging frequently present
  + Associated with usual or differentiated type vulvar intraepithelial neoplasia
  + [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html) / [p40](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdeltaNp63.html) positive
  + [HPV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshpv.html) in situ hybridization positive in HPV associated tumors
  + Mutant pattern [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) immunostaining in a subset of HPV independent tumors
  + [SMARCB1 / INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html) positive (retained)
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) negative
* [Myoepithelial carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuemyoepithelialca.html) ([Am J Surg Pathol 2015;39:836](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25651469)):
  + Less common than epithelioid sarcoma in vulva
  + More varied cytology and more prominent spindle cells
  + Reticulated architecture in myxoid stroma
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) or [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html) positive
  + [SMARCB1 / INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html) IHC negative (lost) in ~ 50% ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:542](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19033866))
  + May harbor *EWSR* rearrangements (not yet detected in vulvar tumors)
* [Extrarenal malignant rhabdoid tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuerhabdoidtumor.html):
  + In adults, most often represents carcinoma, sarcoma or melanoma with rhabdoid morphology (composite rhabdoid tumor)
  + Debated whether malignant rhabdoid tumor is an entity distinct from proximal type epithelioid sarcoma ([Cancer Res 2005;65:4012](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15899790), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2010;29:600](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20881848), [Hum Pathol 2015;46:225](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25479928), [Hum Pathol 2009;40:349](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18973917))
* [Malignant melanoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/vulvamelanoma.html):
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html), [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html), [MelanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html) positive
  + [SMARCB1 / INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html) positive (retained)
  + [Cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) and [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) negative
* [Epithelioid angiosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissueangiosarcoma.html):
  + True vascular spaces lined by multiple layers of tumor cells
  + Vacuolated tumor cells may contain red blood cells
  + [CD31](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd31.html) and [ERG](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsERG.html) positive
  + [Cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) may be positive but [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) is negative
* [Epithelioid malignant peripheral nerve sheath tumor](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuempnst.html):
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html) positive
  + [SMARCB1 / INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html) IHC negative (lost) in ~ 50% ([Am J Surg Pathol 2009;33:542](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19033866))
  + [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) positive in ~ 50%
  + [Cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) and [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) negative
* Epithelioid [leiomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/uteruslms.html):
  + [SMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsalphasmoothmuscleactin.html), [desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [caldesmon](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscaldesmon.html) positive
  + [SMARCB1 / INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html) positive (retained)
  + [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) and [CD34](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd34.html) negative
* [Rhabdomyosarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/vaginarms.html):
  + [Desmin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsdesmin.html), [myogenin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyogenin.html), [myoD1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmyod1.html) positive
  + [SMARCB1 / INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html) positive (retained)
* Epithelioid monophasic [synovial sarcoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/softtissuesynovialsarc.html):
  + [SMARCB1 / INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html) positive (retained)
  + Characteristic t(X;18) fusion, with positive fusion specific immunostain ([Am J Surg Pathol 2020;44:922](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32141887))
* [Necrotizing granulomas](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/ovarynontumorgranulomatous.html) (in differential diagnosis with distal type epithelioid sarcoma):
  + [CD68](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/cdmarkerscd68.html) and [PU.1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainspu1.html) positive
  + [SMARCB1 / INI1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsini1.html) positive (retained)
  + [Cytokeratin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsckgeneral.html) and [EMA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsema.html) negative
  + Infectious organisms, foreign material or autoimmune etiology may be present

Which of the following is true about this tumor?

1. CD34 is expressed in ~ 50%
2. Exposure to ultraviolet radiation is the most common risk factor
3. Fewer than 10% of patients experience local recurrence

Board review style answer #1

**A**. CD34 is expressed in ~ 50%. This is a vulvar epithelioid sarcoma.
