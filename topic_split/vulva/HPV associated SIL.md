Definition / general

* Squamous intraepithelial lesion (SIL) is the morphological manifestation of HPV infection, both low and high risk types

Essential features

* Precursor lesion of HPV associated vulvar carcinoma
* p16 positivity in block type pattern (strong diffuse nuclear or nuclear / cytoplasmic staining)
* Harbors clinically undetected carcinomas in up to 20% of cases ([J Gynecol Oncol 2017;28:e27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28541626))

Terminology

* Low grade squamous intraepithelial lesion (LSIL): vulvar intraepithelial neoplasia 1, low grade vulvar intraepithelial neoplasia, condyloma accuminatum, mild dysplasia, condyloma/VaIN 1 ([Pathology 2016;48:291](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27113549))
* High grade squamous intraepithelial lesion (HSIL): vulvar intraepithelial neoplasia of usual type (u-VIN), high grade vulvar intraepithelial neoplasia, vulvar intraepithelial neoplasia 2, vulvar intraepithelial neoplasia 3, moderate, severe dysplasia, VaIN 2, VaIN 3

ICD coding

* ICD-10: [N90.0](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/N00-N99/N80-N98/N90-/N90.0) - mild vulvar dysplasia
* ICD-10: [N90.1](https://www.icd10data.com/ICD10CM/Codes/N00-N99/N80-N98/N90-/N90.1) - moderate vulvar dysplasia
* ICD-10: [N90.2](https://www.rxreasoner.com/icd10codes/N90.2) - severe vulvar dysplasia, not elsewhere classified
* ICD-O: [8077/0](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=64) - low grade squamous intraepithelial lesion
* ICD-O: [8077/2](https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/96612/9789241548496_eng.pdf?sequence=1#page=64) - high grade squamous intraepithelial lesion

Epidemiology

* Reproductive age women
* High grade squamous intraepithelial lesion is the most frequent (> 90%) vulvar intraepithelial neoplasia type
* More common in patients with HIV
* Risk factors: smoking, oral contraception, herpes infection, HPV infection elsewhere
* Often component of multitopic lower genital tract neoplasia
* References: [Ecancermedicalscience 2015;9:531](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25987900), [Gynecol Oncol 2017;145:298](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28236455)

Sites

* Frequently multifocal (70%)
* Introitus, labia minora, labia majora, posterior fourchette, periclitoral
* Fornix and upper portions of the vagina, multifocal

Pathophysiology

* HPV integrates in the host genome or resides episomally
* Virus proteins E6 and E7 promote cell transformation and proliferation due to suppression of p53, upregulation of telomerase and stabilization of retinoblastoma protein ([Nat Rev Cancer 2002;2:342](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12044010))

Etiology

* High grade squamous intraepithelial lesion: HPV high risk types, most common HPV type 16, others 18, 31, 33, 45
* Low grade squamous intraepithelial lesion: HPV low risk 6 / 11 and high risk types (~40%) ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2017;36:486](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28719486))

Clinical features

* White, pink or red macules or elevated plaques
* Papules or verrucae with pruritus or asymptomatic
* Some lesions are pigmented
* Acetic acid positive
* Reference: [Best Pract Res Clin Obstet Gynaecol 2014;28:1051](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25106700)

Diagnosis

* Punch biopsy

Prognostic factors

* Most low grade squamous intraepithelial lesions regress spontaneously, low risk for progression to high grade squamous intraepithelial lesion or invasive carcinoma
* Up to 9% of high grade squamous intraepithelial lesion progress to squamous cell carcinoma if untreated, 3 - 4% if treated; spontaneous regression possible; recurrence rate ~15% after treatment ([Gynecol Oncol 2005;97:645](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15863172))

Case reports

* 6 young women aged 20 to 36 with regression of vulvar intraepithelial neoplasia 3 ([J Low Genit Tract Dis 2012;16:56](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22207153))
* 36 year old pregnant woman with vulvar intraepithelial neoplasia 3 ([Ann Agric Environ Med 2017;24:459](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28954490))
* 44 year old woman with vulvar intraepithelial neoplasia 3 and Behçet’s disease ([BMC Res Notes 2016;9:172](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26983442))
* 49 year old woman with multinucleated atypia ([Cutis 2005;75:118](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15773533))
* 51 year old woman with vulvar intraepithelial neoplasia 3 ([Int J Clin Oncol 2011;16:610](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21249413))
* 75 year old woman with vulvar intraepithelial neoplasia 3 ([Int J Womens Dermatol 2016;2:35](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28492000))

Treatment

* Wide local excision if cancer is suspected; otherwise local excision, laser ablation, local topical agent imiquimod ([Obstet Gynecol 2016;128:937](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27661648))
* No treatment for low grade squamous intraepithelial lesion if asymptomatic

Microscopic (histologic) description

* High grade squamous intraepithelial lesion
  + Acanthosis, papillomatosis, enlarged atypical nuclei in all cell layers including middle and upper third of the epithelium (full thickness atypia), suprabasal mitoses, atypical mitosis, extension in hair follicles and skin appendages ([Pathology 2013;45:214](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23442737))
  + Warty type vulvar intraepithelial neoplasia: extensive hyper and parakeratosis at the surface, some koilocytosis possible, condylomatous appearance with wide rete pegs which extend into the subepithelial stroma
  + Basaloid type vulvar intraepithelial neoplasia: atypical immature parabasal type cells in the entire epithelium, numerous mitotic figures
  + Rarely pagetoid pattern of vulvar intraepithelial neoplasia is observed simulating extramammary Paget disease
  + Various types often admixed, classification of the predominant type, distinction of types has no known clinical relevance
  + Can be a component of a condyloma
  + Foci of unsuspected invasion in up to 20% cases ([J Gynecol Oncol 2017;28:e27](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28541626))
* Low grade squamous intraepithelial lesion
  + Acanthosis / papillomatosis, atypical koilocytosis in upper layers, usually mild atypia of immature cells and mitoses limited to the lower third, binucleated epithelial cells

Positive stains

* Squamous cell markers: [p63](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp63.html), [CK5/6](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck5and6.html)
* High grade squamous intraepithelial lesion
  + Nuclear or nuclear and cytoplasmic [p16](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp16.html) positivity involving at least the lower one third of epithelium with frequent extension upward ([J Low Genit Tract Dis 2012;16:205](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22820980))
  + Numerous [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) positive nuclei in middle and higher portions
  + Heterogeneous [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) staining with varying intensities especially in lower epithelial layers (wild type pattern)
  + [CK17](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck17.html) shows only patchy, weak to moderate staining in superficial cell layers ([Virchows Arch 2018;473:739](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30187167))
* Low grade squamous intraepithelial lesion
  + [p16](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp16.html) usually negative but may be rarely positive in block type pattern in the lower third of the epithelium
  + [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) is elevated, mostly in the lower third of the epithelium, preserved compartments
  + Immunohistochemically positive [HPV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshpv.html) detection in koilocytic cells in a subset of cases depending on the antibody used

Negative stains

* [CK20](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck20.html), [CDX2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscdx2.html), [GCDFP-15](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsgcdfp15.html)

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* HPV DNA/mRNA positive

Sample pathology report

* Labium minor, left side, biopsy:
  + High grade squamous intraepithelial lesion (vulvar intraepithelial neoplasia 2-3) (see comment)
  + Comment: p16 is positive (block type).

Differential diagnosis

* High grade squamous intraepithelial lesion
  + [Invasive squamous cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/vulvascc.html) (versus involvement of skin adnexa):
    - Desmoplasia, irregular contours, inflammatory response, distorted architecture are features indicative of invasion
  + [Paget disease](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/vulvapagetsdisease.html):
    - Positive for [CK7](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsck7.html), [CEA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainscea.html) and [mucin](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmucins.html)
    - [p16](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp16.html) may be positive ([Int J Gynecol Pathol 2013;32:221](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23370646))
  + [Melanoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/vulvamelanoma.html):
    - Positive for melanoma markers ([S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html), [melanA](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html), [tyrosinase](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainstyrosinase.html), [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html))
  + [Basal cell carcinoma](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/vulvabasalcellcarc.html):
    - Peripherial palisading
    - [BerEP4](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsepcam.html) positive
  + [Seborrheic keratosis](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumornonmelanocyticSK.html):
    - Keratin pearls, uniform cells without atypia
  + [Differentiated vulvar intraepithelial neoplasia](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/vulvadifferentiatedVIN.html):
    - [p16](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp16.html) negative, [HPV](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshpv.html) negative, abnormal (mutated) [p53](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp53.html) expression
* Low grade squamous intraepithelial lesion
  + **Squamous cell hyperplasia**, [lichen simplex](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skinnontumorlichensimplexchronicus.html):
    - [Ki67](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainski67.html) limited to basal cell layers
