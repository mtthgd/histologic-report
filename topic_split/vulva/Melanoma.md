Definition / general

* Primary malignant melanocytic tumor

Essential features

* Malignant melanocytic tumor arising from melanocytes in mucosal or cutaneous areas of the lower genital tract
* Tumor thickness is the most important prognostic factor

Terminology

* Malignant melanoma (MM)
* Primary malignant melanoma of vulva
* Primary malignant melanoma of vagina
* Primary malignant melanoma of lower genital tract

Epidemiology

* Accounts for Lancet Oncol 2008;9:973)
* 200 of 100,000 (0.2%) women per year will be diagnosed with vulvar melanoma ([Obstet Gynecol 2007;110:296](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17666603), [Gynecol Oncol 2011;122:612](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21570710))
* Difference in incidence of vulvar and vaginal melanomas among ethnic groups is significantly smaller than in cutaneous melanomas of other sites ([Melanoma Res 2010;20:153](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20147857))
* Vulvar melanoma
  + Although vulvar melanomas are rare, accounting for 1 - 2% of all melanomas, they represent the most common primary genitourinary (GU) melanomas and primary gynecologic melanomas ([J Natl Compr Canc Netw 2016;14:450](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27059193), [Case Rep Oncol 2021;14:1144](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34413746), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2014;71:1241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25267379), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2016;75:144](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27317515), [Urol Oncol 2016;34:166.e7](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26739672))
    - Review of the Survival Epidemiology and End Results (SEER) database between 1973 - 2010 indicates that 75% of primary GU melanomas are vulvar and 25% are vaginal ([Urol Oncol 2016;34:166.e7](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26739672))
  + Melanoma is the second most common vulvar malignancy following squamous cell carcinoma ([Am J Surg Pathol 2002;26:1450](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12409721), [Lancet Oncol 2008;9:973](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19071254), [Obstet Gynecol 2007;110:296](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17666603))
  + Occurs predominantly in postmenopausal White patients in the fifth to eighth decades ([Obstet Gynecol 2007;110:296](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17666603), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2014;71:1241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25267379))
  + Association with lichen sclerosus has been reported in the pediatric population ([Cancers (Basel) 2022;14:5217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36358637), [Pediatr Dermatol 2016;33:e190](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27040997), [Pediatr Dermatol 2004;21:473](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15283794), [Am J Dermatopathol 1984:6:253](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6528925), [Arch Dermatol 1997;133:345](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9080895))
* Vaginal melanoma: occurs in postmenopausal women in their fifth to seventh decades ([J Gynecol Oncol 2013;24:330](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24167668), [Int J Gynecol Cancer 2014;24:149](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24362720))

Sites

* Vulvar melanoma
  + Labia majora and labia minora > periclitoral / clitoris > midline structures (e.g., periurethral, introitus and posterior fourchette) ([Ann Surg Oncol 2015;22:1959](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25384702))
  + Most common: glabrous (hairless, mucosal) site in about 50% of cases, followed by hairy - glabrous skin junction (38%) and hairy skin (13%) ([Acta Oncol 2004;43:421](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15360046))
* Vaginal melanoma
  + Most common: lower third of vagina, in the anterior wall ([J Cancer Res Ther 2019;15:1392](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31898678))

Pathophysiology

* Pathogenesis of all subtypes is poorly understood ([Biomedicines 2021;9:758](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34209084))
* Vulvar melanoma (sun protected cutaneous melanomas, majority originating in mucosal epithelium)
  + Similar pathway to cutaneous melanomas arising in sun protected areas (e.g., acral and subungual melanomas)
  + It has been hypothesized that a single melanocyte can act as a spontaneous promoter
  + Vitamin D receptor (VDR) expression has been implicated ([Am J Cancer Res 2020;10:4017](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33414983))
  + Molecular profile differs from cutaneous melanomas ([J Low Genit Tract Dis 2023;27:40](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36083687))
    - Rate of *KIT* and *NRAS* mutations is higher than in cutaneous melanomas
    - Both *KIT* and *NRAS* are upstream to *BRAF* and part of Ras / Raf / MEK / ERK pathway
    - *BRAF* is relatively rare (J Low Genit Tract Dis 2015;19:350)
* Vaginal melanomas ([J Low Genit Tract Dis 2023;27:40](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36083687))
  + Although the data on strictly vaginal melanomas are limited and typically grouped with vulvar melanomas, *KIT* mutations appear to play a role in pathogenesis ([Melanoma Res 2014;24:360](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24870295), [Int J Gynecol Pathol 2020;39:587](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31567539), [Br J Dermatol 2017;177:1376](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28734009))

Etiology

* Unlike cutaneous melanomas, ultraviolet (UV) radiation exposure does not appear to impact disease onset ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2014;71:366](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24815565), [J Cutan Pathol 2005;32:191](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15701081))
* HPV infection may play a role ([Oncologist 2021;26:e473](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33191546), [Am J Dermatopathol 2007;29:13](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17284956))

Clinical features

* Clinical presentation is often delayed due to a variety of symptoms and rare self inspection ([Gynecol Oncol 2021;161:202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33514483))
* Symptoms are nonspecific ([Cancers (Basel) 2022;14:5217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36358637), [Asian J Urol 2022;9:407](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36381597), [Gynecol Oncol 2021;161:202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33514483), [Obstet Gynecol 2007;110:296](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17666603))
  + Early symptoms: pruritis, spontaneous bleeding or atypical discharge
  + May present as a distinct mass, swelling or lump sensation
  + Lymphadenopathy in advanced cases
  + May be asymptomatic ([J Low Genit Tract Dis 2016;20:e24](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27030881), [Melanoma Res 2008;18:127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18337649))
* On physical examination ([Cancers (Basel) 2022;14:5217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36358637), [Asian J Urol 2022;9:407](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36381597), [Gynecol Oncol 2021;161:202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33514483), [Obstet Gynecol 2007;110:296](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17666603))
  + ABCD: asymmetry, border irregularity, color variation / discoloration, diameter (concerning, > 6 mm)
  + 25 - 30% of cases are amelanotic, particularly on mucosal surfaces where melanoma may appear as a nonpigmented red polyp ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2004;50:554](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15034504), [Dermatol Ther 2010;23:449](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20868400), [Melanoma Res 2008;18:127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18337649))
  + May be multifocal ([Dermatol Ther 2010;23:449](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20868400))

Diagnosis

* Dermoscopy for nonmodified mucous membranes is useful
  + Dermoscopic evaluation aids in avoiding unnecessary biopsies ([Arch Dermatol 2011;147:1181](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21680757), [Dermatology 2011;222:157](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21311169))
  + Combination of blue, gray or white color with structureless zones and atypical vascularization is highly predictive of melanoma ([Arch Dermatol 2011;147:1181](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21680757), [Dermatology 2011;222:157](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21311169))
  + In the absence of melanoma specific dermoscopic features, shiny white streaks (SWS), inverse pigment network and vascular network in a symmetrical pattern can be seen in atypical melanocytic nevi of genital type (AMNGT) ([Dermatology 2011;222:157](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21311169), [Dermatology 2010;221:55](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20484874))
  + Experienced dermatologist will take a biopsy of clinically or dermoscopically concerning lesions
  + See [table 3](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/vulvamelanoma.html#vulvamelanomatable3)
* Histologic diagnosis is confirmatory

Laboratory

* Lactate dehydrogenase (LDH) has been suggested as a potential predictive and prognostic biomarker ([Cell Mol Biol Lett 2020:25:35](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32528540), [Cancer Immunol Immunother 2014;63:449](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24609989))
  + This has not been widely adopted and may lack sensitivity and specificity depending on LDH isoform utilized

Prognostic factors

* Recurrence rate
  + Overall recurrence rate of 60% ([Gynecol Oncol 2021;161:202](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33514483), [J Low Genit Tract Dis 2016;20:e24](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27030881), [Int J Gynecol Cancer 2016;26:1307](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27465889))
  + Independent prognostic factor for local recurrence: tumor size ([Int J Gynecol Cancer 2016;26:1307](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27465889))
  + Independent prognostic factor for distant recurrence: American Joint Committee on Cancer (AJCC) stage and lymph node involvement ([Int J Gynecol Cancer 2016;26:1307](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27465889), [N Engl J Med 2017;376:2211](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28591523), [Ann Surg Oncol 2000;7:738](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11129421))
* Survival ([Cancers (Basel) 2022;14:5217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36358637))
  + Clinical presentation
    - Central vulvar involvement is associated with worse overall survival (OS) and increased lymph node involvement ([Clin Cancer Res 2017;23:2093](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27864417), [Urol Oncol 2016;34:166.e7](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26739672))
    - Multifocal involvement is associated with worse overall survival ([Urol Oncol 2016;34:166.e7](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26739672))
  + Histopathologic factors
    - 5 year overall survival (5Y OS) is associated with number of mitoses and lymph node status ([J Low Genit Tract Dis 2016;20:e24](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27030881), [Ann Surg Oncol 2000;7:738](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11129421))
    - Factors independently associated with overall survival and disease specific survival (DSS): tumor thickness (≤ 2 mm2 or > 2 mm2) and dermal mitotic rate (2 or ≥ 2 mm2) ([Clin Cancer Res 2017;23:2093](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27864417))
    - Independent prognostic factor: tumor infiltrating lymphocytes ([Cancer Med 2018;7:583](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29464914), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2017;76:264](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27847125))
    - Ulceration is associated with worse survival ([Cancer Med 2018;7:583](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29464914), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2017;76:264](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27847125))
    - Lymphovascular invasion (LVI) is associated with positive sentinel lymph nodes ([Cancer Med 2018;7:583](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29464914), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2017;76:264](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27847125))
  + Stage by AJCC 2017 ([Am J Clin Dermatol 2021;22:639](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34125416))
    - 5 year disease specific survival (5Y DSS) decreased from 32.3% at stage I to 4.9% at stage IV
    - 5 year overall survival decreased from 73.6% to 3.9%, at stage I to stage IV, respectively
  + Other prognosticators
    - Age is a widely accepted independent prognostic factor for 5 year overall survival, disease free survival (DFS) and overall survival ([Obstet Gynecol 2007;110:296](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17666603), [Int J Gynecol Cancer 2013;23:1118](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23765206), [Lancet Oncol 2016;17:757](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27161539), [Clin Cancer Res 2017;23:2093](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27864417), [Urol Oncol 2016;34:166.e7](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26739672))

Treatment

* Vulvar melanoma
  + Clinical management is mainly based on cutaneous melanomas ([Eur J Cancer 2017:81:36](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28600969), [Int J Gynecol Cancer 2014;24:S117](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24987924), [Cancers (Basel) 2022;14:5217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36358637))
  + For localized disease: wide local excision (WLE) with adequate surgical margins
  + Sentinel lymph node biopsy (SLNB) use is recommended ([Cancers (Basel) 2022;14:5217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36358637))
    - If there is clinical or radiologic evidence of positive lymph nodes
    - If Breslow thickness is > 4 mm ([J Obstet Gynaecol Can 2019;41:762](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30391279), [Eur J Cancer 2020:135:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32531566), [Int J Gynecol Cancer 2014;24:S117](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24987924))
    - Benefit of SLNB in intermediate thickness (1 - 4 mm) is unclear ([Int J Gynecol Cancer 2019;29:1077](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31196869))
    - Bilateral SLNB is appropriate if the tumor is 2 cm from the midline ([Int J Gynecol Cancer 2019;29:1077](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31196869), [Cancers (Basel) 2022;14:5217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36358637))
    - If SLNB is positive, the benefit of complete groin dissection is currently unclear; it will be based on a multidisciplinary treatment plan and individual circumstances ([Cancers (Basel) 2022;14:5217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36358637))
    - DeCOG-SLT study suggested a complete groin dissection only for SLN metastases > 1 mm ([Lancet Oncol 2016;17:757](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27161539))
  + In case of inguinofemoral metastases, an elective lymph node dissection (ELND) should be considered ([Eur J Cancer 2020:135:22](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32531566), [Int J Gynecol Cancer 2014;24:S117](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24987924))
  + Data on adjuvant and neoadjuvant therapy in mucosal melanoma is based on retrospective case series, which makes the development of clinical guidelines challenging ([Cancers (Basel) 2022;14:5217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36358637), [Am J Cancer Res 2020;10:4017](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33414983))
  + Personalized therapy is based on the success of cutaneous melanoma treatment and is currently under investigation ([Cancers (Basel) 2022;14:5217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36358637), [Biomedicines 2021;9:758](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34209084), [Am J Cancer Res 2020;10:4017](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33414983))
    - Immunotherapy: programmed death 1 (PD-1), cytotoxic T lymphocyte antigen (CTLA4) checkpoint inhibitors show some success in locally advanced tumors and tumors with distant metastases; however, the clinical data is limited and the trials are ongoing ([Immunotargets Ther 2018:7:35](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29922629))
    - Potential targeted therapy: BRAF, c-KIT, and MEK inhibitors with c-KIT show limited success in comparison to cutaneous melanoma ([Ther Adv Med Oncol 2020:12:1758835920946158](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32821296), [N Engl J Med 2010;363:711](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20525992), [Br J Cancer 2010;102:1219](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20372153))
  + Chemotherapy: currently considered second line treatment ([Cancers (Basel) 2022;14:5217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36358637), [Am J Cancer Res 2020;10:4017](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33414983))
  + Radiation therapy plays a limited role and its utility remains to be investigated ([Cancers (Basel) 2022;14:5217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36358637), [Am J Cancer Res 2020;10:4017](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33414983))
  + See [diagram 1](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/vulvamelanoma.html#vulvamelanomadiag) for a summary of vulvar melanoma management ([Am J Cancer Res 2020;10:4017](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33414983))
* Vaginal melanoma
  + Complete local resection with adequate surgical margins (1 - 2 cm based on Breslow thickness) ([Ann Surg Oncol 2004;11:34](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14699031), [Ann Surg Oncol 2002;9:840](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12417504))
  + Radical surgery alone or in combination with adjuvant radiotherapy can be considered in cases where complete resection is not feasible, the tumor is closely approaching resection margin, the tumor is large (> 3 cm) or the lymph nodes are positive ([Ann Surg Oncol 2004;11:34](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/14699031), [J Gynecol Oncol 2013;24:330](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24167668))
  + Groin or pelvic lymphadenectomy is recommended with evidence of positive lymph nodes on clinical or radiologic evaluation ([Am J Cancer Res 2020;10:4017](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33414983))
  + SLNB utility is currently unclear ([Am J Cancer Res 2020;10:4017](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33414983))
  + Role of chemotherapy remains to be determined ([Am J Cancer Res 2020;10:4017](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33414983))
  + Personalized therapy may be considered with targetable mutations ([Biomedicines 2021;9:758](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/34209084))
    - Potential targeted therapy to be investigated: c-KIT, SF3B1, NRAS, BRAF
    - PD-1 and CTLA4 checkpoint inhibitors have been utilized with mixed success and their utility in specifically vaginal melanoma remains to be determined ([J Gynecol Oncol 2019;30:e94](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31576688), [J Low Genit Tract Dis 2021;25:146](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33252450), [Cancers (Basel) 2022;14:5217](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36358637), [J Eur Acad Dermatol Venereol 2018;32:e39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28750150))
  + See [diagram 2](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/vulvamelanoma.html#vulvamelanomadiag) for treatment strategies for vaginal melanoma ([Am J Cancer Res 2020;10:4017](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33414983))

Microscopic (histologic) description

* Vulvar melanoma
  + Per AJCC and College of American Pathologists (CAP), vulvar melanoma are staged as primary cutaneous melanomas
  + 25 - 30% of tumors are amelanotic ([Cancer 1999;86:1273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10506714), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2004;50:554](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15034504), [Dermatol Ther 2010;23:449](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20868400), [Melanoma Res 2008;18:127](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18337649))
  + Radial growth pattern (lentiginous / mucosal and superficial spreading) >> vertical growth pattern (nodular) > unclassified ([Cancer 1999;86:1273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10506714), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2012;67:598](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22243767))
  + Most tumors are asymmetric or ill defined lesions ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2018:37:91](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30312883))
  + Lentiginous pattern ([Cancer 1999;86:1273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10506714), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2012;67:598](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22243767), [Ann Diagn Pathol 2018:37:91](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30312883))
    - Solitary units of melanocytes with nuclear atypia irregularly distributed along the dermoepidermal junction (DEJ)
    - Occasional nests with skip areas and consumption of the rete ridges
    - Adnexal involvement by isolated melanocytes or small nests is common
    - Follicular extension along the epithelial stromal junction may be evident
    - Overlying epidermis may resemble lichen planus with
      * Hyperkeratosis and hypergranulosis
      * Focal epidermal atrophy
      * Dense lichenoid infiltrate
  + Superficial spreading type ([Cancer 1999;86:1273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10506714), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2012;67:598](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22243767), [Ann Diagn Pathol 2018:37:91](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30312883))
    - In situ component shows isolated cells and nests of atypical epithelioid melanocytes spreading into spinus layer in a pagetoid pattern
    - Variable pagetoid patterns ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2012;67:598](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22243767))
      * Amelanotic pagetoid melanoma in situ may closely resemble extramammary Paget disease and Bowen disease
      * Immunohistochemical studies will support a definitive diagnosis
    - Epithelioid morphology is most common, followed by mixture of epithelioid and spindle cells, although pure spindle cell melanomas have been described ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1518](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15489657))
    - Effacement of dermal rete with moth eaten appearance
    - Dermal component shows no maturation with severely atypical epithelioid or spindle cells with brisk mitoses forming sheets, nests, cords, single cells and rarely fascicles ([Cancer 1999;86:1273](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10506714), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2012;67:598](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22243767), [Ann Diagn Pathol 2018:37:91](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30312883))
  + Nodular subtype ([J Am Acad Dermatol 2013;68:568](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23182058))
    - In situ component should not extend beyond the periphery of the invasive tumor nodule; therefore, caution should be exercised in small biopsies
    - Excisional specimens are the most helpful to examine the entire epidermis adjacent the nodular component
    - May display a wide variation of architectural and cytologic features
      * Usually polypoid / exophytic but can present with endophytic / infiltrative growth
      * Nests with a mixture of fascicles and high cell density with solid growth pattern
    - Ulceration, necrosis and LVI are common
  + Desmoplastic (very rare but can be a diagnostic pitfall) ([Pathology 1997;29:241](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9213352), [Gynecol Oncol 1997;64:180](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8995571), [Pathologica 2011;103:337](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22558891))
    - May induce an extensive desmoplastic reaction ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1518](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15489657), [Ann Diagn Pathol 2018:37:91](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30312883))
    - Typically paucicellular with mild cytologic atypia and low mitotic activity
    - Pure desmoplastic melanoma contains single spindle cells arranged in small nests or short fascicles embedded in the desmoplastic stroma
    - Mixed / combined desmoplastic melanoma contains an additional epithelioid or spindled invasive dermal component ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2018:37:91](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30312883), [Pathologica 2011;103:337](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22558891))
    - Peripheral lymphoid aggregates on low power are a soft sign of desmoplastic melanoma ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2018:37:91](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30312883))
    - Perineural invasion is more common ([Am J Surg Pathol 2004;28:1518](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15489657), [Ann Diagn Pathol 2018:37:91](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30312883))
* Unless it is paucicellular (early onset) of lentiginous pattern, vulvar melanoma is usually associated with variable lymphocytic (typically lymphoplasmacytic) infiltrate
* In the absence of malignant cells, dermal fibrosis, increased vascularity and lymphocytic infiltrate are indicative of regression

Positive stains

* [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html) and [nerve growth factor receptor (NGFR)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsNGFR.html) are the most sensitive markers to visualize invasive melanoma
* Melanocytic markers
  + [MelanA (MART1)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsmart1.html) helps to highlight invasion and intraepidermal melanocytes ([J Cutan Pathol 2020;47:446](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31955450))
    - Caution: staining of dendritic processes from melanocytes wrapped around keratinocytes may lead to overestimation and false positive interpretation
  + [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html) ([Ann Diagn Pathol 2023:67:152211](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37717457), [Biomed Rep 2016;5:327](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27602212), [J Am Acad Dermatol 2012;67:446](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22285619))
    - Homogenous expression in primary conventional melanomas, especially epithelioid
    - Absence of gradient or maturation pattern (does not diminish from top to bottom)
    - May be weak or completely lost in spindle cell and desmoplastic melanomas
* [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html) highlights junctional melanocytes to help differentiate melanoma in situ from benign counterparts ([Appl Immunohistochem Mol Morphol 2014;22:142](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23958542))
* [PRAME](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsPRAME.html) (preferentially expressed antigen in melanoma) is typically diffusely and strongly expressed in melanomas ([J Cutan Pathol 2020;47:1123](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32700786))
* Melanomas of vulva usually lack *BRAF* mutation ([Br J Dermatol 2010;162:677](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19919630))

Negative stains

* [p16](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsp16.html): complete loss of expression is usually seen in melanomas
  + Its utility in differentiation between benign and malignant melanocytic lesions has been questioned

Molecular / cytogenetics description

* Current studies have not consistently separated melanomas arising from glabrous / mucosal from hair bearing vulva; hence, their molecular distinction is challenging ([J Low Genit Tract Dis 2023;27:40](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36083687))
* *KIT* mutations are the most common alterations in mucosal melanomas ([J Clin Oncol 2006;24:4340](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16908931), [Melanoma Res 2014;24:360](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24870295), [J Low Genit Tract Dis 2023;27:40](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36083687))
  + Vulvar melanoma > vaginal melanomas >> cutaneous melanomas
* *NRAS*, *NF1*, *TP53*, *TERT*, *CDKN2A*, *PTEN* have also been identified
* *BRAF* mutation in vulvovaginal melanomas ([J Low Genit Tract Dis 2023;27:40](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/36083687))
  + Rare and identified in
  + Fluorescence in situ hybridization (FISH) or comparative genomic hybridization to confirm or rule out melanoma
    - Sensitivity of both tests may be lower than expected due to decreased cellularity in vulvar lesions
    - For vulvar melanomas, *pTERT* and *CDK4* can be considered but may not be part of each FISH panel ([Nat Commun 2019;10:3163](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/31320640), [Am J Cancer Res 2020;10:4017](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/33414983))

Differential diagnosis

* [Atypical melanocytic nevus of genital type (AMNGT)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/vulvaAMNGT.html):
  + May be most challenging to differentiate from superficial spreading subtype of melanoma
  + Symmetrical lesion with dermal component maturation
  + Rare or very few atypical melanocytes in the epidermis as single cells with rare pagetoid spread, predominantly in the center of the lesion
  + [S100](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainss100.html), [SOX10](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainssox10.html) and [nerve growth factor receptor (NGFR)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsNGFR.html) fail to demonstrate invasive growth
  + [HMB45](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainshmb45.html) highlights a gradient pattern (diminishing or loss of expression from top to bottom)
  + [PRAME](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/stainsPRAME.html) is lost or very weakly expressed ([J Cutan Pathol 2020;47:1123](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/32700786))
  + *BRAF* is the most common alteration in AMNGT ([N Engl J Med 2015;373:1926](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26559571), [J Invest Dermatol 2016;136:1858](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27220476))
* [Pigmented epithelioid melanocytoma (PEM)](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/skintumormelanocyticpigmentedepithelioidmelanocytoma.html) of the vulva:
  + PEM family consists of multiple, usually slow growing, distinct histologic melanocytic entities with potential to metastasize but with a better prognosis than melanoma (WHO 5th edition)
  + Infiltrative deep dermal tumor that may involve subcutis
  + Hypercellular tumor with cells ranging from medium sized epithelioid cells to large epithelioid cells and spindled cells
  + Low mitotic activity
  + *PRKAR1A* loss in 67% of PEMs ([Am J Surg Pathol 2017;41:1333](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28796000), [Am J Surg Pathol 2019;43:480](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30475255))
* [Dysplastic nevus of vulva](https://www.pathologyoutlines.com/topic/vulvadysplasticnevi.html):
  + Differentiation requires clinical - pathologic correlates
  + Relative symmetry
  + Presence of junctional shoulders: extension of junctional component at least 3 rete ridges beyond the dermal component
  + Superficial nests are usually very similar and may show focal bridging or coalescence of the nests
  + Elongation and bridging of the rete ridges with nests
  + Melanocytes may scatter suprabasally (confined to the lower epidermal layer and centrally)
  + 2 tier grading of cytologic atypia is recommended by World Health Organization (WHO) classification and is largely based on nuclear features (WHO 5th edition) ([Hum Pathol 1999;30:500](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10333217))
